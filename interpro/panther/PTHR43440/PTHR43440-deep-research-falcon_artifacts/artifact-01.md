| urease activity | Appropriate for bona fide catalytic large/alpha subunits (UreC or fused eukaryotic catalytic homologs) that retain the conserved active-site architecture and are experimentally shown or strongly inferred to hydrolyze urea. | Not appropriate for accessory proteins (UreD/UreE/UreF/UreG), regulators (e.g., UreR), nickel transporters, or incomplete/pseudogenized homologs lacking catalytic residues. Use caution for highly divergent hits without the carbamylated-Lys/His/Asp motif. | The catalytic site resides in the alpha/large subunit and contains the dinuclear metal center; deletion of the structural urease gene abolishes growth on urea in fungi, and canonical bacterial/plant ureases all place catalysis in the large/fused catalytic chain. | Canonical bacterial UreC; fungal catalytic urease subunit in *Aspergillus fumigatus*; fused plant ureases. | (pqac-00000000, pqac-00000001, pqac-00000016, pqac-00000018) |
| urea catabolic process | Appropriate when the encoded UreC is part of a functional urease system used for urea utilization or pH-altering ureolysis in vivo. | Avoid for accessory proteins alone, isolated nonfunctional homologs, or sequences in genomes where urease function is uncertain. If only biochemical activity is known in vitro, organism-level process annotation should be added cautiously. | Urease catalyzes urea breakdown to ammonia and carbamate/bicarbonate; loss of catalytic structural genes abolishes growth on urea as sole nitrogen source in tested systems. | *A. fumigatus* urease system; *Proteus mirabilis* urease in urinary tract; nitrifiers/anammox with bona fide urease operons. | (pqac-00000000, pqac-00000009, pqac-00000010, pqac-00000028) |
| nickel ion binding | Appropriate for canonical nickel urease alpha/large subunits with conserved dinuclear nickel center and supporting biochemical/phylogenetic context; especially strong when nearby maturation genes or direct structural data support Ni usage. | Do not propagate blindly to all descendants. Exclude or qualify for iron urease paralogs, very divergent homologs, or cases where metal specificity is not demonstrated. Presence/absence of UreE alone is not decisive, since eukaryotes can be nickel ureases without UreE. | Canonical ureases bind two Ni2+ ions bridged by carbamylated Lys; however, iron-containing ureases exist and preserve overall urease architecture while changing metal identity. | Canonical *Klebsiella*, *Yersinia*, *Helicobacter pylori* ureases; fungal/plant ureases remain nickel-dependent despite lacking UreE. | (pqac-00000000, pqac-00000001, pqac-00000013, pqac-00000014, pqac-00000018) |
| iron ion binding | Appropriate only for experimentally supported iron-containing urease paralogs or very close orthologs in the iron-urease clade. | Not appropriate for the great majority of canonical ureases, which are nickel enzymes. Do not infer from general urease family membership alone. | A distinct oxygen-labile ferrous urease class has been described; it retains urease-like architecture but uses Fe instead of Ni and can differ in maturation requirements. | *Helicobacter mustelae* UreA2B2 iron urease paralog; possible related *Helicobacter* dual-cluster systems. | (pqac-00000008, pqac-00000014, pqac-00000015) |
| metalloenzyme maturation / urease maturation involvement | Generally NOT appropriate for UreC itself as a direct gene-product function term; reserve maturation-process roles primarily for accessory proteins that catalyze or mediate metal insertion. | Do not annotate UreC with chaperone/metallocenter-assembly functions unless there is specific evidence of a noncanonical moonlighting role. UreC is the target of maturation, not the maturation factor. | Nickel insertion and lysine carbamylation are mediated by UreD/UreH, UreE, UreF, UreG (or lineage-specific alternatives), whereas UreC houses the nascent active site receiving the metals. | Bacterial UreD/E/F/G systems; fungal/plant UreD/F/G-like maturation without UreE. | (pqac-00000000, pqac-00000001, pqac-00000016, pqac-00000018, pqac-00000020) |
| GTPase activity | Exclude for UreC. | Never appropriate for the urease alpha/large catalytic subunit based on current evidence; belongs to UreG, not UreC. | GTP binding/hydrolysis regulates nickel delivery through UreG during maturation; this function is accessory and separate from the catalytic urease subunit. | UreG in bacterial and eukaryotic maturation pathways. | (pqac-00000000, pqac-00000001, pqac-00000016, pqac-00000020) |
| structural constituent vs accessory protein distinction | Annotate UreC as catalytic structural subunit of urease complex where the multimeric enzyme architecture is known. | Do not confuse UreC with small structural subunits (UreA/UreB) or accessory proteins; avoid assigning catalytic urease activity to UreD/E/F/G or transport/regulatory proteins. | The active site is in the large/alpha subunit, while small subunits contribute assembly/context and accessory proteins mediate maturation rather than catalysis. | *Y. enterocolitica* UreA/UreB/UreC hetero-oligomer; plant/fungal single-chain fusions; bacterial accessory proteins. | (pqac-00000001, pqac-00000004, pqac-00000006, pqac-00000018) |
| nickel-chaperone / nickel transfer annotations | Exclude for UreC. | Not appropriate for the catalytic large subunit, even when it binds nickel in the finished enzyme. Nickel donor/chaperone roles belong to UreE, UreG, HypA/HypB, or lineage-specific maturation proteins. | Evidence supports ordered nickel handoff from accessory proteins to apourease; UreC is the recipient active-site scaffold, not the nickel carrier. | Bacterial UreE→UreG→UreF/UreD→urease pathway; *H. pylori* HypA/HypB-linked maturation; fungal UreG compensation for absent UreE. | (pqac-00000001, pqac-00000016, pqac-00000019, pqac-00000020) |
| broad substrate specificity notes | Keep core annotation as urease activity even where side activity toward ethyl carbamate/urethane is reported, unless a curated ontology term specifically captures the secondary activity and evidence is strong. | Do not replace canonical urease annotation with a different catalytic term solely because low-level urethanase side activity is observed in one lineage. Treat as possible subfamily-specific add-on annotation pending broader confirmation. | Recombinant *Providencia rettgeri* urease showed lower but measurable urethanase activity, best interpreted as broadened specificity around the canonical urease scaffold rather than wholesale family-wide functional reassignment. | Acid urease from *Providencia rettgeri* JN-B815. | (pqac-00000023, pqac-00000024, pqac-00000025) |
| phylogeny-based caution for propagation | Core urease annotations are usually safe within conserved catalytic UreC clades that retain hallmark residues and occur in complete urease loci. | Use caution for long-branch or apparently horizontally transferred UreC homologs in anammox/nitrifier lineages until metal usage and physiological role are confirmed; avoid over-propagating nickel-specific or maturation-specific terms from distant homologs. | UreC phylogenies show distinct anammox clusters and branches separate from canonical nitrifier groups, suggesting independent acquisition and potentially lineage-specific biology despite conserved catalytic residues. | *Scalindua sediminis* and ETNP *Scalindua* ureC branches distinct from Thaumarchaeota, AOB, NOB. | (pqac-00000027, pqac-00000028) |
