# PANTHER Family Research Report: PTHR30633 (InterPro IPR003321) — “Cytochrome c-552 respiratory nitrite reductase” family

## 1) Executive Summary
PANTHER family PTHR30633 (InterPro IPR003321) corresponds to a group of small c-type cytochromes commonly referred to as cytochrome c552/c551 (often encoded by **nirM**) and related proteins (e.g., **nirC**, sometimes **nirN**-associated cytochrome c domains) that function primarily as **periplasmic electron-transfer proteins**, not as catalytic nitrite reductases. In *Pseudomonas aeruginosa*, **NirM and NirC are experimentally demonstrated electron donors to cd1-type nitrite reductase NirS (NIR)** and have measurable kinetic parameters for electron transfer and nitrite reduction in vitro; nirM/nirC mutants show strongly reduced NIR activity. (hasegawa2001twoctypecytochromes pages 5-7, hasegawa2001twoctypecytochromes pages 3-5)

However, the family is **functionally heterogeneous across taxa**. In *Paracoccus denitrificans*, cytochrome c552 is described as a **membrane-bound/anchored electron mediator between cytochrome bc1 (complex III) and cytochrome aa3 oxidase (complex IV)**, and genetic evidence indicates it is **dispensable for denitrification**, implying specialization away from being a NirS electron donor in that organism. (pristovsek2000solutionstructureof pages 1-2, pearson2003amutantof pages 6-7)

A key recent development relevant to evolutionary interpretation is that **c552-family cytochromes are proposed evolutionary precursors** to larger multiheme cytochromes involved in extracellular electron transfer (EET) such as **PioA/MtoA/MtrA** in iron redox pathways, suggesting expansion/repurposing of the c-type cytochrome electron-transfer scaffold beyond denitrification. (li2023sequencesimilaritynetwork pages 16-17, li2023sequencesimilaritynetwork pages 1-2)

**Implication for annotation:** the current family name (“respiratory nitrite reductase”) risks conflating **electron donor proteins** (NirM/NirC/c552) with the **catalytic nitrite reductase NirS**. Blanket propagation of catalytic “nitrite reductase activity” (EC-class/GO MF) from ancestral nodes to all descendants is therefore likely inappropriate. (hasegawa2001twoctypecytochromes pages 5-7, zumft1997cellbiologyand pages 6-7, pearson2003amutantof pages 6-7)

## 2) Key Concepts and Definitions (current understanding)

### 2.1 Denitrification and the role of small periplasmic electron carriers
Denitrification is a form of anaerobic respiration in which nitrate is reduced stepwise to N2 via nitrite, NO, and N2O by nitrate reductase (NAR), nitrite reductase (NIR), nitric oxide reductase (NOR), and nitrous oxide reductase (N2OR). (hasegawa2001twoctypecytochromes pages 1-2)

Within denitrification gene clusters (nir/nor regions), organisms often encode not only the catalytic reductases but also **electron donors, cofactor synthesis/maturation proteins, and regulators**—i.e., a functional module that ensures both assembly and electron supply. (zumft1997cellbiologyand pages 6-7)

### 2.2 What is “cytochrome c552 / NirM”?
In *Pseudomonas aeruginosa*, **nirM encodes cytochrome c551 (cytochrome c8)**, described as a **physiological electron donor** to cd1 nitrite reductase NirS (NIR). (hasegawa2001twoctypecytochromes pages 1-2)

A broader “cytochrome c552” designation is used in multiple bacteria for small c-type cytochromes that shuttle electrons in the periplasm. In *Paracoccus denitrificans*, cytochrome c552 is characterized as an **electron mediator between complex III (cytochrome bc1) and complex IV (aa3-type cytochrome c oxidase)**. (pristovsek2000solutionstructureof pages 1-2)

### 2.3 Structural fold shared by family members
For *P. denitrificans* cytochrome c552, an NMR structure of the functional soluble domain (100 residues; 10.5 kDa) reports a **helix-rich cytochrome c fold**: two long terminal helices plus three shorter helices surrounding the heme, connected by defined loops. (pristovsek2000solutionstructureof pages 1-2)

### 2.4 Typical cofactors, motifs, and axial ligation
Cytochrome c552 is a **c-type heme protein** with covalent thioether linkages at a **conserved CXYCH motif**. The heme iron alternates between Fe(II)/Fe(III) states and is **octahedrally coordinated** by the porphyrin nitrogens plus **axial His and Met ligands** (a common c-type cytochrome ligation scheme). (pristovsek2000solutionstructureof pages 1-2)

In *P. aeruginosa*, NirM and NirC are experimentally verified as c-type cytochromes by heme staining/spectroscopy and are processed as periplasmic proteins with cleaved signal peptides, consistent with periplasmic electron-carrier function. (hasegawa2001twoctypecytochromes pages 3-5, hasegawa2001twoctypecytochromes pages 1-2)

### 2.5 Binding partners and electron-transfer context
In the *P. aeruginosa* nir operon, nirS (NIR) is followed by genes encoding small c-type cytochromes (nirM, nirC, nirN), supporting the concept that the structural gene for NIR is adjacent to genes for electron donors and related accessory factors. (hasegawa2001twoctypecytochromes pages 1-2)

## 3) Ancestral/Core Function Inference
Across the strongest direct evidence in this corpus, the shared/core function best supported for IPR003321/PTHR30633 members is **periplasmic electron transfer via heme c**. This electron-transfer role can feed different terminal enzymes depending on organismal context:

* In denitrifiers like *Pseudomonas*, the electron acceptor is cd1 nitrite reductase NirS (NIR). (hasegawa2001twoctypecytochromes pages 5-7)
* In *Paracoccus*, a homologous cytochrome c552 domain mediates electron transfer from bc1 to aa3 oxidase (aerobic respiration). (pristovsek2000solutionstructureof pages 1-2)

Thus, the most defensible “ancestral” activity at the family level is “c-type cytochrome electron carrier” rather than a specific denitrification enzyme activity. (pristovsek2000solutionstructureof pages 1-2, zumft1997cellbiologyand pages 6-7)

## 4) Subfamily (Functional Group) Analysis
PANTHER indicates “0 subfamilies” for PTHR30633, but the literature supports at least **functional groupings** that behave like subfamilies.

### Group A: NirM (cytochrome c551/c552) — electron donor to cd1 nitrite reductase NirS
**Function:** Periplasmic electron donor to NirS (cd1 nitrite reductase; “NIR”). (hasegawa2001twoctypecytochromes pages 1-2, hasegawa2001twoctypecytochromes pages 5-7)

**Experimental support:** Hasegawa et al. (2001) show that NirM mediates electron donation from membrane electron pathways to NIR and increases nitrite consumption in reconstituted assays. (hasegawa2001twoctypecytochromes pages 5-7)

**Quantitative data (kinetics/statistics):** Reported Km values include NIR-as-cytochrome-c-oxidase Km of **16.7 mM** for NirM; NIR nitrite-reductase Km with NirM **55 mM** (as reported in the excerpt). (hasegawa2001twoctypecytochromes pages 5-7)

**Mutant phenotype:** nirM deletion strongly reduces in vivo/in vitro NIR activities (e.g., in one dataset: in vivo 13% of WT; in vitro 23% of WT). (hasegawa2001twoctypecytochromes pages 3-5)

**EC number:** Not explicitly stated in the retrieved evidence snippets for NirM itself; NirM is an electron-transfer protein rather than a catalytic enzyme with a conventional EC number. (hasegawa2001twoctypecytochromes pages 5-7)

### Group B: NirC — auxiliary electron donor and/or maturation-linked cytochrome
**Function:** Acts as an electron donor to NIR in vitro and can accept electrons from membrane fractions; may also have roles related to NIR maturation/d1-heme related processes depending on organism and mutation design. (hasegawa2001twoctypecytochromes pages 5-7, hasegawa2001twoctypecytochromes pages 1-2)

**Quantitative data:** NIR-as-cytochrome-c-oxidase Km of **28.3 mM** for NirC is reported; NirC’s apparent Km for nitrite reductase is much higher (not saturable in excerpt; estimated ~10× higher than NirM). (hasegawa2001twoctypecytochromes pages 5-7)

**Mutant phenotype:** nirC mutants show decreased NIR activity; Hasegawa et al. discuss that some activity decreases could relate to heme d1 availability in mutants. (hasegawa2001twoctypecytochromes pages 3-5, hasegawa2001twoctypecytochromes pages 2-3)

### Group C: NirN-associated cytochrome c proteins — accessory roles rather than primary NirS donors
Evidence indicates NirN is a cytochrome c–containing protein (or contains cytochrome c domains) in nir clusters, but in the *P. aeruginosa* system, NirN was **not significantly engaged** in the same electron transfer route to NIR as NirM/NirC in vitro. (hasegawa2001twoctypecytochromes pages 5-7)

Review-level synthesis notes NirN encodes a larger protein with an N-terminal cytochrome c domain and that experimental data argue NirN is **not an alternative cd1 nitrite reductase**. (bertini2006cytochromecoccurrence pages 14-15)

### Group D: Paracoccus-type cytochrome c552 — oxidase electron mediator (not denitrification electron donor)
In *P. denitrificans*, cytochrome c552 is described as the genuine electron mediator between complexes III and IV; deletion or antibody inhibition suppresses membrane electron transfer in that aerobic respiratory context. (pristovsek2000solutionstructureof pages 1-2)

Genetic/physiological evidence indicates this cytochrome c552 is **not required for denitrification**, supporting that it is not the key electron donor to cd1 nitrite reductase in this organism. (pearson2003amutantof pages 6-7)

## 5) Functional Divergence: Neo-functionalization and Subfunctionalization

### 5.1 Subfunctionalization within denitrification modules (NirM vs NirC)
Within *Pseudomonas*, NirM and NirC both donate electrons to NirS, but with different kinetic behavior and potentially different interaction determinants (sequence features discussed in Hasegawa et al.). This is consistent with **subfunctionalization** among periplasmic electron carriers in the same pathway (e.g., primary vs auxiliary donors). (hasegawa2001twoctypecytochromes pages 5-7)

### 5.2 Divergence away from denitrification: oxidase-focused cytochrome c552
The *Paracoccus* cytochrome c552 system indicates **functional repurposing** (or retention of a respiratory role outside denitrification): electron transfer between bc1 and aa3 oxidase, with a surface/charge rationale proposed for why it may not productively interact with cd1 nitrite reductase. (pearson2003amutantof pages 6-7, pristovsek2000solutionstructureof pages 1-2)

### 5.3 Neo-functionalization toward extracellular electron transfer (EET) systems (recent development)
A 2023 mSystems study discusses that **PioA/MtoA/MtrA (multiheme EET cytochromes)** may have evolved from **c552-family cytochromes**, suggesting expansion of this electron-transfer scaffold into outer-membrane/extracellular contexts for iron redox metabolisms. (li2023sequencesimilaritynetwork pages 16-17, li2023sequencesimilaritynetwork pages 1-2)

This supports the existence of descendants within a broader “c552-like” scaffold space that participate in **iron oxidation/electron transfer** rather than nitrite respiration. (li2023sequencesimilaritynetwork pages 16-17)

## 6) Branch Length / Evolutionary Divergence (evidence-based constraints)
The provided evidence does not include explicit PANTHER phylogenetic branch lengths or a curated family tree for PTHR30633, so branch-length-based inference cannot be performed directly here.

Nevertheless, the observed functional splits—(i) NirS electron donors in denitrifying operons versus (ii) aa3 oxidase electron mediator in *Paracoccus* versus (iii) proposed evolutionary precursors of EET multiheme cytochromes—support substantial divergence in physiological context and interaction partners even when the core heme-c electron-transfer fold is conserved. (hasegawa2001twoctypecytochromes pages 5-7, pristovsek2000solutionstructureof pages 1-2, li2023sequencesimilaritynetwork pages 16-17)

## 7) GO Annotation Assessment and Recommendations

### 7.1 Likely problematic propagated annotations
**Problem:** The family name suggests “respiratory nitrite reductase,” but multiple primary sources define NirM/NirC/c552 proteins as **electron donors/electron carriers**, not the catalytic nitrite reductase NirS. (hasegawa2001twoctypecytochromes pages 1-2, zumft1997cellbiologyand pages 6-7)

**Risk:** If GO molecular function terms such as “nitrite reductase activity” are propagated to all members of IPR003321/PTHR30633, they will be incorrect for at least:
* *Paracoccus denitrificans* cytochrome c552 (oxidase mediator; dispensable for denitrification). (pearson2003amutantof pages 6-7, pristovsek2000solutionstructureof pages 1-2)
* NirC/NirN-like proteins with maturation/accessory roles rather than being terminal reductases. (hasegawa2001twoctypecytochromes pages 1-2, bertini2006cytochromecoccurrence pages 14-15)

### 7.2 Recommended family-level GO framing
Based on the evidence, a safer baseline is:
* **Electron transfer activity / cytochrome c activity** (heme c binding; periplasmic electron carrier role), and only secondarily a context-specific biological process term when supported by genomic context and experiment. (pristovsek2000solutionstructureof pages 1-2, zumft1997cellbiologyand pages 6-7)

### 7.3 Context-dependent annotations (recommended)
* Apply “electron donor to cd1 nitrite reductase NirS / denitrification electron transport” annotations **only** when:
  * genes are in nir clusters and/or
  * experimental evidence supports NirS electron donation (as in *P. aeruginosa* NirM/NirC). (hasegawa2001twoctypecytochromes pages 5-7, hasegawa2001twoctypecytochromes pages 3-5)
* Apply “electron transfer to cytochrome c oxidase (aa3)” or similar respiratory chain annotations for *Paracoccus*-type c552 when supported. (pristovsek2000solutionstructureof pages 1-2, pearson2003amutantof pages 6-7)
* Avoid assigning catalytic nitrite reductase activity to NirM/NirC/c552 proteins; NirS is the catalytic nitrite reductase. (hasegawa2001twoctypecytochromes pages 1-2, zumft1997cellbiologyand pages 6-7)

Direct industrial/clinical implementations were not a focus of the retrieved sources, but two application-relevant contexts are supported:

## 9) Expert Opinions / Authoritative Syntheses

* Zumft’s 1997 MMBR review emphasizes that denitrification gene clusters encode not only reductases but also electron donors and maturation machinery, and identifies nirM as a small c-type cytochrome electron donor to NirS in *Pseudomonas* contexts. (zumft1997cellbiologyand pages 6-7)
