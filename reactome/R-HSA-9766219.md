# MDM2 binds CDH1
**Reactome ID:** [R-HSA-9766219](https://reactome.org/content/detail/R-HSA-9766219)
**Species:** Homo sapiens

## Summary

<p>CDH1 (E-cadherin) was first identified as a binding partner of the E3 ubiquitin ligase MDM2 (HDM2) in human breast cancer cell line MDA-MB-453 (Yang et al. 2006). The interaction between MDM2 and CDH1 was demonstrated between both endogenous proteins and in-vitro translated MDM2 and purified GST-CDH1, suggesting a direct interaction, independent of a mediator protein (Yang et al. 2006). The interaction between endogenous CDH1 and MDM2 was confirmed in human breast cancer cell lines MCF-7 and MDA-MB-231 (Adhikary et al. 2014) and in human hepatocellular carcinoma cell lines Huh7 and HepG2 (Lu et al. 2015). By immunoelectron microscopy, CDH1 and MDM2 co-localize at the cell surface (Yang et al. 2006).</p><p>BANP (SMAR1), which primarily localizes to the nucleus but is also found in the cytosol (Adhikary et al. 2014, Human Protein Atlas - Thul et al. 2017), was reported to bind to MDM2 in the cytosol and prevent it from binding CDH1 (Adhikary et al. 2014). BANP may also increase CDH1 protein levels indirectly, by repressing transcription of SNAI2 (Slug), which acts as a repressor of the CDH1 gene (Adhikary et al. 2014).</p><p>MTBP, an MDM2-binding nuclear protein (Human Protein Atlas - Thul et al. 2017), is frequently overexpressed in human hepatocellular carcinoma, where its protein level is inversely correlated with the protein level of CDH1 (Lu et al. 2015). MTPB promotes MDM2-mediated ubiquitination and degradation of CDH1 by preventing ubiquitination and proteasome-mediated degradation of MDM2 (Lu et al. 2015).</p><p>MDM2 localizes to the plasma membrane on human and mouse acute leukemia (AML) cells, including leukemia stem cells (LSCs), but not to the membrane of normal hematopoietic stem cells (HSCs) (Wang et al. 2020). A synthetic peptide, PNC-27, binds to MDM2 at the plasma membrane and increases its interaction with CDH1, also leading to an increase in CDH1 ubiquitination (Wang et al. 2020). PNC-27 treatment leads to formation of membrane pores in AML cells which promote cell death and are proposed to be a consequence of CDH1 degradation (Wang et al. 2020).</p><p>In prostate carcinoma cell lines, a long noncoding RNA (lncRNA) UCA1 was reported to associate with CDH1 and prevent its association with MDM2 and MDM2-mediated CDH1 ubiquitination and subsequent degradation (Zhao et al. 2020).</p><p>It has not been investigated whether CDH1 association with catenins is affected by MDM2 binding or whether endogenous catenins, associated with CDH1, are present in the complex of MDM2 and CDH1. Therefore, MDM2 binding to CDH1 in complex with CTNNB1 (beta-catenin) or JUP (plakoglobin, gamma-catenin) is shown, but catenins are not shown as components of the CDH1:MDM2 complex.</p>
