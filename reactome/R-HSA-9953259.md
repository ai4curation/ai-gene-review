# EXTL3 dimer transfers GlcNAc to the GAG linker
**Reactome ID:** [R-HSA-9953259](https://reactome.org/content/detail/R-HSA-9953259)
**Species:** Homo sapiens

## Summary

Exostosin-like 2 (EXTL2) and Exostosin-like 3 (EXTL3) dimer transfer an N-acetylglucosamine moiety from UDP-GlcNAc to a GlcA-Gal-Gal-Xyl moiety, which is the GAG-linker chain on HS-/DS-/CS-proteoglycans (Kitagawa et al., 1999; Kim et al., 2001; Wilson et al., 2022). Mutations in EXTL3 can cause immunoskeletal dysplasia (ISDNA; MIM:617425; Oud et al., 2017; Volpi et al., 2017).
