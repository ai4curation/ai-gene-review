# miR-9 inhibits CDH1 mRNA translation
**Reactome ID:** [R-HSA-9768614](https://reactome.org/content/detail/R-HSA-9768614)
**Species:** Homo sapiens

## Summary

Ectopic expression of miR-9 in epithelial cell lines HMLE (Human Mammary Epithelial Cells) and SUM149 (human breast cancer cells) significantly reduces CDH1 (E-cadherin) protein levels and leads to upregulation of markers of epithelial-to-mesenchymal transition (EMT) (Ma et al. 2010). Inhibition of miR-9 expression by Antagomir-9 in HEK293 cells (Human Embryonic Kidney cells) leads to increase in CDH1 protein levels (Liu et al. 2013). CDH1 mRNA levels, determined by real-time RT-PCR, in hepatocellular carcinoma cell lines SNU-449 and HepG2 transfected with miR-9 are decreased and they increase when cells are transfected with anti-miR-9 (Drakaki et al. 2015). IL10 (Interleukin-10) negatively regulates miR-9 expression, leading to upregulation of CDH1 protein levels by immunoblotting (Nishioka et al. 2017). miR-9 was shown to reduce the activity of a luciferase reporter fused to the wild-type 3′ UTR of the CDH1 mRNA but not to a mutant 3′UTR carrying altered nucleotides in the miR-9 ‘seed-pairing’ recognition site. This implies that downregulation of CDH1 by miR-9 depends directly on a single cognate recognition site in the CDH1 mRNA 3′UTR (Ma et al. 2010; Liu et al. 2013; Drakaki et al. 2015).
