# ACER1 hydrolyzes ceramide (endoplasmic reticulum)
**Reactome ID:** [R-HSA-428231](https://reactome.org/content/detail/R-HSA-428231)
**Species:** Homo sapiens

## Summary

ACER1 (alkaline ceramidase 1) catalyzes the reversible hydrolysis of ceramide to yield a free fatty acid and sphingosine (Sun et al. 2008). ACER1 hydrolyses C20:0-C24:0 ceramides, localizes to the endoplasmic reticulum membrane, and is mainly expressed in skin tissue (Casasampere et al., 2015). ACER1 is believed to be a key regulator of keratinocyte differentiation and has a fundamental role in skin barrier formation (Liakath-Ali et al., 2016; reviewed by Mao & Obeid, 2008; Parveen et al., 2019; Duarte et al., 2020).
