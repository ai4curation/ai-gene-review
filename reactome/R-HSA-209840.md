# Two DITs combine to form thyroxine
**Reactome ID:** [R-HSA-209840](https://reactome.org/content/detail/R-HSA-209840)
**Species:** Homo sapiens

## Summary

TPO catalyzes the transfer of a 4-hydroxy-3,5-diiodophenyl moiety from a donor diiodotyrosine (DIT) residue to an acceptor DIT residue, consuming H2O2 and forming T4 on the acceptor and dehydroalanine on the donor residue (Taurog et al., 1996). A total of seven T4 molecules are synthesized per TG dimer: each dimer has two of the Tyr-24, Tyr-1310, and Tyr-2573 acceptor residues (one per monomer) and a single Tyr-2766 acceptor at the dimer interface (Coscia et al., 2020). This is also called the thyroid hormonogenic coupling reaction.<br><br>TPO is part of the thyroxisome, a complex consisting of at least Dual oxidase (DUOX1/2) (Fortunato et al., 2010), Dual oxidase maturation factor 1/2 (DUOXA1/2) (Grasberger & Refetoff, 2006) and Thioredoxin domain-containing protein 11 (TXNDC11, EFP1) (Wang et al., 2005), which is bound to Caveolin-1 (CAV1) (Senou et al., 2009) structures on the extracellular side of the apical plasma membrane of thyroid cells (reviewed in Godlevska & Banga, 2019; Jing & Zhang, 2022).<br><br>Mutations in the TPO gene can cause Thyroid dyshormonogenesis 2A (TDH2A; MIM:274500; see, e.g. Calaciura et al., 2002; Wu et al., 2002).
