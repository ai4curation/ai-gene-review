# ABL1 phosphorylates PPARG2
**Reactome ID:** [R-HSA-9841924](https://reactome.org/content/detail/R-HSA-9841924)
**Species:** Homo sapiens

## Summary

ABL1 phosphorylates PPARG isoform PPARG2 on evolutionarily conserved tyrosine residues Y78 and Y102. While these tyrosine residues are present in the PPARG isoform PPARG1, PPARG1 is only poorly phosphorylated by ABL1, likely because PPARG1 lacks the PxxP motif involved in ABL1 binding to PPARG2. ABL1-mediated phosphorylation of PPARG2 stabilizes PPARG2 protein likely by interfering with ubiquitin-mediated proteasome degradation of PPARG2 (Keshet et al. 2014).<br><br>In mouse liver, ABL1 and PPARG2 levels are increased in response to high fat diet and ABL1-mediated phosphorylation of PPARG2 is implicated in increased transcriptional activity of PPARG2 which is involved in hepatic steatosis - accumulation of triglycerides within hepatocytes (Kim et al. 2016).
