# Removal of CDH1 signal peptide
**Reactome ID:** [R-HSA-9932162](https://reactome.org/content/detail/R-HSA-9932162)
**Species:** Homo sapiens

## Summary

<p>CDH1 (also known as E-cadherin, epithelial cadherin, Cadherin-1, CADH1, or uvomorulin) contains a signal peptide at the very N-terminus (Herrenknecht and Kemler 1993; Figueiredo et al. 2018) that is likely cleaved by the signal peptidase complex in the endoplasmic reticulum membrane (Liaci et al. 2021), although no direct evidence is available.</p><p>CDH1 gene mutations that disrupt the signal sequence have been reported in the hereditary diffuse gastric cancer and found to interfere with CDH1 translation and translocation to the endoplasmic reticulum (Figueiredo et al. 2018).</p>
