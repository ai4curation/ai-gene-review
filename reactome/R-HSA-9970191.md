# RBBP6 activates CPSF complex
**Reactome ID:** [R-HSA-9970191](https://reactome.org/content/detail/R-HSA-9970191)
**Species:** Homo sapiens

## Summary

<p>RBBP6 activates the endonuclease activity of CPSF3, a component of the CPSF complex (Boreikaite et al. 2022; Schmidt et al. 2022). RBBP6 interacts with CPSF3 (Boreikaite et al. 2022; Schmidt et al. 2022) through the DWNN domain of RBBP6 (Schmidt et al. 2022). CPSF3-mediated cleavage of pre-mRNA at a polyadenylation site (PAS) is also stimulated by binding of ATP to CLP1, a subunit of the Cleavage Factor II complex (CF II), but ATP hydrolysis by CLP1 is not required (Schmidt et al. 2022). While Schmidt et al. 2022 reported that the ubiquitin ligase activity of RBBP6 was not required for CPSF3-mediated cleavage of pre-mRNA, Lin et al. 2024 reported that RBBP6-mediated K63-linked ubiquitination of CPSF3 leads to CPSF3 stabilization, resulting in an overall increase of CPSF3 activity.</p><p>SUMOylation of CPSF3 and SYMPK (symplekin) has also been reported to affect the activity of the cleavage and polyadenylation complex (CPA) (Vethantham et al. 2007).</p>
