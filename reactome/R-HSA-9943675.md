# GSK3B phosphorylates CDC25A
**Reactome ID:** [R-HSA-9943675](https://reactome.org/content/detail/R-HSA-9943675)
**Species:** Homo sapiens

## Summary

<p>GSK3B phosphorylates CDC25A on serine residue S76, which is localized within the minimal GSK3B consensus motif (S/T)XXX(S/T) (Kang et al. 2008). GSK3B-mediated phosphorylation of CDC25A at S76, but not CHEK1-mediated phosphorylation of CDC25A at S76, requires a priming phosphorylation at threonine residue T80 by PLK3 (Kang et al. 2008).</p><p>Endogenous and ectopically expressed CDC25A accumulates in various human cell lines (HeLa, U2OS, H460, A549, MCF-7, Saos2, BT549) treated with GSK3B inhibitors, and the accumulation is more pronounced in the presence of proteasome inhibitor MG132 (Kang et al. 2008). CDC25A levels are reduced in cells expressing constitutively active GSK3B and increased in cells expressing kinase-dead GSK3B (Kang et al. 2008). Knockdown of GSK3B by siRNA leads to CDC25A stabilization (Kang et al. 2008, Honaker and Piwnica-Worms 2010) similarly to CHEK1 knockdown by siRNA (Kang et al. 2008). GSK3B inhibition or siRNA mediated knockdown prolongs the half-life of CDC25A in cells exposed to ionizing radiation (IR) (Kang et al. 2008). In vivo, GSK3B and CHEK1 cooperate in regulating CDC25A proteolysis both in the presence and absence of genotoxic stress (Kang et al. 2008).</p>
