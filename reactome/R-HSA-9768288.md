# RB1 and TFAP2A bind CDH1 gene promoter
**Reactome ID:** [R-HSA-9768288](https://reactome.org/content/detail/R-HSA-9768288)
**Species:** Homo sapiens

## Summary

The retinoblastoma tumor suppressor protein RB1 and the transcription factor TFAP2A were shown to bind the promoter of the E-cadherin (CDH1) gene together in several different studies. Re-expression of the transcription factor TFAP2A in TFAP2A-negative human colon cancer cell line SW480 results in upregulation of E-cadherin (CDH1) levels, which requires direct binding of TFAP2A to the CDH1 gene promoter (Schwartz et al. 2007). Depletion of RB1 tumor suppressor in human RB1-positive breast cancer cell line MCF7 disrupts cell-cell adhesion and induces epithelial-to-mesenchymal transition (EMT) (Arima et al. 2008). In MCF7 cells, RB1 binds to the CDH1 gene promoter in association with TFAP2A (Decary et al. 2002; Arima et al. 2008). RB1 and TFAP2A co-immunoprecipitate with the promoter of the CDH1 gene in Madin-Darby canine kidney cell line (MDCK) and immortalized human keratinocytes (HaCat cell line) (Batsche et al. 1998). The in vitro interaction between TFAP2A and RB1 requires the N-terminal domain of TFAP2A and the oncoprotein and C-terminal domain of RB1. The SV40 large T-antigen can co-immunoprecipitate TFAP2A only in the presence of RB1, which directly binds to the large T-antigen (Batsche et al. 1998). TFAP2A directly binds to the mouse Cdh1 gene promoter in mouse epidermal keratinocytes (Faraldo et al. 1997).
