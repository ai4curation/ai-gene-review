# U1 snRNP suppresses CPSF3-mediated pre-mRNA cleavage at intronic PAS
**Reactome ID:** [R-HSA-9970282](https://reactome.org/content/detail/R-HSA-9970282)
**Species:** Homo sapiens

## Summary

<p>CPSF3 (also known as CPSF73) is the endonuclease that cleaves the pre-mRNA downstream from the polyadenylation (PAS) site, which is a prerequisite for polyadenylation (Ryan et al. 2004; Mandel et al. 2006). The identity of the endonuclease of the cleavage and polyadenylation (CPA) complex remained elusive for a long time because of the dependence of the cleavage on many CPA components that recognize the PAS site and sequences upstream and downstream from it (Ryan et al. 2004; Mandel et al. 2006). CPSF3 is a member of the metallo-beta-lactamase family of Zn2+-dependent hydrolases and requires Zn2+ for catalytic activity (Ryan et al. 2004; Mandel et al. 2006), with two Zn2+ ions bound to its active site (Mandel et al. 2006).</p><p>XRN2 (5'-3' exoribonuclease 2) was reported as an enzyme involved in degradation of the 3' cleavage fragment, which helps to terminate transcription by RNA Polymerase II (RNA Pol II) in a CPSF3 (also known as CPSF73)- and PP1-dependent manner (Eaton et al. 2018; Eaton et al. 2020).</p><p>Changes in relative abundance of a single CPA component can modulate the length of 3' UTRs across the transcriptome by favoring selection of the PAS and thus the cleavage site (Martin et al. 2012). Components of splicing machinery play regulatory roles in the usage of intronic PASs. U1 snRNP was shown to transiently associate with sequences upstream of PASs, through RNA‑RNA interactions between the U1 snRNA and pre‑mRNA, and repress the usage of cryptic intronic PASs near the 5' end of genes, a mechanism known as U1 snRNP telescripting (Berg et al. 2012: human, mouse and Drosophila cells were used; Luo et al. 2013: human cells were used; Li et al. 2015: mouse cells were used; Devany et al. 2016: human colorectal carcinoma cell lines were used; Shi et al. 2019: human cells were used; So et al. 2019: human cells were used; Feng et al. 2023: human cells were used; reviewed in Venters et al. 2019 and Ran et al. 2021). U2 snRNA suppresses the uses of PASs in introns that can be efficiently spliced out (Li et al. 2015: mouse myoblast cell line C2C12 was used).</p>
