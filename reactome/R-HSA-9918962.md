# E is N-glycosylated
**Reactome ID:** [R-HSA-9918962](https://reactome.org/content/detail/R-HSA-9918962)
**Species:** Homo sapiens

## Summary

DENV-2 E protein is known to be N-glycosylated at Asn-67 and Asn-153 (Asn-347 and Asn-433 of the polyprotein, respectively), a process requiring, among others, the oligosaccharyl transferase activity of the host OST complex, which has been identified as essential for DENV infection in previous CRISPR screens (Labeau et al., 2020; Shivaprasad et al., 2024). Glycosyl residues on DENV glycoproteins are highly heterogeneous. High mannose glycans are commonly found, with Man6GlcNAc2 being an example high mannose structure observed in DENV-2 derived from insect cells and in Vero cell-produced DENV vaccine candidates (Bryant et al., 2007; Hacker et al., 2009; Alen et al., 2012; Lei et al., 2015; Dubayle et al., 2015; reviewed in Idris et al., 2016; Yap et al., 2017; Carbaugh & Lazear, 2020; Feng et al., 2022). The dependence of prM, E, and NS1 folding and glycosylation on the dolichyl-phosphate mannosyltransferase complex (DPM1:DPM2:DPM3) has been shown directly; deficiency in this complex leads to abnormal glycosylation of these viral proteins (Labeau et al., 2020).
