# glutamate + NAD(P)+ => alpha-ketoglutarate + NH4+ + NAD(P)H + H+ (GLUD1,2)
**Reactome ID:** [R-HSA-70600](https://reactome.org/content/detail/R-HSA-70600)
**Species:** Homo sapiens

## Summary

Mitochondrial glutamate dehydrogenase 1 (GLUD1) catalyzes the reversible reaction of glutamate and NAD(P)+ to form 2-oxoglutarate, NAD(P)H + H+, and ammonia (Fang et al. 2002). Mature GLUD1 protein lacks the 53 aminoterminal residues of the nascent protein (Julliard and Smith 1979), which function as a mitochondrial import signal. The active form of the enzyme is a hexamer, allosterically activated by ADP and inhibited by GTP (Fang et al. 2002; Smith et al. 2002). GLUD2 is inferred to catalyze the same reaction based on its structural similarity to GLUD1.
