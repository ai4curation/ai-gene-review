# Squalene 2,3-epoxide cyclizes, forming lanosterol
**Reactome ID:** [R-HSA-191366](https://reactome.org/content/detail/R-HSA-191366)
**Species:** Homo sapiens

## Summary

Lanosterol synthase (LSS) catalyzes the cyclization of squalene 2,3-epoxide to lanosterol, a reaction that forms the sterol nucleus (Sung et al. 1995; Van Tamelen et al. 1966). LSS is located on the ER membrane and is active in monomeric form (Ruf et al. 2004).
