# MITF-M-dependent STT3B gene expression
**Reactome ID:** [R-HSA-9926534](https://reactome.org/content/detail/R-HSA-9926534)
**Species:** Homo sapiens

## Summary

STT3B encodes the catalytic subunit of the oligosaccharyl transferase (OST), the enzyme that catalyzes the initial posttranslational addition of glycans to proteins in the endoplasmic reticulum (Ramirez et al, 2019). STT3B was one of a number of genes involved in glycosaminoglycan metabolism identified as an MITF-M target gene in melanoma cells (Dilshat et al, 2021; Laurette et al, 2015; Webster et al, 2014). Although the specific effect of MITF-M-dependent STT3B expression has not been extensively examined, changes in glycosylation of integrins, collagens and other proteins has been shown to impact extracellular matrix organization, formation of focal adhesions, immune evasion and epithelial-to-mesenchymal transitions in cancers (Gardelli et al, 2021; Marsico et al, 2018; Hu et al, 2023)
