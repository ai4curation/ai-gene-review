# Signalase cleaves prepro-NS4B
**Reactome ID:** [R-HSA-9918871](https://reactome.org/content/detail/R-HSA-9918871)
**Species:** Homo sapiens

## Summary

A host signalase cleaves 2K-NS4B (prepro-NS4B), producing NS4B and the small 2K peptide (P2K). Evidence for such cleavage by a signalase-like enzyme has been observed in mammalian cells infected with the DENV-2 strain PR159 (Preugschat & Strauss, 1991), and this processing of 2K-NS4B by a host signalase is a recognized step in NS4B maturation (Kelley et al., 2011). NS4B accumulates at or close to the ER or ER-derived membranes (see, e.g., Muñoz-Jordán et al., 2005).
