# OspC3 ADP-riboxanates CASP4
**Reactome ID:** [R-HSA-9956624](https://reactome.org/content/detail/R-HSA-9956624)
**Species:** Homo sapiens

## Summary

Shigella flexneri (S. flexneri) secretes the type III effector OspC3, which binds caspase-4 (CASP4) via its ankyrin repeat domain and inhibits LPS-induced pyroptosis by interacting with both the pro-caspase (p30) and processed (p20:p10; not depicted in this Reactome event) forms of CASP4 (Kobayashi T. et al., 2013; Li Z. et al., 2021; Hou Y. et al., 2023). OspC3 catalyzes ADP-riboxanation of CASP4 at arginine residue 314 (R314) in the caspase catalytic center by transferring ADP-ribose from NAD⁺ to the Nδ atom of the arginine side chain, followed by deamination of an Nω group to generate an oxazolidine ring structure (Li Z. et al., 2021; Hou Y. et al., 2023). Calcium-free calmodulin (CALM) enhances the catalytic activity of OspC3 by promoting NAD⁺ binding and stabilizing the ternary complex with CASP4, thereby enabling OspC3-mediated ADP-riboxanation that inhibits CASP4 enzymatic function (Li Z. et al., 2021; Hou Y. et al., 2023). Infection with Shigella flexneri lacking ospC3 leads to an enhanced CASP4-dependent inflammatory response in human epithelial cell lines, such as immortalized keratinocytes (HaCaT), colon adenocarcinoma cells (HT29) and epidermoid carcinoma (A431). In vivo, infection with the ospC3-deficient S. flexneri mutant results in decreased bacterial burden and increased survival in animal infection models (Kobayashi T. et al., 2013; Hou Y. et al., 2023). A homologous effector, CopC from Chromobacterium violaceum, shares ADP-riboxanase activity but differs in substrate specificity, targeting apoptotic caspases such as CASP3, CASP7, CASP8, and CASP9. Like OspC3, CopC requires binding to calcium-free calmodulin (CALM) for activation (Liu Y. et al., 2022; Zhang K. et al., 2022).
