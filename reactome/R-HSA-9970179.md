# Recruitment of other CPA components to pre-mRNA-bound CPA core
**Reactome ID:** [R-HSA-9970179](https://reactome.org/content/detail/R-HSA-9970179)
**Species:** Homo sapiens

## Summary

<p>Components of the cleavage and polyadenylation (CPA) complex that are not part of the core co-immunoprecipitate with the core CPA components at substoichiometric amounts, suggesting that they are associated with the CPA either weakly or transiently (Shi et al. 2009). These components include those that associate with the core CPA prior to pre-mRNA cleavage, such as the CF II complex (also known as CFIIm or the cleavage factor II complex, consisting of CLP1 and PCF11), the E3 ubiquitin ligase RBBP6, the complex of the catalytic subunit of the protein phosphatase PP1 with the regulatory subunit PPP1R10 (also known as PNUTS), and a polyA polymerase (Shi et al. 2009).</p><p>In yeast, the PCF11 component of the CF II complex was shown to simultaneously bind pre-mRNA and phosphorylated serine 2 of the C-terminal domain (CTD) of RNA polymerase II (RNA Pol II) (Barilla et al. 2001; Licatalosi et al. 2002) and to displace the elongation complex from RNA Pol II in both yeast (Zhang et al. 2005) and Drosophila (Zhang and Gilmour 2006). PCF11 binds the pre-mRNA without precise sequence specificity but prefers extended G-rich regions (Schäfer et al. 2018). The CLP1 subunit of the CF II complex has a RNA 5' kinase activity whose role in the polyadenylation process has not been determined but which is dispensable for pre-mRNA cleavage (Schäfer et al. 2018). Binding of ATP to CLP1, but not ATP hydrolysis, stimulates pre-mRNA cleavage (Schmidt et al. 2022). SUPT6H (also known as SPT6), a component of the elongation complex, may facilitate the recruitment of PCF11 and PPP1R10 to RNA Pol II (Bejjani et al. 2025).</p><p>RBBP6 binds to the core CPA through interaction of the DWNN domain of RBBP6 with the CSTF2 (also known as CSTF64) subunit of the CSTF complex (Di Giammartino et al. 2014). RBBP6 also interacts with the pre-mRNA and the endonuclease CPSF3 (Boreikaite et al. 2022). RBBP6 associates with nuclear speckles, which are suggested to serve as hubs for RNA Pol II-mediated transcription, pre-mRNA splicing and 3' end processing (Yoon et al. 2025).</p><p>The predominant nuclear RNA polyA polymerase (PAP) that co-immunoprecipitates with the CPA complex and performs polyadenylation of the 3' end of pre-mRNAs is the polyA polymerase alpha (PAPOLA). Alternative nuclear PAPs that can function as part of the CPA and contribute to polyadenylation of pre-mRNAs are polyA gamma (PAPOLG) and TUT1 (also known as Star-PAP or STPAP). All three nuclear PAPS, PAPOLA, PAPOLG and TUT1 are ubiquitously expressed and knockdown experiments show that all three are important for proper mRNA expression (Li et al. 2017). Functions of PAPOLA, PAPOLG and TUT1 are partially redundant, but each PAP is also specifically involved in polyadenylation of a subset of transcripts (Li et al. 2017). PAPOLG structure closely resembles the structure of PAPOLA (Yang et al. 2014). TUT1 activity is regulated by nuclear phosphoinositide signaling, specifically nuclear phosphatidylinositol-4,5-bisphosphate, PI4,5P(2), and TUT1 specifically promotes polyadenylation and expression of mRNAs produced by genes involved in oxidative stress response, such as HMOX1 (Mellman et al. 2008; Li et al. 2012; reviewed in Li et al. 2013), and in general genes with low expression levels (Li et al. 2017).
