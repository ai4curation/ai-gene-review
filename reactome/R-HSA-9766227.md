# MDM2 mediates CDH1 ubiquitination
**Reactome ID:** [R-HSA-9766227](https://reactome.org/content/detail/R-HSA-9766227)
**Species:** Homo sapiens

## Summary

<p>Polyubiquitination of CDH1 (E-cadherin) by MDM2 was demonstrated in human HEK293 cells and shown to depend on the RING finger domain of MDM2, previously shown to harbour catalytic activity, and on Lys48 (K48) ubiquitin linkages (Yang et al. 2006). A significant decrease in CDH1 ubiquitination is seen in human breast carcinoma cell lines in which MDM2 is depleted by siRNA treatment or in which BANP (SMAR1), reported to sequester MDM2 from CDH1, is overexpressed (Adhikary et al. 2014). MDM2-mediated ubiquitination of CDH1 is independent of the ability of MDM2 to bind TP53 (Yang et al. 2006).</p><p>A synthetic peptide, PNC-27, which binds to MDM2 present at the plasma membrane of acute myeloid leukemia (AML) cells, increases the interaction between MDM2 and CDH1, also leading to an increase in CDH1 ubiquitination (Wang et al. 2020).</p><p>In prostate carcinoma cell lines, a long noncoding RNA (lncRNA) UCA1, reported to associate with CDH1 and prevent its association with MDM2, also reduces CDH1 ubiquitination and subsequent degradation (Zhao et al. 2020).</p>
