# SARS-CoV-2 M binds TBK1
**Reactome ID:** [R-HSA-9754827](https://reactome.org/content/detail/R-HSA-9754827)
**Species:** Homo sapiens

## Summary

Ectopic expression of the severe acute respiratory syndrome coronavirus type 2 (SARS-CoV-2) membrane (M) protein was found to suppress production of type I and III IFNs in human embryonic kidney 293T cells (HEK293 cells) induced by Sendai virus infection or overexpression of RIG-I (DDX58), MDA5 (IFIH1), MAVS, TBK1 and IKKε (IKBKE) proteins (Zheng Y et al. 2020; Sui L et al. 2021). The inhibitory effect of viral M on the production of type I and III IFNs was also observed in human alveolar basal epithelial (A549) cells (Zheng Y et al. 2020). Co-immunoprecipitation experiments showed the interaction between SARS-CoV-2 M and human TBK1 upon co-expression in HEK293T (Zheng Y et al. 2020; Sui L et al. 2021). The interaction of viral M with TBK1 is thought to induce ubiquitin-mediated degradation of TBK1 thus inhibiting IRF3 activation in HEK293T cells (Sui L et al. 2021). Besides interacting with TBK1, the viral M co-immunoprecipitated with DDX58, MAVS, IKBKE (Zheng Y et al. 2020) and MDA5 (IFIH1), TRAF3, but not DDX58 (Sui L et al. 2021). SARS‑CoV‑2 M protein is thought to prevent the formation of the TRAF3:TANK:TBK1/IKBKE complex and thereby inhibit TBK1/IKBKE‑dependent activation of IRF3/IRF7 transcription factors downstream of MAVS (Zheng Y et al. 2020; Sui L et al. 2021). Similar results were reported for the SARS-CoV-1 M protein (Siu KL et al. 2009; 2014).<p>This Reactome event shows binding of the ER-localized SARS-CoV-2 M protein to TBK1.<br>
