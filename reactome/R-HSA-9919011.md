# Pre-NS1 folds and is N-glycosylated
**Reactome ID:** [R-HSA-9919011](https://reactome.org/content/detail/R-HSA-9919011)
**Species:** Homo sapiens

## Summary

Pre-NS1 protein, while being folded, is glycosylated at Asn-130 and Asn-207 (Asn-905 and Asn-982 in the polyprotein, respectively). This process involves the alpha-glucosidase activity of the oligosaccharyltransferase (OST) complex, which initially adds high-mannose precursor glycans to these sites, followed by α-glucosidase trimming. In mature secreted NS1, the glycan at Asn-130 is typically processed to a complex glycan type, while Asn-207 retains high-mannose glycans (Prior & Wright, 1994; Somnuke et al., 2011; Wang et al., 2019; Hafirassou et al., 2017; reviewed in Idris et al., 2016; Yap et al., 2017; Feng et al., 2022). The chaperone HSPA5 (GRP78) is required for folding of glycosylated NS1 (Songprakhon et al., 2018; Denolly et al., 2023). Other chaperones binding to NS1 include calnexin (CALX), calreticulin (CALR), and the protein disulfide isomerases 1 (P4HB, PDIA1), and 3 (PDIA3) (Hafirassou et al., 2017; Denolly et al., 2023).
