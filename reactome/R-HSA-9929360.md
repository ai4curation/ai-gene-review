# GSK3B phosphorylates CD274
**Reactome ID:** [R-HSA-9929360](https://reactome.org/content/detail/R-HSA-9929360)
**Species:** Homo sapiens

## Summary

Glycogen synthase kinase 3β (GSK3β) is a serine/threonine protein kinase that interacts with PD-L1 (CD274) and induces phosphorylation-dependent proteasome degradation of PD-L1 by β-TrCP-SCF (BTRC-SCF) complex (Li et al., 2016, Schulz et al., 2019). GSK3β induced phosphorylation of non-glycosylated PD-L1 at T180 and S184 sites which also antagonizes the glycosylation of PD-L1 at N192, N200 and N219 sites. Glycosylation of PD-L1 also hinder the phsophorylation of PD-L1 by GSK3B competitively (Li et al., 2016). NEK2 negatively regulate this process by phosphorylating PD-L1 at T194 and T210 residue (Zhang et al., 2021).
