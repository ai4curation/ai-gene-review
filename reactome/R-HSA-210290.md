# Interaction of PECAM-1 and SHIP
**Reactome ID:** [R-HSA-210290](https://reactome.org/content/detail/R-HSA-210290)
**Species:** Homo sapiens

## Summary

PECAM/CD31 is a member of the immunoglobulin superfamily (IgSF) and has been implicated to mediate the adhesion and trans-endothelial migration of T-lymphocytes into the vascular wall, T cell activation and angiogenesis. It has six Ig homology domains within its extracellularly and an ITIM motif within its cytoplasmic region. <br>PECAM-mediated adhesion is complex, because it is capable of binding both to itself (homophilic adhesion) and to non-PECAM ligands (heterophilic adhesion). The trans-homophilic interaction between the two PECAM-1 molecules is mediated by their NH2-terminal membrane distal Ig homology domains 1 and 2 plus the proper spacing formed by the six Ig-homology domains.
