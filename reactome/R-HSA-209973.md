# Tyrosine is diiodinated
**Reactome ID:** [R-HSA-209973](https://reactome.org/content/detail/R-HSA-209973)
**Species:** Homo sapiens

## Summary

Thyroid peroxidase (TPO) catalyzes the iodination of iodotyrosine residues of the thyroglobulin (TG) dimer, using iodide (I-) and hydrogen peroxide (H2O2) (Alexander, 1961; Nakamura et al., 1984; Taurog et al., 1996; Kessler et al., 2007). Cofactors of TPO are Ca2+ and Heme b (Baker et al., 2023).<br><br>TPO is part of the thyroxisome, a complex consisting of at least Dual oxidase (DUOX1/2) (Fortunato et al., 2010), Dual oxidase maturation factor 1/2 (DUOXA1/2) (Grasberger & Refetoff, 2006) and Thioredoxin domain-containing protein 11 (TXNDC11, EFP1) (Wang et al., 2005), which is bound to Caveolin-1 (CAV1) (Senou et al., 2009) structures on the extracellular side of the apical plasma membrane of thyroid cells (reviewed in Godlevska & Banga, 2019; Jing & Zhang, 2022).<br><br>Mutations in the TPO gene can cause Thyroid dyshormonogenesis 2A (TDH2A; MIM:274500; see, e.g. Calaciura et al., 2002; Wu et al., 2002).
