# Defective RDH12 does not reduce atRAL to atROL
**Reactome ID:** [R-HSA-2466861](https://reactome.org/content/detail/R-HSA-2466861)
**Species:** Homo sapiens

## Summary

Retinol dehydrogenase RDH12 mediates the reversible, NADP(H)-dependent reduction of all-trans-retinal (atRAL) or 11-cis-retinal (11cRAL) to all-trans-retinol (atROL) or 11-cis-retinol (11cROL) respectively in photoreceptor cells.<br><br>Defects in RDH12 cause Leber congenital amaurosis type 13 (LCA13; MIM:612712). LCA defects are early-onset and severe retinal degenerations that are responsible for the most common cause of congenital blindness in infants and children. Mutants that abolish RDH12 activity to result in LCA13 are Y226C, Q189X (Janecke et al, 2004), p.Ala269fxX270 (Perault et al. 2004, Janecke et al, 2004) and H151N (Perault et al. 2004).<br><br>Defects in RDH12 cause retinitis pigmentosa type 53 (RP53; MIM:612712), an autosomal recessive retinal dystrophy characterised by retinal pigment deposits and primary loss of rod photoreceptor cells followed by secondary loss of cone photoreceptor cells. The A126V mutant causes RP53 (Benayoun et al. 2009).
