# miR-10b inhibits CDH1 mRNA translation
**Reactome ID:** [R-HSA-9768618](https://reactome.org/content/detail/R-HSA-9768618)
**Species:** Homo sapiens

## Summary

Direct downregulation of CDH1 by microRNA miR-10b was demonstrated by a luciferase assay in which a wild-type or a mutant CDH1 mRNA 3'UTR was fused to the luciferase gene (Zhang et al. 2015) and a luciferase assay in which a wild-type miR-10b or a mutant miR-10b was used (Ma et al. 2017). In the hepatocellular carcinoma cell line Hep-2 transfected with a miR-10b expression vector, CDH1 was downregulated at both mRNA and protein levels (Zhang et al. 2015). Transfection of the human glioma cell line U251 with a vector expressing miR-10b resulted in downregulation of CDH1 mRNA, while the transfection of antagomiR-10b led to upregulation of CDH1 mRNA (Ma et al. 2017). In human mammary epithelial cell line HMLE and the breast cancer cell line SUM149, miR-10b was found to not have a significant effect on CDH1 protein level (Ma et al. 2010).
