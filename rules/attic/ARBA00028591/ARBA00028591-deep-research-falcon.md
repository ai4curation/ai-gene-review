2. Domain Analysis
- Membranebound Oacyltransferases (MBOAT/LPLAT) and DGAT
   Biochemical role: Acyl transfer to lipids; DGAT catalyzes the terminal step of TAG synthesis; LPCAT/LPEAT/GPL remodeling in Lands cycle; protein acylation (e.g., HHAT, PORCN) within the MBOAT fold. Family displays conserved catalytic core but diverse substrate preferences (acylCoA chain length/unsaturation; headgroup selectivity) (pierce2023arisingtide pages 4-5).
   Multifunctionality: Several LPLATs exhibit overlapping activities (AGPAT/LPLAT/MGAT) and subcellular localizations (ER, LDs, mitochondria), integrating lipid storage, membrane remodeling, and signaling (korbecki2024phospholipidacyltransferasescharacterization pages 2-4, korbecki2024phospholipidacyltransferasescharacterization pages 10-12, korbecki2024phospholipidacyltransferasescharacterization pages 1-2).
   Application: Central to glycerophospholipid and TAG synthesis across eukaryotes, including plant oil engineering and disease contexts (zhou2024regulationofoil pages 14-15).

- ELO/ELOVL fatty acid elongases
   Role: Ratelimiting condensation step in verylongchain fatty acid (VLCFA) elongation supplying substrates for sphingolipids, phospholipids, and neutral lipids. Human disorders and lipidomic phenotypes directly reflect chainlength defects (e.g., ELOVL1, ELOVL5, ELOVL4) (vaz2025disordersoffatty pages 11-12).
   Specificity: Isoformspecific chainlength and 3/6 preferences are well documented; conservation across eukaryotes (vaz2025disordersoffatty pages 11-12).

- ACSL/AMPbinding ligases
   Role: Activate longchain fatty acids to acylCoA for downstream metabolism. Gene expression and functional links to ferroptosis susceptibility and lipid remodeling are evidenced in recent disease studies (e.g., ACSL1/3/4) (wang2024corticallipidmetabolic pages 9-10).
   Caveat: The AMPbinding superfamily is broad; restricting to ACSL subfamilies or combined signatures (e.g., ACSL Cterminal/ANL domains) reduces offtarget annotations (wang2024corticallipidmetabolic pages 9-10).

- CDPalcohol phosphatidyltransferases (CDPAP)
   Role: Catalyze CDPactivated headgroup transfer to DAG, key for phospholipid biosynthesis (PC/PE/PG/PI) in bacteria and eukaryotes; solved structures/mechanism recently clarified, cementing their assignment to lipid biosynthesis (zhou2024regulationofoil pages 14-15).
   Specificity: Some CDPAPfold enzymes act on nonphospholipid donors/acceptors in bacteria; pairing with transmembrane signatures/taxon filters mitigates false positives.

- Sterol pathway (SQS, SQLE, OSC/lanosterol/cycloartenol cyclases, CYP51)
   Role: Mevalonate branch into sterol biosynthesis. SQS/SQLE convert FPPsqualene2,3epoxysqualene; OSC cyclizes to lanosterol/cycloartenol; CYP51 removes C14 methyl. These enzymes are core to sterol metabolism in fungi, plants, protists, and animals (zhou2024regulationofoil pages 14-15).
   Evidence: Contemporary structural/functional studies (20232024) and disease/pathogen research reaffirm pathway roles and clinical targeting (e.g., CYP51; SQLE in cancer; azole and terbinafine pharmacology) (zhou2024regulationofoil pages 14-15).

- PLA/PLD/PNPLA lipases and lipoxygenases (LOX)
   PLA2/cPLA2/sPLA2: Hydrolyze sn2 acyl chains, generate free fatty acids (AA) for oxylipin/eicosanoid biosynthesis and membrane remodeling; widely implicated in inflammation, cancer, and tissuespecific biology (wang2024corticallipidmetabolic pages 9-10).
   LOX: Dioxygenases producing oxylipins; plant and animal LOX contribute to defense, development, and signaling; aligns with GO: oxylipin metabolism (subclass of lipid metabolism) (guo2024researchprogresson pages 17-19).

- OSBP/ORP lipid transfer proteins
   Role: Nonvesicular sterol and phosphoinositide exchange at membrane contact sites; regulate PM/ER sterol homeostasis and signaling (wang2024corticallipidmetabolic pages 9-10).

- Plant terpene synthases (terpenoid cyclases)
   Role: Synthesize terpenoids (isoprenoids), a GO lipid subclass; cladespecific plant families and FunFams reflect diterpene/monoterpene specialization; falls under terpene/lipid metabolism in plants (zhou2024regulationofoil pages 14-15).
