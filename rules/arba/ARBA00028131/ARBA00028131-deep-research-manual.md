## Biological Functions and Processes

### Core Molecular Function
All proteins covered by this rule share the molecular function of **endopeptidase activity** (GO:0004175), which involves:
- Hydrolysis of peptide bonds within polypeptide chains
- Cleavage of proteins at internal sites rather than terminal residues
- Participation in protein processing, degradation, and maturation

1. **Protein Proteolysis**

   - Enable tissue invasion and immune evasion

### Taxonomic Distribution and Specialization

The rule covers an exceptionally broad taxonomic range:

**Bacteria**: Includes various bacterial proteases, many of which are:
- Virulence factors in pathogenic species

- Industrial enzymes in biotechnological applications

**Archaea**: Thermostable proteases adapted to extreme environments

**Eukaryotes**: Diverse range including:
- **Plants**: Cysteine proteases involved in senescence and defense
- **Fungi**: Secreted proteases for nutrient acquisition
- **Animals**: Digestive enzymes, lysosomal cathepsins, immune system proteases

### Mechanistic Classes

1. **Aspartic Proteases**
   - pH optimum around 3-4
   - Two aspartic acid residues in active site
   - Include pepsin, cathepsin D, HIV protease

2. **Cysteine Proteases**
   - Active site cysteine nucleophile
   - Include papain, cathepsins B/H/L
   - Often regulated by pH and inhibitors

3. **Serine Proteases**
   - Catalytic triad (Ser-His-Asp)
   - Include subtilisin, trypsin families
   - Broad substrate specificity

4. **Metalloproteases**
   - Require metal cofactor (usually Zn²⁺)
   - Include thermolysin and aminopeptidases
   - Important in extracellular matrix remodeling
