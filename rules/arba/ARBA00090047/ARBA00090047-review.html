Description

This rule annotates proteins containing the SepA cytokinesis protein domain (CATH FunFam 1.20.58.2220:FF:000006) in Taphrinomycotina fungi with the highly specific GO term GO:1904498 (protein localization to mitotic actomyosin contractile ring). The rule targets a well-characterized cytokinesis regulator but applies an extremely specific localization annotation that requires strong experimental validation.

Review Summary

ARBA00090047 is a well-designed rule that correctly identifies SepA cytokinesis proteins in fungi through a specific CATH FunFam and appropriate taxonomic restriction to Taphrinomycotina. The rule demonstrates good biological understanding and parsimony with a single, mechanistically coherent condition set. However, it suffers from GO term over-specificity: GO:1904498 (protein localization to mitotic actomyosin contractile ring) makes a very precise claim about protein localization that requires experimental validation. While SepA proteins are established cytokinesis regulators, the evidence for their specific localization TO the contractile ring (versus regulation OF contractile ring assembly) is unclear. The rule would benefit from a broader, more defensible cytokinesis-related GO term.

Action Rationale

While the rule correctly identifies SepA as a cytokinesis regulator in fungi, the GO term GO:1904498 is overly specific. SepA proteins are known to be involved in cytokinesis and may regulate contractile ring assembly, but the specific annotation "protein localization to mitotic actomyosin contractile ring" requires strong experimental evidence that SepA proteins themselves localize TO the contractile ring. A broader cytokinesis-related term such as GO:0000281 (mitotic cytokinesis) or GO:0000915 (actomyosin contractile ring assembly) would be more appropriate based on current knowledge.
