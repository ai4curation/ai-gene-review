| 1 | BMP ligand maturation and secretion | BMPs are synthesized as precursor pro-proteins, proteolytically processed to mature cystine-knot dimers, and secreted as homo- or heterodimers competent for receptor engagement. Ligand identity and dimer composition influence signaling output. (pqac-00000002, pqac-00000007) | Obligatory for physiological ligand-driven signaling | Pro-domain processing, dimerization state, ligand dosage, matrix retention and release by extracellular milieu (pqac-00000002, pqac-00000018) |
| 2 | Extracellular ligand regulation by antagonists | Secreted antagonists such as Noggin, Chordin, and Gremlin bind BMP ligands and mask receptor-binding surfaces or sequester ligands in extracellular reservoirs, thereby restricting receptor access. (pqac-00000014, pqac-00000017, pqac-00000018) | Conditional but often critical in tissues and morphogen gradients | Antagonist abundance, spatial localization, heparan-sulfate interactions, ligand–antagonist affinity, developmental context (pqac-00000014, pqac-00000018) |
| 3 | Ligand binding to type II receptors | BMP ligand dimers engage cell-surface serine/threonine kinase receptors, commonly including constitutively active type II receptors such as BMPR2, ACVR2A, or ACVR2B. (pqac-00000000, pqac-00000001, pqac-00000004) | Obligatory in canonical receptor-mediated signaling | Ligand–receptor specificity, receptor expression level, competition among type II receptors, receptor availability at plasma membrane (pqac-00000000, pqac-00000004) |
| 4 | Heterotetrameric receptor complex formation (type I + type II) | Ligand-bound receptors assemble into a heterotetrameric complex containing two type I receptors (e.g., ALK2/3/6) and two type II receptors. This is the core signaling unit for canonical BMP signaling. (pqac-00000001, pqac-00000002, pqac-00000004) | Obligatory | Receptor stoichiometry, preformed versus ligand-induced assemblies, receptor subtype pairing, membrane localization (pqac-00000001, pqac-00000004) |
| 5 | Type II receptor phosphorylates type I receptor GS domain | Constitutively active type II receptor kinases phosphorylate serine/threonine residues in the GS domain of the type I receptor, converting type I receptors into active SMAD kinases. (pqac-00000001, pqac-00000004, pqac-00000022) | Obligatory | Accessibility of GS domain, receptor conformation, inhibitory proteins such as FKBP12, phosphatase activity (pqac-00000019, pqac-00000022) |
| 6 | FKBP12 release from type I receptor | FKBP12 normally binds the type I receptor near the GS domain and suppresses spontaneous activation. Productive signaling requires relief of this inhibitory state, allowing GS-domain phosphorylation and signaling onset. (pqac-00000005, pqac-00000022) | Conditional but functionally important for threshold control | FKBP12 occupancy, ligand-induced conformational change, receptor mutation state, pharmacologic FKBP12 displacement (pqac-00000005, pqac-00000022) |
| 7 | Activated type I receptor phosphorylates SMAD1/5/8 at C-terminal SSXS motif | Activated type I receptors phosphorylate BMP R-SMADs—SMAD1, SMAD5, and SMAD8/9—at the C-terminal SSXS motif, creating the canonical intracellular BMP signal. (pqac-00000000, pqac-00000003, pqac-00000004, pqac-00000018) | Obligatory for canonical SMAD signaling | Type I receptor identity, i-SMAD competition, ubiquitin-mediated turnover of receptors/R-SMADs, phosphatase-mediated dephosphorylation (pqac-00000014, pqac-00000017, pqac-00000018) |
| 8 | R-SMAD–SMAD4 heterotrimeric complex formation | Phosphorylated SMAD1/5/8 typically assemble with SMAD4 into a transcriptionally active complex, often described as a heterotrimer. This is the standard canonical route, but some contexts use SMAD4-independent atypical BMP signaling. (pqac-00000004, pqac-00000006, pqac-00000021, pqac-00000024) | Usually obligatory in canonical signaling; bypassable in atypical SMAD4-independent contexts | SMAD4 availability, competition with other R-SMAD pathways for SMAD4, inhibitory SMAD6/7 interference, context-specific bypass in dental mesenchyme (pqac-00000007, pqac-00000014, pqac-00000021) |
| 9 | Nuclear translocation of SMAD complex | Phosphorylated R-SMAD complexes accumulate in the nucleus, where signaling competence depends on nucleocytoplasmic shuttling and retention. SMAD5 can show context-sensitive shuttling distinct from SMAD1. (pqac-00000004, pqac-00000026, pqac-00000027) | Obligatory for transcriptional output in canonical signaling | Nuclear import/export balance, SMAD phosphorylation state, cell physiology such as intracellular pH for SMAD5, retention by cofactors (pqac-00000026, pqac-00000027) |
| 10 | Transcriptional regulation at BMP response elements (BREs) | Nuclear BMP SMAD complexes bind BRE-associated GC-rich DNA with cofactors/co-regulators and activate or repress target genes such as ID-family and lineage-specific programs. Output depends on partner transcription factors and chromatin context. (pqac-00000004, pqac-00000000, pqac-00000005) | Obligatory for direct canonical transcriptional output | BRE accessibility, co-activators/co-repressors such as p300/CBP, lineage-determining transcription factors, chromatin state, negative feedback by i-SMADs and degradation pathways (pqac-00000000, pqac-00000014, pqac-00000015) |


*Table: This table summarizes the sequential molecular steps of canonical BMP signaling from ligand maturation to SMAD-dependent transcriptional output. It also highlights which steps are obligatory versus context-dependent and identifies major regulatory checkpoints, including antagonists, FKBP12, inhibitory SMADs, and the atypical SMAD4-independent branch.*
