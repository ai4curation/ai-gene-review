# The ERBB2 Signaling Pathway Module: A Concise Review

## 1. Executive Summary

ERBB2 (HER2) is a transmembrane receptor tyrosine kinase that occupies a unique position among the four-member ERBB/HER family (ERBB1–4). Unlike its paralogs, ERBB2 has no known direct ligand; instead, it adopts a constitutively open extracellular conformation with an exposed dimerization arm, making it the preferred heterodimerization partner for other ligand-bound ERBB receptors (wang2022emergingroleof pages 2-4). The most potent signaling unit in this family is the ERBB2–ERBB3 heterodimer, which is activated when neuregulin-1 (NRG1) binds to ERBB3 (cheng2024acomprehensivereview pages 1-3). This heterodimer produces exceptionally strong PI3K–AKT and RAS–MAPK output because ERBB2 contributes robust kinase activity while ERBB3 contributes six phosphotyrosine docking sites for the p85 regulatory subunit of PI3K (cheng2024acomprehensivereview pages 1-3). This module operates in diverse physiological contexts—cardiac development, peripheral nerve myelination, synaptic plasticity—and is pathologically hijacked in breast, gastric, lung, and other cancers through ERBB2 amplification/overexpression, activating ERBB2 or ERBB3 mutations, and oncogenic NRG1 fusions (patil2025crestoneaphase pages 8-10, mikami2026cardioprotectiveroleof pages 3-5, tagliaferro2023thesignalingof pages 7-8).

## 2. Definition and Biological Boundaries

### 2.1 What this system encompasses

The ERBB2 signaling pathway module, as defined here, consists of three obligatory steps: (i) ligand input, in which NRG1 (or NRG2) binds the extracellular domain of ERBB3, inducing a conformational change that exposes its dimerization arm; (ii) ERBB2 kinase activation, in which ligand-bound ERBB3 heterodimerizes with the constitutively open ERBB2, forming an asymmetric kinase dimer in which ERBB3 serves as the allosteric activator and ERBB2 as the catalytically active receiver; and (iii) downstream signal propagation, in which trans-phosphorylation of the ERBB2–ERBB3 C-terminal tails recruits adaptor proteins—principally PIK3R1 (p85) and GRB2—to initiate PI3K–AKT and RAS–MAPK cascades, respectively (pan2024her2pi3kaktpathwayin pages 2-3, pan2024her2pi3kaktpathwayin pages 3-4, cheng2024acomprehensivereview pages 1-3).

### 2.2 Neighboring systems that should be treated separately

Several processes are closely related but mechanistically distinct and should not be conflated with the NRG1–ERBB3→ERBB2 module:

- **EGFR (ERBB1) homodimer signaling** driven by EGF, TGFα, or amphiregulin operates through a different input step and favors distinct downstream adaptors (e.g., stronger Shc/GRB2-mediated MAPK relative to PI3K) (kemmer2022disentanglingerbbsignaling pages 6-8, wang2022emergingroleof pages 2-4).
- **ERBB4 signaling**, which also responds to neuregulins but functions primarily through homodimerization or ERBB2–ERBB4 heterodimers and has important roles in neuronal GABAergic circuits and mammary gland biology that differ from the ERBB2–ERBB3 module (ding2024theroleof pages 1-2, tagliaferro2023thesignalingof pages 4-6).
- **Ligand-independent ERBB2 homodimerization**, which occurs under conditions of pathological receptor overexpression and produces weaker signaling output compared to heterodimers (wang2022emergingroleof pages 2-4, cheng2024acomprehensivereview pages 1-3).
- **Crosstalk with hormone receptor pathways** (ER, PR) and other RTKs (MET, FGFR, IGF1R), which modulate ERBB2 signaling indirectly but are not part of the core module (cheng2024acomprehensivereview pages 5-6, cheng2024acomprehensivereview pages 3-5).

### 2.3 Competing definitions

The term "ERBB2 signaling" is used variably in the literature. Some authors define it broadly to include all ERBB2-containing dimer combinations (ERBB2–ERBB1, ERBB2–ERBB3, ERBB2–ERBB4, and ERBB2 homodimers) and all possible ligand inputs (cheng2024acomprehensivereview pages 1-3). Others restrict it to the NRG1–ERBB3→ERBB2 axis, which is the most potent signaling unit and the one most relevant to normal physiology and cancer biology (cheng2024acomprehensivereview pages 1-3, wang2022emergingroleof pages 2-4). This review focuses on the latter, narrower definition while acknowledging the broader network context.

## 3. Mechanistic Overview

### 3.1 Step 1: Neuregulin–ERBB3 input

NRG1 is an EGF-family ligand that binds to ERBB3 (and ERBB4) through its EGF-like domain (tagliaferro2023thesignalingof pages 2-4). ERBB3 normally exists in an autoinhibited (tethered) conformation in which extracellular domains II and IV are folded together. NRG1 binding between domains I and III of ERBB3 releases this tether, exposing the dimerization arm in domain II and enabling heterodimerization (tagliaferro2023thesignalingof pages 2-4, wang2022emergingroleof pages 2-4). NRG1 can act in paracrine or autocrine fashion; in pathological settings, NRG1 gene fusions create chimeric membrane-tethered or secreted ligands that constitutively activate the pathway (patil2025crestoneaphase pages 8-10).

### 3.2 Step 2: ERBB2 kinase activation

ERBB2 is structurally unique among ERBB receptors: its extracellular domains I and III are in close proximity in an "open" conformation that mimics a ligand-activated state, with a permanently exposed dimerization loop in domain II but no ligand-binding pocket (wang2022emergingroleof pages 2-4). This makes ERBB2 constitutively available for heterodimerization with any ligand-bound ERBB partner. The ERBB2–ERBB3 heterodimer is the most thermodynamically stable back-to-back dimeric construct among all ERBB pairings, as demonstrated by molecular docking studies (mashayekhpoul2023structuralarrangementof pages 3-5).

Upon heterodimerization, the intracellular kinase domains form an asymmetric dimer in which ERBB3's pseudokinase domain serves as the allosteric activator and ERBB2 serves as the catalytic receiver (koivu2024transactivatingmutationsof pages 12-12, koivu2024transactivatingmutationsof pages 5-6). The ERBB3 A676T mutation, for instance, stabilizes the asymmetric active conformation by improving JM-region hydrogen bonding, illustrating how mutations can enhance activator function (koivu2024transactivatingmutationsof pages 5-6). Cryo-EM structures of the HER2–HER3–NRG1β complex have revealed that the extracellular dimer interface is dynamic and that the activator-receiver preference is determined primarily by intracellular regions rather than extracellular asymmetry (koivu2024transactivatingmutationsof pages 12-12, diwanji2021structuresofthe pages 1-3). ERBB2 possesses the most potent catalytic kinase activity among ERBB family members, making it an efficient phosphorylation machine once allosterically activated (pan2024her2pi3kaktpathwayin pages 2-3).

### 3.3 Step 3: PI3K and MAPK output

Activated ERBB2 trans-phosphorylates multiple tyrosine residues on the ERBB3 C-terminal tail. A distinctive feature of ERBB3 is its six binding sites for the SH2 domains of the PIK3R1 (p85) regulatory subunit of class IA PI3K, making the ERBB2–ERBB3 heterodimer an exceptionally strong activator of the PI3K–AKT pathway (cheng2024acomprehensivereview pages 1-3). Upon p85 recruitment, the catalytic subunit p110 generates PIP3 from PIP2 at the plasma membrane. PIP3 recruits AKT via its PH domain, and AKT is then activated by PDK1 (phosphorylating Thr308) and mTORC2 (phosphorylating Ser473), driving cell survival, metabolism, and protein synthesis through mTORC1 and other effectors (pan2024her2pi3kaktpathwayin pages 2-3).

In parallel, phosphotyrosine sites on ERBB2 (and to some extent ERBB3) recruit the adaptor protein GRB2 through its SH2 domain (wang2022emergingroleof pages 2-4). GRB2 constitutively binds the RAS guanine nucleotide exchange factor SOS, which activates RAS at the membrane. Activated RAS initiates the RAF–MEK–ERK cascade, culminating in ERK-mediated transcriptional and proliferative responses (pan2024her2pi3kaktpathwayin pages 3-4, cheng2024acomprehensivereview pages 1-3). Quantitative modeling of breast cancer cell lines has confirmed that ERBB3 is the dominant driver of AKT (PI3K pathway) phosphorylation, while EGFR primarily drives ERK (MAPK pathway) phosphorylation, though both outputs emanate from ERBB2-containing heterodimers (kemmer2022disentanglingerbbsignaling pages 6-8).

An important systems-level feature is reciprocal regulation: inhibition of PI3K–AKT typically triggers compensatory activation of the RAS–ERK pathway, and vice versa, which has profound implications for therapeutic resistance (pan2024her2pi3kaktpathwayin pages 3-4).

## 4. Major Molecular Players and Active Assemblies

### 4.1 NRG1

NRG1 is a member of the EGF ligand superfamily, expressed widely across tissues including heart endocardium, neurons, and epithelial cells. It exists as multiple isoforms (Types I–VI) generated by alternative splicing, with Type III being particularly important for axonal signaling in the peripheral nervous system (tagliaferro2023thesignalingof pages 7-8, tagliaferro2023thesignalingof pages 8-10). NRG1 signals through binding to ERBB3 and ERBB4, but not directly to ERBB1 or ERBB2 (tagliaferro2023thesignalingof pages 2-4). In cancer, NRG1 gene fusions (occurring in ~0.15–0.5% of cancers, with CD74 being the most common fusion partner at 29–31%) create constitutively active ligands that drive ERBB3–ERBB2 heterodimerization (adashek2024neuregulin1andals19 pages 6-7, adashek2024neuregulin1andals19 pages 7-8).

### 4.2 ERBB3 (HER3)

ERBB3 is a pseudokinase—its catalytic domain retains the overall kinase fold but has key substitutions in the catalytic residues that severely impair its intrinsic phosphotransferase activity (koivu2024transactivatingmutationsof pages 12-12, zhao2021trastuzumabblocksthe pages 14-14). Despite this catalytic impairment, ERBB3 is an extraordinarily potent signaling molecule owing to its dual function: it serves as the ligand-sensing receptor for neuregulins and as the allosteric activator of ERBB2's kinase domain in the asymmetric dimer (koivu2024transactivatingmutationsof pages 12-12, koivu2024transactivatingmutationsof pages 5-6). Its six p85-binding phosphotyrosine motifs make it the most direct coupler of ERBB signaling to PI3K–AKT output (cheng2024acomprehensivereview pages 1-3). Cancer-associated ERBB3 mutations can enhance its activator function—an unbiased screen identified 18 transforming ERBB3 mutations, many clustering at the dimerization interface and kinase domain, that promote oncogenic ERBB2–ERBB3 signaling (koivu2024transactivatingmutationsof pages 12-12, koivu2024transactivatingmutationsof pages 11-12, koivu2024transactivatingmutationsof pages 5-6).

### 4.3 ERBB2 (HER2)

ERBB2 is the catalytic engine of the heterodimer. It has no known direct ligand due to the close apposition of its extracellular domains I and III, which precludes formation of a ligand-binding cleft (wang2022emergingroleof pages 2-4). Its permanently exposed domain II dimerization arm makes it constitutively available for partnership (wang2022emergingroleof pages 2-4). ERBB2 has the most potent catalytic kinase activity of all ERBB receptors (pan2024her2pi3kaktpathwayin pages 2-3). In pathological states, ERBB2 gene amplification (present in ~20–30% of breast cancers) leads to receptor overexpression, which can enable ligand-independent homodimerization and constitutive signaling, though heterodimers remain the more potent signaling species (cheng2024acomprehensivereview pages 3-5, cheng2024acomprehensivereview pages 1-3). Activating mutations, including extracellular domain mutations (e.g., S310F) and exon 20 insertions, can induce constitutive activation through covalent dimerization or other allosteric mechanisms (cheng2024acomprehensivereview pages 5-6, cheng2024acomprehensivereview pages 3-5).

### 4.4 Downstream adaptors and effectors

**PIK3R1 (p85):** The regulatory subunit of class IA PI3K is recruited to phosphorylated ERBB3 through its SH2 domains, relieving autoinhibition of the p110 catalytic subunit and enabling PIP3 production (pan2024her2pi3kaktpathwayin pages 2-3, cheng2024acomprehensivereview pages 1-3).

**GRB2:** This SH2/SH3 adaptor protein binds phosphotyrosine residues on activated ERBB receptors and constitutively associates with SOS, linking receptor activation to RAS–MAPK signaling (wang2022emergingroleof pages 2-4).

**AKT:** Recruited to PIP3 via its PH domain and activated by sequential phosphorylation at Thr308 (PDK1) and Ser473 (mTORC2), AKT mediates survival, metabolic, and anti-apoptotic signaling (pan2024her2pi3kaktpathwayin pages 2-3).

**ERK1/2 (MAPK1/3):** Terminal effectors of the RAS–RAF–MEK cascade, driving transcriptional programs for proliferation and differentiation (pan2024her2pi3kaktpathwayin pages 3-4, cheng2024acomprehensivereview pages 1-3).

## 5. Evolutionary and Cell-Biological Variation

### 5.1 Evolutionary conservation and origin

ERBB/EGFR signaling is present across nearly all animal taxa. The EGFR signaling pathway is found from planarians (*Schmidtea mediterranea*) onward, with invertebrates possessing minimal systems: *C. elegans* has a single receptor (LET-23) and one major ligand (LIN-3), while *Drosophila melanogaster* has one receptor (DER) but multiple ligands (Spitz, Gurken, Vein, Keren) (kniazkina2023doesegfrsignaling pages 2-4). The vertebrate four-receptor ERBB system arose through gene duplication and subsequent subfunctionalization. The most striking vertebrate innovations are: (i) ERBB2 lost direct ligand-binding capacity but became the constitutively open preferred dimerization partner, and (ii) ERBB3 lost robust catalytic kinase activity but gained exceptional adaptor-recruitment capacity through its six p85-binding sites (cheng2024acomprehensivereview pages 1-3, kniazkina2023doesegfrsignaling pages 2-4). The core downstream RTK-to-RAS-MAPK signaling module is deeply conserved across metazoa, while the PI3K-dominant output through ERBB3 appears to be a vertebrate elaboration.

### 5.2 Tissue- and cell-type-specific variation

The ERBB2–ERBB3 module operates in markedly different contexts across tissues:

**Nervous system:** Schwann cells express ERBB2 and ERBB3 (but not ERBB4). Axonal NRG1 Type III is the critical signal governing Schwann cell development and peripheral myelination; NRG1-null mice die at E11 from heart failure and lack Schwann cells entirely (tagliaferro2023thesignalingof pages 7-8). In the CNS, ERBB3 expression begins at embryonic day 16 (in rats), peaks postnatally around P20, and correlates with cerebellar lamination (tagliaferro2023thesignalingof pages 6-7). ERBB4, rather than ERBB3, predominates in GABAergic neurons and midbrain dopaminergic circuitry (tagliaferro2023thesignalingof pages 4-6).

**Cardiovascular system:** NRG1 is expressed in the endocardium and vascular endothelial cells while ERBB2 is expressed in cardiomyocytes, establishing a paracrine NRG1→ERBB2/ERBB3 signaling axis essential for cardiac development, cardiomyocyte survival, and metabolic homeostasis. NRG1–ERBB signaling activates PI3K/AKT, ERK/MAPK, JAK/STAT3, and FAK in cardiomyocytes (mikami2026cardioprotectiveroleof pages 3-5, mikami2026cardioprotectiveroleof pages 1-2). This cardioprotective role underlies the cardiotoxicity observed with anti-HER2 cancer therapies.

**Cancer:** In breast cancer, receptor composition varies dramatically by subtype. HER2-positive tumors overexpress ERBB2, while luminal subtypes predominantly express ERBB2 and ERBB3 with lower EGFR. NRG1 shows ligand specificity for ERBB3, and cell-line responses to ligands correlate with ERBB3/EGFR expression ratios (kemmer2022disentanglingerbbsignaling pages 6-8). In head and neck squamous cell carcinoma, NRG promotes directional migration through HER3/HER2 and HER3/FAK interactions, driving downstream AKT and ERK signaling (chen2024targetingher3to pages 3-5, lim2025neuregulininducedher3activationa pages 14-15).

## 6. Constraints, Dependencies, and Failure Modes

### 6.1 Obligatory ordering

The pathway follows a strict sequence: NRG1 binding to ERBB3 must precede productive heterodimerization with ERBB2, which must precede kinase activation and transphosphorylation, which in turn must precede adaptor recruitment and downstream cascade activation. ERBB2 cannot be activated by ligand directly and requires a ligand-bound partner for kinase activation under physiological conditions (kemmer2022disentanglingerbbsignaling pages 17-18, wang2022emergingroleof pages 2-4). The one exception is pathological ERBB2 overexpression, which can enable spontaneous homodimerization—but even these homodimers produce weaker signals than ERBB2–ERBB3 heterodimers (wang2022emergingroleof pages 2-4, cheng2024acomprehensivereview pages 1-3).

### 6.2 Mutual exclusivity and compartment specificity

The PI3K and MAPK outputs emanate from distinct docking sites: ERBB3 phosphotyrosines primarily recruit p85/PI3K, while ERBB2 phosphotyrosines preferentially recruit GRB2/SOS for MAPK activation (cheng2024acomprehensivereview pages 1-3, wang2022emergingroleof pages 2-4). The balance between these outputs is context-dependent and shaped by receptor stoichiometry, ligand concentration, and cellular adaptor availability (kemmer2022disentanglingerbbsignaling pages 6-8). The signal produced by heterodimers is not simply the sum of individual partner signaling properties but reflects emergent properties of the specific heterodimer (wang2022emergingroleof pages 2-4).

### 6.3 Failure modes and disease

**Amplification/overexpression:** ERBB2 gene amplification, present in ~20–30% of breast cancers and variable proportions of gastric, lung, and other cancers, produces constitutive signaling (cheng2024acomprehensivereview pages 3-5, OpenTargets Search: -ERBB2,ERBB3,NRG1). OpenTargets disease-association scores are high for ERBB2 across cancer (0.92), breast carcinoma (0.82), gastric cancer (0.81), and NSCLC (0.81) (OpenTargets Search: -ERBB2,ERBB3,NRG1).

**NRG1 fusions:** These rare but actionable drivers (~0.15–0.5% of cancers) create chimeric ligands that constitutively activate ERBB3–ERBB2 signaling. The CRESTONE Phase II trial demonstrated that seribantumab, an anti-HER3 antibody, achieves ~35% ORR in NRG1-fusion-positive solid tumors with median PFS of 5.4 months and median OS of 20.3 months (patil2025crestoneaphase pages 8-10, patil2025crestoneaphase pages 7-8). Zenocutuzumab, a bispecific HER2/HER3 antibody, also shows activity in NRG1-fusion tumors (chen2024targetingher3to pages 3-5).

**Therapy resistance:** Circulating tumor cell studies have identified NRG1–HER3 signaling as a key pathway for CTC survival and therapy resistance, with FGFR1 signaling serving as a compensatory rescue pathway when NRG1–HER3 is blocked, and vice versa, highlighting the importance of combinatorial targeting strategies (chen2024targetingher3to pages 3-5, pan2024her2pi3kaktpathwayin pages 3-4).

**Cardiotoxicity:** Anti-HER2 therapies (e.g., trastuzumab) can impair the cardioprotective NRG1–ERBB2 axis in cardiomyocytes, manifesting as cardiac dysfunction—a clinically significant on-target toxicity that underscores the physiological importance of this pathway beyond cancer (mikami2026cardioprotectiveroleof pages 3-5, mikami2026cardioprotectiveroleof pages 1-2).

## 7. Controversies and Open Questions

### 7.1 ERBB3 residual kinase activity

Although ERBB3 is classified as a pseudokinase, early studies demonstrated impaired but not completely absent tyrosine kinase activity in purified preparations (zhao2021trastuzumabblocksthe pages 14-14). Whether this residual activity is physiologically meaningful or an artifact of expression systems remains debated. The dominant model treats ERBB3 as a catalytically dead allosteric activator, but some cancer-associated ERBB3 mutations may enhance residual catalytic function in ways that contribute to signaling (koivu2024transactivatingmutationsof pages 12-12, koivu2024transactivatingmutationsof pages 5-6).

### 7.2 Ligand-dependent versus ligand-independent activation

Under physiological conditions, ERBB2 requires a ligand-bound partner for productive signaling (kemmer2022disentanglingerbbsignaling pages 17-18). However, ERBB2 overexpression can drive ligand-independent homodimerization and constitutive signaling, and certain extracellular domain mutations (e.g., S310F) promote covalent dimerization (cheng2024acomprehensivereview pages 5-6, cheng2024acomprehensivereview pages 3-5). The relative contributions of ligand-dependent and ligand-independent mechanisms in HER2-positive tumors remain incompletely resolved. Kemmer et al. (2022) showed that ERBB2's extended tertiary structure does not require conformational changes for heterodimerization, but requires a growth-factor-bound partner, suggesting that physiological activation is obligately ligand-dependent (kemmer2022disentanglingerbbsignaling pages 17-18).

### 7.3 Determinants of activator–receiver asymmetry

The cryo-EM structure of the HER2–HER3–NRG1β complex revealed a dynamic dimer interface (diwanji2021structuresofthe pages 1-3), and subsequent studies demonstrated that activator/receiver preference in the asymmetric kinase dimer is determined primarily by intracellular regions rather than extracellular domain asymmetry (koivu2024transactivatingmutationsof pages 12-12). However, the precise molecular determinants of why ERBB3 preferentially acts as the activator rather than the receiver, despite lacking catalytic activity, remain an active area of structural investigation.

### 7.4 Therapeutic targeting of the NRG1–ERBB3 axis

Whether HER3-directed therapy (e.g., seribantumab) or HER2-directed therapy is superior for NRG1-fusion-driven cancers is being resolved by clinical trials. Early data suggest that anti-ERBB3/HER3 approaches show more profound antitumor activity than anti-ERBB2/HER2 approaches in NRG1-rearranged models (adashek2024neuregulin1andals19 pages 7-8), consistent with the ligand-centric biology of these tumors (patil2025crestoneaphase pages 7-8). Whether combinatorial strategies targeting both HER2 and HER3 will prove superior remains an open clinical question.

### 7.5 Compensatory pathway rewiring

The reciprocal activation between PI3K–AKT and RAS–ERK outputs upon selective pathway inhibition is a well-established phenomenon that complicates monotherapy approaches (pan2024her2pi3kaktpathwayin pages 3-4). FGFR1 signaling has been identified as an additional compensatory pathway for NRG1–HER3 blockade, suggesting that the signaling network around ERBB2 is more resilient and plastic than simple linear pathway models imply.
