| Feature | NOD2-RIPK2 (NODosome) pathway | NLRP3-ASC-Caspase-1 (Inflammasome) pathway |
|---|---|---|
| Sensor | NOD2 (NLRC2), a CARD-containing NLR that detects bacterial peptidoglycan-derived muramyl dipeptide | NLRP3, a PYD-containing inflammasome-forming NLR that responds to cellular perturbation rather than a single defined ligand class (pqac-00000003, pqac-00000008, pqac-00000009) |
| Adaptor | RIPK2 functions as the immediate CARD-binding kinase/adaptor downstream of NOD2 | ASC/PYCARD functions as the canonical adaptor bridging NLRP3 to pro-caspase-1 (pqac-00000017, pqac-00000009) |
| Effector | NF-kB and MAPK activation; in some contexts also TBK1/IKKe-IRF3 signaling and autophagy coupling, rather than inflammatory protease activation as the core output | Caspase-1 activation with downstream cleavage of pro-IL-1β, pro-IL-18, and GSDMD, driving cytokine maturation and pyroptosis (pqac-00000019, pqac-00000009, pqac-00000010) |
| Ligand/Trigger | Muramyl dipeptide (MDP), likely sensed as phospho-MDP in cells; peptidoglycan-derived bacterial products are the canonical trigger | Diverse PAMPs/DAMPs and homeostatic disturbances including ATP, nigericin, toxins, crystals, fibrils, and other triggers that often converge on K+ efflux and organelle stress (pqac-00000017, pqac-00000008, pqac-00000010) |
| Signaling Platform Name | NODosome | NLRP3 inflammasome / ASC speck-containing inflammasome (pqac-00000017, pqac-00000009) |
| N-terminal Domain Type | Tandem CARDs on NOD2 | PYD on NLRP3; ASC contains both PYD and CARD; pro-caspase-1 contains CARD (pqac-00000003, pqac-00000009) |
| Interaction Type with Adaptor | Homotypic CARD-CARD interaction between NOD2 and RIPK2, promoting RIPK2 filament/polymer formation | Homotypic PYD-PYD interaction between NLRP3 and ASC, followed by ASC CARD-CARD recruitment of pro-caspase-1 (pqac-00000017, pqac-00000018, pqac-00000012) |
| Primary Output | Transcriptional inflammatory signaling: induction of cytokines, chemokines, and antimicrobial/inflammatory gene programs | Proteolytic inflammatory signaling: maturation of IL-1β/IL-18 and GSDMD-dependent pore formation/pyroptosis (pqac-00000030, pqac-00000010) |
| Downstream Pathways | TAK1-TAB recruitment, IKK/NEMO activation, NF-kB nuclear signaling, MAPK cascades (ERK, JNK, p38); context-dependent IRF3/TBK1 and autophagy links | Caspase-1 processing, cytokine maturation, gasdermin pore formation, pyroptosis, DAMP release; canonical, non-canonical, and alternative NLRP3-related routes are described in the literature (pqac-00000016, pqac-00000017, pqac-00000014, pqac-00000026) |
| Cell Types | Prominent in myeloid cells and Paneth cells; NOD2 expression is more restricted than NOD1 and is important at epithelial-microbial interfaces | Strongly studied in monocytes, macrophages, dendritic cells, neutrophils, and microglia, with context-dependent activity in non-immune cells as well (pqac-00000030, pqac-00000026) |
| Key PTM Regulation | RIPK2 K63 and M1/linear ubiquitination by XIAP and LUBAC are central for signal propagation; NOD2 S-acylation promotes membrane localization and signaling competence | Priming-associated ubiquitination/phosphorylation/sumoylation regulate competence; NLRP3 S-acylation promotes trans-Golgi localization and NEK7-dependent licensing; additional PTMs restrain aberrant activation (pqac-00000018, pqac-00000032, pqac-00000034, pqac-00000009) |
| Structural Assembly | Ligand-bound NOD2 oligomerizes into a ~7-8 member NODosome and recruits/polymerizes RIPK2 into elongated signaling assemblies | Inactive NLRP3 forms cage/double-ring assemblies; activation involves NEK7-mediated cage opening and assembly of an active disc-like inflammasome that nucleates ASC filaments/specks and recruits caspase-1 (pqac-00000017, pqac-00000012, pqac-00000011) |
| Disease Associations | Loss-of-function NOD2 variants strongly associate with Crohn's disease; gain-of-function variants cause Blau syndrome/early-onset sarcoidosis; broader links to inflammatory bowel and immune dysregulation disorders | CAPS and other autoinflammatory syndromes; broad implication in gout, neurodegeneration, cardiometabolic disease, NASH, and other inflammatory disorders driven by excessive IL-1β/pyroptosis (pqac-00000030, pqac-00000010) |


*Table: This table contrasts the two main NLR signaling modules emphasized in the review: the transcription-coupled NOD2-RIPK2 nodosome and the protease-centered NLRP3-ASC-caspase-1 inflammasome. It highlights their parallel logic but distinct sensors, adaptors, assemblies, outputs, and disease links.*
