# The IL-1 Signaling Pathway Module: Receptor Engagement, Myddosome Assembly, and TRAF6-Dependent Inflammatory Output

## 1. Executive Summary

The interleukin-1 (IL-1) signaling pathway is a foundational innate immune signaling module that converts extracellular IL-1 cytokine signals into NF-κB-driven inflammatory transcriptional responses. The pathway operates through three hierarchically ordered steps: (i) IL-1β/α engagement of the IL-1R1/IL-1RAcP receptor complex, which juxtaposes intracellular TIR domains; (ii) recruitment and oligomerization of the MyD88 adaptor to form a supramolecular organizing center (the myddosome) incorporating IRAK4 and IRAK1/IRAK2 kinases; and (iii) TRAF6-dependent generation of K63-linked ubiquitin scaffolds that, together with LUBAC-derived M1-linked chains, recruit and activate the TAK1 and IKK complexes to liberate NF-κB for nuclear translocation (pereira2023regulationofinnate pages 2-4, shen2024literaturereviewnuclear pages 5-7, zhukova2025structuralanddynamic pages 7-8). This pathway is subject to multilayered negative regulation by receptor antagonists (IL-1Ra), decoy receptors (IL-1R2), inhibitory receptor family members (IL-1R8/SIGIRR), splice variants (MyD88S), and intracellular modulators (TOLLIP, IRAK-M) (garlanda2025il1familycytokines pages 5-6, supino2022negativeregulationof pages 1-2, pustelny2022mechanismofmyd88s pages 9-11). IL-1 family cytokines and their receptors are evolutionarily ancient, with IL-1R-like TIR domain-containing receptors traceable to early vertebrate evolution approximately 420–535 million years ago (wang2025il1superfamilyacross pages 12-14, boraschi2022whatisil1 pages 3-5). The pathway is now a major therapeutic target, with IRAK4 kinase inhibitors and PROTAC degraders in clinical development for inflammatory and oncological indications (kalliolias2024targetingtlrsignaling pages 8-10, kalliolias2024targetingtlrsignaling pages 5-6).

## 2. Definition and Biological Boundaries

### What the System Includes

The IL-1 signaling pathway module, as defined here, encompasses the events spanning from IL-1β (or IL-1α) binding to the IL-1R1 receptor through the formation of the myddosome signaling complex to the TRAF6-dependent activation of NF-κB and MAPK transcriptional programs. The core molecular components include: the ligands IL-1α and IL-1β; the signaling receptor IL-1R1; the obligatory co-receptor IL-1RAcP (IL-1R3); the adaptor MyD88; the kinases IRAK4 and IRAK1/IRAK2; and the E3 ubiquitin ligase TRAF6 (luis2022thetherapeuticprospects pages 2-4, pereira2023regulationofinnate pages 2-4, vick2025iraksignalingin pages 1-3).

### Boundaries and Neighboring Pathways

Several neighboring pathways share molecular components with IL-1 signaling and must be distinguished. The TLR signaling pathway uses the identical MyD88–IRAK–TRAF6 cassette but is initiated by pattern-recognition receptor engagement rather than IL-1 cytokine binding (pereira2023regulationofinnate pages 2-4, vick2025iraksignalingin pages 1-3). The NLRP3 inflammasome pathway generates mature IL-1β upstream of IL-1R engagement via caspase-1-mediated pro-IL-1β cleavage and should be treated as an upstream activating process rather than part of the IL-1 signaling module per se. Other IL-1 family cytokines (IL-18, IL-33, IL-36α/β/γ) signal through related but distinct receptor complexes that also use IL-1RAcP as a shared co-receptor (frenay2022il1rapakey pages 6-8, garlanda2025il1familycytokines pages 5-6). The TNF receptor signaling pathway converges on NF-κB activation via TRAF2/TRAF5 rather than TRAF6 and should be considered a parallel but mechanistically distinct route to inflammatory transcription.

### Competing Definitions

In the literature, the term "IL-1 signaling" is sometimes used broadly to encompass all IL-1 family cytokine signaling (IL-1, IL-18, IL-33, IL-36, IL-37, IL-38), and sometimes narrowly to denote only IL-1α/β–IL-1R1 signaling (garlanda2025il1familycytokines pages 5-6). This review focuses on the canonical IL-1β/α→IL-1R1/IL-1RAcP→MyD88→IRAK→TRAF6→NF-κB axis, while noting that the downstream myddosome-to-NF-κB cassette is shared with other family members and TLRs.

## 3. Mechanistic Overview

### Step 1: IL-1 Receptor Engagement

IL-1β binds IL-1R1 through two distinct binding sites on the receptor's ectodomain. Site A, located at the interface of Ig-like domains D1 and D2, contains conserved residues shared across IL-1 family ligands. Site B, the agonist-specific "trigger site" on the top of the IL-1β β-trefoil barrel, engages domain D3 of IL-1R1 (luis2022thetherapeuticprospects pages 12-14, zhukova2025structuralanddynamic pages 3-5). Three critical IL-1β residues—Arg11, His30, and Gln32—anchor the cytokine to the D1/D2 receptor surface (luis2022thetherapeuticprospects pages 12-14). Simultaneous engagement of both sites causes D3 to adopt a "closed" conformation via a flexible 6-amino acid linker between D2 and D3, creating a complementary surface that enables recruitment of IL-1RAcP (zhukova2025structuralanddynamic pages 3-5). IL-1β binds IL-1R1 with high affinity (Kd ~10⁻⁹ M), forming a trimeric IL-1β/IL-1R1/IL-1RAcP complex (kim2024il1receptordynamics pages 4-5). The extracellular domain of IL-1RAcP engages domains 2 and 3 of the IL-1β–IL-1R1 binary complex from the lateral aspect, but cannot bind free ligand independently (frenay2022il1rapakey pages 6-8, kim2024il1receptordynamics pages 4-5).

Upon trimeric complex formation, the intracellular TIR domains of IL-1R1 and IL-1RAcP are brought into close proximity, forming a heterodimeric signaling platform. Cryo-EM structural analysis has confirmed that the primary interaction interface involves the BB loop and αB helix of one TIR domain contacting the complementary TIR domain, with secondary stabilization through the CD loop and β-strands (zhukova2025structuralanddynamic pages 7-8). This dimerized TIR platform acts as a molecular beacon for MyD88 recruitment via homotypic TIR–TIR interactions (zhukova2025structuralanddynamic pages 7-8, kim2024il1receptordynamics pages 2-4).

Critically, the receptor antagonist IL-1Ra binds IL-1R1 at site A (D1/D2) but, due to structural differences in its surface loops, fails to productively engage D3, thereby keeping the receptor in an "open" conformation that cannot recruit IL-1RAcP (zhukova2025structuralanddynamic pages 3-5). This mechanism elegantly explains how a closely related ligand can competitively block signaling without initiating it.

### Step 2: Myddosome Assembly

Myddosome formation is a stepwise, hierarchically ordered process. Receptor TIR domain dimerization recruits 6–8 MyD88 molecules via TIR–TIR interactions, either directly or through the bridging adaptor Mal/TIRAP (pereira2023regulationofinnate pages 2-4). MyD88 death domains then oligomerize in a "head-to-tail" helical fashion, creating a large oligomeric scaffold that constitutes the structural core of the myddosome (pereira2023regulationofinnate pages 2-4, zhukova2025structuralanddynamic pages 7-8).

IRAK4 is recruited as the first kinase into this MyD88 scaffold at a 6–8 MyD88 : 4 IRAK4 stoichiometry, forming a characteristic three-layered left-handed helical structure (pereira2023regulationofinnate pages 2-4). IRAK4 then undergoes trans-autophosphorylation and recruits IRAK1 (or IRAK2) to the complex, phosphorylating their kinase domains to activate them (pereira2023regulationofinnate pages 2-4, pereira2023regulationofinnate pages 4-6). IRAK1/IRAK2 subsequently undergo auto-hyperphosphorylation in their proline-serine-threonine-rich (ProST) regions. In resting cells, IRAK1 is sequestered by the adaptor TOLLIP, which must be released for productive myddosome incorporation; BLK kinase phosphorylates TOLLIP at tyrosine residues Y76, Y86, and Y152, promoting TOLLIP dissociation from IRAK1 and enabling IRAK1 participation in the myddosome (li2023blkpositivelyregulates pages 10-15, li2023blkpositivelyregulates pages 15-21).

An important temporal dimension exists: early membrane-bound myddosome complexes containing MyD88, IRAK4, and IRAK1 are short-lived (minutes), but sustained IL-1β stimulation promotes formation of stable cytosolic myddosomes enriched in IRAK2, which sustains later-phase signaling (pereira2023regulationofinnate pages 4-6). The MyD88 oligomerization threshold creates a physical size requirement—only assemblies exceeding a certain size generate sufficiently strong signals to proceed (zhukova2025structuralanddynamic pages 7-8).

### Step 3: TRAF6-Dependent Inflammatory Output

Activated IRAK1 or IRAK2 interacts with TRAF6 through C-terminal TRAF6 binding motifs (TBMs), leading to TRAF6 auto-activation via K63-linked auto-ubiquitination (pereira2023regulationofinnate pages 2-4, pereira2023regulationofinnate pages 6-7). TRAF6, functioning as an E3 ubiquitin ligase in complex with the E2 enzymes Ubc13-Uev1a, generates K63-linked polyubiquitin chains that serve as scaffolds for recruiting downstream signaling complexes (li2024tumornecrosisfactor pages 2-4, li2024tumornecrosisfactor pages 1-2). These K63-linked chains are recognized by TAB2/3, which recruits and activates the TAK1 kinase complex (shen2024literaturereviewnuclear pages 5-7, gerpott2023tnip1regulatesmyddosome pages 20-22).

Simultaneously, LUBAC (the linear ubiquitin chain assembly complex, containing the catalytic subunit HOIL1) catalyzes M1-linked linear ubiquitin chain formation. These M1 chains bind NEMO (IKKγ) with approximately 100-fold higher affinity than K63 chains (gerpott2023tnip1regulatesmyddosome pages 20-22). K63 and M1 chains form hybrid ubiquitin assemblies that spatially segregate TAK1 and IKK complex recruitment, facilitating efficient TAK1-dependent phosphorylation and activation of IKKα/IKKβ (gerpott2023tnip1regulatesmyddosome pages 20-22, zhukova2025structuralanddynamic pages 7-8). Activated IKK then phosphorylates IκBα, triggering its K48-linked polyubiquitination and proteasomal degradation, which releases the NF-κB p65/p50 heterodimer for nuclear translocation and inflammatory gene transcription (shen2024literaturereviewnuclear pages 5-7, li2024tumornecrosisfactor pages 2-4).

A major recent insight is that myddosome clustering at the cell membrane critically regulates NF-κB signaling output. Myddosomes dynamically reorganize into multi-myddosome clusters, and this spatial organization dramatically increases the probability of TRAF6 and LUBAC recruitment (cao2023myddosomeclusteringin pages 13-15, cao2023myddosomeclusteringin pages 1-2). LUBAC is especially sensitive to clustering, with approximately 7–10-fold lower recruitment to single myddosomes compared to clustered ones (cao2023myddosomeclusteringin pages 1-2, cao2023myddosomeclusteringin pages 10-11). TRAF6 is recruited first (average ~67.5 seconds), followed by HOIL1/LUBAC (~118.8 seconds), establishing a temporally ordered signalosome assembly (cao2023myddosomeclusteringin pages 10-11). This clustering mechanism enables both digital (on/off) signaling from individual myddosomes and analog (dose-proportional) responses that scale with IL-1 stimulation intensity (cao2023myddosomeclusteringin pages 13-15, cao2023myddosomeclusteringin pages 11-13, cao2023myddosomeclusteringin pages 4-6).

## 5. Evolutionary and Cell-Biological Variation

### Evolutionary Origins and Conservation

The IL-1 signaling system has deep evolutionary roots. IL-1 family cytokines originated approximately 420 million years ago with early vertebrates, achieving structural maturity early in chordate evolution with major expansion during the amphibian-to-mammalian transition (wang2025il1superfamilyacross pages 12-14). The characteristic β-trefoil fold structure of IL-1 ligands is found across bacteria, fungi, plants, invertebrates, and vertebrates, though vertebrate IL-1 genes lack orthologues in invertebrate clades despite IL-1-like activities being documented in echinoderms, tunicates, and insects (boraschi2022whatisil1 pages 2-3). This suggests that vertebrate IL-1s adopted functions performed by different molecules in invertebrates.

IL-1 receptors share the TIR (Toll–IL-1R) signaling domain with Toll-like receptors, representing hybrid receptors that combined TLR-like intracellular signaling domains with distinct Ig-like extracellular portions to provide high binding specificity (boraschi2022whatisil1 pages 2-3, boraschi2022whatisil1 pages 3-5). IL-1R emergence likely coincided with the 1R/2R whole-genome duplication events (535–485 million years ago) (wang2025il1superfamilyacross pages 12-14, wang2025il1superfamilyacross pages 14-15). SIGIRR homologs have been identified even in hagfish (basal vertebrates), and phylogenetic analysis suggests that an ancestral SIGIRR may have given rise to IL-18R1, IL-18RAP, and IL-1RAcP (wang2025il1superfamilyacross pages 11-12, wang2025il1superfamilyacross pages 8-11).

Notably, certain IL-1 system components are more recent innovations: IL-1α, IL-33, and IL-36 arose approximately 160 million years ago, and the decoy receptor IL-1R2 appears unique to mammals (wang2025il1superfamilyacross pages 8-11). The conserved motifs "F-F" and "FES-PG-WF" in IL-1 family ligands exhibit remarkable structural stability throughout evolution, likely representing critical functional domains (wang2025il1superfamilyacross pages 11-12, wang2025il1superfamilyacross pages 14-15).

### Cell-Type Specific Variation

IL-1 signaling exhibits important cell-type specific differences. In CD4⁺ T cells, IL-1R1/IL-1R3 signaling is critical for Th17 cell differentiation, expansion, and survival, regulated by prostaglandin E2, IL-6, IL-21, and TGF-β through STAT3, IRF4, and RORα/γt (kim2024il1receptordynamics pages 5-7). In CD8⁺ T cells, IL-1 signaling enhances expansion, differentiation, and memory responses, with IL-21 predominantly inducing IL-1R1 expression (kim2024il1receptordynamics pages 5-7). Regulatory T cells (Tregs) upregulate IL-1R2 expression through NFAT/FOXP3-mediated transcription, dampening IL-1-dependent inflammatory responses (kim2024il1receptordynamics pages 9-10). In macrophages, M2 polarization is associated with upregulation of IL-1R2, while anti-inflammatory signals such as glucocorticoids, prostaglandins, and Th2 cytokines (IL-4/IL-13) enhance IL-1R2 expression across monocytes, macrophages, and neutrophils (kim2024il1receptordynamics pages 9-10).

## 6. Constraints, Dependencies, and Failure Modes

### Obligatory Sequential Events

Several steps in the IL-1 signaling cascade must occur in a strict order. IL-1RAcP cannot bind free IL-1; it can only be recruited after IL-1 has bound IL-1R1 and induced the D3 conformational change (zhukova2025structuralanddynamic pages 3-5, frenay2022il1rapakey pages 6-8). MyD88 recruitment requires prior TIR domain dimerization at the receptor level. Within the myddosome, IRAK4 must be recruited and activated before IRAK1/IRAK2, as IRAK4 kinase activity is required for IRAK1/IRAK2 phosphorylation (pereira2023regulationofinnate pages 2-4, pereira2022theirak4scaffold pages 10-11). TRAF6 activation requires prior IRAK activation, and NF-κB activation requires the sequential generation of K63-linked (TRAF6) followed by M1-linked (LUBAC) ubiquitin chains (gerpott2023tnip1regulatesmyddosome pages 20-22, cao2023myddosomeclusteringin pages 10-11).

### Negative Regulatory Mechanisms

The IL-1 system employs an extraordinary multilayered negative regulatory architecture. At the extracellular level, IL-1Ra competitively occupies IL-1R1 without triggering co-receptor recruitment (garlanda2025il1familycytokines pages 5-6, zhukova2025structuralanddynamic pages 3-5). IL-1R2, the first identified decoy receptor, sequesters IL-1 ligands and IL-1RAcP through multiple mechanisms: membrane-bound competitive binding, soluble form ligand trapping via ectodomain shedding, prevention of pro-IL-1β cleavage by caspase-1, and intracellular decoy activity preventing pro-IL-1α cleavage by calpain (garlanda2025il1familycytokines pages 5-6, supino2022negativeregulationof pages 2-3). IL-1R8/SIGIRR interferes with productive TIR adaptor recruitment and limits NF-κB and JNK activation (garlanda2025il1familycytokines pages 5-6, supino2022negativeregulationof pages 1-2).

At the intracellular level, MyD88S (an alternatively spliced variant lacking the intermediate domain) is recruited to nucleating myddosomes but stalls their growth by preventing incorporation of additional MyD88 molecules, effectively quenching signal amplification. The critical residue Tyr116 in the intermediate domain is essential for productive myddosome nucleation (pustelny2022mechanismofmyd88s pages 9-11, pustelny2022mechanismofmyd88s pages 1-2). TOLLIP sequesters IRAK1 in resting cells and promotes endocytic downregulation of activated IL-1R1 through lysosomal targeting (pereira2023regulationofinnate pages 6-7, gerpott2023tnip1regulatesmyddosome pages 16-18). IRAK-M (IRAK3) can form myddosomes with MyD88 and IRAK4 but limits productive signaling output, functioning as an intracellular brake particularly in tolerant myeloid states (pereira2023regulationofinnate pages 6-7).

### Failure Modes

Loss of negative regulators leads to uncontrolled inflammation. IL-1R2-deficient mice show augmented germinal center responses, increased Tfh cells, GC B cells, and antigen-specific antibodies—effects reversed by IL-1 blockade—demonstrating that endogenous IL-1 is normally restrained by IL-1R2 in germinal center reactions (kim2024il1receptordynamics pages 4-5). Conversely, loss-of-function mutations in MyD88 or IRAK4 result in severe immunodeficiency characterized by susceptibility to pyogenic bacterial infections, underscoring the essential role of the myddosome in antibacterial defense (vick2025iraksignalingin pages 1-3).

## 7. Controversies and Open Questions

### Kinase vs. Scaffold Function of IRAK4

A major unresolved question concerns the relative importance of IRAK4's kinase activity versus its scaffolding function. The clinical failure of the IRAK4 kinase inhibitor zimlovisertib in rheumatoid arthritis and hidradenitis suppurativa suggests that kinase-only inhibition may be insufficient in some inflammatory contexts (kalliolias2024targetingtlrsignaling pages 8-10, kalliolias2024targetingtlrsignaling pages 5-6). This has motivated the development of PROTAC degraders such as KT-474 (SAR444656), which eliminate both kinase activity and scaffold function by inducing proteasomal degradation of IRAK4 via cereblon-mediated ubiquitination (kalliolias2024targetingtlrsignaling pages 8-10). IRAK4's scaffold function has been demonstrated as essential for integrating TRIF-dependent and MyD88-dependent signaling downstream of TLR4, independent of its kinase activity (pereira2022theirak4scaffold pages 10-11).

### IRAK Compensation and Redundancy

Evidence indicates that IRAK1 can compensate for IRAK4 loss in certain leukemia models, suggesting that monotherapy targeting IRAK4 alone may be insufficient (vick2025iraksignalingin pages 11-12). This has driven the development of dual IRAK1/4 inhibitors such as KME-0584 and R289, which show superior anti-leukemic effects compared to IRAK4-only inhibitors in preclinical models (vick2025iraksignalingin pages 11-12, vick2025iraksignalingin pages 6-7).

### Myddosome Spatial Organization

The recent discovery that myddosome clustering regulates both digital and analog signaling outputs raises questions about how this spatial organization is achieved in different cell types and physiological contexts (cao2023myddosomeclusteringin pages 13-15, cao2023myddosomeclusteringin pages 1-2). Whether the clustering-dependent recruitment of LUBAC (with its ~7–10-fold sensitivity to myddosome density) represents a universal mechanism or varies between immune and non-immune cells remains to be determined (cao2023myddosomeclusteringin pages 1-2, cao2023myddosomeclusteringin pages 10-11).

### Developmental Compensation

Intriguingly, germline IL-1R2 knockout mice do not recapitulate the augmented germinal center phenotype observed upon conditional adult deletion, implying that developmental compensatory mechanisms can counteract IL-1R2 loss when it occurs early in life (kim2024il1receptordynamics pages 4-5). The molecular nature of this compensation is unknown.

### Evolutionary Questions

While TIR domains are found across all kingdoms of life—in bacterial defense systems, plant NLR immunity, and animal innate immunity—the functional relationship between these domains remains incompletely understood (boraschi2022whatisil1 pages 2-3, wang2025il1superfamilyacross pages 11-12). Recent evidence that plant and bacterial TIR domains possess enzymatic activities (NADase function) raises questions about whether ancestral animal TIR domains also had catalytic activities that were subsequently lost or repurposed for scaffolding functions (boraschi2022whatisil1 pages 2-3).
