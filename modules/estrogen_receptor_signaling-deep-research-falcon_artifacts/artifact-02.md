| Tissue/Organ | Predominant ER Subtype | Functional Outcome of ER Signaling | Key Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Breast | ERα dominant | Predominantly proliferative and developmental signaling; in breast cancer, ERα commonly drives growth and hormone-responsive transcription | ERα is expressed in ducts and lobules and is the major receptor in mammary development; ERβ is present in luminal, myoepithelial, and stromal compartments and often counteracts ERα-driven proliferation. The ERα/ERβ ratio is an important determinant of net estrogen response (pqac-00000034, pqac-00000036, pqac-00000038) |
| Bone | Mixed, compartment-specific: ERα in cortical bone; ERβ in trabecular bone/osteoclast-enriched compartments | Regulation of bone growth, remodeling, and maintenance; receptor effects can be partially antagonistic or specialized by compartment | ERα is reported as predominant in cortical bone, whereas ERβ is enriched in trabecular bone and osteoclast-related contexts, indicating spatial specialization rather than a single dominant receptor across all bone (pqac-00000036, pqac-00000039) |
| Brain | Both ERα and ERβ | Region-specific control of neuroendocrine, behavioral, and neural functions | ERα and ERβ are co-expressed but show distinct anatomical distributions: ERα is enriched in hypothalamic and related reproductive/autonomic nuclei, whereas ERβ is enriched in regions such as lateral septum and basolateral amygdala; similar overall expression levels have been reported in some summaries, but functional partitioning is strong (pqac-00000035, pqac-00000037, pqac-00000038) |
| Prostate | ERβ in epithelium; ERα in stromal/basal compartments | Tissue-compartment-specific estrogen responses; ERβ is generally associated with epithelial restraint/differentiation, whereas ERα is linked to stromal signaling | Prostate is one of the clearest examples of compartmentalized ER biology: ERβ predominates in epithelial cells, while ERα is mainly stromal and basal. This distinction is important for understanding divergent estrogen effects in prostate physiology and disease (pqac-00000035, pqac-00000037) |
| Uterus/Endometrium | ERα dominant | Strong estrogen responsiveness linked to endometrial growth, reproductive cycling, and classical genomic signaling | ERα is preferentially expressed in uterus/endometrium and is a major driver of uterine estrogen sensitivity; GPER1 is also detected in endometrium but is generally considered part of neighboring membrane-initiated estrogen signaling rather than the canonical nuclear pathway module (pqac-00000034, pqac-00000035, pqac-00000037) |
| Vasculature | Both ERα and ERβ | Regulation of vascular/endothelial responses, with additional input from membrane-associated estrogen signaling | Both receptors are present in cardiovascular/vascular tissues; GPER1 is also detected in vasculature and heart endothelium, so vascular estrogen biology often blends classical nuclear signaling with membrane-initiated pathways (pqac-00000034, pqac-00000037) |
| Kidney | ERβ dominant | Non-reproductive estrogen responses, likely including tissue-protective and metabolic/regulatory effects | Kidney is repeatedly cited as a tissue with predominant ERβ expression, illustrating the broader pattern that ERβ is enriched outside classic reproductive organs (pqac-00000034) |


*Table: This table summarizes how ERα and ERβ are distributed across major estrogen-responsive tissues and how that distribution shapes functional output. It is useful for interpreting why the same ligand can produce different biological effects in breast, bone, brain, prostate, uterus, vasculature, and kidney.*
