| Component | Gene Symbol | Molecular Function | Role in Pathway | Key Features/Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| IFN-γ ligand | **IFNG** | Secreted type II interferon cytokine; homodimeric ligand | Initiates signaling by binding IFNGR1 first, then promoting cooperative assembly of the full IFNGR1/IFNGR2 receptor complex | Type II IFN is structurally distinct from type I/III IFNs; the signaling complex contains one IFN-γ dimer plus two IFNGR1 and two IFNGR2 chains (1:2:2 stoichiometry) (pqac-00000005, pqac-00000006, pqac-00000035) |
| IFN-γ receptor 1 | **IFNGR1** | High-affinity ligand-binding receptor chain | Binds IFN-γ directly and provides the main receptor docking platform that enables JAK1 activation and STAT1 recruitment after receptor phosphorylation | IFNGR1 dimerization is especially important for full STAT1 phosphorylation; Tyr440 on IFNGR1 is a key phosphotyrosine docking site for STAT1 (pqac-00000001, pqac-00000005, pqac-00000006) |
| IFN-γ receptor 2 | **IFNGR2** | Accessory/signaling receptor chain | Recruited after IFN-γ–IFNGR1 binding to complete the active receptor complex and support JAK2-dependent signal propagation | Site-2 interaction with IFN-γ is less extensive than IFNGR1 binding, but IFNGR2 is required for full signaling output; can be transcriptionally downregulated in regulatory states (pqac-00000005, pqac-00000000, pqac-00000004) |
| JAK1 | **JAK1** | Receptor-associated nonreceptor tyrosine kinase | Associated with IFNGR1; activated upon receptor assembly and participates in receptor-chain phosphorylation and STAT phosphorylation | Core kinase in type II IFN signaling; works with JAK2 rather than TYK2, distinguishing type II from type I/III IFN pathways (pqac-00000000, pqac-00000035, pqac-00000036, pqac-00000037) |
| JAK2 | **JAK2** | Receptor-associated nonreceptor tyrosine kinase | Associated with IFNGR2; trans-activates with JAK1 and phosphorylates receptor tails and STAT1 | JAK2 is a defining proximal kinase of IFN-γ signaling; complete pathway output depends on the JAK1/JAK2 pair (pqac-00000000, pqac-00000003, pqac-00000035) |
| STAT1 | **STAT1** | Signal transducer and transcription factor | Principal canonical effector; recruited to phospho-IFNGR1, phosphorylated mainly at Tyr701, dimerizes, enters nucleus, and binds GAS-containing promoters | Canonical IFN-γ signaling mainly uses STAT1 homodimers rather than ISGF3; Ser727 enhances transcriptional potency; recent work suggests context-dependent noncanonical phosphorylation such as Thr748 in inflammatory settings (pqac-00000001, pqac-00000020, pqac-00000022, pqac-00000023) |
| IRF1 | **IRF1** | Secondary transcription factor downstream of STAT1 | Amplifies and diversifies transcriptional output by activating secondary IFN-γ-responsive genes after primary STAT1/GAS signaling | IRF1 is a major downstream effector and is itself STAT1-dependent; important for antimicrobial, antigen-presentation, and tumor-related outputs; human IRF1 deficiency can phenocopy severe defects in IFN-γ immunity to mycobacteria (pqac-00000045, pqac-00000046, pqac-00000027) |
| SOCS1 | **SOCS1** | Feedback inhibitor of cytokine/JAK signaling | Terminates or dampens IFN-γ signaling after induction by the pathway | Induced by IFN-γ via STAT1/IRF1-linked transcription; inhibits JAKs through its KIR pseudosubstrate region and SH2-mediated binding, including interaction with JAK2 activation-loop phosphotyrosines; major checkpoint in immune homeostasis (pqac-00000012, pqac-00000013) |
| Protein tyrosine phosphatases | **PTPN6 (SHP1), PTPN11 (SHP2), PTPN2 (TC-PTP)** | Dephosphorylation of receptor/JAK/STAT components | Contribute to signal termination/desensitization by removing phosphates from activated pathway components, especially STAT1 | Strong general evidence across IFN systems supports phosphatase-mediated signal shutdown; macrophage studies further support phosphatase-driven signaling memory/desensitization after transient IFN exposure (pqac-00000011, pqac-00000003) |
| PIAS family regulator | **PIAS1** | SUMO-associated transcriptional coregulator/inhibitor of STAT signaling | Generally considered a nuclear negative regulator that restrains STAT1-dependent transcriptional activity | Often discussed as a STAT1 brake in the broader JAK-STAT literature, but this specific evidence set did not provide direct mechanistic detail for IFN-γ-focused claims; interpret cautiously here (pqac-00000012) |
| Unconventional STAT branch | **STAT3** | Alternative STAT-family transcription factor | In some contexts, especially macrophages, IFN-γ can also activate STAT3 alongside STAT1 | Evidence for a direct JAK1/2-STAT3 branch in macrophages is emerging but not yet canonical; reported as rapid and direct, with STAT1:STAT3 heterodimers and limited gene-regulatory contribution relative to STAT1 (pqac-00000018, pqac-00000036) |
| DNA response element | **GAS** | Promoter/enhancer DNA motif recognized by STAT1 homodimers | Primary cis-regulatory element of canonical IFN-γ target genes | Consensus palindromic motif often described as **TTCN2-4GAA**; distinguishes type II IFN transcriptional output from type I/III ISRE-centric signaling (pqac-00000043, pqac-00000045, pqac-00000048) |
| DNA response element | **ISRE** | Interferon-stimulated response element recognized mainly by ISGF3 or related complexes | Not the primary canonical element for IFN-γ, but relevant for overlap/crosstalk with type I/III IFN programs and some secondary responses | Canonical type I/III pathways use STAT1-STAT2-IRF9 (ISGF3) binding to ISREs; IFN-γ is instead predominantly GAS/STAT1-homodimer driven, though pathway overlap exists (pqac-00000035, pqac-00000036, pqac-00000046) |
| Antigen presentation program | **CIITA, HLA/MHC-II genes, NLRC5, LMP/APM genes** | Transcriptional regulators and antigen-processing/presentation machinery | Important downstream outputs that enhance cell-mediated immunity and APC function | IFN-γ strongly induces CIITA and MHC-related pathways; this is conserved in vertebrates including zebrafish for the IFN-γ–IRF1–CIITA–MHCII axis (pqac-00000046, pqac-00000009, pqac-00000010) |
| Chemokine output | **CXCL9, CXCL10, CXCL11** | Chemokines that recruit effector lymphocytes | Downstream immune-effector module of IFN-γ signaling | Frequently used readouts of IFN-γ activity; CXCL9 induction can occur even where broader IFN-γ responses are attenuated, such as in alveolar macrophages (pqac-00000045, pqac-00000049, pqac-00000050) |
| Immune checkpoint/immune evasion ISGs | **CD274 (PD-L1), IDO1** | Immunoregulatory effector genes | Context-dependent downstream targets, especially prominent in tumors and chronically stimulated cells | Illustrate the dual biology of IFN-γ: antitumor immunity versus adaptive resistance/immunosuppression; induction is commonly mediated through JAK1/STAT1/IRF1 signaling (pqac-00000042, pqac-00000044) |
| Antimicrobial and inflammatory ISGs | **CYBB, IFI30, CASP4/5, APOL family, ISG15, IFIT1/3, CCL2/3/4/7** | Effector proteins involved in microbial killing, antigen processing, inflammasome/cell-death pathways, and leukocyte recruitment | Execute cell-type-specific biological outputs downstream of IFN-γ | IFN-γ induces hundreds of genes; these examples span antimicrobial restriction, vesicle biology, chemotaxis, and inflammatory amplification, with substantial variation by cell type and stimulus history (pqac-00000046, pqac-00000047, pqac-00000007) |


*Table: This table summarizes the principal molecular components, regulatory elements, and representative downstream targets of the type II interferon signaling module. It is useful for linking receptor-proximal events to transcriptional outputs, feedback control, and context-dependent variation.*
