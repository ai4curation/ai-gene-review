| Regulator | Target in Pathway | Mechanism of Action | Key References/Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| SOCS1 | JAK1/TYK2; receptor-proximal signaling | Induced as an IFN-stimulated negative-feedback factor; its kinase inhibitory region acts as a JAK pseudosubstrate/JAK-binding inhibitor, blocking substrate phosphorylation and dampening STAT activation | Strongly supported as a receptor-level brake in IFN-I signaling (pqac-00000019, pqac-00000020, pqac-00000022, pqac-00000024) |
| SOCS3 | Receptor complexes; JAK-STAT signaling | IFN-induced feedback inhibitor that associates with receptor complexes via SH2-dependent interactions and suppresses downstream signaling; generally weaker/more context-dependent than SOCS1 in IFN-I pathways | Often grouped with SOCS1 as a canonical feedback regulator; mechanism is supported but less specifically resolved for IFN-I than SOCS1 (pqac-00000021, pqac-00000022, pqac-00000023) |
| USP18/ISG15 | IFNAR2-JAK1 module | USP18 is recruited to IFNAR2 through STAT2 and competitively inhibits/displaces JAK1, functioning as a major receptor brake; ISG15 stabilizes USP18 and prevents its degradation, strengthening this negative-feedback loop | Central termination mechanism; deficiency in USP18/ISG15 is linked to interferonopathy (pqac-00000019, pqac-00000021, pqac-00000022, pqac-00000024) |
| PTP1B | IFNAR1; TYK2 | Dephosphorylates IFNAR1 to restrict receptor ubiquitination/endocytosis and dephosphorylates TYK2, thereby limiting pathway activation | Evidence supports both receptor and kinase control, though details vary by study/system (pqac-00000020, pqac-00000021) |
| SHP1/SHP2 | Receptor and STAT components | Protein tyrosine phosphatases that terminate signaling by dephosphorylating pathway components in cytoplasm and/or nucleus | Broadly supported as phosphatase-mediated off-switches in IFN-I signaling (pqac-00000019, pqac-00000023) |
| PTPN2 | Receptor and STAT components | Tyrosine phosphatase that dephosphorylates receptor/STAT signaling nodes to terminate IFN-I responses | Most often discussed together with SHP1/SHP2 as part of phosphatase-mediated signal shutdown (pqac-00000019) |
| PJA2 | TYK2, JAK1 | E3 ubiquitin ligase identified by proximity labeling; promotes non-degradative ubiquitination of TYK2 and JAK1, limiting TYK2 activating phosphorylation and downstream STAT signaling without degrading the kinases | Recent 2024 mechanistic advance; ubiquitination can occur on non-lysine residues and likely constrains kinase conformation/activity (pqac-00000043, pqac-00000044, pqac-00000045, pqac-00000047, pqac-00000049, pqac-00000050) |
| beta-TrCP (E3 ligase for IFNAR1) | IFNAR1 | Phosphorylation-dependent recruitment to IFNAR1 (notably after Ser535 phosphorylation), promoting receptor ubiquitination and lysosomal degradation | A well-established pathway for attenuating receptor availability and terminating signaling (pqac-00000020) |
| MID1 (E3 ligase for IFNAR2) | IFNAR2 | Mediates K48- and K63-linked polyubiquitination of IFNAR2, promoting lysosomal degradation after prolonged IFN-I exposure | Negative-feedback mechanism reducing receptor abundance; described alongside inhibitory IFNAR2 isoforms (pqac-00000026) |
| SLIM/Smurf1 (E3 ligases for STATs) | STAT1 (and other STAT components) | Promote ubiquitin-dependent degradation of STAT proteins; Smurf1 catalyzes K48-linked polyubiquitination of STAT1, and SLIM similarly drives proteasome-dependent STAT1 turnover | Acts downstream of receptor/JAK control to reduce transcription-factor availability (pqac-00000019, pqac-00000025) |
| BRCC36/BRISC (deubiquitylase) | IFNAR1; indirectly IFN signaling amplitude | Deubiquitylates IFNAR1 to sustain receptor surface abundance and signaling; pharmacologic BRISC inhibition increases IFNAR1 ubiquitination and lowers cell-surface receptor levels, thereby reducing ISG induction | This is a positive regulator of signaling rather than a negative regulator; included here because its inhibition reveals a key feedback-sensitive ubiquitin checkpoint (pqac-00000025) |
| Linear ubiquitin chains on STAT1 | STAT1 | Linear ubiquitination at K652 reduces STAT1 interaction with the IFN-I receptor complex, limiting signaling output | Illustrates non-proteolytic ubiquitin control of signaling competence rather than protein stability (pqac-00000022) |


*Table: This table summarizes the main inhibitory and feedback mechanisms that restrain canonical type I interferon signaling at the receptor, kinase, and STAT levels. It is useful for distinguishing signal-terminating modules from pathway-amplifying components and for identifying points of therapeutic intervention.*
