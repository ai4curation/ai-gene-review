# The Fructose and Mannose Metabolism Boundary Module: Separating Central-Carbon Entry from Envelope-Polysaccharide Biosynthesis in Pseudomonads

## 1. Executive Summary

KEGG pathway map ppu00051 ("fructose and mannose metabolism") for *Pseudomonas putida* KT2440 groups together reactions that are biochemically adjacent but functionally distinct: the phosphoenolpyruvate (PEP)-dependent phosphotransferase system (PTS) for fructose import, the cytoplasmic phosphorylation of fructose-1-phosphate (F1P) to fructose-1,6-bisphosphate (FBP), the GDP-mannose precursor biosynthesis from fructose-6-phosphate (F6P), the irreversible oxidation of GDP-mannose to GDP-mannuronate, the synthase-dependent polymerization/modification/export of alginate, and the GDP-mannose 4,6-dehydratase branch that feeds deoxy-sugar nucleotide pathways for fucose or rhamnose. This review argues that species-level metabolic curation should distinguish these as separate functional sub-modules connected through shared metabolites (principally F6P and GDP-mannose) rather than treating them as a single pathway. The fructose PTS entry reactions are soluble, cytoplasmic, and central-carbon-oriented; the alginate biosynthesis machinery is membrane-spanning, envelope-directed, and subject to entirely different regulatory logic (notably c-di-GMP signaling); and the deoxy-sugar branches serve glycoconjugate-specific roles that vary dramatically across lineages.

### 2.2 What should be treated separately

Several neighboring processes are frequently confused with this module but should be curated independently. Glucose catabolism in *P. putida* proceeds primarily through the Entner-Doudoroff (ED) pathway and pentose phosphate (PP) pathway rather than through classical Embden-Meyerhof-Parnas (EMP) glycolysis, and is regulated by distinct mechanisms including the HexR, GnuR, and GltR/GtrS systems (nguyen2021theanoxicelectrode‐driven pages 2-4). The Psl and Pel exopolysaccharide biosynthesis pathways of *P. aeruginosa*, while sharing some regulatory overlap with alginate, use different precursors and distinct biosynthetic machineries (gheorghita2023pseudomonasaeruginosabiofilm pages 4-5). TDP-L-rhamnose biosynthesis via the Rml pathway (RmlA-D) starts from TDP-glucose rather than GDP-mannose and should not be conflated with the GDP-mannose-derived deoxy-sugar branches discussed here (wagstaff2021ndprhamnosebiosynthesisand pages 6-9).

### 2.3 The KEGG curation problem

KEGG map00051 treats fructose and mannose metabolism as a single pathway entity. However, in *P. putida* KT2440, the fructose PTS genes (*fruBKA*) are under Cra-mediated transcriptional repression and are only activated when fructose is the carbon source (huang2024insightsintothe pages 5-6, huang2024insightsintothe pages 3-5), whereas the alginate biosynthesis genes reside in a separate 12-gene operon whose transcription is governed by the alternative sigma factor AlgU/AlgT and a suite of regulators including AlgR, AlgB, AmrZ, and c-di-GMP-responsive elements (serrato2024bacterialalginatebiosynthesis pages 6-8, balikova2022roleofexopolysaccharides pages 1-2). These regulatory systems are largely non-overlapping, supporting the argument that species-level curation should distinguish soluble central-carbon entry reactions from alginate and envelope-polysaccharide biosynthesis.

## 3. Mechanistic Overview

### 3.1 Sub-module 1: Fructose PTS import

In *P. putida* and other pseudomonads, fructose is imported via a PEP-dependent phosphotransferase system composed of the fructose-specific EIIBC (or EIIC) component FruA and the multiphosphoryl transfer protein FruB (huang2024insightsintothe pages 5-6). This system couples fructose translocation across the inner membrane to concomitant phosphorylation at the C-1 position, yielding cytoplasmic F1P and pyruvate from PEP. The Cra (catabolite repressor/activator) transcription factor represses expression of *fruA*, *fruB*, and *fruK* when fructose is absent; F1P acts as the physiological effector that releases Cra from its operators, de-repressing the fructose operon (huang2024insightsintothe pages 3-5). This regulatory circuit ensures that fructose PTS entry is conditional on substrate availability. Under unusual conditions such as bioelectrochemical cultivation, fructose can be partially isomerized to mannose in the periplasm and then oxidized via the periplasmic oxidation cascade (POC), revealing an alternative non-PTS route (nguyen2021theanoxicelectrode‐driven pages 2-4).

### 3.2 Sub-module 2: Fructose-1-phosphate phosphorylation

The 1-phosphofructokinase FruK phosphorylates F1P to FBP using ATP. This reaction commits the fructose carbon skeleton to central metabolism via the lower glycolytic pathway (huang2024insightsintothe pages 5-6, huang2024insightsintothe pages 3-5). FruK is also implicated in the interconversion of F1P and FBP, as recent evidence suggests FruK can function reversibly, converting FBP back to F1P; this may have regulatory significance for Cra-mediated flux sensing, though this remains debated (huang2024insightsintothe pages 3-5).

### 3.3 Sub-module 3: GDP-mannose precursor synthesis

The pathway from F6P to GDP-mannose is the critical boundary zone where central carbon metabolism feeds into envelope polysaccharide precursor chemistry. Three enzymatic activities, carried out by two gene products in *Pseudomonas*, perform this conversion. AlgA is a bifunctional enzyme: its phosphomannose isomerase (PMI) domain converts F6P to mannose-6-phosphate (M6P), and its guanosine-diphosphomannose pyrophosphorylase (GMP) domain converts mannose-1-phosphate (M1P) to GDP-mannose using GTP (balikova2022roleofexopolysaccharides pages 2-3, serrato2024bacterialalginatebiosynthesis pages 6-8, gheorghita2023pseudomonasaeruginosabiofilm pages 7-8). AlgC is a phosphomannomutase that interconverts M6P and M1P (balikova2022roleofexopolysaccharides pages 2-3, serrato2024bacterialalginatebiosynthesis pages 6-8, gheorghita2023pseudomonasaeruginosabiofilm pages 4-5). Notably, AlgC also participates in rhamnolipid and lipopolysaccharide biosynthesis, making it a shared node between multiple envelope glycan pathways (gheorghita2023pseudomonasaeruginosabiofilm pages 7-8, gheorghita2023pseudomonasaeruginosabiofilm pages 4-5).

### 3.4 Sub-module 4: GDP-mannuronate formation

GDP-mannose dehydrogenase AlgD catalyzes the irreversible NAD⁺-dependent four-electron oxidation of GDP-mannose to GDP-mannuronic acid (GDP-ManA), representing the committed and rate-limiting step in alginate biosynthesis (serrato2024bacterialalginatebiosynthesis pages 6-8, balikova2022roleofexopolysaccharides pages 1-2). The irreversibility of this reaction is a critical regulatory feature: once GDP-mannose is oxidized, the product is committed to the alginate pathway. AlgD purified from mucoid *P. aeruginosa* strains exhibits strong negative cooperativity for its substrate GDP-mannose, with a high-affinity K_M of ~13 μM and a low-affinity K_M of ~3 mM, consistent with a functional dimer (balikova2022roleofexopolysaccharides pages 1-2). This kinetic behavior may serve as a metabolic checkpoint, ensuring that alginate precursor synthesis only proceeds when GDP-mannose levels are sufficient.

### 3.5 Sub-module 5: Alginate polymerization, modification, and export

This sub-module constitutes a synthase-dependent secretion system spanning both membranes of the Gram-negative cell envelope (gheorghita2023pseudomonasaeruginosabiofilm pages 4-5).

**Polymerization.** The inner membrane glycosyltransferase Alg8 polymerizes GDP-ManA into a polymannuronate chain. Alg8 shares homology with class II glycosyltransferases and requires activation by its co-polymerase Alg44 (serrato2024bacterialalginatebiosynthesis pages 6-8, gheorghita2023pseudomonasaeruginosabiofilm pages 7-8). Alg44 contains a cytoplasmic PilZ domain that binds the second messenger cyclic-di-GMP (c-di-GMP); this binding is essential for polymerization to proceed (moradali2020theregulationof pages 233-235, martinezortiz2020cyclicdigmpmediatedregulation pages 3-6, serrato2024bacterialalginatebiosynthesis pages 6-8, gheorghita2023pseudomonasaeruginosabiofilm pages 7-8). The Alg8-Alg44 complex forms a functional polymerase unit whose activity is post-translationally controlled by localized c-di-GMP pools, generated in part by specific diguanylate cyclases such as MucR (martinezortiz2020cyclicdigmpmediatedregulation pages 3-6, ponce2025advancesinalginate pages 4-6).

**Periplasmic modification.** The nascent polymannuronate chain is translocated to the periplasm where it undergoes two major modifications. AlgG, a periplasmic C5-mannuronan epimerase, converts selected β-D-mannuronate residues to α-L-guluronate, altering the polymer's gelling and material properties (serrato2024bacterialalginatebiosynthesis pages 8-11, gheorghita2023pseudomonasaeruginosabiofilm pages 4-5, balikova2022roleofexopolysaccharides pages 5-7). The O-acetylation machinery (AlgI, AlgJ, AlgF, and AlgX) acetylates the polymer at O2 and/or O3 hydroxyl groups; O-acetylation protects alginate from AlgL-mediated degradation and blocks epimerization at acetylated residues (gheorghita2023pseudomonasaeruginosabiofilm pages 4-5, serrato2024bacterialalginatebiosynthesis pages 8-11). AlgL, a periplasmic alginate lyase, degrades non-acetylated alginate via β-elimination and serves a quality-control function by clearing mislocalized polymer (serrato2024bacterialalginatebiosynthesis pages 8-11, gheorghita2023pseudomonasaeruginosabiofilm pages 4-5, balikova2022roleofexopolysaccharides pages 3-5).

**Export.** The periplasmic scaffold proteins AlgX, AlgG, and AlgK guide the polymer toward the outer membrane, where the β-barrel porin AlgE secretes mature alginate to the cell surface (gheorghita2023pseudomonasaeruginosabiofilm pages 4-5, balikova2022roleofexopolysaccharides pages 5-7, balikova2022roleofexopolysaccharides pages 3-5, serrato2024bacterialalginatebiosynthesis pages 11-13). AlgK is a lipoprotein with tetratricopeptide-like repeats that stabilizes AlgE in the outer membrane and interacts with the inner membrane components through Alg44 (balikova2022roleofexopolysaccharides pages 5-7, balikova2022roleofexopolysaccharides pages 3-5, serrato2024bacterialalginatebiosynthesis pages 11-13). Deletion of *algK*, *algX*, or *algG* results in secretion of small uronic acid degradation products rather than intact polymer, demonstrating that a properly assembled transenvelope complex is required for polymer export (gheorghita2023pseudomonasaeruginosabiofilm pages 4-5, serrato2024bacterialalginatebiosynthesis pages 24-27).

### 3.6 Sub-module 6: Deoxy-sugar side branches

GDP-mannose serves as a precursor not only for alginate but also for deoxy-sugar nucleotides. GDP-mannose 4,6-dehydratase (Gmd/GMD) catalyzes the first committed step, converting GDP-mannose to GDP-4-keto-6-deoxy-mannose (wagstaff2021ndprhamnosebiosynthesisand pages 6-9, sraphet2021computationalcharacterizationsof pages 2-6). This intermediate can then be processed by different reductases or epimerases: GDP-4-keto-6-deoxy-D-mannose 3,5-epimerase-4-reductase (GMER/WcaG/FX) produces GDP-β-L-fucose, while GDP-4-keto-rhamnose reductase (Rmd) produces GDP-α-D-rhamnose (wagstaff2021ndprhamnosebiosynthesisand pages 6-9). In rhizobia, the NoeL enzyme (a GDP-mannose 4,6-dehydratase homolog) provides precursors for Nod factor modification critical for symbiotic host recognition (sraphet2021computationalcharacterizationsof pages 2-6). Importantly, the L-rhamnose nucleotide sugar (TDP-β-L-rhamnose) used in many bacterial glycoconjugates is synthesized via the entirely separate Rml pathway from TDP-glucose rather than from GDP-mannose (wagstaff2021ndprhamnosebiosynthesisand pages 6-9). This distinction is important for pathway curation.

## 5. Evolutionary and Cell-Biological Variation

### 5.1 Ancient and conserved components

The GDP-mannose precursor pathway (F6P → M6P → M1P → GDP-mannose) is deeply conserved across bacteria and eukaryotes, reflecting the universal requirement for mannose-containing glycoconjugates (balikova2022roleofexopolysaccharides pages 2-3, serrato2024bacterialalginatebiosynthesis pages 6-8, mazeas2024candidategenesinvolved pages 2-4). GDP-mannose 4,6-dehydratase (Gmd) is likewise ancient and widely distributed, with representatives found in bacteria, archaea, eukaryotes, and even giant viruses (wagstaff2021ndprhamnosebiosynthesisand pages 6-9, sraphet2021computationalcharacterizationsof pages 2-6). The core alginate polymerization machinery (Alg8/Alg44) and its c-di-GMP regulatory logic appear conserved between *Pseudomonas* and *Azotobacter*, both members of the Pseudomonadaceae, suggesting a common ancestral system within this family (ponce2025advancesinalginate pages 3-4, xiao2022mannuronanc5epimerases pages 4-6, martinezortiz2020cyclicdigmpmediatedregulation pages 3-6).

### 5.2 Later elaborations

Mannuronan C5 epimerases show dramatic lineage-specific expansion. *Azotobacter vinelandii* encodes seven extracellular AlgE-type epimerases (AlgE1-AlgE7) in addition to its periplasmic AlgG, enabling fine-tuned control of alginate G-content for cyst formation (xiao2022mannuronanc5epimerases pages 4-6, martinezortiz2020cyclicdigmpmediatedregulation pages 3-6). *Pseudomonas aeruginosa*, by contrast, relies primarily on its single periplasmic AlgG and lacks the extracellular AlgE family (gheorghita2023pseudomonasaeruginosabiofilm pages 4-5, ponce2025advancesinalginate pages 4-6, xiao2022mannuronanc5epimerases pages 4-6). Brown algae have undergone massive ManC5-E expansion, with 105 candidate genes identified in *Saccharina japonica* and 45 in *Ectocarpus siliculosus* (xiao2022mannuronanc5epimerases pages 4-6). Phylogenetic and comparative genomic analyses indicate that brown algae acquired the alginate biosynthetic pathway by horizontal gene transfer (HGT) from bacteria, with subsequent lineage-specific gene family expansions that may have been a prerequisite for the evolution of multicellular complexity (mazeas2024candidategenesinvolved pages 14-15, mazeas2024candidategenesinvolved pages 2-4, mazeas2024candidategenesinvolved pages 1-2). The GMD gene in brown algae shows ~40% sequence identity with proteins from Candidatus Woesebacteriota, an uncultivated bacterium, rather than with pseudomonad sequences, suggesting an independent or early bacterial source for this key enzyme (mazeas2024candidategenesinvolved pages 2-4).

### 5.3 Lineage-specific losses and alternatives

*Escherichia coli* lacks alginate biosynthesis entirely but retains the GDP-mannose pathway for GDP-L-fucose production via GMDS and GMER, illustrating that the GDP-mannose precursor node can function independently of any alginate output (pijnenborg2021fluorinatedrhamnosidesinhibit pages 2-4). In rhizobia, GDP-mannose feeds into Nod factor decoration rather than alginate, showing how the same branch-point metabolite serves lineage-specific glycoconjugate needs (sraphet2021computationalcharacterizationsof pages 2-6). The fructose PTS itself is not universally present across all pseudomonads and related taxa; many soil and rhizosphere bacteria use ABC transporters or alternative uptake systems for sugars (nguyen2021theanoxicelectrode‐driven pages 2-4).

## 6. Constraints, Dependencies, and Failure Modes

### 6.1 Obligatory ordering

The biosynthetic sequence from F6P to GDP-mannuronate is strictly ordered: M6P must be formed before M1P, GDP-mannose before GDP-mannuronate, and the AlgD oxidation step is irreversible, providing a committed checkpoint (serrato2024bacterialalginatebiosynthesis pages 6-8). Within the alginate polymerization machinery, c-di-GMP binding to Alg44's PilZ domain must precede Alg8 activation; without this step, alginate polymerization is completely abolished (moradali2020theregulationof pages 233-235, gheorghita2023pseudomonasaeruginosabiofilm pages 7-8). Polymer modification is partially ordered: O-acetylation by AlgX prevents subsequent epimerization by AlgG at the same residues and blocks AlgL-mediated degradation, creating a modification hierarchy (serrato2024bacterialalginatebiosynthesis pages 8-11, gheorghita2023pseudomonasaeruginosabiofilm pages 4-5).

### 6.2 Mutual exclusivity and compartment constraints

Fructose PTS entry and alginate precursor synthesis occur in different regulatory compartments. Cra-mediated regulation of the *fruBKA* operon responds to cytosolic F1P concentrations, while alginate operon transcription is controlled by AlgU/MucA and a cascade of two-component regulators (AlgR/AlgB) plus c-di-GMP signaling (huang2024insightsintothe pages 5-6, huang2024insightsintothe pages 3-5, serrato2024bacterialalginatebiosynthesis pages 6-8). The alginate biosynthesis complex spans both membranes and includes cytoplasmic (Alg8 catalytic domain), inner membrane (Alg8/Alg44 transmembrane regions), periplasmic (AlgG, AlgX, AlgK, AlgL), and outer membrane (AlgE porin) components. Disruption of any transenvelope complex component (AlgK, AlgX, AlgG) leads to AlgL-mediated degradation rather than productive polymer export (gheorghita2023pseudomonasaeruginosabiofilm pages 4-5, serrato2024bacterialalginatebiosynthesis pages 24-27).

### 6.3 The AlgC bottleneck

AlgC (phosphomannomutase) is encoded outside the alginate operon and serves multiple pathways including alginate, LPS, and rhamnolipid biosynthesis (gheorghita2023pseudomonasaeruginosabiofilm pages 7-8, gheorghita2023pseudomonasaeruginosabiofilm pages 4-5). This makes AlgC a shared metabolic bottleneck: deletion of *algA* in *P. putida* reduces alginate accumulation and alters biofilm properties, reflecting its role at the boundary between central carbon and envelope precursor supply (balikova2022roleofexopolysaccharides pages 2-3).

## 7. Controversies and Open Questions

### 7.1 Cra effector identity

The identity of the true Cra effector remains debated. While in vitro studies demonstrate that F1P is the direct ligand with much higher affinity than FBP, in vivo evidence suggests that FBP influences Cra activity in a flux-dependent manner, possibly through interconversion with F1P via FruK's reverse activity (huang2024insightsintothe pages 3-5). The physiological significance of FBP-dependent Cra regulation and whether it involves direct or indirect binding remains an open question.

### 7.2 Mechanism of Alg44-mediated Alg8 activation

Although c-di-GMP binding to Alg44's PilZ domain is established as essential for alginate polymerization, the precise molecular mechanism by which this binding activates the Alg8 glycosyltransferase remains incompletely resolved (moradali2020theregulationof pages 233-235, gheorghita2023pseudomonasaeruginosabiofilm pages 7-8). By analogy with cellulose synthase (BcsA), an autoinhibition-relief model has been proposed, but the structural arrangement differs significantly: in Alg8/Alg44, the PilZ domain is on a separate protein rather than being a C-terminal extension of the synthase itself (moradali2020theregulationof pages 233-235).

### 7.3 AlgL: quality control or degradation?

The role of the periplasmic alginate lyase AlgL remains contentious. It has been variously proposed as a quality-control enzyme that degrades mislocalized polymer, a maintenance enzyme that clears the periplasm, or a regulated component whose activity is modulated by the transenvelope scaffold (serrato2024bacterialalginatebiosynthesis pages 8-11, gheorghita2023pseudomonasaeruginosabiofilm pages 4-5, balikova2022roleofexopolysaccharides pages 3-5). Whether AlgL activity is constitutive or regulated, and whether it also contributes to alginate molecular weight control, are unresolved.

### 7.4 Brown algal HGT source

While horizontal gene transfer from bacteria to the ancestor of brown algae and *Schizocladia ischiensis* is well supported for the alginate metabolic pathway, the specific bacterial donor lineage remains uncertain. Earlier suggestions of Actinobacteria as the GMD source have not been strongly supported by recent phylogenetic analyses, and Candidatus Woesebacteriota has emerged as a candidate, though data remain limited (mazeas2024candidategenesinvolved pages 14-15, mazeas2024candidategenesinvolved pages 2-4).

### 7.5 KEGG pathway boundary curation

The KEGG-style grouping of these functionally distinct sub-modules into a single pathway remains problematic for metabolic modeling and systems biology. No systematic effort to define species-level sub-module boundaries for KEGG map00051 has been published, though the rationale for such curation is implicit in the distinct regulatory architectures, compartmentalization, and evolutionary histories of the component reactions described throughout this review.
