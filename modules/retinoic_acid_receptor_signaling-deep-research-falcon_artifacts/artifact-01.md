| Step | Event | Key molecules involved | Compartment | Obligatory vs conditional | Key constraints / dependencies |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Retinol uptake from extracellular carrier complexes | Retinol, serum RBP, STRA6, cellular retinol-binding proteins | Plasma membrane → cytosol | Conditional upstream supply step | Depends on vitamin A availability, circulating RBP-retinol complexes, and expression of uptake machinery; important for many vertebrate contexts but not the nuclear signaling step per se (pqac-00000002, pqac-00000004, pqac-00000001) |
| 2 | Retinol oxidation to retinaldehyde | RDH10, ADH/RDH family enzymes | Cytosol / membrane-associated retinoid metabolic sites | Obligatory for de novo RA synthesis | Must precede ALDH1A-mediated RA production; substrate availability and tissue-specific RDH expression constrain flux; RDH10 is a major developmental enzyme (pqac-00000004, pqac-00000018, pqac-00000028) |
| 3 | Retinaldehyde oxidation to retinoic acid (RA) | ALDH1A1/RALDH1, ALDH1A2/RALDH2, ALDH1A3/RALDH3 | Cytosol | Obligatory for canonical RAR signaling when RA is synthesized locally | Irreversible step; isoform usage is tissue- and stage-specific: ALDH1A2 is often dominant in embryogenesis, ALDH1A3 is prominent in eye/anterior structures, ALDH1A1 is more restricted or adult-biased in many contexts (pqac-00000023, pqac-00000024, pqac-00000028) |
| 4 | RA capture by intracellular carriers | CRABP1, CRABP2, FABP5 | Cytosol | Conditional routing step | Not all cells use the same carrier logic; CRABP2 generally favors canonical nuclear RAR delivery, CRABP1 can also channel RA toward metabolism and non-canonical signaling, and FABP5 can bias alternative lipid-receptor outputs (pqac-00000003, pqac-00000026, pqac-00000041) |
| 5 | Nuclear import and ligand delivery to receptor complexes | CRABP2, CRABP1, RA, RAR/RXR complexes | Cytosol → nucleus | Functionally important but context-dependent in mechanism | Canonical transcription requires RA access to nuclear RAR; CRABP2 is the clearest dedicated delivery factor, whereas CRABP1 has mixed roles and vertebrate-specific specialization; some RA may diffuse because it is lipophilic, but carrier-assisted channeling improves specificity (pqac-00000003, pqac-00000007, pqac-00000020, pqac-00000041) |
| 6 | RA binding to RAR within DNA-bound RAR-RXR heterodimer on RAREs | all-trans RA, RARα/β/γ, RXRα/β/γ, RARE DNA elements | Nucleus / chromatin | Obligatory for canonical ligand-dependent activation | RAR-RXR heterodimers are typically prebound to RAREs; RARE architecture and receptor isotype matter; ligand sensitivity differs among RAR isotypes, with RARγ more sensitive than RARα in some stem-cell contexts (pqac-00000006, pqac-00000008, pqac-00000036, pqac-00000037) |
| 7 | Corepressor complex dissociation | NCOR1, NCOR2/SMRT, HDAC3, RAR AF-2 / helix H12 | Nucleus / chromatin | Obligatory for transcriptional activation | RA binding triggers conformational change in the RAR ligand-binding domain, especially H12 repositioning, which destabilizes CoRNR-box-mediated corepressor binding and releases HDAC-linked repression machinery (pqac-00000006, pqac-00000008, pqac-00000030, pqac-00000035) |
| 8 | Coactivator recruitment | NCOA/SRC family (e.g., NCOA1/SRC1), p300, CBP, GCN5, Mediator-associated factors | Nucleus / chromatin | Obligatory for robust activation | Requires ligand-stabilized AF-2 surface; coactivator LXXLL motifs engage the receptor after corepressor release; recruitment is promoter-, cell state-, and receptor isotype-dependent (pqac-00000006, pqac-00000008, pqac-00000032, pqac-00000047) |
| 9 | Chromatin remodeling and transcription initiation | p300/CBP, HAT activity, basal transcription machinery, RNA polymerase II, chromatin at RA target genes | Nucleus / chromatin | Obligatory endpoint of canonical signaling | Histone acetylation and chromatin relaxation enable productive transcription; output depends on local enhancer/promoter context, cooperating transcription factors, and allosteric coupling across the RXR-RAR-DNA complex (pqac-00000006, pqac-00000008, pqac-00000009, pqac-00000010) |
| 10 | RA degradation and negative-feedback homeostasis | CYP26A1, CYP26B1, CYP26C1, CRABP1/2, oxidized RA metabolites | Cytosol / ER-associated metabolic sites | Conditional feedback step, but essential for physiological homeostasis | RA induces CYP26 expression, especially CYP26A1, creating negative feedback; degradation sharpens morphogen gradients, limits signal duration, and prevents ectopic or toxic RA exposure (pqac-00000023, pqac-00000025, pqac-00000027, pqac-00000028) |

It is useful for distinguishing the core pathway from accessory transport and homeostatic modules.*
