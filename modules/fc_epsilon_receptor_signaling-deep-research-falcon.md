# Fc-Epsilon Receptor (FcεRI) Signaling Pathway: A Comprehensive Review

## 1. Executive Summary

The high-affinity IgE receptor FcεRI is the principal initiator of IgE-mediated allergic responses in mast cells and basophils. The receptor exists as a heterotetrameric complex (αβγ₂) that, upon binding of IgE and subsequent antigen crosslinking, initiates a rapid tyrosine kinase cascade culminating in calcium mobilization and granule exocytosis. Recent cryo-EM structural work has fundamentally revised our understanding of the receptor's resting state as a homodimer whose IgE-induced dissociation into monomers exposes ITAM domains for kinase engagement (chen2025molecularmechanismof pages 1-2, chen2025molecularmechanismof pages 6-7). The signaling module is organized around two principal proximal branches—the LYN/SYK/LAT axis and the FYN/GAB2/PI3K axis—that converge on PLCγ activation, IP3/DAG generation, store-operated calcium entry, and PKC-driven degranulation (li2022newmechanisticadvances pages 4-5, pavlyuchenkova2024theroleplayed pages 1-3, burchett2022targetingmastcells pages 6-8). BTK operates as a critical amplifying node linking PI3K-generated PIP3 to PLCγ activation and sustained calcium signaling (pavlyuchenkova2024theroleplayed pages 1-3, mendes‐bastos2022brutonstyrosinekinase pages 2-4). This pathway is counterbalanced by inhibitory receptors including Siglec-8, FcγRIIb, and intracellular phosphatases such as SHIP-1 and SHP-2 (korver2022theinhibitoryreceptor pages 1-2, kanagaratham2020igeandigg pages 6-7). The system is mammalian-specific in its IgE-dependent form, though its constituent kinases and ITAM-based signaling modules are deeply conserved across vertebrates (pritchard2021theevolutionof pages 1-2).

## 2. Definition and Biological Boundaries

### 2.1 What the System Includes

The FcεRI signaling pathway module encompasses the events from antigen-mediated crosslinking of IgE-bound FcεRI on the mast cell or basophil surface through to the proximal effector outputs of calcium mobilization and granule exocytosis. Its core components include: (i) the receptor complex itself (FcεRIα/FCER1A, FcεRIβ/MS4A2, FcεRIγ/FCER1G); (ii) the Src-family kinases LYN and FYN that initiate ITAM phosphorylation; (iii) the cytoplasmic tyrosine kinase SYK recruited to phosphorylated γ-chain ITAMs; (iv) the adaptor scaffolds LAT, NTAL, SLP-76, and GAB2; (v) effector enzymes PLCγ1/2 and PI3K; (vi) the Tec-family kinase BTK; and (vii) the calcium-entry machinery (IP3 receptors, STIM1, Orai1/CRAC channels) and PKC isoforms that execute degranulation (nagata2022fcεriamaster pages 1-2, li2022newmechanisticadvances pages 4-5, pavlyuchenkova2024theroleplayed pages 1-3, li2022newmechanisticadvances pages 5-6).

### 2.2 Neighboring Pathways That Should Be Distinguished

Several processes are often discussed alongside FcεRI signaling but are mechanistically distinct and should not be conflated:

- **MRGPRX2/MAS-related G-protein coupled receptor signaling**: This G-protein-coupled receptor pathway drives non-IgE-dependent mast cell degranulation through Gαi/Gαq and represents a mechanistically independent activation route, though both pathways converge on PI3K and calcium mobilization downstream (pavlyuchenkova2024theroleplayed pages 1-3).
- **KIT (CD117) signaling**: The stem cell factor receptor tyrosine kinase KIT provides survival, proliferation, and co-stimulatory signals that can synergize with FcεRI but operates through its own receptor tyrosine kinase-intrinsic autophosphorylation mechanism (voisset2020srcfamilykinasesin pages 16-17).
- **FcεRII (CD23) signaling**: The low-affinity IgE receptor CD23 is a C-type lectin structurally unrelated to FcεRI. It modulates IgE homeostasis and antigen presentation but does not signal through ITAMs (plattner2023onthecomplexity pages 2-3).
- **Late-phase mediator production**: While FcεRI triggering initiates de novo synthesis of cytokines and lipid mediators through MAPK and NF-κB pathways, these transcriptional programs constitute downstream outputs rather than core components of the proximal signaling module.

### 2.3 Competing Definitions

There is no major definitional controversy about the core FcεRI pathway. However, its precise boundaries are drawn differently depending on the review. Some authors restrict the module to receptor-proximal events (ITAM phosphorylation through SYK activation), while others extend it to include all downstream effector outputs including late-phase cytokine production and eicosanoid synthesis (nagata2022fcεriamaster pages 1-2, pavlyuchenkova2024theroleplayed pages 1-3). In this review, we define the module as encompassing events from receptor crosslinking through calcium-dependent degranulation, while noting downstream transcriptional programs as outputs of the module rather than intrinsic parts of it.

## 3. Mechanistic Overview

### 3.1 Receptor Architecture and IgE Engagement

FcεRI on mast cells and basophils is a heterotetrameric complex (αβγ₂). The α subunit (FCER1A) provides the extracellular IgE-binding domain, consisting of two immunoglobulin-like domains (α1 and α2) that bind the Cε3 domain of IgE asymmetrically at a 1:1 stoichiometry with extraordinarily high affinity (Ka ~10¹⁰ M⁻¹) (nagata2022fcεriamaster pages 1-2, li2022newmechanisticadvances pages 2-4). The β subunit (MS4A2) is a four-transmembrane-spanning member of the MS4A family containing one ITAM, and functions primarily as a signal amplifier (chen2025molecularmechanismof pages 1-2, li2022newmechanisticadvances pages 4-5). The two disulfide-linked γ subunits (FCER1G) each contain one ITAM and serve as the principal, indispensable signaling chains (chen2025molecularmechanismof pages 1-2).

A groundbreaking structural advance from Chen et al. (2025) revealed via cryo-EM at 3.8 Å resolution that FcεRI exists predominantly as a **homodimer** (α₂β₂γ₄) on the cell surface prior to IgE binding. The dimeric interface is mediated by a four-helix bundle of α and γ subunit transmembrane helices on the intracellular side, with cholesterol-like molecules stabilizing the dimeric assembly (chen2025molecularmechanismof pages 2-3, chen2025molecularmechanismof pages 4-5). Critically, in this dimeric state, the γ-chain ITAMs are partially shielded by α-subunit transmembrane helices, sterically hindering their association with downstream kinases (chen2025molecularmechanismof pages 6-7, chen2025molecularmechanismof pages 4-5).

### 3.2 Activation: Dimer-to-Monomer Transition

Upon IgE binding, steric clashes between the two IgE-occupied extracellular α domains force the FcεRI homodimer to dissociate into individual protomers (chen2025molecularmechanismof pages 6-7, chen2025molecularmechanismof pages 4-5). This **dimer-to-monomer transition** is the key activation mechanism: it converts the receptor from a state unfavorable for kinase signaling (dimeric with shielded ITAMs) to a state permissive for downstream signaling (monomeric with exposed ITAMs) (chen2025molecularmechanismof pages 6-7). Subsequent antigen-mediated crosslinking of IgE-FcεRI monomers brings them into proximity, enabling Src-family kinase trans-phosphorylation of now-accessible ITAMs (nagata2022fcεriamaster pages 1-2, li2022newmechanisticadvances pages 4-5).

### 3.3 Proximal Kinase Cascade: The LYN/SYK/LAT Axis

The canonical proximal signaling pathway proceeds as follows. LYN, a Src-family kinase pre-associated with the FcεRIβ cytoplasmic domain, is activated upon receptor crosslinking and phosphorylates the ITAM tyrosines on both β and γ subunits (nagata2022fcεriamaster pages 1-2, li2022newmechanisticadvances pages 4-5, burchett2022targetingmastcells pages 6-8). The phosphorylated γ-chain ITAMs create high-affinity docking sites for the tandem SH2 domains of SYK. Once recruited, SYK undergoes activation through trans-autophosphorylation and phosphorylates key downstream adaptors including LAT, NTAL (LAB), and SLP-76 (li2022newmechanisticadvances pages 4-5, pavlyuchenkova2024theroleplayed pages 1-3). This LAT-centered signalosome recruits PLCγ (primarily PLCγ1 in mast cells) and additional effector enzymes (li2022newmechanisticadvances pages 4-5, burchett2022targetingmastcells pages 6-8).

### 3.4 Parallel Branch: The FYN/GAB2/PI3K Axis

A second, partially parallel signaling branch is initiated by FYN, another Src-family kinase. FYN phosphorylates the adaptor proteins NTAL and GAB2, leading to PI3K activation independently of LAT (pavlyuchenkova2024theroleplayed pages 3-4, burchett2022targetingmastcells pages 6-8). PI3K converts membrane PIP2 to PIP3, which serves as a docking platform for PH domain-containing kinases including BTK, PDK1, and Akt (pavlyuchenkova2024theroleplayed pages 1-3). BTK then phosphorylates and activates PLCγ, providing a convergence point with the LYN/SYK/LAT arm (li2022newmechanisticadvances pages 4-5, pavlyuchenkova2024theroleplayed pages 1-3). Additionally, PI3K activates PKCδ directly, contributing to degranulation regulation, and can mobilize calcium through sphingosine kinase/S1P pathways independently of IP3 (pavlyuchenkova2024theroleplayed pages 3-4).

### 3.5 Calcium Mobilization and Degranulation

PLCγ hydrolyzes phosphatidylinositol 4,5-bisphosphate (PIP2) to generate two critical second messengers: inositol 1,4,5-trisphosphate (IP3) and diacylglycerol (DAG) (li2022newmechanisticadvances pages 4-5, li2022newmechanisticadvances pages 5-6). IP3 binds to IP3 receptors on the endoplasmic reticulum membrane, triggering rapid release of Ca²⁺ from ER stores (li2022newmechanisticadvances pages 5-6, li2022newmechanisticadvances pages 4-5). ER calcium depletion is sensed by the ER-resident protein STIM1, which oligomerizes and translocates to ER-plasma membrane junctions where it activates Orai1/CRAC channels, mediating sustained store-operated calcium entry (SOCE) from the extracellular space (li2022newmechanisticadvances pages 5-6, arthur2020thefcεriβhomologue pages 9-10). DAG activates PKC isoforms which, together with elevated cytosolic calcium, drive the molecular machinery of granule fusion and exocytosis, resulting in the release of preformed mediators including histamine, tryptase, and heparin (li2022newmechanisticadvances pages 4-5, pavlyuchenkova2024theroleplayed pages 1-3).

## 5. Evolutionary and Cell-Biological Variation

### 5.1 Evolutionary Origins

The FcεRI signaling pathway in its full IgE-dependent form is mammalian-specific. IgE itself arose from an IgY gene duplication event approximately 195 million years ago in early insectivorous mammals (pritchard2021theevolutionof pages 1-2). The high-affinity IgE receptor FcεRI evolved from Fc receptor-like (FcRL) molecules during early mammalian evolution (pritchard2021theevolutionof pages 1-2). However, many of the system's constituent elements are far more ancient:

- **Mast cells and basophils** predate IgE and are found throughout the animal kingdom, including in birds, reptiles, and other non-mammalian vertebrates. Mast-cell-like ("test cells") have been identified in the marine urochordate *Styela plicata*, suggesting the lineage originated at least as early as the chordate divergence (pritchard2021theevolutionof pages 1-2).
- **ITAM-based signaling** and the SYK kinase family are deeply conserved across vertebrates and are utilized by multiple immune receptors including Fc gamma receptors, B cell receptors, and T cell receptors (voisset2020srcfamilykinasesin pages 16-17).
- **SYK, LYN, and PI3K** are present across all jawed vertebrates, reflecting their fundamental roles in immune receptor signaling that long precede the appearance of IgE (voisset2020srcfamilykinasesin pages 16-17, pritchard2021theevolutionof pages 1-2).

Thus, the FcεRI pathway represents a mammalian elaboration—coupling a newly evolved immunoglobulin isotype (IgE) to pre-existing ITAM-SYK signaling machinery already operating in ancestral immune cells.

### 5.2 Species Differences: Tetrameric versus Trimeric FcεRI

A critical species difference exists in FcεRI stoichiometry. In rodents (mice, rats), FcεRI is expressed exclusively as the tetrameric αβγ₂ form (plattner2023onthecomplexity pages 2-3, plattner2023onthecomplexity pages 1-2). In humans, FcεRI can exist as either the tetrameric (αβγ₂) or trimeric (αγ₂) form, the latter lacking the β subunit (nagata2022fcεriamaster pages 1-2, chen2025molecularmechanismof pages 1-2):

- **Tetrameric αβγ₂**: Expressed on mast cells and basophils, where the β subunit amplifies signaling and promotes receptor surface expression and stability (nagata2022fcεriamaster pages 1-2, chen2025molecularmechanismof pages 1-2).
- **Trimeric αγ₂**: Expressed on human antigen-presenting cells including dendritic cells, Langerhans cells, monocytes, and eosinophils. On these cells, the trimeric receptor mediates IgE-facilitated antigen uptake and MHC class II presentation rather than degranulation (plattner2023onthecomplexity pages 2-3, chen2025molecularmechanismof pages 1-2).

This difference is important for translational research: mouse models exclusively employ the tetrameric receptor, and findings in murine mast cells may not fully capture the biology of trimeric FcεRI signaling on human antigen-presenting cells.

### 5.3 Cell-Type Variation

Even within cells expressing tetrameric FcεRI, significant variation exists:

- **Mucosal versus connective tissue mast cells** differ substantially in their granule protease repertoire, receptor expression levels, and sensitivity to IgE-mediated activation (nagata2022fcεriamaster pages 1-2).
- **Bone marrow-derived mast cells (BMMCs)** used widely in research differ from tissue-resident mast cells in transcriptomic profile, protease content, and receptor levels, warranting caution in extrapolating BMMC findings to in vivo mast cell biology.
- **Basophils** versus **mast cells** share the tetrameric FcεRI but differ in granule content, cytokine production profiles, and responsiveness to non-IgE stimuli.

### 5.4 MS4A2 Splice Variants and Signal Modulation

The β subunit gene MS4A2 generates functionally significant splice variants. Exon-skipping variants of FcεRIβ can eliminate high-affinity IgE receptor expression on mast cells, while truncated variants regulate mast cell proliferation and survival (arthur2020thefcεriβhomologue pages 10-11). The MS4A4A homologue of FcεRIβ promotes FcεRI signal transduction and store-operated Ca²⁺ entry, representing a parallel amplification mechanism through the MS4A family (arthur2020thefcεriβhomologue pages 10-11, arthur2020thefcεriβhomologue pages 9-10). Knockdown of MS4A4A reduces IgE-dependent mast cell degranulation by approximately 50% (arthur2020thefcεriβhomologue pages 9-10).

## 6. Constraints, Dependencies, and Failure Modes

### 6.1 Ordered Dependencies

The signaling cascade follows a strictly ordered sequence in its canonical form:

1. **IgE must bind FcεRI before antigen crosslinking can occur.** IgE binding is prerequisite to allergen-triggered activation (nagata2022fcεriamaster pages 1-2, chen2025molecularmechanismof pages 1-2).
2. **ITAM phosphorylation by LYN must precede SYK recruitment.** SYK binds only to doubly phosphorylated ITAMs via its tandem SH2 domains (li2022newmechanisticadvances pages 4-5, burchett2022targetingmastcells pages 6-8).
3. **SYK activation must precede LAT/NTAL phosphorylation and signalosome assembly** (li2022newmechanisticadvances pages 4-5, pavlyuchenkova2024theroleplayed pages 1-3).
4. **PLCγ activation must precede IP3-mediated calcium release**, and **ER store depletion must precede STIM1/Orai1 SOCE activation** (li2022newmechanisticadvances pages 5-6, arthur2020thefcεriβhomologue pages 9-10).

### 6.2 Negative Regulatory Checkpoints

Multiple negative regulators impose thresholds and prevent inappropriate activation:

- **SHIP-1** (INPP5D): Dephosphorylates PIP3 to PI(3,4)P2, depleting membrane docking sites for BTK and reducing IP3 formation. Mast cells lacking SHIP-1 degranulate at lower antigen concentrations (kanagaratham2020igeandigg pages 6-7).
- **Siglec-8**: An inhibitory receptor containing cytoplasmic ITIMs that recruits the phosphatase SHP-2 upon phosphorylation. Siglec-8 directly interacts with FcεRI signaling molecules and globally suppresses phosphorylation of SYK, GAB2, Akt, and PKC, dramatically reducing the number of differential phosphosites upon FcεRI activation (from 348 to 71 at 2-fold change threshold) (korver2022theinhibitoryreceptor pages 1-2, korver2022theinhibitoryreceptor pages 3-6).
- **FcγRIIb**: The inhibitory IgG Fc receptor containing an ITIM that recruits SHIP-1 when co-aggregated with FcεRI, suppressing PIP3 accumulation, calcium signaling, and mediator release. This mechanism underlies the protective effects of allergen-specific IgG during immunotherapy (li2022newmechanisticadvances pages 9-10, kanagaratham2020igeandigg pages 6-7).
- **SLAP**: Binds FcεRIβ and downregulates its expression, blocking downstream kinase signaling through SYK, Akt, and ERK (li2022newmechanisticadvances pages 5-6).
- **AMPKα2**: Phosphorylates FcεRIβ, blocking IgE-induced recruitment of signaling proteins to the FcεRI complex (li2022newmechanisticadvances pages 5-6).

### 6.3 Failure Modes and Therapeutic Targeting

Disruption at key nodes produces defined phenotypic consequences:

- **SYK inhibition** blocks the earliest signal propagation step, effectively preventing both the LYN/SYK/LAT and downstream PI3K/BTK branches. SYK inhibitors show rapid onset of action in clinical trials for allergic rhinitis (burchett2022targetingmastcells pages 6-8, cao2024mastcellstabilizers pages 8-9).
- **BTK inhibition** blocks the PI3K-BTK-PLCγ amplification loop. Clinical agents including remibrutinib and fenebrutinib (for chronic spontaneous urticaria) and rilzabrutinib (for pemphigus) are in advanced clinical development (mendes‐bastos2022brutonstyrosinekinase pages 1-2). Ibrutinib eliminates basophil activation in peanut/tree nut allergic patients; acalabrutinib prevents moderate anaphylaxis in preclinical models (burchett2022targetingmastcells pages 6-8, cao2024mastcellstabilizers pages 8-9). Beyond mast cell effects, BTK inhibitors also disrupt B cell receptor signaling and autoantibody production, providing dual mechanisms of benefit in autoimmune urticaria (mendes‐bastos2022brutonstyrosinekinase pages 8-9).
- **Anti-IgE therapy** (omalizumab) blocks the initiating step by preventing IgE from binding FcεRI and downregulating FcεRI surface expression (chen2025molecularmechanismof pages 1-2).

## 7. Controversies and Open Questions

### 7.1 The Dimer-to-Monomer Model

The 2024–2025 cryo-EM structural studies revealing that FcεRI exists as a homodimer that dissociates upon IgE binding represent a significant paradigm shift (chen2025molecularmechanismof pages 1-2, chen2025molecularmechanismof pages 6-7). However, several questions remain: How does the dimer-to-monomer transition interface with lipid raft organization in intact cell membranes? Do cholesterol-like molecules identified in the cryo-EM structure play a regulatory role in vivo, and could membrane cholesterol content modulate signaling thresholds? How does the dimeric resting state reconcile with older models of monomeric FcεRI diffusing freely in the membrane?

### 7.2 Relative Contributions of the Two Proximal Branches

The LYN/SYK/LAT and FYN/GAB2/PI3K axes are often presented as parallel pathways, yet their relative contributions to specific outcomes (degranulation versus cytokine production versus lipid mediator synthesis) remain incompletely resolved (pavlyuchenkova2024theroleplayed pages 1-3, burchett2022targetingmastcells pages 6-8). Much of the evidence comes from murine BMMCs or rat RBL-2H3 cells, and it is uncertain how directly these findings translate to primary human tissue mast cells.

### 7.3 PLCγ1 versus PLCγ2

While the pathway outline specifies PLCG2 as the effector, mast cell studies frequently emphasize PLCγ1. The relative contributions and potential redundancy of PLCγ1 and PLCγ2 in human mast cells versus basophils versus other FcεRI-expressing cells are not fully delineated. Human PLCG2 haploinsufficiency results in immunodeficiency with impacts on multiple immune cell types, underscoring the broad importance of this enzyme family.

### 7.4 Trimeric FcεRI Signaling

The signaling output of trimeric αγ₂ FcεRI on human antigen-presenting cells remains poorly characterized relative to the tetrameric form. IgE crosslinking on monocytes stimulates phosphorylation of intracellular kinases including FGR, STAT5, YES, and LCK, but whether these events produce qualitatively different cellular outcomes from the canonical mast cell pathway remains an active research area (plattner2023onthecomplexity pages 2-3, chen2025molecularmechanismof pages 1-2).

### 7.5 Translation Between Model Systems

A persistent concern is the reliance on murine BMMCs and rat RBL-2H3 cells for mechanistic studies. These model systems differ from primary human tissue mast cells in transcriptomic profiles, protease content, and receptor expression levels. Furthermore, the exclusive expression of tetrameric FcεRI in rodents versus the co-expression of trimeric and tetrameric forms in humans complicates translational extrapolation (nagata2022fcεriamaster pages 1-2, plattner2023onthecomplexity pages 1-2).

### 7.6 Emerging Regulatory Mechanisms

Recent work has implicated autophagy in FcεRI-dependent mast cell activation, with evidence that inhibition of autophagy during activation allows autophagy-related proteins to participate in the degranulation process (pavlyuchenkova2024theroleplayed pages 3-4). The interplay between mTORC1 activation, AMPK inactivation, and FcεRI signaling represents a rapidly evolving area where the integration of metabolic sensing with immune effector function is being defined.
