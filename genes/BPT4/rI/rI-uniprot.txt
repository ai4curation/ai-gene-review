CC   -!- FUNCTION: Involved in lysis inhibition. Senses superinfections and
CC       inhibits the holin, thereby delaying the host cell lysis timing. The
CC       genomic DNA from the superinfecting phage bound to the complex holin-
CC       antiholin probably serves as a signal for the lysis inhibition and
CC       blocks the holin multimerization. {ECO:0000255|HAMAP-Rule:MF_04105,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:17693511, ECO:0000269|PubMed:22389108,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:32562709, ECO:0000269|PubMed:34456892,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:9560373}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homotetramer; in free-form (PubMed:32562709). Homomultimer.
CC       Heterotetramer composed of 2 holin and 2 antiholin (PubMed:32562709).
CC       The holin-antiholin complex binds dsDNA (PubMed:32562709). Interacts
CC       (via C-terminus) with holin (via C-terminus); this interaction blocks
CC       the holin homomultimerization and delays the host cell lysis (By
CC       similarity) (PubMed:16166524). {ECO:0000255|HAMAP-Rule:MF_04105,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:16166524, ECO:0000269|PubMed:32562709}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Host periplasm {ECO:0000255|HAMAP-Rule:MF_04105,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:34456892}.
CC   -!- PTM: Disulfide bond is required for functionality. {ECO:0000255|HAMAP-
CC       Rule:MF_04105, ECO:0000269|PubMed:22389108}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the T4likevirus antiholin family.
CC       {ECO:0000255|HAMAP-Rule:MF_04105}.
