CC   -!- FUNCTION: Plays important roles in recombination-dependent DNA repair
CC       and the reorganization of stalled replication forks during viral DNA
CC       synthesis (PubMed:9233823, PubMed:2388264). Active on in vivo-derived
CC       T4 DNA; viral DNA is highly modified by hydroxymethylation and
CC       glucosylation of cytosine residues (PubMed:17823128). Helps process
CC       Holliday junction (HJ) intermediates to mature products by catalyzing
CC       branch migration (PubMed:17823128, PubMed:23732982). Probably able to
CC       catalyze replication fork regression (Probable) (PubMed:17823128).
CC       Unwinds HJ and Y-branched but not linear double-stranded (ds)DNA;
CC       unwinding requires ATP and Mg(2+) (PubMed:9233823, PubMed:17823128).
CC       Unwinds dsDNA with a 3'-single-stranded (ss)DNA overhang, suggesting it
CC       is a 3'-5' helicase (PubMed:17092935). Another study does not find this
CC       activity (PubMed:17823128). Unwinds D- and R-loops (PubMed:17092935).
CC       Also anneals ssDNA; ATP stimulates annealing (PubMed:17092935,
CC       PubMed:24013402). Has ssDNA and dsDNA-stimulated ATPase activity, also
CC       hydrolyzes GTP in the presence of DNA (PubMed:17092935).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P24230, ECO:0000269|PubMed:17092935,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:17823128, ECO:0000269|PubMed:23732982,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:2388264, ECO:0000269|PubMed:24013402,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:9233823, ECO:0000305|PubMed:17823128}.
