CC   -!- FUNCTION: Structural component of the distal-half of the long-tail
CC       fiber. It constitutes the part of the long-tail fibers that recognizes
CC       the bacterial receptor. The long-tail fiber of T4 is about 1600
CC       Angstroms long with a kink in the middle that divides the fiber into
CC       proximal and distal halves. The thin tip of the distal half-fiber
CC       interacts with the bacterial lipopolysaccharide receptor and specifies
CC       the host range of the phage. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:8709154}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: The long-tail fibers are trimeric, with a stoichiometry of
CC       gp34/gp37/gp36/gp35 of 3:3:3:1. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:8709154}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Virion {ECO:0000305}.
CC   -!- MISCELLANEOUS: The two gp37 protein chains run in parallel, the length
CC       of the distal half-fiber, with the amino end near the center kink of
CC       the fiber and the carboxyl end at the distal tip. The other
CC       polypeptides are distributed uniformly along the length of the distal
CC       half-fiber.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the tail fiber family. {ECO:0000305}.
