Gene Description

HadA is a family III CoA-transferase (EC 2.8.3.24) that catalyzes the transfer of the CoA moiety from 4-methylpentanoyl-CoA (isocaproyl-CoA) to (R)-2-hydroxy-4-methylpentanoate ((R)-2-hydroxyisocaproate), producing (R)-2-hydroxy-4-methylpentanoyl-CoA and 4-methylpentanoate (isocaproate). It functions in the reductive branch of L-leucine fermentation in the strictly anaerobic gut pathogen Clostridioides difficile, where leucine is dismutated to isovalerate (oxidation) and isocaproate (reduction). HadA activates (R)-2-hydroxyisocaproate as its CoA thioester so the downstream radical 2-hydroxyacyl-CoA dehydratase HadBC (activated by HadI) can dehydrate it to (E)-2-isocaprenoyl-CoA. The enzyme is a homodimer with Asp-171 as the catalytic nucleophile (probably forming a mixed-anhydride intermediate with the acyl-CoA substrate); other CoA thioesters such as acetyl-CoA or butyryl-CoA are not accepted as donors (PMID:16957230).

Core Functions

HadA catalyzes the CoA-transfer step of the reductive branch of L-leucine fermentation in C. difficile, transferring CoA from 4-methylpentanoyl-CoA (isocaproyl-CoA) to (R)-2-hydroxy-4-methylpentanoate to yield (R)-2-hydroxy-4-methylpentanoyl-CoA and free isocaproate (EC 2.8.3.24, PMID:16957230). It is a homodimeric family III CoA-transferase with Asp-171 as the catalytic nucleophile and is specific for the leucine-derived branched-chain acyl-CoA (acetyl-CoA and butyryl-CoA are not accepted as donors). The product (R)-2-hydroxyisocaproyl-CoA is the substrate for the radical dehydratase HadBC, coupling HadA's activity to the downstream steps of isocaproate formation.
