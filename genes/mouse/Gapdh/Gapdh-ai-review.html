Gene Description

Mouse glyceraldehyde-3-phosphate dehydrogenase (GAPDH) is a classic moonlighting protein that functions primarily as a key glycolytic enzyme while also participating in diverse non-metabolic processes. As a glycolytic enzyme, GAPDH catalyzes the reversible conversion of D-glyceraldehyde 3-phosphate (G3P) to 3-phospho-D-glyceroyl phosphate using NAD+ as cofactor (EC 1.2.1.12). The protein forms a homotetramer and is abundantly expressed in all tissues. Beyond glycolysis, GAPDH has moonlighting functions in translation regulation as a component of the GAIT complex, in innate immunity through interaction with TRAF2/TRAF3 to promote NF-kappaB activation and type I interferon production, and in nuclear events including transcription, RNA transport, DNA replication, and apoptosis. The nuclear functions are mediated by S-nitrosylase activity that transfers NO groups to nuclear targets SIRT1, HDAC2, and PRKDC. S-nitrosylation at Cys-150 promotes interaction with SIAH1 and nuclear translocation. GAPDH also associates with microtubules via CHP1 and modulates cytoskeletal organization. Sulfhydration at Cys-150 increases catalytic activity.

Core Functions

GAPDH catalyzes the reversible oxidation and phosphorylation of D-glyceraldehyde 3-phosphate to 1,3-bisphosphoglycerate using NAD+ as cofactor. This is a key step in both glycolysis and gluconeogenesis. The enzyme forms a homotetramer and the active site cysteine (Cys-150) is essential for catalysis. Sulfhydration at Cys-150 increases catalytic activity.

description: Mouse glyceraldehyde-3-phosphate dehydrogenase (GAPDH) is a classic moonlighting
  protein that functions primarily as a key glycolytic enzyme while also participating
  in diverse non-metabolic processes. As a glycolytic enzyme, GAPDH catalyzes the
  reversible conversion of D-glyceraldehyde 3-phosphate (G3P) to 3-phospho-D-glyceroyl
  phosphate using NAD+ as cofactor (EC 1.2.1.12). The protein forms a homotetramer
  and is abundantly expressed in all tissues. Beyond glycolysis, GAPDH has moonlighting
  functions in translation regulation as a component of the GAIT complex, in innate
  immunity through interaction with TRAF2/TRAF3 to promote NF-kappaB activation and
  type I interferon production, and in nuclear events including transcription, RNA
  transport, DNA replication, and apoptosis. The nuclear functions are mediated by
  S-nitrosylase activity that transfers NO groups to nuclear targets SIRT1, HDAC2,
  and PRKDC. S-nitrosylation at Cys-150 promotes interaction with SIAH1 and nuclear
  translocation. GAPDH also associates with microtubules via CHP1 and modulates
  cytoskeletal organization. Sulfhydration at Cys-150 increases catalytic activity.
