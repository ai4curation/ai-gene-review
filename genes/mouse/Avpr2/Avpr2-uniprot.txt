CC   -!- FUNCTION: G-protein-coupled receptor for arginine vasopressin, an
CC       antidiuretic that promotes renal water reabsorption. Ligand binding
CC       causes a conformation change that triggers signaling via guanine
CC       nucleotide-binding proteins (G proteins) and modulates the activity of
CC       downstream effectors, such as adenylate cyclase (cAMP). AVPR2 is
CC       coupled to G(s) G alpha proteins and mediates activation of adenylate
CC       cyclase activity. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P30518}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Interacts with ARRDC4. Identified in a complex containing at
CC       least ARRDC4, V2R and HGS. Interacts with TMEM147. Interacts (when
CC       phosphorylated) with ARRB2; the interaction is associated with
CC       internalization of the receptor and short-term desensitization to the
CC       ligand. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P30518}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell membrane {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P30518};
CC       Multi-pass membrane protein {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P30518}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Highly expressed in kidney (at protein level) and
CC       moderately expressed in liver (at protein level). No or extremely low
CC       expression in left ventricule, muscle, bone and brain (at protein
CC       level). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:33052234}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The P-X-P-P motif is phosphorylated in response to ligand
CC       binding, promoting. association with beta-arrestin ARRB2.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P30518}.
CC   -!- PTM: Phosphorylation of the P-X-P-P motif promotes association with
CC       beta-arrestin ARRB2, leading to receptor desensitization and negative
CC       regulation of G-protein coupled receptor signaling.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P30518}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the G-protein coupled receptor 1 family.
CC       Vasopressin/oxytocin receptor subfamily. {ECO:0000255|PROSITE-
CC       ProRule:PRU00521}.
