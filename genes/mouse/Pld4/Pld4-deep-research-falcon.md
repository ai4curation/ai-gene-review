# Research Report: Mouse **Pld4 / PLD4** (UniProt **Q8BG07**)—Functional Annotation

## 0) Target verification (critical identity check)
The UniProt accession **Q8BG07** corresponds to **Mus musculus Pld4 (PLD4; phospholipase D family member 4)**, an endolysosomal/lysosomal, **type II transmembrane** PLD-family protein with the canonical **dual HKD catalytic motifs** typical of the phospholipase D superfamily. This matches primary literature that characterizes mouse PLD4 as a **glycosylated type II transmembrane endolysosomal protein** and assigns it enzymatic activities in the lysosomal lumen at acidic pH. (gavin2018pld3andpld4 pages 1-2, yuan2024structuralandmechanistic pages 1-5)

## 1) Key concepts and definitions (current understanding)

### 1.1 PLD4 as an *endolysosomal nuclease* (acid 5′→3′ exonuclease)
PLD4 (together with its paralog PLD3) is now best understood as an **endolysosomal/lysosomal, acidic 5′→3′ exonuclease** that degrades **single-stranded DNA (ssDNA)** and **single-stranded RNA (ssRNA)** within the endolysosomal system. This enzymatic activity is directly tied to limiting activation of **endosomal nucleic-acid sensors**, particularly TLR pathways. (gavin2018pld3andpld4 pages 1-2, gavin2021cleavageofdna pages 1-2, yuan2024structuralandmechanistic pages 1-5)

A 2024 structural/mechanistic study further refined substrate rules: PLD4 can process very short single-stranded substrates (down to dinucleotides), but **5′-phosphorylation blocks activity**, highlighting an important specificity determinant for which nucleic acids in lysosomes will be efficiently degraded. (yuan2024structuralandmechanistic pages 1-5)

### 1.2 PLD4 as an *S,S-BMP synthase* (new lysosomal lipid-enzyme function)
In 2024, PLD4 (with PLD3) was reported to catalyze synthesis of **S,S-bis(monoacylglycero)phosphate (S,S-BMP)**, a major lipid of lysosomal intraluminal vesicles important for **lysosomal lipid degradation**. The proposed role is to perform a **stereo-inversion step** that yields the lysosomally stable S,S stereoisomer of BMP, thereby enabling proper lysosomal degradation of lipids such as gangliosides. (singh2024pld3andpld4 pages 1-3, singh2024pld3andpld4 pages 7-8, singh2024pld3andpld4 pages 8-10)

This places PLD4 at the intersection of (i) **nucleic-acid catabolism** and (ii) **lysosomal lipid homeostasis**, both occurring in acidic endolysosomal compartments. (singh2024pld3andpld4 pages 1-3, yuan2024structuralandmechanistic pages 1-5)

## 2) Molecular function: reactions, substrates, and mechanism

### 2.1 Acid 5′→3′ exonuclease activity (ssDNA and ssRNA)
**Reaction (conceptual):** processive or distributive removal of nucleotides from the **5′ end** of **single-stranded** nucleic acids under **acidic** conditions typical of endolysosomes/lysosomes. (gavin2018pld3andpld4 pages 1-2, gavin2021cleavageofdna pages 1-2, yuan2024structuralandmechanistic pages 1-5)

**Substrates and specificity:**
- Substrates: **ssDNA** and **ssRNA**; the activity is directly relevant to degradation of nucleic-acid ligands that would otherwise stimulate endosomal receptors (e.g., TLR9 DNA ligands; also RNA ligands relevant to TLR7). (gavin2018pld3andpld4 pages 1-2, gavin2021cleavageofdna pages 1-2)
- Directionality: **5′→3′ exonucleolytic** digestion. (yuan2024structuralandmechanistic pages 1-5)
- A key negative determinant: **5′ phosphorylation inhibits cleavage**, indicating PLD4 preferentially acts on nucleic acids with accessible, appropriately configured 5′ ends. (yuan2024structuralandmechanistic pages 1-5)

**Catalytic motifs/mechanism:**
PLD4 contains two conserved **HxKxxxxD/E (“HKD/E”)** motifs that assemble into a basic catalytic center. Structural/mechanistic evidence indicates catalysis can proceed via a **covalent 3′-phosphohistidine intermediate**, and PLD4 includes an additional **hydrophobic clamp** implicated in stabilizing substrates and influencing product release. (yuan2024structuralandmechanistic pages 1-5)

### 2.2 S,S-BMP synthesis (transphosphatidylation / stereo-inversion chemistry)
**Reaction (reported in 2024):** PLD4 catalyzes formation of **S,S-BMP** in lysosomes via a reaction consistent with **transphosphatidylation/glycerol-exchange** using **lyso-phosphatidylglycerol (lyso-PG)** and **monoacylglycerol (MAG)** as substrates, producing lysosomal BMP species enriched in the S,S stereoisomer. (singh2024pld3andpld4 pages 3-5, singh2024pld3andpld4 pages 7-8)

**Mechanistic evidence:**
- BMP synthesis was observed in vitro at **acidic pH (~5.4)**, consistent with lysosomal lumen conditions. (singh2024pld3andpld4 pages 3-5, singh2024pld3andpld4 pages 13-15)
- Incorporation of a **deuterium-labeled MAG headgroup** into BMP provided direct evidence for **glycerol exchange/transphosphatidylation**, supporting an enzyme-catalyzed remodeling step rather than passive isomerization. (singh2024pld3andpld4 pages 3-5)

**Physiological readouts:** PLD4 loss leads to substantial depletion of BMP in tissues where PLD4 is highly expressed (notably spleen), with downstream lysosomal lipid catabolism defects. (singh2024pld3andpld4 pages 7-8, singh2024pld3andpld4 pages 8-10)

## 3) Subcellular localization, topology, and expression

### 3.1 Localization and topology
Mouse PLD4 is a **type II transmembrane** protein localized to **endolysosomal/lysosomal compartments**. A type II topology implies an N-terminal cytosolic region followed by a transmembrane segment and a luminal catalytic/extracellular domain positioned to act within the acidic lumen. (gavin2018pld3andpld4 pages 1-2, yuan2024structuralandmechanistic pages 1-5)

### 3.2 Cell-type and tissue expression patterns
PLD4 expression is enriched in **dendritic cells and other myeloid-lineage immune cells**, with lower expression in B cells, consistent with a primary role in regulating nucleic-acid sensing in antigen-presenting compartments. (gavin2018pld3andpld4 pages 1-2)

Recent lipid-focused work also reports highest PLD4 mRNA in **murine spleen and myeloid cells**, which aligns with the tissue where PLD4 deletion most strongly reduces BMP abundance. (singh2024pld3andpld4 pages 7-8)

## 4) Biological roles, pathways, and in vivo phenotypes (mouse)

### 4.1 Restraining endosomal nucleic-acid sensing to prevent autoinflammation
In Pld4−/− mice, loss of PLD4 produces an inflammatory syndrome marked by **splenomegaly** and elevated inflammatory/IFN-related features, traced to altered responses of dendritic cells to **TLR9 ligands** (endosomal ssDNA sensing). These data support PLD4 as a lysosomal “brake” on nucleic-acid-driven innate immune activation by degrading nucleic acids within endolysosomes. (gavin2018pld3andpld4 pages 1-2)

### 4.2 Cooperative protection with PLD3 against lethal HLH-like immunopathology
Pld3−/−Pld4−/− double-knockout mice accumulate small ssRNAs and develop spontaneous fatal immune pathology described as **hemophagocytic lymphohistiocytosis (HLH)** with inflammatory liver damage and cytokine dysregulation. Genetic blockade of endosomal TLR signaling by **Unc93b1** deficiency rescues disease, while targeting only TLR7 or TLR9 is less effective, implying that both RNA and DNA sensing contribute. Residual perturbations in the rescued background require **STING (Tmem173)**, connecting lysosomal nucleic-acid catabolism to both endosomal TLR and cytosolic nucleic-acid sensing networks. (gavin2021cleavageofdna pages 1-2)

### 4.3 Lysosomal lipid degradation via BMP homeostasis (2024 advance)
PLD4 knockout mice show **marked depletion of BMP in spleen (~80% reduction)** and concomitant lysosomal lipid degradation defects reflected by **~2-fold accumulation of multiple gangliosides** (e.g., GM3, GD1a, GD2, GT1, GT3) in spleen. These findings support a role for PLD4 in maintaining the lysosomal lipid cofactor BMP required for efficient breakdown of complex glycosphingolipids. (singh2024pld3andpld4 pages 7-8, singh2024pld3andpld4 media b87b0738)

## 5) Recent developments (prioritizing 2023–2024)

### 5.1 2024 structural/mechanistic refinement of nuclease function
A 2024 *Structure* paper provided structural/mechanistic insights into PLD4’s endolysosomal nuclease activity, including: (i) dual HKD motif active site architecture, (ii) **lower pH optimum** for PLD4 compared with PLD3, (iii) inhibitory effect of **5′ phosphorylation**, and (iv) identification of a **3′-phosphohistidine intermediate** and a PLD4-specific clamp feature affecting substrate handling. (Yuan et al., June 2024, https://doi.org/10.1016/j.str.2024.02.019) (yuan2024structuralandmechanistic pages 1-5)

### 5.2 2024 discovery of PLD4 as an S,S-BMP synthase
A 2024 *Cell* study proposed and experimentally supported that PLD4 (and PLD3) catalyze **S,S-BMP** synthesis in lysosomes via transphosphatidylation/stereo-inversion chemistry. This directly revises the functional landscape of PLD4 from “nuclease only” to a **multifunctional lysosomal enzyme** linking lipid and nucleic-acid homeostasis. (Singh et al., Nov 2024, https://doi.org/10.1016/j.cell.2024.09.036) (singh2024pld3andpld4 pages 1-3, singh2024pld3andpld4 pages 3-5)

## 6) Current applications and real-world implementations

### 6.1 Disease mechanism mapping and targetable pathway nodes
Although this report focuses on mouse Pld4, the mechanistic framework has translational implications: PLD4 activity restrains activation of nucleic-acid sensing pathways (endosomal TLR7/TLR9 and a STING-coupled axis), implying therapeutic leverage points at **endosomal TLR trafficking/signaling** and/or **STING/type I IFN** pathways in nucleic-acid-driven inflammatory disease contexts. The double-knockout rescue by Unc93b1 deficiency provides causal pathway positioning and a “proof-of-principle” that suppressing endosomal TLR signaling can overcome consequences of defective lysosomal nucleic-acid catabolism. (gavin2021cleavageofdna pages 1-2)

### 6.2 Lysosomal lipid biology and biomarkers
The 2024 BMP synthesis work provides a functional rationale for using **BMP species** and/or downstream **ganglioside accumulation patterns** as biochemical readouts of PLD4-dependent lysosomal lipid degradation capacity in relevant tissues and cell types. In the mouse spleen, PLD4 knockout produced a large BMP depletion (~80%) and measurable ganglioside increases, illustrating assayable, quantitative endpoints for functional studies. (singh2024pld3andpld4 pages 7-8, singh2024pld3andpld4 media b87b0738)

## 7) Expert synthesis and analysis (authoritative interpretation)
The literature supports a model in which PLD4 is a **luminal endolysosomal enzyme** whose catalytic HKD core has been repurposed (relative to canonical PLD1/PLD2 lipid signaling enzymes) for **lysosomal catabolism/maintenance functions**. Earlier work established PLD4 as an **acid exonuclease** limiting immunostimulatory nucleic acids in endosomes/lysosomes, thereby preventing inappropriate activation of nucleic-acid sensing receptors and autoinflammation. (gavin2018pld3andpld4 pages 1-2, gavin2021cleavageofdna pages 1-2)

The 2024 findings add a second axis: PLD4 is also implicated as a key enzyme in generating the **lysosomally stable S,S stereoisomer of BMP**, a lipid thought to be required for efficient lysosomal lipid breakdown, consistent with ganglioside accumulation in Pld4 knockout spleen. This discovery suggests that PLD4 contributes to immune homeostasis through at least two lysosomal “buffering” functions—**nucleic-acid clearance** and **lipid cofactor production**—each of which can influence innate immune activation (via TLR/STING pathways) when lysosomal physiology is perturbed. (singh2024pld3andpld4 pages 1-3, yuan2024structuralandmechanistic pages 1-5, singh2024pld3andpld4 pages 7-8)

A key open question (not resolved in the cited excerpts) is whether both reactions are catalyzed by the same active-site chemistry under physiological conditions and how cells allocate PLD4’s apparent activities across substrates; nonetheless, both roles are supported by direct biochemical and in vivo evidence and align with PLD4’s endolysosomal localization and acidic pH preference. (yuan2024structuralandmechanistic pages 1-5, singh2024pld3andpld4 pages 3-5)
