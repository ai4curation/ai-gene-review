Gene Description

Tuba1a encodes tubulin alpha-1A, the predominant alpha-tubulin isotype in the developing and adult mouse brain. It heterodimerizes with beta-tubulin to form the structural subunit of microtubules, binding and hydrolyzing GTP. Tuba1a is essential for neuronal migration, cortical lamination, and hippocampal neurogenesis, with mutations causing lissencephaly-like phenotypes in mice. The protein undergoes extensive post-translational modifications including acetylation, detyrosination, glutamylation, and glycylation that regulate microtubule stability, motor protein recruitment, and ciliary/flagellar function.
