## Research report: Mouse **Aldh2** (UniProt **P47738**) — functional annotation of mitochondrial aldehyde dehydrogenase 2

### Executive summary
Mouse **Aldh2** (UniProt P47738) encodes **mitochondrial aldehyde dehydrogenase 2 (ALDH2)**, an NAD\(^+\)-dependent aldehyde-oxidizing enzyme that catalyzes conversion of **acetaldehyde to acetate/acetic acid** and also detoxifies multiple **reactive lipid-derived aldehydes** (notably **4-hydroxynonenal (4-HNE)** and **acrolein**). Across recent mouse studies (2023–2024), ALDH2 emerges as a mitochondrial “aldehydic load” control point that protects tissue function by (i) limiting reactive aldehyde adduction of mitochondrial proteins (BAT thermogenesis and systemic metabolism), (ii) maintaining mitochondrial biogenesis and oxidative phosphorylation capacity during acute kidney injury, (iii) preventing acrolein-driven metabolic rewiring (PKM2–HIF-1α) and fibrosis in kidney disease, (iv) suppressing sepsis-induced ARDS by limiting neutrophil NETosis via PAD4 degradation, and (v) mitigating alcohol-induced gut barrier failure and downstream hippocampal injury. Pharmacologic ALDH2 activation (e.g., **Alda-1**, and the newer **AD-9308/AD-5591** series) and gene delivery (AAV8-ALDH2) are active translational strategies in these systems. (ray2024mitochondrialaldehydedehydrogenase pages 1-2, chang2023acommoneastasian pages 1-2, li2024aldehydedehydrogenase2 pages 1-2, li2024aldehydedehydrogenase2 pages 10-12, xu2024neutrophilaldh2is pages 1-2, li2023aldehydedehydrogenase2 pages 3-8)

## 2) Key concepts and definitions (current understanding)

### 2.1 Enzymatic function: aldehyde oxidation (canonical reaction)
ALDH enzymes are described as NAD(P)\(^+\)-dependent enzymes that **irreversibly oxidize aldehydes to carboxylic acids**; ALDH2 is highlighted as a mitochondrial isoform with prominent roles in detoxification. (karan2023aldehydedehydrogenaseand pages 7-9)

For ALDH2 specifically:
* ALDH2 is described as the **second enzyme in oxidative alcohol metabolism**, converting **acetaldehyde to acetic acid**, and is also capable of oxidizing lipid aldehydes including **acrolein (ACR), 4-HNE, and MDA**. (karan2023aldehydedehydrogenaseand pages 9-10, karan2023aldehydedehydrogenaseand pages 10-12)
* In binge-alcohol mouse studies, ALDH2 is explicitly called the **key enzyme that converts acetaldehyde to acetate**. (ray2024mitochondrialaldehydedehydrogenase pages 1-2)

### 2.2 Substrate specificity and “aldehydic load”
Beyond acetaldehyde, recent work repeatedly emphasizes ALDH2 detoxification of reactive aldehydes generated by lipid peroxidation or inflammation:
* 4-HNE and acrolein are explicitly cited as aldehydes metabolized/detoxified by ALDH2 in kidney and metabolic disease contexts. (chang2023acommoneastasian pages 1-2, li2024aldehydedehydrogenase2 pages 1-2)
* A review of aldehyde metabolism in disease notes ALDH2 oxidizes **ACR, 4-HNE, and MDA**, and can be **inactivated** by ACR and 4-HNE when concentrations exceed ~10 μM (a mechanism for feed-forward aldehyde accumulation). (karan2023aldehydedehydrogenaseand pages 10-12)

### 2.3 Subcellular localization and where ALDH2 acts
ALDH2 is consistently described as **mitochondrial**.
* In renal tubular cells (HK-2), immunofluorescence colocalization is reported: **ALDH2 mainly colocalized with mitochondria**. (li2023aldehydedehydrogenase2 pages 3-8)
* Multiple disease models and reviews treat ALDH2 as a mitochondrial enzyme controlling mitochondrial stress and downstream tissue phenotypes. (ray2024mitochondrialaldehydedehydrogenase pages 1-2, chang2023acommoneastasian pages 1-2, liu2024aldh2mediatesthe pages 1-3)

### 2.4 Regulation: genetic variants and post-translational inhibition
Regulatory concepts emphasized in 2023–2024 sources include:
* **ALDH2 rs671 / E504K** dysfunction (modeled in knock-in mice) is a central framework for impaired aldehyde detoxification and downstream metabolic phenotypes. (chang2023acommoneastasian pages 1-2)
* Oxidative/post-translational modifications can suppress ALDH2 activity; in alcohol exposure settings ALDH2 activity is described as inhibited by oxidative PTMs including modification of the **catalytic cysteine**. (ray2024mitochondrialaldehydedehydrogenase pages 11-13)
* In cardiometabolic remodeling, ALDH2 expression is also presented as epigenetically regulated (DNMT1 and promoter methylation) in the context of SGLT2 inhibitor cardioprotection. (liu2024aldh2mediatesthe pages 1-3)

## 3) Recent developments and latest research (prioritizing 2023–2024)

### 3.1 2023: ALDH2 dysfunction drives metabolic disease via BAT mitochondrial protein adduction; AD-9308 as a next-generation activator
A 2023 *Nature Communications* study using male Aldh2 E504K knock-in mice reports that reduced ALDH2 activity increases aldehydic load (notably **4-HNE**) and identifies **increased 4-HNE-adducted mitochondrial proteins** in brown adipose tissue (BAT), including proteins involved in **fatty acid oxidation (FAO)** and **electron transport chain (ETC)** function. This is linked to impaired adaptive thermogenesis and systemic metabolic disease. (chang2023acommoneastasian pages 1-2, chang2023acommoneastasian pages 7-9)

Key quantitative/statistical points captured in text:
* BAT FAO and mitochondrial respiration are described as **markedly (~70%) reduced** in Aldh2 knock-in mice. (chang2023acommoneastasian pages 7-9)
* The paper states the ALDH2 Glu504Lys mutation affects **~560 million East Asians** (used to motivate translational relevance). (chang2023acommoneastasian pages 7-9)

Therapeutic development:
* **AD-9308** (oral prodrug) is described as a **water-soluble, potent, highly selective ALDH2 activator**; treatment ameliorated diet-induced obesity and fatty liver and improved glucose homeostasis in both WT and knock-in mice. (chang2023acommoneastasian pages 1-2)
* Structural/biophysical support for activator binding is illustrated by figure panels showing AD-9308 conversion to AD-5591 and the binding pocket within ALDH2 (see figure evidence). (chang2023acommoneastasian media 30ab6fb9, chang2023acommoneastasian media 7690b5df)

**Figure-based evidence (cropped panels):** The retrieved images contain key evidence for prodrug conversion, binding-pocket structural depiction, and in vivo dosing/phenotype outcomes for AD-9308. (chang2023acommoneastasian media 30ab6fb9, chang2023acommoneastasian media 7690b5df, chang2023acommoneastasian media 241fbaf7, chang2023acommoneastasian media cac101ef)

### 3.2 2023: ALDH2 activation protects mitochondrial function in acute kidney injury via a PGC-1α axis
A 2023 *Cell Death & Disease* study in cisplatin and maleic-acid AKI models reports that ALDH2 activation restores mitochondrial function by promoting **PGC-1α-mediated mitochondrial biogenesis**, supported by co-immunoprecipitation between ALDH2 and PGC-1α and evidence that PGC-1α knockdown abrogates Alda-1 benefits. (li2023aldehydedehydrogenase2 pages 9-13)

Key quantitative/statistical points:
* Cisplatin AKI model: cisplatin **18 mg/kg i.p.** and sacrifice day 3; **Alda-1 20 mg/kg i.p. for 6 days**. (li2023aldehydedehydrogenase2 pages 3-8)
* Alda-1 improved renal function: serum creatinine **74.42 ± 4.39 → 38.54 ± 4.92 μmol/L** (P<0.0001), BUN **31.88 ± 1.68 → 20.56 ± 0.34** (P<0.001) vs cisplatin AKI. (li2023aldehydedehydrogenase2 pages 3-8)
* Aldh2 knockout worsened injury: in cisplatin AKI, Scr **86.13 ± 6.20** in KO vs **62.29 ± 3.19 μmol/L** in WT (P<0.01). (li2023aldehydedehydrogenase2 pages 3-8)

### 3.3 2024: ALDH2 preserves kidney function by countering acrolein-driven PKM2/HIF-1α metabolic rewiring and fibrosis
A 2024 *JCI Insight* study focuses on ALDH2 in kidney fibrosis, connecting ALDH2 decline to accumulation of **acrolein** (an ALDH2-metabolized aldehyde) and showing acrolein modifies **PKM2**, driving PKM2 nuclear translocation and coactivation of **HIF-1α**, shifting metabolism toward glycolysis and impairing mitochondrial function (contributing to fibrosis). (li2024aldehydedehydrogenase2 pages 1-2, li2024aldehydedehydrogenase2 pages 10-12)

A translationally relevant implementation is **renal AAV delivery**:
* AAV8-ALDH2-EGFP **2 × 10\(^11\)** genome copies via subcapsular injection for **28 days prior to UUO**, with sacrifice **7 days after UUO**, reduced acrolein-protein conjugates and attenuated fibrosis in both WT and mutant backgrounds. (li2024aldehydedehydrogenase2 pages 10-12)

### 3.4 2024: Neutrophil ALDH2 suppresses NETosis and improves sepsis-induced ARDS by promoting PAD4 degradation
A 2024 *Cellular & Molecular Immunology* paper identifies neutrophil ALDH2 as a therapeutic lever in septic ARDS. In mouse sepsis models, Aldh2 deletion or rs671 knock-in worsened ARDS (vascular leak, hypoxemia), with ALDH2 acting by suppressing NETosis. Mechanistically, ALDH2 promotes **CHIP-dependent K48-linked polyubiquitination** of **PAD4** leading to proteasomal degradation, thereby inhibiting NET formation. (xu2024neutrophilaldh2is pages 1-2, xu2024neutrophilaldh2is pages 6-8)

Key quantitative/statistical points extracted:
* In LPS sepsis model, Evans blue vascular leakage is quantified at **24 h** after LPS and survival is tracked; at **96 h** after LPS, Aldh2\(−/−\) survival was **6.7%**. (xu2024neutrophilaldh2is pages 1-2, xu2024neutrophilaldh2is pages 3-6)
* DNase I and PAD4 inhibitor **GSK-484** alleviated ARDS in both WT and Aldh2\(−/−\) mice, supporting a NET-mediated mechanism. (xu2024neutrophilaldh2is pages 3-6)
* Alda-1 reduced NETosis markers (H3Cit, MPO–DNA) in both ALDH2 WT and rs671 neutrophils in reported experiments (exact Alda-1 dosing not captured in extracted text). (xu2024neutrophilaldh2is pages 10-12)

### 3.5 2024: ALDH2 protects against binge alcohol–mediated gut barrier and hippocampal injury (gut–brain axis)
A 2024 *Cells* study using Aldh2 knockout mice reports that ALDH2 protects against binge-alcohol-mediated gut injury and downstream hippocampal damage, implicating gut leakiness/endotoxemia and oxidative stress-mediated PTMs. (ray2024mitochondrialaldehydedehydrogenase pages 1-2, ray2024mitochondrialaldehydedehydrogenase pages 11-13)

Key implementation details:
* Binge ethanol gavage **4 g/kg/dose**, **3 doses**, **12 h** intervals; tissues collected **1 h** after the last dose. (ray2024mitochondrialaldehydedehydrogenase pages 1-2)

### 3.6 2024: ALDH2 as a node in cardiometabolic remodeling and drug response (SGLT2 inhibitors)
A 2024 *Cardiovascular Diabetology* paper proposes that ALDH2 mediates cardioprotective effects of SGLT2 inhibitors (e.g., dapagliflozin), where cardiomyocyte-specific ALDH2 knockout abolishes dapagliflozin protection in pathological hypertrophy models. The proposed pathway includes NHE1 inhibition → reduced ROS → reduced DNMT1 → reduced ALDH2 promoter methylation → increased transcription factor binding (NFYA) and ALDH2 expression. (liu2024aldh2mediatesthe pages 1-3)

## 4) Current applications and real-world implementations

### 4.1 Pharmacologic activation of ALDH2
**Alda-1** and **AD-9308/AD-5591** represent two practical activation strategies applied in vivo:

* **Alda-1 in AKI:** Alda-1 (ALDH2 agonist) is used as an intraperitoneal pre-treatment regimen in mouse AKI models and improves renal function (Scr/BUN) and mitochondrial parameters. (li2023aldehydedehydrogenase2 pages 3-8)
* **AD-9308 in metabolic disease:** AD-9308 is administered daily by oral gavage and reduces obesity/fatty liver and improves glucose homeostasis; the paper provides both biochemical activity assays (recombinant ALDH2) and in vivo outcomes. (chang2023acommoneastasian pages 7-9, chang2023acommoneastasian pages 12-13)

### 4.2 Gene therapy / expression augmentation
* In kidney fibrosis models, **AAV8-mediated renal ALDH2 expression** is applied as a pre-emptive intervention (28 days before UUO) and reduces aldehyde adducts and fibrosis. (li2024aldehydedehydrogenase2 pages 10-12)

### 4.3 NETosis-targeted ARDS interventions in an ALDH2 framework
* In septic ARDS models where ALDH2 is deficient, NET removal (DNase I) or PAD4 inhibition (GSK-484) mitigates disease, supporting a real-world mechanistic implementation pipeline: ALDH2 → PAD4 turnover → NET burden → endothelial injury. (xu2024neutrophilaldh2is pages 3-6)

## 5) Expert opinions and analysis (authoritative sources)

### 5.1 Framing ALDH2 as a dual detoxification enzyme (alcohol and lipid aldehydes)
Authoritative review-level synthesis emphasizes ALDH2’s canonical role in acetaldehyde oxidation and an expanded view in which ALDH2 contributes to detoxification of lipid-derived aldehydes (ACR, 4-HNE, MDA), with inactivation of the enzyme by these aldehydes potentially promoting a vicious cycle of aldehydic stress. (karan2023aldehydedehydrogenaseand pages 10-12)

### 5.2 ALDH2 as a mitochondrial homeostasis regulator
Primary 2023–2024 studies converge on a model in which ALDH2 supports mitochondrial function not only by detoxifying aldehydes but also by influencing mitochondrial programs:
* **BAT:** reduced ALDH2 activity → 4-HNE adduction of mitochondrial FAO/ETC proteins → reduced FAO/respiration → impaired thermogenesis and energy expenditure. (chang2023acommoneastasian pages 7-9)
* **Kidney AKI:** ALDH2 activation → PGC-1α nuclear translocation → mitochondrial biogenesis and improved oxidative phosphorylation; glycolytic shift is suppressed. (li2023aldehydedehydrogenase2 pages 3-8, li2023aldehydedehydrogenase2 pages 9-13)
* **Kidney fibrosis:** aldehyde (acrolein) accumulation → PKM2/HIF-1α axis → glycolytic reprogramming and mitochondrial dysfunction; ALDH2 expression counters this cascade. (li2024aldehydedehydrogenase2 pages 10-12)

### 5.3 Immunometabolic axis: ALDH2 as a brake on NETosis
The septic ARDS study positions neutrophil ALDH2 as an immunometabolic regulator that controls PAD4 protein half-life via ubiquitin–proteasome machinery (CHIP), connecting aldehyde metabolism to chromatin decondensation/NET formation and vascular injury outcomes. (xu2024neutrophilaldh2is pages 6-8)
