Gene Description

FERM and PDZ domain-containing protein 2 (Frmpd2) is a multi-domain scaffold protein that organizes signaling complexes at the cell membrane. It contains an N-terminal KIND domain, a FERM domain that binds phosphatidylinositols for membrane anchoring, and three PDZ domains that mediate protein-protein interactions. In the mouse nervous system, Frmpd2 functions as a postsynaptic scaffold that anchors NMDA receptor subunits (GluN2A/GluN2B) at synapses via its PDZ2 domain, and participates in cone photoreceptor synapse formation through PDZ3-mediated interaction with LRIT1. Based on studies of the human ortholog, Frmpd2 also contributes to epithelial cell polarity by localizing to the basolateral membrane and regulating tight junction formation, and serves as a scaffold for the NOD2 innate immune signalosome in intestinal epithelial cells.

Core Functions

Frmpd2 functions as a signaling adaptor/scaffold protein that organizes multi-protein complexes at the membrane. Its PDZ domains mediate specific protein-protein interactions while the FERM domain anchors it to the membrane via phosphatidylinositol binding. In neurons, it scaffolds NMDA receptor subunits at the postsynaptic density. In epithelial cells, it organizes cell junction complexes at the basolateral membrane.

Research report: Functional annotation of mouse Frmpd2 (UniProt A0A140LI67)

1) Target verification (gene/protein identity)

The UniProt accession A0A140LI67 corresponds to Mus musculus Frmpd2 (also referenced as Gm626 in mouse literature), annotated as “FERM and PDZ domain-containing protein 2,” consistent with experimental descriptions of a modular scaffold protein containing an N‑terminal KIND domain, a FERM domain, and three PDZ domains (PDZ1–3) (lu2018frmpd2anovel pages 1-6, stenzel2009pdzdomaindirectedbasolateraltargeting pages 2-3). Multiple independent studies that explicitly use the name Frmpd2/FRMPD2 report FERM/PDZ-mediated membrane and protein-binding activities aligned with the UniProt domain expectations (stenzel2009pdzdomaindirectedbasolateraltargeting pages 2-3, stenzel2009pdzdomaindirectedbasolateraltargeting pages 8-9, lu2018frmpd2anovel pages 6-10).

2) Key concepts and definitions (current understanding)

FRMPD2 is best understood as a multidomain scaffold/adaptor that couples: (i) membrane targeting (via the FERM domain’s phosphoinositide binding), (ii) protein complex assembly (via PDZ-domain recognition of short C‑terminal motifs), and (iii) in some contexts, cytoskeletal linkage (via FERM–actin interaction). In polarized epithelial models, the FERM domain is sufficient for membrane localization and shows lipid specificity (preferential binding to PtdIns(3,4)P2), while PDZ2 provides “basolateral steering” by binding catenin-family proteins connected to E‑cadherin complexes (stenzel2009pdzdomaindirectedbasolateraltargeting pages 8-9). In neurons, FRMPD2 is described as a postsynaptic scaffold that anchors NMDA receptor subunits via PDZ binding and can influence excitatory synaptic signaling and synapse morphology (lu2018frmpd2anovel pages 6-10, lu2018frmpd2anovel pages 10-13).

3) Domain architecture and binding partners (mechanistic evidence)

3.1 Domain architecture

Epithelial and neuronal studies converge on a common architecture: KIND–FERM–PDZ1–PDZ2–PDZ3 (stenzel2009pdzdomaindirectedbasolateraltargeting pages 2-3, lu2018frmpd2anovel pages 1-6). A schematic depiction of this domain organization and the PDZ-domain binding assays is shown in the Lu et al. synaptic study figures (lu2018frmpd2anovel media e6b2e2dc).

3.2 PDZ2: high-affinity interaction with GluN2A/GRIN2A (hippocampal synapses)

In mouse hippocampal CA1, FRMPD2 was reported to bind NMDA receptor subunits with strong selectivity for GluN2A via its second PDZ domain (PDZ2). Biophysical measurements (SPR) reported KD ≈ 0.31 μM for a GluN2A C‑terminal peptide, compared with KD ≈ 0.25 mM for a GluN2B peptide (~1000‑fold weaker), and removal of the last 10 residues of GluN2A abolished binding (lu2018frmpd2anovel pages 6-10). The paper also reports a PDZ2 crystal structure (PDB 5ZDS), and SPR sensorgrams/structural panels are shown in the figures extracted from the manuscript (lu2018frmpd2anovel media 5a963f2e, lu2018frmpd2anovel media fb031b99).

Functional consequence: manipulations of FRMPD2 expression altered synaptic GluN2A localization and GluN2A-dependent NMDA currents without changing paired-pulse ratio, consistent with a postsynaptic scaffolding role (lu2018frmpd2anovel pages 6-10).

3.3 PDZ3: interaction with LRIT1 (retina)

In mouse retina, Frmpd2 was identified as an interactor of the intracellular domain of Lrit1. A yeast two-hybrid screen and co-immunoprecipitation supported the interaction, and deletion mapping indicated PDZ3 mediates binding to the Lrit1 C‑terminal region (ueno2018lrit1aretinal pages 8-9). The same study notes Frmpd2 localizes to photoreceptor synaptic terminals in an Lrit1-dependent manner (ueno2018lrit1aretinal pages 8-9).

3.4 PDZ2 and cadherin–catenin machinery (polarized epithelia)

In polarized epithelial models, FRMPD2 shows selective basolateral localization and recruitment to cell–cell contacts dependent on E‑cadherin. Domain-function dissection indicates that the FERM+PDZ2 module is sufficient for basolateral targeting, and PDZ2 binds catenin-family proteins including p0071, ARVCF, and δ‑catenin, linking FRMPD2 to cadherin complexes (stenzel2009pdzdomaindirectedbasolateraltargeting pages 8-9, stenzel2009pdzdomaindirectedbasolateraltargeting pages 1-2). An observed endogenous band at ~140 kDa (predicted 144 kDa) supported detection of the full-length protein in epithelial cells (stenzel2009pdzdomaindirectedbasolateraltargeting pages 2-3).

4) Subcellular localization (where it functions)

Evidence supports context-specific peripheral membrane localization:
- Basolateral membrane and junctional regions (epithelia): FRMPD2 is basolateral in polarized epithelial cells and partially overlaps with junctional markers; basolateral restriction depends on E‑cadherin and PDZ2 partner binding (stenzel2009pdzdomaindirectedbasolateraltargeting pages 1-2, stenzel2009pdzdomaindirectedbasolateraltargeting pages 8-9).
- Postsynaptic density (neurons): FRMPD2 is enriched in PSD fractions and colocalizes with GluN2A at postsynaptic membranes; figures show domain schematics, pull-down assays, and PSD-related images (lu2018frmpd2anovel pages 6-10, lu2018frmpd2anovel media e6b2e2dc).
- Photoreceptor synaptic terminals (retina): Frmpd2 is enriched in photoreceptors and localizes to photoreceptor synaptic terminals in an Lrit1-dependent manner (ueno2018lrit1aretinal pages 8-9).

5) Biological functions and pathways (tissue-resolved)

5.1 Neuronal function: NMDAR trafficking/anchoring and excitability

A mouse hippocampal study (preprint, 2018) reports that FRMPD2 anchors GluN2A-containing NMDARs at synapses and bidirectionally modulates NMDAR-mediated transmission and neuronal excitability. Quantitatively, lentiviral knockdown reduced hippocampal FRMPD2 by ~40%, reduced spontaneous action potential frequency by 71%, and reduced GluN2A-sensitive evoked NMDAR currents by ~65%; overexpression increased FRMPD2 1.9–2.1×, increased AP frequency 2.28×, and increased GluN2A-sensitive currents 2.38× (lu2018frmpd2anovel pages 6-10).

Mechanistically, the same work reports FERM-domain interaction with F-actin, consistent with a scaffold that couples receptor anchoring to cytoskeletal organization and synapse morphology (lu2018frmpd2anovel pages 10-13). PSD/structural phenotypes (shorter/thinner PSD with knockdown; larger PSD with overexpression) are illustrated in extracted figures (lu2018frmpd2anovel media ec8074a2).

5.2 Retina: photoreceptor expression and synaptic organization

Frmpd2 shows retina-enriched expression and a single 5.7‑kb transcript in mouse retina (not detected in other tested tissues), with developmental upregulation from weak ONL signal at P9 to stronger signal at P21 (ueno2018lrit1aretinal pages 8-9). Functionally, it binds LRIT1 via PDZ3 and is implicated as part of a photoreceptor synaptic terminal scaffold whose localization depends on LRIT1 (ueno2018lrit1aretinal pages 8-9).

5.3 Intestinal epithelium/innate immunity: spatial organization of NOD2–RIPK2–NF-κB signaling

In intestinal epithelial cells, FRMPD2 functions as a basolateral organizer of the NOD2 signalosome. A PNAS study reports FRMPD2 colocalizes with NOD2 at the basolateral membrane and supports membrane recruitment/retention of NOD2 and RIPK2, enabling MDP-induced NF‑κB activation and downstream antimicrobial/inflammatory outputs (e.g., IL‑8 and defensin programs), with loss-of-function (FRMPD2 silencing) reducing these responses (lipinski2012rnaiscreeningidentifies pages 2-4, lipinski2012rnaiscreeningidentifies pages 2-2).

Disease-relevant mechanism: a Crohn-associated NOD2 frameshift (L1007fsinsC) disrupts the NOD2–FRMPD2 interaction and is linked to impaired MDP responses; in addition, FRMPD2 expression is reported as downregulated in inflamed intestinal mucosa and decreased in a mouse DSS colitis model (lipinski2012rnaiscreeningidentifies pages 4-5, lipinski2012rnaiscreeningidentifies pages 4-4).

5.4 Polarized epithelia: basolateral polarity and tight-junction assembly

In epithelial models, FRMPD2 knockdown impairs tight junction formation (stenzel2009pdzdomaindirectedbasolateraltargeting pages 1-2). Mechanistically, the prevailing model is that FERM-mediated membrane binding (including PtdIns(3,4)P2 specificity) and PDZ2-mediated catenin binding combine to achieve E‑cadherin-dependent basolateral targeting (stenzel2009pdzdomaindirectedbasolateraltargeting pages 8-9).

6) Recent developments (prioritizing 2023–2024)

Direct 2023–2024 primary experimental studies specifically on Frmpd2 in mouse were limited among retrievable sources in this search. However, authoritative 2024 review literature explicitly places FRMPD2 into the contemporary framework of GluN2A C-terminal scaffolding networks:
- 2024 (peer-reviewed review): A review on GluN2A as an antidepressant-relevant target lists FRMPD2 among the GluN2A C-terminal interacting scaffolding proteins and emphasizes that the GluN2A CTD forms multiprotein complexes controlling NMDAR trafficking, clustering, localization, and signaling (Wang et al., Aug 2024, International Journal of Neuropsychopharmacology; https://doi.org/10.1093/ijnp/pyae037) (wang2024glun2aapromising pages 4-6).

7) Current applications and real-world implementations

Although there is not yet clear evidence of FRMPD2 being used clinically as a biomarker or therapeutic target in the retrieved texts, several real-world–proximal implementations are evident in experimental and translational research workflows:
1. Epilepsy mechanism interrogation in vivo: FRMPD2 knockdown in chronic seizure models (PTZ and kainic acid) is used to modulate seizure measures and EEG seizure-like events, with FRMPD2 protein levels reported higher in epileptic patient tissue and epileptic mouse brain; this positions FRMPD2 as a candidate mechanistic modulator of hyperexcitability (lu2018frmpd2anovel pages 13-17, lu2018frmpd2anovel media ec8074a2).
2. Inflammatory bowel disease mechanism (Crohn’s disease context): FRMPD2-dependent basolateral assembly of NOD2 signaling provides a mechanistic explanation for spatial specificity of microbial sensing and how Crohn-associated NOD2 variants may lead to impaired antibacterial responses; FRMPD2 downregulation in inflamed mucosa and colitis models supports disease relevance (kabi2012frmbp2directsnod2 pages 1-2, lipinski2012rnaiscreeningidentifies pages 4-4).
3. Drug-discovery framing via GluN2A networks: FRMPD2 is included in 2024 therapeutic discussions as part of GluN2A CTD scaffolding networks relevant to NMDAR regulation in neuropsychiatric disease, suggesting FRMPD2-defined protein–protein interfaces may be relevant when considering “receptor trafficking/signaling” targets (wang2024glun2aapromising pages 4-6).

8) Expert opinions and analysis (authoritative synthesis)

Scaffold integrating membrane identity and partner selection. A FERM-protein family review interprets FRMPD2 as a membrane-associated scaffold in epithelial polarity that couples phosphoinositide binding (PtdIns(3,4)P2 preference) with adhesion-complex interactions, but highlights key open questions about how phospholipid binding regulates recruitment/binding of other proteins to FERM domains (Moleirinho et al., Jun 2013, Biochemical Journal; https://doi.org/10.1042/bj20121642) (moleirinho2013theexpandingfamily pages 2-3, moleirinho2013theexpandingfamily pages 3-4).

Spatial innate immune signaling. A PNAS commentary emphasizes FRMPD2 as a determinant of NOD2 basolateral localization and thus spatially restricted signaling; it also notes field-wide limitations (e.g., low endogenous NOD2 expression, poor reagents, and uncertainty about how variants reduce NOD2 function), indicating continued uncertainty in mapping genotype-to-localization-to-function for this pathway (Kabi & McDonald, Dec 2012, PNAS; https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.1219395110) (kabi2012frmbp2directsnod2 pages 1-2).

Synaptic specialization and selectivity. The hippocampal work suggests FRMPD2 is a GluN2A-selective scaffold with a strong PDZ2 interaction and additional FERM–actin coupling, but also explicitly raises mechanistic uncertainties (e.g., why GluN2B fails to co-precipitate despite weak in vitro binding; how FRMPD2’s FERM-dependent synapse formation operates) (lu2018frmpd2anovel pages 13-17, lu2018frmpd2anovel pages 6-10).

9) Key statistics and data highlights (from recent and foundational studies)

  • Protein–peptide binding affinity (mouse FRMPD2 PDZ2): KD ~0.31 μM (GluN2A CTD peptide) vs ~0.25 mM (GluN2B peptide) (lu2018frmpd2anovel pages 6-10).
  • Synaptic physiology (mouse CA1; FRMPD2 manipulation): knockdown reduced GluN2A-sensitive NMDAR currents by ~65%; overexpression increased them 2.38×; spontaneous AP frequency changed −71% / +2.28×; FRMPD2 protein levels changed ~−40% / +1.9–2.1× (lu2018frmpd2anovel pages 6-10).
  • Retina transcript: 5.7 kb Frmpd2 transcript detected in mouse retina; developmental increase between P9 and P21 (ueno2018lrit1aretinal pages 8-9).

10) Limitations of the current evidence base

  • Some of the most detailed neuronal mechanistic and epilepsy evidence in the retrieved set is from a 2018 preprint, which should be treated cautiously until confirmed in peer-reviewed form and/or independently replicated (lu2018frmpd2anovel pages 6-10, lu2018frmpd2anovel pages 13-17).
  • Several clinically relevant quantitative details (e.g., exact seizure/EEG effect sizes; some NF‑κB effect sizes) appear to reside in figures or sections not fully available as text evidence in this run (lu2018frmpd2anovel pages 13-17, lipinski2012rnaiscreeningidentifies pages 4-4).
  • Recent (2023–2024) primary research directly focused on FRMPD2 in mouse was limited among retrievable sources; the principal 2024 update is review-level contextualization within GluN2A networks (wang2024glun2aapromising pages 4-6).

Frmpd2 - Research Notes

Gene Overview

Frmpd2 (FERM and PDZ domain-containing protein 2) is a multi-domain scaffold protein
consisting of an N-terminal KIND domain, a FERM domain, and three PDZ domains. It functions
as a molecular adaptor/scaffold in multiple cellular contexts, including epithelial cell
polarity, synaptic transmission, retinal synapse formation, and innate immune signaling.

UniProt: A0A140LI67 | MGI: MGI:2685472 | Synonyms: Gm626

Domain Architecture

  • KIND domain (aa 15-197): Function not well characterized for FRMPD2 specifically
  • FERM domain (aa 342-642): Binds phosphatidylinositols (especially PtdIns(3,4)P2);
    sufficient for membrane localization; interacts with actin filaments
  • PDZ1 domain (aa 775-861): Function less characterized
  • PDZ2 domain (aa 950-1035): Key interaction domain; binds GluN2A/GluN2B C-termini,
    p0071/PKP4, ARVCF, CTNND2; crystal structure solved (PDB: 5ZDS)
  • PDZ3 domain (aa 1079-1167): Binds LRIT1 C-terminus

Key Literature

1. Epithelial cell polarity and tight junctions

[PMID:19706687 Stenzel et al. 2009, "PDZ-domain-directed basolateral targeting of the
peripheral membrane protein FRMPD2 in epithelial cells"]

Key findings:
- FRMPD2 localizes to basolateral membrane in epithelial cells (human cell lines)
- Partially colocalizes with ZO-1 (TJP1) at tight junctions
- Knockdown in Caco-2 cells impairs tight junction formation
- FERM domain binds phosphatidylinositols, sufficient for membrane localization
- E-cadherin-dependent recruitment to cell-cell junctions
- PDZ2 binds p0071 (armadillo protein PKP4) for basolateral restriction
- Catenin family proteins identified as binding partners

2. NMDA receptor scaffolding at synapses

[PMID:31196628 Lu et al. 2019, "The second PDZ domain of scaffold protein Frmpd2 binds
to GluN2A of NMDA receptors"]

Key findings (mouse):
- Frmpd2 specifically expressed at postsynaptic membrane
- PDZ2 domain (not PDZ1 or PDZ3) binds GluN2A and GluN2B C-termini
- Higher affinity for GluN2A than GluN2B (SPR data)
- Crystal structure of mouse PDZ2 solved at 1.80 A (PDB: 5ZDS)
- Suggests role in synaptic NMDAR-mediated neural excitatory transmission

Additional data from bioRxiv preprint (doi:10.1101/403360):
- FRMPD2 anchors GluN2A at synapses without changing total NMDAR expression
- FERM domain directly interacts with actin filaments
- Modifies excitatory synapse morphogenesis
- Mediates behavioral and field potential phenotypes of epilepsy

3. Cone photoreceptor synapse formation

[PMID:29590622 Ueno et al. 2018, "Lrit1, a Retinal Transmembrane Protein, Regulates
Selective Synapse Formation in Cone Photoreceptor Cells and Visual Acuity"]

Key findings (mouse retina):
- Frmpd2 described as "photoreceptor scaffold protein"
- PDZ3 domain interacts with LRIT1 C-terminus
- Part of Frmpd2-Lrit1-mGluR6 axis
- Regulates selective synapse formation between cone photoreceptors and cone ON-bipolar cells
- Lrit1-deficient retinas show aberrant cone pedicle morphology
- Lrit1-null mice show impaired visual acuity

4. NOD2 innate immune signaling

[PMID:23213202 Lipinski et al. 2012, "RNAi screening identifies mediators of NOD2
signaling: Implications for spatial specificity of MDP recognition"]

Key findings (human cell lines):
- FRMPD2 identified in RNAi screen as positive NF-kB regulator
- Interacts with NOD2 via leucine-rich repeats
- Forms complex with membrane-associated protein ERBB2IP
- Spatially assembles NOD2-signaling complex (NOD2-RIPK2) at basolateral membrane
- Restricts NOD2-mediated immune responses to basolateral compartment
- Crohn disease NOD2 truncation mutations impair FRMPD2 interaction
- Intestinal inflammation leads to FRMPD2 downregulation

Assessment Notes

The core function of Frmpd2 is as a signaling adaptor/scaffold protein that organizes
multi-protein complexes at the membrane. The specific biological contexts are:

  1. Primary in mouse: Synaptic scaffolding (direct experimental evidence in mouse)
  2. NMDAR anchoring at postsynaptic density (PMID:31196628)

  3. Cone photoreceptor synapse formation via LRIT1 (PMID:29590622)

  4. Inferred from human ortholog (Q68DX3): Epithelial polarity

  5. Basolateral membrane targeting (PMID:19706687)
  6. Tight junction regulation (PMID:19706687)
  7. NOD2 signalosome assembly (PMID:23213202)

The tight junction and NOD2 functions are based on human FRMPD2 studies and transferred
by ISO/IBA. The synaptic functions have direct experimental evidence in mouse.
