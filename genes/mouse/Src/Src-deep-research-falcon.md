## Research report: Mouse Src (UniProt P05480) functional annotation

### 0) Mandatory verification: correct gene/protein
The UniProt accession **P05480** corresponds to **Mus musculus Src (proto-oncogene tyrosine-protein kinase Src; c-Src; pp60c-src)**, a **Src-family non-receptor tyrosine kinase** with conserved **SH4–Unique–SH3–SH2–kinase (SH1)–C-terminal tail** architecture typical of Src-module kinases. This aligns with the literature describing Src-family kinases and the canonical regulatory tyrosines controlling c-Src activity. (pelantova2021analysisofsrc pages 16-19, raji2023roleofcsrc pages 3-5, min2022crosstalkbetweenwnt pages 2-4, raji2023roleofcsrc pages 2-3)

### 1) Key concepts and definitions (current understanding)

#### 1.1 What Src is
Src is the prototypical **non-receptor protein tyrosine kinase (PTK)**. Like other Src-family kinases (SFKs), it is a modular, multi-domain enzyme whose signaling specificity depends heavily on **domain-mediated localization and docking** rather than only on intrinsic catalytic-site peptide preferences. (raji2023roleofcsrc pages 2-3, shah2018thesrcmodule pages 14-16)

**Domain architecture (core definitions)**
- **SH4 domain (N-terminus):** contains the **myristoylation site** (e.g., Gly2) required for membrane targeting. (raji2023roleofcsrc pages 3-5, raji2023roleofcsrc pages 2-3)
- **Unique domain (UD):** intrinsically disordered region contributing to Src-family-specific interactions and regulation; can interact with lipids/phosphoinositides (reviewed). (raji2023roleofcsrc pages 5-7, raji2023roleofcsrc pages 2-3)
- **SH3 domain:** binds **proline-rich motifs** (PXXP-type), contributing to substrate recruitment and autoinhibition. (maffei2015structuralandfunctional pages 14-19, shah2018thesrcmodule pages 14-16)
- **SH2 domain:** binds **phosphotyrosine motifs**, including a high-affinity consensus exemplified by **pYEEI**; also mediates autoinhibition via binding to the phosphorylated C-terminal tail. (cai2020roleofco pages 6-7, maffei2015structuralandfunctional pages 14-19)
- **SH1/kinase domain:** catalytic PTK domain that transfers phosphate from ATP to tyrosine residues on protein substrates. (maffei2015structuralandfunctional pages 14-19, cuestahernandez2023anallostericswitch pages 1-2)
- **C-terminal regulatory tail:** contains the inhibitory regulatory tyrosine (Tyr527 in chicken numbering / Tyr530 human numbering; mouse Src uses the conserved orthologous site). (raji2023roleofcsrc pages 3-5, min2022crosstalkbetweenwnt pages 2-4)

#### 1.2 Enzymatic function (reaction and substrates)
Src catalyzes **ATP-dependent phosphorylation of protein tyrosine residues** (EC 2.7.10.2 per UniProt context; mechanistically consistent with PTK activity described in reviews). This includes phosphorylation of downstream signaling proteins and **autophosphorylation** events that modulate Src conformation and activity. (maffei2015structuralandfunctional pages 14-19, shah2018thesrcmodule pages 14-16, cuestahernandez2023anallostericswitch pages 1-2)

#### 1.3 Regulation: the “Src switch” (closed/inactive vs open/active)
A central concept in Src biology is that activity is controlled by two conserved tyrosines:
- **Activation-loop tyrosine:** **Tyr416 (chicken) / Tyr419 (human)** whose phosphorylation is associated with the active state and maximal catalytic activity. (raji2023roleofcsrc pages 3-5, min2022crosstalkbetweenwnt pages 2-4)
- **C-terminal inhibitory tyrosine:** **Tyr527 (chicken) / Tyr530 (human)**. When phosphorylated, it binds the SH2 pocket intramolecularly to stabilize a **closed, autoinhibited conformation**; dephosphorylation releases the clamp and allows activation-loop phosphorylation. (raji2023roleofcsrc pages 3-5, pelantova2021analysisofsrc pages 19-23, min2022crosstalkbetweenwnt pages 2-4)

Endogenous regulators highlighted in the retrieved literature include:
- **Csk** phosphorylates the inhibitory C-terminal tail tyrosine to enforce autoinhibition. (raji2023roleofcsrc pages 3-5, min2022crosstalkbetweenwnt pages 2-4)
- Multiple **protein tyrosine phosphatases** (e.g., **PTPα, SHP1/2, PTP1B, CD45**) can dephosphorylate the tail tyrosine and promote activation. (pelantova2021analysisofsrc pages 19-23)

#### 1.4 Substrate specificity (what Src phosphorylates and why)
Current understanding emphasizes that Src substrate selection is strongly shaped by **colocalization and docking via SH2/SH3 domains**, often more than by short linear sequence motifs at the catalytic site.
- SH3-mediated proline-rich interactions and SH2-mediated phosphotyrosine docking can promote **processive phosphorylation** and pathway-specific substrate engagement (e.g., p130Cas models discussed in Src-module reviews). (shah2018thesrcmodule pages 27-28, shah2018thesrcmodule pages 14-16)
- SH2 sequence preference includes high affinity for **pYEEI-like motifs** (canonical for Src-family SH2 domains). (maffei2015structuralandfunctional pages 14-19)

### 2) Subcellular localization (where Src acts)
Src is primarily a **membrane-associated kinase** because N-terminal lipidation and basic residues promote membrane binding. (pelantova2021analysisofsrc pages 16-19, raji2023roleofcsrc pages 2-3)

A key functional localization theme is that **activated Src is enriched at peripheral adhesion sites (focal adhesions)** rather than being uniformly distributed. (cai2020roleofco pages 6-7)

Beyond the plasma membrane/cortical regions, reviews also note Src pools in the **cytosol, mitochondria, and nucleus** in context-dependent ways, consistent with broad signaling roles across compartments. (min2022crosstalkbetweenwnt pages 2-4)

### 3) Biological processes and pathways (functional annotation)

#### 3.1 Adhesion and cytoskeletal signaling (FAK/paxillin/p130Cas axis)
Src is repeatedly linked to **focal-adhesion signaling** involving **FAK, paxillin, and p130Cas**, supporting roles in **adhesion remodeling, actin dynamics, migration, and invasive behavior**. (pelantova2021analysisofsrc pages 16-19, raji2023roleofcsrc pages 19-21, shah2018thesrcmodule pages 14-16)

Mechanistically, focal-adhesion signaling provides an archetype for Src specificity: a substrate such as **FAK** can autophosphorylate to create an SH2 docking site, localizing Src to focal adhesions and facilitating phosphorylation of proximal targets. (shah2018thesrcmodule pages 14-16)

#### 3.2 Wnt signaling crosstalk
A 2022 review specifically highlights bidirectional crosstalk between **Wnt signaling and Src**, including Src regulation of Wnt through **LRP6 tyrosine phosphorylation**, and Wnt-associated regulation of Src activity. (min2022crosstalkbetweenwnt pages 2-4)

#### 3.3 Oncogenic signal transduction programs
A 2023 review consolidates c-Src roles in tumor biology and drug resistance, emphasizing downstream pathway engagement including **PI3K–AKT**, **Ras–MAPK**, **JAK–STAT3**, and focal-adhesion programs (FAK/paxillin), which collectively support proliferation, survival, migration, invasion and metastasis in cancer contexts. (raji2023roleofcsrc pages 3-5)

### 4) Genetics and in vivo function in mouse: src−/− phenotypes (real-world functional evidence)
The most specific, mechanism-informing in vivo evidence for mouse Src function comes from **src-deficient mice**, which develop **osteopetrosis** due to a defect in **osteoclast function**, not osteoclast formation.

Key findings:
- Osteoclasts are present but exhibit a cell-autonomous defect: they **fail to form a normal ruffled border**, a specialized polarized membrane required for bone resorption. (lowell1996knockoutsofsrcfamily pages 3-4, lowell1996knockoutsofsrcfamily pages 4-5)
- Ultrastructural/marker evidence shows wild-type osteoclasts accumulate proton-pump components at the ruffled border, whereas src−/− osteoclasts **do not form a ruffled border and show no proton-pump accumulation**, consistent with failed polarization and resorptive machinery targeting. (amling2000progressiveincreasein pages 6-7)

**Quantitative histomorphometry/statistics (from Amling et al., 2000; publication date Nov 2000; URL: https://doi.org/10.1016/S8756-3282(00)00373-2)**
- **Bone volume fraction (BV/TV)** in src-deficient mice increased with age: **33.61 ± 3.28% (3 weeks), 48.39 ± 8.80% (16 weeks), 57.45 ± 5.68% (24 weeks)**, demonstrating progressive high bone mass consistent with severe osteopetrosis. (amling2000progressiveincreasein pages 3-6)
- Despite impaired resorption, osteoclast metrics were elevated at 3 weeks (examples): **Oc.S/BS 10.49 ± 2.52 vs 2.35 ± 1.13 (WT)** and **N.Oc/B.Pm 3.99 ± 0.85 vs 1.16 ± 0.51 (WT)**—i.e., increased osteoclast number/surface but defective function. (amling2000progressiveincreasein pages 3-6)

### 5) Recent developments and latest research (prioritizing 2023–2024)

#### 5.1 2023: New allosteric model of Src autophosphorylation and C-terminal regulation
**Cuesta-Hernández et al., 2023 (Nature Communications; Oct 2023; URL: https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-023-41890-7)** provided mechanistic and structural evidence that extends the classic two-tyrosine switch model:
- They report that **C-terminal Tyr530 is a bona fide c-Src autophosphorylation site** with **slow, largely intermolecular (trans) kinetics**, while activation-loop **Tyr419** undergoes faster kinetics and acts as a priming/specificity-related switch. (cuestahernandez2023anallostericswitch pages 1-2, cuestahernandez2023anallostericswitch pages 13-14)
- Crystal structural data capture engagement of a **C-terminal palindromic phospho-motif** around Tyr530 with the kinase **G-loop**, rationalizing how the C-terminus can act as an intermolecular substrate element. (cuestahernandez2023anallostericswitch pages 1-2)

**Visual evidence from this study**: the retrieved figure panels illustrate the proposed autophosphorylation sequence/model and a structural snapshot of C-terminal motif engagement (cuestahernandez2023anallostericswitch media bf32de74, cuestahernandez2023anallostericswitch media ede771e1, cuestahernandez2023anallostericswitch media 850c9bfe).

#### 5.2 2024: c-SRC rewires lipid metabolism in glioblastoma via ACSS2/ACLY phosphorylation
**Zhao et al., 2024 (Nature Communications; Aug 2024; URL: https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-024-51444-0)** report that activated c-SRC supports glioblastoma progression by coordinating cytosolic acetyl-CoA and NADPH supply for fatty-acid synthesis:
- c-SRC phosphorylates **ACSS2 (Tyr530, Tyr562)** to stimulate acetate-to-acetyl-CoA conversion in the cytosol, and phosphorylates **ACLY (Tyr682)** to reduce citrate-derived acetyl-CoA and redirect citrate toward **IDH1-dependent NADPH production**. (zhao2024theprotooncogenetyrosine pages 1-2)
- The paper reports physiologic context for acetate availability (plasma acetate **~50–200 µM**) and shows c-SRC activation (pY419) across clinical glioma samples and primary isolates, with inhibitor sensitivity in c-SRC-activated primary glioma cells. (zhao2024theprotooncogenetyrosine pages 1-2, zhao2024theprotooncogenetyrosine pages 2-4)

This work expands Src functional annotation beyond “signaling hub” to include **direct control of metabolic enzyme function via tyrosine phosphorylation** in a disease-relevant setting. (zhao2024theprotooncogenetyrosine pages 1-2)

#### 5.3 2024: Chemoresistant TNBC exhibits SFK upregulation and actionable inhibitor sensitivity
**Egeland et al., 2024 (British Journal of Cancer; Oct 2024; URL: https://doi.org/10.1038/s41416-024-02875-5)** used isogenic paclitaxel-sensitive and -resistant TNBC PDX models and patient cohorts:
- Resistant PDX tumors showed increased activity in **SFK and MAPK/ERK pathways**, and SFK/MEK inhibitors were more potent in resistant explants. (egeland2024thesrcfamilyserves pages 1-2, egeland2024thesrcfamilyserves pages 5-6)
- In metastatic TNBC lesions, **high SFK expression (SRC, FYN and/or YES1)** was associated with substantially shorter **median disease-free interval: 27 months vs 105 months**. (egeland2024thesrcfamilyserves pages 1-2)
- Reported PDXC **IC50 (µM)** values were lower in the resistant model for **saracatinib (4.28 vs 0.79 µM)** and **dasatinib (8.22 vs 1.31 µM)** (values presented for sensitive vs resistant). (egeland2024thesrcfamilyserves pages 6-7)

These data support a real-world implementation: using SFK pathway markers to stratify chemoresistant disease and evaluate SFK inhibitors as next-line strategies. (egeland2024thesrcfamilyserves pages 1-2)

#### 5.4 2024: Saracatinib (AZD0530) as a Src-directed tool compound and repurposing candidate
**Ramos & Vale, 2024 (International Journal of Molecular Sciences; Apr 2024; URL: https://doi.org/10.3390/ijms25084565)** summarize saracatinib pharmacology and repurposing:
- Reported **oral bioavailability >90%** and **half-life ~40 h**, consistent with once-daily dosing. (ramos2024dualdrugrepurposing pages 2-4)
- Phase I **maximum tolerated dose (MTD)** differed by population: **125 mg (Japanese) vs 175 mg (Caucasian)**, with similar PK; adverse events included cytopenias and pulmonary toxicity among others. (ramos2024dualdrugrepurposing pages 2-4)
- The review highlights recent preclinical delivery strategies: nanoparticle saracatinib **10 mg/kg** outperforming free drug **20 mg/kg** in vivo (as summarized), and combination delivery (e.g., saracatinib + capivasertib) as a way to enhance efficacy and/or reduce toxicity. (ramos2024dualdrugrepurposing pages 8-9)

#### 5.5 2024: Stromal targeting via Src inhibition in gastric cancer–activated CAFs
**Choi et al., 2024 (Cell Communication and Signaling; Jan 2024; URL: https://doi.org/10.1186/s12964-023-01396-7)** show that GC-conditioned medium induces a CAF effector program involving **CTGF**, and that SRC-family activity is functionally required:
- CTGF induction was inhibited by **SFK inhibitors** (dasatinib/saracatinib/bosutinib) tested at **500 nM**, and dasatinib effects were detectable over **50–1000 nM** in the described assays. (choi2024characterizationofgastric pages 8-11)
- In one treatment context they used **5 µM dasatinib** (6 h) when comparing multiple pathway inhibitors, and they report that PDGFR/AKT inhibition did not block CTGF induction. (choi2024characterizationofgastric pages 8-11)

### 6) Current applications and real-world implementations

#### 6.1 Mouse genetics as functional validation tools
The **src−/− osteopetrosis phenotype** (ruffled-border failure) remains a gold-standard functional readout linking Src to **polarized vesicle trafficking/cytoskeletal organization in osteoclasts** and thus bone resorption. It is used conceptually to interpret SFK redundancy and osteoclast signaling networks and to validate osteoclast pathway perturbations. (lowell1996knockoutsofsrcfamily pages 3-4, lowell1996knockoutsofsrcfamily pages 4-5)

#### 6.2 Disease models and translational frameworks
- **Chemoresistant TNBC PDX/explant pipelines** are used to identify actionable kinase dependencies; Egeland et al. provide a concrete example where SFK inhibitors show increased potency in resistant tumors, and patient metastasis SFK expression correlates with shorter disease-free interval. (egeland2024thesrcfamilyserves pages 1-2, egeland2024thesrcfamilyserves pages 6-7)
- **Glioblastoma metabolic rewiring** suggests an application space for Src inhibitors as metabolic intervention tools (via ACSS2/ACLY phosphorylation axis). (zhao2024theprotooncogenetyrosine pages 1-2)
- **Tumor microenvironment (CAF) targeting**: SRC/SFK inhibitors reduce CAF CTGF induction and related pro-migratory phenotypes in gastric cancer-conditioned models. (choi2024characterizationofgastric pages 8-11)

#### 6.3 Pharmacologic inhibition in practice
Src-directed inhibitors are widely used as research tools and therapeutic candidates; however, selectivity and polypharmacology are recurring issues due to the conserved kinase domain among SFKs. Saracatinib is explicitly described as binding an inactive kinase conformation and altering the ATP site/hydrophobic pocket, a classical kinase inhibitor mechanism. (ramos2024dualdrugrepurposing pages 2-4)

### 7) Expert synthesis and analysis (authoritative perspectives)

#### 7.1 Mechanistic consensus: modular “Src module” principles
Authoritative Src-module reviews emphasize that Src-family kinases serve as a paradigm for **multi-domain allostery** and **localization-driven specificity**, where SH2/SH3 domains both regulate activity and recruit substrates, thereby defining “when and where” phosphorylation occurs. (shah2018thesrcmodule pages 14-16, shah2018thesrcmodule pages 27-28)

#### 7.2 Emerging consensus: regulation extends beyond the classic two-tyrosine switch
The 2023 structural/mechanistic work indicates the C-terminus is not merely a passive inhibitory element targeted by Csk, but can also behave as an **autophosphorylation substrate element** under specific kinetic regimes, implying richer state spaces for Src regulation and potential new vulnerabilities. (cuestahernandez2023anallostericswitch pages 1-2, cuestahernandez2023anallostericswitch pages 13-14)

#### 7.3 Translational note: SFK signaling as a recurring resistance node
Recent TNBC PDX/patient evidence supports the broader expert view that Src-family signaling is a common resistance-associated node that can be therapeutically targeted—though with practical challenges from inhibitor specificity and context dependency. (egeland2024thesrcfamilyserves pages 1-2, egeland2024thesrcfamilyserves pages 6-7, ramos2024dualdrugrepurposing pages 2-4)

### 9) Key statistics and data (recent and classic; selected)
- **Src−/− mouse osteopetrosis progression**: BV/TV increases to **~57% by 24 weeks**; osteoclast surface/number elevated despite impaired resorption. (amling2000progressiveincreasein pages 3-6)
