Gene Description

Mouse SGLT1 (Sodium/glucose cotransporter 1) is a high-affinity electrogenic Na+-coupled symporter that actively transports D-glucose or D-galactose across the plasma membrane with a 2:1 Na+:sugar stoichiometry. It is pivotal for intestinal mass absorption of D-glucose and D-galactose across the apical brush-border membrane of enterocytes. SGLT1 knockout mice develop glucose-galactose malabsorption (GGM) syndrome and die within 2 days after weaning on standard diet. SGLT1 also triggers glucose-induced secretion of incretins GIP and GLP-1 in enteroendocrine cells, and reabsorbs approximately 3% of filtered glucose in the S3 segment of renal proximal tubules. The transporter also functions as a water channel enabling passive water transport.

Verification of Target Identity and Family Context
- Identity and organism: Slc5a1 encodes sodium/glucose cotransporter 1 (SGLT1), consistent with the UniProt description for Mus musculus (Q8C3K6). SGLT1 is a member of the SLC5 (sodium:solute symporter) family with the characteristic 14‚Äìtransmembrane-helix fold shared by SSS/SSF transporters (PF00474). This places Slc5a1 squarely in the Na/solute symporter superfamily described for intestinal Na+-coupled hexose transport (Koepsell 2020; Gyimesi 2020) (koepsell2020glucosetransportersin pages 1-2, gyimesi2020sodiumcoupledglucosetransport pages 2-4).

1) Key concepts and definitions with current understanding
- Primary function and substrates: SGLT1 mediates electrogenic, Na+-coupled uptake of D-glucose and D-galactose across the apical (brush-border) membrane of absorptive epithelia, driven by the inward Na+ electrochemical gradient established by basolateral Na+/K+-ATPase. SGLT1 does not transport fructose but recognizes it at the binding site (see binding data below) (Gyimesi 2020; Watabe 2024) (gyimesi2020sodiumcoupledglucosetransport pages 2-4, watabe2024sugarbindingof pages 1-2).
- Stoichiometry: Transport is classically 2 Na+ per 1 hexose for SGLT1, which underlies its high affinity and electrogenicity (Gyimesi 2020) (gyimesi2020sodiumcoupledglucosetransport pages 2-4).
- Cellular localization: In small intestine, SGLT1 is constitutively localized to the brush-border membrane (BBM) of enterocytes; GLUT2 mediates basolateral efflux. BBM SGLT1 abundance increases with high luminal glucose (Koepsell 2020) (koepsell2020glucosetransportersin pages 1-2).
- Tissue distribution and physiological roles: Highest expression is in small intestine, with renal expression in the late proximal tubule (S3) where SGLT1 reclaims the fraction of filtered glucose that escapes SGLT2. SGLT1 participates in coupling nutrient uptake to water absorption and contributes to incretin secretion from enteroendocrine cells (Koepsell 2020; Gyimesi 2020; Kaijage 2025 review) (koepsell2020glucosetransportersin pages 1-2, gyimesi2020sodiumcoupledglucosetransport pages 2-4, kaijage2025molecularmechanismsand pages 1-2).

- Structural mechanism by cryo-EM: High-resolution structures now clarify SGLT conformational states and inhibitor binding. A cryo-EM structure of human SGLT1 bound to the high-affinity inhibitor LX2761 shows the inhibitor wedging in the substrate site and extracellular vestibule, locking an outward-open conformation and blocking a putative water pathway.   Mouse SGLT1 (Sodium/glucose cotransporter 1) is a high-affinity electrogenic Na+-coupled
  symporter that actively transports D-glucose or D-galactose across the plasma membrane
  with a 2:1 Na+:sugar stoichiometry. It is pivotal for intestinal mass absorption of
  D-glucose and D-galactose across the apical brush-border membrane of enterocytes.

  within 2 days after weaning on standard diet. SGLT1 also triggers glucose-induced

  3% of filtered glucose in the S3 segment of renal proximal tubules. The transporter also
  functions as a water channel enabling passive water transport.
