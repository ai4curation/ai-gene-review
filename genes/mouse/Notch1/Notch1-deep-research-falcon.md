# Research Report: Functional Annotation of **Mouse Notch1** (UniProt **Q01705**)

## 0) Target verification (mandatory)
The target is **Notch1** from **Mus musculus** (mouse), UniProt accession **Q01705**, described as *Neurogenic locus notch homolog protein 1 (Notch 1)*, a canonical **NOTCH-family** single-pass transmembrane receptor with a large EGF-repeat extracellular domain and an intracellular ankyrin-repeat signaling domain. The domain architecture and activation mechanism described in recent authoritative sources (EGF repeats; NRR with LNR+HD; intracellular RAM/ANK/TAD/PEST; cleavage to NEXT/NICD) match the NOTCH-family/domain expectations given in the user-provided UniProt context, supporting correct gene/protein identification. (sanlidag2025molecularandtissuelevel pages 19-22, bray2025modesofnotch pages 27-33, czerwonka2023modulationofnotch pages 1-3)

## 1) Key concepts and definitions (current understanding)

### 1.1 Notch signaling is juxtacrine and force dependent
Notch signaling is a **cell–cell contact (juxtacrine) pathway** in which a membrane-tethered ligand (DLL/Delta-like or Jagged) on a “sending” cell binds Notch receptors on an adjacent “receiving” cell; ligand endocytosis generates a **pulling force** that opens the receptor’s negative regulatory region (NRR) and enables proteolytic activation. (lv2024evolutionandfunction pages 2-4, bray2025modesofnotch pages 27-33, bray2025modesofnotch media f72360ab)

### 1.2 Protein architecture (functional modules)
Mouse Notch1 is a type I transmembrane receptor whose **extracellular domain** contains **multiple EGF-like repeats** involved in ligand binding and specificity. A membrane-proximal **NRR** (negative regulatory region) composed of **three LNR repeats plus a heterodimerization (HD) domain** protects the receptor from ligand-independent cleavage by masking the S2 site. The **intracellular domain (NICD)** contains a **RAM** region (high-affinity CSL/RBPJ binding), **seven ankyrin repeats (ANK)**, a **transactivation domain (TAD)**, and a C-terminal **PEST** motif that promotes turnover. (sanlidag2025molecularandtissuelevel pages 19-22, czerwonka2023modulationofnotch pages 1-3, sen2023theintricatenotch pages 1-2, bray2025modesofnotch media 3ef0fbcb)

### 1.3 Canonical activation mechanism and key cleavage steps (S1/S2/S3)
Notch1 activation is mediated by sequential proteolysis:
- **S1 cleavage** by a **furin-like convertase** in the Golgi yields a mature heterodimeric receptor that localizes at the plasma membrane. (sanlidag2025molecularandtissuelevel pages 19-22, lv2024evolutionandfunction pages 2-4, wang2024mechanismofnotch pages 3-5)
- **S2 cleavage** is performed by **ADAM metalloproteases (ADAM10/ADAM17)** after ligand-dependent opening of the NRR; this produces the membrane-tethered intermediate called **NEXT** (Notch extracellular truncation). (sanlidag2025molecularandtissuelevel pages 19-22, lv2024evolutionandfunction pages 2-4, wang2024mechanismofnotch pages 3-5, bray2025modesofnotch media f72360ab)
- **S3 cleavage** by **γ-secretase** releases the **NICD**. In mouse Notch1, a key S3-adjacent residue highlighted in recent literature is **V1744** (mouse numbering). (sanlidag2025molecularandtissuelevel pages 19-22, medina2023newtricksfor pages 2-4)

After S3 cleavage, **NICD translocates to the nucleus**, switching Notch from a membrane receptor to a transcriptional effector. (sanlidag2025molecularandtissuelevel pages 19-22, lv2024evolutionandfunction pages 2-4, wang2024mechanismofnotch pages 3-5, bray2025modesofnotch media f72360ab)

### 1.4 Nuclear transcriptional complex and canonical targets
In the nucleus, NICD binds the DNA-binding transcription factor **CSL/RBPJ** and recruits **MAML** (Mastermind-like) to form an activating complex that drives transcription of canonical Notch targets. Canonical target gene families include **Hes** and **Hey** (e.g., **Hes1, Hes5, Hey1**). (sanlidag2025molecularandtissuelevel pages 19-22, lv2024evolutionandfunction pages 2-4, wang2024mechanismofnotch pages 3-5, bray2025modesofnotch pages 27-33)

### 1.5 Subcellular localization summary
Notch1 is synthesized in the **ER**, processed in the **Golgi** (including S1 cleavage), then functions at the **plasma membrane** as a heterodimeric receptor. Upon activation, the NICD relocates to the **nucleus**; in some contexts, S3 cleavage and signaling can involve **endocytic compartments**. (sanlidag2025molecularandtissuelevel pages 19-22, lv2024evolutionandfunction pages 2-4, wang2024mechanismofnotch pages 3-5, feng2024thestructuraland pages 21-25)

## 2) Recent developments and latest research (prioritizing 2023–2024)

### 2.1 Vascular homeostasis: Dll4 loss does not phenocopy Notch1/Rbpj loss (adult endothelium)
A 2023 Nature Cardiovascular Research study systematically compared genetic perturbations across Notch pathway members in adult mouse endothelium and reported that **Dll4 loss** triggers a strong **Myc-driven** transcriptional switch toward proliferation and endothelial “tip-cell” programs, whereas **Notch1 or Rbpj loss** yields a distinct vascular cell state characterized by **MAPK activation (p-ERK)**, **p21 upregulation**, enlarged nuclei, binucleation, and signatures consistent with replicative stress/senescence. Importantly, EC-specific expression of **Notch1 intracellular domain (N1ICD)** could rescue Dll4-loss vascular pathology, and **Myc deletion** largely suppressed the Dll4-driven proliferative/tip-cell transcriptional program but did not fully prevent abnormal dilated vessels and pathology, indicating a separation between transcriptional state and tissue-level phenotype. (Apr 2023; https://doi.org/10.1038/s44161-023-00272-4) (fernandezchacon2023incongruencebetweentranscriptional pages 5-6, fernandezchacon2023incongruencebetweentranscriptional pages 7-8, fernandezchacon2023incongruencebetweentranscriptional pages 4-5)

### 2.2 Brain endothelium/BBB integrity: DLL4–NOTCH1–CLDN5 axis and hyperoxia vulnerability
Ke et al. (Apr 2024) dissected a **DLL4–NOTCH–CLDN5** pathway in developing mouse brain endothelium. They provided mechanistic evidence that **NOTCH1 binds the CLDN5 promoter** at an RBPJ site (ChIP; P=0.014) and that **NICD increases CLDN5 promoter activity** (luciferase; P≈0.0058–0.0059). Pharmacologic **γ-secretase inhibition** (DAPT/DBZ) reduced **CLDN5 mRNA by ~40–45%** and reduced the Notch target **HEY1 by ~70–75%** (P<0.0001), supporting CLDN5 as a Notch-dependent barrier gene in brain endothelium. In vivo neonatal **hyperoxia (85% FiO2, P1–P14)** reduced vascular CD31 density in multiple brain regions and decreased Dll4 expression (sex-stratified p-values reported), linking oxygen stress to suppressed DLL4/Notch signaling and impaired vascular development. (Apr 2024; https://doi.org/10.1113/jp285716) (ke2024deltalike4 pages 14-15, ke2024deltalike4 pages 15-15, ke2024deltalike4 pages 11-12)

### 2.3 CNS neuroinflammation: astrocytic DLL4–NOTCH1 drives IL-6/STAT3 reactivity and BBB dysfunction
Mora et al. (Oct 2024) report that in neuroinflammatory contexts, **astrocytic DLL4 is upregulated** and signals through **NOTCH1** on neighboring astrocytes, driving **IL-6** expression and **STAT3 phosphorylation** (pSTAT3), increasing astrocyte reactivity markers and secretion of pro-permeability factors, thereby destabilizing the BBB and promoting inflammatory infiltration. In mouse EAE and an acute IL-1β lesion model, astrocyte-specific Dll4 inactivation reduced IL-6/pSTAT3 and was associated with reduced BBB disruption and inflammatory infiltration; antibody-mediated DLL4 blockade improved EAE symptoms, highlighting a potentially actionable DLL4–NOTCH1 axis in CNS inflammation. (Oct 2024; https://doi.org/10.1186/s12974-024-03246-w) (mora2024astrocyticdll4notch1signaling pages 9-12, mora2024astrocyticdll4notch1signaling pages 1-2)

### 2.4 Metabolism/cancer cachexia: endothelial Notch1 in adipose tissue as a long-range wasting driver
Taylor et al. (Nature Cancer, Sep 2023) established that tumors can remotely **overactivate Notch1 signaling in distant WAT endothelium** during precachexia; sustained endothelial Notch1 activation was sufficient to induce a **WAT-wasting phenotype** mediated by increased **retinoic acid (RA)** production. In vivo, endothelial Notch activation was linked to elevated **IL-33**, increased ALDH activity in WAT myeloid populations, and transcriptional induction of RA targets (including **Igfbp3**). A key translational finding was that downstream targeting of RA signaling with the pan-RAR antagonist **BMS493** (oral; 5 mg/kg every other day) in a KPC tumor-bearing cachexia model **prevented WAT loss and adipocyte size changes** without reducing tumor mass (reported n=5–6 per group). (Sep 2023; https://doi.org/10.1038/s43018-023-00622-y) (taylor2023endothelialnotch1signaling pages 10-11, taylor2023endothelialnotch1signaling pages 1-2, taylor2023endothelialnotch1signaling pages 11-12, taylor2023endothelialnotch1signaling pages 5-6)

### 2.5 Immune function in vivo: Notch receptor signaling in CD4+ T cells during tissue repair
Melvin et al. (Aug 2024, JCI Insight) linked Notch receptor signaling to **CD4+ T cell differentiation** during wound repair in mouse models, with epigenetic regulation by MLL1 increasing Notch receptor expression and Notch signaling. They reported that loss of upstream Notch pathway mediators or receptors in CD4+ T cells resulted in loss of Th differentiation in wounds, and that in diabetes, Notch signaling is upregulated in wound CD4+ T cells promoting Th17 skewing; MLL1 inhibition reduced Th17 cells and IL-17A. (Aug 2024; https://doi.org/10.1172/jci.insight.179012) (melvin2024thehistonemethyltransferase pages 1-2)

## 3) Functional annotation: primary molecular function, pathways, localization

### 3.1 Primary molecular function (signaling role)
Notch1 is not an enzyme or transporter; its **primary function** is as a **mechanosensitive receptor** that converts extracellular ligand binding into **nuclear transcriptional regulation** by releasing NICD, which then reprograms gene expression via CSL/RBPJ and MAML. (lv2024evolutionandfunction pages 2-4, wang2024mechanismofnotch pages 3-5, bray2025modesofnotch pages 27-33)

### 3.2 Pathway placement and core steps (mouse Notch1)
A concise pathway view:
1) Membrane Notch1 binds a neighboring ligand (DLL4/Jagged). (bray2025modesofnotch pages 27-33, bray2025modesofnotch media f72360ab)
2) Mechanical activation opens NRR → ADAM10/17 S2 cleavage → **NEXT**. (sanlidag2025molecularandtissuelevel pages 19-22, lv2024evolutionandfunction pages 2-4, bray2025modesofnotch media f72360ab)
3) γ-secretase S3 cleavage (mouse residue context includes **V1744**) → **NICD** release. (sanlidag2025molecularandtissuelevel pages 19-22, medina2023newtricksfor pages 2-4)
4) NICD nuclear entry and assembly with **CSL/RBPJ + MAML** → transcription of targets such as **Hes/Hey**. (sanlidag2025molecularandtissuelevel pages 19-22, lv2024evolutionandfunction pages 2-4, wang2024mechanismofnotch pages 3-5)

A visual schematic of these steps and the domain architecture is available in the retrieved figure crops. (bray2025modesofnotch media f72360ab, bray2025modesofnotch media 3ef0fbcb)

### 3.3 Protein turnover and signal termination
The **PEST** domain promotes NICD degradation (e.g., phosphorylation/ubiquitylation pathways including FBXW7-mediated turnover), providing temporal control of Notch1 signaling. (sanlidag2025molecularandtissuelevel pages 19-22, lv2024evolutionandfunction pages 2-4, wang2024mechanismofnotch pages 3-5)

## 4) Current applications and real-world implementations

### 4.1 Therapeutic modulation strategies (expert review synthesis)
Recent reviews emphasize that Notch can be modulated at multiple points: receptor/ligand interactions, ADAM cleavage, γ-secretase cleavage, receptor trafficking/glycosylation, and nuclear transcription complex formation. (Sep 2023; https://doi.org/10.3390/cancers15184563) (czerwonka2023modulationofnotch pages 1-3)
A 2023 Trends in Pharmacological Sciences review highlights emerging Notch-based biotechnologies and therapeutics, including structural insights into proteolysis and strategies to target the **NICD–CSL–MAML** complex with inhibitory compounds (e.g., CB-103 and others mentioned), illustrating the push toward more selective pathway control beyond pan–γ-secretase blockade. (Dec 2023; https://doi.org/10.1016/j.tips.2023.09.011) (medina2023newtricksfor pages 2-4)

### 4.2 Receptor-selective targeting: anti-Notch1 antibody in melanoma models (2024)
De Freitas et al. (Nov 2024) developed a **selective anti-Notch1 neutralizing antibody** that reduced cleaved Notch1 (NICD) in a dose-dependent manner and showed strong direct anti-melanoma effects.
- In vitro, 72 h treatment caused **~75%** (K457), **~80%** (YUMM1.7), and **~55%** (YUMM2.1) melanoma cell death at 25 μg/ml; multiple other human lines showed substantial killing, and 3D spheroids showed ~4-fold shifts toward cell death. (freitas2024notch1blockadeby pages 7-10, freitas2024notch1blockadeby pages 5-7)
- In vivo (syngeneic YUMM2.1), dosing was **10 mg/kg i.p. every other day**; anti-Notch1 delayed tumor growth and reshaped the tumor immune microenvironment (reduced MDSCs and Tregs; increased CD8+ T cells). Combination therapy with anti-PD-1 produced sustained separation in tumor growth by day 22. (Nov 2024; https://doi.org/10.1186/s13046-024-03214-5) (freitas2024notch1blockadeby pages 7-10, freitas2024notch1blockadeby pages 10-12)
This study provides a concrete example of receptor-specific pathway targeting intended to mitigate the toxicity of broader Notch inhibition while maintaining anti-tumor activity. (freitas2024notch1blockadeby pages 7-10, freitas2024notch1blockadeby pages 5-7)

### 4.3 Targeting downstream effector pathways: RA blockade in cachexia (2023)
Taylor et al. show a “downstream” approach: pharmacologic blockade of RA signaling (BMS493) prevented WAT loss in tumor-bearing mice without affecting tumor mass, demonstrating how mechanistic functional annotation can guide selection of intervention points with potentially improved tolerability. (Sep 2023; https://doi.org/10.1038/s43018-023-00622-y) (taylor2023endothelialnotch1signaling pages 10-11)

## 6) Expert interpretation and synthesis (authoritative perspective)

### 6.1 Why Notch1 is “context-dependent” yet mechanistically precise
A recurring expert conclusion is that the core Notch mechanism (force-dependent proteolysis → NICD → CSL/MAML transcription) is highly conserved and molecularly well-defined, but biological outcomes depend on (i) which ligand is presented (DLL vs Jagged), (ii) receptor post-translational processing/trafficking and NRR conformational control, and (iii) the chromatin/transcriptional environment of a given cell type. (lv2024evolutionandfunction pages 2-4, wang2024mechanismofnotch pages 3-5, bray2025modesofnotch pages 27-33)

### 6.2 A 2023–2024 refinement: ligand vs receptor perturbations are not interchangeable in adult vessels
The 2023 adult vascular genetics study highlights that inhibiting a ligand (Dll4) can push endothelium into a **Myc-driven proliferative program**, whereas Notch1/Rbpj loss can yield a **MAPK/p21/senescence-like** phenotype. This strengthens the interpretation that in vivo Notch pathway “nodes” are not equivalent intervention points; receptor- vs ligand- vs downstream targeting can yield meaningfully different outcomes. (fernandezchacon2023incongruencebetweentranscriptional pages 5-6, fernandezchacon2023incongruencebetweentranscriptional pages 7-8)
