CC   -!- FUNCTION: The small GTPases Rab are key regulators of intracellular
CC       membrane trafficking, from the formation of transport vesicles to their
CC       fusion with membranes. Rabs cycle between an inactive GDP-bound form
CC       and an active GTP-bound form that is able to recruit to membranes
CC       different sets of downstream effectors directly responsible for vesicle
CC       formation, movement, tethering and fusion. In its active state, RAB7A
CC       binds to a variety of effector proteins playing a key role in the
CC       regulation of endo-lysosomal trafficking. Governs early-to-late
CC       endosomal maturation, microtubule minus-end as well as plus-end
CC       directed endosomal migration and positioning, and endosome-lysosome
CC       transport through different protein-protein interaction cascades. Also
CC       plays a central role in growth-factor-mediated cell signaling,
CC       nutrient-transporter-mediated nutrient uptake, neurotrophin transport
CC       in the axons of neurons and lipid metabolism. Also involved in
CC       regulation of some specialized endosomal membrane trafficking, such as
CC       maturation of melanosomes, pathogen-induced phagosomes (or vacuoles)
CC       and autophagosomes. Plays a role in the maturation and acidification of
CC       phagosomes that engulf pathogens, such as S.aureus and Mycobacteria.
CC       Plays a role in the fusion of phagosomes with lysosomes. In concert
CC       with RAC1, plays a role in regulating the formation of RBs (ruffled
CC       borders) in osteoclasts. Controls the endosomal trafficking and neurite
CC       outgrowth signaling of NTRK1/TRKA. Regulates the endocytic trafficking
CC       of the EGF-EGFR complex by regulating its lysosomal degradation (By
CC       similarity). Involved in the ADRB2-stimulated lipolysis through
CC       lipophagy, a cytosolic lipase-independent autophagic pathway
CC       (PubMed:23708524). Required for the exosomal release of SDCBP, CD63 and
CC       syndecan (By similarity). Required for vesicular trafficking and cell
CC       surface expression of ACE2 (By similarity). May play a role in PRPH
CC       neuronal intermediate filament assembly (PubMed:23179371).
