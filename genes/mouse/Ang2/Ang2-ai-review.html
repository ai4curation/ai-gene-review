Gene Description

Ang2 encodes mouse angiogenin-related protein (Angrp), a secreted RNase A family paralog of canonical mouse/human angiogenin. The directly supported function is RNA/tRNA cleavage activity. In contrast, a systematic trace of the local ISO block shows that all 46 ISO annotations were transferred from human ANG (UniProtKB:P03950) via GO_REF:0000119, and most of the transferred angiogenic, signaling, stress-response, and immune terms overstate the biology of the divergent non-angiogenic mouse paralog.

Research Report: Functional Annotation of mouse Ang2 / Angrp (UniProt Q64438) — angiogenin-2 (angiogenin-related protein)

1) Target verification (critical disambiguation)

Ang2 is an ambiguous symbol in the literature because “Ang-2” frequently denotes angiopoietin-2 (ANGPT2) in vascular biology. The UniProt target provided (Q64438) is instead a murine angiogenin paralog, commonly referred to as mouse angiogenin-2 (mAng2) or angiogenin-related protein (Angrp), and is part of the pancreatic RNase A superfamily (angiogenin branch). Mouse angiogenins comprise a multi-gene paralog set (variously summarized as 5–6 genes across reviews, often on chromosome 14), explicitly including mAng2/Angrp. (sultana2022humanandmouse pages 5-6, sultana2022humanandmouse pages 1-2, sheng2016threedecadesof pages 1-2, adams2004theangiogeninsan pages 6-8, holloway2001highlevelexpressionof pages 1-2)

2) Key concepts and definitions (current understanding)

2.1 Angiogenins as RNase A superfamily members

Angiogenins are secreted vertebrate-specific ribonucleases within the pancreatic RNase A superfamily. They are typically synthesized as preproteins with an N-terminal signal peptide, then processed to a mature ~120 aa, disulfide-rich RNase-fold protein. (lyons2017rnabiologyof pages 1-3, holloway2001highlevelexpressionof pages 1-2)

Angiogenin-family RNases are unusual in that their “classic” RNase activity on small substrates is low, yet they have potent biological effects, including RNA cleavage events (notably tRNA cleavage) and cell signaling activities in some contexts. (garnett2021emergingbiologicalfunctions pages 6-8, lyons2017rnabiologyof pages 4-6)

2.2 Catalyzed reaction and substrate specificity

For angiogenins generally, the enzymatic function is endoribonucleolytic cleavage of RNA, with reported preference for cleavage on the 3′ side of pyrimidines (yielding cyclic phosphate intermediates), and activity against tRNA (and in some studies rRNA). (adams2004theangiogeninsan pages 2-3)

For mouse Ang2/Angrp (Q64438) specifically, multiple sources support that it is an active RNase, with notable activity toward tRNA and dinucleotide substrates (e.g., CpA, CpG). (holloway2001highlevelexpressionof pages 1-2, fu1999mangiogenin3atarget pages 5-7)

3) Gene-/protein-specific functional evidence for Ang2/Angrp (mAng2)

3.1 Biochemical activity (direct evidence)

Ang2/Angrp is enzymatically active as a ribonuclease. Recombinant mAngrp cleaves tRNA test substrates in vitro. (holloway2001highlevelexpressionof pages 1-2)

A comparative enzymology table (values attributed to Nobile et al., 1996, as reported in Fu et al., 1999) gives relative activities for mAngrp: tRNA activity 90.0 (with hANG set to 100), and dinucleotide cleavage values 48.5 (CpA) and 12.3 (CpG). These data support robust catalytic activity (at least relative to other angiogenin-family members) on these substrates. (fu1999mangiogenin3atarget pages 5-7)

Across reviews, Ang2/Angrp is described as having greater RNase activity toward tRNA and dinucleotide substrates than mouse angiogenin (mAng1), despite being weak/non-angiogenic. (adams2004theangiogeninsan pages 6-8, sultana2022humanandmouse pages 5-6)

3.2 Angiogenic activity (direct evidence: negative)

A defining functional divergence of Ang2/Angrp is that it is non-angiogenic (or far less angiogenic than canonical angiogenin) in standard assays.

  • In a chick chorioallantoic membrane (CAM) assay, mAngrp showed no angiogenic activity even at 100 ng, whereas other angiogenins (hANG, mAng1, mAng-3) were angiogenic at substantially lower doses (down to ~1–2 ng for hANG and mAng1). (holloway2001highlevelexpressionof pages 1-2)
  • Reviews likewise summarize CAM evidence that mAng2/Angrp is non-angiogenic, with a proposed explanation that substitutions at key cell-binding residues impair receptor/cell interaction. (sultana2022humanandmouse pages 5-6)

Mechanistically, Fu et al. summarize residue differences enriched in non-angiogenic Angrp and report mutational tests (e.g., combined mutations including Lys60→Asn and Arg66→Glu) that did not restore angiogenic activity, consistent with impaired cell binding and/or additional determinants beyond those substitutions. (fu1999mangiogenin3atarget pages 11-12)

3.3 Localization and cellular compartment of action (direct vs inferred)

Direct Ang2/Angrp-specific localization evidence was not retrieved in this run. However, as an angiogenin-family member, Ang2/Angrp is expected to be secreted (signal peptide) and potentially internalized by cells.

For angiogenin-family proteins in general:

  • ANG is secreted and can be taken up by cells via receptor-mediated endocytosis or other routes (the receptor identity can vary by context; syndecan-4 and other proteins have been proposed). (sarangdhar2021angiogenin(ang)—ribonucleaseinhibitor pages 2-3, lyons2017rnabiologyof pages 1-3)
  • ANG can localize to the nucleus/nucleolus, where it promotes rRNA transcription and growth-associated ribosome biogenesis programs, in part through RNA cleavage events (e.g., pRNA cleavage) and rDNA promoter regulation. (sarangdhar2021angiogenin(ang)—ribonucleaseinhibitor pages 2-3, garnett2021emergingbiologicalfunctions pages 8-9, lyons2017rnabiologyof pages 4-6)
  • Under stress, angiogenin can accumulate in the cytosol and cleave tRNAs to generate tRNA-derived stress-induced fragments (tiRNAs), which can inhibit translation initiation and promote stress granule formation (e.g., 5′-tiRNAs with oligo-G motifs interacting with YB-1). (sheng2016threedecadesof pages 4-5, lyons2017rnabiologyof pages 4-6)

For Ang2/Angrp, tRNA cleavage is direct evidence, but nuclear/nucleolar roles and tiRNA-mediated translation regulation should be treated as family-based hypotheses unless demonstrated directly for mAng2/Angrp. (holloway2001highlevelexpressionof pages 1-2, lyons2017rnabiologyof pages 4-6)

3.4 Regulation and pathway context (RNH1/RI system)

No Ang2-specific RNH1-binding constant was retrieved. For angiogenin-family proteins broadly, the cytosolic ribonuclease inhibitor (RI/RNH1) binds angiogenin with extremely high affinity (reported Kd ~1 fM) and suppresses RNase activity. (sarangdhar2021angiogenin(ang)—ribonucleaseinhibitor pages 2-3, garnett2021emergingbiologicalfunctions pages 6-8)

This ANG–RNH1 system is positioned as a regulator of translation and ribosome biogenesis: ANG can promote rRNA transcription in the nucleus under homeostasis and generate tRNA fragments under stress; RNH1 inhibits ANG RNase activity and is essential in mouse development (RNH1 knockout embryonic lethal). (sarangdhar2021angiogenin(ang)—ribonucleaseinhibitor pages 2-3)

Given Ang2/Angrp’s strong RNase activity toward tRNA, its physiological impact may depend strongly on RI/RNH1 availability and compartmentalization, but this remains to be tested directly for Q64438. (sarangdhar2021angiogenin(ang)—ribonucleaseinhibitor pages 2-3)

4) Expression and biological roles

4.1 Genomic and evolutionary context (mouse-specific paralog expansion)

Mouse differs from humans in having multiple angiogenin paralogs rather than a single ANG gene. This paralog expansion complicates phenotype interpretation because paralogs can partially compensate for one another. (kulka2009humanmastcells pages 1-2, lyons2017rnabiologyof pages 1-3, sarangdhar2021angiogenin(ang)—ribonucleaseinhibitor pages 2-3)

Ang2/Angrp shares ~78% sequence identity with mAng1 but appears functionally diverged—especially in angiogenic capacity—likely reflecting evolutionary subfunctionalization/neofunctionalization among murine paralogs. (sultana2022humanandmouse pages 5-6, fu1999mangiogenin3atarget pages 1-2)

4.2 Tissue/cell-type expression

This run did not retrieve a modern Ang2/Angrp-specific expression atlas or single-cell dataset. Older and review literature indicates different paralogs have distinct tissue roles (e.g., intestinal Ang4 from Paneth/goblet cells), but Ang2/Angrp expression patterns remain insufficiently evidenced here. (sultana2022humanandmouse pages 5-6)

5) Recent developments (prioritize 2023–2024) and how they relate to Ang2

Key limitation: no 2023–2024 primary studies explicitly centered on mouse Ang2/Angrp were retrieved here.

Nevertheless, recent murine angiogenin-paralog work provides updated context for angiogenin-family functions in vivo:

  • Hu et al., Mar 2023 (PLOS ONE) used an AOM-DSS colitis-associated cancer model comparing WT vs Ang1 knockout mice, showing Ang1 deletion worsened colitis but reduced tumor formation. Quantitatively, WT developed 134 tumors (4.6 tumors/mouse) vs Ang1-KO 46 tumors (1.5 tumors/mouse), and Ang1-KO tumor-bearing mice showed a 34-fold decrease in Ang4 relative to WT (with Ang1 absent). These results reinforce the concept that murine angiogenins can couple mucosal inflammation, antimicrobial activity, microbiome composition, and cancer risk, but they do not directly address Ang2/Angrp. 6.1 Applications (largely for ANG/RNase5 rather than Ang2/Angrp)

Most translational applications in the retrieved literature focus on human angiogenin (ANG/RNase5) rather than mouse Ang2/Angrp.

  • Cancer biomarker / therapy stratification: A high-impact study reports ANG as an EGFR ligand and a serum biomarker for erlotinib sensitivity in pancreatic ductal adenocarcinoma, suggesting clinical stratification and therapeutic targeting of the ANG–EGFR axis. (Wang et al., Apr 2018, URL: https://doi.org/10.1016/j.ccell.2018.02.012) (wang2018angiogeninribonuclease5is pages 1-4, wang2018angiogeninribonuclease5is pages 8-9)

6.2 Implications for Ang2/Angrp applications

Ang2/Angrp is non-angiogenic in CAM assays and is described as undetectable in human serum in a review, arguing against direct translation of pro-angiogenic/blood biomarker roles from ANG to Ang2/Angrp. (holloway2001highlevelexpressionof pages 1-2, adams2004theangiogeninsan pages 6-8)

Given its strong RNase activity (tRNA/dinucleotide substrates) but poor angiogenic activity, Ang2/Angrp’s more plausible application space is as a model RNase paralog for studying how angiogenin-family proteins can decouple RNA catalysis from angiogenesis/cell-binding determinants, and potentially for probing host-defense RNase biology in mouse systems. (holloway2001highlevelexpressionof pages 1-2, fu1999mangiogenin3atarget pages 5-7, fu1999mangiogenin3atarget pages 11-12)

7) Expert synthesis and interpretive analysis (authoritative perspectives)

Authoritative reviews converge on a working model where angiogenins integrate extracellular secretion and uptake with intracellular RNA biology.

  • Under growth conditions, angiogenin-family proteins can accumulate in the nucleolus and promote rRNA transcription, thereby supporting proliferative capacity. (sarangdhar2021angiogenin(ang)—ribonucleaseinhibitor pages 2-3, lyons2017rnabiologyof pages 4-6)
  • Under stress, angiogenin can shift to the cytosol and generate tiRNAs that suppress global translation and promote stress granules. (sheng2016threedecadesof pages 4-5, lyons2017rnabiologyof pages 4-6)
  • RI/RNH1 is a central brake on angiogenin RNase activity and therefore a plausible determinant of when/where enzymatic activity is permitted. (sarangdhar2021angiogenin(ang)—ribonucleaseinhibitor pages 2-3, garnett2021emergingbiologicalfunctions pages 6-8)

Within this framework, mouse Ang2/Angrp stands out as a paralog with preserved or enhanced RNase activity but lost angiogenic potential, likely due to alterations in cell-binding/internalization determinants (e.g., residues around positions ~60–70 implicated as receptor/cell-binding in angiogenin-family proteins). (sultana2022humanandmouse pages 5-6, fu1999mangiogenin3atarget pages 11-12)

Ang2 ISO Source Trace Results

Command used:

python3 genes/mouse/Ang2/Ang2-bioinformatics/trace_iso_sources.py

Summary

  • The local Ang2-goa.tsv file contains 46 ISO annotations for mouse Ang2.
  • Every one of those 46 ISO rows is transferred through GO_REF:0000119 from the same source entity, UniProtKB:P03950 (human angiogenin/ANG).
  • The current QuickGO trace divides those source terms into three buckets:
  • 34 with current direct experimental support on human ANG.
  • 4 with only inferred support on human ANG (GO:0019731, GO:0045087, GO:0050830, GO:0061844).
  • 8 with no current matching human ANG source term (GO:0001878, GO:0002181, GO:0006898, GO:0005694, GO:0015629, GO:0019732, GO:0033554, GO:0036416).
  • 9 ISO terms overlap a non-ISO mouse Ang2 annotation for the same GO term in the local GOA export, so those transfers are redundant even before biological review.

Biological Interpretation

  • Mouse Ang2 is Angrp / angiogenin-related protein, a paralog of canonical mouse/human angiogenin rather than a simple one-to-one functional equivalent. The original biochemical characterization found that mouse Ang is angiogenic but Angrp is not, while Angrp still has appreciable ribonucleolytic activity toward tRNA and related substrates PMID:8633065.
  • The later structural study reinforced paralog divergence and showed that mAng-2 is almost 80% active compared to the human form in comparative tRNA cleavage assays, while also noting that mAng-2 is not angiogenic among the murine paralogues [PMID:23170778 "mAng-2 is almost 80% active compared to the human form" ; "Experiments have shown that mAng, mAng-3 and mAng-4 have angiogenic activities comparable to that of hAng, whereas mAng-2 is not angiogenic"].
  • Because of that divergence, even ISO terms with real experimental support on human ANG are unsafe to accept automatically for mouse Ang2 when they concern angiogenesis, receptor binding, signaling, stress-granule biology, nuclear trafficking, or immune effector roles.

Review Implications

  • GO:0004540 and GO:0004549 are the main ISO terms that remain biologically plausible for Ang2, because mouse Ang2 has direct biochemical evidence for RNase/tRNA cleavage activity PMID:8633065 PMID:23170778.
  • Immune/antimicrobial ISO terms are particularly weak because their current human ANG source is itself inferred rather than experimental.
  • ISO terms with no current human source term are best treated as stale transfers.
  • The non-ISO experimental block in the local GOA file also contains clear homonym errors:
  • PMID:17670746 is about angiopoietin-2, not angiogenin-2 PMID:17670746.
  • PMID:25313067 and PMID:28096417 are about angiotensin II (AngII) signaling and WNK4, not Ang2/angiogenin-related protein PMID:25313067 PMID:28096417.
  • PMID:29065170 is a ZNF418 cardiac hypertrophy paper that uses Ang II as a stimulus, not an Ang2 gene study PMID:29065170.
