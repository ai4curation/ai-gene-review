CC   -!- FUNCTION: Thiol protease that acts as a major effector caspase involved
CC       in the execution phase of apoptosis (PubMed:16469926, PubMed:8934524).
CC       Following cleavage and activation by initiator caspases (CASP8, CASP9
CC       and/or CASP10), mediates execution of apoptosis by catalyzing cleavage
CC       of many proteins (PubMed:16469926, PubMed:8934524). At the onset of
CC       apoptosis, it proteolytically cleaves poly(ADP-ribose) polymerase PARP1
CC       at a '216-Asp-|-Gly-217' bond. Cleaves and activates sterol regulatory
CC       element binding proteins (SREBPs) between the basic helix-loop-helix
CC       leucine zipper domain and the membrane attachment domain. Cleaves and
CC       activates caspase-6, -7 and -9 (CASP6, CASP7 and CASP9, respectively).
CC       Cleaves and inactivates interleukin-18 (IL18) (By similarity). Triggers
CC       cell adhesion in sympathetic neurons through RET cleavage (By
CC       similarity). Cleaves DSG2 in response to apoptosis resulting in a loss
CC       of full length DSG2 at desmosome cell junctions and subsequent loss of
CC       cell-cell adhesion (By similarity). Also cleaves JUP in response to
CC       apoptosis (By similarity). Cleaves IL-1 beta between an Asp and an Ala,
CC       releasing the mature cytokine which is involved in a variety of
CC       inflammatory processes (By similarity). Cleaves and inhibits
CC       serine/threonine-protein kinase AKT1 in response to oxidative stress
CC       (PubMed:12124386). Acts as an inhibitor of type I interferon production
CC       during virus-induced apoptosis by mediating cleavage of antiviral
CC       proteins CGAS, IRF3 and MAVS, thereby preventing cytokine
CC       overproduction (PubMed:30878284). Also involved in pyroptosis by
CC       mediating cleavage and activation of gasdermin-E (GSDME) (By
CC       similarity). Cleaves XRCC4 and phospholipid scramblase proteins XKR4,
CC       XKR8 and XKR9, leading to promote phosphatidylserine exposure on
CC       apoptotic cell surface (PubMed:25231987, PubMed:33725486). Cleaves
CC       BIRC6 following inhibition of BIRC6-caspase binding by DIABLO/SMAC (By
CC       similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P42574,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q60431, ECO:0000269|PubMed:12124386,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:16469926, ECO:0000269|PubMed:25231987,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:30878284, ECO:0000269|PubMed:33725486,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:8934524}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY:
CC       Reaction=Strict requirement for an Asp residue at positions P1 and P4.
CC         It has a preferred cleavage sequence of Asp-Xaa-Xaa-Asp-|- with a
CC         hydrophobic amino-acid residue at P2 and a hydrophilic amino-acid
CC         residue at P3, although Val or Ala are also accepted at this
CC         position.; EC=3.4.22.56; Evidence={ECO:0000269|PubMed:12124386,
CC         ECO:0000269|PubMed:25231987, ECO:0000269|PubMed:33725486};
CC   -!- ACTIVITY REGULATION: Inhibited by BIRC6; following inhibition of BIRC6-
CC       caspase binding by DIABLO/SMAC, BIRC6 is subjected to caspase cleavage,
CC       leading to an increase in active caspases.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P42574}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Heterotetramer that consists of two anti-parallel arranged
CC       heterodimers, each one formed by a 17 kDa (p17) and a 12 kDa (p12)
CC       subunit. Interacts with BIRC6/bruce. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P42574}.

CC       and heart (PubMed:9038361). Lower expression in brain, skeletal muscle
CC       and testis (PubMed:9038361). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:9038361}.
CC   -!- PTM: Cleavage by granzyme B, caspase-6, caspase-8 and caspase-10
CC       generates the two active subunits. Additional processing of the
CC       propeptides is likely due to the autocatalytic activity of the
CC       activated protease. Active heterodimers between the small subunit of
CC       caspase-7 protease and the large subunit of caspase-3 also occur and
CC       vice versa. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P42574}.
CC   -!- PTM: S-nitrosylated on its catalytic site cysteine in unstimulated cell
CC       lines and denitrosylated upon activation of the Fas apoptotic pathway,
CC       associated with an increase in intracellular caspase activity. Fas
CC       therefore activates caspase-3 not only by inducing the cleavage of the
CC       caspase zymogen to its active subunits, but also by stimulating the
CC       denitrosylation of its active site thiol.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P42574}.
CC   -!- PTM: Ubiquitinated by BIRC6; this activity is inhibited by DIABLO/SMAC.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P42574}.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: Lethality; the majority of mice on the 129/Svj
CC       background die in utero or within weeks of birth because of many extra
CC       cells within the brains of these animals (PubMed:8934524). Defects are
CC       caused by impaired apoptosis (PubMed:8934524). Mice lacking Casp3 on
CC       the C57BL/6J background are viable (PubMed:16469926). Mice lacking
CC       Casp3 and Casp7 on the C57BL/6J background die immediately after birth
CC       because of defective heart development (PubMed:16469926). This suggests
CC       that Casp7 can partially rescue Casp3 in certain conditions
CC       (PubMed:16469926). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:16469926,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:8934524}.
