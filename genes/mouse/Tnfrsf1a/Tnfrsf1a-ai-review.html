Gene Description

Tnfrsf1a encodes mouse TNF receptor 1 (p55/CD120a), a type I transmembrane TNF receptor with extracellular cysteine-rich ligand-binding domains and an intracellular death domain. TNFR1 binds TNF/lymphotoxin-alpha and initiates TNF-mediated signaling, especially canonical NF-kappaB/inflammatory signaling and extrinsic apoptotic signaling.

Core Functions

TNFRSF1A/TNFR1 binds TNF-family ligands at the cell surface and nucleates receptor signaling complexes that activate canonical NF-kappaB and inflammatory gene programs.

Directly Involved In:

1. Key concepts and definitions (current understanding)

1.1 What Tnfrsf1a encodes

Tnfrsf1a encodes TNFR1, a broadly expressed cell-surface receptor for tumor necrosis factor (TNF), central to inflammatory signaling and regulated cell death decisions. (shi2023tnfr1andtnfr2 pages 1-2, preedy2024cellularheterogeneityin pages 1-2)

1.2 Structural organization and domain-function relationships

TNFR1 is described as a ~55 kDa type I transmembrane receptor with:
- Extracellular region containing four cysteine-rich domains (CRD1–4) (TNFR-family ligand binding architecture)
- A single-pass transmembrane helix
- An intracellular region with a C-terminal death domain (DD) enabling DD-mediated adaptor recruitment (e.g., TRADD) and assembly of downstream signaling complexes. (li2025exploringtnfr1from pages 2-4, yuk2024tnfinhuman pages 2-4, shi2023tnfr1andtnfr2 pages 1-2)

Mechanistic structural notes highlighted in a translational review include: a pre-ligand assembly domain (PLAD; aa 1–54) supporting receptor pre-assembly; mapped ligand-contact residues in CRD2/CRD3; and the DD serving as the key signaling hub for TRADD/RIPK1 recruitment. (li2025exploringtnfr1from pages 2-4)

1.3 Cellular localization and trafficking concepts

TNFR1 initiates signaling at the plasma membrane following trimeric TNF engagement. Signaling outcomes depend on the assembly of receptor-proximal vs cytosolic complexes, and receptor internalization is a recurring regulatory theme in TNFR1 cytotoxic signaling models. (preedy2024cellularheterogeneityin pages 2-3, li2025exploringtnfr1from pages 6-8)

1.4 Canonical TNFR1 signaling logic: Complex I vs Complex II

A core modern concept is that TNFR1 drives divergent outcomes—pro-survival/pro-inflammatory transcription vs apoptosis/necroptosis—depending on the composition and post-translational regulation (especially ubiquitination) of TNFR1-associated signaling complexes.

Complex I (membrane; pro-survival/inflammatory)
After TNF binds TNFR1, the receptor DD recruits TRADD and RIPK1, which together recruit TRAF2/5 and E3 ligases cIAP1/2 to build Complex I. cIAP-mediated K63 ubiquitination of RIPK1 and recruitment of LUBAC (linear/M1 ubiquitin ligase complex) promote M1/hybrid ubiquitin scaffolds that recruit TAK1/TABs and the IKK complex (via NEMO), activating NF-κB and MAPK pathways. (preedy2024cellularheterogeneityin pages 2-3, li2025exploringtnfr1from pages 6-8, yuk2024tnfinhuman pages 2-4, xiao2025theroleof pages 2-4)

Complex II (cytosolic; cytotoxic checkpoints)
When ubiquitin-dependent checkpoints fail or are rewired, TNFR1 signaling can shift to cytosolic Complex II containing FADD and caspase-8, triggering extrinsic apoptosis (often parsed into Complex IIa/IIb variants). When caspase-8 activity is absent/inhibited, RIPK1 engages RIPK3 to form a necroptotic complex (necrosome), activating MLKL to execute necroptosis. (li2025exploringtnfr1from pages 6-8, yuk2024tnfinhuman pages 2-4, xiao2025theroleof pages 2-4)

A schematic overview of these pathways and their regulatory nodes (including Complex I→NF-κB and Complex II→apoptosis/necroptosis) is shown in a figure extracted from a 2024 review. (preedy2024cellularheterogeneityin media 542df94c)

2. Recent developments and latest research (prioritizing 2023–2024)

2.1 2024: TBK1 checkpointing of TNFR1-driven RIPK1-dependent cell death (TANK/AZI2 adapters)

A 2024 Nature Communications study reported that the TBK1 adaptors TANK and AZI2 cooperatively protect mice from TNF/TNFR1-driven pathology by sustaining TBK1 activation at the TNFR1 signaling complex. Double deficiency (Tank/Azi2 DKO) produced severe multi-organ inflammation and early mortality, and the phenotype was rescued by TNFR1 deficiency and improved by kinase-dead RIPK1, supporting a TNFR1→RIPK1 kinase axis as causal. Mechanistically, TBK1 restrains RIPK1 autophosphorylation (Ser166) and downstream caspase-8 activation in response to TNF. (ujevic2024tbk1associatedadapterstank pages 1-2, ujevic2024tbk1associatedadapterstank pages 2-3)

Interpretation for functional annotation: These data reinforce that TNFR1 signaling is not a simple binary switch but contains multiple parallel checkpoints (NF-κB transcriptional survival programs and kinase checkpoints like TBK1) that determine whether TNFR1 signaling terminates in inflammation/survival vs RIPK1-dependent death. (ujevic2024tbk1associatedadapterstank pages 2-3)

2.2 2023: LC3-independent selective autophagy as a TNFR1 complex II “garbage disposal” pathway

A 2023 Autophagy paper emphasized that an unconventional LC3-independent selective autophagy pathway degrades cytotoxic TNFR1 complex II to prevent TNF cytotoxicity. Genetic screening implicated ATG9A and RB1CC1/FIP200 (initiation machinery), and mechanistically the selective autophagy receptor TAX1BP1 recognizes M1 ubiquitin on complex II (deposited by LUBAC) and recruits RB1CC1 to deliver complex II to lysosomes. Physiologically, this pathway protected mice from TNFR1-driven embryonic lethality and skin inflammation. (priem2023lc3independentautophagyis pages 1-4)

Interpretation: TNFR1 functional output is determined not only by ubiquitination and kinase checkpoints, but also by selective turnover of death complexes—a mechanistic layer directly relevant to annotation of pathway involvement and cellular compartment transitions (membrane → cytosol → lysosome). (priem2023lc3independentautophagyis pages 1-4)

2.3 2024: single-cell dynamics and heterogeneity as an emerging “systems” view of TNFR1 signaling

A 2024 review in Cell Death & Disease highlighted that TNF/TNFR1 outcomes are heterogeneous across single cells. It summarizes how stochastic TNF–TNFR1 binding and feedback control by NF-κB regulators (e.g., IκBα and A20) can yield distinct nuclear NF-κB dynamics (single pulse vs oscillations) and different fates (survival vs apoptosis/necroptosis). It also argues that live-cell imaging is underutilized in mapping pathway crosstalk that drives cell-to-cell variability—an increasingly important perspective for interpreting TNFR1 pathway perturbations in disease and therapy. (preedy2024cellularheterogeneityin pages 2-3, preedy2024cellularheterogeneityin pages 1-2)

2.4 2024: TNFR1 in infection and disease context (example: tuberculosis review)

A 2024 tuberculosis-focused review reiterated core TNFR1 mechanisms (Complex I ubiquitin scaffolds for NF-κB/MAPK vs Complex II death programs when ubiquitination is lost) and connects these to host defense outcomes; it notes that TNFR1-deficient mice can show increased pathogen burden and mortality in mycobacterial infection models (as summarized in the review). (yuk2024tnfinhuman pages 2-4)

A major translational idea is that TNFR1 is the principal mediator of many detrimental soluble TNF effects, whereas TNFR2 often mediates repair/protective functions in specific contexts; thus, TNFR1-selective antagonism is pursued to reduce adverse effects associated with broad anti-TNF therapies. 4.1 TNFR1 as a “decision node” integrating ubiquitin, kinase, and trafficking checkpoints

The 2024 single-cell imaging review argues that bulk assays can miss subpopulation behaviors (e.g., fractional killing; transient signaling), and that understanding TNFR1 pathway crosstalk requires dynamic, single-cell measurements—an expert methodological perspective shaping how current studies interpret TNFR1 function in disease and therapeutic contexts. Tnfrsf1a curation notes

Evidence synthesis

  • Core receptor identity: Tnfrsf1a encodes mouse TNF receptor 1 / p55 / CD120a, a single-pass type I membrane receptor. UniProt states that it is a "Receptor for TNFSF2/TNF and homotrimeric TNFSF1/lymphotoxin-alpha" and that receptor-associated proteins activate "apoptosis and NF-kappa-B signaling." [file:mouse/Tnfrsf1a/Tnfrsf1a-uniprot.txt]

  • Mouse TNF binding/receptor evidence: Goodwin et al. cloned murine TNF receptor type 1 and reported that the "ligand-binding characteristics of the recombinant murine receptors mirror those of the human homologs." Barrett et al. cloned the murine p55 TNFR and reported that "This receptor binds murine and human TNF with equal affinity." [PMID:1645445 "Molecular cloning and expression of the type 1 and type 2 murine receptors for tumor necrosis factor.", "ligand-binding characteristics of the recombinant murine receptors mirror those of the human homologs"] [PMID:1647956 "Cloning, expression and cross-linking analysis of the murine p55 tumor necrosis factor receptor.", "This receptor binds murine and human TNF with equal affinity"]

  • NF-kappaB and inflammatory signaling: TNF-R55-deficient fibroblasts show loss of major TNF-induced functions, including "NF-kappa B activation." TNFRp55-deficient mice also show that "TNF signaling is largely abolished, as judged by the failure of TNF to induce NF-kappa B." These support TNF-mediated signaling and canonical NF-kappaB activation as core pathway outputs. [PMID:7525730 "Differential responses of fibroblasts from wild-type and TNF-R55-deficient mice to mouse and human TNF-alpha activation.", "TNF-R55 controlled four major TNF-induced fibroblast functions"] [PMID:8387893 "Mice deficient for the 55 kd tumor necrosis factor receptor are resistant to endotoxic shock, yet succumb to L. monocytogenes infection.", "TNF signaling is largely abolished, as judged by the failure of TNF to induce NF-kappa B"]

  • Inflammation and host defense phenotypes: p55 and p75 receptor knockouts reveal that "The majority of biologic responses classically attributed to TNF are mediated by p55." Tnfrsf1a therefore directly supports inflammatory and host-defense annotations, but broad defense/developmental phenotypes should be non-core relative to receptor signaling. [PMID:9551933 "TNF receptor-deficient mice reveal divergent roles for p55 and p75 in several models of inflammation.", "The majority of biologic responses classically attributed to TNF are mediated by p55"]

  • Apoptosis and death-domain signaling: UniProt notes that FADD recruits caspase-8 to the activated receptor and that the receptor complex activates apoptosis and NF-kappaB signaling. Mouse experiments in infection/encephalopathy models report TNFR1-dependent apoptosis and inflammatory brain responses. These support apoptotic signaling annotations, especially extrinsic/death-domain receptor terms, but not every downstream apoptotic phenotype as core. [file:mouse/Tnfrsf1a/Tnfrsf1a-uniprot.txt] [PMID:17884256 "TNF is a key mediator of septic encephalopathy acting through its receptor, TNF receptor-1.", "all of which appear to be mediated by TNF-alpha signaling through TNFR1"]

  • Membrane and complex localization: UniProt describes TNFRSF1A as a type I membrane protein with extracellular ligand-binding and intracellular death-domain regions. The receptor is annotated to plasma membrane/cell surface and receptor complexes; membrane-raft and Golgi annotations reflect trafficking or shedding contexts. [file:mouse/Tnfrsf1a/Tnfrsf1a-uniprot.txt]

  • Protein-interaction curation: Several IPI annotations to protein binding reflect specific pathway interactors such as ERN1, TRADD/RIPK1-associated proteins, cIAP/A20 pathway proteins, FAN/RACK1, or shedding regulators. These interactions can be biologically real, but the GO term protein binding is too vague to capture TNFR1 function and should not be treated as a core molecular function.

  • Curation judgment: TNFR1 is not primarily a JAK-STAT receptor, and generic signal transduction, gene expression, developmental process, extracellular matrix secretion, and mechanical-stimulus terms are downstream or context-specific. Prefer TNF receptor activity, TNF binding, TNF-mediated signaling, canonical NF-kappaB signaling, and extrinsic apoptotic signaling as the central annotation set.

  • Falcon integration: the Falcon report reinforces TNFR1 as a cell-surface/type I transmembrane TNF receptor with extracellular cysteine-rich ligand-binding domains and an intracellular death domain. It supports TNF binding, plasma-membrane receptor activity, Complex I NF-kappaB/MAPK inflammatory signaling, and Complex II apoptosis/necroptosis decisions, with recent 2023-2024 work emphasizing TBK1, selective autophagy, and single-cell signaling heterogeneity as modifiers of the core TNFR1 pathway. [file:mouse/Tnfrsf1a/Tnfrsf1a-deep-research-falcon.md "TNFR1 is a TNF receptor"; "Activation of NF-kB and MAPK pathways via ubiquitin-scaffolded Complex I"]
