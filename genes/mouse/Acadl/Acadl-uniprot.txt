CC   -!- FUNCTION: Catalyzes the first of the four reactions of the
CC       mitochondrial fatty acid beta-oxidation (FAO) pathway, which consists
CC       in the proR-proR stereospecific alpha, beta-dehydrogenation of fatty
CC       acyl-CoA thioesters using the electron transfer flavoprotein (ETF) as
CC       their physiologic electron acceptor, resulting in the formation of
CC       trans-2-enoyl-CoA ((2E)-enoyl-CoA). The mitochondrial FAO pathway is
CC       the major energy-producing process in tissues and is performed through
CC       cycles of four consecutive reactions. Each FAO cycle shortens the fatty
CC       acyl-CoA by two carbons, yielding one acetyl-CoA (for the citric acid
CC       cycle), one FADH(2), and one NADH (which donate electrons to the
CC       respiratory chain for ATP production) (By similarity). Among the
CC       different mitochondrial acyl-CoA dehydrogenases, long-chain specific
CC       acyl-CoA dehydrogenase activity overlaps with that of ACADV and ACAD9,
CC       acting on saturated and unsaturated acyl-CoAs with 6 to 24 carbons with
CC       a preference for 8 to 18 carbons long primary chains (PubMed:9861014).
CC       Can use (4Z,7Z,10Z,13Z,16Z,19Z)-docosahexaenoate as substrate in vitro
CC       (which is not primarily used for energy but mainly beta-oxidized in the
CC       peroxisomes). In addition, based on its established catalytic
CC       mechanism, and combined genetic interaction or mutant phenotype
CC       evidence, it is predicted to act also on substrates that have not been
CC       tested experimentally but are metabolized by mitochondrial FAO,
CC       including long-chain unsaturated fatty acids such as linoleate (9Z,12Z-
CC       octadecadienoate), linolenate (9Z,12Z,15Z-octadecatrienoate), and
CC       others. Plays a primary role in FAO in tissues where it is the main
CC       long-chain ACAD expressed. Probably responsible for beta-oxidation of
CC       bulky substrates including branched chain fatty acyl-CoAs and sterol
CC       derivatives thanks to its enlarged substrate-binding cavity (By
CC       similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P28330,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:9861014}.
