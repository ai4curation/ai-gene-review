CC   -!- FUNCTION: Non-receptor tyrosine-protein kinase that plays a role in
CC       many biological processes including regulation of cell growth and
CC       survival, cell adhesion, integrin-mediated signaling, cytoskeletal
CC       remodeling, cell motility, immune response and axon guidance
CC       (PubMed:12218089, PubMed:12526739, PubMed:12640114, PubMed:12681493,
CC       PubMed:14999081, PubMed:8007959). Inactive FYN is phosphorylated on its
CC       C-terminal tail within the catalytic domain (By similarity). Following
CC       activation by PKA, the protein subsequently associates with PTK2/FAK1,
CC       allowing PTK2/FAK1 phosphorylation, activation and targeting to focal
CC       adhesions (By similarity). Involved in the regulation of cell adhesion
CC       and motility through phosphorylation of CTNNB1 (beta-catenin) and
CC       CTNND1 (delta-catenin) (PubMed:12640114). Regulates cytoskeletal
CC       remodeling by phosphorylating several proteins including the actin
CC       regulator WAS and the microtubule-associated proteins MAP2 and MAPT (By
CC       similarity). Promotes cell survival by phosphorylating AGAP2/PIKE-A and
CC       preventing its apoptotic cleavage (By similarity). Participates in
CC       signal transduction pathways that regulate the integrity of the
CC       glomerular slit diaphragm (an essential part of the glomerular filter
CC       of the kidney) by phosphorylating several slit diaphragm components
CC       including NPHS1, KIRREL1 and TRPC6 (By similarity). Plays a role in
CC       neural processes by phosphorylating DPYSL2, a multifunctional adapter
CC       protein within the central nervous system, ARHGAP32, a regulator for
CC       Rho family GTPases implicated in various neural functions, and SNCA, a
CC       small pre-synaptic protein (By similarity). Involved in reelin
CC       signaling by mediating phosphorylation of DAB1 following reelin (RELN)-
CC       binding to its receptor (PubMed:12526739). Participates in the
CC       downstream signaling pathways that lead to T-cell differentiation and
CC       proliferation following T-cell receptor (TCR) stimulation (By
CC       similarity). Phosphorylates PTK2B/PYK2 in response to T-cell receptor
CC       activation (By similarity). Also participates in negative feedback
CC       regulation of TCR signaling through phosphorylation of PAG1 and PDCD1
CC       (By similarity). Phosphorylation of PAG1 promotes interaction between
CC       PAG1 and CSK and recruitment of CSK to lipid rafts (By similarity).
CC       Phosphorylation of PDCD1 leads to the recruitment of PTPN11/SHP-2 that
CC       mediates dephosphorylation of key TCR proximal signaling molecules (By
CC       similarity). CSK maintains LCK and FYN in an inactive form
CC       (PubMed:12218089). Promotes CD28-induced phosphorylation of VAV1 (By
CC       similarity). In mast cells, phosphorylates CLNK after activation of
CC       immunoglobulin epsilon receptor signaling (PubMed:12681493). Can also
CC       promote CD244-mediated NK cell activation (By similarity).
