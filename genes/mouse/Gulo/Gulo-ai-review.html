Gene Description

Gulo encodes mouse L-gulonolactone oxidase, an FAD-dependent oxygen-dependent oxidoreductase that catalyzes the terminal step of L-ascorbate biosynthesis by oxidizing L-gulono-1,4-lactone with molecular oxygen to produce L-ascorbate and hydrogen peroxide. The protein is highly expressed in liver and is associated with the microsomal/endoplasmic reticulum membrane.

1) Key concepts and definitions (current understanding)
- Protein/gene and enzyme class: Gulo encodes L‑gulono‑γ‑lactone oxidase (GULO), a VAO/aldonolactone oxidoreductase (AlOR) family FAD enzyme that converts L‑gulono‑1,4‑lactone (GUL) to L‑ascorbate (vitamin C) using O2 as electron acceptor, generating H2O2; catalytic activity corresponds to EC 1.1.3.8 (Oct 2024; https://doi.org/10.3390/cimb46100657) (gad2024lgulonoγlactoneoxidasethe pages 5-7, gad2024lgulonoγlactoneoxidasethe pages 7-9, gad2024lgulonoγlactoneoxidasethe pages 9-11).
- Reaction and substrate specificity: Terminal step of the mammalian ascorbate pathway: L‑gulono‑1,4‑lactone + O2 → L‑ascorbate + H2O2; preference for L‑gulono‑1,4‑lactone, with in‑vitro oxidase/dehydrogenase activity also reported for related aldonolactones in the AlOR family (Oct 2024; https://doi.org/10.3390/cimb46100657; Feb 2025; https://doi.org/10.3390/life15020238) (gad2024lgulonoγlactoneoxidasethe pages 7-9, gradinaru2025vitamincfrom pages 16-17, gad2024lgulonoγlactoneoxidasethe pages 9-11).
- Cofactor and domains: Mammalian GULOs are FAD‑dependent, typically with covalently bound 8α‑N1‑histidyl‑FAD; conserved architecture includes an N‑terminal FAD‑binding region and a C‑terminal ALO catalytic domain harboring a hallmark HWXK motif implicated in flavin binding and catalysis (Oct 2024; https://doi.org/10.3390/cimb46100657) (gad2024lgulonoγlactoneoxidasethe pages 7-9, gad2024lgulonoγlactoneoxidasethe pages 9-11).
- Subcellular localization: GULO is localized to microsomes/ER and functions as an integral membrane protein with its active site oriented to the ER lumen, where products are discharged; historical purifications detected activity in liver microsomes of mammals (Oct 2024; https://doi.org/10.3390/cimb46100657) (gad2024lgulonoγlactoneoxidasethe pages 5-7, gad2024lgulonoγlactoneoxidasethe pages 7-9).
- Tissue/organ expression: In mammals, Gulo expression and enzymatic activity are centered in liver (contrast: kidney in birds/reptiles); mouse and rat Gulo transgenes restore vitamin C biosynthesis in deficient systems, underscoring hepatic locus of function in rodents (Oct 2024; https://doi.org/10.3390/cimb46100657) (gad2024lgulonoγlactoneoxidasethe pages 5-7, gad2024lgulonoγlactoneoxidasethe pages 1-2).
- Pathway placement: GULO catalyzes the terminal oxidation step of the UDP‑glucuronate → D‑glucuronate → L‑gulonate → L‑gulono‑1,4‑lactone → L‑ascorbate pathway, coupling O2 reduction to H2O2 formation. The H2O2 byproduct links GULO activity to ER redox/oxidative folding processes (Oct 2024; https://doi.org/10.3390/cimb46100657) (gad2024lgulonoγlactoneoxidasethe pages 7-9).

2) Recent developments and latest research (2023–2024 priority)
- Liver omics (2024): Integrated transcriptomics/proteomics in Gulo−/− mice supplied with sub‑optimal vs optimal ascorbate revealed sex‑dependent effects. Hepatic ascorbate positively correlates with lipid/xenobiotic/organic acid/acetyl‑CoA/steroid metabolism proteins (primarily transcriptional, especially in females), while multiple mitochondrial complex III subunits (Uqcrh, Uqcrb, Uqcrc1/2, Uqcr10, Uqcrq, Uqcrfs1, Cyc1) correlate with ascorbate at the protein but not mRNA level, indicating post‑transcriptional regulation; polysome profiling showed no differential ribosome loading of these mRNAs (May 2024; https://doi.org/10.1186/s40659-024-00509-x) (aumailley2024combinedtranscriptomicsand pages 1-2, aumailley2024combinedtranscriptomicsand pages 12-14).

3) Current applications and real‑world implementations
- Disease‑relevant modeling: Gulo−/− mice enable a human‑like dependence on dietary vitamin C to interrogate deficiency and repletion in controlled settings. 2024 liver and multi‑organ studies implemented stepwise withdrawal and graded repletion in drinking water, enabling quantitative mapping of ascorbate‑responsive pathways with sex resolution, informing non‑alcoholic fatty liver disease (NAFLD)–related biology (May 2024; https://doi.org/10.1186/s40659-024-00509-x; Oct 2024; https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pone.0311857) (aumailley2024combinedtranscriptomicsand pages 1-2, aumailley2024combinedtranscriptomicsand pages 12-14, aumailley2024sexandorgan pages 1-2).
- Translational utility: Proteomic and transcriptomic signatures under sub‑optimal vs optimal ascorbate identify mitochondrial complex III proteins and lipid/sterol/xenobiotic metabolic pathways as vitamin C–responsive nodes in liver, offering biomarkers and mechanistic targets for dietary or pharmacologic interventions that manipulate systemic or hepatic ascorbate status (May 2024; https://doi.org/10.1186/s40659-024-00509-x; Oct 2024; https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pone.0311857) (aumailley2024combinedtranscriptomicsand pages 1-2, aumailley2024combinedtranscriptomicsand pages 12-14, aumailley2024sexandorgan pages 7-10, aumailley2024sexandorgan pages 23-24).

4) Expert opinions and analysis from authoritative sources
- Enzymology and localization consensus: Reviews emphasize that mammalian GULO is a microsomal/ER‑associated, membrane‑embedded, FAD‑dependent aldonolactone oxidoreductase with a conserved C‑terminal HWXK motif, catalyzing GUL→ascorbate with H2O2 generation and with the active site facing the ER lumen. These properties are conserved across vertebrate GULO orthologs and underlie the enzyme’s integration into ER redox metabolism (Oct 2024; https://doi.org/10.3390/cimb46100657) (gad2024lgulonoγlactoneoxidasethe pages 5-7, gad2024lgulonoγlactoneoxidasethe pages 7-9, gad2024lgulonoγlactoneoxidasethe pages 9-11).
- Evolutionary and physiological framing: Authoritative perspectives summarize that mammalian vitamin C biosynthesis localizes predominantly to liver, with species‑specific losses of Gulo (e.g., primates, guinea pigs) conferring dietary dependence; mechanistically, the H2O2 byproduct situates GULO at the interface of antioxidant demand and ER oxidative folding/ROS homeostasis (Feb 2025; https://doi.org/10.3390/life15020238) (gradinaru2025vitamincfrom pages 16-17).

- Liver integrated omics: Among proteins with matched transcripts (n=2,607 females; n=2,496 males), Spearman correlation analyses (|rho| ≥ 0.46876 threshold used) revealed more ascorbate‑correlated functional modules in females (13) than males (3).
