Gene Description

Ednra encodes endothelin receptor type A, a plasma-membrane class A GPCR that binds endothelins, especially endothelin-1, and signals through G proteins to phosphatidylinositol-calcium, Rho/Cdc42, ERK, and related pathways. Its best-supported mouse roles are vasoconstrictive endothelin signaling and Edn1-dependent cranial/cardiac neural crest patterning.

Research report: Mouse Ednra (UniProt Q61614) — functional annotation and recent literature (2023–2024 prioritized)

1) Target verification (mandatory)

The UniProt accession Q61614 corresponds to mouse Ednra (syn. Gpcr10), encoding endothelin receptor type A (ETAR/ETA/EDNRA), a class A (rhodopsin-like) 7-transmembrane G protein–coupled receptor (GPCR). This identity is consistent with mechanistic work explicitly describing ETAR as a class A GPCR and studying mouse Ednra knock-in models. (kurihara2023mandibulofacialdysostosiswith pages 1-2, kurihara2023mandibulofacialdysostosiswith pages 2-3)

2) Key concepts and definitions (current understanding)

2.1 Endothelin system overview and EDNRA’s role

The endothelin system is centered on endothelin peptides (ET-1/ET-2/ET-3) and endothelin receptors. ETA/EDNRA is described as the principal receptor mediating ET‑1–driven vasoconstriction in the vasculature and producing strong, sustained constrictor responses; it is functionally localized on vascular smooth muscle, where ETA antagonism can reverse ET‑1–mediated constriction. (abraham2023currentandfuture pages 1-4, abraham2023currentandfuture pages 39-46)

In craniofacial development, EDNRA acts as the receptor for EDN1 in pharyngeal arch contexts and is central for mandibular (lower jaw) identity specification in neural crest derivatives. (kurihara2023mandibulofacialdysostosiswith pages 1-2, seto2024invitroinduction pages 9-10)

2.2 Molecular function (what the protein does)

EDNRA is a peptide hormone receptor GPCR that binds endothelins and transduces signals by activating heterotrimeric G proteins. In its canonical state, ETAR shows higher affinity for ET1 and ET2 than ET3, though receptor variants can shift ligand preference. (kurihara2023mandibulofacialdysostosiswith pages 1-2, kurihara2023mandibulofacialdysostosiswith pages 4-5)

2.3 Subcellular/cellular localization (where it acts)

Functionally, EDNRA is primarily described on vascular smooth muscle cells (VSMCs) mediating ET‑1 constriction; ET‑1/ETA complexes can undergo endosomal internalization, with potential receptor recycling to the plasma membrane. (abraham2023currentandfuture pages 39-46)

EDNRA signaling is also functionally implicated in the kidney nephron/collecting duct, where genetic evidence indicates ETA in these compartments contributes to sodium/water handling relevant to drug side effects (fluid retention). (abraham2023currentandfuture pages 4-7)

2.4 Core signaling pathways downstream of EDNRA

A central downstream signaling mode is Gq/11 coupling, which increases intracellular Ca2+ and activates ERK phosphorylation, consistent with ETAR’s roles in contraction and mitogenic signaling. (kurihara2023mandibulofacialdysostosiswith pages 2-3, kurihara2023mandibulofacialdysostosiswith pages 4-5)

Recent mechanistic work also supports signaling diversification/“bias” including G12/13 engagement (linked to RhoA/MLCK and mitogenic outputs) and context-dependent Gs/cAMP signaling. (gyamfi2024thestructuraland pages 48-51, gyamfi2024thestructuraland pages 54-58)

3) Recent developments and latest research (2023–2024)

3.1 2023: Allosteric GPCR mechanisms and craniofacial disease—EDNRA gain-of-function

A high-impact 2023 study (Kurihara et al., Journal of Clinical Investigation, Feb 2023; https://doi.org/10.1172/jci151536) established that mandibulofacial dysostosis with alopecia (MFDA) can result from EDNRA gain-of-function missense variants and explained these using allosteric GPCR mechanisms.

Key mechanistic concepts from this work:
- ETAR activation involves orthosteric agonist binding and outward movement of TM6 to accommodate G-protein binding; G-protein binding can allosterically enhance agonist affinity. A conserved Na+/water pocket stabilizes the inactive state. (kurihara2023mandibulofacialdysostosiswith pages 1-2)
- E303K (intracellular end of TM6) increases TM6 flexibility and favors G-protein engagement, increasing agonist affinity; Y129F (under ligand pocket) disrupts the sodium-water network to favor ET3 binding. (kurihara2023mandibulofacialdysostosiswith pages 1-2, kurihara2023mandibulofacialdysostosiswith pages 8-11)

Quantitative/mechanistic results (useful for functional annotation):
- Disease-associated variants increase ET3 affinity (reported approximately ~27-fold for Y129F and ~10- to 5-fold for E303K across assays) and potentiate Gq/11 outputs; E303K also increases basal signaling in ligand-free Ca2+/IP1 readouts. (kurihara2023mandibulofacialdysostosiswith pages 4-5)
- In cell assays, variants enhance ET3-induced ERK phosphorylation and intracellular Ca2+ mobilization; Gq/11 coupling is implicated as a key mediator. (kurihara2023mandibulofacialdysostosiswith pages 2-3, kurihara2023mandibulofacialdysostosiswith pages 4-5)

Developmental interpretation:
- In craniofacial development, EDN1–EDNRA signaling to ETAR-expressing migrating cranial neural crest specifies lower-jaw identity; loss leads to transformations/duplications, and inappropriate activation can drive fate conversion. (kurihara2023mandibulofacialdysostosiswith pages 1-2, kurihara2023mandibulofacialdysostosiswith pages 2-3)

Visual evidence supporting the ET3-dependence and rescue concept:
- Kurihara et al. provide figure panels showing craniofacial phenotype rescue of EDNRA GOF mutants by Edn3 deletion and schematic models of mutant receptor activation. (kurihara2023mandibulofacialdysostosiswith media 5cc1a389, kurihara2023mandibulofacialdysostosiswith media 2cece4c7, kurihara2023mandibulofacialdysostosiswith media 00ca822b)

3.2 2024: Human PSC branchial-arch model recapitulating EDN1–EDNRA jaw patterning logic

Seto et al. (Nature Communications, Feb 2024; https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-024-45285-0) developed a patterned branchial-arch-like aggregate model from human pluripotent stem cells.

Relevance to EDNRA functional annotation:
- NCC-like cells in the model express EDNRA, and the authors interpret EDN1 signaling as governing maxillary vs mandibular fate, with EDN1 promoting mandibular specifiers such as DLX5/6; low EDN1 levels in the model are hypothesized to favor maxillary-like identity. 4) Current applications and real-world implementations

4.1 Pharmacologic EDNRA targeting: ETA antagonists and beyond

A 2023 review focused on cardiovascular strategy (Abraham et al., Dec 2023; https://doi.org/10.17863/cam.104737) describes ETA antagonism as an established therapeutic approach, historically limited to pulmonary arterial hypertension (PAH) but with renewed interest across cardiovascular diseases. (abraham2023currentandfuture pages 1-4)

Clinical/real-world and translational highlights:
- ETA antagonists can produce clinically meaningful vasodilation in humans; ETA is the primary receptor mediating sustained ET‑1 constriction in vessels. (abraham2023currentandfuture pages 1-4, abraham2023currentandfuture pages 39-46)
- A critical on-target limitation is fluid retention/edema, tied to renal endothelin biology (see quantitative statistics below). (abraham2023currentandfuture pages 1-4, abraham2023currentandfuture pages 4-7)

Emerging modality: anti-EDNRA monoclonal antibody
- The same review describes getagozumab (GMA301), a humanized anti-ETA monoclonal antibody, providing quantitative binding and PK parameters and noting ongoing PAH trials to assess safety signals including fluid retention. (abraham2023currentandfuture pages 7-11)

4.2 Developmental biology applications

EDNRA signaling is a major pathway node used to:
- Interpret and experimentally manipulate jaw patterning and cranial neural crest fate decisions. (kurihara2023mandibulofacialdysostosiswith pages 1-2, seto2024invitroinduction pages 9-10)
- Construct mechanistic genotype-to-phenotype models for craniofacial disorders caused by GPCR allostery and ligand bias. (kurihara2023mandibulofacialdysostosiswith pages 1-2, kurihara2023mandibulofacialdysostosiswith pages 4-5)

5) Expert opinions and analysis (authoritative synthesis)

5.1 EDNRA/ETA as a central vasoconstrictor axis

The 2023 cardiovascular strategy review frames ETA as the dominant receptor driving ET‑1 vasoconstriction in the arterial wall and notes that translational work has “reignited” interest in targeting the endothelin pathway beyond PAH. (abraham2023currentandfuture pages 1-4)

Mechanistic interpretation consistent with these views is supported by functional localization to VSMCs and pharmacologic reversal of ET‑1 constriction by ETA antagonists. (abraham2023currentandfuture pages 39-46)

5.2 EDNRA as an instructive developmental signaling receptor

Recent primary evidence and discussion emphasize EDNRA not merely as permissive signaling but as instructive for tissue identity (mandibular fate) in cranial neural crest-derived structures, with both loss-of-function and ectopic activation producing predictable homeotic transformations. (kurihara2023mandibulofacialdysostosiswith pages 1-2, seto2024invitroinduction pages 9-10)

5.3 EDNRA signaling complexity and bias

The 2024 mechanistic evidence indicates that EDNRA can engage multiple G-protein pathways and that non-canonical agonists (e.g., autoantibodies) may produce distinct outputs (e.g., cAMP differences), supporting the modern view of biased agonism/state dependence in GPCR biology. (gyamfi2024thestructuraland pages 48-51, gyamfi2024thestructuraland pages 54-58)

Ednra curation notes

Evidence synthesis

  • Core receptor identity: Ednra encodes endothelin receptor type A, a seven-transmembrane G protein-coupled receptor. UniProt states that EDNRA is a "Receptor for endothelin-1" that mediates action "by association with G proteins that activate a phosphatidylinositol-calcium second messenger system." [file:mouse/Ednra/Ednra-uniprot.txt]

  • Core receptor/signaling evidence: Mouse Ednra knockout and antagonist studies place endothelin-A receptor signaling in neural-crest and cardiovascular development. Clouthier et al. report that ETA receptor mRNA is expressed in neural crest-derived ectomesenchyme and that ET-1 ligand is expressed by surrounding arch tissues, supporting a paracrine Edn1-Ednra signaling pathway. [PMID:9449664 "Cranial and cardiac neural crest defects in endothelin-A receptor-deficient mice.", "ETA receptor mRNA is expressed by the neural crest-derived ectomesenchymal cells of pharyngeal arches and cardiac outflow tissues"]

  • Gq/G11 pathway specificity: Sato et al. report that "the dorsoventral axis patterning of pharyngeal arches is regulated by the Ednra-selective, G(q)/G(11)-dependent signaling." This supports Ednra-mediated GPCR signaling, phospholipase/calcium pathway terms, and pharyngeal arch patterning, but not unrelated endothelin receptor family phenotypes. [PMID:18199583 "Recombinase-mediated cassette exchange reveals the selective use of Gq/G11-dependent and -independent endothelin 1/endothelin type A receptor signaling in pharyngeal arch development.", "regulated by the Ednra-selective, G(q)/G(11)-dependent signaling"]

  • Craniofacial timing and identity: Neural crest-specific deletion and antagonist experiments show Ednra function is required between E8.25 and E9.0 for cranial neural crest patterning, with defects including homeotic transformation of lower jaw structures. This supports face, head, mandibular/maxillary identity, middle-ear, and cranial skeletal annotations as non-core developmental roles. [PMID:19185569 "Elucidating timing and function of endothelin-A receptor signaling during craniofacial development using neural crest cell-specific gene deletion and receptor antagonism.", "Ednra function is crucial for NCC development between E8.25 and E9.0"]

  • Endothelin switch: Constitutive activation of Ednra "induced the transformation of upper jaw, maxillary, structures into lower jaw, mandibular, structures" and the authors conclude that Edn1/Ednra signaling is responsible for maxillomandibular identity choice. [PMID:19017795 "An endothelin-1 switch specifies maxillomandibular identity.", "Constitutive activation of Ednra induced the transformation of upper jaw, maxillary, structures into lower jaw, mandibular, structures"]

  • Cardiac neural crest and sympathetic axon evidence: Ednra signaling activates Cdc42 to control cardiac neural crest migration in the outflow tract, and venous Edn1-Ednra signaling guides cardiac sympathetic axons. These support specific neural crest migration and sympathetic axon guidance terms but do not make general neuron remodeling or heart process terms core. [PMID:31219639 "Cdc42 activation by endothelin regulates neural crest cell migration in the cardiac outflow tract.", "Cdc42 is part of the signaling pathway activated by endothelin specifically in OFT CNCC to control their migration"] [PMID:24875861 "Venous endothelin guides sympathetic innervation of the developing mouse heart.", "venous endothelium-derived endothelin-1, acting through its specific receptor Ednra"]

  • Vascular tone and blood pressure: Smooth-muscle Ednra signaling contributes to vascular tone; age-dependent hypertension is normalized by ETA blockade and smooth-muscle Ednra inactivation. This supports vasoconstriction and blood-pressure annotations as non-core physiological outputs of EDNRA receptor signaling. [PMID:26531127 "Age-dependent blood pressure elevation is due to increased vascular smooth muscle tone mediated by G-protein signalling.", "Age-dependent hypertension could be normalized by ETA receptor blockade, smooth muscle-specific inactivation of the gene encoding the ETA receptor"]

  • Renal/podocyte disease-model evidence: Podocyte endothelin receptor signaling elicits calcium transients and contributes to glomerulosclerosis; glomerular endothelial surface layer injury can be prevented by ETA antagonism. These annotations are direct in kidney disease contexts but are not core receptor function. [PMID:24722437 "Direct action of endothelin-1 on podocytes promotes diabetic glomerulosclerosis.", "elicited rapid calcium transients mediated by endothelin type A receptors"] [PMID:31402170 "Endothelin receptor-A mediates degradation of the glomerular endothelial surface layer via pathologic crosstalk between activated podocytes and glomerular endothelial cells.", "prevented by endothelin receptor-A antagonism"]

  • Curation judgment: developmental pigmentation and enteric nervous system development are better-known EDNRB/EDN3-axis phenotypes and are not supported by the mouse Ednra evidence reviewed here. Treat them as paralog/family over-transfer for Ednra unless direct Ednra evidence is found.

  • Falcon integration: the Falcon report reinforces Ednra as the ETA/endothelin receptor type A class A GPCR, the principal receptor for ET-1-driven vascular smooth muscle vasoconstriction and a receptor for EDN1 in pharyngeal arch neural crest patterning. It specifically supports endothelin peptide receptor activity, plasma-membrane GPCR localization, endothelin receptor signaling, Gq/11-calcium/ERK signaling, vasoconstriction, and mandibular identity roles. [file:mouse/Ednra/Ednra-deep-research-falcon.md "peptide hormone receptor GPCR"; "principal receptor mediating ET-1-driven vasoconstriction"]
