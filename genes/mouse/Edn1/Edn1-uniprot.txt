CC   -!- FUNCTION: Endothelins are endothelium-derived vasoconstrictor peptides
CC       (By similarity). Probable ligand for G-protein coupled receptors EDNRA
CC       and EDNRB which activates PTK2B, BCAR1, BCAR3 and, GTPases RAP1 and
CC       RHOA cascade in glomerular mesangial cells (By similarity). Also binds
CC       the DEAR/FBXW7-AS1 receptor (PubMed:16293765). Promotes mesenteric
CC       arterial wall remodeling via activation of ROCK signaling and
CC       subsequent colocalization of NFATC3 with F-actin filaments
CC       (PubMed:20495147, PubMed:21525433). NFATC3 then translocates to the
CC       nucleus where it subsequently promotes the transcription of the smooth
CC       muscle hypertrophy and differentiation marker ACTA2 (PubMed:20495147).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P05305, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P09558,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:16293765, ECO:0000269|PubMed:20495147,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:21525433}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Secreted.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Highest expression in the adult is in lung. Lower
CC       levels found in heart, kidney, brain and intestine. In the embryo,
CC       expressed in outer and inner pharyngeal arch surfaces. Also expressed
CC       in endothelium of dorsal aorta and arch arteries, and in epithelium of
CC       pharyngeal pouches. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:8824799}.
CC   -!- DEVELOPMENTAL STAGE: Expression in lung is low at 17.5 dpc fetal mice
CC       and increases progressively into adulthood.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:8824799}.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: Induced by intermittent hypoxia in the lung
CC       (PubMed:20495147). Induced by chronic hypoxia in the intrapulmonary
CC       arteries (PubMed:21525433). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:20495147,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:21525433}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the endothelin/sarafotoxin family.
CC       {ECO:0000305}.
