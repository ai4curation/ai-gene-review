## Research report: Mouse **Edn1** (UniProt **P22387**) — functional annotation of Endothelin-1 (ET-1)

### 0) Target verification (critical disambiguation)
The requested target is **mouse (Mus musculus) Edn1**, UniProt **P22387**, which encodes the **Endothelin-1 (ET-1) precursor (preproendothelin-1)**. The literature retrieved consistently defines **Edn1/EDN1** as encoding **preproendothelin-1**, processed to **big endothelin-1** and then the mature **21-aa ET-1**, a secreted signaling peptide acting primarily through **ETA/ETB receptors**; this matches the UniProt description and endothelin/sarafotoxin family membership. (schiffrin2024endothelinsystemin pages 1-2, schiffrin2025endothelinantagonistsfor pages 1-2, patel2024comprehensiveanalysisof pages 1-2)

### 1) Key concepts and definitions (current understanding)

#### 1.1 What Edn1 encodes
**Edn1** encodes a **secreted peptide hormone precursor** that yields the mature vasoactive peptide **endothelin-1 (ET-1)** (21 amino acids). ET-1 is widely regarded as one of the most potent endogenous vasoconstrictors, but also functions as a developmental morphogen in specific embryonic contexts. (schiffrin2024endothelinsystemin pages 1-2, clouthier2004understandingendothelin1function pages 1-2)

#### 1.2 Biosynthesis and peptide processing: prepro → big ET-1 → ET-1
ET-1 is produced by **stepwise proteolysis**:
- The Edn1 product is synthesized as a **preproendothelin-1** precursor.
- **Furin/furin-like proteases** cleave proendothelin to **“big endothelin-1”** (38–39 aa).
- **Endothelin-converting enzymes (ECE-1/ECE-2)** (zinc-dependent endoproteases) cleave big ET-1 to produce mature **ET-1 (21 aa)**.
Additional proteases (e.g., MMP-2, chymase, neutral endopeptidase) can contribute to ET-1 generation or alternative intermediates in some contexts. (schiffrin2024endothelinsystemin pages 1-2, schiffrin2024endothelinsystemin pages 2-4, schiffrin2025endothelinantagonistsfor pages 1-2)

#### 1.3 Receptors and canonical pathway definition
ET-1 signals primarily via **two class A GPCRs**:
- **ETA (EDNRA/ETAR)**: classically enriched on **vascular smooth muscle cells (VSMCs)** and mediates **potent, sustained vasoconstriction** and growth/remodeling programs.
- **ETB (EDNRB/ETBR)**: enriched on **endothelial cells** (among other cell types); **endothelial ETB** can stimulate release of vasodilators (notably **NO and prostacyclin**) and ETB is also described as contributing to **ET-1 clearance**. (schiffrin2024endothelinsystemin pages 2-4, arndt2024endothelinandthe pages 2-2, prepeliuc2025theinvolvementof pages 4-6)

### 2) Molecular function, signaling mechanisms, and localization

#### 2.1 Subcellular/extracellular localization and secretion
ET-1 functions as an **extracellular ligand** produced predominantly by **vascular endothelial cells** (also by certain epithelial cells, including in kidney). In kidney tissue, ET-1 and ECE1 protein localize in **vessels and glomeruli**, supporting local endothelial/glomerular production and paracrine signaling. (schiffrin2024endothelinsystemin pages 1-2, patel2024comprehensiveanalysisof media f464fe7a)

A mechanistic model of endothelial packaging is that ET-1 precursors and processing components can be **stored in endothelial secretory vesicles**, particularly **Weibel–Palade bodies**, with reported co-localization of **pro-ET-1 and ECE-1**; this supports regulated secretion and local processing capacity near the endothelial surface. (prepeliuc2025theinvolvementof pages 4-6)

#### 2.2 Downstream signaling pathways (ETA/ETB)
Across authoritative sources, ET receptor activation is linked to:
- **Gq/11 → PLCβ → IP3/DAG → Ca2+ mobilization and PKC activation**, directly promoting VSMC contraction.
- **ROS generation** (e.g., via NADPH oxidase) and downstream signaling contributing to vascular remodeling.
- **MAPK cascade (ras–raf–MAPK/ERK)** and growth factor receptor transactivation, supporting hypertrophy and remodeling.
- Additional coupling through **Gi/o, Gs, G12/13** and **β-arrestin–linked signaling**, providing cell-type and context-specific outputs including inflammatory transcriptional programs (e.g., NF-κB-linked). (schiffrin2024endothelinsystemin pages 2-4, prepeliuc2025theinvolvementof pages 4-6, maiza2026endothelin1inthe pages 1-2)

### 3) In vivo physiological roles in mouse: primary functional annotation

#### 3.1 Adult physiology: vascular tone and renal endothelin system
ET-1 is a central regulator of **vasomotor tone** and contributes to blood pressure regulation and vascular remodeling in physiology and disease, with receptor-specific effects (ETA largely vasoconstrictive; endothelial ETB vasodilatory via NO/prostacyclin). (schiffrin2024endothelinsystemin pages 2-4, schiffrin2025endothelinantagonistsfor pages 1-2)

In kidney, a 2024 cross-species analysis combining scRNA-seq re-analysis and immunolocalization reported that **ECE1 and ET-1 are prominent in glomeruli and endothelium**, and that **Ece1 mRNA is enriched in endothelium**; the study also highlights a key reproducibility issue: **Edn1/EDN1 mRNA is often rarely detected by scRNA-seq despite clear protein immunolocalization and measurable plasma/urinary ET-1**, implying transcript-level under-detection relative to peptide/protein presence. (patel2024comprehensiveanalysisof pages 1-2, patel2024comprehensiveanalysisof media f464fe7a)

#### 3.2 Embryonic development: Edn1 as a pharyngeal-arch morphogen for craniofacial patterning
A defining mouse-specific functional role of Edn1 is as a **developmental signal in the pharyngeal arches**:
- **Ednra (ETA)** is expressed in **neural crest–derived ectomesenchyme**.
- **ET-1 (Edn1)** is expressed in the **arch environment**, including **arch epithelium/ectoderm**, **pharyngeal pouch endoderm**, and the **paraxial mesoderm-derived arch core**; **ECE-1** is coexpressed at key sites.
- Loss of Ednra signaling disrupts post-migratory neural crest transcriptional programs in arches: reduced/absent expression of **goosecoid, Dlx-2, Dlx-3, dHAND (Hand2), eHAND, and Barx1**, while some factors (e.g., Hoxa-2, Ufd1) are unaffected, indicating pathway specificity.
- Phenotypically, loss of ET-1/ETA/ECE-1 signaling causes severe craniofacial malformations and perinatal lethality (mechanical airway compromise), with mandibular and throat defects and associated cardiovascular outflow tract defects described in the developmental literature syntheses. (clouthier2000signalingpathwayscrucial pages 1-2, clouthier2010regulationoffacial pages 6-8, clouthier2010regulationoffacial pages 2-4)

A key refinement from conditional mouse genetics is that **ectodermal-derived Edn1** is required for mandibular arch patterning; a study using tissue-specific Cre drivers concluded that facial patterning defects occur **only when Edn1 is inactivated in ectoderm**, and that critical Edn1 function occurs in a narrow developmental window around **E8.5–E9.0**. (tavares2012ectodermalderivedendothelin1is pages 1-2, tavares2012ectodermalderivedendothelin1is pages 2-4)

### 4) Recent developments (prioritizing 2023–2024) and real-world implementations

Although Edn1 is a mouse gene, the **EDN1/ET-1 pathway** is intensively targeted translationally; 2023–2024 sources summarize a renewed clinical focus on endothelin pathway modulation.

#### 4.1 Drug development and clinical implementation (endothelin pathway targeting)
A 2023 cardiovascular translational review highlights expanding strategies beyond pulmonary arterial hypertension, including **precision approaches** using EDN1-regulatory genetics to enrich for response and **new therapeutic modalities** (e.g., long-acting biologics). (abraham2023currentandfuture pages 1-4, abraham2023currentandfuture pages 7-11)

**Sparsentan** (dual ETA/AT1 antagonist) is reported as receiving **approval in 2023 for IgA nephropathy**; pooled DUET phase II data in that review reported a **44.8%** reduction in urine protein-to-creatinine ratio versus **18.5%** with irbesartan and **partial remission** rates of **28.1% vs 9.4%**, respectively. (abraham2023currentandfuture pages 17-20)

A key clinical limitation of endothelin receptor antagonists is **fluid retention/edema**; the 2023 review cites **peripheral edema in 17%** of pulmonary arterial hypertension patients treated with **bosentan**, and discusses fluid overload as a class effect relevant to clinical management and combination strategies. (abraham2023currentandfuture pages 1-4)

#### 4.2 Biomarkers and human tissue evidence supporting endothelin-axis targeting
A 2024 human portal vein transcriptomic study (portal hypertension vs controls) provides a contemporary example of the endothelin axis appearing among top enriched pathways, supporting interest in endothelin antagonism in portal hypertension. In portal vein samples (PH n=12; controls n=9), the authors detected 9,964 genes above background and identified 2,544 differentially expressed genes (adjusted P<0.05; fold-change >1.5), with 49 enriched pathways; the endothelin pathway ranked 10th, with **EDN1 up-regulated** and **EDNRA down-regulated** (adaptive response) and **EDNRB unchanged**. (owen2024microarrayanalysisdemonstrates pages 1-2)
