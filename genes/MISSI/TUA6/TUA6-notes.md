# TUA6 notes

- The GOA file is header-only, so there are no existing GOA annotations to review or convert into NEW rows.
- UniProt identifies TUA6 as an alpha-tubulin chain and describes tubulin as the major constituent of microtubules composed of alpha/beta heterodimers [file:MISSI/TUA6/TUA6-uniprot.txt "Tubulin is the major constituent of microtubules"] [file:MISSI/TUA6/TUA6-uniprot.txt "SUBUNIT: Dimer of alpha and beta chains"].
- Core curation uses structural constituent of cytoskeleton, microtubule-based process, and microtubule location [file:MISSI/TUA6/TUA6-uniprot.txt "GO; GO:0005200; F:structural constituent of cytoskeleton"] [file:MISSI/TUA6/TUA6-uniprot.txt "GO; GO:0007017; P:microtubule-based process"] [file:MISSI/TUA6/TUA6-uniprot.txt "GO; GO:0005874; C:microtubule"].
- I did not make generic hydrolase activity or metal ion binding core functions; GTP binding/hydrolysis is part of tubulin polymerization biology, but the main curated role is the structural microtubule subunit.
