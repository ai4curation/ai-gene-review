Gene Description

PbpC (also known as PBP3x or PBP3a, encoded by PA2272) is a class B high-molecular-mass penicillin-binding protein (HMM bPBP) in Pseudomonas aeruginosa. It is a membrane-anchored protein with an N-terminal transmembrane segment and a periplasmic D,D-transpeptidase domain containing the conserved SXXK/SXN/KT(S)G motifs characteristic of penicillin-binding proteins. PbpC is a nonessential paralog of the essential septal transpeptidase PBP3 (FtsI/PbpB). Unlike PBP3, pbpC knockout causes no detectable growth or morphology defects under standard laboratory conditions. PbpC is expressed only during stationary phase and shows low/conditional expression. While it binds penicillin in labeling assays, there is no direct experimental evidence for specific enzymatic activity, and its physiological role remains unclear. Overexpression of pbpC does not alter beta-lactam susceptibility, in contrast to PBP3.
