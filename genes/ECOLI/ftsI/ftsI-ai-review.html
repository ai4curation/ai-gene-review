Gene Description

FtsI (also known as PBP3/PbpB) is an essential peptidoglycan D,D-transpeptidase required for cell division in E. coli. It is a Class B high-molecular-mass penicillin-binding protein that catalyzes cross-linking of peptidoglycan at the division septum. FtsI is a monofunctional transpeptidase - unlike Class A PBPs, it lacks glycosyltransferase activity. FtsI forms a subcomplex with FtsW (a SEDS protein that provides glycosyltransferase activity) and interacts with multiple divisome proteins including FtsQ, FtsL, FtsN, and PBP1b. FtsI localizes to the division septum during later stages of cell growth and throughout septation. Inhibition by beta-lactam antibiotics like penicillin blocks cell division causing filamentation.

Core Functions

Septal peptidoglycan cross-linking: FtsI catalyzes the transpeptidation reaction that cross-links peptidoglycan strands specifically at the division septum. It is a D,D-transpeptidase (EC 3.4.16.4) that cleaves D-Ala-D-Ala bonds and forms peptide cross-links. This is the primary enzymatic activity of FtsI [PMID:3531167, PMID:7030331].

Molecular Function:
serine-type D-Ala-D-Ala carboxypeptidase activity

Penicillin binding: FtsI is one of the major penicillin-binding proteins (PBP3). Beta-lactam antibiotics inhibit FtsI by binding to its active site serine, blocking cell division [PMID:1103132]. This property makes FtsI a key antibiotic target.

Molecular Function:
penicillin binding
