## Research report: *Escherichia coli* K-12 **skp** (UniProt P0AEU7) — functional annotation

### 0) Target verification (mandatory disambiguation)
The literature synthesized here concerns the *Escherichia coli* K-12 periplasmic chaperone **Skp** (also called **Seventeen kilodalton protein**, and reported synonyms **OmpH/hlpA** in the reviewed sources), consistent with the UniProt P0AEU7 description of “Chaperone protein Skp” and the Skp-family holdase chaperone involved in outer membrane protein (OMP) biogenesis. Skp is repeatedly described as a ~17 kDa periplasmic chaperone for β-barrel OMPs and as a trimeric “jellyfish-like” protein, matching the provided identity context. (mas2019theperiplasmicchaperones pages 1-4, stull2018periplasmicchaperonesand pages 5-6)

### 1) Key concepts and definitions (current understanding)

#### 1.1 What Skp is
Skp is an **ATP-independent periplasmic holdase chaperone** that binds unfolded β-barrel OMPs to prevent aggregation during their transit across the periplasm and to support their eventual folding/insertion into the outer membrane. (stull2018periplasmicchaperonesand pages 4-5, mas2019theperiplasmicchaperones pages 4-7)

#### 1.2 Where Skp acts in the cell (cellular localization)
Skp functions in the **periplasm** after inner-membrane translocation of OMP precursors by the **SecYEG** machinery and before outer-membrane insertion by the **β-barrel assembly machinery (BAM)**. Reviews describe Skp and SurA as the principal parallel periplasmic escort pathways that deliver unfolded OMPs to BAM. (mas2019theperiplasmicchaperones pages 4-7, mas2019theperiplasmicchaperones pages 1-4)

#### 1.3 Pathway context: the periplasmic OMP chaperone network
Outer-membrane β-barrel proteins are synthesized in the cytoplasm, exported across the inner membrane via SecYEG, then escorted across the periplasm by chaperones (notably **SurA** and **Skp**) to BAM; **DegP** is described as a quality-control protease that removes off-pathway/stalled OMPs. (mas2019theperiplasmicchaperones pages 1-4, stull2018periplasmicchaperonesand pages 5-6)

### 2) Protein structure, mechanism, and quantitative biophysical properties

#### 2.1 Oligomeric state and architecture
Skp’s crystal structure shows a **homotrimer** whose subunits connect through an intermolecular β-barrel “trimerization” core and extend **three long α-helical coiled-coil arms/tentacles**, forming a **central cavity** that can encapsulate unfolded clients—hence the characteristic “jellyfish-like” shape. A conserved hinge (noted at Phe30) supports large-amplitude arm flexibility. (mas2019theperiplasmicchaperones pages 4-7)

A figure from an authoritative *EcoSal Plus* review depicts Skp in the periplasmic chaperone network and illustrates this “jellyfish-like” trimer/cavity architecture alongside SurA, DegP, and BAM, supporting the canonical pathway placement. (stull2018periplasmicchaperonesand media 95ac5996)

#### 2.2 Client binding mode and dynamics (quantitative)
Solution NMR/biophysics summaries indicate the OMP polypeptide is **disordered** inside the Skp cavity and binds through **multiple weak hydrophobic and electrostatic contacts**. Two time scales are emphasized: **local contact lifetimes ≤1 ms**, contrasted with a **global complex lifetime on the order of hours**, consistent with avidity from many weak interactions. (mas2019theperiplasmicchaperones pages 4-7)

A notable quantitative structural consequence is that an encapsulated OMP can exhibit **~2× reduced compactness** relative to denaturant conditions, consistent with Skp maintaining an expanded, aggregation-resistant ensemble. (mas2019theperiplasmicchaperones pages 4-7)

#### 2.3 Capacity, client size range, and stoichiometry (quantitative)
Evidence in the reviews indicates Skp clients span a wide size range, e.g. ~150 residues (**OmpX**) to >700 residues (**FhuA**). (mas2019theperiplasmicchaperones pages 4-7)

Skp adapts to different client sizes in two ways: (i) **cavity expansion via arm opening/hinges**, and (ii) **higher Skp:OMP stoichiometries** for larger β-barrels. Summaries report that larger substrates can require **multiple Skp units** per client and that **higher Skp:OMP ratios** are required for large OMPs (e.g., 16-stranded). (mas2019theperiplasmicchaperones pages 4-7, stull2018periplasmicchaperonesand pages 5-6)

A later review synthesizes a model in which Skp can exist in a monomer–trimer equilibrium and forms encapsulating complexes with reported **1:1 (small substrates) or 2:1 (large substrates)** Skp:substrate stoichiometries. (figaj2022suralikeandskplike pages 7-10, figaj2022suralikeandskplike pages 10-11)

#### 2.4 Binding affinity (quantitative)
The *EcoSal Plus* review reports Skp binds unfolded OMPs with affinities in the **nanomolar range** (for the studied systems summarized there). (stull2018periplasmicchaperonesand pages 4-5)

### 3) Substrates/clients and primary biological processes

#### 3.1 Experimentally identified client spectrum (“clientome”)
Skp was originally identified as an OMP binder in a pull-down with unfolded **OmpF**, and a later affinity/proteomics mapping identified **>30 Skp-associated proteins**. (mas2019theperiplasmicchaperones pages 4-7)

Within that mapping, the review reproduces a list of unambiguously identified outer-membrane localized clients, including transporters/porins and other OMPs such as **LamB, OmpA, OmpC, OmpF, OmpX, FadL, Tsx, TolC**, and siderophore/iron uptake receptors (**BtuB, CirA, FecA, FepA, Fiu, YncD**), among others. (mas2019theperiplasmicchaperones pages 4-7)

Additional experimentally discussed clients/substrates include **LptD, FhuA, and PhoE** (with PhoE noted to be bound soon after translocation). (stull2018periplasmicchaperonesand pages 4-5, stull2018periplasmicchaperonesand pages 13-14)

#### 3.2 Biological process annotation
Taken together, the evidence supports annotating Skp as a key periplasmic factor in **β-barrel outer membrane protein biogenesis**: preventing aggregation of unfolded OMPs during periplasmic transit and supporting delivery/hand-off to **BAM** for insertion into the outer membrane. (mas2019theperiplasmicchaperones pages 4-7, stull2018periplasmicchaperonesand pages 5-6)

### 4) Genetic/physiological evidence for function and pathway placement

#### 4.1 Parallel pathway architecture with SurA and DegP
Skp is described as **nonessential when deleted alone**, but **genetically redundant** with other periplasmic folding factors. Specifically, **Skp–SurA double mutants are inviable**, and **skp degP mutants are not viable at 37°C**, consistent with partially overlapping roles in periplasmic proteostasis and OMP biogenesis. (stull2018periplasmicchaperonesand pages 5-6)

#### 4.2 “Primary vs backup” role relative to SurA
In vivo evidence summarized in the Skp/SurA review notes that depletion of **SurA** causes **severely reduced OMP levels**, whereas depletion of **Skp** yields **milder phenotypes**, motivating a prevailing model that **SurA is the primary periplasmic OMP chaperone pathway**, with **Skp as a secondary/backup pathway** important for certain clients or stress conditions. (mas2019theperiplasmicchaperones pages 12-15)

### 5) Regulation and envelope stress responses
Skp function is tied to envelope proteostasis surveillance. The *EcoSal Plus* review reports that deletion of skp **increases σE activity**, consistent with σE activation by unfolded OMP accumulation. (stull2018periplasmicchaperonesand pages 5-6)

A 2024 review on envelope biogenesis and stress responses provides current conceptual context: **σE, Rcs, and Cpx** systems monitor envelope assembly, and σE is activated by accumulated unfolded OMPs in the periplasm through **RseA proteolysis**. While this review does not provide Skp-specific quantitative regulation data, it supports the mechanistic link between OMP folding defects (where Skp participates) and σE-type envelope stress signaling. (bisht2024breakingbarriersexploiting pages 2-3)

### 6) Recent developments (prioritizing 2023–2024 sources)

#### 6.1 2024: envelope biogenesis as an antimicrobial strategy space
A 2024 review emphasizes that the Gram-negative outer membrane is a formidable barrier and that interconnected systems for OM biogenesis (BAM/OMP assembly, Lpt/LPS transport, Lol/lipoprotein trafficking) and envelope stress responses form an actionable **antimicrobial target space**. This provides a contemporary framework in which periplasmic chaperones such as Skp are viewed as part of the envelope homeostasis network whose disruption can compromise OM integrity. However, the retrieved text does not provide Skp-specific inhibitor data. (bisht2024breakingbarriersexploiting pages 2-3)

#### 6.2 2023–2024: Skp mechanistic synthesis updates available in recent reviews
A recent mechanistic synthesis highlights: (i) Skp’s **monomer–trimer equilibrium** with **substrate-induced oligomerization**, (ii) **1:1 vs 2:1** binding stoichiometries depending on substrate size, and (iii) a quality-control model where Skp can sequester defective OMPs and target them for DegP-mediated degradation, potentially with **Skp being degraded alongside its substrate** (“sacrificial adaptor” concept). These points represent updated integrative interpretations rather than new primary structural measurements in the retrieved corpus. (figaj2022suralikeandskplike pages 7-10, figaj2022suralikeandskplike pages 10-11)

#### 6.3 Limitations of direct 2023–2024 Skp-specific primary evidence in retrieved text
The tool-retrieved 2024 primary papers in this session were largely focused on SurA/BAM mechanisms, and the accessible excerpts did not yield Skp-specific primary quantitative results suitable for citation here. Consequently, this report emphasizes well-supported mechanistic and quantitative data from authoritative 2018–2019 reviews and incorporates 2024 reviews mainly for contextual framing of envelope stress/antimicrobial relevance. (bisht2024breakingbarriersexploiting pages 2-3, stull2018periplasmicchaperonesand pages 5-6)

### 7) Current applications and real-world implementations

#### 7.1 Recombinant protein expression and folding assistance
Skp chaperone activity has been used in **protein expression applications**: a review notes that Skp can improve expression yields of recombinant proteins not only in the periplasm but also in the cytosol when using a Skp variant lacking its signal sequence. This is a practical implementation of Skp’s holdase/anti-aggregation activity. (mas2019theperiplasmicchaperones pages 4-7)

#### 7.2 Translational relevance: outer membrane biogenesis/proteostasis as drug-development context
Recent expert reviews argue that envelope biogenesis and envelope stress responses are promising drug-target areas in Gram-negative pathogens; Skp (as a periplasmic OMP chaperone) is part of the proteostasis layer that supports OM integrity. While direct Skp-targeting compounds are not provided in the retrieved excerpts, the logic connecting OMP biogenesis disruption to compromised barrier function is explicit. (bisht2024breakingbarriersexploiting pages 2-3, stull2018periplasmicchaperonesand pages 5-6)

### 8) Expert opinions / authoritative synthesis (interpretive consensus)
Across authoritative reviews, a consistent expert synthesis emerges:
- Skp is an **ATP-independent holdase** specialized for unfolded β-barrel OMPs in the periplasm, with a trimeric cavity that **encapsulates** clients in dynamic ensembles to prevent aggregation. (mas2019theperiplasmicchaperones pages 4-7, stull2018periplasmicchaperonesand pages 4-5)
- The periplasmic chaperone network exhibits **redundancy and pathway partitioning**, with SurA typically the **dominant** route and Skp functioning as **auxiliary/backup** or client-specific pathway; this is supported by the contrast in deletion phenotypes and synthetic lethal genetic interactions. (mas2019theperiplasmicchaperones pages 12-15, stull2018periplasmicchaperonesand pages 5-6)
- Skp’s mechanism depends on **multivalent weak contacts** and high conformational plasticity; these properties are treated as central design features enabling the escort of hydrophobic, aggregation-prone OMPs without ATP. (mas2019theperiplasmicchaperones pages 4-7)

### 9) Key statistics and data points (from the retrieved evidence)
- ~**35%** of the *E. coli* proteome is directed to/associated with the envelope context in which periplasmic chaperones operate. (mas2019theperiplasmicchaperones pages 1-4, mas2018conformationalplasticityof pages 1-2)
- Skp clientome mapping identified **>30** Skp-associated proteins and an “unambiguous” outer-membrane client list including **16** OMPs (as reproduced in the review table). (mas2019theperiplasmicchaperones pages 4-7)
- Client size span described: **~150 residues to >700 residues** (e.g., OmpX to FhuA). (mas2019theperiplasmicchaperones pages 4-7)
- Skp–client binding: **nanomolar-range** affinities (reported in review). (stull2018periplasmicchaperonesand pages 4-5)
- Dynamics: local contacts **≤1 ms** vs global complex lifetimes **hours**; client compactness in cavity **~2× less compact** than denaturant. (mas2019theperiplasmicchaperones pages 4-7)
- Stoichiometry models: **1:1 (small) vs 2:1 (large)** Skp:substrate. (figaj2022suralikeandskplike pages 7-10)

### Functional annotation (concise)
**skp (P0AEU7)** encodes a periplasmic, ATP-independent holdase chaperone that forms a trimeric cavity (“jellyfish-like” Skp trimer) to bind and stabilize unfolded β-barrel outer membrane proteins during periplasmic transit, preventing aggregation and enabling delivery to the BAM complex for insertion/folding into the outer membrane; Skp acts in a genetically redundant network with SurA and DegP and is linked to envelope stress signaling (σE) when impaired. (mas2019theperiplasmicchaperones pages 4-7, stull2018periplasmicchaperonesand pages 5-6, stull2018periplasmicchaperonesand media 95ac5996)
