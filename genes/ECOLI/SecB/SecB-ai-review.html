Gene Description

SecB is a dedicated export chaperone in E. coli that functions as a cytosolic homotetramer (dimer of dimers) in the general secretory (Sec) pathway. It binds newly synthesized precursor proteins in an unfolded or molten-globule state, preventing their premature folding and aggregation, and delivers them to the membrane-associated ATPase SecA for translocation across the inner membrane via the SecYEG translocon (PMID:2170023). SecB functions as an ATP-independent holdase/carrier: it captures unfolded preproteins, wraps them around its tetrameric surface (PMID:16962134), maintains them in a translocation-competent state (PMID:2848249), and then transfers them to SecA through specific binding at the SecA C-terminus (PMID:9321390). Its substrates include a defined subset of exported proteins such as MalE (MBP), OmpA, OmpF, LamB, PhoA, and others (PMID:16352602). SecB was first identified through mutations causing defective protein localization (PMID:6403503). The crystal structure shows a beta-sheet-rich tetramer with helical elements (PDB:1QYN), and NMR studies reveal that substrates wrap around the tetramer, making contact with a large portion of the chaperone surface (PMID:16962134, PMID:27501151).

Core Functions

SecB functions as a dedicated export chaperone/carrier in the Sec-dependent protein translocation pathway. It binds unfolded preprotein substrates in the cytosol, prevents their premature folding (holdase/antifolding activity), and delivers them to SecA at the inner membrane translocon (carrier activity). This is an ATP-independent process.

Research report: secB (UniProt P0AG86) functional annotation in Escherichia coli K-12

1) Identity verification (mandatory)

The gene symbol secB in Escherichia coli (strain K-12) corresponds to the protein-export protein SecB (also called chaperone SecB), a cytosolic secretion chaperone in the bacterial Sec pathway. In authoritative E. coli Sec-system reviews, SecB is explicitly described as a cytoplasmic factor whose binding partners are precursor proteins and SecA, matching the UniProt-provided identity for accession P0AG86 and the SecB-family annotation (SecB/PF02556). (crane2017thesecsystem pages 2-4, crane2017thesecsystem pages 4-6)

2) Key concepts and definitions (current understanding)

2.1. What SecB is (molecular function)

SecB is classically defined as an export chaperone / holdase: it binds secretory preproteins (precursor proteins carrying an N-terminal signal peptide) in nonnative/unfolded conformations, preventing premature folding and aggregation, thereby maintaining translocation competence (the ability of a preprotein to be transported through the Sec channel). (crane2017thesecsystem pages 7-9, crane2017thesecsystem pages 4-6)

SecB is a small acidic protein (155 aa) whose functional unit is a homotetramer (a dimer of dimers; ~68.6 kDa total). (crane2017thesecsystem pages 4-6, findik2017determinationofthe pages 1-2, crane2017thesecsystem pages 7-9)

2.2. “Post-translational Sec pathway” and “translocation-competent state”

In the SecA/SecB post-translational route, preproteins are typically synthesized in the cytosol and must remain devoid of their final stable tertiary structure long enough to be targeted to the Sec translocase (SecA + SecYEG). SecB contributes by capturing nascent or newly synthesized precursors and holding them in an unfolded, soluble, secretion-competent state until handoff to SecA. (crane2017thesecsystem pages 7-9, crane2017thesecsystem pages 4-6)

Mechanistically, SecB uses multiple low-specificity binding subsites and binds unfolded chains promiscuously; peptide binding studies indicate preference for 8–9 aa segments enriched in aromatic and basic residues. For many experimentally tested substrates (36–61 kDa), the binding stoichiometry is typically one polypeptide per SecB tetramer. (crane2017thesecsystem pages 7-9)

2.3. Pathway role and interaction partners

A central concept is that SecB targets and hands off preproteins to SecA, which in turn drives translocation through SecYEG using ATP hydrolysis (with additional contributions from the proton motive force in vivo). (crane2017thesecsystem pages 4-6)

SecB has specific interaction sites for SecA (a “side site” on the SecB tetramer and mobile C-terminal “tail” contacts). Full SecA:SecB complexes can bind two SecA protomers per SecB tetramer and are described as more efficient in coupling SecA ATPase cycles to translocation than partial-contact complexes. (crane2017thesecsystem pages 12-13)

SecB also sits within a broader cytosolic chaperone network; SecB’s requirement for export is often substrate- and condition-dependent, with other chaperones (e.g., DnaK/DnaJ, GroEL/GroES, and trigger factor) sometimes overlapping or modulating export efficiency. (crane2017thesecsystem pages 11-12, crane2017thesecsystem pages 12-13)

3) Cellular localization and biological processes

SecB is a cytosolic chaperone. Its biological role is in Sec-dependent protein export, primarily supporting the post-translational targeting/translocation of a subset of periplasmic and outer-membrane proteins by maintaining precursor proteins in an export-competent state and delivering them to SecA/SecYEG. (crane2017thesecsystem pages 2-4, crane2017thesecsystem pages 4-6)

SecB deletion is not universally lethal in E. coli but can cause cytosolic accumulation of secretory substrates and stress responses; a 2023 Sec-machinery thesis summarizes that secB perturbation leads to accumulation of Sec substrates in the cytoplasm and induction of the σ32 heat-shock response, consistent with loss of cytosolic holdase capacity. (wynne2023investigationofthe pages 33-39)

Multiple quantitative binding parameters have been compiled in the EcoSal Sec-system review:

The Sec machinery is frequently analyzed as an antibiotic target space because it is responsible for exporting most extracytoplasmic proteins in E. coli. 7) Expert opinions / authoritative analysis

Two authoritative review-level perspectives converge on the following expert interpretation:

  • SecB is best understood as a substrate-selective secretion holdase that improves the efficiency and fidelity of post-translational Sec export by buffering a subset of preproteins against folding/aggregation and enhancing productive engagement with SecA/SecYEG. (crane2017thesecsystem pages 11-12, allen2023aunifyingmechanism pages 2-3)
  • In modern Sec-mechanism models, SecB remains important but is one of several cytosolic factors shaping the kinetics of targeting and translocation (e.g., interplay with trigger factor and other chaperones), which helps explain why SecB dependence is conditional and substrate-dependent. (crane2017thesecsystem pages 11-12, crane2017thesecsystem pages 12-13, wynne2023investigationofthe pages 33-39)
