Gene Description

SurA is a periplasmic chaperone that functions as the primary pathway for delivery of unfolded outer membrane proteins (OMPs) to the BAM complex for insertion into the outer membrane. It also possesses peptidyl-prolyl cis-trans isomerase (PPIase) activity via its second parvulin domain, but this enzymatic activity is dispensable for its in vivo chaperone function. The N-terminal domain and C-terminal tail are necessary and sufficient for chaperone activity and OMP substrate recognition. SurA binds preferentially to unfolded OMPs, recognizing aromatic-rich peptide motifs characteristic of integral outer membrane beta-barrel proteins. It functions as a holdase, stabilizing unfolded OMPs in a dynamic state to prevent aggregation while enabling stepwise membrane insertion of beta-hairpins. SurA is required for the efficient folding of OmpA, OmpF, LamB, and FimD, and for overall outer membrane integrity. Loss of SurA leads to decreased outer membrane density and activates the sigmaE stress response.

Core Functions

Chaperone activity -- SurA functions as the primary periplasmic chaperone for outer membrane protein biogenesis. It binds unfolded OMPs via its N-terminal and C-terminal domains (independent of PPIase activity) and escorts them to the BAM complex. SurA acts as a holdase, stabilizing OMPs in a dynamic unfolded state to prevent aggregation while enabling stepwise beta-hairpin membrane insertion.
