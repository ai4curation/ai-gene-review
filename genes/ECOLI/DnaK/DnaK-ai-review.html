Gene Description

DnaK is the E. coli HSP70 homolog and the canonical bacterial chaperone. It is an ATP-dependent foldase that works in concert with its J-domain co-chaperone DnaJ (Hsp40) and nucleotide exchange factor GrpE to assist de novo protein folding and repair heat-damaged proteins. DnaK undergoes ATP-driven conformational cycles: ATP-bound DnaK has an open substrate-binding domain with fast on/off rates; ATP hydrolysis (stimulated by DnaJ and substrate) closes the lid and stabilizes substrate binding; GrpE-catalyzed ADP release and ATP rebinding triggers substrate release for folding. Multiple rounds of this cycle are required for efficient folding of many substrates (PMID:7937953). DnaK also functions as a holdase under some conditions, protecting proteins from aggregation in an ATP-independent manner (PMID:2203539). It cooperates with trigger factor in chaperoning nascent polypeptides (PMID:10380927), and collaborates with ClpB (Hsp100) in disaggregation of protein aggregates. DnaK also plays a regulatory role by binding and inactivating the heat shock transcription factor sigma32 (RpoH) through chaperone-mediated feedback (PMID:8599944). Additionally, DnaK is essential for phage lambda DNA replication and participates in chromosomal DNA replication via interaction with DnaA.

Core Functions

DnaK is the E. coli HSP70 homolog that functions as an ATP-dependent protein folding chaperone (foldase). It binds unfolded polypeptide segments, undergoes ATP hydrolysis- driven conformational changes (with DnaJ and GrpE co-chaperones), and releases substrates to allow folding. Multiple rounds of binding and release are required for efficient folding of most substrates (PMID:7937953). DnaK also protects proteins from heat inactivation (holdase function, ATP-independent; PMID:2203539) and cooperates with trigger factor to chaperone nascent polypeptides (PMID:10380927).
