Gene Description

DnaJ (Hsp40) is the founding member of the J-domain protein family and the primary co-chaperone of the DnaK (Hsp70) chaperone system in E. coli. DnaJ is a modular protein consisting of four domains: an N-terminal J-domain (residues 3-72) that stimulates DnaK ATPase activity via a conserved HPD motif, a glycine/phenylalanine-rich region, a cysteine-rich zinc finger domain (residues 131-209) containing two Zn(2+) centers, and a C-terminal substrate-binding domain. DnaJ functions as a homodimer that binds unfolded or misfolded polypeptides and delivers them to DnaK, stimulating DnaK ATPase activity to form a stable DnaK-substrate complex. Together with GrpE (nucleotide exchange factor), the DnaK/DnaJ/GrpE system constitutes a chaperone machine essential for protein folding, refolding of stress-denatured proteins, regulation of the heat shock response (via sigma32 inactivation), and initiation of phage lambda and plasmid DNA replication. DnaJ also possesses DnaK-independent chaperone activity dependent on zinc center 1, and thiol-disulfide oxidoreductase activity mediated by its zinc finger cysteines. DnaJ is not an independent foldase but rather a co-chaperone that assists the DnaK folding cycle.

Core Functions

DnaJ functions as the primary co-chaperone of the DnaK (Hsp70) chaperone system. It binds unfolded or misfolded substrates via its C-terminal domain and delivers them to DnaK, stimulating DnaK ATPase activity through the conserved HPD motif in its J-domain. Together with GrpE (nucleotide exchange factor), the DnaK/DnaJ/GrpE system refolds stress-denatured proteins and converts stably misfolded proteins into intermediates that spontaneously refold to the native state (PMID:20953191). DnaJ is the founding member of the J-domain protein family.

- description: DnaJ functions as the primary co-chaperone of the DnaK (Hsp70) chaperone
    system. It binds unfolded or misfolded substrates via its C-terminal domain and
    delivers them to DnaK, stimulating DnaK ATPase activity through the conserved
    HPD motif in its J-domain. Together with GrpE (nucleotide exchange factor), the
    DnaK/DnaJ/GrpE system refolds stress-denatured proteins and converts stably misfolded
    proteins into intermediates that spontaneously refold to the native state (PMID:20953191).
    DnaJ is the founding member of the J-domain protein family.
