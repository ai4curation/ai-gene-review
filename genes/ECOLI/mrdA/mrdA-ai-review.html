Gene Description

MrdA (also known as PBP2 or PbpA) is a class B penicillin-binding protein that functions as a monofunctional D,D-transpeptidase (EC 3.4.16.4) essential for lateral cell wall synthesis and rod shape determination in E. coli. It catalyzes the formation of 4-3 peptide crosslinks in peptidoglycan by transferring the terminal D-Ala from stem peptides to acceptor meso-diaminopimelate (mDAP) residues on neighboring glycan strands. MrdA works in partnership with RodA (a SEDS-family glycan polymerase) as the core elongasome synthase complex, with additional components including MreB, MreC, MreD, and RodZ. The protein is anchored in the inner membrane with its large catalytic domain exposed to the periplasm. MrdA is specifically inhibited by mecillinam (amdinocillin) and is also targeted by other beta-lactam antibiotics and diazabicyclooctane (DBO) inhibitors like avibactam. Loss of MrdA function causes cells to become spherical (cocci), demonstrating its essential role in maintaining rod morphology.

Proposed New Ontology Terms

peptidoglycan D,D-transpeptidase activity

Definition: Catalysis of the formation of 4-3 peptide crosslinks in peptidoglycan by transferring the penultimate D-alanine residue from position 4 of one stem peptide to the meso-diaminopimelate (mDAP) at position 3 of an acceptor stem peptide on a neighboring glycan strand. This is distinct from L,D-transpeptidase activity which forms 3-3 crosslinks.

Justification: GO currently has GO:0071972 (peptidoglycan L,D-transpeptidase activity) but no corresponding term for D,D-transpeptidase activity. D,D-transpeptidases (class B PBPs like MrdA/PBP2) and L,D-transpeptidases (like YcbB/LdtD) catalyze fundamentally different crosslinking reactions and have different substrate specificities, inhibitor sensitivities, and active site chemistry. A specific term would improve annotation accuracy. Currently GO:0009002 is used as a proxy but it emphasizes the carboxypeptidase aspect rather than the transpeptidation.

Parent term: aminoacyltransferase activity

proposed_new_terms:
  - proposed_name: peptidoglycan D,D-transpeptidase activity
    proposed_definition: >
      Catalysis of the formation of 4-3 peptide crosslinks in peptidoglycan by transferring
      the penultimate D-alanine residue from position 4 of one stem peptide to the
      meso-diaminopimelate (mDAP) at position 3 of an acceptor stem peptide on a neighboring
      glycan strand. This is distinct from L,D-transpeptidase activity which forms
      3-3 crosslinks.
    justification: >
      GO currently has GO:0071972 (peptidoglycan L,D-transpeptidase activity) but
      no corresponding
      term for D,D-transpeptidase activity. D,D-transpeptidases (class B PBPs like
      MrdA/PBP2) and
      L,D-transpeptidases (like YcbB/LdtD) catalyze fundamentally different crosslinking
      reactions
