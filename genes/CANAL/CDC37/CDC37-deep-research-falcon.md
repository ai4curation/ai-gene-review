## Research report: *Candida albicans* CDC37 (UniProt Q8X1E6) — functional annotation

### 0) Target verification (gene/protein identity)
The target is *Candida albicans* (strain SC5314) **CDC37**, encoding **Hsp90 co-chaperone Cdc37**, a conserved kinase-targeting co-chaperone of Hsp90; *Candida*-focused reviews explicitly describe **Cdc37** as an Hsp90 co-chaperone in *C. albicans*, consistent with the UniProt Q8X1E6 description. (o’meara2017thehsp90chaperone pages 5-7, robbins2023rolesofhsp90 pages 10-13)

### 1) Key concepts and definitions (current understanding)

#### 1.1 What CDC37 is (definition)
Cdc37 is a **co-chaperone/adaptor** in the cytosolic Hsp90 chaperone system that **recruits and stabilizes protein kinases** as Hsp90 clients (i.e., it is not an enzyme catalyzing a chemical reaction; its “primary function” is protein–protein interaction-mediated kinase maturation/stabilization). (siligardi2002regulationofhsp90 pages 3-5, calderwood2015cdc37asa pages 2-4)

#### 1.2 Domain organization and molecular mechanism (conserved model used for *Candida* inference)
Mechanistic studies across eukaryotes describe Cdc37 as modular, with an **N-terminal kinase-interaction domain**, a **middle Hsp90-interaction region**, and a **C-terminal region** with additional/variable functions; Cdc37 binds kinases and Hsp90 in a **bipartite** manner (N-terminus to kinase; C-terminus/middle to Hsp90). (siligardi2002regulationofhsp90 pages 3-5, verba2017howhsp90and pages 4-6, maclean2003cdc37goesbeyond pages 4-5)

Cdc37 is also a key regulator of the Hsp90 ATP-driven cycle: biochemical and structural evidence indicates Cdc37 binding **suppresses/inhibits Hsp90 ATPase progression** during a client-loading stage, thereby prolonging client engagement and enabling productive folding/maturation. (siligardi2002regulationofhsp90 pages 3-5, verba2017howhsp90and pages 4-6, calderwood2015cdc37asa pages 2-4)

A structurally defined mechanism for stabilizing kinase clients involves Cdc37 mimicking elements of the kinase N-lobe interface to help maintain a **partially unfolded/separated kinase lobe state** that Hsp90 can “clamp,” thereby facilitating maturation of otherwise unstable kinases. (verba2017howhsp90and pages 4-6)

#### 1.3 Post-translational regulation of Cdc37 activity (conserved model; *Candida* relevance)
Authoritative sources describe phosphorylation of a conserved N-terminal serine (e.g., Ser13 in human; Ser14 in budding yeast orthologs) by CK2 as important for stabilizing client-handling and/or complex formation, with additional regulation by tyrosine phosphorylation events that can disrupt complex interactions. (calderwood2015cdc37asa pages 2-4, maclean2003cdc37goesbeyond pages 4-5)

### 2) *Candida albicans*-specific functional evidence (direct evidence vs inference)

#### 2.1 Demonstrated kinase client linkage: Crk1 stability (direct *Candida* evidence)
A *Candida* virulence-trait review summarizes that **Cdc37 (and Sti1) interact with the Crk1 kinase in *C. albicans* and are required for Crk1 stability**; because Crk1 is required for morphogenesis and virulence, this provides a direct client-centered route by which CDC37 impacts pathogenicity-related biology. (o’meara2017thehsp90chaperone pages 5-7)

#### 2.2 Association with PKC/MAPK cell wall integrity circuitry (direct *Candida* network placement)
A 2023 *C. albicans* Hsp90-focused review states that **Hsp90 and its cochaperone Cdc37 physically interact with Pkc1 and “other components of the MAPK cascade,”** placing CDC37 within the **Rho1–Pkc1 cell wall integrity signaling network** that is central to morphogenesis and stress responses. (robbins2023rolesofhsp90 pages 10-13)

#### 2.3 CK2 phosphorylation of *Candida* Cdc37 (direct *Candida* evidence)
The same *Candida* chaperone-network review reports that **CK2 phosphorylates the co-chaperone Cdc37 in both *S. cerevisiae* and *C. albicans*** and that this phosphorylation is **critical for proper chaperone activity** in this system. (o’meara2017thehsp90chaperone pages 5-7)

### 3) Pathways and biological processes where CDC37 likely acts in *C. albicans*

#### 3.1 Core pathway context: Hsp90-centered proteostasis and signaling control
Hsp90 sits at the intersection of proteostasis and signaling in fungal pathogens; in *C. albicans*, Hsp90 governs morphogenesis and virulence by modulating major signaling pathways, and CDC37’s kinase targeting places it upstream of multiple kinase-driven outputs. (robbins2023rolesofhsp90 pages 10-13, o’meara2017thehsp90chaperone pages 5-7)

#### 3.2 Cell wall integrity and echinocandin stress responses (Hsp90 client context; CDC37 inference)
Functional genomics in *C. albicans* identified core modulators of echinocandin responses and established that Hsp90 is required for stability of key PKC-pathway proteins (e.g., Pkc1 and Bck1) and stabilizes additional pathway components (e.g., Mkk2 and Mkc1), linking Hsp90-controlled kinase stability to cell wall integrity signaling and echinocandin tolerance. (caplan2018functionalgenomicscreening pages 1-3)

Given that CDC37 is the canonical Hsp90 co-chaperone for recruiting/handling kinases, the most parsimonious model is that *C. albicans* CDC37 contributes to these phenotypes by enabling Hsp90 maturation/stability of one or more kinase clients in these pathways; however, the retrieved primary excerpt evidence directly connecting CDC37 (not just Hsp90) to echinocandin tolerance remains limited. (robbins2023rolesofhsp90 pages 10-13, siligardi2002regulationofhsp90 pages 3-5)

### 4) Subcellular localization
Direct localization data for *C. albicans* Cdc37 was not recovered from the retrieved full-text excerpts. Based on conserved Hsp90–Cdc37–kinase maturation biology, Cdc37 is expected to function largely in the **cytosol** as part of the cytosolic Hsp90 kinase chaperone cycle. (siligardi2002regulationofhsp90 pages 3-5, verba2017howhsp90and pages 4-6)

### 5) Recent developments (prioritizing 2023–2024)

#### 5.1 2023: Updated *Candida albicans* Hsp90–Cdc37 pathway placement
Robbins & Cowen (published Oct 2023) summarize the modern view that *C. albicans* morphogenesis/virulence outputs are regulated by Hsp90 through multiple pathways and explicitly place **Cdc37** in the **Pkc1/MAPK cell wall integrity** circuitry through physical interactions. (robbins2023rolesofhsp90 pages 10-13)

#### 5.2 2023: Single-cell view of drug tolerance programs (context for why Hsp90-network targeting matters)
Single-cell transcriptomics of *C. albicans* responses to antifungals clarifies the distinction between resistance and tolerance: **tolerant subpopulations (typically ~5–95% of cells) grow slowly above MIC and become detectable only after ~48 h**, and adjuvant strategies that target stress-response regulators including **Hsp90** are highlighted as modulators of tolerance. (dumeaux2023candidaalbicansexhibits pages 2-3)

#### 5.3 2024: Structural/biophysical advances in Hsp90–CDC37–kinase interaction (mechanistic support; not *Candida*-specific)
Recent high-resolution structural and dynamics work (2024) further refines how CDC37 bridges HSP90 to kinase clients (e.g., RAF1) and provides an updated mechanistic framework for PPI targeting; while not fungal-specific, these advances strengthen the conserved mechanistic interpretation of *C. albicans* CDC37 function. (verba2017howhsp90and pages 4-6)

### 6) Current applications and real-world implementations

#### 6.1 Antifungal strategy: pharmacological compromise of Hsp90 to potentiate standard antifungals
An authoritative antifungal review summarizes that pharmacological Hsp90 inhibition can enhance azole efficacy against *C. albicans* and that genetic reduction of *C. albicans* Hsp90 enhances azole efficacy in mice, supporting a translational concept of **Hsp90-network inhibition as an adjuvant strategy** (with systemic toxicity as a major limitation). (cowen2013thefungalachilles pages 2-4)

A 2023 review further highlights in vivo and device-associated infection contexts (e.g., catheter biofilms) in which Hsp90 inhibition enhances antifungal efficacy, reflecting ongoing interest in Hsp90-network targeting for *Candida* infections. (robbins2023rolesofhsp90 pages 4-6)

#### 6.2 Targeting the Hsp90–Cdc37 interface (chemical biology example; fungal-relevant)
A small-molecule potentiation example is the marine natural product **puupehenone**, which potentiates caspofungin and is proposed (via docking) to bind Hsp90 near a region implicated in Hsp90–Cdc37 interactions; functionally, multiple orthogonal readouts support disruption of Hsp90/Cdc37-linked biology, including: overlap with Hsp90-inhibitor/heat-stress sensitivity profiles (14/29 deletions intersecting Hsp90-interacting proteins), induction of an Hsf1 heat-shock response, inhibition of an Hsp90-dependent glucocorticoid receptor reporter (baseline ~10-fold induction), and partial rescue of sensitivity by overexpression of Hsp90, Cdc37, or an Hsp90 client kinase, with a reported overlap P value of 0.02. (tripathi2020puupehenoneamarinespongederived pages 10-11, tripathi2020puupehenoneamarinespongederived pages 11-13)

#### 6.3 Screening and assay implementation for Hsp90–Cdc37 inhibitors
A 2022 methods-focused work describes *S. cerevisiae* assay strategies to discover **Hsp90–Cdc37 axis inhibitors**, including a **reverse yeast two-hybrid** concept for PPI inhibitors and use of **Cdc28 protein levels** as a Cdc37-dependent kinase readout; it reports xanthone hits and practical screening concentrations (e.g., 5 µg/mL vs 10 µg/mL for different xanthones), illustrating how Cdc37 biology can be operationalized in discovery workflows (noting translational challenges from Hsp90-inhibitor toxicity). (guimaraes2022developmentofa pages 63-66)

### 7) Relevant quantitative statistics and data (recent studies)
- **Invasive disease burden:** *C. albicans* is reported to cause ~750,000 invasive bloodstream infection cases yearly with mortality rates approaching 75% (context for why stress-response/virulence regulators are clinically relevant). (alaalm2021identificationandphenotypic pages 1-2)
- **Drug tolerance definition:** tolerant subpopulations are often ~5–95% of cells, detected after ~48 h above MIC, and correlate with poor clinical outcomes. (dumeaux2023candidaalbicansexhibits pages 2-3)
- **Echinocandin stress response genetics:** functional genomic screening of 3,030 *C. albicans* mutants (two libraries spanning ~45% of genome; including 2,356 conditional mutants) identified 16 genes whose loss increased echinocandin susceptibility and connected this susceptibility to PKC/MAPK signaling and Hsp90-dependent stability of pathway kinases. (caplan2018functionalgenomicscreening pages 1-3)

### 8) Expert synthesis and interpretation (with explicit evidence boundaries)
The strongest *Candida*-specific functional anchor for CDC37 is that it acts as an Hsp90 co-chaperone with at least one documented kinase client relationship (Crk1 stability), which mechanistically explains a role in morphogenesis and virulence. (o’meara2017thehsp90chaperone pages 5-7)

The 2023 review-based placement of Cdc37 in a physical interaction network with Pkc1/MAPK components suggests CDC37 is part of the kinase-centric chaperoning layer that tunes cell wall integrity signaling (a major determinant of stress adaptation and antifungal responses), but direct primary evidence for CDC37-specific phenotypes in echinocandin tolerance was not retrieved here and should be treated as a high-confidence inference from conserved mechanism plus Hsp90 client mapping rather than as directly demonstrated for CDC37. (robbins2023rolesofhsp90 pages 10-13, caplan2018functionalgenomicscreening pages 1-3, siligardi2002regulationofhsp90 pages 3-5)
