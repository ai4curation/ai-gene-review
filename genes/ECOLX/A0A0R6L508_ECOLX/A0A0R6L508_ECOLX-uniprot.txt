CC   -!- FUNCTION: Probably catalyzes the addition of a phosphoethanolamine
CC       moiety to lipid A (PubMed:26603172, PubMed:29079699).
CC       Phosphoethanolamine modification of lipid A confers polymyxin
CC       resistance (PubMed:26603172). Confers resistance to polymyxin-type
CC       antibiotics such as colistin; in the E.coli strain W3110
CC       (PubMed:26603172). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:26603172,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:29079699}.

CC   -!- MISCELLANEOUS: This is the first (November 2015) reported plasmid-
CC       mediated resistance to polymyxin antibiotics; although it does not
CC       confer a high MIC it protects against colistin treatment in a mouse
CC       infection model (PubMed:26603172). Colistin is one of the last-resort
CC       antibiotics available for multidrug-resistant Gram-negative bacteria
CC       such as K.pneumoniae, P.aeruginosa and Acinetobacter. First identified
CC       from a pig farm in Shanghai, China in July 2013, retrospective
CC       screening detects the gene in (slowly) increasing proportions between
CC       2011 and 2015 from pigs and chickens, and also in hospitalized patients
CC       in China. Colistin is very widely used in veterinary agriculture
CC       (PubMed:26603172). The pHNSHP45 plasmid can transfer efficiently to
CC       other E.coli strains by conjugation and increases polymxin MIC by 8- to
CC       16-fold; it may not require selective pressure to be maintained in the
CC       cell. When transformed into K.pneumoniae or P.aeruginosa it also
CC       increases polymxin MIC 8- to 16-fold (PubMed:26603172).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:26603172}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the phosphoethanolamine transferase family.
CC       {ECO:0000305}.
