| Identity/Domain | • **Verified target:** zebrafish **hif1an / fih-1** encodes **Factor Inhibiting HIF-1 (FIH-1)**, matching UniProt **P59723**; literature consistently treats it as the zebrafish ortholog of HIF1AN/FIH. • FIH is a **JmjC/cupin-fold Fe(II)/2-oxoglutarate oxygenase**; dimerization is required for full activity. • Functional conservation is high in fish; zebrafish enzymatic JmjC region was reported as **96% homologous** to human FIH. Sources: Hyvärinen 2010; Elks et al. 2015, https://doi.org/10.1242/dmm.021865; Volkova et al. 2022, https://doi.org/10.1089/ars.2022.0003 | (pqac-00000004, pqac-00000016, pqac-00000018, pqac-00000019) |
| Enzymatic reaction & cofactors | • FIH is a **Fe(II)/2-oxoglutarate-dependent dioxygenase**: one O atom is inserted into the substrate residue and the other oxidatively decarboxylates **2-oxoglutarate → succinate + CO2**. • Requires **Fe(II)**, **O2**, **2-oxoglutarate**, and typically a reductant such as **ascorbate**; catalysis proceeds via a **ferryl Fe(IV)=O** intermediate. • Ordered binding reported as **Fe2+ → 2-OG → peptide substrate → O2**. Sources: Judson 2012; Hyvärinen 2010; Volkova et al. 2022, https://doi.org/10.1089/ars.2022.0003 | (pqac-00000001, pqac-00000014, pqac-00000015, pqac-00000016, pqac-00000018, pqac-00000019) |
| Canonical substrate & mechanism | • Canonical substrate is the **C-terminal transactivation domain (C-TAD)** of HIF-α; FIH hydroxylates the conserved **Asn803 of HIF-1α** (and analogous site in HIF-2α). • This hydroxylation blocks **p300/CBP** co-activator recruitment, thereby repressing **HIF transcriptional activity** without using the VHL-dependent degradation route. • Adjacent features can tune regulation; e.g., **Thr796 phosphorylation** can reduce Asn803 hydroxylation and favor transactivation. Sources: Judson 2012; Elks et al. 2015, https://doi.org/10.1242/dmm.021865; Volkova et al. 2022, https://doi.org/10.1089/ars.2022.0003 | (pqac-00000001, pqac-00000004, pqac-00000006, pqac-00000007, pqac-00000014, pqac-00000015) |
| Oxygen sensitivity/kinetics | • Reported **KM(O2) ≈ 90 ± 20 µM** for FIH, lower than many PHD values; this supports the idea that **PHDs shut off earlier**, whereas FIH can remain active until **more severe hypoxia**. • Because physiological cellular O2 is often below saturation, both PHDs and FIH can function as oxygen sensors, but with distinct set points. • Zebrafish-specific kinetic constants were **not identified** in the retrieved evidence; current quantitative kinetics are largely inferred from mammalian biochemical work. Sources: Judson 2012; Volkova et al. 2022, https://doi.org/10.1089/ars.2022.0003 | (pqac-00000006, pqac-00000014, pqac-00000015) |
| Non-HIF substrates (general vertebrate) | • FIH has broad substrate scope beyond HIF, especially **ankyrin repeat domain (ARD)** proteins; proposed loose motif: **LXXXXX[D/E]VN**, though structural context is critical. • Reported non-HIF substrates include **OTUB1 (Asn22)**, **Cezanne/OTUD7B (Asn35)**, **TRPV3**, **HACE1**, **RIPK4**, **G9a/GLP**, **IκBα**, **Notch ARD**, **ASPP2**, and possibly **NAA10**; FIH can also hydroxylate residues other than Asn and form **oxygen-dependent “oxomers.”** • Several newer studies emphasize FIH as a regulator of signaling, ubiquitin biology, inflammation, and cancer progression, not just HIF. Sources: Volkova et al. 2022, https://doi.org/10.1089/ars.2022.0003; García-del Río et al. 2023, https://doi.org/10.1172/jci.insight.167394 | (pqac-00000005, pqac-00000020, pqac-00000021, pqac-00000022, pqac-00000023, pqac-00000025, pqac-00000026) |
| Zebrafish phenotypes & developmental roles | • **Maternal and early embryonic expression** is present; morpholino knockdown caused **ectopic intersegmental vessel sprouts** at ~**80 and 122 hpf**, increased endothelial proliferation, and elevated **vegf-aa165**, consistent with an **anti-angiogenic** role. • **fih-1 overexpression** reduced ISV formation and circulation; co-expression of **vegf-aa165** rescued this, placing FIH upstream of **HIF/VEGF-A signaling** in vascular development. • Genetic loss-of-function produced milder basal phenotypes than MO studies, but in **vhl-deficient** backgrounds enhanced HIF/vascular phenotypes (greater branching, larger heart/heart cavity, higher vegfaa, increased endothelial cell number); review evidence also states CRISPR deletion can **increase hypoxia tolerance** and reduce brain apoptosis after hypoxia. Sources: So et al. 2014, https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pone.0109517; Judson 2012; Volkova et al. 2022, https://doi.org/10.1089/ars.2022.0003 | (pqac-00000002, pqac-00000008, pqac-00000009, pqac-00000010, pqac-00000011, pqac-00000012, pqac-00000027) |
| Localization/expression | • FIH is described generally as **mainly cytoplasmic**, though vertebrate studies show it can also be **nuclear**; zebrafish/cultured-cell work reported **colocalization with Mindbomb/Mib1**. • In zebrafish, **fih-1** is detectable from very early development (including **4-cell stage**), then becomes enriched in **ventral mesoderm, eye, and midbrain-hindbrain boundary**. • Review evidence notes tissue variation across fish and states endogenous zebrafish FIH expression is highest in **liver** in one dataset.
