Gene Description

spns1 (nrs/spinl) encodes a lysosomal-membrane major facilitator superfamily (spinster-family) transporter, experimentally deorphanized as a proton-dependent lysophospholipid efflux transporter with substrate specificity for the zwitterionic lysophospholipids lysophosphatidylcholine (LPC) and lysophosphatidylethanolamine (LPE) (He et al. 2022 PNAS, PMID:36161949). It exports these lysolipids from the lysosomal lumen to the cytosolic side, enabling lysosomal phospholipid salvage and maintaining lysosomal homeostasis. Transport is pH-dependent (acidic optimum ~5-6), consistent with proton coupling for an MFS transporter at the lysosome. A mammalian genetics study additionally proposes a role in lysosomal sphingosine release (Ha et al. 2024 JCI Insight, PMID:38451736), but the highest-confidence substrate assignment remains LPC/LPE. Autophagy, autophagosome maturation, cardiac valve morphogenesis (via notch1 signaling; Chávez et al. 2024 iScience, PMID:39720516), and cellular/developmental senescence are downstream consequences of impaired lysosomal lipid efflux rather than the molecular core, which is lysosomal lysophospholipid transmembrane transport.

Proposed New Ontology Terms

proton-dependent lysosomal lysophospholipid efflux transporter activity

Definition: Catalysis of the proton-dependent transfer of lysophospholipids (e.g. lysophosphatidylcholine and lysophosphatidylethanolamine) from the lysosomal lumen across the lysosomal membrane to the cytosolic side.

Justification: Spns1 is experimentally demonstrated to be a proton-dependent lysosomal efflux transporter selective for the zwitterionic lysophospholipids LPC and LPE (PMID:36161949 He et al. 2022 PNAS; falcon deep research). No existing GO molecular-function term captures this activity: GO:0022857 (transmembrane transporter activity) is far too broad, and GO:0051978 (the only lysophospholipid transporter MF term) is specifically a lysophospholipid:SODIUM symporter, whereas Spns1 is proton-coupled and operates in the efflux direction. The proposed parent GO:0015562 (efflux transmembrane transporter activity) is OLS-verified as a valid, non-obsolete MF term (child of transmembrane transporter activity GO:0022857 under transporter activity GO:0005215), making it an appropriate is_a parent.

Research report: Zebrafish spns1 (UniProt Q7ZU13) — functional annotation and biological roles

0) Target verification (critical disambiguation)

The target is Danio rerio spns1 (synonyms nrs, spinl), encoding Protein spinster homolog 1, a major facilitator superfamily (MFS) spinster-like multi-pass membrane protein (UniProt Q7ZU13). The literature synthesized here explicitly concerns zebrafish spns1/nrs and/or orthologous SPNS1/Spns1 that matches the same MFS-spinster family and lysosomal transporter role; studies focused on SPNS2 are not used to annotate spns1 function except as contextual comparison. (he2022spns1isa pages 3-5, chavez2024spns1dependentendocardiallysosomal pages 1-4, ha2024lackofspns1 pages 1-2)

1) Key concepts and definitions (current understanding)

1.1 Spinster (SPNS) family and MFS transporters

SPNS1 is part of the spinster-like branch of the MFS transporter superfamily, which typically mediates small-molecule transport across membranes using ion gradients (often H+ coupling). In the context of SPNS1, the key concept is lysosomal metabolite efflux: lysosomes hydrolyze complex lipids, but the breakdown products must exit the lysosome for reuse; impaired efflux can lead to lysosomal storage–like phenotypes. (he2022spns1isa pages 1-2, he2022spns1isa pages 5-7)

1.2 “Lysosomal phospholipid salvage”

He et al. define a lysosomal salvage pathway in which lysophospholipids generated during lysosomal phospholipid catabolism (notably LPC from phosphatidylcholine and LPE from phosphatidylethanolamine) are exported from lysosomes and subsequently re-acylated to replenish cytosolic membrane phospholipids. They emphasize that LPC and LPE are zwitterionic and do not readily diffuse across membranes, motivating the need for a transporter. (he2022spns1isa pages 1-2, he2022spns1isa pages 3-5)

2) Primary molecular function of zebrafish Spns1 (substrate specificity and mechanism)

2.1 Demonstrated substrate: LPC and LPE (primary, best-supported)

A major advance is the functional deorphanization of SPNS1/Spns1 as a proton-dependent lysophospholipid transporter with specificity for lysophosphatidylcholine (LPC) and lysophosphatidylethanolamine (LPE). Transport assays show significant uptake activity for labeled LPC, with specificity excluding several other lipid classes (e.g., sphingosine-1-phosphate and multiple anionic lysolipids in the tested assay conditions). (he2022spns1isa pages 1-2, he2022spns1isa pages 2-3)

Visual support from the primary paper’s transport-assay figure (showing mutant effects, pH dependence, and lysolipid specificity) is provided in the retrieved figure crops. (he2022spns1isa media 37123e07, he2022spns1isa media 9b17cfa8, he2022spns1isa media 455e4d18, he2022spns1isa media 26578186, he2022spns1isa media 9b281958)

2.2 Coupling and key residues (mechanistic inference)

Transport is pH-dependent with an optimum in the acidic pH range (~5–6), consistent with proton coupling expected for an MFS transporter operating at the lysosome. Conserved residues R76, E164, and H427 are experimentally implicated: mutating these markedly reduces/abolishes transport activity, consistent with essential roles in substrate/ion coupling or conformational cycling. (he2022spns1isa pages 2-3, he2022spns1isa media 37123e07)

2.3 Is SPNS1 also a sphingosine exporter?

A 2024 mouse/human genetics study argues that SPNS1 is required for lysosomal sphingosine release in addition to lysoglycerophospholipid export, supported by lipid accumulation patterns and lysosome-release assays, and noting that some assays show reduced S1P synthesis consistent with impaired sphingosine availability. However, this work also acknowledges that direct demonstration of sphingosine transport by the same cell-surface uptake approach used for LPC has been challenging in prior work, so the highest-confidence substrate assignment remains LPC/LPE across studies. (ha2024lackofspns1 pages 1-2, ha2024lackofspns1 pages 6-7, ha2024lackofspns1 pages 9-11)

3) Subcellular localization and site of action

SPNS1/Spns1 is primarily a lysosomal/late endosomal membrane protein. In mammals, it colocalizes with LAMP1 (lysosomes) and Rab7 (late endosomes), consistent with trafficking through the endocytic system and a role in lysosomal efflux. In zebrafish, functional imaging and ultrastructure place spns1 activity in autolysosomal/lysosomal compartments in specific tissues such as the endocardium during valve morphogenesis, with abnormal lysosomal cargo accumulation upon loss of spns1. (ha2024lackofspns1 pages 2-4, chavez2024spns1dependentendocardiallysosomal pages 8-10)

4) Biological processes and pathways in zebrafish

4.1 Lysosome homeostasis and autophagy-associated processes

Loss of Spns1 causes enlarged/abnormal lysosomes and biochemical signatures consistent with lysosomal dysfunction and perturbation of autophagy-associated flux (e.g., elevated LC3 markers in mammalian knockdown/KO contexts; zebrafish tissues show accumulated autolysosomal/lysosomal structures and partially degraded cargo by TEM). These phenotypes are consistent with a model in which lysosomal lipid efflux is required to maintain normal lysosomal composition, pH-dependent hydrolase function, and degradative capacity. (he2022spns1isa pages 2-3, he2022spns1isa pages 5-7, chavez2024spns1dependentendocardiallysosomal pages 8-10)

4.2 Valve morphogenesis via endocardial lysosomal function and Notch1 signaling (2024 zebrafish development)

A 2024 iScience study provides direct zebrafish mechanistic insight: spns1 (nrs) is required cell-autonomously in endocardial cells for lysosomal/autophagic processing that enables cardiac valve morphogenesis through notch1 signaling. The study reports strong penetrance of valve-cell marker loss: ALCAM+ valve endothelial cells were frequent in siblings but substantially reduced in mutants (e.g., at 3 dpf OFT 91% siblings vs 40% mutants; AVC 73% vs 26%; and at 4 dpf OFT 100% vs 31%; AVC 85% vs 28%, with reported sample sizes in the paper). Endocardial-specific spns1 overexpression partially rescues valve structure/function and reduces accumulation of partially degraded lysosomal contents by TEM. (chavez2024spns1dependentendocardiallysosomal pages 10-12, chavez2024spns1dependentendocardiallysosomal pages 12-13, chavez2024spns1dependentendocardiallysosomal pages 8-10)

This positions zebrafish spns1 as a key node linking lysosome functional state to developmental signaling output (Notch-dependent valve morphogenesis). (chavez2024spns1dependentendocardiallysosomal pages 12-13)

4.3 Lysosomal pH-linked tissue degeneration (zebrafish skeletal muscle)

In zebrafish, spns1 loss has been linked to dysregulated lysosomal pH with downstream tissue consequences. A muscle-focused study reports that muscle development is initially normal, but degeneration appears around 3.5 dpf, with evidence of altered ECM regulation and strong upregulation of MMP-9 (reported as ~15.1-fold mRNA increase) and statistically significant protein-level differences in their assays; the authors emphasize lysosomal acidity as essential for hydrolase activity (pH ~4.5–5) and connect spns1 loss to impaired lysosomal proteolysis. (coffey2021lysosomalfunctionimpacts pages 17-19, coffey2021lysosomalfunctionimpacts pages 19-20)

5) Quantitative evidence and key statistics (selected)

5.1 Transport and lipidomics metrics supporting the lysolipid-export model

  • In Spns1 KO cells, lysosome-enriched fractions show ~5-fold higher labeled LPC accumulation, and KO cells show ~25% less incorporation of [14C]-oleate into phosphatidylcholine over a short labeling interval, consistent with impaired lysosomal lysolipid salvage into PC pools. (he2022spns1isa pages 2-3)
  • In mouse liver Spns1 knockdown, lysosome-enriched fractions show 8–13-fold accumulation of LPC/LPE/LPG species, ~12-fold higher BMP, and ~3-fold higher total lysosomal lipid (normalized to protein), consistent with lysosomal lipid storage driven by export failure. (he2022spns1isa pages 5-7)

6) Recent developments (prioritizing 2023–2024)

6.1 2024: Human disease relevance and expanded substrate considerations

A 2024 JCI Insight study links SPNS1 dysfunction to lysosomal storage disease–like phenotypes in mouse models and reports three human siblings with a homozygous SPNS1 variant presenting neurodevelopmental impairment (developmental delay, neurological impairment/intellectual disability, cerebellar hypoplasia). The study supports a model in which SPNS1 is required for export of LPC (and other lysolipids) and sphingosine from lysosomes, and identifies downstream impacts including altered PI3K/AKT signaling (reduced Akt phosphorylation in their assays). (ha2024lackofspns1 pages 1-2, ha2024lackofspns1 pages 7-9, ha2024lackofspns1 pages 6-7)

6.2 2024: Developmental organogenesis mechanism (zebrafish heart valves)

The 2024 iScience work is a major zebrafish-specific advance because it connects spns1-dependent lysosomal function to a defined developmental signaling program (Notch1) and a discrete morphogenetic outcome (valve formation), supported by transcriptomics, imaging reporters, and rescue. (chavez2024spns1dependentendocardiallysosomal pages 1-4, chavez2024spns1dependentendocardiallysosomal pages 12-13)

7) Current applications and real-world implementations

  1. Zebrafish as an in vivo lysosome/autophagy and organogenesis model: spns1 mutants/crispants provide a tractable vertebrate system to interrogate how lysosomal lipid transport affects autolysosomal function, tissue remodeling, and developmental signaling (Notch-driven valve morphogenesis). (chavez2024spns1dependentendocardiallysosomal pages 1-4, chavez2024spns1dependentendocardiallysosomal pages 8-10)
  2. Disease modeling for lysosomal storage-like disorders: Mammalian KO/KD phenotypes and emerging human genetics support SPNS1 as a candidate in lysosome-related lipid storage pathologies, motivating SPNS1 pathway interrogation in translational research. (ha2024lackofspns1 pages 1-2, ha2024lackofspns1 pages 7-9)
  3. Metabolic stress/nutrient limitation implications: The lysosomal phospholipid salvage pathway provides a mechanistic basis for why SPNS1 could be important when lipid precursors are limiting, since export/recycling of lysolipids contributes to membrane lipid maintenance and broader cellular homeostasis. (he2022spns1isa pages 5-7, ha2024lackofspns1 pages 13-14)

8) Expert opinions and interpretation (evidence-weighted synthesis)

Best-supported primary function in zebrafish (DANRE/Q7ZU13): Spns1 is most strongly supported as a lysosomal proton-coupled exporter of LPC and LPE, enabling lysosomal phospholipid salvage and maintaining lysosome functional integrity. (he2022spns1isa pages 1-2, he2022spns1isa pages 2-3, he2022spns1isa pages 3-5)

How this explains zebrafish phenotypes: Failure to export lysolipids likely leads to lysosomal lipid buildup, altered membrane properties/pH regulation, impaired degradative processing, and tissue-specific consequences. In the heart endocardium, impaired lysosomal processing is linked to altered Notch pathway output and valve morphogenesis defects; in muscle, lysosomal dysfunction correlates with degeneration and ECM remodeling responses. (chavez2024spns1dependentendocardiallysosomal pages 8-10, chavez2024spns1dependentendocardiallysosomal pages 10-12, coffey2021lysosomalfunctionimpacts pages 17-19)

Open questions: Whether zebrafish Spns1 transports sphingosine directly (as suggested by mammalian physiology/genetics) remains less certain mechanistically than LPC/LPE export, and may depend on assay context or additional factors. (ha2024lackofspns1 pages 9-11, ha2024lackofspns1 pages 1-2)
