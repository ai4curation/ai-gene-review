Gene Description

hsd11b2 encodes zebrafish 11-beta-hydroxysteroid dehydrogenase type 2, a membrane short-chain dehydrogenase/reductase that oxidizes cortisol and related active 11-beta-hydroxy steroids to inactive 11-keto forms. Its core role is NAD(+)-dependent glucocorticoid/cortisol catabolism, with stress-axis effects downstream of this steroid-inactivating activity.

Core Functions

hsd11b2 performs NAD(+)-dependent 11-beta-hydroxysteroid dehydrogenase activity, oxidizing cortisol to cortisone and thereby contributing directly to cortisol and glucocorticoid metabolic processes at membranes.

Notes

(hsd11b2-notes.md)

Notes for DANRE hsd11b2

  • Core function is NAD(+)-dependent cortisol/11-beta-hydroxysteroid oxidation to inactive 11-keto forms, not a generic steroid dehydrogenase assignment PMID:23042946.
  • Stress response annotations are retained as downstream physiology because hsd11b2 controls cortisol catabolism during stress-axis recovery PMID:23349977.
  • NADP(+)-dependent 11-beta-HSD and 7-beta-HSD assignments are not supported for zebrafish Hsd11b2; UniProt and experimental papers support NAD(+)-dependent cortisol to cortisone conversion.
