CC   -!- FUNCTION: The mitochondrial D-lactate dehydrogenase is a
CC       stereoselective dehydrogenase that targets a wide variety of D-2-
CC       hydroxyacids, particularly those with small to moderately sized
CC       hydrophobic groups attached to the C2 atom. It includes D-lactate which
CC       is generated in small amounts either endogenously through the
CC       methylglyoxal metabolism pathway or exogenously via intestinal
CC       bacterial activity and dietary intake. The dehydrogenase acts
CC       specifically on D-lactate, not on its stereoisomer L-lactate, and
CC       prevents the toxic accumulation of D-lactate in the organism
CC       (PubMed:30931947). By converting branched-chain D-2-hydroxyacids into
CC       branched-chain ketoacids, it may indirectly regulate branched-chain
CC       amino acid metabolism (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q7TNG8,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:30931947}.
