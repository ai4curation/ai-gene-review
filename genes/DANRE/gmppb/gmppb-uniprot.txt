CC   -!- FUNCTION: Catalytic subunit of the GMPPA-GMPPB mannose-1-phosphate
CC       guanylyltransferase complex (By similarity). Catalyzes the formation of
CC       GDP-mannose, an essential precursor of glycan moieties of glycoproteins
CC       and glycolipids (PubMed:33986552). Can catalyze the reverse reaction in
CC       vitro (By similarity). Together with GMPPA regulates GDP-alpha-D-
CC       mannose levels (PubMed:33986552). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9Y5P6,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:33986552}.

CC   -!- ACTIVITY REGULATION: Enzyme activity is reduced by incorporation into
CC       the GMPPA-GMPPB mannose-1-phosphate guanylyltransferase complex.
CC       Allosterically inhibited, when part of the GMPPA-GMPPB complex, by GDP-
CC       alpha-D-mannose binding to GMPPA. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9Y5P6}.
CC   -!- PATHWAY: Nucleotide-sugar biosynthesis; GDP-alpha-D-mannose
CC       biosynthesis; GDP-alpha-D-mannose from alpha-D-mannose 1-phosphate (GTP
CC       route): step 1/1. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:33986552}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Component of the GMPPA-GMPPB mannose-1-phosphate
CC       guanylyltransferase complex composed of 4 gmppa subunits and 8 gmppb
CC       subunits; the complex is organized into three layers, a central layer
CC       made up of 2 gmppa dimers sandwiched between two layers each made up of
CC       2 gmppb dimers (By similarity). Catalytic activity of gmppb is reduced
CC       when part of the complex and binding of GDP-alpha-D-Mannose by gmppa
CC       induces allosteric feedback inhibition of gmppb (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9Y5P6}.
CC   -!- DEVELOPMENTAL STAGE: Expressed throughout all stages of development.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:23768512}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The N-terminal substrate-binding domain adopts a Rossman-like
CC       fold and has a binding pocket for GTP or GDP-alpha-D-mannose (By
CC       similarity). Substrate binding is coordinated by an Mg(2+) ion (By
CC       similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9Y5P6}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The C-terminal domain consists of a series of tandem
CC       hexapeptide repeats that adopt a beta-helix conformation (By
CC       similarity). The beta-helix forms several protein interaction surfaces
CC       involved in assembly of the GMPPA-GMPPB mannose-1-phosphate
CC       guanylyltransferase complex (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9Y5P6}.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: Morpholino knockdown of the gene results in
CC       smaller embryos with multiple anomalies, including bent tails,
CC       hypopigmentation, microphthalmia, hydrocephalus, and reduced motility
CC       (PubMed:23768512). Caudal primary motor neurons are shortened
CC       (PubMed:33986552). Muscle fibers are sparse and disorganized, and the
CC       myosepta are damaged or incompletely developed (PubMed:23768512,
CC       PubMed:33986552). There is also evidence of sarcolemmal damage
CC       (PubMed:23768512). Immunostaining shows defective glycosylation of DAG1
CC       associated with abnormal structure of the basement membrane
CC       (PubMed:23768512). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:23768512,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:33986552}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the transferase hexapeptide repeat family.
CC       {ECO:0000305}.
