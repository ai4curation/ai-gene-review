## Research Report: Functional Annotation of **cmpk** (UniProt **Q7ZWE9**) in *Danio rerio*

### Executive summary and identity verification
The target protein is **cmpk** from *Danio rerio* (zebrafish), UniProt accession **Q7ZWE9**, annotated as **UMP-CMP kinase** (EC **2.7.4.14**) in the **adenylate kinase family** (P-loop NTPase fold), with alternate naming including **deoxycytidylate kinase (dCMP kinase)**. The key curation risk is ambiguity with **CMPK2 (UMP-CMP kinase 2)**, a distinct **mitochondrial** paralog in vertebrates that is frequently discussed in recent (2023–2024) inflammation/innate immunity literature but is **not** the UniProt Q7ZWE9 target. In the tool-retrieved literature, **no primary papers explicitly referencing zebrafish UniProt Q7ZWE9** (or a clearly matched zebrafish cmpk/CMPK1 ortholog) were obtained; therefore, functional annotation below is necessarily **inference-heavy**, grounded in **conserved enzyme-family biochemistry/structure** and **paralog-disambiguating evidence**. (taweechai2025validatedantimalarialdrug pages 6-8, xu2008humanumpcmpkinase pages 7-8)

### 1) Key concepts and definitions (current understanding)

#### 1.1 What a UMP-CMP kinase (CMPK1-like) does
UMP-CMP kinases are nucleoside monophosphate kinases that catalyze **ATP-dependent phosphorylation** of pyrimidine monophosphates to diphosphates (general form):

**NMP + ATP → NDP + ADP**,

where relevant NMP substrates include **CMP** and **UMP**, and in some contexts **dCMP** can be evaluated as a substrate in UMP/CMP kinase assays. Evidence that CMP/UMP/dCMP are standard substrates for UMP/CMP kinases comes from modern enzymology workflows that explicitly test **CMP, UMP, and dCMP** and determine Michaelis–Menten parameters using coupled spectrophotometric assays. (taweechai2025validatedantimalarialdrug pages 6-8)

#### 1.2 Protein family/domain architecture and catalytic mechanism (adenylate-kinase fold)
UMP/CMP kinases belong to the **adenylate kinase family** (P-loop NTPase), and their catalytic mechanism involves coordinated **domain closure** around substrate and phosphate donor/acceptor. A high-resolution (2.2 Å) crystal structure of a UMP/CMP kinase complexed with a **bisubstrate inhibitor (UP5A)** and **Mg2+** shows hallmark mechanistic features: closure of the **NMP domain** over the nucleobase region and the **LID domain** over phosphate groups; multiple conserved basic residues contacting phosphates; and essential involvement of the **P-loop Lys** in phosphoryl transfer. The structure further supports **water-mediated base recognition** for uracil in the UMP-binding site, providing a mechanistic rationale for pyrimidine specificity. (scheffzek1996crystalstructureof pages 4-6)

### 2) Substrate specificity and reaction chemistry (what can be supported)

#### 2.1 What is directly supported in the retrieved corpus
The retrieved corpus provides strong quantitative specificity evidence for the **mitochondrial paralog CMPK2**, which can phosphorylate **dUMP, dCMP, CMP, and UMP** using ATP, but with a strong preference for **deoxyribonucleotides** (especially **dUMP**) over ribonucleotides. For CMPK2, kinetic parameters show high catalytic efficiency for dUMP and markedly poorer performance for UMP; for example, reported Km and Vmax/Km values (units as reported) include **dUMP Km ~0.10 mM (Vmax/Km ~48)** and **UMP Km ~6.30 mM (Vmax/Km ~0.03)**, supporting a large substrate preference difference. (xu2008humanumpcmpkinase pages 6-7)

CMPK2 is explicitly contrasted against the **cytosolic UMP-CMP kinase (CMPK1)** in that cytosolic UMP-CMPK is described as preferentially phosphorylating **ribonucleotides**, while CMPK2 has higher efficacy for **deoxyribonucleotides**. This contrast is crucial for interpreting literature that might otherwise be incorrectly mapped onto zebrafish Q7ZWE9. (xu2008humanumpcmpkinase pages 7-8)

#### 2.2 What can be inferred for zebrafish Q7ZWE9 (CMPK/CMPK1-like)
Because UniProt Q7ZWE9 is annotated as an adenylate-kinase-family **UMP-CMP kinase** (EC 2.7.4.14) and the enzyme class is biochemically defined, the most defensible functional statement for zebrafish cmpk is:

*Zebrafish CMPK (Q7ZWE9) is expected to catalyze ATP-dependent phosphorylation of pyrimidine monophosphates (classically CMP and UMP, and potentially dCMP depending on enzyme and conditions) to the corresponding diphosphates.*

This inference is consistent with how UMP/CMP kinases are assayed (CMP/UMP/dCMP tested) and with the conserved structural mechanism across UMP/CMP kinases. (taweechai2025validatedantimalarialdrug pages 6-8, scheffzek1996crystalstructureof pages 4-6)

However, **zebrafish Q7ZWE9-specific kinetic constants (Km, Vmax) and quantitative substrate ranking** were not found in the retrieved evidence and thus cannot be stated as organism-specific facts.

### 3) Subcellular localization and cellular context

#### 3.1 What is known for the commonly-confused paralog CMPK2
CMPK2 provides clear, direct localization evidence: it contains an N-terminal **mitochondrial targeting signal** (first ~22 amino acids), and GFP fusion constructs co-localize with mitochondria (MitoTracker) in cells, demonstrating mitochondrial localization. (xu2008humanumpcmpkinase pages 5-6, xu2008humanumpcmpkinase pages 2-3)

#### 3.2 What can be said for zebrafish cmpk (Q7ZWE9)
No direct localization experiments for zebrafish Q7ZWE9 were retrieved. Therefore, **subcellular localization in zebrafish should be treated as unresolved in this evidence set**.

Practically, for annotation purposes, the **key defensible statement** is negative/disambiguating: zebrafish Q7ZWE9 should **not** be automatically assumed to be mitochondrial like CMPK2, because CMPK2 mitochondrial localization relies on a specific N-terminal targeting signal and represents a separate paralogous family with different substrate preferences. (xu2008humanumpcmpkinase pages 7-8, xu2008humanumpcmpkinase pages 5-6)

### 4) Biological pathways and physiological roles

#### 4.1 Pyrimidine nucleotide metabolism and nucleotide pool homeostasis
UMP/CMP kinases occupy a core position in **pyrimidine nucleotide interconversion**, generating NDP substrates that can be further converted to NTPs/dNTPs by downstream kinases (e.g., nucleoside diphosphate kinases), thereby supporting RNA synthesis, DNA synthesis, and repair. Although zebrafish-specific pathway studies were not retrieved, this pathway role is anchored in the enzyme class definition and structural conservation of UMP/CMP kinases. (scheffzek1996crystalstructureof pages 4-6)

For mitochondria, CMPK2 is proposed to support mitochondrial pools specifically, with conclusions that it should be responsible for phosphorylation of **dCMP and dUMP in mitochondria**, connecting it to mtDNA precursor supply. (xu2008humanumpcmpkinase pages 8-8)

#### 4.2 Drug-activation pathway relevance (CMPK1-family relevance; real-world implementation)
In cancer pharmacology, CMPK1 (referred to as UMP/CMP kinase encoded by CMPK1) is implicated in phosphorylation of **cytidine and uridine analogue monophosphates**, influencing drug activation and therapeutic response. In one review, CMPK1 expression is described as predictive for **5-FU sensitivity** across a panel of **80 xenograft models**, and CMPK1 polymorphisms are reported as associated with overall survival in patient cohorts treated with gemcitabine-class therapy (dF-dC). These are clinically oriented association-level statements (not mechanism proof in zebrafish) but represent real-world relevance of the enzyme class. (tsesmetzis2018nucleobaseandnucleoside pages 13-15)

Separately, CMPK2 is experimentally shown to phosphorylate monophosphate forms of multiple nucleoside analogs (including **ddC, dFdC, araC, BVDU, FdUrd**), which supports a mechanistic hypothesis for mitochondrial involvement in analog-related mtDNA depletion/toxicity during long-term treatment. (xu2008humanumpcmpkinase pages 1-2)

### 5) Recent developments (2023–2024) and expert perspectives
Within the retrieved tool corpus, the clearest 2023–2024 “recent developments” relate to **CMPK2**, not CMPK1/cmpk (Q7ZWE9). This reflects a broader trend: CMPK2 has become prominent in inflammation/innate immunity discussions, but this should not be conflated with the CMPK1-like cytosolic UMP/CMP kinase encoded by cmpk in zebrafish. Evidence supporting this disambiguation is the explicit mitochondrial localization and deoxyribonucleotide preference of CMPK2 relative to cytosolic UMP-CMPK. (xu2008humanumpcmpkinase pages 7-8, xu2008humanumpcmpkinase pages 5-6)

As an authoritative biochemical baseline, the 2008 JBC study remains a cornerstone reference for CMPK2 localization/substrate preferences. (xu2008humanumpcmpkinase pages 6-7, xu2008humanumpcmpkinase pages 5-6, xu2008humanumpcmpkinase pages 1-2)

*Danio rerio* cmpk (UniProt Q7ZWE9) appears to be underrepresented in the tool-retrieved literature set. No zebrafish-specific measurements were retrieved for:
