CC   -!- FUNCTION: A cytochrome P450 monooxygenase involved in the metabolism of
CC       retinoates (RAs), the active metabolites of vitamin A, and critical
CC       signaling molecules in animals (PubMed:15661642). RAs exist as at least
CC       four different isomers: all-trans-RA (atRA), 9-cis-RA, 13-cis-RA, and
CC       9,13-dicis-RA, where atRA is considered to be the biologically active
CC       isomer, although 9-cis-RA and 13-cis-RA also have activity (By
CC       similarity). Catalyzes the hydroxylation of atRA primarily at C-4 and
CC       C-18, thereby contributing to the regulation of atRA homeostasis and
CC       signaling (By similarity). Hydroxylation of atRA limits its biological
CC       activity and initiates a degradative process leading to its eventual
CC       elimination (By similarity). Involved in the convertion of atRA to all-
CC       trans-4-oxo-RA. Can oxidize all-trans-13,14-dihydroretinoate (DRA) to
CC       metabolites which could include all-trans-4-oxo-DRA, all-trans-4-
CC       hydroxy-DRA, all-trans-5,8-epoxy-DRA, and all-trans-18-hydroxy-DRA (By
CC       similarity). Plays a role in skeletal development, both at the level of
CC       patterning and in the ossification of bone and the establishment of
CC       some synovial joints (PubMed:22019272). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q811W2,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9NR63, ECO:0000269|PubMed:15661642,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:22019272}.
