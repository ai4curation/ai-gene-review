Gene Description

cyp26b1 encodes cytochrome P450 26B1, an endoplasmic-reticulum retinoic-acid hydroxylase that catabolizes all-trans retinoic acid and limits retinoic-acid receptor signaling during embryonic patterning. The core function is retinoic-acid hydroxylation/catabolism; skeletal, tendon, craniofacial, and hindbrain phenotypes are retained as non-core developmental consequences of altered retinoic-acid homeostasis.

Research Report: Functional Annotation of cyp26b1 (UniProt Q6EIG3) in Danio rerio

1) Target verification (gene/protein identity)

The evidence base used here is explicitly for zebrafish (Danio rerio) cyp26b1, encoding Cytochrome P450 26B1 (Cyp26b1), a member of the CYP26 subfamily of cytochrome P450 enzymes that metabolize retinoic acid (RA). Zebrafish genetic alleles (e.g., dolphin, stocksteif), mapping, and functional phenocopy/rescue experiments all identify cyp26b1 as an RA-oxidizing/catabolic enzyme required for normal craniofacial and axial skeletal development, consistent with the UniProt identity Q6EIG3. (laue2008restrictionofretinoic pages 4-5, spoorendonk2008retinoicacidand pages 1-2, laue2008restrictionofretinoic pages 1-2)

2) Key concepts and current understanding

2.1 Retinoic acid signaling and homeostasis

All-trans retinoic acid (atRA/RA) is a small lipophilic morphogen-like ligand that activates nuclear RA receptors (RARs) to regulate transcriptional programs during embryogenesis and tissue remodeling. A central concept in RA biology is that RA availability is controlled by balancing synthesis (e.g., by Aldh enzymes) and degradation, with degradation being crucial for protecting RA-sensitive tissues and shaping spatial domains of RA responsiveness. (hernandez2007cyp26enzymesgenerate pages 1-2, roberts2020regulatingretinoicacid pages 5-7)

2.2 What is Cyp26b1?

Cyp26b1 is one of the vertebrate CYP26 enzymes (Cyp26a1/b1/c1) that restrict RA signaling by enzymatically converting RA to more polar metabolites, thereby reducing receptor-available RA and helping generate RA-negative domains or RA gradients in vivo. (hernandez2007cyp26enzymesgenerate pages 1-2, roberts2020regulatingretinoicacid pages 5-7)

3) Molecular/biochemical function (reaction, substrate specificity)

3.1 Primary catalytic activity and substrate

CYP26 enzymes (including Cyp26b1) are described as having high catalytic activity toward all-trans retinoic acid; a biochemical summary reports apparent affinity in the sub-100 nM Km range with turnover on the order of ~1–10 pmol/min/pmol enzyme (values reported from in vitro/transfected systems, used broadly to contextualize vertebrate CYP26 function). (roberts2020regulatingretinoicacid pages 7-10)

3.2 Reaction products (metabolites)

CYP26 enzymes are reported to hydroxylate/oxidize RA to metabolites including 4-hydroxy-RA, 4-oxo-RA, 18-hydroxy-RA, and 5,8-epoxy all-trans-RA, which are then further processed (e.g., conjugation) and cleared. (roberts2020regulatingretinoicacid pages 7-10)

3.3 Enzymatic framing in recent zebrafish work

A 2024 zebrafish cartilage regeneration preprint explicitly states that ATRA is inactivated through hydroxylation by Cytochrome P450 26B1 (Cyp26b1), directly connecting zebrafish cyp26b1 to ATRA catabolism in a contemporary experimental context. (arata2024retinoicacidsignaling pages 6-8)

4) Cellular and subcellular localization

4.1 Subcellular compartment

CYP26 enzymes are described as microsomal/membrane-anchored class II cytochrome P450s localized to the endoplasmic reticulum (ER), requiring cytochrome P450 oxidoreductase (POR) and NADPH for catalysis. This ER localization is important because it positions Cyp26 enzymes to regulate intracellular RA levels and, by extension, RA-dependent transcriptional programs. (roberts2020regulatingretinoicacid pages 7-10)

4.2 Tissue/cell-type expression contexts in zebrafish

Zebrafish cyp26b1 is reported in multiple developmental and regenerative cellular contexts, including:
- Hindbrain rhombomeres (early expression initiating later than cyp26a1). (roberts2020regulatingretinoicacid pages 17-19)
- Cranial precartilage condensations, perichondrial cells, and osteoblasts/osteoblast precursors in craniofacial and axial skeletal development. (laue2008restrictionofretinoic pages 4-5, spoorendonk2008retinoicacidand pages 1-2)
- Fin regeneration blastemal fibroblasts and stump osteoblasts, where it behaves as an RA sink that shapes a proximodistal RA pattern. (roberts2020regulatingretinoicacid pages 30-32, roberts2020regulatingretinoicacid pages 29-30)
- Newly forming chondrocytes during cartilage regeneration, contrasted with perichondrial RA synthesis gene expression. (arata2024retinoicacidsignaling pages 6-8)

5) Pathway integration: how cyp26b1 shapes RA-dependent patterning

5.1 Hindbrain patterning (“RA responsiveness sculpting”)

A foundational zebrafish study demonstrates that Cyp26 enzymes function redundantly to shape RA-dependent gene-expression domains in the hindbrain, and proposes a “gradient-free” model in which dynamic expression of RA-degrading enzymes (including cyp26b1) can define spatial RA responsiveness even when RA is broadly available. (hernandez2007cyp26enzymesgenerate pages 1-2)

5.2 Skeletal development: restricting RA to control osteogenesis

Two zebrafish studies from Development provide strong genetic and pharmacologic evidence that cyp26b1-mediated restriction of RA activity is required for proper timing and patterning of osteogenesis. Loss of cyp26b1 function results in hyperossification and vertebral fusion, and these phenotypes can be phenocopied by RA exposure or CYP26 inhibition, supporting a causal link to excess RA signaling. (spoorendonk2008retinoicacidand pages 1-2, laue2008restrictionofretinoic pages 1-2)

5.3 Regeneration: RA sinks and regeneration gradients

A synthesis of zebrafish regeneration literature describes cyp26b1 expression during fin regeneration as contributing to an RA pattern: proximal cyp26b1 expression acts as an “RA sink”, helping establish a proximal-low / distal-high RA environment that coordinates preosteoblast proliferation and redifferentiation along the proximodistal axis. (roberts2020regulatingretinoicacid pages 30-32, roberts2020regulatingretinoicacid pages 29-30)

6) Biological roles and phenotypes in zebrafish (experimental evidence)

6.1 Genetic evidence: alleles and mapping (quantitative genetics/statistics)

The dolphin (dol) phenotype was mapped to a 0.38 cM interval, and the study reports no recombination in 4500 meioses in their mapping effort. (laue2008restrictionofretinoic pages 4-5)

Molecular lesion quantification included a cDNA-clone analysis in which a downstream splice donor was used in 50/50 mutant cDNA clones and 0/50 wild-type clones, supporting a specific splice defect leading to a frameshift and truncation that removes key P450 catalytic motifs. (laue2008restrictionofretinoic pages 4-5)

6.2 Phenotypic outcomes: cartilage and bone

Across zebrafish primary studies, cyp26b1 loss-of-function is associated with:
- Reduction of midline cartilage structures in the neurocranium and pharyngeal arches. (laue2008restrictionofretinoic pages 1-2, roberts2020regulatingretinoicacid pages 15-17)
- Hyperossification of craniofacial bones and axial skeleton.
- Fusion of vertebral primordia / fused vertebral centra (axial skeletal fusion/over-ossification phenotype). (spoorendonk2008retinoicacidand pages 1-2, laue2008restrictionofretinoic pages 1-2)

These developmental skeletal phenotypes are also supported visually by figure panels showing jaw/fin defects, midline cartilage loss, craniofacial hyperossification, and axial vertebral fusion in dol mutants and morphants, as well as RA phenocopy and DEAB rescue paradigms. (laue2008restrictionofretinoic media a569dee6)

6.3 Chemical-genetic perturbations and implementation details (quantitative experimental parameters)

The same zebrafish Development work reports practical perturbation parameters used to manipulate the pathway:
- Morpholino knockdown: a splice-site morpholino was injected at ~0.5 pmol/embryo for full knockdown and 0.2 pmol/embryo for hypomorphic effects. (laue2008restrictionofretinoic pages 2-4)
- RA-pathway chemical manipulations included RA and inhibitors with explicit concentrations/timing (e.g., 0.5 μM RA before 50 hpf, 10 μM RA after 48 hpf, DEAB 10 μM before 50 hpf or 50 μM after 48 hpf, and 10 μM R115866). (laue2008restrictionofretinoic pages 2-4)

These quantitative parameters represent widely used “real-world” experimental implementations for tuning RA levels or CYP26 activity in vivo. (laue2008restrictionofretinoic pages 2-4)

7) Recent developments (prioritizing 2023–2024)

7.1 2024: Chromatin/epigenome rewiring under exogenous RA

A 2024 Nucleic Acids Research study treated zebrafish embryos with 0.1 μM atRA at multiple stages (including 80% epiboly, 12-somite stage, 24 hpf) and assessed genome-wide effects. At 80% epiboly, ATAC-seq found 1,275 increased vs 241 decreased differentially accessible regions (DARs) at FDR < 0.1; among RA-pathway genes associated with increased accessibility was cyp26b1 (listed alongside raraa, rxrga, roraa, dhrs3b). This supports the view that cyp26b1 sits within the RA-responsive regulatory network during early development, at least at the level of chromatin accessibility. (morenoonate2024rewiringofthe pages 7-9)

Publication details: Moreno-Oñate et al., 2024-02 (Feb 2024), URL: https://doi.org/10.1093/nar/gkae065. (morenoonate2024rewiringofthe pages 7-9)

7.2 2024: Cartilage development/regeneration and RA suppression of chondrocyte identity

A 2024 zebrafish preprint developed a nitroreductase/MTZ-based cartilage ablation model and reported coordinated RA-metabolism gene expression during regeneration: aldh1a2 expression enriched in perichondrium, while cyp26b1 is enriched in col2a1a+ newly forming chondrocytes. The study reports that both aldh1a2 and cyp26b1 are strongly upregulated at 3–4 days post ablation and decline by 6 dpa, suggesting dynamic RA synthesis/degradation compartmentalization during regenerative chondrogenesis. (arata2024retinoicacidsignaling pages 6-8)

Quantitative outcome statistics: treatments that activate RA signaling blocked new chondrocyte formation—palovarotene (PVO) 3/3 and ATRA 3/3 prevented new chondrocytes compared with 9/12 DMSO controls showing new chondrocytes at 6 dpa. (arata2024retinoicacidsignaling pages 6-8)

Publication details: Arata et al., 2024-05 (May 2024), URL: https://doi.org/10.1101/2024.05.10.593640. (arata2024retinoicacidsignaling pages 6-8)

Methodological implementation details relevant to reproducibility and real-world use include: 10 mM MTZ for 24 h to ablate cartilage, and palovarotene working concentrations of 0.2–2 μM (from a 12.05 mM stock). (arata2024retinoicacidsignaling pages 13-15)

A 2023 Bone review on genetically engineered zebrafish skeletal models notes that ENU-derived zebrafish cyp26b1 mutants (allele t24295) have been used as models and are reported to show failure to form select cranial sutures, with similarities to mouse and human phenotypes, emphasizing translational relevance of CYP26B1-mediated RA control in cranial suture development. (henke2023geneticallyengineeredzebrafish pages 9-11)

Publication details: Henke et al., 2023-11 (Nov 2023), URL: https://doi.org/10.1016/j.bone.2022.116611. (henke2023geneticallyengineeredzebrafish pages 9-11)

12) Limitations of the retrieved evidence
