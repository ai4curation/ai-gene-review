| Enzyme function | Zebrafish Cyp26b1 is a cytochrome P450 retinoic-acid catabolic enzyme/retinoic-acid hydroxylase that restricts RA signaling; CYP26 enzymes convert RA to hydroxylated/polar derivatives and require POR/NADPH for catalysis. | Roberts 2020; Hernandez et al. 2007 (pqac-00000004, pqac-00000003) | https://doi.org/10.3390/jdb8010006 ; https://doi.org/10.1242/dev.02706 |
| Substrates/metabolites | Primary substrate is all-trans retinoic acid; reported CYP26 metabolites include 4-hydroxy-RA, 4-oxo-RA, 18-hydroxy-RA, and 5,8-epoxy all-trans-RA. In the 2024 regeneration study, cyp26b1 is described as inactivating ATRA by hydroxylation. | Roberts 2020; Arata et al. 2024 (pqac-00000004, pqac-00000008) | https://doi.org/10.3390/jdb8010006 ; https://doi.org/10.1101/2024.05.10.593640 |
| Localization | CYP26 enzymes are described as microsomal, membrane-anchored class II P450s localized to the endoplasmic reticulum; zebrafish tissue expression includes osteoblasts, osteoblast precursors, perichondrial cells, cranial condensations, hindbrain rhombomeres, blastemal fibroblasts, and stump osteoblasts. | Roberts 2020; Laue et al. 2008 (pqac-00000004, pqac-00000001, pqac-00000010) | https://doi.org/10.3390/jdb8010006 ; https://doi.org/10.1242/dev.021238 |
| Developmental roles | cyp26b1 helps shape RA response domains in hindbrain development; zebrafish cyp26b1 expression begins around 10 hpf in r3/r4 and expands to r2 by 15 hpf. It is also required to restrict RA during osteogenesis: expression occurs near postmigratory cranial neural crest at 26 hpf, in sox9a+ condensations at 48 hpf, and in axial osteogenic domains from 96–156 hpf as cyp26b1+ cells decline while col10a1/opn+ osteoblasts increase. | Hernandez et al. 2007; Roberts 2020; Laue et al. 2008 (pqac-00000003, pqac-00000006, pqac-00000001) | https://doi.org/10.1242/dev.02706 ; https://doi.org/10.3390/jdb8010006 ; https://doi.org/10.1242/dev.021238 |
| Regeneration roles | During fin regeneration, cyp26b1 is expressed proximally in blastemal fibroblasts and in stump osteoblasts, acting as an RA sink to establish a proximal-low/distal-high RA gradient that supports distal preosteoblast proliferation and proximal redifferentiation. In 2024 cartilage regeneration work, aldh1a2 is enriched in perichondrium while cyp26b1 is enriched in newly forming col2a1a+ chondrocytes at 4 dpa. | Roberts 2020; Arata et al. 2024 (pqac-00000010, pqac-00000012, pqac-00000008) | https://doi.org/10.3390/jdb8010006 ; https://doi.org/10.1101/2024.05.10.593640 |
| Perturbation phenotypes | Loss of cyp26b1 causes reduced midline cartilage, hyperossification of craniofacial and axial bones, and vertebral fusion; RA treatment or CYP26 inhibitor R115866 phenocopies these defects. dolphin maps to a 0.38 cM interval; no recombination was found in 4500 meioses. The ti230g splice mutant used a downstream GT in 50/50 cDNA clones versus 0/50 in WT; morpholino full knockdown used ~0.5 pmol/embryo. Image evidence shows beak-like jaw, pectoral fin defects, midline cartilage loss, craniofacial hyperossification, and axial vertebral fusion. | Laue et al. 2008; Spoorendonk et al. 2008; figure summary from Laue et al. 2008 (pqac-00000001, pqac-00000002, pqac-00000000, pqac-00000014) | https://doi.org/10.1242/dev.021238 ; https://doi.org/10.1242/dev.024034 |
| Recent 2023-2024 advances | A 2024 NAR study showed that 0.1 μM atRA treatment at 80% epiboly, 12 somites, and 24 hpf altered chromatin accessibility; cyp26b1 was among RA-pathway genes linked to increased DARs at 80% epiboly (1,275 increased vs 241 decreased DARs, FDR < 0.1). A 2024 zebrafish cartilage-regeneration preprint found cyp26b1 upregulated at 3–4 dpa and waning by 6 dpa; PVO or ATRA prevented new chondrocytes (3/3 and 3/3 treated vs 9/12 DMSO controls showing new chondrocytes). A 2023 Bone review highlighted ENU-derived cyp26b1 mutant t24295 as a model with cranial suture defects analogous to mouse and human disease. | Moreno-Oñate et al. 2024; Arata et al. 2024; Henke et al. 2023 (pqac-00000013, pqac-00000008, pqac-00000024) | https://doi.org/10.1093/nar/gkae065 ; https://doi.org/10.1101/2024.05.10.593640 ; https://doi.org/10.1016/j.bone.2022.116611 |
