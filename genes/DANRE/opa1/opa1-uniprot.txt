CC   -!- FUNCTION: Dynamin-related GTPase that is essential for normal
CC       mitochondrial morphology by mediating fusion of the mitochondrial inner
CC       membranes, regulating cristae morphology and maintaining respiratory
CC       chain function. Exists in two forms: the transmembrane, long form
CC       (Dynamin-like GTPase OPA1, long form; L-OPA1), which is tethered to the
CC       inner mitochondrial membrane, and the short soluble form (Dynamin-like
CC       GTPase OPA1, short form; S-OPA1), which results from proteolytic
CC       cleavage and localizes in the intermembrane space. Both forms (L-OPA1
CC       and S-OPA1) cooperate to catalyze the fusion of the mitochondrial inner
CC       membrane. The equilibrium between L-OPA1 and S-OPA1 is essential:
CC       excess levels of S-OPA1, produced by cleavage by OMA1 following loss of
CC       mitochondrial membrane potential, lead to an impaired equilibrium
CC       between L-OPA1 and S-OPA1, inhibiting mitochondrial fusion (By
CC       similarity). The balance between L-OPA1 and S-OPA1 also influences
CC       cristae shape and morphology (By similarity). Its role in mitochondrial
CC       morphology is required for mitochondrial genome maintenance (By
CC       similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O60313,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P58281}.
CC   -!- FUNCTION: [Dynamin-like GTPase OPA1, long form]: Constitutes the
CC       transmembrane long form (L-OPA1) that plays a central role in
CC       mitochondrial inner membrane fusion and cristae morphology. L-OPA1 and
CC       the soluble short form (S-OPA1) form higher-order helical assemblies
CC       that coordinate the fusion of mitochondrial inner membranes. Inner
CC       membrane-anchored L-OPA1 molecules initiate membrane remodeling by
CC       recruiting soluble S-OPA1 to rapidly polymerize into a flexible
CC       cylindrical scaffold encaging the mitochondrial inner membrane. Once at
CC       the membrane surface, the formation of S-OPA1 helices induce bilayer
CC       curvature. OPA1 dimerization through the paddle region, which inserts
CC       into cardiolipin-containing membrane, promotes GTP hydrolysis and the
CC       helical assembly of a flexible OPA1 lattice on the membrane, which
CC       drives membrane curvature and mitochondrial fusion. Plays a role in the
CC       maintenance and remodeling of mitochondrial cristae, some invaginations
CC       of the mitochondrial inner membrane that provide an increase in the
CC       surface area (By similarity). Probably acts by forming helical
CC       filaments at the inside of inner membrane tubes with the shape and
CC       dimensions of crista junctions (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:G0SGC7, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O60313}.
CC   -!- FUNCTION: [Dynamin-like GTPase OPA1, short form]: Constitutes the
CC       soluble short form (S-OPA1) generated by cleavage by OMA1, which plays
CC       a central role in mitochondrial inner membrane fusion and cristae
CC       morphology. The transmembrane long form (L-OPA1) and the S-OPA1 form
CC       higher-order helical assemblies that coordinate the fusion of
CC       mitochondrial inner membranes. Inner membrane-anchored L-OPA1 molecules
CC       initiate membrane remodeling by recruiting soluble S-OPA1 to rapidly
CC       polymerize into a flexible cylindrical scaffold encaging the
CC       mitochondrial inner membrane. Once at the membrane surface, the
CC       formation of S-OPA1 helices induce bilayer curvature. OPA1 dimerization
CC       through the paddle region, which inserts into cardiolipin-containing
CC       membrane, promotes GTP hydrolysis and the helical assembly of a
CC       flexible OPA1 lattice on the membrane, which drives membrane curvature
CC       and mitochondrial fusion. Excess levels of S-OPA1 produced by cleavage
CC       by OMA1 following stress conditions that induce loss of mitochondrial
CC       membrane potential, lead to an impaired equilibrium between L-OPA1 and
CC       S-OPA1, thereby inhibiting mitochondrial fusion. Plays a role in the
CC       maintenance and remodeling of mitochondrial cristae, some invaginations
CC       of the mitochondrial inner membrane that provide an increase in the
CC       surface area (By similarity). Probably acts by forming helical
CC       filaments at the inside of inner membrane tubes with the shape and
CC       dimensions of crista junctions (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:G0SGC7, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O60313}.

CC   -!- DOMAIN: The paddle region plays a major role in driving mitochondrial
CC       inner membrane fusion. It binds lipid membranes enriched in negatively
CC       charged phospholipids, such as cardiolipin, and promotes membrane
CC       tubulation. A conserved intramembrane region, named membrane insertion
CC       loop (MIL), within the paddle region inserts deeply into the bilayer,
CC       further stabilizing the interactions with cardiolipin-enriched
CC       membranes. OPA1 dimerization through the paddle domain promotes the
CC       helical assembly of a flexible OPA1 lattice on the membrane, driving
CC       mitochondrial fusion in cells. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O60313}.
CC   -!- PTM: Cleaved by OMA1 or YME1L downstream of the transmembrane region in
CC       response to different signals to generate soluble forms. Cleaved by
CC       OMA1 at position S1 following stress conditions, generating the short
CC       soluble form (Dynamin-like GTPase OPA1, short form; S-OPA1).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O60313}.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: Morpholino knockdown of the protein results in
CC       disrupted mitochondrial morphology, leading to bioenergetic defects
CC       (PubMed:23516612). Embryos show abnormal blood circulation and heart
CC       defects, as well as small eyes and small pectoral fin buds
CC       (PubMed:23516612). Moreover, startle response is reduced and locomotor
CC       activity is impaired (PubMed:23516612). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:23516612}.
