| Molecular function/reaction | **Danio rerio ak2 (UniProt Q1L8L9)** matches **adenylate kinase 2, mitochondrial/intermembrane-space isozyme**. Adenylate kinases catalyze the reversible phosphotransfer reaction **ATP + AMP ⇌ 2 ADP**; broader AK chemistry can be written as **NTP + AMP ⇌ NDP + ADP**. AK2 is described as the primary mitochondrial **ADP-generating** adenylate kinase that helps maintain adenine nucleotide homeostasis and supplies ADP for ATP synthesis/transport coupling (pqac-00000000, pqac-00000002, pqac-00000008, pqac-00000009, pqac-00000016). |
| Substrate specificity | Canonical substrates are **AMP plus ATP** for the EC 2.7.4.3 reaction; AK enzymes can also use other **purine or pyrimidine NTPs** as phosphate donors in the generalized reaction, while **AMP is the acceptor**. Adenylate kinases act on **magnesium nucleotide complexes**, although AMP itself can be phosphorylated without Mg2+ bound, affecting apparent equilibrium behavior. For functional annotation of zebrafish **ak2**, the strongest supported assignment remains **adenylate kinase activity on ATP/AMP to produce ADP** rather than broad nucleoside diphosphate kinase activity (pqac-00000008, pqac-00000009). |
| Subcellular localization | AK2 is the **only AK isozyme localized to the mitochondrial intermembrane space (IMS)**; reviews and disease papers consistently distinguish it from **AK3/AK4**, which are matrix-localized. AK2 is proposed to **supply ADP to the adenine nucleotide transporter (ANT)** at the inner membrane, coupling cytosolic/IMS adenine nucleotide pools to oxidative phosphorylation (pqac-00000000, pqac-00000009, pqac-00000010, pqac-00000016, pqac-00000039). |
| Key domains/structure | UniProt/domain assignment for Q1L8L9 places zebrafish Ak2 in the **adenylate kinase family, AK2 subfamily**, with **Adenyl_kin_sub / Adenylat-UMP/CMP kinase-like core** and a characteristic **lid domain**. Experimental zebrafish genetics support conserved AK fold organization: the **L124P** missense allele lies in a region connecting **CORE** and **LID** domains and was predicted to impair stability/activity; Hoenig et al. summarize three functional regions/domains as **P-loop, NMPbind, and LID** (pqac-00000004, pqac-00000007, pqac-00000016). |
| Pathways/biological processes | Main inferred pathway role is **mitochondrial adenine nucleotide interconversion** and **energy homeostasis**, especially ADP provision linked to ATP synthase/ANT. In zebrafish and human models, AK2 is required for **hematopoietic stem/progenitor maintenance**, **myeloid and lymphoid differentiation**, and protection from **oxidative stress** during hematopoiesis. Reviews also note roles in **mitochondrial apoptosis signaling** and broader mitochondrial energy transfer (pqac-00000000, pqac-00000001, pqac-00000002, pqac-00000010, pqac-00000016, pqac-00000036). |
| Zebrafish expression sites & stages | In zebrafish, **ak2 transcripts are maternally inherited**; qRT-PCR showed transcript decline after zygotic transcription onset. Whole-mount in situ hybridization localized expression to hematopoietic regions: **intermediate cell mass (ICM) at ~22–24 hpf** and **caudal hematopoietic tissue (CHT) at 5 dpf**. Figure retrieval from the primary study visually confirms these sites/stages (pqac-00000003, pqac-00000013). |
| Zebrafish mutant alleles | Rissone et al. generated/used multiple zebrafish alleles: **ak2hg14 = c.45delAA, p.K16RfsX62 (ak2del2)**; **ak2hg15 = c.41insACGG, p.K16TfsX64 (ak2ins4)**; **ak2hg16 = c.T371C, p.L124P (ak2L124P)**. Loss-of-function was also modeled with **morpholinos** targeting ak2. Zebrafish ak2 was reported to express an isoform corresponding to **human AK2 isoform B** (pqac-00000002, pqac-00000004, pqac-00000007). |
| Key phenotypes | **ak2 deficiency impairs definitive hematopoiesis**: reduced **c-myb**+ HSPCs from 2–5 dpf, defective **lymphoid development** (reduced **ikaros**, **rag1**), defective **myeloid development** (**mpx** reduction, fewer Sudan black+ granulocytes), increased **mitochondrial/cellular ROS**, induction of **hmox1a** in CHT, and increased apoptosis in hematopoietic tissues. Human AK2 deficiency causes **reticular dysgenesis**, and zebrafish phenotypes closely model this biology (pqac-00000004, pqac-00000005, pqac-00000006, pqac-00000016, pqac-00000021, pqac-00000031). |
| Rescue/perturbation experiments | In zebrafish, **N-acetyl-L-cysteine (NAC)** treatment rescued hematopoietic marker defects and reduced oxidative-stress readouts; figure review shows dose-dependent rescue of **c-myb**, **rag1**, and **hmox1a** abnormalities. In AK2-mutant human iPSC myeloid differentiation, antioxidants including **glutathione (GSH)** rescued myeloid maturation. These data support a mechanistic model in which AK2 protects hematopoiesis from **oxidative stress** downstream of nucleotide disequilibrium (pqac-00000000, pqac-00000002, pqac-00000005, pqac-00000013, pqac-00000024). |
| Key quantitative data | Reported quantitative details include: **NAC 10, 20, 50 µM** until 5 dpf significantly rescued zebrafish phenotypes (**P < 0.005**, Z-test); **GSH 3 mM** gave maximal rescue of AK2-mutant iPSC myeloid differentiation; zebrafish assays often analyzed **≥25 embryos/sample**; some developmental WISH experiments reported **35/32/26 embryos** at 29/32/36 hpf and **33/29/20/27 embryos** at 24/29/32/36 hpf. Biochemically, AK2-deficient cells showed **increased AMP/ADP ratio** and **decreased ADP**; later mechanistic work reported **>30-fold IMP accumulation** in AK2-deficient myeloid cells, reinforcing purine disequilibrium as a major consequence of AK2 loss (pqac-00000005, pqac-00000017, pqac-00000025, pqac-00000026, pqac-00000028, pqac-00000034). |
