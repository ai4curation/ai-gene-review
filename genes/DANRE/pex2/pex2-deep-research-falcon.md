## Research Report: Functional Annotation of **Danio rerio pex2** (UniProt **E7F4V8**) — Peroxisome Biogenesis Factor 2 (Peroxin-2)

### 0) Target verification (mandatory disambiguation)
The target is **pex2** from **Danio rerio (zebrafish)** encoding **Peroxisome biogenesis factor 2 (Pex2/Peroxin-2)**. A zebrafish peroxisome inventory explicitly lists **Pex2 (UniProt E7F4V8)** alongside **Pex10** and **Pex12** as part of the peroxisomal matrix-protein import machinery (“dynamic translocon”), confirming the accession-to-gene mapping and family context (PEX2/PEX10/PEX12) for this organism (kamoshita2022insightsintothe pages 4-5).

### 1) Key concepts, definitions, and current understanding

#### 1.1 Peroxisome matrix protein import and the PEX5 cycle
Peroxisomal matrix proteins are imported post-translationally by shuttling receptors (notably **PEX5** for PTS1 cargo). A defining feature of peroxisomal import is that PEX5 can transport **folded and even oligomeric** proteins; in current models, the receptor can accompany cargo deeply into (or fully into) the peroxisomal lumen before being exported back to the cytosol for reuse (bajdzienko2024mammalianpexophagyat pages 2-3, skowyra2024towardssolvingthe pages 1-3).

A key requirement for continued import is **receptor recycling**, which depends on **ubiquitylation** of PEX5 and ATP-dependent extraction by the AAA ATPase machinery (**PEX1–PEX6**) (skowyra2024towardssolvingthe pages 1-3).

#### 1.2 What PEX2 is (functional definition)
**PEX2** is a **peroxisomal membrane peroxin** that is part of a membrane-embedded **RING-finger E3 ubiquitin ligase complex** with **PEX10 and PEX12** (often written **PEX2–PEX10–PEX12**). This complex catalyzes **PEX5 monoubiquitination** (on a conserved N-terminal cysteine in mammals, described as **Cys11**) to initiate receptor recycling (skowyra2024towardssolvingthe pages 1-3, honsho2021peroxisomebiogenesisdisorders. pages 17-18).

In zebrafish-focused literature, Pex2 is described as an **E3 ubiquitin ligase with a zinc RING-finger domain** that resides in the **peroxisomal membrane** and is involved in **matrix protein import and receptor recycling** (kamoshita2022insightsintothe pages 6-8).

#### 1.3 Enzymatic activity and “substrate specificity” (as an E3 ligase)
For PEX2, the relevant “reaction” is **ubiquitin transfer** (E3 ligase activity) rather than small-molecule catalysis. The principal, best-supported substrate in the import cycle is the **PEX5 import receptor**, which is **monoubiquitinated** on the conserved N-terminal cysteine to enable recycling (skowyra2024towardssolvingthe pages 1-3, honsho2021peroxisomebiogenesisdisorders. pages 17-18). The fate of ubiquitinated receptors depends on ubiquitin topology: monoubiquitination supports recycling, while polyubiquitination is associated with quality control and degradation of stalled receptors (skowyra2024towardssolvingthe pages 8-9, aguirrelopez2024theperoxisomeprotein pages 1-2).

### 2) Molecular function, pathway placement, and localization (with mechanistic detail)

#### 2.1 Localization
Zebrafish Pex2 is positioned as a component of the peroxisomal import machinery: it is listed in the “dynamic translocon” together with Pex10 and Pex12 in zebrafish, consistent with a **peroxisomal membrane** role (kamoshita2022insightsintothe pages 4-5). Independent zebrafish-oriented discussion describes Pex2 as a peroxisomal membrane RING E3 ligase (kamoshita2022insightsintothe pages 6-8).

#### 2.2 Pathway placement: the ubiquitin-dependent receptor export/recycling module
Authoritative mechanistic synthesis (Trends in Cell Biology, 2024) states that **PEX5 recycling requires monoubiquitination on a conserved N-terminal cysteine** by the **PEX2–PEX10–PEX12** membrane RING E3 complex, followed by extraction by the **hexameric AAA ATPase** composed of alternating PEX1 and PEX6 subunits; deubiquitinases then reset PEX5 (skowyra2024towardssolvingthe pages 1-3).

#### 2.3 Link to peroxisome quality control and pexophagy
Ubiquitin marks on peroxisomal proteins can signal **pexophagy** (selective autophagy of peroxisomes). A 2024 expert overview describes the docking/translocation module interfacing with the **PEX2/PEX10/PEX12 RING E3 ligase complex**, and notes that ubiquitylation can act as a pexophagy trigger; experimentally, **fusion of a single ubiquitin to a peroxisomal membrane protein (PMP34)** is sufficient to trigger pexophagy, with ubiquitin-binding autophagy receptors (e.g., **NBR1**, with **p62** enhancing efficiency) recognizing these signals (bajdzienko2024mammalianpexophagyat pages 2-3).

### 3) Recent developments (prioritizing 2023–2024)

#### 3.1 2024: Structural model of the PEX2–PEX10–PEX12 complex as a retrotranslocon
A 2024 Trends in Cell Biology review summarizes recent structural and mechanistic evidence that the PEX2–PEX10–PEX12 ubiquitin ligase complex forms a **membrane channel** (retrotranslocon) for receptor export. Key points include:
- Cryo-EM architecture in which **each subunit contributes five transmembrane segments**, forming a pore with an **open diameter of ~10 Å**, and the three RING fingers form a **cytosolic tower** (skowyra2024towardssolvingthe pages 8-9).
- The complex is proposed to be **constitutively open** and to function in PEX5 export, with PEX5’s N-terminus threading to allow access of the conserved cysteine for monoubiquitination (skowyra2024towardssolvingthe pages 8-9, skowyra2024towardssolvingthe pages 21-27).
- A division of labor is suggested: **PEX2’s RING** is most directly linked to **PEX5 monoubiquitination**, whereas **PEX10/PEX12 RINGs** are linked to a **polyubiquitination (RADAR) pathway** targeting stalled receptors for degradation (skowyra2024towardssolvingthe pages 8-9).

These 2024 developments sharpen current understanding of PEX2 from a “RING E3 in a complex” to a dual-function component of a **combined ubiquitin ligase + protein-conducting channel** supporting receptor retrotranslocation (skowyra2024towardssolvingthe pages 8-9, skowyra2024towardssolvingthe pages 21-27).

#### 3.2 2024: Quantitative peroxisome turnover context
A 2024 pexophagy review reports that in cultured **CHO cells**, peroxisomes have an approximate **half-life of ~2 days** under basal conditions, providing a quantitative context for peroxisome maintenance and turnover pathways that involve ubiquitin-dependent signaling (bajdzienko2024mammalianpexophagyat pages 2-3).

### 4) Zebrafish-specific evidence for pex2 (models, phenotypes, and biochemical readouts)

#### 4.1 pex2 is part of zebrafish peroxisomal import machinery (accession-level evidence)
The zebrafish peroxisome inventory explicitly places **Pex2 (E7F4V8)** with **Pex10** and **Pex12** in the matrix cargo translocation machinery (“dynamic translocon”), grounding zebrafish pex2 in the conserved peroxisomal import system (kamoshita2022insightsintothe pages 4-5).

#### 4.2 Zebrafish pex2 loss-of-function models and phenotypes
A zebrafish-focused review of peroxisomal disorders describes targeted genome disruption of zebrafish **pex2** via **TALEN microinjection at the 1-cell stage** and reports that zebrafish Pex2 shares **~57% amino-acid identity** with human PEX2 (jiang2025modellingperoxisomaldisorders pages 8-9). Reported consequences include:
- **Peroxisomal matrix protein import disruption** and “**ghost peroxisomes**” (peroxisomal membrane remnants lacking normal matrix content and activity) (jiang2025modellingperoxisomaldisorders pages 8-9).
- Organismal phenotypes: **hepatic lipid droplet accumulation/steatosis**, **reduced locomotion**, **feeding difficulties**, and **reduced survival/early death** (jiang2025modellingperoxisomaldisorders pages 8-9).
- Lipid/redox readouts: **organ-specific accumulation of very-long-chain fatty acids (VLCFAs)** including highly polyunsaturated VLCFAs in **brain and eyes**, **reduced ether phospholipids/plasmalogens**, and decreased antioxidant activities (**catalase** and **glutathione peroxidase**) (jiang2025modellingperoxisomaldisorders pages 8-9).
- Stress and tissue readouts: **elevated ER stress**, and **reduced crystallin expression** in the lens (jiang2025modellingperoxisomaldisorders pages 8-9).
- Reproductive phenotypes: **sex-specific infertility**, including **under-developed oocytes** in females with **motile sperm** in males (jiang2025modellingperoxisomaldisorders pages 8-9).

An additional zebrafish inventory/review article notes that disruption of zebrafish **pex2** produced phenotypes resembling human Zellweger spectrum disorders, including **locomotor defects and eating disabilities**, and reiterates Pex2 as a peroxisomal membrane RING E3 ligase involved in matrix protein import/receptor recycling (kamoshita2022insightsintothe pages 6-8).

### 5) Current applications and real-world implementations

#### 5.1 Clinical relevance: PEX2 and Zellweger spectrum disorders (ZSD)
PEX2 is clinically important because biallelic defects in PEX genes cause peroxisome biogenesis disorders (ZSD as the major category). A zebrafish-focused disorders review states that human **PEX2 deficiency** causes a severe Zellweger syndrome phenotype with hypotonia and neurologic abnormalities, and is associated with accumulation of peroxisome-related diagnostic metabolites; it also notes that this severe form **typically results in death within the first year of life** (jiang2025modellingperoxisomaldisorders pages 8-9).

#### 5.2 Diagnostics: biochemical markers and genetic testing
Across the retrieved clinical and model-organism literature, diagnostic readouts repeatedly used for peroxisome biogenesis defects include **VLCFAs**, **phytanic/pristanic acids**, **C27 bile acid intermediates**, and **reduced ether phospholipids/plasmalogens** (jiang2025modellingperoxisomaldisorders pages 8-9, jiang2025modellingperoxisomaldisorders pages 6-8). A 2023 ClinGen-oriented evaluation emphasizes that peroxisomal disorders often require **molecular testing** and highlights **newborn screening** using **C26:0-lysophosphatidylcholine (C26:0-LPC)** alongside biochemical and genetic testing (gene panels/exome sequencing) (mohan2023evaluatingthestrength pages 1-3).

#### 5.3 Zebrafish as an implementation platform
Because zebrafish pex2 mutants recapitulate key biochemical and organismal hallmarks of ZSD (lipid accumulation, VLCFA changes, oxidative stress markers, developmental/behavioral phenotypes), they represent an in vivo platform for mechanistic studies and (as discussed more broadly for zebrafish peroxisomal models) can be leveraged for phenotype-driven screening approaches (jiang2025modellingperoxisomaldisorders pages 8-9).

### 6) Expert opinions and authoritative synthesis
Two 2024 authoritative sources synthesize the field’s current expert view of PEX2:
- **Skowyra, Feng & Rapoport (Trends in Cell Biology; May 2024)** frames the central “mystery” of peroxisomal import around how folded cargo crosses the membrane and emphasizes that receptor export (PEX5 extraction) provides the energetic driver of import; within this framework, **PEX2–PEX10–PEX12** is the membrane E3 ligase and pore-like export apparatus required for recycling (skowyra2024towardssolvingthe pages 1-3, skowyra2024towardssolvingthe pages 8-9).
- **Bajdzienko & Bremm (Journal of Cell Science; May 2024)** positions the **PEX2/10/12 ubiquitylation machinery** as a key node connecting import to **pexophagy**, highlighting what is known and underscoring gaps such as incomplete mapping of peroxisomal ubiquitylation sites and unclear roles of PMP polyubiquitylation in turnover (bajdzienko2024mammalianpexophagyat pages 2-3).

### 7) Relevant statistics and quantitative data (from recent sources)
- **PEX2/10/12 pore size:** ~**10 Å** open pore reported for the PEX2–PEX10–PEX12 complex in structural work summarized in 2024 (skowyra2024towardssolvingthe pages 8-9).
- **Transmembrane architecture:** **five transmembrane segments per subunit** in the PEX2/10/12 complex (skowyra2024towardssolvingthe pages 8-9).
- **Peroxisome half-life in cultured CHO cells:** ~**2 days** under basal conditions (bajdzienko2024mammalianpexophagyat pages 2-3).
- **ZSD incidence estimates:** ~**1/50,000** (or ~**1/80,000**) in a 2023 ClinGen-oriented synthesis (mohan2023evaluatingthestrength pages 1-3).
- **ZSD gene-frequency context (not PEX2-specific but important clinically):** one clinical genetics report states that **>90%** of ZSD cases involve **PEX1/PEX6/PEX10/PEX12/PEX26**, with **PEX1 ~70%** and **PEX26 ~10%** (alayoubi2025zellwegersyndrome;identification pages 1-2).
- **Zebrafish–human sequence identity:** zebrafish Pex2 is ~**57% identical** to human PEX2 (jiang2025modellingperoxisomaldisorders pages 8-9).
