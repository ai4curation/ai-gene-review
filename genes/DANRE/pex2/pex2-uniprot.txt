CC   -!- FUNCTION: E3 ubiquitin-protein ligase component of a retrotranslocation
CC       channel required for peroxisome organization by mediating export of the
CC       PEX5 receptor from peroxisomes to the cytosol, thereby promoting PEX5
CC       recycling (By similarity). The retrotranslocation channel is composed
CC       of PEX2, PEX10 and PEX12; each subunit contributing transmembrane
CC       segments that coassemble into an open channel that specifically allows
CC       the passage of PEX5 through the peroxisomal membrane. PEX2 also
CC       regulates peroxisome organization by acting as a E3 ubiquitin-protein
CC       ligase (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P28328,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P32800}.

CC   -!- DOMAIN: The three subunits of the retrotranslocation channel (PEX2,
CC       PEX10 and PEX12) coassemble in the membrane into a channel with an open
CC       10 Angstrom pore. The RING-type zinc-fingers that catalyze PEX5
CC       receptor ubiquitination are positioned above the pore on the cytosolic
CC       side of the complex. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:G2Q1C9}.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: Mutant fishes show a loss of peroxisomal
CC       structure, associated with high-mortality (PubMed:34016526). Defects
CC       are similar to human Zellweger syndrome (ZS), characterized by
CC       developmental abnormalities in many organs, organ-specific accumulation
CC       and reduction of distinct fatty acid species, such as an accumulation
CC       of ultra-very-long-chain polyunsaturated fatty acids (ultra-VLC-PUFAs)
CC       (PubMed:34016526). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:34016526}.
