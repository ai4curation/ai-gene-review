| Gene/protein identity | **Danio rerio nags** encodes mitochondrial **N-acetylglutamate synthase (NAGS; EC 2.3.1.1)**, matching UniProt **E7FCP8** and zebrafish-specific biochemical literature; this disambiguates it from unrelated acetyltransferases with overlapping family/domain labels. The zebrafish protein was experimentally studied as precursor-derived mature and conserved forms, consistent with the UniProt precursor annotation. (pqac-00000003, pqac-00000004) | Zebrafish constructs defined as **zfNAGS-M** (after predicted MTS cleavage) and **zfNAGS-C** (further lacking the variable segment). (pqac-00000003, pqac-00000005) | Caldovic et al., **2014-01**, PLoS ONE, https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pone.0085597 (pqac-00000003, pqac-00000005) |
| Enzyme/reaction | Zebrafish NAGS catalyzes the canonical reaction **acetyl-CoA + L-glutamate → N-acetyl-L-glutamate (NAG) + CoA**. In animals, NAG functions mainly as the obligatory allosteric activator of carbamoyl phosphate synthetase (CPS), linking NAGS activity to ureagenesis and nitrogen metabolism. (pqac-00000003, pqac-00000024) | Zebrafish NAGS has relatively **low apparent Km values for acetyl-CoA and glutamate**, reported as about **3–4-fold lower than mammalian NAGS** in comparative discussion. (pqac-00000006) | Caldovic et al., **2014-01**, https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pone.0085597; Caldovic et al., **2010-01**, https://doi.org/10.1016/j.ymgme.2010.02.018 (pqac-00000003, pqac-00000006, pqac-00000024) |
| Substrates/products and biochemical role | The immediate substrates are **acetyl-CoA** and **L-glutamate**; the product **NAG** is required to activate fish CPSIII and mammalian CPS1 analogously. Thus the primary biochemical function of zebrafish NAGS is NAG production rather than direct ammonia detoxification per se. (pqac-00000003, pqac-00000008, pqac-00000018) | In variant/mechanistic work on human NAGS, altered **Km for glutamate** and impaired arginine activation were major pathogenic mechanisms, underscoring the importance of these substrate/regulatory interactions for conserved NAGS function. (pqac-00000020, pqac-00000022) | Caldovic et al., **2014-01**, https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pone.0085597; Gougeard et al., **2024-05**, https://doi.org/10.1002/jimd.12747; Caldovic et al., **2010-01**, https://doi.org/10.1016/j.ymgme.2010.02.018 (pqac-00000003, pqac-00000008, pqac-00000018, pqac-00000020, pqac-00000022) |
| Cellular localization | Zebrafish NAGS is inferred to be **mitochondrial**, with an N-terminal **mitochondrial targeting sequence (MTS)** predicted to be cleaved to produce the mature enzyme. This aligns with vertebrate NAGS localization in the mitochondrial matrix, where CPS-dependent nitrogen metabolism occurs. (pqac-00000004, pqac-00000006, pqac-00000009) | Fish NAGS proteins have an N-terminal MTS of roughly **40–58 aa**; for zebrafish, the mature recombinant form was defined with an N-terminus at **Met47** based on MitoProt prediction. (pqac-00000004, pqac-00000005, pqac-00000006) | Caldovic et al., **2014-01**, https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pone.0085597; Haskins et al., **2008-09**, https://doi.org/10.1186/1471-2091-9-24 (pqac-00000004, pqac-00000005, pqac-00000006, pqac-00000009) |
| Domain architecture | Zebrafish NAGS has three major segments: **MTS**, a **variable segment**, and a **conserved C-terminal region** containing **AAK** and **NAT/GNAT-like** domains. The conserved domain harbors catalytic function and the arginine-responsive regulatory site, matching the UniProt/interpro family assignments. (pqac-00000004, pqac-00000006, pqac-00000026) | The MTS is followed by a variable segment of about **10–45 aa** in fish; the **NAT domain is more conserved than the AAK domain** across fish sequences (**~62% vs ~38%** conservation in the cited comparison). (pqac-00000006) | Caldovic et al., **2014-01**, https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pone.0085597; image summary from same paper (pqac-00000026) (pqac-00000004, pqac-00000006, pqac-00000026) |
| Regulation by arginine | Zebrafish NAGS is **partially inhibited by L-arginine**, unlike mammalian NAGS (activated) and unlike many bacterial NAGS enzymes (more strongly inhibited). This positions fish NAGS as an evolutionary intermediate in the inversion of arginine allostery during vertebrate evolution. (pqac-00000003, pqac-00000006, pqac-00000009, pqac-00000023) | Recombinant zebrafish NAGS specific activity fell from **10.638 ± 0.090** to **5.475 ± 0.121 pmol·min⁻¹·mg⁻¹** with **1 mM arginine** (~50% inhibition). In kinetic analyses, arginine caused about **30% Vmax decrease for zfNAGS-M** and about **50% for zfNAGS-C**; half-maximal effect was at **≤0.65 mM** arginine. (pqac-00000006, pqac-00000009) | Haskins et al., **2008-09**, https://doi.org/10.1186/1471-2091-9-24; Caldovic et al., **2014-01**, https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pone.0085597; Caldovic et al., **2024-11**, https://doi.org/10.1002/jimd.12687 (pqac-00000006, pqac-00000009, pqac-00000023) |
| Kinetic effects of arginine | Mechanistically, arginine reduces catalytic output mainly by lowering **apparent Vmax** and increasing **apparent Km for acetyl-CoA**, while **Km for glutamate remains essentially unchanged** in zebrafish constructs. This supports allosteric regulation affecting acetyl-CoA utilization/active conformation rather than glutamate binding in zebrafish. (pqac-00000003, pqac-00000004, pqac-00000006) | Arginine decreased activity by roughly **30–50%**, increased **Kmapp for AcCoA**, and did **not** materially change **Kmapp for glutamate**. (pqac-00000003, pqac-00000004, pqac-00000006) | Caldovic et al., **2014-01**, https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pone.0085597 (pqac-00000003, pqac-00000004, pqac-00000006) |
| Oligomerization/biophysical properties | Recombinant **zfNAGS-M** and **zfNAGS-C** are oligomeric, with evidence supporting a predominantly **tetrameric** state in the absence of arginine. Arginine changes hydrodynamic behavior/oligomerization, and the variable segment appears to help stabilize the tetrameric form. (pqac-00000003, pqac-00000004, pqac-00000026) | Both zfNAGS-M and zfNAGS-C are reported as **tetramers without L-arginine**; addition of **1 mM L-arginine** shifts gel-filtration behavior toward altered/higher oligomerization states. Solubility is limited, with aggregation above about **1.3 mg/mL (zfNAGS-M)** and **2.0 mg/mL (zfNAGS-C)**. (pqac-00000004, pqac-00000005, pqac-00000026) | Caldovic et al., **2014-01**, https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pone.0085597; image summary from same paper (pqac-00000026) (pqac-00000004, pqac-00000005, pqac-00000026) |
| Stability and segment-specific effects | The truncated conserved form (**zfNAGS-C**) is more active and unfolds over a broader temperature range than **zfNAGS-M**, suggesting the variable segment is not required for catalysis and may instead tune oligomeric stability/regulation. Arginine also stabilizes zfNAGS-M thermally. (pqac-00000003, pqac-00000004) | zfNAGS-C has about **2-fold higher apparent Vmax** than zfNAGS-M; adding **1 or 10 mM L-arginine** increased zfNAGS-M thermal stability by about **1°C** and **3°C**, respectively. (pqac-00000004, pqac-00000006) | Caldovic et al., **2014-01**, https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pone.0085597 (pqac-00000003, pqac-00000004, pqac-00000006) |
| Developmental expression | **nags** transcripts are present maternally and are strongly expressed during the **first four days of zebrafish development**, together with multiple other urea-cycle genes/transporters. This supports an early developmental metabolic role rather than a liver-restricted adult-only function. (pqac-00000003, pqac-00000007, pqac-00000026) | Expression is detectable as early as the **32-cell stage** and remains high across approximately the first **4 days post-fertilization**; adult expression is also high. During 24–48 hpf, **NAGS, CPS3, OTC, ASS, ASL** plus transporters are expressed, whereas **Arg1** is absent in the first 24 hpf. (pqac-00000003, pqac-00000007, pqac-00000008, pqac-00000026) | Caldovic et al., **2014-01**, https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pone.0085597; image summary from same paper (pqac-00000026) (pqac-00000003, pqac-00000007, pqac-00000008, pqac-00000026) |
| Proposed physiological role in zebrafish | The best-supported interpretation is that zebrafish NAGS contributes to **citrulline/arginine biosynthesis early in development** and to **ammonia detoxification/ureagenesis after day 1**, although the first 24 hpf remain incompletely explained because **Arg1** is not yet expressed. Thus the role is developmental and nitrogen-metabolic, not solely a classic adult hepatic urea-cycle role. (pqac-00000003, pqac-00000004, pqac-00000007) | Developing zebrafish are reported to be **ureotelic during the first 48 h**, but the absence of **Arg1 mRNA** in the first 24 hpf leaves an unresolved mechanistic gap; alternative contributors such as **Arg2** have been proposed. (pqac-00000007) | Caldovic et al., **2014-01**, https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pone.0085597 (pqac-00000003, pqac-00000004, pqac-00000007) |
| Evidence from broader zebrafish metabolic studies | Independent zebrafish work on hyperammonemia and glucagon-receptor mutants references **nags** within ureagenic/nitrogen-metabolism networks, supporting functional integration of NAGS into zebrafish ammonia handling and amino-acid metabolism. (pqac-00000007, pqac-00000013) | Hyperammonemia rescue studies identify NAGS as required for N-acetylglutamate provision to CPS-dependent pathways; transcriptomic work reports **nags** among ureagenesis-related genes altered in metabolic mutants. | Recent 2024 mechanistic developments | New 2024 work on recombinant **human** NAGS refined current understanding of domain function and disease mechanisms: the **AAK domain** mediates arginine activation, whereas the **GNAT/NAT domain** mediates catalysis/substrate binding; many patient variants act through misfolding, loss of arginine activation, or increased **Km** for glutamate. These findings are not zebrafish-specific but strengthen interpretation of conserved NAGS biochemistry. (pqac-00000019, pqac-00000020, pqac-00000022) | In one 2024 study, **23** patient-derived nonsynonymous variants were tested; **17/23** showed increased misfolding tendency, and for **all but one** variant the biochemical defect explained disease causation. Some variants increased glutamate **Km 3–8-fold**. (pqac-00000019, pqac-00000022) | Gougeard et al., **2024-05**, JIMD, https://doi.org/10.1002/jimd.12747 (pqac-00000019, pqac-00000020, pqac-00000022) |
| Human/clinical relevance | NAGS is clinically important because **NAGS deficiency (NAGSD)** is a urea-cycle disorder that is uniquely treatable with **N-carbamylglutamate/carglumic acid**, a stable NAG analog that activates CPS1. Zebrafish NAGS biochemistry is therefore relevant for comparative nitrogen metabolism and evolutionary interpretation, even though direct zebrafish disease models for nags were not retrieved here. (pqac-00000011, pqac-00000016, pqac-00000018) | Recent estimates describe NAGSD as extremely rare: **<1 in 2,000,000 live births** in one 2024 case series and about **1:950,000 prevalence** in a 2024 datamining analysis. A 2024 seven-patient case series found all patients responded to carglumic acid with normalization of ammonia/citrulline and abnormal urine orotic acid. | Real-world implementation relevance | Expert consensus and case-series evidence show that carglumic acid can permit liberalization of protein intake, but treatment success depends on reliable access and prompt use in hyperammonemia.
