## 1. Key concepts and definitions (current understanding)

### 1.1 What ADSS1 is
Adenylosuccinate synthetase (AdSS; ADSS1/ADSS2 isoforms in vertebrates) is an enzyme in purine metabolism that converts **inosine monophosphate (IMP)** toward **adenosine monophosphate (AMP)** through the intermediate **adenylosuccinate (also called S-AMP)**. (kim2026lossofadenylosuccinate pages 1-5, patil2025adenylosuccinatesynthetasedeficiency pages 1-3)

### 1.2 Core catalytic step and substrate specificity
**Reaction step supported by evidence:** ADSS/ADSS1 catalyzes the conversion **IMP → adenylosuccinate (S-AMP)**, which is then converted to **AMP** by adenylosuccinate lyase (ADSL). (patil2025adenylosuccinatesynthetasedeficiency pages 1-3)

**Substrate usage supported by biochemical assays (enzyme-level evidence):** In an enzymology study on *Helicobacter pylori* adenylosuccinate synthetase, the enzyme reaction assays used **IMP, L-aspartate, and GTP** as substrates, and quantified formation of **adenylosuccinate**; **Mg2+** was included in the reaction buffer. This provides direct experimental support for the canonical substrate set used by adenylosuccinate synthetases (though this particular experiment is bacterial, the core reaction chemistry is conserved across AdSS enzymes). (wojtys2024vitaminb6inhibits pages 3-4, wojtys2024vitaminb6inhibits pages 2-3)

**Note on products:** The retrieved snippets did not explicitly state GDP/Pi as products; thus, only the substrate/product relations above are asserted from evidence. (wojtys2024vitaminb6inhibits pages 3-4, wojtys2024vitaminb6inhibits pages 2-3)

### 1.3 Pathway placement: de novo purine synthesis and the purine nucleotide cycle
- **Branch point context:** IMP is a branch point that can be converted into AMP through adenylosuccinate (S-AMP). (patil2025adenylosuccinatesynthetasedeficiency pages 1-3)
- **Purine nucleotide cycle (PNC):** ADSS and ADSL, together with AMP deaminase (AMPD; converts AMP back to IMP), are described as key enzymes of the **purine nucleotide cycle**, which supports **energy homeostasis in muscle** and generates by-products including **ammonia and fumarate** (with links to mitochondrial health). (patil2025adenylosuccinatesynthetasedeficiency pages 1-3)
- **Muscle adenine nucleotide pool entry:** ADSS1-mediated **IMP→S-AMP** is described as a canonical route by which newly synthesized purines enter the **adenine nucleotide pool in muscle**. (kim2026lossofadenylosuccinate pages 8-12)

## 2. Biological role of ADSS1 (mechanistic narrative)

### 2.1 Primary biochemical function
Across retrieved sources, ADSS1’s central function is consistently framed as catalyzing the **rate-limiting** first step of converting **IMP toward AMP** via adenylosuccinate in the purine nucleotide cycle. (kim2026lossofadenylosuccinate pages 1-5)

### 2.2 Isoforms and tissue specificity (inference for zebrafish ADSS1)
Although zebrafish-specific expression evidence was not retrieved, the vertebrate isoform concept is supported:
- ADSS1 is described as a **predominantly (not exclusively) skeletal muscle-expressed** isoform, with relevance to striated muscle physiology. (patil2025adenylosuccinatesynthetasedeficiency pages 1-3)
- ADSS2 is discussed as a more broadly expressed **non-muscle** isoform that catalyzes the same reaction and is considered a potential compensatory enzyme. (kim2026lossofadenylosuccinate pages 8-12, rybalka2024currentinsightsin pages 7-8)

Given UniProt Q6NSN5 is annotated as zebrafish **ADSS1**, the most evidence-aligned functional hypothesis is that zebrafish Adss1 is the **muscle-type adenylosuccinate synthetase** supporting high-demand adenine nucleotide metabolism.

### 2.3 Subcellular localization and “purinosomes” (what is known; what is not for zebrafish ADSS1)
**Purinosome concept:** Recent literature emphasizes that de novo purine synthesis enzymes can assemble into multi-enzyme structures (“purinosomes”) that regulate metabolic flux in space and time. (ayoub2024ajourneyinto pages 24-25)

**Localization evidence (general, not ADSS1-specific in zebrafish):** A 2024 review summarizes that purinosomes can **colocalize with mitochondria** (e.g., in HeLa cells) and that purine biosynthetic enzymes can be **localized around mitochondria** (e.g., in hippocampal neurons). It also reports that microtubule disruption can inhibit purinosome formation and de novo purine biosynthesis. (xie2024purinosomesinvolvedin pages 2-3)

**Evidence gap:** None of the retrieved full texts directly demonstrated that **zebrafish ADSS1 (Q6NSN5)** localizes to a specific compartment or is physically incorporated into purinosomes in zebrafish tissues/cells. Therefore, any zebrafish-specific localization should be treated as **unresolved** from the present evidence set.

## 3. Recent developments and latest research (prioritizing 2023–2024)

### 3.1 ADSS1 myopathy: coordinated research efforts and new assays (2024)
A 2024 ADSS1-myopathy meeting report (Orphanet Journal of Rare Diseases; publication date **Nov 2024**; URL https://doi.org/10.1186/s13023-024-03429-x) highlights several translational developments:
- Development of a **mass-spectrometry assay** to quantify ADSS reaction-related metabolites including **IMP, aspartate, S-AMP/ASA and AMP**. (rybalka2024currentinsightsin pages 11-12)
- Structural insight that ADSS1 is **dimeric**, and that a disease-associated Asp residue at the **dimer interface** is distant from the active site, suggesting oligomerization can be functionally important. (rybalka2024currentinsightsin pages 11-12, rybalka2024currentinsightsin pages 12-14)
- Availability of **purified wild-type and mutant ADSS1 proteins** from NIH and implementation of **AAV-based gene delivery** approaches for expressing human ADSS1 in muscle tissues in model systems. (rybalka2024currentinsightsin pages 11-12)
- Discussion that enzymatic inhibition did not phenocopy disease, raising the possibility of **non-canonical roles** for ADSS1 beyond catalytic activity in some settings. (rybalka2024currentinsightsin pages 7-8)

### 3.2 Renewed emphasis on regulation/organization of de novo purine synthesis (2024)
A 2024 review in *Frontiers in Pharmacology* (publication date **Feb 2024**; URL https://doi.org/10.3389/fphar.2024.1329011) describes renewed interest in regulation of de novo purine biosynthesis, including supramolecular assemblies and spatiotemporal control (purinosome-related biology) and cites multiple studies on enzyme interaction networks and purinosome regulation. (ayoub2024ajourneyinto pages 24-25, ayoub2024ajourneyinto pages 28-28)

### 3.3 Enzyme inhibition as application: AdSS as antimicrobial target (2024)
A 2024 study in *Journal of Enzyme Inhibition and Medicinal Chemistry* (publication date **Aug 2024**; URL https://doi.org/10.1080/14756366.2024.2372734) demonstrates that pyridoxal 5′-phosphate (PLP; active vitamin B6) can inhibit *H. pylori* AdSS by competing with GTP, using enzyme assays with **IMP, Asp, GTP** and measuring adenylosuccinate formation. This supports continuing interest in AdSS enzymes as drug targets and provides concrete assay conditions relevant to AdSS enzymology. (wojtys2024vitaminb6inhibits pages 2-3, wojtys2024vitaminb6inhibits pages 3-4)

## 5. Expert opinions/analysis from authoritative sources

### 5.1 Consensus view: ADSS1 as a muscle-critical purine enzyme
Multiple sources converge on the idea that ADSS1 is especially critical in **striated muscle** where energy demand is high, and that disruptions can produce characteristic myopathic phenotypes. (patil2025adenylosuccinatesynthetasedeficiency pages 1-3, rybalka2024currentinsightsin pages 7-8)

### 5.2 Mechanistic interpretation: bottleneck at IMP→adenylosuccinate
Metabolite signatures in ADSS1-deficiency models are interpreted as a biochemical bottleneck at the adenylosuccinate step, reflected by increases in IMP and changes in AMP/ADP patterns. (rybalka2024currentinsightsin pages 12-14)

## 6. Relevant statistics and data (from recent studies)

### 6.1 Quantitative metabolite changes linked to ADSS1 deficiency
In the 2024 meeting report, biochemical profiling of Adss1 knockout muscle reported that **IMP levels were ~3-fold higher** in non-contracted EDL muscle, consistent with impaired flux past IMP. (rybalka2024currentinsightsin pages 12-14)

### 6.2 Analytical performance for purine metabolite assays
The same report describes an ultra-performance LC method capable of quantifying purines and degradation products down to **< pmol/sample**, supporting feasibility of sensitive metabolite biomarkers. (rybalka2024currentinsightsin pages 12-14)

### 6.3 Enzymology assay substrate concentrations (AdSS enzyme assays)
In the 2024 AdSS inhibition study, saturating substrate concentrations used for activity/inhibition measurements included **0.06 mM GTP, 0.15 mM IMP, and 5 mM aspartate**, with Mg2+ in buffer, and adenylosuccinate formation monitored spectrophotometrically. (wojtys2024vitaminb6inhibits pages 3-4, wojtys2024vitaminb6inhibits pages 2-3)
