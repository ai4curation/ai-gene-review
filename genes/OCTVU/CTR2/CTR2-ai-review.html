Gene Description

CTR2 (cephalotocin receptor 2) is a G protein-coupled receptor of the oxytocin/vasopressin receptor superfamily in Octopus vulgaris. It is the second cephalotocin receptor identified in octopus (after CTR1). Functional expression in Xenopus oocytes demonstrated that CTR2 is specifically activated by cephalotocin (CT) via calcium-mediated signaling, but not by octopressin (OP) or vertebrate OT/VP peptides. Unlike CTR1, which is expressed primarily in the nervous system, CTR2 mRNA is mainly distributed in peripheral tissues with highest expression in branchia and vas deferens. Based on expression patterns and vertebrate analogy, CTR2 is proposed to mediate ammonia excretion (via branchia) and production/secretion of steroid hormones (via vas deferens and testis).

Core Functions

CTR2 is a G-protein coupled receptor specific for cephalotocin, a nonapeptide of the oxytocin/vasopressin superfamily. It signals via the calcium/inositol phosphate pathway as demonstrated by functional expression in Xenopus oocytes. It is not activated by octopressin or vertebrate OT/VP peptides. Unlike CTR1, which is expressed primarily in the nervous system, CTR2 is mainly distributed in peripheral tissues (branchia, vas deferens, testis), suggesting roles in ammonia excretion and steroid hormone regulation rather than neurotransmission.
