# DDO (D-aspartate oxidase) — Octopus vulgaris — Research Notes

## Gene Overview

DDO (D-aspartate oxidase, EC 1.4.3.1) is a FAD-dependent flavoenzyme that selectively catalyzes
the oxidative deamination of acidic D-amino acids, primarily D-aspartate and D-glutamate. It is
one of the best biochemically characterized cephalopod enzymes, thanks to extensive work by the
D'Aniello lab at Stazione Zoologica A. Dohrn, Naples.

UniProt: A0A7E6FSU6 (Swiss-Prot reviewed), 336 AA, monomer, MW ~37 kDa.

## Key Findings from Literature

### Enzyme Properties (PMID:7915543)
- Purified from O. vulgaris hepatopancreas
- Monomer of Mr 37,000 containing 1 mol FAD per mol protein
- Two enzyme forms: active (FAD-bound) and inactive (6-OH-FAD-bound, artifact of purification)
- N-terminal sequence shows similarity to bovine D-aspartate oxidase and porcine D-amino acid oxidase
- Kinetic parameters: KM 4.3 mM for D-Asp, 9.7 mM for D-Glu, 47.7 mM for NMDA
- Two active-site residues with pKa 7.1 and 9.1 critical for catalysis
- kcat 6.8 s-1 with D-Asp, 11 s-1 with D-Glu

### Substrate Specificity (PMID:8103425)
- D-AspO specifically oxidizes: D-Asp, D-Glu, D-Asn, D-Gln, D-Asp-dimethylester, N-methyl-D-Asp
- D-Pro, D-Leu, D-Ala, D-Met oxidized at very low rate (0.2-0.6% of D-Asp rate)
- All L-amino acids NOT oxidized — strict D-stereoselectivity
- Clearly distinct from D-amino acid oxidase (DAO) which handles neutral/hydrophobic D-amino acids

### Biological Role — D-amino acid detoxification (PMID:7903300)
- D-amino acids administered to animals are absorbed and oxidized by D-AspO and DAO in tissues
- Inverse relationship: younger animals have less oxidase → more free D-amino acids
- If not metabolized, D-amino acids accumulate and cause toxicity (suppress enzyme synthesis, inhibit growth)
- Enzyme is induced in young rats when mothers ingest D-Asp during pregnancy/suckling
- Biological role: **detoxification of D-amino acids** that accumulate during aging

### D-Aspartate in Cephalopod Nervous Tissue (PMID:8446003)
- Free D-Asp found at high concentrations in CNS of cephalopods (O. vulgaris, L. vulgaris, S. officinalis) and Aplysia
- D-Asp was 8.3% of total aspartate in Aplysia ganglia
- No other free D-amino acids detected in nervous tissue
- Suggests D-Asp may have a specific **neurological function** in molluscan CNS
- D-AspO from Octopus was used as analytical tool to measure D-Asp levels

### NMDA Receptor Context (PMID:18703100)
- NMDA receptors are present in Octopus olfactory lobes
- NMDA enhances GnRH mRNA expression in dose-response manner
- L-glutamate/NMDA/NO pathway controls reproductive maturation in O. vulgaris
- D-aspartate (the DDO substrate) can act as NMDA receptor agonist
- DDO therefore plays a role in modulating NMDA receptor-mediated signaling by regulating D-Asp levels

### Use as Analytical Tool (PMID:2306473)
- Octopus D-AspO widely used as analytical reagent for measuring D-amino acids
- Can detect 0.05 nmol D-amino acid in presence of 20 µmol L-amino acids (ratio 0.00025%)

## Summary of Core Functions

1. **Catalytic function**: FAD-dependent oxidative deamination of D-Asp and D-Glu (EC 1.4.3.1)
2. **Detoxification**: Protection against toxicity of D-amino acids
3. **Neuromodulation**: Regulation of D-Asp levels in CNS, thereby modulating NMDA receptor signaling
4. **Subcellular location**: Peroxisome matrix (by similarity to mammalian orthologs)
5. **Developmental regulation**: Expression increases postnatally in liver/kidney
