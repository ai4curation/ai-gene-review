Gene Description

Lss encodes lanosterol synthase/2,3-oxidosqualene cyclase, an endoplasmic-reticulum-associated enzyme that cyclizes (S)-2,3-epoxysqualene to lanosterol in sterol and cholesterol biosynthesis. The review accepts lanosterol synthase activity and cholesterol/sterol biosynthesis annotations, keeps membrane/lipid-droplet and lens protein-stability contexts as non-core, and modifies generic transferase or sterol-metabolism parents to the specific activity/pathway.

Research report: Rat Lss (lanosterol synthase/oxidosqualene cyclase; UniProt P48450) — functional annotation

1. Target verification (critical identity check)

The UniProt target P48450 from Rattus norvegicus corresponds to lanosterol synthase (also called oxidosqualene cyclase, OSC; gene symbol Lss, synonym Osc). Across the retrieved literature, the symbol Lss/LSS is consistently used for the enzyme that converts (S)-2,3-oxidosqualene (2,3-epoxysqualene) into lanosterol, i.e., the canonical OSC step in sterol/cholesterol biosynthesis. (zhao2021defectoflss pages 8-10, peeples2024chemicalinhibitionof pages 7-9)

No evidence in the retrieved corpus suggested that “Lss” refers to a different, ambiguous gene in rat in this context; rat studies explicitly place Lss among cholesterol biosynthesis genes. (chen2023hubgenestranscriptionally pages 1-2, nemoto2013comparisonofconstitutive pages 2-4)

2. Key concepts and definitions (current understanding)

2.1 Enzymatic function and reaction catalyzed

Lanosterol synthase (LSS/OSC; EC 5.4.99.7) catalyzes the cyclization of (S)-2,3-oxidosqualene → lanosterol, which is described as a key step in the cholesterol biosynthesis pathway. (zhao2021defectoflss pages 8-10, peeples2024chemicalinhibitionof pages 7-9)

Substrate specificity (evidence-limited statement): In the retrieved evidence, the substrate is explicitly stated as 2,3-oxidosqualene and the product as lanosterol; no alternative mammalian substrate/product specificity parameters (e.g., kinetic constants or alternate cyclization products) were provided in these sources. (zhao2021defectoflss pages 8-10, peeples2024chemicalinhibitionof pages 7-9)

2.2 Pathway placement: cholesterol/sterol biosynthesis

LSS sits downstream of squalene epoxidase (SQLE) (which generates oxidosqualene) and upstream of multiple “post-lanosterol” enzymes that remodel lanosterol toward cholesterol. Disruption of Lss in a mammalian lens model downregulated multiple cholesterol-pathway genes (including Lss, Cyp51, Tm7sf2, Msmo1, Fdft1, Sqle) consistent with its placement in sterol biosynthesis. (zhao2021defectoflss pages 8-10)

2.3 Subcellular localization and topology

Cholesterol biosynthesis is described as occurring in the endoplasmic reticulum (ER), where ER-associated feedback mechanisms regulate the pathway. (trapani2012regulationandderegulation pages 2-3)

For LSS/OSC specifically, the enzyme is described in the clinical genetics review context as a monotopic membrane protein, i.e., a membrane-associated enzyme rather than a multi-pass transmembrane protein. (kang2024clinicalandgenetic pages 9-9)

Important scope note: The strongest localization/topology evidence in this retrieval set is general (ER as the site of cholesterol biosynthesis machinery) plus “monotopic membrane protein” for human OSC; explicit immunolocalization of rat LSS protein to a specific ER subdomain was not found in the retrieved rat-specific papers. (kang2024clinicalandgenetic pages 9-9, trapani2012regulationandderegulation pages 2-3)

2.4 Regulation: SREBP/SREBP2 axis and cholesterol feedback

A canonical regulatory concept for cholesterol homeostasis is that genes involved in cholesterol metabolism (including biosynthetic enzymes) are transcriptionally controlled by sterol regulatory element binding proteins (SREBPs), which are synthesized and retained at the ER membrane and activated under sterol-depleted conditions. (trapani2012regulationandderegulation pages 2-3)

A 2024 mechanistic cancer study provides a contemporary example connecting cholesterol pathway activation to a USF1–SREBP2 axis, linking transcriptional control of cholesterogenic genes to accumulation of the intermediate lanosterol (the LSS product). (zhou2024inhibitionofdusp18 pages 2-4)

3. Rat-specific biological evidence (expression/regulation/contexts)

3.1 Rat liver: constitutive differences in Lss expression and Srebf2

In rat liver, Nemoto et al. compared expression of cholesterol-biosynthesis genes between SHRSP and WKY rats. Lss mRNA was substantially lower in SHRSP, with both microarray and RT-PCR support.

  • Lss: microarray ratio 0.46; real-time RT-PCR ratio 0.39 ± 0.094 (SHRSP relative to WKY), n = 4/strain, *p < 0.001. (nemoto2013comparisonofconstitutive pages 2-4)
  • Sqle: RT-PCR ratio 0.21 ± 0.032 (*p < 0.001). (nemoto2013comparisonofconstitutive pages 2-4)
  • Srebf2 (SREBP-2): microarray ratio 0.58–0.61; RT-PCR ratio 0.73 ± 0.13 (p < 0.05). (nemoto2013comparisonofconstitutive pages 2-4)

This provides rat in vivo evidence that Lss participates in a coordinated hepatic cholesterogenic gene program and covaries with Srebf2 expression across strains. (nemoto2013comparisonofconstitutive pages 2-4)

3.2 Rat lung inflammation model (2023): Lss induction in ATII cells

A 2023 transcriptomic study of rat alveolar type II cells (RLE-6TN) exposed to LPS (ARDS-like injury model) identified Lss among “hub genes” with increased transcription after LPS 5 μg/mL for 24 h, and qPCR validation supported the RNA-seq directionality. (chen2023hubgenestranscriptionally pages 1-2)

While fold-changes were not present in the retrieved excerpt, this study supports that Lss is inducible in a rat inflammatory context and is co-regulated with lipid metabolism/cholesterol biosynthesis genes potentially relevant to surfactant lipid remodeling. (chen2023hubgenestranscriptionally pages 1-2)

4. Recent developments (prioritized 2023–2024)

4.1 2024 immunometabolism mechanism: lanosterol as a tumor-derived immunoregulatory metabolite

A 2024 Nature Communications study identified a mechanism in colorectal cancer in which DUSP18 promotes immune evasion via a transcriptional axis that increases cholesterol biosynthesis and enables tumor cells to accumulate and release lanosterol into the tumor microenvironment. The mechanism is explicitly linked to the USF1–SREBP2 axis and to downstream impairment of CD8+ T-cell function via suppression of the mevalonate pathway and reduced KRAS prenylation. (zhou2024inhibitionofdusp18 pages 2-4)

This paper also reports use of Ro 48-8071, described as an LSS inhibitor, to block endogenous lanosterol production in mechanistic experiments, demonstrating continued relevance of LSS inhibitors as pathway probes in vivo-like settings. (zhou2024inhibitionofdusp18 pages 2-4)

4.2 2024 chemical biology and toxicology perspective: LSS as a druggable node and off-target liability

A 2024 review (“Chemical inhibition of sterol biosynthesis”) explicitly frames LSS inhibition as relevant to pharmacology and toxicology, cataloguing inhibitors and noting diverse biological consequences of modulating sterol flux, including diversion into a 24(S),25-epoxycholesterol shunt pathway in some contexts. It lists compounds used as LSS inhibitors or modulators, including BIBB-515, MM0299, and Ro 48-8071, and provides example screening concentrations that vary widely (e.g., 500 μM eremanthine; 20–50 μM in some screens; 0.25–0.5 μM diacylglycerols). (peeples2024chemicalinhibitionof pages 7-9)

4.3 2024 genetics and translational debate: lanosterol and cataract

A 2024 UK Biobank-based genetics study tested whether variants in the LSS gene region or a lanosterol-related genetic risk score support a protective role of lanosterol for cataract. After QC, it reports 45,449 cases and 353,371 controls, and found no statistically significant association in the LSS region under multiple-testing correction; the lanosterol-pathway GRS analysis found OR = 1.002, p = 0.568, arguing against a strong protective causal role of lanosterol in cataract at the population level (with caveats about cataract subtypes). (hashimi2024usinggeneticsto pages 3-4)

A 2024 review on cataract therapeutics summarizes mixed preclinical findings for lanosterol-based treatments. It reports, for example, that 25 mM lanosterol failed to reverse opacification of human senile cataract nuclei in one study, while other studies report benefit using animal delivery systems (e.g., intravitreal 2 mg/mL lanosterol nanoparticles every 3 days in dogs; a rat selenite cataract study using a nanosuspension containing 0.5% lanosterol + 0.6% nilvadipine for 28 days). The review also notes aqueous humor lanosterol levels varying 0 to 31.61 ng/mL in a cited primate study context. (wang2024researchprogresson pages 6-7)

4.4 2024 clinical genetics: LSS deficiency phenotypes

A 2024 clinical report expands the variant spectrum for LSS-related APMR4 syndrome (alopecia, intellectual disability, developmental delay/epilepsy), identifying two novel biallelic variants (c.401T>G, c.369C>G) and reporting that one variant reduced LSS protein expression (WB). (kang2024clinicalandgenetic pages 9-9)

5. Current applications and real-world implementations

5.1 LSS inhibitors as pathway probes and therapeutic leads

LSS inhibitors are being used as:

  • Mechanistic probes to block lanosterol production in immunometabolism studies (Ro 48-8071 in CRC tumor immunity work). (zhou2024inhibitionofdusp18 pages 2-4)
  • Drug discovery leads across diverse areas (antifungal/antiviral/anticancer) as summarized in the 2024 sterol-inhibition review, which explicitly discusses LSS inhibitors (BIBB-515, MM0299, Ro 48-8071) and their reported phenotypic effects in different systems. (peeples2024chemicalinhibitionof pages 7-9)

5.2 Lanosterol as a candidate anti-aggregation molecule in lens disease (with limitations)

Lanosterol delivery approaches (nanosuspensions, nanoparticles, local delivery systems) have been tested in animal cataract models, with evidence of reduced opacity in some settings and limitations in nuclear cataracts and in some human lens contexts; thus, lanosterol/LSS are part of an active translational debate rather than an established therapy. (wang2024researchprogresson pages 6-7, hashimi2024usinggeneticsto pages 3-4)

6. Expert opinions and authoritative synthesis

Authoritative synthesis from reviews and integrative studies emphasize:

  • The ER as the central location for cholesterol biosynthesis and SREBP-mediated feedback regulation. (trapani2012regulationandderegulation pages 2-3)
  • The broad uncertainty and potential safety implications of chemical sterol biosynthesis inhibition across enzymes, including LSS, with calls for improved mechanistic understanding and safety guidelines (especially during pregnancy) given that many exposures (including medications) can alter sterol pathways. (peeples2024chemicalinhibitionof pages 7-9)
  • A 2024 preprint proposes that mitochondria-associated ER membranes (MAM) function as a regulatory hub linking exogenous cholesterol uptake (HDL) to suppression of de novo biosynthesis machinery—contextually important for ER-localized enzymes such as LSS even though LSS is not singled out in the excerpt. (montesinos2024thecontributionof pages 1-4)

9. Conclusions (functional annotation for rat Lss; UniProt P48450)

Rat Lss (P48450) encodes an ER-associated, membrane-interacting sterol cyclase (OSC/LSS) whose primary biochemical function is the conversion of 2,3-oxidosqualene to lanosterol, a central gateway reaction into the cyclic sterol pathway leading to cholesterol. (zhao2021defectoflss pages 8-10, peeples2024chemicalinhibitionof pages 7-9, trapani2012regulationandderegulation pages 2-3)

Rat data support that Lss expression is coordinated with broader cholesterogenic programs (including Srebf2) in vivo (rat liver strain comparison) and is responsive to inflammatory stress in vitro (ATII LPS model). (nemoto2013comparisonofconstitutive pages 2-4, chen2023hubgenestranscriptionally pages 1-2)

Recent 2024 research highlights that the LSS product lanosterol can act as a biologically active intermediate in disease contexts (e.g., tumor immune microenvironment), and that LSS inhibitors remain actively used as mechanistic tools and potential therapeutic leads, while lanosterol-based cataract interventions show mixed efficacy and are challenged by contemporary human genetic evidence. (zhou2024inhibitionofdusp18 pages 2-4, peeples2024chemicalinhibitionof pages 7-9, wang2024researchprogresson pages 6-7, hashimi2024usinggeneticsto pages 3-4)
