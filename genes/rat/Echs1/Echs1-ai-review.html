Gene Description

Echs1 encodes mitochondrial short-chain enoyl-CoA hydratase. It hydrates trans-2-enoyl-CoA substrates and has lower enoyl-CoA isomerase activity, supporting fatty acid beta-oxidation and related valine/lysine catabolic steps. The review accepts specific hydratase/isomerase activities and direct catabolic processes, while keeping mitochondrial localization as context and modifying generic catalytic activity.

Core Functions

Echs1 hydrates short- and medium-chain enoyl-CoA substrates in mitochondrial beta-oxidation.

Molecular Function:
enoyl-CoA hydratase activity
Directly Involved In:
fatty acid beta-oxidation
Supporting Evidence:
  • UniProtKB:P14604
    FUNCTION: Converts unsaturated trans-2-enoyl-CoA species to the corresponding 3(S)-3-hydroxyacyl-CoA species and plays a key role in the beta-oxidation spiral of short- and medium-chain fatty acid oxidation.

Echs1 also catalyzes enoyl-CoA isomerization that feeds substrates into the hydratase step.

delta(3)-delta(2)-enoyl-CoA isomerase activity
