> - Rat **Gstt1/GSTT1-1** corresponds to the historical rat **GST 5-5** enzyme: a **theta-class, soluble cytosolic glutathione S-transferase** purified from rat liver cytosol as a homodimer with an apparent subunit size of about **28 kDa** (pqac-00000003, pqac-00000008).
> - Its core reaction is the canonical GST reaction (**EC 2.5.1.18**): **glutathione conjugation of electrophilic substrates**; theta-class GSTT1-1 is especially notable because some conjugation reactions contribute to **bioactivation**, not just detoxification (pqac-00000001, pqac-00000009).
> - In rat biochemical assays, GSTT1-1 shows substantial activity toward **dichloromethane (DCM)** and model substrates including **EPNP** and **4-nitrobenzyl chloride**, with reported specific activities of **7.8, 35.2, and 7.8 mmol/min/mg**, respectively; it also has **glutathione peroxidase activity** toward **cumene hydroperoxide** (**24.7 mmol/min/mg**) (pqac-00000005).
> - Substrate preference distinguishes rat GSTT1-1 from the related rat theta enzyme GSTT2-2: **GSTT1-1 prefers DCM and cumene hydroperoxide**, whereas GSTT2-2 is functionally distinct and shows different hydroperoxide/sulfate-ester preferences (pqac-00000003, pqac-00000005, pqac-00000008).
> - In rat liver cytosol, theta-class GSTs are a **minor but measurable fraction** of soluble protein: GSTT1-1 and GSTT2-2 together account for about **0.5% of total cytosolic protein**, and GSTT1-1 is less abundant than GSTT2-2 at an approximate **1:7 ratio** (pqac-00000003).
> - Recent rat in vivo proteomics still supports functional relevance of **Gstt1** in hepatic redox biology: in a **2024 NAFLD intervention study**, Gstt1 was identified as an **upregulated keystone protein** linked to **glutathione metabolism**, consistent with a role in antioxidant/xenobiotic defense pathways (pqac-00000016, pqac-00000017)
