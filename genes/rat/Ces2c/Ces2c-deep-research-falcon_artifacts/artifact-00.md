| Evidence summary for rat Ces2c (UniProt O70631): Gene/protein identity | Rat Ces2c is a CES2-family/type-B carboxylesterase in the serine hydrolase superfamily; CES enzymes have an α/β-hydrolase fold, a Ser-His-Glu catalytic triad, and glycine-based oxyanion-hole residues. CES2 is described as monomeric. Rodent CES proteins differ from human CES proteins in ER-retention sequence usage: human CES proteins commonly carry C-terminal HXEL motifs that support KDEL-receptor-mediated ER retention, whereas rodent CES proteins can carry HTEHK-like tails that do not bind KDEL receptor efficiently, helping explain why some rodent CES proteins are secreted. | Mechanistic and motif evidence is family-level or rodent-comparative; retrieved evidence did not provide a rat Ces2c-specific sequence validation beyond its placement in the rat CES2 group. | (pqac-00000004, pqac-00000000, pqac-00000002) |
| Evidence summary for rat Ces2c (UniProt O70631): Enzymatic reaction class and substrate preferences | CES enzymes catalyze hydrolysis of ester, thioester, amide, and carbamate bonds and can also support transesterification. CES2-family enzymes generally prefer substrates with a large alcohol group and relatively small acyl moiety, whereas CES1-family enzymes tend to prefer smaller alcohol groups and larger acyl groups. Historical rat liver literature recognizes acylcarnitine hydrolase activity (EC 3.1.1.28) within the broader carboxylesterase system, consistent with the UniProt annotation of O70631 as an acylcarnitine hydrolase; however, direct Ces2c-specific kinetic constants or purified-enzyme substrate panels were not retrieved here. | The acylcarnitine hydrolase assignment is supported by historical carboxylesterase context and UniProt target definition, but direct rat Ces2c kinetics were not available in retrieved full-text evidence. | (pqac-00000002, pqac-00000004, pqac-00000005, pqac-00000006) |
| Evidence summary for rat Ces2c (UniProt O70631): Tissue distribution in rat vs human vs mouse | Comparative distribution data indicate rat CES2 expression is concentrated in small intestine and is essentially undetectable in rat liver, kidney, and lung in the cited comparative table. In contrast, human CES2 is strongest in small intestine with detectable liver expression, while mouse CES2-family expression is broader and includes strong liver and small-intestine expression. | These data refer to species-level CES2 distribution patterns and not uniquely to rat Ces2c transcript/protein quantification. They are still useful for avoiding direct human/mouse-to-rat extrapolation. | (pqac-00000000, pqac-00000007, pqac-00000003) |
| Evidence summary for rat Ces2c (UniProt O70631): Subcellular localization | CES enzymes are synthesized into the ER lumen and are commonly recovered in microsomal fractions; CES1 and CES2 can also be detected in both microsomal and cytosolic liver fractions in some preparations. CES2 is described as a monomer in the ER. General localization rules indicate proteins with ER retrieval motifs remain luminal ER residents, whereas those lacking effective motifs can be secreted. | Direct rat Ces2c subcellular localization experiments were not retrieved; localization is inferred from CES-family cell biology and rodent-vs-human motif behavior. | (pqac-00000004, pqac-00000005, pqac-00000006) |
| Evidence summary for rat Ces2c (UniProt O70631): Regulation and pathway links | CES genes are regulated by nuclear receptors/transcription factors including PXR, CAR, GR, Nrf2, NR1H3/LXR, and PPARα, linking them to xenobiotic response and lipid-metabolic pathways. Inflammation and injury suppress CES expression/function in rodent models. Mouse Ces2c is especially inducible by the rodent CAR agonist TCPOBOP, more strongly than by the PXR agonist PCN; PPARα and other nuclear-receptor agonists can also increase hepatic Ces2c-family expression in mouse studies. | Retrieved regulation data are mostly human general CES2 or mouse Ces2c, not rat Ces2c-specific induction studies. Applicability to rat should be treated as plausible but unconfirmed. | (pqac-00000010, pqac-00000012, pqac-00000001, pqac-00000003, pqac-00000008) |
| Evidence summary for rat Ces2c (UniProt O70631): Recent 2024 developments | A 2024 expert review reports that CES2 has three promoters (P1, P2, P3), with P3 supporting low basal expression and P1/P2 contributing to tissue specificity. CES2 regulation is affected by drugs, inflammation, and coding/splice variants; some variants can preserve protein abundance yet abolish catalytic activity. The review highlights growing interest in plasma/extracellular-vesicle proteomics and endogenous metabolite signatures as future CES biomarkers, while noting that CES2 biomarker development remains immature. The same review emphasizes that tissue-specific CES2 expression has practical value for prodrug design and that CES2 has high catalytic relevance for irinotecan activation. | These are human-focused 2024 developments and should not be over-interpreted as rat Ces2c-specific biology. They are most useful for translational context and application framing. | (pqac-00000008, pqac-00000009, pqac-00000010, pqac-00000011) |
| Evidence summary for rat Ces2c (UniProt O70631): Quantitative statistics and recent data points | Human quantitative proteomics reported CES2 at about 30-174 pmol/mg protein in human liver microsomes and about 30 pmol/mg protein in intestinal microsomes; CES1 in human liver was much higher, about 176-1,664 pmol/mg protein. Comparative qualitative scoring showed rat CES2 as “+++” in small intestine and “-” in liver, kidney, and lung; human CES2 as “+++” in small intestine and “+” in liver; mouse CES2 as “+++” in both liver and small intestine. A 2024 review notes that the CES2 promoter SNP 830C>G was not associated with irinotecan AUC/response, and that some variants such as R34W and V142M can abolish catalytic activity despite increased protein. | Quantitative values are mainly human or comparative species-level measurements; rat Ces2c-specific enzyme kinetics, expression copy numbers, or fold-changes were not retrieved. | (pqac-00000007, pqac-00000003, pqac-00000008, pqac-00000011) |
