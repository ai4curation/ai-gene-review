| Enzymatic reaction | CFD/adipsin is the rate-limiting serine protease of the alternative complement pathway; it cleaves factor B when factor B is bound to C3b or C3(H2O), generating Ba and Bb and forming the C3 convertases C3bBb or C3(H2O)Bb (pqac-00000003, pqac-00000007) | Cleavage site reported as the FB Arg259-Lys260 bond in one primary study (pqac-00000003) | Primarily human biochemical data; conserved mammalian complement mechanism relevant to rat Cfd | Dani et al. 2023, *Frontiers in Immunology* — https://doi.org/10.3389/fimmu.2023.1197023 |
| Substrate specificity | CFD shows extreme preference for C3b-bound factor B (C3bB) over free factor B; this is a defining feature of its physiological specificity (pqac-00000002, pqac-00000026) | C3bB is ~20,000,000-fold better substrate than free FB; kobs/[E]T = 6.5 × 10^6 M^-1 s^-1 for C3bB versus 0.3 M^-1 s^-1 for free FB (pqac-00000002, pqac-00000026) | Human purified proteins / serum assays | Dani et al. 2023, *Frontiers in Immunology* — https://doi.org/10.3389/fimmu.2023.1197023 |
