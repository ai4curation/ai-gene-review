| Reaction | Mammalian ELOVL7 catalyzes the first, rate-limiting fatty-acid elongation step: condensation of acyl-CoA with malonyl-CoA to form 3-ketoacyl-CoA; this is the ELOVL-family 3-ketoacyl-CoA synthase activity consistent with EC 2.3.1.199. | Biochemical assay; structure; review | Product identified as 3-ketoacyl-CoA; +2 carbon elongation per cycle | Naganuma et al., 2011; Nie et al., 2021; Kihara, 2012 | https://doi.org/10.1016/j.febslet.2011.09.024 ; https://doi.org/10.1101/2020.11.11.378570 ; https://doi.org/10.1093/jb/mvs105 | (pqac-00000000, pqac-00000005, pqac-00000007) |
| Substrates | ELOVL7 elongates C16-C20 acyl-CoAs with strongest activity toward C18 substrates; active on saturated and unsaturated substrates, with highest activity reported for C18:3(n-3)-CoA and C18:3(n-6)-CoA in purified-enzyme assays; little or no activity toward C22-C26 saturated acyl-CoAs. | Biochemical assay; review | Preferred range C16-C20; highest toward C18:3(n-3) and C18:3(n-6); weak or none for C22-C26 saturated substrates | Naganuma et al., 2011; Kihara, 2012 | https://doi.org/10.1016/j.febslet.2011.09.024 ; https://doi.org/10.1093/jb/mvs105 | (pqac-00000000, pqac-00000003, pqac-00000007, pqac-00000008) |
| Kinetics | Purified, reconstituted mammalian ELOVL7 shows micromolar affinity for both fatty acyl-CoA substrate and malonyl-CoA; kinetic plots were linear, arguing against homotropic or allosteric regulation under assay conditions. | Biochemical assay | For C18:3(n-3)-CoA: Km 2.6 μM, Vmax 0.33 pmol/min/μg; for malonyl-CoA: Km 11.7 μM, Vmax 0.31 pmol/min/μg | Naganuma et al., 2011 | https://doi.org/10.1016/j.febslet.2011.09.024 | (pqac-00000000, pqac-00000003, pqac-00000009) |
| Essential motif | The conserved HxxHH motif is essential for catalysis; in mammalian ELOVL7 the motif is HVFHH, and mutation of this motif abolishes activity. | Biochemical mutagenesis; structural mechanism | HVFHH to HVFAA abolished activity | Naganuma et al., 2011; Nie et al., 2021 | https://doi.org/10.1016/j.febslet.2011.09.024 ; https://doi.org/10.1101/2020.11.11.378570 | (pqac-00000000, pqac-00000002) |
| Membrane requirement/localization | ELOVL7 is a membrane-embedded elongase of the ER. Activity is lost upon detergent solubilization and restored by reconstitution into phosphatidylcholine proteoliposomes, indicating a requirement for membrane embedding and lipid bilayer context. | Biochemical reconstitution; structure; review | Activity lost with Triton X-100 solubilization; restored after proteoliposome reconstitution | Naganuma et al., 2011; Nie et al., 2021; Wang et al., 2023 review | https://doi.org/10.1016/j.febslet.2011.09.024 ; https://doi.org/10.1101/2020.11.11.378570 ; https://doi.org/10.1186/s40001-023-01523-7 | (pqac-00000000, pqac-00000001, pqac-00000006, pqac-00000014) |
| Structural mechanism | Human ELOVL7 structure shows seven TM helices forming an inverted transmembrane barrel with a ~35 Å tunnel and active site deep in the membrane. Catalysis proceeds by a ping-pong mechanism involving an acyl-imidazole intermediate; H150 is the main nucleophile and H181 also participates in the covalent adduct seen structurally. | X-ray structure; intact-protein MS; mutagenesis | 2.6 Å structure; greater than 90 percent of purified protein carried a ~1073.66 Da covalent adduct; tunnel accommodates ~C20 with room up to ~C22 | Nie et al., 2021 | https://doi.org/10.1101/2020.11.11.378570 | (pqac-00000001, pqac-00000002) |
| Tissue expression pattern | Review evidence indicates ELOVL7 mRNA is present in most tissues except heart and skeletal muscle; relatively high expression in pancreas, kidney, prostate, and colon; lower in lung, ovary, spleen, and thymus. | Review | Qualitative tissue pattern; no single numeric expression value reported in extracted text | Wang et al., 2023 review | https://doi.org/10.1186/s40001-023-01523-7 | (pqac-00000014, pqac-00000016) |
