## Research Report: Rat **Casp3** (Caspase-3) — Functional Annotation (UniProt P55213)

### Executive summary
Rat **Casp3** encodes **caspase-3**, the canonical executioner (effector) caspase in apoptosis, functioning as a cysteine protease that cleaves substrates **after Asp residues** and preferentially recognizes **DExD/DEVD**-type motifs. Its activation requires proteolytic processing into **p17** and **p12** subunits (cleavage at **Asp28-Ser29** and **Asp175-Ser176**) and assembly into an active complex. Beyond apoptosis, recent 2024 work highlights caspase-3 as a node of crosstalk between apoptosis and pyroptosis via gasdermin cleavage and as a target of bacterial immune evasion strategies. (cohen1997caspasestheexecutioners pages 6-7, nicholson1999caspasestructureproteolytic pages 1-2, wright2024abacterialtoxin pages 1-3, ge2024phosphorylationofcaspases pages 5-5)

### 0) Mandatory identity verification (gene/protein disambiguation)
**Target identity is consistent and unambiguous** with the user-provided UniProt context (P55213; rat Casp3; caspase-3/CPP32/apopain/Yama). Seminal biochemical reviews explicitly equate **caspase-3** with **CPP32/Yama/apopain** and describe its hallmark processing into **p17/p12** and **DEVD**-based substrate/inhibitor usage. (cohen1997caspasestheexecutioners pages 5-6, cohen1997caspasestheexecutioners pages 6-7)

**Synonyms used in literature:** CPP32, apopain, Yama; caspase-3 is an effector caspase in the caspase family with conserved active-site motif **QACXG/QACRG**. (cohen1997caspasestheexecutioners pages 5-6, cohen1997caspasestheexecutioners pages 6-7)

### 1) Key concepts and definitions (current understanding)

#### 1.1 Caspase-3 biochemical function and catalytic mechanism
Caspases are cysteine proteases with an **absolute requirement for Asp at P1** of the scissile bond; caspase-3 is classified among effector caspases and is a principal apoptosis executioner. (nicholson1999caspasestructureproteolytic pages 1-2, zhra2024acomprehensiveexploration pages 1-2)

Caspase-3 is described as a cysteine protease (CPP32) that cleaves key apoptotic substrates such as **PARP**; the PARP cleavage site **DEVD/G** motivated the widely used synthetic substrate **Ac-DEVD-AMC** and corresponding inhibitors used to report caspase-3 activity. (cohen1997caspasestheexecutioners pages 5-6, chen1998inductionofcaspase3like pages 12-14)

#### 1.2 Substrate specificity (reaction and substrate preference)
At a functional-annotation level, caspase-3 catalyzes **peptide bond hydrolysis C-terminal to Asp residues** within preferred recognition motifs. Caspase-3 (group II caspase) is more stringent at **S4**, typically requiring **P4 Asp**, with a preferred motif described as **DExD**, consistent with the canonical **DEVD** motif widely used in assays. (nicholson1999caspasestructureproteolytic pages 1-2, cohen1997caspasestheexecutioners pages 5-6)

A recent protein-biosensor study reinforces that caspase-3 minimally recognizes a short motif and that DEVD↓-like motifs are canonical; it also emphasizes that **protein context/exosites** can shape apparent motif tolerance relative to peptide libraries. (blais2026usingabioluminescence pages 1-2, blais2026usingabioluminescence pages 12-13)

#### 1.3 Zymogen processing and activation into p17/p12
Caspase-3 is synthesized as an inactive precursor and activated by proteolytic processing into **p17** (large) and **p12** (small) subunits. A seminal review specifies cleavage at **Asp28-Ser29** and **Asp175-Ser176** to generate the active enzyme composed of p17 and p12. (cohen1997caspasestheexecutioners pages 6-7)

Mechanistic synthesis further describes that initiator caspase cleavage at **Asp175** separates large and small subunits, followed by **autocatalytic cleavage at Asp28** to remove the prodomain; activation increases activity on the order of ~**100-fold**. (malavez2012mechanismsofcaspase3 pages 36-41)

A 2024 Legionella study explicitly refers to self-processing at the prodomain linker **ESMD28-S29** as part of maturation to the p17 subunit and indicates mature p17 forms an active complex with p12. (ge2024phosphorylationofcaspases pages 5-6)

#### 1.4 Pathway placement: intrinsic and extrinsic apoptosis cascades
Apoptosis can proceed through:
- **Extrinsic pathway** (death receptor/DISC): initiator **caspase-8** (±10) activates effector caspases including caspase-3. (blais2026usingabioluminescence pages 2-3, nicholson1999caspasestructureproteolytic pages 1-2)
- **Intrinsic pathway** (mitochondrial): cytochrome c and APAF-1 form the apoptosome to activate **caspase-9**, which activates caspase-3. (blais2026usingabioluminescence pages 2-3, nicholson1999caspasestructureproteolytic pages 1-2)

### 2) Recent developments and latest research (prioritizing 2023–2024)

#### 2.1 Apoptosis–pyroptosis crosstalk via gasdermin cleavage (2024)
A 2024 Cell Reports study shows that bacterial **Shiga toxin (Stx)** suppresses noncanonical inflammasome signaling by activating **caspase-3**, which then cleaves and inactivates the active pore-forming **NT-GSDMD** fragment generated by caspase-11, thereby limiting pyroptosis and IL-1β maturation. (Wright et al., published Apr 23, 2024; https://doi.org/10.1016/j.celrep.2024.114004) (wright2024abacterialtoxin pages 1-3)

This work operationally reframes caspase-3 from being solely an apoptosis executor to also being a **regulator of inflammatory lytic cell death** through cleavage of gasdermin effectors in infection contexts. (wright2024abacterialtoxin pages 1-3)

#### 2.2 Pathogen immune evasion via caspase phosphorylation (Legionella LegK3; 2024)
A 2024 Nature Communications paper identifies the Legionella effector kinase **LegK3** as a direct modifier of apoptosis signaling. LegK3 phosphorylates **caspase-3 at Ser29** and reduces its cleavage/activation by initiator caspases while leaving intrinsic proteolytic activity intact. (Ge et al., accepted Sep 20, 2024; https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-024-52817-1) (ge2024phosphorylationofcaspases pages 1-2, ge2024phosphorylationofcaspases pages 5-5)

Mechanistically, Ser29 lies in the prodomain linker (ESMD28-S29) associated with maturation to the p17 subunit; LegK3-mediated phosphorylation makes procaspase-3 less suitable as a substrate for caspase-8/-9. (ge2024phosphorylationofcaspases pages 5-6, ge2024phosphorylationofcaspases pages 5-5)

Cropped figure panels from this paper document the Ser29 phosphosite identification by mass spectrometry and functional inhibition of activation cleavage. (ge2024phosphorylationofcaspases media 7149fc8b, ge2024phosphorylationofcaspases media bca9b615)

#### 2.3 Methodological advances in defining caspase-3 specificity (2024–2026)
A 2024 methods review summarizes a decade of innovation in caspase detection, highlighting advances enabling temporal/spatial measurement (live-cell imaging; FRET; FLICAs; activatable probes), microfluidic platforms, and MS-based degradomics and substrate mapping. (Zhra et al., May 2024; https://doi.org/10.3390/ijms25105460) (zhra2024acomprehensiveexploration pages 1-2)

A recent recombinant-protein BRET2 caspase-3 biosensor system supports quantitative kinetic measurements (kcat/KM) of cleavage-site variants in a protein context, emphasizing that protein-context specificity can be narrower than peptide library estimates. (Blais & Denault, Mar 2026; https://doi.org/10.1042/BSR20254030) (blais2026usingabioluminescence pages 1-2)

### 4) Current applications and real-world implementations (emphasis on 2023–2024)

#### 4.1 Quantitative analytical assays (electrochemical biosensing; 2024)
A 2024 Molecules paper demonstrates an electrochemical “signal-on” caspase-3 assay using a DEVD-containing peptide (Ac-GDEVDGGGPPPPC) on a gold electrode and methylene blue-loaded MOFs for amplification. It reports a **linear range of 0.1–25 pg/mL** and **LOD of 0.04 pg/mL**, and quantifies active caspase-3 in apoptosis-induced HeLa cells. (Huang et al., Aug 2024; https://doi.org/10.3390/molecules29153700) (huang2024signalondetectionof pages 1-2)

#### 4.2 Imaging and live-cell readouts (2024 review synthesis)
The 2024 IJMS review catalogs widely implemented modalities for caspase-3 activity, including:
- Fluorescent-labeled inhibitors (FLIs/FLICAs) and activity-based probes for live-cell labeling
- FRET sensors and genetically encoded reporters
- Activatable multifunctional probes for in vivo imaging
- Microfluidic + electrochemical platforms for live-cell caspase readouts
- MS-based formats (SAMDI-MS, LC-MS/MS, MALDI-TOF) for substrate/cleavage-product quantification and inhibitor screening
It also notes that noninvasive optical imaging of apoptosis using caspase-targeted probes has been demonstrated in **preclinical and clinical** settings. (Zhra et al., May 2024; https://doi.org/10.3390/ijms25105460) (zhra2024acomprehensiveexploration pages 12-13)

The same review reports quantitative performance examples across platforms, including an organic electrochemical transistor format with detection limit down to **0.1 pM** and an SPR-based approach with **1 pg/mL** LOD (in cited implementations). (zhra2024acomprehensiveexploration pages 9-11)

#### 4.3 Ratiometric fluorescent probes (2024)
A 2024 Frontiers in Chemistry paper reports a DEVD-based, self-immolative ratiometric probe (Ac-DEVD-PABC-Naph) for caspase-3, stating that **Km, kcat, and LOD** were obtained and that the probe is selective for caspase-3 over other endogenous species; however, numeric values are not present in the excerpted pages. (Wynne & Elmes, Jul 2024; https://doi.org/10.3389/fchem.2024.1418378) (wynne2024utilisinga18naphthalimide pages 1-2)

### 5) Rat-specific functional evidence and data (in vivo)
A rat transient global ischemia model provides direct rat-specific evidence: caspase-3 mRNA/protein induction in hippocampus and caudate-putamen after ischemia, increased p17 immunoreactivity over 4–72 h, increased DEVDase activity, and neuroprotection by intracerebroventricular infusion of the caspase-3 inhibitor **Z-DEVD-FMK** (reduced caspase-3-like activity, DNA fragmentation, and delayed CA1 neuronal loss). (Chen et al., Jul 1998; https://doi.org/10.1523/JNEUROSCI.18-13-04914.1998) (chen1998inductionofcaspase3like pages 12-14)

### 6) Expert opinions and authoritative analysis
Authoritative reviews (1997–1999, and updated 2024 methods synthesis) converge on the conceptual model that apoptotic signaling cascades converge on **caspase-3 activation** as a dominant effector step that drives widespread but selective proteolysis of apoptosis substrates (e.g., PARP), with high specificity for Asp-containing motifs. (cohen1997caspasestheexecutioners pages 5-6, nicholson1999caspasestructureproteolytic pages 1-2, zhra2024acomprehensiveexploration pages 1-2)

Recent expert synthesis emphasizes that detection technology has evolved from endpoint immunoblots to tools enabling **spatiotemporal monitoring** and **high-throughput drug screening**, including live-cell imaging and MS-based substrate mapping. (zhra2024acomprehensiveexploration pages 1-2, zhra2024acomprehensiveexploration pages 12-13)
