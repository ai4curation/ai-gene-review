Gene Description

Casp3 encodes rat caspase-3, the conserved effector cysteine protease that executes apoptosis after initiator-caspase activation. The ISO trace shows that the best-supported conserved transfers are cysteine-type endopeptidase activity, proteolysis, cytoplasmic/cytosolic localization, apoptotic process, execution phase of apoptosis, and apoptotic DNA fragmentation. Many additional ISO rows are second-hop transitive transfers or over-specific context terms from human, mouse, dog, pig, and chinchilla ortholog records, especially for stimulus-specific responses, pyroptosis, and pleiotropic developmental or immune phenotypes; those are kept as non-core, modified to more precise core terms, or removed.

Research Report: Rat Casp3 (Caspase-3) — Functional Annotation (UniProt P55213)

Executive summary

Rat Casp3 encodes caspase-3, the canonical executioner (effector) caspase in apoptosis, functioning as a cysteine protease that cleaves substrates after Asp residues and preferentially recognizes DExD/DEVD-type motifs. Its activation requires proteolytic processing into p17 and p12 subunits (cleavage at Asp28-Ser29 and Asp175-Ser176) and assembly into an active complex. Beyond apoptosis, recent 2024 work highlights caspase-3 as a node of crosstalk between apoptosis and pyroptosis via gasdermin cleavage and as a target of bacterial immune evasion strategies. (cohen1997caspasestheexecutioners pages 6-7, nicholson1999caspasestructureproteolytic pages 1-2, wright2024abacterialtoxin pages 1-3, ge2024phosphorylationofcaspases pages 5-5)

1) Key concepts and definitions (current understanding)

1.1 Caspase-3 biochemical function and catalytic mechanism

Caspases are cysteine proteases with an absolute requirement for Asp at P1 of the scissile bond; caspase-3 is classified among effector caspases and is a principal apoptosis executioner. (nicholson1999caspasestructureproteolytic pages 1-2, zhra2024acomprehensiveexploration pages 1-2)

Caspase-3 is described as a cysteine protease (CPP32) that cleaves key apoptotic substrates such as PARP; the PARP cleavage site DEVD/G motivated the widely used synthetic substrate Ac-DEVD-AMC and corresponding inhibitors used to report caspase-3 activity. (cohen1997caspasestheexecutioners pages 5-6, chen1998inductionofcaspase3like pages 12-14)

1.2 Substrate specificity (reaction and substrate preference)

At a functional-annotation level, caspase-3 catalyzes peptide bond hydrolysis C-terminal to Asp residues within preferred recognition motifs. Caspase-3 (group II caspase) is more stringent at S4, typically requiring P4 Asp, with a preferred motif described as DExD, consistent with the canonical DEVD motif widely used in assays. (nicholson1999caspasestructureproteolytic pages 1-2, cohen1997caspasestheexecutioners pages 5-6)

A recent protein-biosensor study reinforces that caspase-3 minimally recognizes a short motif and that DEVD↓-like motifs are canonical; it also emphasizes that protein context/exosites can shape apparent motif tolerance relative to peptide libraries. (blais2026usingabioluminescence pages 1-2, blais2026usingabioluminescence pages 12-13)

1.3 Zymogen processing and activation into p17/p12

Caspase-3 is synthesized as an inactive precursor and activated by proteolytic processing into p17 (large) and p12 (small) subunits. A seminal review specifies cleavage at Asp28-Ser29 and Asp175-Ser176 to generate the active enzyme composed of p17 and p12. (cohen1997caspasestheexecutioners pages 6-7)

Mechanistic synthesis further describes that initiator caspase cleavage at Asp175 separates large and small subunits, followed by autocatalytic cleavage at Asp28 to remove the prodomain; activation increases activity on the order of ~100-fold. (malavez2012mechanismsofcaspase3 pages 36-41)

A 2024 Legionella study explicitly refers to self-processing at the prodomain linker ESMD28-S29 as part of maturation to the p17 subunit and indicates mature p17 forms an active complex with p12. (ge2024phosphorylationofcaspases pages 5-6)

1.4 Pathway placement: intrinsic and extrinsic apoptosis cascades

Apoptosis can proceed through:
- Extrinsic pathway (death receptor/DISC): initiator caspase-8 (±10) activates effector caspases including caspase-3. (blais2026usingabioluminescence pages 2-3, nicholson1999caspasestructureproteolytic pages 1-2)
- Intrinsic pathway (mitochondrial): cytochrome c and APAF-1 form the apoptosome to activate caspase-9, which activates caspase-3. (blais2026usingabioluminescence pages 2-3, nicholson1999caspasestructureproteolytic pages 1-2)

The curated review includes several non-obvious annotations: neuron differentiation (GO:0030182), keratinocyte differentiation (GO:0030216), erythrocyte differentiation (GO:0030218), positive regulation of neuron apoptotic process (GO:0043525), enzyme activator activity (GO:0008047), and association with the death-inducing signaling complex (GO:0031264). These developmental and differentiation roles are well-established for Casp3 and represent important biology beyond simple apoptosis execution. BioReason captures only the core protease/apoptosis function without addressing the broader biological context.
