Gene Description

Rat n 1 (major urinary protein), a secreted lipocalin (calycin superfamily, lipocalin family) and the major rat allergen. MUPs bind and release volatile pheromones in their beta-barrel calyx and may protect them from oxidation, regulating social behaviours (aggression, mating, territory) and acting as kairomones. Rat n 1 is shed in urine and dander and is the dominant rat inhalant allergen (laboratory-animal allergy).

Suggested Questions for Experts

Q: What is the specific volatile pheromone/odorant carried by this MUP, and how does ligand identity relate to its behavioural (and kairomone) effects?

Q: Are the ISS-propagated metabolic roles (insulin-receptor/glucose-lipid regulation) genuine for any MUP, or an artifact of family-level transfer from one studied paralog? They are not supported for this secreted lipocalin.

Suggested Experiments

Experiment: Identify endogenous ligands co-purifying with the native protein by GC-MS and confirm binding/release kinetics of candidate volatiles to the recombinant protein by fluorescent-probe displacement.

Hypothesis: This MUP binds and slowly releases a specific volatile pheromone in its lipocalin calyx.
