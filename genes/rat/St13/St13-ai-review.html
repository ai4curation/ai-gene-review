Gene Description

Hsc70-interacting protein (Hip/ST13) is a TPR-containing co-chaperone that regulates the Hsp70 chaperone cycle. Hip forms homo-oligomers (tetramers) and binds the ATPase domain of Hsc70, stabilizing the ADP-bound state which has high affinity for substrate proteins (PMID:7585962). By slowing ADP dissociation from Hsc70, Hip attenuates active cycling and enhances aggregation prevention (PMID:23812373). Hip possesses its own chaperone activity, binding non-native proteins but unable to facilitate their refolding independently (PMID:9183013). Hip competes with BAG-1 (a nucleotide exchange factor) for binding to the Hsc70 ATPase domain (PMID:9528774).

Core Functions

Hip/St13 functions as a co-chaperone that stabilizes the ADP-bound state of Hsc70, promoting substrate retention and enhancing the productive protein folding cycle. It binds the ATPase domain of Hsc70 via its TPR domain, attenuating the Hsp70 ATPase cycle and competing with nucleotide exchange factors.
