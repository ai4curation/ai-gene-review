| Verified identity / synonyms | **Target verified as rat `Pgp` (UniProt D3ZDK7)** encoding a **HAD-like hydrolase** identified in the literature as **phosphoglycolate phosphatase (PGP)**, **glycerol-3-phosphate phosphatase (G3PP)**, and **AUM**. | Substrate specificity | Strongest physiological evidence supports **Gro3P as the primary in-cell substrate**. Activity toward **DHAP, glyceraldehyde-3-phosphate, 3-phosphoglycerate, creatine phosphate, glucose-6-phosphate, ribulose-5-phosphate** is much lower. Although mammalian PGP can hydrolyze **2-phosphoglycolate (2-PG)** in some assays and human enzyme showed affinity near **~1.5 mM**, measured cellular 2-PG in hepatocytes was **<0.02 mM**, far below Gro3P (**~2–4.5 mM**) and below enzyme Km, arguing that 2-PG is unlikely the main physiological substrate in these settings. | Mammalian biochemical and metabolomic inference; directly relevant to rat hepatocytes/beta-cell models | 2016 | 10.1073/pnas.1514375113; https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.1514375113 | (pqac-00000000, pqac-00000001, pqac-00000002) |
| Pathway placement / localization inference | Functions in **intermediary carbon and glycerolipid metabolism**, controlling the **Gro3P/DHAP node**, glycerol release, glycolysis/lactate production, NADH/NAD+ balance, ATP production, lipogenesis/re-esterification, and gluconeogenesis from glycerol. The available evidence in the retrieved sources supports a **soluble intracellular/cytosolic metabolic role**; no firm subcellular localization experiment was reported in the extracted passages. | Rat and mammalian functional inference; localization evidence limited in retrieved texts | 2016 | 10.1073/pnas.1514375113; https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.1514375113 | (pqac-00000000, pqac-00000003, pqac-00000014) |
| Beta-cell roles | In pancreatic beta cells/islets, G3PP explains **glucose-stimulated glycerol release** that is resistant to lipase inhibition, because glycerol can arise directly from Gro3P hydrolysis rather than lipolysis. **Knockdown** lowers glucose-induced glycerol release, increases Gro3P-derived glycerolipid synthesis, raises ATP/O2 consumption, and **enhances GSIS**; **overexpression** increases glycerol release, lowers GSIS, and protects against **glucotoxicity / glucolipotoxicity** and apoptosis. | Rat islets and INS832/13 beta-cell models | 2016 | 10.1073/pnas.1514375113; https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.1514375113 | (pqac-00000001, pqac-00000002, pqac-00000010) |
| Hepatocyte roles | In hepatocytes, G3PP lowers Gro3P and shifts flux away from glycolysis-linked NADH generation and glycerolipid synthesis. **Overexpression** decreases DHAP, ATP formation, lactate production, and cellular NADH/NAD+ pools; **knockdown** has opposite effects and increases ATP. In primary rat hepatocytes, knockdown markedly **increases gluconeogenesis** from glycerol and from pyruvate+lactate, whereas hG3PP overexpression can **virtually abolish gluconeogenesis** in the reported experiments. | Primary rat hepatocytes and in vivo rat liver overexpression | 2016 | 10.1073/pnas.1514375113; https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.1514375113 | (pqac-00000000, pqac-00000003, pqac-00000010) |
| Tissue expression highlights | Reported as **widely expressed** with relatively **high expression in testis, heart, skeletal muscle, and islets**, and **lower expression in liver, kidney, intestine, and visceral white adipose tissue**. Expression is nutritionally regulated, with differences across adipose depots and feeding states. | Mammalian expression profiling, including rat-relevant physiology context | 2016 | 10.1073/pnas.1514375113; https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.1514375113 | (pqac-00000001, pqac-00000005) |
| In vivo rat phenotypes after liver overexpression | Adenoviral liver overexpression in rats increased **plasma glycerol**, reduced **hepatic glucose production from glycerol** during glycerol load testing, lowered **plasma triglycerides**, reduced **body weight gain/body weight**, and one excerpt also notes increased **plasma HDL**. These findings support G3PP as a modulator of whole-body glucose and lipid homeostasis. | **Direct rat in vivo evidence** | 2016 | 10.1073/pnas.1514375113; https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.1514375113 | (pqac-00000000, pqac-00000003, pqac-00000005) |
