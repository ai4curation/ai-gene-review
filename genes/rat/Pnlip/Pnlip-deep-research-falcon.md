## Research Report: Rat **Pnlip** (UniProt P27657) — Pancreatic Triacylglycerol Lipase (EC 3.1.1.3)

### 0) Target verification (critical identity check)
The UniProt target provided (P27657; **Rattus norvegicus** gene **Pnlip**) corresponds to **pancreatic triacylglycerol lipase** (also called pancreatic lipase; **EC 3.1.1.3**), a major **exocrine pancreatic digestive enzyme** responsible for hydrolysis of dietary triacylglycerols. This identity matches the terminology used in the primary literature on PNLIP as “pancreatic triglyceride lipase,” including explicit assignment of **EC 3.1.1.3** and exocrine pancreatic localization. (toldi2021missensepnlipmutations pages 1-2, khatua2019carboxylesterlipase pages 1-2)

Structurally, literature descriptions of PNLIP as an N‑terminal catalytic domain plus C‑terminal β‑sandwich domain involved in colipase binding align with the UniProt-reported membership in the lipase/AB-hydrolase fold superfamily. (toldi2021missensepnlipmutations pages 1-2)

### 1) Key concepts and definitions (current understanding)

#### 1.1 Enzymatic function (primary functional annotation)
**Pancreatic triacylglycerol lipase (PNLIP)** catalyzes hydrolysis of **dietary triacylglycerols (TAGs)** in the gastrointestinal tract. In the intestinal lumen (classically the duodenum), TAG hydrolysis generates absorbable lipid products, typically described as **monoacylglycerols and free fatty acids (FFAs)**, enabling intestinal uptake. (toldi2021missensepnlipmutations pages 1-2, subramaniyan2025roleofpancreatic pages 3-4)

A core definition supported by multiple sources is that PNLIP “breaks down the majority of dietary triacylglycerols in the gastrointestinal tract,” emphasizing its dominant contribution to adult/older-child fat digestion relative to other lipases. (toldi2021missensepnlipmutations pages 1-2, lin2023characterizationofnovel pages 1-3)

#### 1.2 Substrate specificity and biochemical assays
Physiologically, PNLIP targets long‑chain dietary TAGs at the lipid–water interface; experimentally, activity is often measured using model TAG substrates such as **tributyrin**. In a quantitative enzymatic assay, soluble wild-type PNLIP released **1.8 ± 0.097 μmol fatty acid/min** from tributyrin (**n = 3**), whereas a misfolding mutant (T221M) had **no detectable activity** in the same assay context—supporting that lipolysis is the primary biochemical readout for function and that proper folding is essential for catalysis. (szabo2015anovelmutation pages 2-3)

#### 1.3 Cofactor dependence: colipase and bile salts
A defining mechanistic feature of pancreatic lipase biology is the requirement for **colipase** to maintain activity under intestinal conditions that include **bile salts** and other surfactants.

* **Colipase function:** Colipase stabilizes the active state of PNLIP and restores enzymatic activity in the presence of inhibitory substances (including bile salts), effectively enabling productive adsorption/anchoring of lipase at the lipid interface. (toldi2021missensepnlipmutations pages 1-2, jagtap2025syntheticpancreaticlipase pages 5-6)
* **Colipase processing:** Colipase is synthesized as a precursor and its N‑terminal propeptide is cleaved by **trypsin**, producing the active cofactor. (toldi2021missensepnlipmutations pages 1-2)
* **Bile salt inhibition:** Bile salts can inhibit pancreatic lipase by interfering with lipase adsorption to the triglyceride–water interface; colipase counteracts this inhibition and supports interfacial catalysis. (toldi2021missensepnlipmutations pages 1-2, jagtap2025syntheticpancreaticlipase pages 5-6)

#### 1.4 Domain architecture, catalytic machinery, and activation mechanism
PNLIP comprises a **globular N‑terminal catalytic domain** and a **C‑terminal β‑sandwich domain** that contains key determinants of colipase binding. (toldi2021missensepnlipmutations pages 1-2)

Catalysis uses a canonical serine hydrolase **catalytic triad (Ser–Asp–His)** typical of mammalian pancreatic lipases, and activity depends on interfacial activation involving movement of a surface “lid” that exposes the active site at the lipid–water interface. (jagtap2025syntheticpancreaticlipase pages 5-6)

Unlike many digestive proteases, activation of PNLIP is described as depending on **structural rearrangements of surface loops** rather than proteolytic cleavage of the lipase itself. (toldi2021missensepnlipmutations pages 1-2)

### 2) Expression and localization (rat/rodent-relevant evidence)

#### 2.1 Cellular source and secretion pathway
PNLIP is an exocrine enzyme produced by **pancreatic acinar cells** and secreted into the gastrointestinal tract as part of pancreatic juice, where it performs extracellular digestion of dietary lipids. (toldi2021missensepnlipmutations pages 1-2, subramaniyan2025roleofpancreatic pages 3-4)

Rodent acinar-cell localization evidence (mouse) shows that properly folded PNLIP traffics to the regulated secretory pathway: in wild-type acinar cells, PNLIP localizes to punctate structures that **co-localize with amylase in zymogen granules**. (zhu2023preclinicalmousemodel pages 4-6)

#### 2.2 Site of action
Functional descriptions place pancreatic lipase action in the **duodenum / intestinal lumen**, where dietary TAGs are hydrolyzed to products for absorption. (subramaniyan2025roleofpancreatic pages 3-4)

### 3) Recent developments and latest research (prioritizing 2023–2024)

#### 3.1 2023: Pnlip misfolding variant as a causal driver of chronic pancreatitis (mechanistic animal model)
A major 2023 advance is a **preclinical knock-in mouse model** demonstrating that a misfolding Pnlip variant can cause **progressive chronic pancreatitis (CP)** via proteotoxic stress mechanisms.

* **Study:** Zhu et al., *Gut* (publication year 2023; DOI: https://doi.org/10.1136/gutjnl-2022-327960; online/issue dated 2023). (zhu2023preclinicalmousemodel pages 1-3)
* **Key concept:** CP can result from **mutation-induced misfolding** of a highly expressed acinar secretory enzyme, causing **ER retention**, unfolded protein response activation, inflammation, and pancreatic atrophy—rather than CP being driven solely by abnormal trypsin activation.
* **Localization/trafficking evidence:** In acinar cells, wild-type PNLIP localizes to zymogen granules (amylase-positive puncta), whereas **PNLIP T221M** displays diffuse staining with increased overlap with the ER marker **calreticulin**, indicating **ER retention** and altered secretory trafficking. (zhu2023preclinicalmousemodel pages 4-6)
* **Quantitative functional impairment:** In homozygous Pnlip T221M mice, **pancreatic lipolytic activity is ~5–15% of wild-type** and animals develop **steatorrhea on a 60% fat diet**, linking loss of secreted enzymatic function to maldigestion. (zhu2023preclinicalmousemodel pages 4-6)
* **Aggregation burden statistic:** Detergent-insoluble aggregates of T221M in heterozygotes were reported to account for **<1% of total intracellular PNLIP** across ages 1–12 months, yet disease still progressed in heterozygotes more slowly—supporting a model where even modest proteotoxic burden in long-lived acinar cells can be pathologic. (zhu2023preclinicalmousemodel pages 4-6)
* **ER stress/UPR mechanisms:** The model documents increased BiP/Hspa5, UPR pathway activation, CHOP/Ddit3 induction, NF‑κB activation, and cell death markers, consistent with **proteotoxic ER stress** in acinar cells as a driver of CP. (zhu2023preclinicalmousemodel pages 23-26, zhu2023preclinicalmousemodel pages 8-9)

**Expert synthesis/analysis:** Collectively, this work strengthens an emerging framework in pancreatitis genetics: highly expressed digestive enzymes (including PNLIP) can behave as “acinar proteostasis liabilities,” where misfolding variants trigger ER stress and inflammatory remodeling. This is mechanistically distinct from canonical trypsin-centric models and is directly relevant to functional annotation because it emphasizes that correct folding/trafficking is central to PNLIP’s physiological role and that intracellular retention is a disease mechanism. (zhu2023preclinicalmousemodel pages 1-3, zhu2023preclinicalmousemodel pages 8-9)

#### 3.2 2023: Acinar transcriptional regulation including Pnlip (NFIC)
Cobo et al. (Nature Communications, 2023; DOI: https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-023-39291-x) identify **NFIC** as a regulator of the acinar gene program, including digestive enzymes.

* Pnlip is explicitly included among loci/promoters with enriched occupancy in **NFIC** and **NR5A2** ChIP analyses (reported **P = 0.002** for Pnlip enrichment in the figure legend context), supporting direct transcriptional linkage between acinar differentiation factors and Pnlip expression. (cobo2023nficregulatesribosomal pages 2-3)

**Expert synthesis/analysis:** For functional annotation, this provides evidence that Pnlip is not merely “expressed in pancreas,” but is part of the core acinar differentiation/transcriptional network that coordinates secretory capacity and proteostasis (relevant given the ER-stress susceptibility described above). (cobo2023nficregulatesribosomal pages 2-3)

#### 3.3 2023: Human genetics/variant characterization informs conserved PNLIP biology
Lin et al. (Pancreatology, Dec 2023; DOI: https://doi.org/10.1016/j.pan.2023.10.022) characterized novel human PNLIP variants causing congenital pancreatic lipase deficiency.

* PNLIP is described as the **primary enzyme for dietary fat digestion** in older children/adults. (lin2023characterizationofnovel pages 1-3)
* Variants (e.g., **R188C**, **W102\***, **W419\*** ) exhibit secretion defects and (for some) detergent-insoluble aggregation and ER stress marker induction (BiP), reinforcing the conserved relationship among folding, secretion, and digestive function. (lin2023characterizationofnovel pages 1-3)

### 4) Current applications and real-world implementations

#### 4.1 Drug targeting of pancreatic lipase (anti-obesity approach)
Pancreatic lipase is a prominent therapeutic target for reducing dietary fat absorption. Contemporary reviews continue to frame lipase inhibition as a key mechanism in obesity pharmacotherapy, with **orlistat** highlighted as a benchmark clinically used inhibitor of gastric/pancreatic lipases in this class. (subramaniyan2025roleofpancreatic pages 3-4)

Mechanistically, inhibitor discovery frequently leverages the conserved catalytic triad, lid dynamics, and colipase/interface interactions of mammalian pancreatic lipases. (jagtap2025syntheticpancreaticlipase pages 5-6)

#### 4.2 Laboratory and diagnostic usage: activity assays and secretion readouts
PNLIP function is commonly quantified by hydrolysis assays (e.g., tributyrin substrate assays and pH‑stat assays of conditioned media). These are used in cell models (including pancreatic/acinar models) to distinguish secreted, active enzyme from intracellularly retained inactive variants. (szabo2015anovelmutation pages 2-3)

### 5) Relevant statistics and data points (recent and/or high-confidence)

* **WT enzymatic activity in vitro (tributyrin substrate):** 1.8 ± 0.097 μmol fatty acid/min (n = 3) for soluble wild-type PNLIP; mutant T221M shows no detectable activity. (szabo2015anovelmutation pages 2-3)
* **Misfolding/aggregation quantitative signals:** intracellular T221M levels significantly higher than WT (P ≤ 0.001) and ~7‑fold more mutant in detergent‑insoluble fraction (P ≤ 0.001) in a secretion/misfolding study. (szabo2015anovelmutation pages 2-3)
* **In vivo functional deficit in 2023 knock-in model:** Pnlip T221M homozygotes show ~5–15% of WT pancreatic lipolytic activity and steatorrhea on a 60% fat diet. (zhu2023preclinicalmousemodel pages 4-6)
* **Aggregate fraction statistic:** detergent‑insoluble aggregates account for <1% of total intracellular PNLIP in heterozygotes across 1–12 months, despite progressive pathology. (zhu2023preclinicalmousemodel pages 4-6)
* **Variant spectrum statistic:** one study evaluated 19 rare heterozygous PNLIP mutations in the context of secretion defects and ER stress. (toldi2021missensepnlipmutations pages 1-2)
* **Pathophysiology-associated measurement:** in human fat necrosis/panniculitis samples associated with pancreatitis, free fatty acids were reported as >10 mmol/L; PNLIP immunoreactivity is increased in fat necrosis and pancreatic lipases can be lipotoxic when leaked. (khatua2019carboxylesterlipase pages 1-2)

### 6) Limitations of the retrieved evidence (important for annotation quality)
Although the target is rat Pnlip (UniProt P27657), much of the most detailed mechanistic and quantitative evidence available in the retrieved full texts is from **mouse** models and **human** variant studies, with rat-specific experimental evidence mainly appearing through rat acinar cell model usage in secretion/activity assays rather than comprehensive rat tissue atlases in the retrieved set. The core functional annotation (EC 3.1.1.3 TAG hydrolase; acinar secretion; colipase/bile-salt dependence) is, however, strongly conserved across mammals and is directly supported by rodent-relevant studies. (toldi2021missensepnlipmutations pages 1-2, szabo2015anovelmutation pages 2-3, zhu2023preclinicalmousemodel pages 4-6)
