| Identity | UniProt P70711 is provided as rat **Ube2d2b/Ube2d2**, a **UBE2D-family ubiquitin-conjugating E2 enzyme** with a canonical **UBC catalytic domain**; retrieved literature supports this family assignment and does not indicate a conflicting protein identity. UBE2D proteins are canonical E2 intermediates that transfer ubiquitin from E1 to substrates via E3 ligases. (pqac-00000000, pqac-00000003) | Roman-Trufero & Dillon 2022; Alpi et al. 2016 | https://doi.org/10.3389/fcell.2022.1058751; https://doi.org/10.1042/bcj20160028 |
| Reaction | Functional annotation is consistent with **EC 2.3.2.23** ubiquitin-conjugating enzyme activity: ubiquitin is activated by E1, transferred to the E2 **active-site cysteine** by **transthiolation**, then passed to substrate lysine (often through a RING/U-box E3) or to a HECT/RBR E3 intermediate. This matches the UniProt description for P70711. (pqac-00000004, pqac-00000002) | Roman-Trufero & Dillon 2022; Du et al. 2021 | https://doi.org/10.3389/fcell.2022.1058751; https://doi.org/10.3390/ijms22073440 |
| Mechanism | E2 enzymes share a ~150 aa UBC fold with catalytic cysteine in a shallow groove; the active site is supported by a conserved **His-Pro-Asn** motif, and a negatively charged residue in the α3–α4 loop helps align the incoming lysine. UBE2D~Ub toggles from an **open/inactive** to **closed/active** conformation upon E3 binding; RING/U-box E3s stabilize the closed state, and **backside ubiquitin binding** can enhance processivity. (pqac-00000001, pqac-00000007) | Alpi et al. 2016; Roman-Trufero & Dillon 2022 | https://doi.org/10.1042/bcj20160028; https://doi.org/10.3389/fcell.2022.1058751 |
| Specificity | UBE2D family members are described as **relatively promiscuous** E2 donors that can work with many E3s and can support monoubiquitination or different chain-building outputs depending on the E3/context. Direct intrinsic linkage specificity for rat Ube2d2b specifically was not established in the retrieved evidence; instead, specificity appears largely **partner/E3/context-dependent**. (pqac-00000007, pqac-00000009) | Roman-Trufero & Dillon 2022; McAlpine et al. 2024 | https://doi.org/10.3389/fcell.2022.1058751; https://doi.org/10.1101/2024.03.10.583603 |
| Pathways | UBE2D enzymes participate in signaling and chromatin pathways including **RTK turnover**, **Hedgehog**, **TGFβ**, **NF-κB**, **DNA repair**, **p53 regulation**, APC/C-mediated cell-cycle ubiquitination, and histone/DNA methylation control via **Ring1B** and **UHRF1**. These pathway assignments are family-level and are the best-supported inference for rat Ube2d2b. (pqac-00000004, pqac-00000007) | Roman-Trufero & Dillon 2022 | https://doi.org/10.3389/fcell.2022.1058751 |
| E3 partners/examples | Experimentally or review-supported examples for UBE2D/UBE2D2 include **CBL** (growth factor receptor regulation), **BRCA1** (DNA repair), **RNF4**, **SCFβTRCP** (Gli2/3 processing), **RNF111/Arkadia** (SnoN degradation in TGFβ), **RNF138** (IκB/CtIP-related functions), **UHRF1**, **APC/C**, and experimental assays with **RNF12 RING**. These examples support broad E3 compatibility rather than a single dedicated substrate class. (pqac-00000000, pqac-00000007, pqac-00000011) | Roman-Trufero & Dillon 2022; McAlpine et al. 2024 | https://doi.org/10.3389/fcell.2022.1058751; https://doi.org/10.1101/2024.03.10.583603 |
| Regulation | UBE2D proteins can be regulated by **phosphorylation, acetylation, S-nitrosylation, and self-ubiquitination**; for UBE2D3, phosphorylation can alter folding and reduce stability. Backside ubiquitin binding also functions as a regulatory input by promoting assembly/processivity. (pqac-00000000, pqac-00000007) | Roman-Trufero & Dillon 2022 | https://doi.org/10.3389/fcell.2022.1058751 |
| Tool compounds / engineering | Recent 2024 work produced **linked-domain protein inhibitors** that bind both the **RING-binding site** and **backside site** on UBE2D, with affinities spanning **~3×10^-6 to ~1×10^-9 M**; a related 2024 study identified **six ubiquitin variants (UbVs)** that inhibit Ube2d2 chain building with **low micromolar affinity** and high intra-family specificity. A 2024 engineered E2-bioPROTAC study further highlights the tractability of E2 enzymes for targeted degradation technologies, although not rat-specific. (pqac-00000005, pqac-00000009, pqac-00000012) | Bukhari et al. 2024; McAlpine et al. 2024; Taylor et al. 2024 | https://doi.org/10.1101/2024.09.02.610852; https://doi.org/10.1101/2024.03.10.583603; https://doi.org/10.1038/s42003-024-06803-4 |
