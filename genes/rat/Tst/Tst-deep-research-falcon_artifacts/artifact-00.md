| Identity / verification | Rat **Tst** (UniProt **P24329**) corresponds to **thiosulfate sulfurtransferase / rhodanese**, a mitochondrial rhodanese-fold sulfurtransferase. Rodent TST is reported as ~320 aa and ~33 kDa, with a catalytic Cys in the C-terminal rhodanese domain; this matches the UniProt description and rhodanese-family domain architecture (pqac-00000007, pqac-00000013). | Alsohaibani et al., 2023, https://doi.org/10.3390/antiox12040843; Kruithof et al., 2020, https://doi.org/10.1016/j.bbadis.2020.165716 |
| Primary reaction / catalytic function | Canonical reaction: **thiosulfate + cyanide ⇄ sulfite + thiocyanate**, the classical assay reaction underlying its historical role in cyanide detoxification. More broadly, TST transfers a sulfane sulfur atom from sulfane-containing donors to nucleophilic acceptors, forming enzyme-bound persulfide intermediates (pqac-00000007, pqac-00000011, pqac-00000005). | Kruithof et al., 2020, https://doi.org/10.1016/j.bbadis.2020.165716; Alsohaibani et al., 2023, https://doi.org/10.3390/antiox12040843 |
| Substrate specificity / current understanding | Recent review evidence indicates TST is not limited to thiosulfate: **GSSH (glutathione persulfide)** is a strong physiological substrate candidate in mitochondria, and human TST shows substantially higher catalytic efficiency with GSSH than with thiosulfate; sulfite + GSSH → thiosulfate is strongly favored. Kinetic cooperativity/hysteresis complicates strict substrate ranking, so “physiological specificity” is still being refined (pqac-00000003, pqac-00000010, pqac-00000012). | Alsohaibani et al., 2023, https://doi.org/10.3390/antiox12040843; Kruithof et al., 2020, https://doi.org/10.1016/j.bbadis.2020.165716 |
| Subcellular localization | TST is consistently described as **mitochondrial**, including in the **mitochondrial matrix** / mitochondrial sulfur oxidation machinery. Human-focused mechanistic reviews note mitochondrial targeting despite lack of a classical N-terminal targeting sequence; this is commonly extrapolated to mammalian TST including rat orthologs (pqac-00000002, pqac-00000012, pqac-00000008). | Alsohaibani et al., 2023, https://doi.org/10.3390/antiox12040843; Kruithof et al., 2020, https://doi.org/10.1016/j.bbadis.2020.165716 |
| Pathway placement | TST functions in the **mitochondrial sulfide oxidation unit/pathway**, downstream of **SQOR** and **ETHE1/PDO**, helping convert sulfane sulfur/persulfide equivalents into **thiosulfate** and supporting detoxification of mitochondrial H2S/reactive sulfur species. Reviews describe the pathway as **SQOR → ETHE1/PDO → TST**, with SQOR-generated GSSH feeding TST chemistry (pqac-00000002, pqac-00000012, pqac-00000010). | Alsohaibani et al., 2023, https://doi.org/10.3390/antiox12040843; Dawoud et al., 2024, https://doi.org/10.1080/13510002.2024.2437338; Kruithof et al., 2020, https://doi.org/10.1016/j.bbadis.2020.165716 |
| Biological roles | Supported roles include **cyanide detoxification**, **mitochondrial H2S disposal**, **sulfane sulfur / persulfide trafficking**, **selenium metabolism**, and contribution to **iron–sulfur cluster restoration/maintenance** in mitochondrial proteins. TST also interfaces with antioxidant systems and can influence ROS handling through sulfurtransferase activity (pqac-00000008, pqac-00000010, pqac-00000004). | Kruithof et al., 2020, https://doi.org/10.1016/j.bbadis.2020.165716; Kaleta et al., 2024, https://doi.org/10.3390/biom14070746 |
| Structural features | TST has a **two-domain rhodanese fold** with a single catalytic cysteine in the C-terminal domain; active-site residues reported to contribute to catalysis/substrate interaction include **Cys247/Cys248**, **Arg186**, and **Lys249**. Figure evidence from a 2023 review shows domain organization and sulfur-transfer reaction scheme (pqac-00000015, pqac-00000007, pqac-00000013). | Alsohaibani et al., 2023, https://doi.org/10.3390/antiox12040843; Kruithof et al., 2020, https://doi.org/10.1016/j.bbadis.2020.165716 |
| Regulation | TST activity appears **post-translationally regulated**, especially by **phosphorylation**; reviews cite predicted/observed phosphosites and propose phosphorylation-dependent switching of sulfur transfer directionality, iron–sulfur cluster mobilization/repair, and respiratory output. **S-nitrosylation** can inhibit catalytic activity. These mechanisms are plausible for rat TST but remain incompletely resolved experimentally (pqac-00000008, pqac-00000014, pqac-00000007). | Kruithof et al., 2020, https://doi.org/10.1016/j.bbadis.2020.165716; Alsohaibani et al., 2023, https://doi.org/10.3390/antiox12040843 |
| Expression / tissue context | Mammalian TST is described as abundant in **liver, kidney, and colon**, consistent with detoxification and sulfide-handling roles. In immune-cell atlases, Tst is detectable across leukocyte populations and can be elevated in activated macrophages/neutrophils, though these data are mouse/human rather than rat-specific (pqac-00000012, pqac-00000006). | Alsohaibani et al., 2023, https://doi.org/10.3390/antiox12040843; Kaleta et al., 2024, https://doi.org/10.3390/biom14070746 |
| Phenotypes / metabolic disease links | A 2020 review synthesizes evidence that TST is a **positive predictor of metabolic health**, with adipose TST expression negatively associated with adiposity/BMI and positively associated with insulin sensitivity markers. TST has been linked genetically to **“healthy leanness”** in mice, and thiosulfate treatment improved glucose uptake/insulin-resistance phenotypes in cited experimental systems (pqac-00000010, pqac-00000013). | Kruithof et al., 2020, https://doi.org/10.1016/j.bbadis.2020.165716; Šimon et al., 2024, https://doi.org/10.1007/s13258-024-01507-9 |
| Kidney / liver relevance | Gathered evidence supports TST as relevant to **hepatic and renal mitochondrial sulfur metabolism**. Reviews note thiosulfate protection in experimental **cardiac and renal disease**, and more recent literature summarized in search results points to TST deficiency worsening tubular mitochondrial fatty-acid oxidation in diabetic kidney disease, though detailed extraction was not available in-chat; therefore kidney/liver claims should be treated as review-supported rather than fully resolved here (pqac-00000014, pqac-00000008). | Kruithof et al., 2020, https://doi.org/10.1016/j.bbadis.2020.165716 |
| Cancer links | 2024 reviews place TST within cancer sulfur-metabolism networks, especially as part of **H2S catabolism**. In leukemia datasets/cell lines, TST expression varies by lineage: one review reports higher TST in **acute lymphoblastic leukemia vs chronic lymphocytic leukemia** and relatively high expression in **K562** cells, but emphasizes that direct functional studies on TST in cancer remain limited (pqac-00000004). | Kaleta et al., 2024, https://doi.org/10.3390/biom14070746; Dawoud et al., 2024, https://doi.org/10.1080/13510002.2024.2437338 |
| Quantitative recent data | Recent quantitative values available in the gathered evidence are mostly from non-rat systems: immune-cell atlas values show **mouse Tst** expression of **319.041** in thioglycollate-elicited peritoneal macrophages and **599.476** in bone marrow neutrophils; leukemia analysis reported **ALL vs CLL p < 0.001, Log2FC = -3.421** for TST. No 2023-2024 rat-specific catalytic constants or expression/activity measurements were retrieved in-chat (pqac-00000006, pqac-00000004). | Kaleta et al., 2024, https://doi.org/10.3390/biom14070746 |
| Real-world applications | TST biology is relevant to **cyanide antidote pharmacology** (thiosulfate-to-thiocyanate conversion), **thiosulfate therapeutics** (e.g., calciphylaxis via sulfurtransferase-dependent metabolism), and potentially **metabolic disease biomarker/target** development. Expert reviews highlight TST and thiosulfate metabolism as translationally important but still mechanistically incomplete (pqac-00000014, pqac-00000010). | Kruithof et al., 2020, https://doi.org/10.1016/j.bbadis.2020.165716; Hayden & Tyagi, 2024, https://doi.org/10.3390/ph17121741 |
