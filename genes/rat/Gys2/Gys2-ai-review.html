Organism: Rattus norvegicus

📝 Provide Detailed Feedback

Gene Description

Gys2 encodes rat liver glycogen synthase, a UDP-glucose-dependent alpha-1,4-glucan glucosyltransferase that extends glycogen chains during glycogen biosynthesis. The review accepts the transferase activity and glycogen biosynthetic/metabolic process annotations as core/direct, and treats glucose binding, cytoskeletal localization, and glucose-response terms as non-core context.

Core Functions

Gys2 encodes rat liver glycogen synthase. FUNCTION: Glycogen synthase participates in the glycogen biosynthetic process along with glycogenin and glycogen branching enzyme. Extends the primer composed of a few glucose units formed by glycogenin by adding new glucose units to it.

Gys2 review notes

Evidence summary

  • [UniProtKB:P17625] UniProt describes Gys2 as extending glycogen primers by transferring glucose from UDP-glucose to the non-reducing end of alpha-1,4-glucan.
  • PMID:1731614 UniProt cites this publication for the glycogen synthase EC reaction and phosphorylation-dependent regulation.

Curation decisions

  • Core function: liver glycogen synthase (alpha-1,4-glucan glucosyltransferase (UDP-glucose donor) activity, GO:0004373).
  • Specific catalytic activities and direct metabolic processes were accepted.
  • Broad parent, localization, binding, and stimulus-response annotations were modified, kept non-core, or marked over-annotated according to support.
