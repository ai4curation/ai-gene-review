| Pathway context | GYS2 is the rate-limiting glycogenesis enzyme in the **hepatic glycogen synthesis arm of glucose homeostasis**. Maximal hepatic glycogen deposition requires **hyperinsulinaemia plus hyperglycaemia**: insulin activates glycogen synthesis, whereas glucose suppresses glycogenolysis. GYS2 works in concert with **glucokinase (GCK)**, **glycogen phosphorylase**, **PP1/GL**, **GSK3**, **AKT**, and cAMP/PKA signaling. (pqac-00000013, pqac-00000014, pqac-00000017, pqac-00000018) | Portal insulin is normally ~**3-fold** higher than systemic insulin; hepatic glucose uptake is roughly **one-third** of a moderate enteral glucose load; in a human study, **10 mM glucose** and **400 pM insulin** together gave maximal glycogen synthesis, whereas hyperglycaemia alone suppressed glycogenolysis but did not stimulate synthesis.
