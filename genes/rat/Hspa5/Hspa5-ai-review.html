Gene Description

Hspa5 (BiP/GRP78) is the ER-resident member of the HSP70 chaperone family. It functions as an ATP-dependent protein folding chaperone in the ER lumen, assisting in de novo folding of nascent polypeptides entering the secretory pathway and in quality control of misfolded proteins. BiP is a key regulator of the unfolded protein response (UPR), acting as a repressor of both IRE1 and PERK signaling arms by binding their luminal domains in the unstressed state and releasing them upon accumulation of misfolded proteins. It also participates in ERAD, post- translational translocation, and ER protein retention. BiP forms part of large ER chaperone complexes with co-chaperones (e.g., ERdj4, ERdj5), GRP94, and protein disulfide isomerases. It is also found at the cell surface in some contexts where it can act as a receptor for extracellular ligands.

Core Functions

BiP/GRP78 is the ER-resident HSP70 chaperone that assists protein folding through ATP-dependent iterative binding and release cycles. This is its primary molecular function, supported by IBA (GO_REF:0000033), ISO (GO_REF:0000121), and extensive literature. UniProt classifies it as EC 3.6.4.10 with documented nucleotide-binding and substrate-binding domains. BiP sequesters IRE1 and PERK luminal domains in unstressed ER and releases them upon misfolded protein accumulation to activate UPR signaling. It also participates in ERAD through interaction with co-chaperones (DNAJC10/ERdj5) and ERAD machinery components. Per UPB project rules, HSP70 family members use GO:0044183 as the primary foldase term; holdase aspect awaits NTR.
