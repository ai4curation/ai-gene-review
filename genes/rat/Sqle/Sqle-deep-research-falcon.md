## Research Report: Rat **Sqle/Erg1** (UniProt **P52020**) — Squalene Monooxygenase (Squalene Epoxidase; EC 1.14.14.17)

### 0) Target verification (mandatory)
The UniProt accession **P52020** corresponds to rat **Sqle** (synonym **Erg1**) encoding **squalene monooxygenase / squalene epoxidase (SQLE)**, the sterol-pathway enzyme that epoxidizes squalene to oxidosqualene. The central biochemical function, enzyme family features (FAD-dependent monooxygenase), and endomembrane localization described in mammalian SQLE studies match the provided UniProt description, so the literature summarized below is aligned with the intended target. (padyana2019structureandinhibition pages 1-2, zhang2024squaleneepoxidaseits pages 2-4)

### 1) Key concepts and definitions (current understanding)

#### 1.1 Enzymatic reaction and pathway position
SQLE catalyzes the **stereospecific epoxidation** of **squalene** to **2,3(S)-oxidosqualene** (also termed 2,3-epoxysqualene), which is the **first oxygenation step** and a committed transformation within the sterol branch of the mevalonate–cholesterol pathway. (padyana2019structureandinhibition pages 1-2, padyana2019structureandinhibition pages 9-9)

SQLE is widely described as one of the key **rate-limiting control points** in cholesterol synthesis (often discussed downstream of HMG-CoA reductase), so changes in its abundance/activity can substantially redirect pathway flux. (padyana2019structureandinhibition pages 1-2, zhang2024squaleneepoxidaseits pages 1-2)

#### 1.2 Cofactors and electron transfer requirements
**Cofactor:** SQLE is **FAD-dependent**; crystal structures of the human catalytic domain show SQLE bound to **FAD**. (padyana2019structureandinhibition pages 9-9, zhang2024squaleneepoxidaseits pages 2-4)

**Electron donor system:** SQLE is an electron-requiring microsomal (ER) enzyme. The principal electron donation route is **NADPH → NADPH–cytochrome P450 reductase (POR/CPR/P450R) → SQLE**. POR is an ER-associated flavoprotein that uses **NADPH exclusively** and transfers electrons to SQLE in cholesterol synthesis. (porter2015electrontransferpathways pages 2-4, porter2015electrontransferpathways pages 1-2)

**Physiological nuance:** Although POR is classically considered the key donor, hepatic **POR-null** mouse experiments indicate that **~40%** of hepatic microsomal SQLE activity can persist, implying an additional microsomal **NADPH-dependent** reductase may contribute under some conditions. (porter2015electrontransferpathways pages 4-5)

#### 1.3 Subcellular localization and membrane topology
SQLE is an **endoplasmic reticulum (ER) membrane-associated** protein. A regulatory **N-terminal ~100 aa (N100)** region mediates lipid sensing and degradation, while the **catalytic domain** spans the C-terminus (approximately aa 101–574 in human; highly conserved across mammals). (zhang2024squaleneepoxidaseits pages 2-4)

Topological features described for mammalian SQLE include an **N-terminal re-entrant loop** (aa 24–33) and an **amphipathic helix** (aa 62–73) that reversibly associates with the ER membrane and functions as a cholesterol-responsive structural element (degron mechanism). (zhang2024squaleneepoxidaseits pages 2-4, zhang2024squaleneepoxidaseits pages 4-6)

### 2) Regulation and systems-level biology

#### 2.1 Transcriptional control (SREBP-centered)
SQLE is a transcriptional target of **SREBP2**, the master transcriptional regulator of cholesterol biosynthesis genes. Under low cholesterol, SREBP2 activation increases SQLE expression; promoter-level regulation involves cooperating transcription factors including **NF-Y**, **Sp1**, and **YY1**. (zhang2024squaleneepoxidaseits pages 8-9, zhang2024squaleneepoxidaseits pages 6-8)

#### 2.2 Cholesterol-accelerated degradation via ERAD (MARCH6 axis)
A central contemporary concept is that SQLE abundance is tuned by **cholesterol-accelerated, proteasome-dependent degradation** mediated by **ER-associated degradation (ERAD)** machinery.

Mechanistically, the ER E3 ubiquitin ligase **MARCH6** (TEB4/RNF176) promotes SQLE ubiquitination and degradation. A CRISPR/Cas9 screen identified **UBE2J2** as a principal E2 supporting MARCH6-dependent SQLE degradation, and the process involves **VCP**. (zhang2024squaleneepoxidaseits pages 8-9)

Noncanonical ubiquitination is emphasized: SQLE degradation does not strictly require lysine ubiquitination; **serine residues** in N100 (including **Ser59, Ser61, Ser83, Ser87**) are critical, and **Ser83** is described as a ubiquitination site. (zhang2024squaleneepoxidaseits pages 8-9)

At the membrane-structural level, increased cholesterol triggers conformational changes in the N100 region and deformation/ejection of the amphipathic helix, exposing a hydrophobic signal that targets SQLE for proteasomal turnover. (zhang2024squaleneepoxidaseits pages 4-6, zhang2024squaleneepoxidaseits pages 2-4)

#### 2.3 Substrate feedback (squalene stabilization)
SQLE’s substrate, **squalene**, can bind the N100 region and reduce interaction/ubiquitination by MARCH6, stabilizing SQLE at the ER—an example of feedback where upstream metabolite accumulation buffers enzyme degradation. (zhang2024squaleneepoxidaseits pages 4-6)

### 3) Recent developments and latest research (prioritizing 2023–2024)

#### 3.1 Cholesterol, SQLE degradation, and ferroptosis (2023)
A 2023 study in *Communications Biology* reports that **cholesterol alters mevalonate-pathway flux by promoting SQLE degradation** (described as a rate-limiting step), which increases levels of **squalene** and **CoQ10** and thereby suppresses ferroptosis vulnerability. The authors further report that simultaneous inhibition of both CoQ10 and squalene biosynthesis abrogates cholesterol’s ferroptosis-protective effect, and mouse injury models (ischemia–reperfusion and doxorubicin-induced hepatotoxicity) support physiological relevance. (sun2023cholesterolmediatedferroptosis pages 1-2)

#### 3.2 SQLE as an immunometabolic node in MASH-HCC (2024, *Gut*)
Wen et al. (May 2024, *Gut*) used hepatocyte-specific **Sqle transgenic** and **hepatocyte-specific Sqle knockout** mouse models under a choline-deficient high-fat diet plus DEN to induce **MASH-HCC** and showed that SQLE promotes tumor burden and immunosuppression. Specifically, Sqle transgenic mice showed increased tumor burden with reduced functional CD8+ T cell markers (IFN-γ+, Granzyme B+) and increased Arg-1+ MDSCs, whereas hepatocyte-specific Sqle knockout suppressed tumor growth and reversed these immune features. (wen2024targetingsqualeneepoxidase pages 1-2)

In orthotopic models (RIL-175, Hepa1-6), the excerpt provides **group sizes (e.g., n=5/group)** and dosing/depletion regimens for mechanistic immune tests, and in humanized CD34+ mice, treatment with the SQLE inhibitor **terbinafine** reduced tumor burden (**n=8/group**) and increased cytotoxic CD8+ markers. The authors conclude that targeting SQLE (genetic or terbinafine) can restore anti-PD-1 efficacy in MASH-HCC models. (wen2024targetingsqualeneepoxidase pages 5-6, wen2024targetingsqualeneepoxidase pages 1-2)

#### 3.3 SQLE-mediated squalene removal and tumor immune evasion (2024, pancreatic cancer)
A 2024 study in *Frontiers in Immunology* frames SQLE as an enzyme whose activity **removes squalene** (by converting it to oxidosqualene), and reports that SQLE knockdown in pancreatic tumor models increases intracellular squalene and reshapes immune infiltration. In an immune-competent C57BL/6 model, KPC tumors were generated by injecting **1.8×10^6 cells** (**n=5/group**). Direct administration of **squalene 200 mg/kg intraperitoneally every two days** reduced tumor growth; the excerpt notes **one tumor disappeared** in the SQLE-knockdown group and reports highly significant differences (****, **p<0.0001** for some comparisons). (pan2024sqlemediatedsqualenemetabolism pages 5-8)

Mechanistically, elevated squalene was linked to reduced NF-κB activity (p65 and p65 S536 phosphorylation) and reduced CXCL1 expression, with downstream effects on recruitment of immune-suppressive myeloid populations. (pan2024sqlemediatedsqualenemetabolism pages 5-8)

#### 3.4 Translational targeting and biomarker development (2024, osteosarcoma)
Wang et al. (Feb 2024, *Clinical and Translational Medicine*) identify SQLE as a metabolic vulnerability in high-risk osteosarcoma using an AI-derived prognostic index and multi-cohort validation. The excerpt reports that SQLE and MYC are overexpressed and correlated with copy-number amplification and that SQLE and MYC mRNA are independent predictors of mortality by multivariate Cox regression; in a 77-sample TMA, an outcome association is reported (**p=0.037**). Functionally, SQLE knockdown reduced malignant phenotypes and lowered tumor cholesterol, and the SQLE inhibitor **FR194738** showed in vivo anti-osteosarcoma effects and synergy with chemotherapy in the reported study. (wang2024identifyingsqualeneepoxidase pages 14-17)

### 4) Current applications and real-world implementations

#### 4.1 Antifungal therapy (direct clinical implementation)
SQLE inhibition is a clinically proven strategy in fungi; the allylamine **terbinafine** targets fungal squalene epoxidase. Human SQLE structural work provides mechanistic rationale for inhibitor specificity and helps interpret clinically encountered terbinafine-resistant mutants (in fungi) as well as informing next-generation inhibitor design. (padyana2019structureandinhibition pages 1-2)

#### 4.2 Emerging oncology/immunotherapy combinations (translational implementation)
Recent in vivo work supports repurposing/leveraging SQLE inhibition to modulate tumor immunity and therapy response. In MASH-HCC models, terbinafine (pharmacologic SQLE inhibition) combined with immunotherapy is reported to restore anti-PD-1 efficacy. (wen2024targetingsqualeneepoxidase pages 1-2)

#### 4.3 Chemical tool compounds for pathway probing
Multiple SQLE inhibitors are used as research tools (e.g., **NB-598**, **Cmpd-4**; and FR194738 in osteosarcoma work), enabling mechanistic dissection of cholesterol synthesis dependency versus squalene-mediated effects. (padyana2019structureandinhibition pages 1-2, wang2024identifyingsqualeneepoxidase pages 14-17)

### 5) Expert opinions and authoritative syntheses
A 2024 review in *International Journal of Molecular Sciences* synthesizes SQLE regulation across transcriptional control (SREBP2 and co-regulators) and post-translational ERAD (MARCH6 axis) and contextualizes SQLE as both a metabolic control node and a cancer-linked enzyme with therapeutic potential. This review also highlights less intuitive regulatory features such as N100-based cholesterol sensing and truncation mechanisms that may yield constitutively active forms. (zhang2024squaleneepoxidaseits pages 1-2, zhang2024squaleneepoxidaseits pages 8-9)

A domain-focused review in *Lipids* (2015) provides a mechanistic framework for **electron transfer pathways** on the ER supporting sterol synthesis enzymes, emphasizing POR as a central NADPH-dependent electron donor for SQLE while noting evidence for additional donors based on knockout/residual activity observations. (porter2015electrontransferpathways pages 4-5, porter2015electrontransferpathways pages 2-4)

### 6) Statistics and quantitative data highlights (from retrieved 2023–2024 sources)
Because the tool-accessible excerpts do not include full numeric effect sizes for many endpoints (e.g., hazard ratios, AUC values, tumor volumes), the quantitative highlights below are restricted to explicit numbers present in the retrieved text chunks:

- **Pancreatic cancer model:** KPC implantation **1.8×10^6 cells**, **n=5/group**; squalene dosing **200 mg/kg i.p. every 2 days**; some comparisons reported at **p<0.0001**; one tumor disappearance noted in SQLE-KD group. (pan2024sqlemediatedsqualenemetabolism pages 5-8)
- **MASH-HCC models:** MASH induction reported as **20 weeks** of CDHFD feeding; orthotopic model group sizes **n=5/group**; humanized mouse terbinafine experiment **n=8/group**; explicit immune depletion dosing regimens reported (e.g., CD8 depletion 400 μg then 200 μg q3d). - **Electron transfer nuance:** POR-null mouse liver microsomes retained **~40%** SQLE activity, motivating the concept of alternative reductase support. (porter2015electrontransferpathways pages 4-5)

### 7) Functional annotation summary for rat Sqle (P52020)
Rat **Sqle/Erg1** encodes an ER-associated, FAD-dependent monooxygenase that epoxidizes squalene to 2,3(S)-oxidosqualene, receiving reducing equivalents mainly through NADPH-dependent POR/CPR on the ER membrane. Its N-terminal regulatory region implements cholesterol-responsive ERAD (MARCH6/UBE2J2/VCP axis), making SQLE a metabolic “valve” controlling sterol pathway flux and altering levels of squalene and downstream cholesterol-dependent cellular states. Recent 2023–2024 studies extend SQLE’s relevance to ferroptosis modulation and tumor immunity, with pharmacologic inhibition (notably terbinafine) being evaluated in vivo for cancer-immunity contexts. (padyana2019structureandinhibition pages 1-2, zhang2024squaleneepoxidaseits pages 4-6, sun2023cholesterolmediatedferroptosis pages 1-2, wen2024targetingsqualeneepoxidase pages 1-2, pan2024sqlemediatedsqualenemetabolism pages 5-8, porter2015electrontransferpathways pages 4-5, porter2015electrontransferpathways pages 2-4)
