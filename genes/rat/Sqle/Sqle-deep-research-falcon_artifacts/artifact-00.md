| Enzyme function | Rat **Sqle** (UniProt **P52020**; synonym **Erg1**) matches mammalian **squalene monooxygenase/squalene epoxidase**, the first oxygenation step in sterol biosynthesis and a major rate-limiting control point downstream of HMGCR. | Reaction | SQLE catalyzes the **stereospecific epoxidation of squalene to 2,3(S)-oxidosqualene** (also called 2,3-epoxysqualene), the committed oxygenation step preceding cyclization to lanosterol in animals. | (pqac-00000000, pqac-00000007, pqac-00000019) | Padyana et al., *Nat Commun* (Jan 2019), https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-018-07928-x; Zhang et al., *Int J Mol Sci* (Mar 2024), https://doi.org/10.3390/ijms25073874 |

| Cofactors/electron donors | SQLE is an **FAD-dependent monooxygenase**. In mammalian ER membranes, reducing equivalents are supplied mainly from **NADPH via NADPH-cytochrome P450 reductase (POR/CPR/P450R)**. Reconstitution assays used recombinant P450R; review evidence indicates POR is the major donor, although residual SQLE activity in hepatic POR-null mice suggests an additional microsomal **NADPH-dependent** reductase may contribute in some contexts. | (pqac-00000000, pqac-00000016, pqac-00000017, pqac-00000018) | Porter, *Lipids* (Sep 2015), https://doi.org/10.1007/s11745-015-4065-1; Padyana et al., *Nat Commun* (Jan 2019), https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-018-07928-x |
| Subcellular localization/topology | SQLE is an **ER membrane-associated** protein. The protein has an **N-terminal regulatory region (N100)** with a **re-entrant loop (aa 24-33)** and **amphipathic helix (aa 62-73)** that reversibly associate with the ER membrane, plus a **C-terminal catalytic domain (aa 101-574)**. This topology underlies cholesterol sensing and degradation control. | (pqac-00000002, pqac-00000003, pqac-00000011, pqac-00000019) | Zhang et al., *Int J Mol Sci* (Mar 2024), https://doi.org/10.3390/ijms25073874 |
| Regulation | SQLE is regulated both **transcriptionally** and **post-translationally**. **SREBP2** activates SQLE transcription under low-cholesterol conditions, with **NF-Y, Sp1, YY1** as promoter-associated regulators. Excess cholesterol triggers **ER-associated degradation (ERAD)** through **MARCH6**, **UBE2J2**, and **VCP**, acting on the N100 degron; critical residues include **Ser59, Ser61, Ser83, Ser87**. Squalene itself can stabilize SQLE by reducing MARCH6-dependent ubiquitination. | (pqac-00000003, pqac-00000004, pqac-00000006, pqac-00000008, pqac-00000013) | Zhang et al., *Int J Mol Sci* (Mar 2024), https://doi.org/10.3390/ijms25073874 |
| Pathway role | SQLE functions in the **mevalonate-cholesterol pathway** after squalene synthase and before oxidosqualene cyclase, controlling flux into sterol production. Because it is one of the principal regulated steps in cholesterol synthesis, altered SQLE activity changes levels of cholesterol, squalene, and related metabolites such as CoQ pathway outputs indirectly via pathway flux redistribution. | (pqac-00000005, pqac-00000009, pqac-00000021) | Zhang et al., *Int J Mol Sci* (Mar 2024), https://doi.org/10.3390/ijms25073874; Sun et al., *Commun Biol* (Nov 2023), https://doi.org/10.1038/s42003-023-05477-8; Porter, *Lipids* (Sep 2015), https://doi.org/10.1007/s11745-015-4065-1 |
| Inhibitors/applications | SQLE is a validated drug target. **Terbinafine** clinically targets fungal squalene epoxidase and human SQLE structures explain selectivity/resistance. Experimental mammalian SQLE inhibitors include **NB-598**, **Cmpd-4** and **FR194738**. Current application areas include antifungal therapy, investigation of hypercholesterolemia/cholesterol metabolism, and emerging oncology/immunotherapy combinations. | (pqac-00000000, pqac-00000002, pqac-00000012, pqac-00000010) | Padyana et al., *Nat Commun* (Jan 2019), https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-018-07928-x; Wen et al., *Gut* (May 2024), https://doi.org/10.1136/gutjnl-2023-331117; Wang et al., *Clin Transl Med* (Feb 2024), https://doi.org/10.1002/ctm2.1586 |
| Recent disease/cancer findings (2023-2024) with quantitative details | **2023 ferroptosis:** cholesterol promoted SQLE degradation, increasing squalene/CoQ and suppressing ferroptosis in mouse injury models (Sun 2023). **2024 MASH-HCC:** hepatocyte-specific Sqle transgenic/knockout mouse models showed that Sqle promotes tumor burden and immunosuppression; orthotopic and humanized mouse experiments used **n=5/group** and **n=8/group**, respectively, and terbinafine restored anti-PD-1 response (Wen 2024). **2024 pancreatic cancer:** shRNA SQLE knockdown with **1.8×10^6 cells injected**, **n=5/group**, and **squalene 200 mg/kg i.p. every 2 days** suppressed tumor growth; one SQLE-KD tumor disappeared and some effects reached **p<0.0001** (Pan 2024). **2024 osteosarcoma:** SQLE high expression associated with poorer prognosis; TMA analysis reported **p=0.037**, and SQLE knockdown reduced xenograft growth and chemotherapy IC50 values (Wang 2024). | (pqac-00000009, pqac-00000012, pqac-00000014, pqac-00000015, pqac-00000010) | Sun et al., *Commun Biol* (Nov 2023), https://doi.org/10.1038/s42003-023-05477-8; Wen et al., *Gut* (May 2024), https://doi.org/10.1136/gutjnl-2023-331117; Pan et al., *Front Immunol* (Dec 2024), https://doi.org/10.3389/fimmu.2024.1512981; Wang et al., *Clin Transl Med* (Feb 2024), https://doi.org/10.1002/ctm2.1586 |
