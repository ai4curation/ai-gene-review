It operates chiefly in the nucleus to coordinate checkpoint and tumor-suppressive pathways, with a cytoplasmic pool reflecting regulated nucleocytoplasmic shuttling. Its N-terminal activation region engages chromatin-modifying complexes, the central DNA-binding core confers target specificity, and the C-terminal oligomerization module stabilizes high-avidity promoter occupancy that enforces cell-cycle arrest and apoptosis.

UniProt Summary

Acts as a tumor suppressor in many tumor types; induces growth arrest or apoptosis depending on the physiological circumstances and cell type. Involved in cell cycle regulation as a transcriptional activator of genes that negatively regulate cell division. Transcriptional activity is enhanced by cooperative binding to DNA response elements in the promoter region of target genes. May act in multiple pathways regulating DNA damage, cell cycle arrest and apoptosis (By similarity).
