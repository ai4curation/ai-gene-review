Gene Description

gas7a encodes Growth arrest-specific protein 7a in Takifugu rubripes, a member of the GAS7 family of F-BAR domain-containing proteins. The protein contains an N-terminal FCH (Fes/CIP4 homology) domain connected via a WW-FCH linker to a C-terminal F-BAR domain (residues 121-381). F-BAR domains form crescent-shaped dimers that sense and induce membrane curvature, typically associating with the cytoplasmic face of the plasma membrane. In mammals, GAS7 was originally identified as a gene upregulated during growth arrest and is required for neurite outgrowth in cultured cerebellar neurons. GAS7 family members have been shown to participate in membrane dynamics including clathrin-mediated endocytosis, where the F-BAR domain facilitates recruitment to sites of membrane invagination such as clathrin-coated pits. The Takifugu gas7a ortholog is predicted (PE level 4) with no direct experimental characterization, so functional inference relies on domain architecture and phylogenetic transfer from characterized mammalian orthologs via TreeGrafter/PANTHER.
