Gene Description

Type I non-reducing polyketide synthase that produces anthraquinone-derived pigments essential for conidial pigmentation and environmental stress resistance. Contains modular domains (SAT-KS-MAT-PT-ACP-ACP-TE) that iteratively condense acetyl-CoA and malonyl-CoA units to form 1-acetyl-2,4,6,8-tetrahydroxy-9,10-anthraquinone, which is further processed by EthD and Mlac1 to generate mature conidial pigments that protect against UV radiation and temperature extremes.

Key Findings from Literature

Primary Function

  • Polyketide synthase producing anthraquinone-derived conidial pigments PMID:29958281
  • Essential for UV protection and temperature stress resistance PMID:29958281
  • Type I non-reducing PKS with modular domain architecture

Biosynthetic Details

  • Produces 1-acetyl-2,4,6,8-tetrahydroxy-9,10-anthraquinone [UniProt annotation]
  • Works with EthD (dehydratase) and Mlac1 (laccase) to produce final pigment [PMID:29958281, UniProt]
  • Contains SAT, KS, MAT, PT, dual ACP, and TE domains

Regulation

  • Highly expressed during conidiation PMID:29958281
  • Regulated by BrlA-AbaA-WetA conidiation pathway [UniProt annotation]
  • Controlled by Hog1 and Slt2 MAPK pathways PMID:29958281

Evolutionary Significance

  • Part of duplicated gene cluster that underwent functional diversification PMID:29958281
  • Enhanced adaptive flexibility of Metarhizium species PMID:29958281
