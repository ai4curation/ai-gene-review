Gene Description

TEP6 (thioester-containing protein 6) is a member of the insect TEP family in Anopheles gambiae, which are secreted complement-like proteins that circulate in the hemolymph and function in innate immunity. TEP proteins contain alpha-2-macroglobulin-like domains including a thioester-containing domain (TED) that enables covalent binding to pathogen surfaces. Unlike vertebrate alpha-2-macroglobulins which function as protease inhibitors, insect TEPs function primarily as opsonins in complement-like immunity. TEP6 shows evolutionary mosaicism with TEP1 through gene conversion events, suggesting functional relatedness within the family. Direct experimental characterization of TEP6 is lacking, but its domain architecture and evolutionary relationship to the well-characterized TEP1 suggest a role in hemolymph-mediated pathogen recognition and clearance.
