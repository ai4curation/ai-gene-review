CC   -!- FUNCTION: Serine protease which plays a role in the innate immune
CC       response against protozoan and bacterial pathogens, such as Plasmodium
CC       bergei, Staphylococcus aureus, Micrococcus luteus and Escherichia coli,
CC       by activating the melanization cascade (PubMed:16922859,
CC       PubMed:37703846). Cleaves and activates CLIPB8 (PubMed:37703846). In
CC       the resistant strain L3-5, involved in the melanization of killed
CC       parasite P.berghei ookinetes which results in their clearance
CC       (PubMed:16922859). In the susceptible strain G3, appears to be
CC       dispensable for ookinete elimination which occurs by lysis
CC       (PubMed:16922859). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:16922859,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:37703846}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Interacts with SRPN2 in the hemolymph of immune-challenged
CC       female mosquitoes; the interaction results in CLIPB4 inhibition.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:37703846}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Secreted {ECO:0000305}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: In females, expressed in fat body, cuticle, thorax
CC       and ovaries. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10469250}.
CC   -!- DEVELOPMENTAL STAGE: Expressed in eggs, larvae, pupae, and male and
CC       female adults. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10469250}.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: Induced following a blood meal (PubMed:10469250). Induced in
CC       response to bacterial infection (PubMed:10469250, PubMed:10646969).
CC       Induced in response to P.berghei infection (PubMed:10646969,
CC       PubMed:16922859). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10469250,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:10646969, ECO:0000269|PubMed:16922859}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The clip domain consists of 35-55 residues which are 'knitted'
CC       together usually by 3 conserved disulfide bonds forming a clip-like
CC       compact structure. {ECO:0000255|PROSITE-ProRule:PRU01236}.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: RNAi-mediated knockdown in the resistant strain
CC       L3-5 infected with P.berghei, causes a 5-fold reduction in the number
CC       of melanized ookinetes (PubMed:16922859). Simultaneous RNAi-mediated
CC       knockdown of CLIPB4 and CLIPB17 in the resistant strain L3-5 infected
CC       with P.berghei, causes a 52-fold reduction in the number of melanized
CC       ookinetes (PubMed:16922859). RNAi-mediated knockdown in the susceptible
CC       strain G3 infected with P.berghei has no effect on the response to
CC       parasite infection (PubMed:16922859). Simultaneous RNAi-mediated
CC       knockdown of CLIPB4 and CTL4 in the susceptible strain G3 infected with
CC       P.berghei, abolishes ookinete increased melanization caused by RNAi-
CC       mediated knockdown of CTL4 (PubMed:16922859). Simultaneous RNAi-
CC       mediated knockdown of CLIPB4 and SRPN2 results in increased survival
CC       and reduced spontaneous melanization compared to a single SRPN2
CC       knockdown (PubMed:37703846). RNAi-mediated knockdown results in reduced
CC       melanization after microbial challenge (PubMed:37703846). RNAi-mediated
CC       knockdown rescues infection-induced depletion of CLIPB8 after microbial
CC       challenge (PubMed:37703846). No significant effects on lifespan
CC       (PubMed:37703846). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:16922859,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:37703846}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the peptidase S1 family. CLIP subfamily.
CC       {ECO:0000255|PROSITE-ProRule:PRU01236}.
