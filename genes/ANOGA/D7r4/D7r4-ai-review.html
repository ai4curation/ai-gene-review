Gene Description

D7r4 is a short-form salivary protein from female Anopheles gambiae mosquitoes that facilitates blood feeding by binding and sequestering host biogenic amines (serotonin, histamine, norepinephrine, and tryptamine). It functions as a "kratagonist" - capturing host signaling molecules with high affinity to prevent their vasoconstrictive and inflammatory effects. The crystal structure of D7r4 has been solved (PDB: 2PQL, 2QEB, 2QEH, 2QEO, 2QEV) at high resolution (2.0-2.31 Angstroms), revealing a modified OBP-like fold with eight alpha-helices, three disulfide bonds, and a binding pocket adapted to amine ligands. D7r4 is secreted into saliva during blood feeding and belongs to the PBP/GOBP (Pheromone/Odorant-Binding Protein) structural family but has evolved a distinct function unrelated to olfaction.

Key concepts and definitions
- D7 family: Highly expressed salivary proteins of blood-feeding mosquitoes that bind and sequester host small-molecule mediators of hemostasis and inflammation (biogenic amines, eicosanoids), mitigating vasoconstriction, platelet activation, and inflammation at the bite site (Andersen 2010; https://doi.org/10.1016/j.toxicon.2009.11.002) (andersen2010structureandmechanism pages 8-9).
- Short-form D7s: Single-domain OBP-like proteins typified by D7r4 in A. gambiae that bind biogenic amines (Mans et al., J. Biol. Chem. 2007; https://doi.org/10.1074/jbc.M706410200) (mans2007thecrystalstructure pages 1-1, mans2007thecrystalstructure pages 8-9).

Functional annotation of D7r4
- Biochemical function and ligand specificity: Recombinant and crystallographic analyses established that D7r4 binds biogenic amines, including serotonin, norepinephrine, histamine, and tryptamine, consistent with a role in scavenging vasoactive and pro-inflammatory amines during feeding (Mans et al., J. Biol. Chem., Dec 2007; https://doi.org/10.1074/jbc.M706410200) (mans2007thecrystalstructure pages 1-1, mans2007thecrystalstructure pages 8-9). Structural interpretation indicates hydrogen bonding between the protonated amine and acidic residues (e.g., Asp/Glu) and packing of the aromatic ring in a hydrophobic pocket; histamine binds with somewhat lower affinity than serotonin/catecholamines (Andersen, Toxicon, Dec 2010; https://doi.org/10.1016/j.toxicon.2009.11.002) (andersen2010structureandmechanism pages 8-9).
- Mechanistic role in pathways: By sequestering biogenic amines, D7r4 is inferred to dampen vasoconstriction (e.g., norepinephrine), itch/inflammation (e.g., histamine), and platelet activation (e.g., serotonin), thereby facilitating blood meal acquisition at the skin interface (Mans 2007; https://doi.org/10.1074/jbc.M706410200; Andersen 2010; https://doi.org/10.1016/j.toxicon.2009.11.002) (mans2007thecrystalstructure pages 1-1, andersen2010structureandmechanism pages 8-9).

Conclusion
D7r4 (AGAP008281; Q7PNF2) is a secreted, short-form D7 salivary protein of Anopheles gambiae with an OBP-like fold that binds and sequesters biogenic amines, notably serotonin and norepinephrine, reducing vasoconstriction, platelet activation, and inflammatory signaling at the bite site.
