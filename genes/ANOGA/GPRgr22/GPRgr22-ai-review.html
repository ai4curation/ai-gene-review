Gene Description

Gustatory receptor 22 (Gr22/AGAP009999) is a seven-transmembrane gustatory/odorant receptor belonging to the insect chemoreceptor superfamily. It functions as a modulatory subunit of the heteromeric CO2 receptor complex (Gr22/Gr23/Gr24) in the maxillary palp capitate-peg sensilla of Anopheles gambiae. CRISPR gene-editing studies demonstrate that Gr23 and Gr24 are obligate (essential) for CO2 sensitivity, while Gr22 enhances sensitivity but is not required for basic CO2 detection. The receptor complex is expressed in the cpA neuron of capitate-peg sensilla on maxillary palps and responds to dissolved CO2 per se (not bicarbonate). This CO2 detection pathway is critical for host-seeking behavior in blood-feeding mosquitoes.
