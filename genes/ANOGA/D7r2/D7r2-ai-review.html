Gene Description

D7r2 is a short-form salivary protein from female Anopheles gambiae mosquitoes that facilitates blood feeding by binding and sequestering host biogenic amines (serotonin, histamine, and norepinephrine). It functions as a "kratagonist" - capturing host signaling molecules with high affinity to prevent their vasoconstrictive, platelet-aggregating, and inflammatory effects. The protein is secreted into saliva during blood feeding. D7r2 belongs to the PBP/GOBP (Pheromone/Odorant-Binding Protein) structural family but has evolved a distinct function unrelated to olfaction.

Conclusions
Anopheles gambiae D7r2 (AGAP008282; UniProt Q9UB31) is a salivary-gland, short-form D7 protein belonging to the OBP/PBP-GOBP superfamily. It localizes to saliva and functions at the bite site as a high-affinity kratagonist of biogenic amines‚Äîparticularly serotonin and catecholamines‚Äîthereby diminishing vasoconstriction, platelet aggregation, and inflammatory signaling to facilitate blood feeding. Structural and phylogenetic data support conserved OBP-like architecture and selective constraint on functional residues. Recent 2023 functional genetics in mosquitoes reinforce the central role of D7 proteins in feeding and pathogen interactions, while translational work highlights D7s as candidate exposure biomarkers and potential components of vector-targeted vaccines.
