CC   -!- FUNCTION: Short-chain dehydrogenase; part of the gene cluster that
CC       mediates the biosynthesis of flavoglaucin and congeners (including
CC       aspergin, dihydroauroglaucin and auroglaucin), prenylated
CC       salicylaldehyde derivatives carrying a saturated or an unsaturated C-7
CC       side chain (PubMed:32134669). The PKS fogA releases the carboxylic acid
CC       (8E,10E,12E)-3,5,7-trihydroxytetradeca-8,10,12-trienoic acid as its
CC       product, as well as derivatives with one and two double bonds
CC       (PubMed:32134669). FogA is indeed able to reduce the initial triketide,
CC       thus being at least partially responsible for the differently saturated
CC       heptyl side chains of flavoglaucin congeners (PubMed:32134669). The
CC       oxidoreductases fogB, fogC and fogD modify the nascent polyketide in
CC       fogA-bound form and, together, fogA, fogB, fogC and fogD are necessary
CC       for the formation of the aromatic core and the cyclized PKS products
CC       are released as salicyl alcohols (PubMed:32134669). In particular, fogB
CC       is responsible for oxidation of a hydroxyl group or reduction of
CC       remaining double bond(s) at the C-7 residue whereas fogD is probably
CC       involved in the reductive release of the modified PKS products
CC       (PubMed:32134669). The cytochrome P450 monooxygenase fogE is then
CC       responsible for the hydroxylation at C-3 of the benzene ring
CC       (PubMed:32134669). The fogE products are substrates of the
CC       prenyltransferase fogH and the prenylated benzyl alcohols are
CC       subsequently oxidized by the fogF to produce the final aryl aldehydes
CC       flavoglaucin and congeners (PubMed:32134669). The short-chain
CC       dehydrogenase fogG does not seem to be involved in the biosynthesis of
CC       the prenylated salicylaldehyde derivatives (PubMed:32134669).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:32134669}.
CC   -!- PATHWAY: Secondary metabolite biosynthesis.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:32134669}.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: Impairs the production of flavoglaucin and
CC       congeners, and leads to very low accumulation of the carboxylic acid
CC       (8E,10E,12E)-3,5,7-trihydroxytetradeca-8,10,12-trienoic acid.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:32134669}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the short-chain dehydrogenases/reductases (SDR)
CC       family. {ECO:0000305}.
