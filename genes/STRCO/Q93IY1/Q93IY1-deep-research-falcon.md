## Research report: functional annotation of *Streptomyces coelicolor* SCO5104 (UniProt Q93IY1)

### Executive summary
SCO5104 (UniProt Q93IY1) from *Streptomyces coelicolor* A3(2) M145 encodes a predicted **multidomain cytosolic sensory/signaling protein** containing **PAS** and **GAF** sensory domains and an **HATPase_c** module plus a **SpoIIE-like** domain (a PP2C-type phosphatase domain often found in stress-response signaling proteins). Direct experimental evidence for SCO5104 itself is sparse: it was identified as an **OsaC-like paralogue** (within a small family of proteins sharing related architectures), and a **SCO5104 mutant showed no detectable phenotype under the specific hyperosmotic growth conditions tested** in the primary study that examined this protein family. Consequently, a cautious annotation is that SCO5104 is likely a **stress/development-associated sensor–effector regulator**, but its precise physiological role, ligand(s), and regulated targets remain **unresolved** in the accessible literature for this run. (martinez2009osmoregulationinstreptomyces pages 4-5, mclean2019sensingandresponding pages 4-5)

### 1) Identity verification (critical disambiguation)
- **Target locus/organism match.** SCO5104 is explicitly discussed as a *S. coelicolor* gene in the OsaC-family context, and a later comparative TCS/sensor-kinase review maps a *Streptomyces venezuelae* locus (vnz_23470) to **SCO5104**, supporting cross-species orthology within Streptomyces. (martinez2009osmoregulationinstreptomyces pages 4-5, mclean2019sensingandresponding pages 4-5)
- **Consistency with UniProt domain expectations.** The domain composition reported for the SCO5104 ortholog in the Streptomyces sensor kinase survey includes PAS/GAF and HATPase_c plus SpoIIE, consistent with UniProt’s InterPro domain set indicating PAS/GAF-like sensory domains and HATPase-related modules. (mclean2019sensingandresponding pages 4-5, mclean2019sensingandresponding media 2f0c5d23)

### 2) Key concepts and definitions (current understanding)
#### 2.1 PAS and GAF domains as sensory modules
PAS and GAF domains are widespread small-molecule/physicochemical sensory domains in bacterial signaling proteins. In the closest characterized paralogue **OsaC**, PAS and GAF are described as **sensory** domains “implicated as redox/light/metabolite sensory and cyclic nucleotide binding domains” (the paper emphasizes this as domain-based inference rather than identification of a specific ligand). (martinez2009osmoregulationinstreptomyces pages 4-5)

#### 2.2 HATPase_c / GHKL kinase-like modules
The OsaC-family N-terminus is discussed as containing a GHKL superfamily kinase-like fold (ATP-binding “Bergerat fold”), related to anti-sigma factor kinases in other Gram-positive bacteria. This provides a mechanistic precedent for how an OsaC-like protein could couple sensory input to sigma-factor regulation, even when the downstream outputs differ among paralogues. (martinez2009osmoregulationinstreptomyces pages 4-5)

#### 2.3 SpoIIE (PP2C) phosphatase-like domains
In *Bacillus subtilis*, SpoIIE is a PP2C-type phosphatase involved in sporulation signaling; in Streptomyces, “SpoIIE” commonly appears as a domain annotation for regulatory phosphatases rather than necessarily implying endospore formation. In the Streptomyces sensor-kinase review table, the presence of a SpoIIE domain in an SCO5104 ortholog is used as an architectural descriptor (not a demonstrated sporulation function). (mclean2019sensingandresponding pages 4-5, mclean2019sensingandresponding media 2f0c5d23)

### 3) What is known specifically about SCO5104/Q93IY1 (direct evidence)
#### 3.1 Domain architecture and predicted protein type
- A Streptomyces comparative analysis annotates the SCO5104 ortholog as “probably incomplete” but reports the following domain architecture: **2× PAS_4, 1× GAF, 1× SpoIIE, 1× HATPase_c**; and reports **0 predicted transmembrane helices**, implying a **cytosolic** protein. (mclean2019sensingandresponding pages 4-5, mclean2019sensingandresponding media 2f0c5d23)
- In the OsaC study, SCO5104 is listed among *S. coelicolor* genes encoding proteins with a **domain composition related to OsaC** (a family characterized by sensory PAS/GAF modules and kinase-/phosphatase-like signaling outputs). (martinez2009osmoregulationinstreptomyces pages 4-5)

#### 3.2 Mutant phenotype under osmotic stress (negative result)
A primary study investigating osmoadaptation regulators reports that mutants of several OsaC-like paralogues—including **SCO5104**—showed **no observable phenotypic difference vs. wild-type M145 under hyperosmotic conditions** (the result is stated but “results not shown”). This is currently the most direct experimental statement about SCO5104 function retrieved here. (martinez2009osmoregulationinstreptomyces pages 4-5, martinez2009osmoregulationinstreptomyces media f3701063)

**Interpretation constraint:** This negative phenotype does not exclude roles for SCO5104 in other stresses, developmental stages, media, timepoints, or redundant genetic backgrounds.

### 4) Pathway context inferred from the closest characterized paralogue (OsaC) and how it constrains SCO5104 hypotheses
Because SCO5104 itself lacks direct mechanistic characterization in the retrieved literature, the best-supported biological context comes from the characterized family member **OsaC (SCO5747)**, which defines a model for PAS/GAF–kinase/phosphatase regulatory proteins in Streptomyces osmotic stress responses.

#### 4.1 The SigB-linked osmotic stress response (OSR) and osmoadaptation response (OAR)
- The OsaC study describes a signaling framework where **SigB (σB / sB)** is induced by osmotic stress and controls osmotic stress response genes, including osaB. (martinez2009osmoregulationinstreptomyces pages 1-2, martinez2009osmoregulationinstreptomyces pages 2-4)
- Quantitative promoter induction kinetics reported:
  - **sigBp1** induction peaks at **~7.5-fold** above constitutive levels **0.75–1.5 h** after osmotic upshift and returns to basal levels by **~9–12 h**. (martinez2009osmoregulationinstreptomyces pages 4-5)
  - For an sB-dependent osaB promoter (osaBp2), induction becomes detectable at **~60 min**, with reporter activity peaking around **~1.5–3 h** and returning to basal by **~12 h** (luciferase time course). (martinez2009osmoregulationinstreptomyces pages 2-4, martinez2009osmoregulationinstreptomyces media f1a68b8f)

These dynamics provide a quantitative reference for the timescale on which an OsaC-like sensory regulator could act.

#### 4.2 OsaC modulates SigB activity and physically associates with SigB
- OsaC is reported to be required to return **osaB** and **sigB** expression to constitutive levels after osmotic stress (i.e., it attenuates the response). (martinez2009osmoregulationinstreptomyces pages 1-2)
- Co-purification (western blot) provides evidence that **SigB co-purifies with His-tagged OsaC**, and the interaction strength depends on prior osmotic stress. (martinez2009osmoregulationinstreptomyces pages 5-7, martinez2009osmoregulationinstreptomyces media 53cd4e9d)

#### 4.3 OsaC-family modularity: kinase-like vs phosphatase-like roles
In OsaC, the **C-terminal phosphatase domain** is required for osmoadaptation phenotypes, whereas the **N-terminal kinase-like domain** (including the conserved N-box residue) is reported as dispensable for the OAR phenotype in certain complementation tests. This highlights that family members can have complex, possibly condition-specific division of labor across domains. (martinez2009osmoregulationinstreptomyces pages 4-5, martinez2009osmoregulationinstreptomyces pages 5-7)

#### 4.4 How these OsaC findings inform SCO5104 (bounded inference)
Given that SCO5104 is an OsaC-like paralogue but **does not phenocopy osaC mutants under tested hyperosmotic conditions**, the most conservative inference is:
- SCO5104 is **unlikely to be the primary non-redundant attenuator** of SigB-dependent osmotic stress responses under those specific laboratory conditions; and/or
- SCO5104 may operate under **different environmental signals**, **developmental states**, or **alternative sigma factor networks**, or may have **redundant roles** masked in single mutants. (martinez2009osmoregulationinstreptomyces pages 4-5)

### 5) Subcellular localization
