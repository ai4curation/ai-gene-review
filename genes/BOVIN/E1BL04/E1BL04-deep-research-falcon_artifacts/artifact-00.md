| Gene/protein identity | XIRP2 is the Xin actin-binding repeat-containing 2 protein, a member of the Xin family of striated-muscle actin-associated proteins | The Xin family is described as striated muscle-restricted and intercalated-disc-associated, with conserved Xin repeats and a conserved β-catenin-binding region; UniProt assignment of bovine XIRP2 to the Xin family is therefore consistent with the family literature | Supports annotation of bovine E1BL04/XIRP2 as a structural actin-associated Xin-family protein rather than an enzyme or transporter | (pqac-00000003, pqac-00000012, pqac-00000013) |
| Primary molecular function | Structural actin-binding and actin-organizing protein | Xin repeat regions define an actin-binding module; at least some Xin-family proteins also bundle actin filaments. Recent work on XIRP2 further shows recruitment to damaged actin structures and promotion of local actin remodeling/repair | Best interpreted as a cytoskeletal organizer that stabilizes actin-rich junctional or mechanosensory structures | (pqac-00000012, pqac-00000005, pqac-00000006) |
| Actin-binding domain architecture | Xin-repeat region is the core actin-binding feature | The Wang review states that the Xin repeat defines a new class of actin-binding domains and maps the actin-binding motifs to the Xin repeat region; a conserved β-catenin-binding domain lies within/associated with this repeat-rich region | Domain architecture explains why XIRP2 is suited to linking junctional complexes to actin filaments | (pqac-00000012, pqac-00000013) |
| β-catenin interaction | Xin-family proteins contain a conserved β-catenin-binding domain; by family inference XIRP2 is likely capable of junctional β-catenin association | The review identifies a highly conserved β-catenin-binding domain within Xin proteins and discusses Xin-mediated stabilization of adherens junctions through β-catenin/actin linkage | Suggests XIRP2 may help couple cadherin-catenin complexes to the actin cytoskeleton at intercalated discs or related junctional structures | (pqac-00000009, pqac-00000011, pqac-00000012) |
| Filamin-related interactions | Direct filamin C binding is well established for some Xin isoforms, while XIRP2 association is presently supported more by disease/remodeling co-occurrence than by the same level of direct binding evidence in the retrieved texts | The Wang review maps a filamin C-binding region to human/mouse Xinα but notes this motif is not found in mXinβ; recent cardiac work shows Xirp2 dysregulation together with FLNC/cytoskeletal remodeling in diseased hearts | For XIRP2 specifically, a cautious conclusion is that it participates in overlapping cytoskeletal networks with FLNC, but direct XIRP2–FLNC binding should not be overclaimed from the present evidence set | (pqac-00000012, pqac-00000007, pqac-00000017) |
| Additional binding/association logic | Likely links actin filaments to adherens-junction protein complexes and other actin-associated proteins | Xin-family proteins are discussed as stabilizers of adherens junctions and possible links between cadherin complexes and actin; recent stereocilia work shows XIRP2 also facilitates γ-actin enrichment at damage sites | Connects cell-cell junction mechanics with actin turnover/repair | (pqac-00000011, pqac-00000006) |
| Cardiac subcellular localization | Intercalated disc-associated in striated muscle by family assignment; disease literature places XIRP2 among intercalated-disc/cytoskeletal markers | Reviews of intercalated-disc organization discuss Xin proteins as intercalated-disc residents; XIRP2 appears among dysregulated intercalated-disc/cytoskeletal proteins in cardiac disease models | Supports a role at the cardiomyocyte end-to-end junction, where mechanical coupling and electrical organization intersect | (pqac-00000003, pqac-00000004, pqac-00000007, pqac-00000018) |
| Transitional junction / Z-disc-like region | Likely functions near actin-anchorage interfaces analogous to transitional-junction/Z-disc-linked compartments | The intercalated disc contains a transitional junction with actin-rich, Z-disc-like character; Xin-family proteins are discussed in the context of linking junctions and myofibrils, making this a plausible site of action for XIRP2 in muscle | Fits a role in anchoring and remodeling terminal sarcomeric actin networks rather than catalysis | (pqac-00000008, pqac-00000004, pqac-00000003) |
| Inner-ear localization | Isoform-specific localization in hair cells: long isoform to cuticular plate and cell junctions; short isoform enriched in stereocilia | Wagner et al. summarize prior work and show XIRP2 colocalizes with F-actin-based structures in hair cells, with distinct localization of long and short isoforms | Indicates XIRP2 is deployed to different actin-rich compartments depending on isoform and mechanical demand | (pqac-00000005, pqac-00000006) |
| Recruitment to damaged actin | XIRP2 accumulates at stereocilia F-actin gaps and fibroblast stress-fiber strain sites | In 2023 eLife work, XIRP2 localization to lesions was CTD-dependent and force-dependent; knockout impaired repair of stereocilia actin-core damage | Strong recent evidence that XIRP2 acts in actin-damage sensing and repair, not just static scaffolding | (pqac-00000005, pqac-00000006) |
| Tissue expression pattern | Predominantly striated muscle, with additional specialized expression in inner-ear hair cells | Xin proteins are striated-muscle restricted in the review; recent studies identify XIRP2 in cardiac disease proteomes, skeletal muscle molecular profiles, and hair cells | Supports expected bovine expression in cardiac/skeletal muscle and possible conserved sensory-hair-cell roles across vertebrates | (pqac-00000003, pqac-00000016, pqac-00000018, pqac-00000006) |
| Cardiac biological role | Structural support/remodeling marker in stressed or diseased myocardium | Xirp2 is upregulated in aged-mouse MI and TAC models, alongside other heart-disease markers; related literature links Xin proteins broadly to cardiac morphogenesis, intercalated-disc maturation, and cardiomyopathy/conduction defects | XIRP2 likely participates in remodeling of actin-linked junctional architecture during cardiac stress and failure | (pqac-00000018, pqac-00000003, pqac-00000010) |
| Skeletal muscle role | Associated with sarcomeric/developmental muscle programs and lesion/remodeling signatures | Comparative human skeletal muscle profiling noted XIRP2 enrichment in young fibers and detection around microlesions in adults, consistent with developmental and structural-maintenance roles | Suggests a conserved role in organizing or repairing mechanically stressed myofibrillar domains | (pqac-00000016, pqac-00000015) |
| Intercalated-disc maturation role | Best-established directly for Xin family overall; XIRP2 is inferred to contribute as a family member in bovine muscle | The 2012 review shows Xin proteins are required for intercalated-disc maturation, N-cadherin redistribution, and stabilization of adherens junctions via actin linkage | For bovine XIRP2, strongest evidence is family-based functional inference rather than direct cattle-specific knockout data | (pqac-00000003, pqac-00000011, pqac-00000012, pqac-00000014) |
| Mechanosensing role | XIRP2 contains a C-terminal mechanosensor activity in recent primary research | Wagner et al. show force-dependent recruitment of the XIRP2 CTD to strain sites and propose a novel mechanosensor domain that helps recognize F-actin lesions | Extends the view of XIRP2 from scaffold to active mechanochemical responder | (pqac-00000005, pqac-00000006) |
| Best current functional model for bovine XIRP2 | Actin-binding cytoskeletal adapter that couples actin filaments to junctional/mechanical structures and helps maintain or repair those structures under stress | Integrating the Xin-family review with 2023–2024 studies supports a model in which XIRP2 acts at actin-rich membrane–myofibril interfaces, contributes to remodeling in stressed heart and skeletal muscle, and mediates actin lesion sensing/repair in specialized cells | Most likely primary function in Bos taurus is structural/mechanical rather than enzymatic: maintenance of actin-based architecture in striated muscle | (pqac-00000003, pqac-00000012, pqac-00000005, pqac-00000018) |
