Gene Description

OsCCaMK / OsDMI3 (Q6AVM3; Os05g0489900, LOC_Os05g41090) is the rice ortholog of the legume common-symbiosis-pathway kinase DMI3/CCaMK - a calcium- and calcium/calmodulin-dependent serine/threonine protein kinase (EC 2.7.11.17). It has an N-terminal protein-kinase domain, a calmodulin-binding/autoinhibitory region and three C-terminal EF-hand Ca2+-binding motifs, an architecture that lets it act as the intracellular "decoder" of the nuclear Ca2+ spiking signal in the Common Symbiosis Signaling Pathway (CSSP). Upon perception of fungal/bacterial symbiotic signals, receptor-kinase complexes trigger nuclear Ca2+ oscillations that activate CCaMK, which then phosphorylates the transcriptional regulator CYCLOPS/IPD3 (with DELLA) to switch on RAM1 and the arbuscule-development program [file:ORYSJ/CCAMK/CCAMK-deep-research-falcon.md; PMID:17965173]. Crucially, rice is a NON-nodulating cereal (Poaceae): it does NOT form nitrogen-fixing root nodules with rhizobia. In rice, the demonstrated symbiotic role of OsCCaMK is in ARBUSCULAR MYCORRHIZAL (AM) symbiosis - Osccamk loss-of-function mutants are Myc-defective (no cortex invasion, no arbuscule formation) [file:ORYSJ/CCAMK/CCAMK-deep-research-falcon.md]. The protein's symbiotic competence is broadly conserved: rice OsCCaMK can restore both mycorrhization and rhizobial nodulation when expressed in a Lotus japonicus or Medicago truncatula dmi3/ccamk mutant (a heterologous gain-of-function complementation), but rice itself has no nodulation program for the kinase to act in. Beyond symbiosis, OsCCaMK/OsDMI3 has a separable abiotic-stress role: it is a component of abscisic-acid (ABA) signaling that induces antioxidant defense - OsDMI3 is induced by ABA, H2O2 and dehydration, acts upstream of OsMPK1, and phosphorylates the NADPH oxidase OsRBOHB to promote H2O2 production and potentiate ABA signaling during water-deprivation stress [PMID:22869603; file:ORYSJ/CCAMK/CCAMK-deep-research-falcon.md]. OsDMI3 localizes to the nucleus, cytoplasm and plasma membrane [PMID:22869603]. The "Nodulation" UniProt keyword (-> GO:0009877) is a family-signature term inherited from legume DMI3/CCaMK orthologs and is a pathway/organism -context over-annotation in rice, directly analogous to the PPC16 "photosynthesis"-keyword case in this subproject: a process the species does not perform.

Research report: rice CCAMK / OsCCaMK / OsDMI3 (UniProt Q6AVM3; Os05g0489900 / LOC_Os05g41090)

0) Target verification (mandatory disambiguation)

The protein of interest is rice (Oryza sativa subsp. japonica) OsCCaMK/OsDMI3, explicitly mapped in a rice arbuscular mycorrhiza (AM) review to Os05g0489900 and named OsCCaMK/OsDMI3 (Ca2+/CaM-dependent protein kinase), with rice mutant alleles annotated for AM phenotypes (nakagawa2015ricearbuscularmycorrhiza pages 2-4). This matches the UniProt-provided identity for Q6AVM3 (CCAMK; DMI3; Ca2+/CaM-dependent Ser/Thr kinase with EF-hands) and no conflicting “CCAMK” identities were encountered in the retrieved sources.

1) Key concepts and definitions (current understanding)

1.1 CCaMK as a Ca2+ signal “decoder” in the Common Symbiosis Signaling Pathway (CSSP)

In plant intracellular symbioses, perception of fungal/bacterial symbiotic signals triggers nuclear Ca2+ spiking/oscillations, which are decoded by CCaMK (Ca2+/calmodulin-dependent protein kinase) to activate downstream transcriptional programs (wu2023receptorkinasesand pages 3-7, nakagawa2015ricearbuscularmycorrhiza pages 1-2). A widely used conceptual model is a CCaMK–CYCLOPS–DELLA module that promotes AM symbiosis by activating RAM1 expression (wu2023receptorkinasesand pages 1-3, wu2023receptorkinasesand pages 3-7).

1.2 Enzymatic definition (EC 2.7.11.17)

OsCCaMK/OsDMI3 is a serine/threonine protein kinase whose biochemical activity is Ca2+/calmodulin-dependent (shown by kinase assay conditions including Ca2+ and CaM, and by calmodulin-binding assays for CCaMK family members) (ni2020calciumcalmodulindependentproteinkinase pages 3-4, routray2012structuralanalysisand pages 61-66). In vitro, the generic substrate myelin basic protein (MBP) is commonly used to read out activity for CCaMK/DMI3 orthologs, including rice OsDMI3 (ni2020calciumcalmodulindependentproteinkinase pages 3-4, routray2012structuralanalysisand pages 61-66).

1.3 Domain architecture and regulation

CCaMK/DMI3 proteins are described as comprising an N-terminal kinase domain, an autoinhibitory/CaM-binding region, and C-terminal EF-hand Ca2+-binding motifs, enabling regulation by Ca2+ signatures and calmodulin binding (routray2012structuralanalysisand pages 80-95, routray2012structuralanalysisand pages 52-57). Autophosphorylation contributes to regulatory logic; reported CCaMK/DMI3 autophosphorylation sites (characterized in a legume ortholog but used mechanistically for the CCaMK family) include Thr-271, Ser-9, and Ser-344, where Ser-344 phosphorylation can negatively regulate kinase output and symbiotic signaling (routray2012structuralanalysisand pages 52-57).

2) Recent developments and latest research (prioritizing 2023–2024)

2.1 2023 synthesis of AM receptor-to-CCaMK signaling (review/chapter)

A 2023 synthesis of AM signaling emphasizes that Myc/Nod-factor perception via receptor kinase complexes triggers nuclear calcium spiking, which activates CCaMK, which then interacts with and phosphorylates CYCLOPS; DELLAs bind this complex to promote RAM1 expression for AM development (wu2023receptorkinasesand pages 3-7). This provides the current pathway consensus for how CCaMK connects Ca2+ oscillations to transcriptional reprogramming.

Source (publication date/URL): Wu et al., 2023-03, Arbuscular Mycorrhizal Fungi in Agriculture – New Insights (IntechOpen), https://doi.org/10.5772/intechopen.107261 (wu2023receptorkinasesand pages 3-7).

2.2 2024 evidence for deep evolutionary conservation of CCaMK/CYCLOPS module with quantitative phenotype data (preprint)

A 2024 preprint testing the common symbiosis pathway (CSP) in an early-diverging land plant provides quantitative evidence that CCaMK is essential for AM colonization and supports the model that CCaMK phosphorylates CYCLOPS following nuclear Ca2+ spiking, consistent with angiosperm (including rice) pathway models (vernie2024conservationofsymbiotic pages 3-6). Importantly, it gives clear numerical colonization outcomes: 44/54 control plants colonized vs 0/12 ccamk mutants colonized after 5 weeks, and a partial mutant with 32/40 plants normally colonized (vernie2024conservationofsymbiotic pages 3-6). While not rice-specific, this strengthens confidence that CCaMK’s core function (Ca2+ decoding for intracellular symbiosis) is deeply conserved.

The same study reports large transcriptomic outputs upon synthetic activation (autoactive CCaMK constructs), with ~1156–1410 genes upregulated and ~1348–1606 downregulated, illustrating how CCaMK activation can reprogram gene expression at scale (vernie2024conservationofsymbiotic pages 3-6).

Source (publication date/URL): Vernié et al., 2024-01-16, bioRxiv, https://doi.org/10.1101/2024.01.16.575147 (vernie2024conservationofsymbiotic pages 3-6).

2.3 2024 review: OsDMI3 links Ca2+/CaM kinase signaling to ROS/ABA via NADPH oxidase phosphorylation

A highly cited 2024 ROS review reports that OsDMI3 phosphorylates the NADPH oxidase OsRBOHB, promoting H2O2 production that potentiates abscisic acid (ABA) signaling and enhances drought tolerance (wang2024reactiveoxygenspecies pages 20-21). This positions OsDMI3 as a mechanistic bridge between calcium/CaM-dependent kinase signaling and ROS-mediated stress hormone responses.

Source (publication date/URL): Wang et al., 2024-01, Journal of Integrative Plant Biology, https://doi.org/10.1111/jipb.13601 (wang2024reactiveoxygenspecies pages 20-21).

2.4 2024 rice alkali-tolerance GWAS paper cites OsDMI3 as a known alkali-tolerance regulator

A 2024 Scientific Reports GWAS paper on seedling-stage alkali tolerance cites OsDMI3 as a Ca2+/CaM-dependent protein kinase that reduces Na+ and H+ influx in roots, maintains ion balance, promotes root elongation, and improves alkaline tolerance, demonstrating its prominence as a referenced stress-tolerance gene in contemporary rice breeding literature (sheng2024candidategenesfor pages 1-2).

Source (publication date/URL): Sheng et al., 2024-12, Scientific Reports, https://doi.org/10.1038/s41598-024-79273-7 (sheng2024candidategenesfor pages 1-2).

3) Molecular function, targets, pathways, and localization

3.1 Primary molecular function: Ca2+/CaM-dependent Ser/Thr protein kinase activity

Direct rice biochemical evidence in a stress-context study shows OsDMI3 kinase activity assayed after immunoprecipitation using an in-gel kinase assay with MBP, in the presence of Ca2+ (CaCl2) and calmodulin, which is consistent with Ca2+/CaM-dependent kinase biochemistry (ni2020calciumcalmodulindependentproteinkinase pages 3-4). This supports the fundamental reaction: transfer of phosphate from ATP to protein Ser/Thr residues.

3.2 Downstream signaling targets (what is currently supported by retrieved sources)

CYCLOPS/IPD3: A rice AM review states that CYCLOPS is a phosphorylation target of CCaMK (nakagawa2015ricearbuscularmycorrhiza pages 4-5). A 2023 AM signaling synthesis also states that activated CCaMK interacts with and phosphorylates CYCLOPS, which then participates in transcriptional activation with DELLA involvement (wu2023receptorkinasesand pages 3-7). These sources do not provide the underlying rice experimental methods in the retrieved excerpts, so they should be treated as pathway-consensus statements rather than fully documented primary evidence within this run.

OsRBOHB (NADPH oxidase): A 2024 ROS review cites a recent finding that OsDMI3 phosphorylates OsRBOHB, promoting H2O2 production and potentiating ABA signaling (wang2024reactiveoxygenspecies pages 20-21). The excerpt does not include residue-level or assay details.

OsUXS3: A 2023 plant transcriptomics paper excerpt cites a report that OsDMI3-mediated OsUXS3 phosphorylation improves oxidative-stress tolerance by modulating OsCATB abundance (chen2023pacbiofulllengthtranscriptome pages 18-19). Again, the excerpt is a citation-level summary without methodological detail.

3.3 Pathways and biological processes

Arbuscular mycorrhizal symbiosis / CSSP: CCaMK is positioned downstream of receptor-mediated signaling that triggers nuclear Ca2+ spiking, and upstream of transcriptional regulators (CYCLOPS/DELLA/RAM1) that enable arbuscule development (wu2023receptorkinasesand pages 3-7). In rice, OsCCaMK/OsDMI3 is listed among OsCSP genes directly involved in AM symbiosis (nakagawa2015ricearbuscularmycorrhiza pages 1-2).

Temporal expression during AM presymbiotic phase: In rice roots challenged with Glomus intraradices, OsCCaMK transcript levels rose relative to mock controls at ~72–96 hours post-inoculation, later than certain CDPK/CPKs tested in the same study (campossoriano2011aricecalciumdependent pages 2-5).

Abiotic stress (saline-alkaline tolerance in roots): OsDMI3 mediates a physiological root response to alkaline stress by decreasing Na+ and H+ influx and upregulating ion-homeostasis genes (ni2020calciumcalmodulindependentproteinkinase pages 4-6).

Microbiome/CH4 cycling association in paddy context: In a rice AM review, Osccamk-1 is associated with altered root-associated bacterial community composition and increased methane flux, consistent with CCaMK impacting microbial community structure and methane oxidation dynamics (nakagawa2015ricearbuscularmycorrhiza pages 5-6).

3.4 Subcellular localization (current evidence and limitations)

Within the retrieved evidence, the clearest direct localization evidence is from Lotus japonicus, where nuclear localization of deregulated/GOF CCaMK is required to activate symbiotic responses; forcing GOF-CCaMK out of nuclei (NES fusion) prevented activation of AM marker expression, while nuclear-localized constructs induced cortical-cell reporter activation and PPA-like structures (takeda2012nuclearlocalizedandderegulated pages 6-7). Rice-specific subcellular localization of OsDMI3/OsCCaMK was not directly documented in the retrieved full texts for this run; thus, nuclear localization in rice should be presented as an inference from conserved CCaMK biology rather than as a rice-specific experimental fact here.

4) Evidence from genetics: rice mutant phenotypes supporting function

A rice AM review compiles experimentally supported AM genes and identifies OsCCaMK/OsDMI3 (Os05g0489900) with rice mutants including Osccamk-1 (NE1115) and Osccamk-2 (NF8513); Osccamk-1 is annotated as Myc− (no cortex invasion/no arbuscule formation, per the review’s phenotype legend) (nakagawa2015ricearbuscularmycorrhiza pages 2-4, nakagawa2015ricearbuscularmycorrhiza pages 4-5). The corresponding table is captured in the cited figure panels (nakagawa2015ricearbuscularmycorrhiza media 4569d13e, nakagawa2015ricearbuscularmycorrhiza media ea83070a).

5) Quantitative data and statistics (recent and key)

5.1 Saline-alkaline stress physiology (rice; primary study)

In rice roots under 75 mM NaHCO3 (pH 8), OsDMI3 transcript and kinase activity are induced and contribute to improved root performance; OsDMI3 kinase activity increases by ~1 hour after treatment (ni2020calciumcalmodulindependentproteinkinase pages 3-4). Experimental design includes qRT-PCR at 6 hours, and noninvasive micro-test technology (NMT) flux measurements after 24 hours at ~500 µm from the root tip (ni2020calciumcalmodulindependentproteinkinase pages 3-4). Overexpression lines show reduced Na+ and H+ influx and reduced Na+ accumulation relative to wild type, while knockouts show the opposite pattern (ni2020calciumcalmodulindependentproteinkinase pages 3-4).

Source (publication date/URL): Ni et al., 2020-08, Plant Signaling & Behavior, https://doi.org/10.1080/15592324.2020.1813999 (ni2020calciumcalmodulindependentproteinkinase pages 3-4).

5.2 AM colonization genetics (2024; non-rice but mechanistically informative)

The 2024 Vernié et al. preprint provides strongly quantitative colonization outcomes for CCaMK genetic disruption: 44/54 colonized in controls vs 0/12 in ccamk mutants at 5 weeks (vernie2024conservationofsymbiotic pages 3-6). It also quantifies gene-expression reprogramming triggered by synthetic CCaMK activation (1156–1410 up; 1348–1606 down) (vernie2024conservationofsymbiotic pages 3-6).

6) Current applications and real-world implementations

6.1 Productivity and sustainable nutrient acquisition via AM symbiosis (applied framing)

A rice-focused AM review frames AM symbiosis as a tool to study fungal symbiosis and “a potential target to increase productivity,” emphasizing AM benefits for nutrient acquisition (notably phosphate uptake in nutrient-poor soils) and the importance of conserved CSSP components such as OsCCaMK/OsDMI3 for enabling AM (nakagawa2015ricearbuscularmycorrhiza pages 1-2). This is application-relevant because OsCCaMK is genetically required for AM accommodation (Myc− mutants) (nakagawa2015ricearbuscularmycorrhiza pages 2-4).

6.2 Stress tolerance targets for breeding/engineering

OsDMI3 is referenced in a 2024 alkali tolerance GWAS context as a known positive regulator of alkaline tolerance (through ion influx reduction and root elongation), supporting its status as a candidate in stress-resilient rice improvement narratives (sheng2024candidategenesfor pages 1-2). Mechanistically, OsDMI3’s placement upstream of ion homeostasis gene induction (OsSOS1; PM H+-ATPases) under NaHCO3 stress provides a plausible route for engineering improved root performance on saline-alkaline soils (ni2020calciumcalmodulindependentproteinkinase pages 4-6).

7) Expert synthesis and analysis (with evidence-based caveats)

  1. Primary function and pathway role: Across rice-focused and broader plant literature, CCaMK/OsDMI3 is best supported as a Ca2+ signal-decoding kinase that sits at a convergence point between nuclear Ca2+ oscillations and transcriptional reprogramming required for intracellular symbiosis (CSSP) (wu2023receptorkinasesand pages 3-7, nakagawa2015ricearbuscularmycorrhiza pages 1-2). The rice mutant compilation showing Osccamk Myc− phenotypes supports this being essential, not redundant, in rice AM signaling (nakagawa2015ricearbuscularmycorrhiza pages 2-4).

  2. Substrate specificity remains only partially resolved in the retrieved evidence: While pathway models consistently include CYCLOPS phosphorylation by CCaMK, the retrieved excerpts do not supply rice-specific biochemical details (e.g., phosphosites, in vitro kinetics, co-IP/biFC), so this report treats CYCLOPS as a strongly supported downstream effector but notes that evidence granularity is limited in the retrieved snippets (wu2023receptorkinasesand pages 3-7, nakagawa2015ricearbuscularmycorrhiza pages 4-5). For abiotic stress, the most explicit mechanistic “target” summary in recent review evidence is OsRBOHB phosphorylation for ROS/ABA potentiation (wang2024reactiveoxygenspecies pages 20-21), but again primary data details are not in the excerpt.

  3. Localization: The strongest direct evidence in the retrieved set indicates that nuclear localization of active CCaMK is functionally important (Lotus), which aligns with CCaMK’s role in decoding nuclear Ca2+ spiking (takeda2012nuclearlocalizedandderegulated pages 6-7). However, rice-specific localization is not directly established by the retrieved full texts in this run; any rice localization statements should be treated as inferred from conserved biology.

  OsCCaMK / OsDMI3 (Q6AVM3; Os05g0489900, LOC_Os05g41090) is the rice ortholog of the
  legume common-symbiosis-pathway kinase DMI3/CCaMK - a calcium- and
  calcium/calmodulin-dependent serine/threonine protein kinase (EC 2.7.11.17). It has an
  N-terminal protein-kinase domain, a calmodulin-binding/autoinhibitory region and three
  C-terminal EF-hand Ca2+-binding motifs, an architecture that lets it act as the
  intracellular "decoder" of the nuclear Ca2+ spiking signal in the Common Symbiosis
  Signaling Pathway (CSSP). Upon perception of fungal/bacterial symbiotic signals,
  receptor-kinase complexes trigger nuclear Ca2+ oscillations that activate CCaMK, which
  then phosphorylates the transcriptional regulator CYCLOPS/IPD3 (with DELLA) to switch on
  RAM1 and the arbuscule-development program [file:ORYSJ/CCAMK/CCAMK-deep-research-falcon.md;
  PMID:17965173]. Crucially, rice is a NON-nodulating cereal (Poaceae): it does NOT form
  nitrogen-fixing root nodules with rhizobia. In rice, the demonstrated symbiotic role of
  OsCCaMK is in ARBUSCULAR MYCORRHIZAL (AM) symbiosis - Osccamk loss-of-function mutants are
  Myc-defective (no cortex invasion, no arbuscule formation) [file:ORYSJ/CCAMK/CCAMK-deep-research-falcon.md].
  The protein's symbiotic competence is broadly conserved: rice OsCCaMK can restore both
  mycorrhization and rhizobial nodulation when expressed in a Lotus japonicus or Medicago
  truncatula dmi3/ccamk mutant (a heterologous gain-of-function complementation), but rice
  itself has no nodulation program for the kinase to act in. Beyond symbiosis, OsCCaMK/OsDMI3
  has a separable abiotic-stress role: it is a component of abscisic-acid (ABA) signaling
  that induces antioxidant defense - OsDMI3 is induced by ABA, H2O2 and dehydration, acts
  upstream of OsMPK1, and phosphorylates the NADPH oxidase OsRBOHB to promote H2O2 production
  and potentiate ABA signaling during water-deprivation stress [PMID:22869603;
  file:ORYSJ/CCAMK/CCAMK-deep-research-falcon.md]. OsDMI3 localizes to the nucleus, cytoplasm
  and plasma membrane [PMID:22869603]. The "Nodulation" UniProt keyword (-> GO:0009877) is a

  -context over-annotation in rice, directly analogous to the PPC16 "photosynthesis"-keyword
