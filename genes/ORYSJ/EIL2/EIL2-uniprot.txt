CC   -!- FUNCTION: Transcription factor acting as a positive regulator in the
CC       ethylene response pathway (PubMed:19798512, PubMed:25995326). Involved
CC       in wound signaling by binding specifically to the DNA sequence 5'-
CC       ATGTACCT-3' found in the promoter of some wound-inducible genes
CC       (PubMed:19798512). Required for ethylene-promoted coleoptile elongation
CC       (PubMed:25995326). Acts as a negative regulator of salt tolerance
CC       (PubMed:25995326). During salt stress, activates the cation transporter
CC       HKT1, which mediates increased sodium uptake in roots, and contributes
CC       to sodium accumulation and salt toxicity (PubMed:25995326). Possesses
CC       transactivation activity in protoplasts (PubMed:25995326).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:19798512, ECO:0000269|PubMed:25995326}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Nucleus {ECO:0000269|PubMed:25995326}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Expressed in roots (PubMed:19798512). Expressed at
CC       low levels in panicles (PubMed:19798512). Expressed in roots, leaf
CC       blades, leaf sheaths, stems, anthers of young flowers, shells, and
CC       developing grains (PubMed:25995326). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:19798512,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:25995326}.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: Induced by wounding and metyhl jasmonate.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:19798512}.
CC   -!- MISCELLANEOUS: Plants silencing EIL2 exhibit insensitivity in ethylene-
CC       promoted coleoptile elongation. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:25995326}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the EIN3 family. {ECO:0000305}.
