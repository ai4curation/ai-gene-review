Gene Description

Cds1 (Rv3684) is an L-cysteine desulfhydrase (EC 4.4.1.1) that catalyzes the PLP-dependent beta-elimination of L-cysteine to produce hydrogen sulfide (H2S), pyruvate, and ammonia. It is a major source of endogenous H2S in M. tuberculosis. The H2S produced stimulates respiration primarily via cytochrome bd, modulates the balance between oxidative phosphorylation and glycolysis, contributes to redox homeostasis by affecting ergothioneine (EGT) and mycothiol (MSH) levels, and influences drug susceptibility. The enzyme likely also functions to eliminate toxic levels of cysteine. Kinetic parameters: KM = 11.26 mM for cysteine, kcat = 78.71 sec(-1). Activity is inhibited by aminooxyacetic acid (AOAA), a PLP inhibitor, but NOT by DL-propargylglycine (PAG), a cystathionine gamma-lyase inhibitor, confirming its identity as a cysteine desulfhydrase rather than a cystathionine gamma-lyase. Part of the Rv3682-Rv3683-cds1 operon.

core_functions:
  - description: >-
      PLP-dependent L-cysteine desulfhydrase catalyzing beta-elimination of
      L-cysteine to produce hydrogen sulfide (H2S), pyruvate, and ammonia.
      This is the major H2S-producing enzyme in M. tuberculosis. The H2S
      produced serves as a sulfur recycling sink, returning sulfur atoms
      back to the cysteine biosynthetic pathway to maintain sulfur homeostasis.
      Kinetics: KM = 11.26 mM for cysteine, kcat = 78.71 sec(-1). Activity is
      inhibited by AOAA (PLP inhibitor) but NOT by PAG (cystathionine gamma-lyase
      inhibitor), confirming identity as a true cysteine desulfhydrase.
