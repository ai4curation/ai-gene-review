CC   -!- FUNCTION: A cysteine desulfhydrase that generates hydrogen sulfide,
CC       H(2)S. The H(2)S produced by this enzyme stimulates respiration in
CC       M.tuberculosis, mediated primarily via cytochrome bd with a lesser
CC       contribution from cytochrome bc1/aa3. H(2)S modulates the balance
CC       between respiration and glycolysis, and also contributes to redox
CC       homeostasis. Probably eliminates toxic levels of Cys (which can induce
CC       oxidative stress). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:34439535}.

CC   -!- INDUCTION: Probably part of the Rv3682-Rv3683-cds1 operon.
CC       {ECO:0000305|PubMed:34439535}.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: Significantly reduced production of H(2)S in all
CC       media tested, cells grow significantly less well in the presence of 4
CC       mM cysteine, no change in survival in mouse macrophages. 40% reduction
CC       in basal oxygen consumption (respiration rate), increased levels of
CC       amino acids, glycolytic and TCA cycle metabolites, decreased levels of
CC       the redox couple ergothioneine and mycothiol. Increased ATP levels in
CC       the presence of 100 uM exogenous Cys. Increased cell survival after
CC       clofazimine or rifampicin treatment. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:34439535}.
CC   -!- MISCELLANEOUS: There is a large variation in the ability of different
CC       strains to produce H(2)S; H37Rv, CDC 1551 and 2 clinical strains were
CC       the highest producers of H(2)S. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:34439535}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the cysteine synthase/cystathionine beta-
CC       synthase family. Cds1 subfamily. {ECO:0000255|HAMAP-Rule:MF_00868}.
