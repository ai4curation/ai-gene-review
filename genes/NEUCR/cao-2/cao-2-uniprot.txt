CC   -!- FUNCTION: Torulene dioxygenase; part of pathway that mediates the
CC       biosynthesis of neurosporaxanthin, a carboxylic apocarotenoid acting as
CC       an essential protective pigments and leading to orange pigmentation
CC       (PubMed:17610084). Cao-2 mediates the cleavage of torulene into beta-
CC       apo-4'-carotenal, the aldehyde corresponding to the acidic
CC       neurosporaxanthin (PubMed:17610084). Is not able to use gamma-carotene
CC       (that it is not desaturated at the C4'-C5' bond) as substrate, which
CC       suggests a high specificity of cao-2 in cleaving the C4'-C5' double
CC       bond (PubMed:17610084). Neurosporaxanthin is synthesized from geranyl-
CC       geranyl pyrophosphate (GGPP) through several enzymatic activities.
CC       Phytoene synthase activity performed by the bifunctional enzyme al-2
CC       first produces phytoene from geranyl-geranyl pyrophosphate (GGPP). The
CC       phytoene dehydrogenase al-1 then introduces 5 desaturations to lead to
CC       3,4-didehydrolycopene via the intermediates phytofluene, zeta-carotene,
CC       neurosporene and lycopene. Al-2 cyclase activity then converts 3,4-
CC       didehydrolycopene into torulene. Al-2 can also convet lycopene into
CC       gamma-carotene which in turn is converted to beta-carotene by an
CC       additional al-2 cyclization reaction. Torulene is the substrate of the
CC       dioxidase cao-2 that breaks the molecule, removing five carbon atoms to
CC       yield beta-apo-4'-carotenal, whereas the aldehyde dehydrogenase ylo-1
CC       mediates the last step by converting beta-apo-4'-carotenal into
CC       neurosporaxanthin (Probable). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:17610084,
CC       ECO:0000305|PubMed:17610084}.
