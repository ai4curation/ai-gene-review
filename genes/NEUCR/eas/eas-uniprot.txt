CC   -!- FUNCTION: Aerial growth, conidiation, and dispersal of filamentous
CC       fungi in the environment rely upon a capability of their secreting
CC       small amphipathic proteins called hydrophobins (HPBs) with low sequence
CC       identity. Class I can self-assemble into an outermost layer of rodlet
CC       bundles on aerial cell surfaces, conferring cellular hydrophobicity
CC       that supports fungal growth, development and dispersal; whereas class
CC       II form highly ordered films at water-air interfaces through
CC       intermolecular interactions but contribute nothing to the rodlet
CC       structure (Probable). Eas is a class I hydrophobin that forms
CC       functional amyloid fibrils called rodlets that facilitate spore
CC       formation and dispersal (PubMed:1459460, PubMed:22308366).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:1459460, ECO:0000269|PubMed:22308366, ECO:0000305}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Self-assembles to form functional amyloid fibrils called
CC       rodlets. Self-assembly into fibrillar rodlets occurs spontaneously at
CC       hydrophobic:hydrophilic interfaces and the rodlets further associate
CC       laterally to form amphipathic monolayers. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:16537446,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:18674544, ECO:0000269|PubMed:22308366}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Secreted {ECO:0000269|PubMed:16537446,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:18674544, ECO:0000269|PubMed:22308366}. Spore wall
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:16537446, ECO:0000269|PubMed:18674544,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:22308366}.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: Expression is controlled by blue light and by a circadian
CC       clock (PubMed:11532152, PubMed:1459460, PubMed:8552078). A positive
CC       activating clock element (ACE) is found to reside in a 45-bp region,
CC       just upstream from the TATA box (PubMed:8552078). Expression is
CC       regulated both developmentally and by light and is abundant in
CC       illuminated mycelia and conidiophores but is not detectable or is
CC       barely detectable in dark-grown mycelia, mature macroconidia,
CC       microconidia, and ascospores (PubMed:1459459).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11532152, ECO:0000269|PubMed:1459459,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:1459460, ECO:0000269|PubMed:8552078}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: the Cys3-Cys4 loop (residies Cys-45 to Cys-71) does not have an
CC       integral role in the formation or structure of the rodlets and that the
CC       major determinant of the unique properties of these proteins is the
CC       amphipathic core structure. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:18674544}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the fungal hydrophobin family. {ECO:0000305}.
