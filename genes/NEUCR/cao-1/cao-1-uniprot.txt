CC   -!- FUNCTION: Dioxygenase that cleaves the interphenyl C-alpha-C-beta
CC       double bond of resveratrol to yield 3,5-dihydroxybenzaldehyde and 4-
CC       hydroxybenzaldehyde (PubMed:23893079, PubMed:28493664). Also cleaves
CC       piceatannol, a compound that differs from resveratrol only in the
CC       occurrence of an additional hydroxyl group, which leads to the
CC       production of 3,4-dihydroxybenzaldehyde and 3,5-hydroxybenzaldehyde
CC       (PubMed:23893079, PubMed:28493664). Is not able to cleave trans-
CC       stilbene, 4-monohydroxy-trans-stilbene, 3,5-dihydroxy-trans-stilbene
CC       (pinosylvin), trismethoxy-resveratrol, and 3,3',5-trihydroxy-4'-
CC       methoxystilbene-3-O-beta-D-glucoside (PubMed:23893079). Is not involved
CC       in carotenoid metabolism (PubMed:23893079).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:23893079, ECO:0000269|PubMed:28493664}.
