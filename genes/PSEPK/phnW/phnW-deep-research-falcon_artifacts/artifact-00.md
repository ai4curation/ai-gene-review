| Identity | UniProt Q88KT0 is annotated as **PhnW**, a **2-aminoethylphosphonate:pyruvate aminotransferase** (AEPT; EC 2.6.1.37), a PLP-dependent aminotransferase that initiates bacterial AEP/ciliatine utilization; this assignment matches the canonical PhnW function described across bacteria. | Enzyme class: EC 2.6.1.37; subunit mass ~40–42 kDa; native enzyme ~100 kDa, consistent with homodimeric state. | (pqac-00000002, pqac-00000020) | Kim et al. 2002, *Journal of Bacteriology*, https://doi.org/10.1128/jb.184.15.4134-4140.2002 |
| Reaction | PhnW catalyzes the reversible transamination **AEP + pyruvate ⇄ phosphonoacetaldehyde (PAA/P-Ald) + L-alanine** via a PLP-dependent ping-pong mechanism. This is the chemically activating step that converts an aminophosphonate into a carbonyl-containing phosphonate. | Keq ~0.5; forward kcat ~7 s^-1; reverse kcat ~9.3 s^-1. | (pqac-00000002, pqac-00000000, pqac-00000010) | Kim et al. 2002, *Journal of Bacteriology*, https://doi.org/10.1128/jb.184.15.4134-4140.2002; Ruffolo et al. 2023, *Molecules*, https://doi.org/10.3390/molecules28196863 |
| Pathway context | In the canonical **PhnWX** pathway, PhnW generates PAA, which is then hydrolyzed by **PhnX** to acetaldehyde + phosphate. In the alternative **PhnWAY** route, PAA is oxidized by **PhnY** to phosphonoacetate and then hydrolyzed by **PhnA**. PhnW can also function in AEP biosynthesis in some taxa. | No single pathway flux value reported in the snippets. | (pqac-00000010, pqac-00000005, pqac-00000007) | Ruffolo et al. 2023, *Molecules*, https://doi.org/10.3390/molecules28196863; Villarreal-Chiu et al. 2012, *Frontiers in Microbiology*, https://doi.org/10.3389/fmicb.2012.00019; Zangelmi et al. 2023, *iScience*, https://doi.org/10.1016/j.isci.2023.108108 |
| Regulation | AEP catabolic genes are regulated transcriptionally. In **Pseudomonas** spp., **LysR-family regulators** can induce phnWX-like operons in response to AEP; in **Pseudomonas putida BIRD1**, **AepR** is required for PhnWX expression under carbon/nitrogen limitation with AEP as co-inducer. In other bacteria, phn genes may also be under **Pho regulon** control during phosphate limitation. | No kinetic constant for regulation; qualitative induction by AEP and nutrient limitation. | (pqac-00000009, pqac-00000011, pqac-00000002) | Ruffolo et al. 2023, *Molecules*, https://doi.org/10.3390/molecules28196863; Epiktetov et al. 2024, *International Journal of Molecular Sciences*, https://doi.org/10.3390/ijms25126409; Kim et al. 2002, *Journal of Bacteriology*, https://doi.org/10.1128/jb.184.15.4134-4140.2002 |

| Kinetics | Steady-state kinetics support a **Bi-Bi ping-pong** mechanism. The enzyme is moderately efficient with AEP/pyruvate and reverse substrates PAA/L-Ala, with pH optimum around 8.5 and broad activity over alkaline pH in one direction. | Km(AEP) 1.11 ± 0.03 mM; Km(pyruvate) 0.15 ± 0.02 mM; Km(PAA/P-Ald) 0.09 ± 0.01 mM; Km(L-Ala) 1.4 ± 0.03 mM; kcat ~7 s^-1 forward, ~9.3 s^-1 reverse; Vmax for PAA-forming direction approximately constant from pH 6.5–9.5. | (pqac-00000002, pqac-00000000, pqac-00000020) | Kim et al. 2002, *Journal of Bacteriology*, https://doi.org/10.1128/jb.184.15.4134-4140.2002 |
| Substrate specificity | PhnW is highly specialized for **AEP as amino donor** and **pyruvate as amino acceptor**. | Transport context | PhnW acts downstream of AEP uptake systems. AEP-specific transport can involve **PhnCDE/PhnD** or **AepXVW/AepP**-type systems, and these transporter genes are commonly associated with **phnWX/phnWAY** clusters.
