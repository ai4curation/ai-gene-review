Gene Description

pqqB encodes a conserved PQQ-pathway metallo-beta-lactamase-fold protein in P. putida KT2440. UniProt conservatively suggests transport of PQQ or a precursor, but structure and biochemical literature support a PqqB hydroxylase role in converting PqqA-derived cross-linked intermediates during pyrroloquinoline quinone biosynthesis.

Proposed New Ontology Terms

PqqB hydroxylase activity

Definition: Catalysis of oxygen-insertion/hydroxylation reactions on a PqqA-derived cross-linked amino acid intermediate during pyrroloquinoline quinone biosynthesis.

Justification: Primary biochemical and structural studies support PqqB as a non-heme hydroxylase in PQQ biosynthesis, but GO lacks a PqqB-specific hydroxylase molecular-function term.

Parent term: oxidoreductase activity, acting on paired donors, with incorporation or reduction of molecular oxygen

Supporting Evidence:

  • PMID:30811189 : show that PqqB is a previously uncharacterized hydroxylase
  • PMID:30811189 : strongly implicate PqqB as a novel non-heme hydroxylase
