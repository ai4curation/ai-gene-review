# crcB / fluC notes

- Identity: KT2440 PP_4001 is the standard gene symbol `fluC`; `crcB` is a synonym. UniProt Q88FT1 describes a 124 aa "Fluoride-specific ion channel FluC" [UniProt:Q88FT1 "GN   Name=fluC"; "GN   Synonyms=crcB"; "GN   OrderedLocusNames=PP_4001"; "Fluoride-specific ion channel FluC"].
- Localization: FluC is a small multi-pass inner membrane protein with four predicted transmembrane helices [UniProt:Q88FT1 "Cell inner membrane"; "Multi-pass membrane protein"; "TRANSMEM        1..21"; "TRANSMEM        37..57"; "TRANSMEM        69..89"; "TRANSMEM        99..119"].
- Channel family/function: Family-level studies established Fluc/CrcB proteins as fluoride-specific ion channels with dual-topology architecture and a double-barrelled channel assembly [PMID:23991286 "A family of fluoride-specific ion channels with dual-topology architecture"; PMID:26344196 "Crystal structures of a double-barrelled fluoride ion channel"].
- Transport/detoxification: UniProt annotates the physiological reaction as fluoride(in) = fluoride(out) and states that the protein is "Important for reducing fluoride concentration in the cell, thus reducing its toxicity" [UniProt:Q88FT1 "Reaction=fluoride(in) = fluoride(out)"; "Important for reducing fluoride concentration in the cell, thus reducing its toxicity"].
- P. putida-specific evidence: In P. putida KT2440, loss of CrcB/FluC causes marked fluoride sensitivity and intracellular fluoride accumulation, supporting a primary role in fluoride export and detoxification [PMID:35726888 "knocking out the gene encoding this exporter led to a significant intracellular accumulation"; PMID:35726888 "Role of the CrcB transporter of Pseudomonas putida in the multi-level stress response elicited by mineral fluoride"].
- Structural sodium note: Na(+) is not transported but is required structurally for Fluc-family channel function [UniProt:Q88FT1 "Na(+) is not transported, but it plays an essential structural role"; PMID:31945374 "An interfacial sodium ion is an essential structural feature of Fluc family fluoride channels"].
