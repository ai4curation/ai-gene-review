| Aspect | Finding |
|---|---|
| Enzyme class / reaction | **alr / PP_3722 / UniProt Q88GJ9** in *Pseudomonas putida* KT2440 encodes a **broad-spectrum amino-acid racemase (BSAR)** in the alanine racemase family, catalyzing reversible interconversion of L- and D-amino acids; physiologically, the strongest evidence supports racemization of **Lys** and **Arg** rather than a primary alanine-specific role (pqac-00000003, pqac-00000007, pqac-00000012) |
| Cofactor & mechanism | Family/domain context and experimental literature are consistent with a **PLP-dependent alanine racemase-like mechanism**. Recent alanine racemase structural work supports the canonical two-base mechanism involving PLP and Lys/Tyr catalytic residues, with substrate carboxylate-assisted proton transfer proposed in reaction intermediates; this is mechanistic context for Alr/BSAR family members rather than a PP_3722-specific structure (pqac-00000018, pqac-00000020, pqac-00000021) |
| Substrate specificity | In vitro, Alr showed measurable activity on **9 of 19** chiral amino acids and was described as the **broadest-specificity** racemase recovered in KT2440. It strongly prefers **lysine**, and the 2018 study states it preferentially interconverts **L-/D-Lys** and **L-/D-Arg** in vivo/physiology (pqac-00000007, pqac-00000009, pqac-00000003) |
| Key kinetic parameters | **Alr on lysine:** D→L Km **0.36 ± 0.06 mM**, kcat **274.5 ± 6.7 s⁻¹**, kcat/Km **~7.63 × 10^5 M⁻¹s⁻¹**; L→D Km **8.96 ± 1.52 mM**, kcat **1681.7 ± 161.7 s⁻¹**, kcat/Km **~1.88 × 10^5 M⁻¹s⁻¹**. **Alr on alanine:** D→L Km **15.71 ± 2.79 mM**, kcat **8.83 s⁻¹**, kcat/Km **~5.62 × 10^2**; L→D Km **12.62 ± 0.74 mM**, kcat **7.33 s⁻¹**, kcat/Km **~5.81 × 10^2**. Thus lysine catalytic efficiency is ~**3 orders of magnitude** higher than alanine. By comparison, **DadX (PP_5269)** is alanine-specialized: D→L alanine Km **7.73 ± 0.63 mM**, kcat **37.47 ± 2.67 s⁻¹**, kcat/Km **~4.85 × 10^3**; L→D alanine Km **24.57 ± 2.64 mM**, kcat **92.0 ± 11.6 s⁻¹**, kcat/Km **~3.74 × 10^3** (pqac-00000009, pqac-00000010, pqac-00000011) |
| Subcellular localization | Alr activity was experimentally enriched in the **periplasmic fraction**, consistent with a predicted secretion/targeting signal. In stationary phase, specific activity was **134.5 ± 29.8 nmol min⁻¹ mg⁻¹** in the periplasmic fraction versus **209.7 ± 8.2 nmol min⁻¹ mg⁻¹** total (~**64%** of total). Fraction quality controls showed periplasmic alkaline phosphatase recovery **72–77%** of total and cytoplasmic G6PDH contamination only **13.3–16.7%**, supporting genuine periplasmic localization, though minor contamination could not be fully excluded (pqac-00000001, pqac-00000000) |
| Physiological role | The best-supported organism-specific function is **basic amino-acid catabolism**, especially **L-Lys** and **L-Arg** utilization via racemization to their D-forms. The authors propose **L-Lys → D-Lys** feeds the **L-pipecolate pathway**, linking extracellular/periplasmic racemization to intracellular catabolism; D-Arg catabolism is implicated but the downstream route in KT2440 remains unresolved (pqac-00000003, pqac-00000005, pqac-00000006) |
| Mutant / fitness phenotypes | A markerless **Δalr** mutant lost detectable Alr activity and failed to accumulate detectable D-Lys in LB. It showed markedly reduced capacity to use **L-Lys** and **L-Arg** as sole carbon and nitrogen sources, whereas effects on many other amino acids were limited. In RB-TnSeq/BarSeq, **PP_3722** had a modest quantitative fitness defect of about **-1.0** in arginine-related conditions, consistent with a conditional but real contribution to Arg metabolism; DadX appeared primary for alanine metabolism (pqac-00000002, pqac-00000005, pqac-00000008) |
| In vivo metabolite evidence | In LB, wild type accumulated **<100 µM D-Lys at 4 h** and about **~1 mM D-Lys at 8 h**; the **Δalr** strain produced **no detectable D-Lys**. Meanwhile, Δalr supernatants accumulated more **L-Lys** (about **~1.5 mM to ~2.5 mM by 10 h**), supporting in vivo conversion of secreted/periplasmic L-Lys into D-Lys by Alr and increased overall lysine consumption in the wild type (pqac-00000000, pqac-00000001) |
| Regulation / omics | RNA-seq comparing wild type and Δalr found differential expression largely restricted to **amino-acid metabolism** genes, not a broad signaling regulon. In wild type under D-Lys-producing conditions, genes linked to D-Lys catabolism were upregulated, including **PP3596** (D-amino-acid dehydrogenase), **PP3591** (Pip2C/Pyr2C reductase), downstream genes **PP5257/PP5258**, and a periplasmic amino-acid transporter **PP3593** with fold-change **27**; among overexpressed genes, **PP3722** itself differed by about **~100-fold RPKM** (pqac-00000000, pqac-00000002) |
| Peptidoglycan impact | Despite being an amino-acid racemase, **Alr products were not detectably incorporated into peptidoglycan under the tested conditions**. Stationary-phase peptidoglycan composition did **not differ significantly** between wild type and Δalr (**p > 0.05**), arguing against a major structural cell-wall role for PP_3722 in KT2440 during the examined growth state (pqac-00000000, pqac-00000003, pqac-00000006) |
| Related enzymes / ortholog context | *P. putida* carries at least two distinct alanine-racemase-family enzymes with different niches: **Alr/PP_3722** is the **broad-spectrum**, Lys/Arg-biased, periplasm-associated racemase, whereas **DadX/PP_5269** is the **alanine-specialized** racemase. Recent work in *Vibrio fischeri* identified **BsrF**, a member of the **periplasmic broad-spectrum racemase family**, supporting the broader existence of this family in Gram-negative bacteria and showing that altered localization can redirect such enzymes toward peptidoglycan precursor supply and D-cycloserine resistance (pqac-00000009, pqac-00000010, pqac-00000013, pqac-00000015, pqac-00000016) |
| Applications / biotech relevance | Although PP_3722 itself has not been widely deployed industrially, broad-spectrum racemases are relevant for **D-amino-acid bioproduction**, **biofilm control**, and **microbial competition**. Reviews highlight D-amino-acid uses in antibiotics, sweeteners, and pharmaceutical intermediates; engineered systems reached **44 g/L D-glutamate**, **19.76 g/L D-HPG in 10 h** (and **42.69 g/L** at 3-L scale), and a broad-specificity *Pseudomonas putida* racemase variant reportedly produced **1.8 g/L D-tryptophan**. D-amino acids also enhance biocide efficacy against biofilms and are being explored for orthopedic infection control (pqac-00000023, pqac-00000025, pqac-00000027, pqac-00000029, pqac-00000024, pqac-00000030) |
| Key recent (2023–2024) developments | Recent studies did not substantially revise the core PP_3722 annotation, but they strengthened **family-level context**. In **2024**, Bsr-family work in *V. fischeri* showed a periplasmic broad-spectrum racemase can, when mislocalized to the cytoplasm, rescue **D-Glu** and **D-Ala** auxotrophy and increase **D-cycloserine resistance**, illustrating latent functional plasticity in Bsr enzymes. Also in **2024**, serial femtosecond crystallography captured alanine racemase reaction states at **2.3 Å** from **68,582 indexed images**, refining understanding of PLP-mediated racemization dynamics applicable to the broader enzyme family (pqac-00000013, pqac-00000014, pqac-00000015, pqac-00000022, pqac-00000018, pqac-00000020) |


*Table: This table summarizes the best-supported functional annotation for Pseudomonas putida KT2440 alr (PP_3722; UniProt Q88GJ9), integrating organism-specific biochemistry, localization, mutant phenotypes, omics, and recent family-level developments. It is useful as a compact evidence map for distinguishing PP_3722 from alanine-specific racemases and for understanding its likely role in Lys/Arg catabolism.*
