Gene Description

alr encodes a broad-specificity periplasmic amino-acid racemase in P. putida KT2440. Although annotated as alanine racemase by family inference, experimental work shows the enzyme has much higher catalytic efficiency for lysine and arginine than alanine and supports a primary role in L-lysine and L-arginine catabolism through conversion to the corresponding D-enantiomers.

Research Report: Functional Annotation of alr (PP_3722; UniProt Q88GJ9) in Pseudomonas putida KT2440

1. Target verification (gene/protein identity)

The literature retrieved unambiguously links the locus PP_3722 in Pseudomonas putida KT2440 to alr, encoding a broad-spectrum amino-acid racemase (often termed BSAR) rather than the canonical alanine-specific housekeeping racemase. In the same genome, DadX (PP_5269) is the alanine-specialized racemase, and multiple experiments distinguish these two enzymes by substrate preference and physiological roles. (radkov2013aminoacidracemization pages 1-2, radkov2013aminoacidracemization pages 4-5, radkov2013aminoacidracemization pages 7-8)

2. Key concepts and definitions (current understanding)

2.1 Amino-acid racemases and D-amino acids

Amino-acid racemases catalyze reversible L↔D stereoinversion of amino acids. In bacteria, D-amino acids have canonical structural roles (e.g., in peptidoglycan) and noncanonical roles as secreted metabolites impacting physiology and microbial interactions. Reviews note that many bacteria release D-amino acids into their environment at millimolar concentrations, produced by racemases/epimerases/aminotransferases, and that D-amino acids can affect adhesion and biofilm dynamics. (caldwell2023promisingapplicationsof pages 6-7)

2.2 Broad-spectrum racemases (Bsr/BSAR) vs alanine-specific racemases

In P. putida KT2440, Alr/PP_3722 is experimentally supported as a broad-substrate racemase with strongest activity on basic amino acids (notably Lys; also Arg), whereas DadX/PP_5269 is alanine-specific (catabolic alanine racemase). This distinction is essential for functional annotation because the symbol alr is often used for alanine racemase in many organisms but in KT2440 PP_3722 functions primarily as a broader racemase. (radkov2013aminoacidracemization pages 1-2, radkov2013aminoacidracemization pages 4-5, radkov2013aminoacidracemization pages 7-8)

2.3 Mechanistic context (PLP-dependent alanine-racemase family)

Although PP_3722 itself was not structurally solved in the retrieved corpus, its classification within the alanine racemase family implies a PLP-dependent mechanism. Recent high-resolution, room-temperature serial femtosecond crystallography of Bacillus subtilis alanine racemase captured multiple binding/reaction states and supports a mechanistic model in which catalytic bases and substrate positioning enable racemization; the study reports an apo structure at 2.3 Å resolution from 68,582 indexed images, with reaction-state datasets collected using ~200 mM L-Ala after mixing. These results provide updated mechanistic interpretation applicable to alanine-racemase-family enzymes such as PP_3722. (kim2024exploringthereaction pages 2-3, kim2024exploringthereaction pages 4-6, kim2024exploringthereaction pages 10-11)

3. Core function of P. putida KT2440 Alr (PP_3722)

3.1 Enzymatic reaction and substrate specificity (direct biochemical evidence)

Radkov & Moe (2013; Journal of Bacteriology, Nov 2013; https://doi.org/10.1128/JB.00761-13) report that Alr has the broadest substrate specificity among recovered KT2440 racemases, with measurable racemase activity on 9 of 19 chiral amino acids, and that its highest catalytic efficiency is on lysine. (radkov2013aminoacidracemization pages 1-2, radkov2013aminoacidracemization pages 4-5)

Quantitative kinetics (Table 2; 2013)

Key kinetic parameters (selected substrates) for Alr/PP_3722 include:
- Lysine (D→L): Km 0.36 ± 0.06 mM, kcat 274.5 ± 6.7 s⁻¹, kcat/Km ~7.63 × 10^5 M⁻¹·s⁻¹.
- Lysine (L→D): Km 8.96 ± 1.52 mM, kcat 1681.7 ± 161.7 s⁻¹, kcat/Km ~1.88 × 10^5 M⁻¹·s⁻¹.
- Alanine (D→L): Km 15.71 ± 2.79 mM, kcat 8.83 s⁻¹, kcat/Km ~5.62 × 10^2 M⁻¹·s⁻¹.
- Alanine (L→D): Km 12.62 ± 0.74 mM, kcat 7.33 s⁻¹, kcat/Km ~5.81 × 10^2 M⁻¹·s⁻¹.
Thus, Alr’s catalytic efficiency on lysine is approximately three orders of magnitude higher than on alanine. (radkov2013aminoacidracemization pages 4-5)

In contrast, DadX/PP_5269 shows substantially higher catalytic efficiency than Alr on alanine, supporting that DadX is the principal alanine racemase function in KT2440:
- DadX on alanine (D→L): kcat/Km ~4.85 × 10^3 M⁻¹·s⁻¹.
- DadX on alanine (L→D): kcat/Km ~3.74 × 10^3 M⁻¹·s⁻¹.
(radkov2013aminoacidracemization pages 4-5, radkov2013aminoacidracemization pages 7-8)

3.2 Subcellular localization (where the enzyme functions)

Radkov & Moe (2018; Frontiers in Microbiology, Jun 2018; https://doi.org/10.3389/fmicb.2018.01343) experimentally localized Alr activity predominantly to the periplasmic fraction, consistent with a predicted targeting sequence. In stationary phase, Alr specific activity was 134.5 ± 29.8 nmol·min⁻¹·mg⁻¹ in the periplasmic fraction versus 209.7 ± 8.2 nmol·min⁻¹·mg⁻¹ in the total fraction (periplasmic ~64% of total). Marker enzymes supported fraction integrity: alkaline phosphatase retained 72–77% of total periplasmic activity, while cytosolic G6PDH contamination in the periplasmic fraction was only 13.3–16.7%. (radkov2018abroadspectrum pages 3-4)

4. Physiological role in P. putida KT2440 (pathways, processes, and evidence)

4.1 Primary biological role: lysine and arginine catabolism

Multiple lines of evidence indicate PP_3722’s primary in vivo role is amino-acid catabolism, especially L-lysine and L-arginine, via racemization to D-forms.

In vivo metabolite evidence (D-Lys accumulation)

In LB medium, wild-type KT2440 accumulated D-lysine in the extracellular milieu: <100 µM by 4 h and approximately ~1 mM by 8 h. A markerless Δalr mutant produced no detectable D-Lys, while showing increased accumulation of extracellular L-Lys (reported as rising from ~1.5 mM to ~2.5 mM by 10 h), supporting that Alr converts L-Lys to D-Lys in vivo and increases overall lysine consumption. (radkov2018abroadspectrum pages 4-6, radkov2018abroadspectrum pages 3-4)

Growth phenotypes (catabolism as sole C/N)

The Δalr strain showed a diminished capacity to grow when L-Lys or L-Arg was provided as the sole carbon and nitrogen source, consistent with Alr being required to efficiently catabolize these L-amino acids (via conversion to their D-enantiomers). (radkov2018abroadspectrum pages 8-9, radkov2018abroadspectrum pages 6-7)

Pathway hypothesis (compartment-aware model)

Radkov & Moe propose that L-Lys is exported/accessible in the periplasm, racemized by periplasmic Alr to D-Lys, and then further catabolized intracellularly via pathways including the L-pipecolate route; they also conclude that Alr supports L-Arg catabolism, although the downstream D-Arg catabolic route in KT2440 remained unclear at the time. (radkov2018abroadspectrum pages 8-9, radkov2018abroadspectrum pages 1-2)

4.2 Transcriptomics: regulation is focused on amino-acid metabolism (not broad signaling)

RNA-seq comparing wild type vs Δalr indicated that differential expression was limited primarily to amino-acid metabolism genes, consistent with a metabolic (catabolic) function rather than broad signaling effects from secreted D-amino acids. Among upregulated genes in wild type under D-Lys-producing conditions were D-Lys catabolism-associated genes (e.g., PP3596, PP3591, PP5257/PP5258) and a periplasmic amino-acid transporter PP3593 reported with fold-change 27; PP_3722 itself was among the highly differentially expressed genes (reported ~100-fold RPKM difference among overexpressed genes). (radkov2018abroadspectrum pages 4-6, radkov2018abroadspectrum pages 2-3)

4.3 Peptidoglycan: no detectable structural incorporation under tested conditions

Despite being a racemase-family enzyme, PP_3722 does not appear to be a major contributor to peptidoglycan remodeling under the tested conditions in KT2440. Stationary-phase peptidoglycan composition was reported as not significantly different between wild type and Δalr (p > 0.05), and authors state Alr products were not incorporated into peptidoglycan under these conditions. (radkov2018abroadspectrum pages 4-6, radkov2018abroadspectrum pages 8-9)

4.4 Fitness/functional genomics evidence (supporting but secondary)

A BarSeq/RB-TnSeq-derived phenotype summary reports a modest fitness defect for PP_3722 of approximately −1.0 in an arginine-related condition, consistent with a conditional contribution to arginine utilization; it also supports that DadX is more central for alanine metabolism than Alr. (schmidt2023exploitingthepotential pages 73-76)

5. Recent developments (2023–2024 prioritized) relevant to PP_3722 functional interpretation

5.1 Broad-spectrum racemase family plasticity and antibiotic resistance (2024)

Coppinger et al. (2024; Journal of Bacteriology, Mar 2024; https://doi.org/10.1128/jb.00333-23) describe BsrF, a member of the periplasmic broad-spectrum racemase family, and show that changing localization by deleting a secretion signal (ΔSS-BsrF) can rescue D-Glu and D-Ala auxotrophies and increase D-cycloserine resistance. This provides a contemporary family-level insight: periplasmic broad-spectrum racemases can, under certain genetic/localization contexts, substitute for canonical cell-wall racemases and influence antibiotic susceptibility—even if PP_3722 in KT2440 is primarily catabolic under normal conditions. Quantitative observations include unsuccessful selection of suppressors on unsupplemented media despite plating >10^11 cells and reported amplification frequency/instability in suppressor strains. (coppinger2024aprototrophicsuppressor pages 1-2, coppinger2024aprototrophicsuppressor pages 2-5)

5.2 Updated mechanistic structural insight for alanine racemases (2024)

Kim et al. (2024; Scientific Reports, Dec 2024; https://doi.org/10.1038/s41598-024-83045-8) used room-temperature XFEL/SFX to capture heterogenous reaction states of alanine racemase and provide a refined view of PLP/substrate dynamics and potential proton-transfer routes. Although not PP_3722-specific, these data strengthen confidence in mechanistic inference for alanine-racemase-family enzymes used in annotation and inhibitor design. (kim2024exploringthereaction pages 2-3, kim2024exploringthereaction pages 4-6, kim2024exploringthereaction pages 10-11)

6. Current applications and real-world implementations linked to racemases / D-amino acids

6.1 Infection control and biofilms (2023)

A 2023 orthopedic infection review highlights D-amino acids as candidate tools for biofilm inhibition and dispersal, with relevance to periprosthetic joint infection (PJI). It notes that bacterial D-amino-acid release can occur at millimolar concentrations, and that D-amino acids can modulate peptidoglycan metabolism and biofilm dynamics—mechanistic context for interventions that manipulate D-amino acids or their biosynthetic enzymes. It also provides a clinically relevant statistic: spore-forming organisms account for ~3–8% of implant infections, and D-Ala can function as an anti-germinant via alanine racemase-linked mechanisms. (caldwell2023promisingapplicationsof pages 6-7)

6.2 Industrial D-amino-acid production and enzyme engineering (2024)

A 2024 review summarizes industrial motivations and reports quantitative examples of high-titer D-amino-acid production using engineered enzymes/cascades (including racemases as components or targets for engineering): e.g., ~44 g/L D-glutamate from engineered E. coli in a multi-enzyme system; production systems achieving 98% molar yield (48 h) and >99% ee for various D-amino acids; and examples of racemase engineering altering substrate scope and titers (including a reported 1.8 g/L D-tryptophan using a mutated Pseudomonas putida broad-specificity racemase). These data support that broad-specificity racemases like PP_3722 are relevant scaffolds for biocatalysis, even if KT2440’s native role is catabolic. (joshua2024challengesandsolutions pages 2-4, joshua2024challengesandsolutions pages 11-12)

7. Expert synthesis and functional annotation summary

Most supported annotation: PP_3722 encodes a periplasm-localized broad-spectrum amino-acid racemase with a strong kinetic preference for lysine and physiologically important activity in Lys/Arg catabolism; it is not the primary alanine racemase for peptidoglycan D-Ala supply in KT2440 (a role better supported for DadX/PP_5269). (radkov2013aminoacidracemization pages 4-5, radkov2018abroadspectrum pages 3-4, radkov2018abroadspectrum pages 8-9)

Cellular site of action: Periplasm, supported by fractionation and activity measurements with compartment markers. (radkov2018abroadspectrum pages 3-4)

Pathway context: Best-supported involvement is in amino-acid catabolism (L-Lys → D-Lys → downstream catabolism, likely involving the pipecolate route; Arg involvement supported but downstream D-Arg steps uncertain). (radkov2018abroadspectrum pages 8-9, radkov2018abroadspectrum pages 1-2)

Key statistics/data points (KT2440-specific):
- Lysine racemization kinetics: kcat/Km ~7.63×10^5 (D→L) and 1.88×10^5 (L→D) M⁻¹·s⁻¹; alanine kcat/Km ~5.6×10^2 M⁻¹·s⁻¹. (radkov2013aminoacidracemization pages 4-5)
- Periplasmic specific activity: 134.5 ± 29.8 nmol·min⁻¹·mg⁻¹ (periplasm) vs 209.7 ± 8.2 nmol·min⁻¹·mg⁻¹ (total). (radkov2018abroadspectrum pages 3-4)
- In vivo extracellular D-Lys: <100 µM at 4 h, ~1 mM at 8 h in wild type; absent in Δalr. (radkov2018abroadspectrum pages 4-6)
- Peptidoglycan composition unchanged in Δalr (stationary phase; p > 0.05). (radkov2018abroadspectrum pages 4-6)

Recent alanine racemase structural work supports the canonical two-base mechanism involving PLP and Lys/Tyr catalytic residues, with substrate carboxylate-assisted proton transfer proposed in reaction intermediates; this is mechanistic context for Alr/BSAR family members rather than a PP_3722-specific structure (kim2024exploringthereaction pages 7-9, kim2024exploringthereaction pages 9-10, kim2024exploringthereaction pages 10-11)
Substrate specificity In vitro, Alr showed measurable activity on 9 of 19 chiral amino acids and was described as the broadest-specificity racemase recovered in KT2440. It strongly prefers lysine, and the 2018 study states it preferentially interconverts L-/D-Lys and L-/D-Arg in vivo/physiology (radkov2013aminoacidracemization pages 1-2, radkov2013aminoacidracemization pages 4-5, radkov2018abroadspectrum pages 8-9)
Key kinetic parameters Alr on lysine: D→L Km 0.36 ± 0.06 mM, kcat 274.5 ± 6.7 s⁻¹, kcat/Km ~7.63 × 10^5 M⁻¹s⁻¹; L→D Km 8.96 ± 1.52 mM, kcat 1681.7 ± 161.7 s⁻¹, kcat/Km ~1.88 × 10^5 M⁻¹s⁻¹. Alr on alanine: D→L Km 15.71 ± 2.79 mM, kcat 8.83 s⁻¹, kcat/Km ~5.62 × 10^2; L→D Km 12.62 ± 0.74 mM, kcat 7.33 s⁻¹, kcat/Km ~5.81 × 10^2. Thus lysine catalytic efficiency is ~3 orders of magnitude higher than alanine. By comparison, DadX (PP_5269) is alanine-specialized: D→L alanine Km 7.73 ± 0.63 mM, kcat 37.47 ± 2.67 s⁻¹, kcat/Km ~4.85 × 10^3; L→D alanine Km 24.57 ± 2.64 mM, kcat 92.0 ± 11.6 s⁻¹, kcat/Km ~3.74 × 10^3 (radkov2013aminoacidracemization pages 4-5, radkov2013aminoacidracemization pages 7-8, radkov2013aminoacidracemization pages 1-2)
Subcellular localization Alr activity was experimentally enriched in the periplasmic fraction, consistent with a predicted secretion/targeting signal. In stationary phase, specific activity was 134.5 ± 29.8 nmol min⁻¹ mg⁻¹ in the periplasmic fraction versus 209.7 ± 8.2 nmol min⁻¹ mg⁻¹ total (~64% of total). Fraction quality controls showed periplasmic alkaline phosphatase recovery 72–77% of total and cytoplasmic G6PDH contamination only 13.3–16.7%, supporting genuine periplasmic localization, though minor contamination could not be fully excluded (radkov2018abroadspectrum pages 3-4, radkov2018abroadspectrum pages 4-6)
Physiological role The best-supported organism-specific function is basic amino-acid catabolism, especially L-Lys and L-Arg utilization via racemization to their D-forms. The authors propose L-Lys → D-Lys feeds the L-pipecolate pathway, linking extracellular/periplasmic racemization to intracellular catabolism; D-Arg catabolism is implicated but the downstream route in KT2440 remains unresolved (radkov2018abroadspectrum pages 8-9, radkov2018abroadspectrum pages 6-7, radkov2018abroadspectrum pages 1-2)
Mutant / fitness phenotypes A markerless Δalr mutant lost detectable Alr activity and failed to accumulate detectable D-Lys in LB. It showed markedly reduced capacity to use L-Lys and L-Arg as sole carbon and nitrogen sources, whereas effects on many other amino acids were limited. In RB-TnSeq/BarSeq, PP_3722 had a modest quantitative fitness defect of about -1.0 in arginine-related conditions, consistent with a conditional but real contribution to Arg metabolism; DadX appeared primary for alanine metabolism (radkov2018abroadspectrum pages 2-3, radkov2018abroadspectrum pages 6-7, schmidt2023exploitingthepotential pages 73-76)
In vivo metabolite evidence In LB, wild type accumulated <100 µM D-Lys at 4 h and about ~1 mM D-Lys at 8 h; the Δalr strain produced no detectable D-Lys. Meanwhile, Δalr supernatants accumulated more L-Lys (about ~1.5 mM to ~2.5 mM by 10 h), supporting in vivo conversion of secreted/periplasmic L-Lys into D-Lys by Alr and increased overall lysine consumption in the wild type (radkov2018abroadspectrum pages 4-6, radkov2018abroadspectrum pages 3-4)
Regulation / omics RNA-seq comparing wild type and Δalr found differential expression largely restricted to amino-acid metabolism genes, not a broad signaling regulon. In wild type under D-Lys-producing conditions, genes linked to D-Lys catabolism were upregulated, including PP3596 (D-amino-acid dehydrogenase), PP3591 (Pip2C/Pyr2C reductase), downstream genes PP5257/PP5258, and a periplasmic amino-acid transporter PP3593 with fold-change 27; among overexpressed genes, PP3722 itself differed by about ~100-fold RPKM (radkov2018abroadspectrum pages 4-6, radkov2018abroadspectrum pages 2-3)
Peptidoglycan impact Despite being an amino-acid racemase, Alr products were not detectably incorporated into peptidoglycan under the tested conditions. Stationary-phase peptidoglycan composition did not differ significantly between wild type and Δalr (p > 0.05), arguing against a major structural cell-wall role for PP_3722 in KT2440 during the examined growth state (radkov2018abroadspectrum pages 4-6, radkov2018abroadspectrum pages 8-9, radkov2018abroadspectrum pages 1-2)
Related enzymes / ortholog context P. putida carries at least two distinct alanine-racemase-family enzymes with different niches: Alr/PP_3722 is the broad-spectrum, Lys/Arg-biased, periplasm-associated racemase, whereas DadX/PP_5269 is the alanine-specialized racemase. Recent work in Vibrio fischeri identified BsrF, a member of the periplasmic broad-spectrum racemase family, supporting the broader existence of this family in Gram-negative bacteria and showing that altered localization can redirect such enzymes toward peptidoglycan precursor supply and D-cycloserine resistance (radkov2013aminoacidracemization pages 4-5, radkov2013aminoacidracemization pages 7-8, coppinger2024aprototrophicsuppressor pages 1-2, coppinger2024aprototrophicsuppressor pages 8-10, coppinger2024aprototrophicsuppressor pages 2-5)
Applications / biotech relevance Although PP_3722 itself has not been widely deployed industrially, broad-spectrum racemases are relevant for D-amino-acid bioproduction, biofilm control, and microbial competition. Reviews highlight D-amino-acid uses in antibiotics, sweeteners, and pharmaceutical intermediates; engineered systems reached 44 g/L D-glutamate, 19.76 g/L D-HPG in 10 h (and 42.69 g/L at 3-L scale), and a broad-specificity Pseudomonas putida racemase variant reportedly produced 1.8 g/L D-tryptophan. D-amino acids also enhance biocide efficacy against biofilms and are being explored for orthopedic infection control (joshua2024challengesandsolutions pages 1-2, joshua2024challengesandsolutions pages 2-4, joshua2024challengesandsolutions pages 11-12, joshua2024challengesandsolutions pages 10-11, caldwell2023promisingapplicationsof pages 6-7, caldwell2023promisingapplicationsof pages 3-6)
Key recent (2023–2024) developments Recent studies did not substantially revise the core PP_3722 annotation, but they strengthened family-level context. In 2024, Bsr-family work in V. fischeri showed a periplasmic broad-spectrum racemase can, when mislocalized to the cytoplasm, rescue D-Glu and D-Ala auxotrophy and increase D-cycloserine resistance, illustrating latent functional plasticity in Bsr enzymes. Also in 2024, serial femtosecond crystallography captured alanine racemase reaction states at 2.3 Å from 68,582 indexed images, refining understanding of PLP-mediated racemization dynamics applicable to the broader enzyme family (coppinger2024aprototrophicsuppressor pages 1-2, coppinger2024aprototrophicsuppressor pages 5-8, coppinger2024aprototrophicsuppressor pages 8-10, kim2024exploringthereaction pages 2-3, kim2024exploringthereaction pages 7-9, kim2024exploringthereaction pages 9-10)
