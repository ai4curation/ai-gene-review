Gene Description

Penicillin-binding protein 1A (PBP1A) is a bifunctional class A high-molecular-mass penicillin-binding protein that catalyzes both glycosyltransferase (GT) and D,D-transpeptidase (TP) activities essential for peptidoglycan biosynthesis. The N-terminal GT domain (EC 2.4.99.28) polymerizes glycan strands from lipid II precursors, while the C-terminal TP domain (EC 3.4.16.4) cross-links peptide stems to form the mature peptidoglycan sacculus. The enzyme is inner membrane-anchored with catalytic domains operating in the periplasmic space. In Gram-negative bacteria, PBP1A activity is regulated by outer membrane lipoprotein activators (LpoA/LpoP family). The TP domain is the target of beta-lactam antibiotics while the GT domain is inhibited by moenomycin. PBP1A functions redundantly with PBP1B (mrcB) for core peptidoglycan synthesis but shows stress-specific requirements.
