# lapG notes

- Q88RG6 / PP_0164 is a 230 aa LapG-family cysteine protease candidate in *Pseudomonas putida* KT2440, with `CystProtLapG` and `Peptidase_C93` family assignments in UniProt. [file:PSEPK/lapG/lapG-uniprot.txt, "DR   NCBIfam; NF045674; CystProtLapG; 1."] [file:PSEPK/lapG/lapG-uniprot.txt, "DR   Pfam; PF06035; Peptidase_C93; 1."]
- In KT2440, nutrient starvation relieves LapD inhibition of LapG, leading to LapA proteolysis and release of biofilm cells. [PMID:29273811 The stringent response promotes biofilm dispersal in Pseudomonas putida, "releasing inhibition of protease LapG by the c-di-GMP effector protein LapD"] [PMID:29273811 The stringent response promotes biofilm dispersal in Pseudomonas putida, "proteolysis of the adhesin LapA and the subsequent release of biofilm cells"]
- DibA modulates LapA levels through LapD and thereby inhibits biofilm formation in P. putida KT2440. This places LapG in a broader LapD-centered c-di-GMP control circuit for adhesin retention. [PMID:37927230 The phosphodiesterase DibA interacts with the c-di-GMP receptor LapD and specifically regulates biofilm in Pseudomonas putida, "modulates the cell surface adhesin LapA level in a c-di-GMP receptor LapD-dependent manner"] [PMID:37927230 The phosphodiesterase DibA interacts with the c-di-GMP receptor LapD and specifically regulates biofilm in Pseudomonas putida, "thereby inhibiting biofilm formation"]
