CC   -!- FUNCTION: Part of the tripartite efflux system PvdRT-OpmQ required for
CC       the secretion into the extracellular milieu of the siderophore
CC       pyoverdine (PVD), which is involved in iron acquisition
CC       (PubMed:30346656, PubMed:36807028). This subunit binds PVD and drives
CC       its secretion by hydrolyzing ATP (PubMed:36807028). The system is
CC       responsible for export of newly synthesized PVD after the final steps
CC       of biosynthesis have taken place in the periplasm (By similarity). It
CC       is also responsible for recycling of PVD after internalization of
CC       ferri-PVD into the periplasm by the outer-membrane receptor FpvA and
CC       release of iron from PVD, thus making PVD available for new cycles of
CC       iron uptake (By similarity). Contributes to resistance against
CC       ampicillin (PubMed:30346656). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9I191,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:30346656, ECO:0000269|PubMed:36807028}.
CC   -!- ACTIVITY REGULATION: Has a basal ATPase activity that is stimulated by
CC       PvdR (PubMed:36807028). In vitro, interaction with PVD influences the
CC       affinity of PvdT to PvdR (PubMed:36807028).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:36807028}.

CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Part of the tripartite efflux system PvdRT-OpmQ, which is
CC       composed of an inner membrane component with both ATPase and permease
CC       domains, PvdT, a periplasmic membrane fusion protein, PvdR, and an
CC       outer membrane component, OpmQ. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:36807028,
CC       ECO:0000305|PubMed:30346656}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell inner membrane
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:36807028}; Multi-pass membrane protein
CC       {ECO:0000255}.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: Expression is stimulated by iron limitation.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:30346656}.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: Deletion of pvdRT-opmQ leads to reduced amounts
CC       of PVD in the medium and decreased growth under iron limitation
CC       (PubMed:30346656). Deletion of both PvdRT-OpmQ and MdtABC-OpmB systems
CC       strongly affects growth under iron limitation as well as PVD secretion
CC       (PubMed:30346656). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:30346656}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the ABC transporter superfamily. Macrolide
CC       exporter (TC 3.A.1.122) family. {ECO:0000305}.
