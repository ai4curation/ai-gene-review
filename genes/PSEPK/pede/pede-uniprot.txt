CC       ethanol, 1-butanol, and 2-phenylethanol in the absence of La(3+), but

CC   -!- MISCELLANEOUS: The soil-dwelling organism P.putida KT2440 produces 2
CC       PQQ-ADHs, namely, PedE, which is Ca(2+)-dependent, and PedH, which is
CC       lanthanide-dependent. These enzymes are crucial for efficient bacterial
CC       growth with a variety of volatile alcohols. The functional redundancy
CC       of the PQQ-ADHs and inverse transcriptional regulation of PedE and PedH
CC       represent an adaptive strategy of P.putida KT2440 to optimize growth
CC       with volatile alcohols and aldehyde substrates in response to the
CC       availability of different lanthanides in the natural environment of the
CC       bacterium. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:28655819}.
