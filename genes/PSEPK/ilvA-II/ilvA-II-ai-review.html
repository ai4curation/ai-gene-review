Gene Description

IlvA-II is a full-length, PLP-dependent biosynthetic threonine deaminase that converts L-threonine to 2-oxobutanoate, the entry metabolite for L-isoleucine biosynthesis. It contains the catalytic serine/threonine-dehydratase domain and two C-terminal ACT-like regulatory domains. KT2440 also encodes the closely related IlvA-I, and their relative physiological contributions have not been established.
