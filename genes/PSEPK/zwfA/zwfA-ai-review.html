Gene Description

zwfA encodes the major glucose-6-phosphate 1-dehydrogenase isozyme G6PDH-A of Pseudomonas putida KT2440. The enzyme oxidizes D-glucose 6-phosphate to 6-phosphogluconolactone at the oxidative pentose-phosphate/Entner-Doudoroff interface, contributing to 6-phosphogluconate supply and redox cofactor balance during sugar catabolism. It is a predicted soluble cytosolic G6PDH-family enzyme and is part of the zwfA-pgl-eda upper glucose-catabolism operon; isozyme-level evidence indicates that zwfA and zwfB carry most G6PDH activity in KT2440, whereas the PP_5351 zwfC paralog is a minor, low-expression isozyme.

  • UniProt Q88P31 is the Pseudomonas putida KT2440 zwfA gene product at ordered locus PP_1022, annotated as glucose-6-phosphate 1-dehydrogenase / G6PD (EC 1.1.1.49). [file:PSEPK/zwfA/zwfA-uniprot.txt, "RecName: Full=Glucose-6-phosphate 1-dehydrogenase"; "GN Name=zwfA"; "OrderedLocusNames=PP_1022"]
  • The OpenScientist gene-level research attempt on 2026-07-14 failed before writing an output file (UnixHTTPConnectionPool ... Read timed out). Per project rules, no zwfA-deep-research-openscientist.md file was created manually.
  • The key KT2440 isozyme paper supports treating zwfA as one of the dominant physiological G6PDH isozymes, distinct from the already-reviewed minor zwf/PP_5351 paralog. [PMID:33727391 Cofactor Specificity of Glucose-6-Phosphate Dehydrogenase Isozymes in Pseudomonas putida Reveals a General Principle Underlying Glycolytic Strategies in Bacteria., "We show that the three G6PDHs of strain KT2440 have different cofactor specificities and that the isoforms encoded by zwfA and zwfB carry most of the activity"]
  • Pseudomonas glucose-catabolism reviews place the Zwf/Pgl step upstream of 6-phosphogluconate, which then feeds Entner-Doudoroff metabolism. This supports including zwfA as a hole-fill gene for the ED/gluconeogenesis module even though KEGG assigns the locus to a neighboring pentose-phosphate bucket. [PMID:29607620 Regulation of carbohydrate degradation pathways in Pseudomonas involves a versatile set of transcriptional regulators., "Next, the combined action of glucose 6-phosphate dehydrogenase (Zwf) and 6-phosphogluconolactonase (Pgl) convert G6P into 6-phosphogluconate (6PG)"]
  • Curation interpretation: retain GO:0004345 and the oxidative pentose-phosphate process annotations as core; retain GO:0050661 because cofactor specificity is biologically informative for KT2440 Zwf paralogs; mark the generic oxidoreductase parent term as over-annotated.
