1. Summary (Answer to the Research Question)

NadE (Q88DF6) is the enzyme that catalyzes the final, committed step of NAD⁺ biosynthesis. It performs the ATP-dependent amidation of nicotinic acid adenine dinucleotide (NaAD, "deamido-NAD⁺") to NAD⁺, using ammonia (NH₄⁺) as the nitrogen donor:

deamido-NAD⁺ + NH₄⁺ + ATP → NAD⁺ + AMP + diphosphate (PPi) + H⁺ (EC 6.3.1.5)

It is a cytoplasmic homodimer that acts through a two-step mechanism via a covalent NaAD-adenylate intermediate, and it is the single terminal enzyme on which both the de novo and salvage NAD⁺ pathways converge — making it central to redox metabolism and typically essential.

2. Primary Function: Reaction Catalyzed and Substrate Specificity

Reaction (from UniProt catalytic-activity annotation): deamido-NAD⁺ + NH₄⁺ + ATP = AMP + diphosphate + NAD⁺ + H⁺ (EC 6.3.1.5).

Pathway position: "Cofactor biosynthesis; NAD⁺ biosynthesis; NAD⁺ from deamido-NAD⁺ (ammonia route): step 1/1" — i.e., NadE is the terminal step that installs the amide nitrogen that chemically distinguishes NAD⁺ from its precursor NaAD.

The final step of NAD⁺ biosynthesis "includes an amide transfer to nicotinic acid adenine dinucleotide (NaAD) catalyzed by NAD⁺ synthetase" (Symersky et al., 2002, 12077433). The enzyme is ubiquitous and "transform[s] deamido-NAD⁺ into NAD⁺ by a two-step reaction" (Rizzi et al., 1998, 9753692).

Substrate specificity / nitrogen-donor selectivity. Q88DF6 is a short-form, NH₃-dependent NAD synthetase: at 275 aa it consists of only the synthetase (Rossmann-like α/β/α) catalytic domain and lacks the N-terminal glutaminase (glutamine amidotransferase) domain found in glutamine-dependent enzymes (e.g., yeast Qns1, ~700 aa). Consequently it uses free ammonium from the cellular pool as the amide donor rather than generating ammonia in situ from glutamine. This contrasts with the two-domain glutamine-dependent enzyme, which "utilizes a glutamine aminotransferase domain to supply ammonia for amidation of nicotinic acid adenine dinucleotide (NaAD⁺) to NAD⁺" (Wojcik et al., 2006, 16954203). (Note: many bacterial "NH₃-dependent" NadE enzymes retain some capacity to use glutamine in vitro, but the diagnostic architectural feature is the absence of a dedicated glutaminase domain; the physiological/annotated donor for Q88DF6 is ammonia.)

Cofactor requirements: ATP (hydrolyzed to AMP + PPi, characteristic of the N-type ATP pyrophosphatase superfamily) and divalent Mg²⁺ (a binuclear Mg²⁺ cluster at the ATP site).

3. Catalytic Mechanism (Structural/Mechanistic Evidence)

NadE is a member of the N-type ATP pyrophosphatase / PP-loop amidotransferase superfamily. Catalysis proceeds in two chemical steps:

  1. Adenylation: the nicotinate carboxylate of NaAD attacks the α-phosphate of ATP to form a reactive NaAD-adenylate (NAD-adenylate) intermediate, releasing pyrophosphate; this step is assisted by a binuclear Mg²⁺ (trimetallic) constellation rather than by protein side-chain catalysis.
  2. Amidation: ammonia performs a nucleophilic attack on the adenylate intermediate, displacing AMP and yielding NAD⁺.

The mechanism is directly supported by high-resolution B. subtilis crystal structures that trapped the intermediate: the enzyme was solved "in complex with the trapped reaction intermediate NAD-adenylate" (Rizzi et al., 1998, 9753692), and an atomic-resolution (1.0 Å) structure showed that "both binding sites are occupied by the NAD-adenylate intermediate, pyrophosphate and two magnesium ions," with "a mechanism … proposed for the second catalytic step, which includes a nucleophilic attack by the ammonia molecule on the intermediate" (Symersky et al., 2002, 12077433).

Active-site / ligand-binding residues in Q88DF6 (UniProt features): an ATP/Mg-binding P-loop (SGGxDS motif, ⁵⁰GISGGVDS⁵⁷) and additional substrate-contacting residues at positions 56, 147, 167, 172, 180, 187, 196, 218, and 267–268. The SGGxD signature is the hallmark of the ATP-pyrophosphatase family and directly rationalizes the AMP + PPi product stoichiometry.

5. Pathway Context and Biological Role

NAD⁺ biosynthesis in bacteria funnels multiple inputs to a common intermediate, nicotinic acid mononucleotide (NaMN), which is adenylylated by NadD (NaMN adenylyltransferase) to NaAD, and finally amidated by NadE to NAD⁺:

 de novo:  L-aspartate → (NadB/NadA/NadC) → NaMN
 salvage:  nicotinamide/nicotinic acid/NR → ... → NaMN
                                   │
                                   ▼
                     NaMN --NadD--> NaAD --NadE--> NAD⁺

Because both the de novo (aspartate) route and the salvage routes converge on NaAD, NadE is the single terminal enzyme producing NAD⁺ in the cell; its activity cannot be bypassed by nicotinamide/nicotinic acid salvage. NAD⁺ (with NADH, and NADP(H) after phosphorylation by NAD kinase) is the electron carrier for respiration, redox balancing, and hundreds of biosynthetic and catabolic reactions.

Essentiality and target validation (inference from close relatives). In M. tuberculosis, NadD and NadE "were selected as promising candidate drug targets," and induced degradation of either enzyme "showed a strong bactericidal effect which coincided with anticipated changes in relative levels of NaMN and NaAD intermediates (substrates of NadD and NadE, respectively) and ultimate depletion of the NAD(H) pool," producing a metabolic catastrophe (Rodionova et al., 2014, 24549842). More recent work confirms NadE (NAD synthesis) as metabolically distinct from and essential alongside NAD kinase in defining NAD(P) essentiality (Sharma et al., 2023, 37350592). By strong homology and identical pathway position, NadE is expected to be essential in P. putida KT2440, where a robust NAD(H)/NADPH supply underpins the organism's noted redox-balancing systems (e.g., PntAB/SthA transhydrogenases, 42335895) and industrial biotransformation capacity. Consistent with this, genome-wide transposon (Tn-seq/RB-TnSeq) studies of KT2440 build saturating libraries that disrupt "nearly all (4,778) non-essential genes" (Eng et al., 2021, 33964456) — the terminal NAD-biosynthesis genes are expected to reside in the complementary essential-gene set that tolerates no insertions, although PP_4869 is not individually called out in those reports.
