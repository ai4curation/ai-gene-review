## Research report: Functional annotation of **galC** (UniProt **Q88JX9**, locus **PP_2514**) from *Pseudomonas putida* KT2440

### 0) Target verification and disambiguation (critical)
The target protein (UniProt Q88JX9; gene **galC**; locus PP_2514) is described by UniProt as **4-carboxy-4-hydroxy-2-oxoadipate aldolase** (CHA aldolase; also called **HMG/CHA aldolase**; “gallate degradation protein C”), belonging to the **LigK/PcmE family** with an **RraA/RraA-like fold**. In the retrieved KT2440-focused literature, the name **GalC** is consistently used for the **HMG/CHA aldolase** step in the **gallate (gallic acid) 4,5-cleavage pathway**, downstream of GalA/GalD/GalB and yielding central metabolites. This matches the UniProt description, supporting that the “galC” symbol here corresponds to the aromatic-catabolic enzyme (not the lactose/galactose “gal” genes found in many bacteria). (mazurkewich2016…the4carboxy2hydroxymuconate pages 23-28, mazurkewich2016…the4carboxy2hydroxymuconate pages 110-116)

**Limitation:** In the retrieved excerpts, the explicit cross-reference **galC ↔ PP_2514 ↔ UniProt Q88JX9** and the explicit printing of **EC 4.1.3.17** were **not observed**; therefore, the accession/locus/EC mapping is treated as consistent with the user-provided UniProt record but not independently re-demonstrated from the retrieved texts. (mazurkewich2016…the4carboxy2hydroxymuconate pages 110-116, mazurkewich2016…the4carboxy2hydroxymuconate pages 23-28)

### 1) Key concepts, definitions, and current understanding
#### 1.1 Pathway concept: “bio-funneling” of lignin-derived aromatics
Hardwood (syringyl-rich) lignin depolymerization yields a mixture of aromatic monomers (e.g., syringate/syringaldehyde/vanillate). Many bacteria “funnel” these diverse compounds into a smaller set of intermediates that enter core carbon metabolism. Gallate (gallic acid) and its ring-cleavage intermediates are part of one such funnel. (gu2024bacterialtransformationof pages 21-22, zhang2024tappingintothe pages 10-11)

#### 1.2 The gallate (gallic acid) 4,5-cleavage “Gal” pathway
A KT2440 gallate-utilization gene cluster is described as **galDCBRPAT**, encoding enzymatic steps for gallate catabolism plus uptake/regulation. In this model, the aromatic ring of gallate is opened by **GalA (gallate dioxygenase)**; the resulting meta-cleavage intermediate is processed through **GalD (OMA tautomerase)** and **GalB (CHM hydratase)** to generate **CHA**, which is then cleaved by **GalC (HMG/CHA aldolase)** into central metabolites. (mazurkewich2016…the4carboxy2hydroxymuconate pages 23-28)

#### 1.3 Definition of GalC activity (CHA aldolase)
“CHA aldolase” refers to the enzyme catalyzing retro-aldol cleavage of **4-carboxy-4-hydroxy-2-oxoadipate (CHA)** to produce **pyruvate** and **oxaloacetate**, i.e., direct entry points into central metabolism (TCA/anaplerosis). (mazurkewich2016…the4carboxy2hydroxymuconate pages 110-116, mazurkewich2016…the4carboxy2hydroxymuconate pages 39-44, mazurkewich2016…the4carboxy2hydroxymuconate pages 44-50)

### 2) Primary function of galC (enzyme reaction, substrates, specificity)
#### 2.1 Catalyzed reaction and products
Across the biochemical/pathway descriptions available in the retrieved texts, GalC is identified as the **HMG/CHA aldolase** responsible for converting CHA into the TCA-cycle metabolites **pyruvate and oxaloacetate**. (mazurkewich2016…the4carboxy2hydroxymuconate pages 110-116, mazurkewich2016…the4carboxy2hydroxymuconate pages 105-110, mazurkewich2016…the4carboxy2hydroxymuconate pages 23-28)

#### 2.2 Substrate scope and stereochemical preference
The same enzyme family is reported to accept **both CHA and HMG (4-hydroxy-4-methyl-2-oxoglutarate)** as substrates, motivating the common name “HMG/CHA aldolase.” Prior characterizations summarized in the retrieved evidence report stereochemical preferences for **(-)-CHA** and **(-)-HMG** and note that (-)-CHA is considered the physiological enantiomer (though the absolute configuration is described as not established in that summary). (mazurkewich2016…the4carboxy2hydroxymuconate pages 150-155, mazurkewich2016…the4carboxy2hydroxymuconate pages 160-164)

#### 2.3 Enzyme class and mechanism (LigK/PcmE; RraA-like)
Biochemical/structural synthesis in the retrieved evidence describes HMG/CHA aldolases as **class II, divalent metal-dependent pyruvate aldolases**, operating via an **enolate** mechanism (not a Schiff-base). A solved structure (discussed for *Pseudomonas putida* F1 homologs) shows an **αββα sandwich fold** that is **structurally similar to RraA family proteins**, consistent with the family/domain information provided in UniProt for Q88JX9. (mazurkewich2016…the4carboxy2hydroxymuconate pages 44-50, mazurkewich2016…the4carboxy2hydroxymuconate pages 39-44, mazurkewich2016…the4carboxy2hydroxymuconate pages 54-59)

#### 2.4 Cofactor dependence, inhibition, and mechanistic features
The enzyme is described as **divalent-metal dependent**; Mg2+ appears in structural discussion, while assays/reconstitution also used Co2+ and comparisons among homologs indicate variable metal preferences. (mazurkewich2016…the4carboxy2hydroxymuconate pages 34-39, mazurkewich2016…the4carboxy2hydroxymuconate pages 54-59, mazurkewich2016…the4carboxy2hydroxymuconate pages 50-54)

A notable feature summarized is that **inorganic phosphate (Pi)** can act as an **uncompetitive activator** of the aldolase reaction (increasing both KM and kcat in the cited characterization), though the physiological basis is described as unclear in that account. (mazurkewich2016…the4carboxy2hydroxymuconate pages 34-39)

Competitive inhibition by metabolites/analogs is reported in the retrieved biochemical synthesis, including inhibition by **oxalate** (a pyruvate-enolate analog) and by **2-oxoglutarate**/**2-oxoadipate** (reported Ki values in the mM range in the summarized work). (mazurkewich2016…the4carboxy2hydroxymuconate pages 39-44, mazurkewich2016…the4carboxy2hydroxymuconate pages 160-164)

#### 2.5 Secondary activity (oxaloacetate decarboxylase)
HMG/CHA aldolases are reported to show **secondary oxaloacetate decarboxylase activity**, rationalized by a shared pyruvate-enolate intermediate. This is important for interpretation of in vitro assays and potential side reactions during pathway engineering. (mazurkewich2016…the4carboxy2hydroxymuconate pages 39-44, mazurkewich2016…the4carboxy2hydroxymuconate pages 44-50)

### 3) Biological process and pathway role in *P. putida* KT2440
#### 3.1 Placement in gallate utilization and aromatic catabolism
In the KT2440 gallate pathway summary, GalC is the terminal carbon–carbon cleavage step that converts the ring-cleavage-derived intermediate (CHA) into **pyruvate + oxaloacetate**, thereby completing conversion of an aromatic compound into central metabolism. (mazurkewich2016…the4carboxy2hydroxymuconate pages 23-28)

#### 3.2 Relationship to syringate metabolism (source of gallate)
Syringate (a syringyl lignin-related aromatic) can be converted to **3-O-methylgallate (3-MGA)** and then **gallate** by **VanAB O-demethylation**; gallate can then enter the Gal pathway including GalC. This connects galC function to lignin-derived aromatic assimilation. (dumalo2020dioxygenasesinthe pages 32-39)

### 4) Subcellular localization / cellular context
The retrieved evidence describes the gal cluster as including dedicated uptake functions (outer membrane porin **GalP** and inner-membrane transporter **GalT**) and a transcriptional regulator (**GalR**), while GalC is described as a soluble metabolic enzyme acting on cytosolic intermediates to produce central metabolites. Thus, the most defensible annotation from these sources is that GalC functions **intracellularly (likely cytosolic)** as part of central aromatic catabolism. (mazurkewich2016…the4carboxy2hydroxymuconate pages 23-28, ookawa2025catabolicsystemof pages 10-12)

**Limitation:** none of the retrieved excerpts explicitly states “cytosolic” for GalC; the localization is inferred from pathway organization and the enzyme’s soluble aldolase role. (mazurkewich2016…the4carboxy2hydroxymuconate pages 23-28)

### 5) Recent developments (prioritizing 2023–2024)
#### 5.1 2024: Increased emphasis on lignin valorization via aromatic funnels (context for galC)
A 2024 Green Chemistry review highlights the broader biotechnology push to convert lignin streams into higher-value chemicals and reports quantitative performance for **gallic acid production** routes (relevant because gallate is both a product target and an intermediate in syringyl-aromatic funnels that then require downstream catabolism such as GalC for assimilation). Reported examples include:

#### 5.2 2024: Engineering logic of 4,5-cleavage modules and CHA-processing
A 2024 review of bacterial lignin transformation summarizes syringate → 3MGA → gallate routing and downstream ring cleavage leading to intermediates including **CHA**, which is then processed by hydratase/aldolase steps (LigJ/LigK class) to **pyruvate and oxaloacetate**—the same biochemical endpoint attributed to GalB/GalC in the dedicated gallate pathway. ### 6) Current applications and real-world implementations
#### 6.1 Lignin-derived aromatic upgrading: PDC and funnel intermediates
While not a GalC-specific performance metric, syringyl-aromatic processing pipelines frequently aim to produce platform chemicals such as **2-pyrone-4,6-dicarboxylate (PDC)** or muconate. Downstream CHA-aldolase activity (GalC/LigK-like) is key when the design goal is **assimilation** rather than **accumulation**. (gu2024bacterialtransformationof pages 21-22, mazurkewich2016…the4carboxy2hydroxymuconate pages 23-28)

A comparative engineering study (2025; included as near-term implementation context) reports very high performance in PDC production when combining enzymes from *P. putida* KT2440 and *Sphingobium* SYK-6: co-expression of **vanAB from P. putida KT2440** with **ligABC from SYK-6** produced **PDC titer 99.9 g/L** and **productivity 1.69 g/L/h** from lignin monomers including syringate/vanillate. This illustrates industrially relevant deployment of *Pseudomonas*-derived aromatic-catabolic modules (upstream of CHA formation) in engineered systems. (tuomela2025conversionandupgradinga pages 1-2)

#### 6.2 Operational constraints relevant to pathway deployment
Practical considerations for syringate/gallate processing include formaldehyde generation during O-demethylation, redox balancing (NADH consumption), and gallate oxidation/precipitation behavior—factors that affect whether flux is pushed through assimilation (requiring GalC) or diverted to products. (tuomela2025conversionandupgradinga pages 15-17)

### 10) Final functional annotation statement (concise)
**galC (UniProt Q88JX9; PP_2514) in *Pseudomonas putida* KT2440 encodes a cytosolic class II, divalent metal-dependent HMG/CHA aldolase (CHA aldolase) that catalyzes retro-aldol cleavage of 4-carboxy-4-hydroxy-2-oxoadipate (CHA) to pyruvate and oxaloacetate, acting as the terminal carbon-splitting step of the gallate (gallic acid) 4,5-cleavage pathway (galDCBRPAT) downstream of GalA/GalD/GalB and linking lignin-derived aromatic funnels to central metabolism.** (mazurkewich2016…the4carboxy2hydroxymuconate pages 23-28, mazurkewich2016…the4carboxy2hydroxymuconate pages 110-116, mazurkewich2016…the4carboxy2hydroxymuconate pages 44-50)
