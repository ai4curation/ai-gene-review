Functional Annotation of acnA-II (PP_2336, UniProt Q88KF4) in Pseudomonas putida KT2440

Target: Gene acnA-II; OrderedLocusName PP_2336; UniProt Q88KF4 (TrEMBL, unreviewed; protein existence = inferred from homology). Organism: Pseudomonas putida (strain ATCC 47054 / DSM 6125 / NCIMB 11950 / KT2440). Report date: 2026-07-23.

1. Summary (answer to the research question)

PP_2336 encodes a cytoplasmic, [4Fe‑4S]‑cluster–dependent dehydratase of the aconitase superfamily. Although the automated gene symbol ("acnA‑II") and the generic UniProt label ("aconitate hydratase, EC 4.2.1.3") suggest an ordinary tricarboxylic‑acid (TCA)‑cycle aconitase, the ortholog assignment, protein‑family signature, and — most decisively — its position within the P. putida methylcitrate (prp) operon identify it as an AcnD‑type 2‑methylcitrate dehydratase (EC 4.2.1.117; KEGG K20455). Its specific, committed role is the dehydration of (2S,3S)‑2‑methylcitrate to 2‑methyl‑trans‑aconitate (and, by the same aconitase‑like half‑reaction, citrate → cis‑aconitate) in the 2‑methylcitrate cycle that catabolises propionate (and propionyl‑CoA from odd‑chain fatty acids / amino‑acid degradation) to pyruvate and succinate. It works obligately with the adjacent accessory isomerase PrpF (PP_2337), which converts the trans product to 2‑methyl‑cis‑aconitate for downstream hydration by the housekeeping aconitase AcnB (PP_2339).

All members of the aconitase superfamily are soluble cytoplasmic enzymes that carry a catalytic [4Fe‑4S] cluster in which three iron atoms are Cys‑ligated and the fourth (Fe‑a) is solvent‑exposed and coordinates the substrate hydroxyl/carboxylate during dehydration–rehydration [Lauble 1994, 8151704]. Bacterial aconitases are "distantly related monomeric Fe‑S proteins that contain different arrangements of four structural domains" [Jordan 1999, 10585860] — exactly the two‑lobe (large α/β/α + swivel) architecture annotated for PP_2336.

3. Primary function: the reaction catalysed and substrate specificity

PP_2336 catalyses the dehydration of 2‑methylcitrate to 2‑methyl‑trans‑aconitate (EC 4.2.1.117), and the analogous aconitase‑like dehydration of citrate to cis‑aconitate / hydration of cis‑aconitate. It does not carry out the second (isocitrate‑forming) half of the classical aconitase reaction.

The definitive biochemistry comes from the experimentally characterised AcnD orthologs (Shewanella oneidensis MR‑1 and Vibrio cholerae), whose sequences, KO (K20455) and family signature (IPR012708/COG1048) are shared by PP_2336:

  • "AcnD dehydrated 2‑methylcitrate and citrate to 2‑methyl‑cis‑aconitate and cis‑aconitate, respectively; AcnD also hydrated cis‑aconitate. However, 2‑methylisocitrate and isocitrate were not substrates for AcnD, indicating that AcnD only catalyzes the first half of the aconitase‑like dehydration reactions." [Grimek & Escalante‑Semerena 2004, 14702315]
  • "The AcnD protein required reconstitution of an Fe/S cluster for activity. All detectable AcnD activity was lost after incubation with iron‑chelating agents…" [PMID 14702315] — consistent with the [4Fe‑4S] cofactor annotated for Q88KF4.
  • The KEGG EC 4.2.1.117 designation ("2‑methyl‑trans‑aconitate forming") and later mechanistic work show the AcnD product is the trans (not cis) isomer, which is why a dedicated isomerase (PrpF) is required downstream [Rocco et al. 2017, 29145506].

Substrate specificity, precisely stated: tricarboxylate substrates bearing a C2 hydroxyl and (for the physiological substrate) a 2‑methyl branch — i.e. (2S,3S)‑2‑methylcitrate (physiological) and citrate (promiscuous) — undergoing anti/syn elimination of water to the corresponding aconitate. The enzyme is specialised for the dehydration (first) half‑reaction only.

Reaction sequence of the cycle (propionate entry): 1. Propionyl‑CoA + oxaloacetate → 2‑methylcitrate (PrpC, PP_2335). 2. 2‑methylcitrate → 2‑methyl‑trans‑aconitate — AcnD (PP_2336). 3. 2‑methyl‑trans‑aconitate → 2‑methyl‑cis‑aconitate — PrpF (PP_2337). 4. 2‑methyl‑cis‑aconitate → (2R,3S)‑2‑methylisocitrate — hydration by AcnB (PP_2339). 5. 2‑methylisocitrate → pyruvate + succinate — PrpB (PP_2334).

The obligatory hand‑off is established directly: "We show that the SoPrpF protein isomerizes the product of the AcnD reaction into the PrpD product (2‑MCA], a known substrate of the housekeeping aconitase (AcnB]" [Rocco et al. 2017, 29145506], and the AcnD+PrpF pair is functionally sufficient: "the AcnD and PrpF proteins restored the ability of a prpD mutant strain of Salmonella enterica to grow on propionate as a source of carbon and energy" [Grimek & Escalante‑Semerena 2004, 14702315].

Why two routes? KT2440 encodes both an Fe/S‑independent PrpD (PP_2338; forms 2‑methyl‑cis‑aconitate directly [Horswill & Escalante‑Semerena 2001, 11294638; Brock et al. 2002, 12473114]) and the AcnD–PrpF pair. These provide parallel/redundant 2‑methylcitrate‑dehydration capacity; the AcnD route (Fe/S‑dependent) is thought to predominate or be favoured under particular redox/iron conditions, whereas PrpD (Fe/S‑independent) is robust to oxidative stress. This redundancy explains why acnA/acnD single mutants in related organisms often show no propionate‑growth defect [Horswill 2001, 11294638].

Biological significance: the methylcitrate cycle is the principal aerobic route for catabolising propionate/propionyl‑CoA — derived from odd‑chain fatty acids, branched‑chain amino acids (Ile, Val, Met, Thr), cholesterol side chains and environmental short‑chain acids — and for detoxifying the growth‑inhibitory intermediate propionyl‑CoA in this soil bacterium. This is directly relevant to KT2440's role as a metabolically versatile bioremediation/biotechnology chassis.

5. Distinction from the housekeeping TCA aconitases

P. putida KT2440 encodes several aconitase‑superfamily proteins with distinct jobs (KEGG EC linkage): - PP_2112 and PP_2339 (AcnB) — EC 4.2.1.3 / 4.2.1.99, the canonical TCA‑cycle aconitases (full citrate ⇌ isocitrate isomerisation) and AcnB additionally the 2‑methylisocitrate dehydratase. - PP_2338 (PrpD) — EC 4.2.1.79, Fe/S‑independent 2‑methylcitrate dehydratase. - PP_2336 (this gene) — the unique EC 4.2.1.117 / AcnD, specialised for the trans‑forming first half‑reaction in the methylcitrate cycle.

Thus PP_2336 is not the enzyme that supplies isocitrate to the TCA cycle; that role belongs to PP_2112/PP_2339. The bacterial paradigm in which two aconitases (AcnA = stationary‑phase/stress‑induced, more stable, moonlighting mRNA‑binding; AcnB = major exponential‑phase enzyme) partition function and can act as post‑transcriptional regulators [Tang & Guest 1999, 10589714; Tang et al. 2002, 11932448; Baothman et al. 2013, 23637460] is informative background, but PP_2336 belongs to the methylcitrate‑dedicated AcnD subfamily, a functionally separate branch. There is no evidence that PP_2336 itself moonlights as an RNA‑binding regulator.

6. Localization

The enzyme functions in the cytoplasm. Aconitase‑superfamily proteins are soluble; the sequence lacks a signal peptide or transmembrane region, and the methylcitrate‑cycle intermediates it processes are cytosolic. No experimental localization data exist for PP_2336 specifically; this assignment is by strong homology and biochemical logic.

8. Limitations and future directions

  • Evidence is inferential for PP_2336 itself (UniProt PE3 "inferred from homology"; unreviewed TrEMBL). All enzymology derives from AcnD/PrpF orthologs in Shewanella, Vibrio, and Salmonella, plus the KT2440 genomic context — no purified‑enzyme assay, kinetics, or structure exists for Q88KF4.
  • Recommended validation: (i) heterologous expression + in‑vitro assay of PP_2336 with 2‑methylcitrate/citrate (product stereochemistry by NMR) and Fe/S reconstitution; (ii) ΔPP_2336, ΔPP_2337, ΔPP_2338 and combinatorial mutants tested for growth on propionate vs glucose to define redundancy with PrpD; (iii) transcriptional analysis of the prp operon (PP_2333 PrpR/2‑methylcitrate induction); (iv) an AlphaFold model + docking to confirm the [4Fe‑4S] active site and trans‑forming geometry.
  • Open mechanistic question: the precise identity of the AcnD product isomer (2‑methyl‑trans‑aconitate vs 4‑methylaconitate) and the exact accessory role of PrpF (isomerase vs Fe/S‑protecting) remain debated even in the model organisms [Rocco et al. 2017, 29145506].
