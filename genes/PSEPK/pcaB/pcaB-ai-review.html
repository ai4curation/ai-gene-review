Gene Description

PcaB is a 3-carboxy-cis,cis-muconate cycloisomerase (EC 5.5.1.2) in the protocatechuate branch of the beta-ketoadipate pathway. It is a fumarase class II cycloisomerase that catalyzes anti cycloisomerization of 3-carboxy-cis,cis-muconate during aromatic compound catabolism. Structural work on the Pseudomonas putida enzyme showed that it is a homotetrameric fumarase class II family enzyme, consistent with the UniProt/NCBIfam assignment of pcaB.

Executive summary

The Pseudomonas putida KT2440 gene pcaB (UniProt Q88N37, locus PP_1379) encodes 3-carboxy-cis,cis-muconate cycloisomerase (EC 5.5.1.2), an enzyme of the protocatechuate (PCA) branch of the β-ketoadipate pathway that catalyzes cycloisomerization (lactonization) of 3-carboxy-cis,cis-muconate to γ-carboxymuconolactone / 4-carboxymuconolactone. This step sits immediately downstream of protocatechuate ring cleavage by PcaHG and upstream of decarboxylase/lactonase steps leading to β-ketoadipate and central carbon metabolites (acetyl-CoA and succinyl-CoA). Evidence includes KT2440 proteomics under vanillin growth (strong induction of PcaB and related pca proteins), pathway reconstructions and in vitro biochemistry supporting the same substrate→product transformation, and metabolic engineering implementations that route carbon through the PCA/β-ketoadipate module containing PcaB to produce β-ketoadipic acid from PET-derived monomers. (simon2014comprehensiveproteomeanalysis pages 8-9, simon2014comprehensiveproteomeanalysis pages 6-8, werner2021tandemchemicaldeconstruction pages 2-3, yamanashi2015invitroreconstitution pages 5-6, romerosilva2013genomicandfunctional pages 10-11)

1) Target verification: ensuring correct gene/protein identity

1.1 UniProt-to-locus mapping and enzyme identity

In P. putida KT2440, PP_1379 is annotated as pcaB encoding “3-Carboxy-cis,cis-muconate cycloisomerase” in a KT2440 vanillin-response proteomics study, matching the UniProt-provided identity (Q88N37; gene name pcaB; ordered locus PP_1379). (simon2014comprehensiveproteomeanalysis pages 8-9, simon2014comprehensiveproteomeanalysis pages 6-8)

Conclusion: The research herein refers specifically to pcaB/PP_1379/Q88N37 from P. putida KT2440, not other organisms’ “pcaB” homologs. (simon2014comprehensiveproteomeanalysis pages 8-9)

2) Key concepts and definitions (current understanding)

2.1 The β-ketoadipate pathway and the protocatechuate branch

The β-ketoadipate pathway is a central aerobic aromatic-catabolic route that “funnels” diverse lignin- and xenobiotic-derived aromatics into central metabolism. A common intermediate is protocatechuate (PCA), which undergoes intradiol ring cleavage and subsequent conversions to yield metabolites feeding the TCA cycle (e.g., acetyl-CoA and succinyl-CoA). (simon2014comprehensiveproteomeanalysis pages 6-8, sodre2024sustainableproductionof pages 6-7)

2.2 Definition of PcaB activity (EC 5.5.1.2)

Within the protocatechuate branch, protocatechuate 3,4-dioxygenase (PcaHG) cleaves PCA to form 3-carboxy-cis,cis-muconate; PcaB then cycloisomerizes/lactonizes this compound to γ-carboxymuconolactone (4-carboxymuconolactone). Multiple sources describe this same substrate→product step and position within the pathway. (chow2024confirmationofgenesa pages 11-15, romerosilva2013genomicandfunctional pages 10-11, yamanashi2015invitroreconstitution pages 5-6, yamanashi2015invitroreconstitution pages 1-2)

A pathway description consistent with KT2440’s physiology links the downstream products to central metabolism: protocatechuate → 3-carboxy-cis,cis-muconate → 4-carboxymuconolactone → β-ketoadipate intermediates → acetyl-CoA + succinyl-CoA. (romerosilva2013genomicandfunctional pages 10-11, simon2014comprehensiveproteomeanalysis pages 6-8)

3) Molecular function and substrate specificity

3.1 Catalyzed reaction and substrate/product specificity

Primary function: 3-carboxy-cis,cis-muconate cycloisomerase converts 3-carboxy-cis,cis-muconate (also called 3-carboxy-cis,cis-muconic acid) to the lactone γ-carboxymuconolactone / 4-carboxymuconolactone. (chow2024confirmationofgenesa pages 11-15, romerosilva2013genomicandfunctional pages 10-11, yamanashi2015invitroreconstitution pages 5-6, yamanashi2015invitroreconstitution pages 1-2)

3.2 Evidence from enzymology/biochemistry (supporting the chemistry)

An in vitro reconstitution of the protocatechuate branch in another bacterium (with explicit homology to P. putida PcaB) reports the product formation after the PcaB step: after ring cleavage, “following cycloisomerization by PcaB, the formation of the lactone ring gives” the lactone intermediate. This work states the Rhodococcus enzyme is 41% identical to the P. putida “3-carboxy-cis,cis-muconate lactonizing enzyme,” supporting functional conservation of the PcaB reaction across taxa. (yamanashi2015invitroreconstitution pages 6-7)

Note: The retrieved corpus did not contain KT2440 PcaB kinetic constants (Km, kcat) or direct biochemical assays using purified KT2440 PcaB; therefore, substrate specificity statements here are limited to the pathway-defined substrate/product and conserved enzymatic role rather than quantitative enzyme parameters. (yamanashi2015invitroreconstitution pages 5-6, simon2014comprehensiveproteomeanalysis pages 8-9)

4) Pathway context, genetics, and regulation in P. putida KT2440

4.1 Operon/cluster organization and regulon context

A KT2440 proteomics study reports that genes involved in vanillin degradation via the protocatechuate branch are arranged in three gene clusters and shows a genomic organization diagram (“pca-cluster”). (simon2014comprehensiveproteomeanalysis pages 8-9)

The same work reports that the PCA transporter PcaK and transcriptional regulator PcaR “belong to the same regulon as PcaB, PcaC, PcaD and PcaF,” placing pcaB in a regulated aromatic-catabolic module responding to aromatic substrates (vanillin-derived carbon). (simon2014comprehensiveproteomeanalysis pages 6-8)

4.2 Functional role during aromatic growth (vanillin example)

When P. putida KT2440 is grown on vanillin as a sole carbon source, enzymes in the PCA branch of the β-ketoadipate pathway—including PcaB—increase in abundance, consistent with vanillin being metabolized to PCA (via vanillate) and then funneled into central metabolism. (simon2014comprehensiveproteomeanalysis pages 6-8)

5) Cellular localization and where the enzyme acts

5.1 Likely cellular compartment based on available evidence

The retrieved sources do not provide a definitive subcellular localization statement for KT2440 PcaB (e.g., explicit “cytosolic enzyme”). In the vanillin-response study, PcaB is quantified in both a whole-cell proteomics workflow and a “membrane fraction” workflow (likely reflecting experimental fractionation rather than a confirmed membrane association), and the authors also report that a majority of differentially abundant proteins were predicted cytoplasmic overall. Consequently, the strongest evidence-supported statement is that PcaB is detected in proteomics experiments and functions in the intracellular catabolic conversion of PCA-derived ring-fission intermediates, but the corpus does not contain a direct localization assertion for PP_1379. (simon2014comprehensiveproteomeanalysis pages 6-8, simon2014comprehensiveproteomeanalysis pages 8-9)

6) Recent developments (prioritizing 2023–2024)

6.1 2024: Systems-level functional genomics for KT2440 metabolism

A 2024 mSystems paper applies independent component analysis (ICA) to a large compendium of RB-TnSeq fitness data in KT2440 (179 conditions) to infer “functional modules” spanning many processes, including aromatic-catabolic and β-ketoadipate-linked functions (the work includes an annotated module named “Beta-ketoadipate and levulinic acid”). This type of analysis is a contemporary route for refining functional annotation and understanding gene networks relevant to aromatic catabolism. (borchert2024machinelearninganalysis pages 6-7)

6.2 2024: Reviews connecting β-ketoadipate/PCA funneling to lignin valorization

A 2024 review on sustainable production of aromatic chemicals from lignin highlights PCA as a central intermediate and discusses its metabolism through β-ketoadipate-type routes, explicitly noting P. putida KT2440 among organisms of interest and framing this metabolism as part of microbial “funneling” for lignin-derived aromatics. While not pcaB-specific, this review reflects current consensus on why the pathway module containing PcaB is biotechnologically important. (sodre2024sustainableproductionof pages 6-7)

6.3 2023: High-titer production from non-canonical/lignin-derived carbon streams (context)

A 2023 bioRxiv study describes iterative metabolic engineering in P. putida KT2440 to improve bioproduction from an aromatic substrate (para-coumarate) and reports high titers (e.g., 7.3 g/L under defined conditions; and improved titers in engineered strains, including performance in lignin-derived BCD liquor). Although pcaB is not singled out in the retrieved pages, such work exemplifies active R&D in aromatic feedstock bioconversion that relies on native aromatic-catabolic capability (commonly involving PCA/β-ketoadipate funneling). (eng2023ensembleanditerative pages 1-3, eng2023ensembleanditerative pages 16-17)

7) Current applications and real-world implementations

7.1 PET plastic upcycling routed through PCA/β-ketoadipate metabolism

A peer-reviewed metabolic engineering study (2021) demonstrates a process to upcycle PET glycolysis products to β-ketoadipic acid (βKA) using engineered P. putida KT2440 and explicitly depicts PcaB in the engineered pathway map (BHET/TPA → PCA → βKA). The work reports 15.1 ± 0.6 g/L βKA produced from commercial BHET in bioreactor cultivation, illustrating real-world relevance of the pathway segment containing PcaB for plastics upcycling. (werner2021tandemchemicaldeconstruction pages 2-3)

7.2 Lignin-derived aromatic funneling as a platform function

The 2024 lignin bioconversion review emphasizes that microbial funneling pathways (including β-ketoadipate metabolism) enable conversion of heterogeneous lignin-derived aromatics toward central metabolites and engineered products; KT2440 is explicitly mentioned among strains used in lignin-related bioprocess contexts. (sodre2024sustainableproductionof pages 6-7)

8) Quantitative statistics and data (from retrieved studies)

8.1 Proteomics fold-changes for PcaB under vanillin metabolism

In KT2440 grown on vanillin, PcaB (PP_1379) shows large abundance increases in multiple proteomics modalities: 92.7-fold (GeLC–MS/MS), 117-fold (membrane fraction LC–MS/MS), and 6.1-fold (2-D DIGE) per the study’s Table 1. These data support strong pathway induction when aromatic carbon is routed through PCA catabolism. (simon2014comprehensiveproteomeanalysis pages 8-9)

8.2 Bioprocess titer demonstrating engineered β-ketoadipate production

As noted above, a PET-upcycling implementation reports 15.1 ± 0.6 g/L β-ketoadipic acid from BHET in bioreactor culture, with PcaB explicitly in the depicted metabolic pathway. (werner2021tandemchemicaldeconstruction pages 2-3)

9) Expert synthesis and analysis (evidence-grounded)

  1. pcaB is a core “funneling” enzyme: PcaB catalyzes a chemically defining step (cycloisomerization/lactonization) that converts a ring-cleavage product into a lactone intermediate that can be further processed to β-ketoadipate and then into central metabolism. This is supported by pathway descriptions and in vitro product formation evidence. (chow2024confirmationofgenesa pages 11-15, yamanashi2015invitroreconstitution pages 5-6, yamanashi2015invitroreconstitution pages 1-2)
  2. pcaB is part of a coordinated regulon responding to aromatics: KT2440 proteomics indicates that PcaB abundance increases strongly during growth on vanillin, and that PcaK/PcaR share regulon membership with key pca enzymes, consistent with regulated pathway deployment. (simon2014comprehensiveproteomeanalysis pages 6-8, simon2014comprehensiveproteomeanalysis pages 8-9)
  3. Industrial/biotech relevance derives from pathway modularity: The same pathway unit containing PcaB is reused in engineered processes converting plastics-derived aromatics (TPA→PCA) into β-ketoadipate at multi-gram per liter titers, illustrating how native catabolism can be leveraged for circular-economy applications. (werner2021tandemchemicaldeconstruction pages 2-3)
  4. Gaps remaining in this retrieved corpus: Despite UniProt indicating family/domain membership (class-II fumarase/aspartase family) and specific InterPro domains, the retrieved literature excerpts did not provide direct family/domain statements for KT2440 PcaB, nor enzyme kinetic constants; therefore, those details cannot be supported with tool-retrieved citations here and should be treated as database-derived inference unless further primary structural/biochemical literature is retrieved. (simon2014comprehensiveproteomeanalysis pages 8-9)
