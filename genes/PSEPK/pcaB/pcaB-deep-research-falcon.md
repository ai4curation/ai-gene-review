# Research Report: Functional Annotation of **pcaB** (UniProt Q88N37; locus **PP_1379**) in *Pseudomonas putida* KT2440

## Executive summary
The *Pseudomonas putida* KT2440 gene **pcaB** (UniProt **Q88N37**, locus **PP_1379**) encodes **3-carboxy-cis,cis-muconate cycloisomerase** (EC **5.5.1.2**), an enzyme of the **protocatechuate (PCA) branch of the β-ketoadipate pathway** that catalyzes cycloisomerization (lactonization) of **3-carboxy-cis,cis-muconate** to **γ-carboxymuconolactone / 4-carboxymuconolactone**. This step sits immediately downstream of protocatechuate ring cleavage by PcaHG and upstream of decarboxylase/lactonase steps leading to β-ketoadipate and central carbon metabolites (acetyl-CoA and succinyl-CoA). Evidence includes KT2440 proteomics under vanillin growth (strong induction of PcaB and related pca proteins), pathway reconstructions and in vitro biochemistry supporting the same substrate→product transformation, and metabolic engineering implementations that route carbon through the PCA/β-ketoadipate module containing PcaB to produce β-ketoadipic acid from PET-derived monomers. (simon2014comprehensiveproteomeanalysis pages 8-9, simon2014comprehensiveproteomeanalysis pages 6-8, werner2021tandemchemicaldeconstruction pages 2-3, yamanashi2015invitroreconstitution pages 5-6, romerosilva2013genomicandfunctional pages 10-11)

## 2) Key concepts and definitions (current understanding)
### 2.1 The β-ketoadipate pathway and the protocatechuate branch
The **β-ketoadipate pathway** is a central aerobic aromatic-catabolic route that “funnels” diverse lignin- and xenobiotic-derived aromatics into central metabolism. A common intermediate is **protocatechuate (PCA)**, which undergoes **intradiol ring cleavage** and subsequent conversions to yield metabolites feeding the TCA cycle (e.g., acetyl-CoA and succinyl-CoA). (simon2014comprehensiveproteomeanalysis pages 6-8, sodre2024sustainableproductionof pages 6-7)

### 2.2 Definition of PcaB activity (EC 5.5.1.2)
Within the protocatechuate branch, **protocatechuate 3,4-dioxygenase (PcaHG)** cleaves PCA to form **3-carboxy-cis,cis-muconate**; **PcaB** then **cycloisomerizes/lactonizes** this compound to **γ-carboxymuconolactone (4-carboxymuconolactone)**. Multiple sources describe this same substrate→product step and position within the pathway. (chow2024confirmationofgenesa pages 11-15, romerosilva2013genomicandfunctional pages 10-11, yamanashi2015invitroreconstitution pages 5-6, yamanashi2015invitroreconstitution pages 1-2)

A pathway description consistent with KT2440’s physiology links the downstream products to central metabolism: protocatechuate → 3-carboxy-cis,cis-muconate → 4-carboxymuconolactone → β-ketoadipate intermediates → **acetyl-CoA + succinyl-CoA**. (romerosilva2013genomicandfunctional pages 10-11, simon2014comprehensiveproteomeanalysis pages 6-8)

## 3) Molecular function and substrate specificity
### 3.1 Catalyzed reaction and substrate/product specificity
**Primary function:** 3-carboxy-cis,cis-muconate cycloisomerase converts **3-carboxy-cis,cis-muconate** (also called 3-carboxy-cis,cis-muconic acid) to the lactone **γ-carboxymuconolactone / 4-carboxymuconolactone**. (chow2024confirmationofgenesa pages 11-15, romerosilva2013genomicandfunctional pages 10-11, yamanashi2015invitroreconstitution pages 5-6, yamanashi2015invitroreconstitution pages 1-2)

### 3.2 Evidence from enzymology/biochemistry (supporting the chemistry)
An in vitro reconstitution of the protocatechuate branch in another bacterium (with explicit homology to *P. putida* PcaB) reports the product formation after the PcaB step: after ring cleavage, “following cycloisomerization by PcaB, the formation of the lactone ring gives” the lactone intermediate. This work states the *Rhodococcus* enzyme is **41% identical** to the *P. putida* “3-carboxy-cis,cis-muconate lactonizing enzyme,” supporting functional conservation of the PcaB reaction across taxa. (yamanashi2015invitroreconstitution pages 6-7)

**Note:** The retrieved corpus did not contain KT2440 PcaB kinetic constants (Km, kcat) or direct biochemical assays using purified KT2440 PcaB; therefore, substrate specificity statements here are limited to the pathway-defined substrate/product and conserved enzymatic role rather than quantitative enzyme parameters. (yamanashi2015invitroreconstitution pages 5-6, simon2014comprehensiveproteomeanalysis pages 8-9)

## 4) Pathway context, genetics, and regulation in *P. putida* KT2440
### 4.1 Operon/cluster organization and regulon context
A KT2440 proteomics study reports that genes involved in vanillin degradation via the protocatechuate branch are arranged in **three gene clusters** and shows a genomic organization diagram (“pca-cluster”). (simon2014comprehensiveproteomeanalysis pages 8-9)

The same work reports that the **PCA transporter PcaK** and transcriptional regulator **PcaR** “belong to the same regulon as PcaB, PcaC, PcaD and PcaF,” placing pcaB in a regulated aromatic-catabolic module responding to aromatic substrates (vanillin-derived carbon). (simon2014comprehensiveproteomeanalysis pages 6-8)

### 4.2 Functional role during aromatic growth (vanillin example)
When *P. putida* KT2440 is grown on **vanillin** as a sole carbon source, enzymes in the PCA branch of the β-ketoadipate pathway—including **PcaB**—increase in abundance, consistent with vanillin being metabolized to PCA (via vanillate) and then funneled into central metabolism. (simon2014comprehensiveproteomeanalysis pages 6-8)

## 7) Current applications and real-world implementations
### 7.1 PET plastic upcycling routed through PCA/β-ketoadipate metabolism
A peer-reviewed metabolic engineering study (2021) demonstrates a process to upcycle PET glycolysis products to **β-ketoadipic acid (βKA)** using engineered *P. putida* KT2440 and explicitly depicts **PcaB** in the engineered pathway map (BHET/TPA → PCA → βKA). The work reports **15.1 ± 0.6 g/L βKA** produced from commercial BHET in bioreactor cultivation, illustrating real-world relevance of the pathway segment containing PcaB for plastics upcycling. (werner2021tandemchemicaldeconstruction pages 2-3)
