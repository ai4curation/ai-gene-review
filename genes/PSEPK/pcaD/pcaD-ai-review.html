Gene Description

pcaD encodes a 3-oxoadipate enol-lactonase (EC 3.1.1.24) of the PcaD/CatD alpha/beta-hydrolase family. The enzyme hydrolyzes 3-oxoadipate enol-lactone to beta-ketoadipate in the lower beta-ketoadipate funnel for aromatic compound degradation. Classical genetics indicates that in Pseudomonas putida the pcaDE module supplies the shared enol-lactonase/CoA-transferase functions needed for both cat and pca branches, supporting pcaD as a core catabolic enzyme rather than a generic hydrolase.

Comprehensive Research Report: pcaD (PP_1380; UniProt Q88N36) in Pseudomonas putida KT2440

Executive summary

The Pseudomonas putida KT2440 gene pcaD (locus tag PP_1380, UniProt Q88N36) encodes 3-oxoadipate enol-lactonase (also termed β-ketoadipate/3-oxoadipate enol-lactone hydrolase; EC 3.1.1.24 per UniProt context). In KT2440 transcriptome datasets, PP_1380 is explicitly annotated as pcaD with product “3-oxoadipate enol-lactonase / enol-lactone hydrolase,” and appears with the canonical neighboring pca genes of the protocatechuate (PCA) / β-ketoadipate (3-oxoadipate) pathway. (duuren2011generationofa pages 6-7, duuren2011generationofa pages 7-8, wierckx2008transcriptomeanalysisof pages 4-5, duuren2011generationofa media b785a4ab)

Functionally, PcaD catalyzes hydrolysis of 3-oxoadipate-enol-lactone to the linear diacid β-ketoadipate (3-oxoadipate), a central intermediate that can be further activated and funneled into central metabolism. This reaction and its structural/mechanistic basis are well supported by high-resolution crystallographic analysis of a bacterial PcaD homolog showing an α/β-hydrolase fold enzyme with a Ser–Asp–His catalytic triad and a cap domain that enforces substrate selectivity for a kinked lactone substrate. (bains2011aproductanalog pages 7-8, bains2011aproductanalog pages 4-7, bains2011aproductanalog pages 3-4, bains2011aproductanalog pages 1-3)

In 2023–2024, the P. putida KT2440 protocatechuate/β-ketoadipate funnel has been heavily leveraged for lignin- and biomass-derived aromatic upcycling. Although most engineering targets upstream or parallel nodes (e.g., ring cleavage, CoA-transferase, regulation), these bioprocesses rely on intact downstream β-ketoadipate metabolism (where pcaD resides) or deliberately block downstream steps to accumulate products, highlighting the pathway’s industrial relevance. (werner2023ligninconversionto pages 2-4, jin2024biologicalvalorizationof pages 7-9, jin2024biologicalvalorizationof pages 4-7)

2. Key concepts and definitions (current understanding)

2.1 The protocatechuate / β-ketoadipate (3-oxoadipate) pathway

In Pseudomonas and many soil bacteria, diverse aromatic compounds are “funneled” into central catabolism via ring-cleavage pathways, with the β-ketoadipate (3-oxoadipate) pathway acting as a central integration route. In KT2440, this funnel can channel lignin-related aromatic substrates toward β-ketoadipate, which can be further metabolized into the TCA cycle, and its atom economy is often described as 1 mol/mol theoretical conversion from certain aromatics to β-ketoadipate under idealized stoichiometries. (werner2023ligninconversionto pages 1-2)

2.2 What PcaD does (enzyme definition)

3-oxoadipate enol-lactonase (PcaD; CatD-type) is a carboxylic-ester hydrolase within the α/β-hydrolase superfamily. It catalyzes hydrolysis of 3-oxoadipate-enol-lactone to linear β-ketoadipate (3-oxoadipate). (bains2011aproductanalog pages 7-8, bains2011aproductanalog pages 1-3)

2.3 Reaction placement and biochemical logic

Within the protocatechuate branch, ring cleavage and subsequent rearrangements generate a lactone intermediate (“enol-lactone”), which must be hydrolyzed to a linear diacid for downstream activation/processing. Work reconstituting the protocatechuate branch in another bacterium (Rhodococcus) notes that the relevant enol-lactonase activity can be provided by a PcaD-like domain and that this domain is substantially similar to the P. putida enzyme, consistent with conserved pathway logic across taxa. (yamanashi2015invitroreconstitution pages 6-7)

3. Molecular mechanism and structural biology (evidence-driven functional annotation)

3.1 Overall fold, oligomeric state, and active site architecture

A bacterial PcaD homolog has been crystallized at high resolution (1.9 Å), showing:

  • Dimeric organization with a substantial interface (supporting biological relevance of the dimer). (bains2011aproductanalog pages 3-4)
  • A core α/β-hydrolase (ABH) fold plus an inserted cap domain that shapes the active site and contributes to dimerization. (bains2011aproductanalog pages 3-4, bains2011aproductanalog pages 1-3)
  • A conserved catalytic triad (Ser–Asp–His), with residues reported as S100, D217, H244 in the crystallized homolog. (bains2011aproductanalog pages 4-7, bains2011aproductanalog pages 3-4)

3.2 Substrate specificity: why “enol-lactonase” is selective

Structural analysis with a product analog (levulinic acid formed in-crystal from a soaked substrate analog) indicates that the cap domain sculpts a deep, occluded pocket that preferentially accommodates small or kinked substrates, consistent with the shape constraints of the native 3-oxoadipate-enol-lactone. (bains2011aproductanalog pages 7-8, bains2011aproductanalog pages 8-9)

Mechanistic interpretation from the structure suggests that substrate binding is coordinated by an arginine pair (to orient the anionic functionality) while aromatic residues (e.g., tryptophans) contribute to pocket geometry and possibly to the oxyanion hole environment—features that together rationalize why PcaD is tuned to this specific β-ketoadipate pathway intermediate rather than being a broad-spectrum esterase. (bains2011aproductanalog pages 4-7, bains2011aproductanalog pages 8-9)

3.3 Evidence from P. putida protein production (KT2440-specific)

Although not a pathway study per se, one experimental report explicitly cloned P. putida KT2440 pcaD, expressed it in E. coli under IPTG induction, and purified His-tagged PcaD, providing direct evidence that the KT2440 pcaD coding sequence corresponds to a soluble enzyme amenable to purification/biochemical use. (tseng2006identificationandcharacterization pages 2-3, tseng2006identificationandcharacterization pages 3-4)

4. Regulation, pathway context, and cellular localization

4.1 Induction logic and regulatory coupling (expert-level functional inference)

In P. putida (strain F1, closely related physiology for aromatic catabolism), a pcaD deletion blocked induction of a pcaY-lacZ reporter by 4-hydroxybenzoate (4-HBA), and complementation restored induction. Additionally, adipate (a non-metabolizable β-ketoadipate analog) induced the reporter ~3-fold, supporting the conclusion that β-ketoadipate (β-KA) (a downstream product of the pca route) is a key inducer signal in the regulatory network and that pcaD is required to generate that inducer under the tested conditions. (luu2015integrationofchemotaxis pages 9-13)

This provides a systems-level interpretation: pcaD is not only a catalytic step but also indirectly shapes signal generation (β-KA pool) that can control pathway expression and chemotaxis-associated behavior via linked regulation. (luu2015integrationofchemotaxis pages 9-13)

4.2 Localization

No direct cellular localization experiment for KT2440 PcaD was retrieved in the accessed sources. However, multiple lines of evidence are consistent with a soluble intracellular (cytosolic) enzyme typical of central aromatic catabolism: (i) recombinant expression and purification from E. coli without any secretion context, and (ii) structural characterization as a soluble dimeric α/β-hydrolase enzyme. This localization should be treated as an evidence-based inference rather than a directly measured KT2440 localization claim. (bains2011aproductanalog pages 3-4, tseng2006identificationandcharacterization pages 2-3)

5. Recent developments (prioritizing 2023–2024)

Recent work has focused less on re-characterizing PcaD biochemistry and more on engineering pathway flux through the PCA/β-ketoadipate hub, demonstrating modern relevance of the pathway node that pcaD serves.

5.1 2023: High-titer β-ketoadipate production from lignin-related aromatics (KT2440)

A 2023 Science Advances study engineered P. putida KT2440 to convert mixed lignin-related aromatic compounds (LRCs) into β-ketoadipic acid, reporting industrially relevant bioprocess metrics including:

  • β-ketoadipate titers: 44.5 g/L (model LRCs) and 25 g/L (corn stover-derived LRCs). (werner2023ligninconversionto pages 1-2)
  • Productivities: 1.15 g·L−1·h−1 (model LRCs) and 0.66 g·L−1·h−1 (corn stover-derived LRCs). (werner2023ligninconversionto pages 1-2)
  • Fed-batch details: DO-stat and constant feed conditions reaching ~43.5 ± 1.6 g/L and 44.5 ± 1.85 g/L with reported productivities including 0.55 ± 0.02 and 0.85 ± 0.04 g·L−1·h−1 (depending on strategy). (werner2023ligninconversionto pages 2-4)
  • Yield framing: yields reported up to 1.0 mol/mol (100% theoretical) and an overall yield of 0.10 g product per g corn stover-derived lignin for the lignin stream case. (werner2023ligninconversionto pages 2-4, werner2023ligninconversionto pages 1-2)
  • Technoeconomic analysis: the study reports a predicted minimum selling price of $2.01 per kg β-ketoadipate and compares this to fossil-derived adipic acid market pricing. (werner2023ligninconversionto pages 2-4)

While this work targeted steps such as pcaIJ (to accumulate β-ketoadipate) and upstream conversions, it underscores a key point for functional annotation: pcaD sits within a pathway that is currently being pushed to multi–tens of g/L product levels in KT2440-based processes, making the integrity and regulation of the pca route practically important. (werner2023ligninconversionto pages 2-4, werner2023ligninconversionto pages 1-2)

5.2 2024: Protocatechuic acid (PCA) production from hydrolysate aromatics (KT2440)

A 2024 applied study engineered KT2440 to accumulate protocatechuic acid (PCA) from lignin-derived aromatics by blocking downstream PCA catabolism (notably pcaGH), reporting:

  • 6.11 g/L PCA from 10 g/L p-coumaric acid after 72 h (complete conversion at that loading). (jin2024biologicalvalorizationof pages 7-9)
  • Slower conversion of ferulate with a maximum PCA concentration of ~1.9 g/L while consuming ~2.5 g/L ferulate in 72 h. (jin2024biologicalvalorizationof pages 7-9)
  • Very high reported yields in specific conversions (e.g., 97.7% from p-coumarate; 98.5% from 4-HBAL; 93.1% from 4-HBA). (jin2024biologicalvalorizationof pages 4-7)

These results further emphasize the centrality of the pca system in modern “bio-funneling” designs: when downstream β-ketoadipate catabolism is blocked, intermediates like PCA can accumulate; when it is active, it supports complete mineralization or flux to downstream products. (jin2024biologicalvalorizationof pages 7-9, jin2024biologicalvalorizationof pages 4-7)

5.3 2023 expert synthesis: rerouting the PCA/β-ketoadipate hub to cis,cis-muconate

A 2023 review focused on lignin valorization highlights metabolic engineering strategies that reroute flux at the PCA node toward catechol and cis,cis-muconic acid (ccMA) (rather than β-ketoadipate), and summarizes multiple reported performance metrics including ccMA titers of 13.5 g/L, 43 g/L, and up to 64.2 g/L, with yields up to 96 mol% and productivity around 0.4 g·L−1·h−1 in a cited case. (rosini2023ligninvalorizationproduction pages 13-15)

Although pcaD itself is not the primary engineering target in these ccMA designs, these expert syntheses reinforce the field consensus that the PCA/β-ketoadipate hub is a high-value “control point” for aromatic valorization in P. putida. (rosini2023ligninvalorizationproduction pages 13-15)

6. Current applications and real-world implementations

6.1 Lignin upcycling to β-ketoadipate (polymer precursor)

The 2023 Science Advances process is a clear example of translation from pathway knowledge to an engineered bioprocess, including both bioprocess development and technoeconomic analysis, and demonstrates that P. putida KT2440 can convert heterogeneous lignin-derived aromatic streams to a single product (β-ketoadipate) at high titers and economically framed costs. (werner2023ligninconversionto pages 2-4, werner2023ligninconversionto pages 1-2)

6.2 Hydrolysate funneling to protocatechuate

The 2024 Molecules study demonstrates production directly from lignocellulosic hydrolysate streams (corncob hydrolysates), emphasizing practical constraints such as co-substrate effects (e.g., glucose slowing aromatic consumption early in cultivation) and providing titer/yield data relevant to process deployment. (jin2024biologicalvalorizationof pages 4-7)

7. Functional annotation conclusion for pcaD (PP_1380; Q88N36)

7.1 Primary biochemical function

The best-supported functional annotation for KT2440 pcaD (PP_1380) is:

Enzyme: 3-oxoadipate enol-lactonase / β-ketoadipate enol-lactone hydrolase (EC 3.1.1.24; UniProt context)

Reaction: hydrolysis of 3-oxoadipate-enol-lactone to linear β-ketoadipate (3-oxoadipate). (bains2011aproductanalog pages 7-8, bains2011aproductanalog pages 1-3)

7.2 Pathway role

PcaD is a core step in the protocatechuate branch of the β-ketoadipate (3-oxoadipate) pathway, consistent with KT2440 pca gene cluster annotations and their co-regulated presence in aromatic metabolism transcriptomes. (duuren2011generationofa pages 7-8, wierckx2008transcriptomeanalysisof pages 4-5, duuren2011generationofa pages 8-9)

7.3 Cellular compartment

Most consistent with a cytosolic enzyme involved in central aromatic catabolism; no direct KT2440 localization assay was obtained in this tool run. (bains2011aproductanalog pages 3-4, tseng2006identificationandcharacterization pages 2-3)
