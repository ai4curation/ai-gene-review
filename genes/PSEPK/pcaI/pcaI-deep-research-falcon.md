# Research Report: Functional Annotation of **pcaI** (UniProt: Q88FX5) in *Pseudomonas putida* KT2440

## 0) Identity verification (critical disambiguation)
The gene symbol **pcaI** in *Pseudomonas putida* has a well-established meaning in the aromatic-catabolic literature: **pcaI** and **pcaJ** are two adjacent genes encoding the two nonidentical subunits of **β-ketoadipate (3-oxoadipate):succinyl-CoA transferase** (EC **2.8.3.6**) in the **β-ketoadipate pathway**. This was demonstrated by complementation of CoA-transferase-deficient mutants, expression of the cloned region in *E. coli*, and DNA sequencing showing two ORFs separated by 8 bp (operon-like organization). (parales1993characterizationofthe pages 1-1, parales1993characterizationofthe pages 4-5, parales1993characterizationofthe pages 5-5)

**Scope note (PP_3951 / UniProt mapping):** The primary papers retrieved here (focused on biochemical function) predate the KT2440 locus-tag system and therefore do **not** directly mention **PP_3951** or **UniProt Q88FX5**. The enzymatic function and gene symbol usage (pcaI) are strongly supported by primary literature, but the specific mapping from **pcaI → PP_3951 → Q88FX5** should be regarded as a database cross-reference supplied by UniProt/GenBank rather than something explicitly stated in the retrieved primary articles. (parales1993characterizationofthe pages 1-1, parales1993characterizationofthe pages 4-5)

## 1) Key concepts and definitions (current understanding)

### 1.1 The β-ketoadipate pathway (“β-ketoadipate / 3-oxoadipate pathway”)
The **β-ketoadipate pathway** is a central bacterial funnel for aerobic catabolism of many aromatic compounds, including compounds entering via **benzoate** and **4-hydroxybenzoate**, ultimately producing tricarboxylic acid (TCA) cycle-relevant intermediates. In *P. putida*, **β-ketoadipate:succinyl-CoA transferase** is described as the **penultimate step** in converting benzoate and 4-hydroxybenzoate to central metabolism intermediates. (parales1993characterizationofthe pages 1-1)

### 1.2 Definition of PcaI/PcaJ enzyme function
The enzyme encoded by **pcaI + pcaJ** catalyzes a reversible **CoA-transfer** reaction that “activates” the dicarboxylate **β-ketoadipate (3-oxoadipate)** by transferring CoA from **succinyl-CoA** to form **3-oxoadipyl-CoA (β-ketoadipyl-CoA)**. (parales1993characterizationofthe pages 1-1)

This CoA-thioester product is then cleaved by a thiolase in the pathway (classically PcaF in *Pseudomonas* nomenclature) to yield central metabolites (acetyl-CoA and succinyl-CoA in canonical descriptions/ortholog systems). (kaschabek2002degradationofaromatics pages 1-2)

## 2) Gene/protein organization and mechanistic implications

### 2.1 Operon structure and subunits
In *P. putida*, **pcaI and pcaJ** are separated by **8 bp**, suggesting operon organization, and expression experiments produced two proteins consistent with nonidentical subunits of the transferase. (parales1993characterizationofthe pages 5-5, parales1993characterizationofthe pages 1-1, parales1993characterizationofthe pages 4-5)

### 2.2 Enzyme architecture (biochemical evidence from *Pseudomonas* ortholog)
Purification/characterization of the analogous **3-oxoadipate:succinyl-CoA transferase** from *Pseudomonas* sp. strain B13 found a native enzyme mass ~115–120 kDa with subunits of ~32.9 kDa (A) and ~27 kDa (B), consistent with an **A2B2 heterotetramer**. (kaschabek2002degradationofaromatics pages 1-2, kaschabek2002degradationofaromatics pages 4-5)

### 2.3 Kinetics and substrate specificity (best-available enzymology in retrieved sources)
In *Pseudomonas* sp. B13, reported kinetic and specificity features included:
- **pH optimum ~8.4**. (kaschabek2002degradationofaromatics pages 1-2, kaschabek2002degradationofaromatics pages 5-5)
- **Km ≈ 0.4 mM** for **3-oxoadipate** and **Km ≈ 0.2 mM** for **succinyl-CoA**. (kaschabek2002degradationofaromatics pages 1-2, kaschabek2002degradationofaromatics pages 5-5)
- **Donor specificity:** acetyl-CoA could not replace succinyl-CoA as the CoA donor. (kaschabek2002degradationofaromatics pages 5-5)
- **Acceptor specificity:** no activity with **2-chloro-** or **2-methyl-3-oxoadipate**; some activity with **4-methyl-3-oxoadipate**; poor activity with **2-oxoadipate** and **3-oxoglutarate**. (kaschabek2002degradationofaromatics pages 1-2)
- **Metal ion requirement:** chelators (EDTA, o-phenanthroline) did not inhibit activity and the authors concluded no significant requirement for common divalent metals, although Mg2+ was used in some spectrophotometric assay formats due to a detectable 3-oxoadipyl-CoA–Mg2+ complex. (kaschabek2002degradationofaromatics pages 5-5, kaschabek2002degradationofaromatics pages 2-2)

### 2.4 Mechanistic notes from sequence comparisons
The original *P. putida* gene characterization used cross-species sequence comparisons and reported that **conserved functional groups important to catalytic activity of CoA transferases** could be identified, and discussed evolutionary relationships between bacterial heteromeric enzymes and mammalian homodimeric CoA-transferases. (parales1993characterizationofthe pages 1-1)

## 4) Recent developments (prioritizing 2023–2024)

### 4.1 2023: Engineering *P. putida* KT2440 to accumulate β-ketoadipate by blocking pcaIJ
A 2023 **Science Advances** study engineered *P. putida* KT2440 for conversion of lignin-related aromatic compounds (LRCs) to **β-ketoadipate** and explicitly used **deletion of pcaIJ** (creating strain CJ263) as a key strategy to **prevent downstream consumption** and enable accumulation of β-ketoadipate. (werner2023ligninconversionto pages 2-4)

**Bioprocess performance / statistics (2023):**
- From model LRCs: β-ketoadipate titer **44.5 g/L** and productivity values reported up to **~1.15 g/L/h** in the study’s summary metrics. (werner2023ligninconversionto pages 1-2)
- From corn stover–derived LRCs: β-ketoadipate titer **25 g/L** and productivity **0.66 g/L/h**, with overall yield **0.10 g product per g corn stover–derived lignin**. (werner2023ligninconversionto pages 1-2, werner2023ligninconversionto pages 2-4)
- The study’s technoeconomic analysis estimated a **minimum selling price (MSP) of $2.01/kg** for β-ketoadipate. (werner2023ligninconversionto pages 1-2, werner2023ligninconversionto pages 2-4)

These results demonstrate that the pcaIJ step (and by implication pcaI/PcaI) is a critical control point for whether carbon is **mineralized** vs **accumulated** as a product/intermediate in KT2440-based lignin valorization. (werner2023ligninconversionto pages 2-4)

**URL / publication date:** Werner et al., *Science Advances*, Sep 2023. https://doi.org/10.1126/sciadv.adj0053 (werner2023ligninconversionto pages 1-2)

### 4.2 2024: Review synthesis connecting lignin depolymerization to β-ketoadipate funneling
A 2024 review in *Chemical Communications* highlights *P. putida* KT2440 as a frequently used bacterial host for lignin-related aromatic metabolism and metabolic engineering, and emphasizes that protocatechuate (PCA) and related aromatics are typically routed into the **β-ketoadipate pathway** for downstream metabolism. (sodre2024sustainableproductionof pages 3-4)

The review also describes a process concept where lignin-derived streams are processed to increase catechol content and then fed to engineered *P. putida* to produce **cis,cis-muconic acid** (a β-ketoadipate-pathway-related intermediate), which can then be converted to adipic acid. (sodre2024sustainableproductionof pages 13-14)

**URL / publication date:** Sodré & Bugg, *Chem. Commun.*, Nov 2024. https://doi.org/10.1039/d4cc05064a (sodre2024sustainableproductionof pages 3-4)

### 4.3 2024: Functional genomics / systems biology (RB-TnSeq + machine learning)
A 2024 *mSystems* paper analyzed a compendium of *P. putida* KT2440 RB-TnSeq fitness data (reported as **332 samples** spanning **179 unique conditions**) and used independent component analysis to identify “functional modules” that recapitulate aromatic-catabolic groupings, including modules and iModulon links that cover genes for aromatic funneling to **β-ketoadipate** (including protocatechuate- and benzoate-to-β-ketoadipate segments). (borchert2024machinelearninganalysis pages 2-4, borchert2024machinelearninganalysis pages 13-15)

This provides recent, data-driven support that β-ketoadipate-pathway genes constitute coherent functional groupings under diverse growth conditions—useful for annotation, metabolic modeling, and strain design. (borchert2024machinelearninganalysis pages 13-15, borchert2024machinelearninganalysis pages 2-4)

**URL / publication date:** Borchert et al., *mSystems*, Mar 2024. https://doi.org/10.1128/msystems.00942-23 (borchert2024machinelearninganalysis pages 13-15)

## 5) Current applications and real-world implementations

### 5.1 Biomanufacturing from lignin-derived aromatics
The 2023 *Science Advances* work demonstrates a scalable bioprocess concept: using engineered *P. putida* KT2440 to funnel heterogeneous lignin-related aromatics to a single product, **β-ketoadipate**, with reported titers up to **44.5 g/L** and TEA-derived MSP estimate **$2.01/kg**—metrics positioned as economically relevant for bio-based polymer precursors. (werner2023ligninconversionto pages 1-2, werner2023ligninconversionto pages 2-4)

### 5.2 Platform chemical routes (muconate → adipic acid)
The 2024 review literature frames engineered *P. putida* routes producing **cis,cis-muconic acid** from processed lignin streams as a platform route that can feed chemical upgrading (hydrogenation) to **adipic acid**, linking aromatic-catabolic pathway modules (including β-ketoadipate pathway segments) to industrial monomer supply chains. (sodre2024sustainableproductionof pages 13-14)

### 5.3 Functional-module approaches for faster annotation and engineering
The 2024 RB-TnSeq + ICA approach provides a methodology and resource to connect gene sets with specific aromatic catabolic functions (e.g., benzoate/protocatechuate to β-ketoadipate), which can accelerate identifying which genes (including downstream pathway steps) most constrain growth or production under particular aromatic feedstocks. (borchert2024machinelearninganalysis pages 13-15, borchert2024machinelearninganalysis pages 2-4)

## 6) Expert opinions and analysis (authoritative interpretations)

### 6.1 Why pcaI/pcaJ matter as engineering levers
The 2023 study’s explicit **pcaIJ deletion** to enable product accumulation indicates that, in KT2440, the pcaIJ-encoded CoA-transferase is not just a “housekeeping catabolic enzyme” but a **key metabolic valve** that determines whether β-ketoadipate is catabolized to central metabolism or retained as a product. (werner2023ligninconversionto pages 2-4)

### 6.2 Systems-level support for β-ketoadipate pathway coherence
The 2024 functional module analysis supports the view that aromatic pathways feeding β-ketoadipate are strongly structured in genotype–phenotype space, and that regulatory/functional decompositions can recover known aromatic-catabolic partitions (e.g., benzoate module genes distributed across regulatory clusters). This is consistent with decades of biochemical genetics but provides a modern, scalable framework for annotation and design. (borchert2024machinelearninganalysis pages 13-15)

## 8) Concise functional annotation statement (for genome annotation use)
**pcaI (Q88FX5)** in *Pseudomonas putida* KT2440 encodes the **A (α) subunit** of **β-ketoadipate (3-oxoadipate):succinyl-CoA transferase** (EC **2.8.3.6**), which forms a heteromeric complex with **PcaJ (B/β subunit)**. The enzyme catalyzes CoA transfer from **succinyl-CoA** to **β-ketoadipate (3-oxoadipate)** to generate **3-oxoadipyl-CoA**, a key activation step in the lower β-ketoadipate pathway. The pcaI and pcaJ genes are adjacent (8 bp spacing) and behave like an operon. Substrate specificity includes succinyl-CoA as the required donor and selectivity against several substituted dicarboxylates based on *Pseudomonas* ortholog enzymology. The most consistent localization is **cytosolic**, with explicit caveat that KT2440-specific localization experiments were not retrieved.
