Gene Description

Research Report: Functional Annotation of benD (UniProt Q88I37; locus PP_3164) in Pseudomonas putida KT2440

1. Target verification (gene/protein identity)

The UniProt target Q88I37 corresponds to Pseudomonas putida KT2440 locus PP_3164, annotated as 1,6-/1,2-dihydroxycyclohexa-2,4-/3,5-diene-1-carboxylate dehydrogenase (benzoate cis-dihydrodiol dehydrogenase), EC 1.3.1.25, and placed in the ben (benzoate) gene cluster with benzoate 1,2-dioxygenase genes (benABC) and regulator (benR) (zavalameneses2024proteogenomiccharacterizationof pages 8-9, yun2011proteomiccharacterizationof pages 4-5). This matches classic genetic/biochemical definitions of benD as the enzyme acting immediately downstream of benzoate 1,2-dioxygenase to generate catechol (jeffrey1992characterizationofpseudomonas pages 1-1).

2. Key concepts and definitions (current understanding)

2.1 Benzoate-to-catechol “ben” module

Aerobic benzoate catabolism in many Proteobacteria begins with benzoate 1,2-dioxygenase (BenABC) forming a non-aromatic cis-dihydrodiol (“benzoate diol”; also described as cis-1,2-dihydroxycyclohexa-3,5-diene-1-carboxylate, DHCDC), followed by BenD oxidizing this dihydrodiol to catechol, which is then cleaved (typically via catechol 1,2-dioxygenase) and assimilated through central aromatic pathways such as the β-ketoadipate pathway (jeffrey1992characterizationofpseudomonas pages 1-1, zavalameneses2024proteogenomiccharacterizationof pages 8-9).

2.2 BenD (EC 1.3.1.25) reaction and role

BenD is a cis-diol dehydrogenase that converts the benzoate cis-dihydrodiol intermediate into catechol (with aromatization) as part of benzoate assimilation (jeffrey1992characterizationofpseudomonas pages 1-1, cowles2000benraxyls pages 4-5). Genetic evidence supporting this step includes benD-defective mutants that accumulate the benzoate diol and lack benzoate diol dehydrogenase activity (jeffrey1992characterizationofpseudomonas pages 5-6, jeffrey1992characterizationofpseudomonas pages 1-1).

2.3 Regulatory context (BenR)

In P. putida, BenR (a XylS-like regulator) controls expression of the benzoate catabolic genes: BenR activates benABC in response to benzoate and influences expression from aromatic-responsive promoters; reporter assays showed strong BenR-dependent activation (cowles2000benraxyls pages 4-5). Earlier genetics demonstrated that a chromosomal region termed benR could substitute for xylS in activating the TOL lower-pathway promoter in response to benzoate, placing benR adjacent to benABC and benD in a coordinated regulatory module (jeffrey1992characterizationofpseudomonas pages 1-1, jeffrey1992characterizationofpseudomonas pages 6-7).

3. Gene/protein function in P. putida KT2440

3.1 Enzymatic function and substrate specificity (as supported by KT2440/close Pseudomonas evidence)

Primary function: BenD/PP_3164 catalyzes oxidation of the benzoate cis-dihydrodiol (DHCDC; “benzoate diol”) to catechol, acting after BenABC in aerobic benzoate utilization (jeffrey1992characterizationofpseudomonas pages 1-1, zavalameneses2024proteogenomiccharacterizationof pages 8-9).

Substrate: the immediate substrate is the cis-1,2-dihydrodiol product of benzoate 1,2-dioxygenase (jeffrey1992characterizationofpseudomonas pages 1-1). In engineered P. putida strains, deletion of benD prevents this conversion and enables accumulation of DHCDC from benzoate, providing functional support for BenD’s substrate assignment (sun2008microbialproductionof pages 1-2, sun2008microbialproductionof pages 4-6).

Cofactor: Although BenD is annotated as an SDR-family dehydrogenase in UniProt (user-provided) and literature commonly describes this enzyme class as NAD(P)+-dependent, the retrieved KT2440/mt-2 excerpts do not include a definitive purified-enzyme cofactor assay for PP_3164; thus, cofactor usage should be treated as annotation-level inference rather than directly demonstrated here (zavalameneses2024proteogenomiccharacterizationof pages 8-9, jeffrey1992characterizationofpseudomonas pages 1-1).

3.2 Pathway placement: benzoate → catechol → β-ketoadipate module

Proteomics in KT2440 identified PP_3164 (BenD) among proteins assigned to the β-ketoadipate pathway / aromatic assimilation response and found it upregulated during growth on benzoate compared with succinate, consistent with BenD acting in benzoate assimilation (yun2011proteomiccharacterizationof pages 5-7, yun2011proteomiccharacterizationof pages 4-5). Comparative genomics/proteogenomics (2024) explicitly depicts benD in the ben gene cluster alongside benABC and in proximity to catA and associated transport/regulatory genes in Pseudomonas (including KT2440), supporting the canonical benzoate-to-catechol entry point that feeds catechol cleavage and downstream assimilation (zavalameneses2024proteogenomiccharacterizationof pages 8-9).

3.3 Cellular localization

Direct subcellular localization experiments (e.g., fluorescence tagging, fractionation validated for BenD specifically) were not found in the retrieved KT2440-focused literature. However, BenD/PP_3164 was detected in a cytosolic proteome fraction prepared for benzoate vs succinate growth comparisons, which is consistent with BenD being a soluble/cytosolic enzyme (noting that some membrane/periplasmic proteins can appear in soluble fractions) (yun2011proteomiccharacterizationof pages 4-5).

4. Quantitative evidence and key statistics

4.1 Induction in KT2440 proteomics (benzoate vs succinate)

In a KT2440 comparative proteomics study, BenD/PP_3164 was detected and quantified as upregulated on benzoate, with iTRAQ Ben/Suc ratio = 1.626 (reported also as ~1.63), 10 peptides, emPAI 2.42, and a Percolator score of 221 (yun2011proteomiccharacterizationof pages 5-7, yun2011proteomiccharacterizationof pages 4-5). These data support condition-dependent expression consistent with benzoate assimilation.

4.2 Genetic/functional mapping and regulatory assay statistics (classic Pseudomonas evidence)

A foundational genetic study of benzoate catabolism mutants (in Pseudomonas putida mt-2) found that benD mutants accumulate benzoate diol and lack benzoate diol dehydrogenase activity, consistent with BenD’s step in the pathway (jeffrey1992characterizationofpseudomonas pages 5-6, jeffrey1992characterizationofpseudomonas pages 1-1). That work also provided quantitative reporter/enzyme activity readouts used to localize regulatory activity (benR region) and characterize mutants; the relevant activity tables are shown in the extracted table images (jeffrey1992characterizationofpseudomonas media bca4fbc4, jeffrey1992characterizationofpseudomonas media 07807de9).

4.3 BenR-dependent regulation metrics (reporter assays)

BenR-dependent activation was quantified by reporter assays in P. putida: presence of BenR yielded approximately 13,000 Miller units (versus undetectable background), and addition of benzoate increased activity to about 17,000 Miller units in the reported system (cowles2000benraxyls pages 4-5). These data support BenR as a strong activator in benzoate-responsive regulation.

4.4 Metabolic engineering/application metrics: DHCDC production enabled by benD deletion

A biotechnology study used a KT2440-derived background (KT2442) to delete benD (creating KTSY01) and overexpress benABC to accumulate the BenABC product (DHCDC) by blocking its BenD-mediated oxidation to catechol. Reported titers and process statistics include:
- 2.23–2.3 g/L DHCDC after 24 h in shake flasks (≈73% yield) (sun2008microbialproductionof pages 1-2, sun2008microbialproductionof pages 4-6)
- >17 g/L DHCDC after 48 h in a 6-L fed-batch fermentor (≈61% yield), average productivity 0.356 g/L/h and peak 0.936 g/L/h (sun2008microbialproductionof pages 1-2, sun2008microbialproductionof pages 4-6)
- Increased benzoate dioxygenase activity in the overexpression strain: 1.2 mmol benzoate g⁻¹ CDW L⁻¹ h⁻¹ vs 0.09 mmol g⁻¹ CDW L⁻¹ h⁻¹ in the parent benD deletion strain (sun2008microbialproductionof pages 4-6)
These results demonstrate a direct, real-world implementation where BenD is a key control point that determines whether carbon flows to catechol metabolism or accumulates as cis-dihydrodiol product.

5. Recent developments (prioritizing 2023–2024)

5.1 Updated comparative genomic context (2024)

A 2024 proteogenomic/comparative study that includes KT2440 explicitly annotates benD as EC 1.3.1.25 and presents its position within a ben/cat genomic neighborhood (including benA/benB/benC/benD, transporters, and nearby catA context) across Pseudomonas strains, reinforcing current genome-informed annotation of PP_3164 and its operon-level context (zavalameneses2024proteogenomiccharacterizationof pages 8-9).

5.2 Limits of 2023–2024 KT2440 benD-specific mechanistic literature retrieved here

Tool-based searches retrieved few 2023–2024 publications with direct, KT2440-specific mechanistic/biochemical characterization of BenD (e.g., purified enzyme kinetics, cofactor specificity, structural studies) beyond genomic context. Therefore, the most detailed functional evidence in this report necessarily relies on classic genetic experiments, KT2440 proteomics, and metabolic engineering demonstrations, supplemented by a 2024 comparative genomic schematic that explicitly includes KT2440 benD/PP_3164 (zavalameneses2024proteogenomiccharacterizationof pages 8-9, yun2011proteomiccharacterizationof pages 5-7, jeffrey1992characterizationofpseudomonas pages 1-1).

6. Current applications and real-world implementations

6.1 Biocatalysis: production of cis-dihydrodiols from benzoate

BenD knockout strains can be used as whole-cell biocatalysts to accumulate the valuable cis-dihydrodiol product (DHCDC) produced by BenABC from benzoate, preventing its conversion to catechol. The demonstrated production reached >17 g/L in fed-batch fermentation, showing feasibility at process-relevant scales (sun2008microbialproductionof pages 4-6).

6.2 Environmental biotechnology: aromatic compound assimilation modules

BenD is part of a broadly deployed aerobic benzoate assimilation module (ben genes) that supports aromatic compound biodegradation capacity. In KT2440, BenD is induced during benzoate growth and grouped with β-ketoadipate pathway proteins, consistent with a physiological role in environmental aromatic assimilation (yun2011proteomiccharacterizationof pages 5-7, yun2011proteomiccharacterizationof pages 4-5).

7. Expert interpretation and analysis (evidence-constrained)

  1. BenD is a pathway gatekeeper: It is the committed step converting the non-aromatic cis-dihydrodiol into catechol. Disrupting benD reroutes metabolism from complete benzoate assimilation toward accumulation of the cis-dihydrodiol product, which has been exploited for production purposes (jeffrey1992characterizationofpseudomonas pages 1-1, sun2008microbialproductionof pages 4-6).
  2. Regulation is tightly coupled to substrate presence: BenR provides strong transcriptional activation in benzoate-responsive systems, aligning expression of upstream oxygenation (benABC) with downstream oxidation (benD) and catechol assimilation (cowles2000benraxyls pages 4-5, jeffrey1992characterizationofpseudomonas pages 1-1).
  3. KT2440-specific localization remains under-characterized in the retrieved literature: Current evidence supports BenD as soluble/cytosolic by proteomic detection in cytosolic fractions, but dedicated localization studies would strengthen annotation (yun2011proteomiccharacterizationof pages 4-5).
