Gene Description

rsgA encodes a circularly permuted GTPase that assists late maturation of the bacterial 30S ribosomal subunit, including release of RbfA from mature subunits. It appears in KEGG thiamine metabolism because of guanine nucleotide side chemistry, not because it is part of thiamine biosynthesis.

Ensembl [5] and NCBI [6] for instance, have a strong focus on genome annotation with the help of RNA transcript information while UniProt has a pronounced emphasis on protein-coding genes and biological function of proteins.
