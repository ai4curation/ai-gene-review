# Research report: **catA-I (PP_3713)** / catechol 1,2-dioxygenase in *Pseudomonas putida* KT2440 (UniProt **Q88GK8**)

## Executive summary
The target protein **CatA-I (PP_3713)** in *Pseudomonas putida* KT2440 is a **catechol 1,2-dioxygenase (C1,2O; EC 1.13.11.1)** that catalyzes **intradiol (ortho) ring cleavage** of **catechol → cis,cis-muconate**, a central step in the **benzoate/catechol (ben/cat) branch of the β-ketoadipate pathway** that funnels aromatic carbon to **succinyl-CoA and acetyl-CoA**. Direct genetic and biochemical evidence shows CatA (PP_3713) provides the bulk of C1,2O capacity in KT2440, while a paralog **CatA2 (PP_3166)** contributes less under typical conditions but becomes important at high catechol load as a “safety valve”. (jimenez2014asecondchromosomal pages 1-4, jimenez2014asecondchromosomal pages 4-7)

## 1. Verification of correct gene/protein identity (mandatory disambiguation)
### 1.1 Target identity check
* **UniProt Q88GK8** is annotated as **catechol 1,2-dioxygenase** (intradiol ring-cleavage dioxygenase family), gene **catA-I**, locus **PP_3713** (user-provided context).
* The primary literature explicitly maps **CatA = PP_3713** in *P. putida* KT2440 and defines it as **catechol 1,2-dioxygenase (C1,2O)** catalyzing **ortho cleavage of catechol to cis,cis-muconate**. (jimenez2014asecondchromosomal pages 1-4)

### 1.2 Ambiguity note: multiple catA-like genes in KT2440
KT2440 encodes **two catA-type catechol 1,2-dioxygenases**:
* **catA / CatA (PP_3713)**: canonical chromosomal enzyme; dominant activity. (jimenez2014asecondchromosomal pages 1-4, jimenez2014asecondchromosomal pages 4-7)
* **catA2 / CatA2 (PP_3166)**: second chromosomal copy downstream of the ben operon; functional C1,2O, proposed as a safety valve at high catechol. (jimenez2014asecondchromosomal pages 1-4, jimenez2014asecondchromosomal pages 4-7)

Because the question targets **Q88GK8 = PP_3713**, this report focuses on **CatA (PP_3713)**, referencing CatA2 only where it informs CatA’s physiological context.

## 2. Key concepts & definitions (current understanding)
### 2.1 Catechol 1,2-dioxygenase (intradiol ring-cleavage dioxygenase)
Catechol 1,2-dioxygenases are **intradiol** aromatic ring-cleaving enzymes that perform **ortho cleavage** between the two hydroxylated carbons of catechol to yield **cis,cis-muconate (muconic acid)** (terminology varies by protonation state). For KT2440 CatA, this is described as **ortho cleavage of catechol → cis,cis-muconate**. (jimenez2014asecondchromosomal pages 1-4, jimenez2002genomicanalysisof pages 5-6)

### 2.2 Pathway context: ben/cat → β-ketoadipate pathway
In KT2440, benzoate is converted to catechol via the **ben** genes, and catechol is processed via the **cat** genes (including catA) as part of the chromosome-encoded **ortho/β-ketoadipate route** to central metabolism. (jimenez2014asecondchromosomal pages 1-4, jimenez2014asecondchromosomal pages 18-22)

### 2.3 Regulation logic (benzoate and muconate as inducers)
A key regulatory architecture is:
* **BenR and XylS respond to benzoate** (inducing ben-related functions). (jimenez2014asecondchromosomal pages 18-22)
* **CatR responds to cis,cis-muconate** and induces the **cat operon** (catBCA and related genes). (jimenez2014asecondchromosomal pages 18-22, duuren2011generationofa pages 8-9)
A CatR mutation can prevent cat operon induction and alter muconate accumulation phenotypes. (duuren2011generationofa pages 8-9)

## 3. Primary function: reaction, substrate specificity, and biochemical properties
### 3.1 Catalyzed reaction and primary substrate
**Primary function:** Catechol 1,2-dioxygenase **CatA (PP_3713)** catalyzes the **ring cleavage of catechol** to **cis,cis-muconate**, measured by product formation at 260 nm in enzymatic assays. (jimenez2014asecondchromosomal pages 1-4, jimenez2014asecondchromosomal pages 12-15)

### 3.2 Quantitative enzymology (KT2440 CatA vs CatA2)
Kinetic comparisons in KT2440 show CatA is a high-capacity, high-affinity enzyme relative to CatA2:
* **CatA (PP_3713)**: apparent **Km ~1.3 ± 0.2 µM**, **Vmax ~6.6 ± 0.3 µmol·min⁻¹·mg⁻¹**. (jimenez2014asecondchromosomal pages 4-7)
* **CatA2 (PP_3166)**: apparent **Km ~7.4 ± 1.4 µM**, **Vmax ~0.3 ± 0.1 µmol·min⁻¹·mg⁻¹**. (jimenez2014asecondchromosomal pages 4-7)
These kinetics support a model where CatA2 contributes disproportionately when catechol is high (CatA saturation). (jimenez2014asecondchromosomal pages 4-7)

### 3.3 Substrate range and specificity (substituted catechols)
Both KT2440 enzymes cleave catechol most efficiently; substituted catechols are accepted with reduced activity. Reported relative activities include:
* **4-methylcatechol**: CatA 44%, CatA2 59%
* **3-methylcatechol**: CatA 5%, CatA2 7%
* **4-chlorocatechol**: CatA 1.7%, CatA2 5%
* **Protocatechuate**: CatA <1%, CatA2 not detected (n.d.)
(jimenez2014asecondchromosomal pages 35-37)

### 3.4 “Division of labor” between CatA and CatA2 in vivo
In KT2440 during benzoate use, CatA is estimated to process **>95%** of benzoate-derived catechol flux, with CatA2 contributing **<5%** under standard conditions. (jimenez2014asecondchromosomal pages 4-7)

## 4. Cellular localization and where the enzyme acts
Direct subcellular localization experiments (e.g., fluorescence tagging) were not retrieved in the present evidence set. However, multiple independent lines support **intracellular/soluble (cytosolic) localization** consistent with canonical intradiol dioxygenases:
* CatA/CatA2 activity is assayed from **crude cell extracts** obtained after **cell lysis**, indicating an intracellular soluble enzyme fraction. (jimenez2014asecondchromosomal pages 12-15)
* CatA/CatA2 were expressed and recovered as soluble proteins for biochemical assays in heterologous expression contexts, consistent with soluble cytosolic enzymes rather than secreted/periplasmic proteins. (jimenez2014asecondchromosomal pages 4-7)

## 5. Biological role and phenotypes (experimental genetics)
### 5.1 Loss-of-function phenotypes on benzoate
Deletion analysis in KT2440 shows:
* **ΔcatA2**: no detectable growth phenotype under tested benzoate conditions. (jimenez2014asecondchromosomal pages 4-7)
* **ΔcatA**: significantly delayed growth on benzoate; reduced C1,2O activity. (jimenez2014asecondchromosomal pages 4-7)
* **ΔcatA ΔcatA2**: no detectable C1,2O activity and inability to grow on **5 mM benzoate**; catechol accumulation visible as dark/brown pigments. (jimenez2014asecondchromosomal pages 4-7, jimenez2014asecondchromosomal pages 1-4)

Measured crude-extract activities strongly support CatA dominance:
* WT: **1.2 ± 0.2 µmol·min⁻¹·mg⁻¹**
* ΔcatA2: **0.96 ± 0.07**
* ΔcatA: **0.08 ± 0.02**
(jimenez2014asecondchromosomal pages 4-7)

### 5.2 CatA2 as a “safety valve” at high benzoate/catechol load
A biosensor-based intracellular catechol assay indicates that while wild type can remove intracellular catechol even at high benzoate, strains lacking CatA2 accumulate catechol above roughly ~1 mM benzoate, consistent with CatA saturation and CatA2 engagement. (jimenez2014asecondchromosomal pages 4-7)

## 6. Mechanism and protein family context (2024 sources; authoritative, recent)
Although no structure or metal-cofactor measurements for KT2440 CatA (PP_3713) were retrieved directly, 2024 literature summarizes consensus for **intradiol catechol 1,2-dioxygenases**:
* Intradiol dioxygenases catalyze **ortho cleavage** and contain **Fe(III)** in the active site. (davletgildeeva2024bioremediationofpolycyclic pages 14-16)
* Mechanistic proposals include Fe(III)-assisted substrate activation (iron(II)-semiquinone formation), reaction with O2 to a peroxide/hydroperoxide intermediate, and rearrangement steps leading to muconic products. (lister2024…codeexpansion pages 23-28, lister2024…codeexpansion pages 17-23)
* A typical intradiol dioxygenase architecture comprises ~300-aa subunits with an N-terminal dimerization domain and catalytic domain, and an Fe(III) coordination sphere commonly involving **two histidines and two tyrosines plus water/hydroxide**. (lister2024…codeexpansion pages 23-28)
Given that KT2440 CatA is explicitly classified as an **intradiol dioxygenase** and is ~311 aa, these family-level properties are a reasonable mechanistic framing but should be treated as **inference from family membership**, not KT2440-specific measurement. (jimenez2002genomicanalysisof pages 5-6, lister2024…codeexpansion pages 23-28)

## 7. Pathway integration and regulation in KT2440 (evidence-based)
### 7.1 Genomic organization
Aromatic catabolic genes in KT2440 include a canonical cat cluster and a second catA-like gene within the ben cluster. Early genome-scale analysis identified both **catA** and **catA2** annotated as catechol 1,2-dioxygenases, with **catA2 located in the benzoate (ben) cluster**. (jimenez2002genomicanalysisof pages 5-6)

### 7.2 Transcriptional regulation by CatR and physiological consequences
CatR is a LysR-type regulator that divergently induces cat genes in response to **cis,cis-muconate**; a point mutation in catR can abolish cat operon induction, altering catechol processing capacity and enabling muconate accumulation phenotypes. (duuren2011generationofa pages 8-9)

## 8. Applications and real-world implementations
CatA (PP_3713) is a central control point because it sits at the catechol node, which is both a **toxicity hotspot** and an **industrial platform-chemical precursor** node.

### 8.1 Engineering KT2440 to accumulate catechol (lignin-derived aromatics)
An applied metabolic engineering strategy is to **knock out catA and catA2** to prevent catechol cleavage and accumulate catechol from benzoate/aromatic streams:
* On sodium benzoate, ΔcatA showed transient catechol accumulation and **27.47% molar catechol yield**; ΔcatAΔcatA2 improved to **57.68% molar yield** with stable accumulation, while growth rates decreased from **0.27 h⁻¹ (WT)** to **0.22 h⁻¹ (ΔcatA)** and **0.18 h⁻¹ (ΔcatAΔcatA2)**. (upadhyay2022engineeredpseudomonasputida pages 5-7)
These data demonstrate CatA’s role as the primary sink preventing catechol accumulation and underscore why it is a primary engineering target.

### 8.2 Muconate production and industrial biotechnology context
Industrial biotechnology perspectives identify the **catechol → muconate** step (CatA/C1,2O) as critical for improving catechol tolerance and muconate productivity. One reported approach is introducing an extra copy of **catA2 under the native Pcat promoter** to increase catechol 1,2-dioxygenase activity, improving catechol tolerance and degradation. (weimer2020industrialbiotechnologyof pages 11-12)
Process-level metrics cited for engineered *P. putida* systems include:
* **64.2 g·L⁻¹ cis,cis-muconate** produced from catechol via fed-batch strategies that address energy limitation, and a stated **kilogram-scale pilot** with **98% product purity**. (weimer2020industrialbiotechnologyof pages 11-12)
These values indicate real-world scaling and provide context for why CatA-level control is industrially important.

### 8.3 2023 plastic upcycling via engineered *Pseudomonas* and cat locus modifications
A 2023 high-impact study engineered *Pseudomonas putida* for plastic upcycling and muconate production with explicit manipulation of the **catRBCA** region:
* A knockout of the **catRBCA cluster** was used to eliminate muconate utilization in one engineered strain background. (bao2023engineeringmicrobialdivision pages 9-10)
* A separate construct deleted **catRBC** and inserted a **Ptac promoter upstream of catA** (placing catA under a strong/inducible promoter) to increase catechol 1,2-dioxygenase expression and drive catechol → muconate. (bao2023engineeringmicrobialdivision pages 9-10)
* In a division-of-labor consortium, the system produced **33.28 ± 1.34 mM (4.73 ± 0.19 g·L⁻¹) cis,cis-muconic acid**, reported as a **2.84-fold improvement** over a comparator fermentation. (bao2023engineeringmicrobialdivision pages 8-9)
This is a concrete, recent (2023) example of direct catA locus engineering for muconate as a product from plastic-derived feedstocks.

## 9. Expert interpretation and analysis (evidence-grounded)
1. **CatA (PP_3713) is the dominant catechol-processing enzyme in KT2440**, with CatA2 acting as a secondary capacity reserve. Quantitative enzyme kinetics and crude extract activities are consistent with a design where CatA2 is “silent” at low catechol but becomes important when catechol rises (higher Km, lower Vmax), which may mitigate toxic quinone formation and pigment-like polymerization associated with catechol accumulation. (jimenez2014asecondchromosomal pages 4-7)
2. **Engineering efforts frequently target CatA/CatA2 in opposite directions depending on the product goal:**
   * For **catechol production**, delete catA/catA2 to block catechol consumption (demonstrated yields and growth tradeoffs). (upadhyay2022engineeredpseudomonasputida pages 5-7)
   * For **muconate production**, increase catechol 1,2-dioxygenase flux (e.g., promoter replacement upstream of catA, extra catA2 copies) while blocking downstream catB steps to prevent muconate consumption (strategy described in engineered *Pseudomonas* and general engineering logic). (bao2023engineeringmicrobialdivision pages 9-10, sitompul2024improvinginhibitoranda pages 51-54, weimer2020industrialbiotechnologyof pages 11-12)
3. **Mechanistic consensus (2024) indicates Fe(III)-dependent intradiol chemistry**, which implies that media composition (iron availability), oxidative stress conditions, and protein stability are plausible levers for improving CatA performance; however, these are hypotheses unless validated for KT2440 CatA specifically. (davletgildeeva2024bioremediationofpolycyclic pages 14-16, lister2024…codeexpansion pages 23-28)
