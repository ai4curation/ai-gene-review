Gene Description

hisD encodes histidinol dehydrogenase (HDH; EC 1.1.1.23), a bifunctional, zinc-dependent, NAD-dependent oxidoreductase that catalyzes the final two steps of L-histidine biosynthesis. It carries out the sequential four-electron oxidation of L-histidinol first to the intermediate L-histidinaldehyde and then to L-histidine, reducing two molecules of NAD+ to NADH at a single active site. A catalytic zinc ion is required to position the substrate for both oxidation steps. The enzyme is a soluble cytoplasmic protein and completes the histidine biosynthetic pathway (step 9/9 from PRPP).
