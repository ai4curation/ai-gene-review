|---|---|
| Protein identity / annotation | **Target verified:** UniProt **Q88DD7** corresponds to **hisZ / PP_4890** in *Pseudomonas putida* KT2440, annotated as the **ATP phosphoribosyltransferase regulatory subunit**. In bacterial short-form ATPPRT systems, **HisZ is a catalytically inactive, histidyl-tRNA synthetase–like paralog** that regulates the catalytic HisGS subunit rather than catalyzing chemistry itself; this matches the provided HisRS-like family/domain context (pqac-00000002, pqac-00000004, pqac-00000005, pqac-00000009, pqac-00000010) |
| Complex architecture / oligomeric state | Short-form ATP phosphoribosyltransferase (**ATPPRT**) is a **hetero-octamer** composed of **4 HisGS catalytic subunits + 4 HisZ regulatory subunits**. Structural work describes a **tetrameric HisZ core sandwiched by two HisGS dimers** (pqac-00000000, pqac-00000001, pqac-00000002, pqac-00000004, pqac-00000006) |
| Biochemical reaction catalyzed by HisG/HisZ (ATPPRT) | The **HisG/HisZ holoenzyme** catalyzes the **first committed step of histidine biosynthesis**: **ATP + 5-phospho-α-D-ribosyl-1-pyrophosphate (PRPP) → N1-(5-phospho-β-D-ribosyl)-ATP (PRATP) + PPi**; the reaction is **Mg2+-dependent** and proceeds via nucleophilic attack of ATP N1 on PRPP C1′, consistent with an **SN2-like transition state** (pqac-00000000, pqac-00000001, pqac-00000002, pqac-00000004, pqac-00000006) |
| Allosteric regulation by histidine | **HisZ activates HisGS** in the absence of histidine but also **mediates feedback inhibition by histidine**. Histidine binds **to HisZ, not the catalytic active site**, and inhibition is described as **noncompetitive** in short-form systems studied. Structural evidence indicates histidine binding repositions **HisZ loops** and perturbs the HisZ–HisGS allosteric communication pathway rather than dissociating the complex (pqac-00000000, pqac-00000002, pqac-00000003, pqac-00000004) |
| Representative kinetic parameters | **Lactococcus lactis ATPPRT:** **Km(PRPP) = 18.4 ± 3.5 µM**, **Km(ATP) = 2.7 ± 0.3 mM**, **kcat = 2.7 ± 0.3 s−1**, **Ki(histidine) ≈ 81.1 µM**; histidine reported as noncompetitive inhibitor, AMP as competitive inhibitor (**Ki ≈ 1.44 mM**) (pqac-00000000). **A. baumannii ATPPRT (2023):** maximal catalysis above **pH 8.0**; at **25 °C**, **ADP can support a higher kcat than ATP** for the holoenzyme; single-turnover rate at **5 °C** exceeds steady-state **kcat**, consistent with post-chemistry limitation (pqac-00000002). **P. putida-specific enzyme kinetics were not found in the retrieved evidence.** |
| Mechanistic / structural insights | Catalysis involves active-site **Arg32** interacting with ATP/PRPP phosphates and a **Mg2+** ion reducing electrostatic repulsion between substrates. **HisZ binding repositions/properly constrains HisGS**, including **Arg56** from the neighboring HisGS subunit, to stabilize pyrophosphate leaving-group departure. The **HisZ:HisGS interface lies ~20 Å from the active site**, demonstrating long-range allostery. Histidine-bound structures show motion of the **histidine-binding loop** and **Asp101–Leu117 loop** in HisZ; a **Tyr263–His104** interaction contributes to inhibitory signal transmission. Mutational/MD work supports HisZ-driven **active-site electrostatic preorganization** (pqac-00000001, pqac-00000003, pqac-00000004, pqac-00000006) |
| *P. putida* phenotype / essentiality | In *P. putida* KT2440, **hisZ (PP_4890)** was recovered in a genome-wide screen as a mutant **unable to grow on M9 minimal medium**; Table 4 identifies it as a **histidine auxotroph** with **no growth on M9**, **growth restored by L-histidine**, and **no rescue by D-histidine**. The authors note that **hisZ encodes the regulatory subunit of ATP phosphoribosyltransferase** and functions in the **first step of histidine biosynthesis**; notably, this mutant was found even though it was **not predicted by the in silico model** (pqac-00000008, pqac-00000009, pqac-00000010) |
| 2023–2024 developments | **2023:** structure/kinetics of *A. baumannii* ATPPRT further refined the activated mechanism, showing **chemistry-limited HisGS** versus **product-release-limited holoenzyme**, with direct observation of a low-temperature pre-steady-state burst and evidence that **PRATP release/diffusion** limits ATPPRT turnover (pqac-00000002). **2024:** heavy-isotope “protein-mass modulation” work showed that, in the **HisZ-activated** enzyme, catalytic rate decreases in a **strictly mass-dependent** manner at low temperature, linking allosteric activation to **fast protein motions governing product release**, rather than the chemical step itself (pqac-00000002, pqac-00000004) |
