# hemD assessment notes

## Identity and core reaction

Q88RE4 (PP_0187) is a predicted uroporphyrinogen-III synthase. UniProt assigns
cyclization of hydroxymethylbilane with terminal-ring inversion to produce
uroporphyrinogen III and places the protein in the corresponding enzyme family
[file:PSEPK/hemD/hemD-uniprot.txt "Reaction=hydroxymethylbilane = uroporphyrinogen III + H2O;"]
[file:PSEPK/hemD/hemD-uniprot.txt "Belongs to the uroporphyrinogen-III synthase family."].
These assignments are electronic rather than direct KT2440 experiments.

## OpenScientist assessment

The OpenScientist report correctly identifies the target, reaction, and
importance of ring-D inversion and explicitly notes the lack of direct KT2440
characterization. It presents unsaved sequence and hydropathy calculations,
infers monomeric and cytoplasmic status, and transfers detailed structural
mechanism and essentiality from orthologs. Gene adjacency supports pathway
context but does not by itself establish an operon or quantitative branch flux.
The report's pathway diagram also substitutes HemG/HemY for the KT2440
HemJ-family PP_0431 step. These extrapolations were not used as target evidence.

## Curation decisions

GO:0004852 is accepted as the core molecular function and GO:0006780 as the
specific product-forming process. The broader tetrapyrrole-biosynthesis row is
retained as non-core. No target-specific localization, oligomeric-state,
essentiality, or branch-dominance assertion is added.
