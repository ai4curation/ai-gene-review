Gene Description

PvdE is a predicted inner membrane ABC exporter required for pyoverdine biosynthesis in Pseudomonas putida KT2440. It is a fused transporter with an N-terminal multi-pass transmembrane domain and a C-terminal ABC ATPase domain. Based on conserved domain architecture, the UniProt annotation, and experimental work on homologous Pseudomonas PvdE proteins, its most likely role is ATP-driven export of the pyoverdine precursor PVDIq from the cytoplasmic membrane to the periplasm, where downstream maturation steps generate fluorescent pyoverdine. Direct biochemical evidence for the KT2440 protein itself appears limited, so substrate specificity should be interpreted as a strong orthology-based inference rather than a strain-specific demonstration.

Research Report: Functional Annotation of pvdE (UniProt Q88F82; locus PP_4216) in Pseudomonas putida KT2440

0) Target verification (critical identity check)

The UniProt accession Q88F82 corresponds to PvdE in Pseudomonas putida strain KT2440 (ATCC 47054/DSM 6125), with ordered locus name PP_4216. In KT2440-focused literature, pvdE is explicitly identified as PP_4216 and described as a cytoplasmic (inner) membrane ABC transporter responsible for transporting a non-fluorescent pyoverdine precursor into the periplasm, matching the UniProt description of a fused ATPase+permease exporter (stein2023navigatingpyoverdineand pages 22-25). This matches primary mechanistic work in Pseudomonas aeruginosa that characterizes PvdE as an export-type ABC transporter with fused ATPase and permease components required for periplasmic steps of pyoverdine maturation (yeterian2010synthesisofthe pages 6-8, yeterian2010synthesisofthe pages 2-4).

1) Key concepts and definitions (current understanding)

1.1 Pyoverdine and the “pyoverdine circuit”

Pyoverdine (PVD) is a high-affinity siderophore used by fluorescent pseudomonads to scavenge ferric iron (Fe3+) under iron limitation. The KT2440 pyoverdine system is described as a multi-compartment “circuit” spanning (i) cytoplasmic synthesis, (ii) periplasmic maturation, (iii) export to the extracellular space, and (iv) re-uptake of ferripyoverdine via TonB-dependent receptors (henriquez2019pvdrt‐opmqandmdtabc‐opmb pages 1-5, stein2023navigatingpyoverdineand pages 22-25).

1.2 What PvdE is (functional class)

PvdE is an inner membrane ABC exporter (ATP-binding cassette transporter) that—unusually for many bacterial ABC exporters—is described here as a single polypeptide with fused ATPase and permease components, mediating export of a biosynthetic intermediate rather than export of a final metabolite (yeterian2010synthesisofthe pages 6-8, yeterian2010synthesisofthe pages 2-4).

1.3 What PvdE transports (substrate definition)

Multiple sources describe the substrate as a non-fluorescent pyoverdine precursor, also referred to as ferribactin (or ferribactin-like precursor) in review summaries. This precursor is exported from the cytoplasm to the periplasm, where chromophore maturation produces the fluorescent pyoverdine scaffold (janakiev2023secondarymetabolitesof pages 1-3, yeterian2010synthesisofthe pages 6-8, stein2023navigatingpyoverdineand pages 22-25).

2) Functional role of PvdE in the pyoverdine pathway

2.1 Cellular localization and directionality

For KT2440, PvdE (PP_4216) is described as present in the cytoplasmic membrane and responsible for moving a precursor into the periplasmic space (stein2023navigatingpyoverdineand pages 22-25). In P. aeruginosa, experimental fractionation and microscopy support a model in which pyoverdine biosynthesis begins in the cytoplasm and continues in the periplasm after PvdE-dependent transport (yeterian2010synthesisofthe pages 6-8, yeterian2010synthesisofthe pages 1-2).

2.2 Mechanistic placement: linking cytoplasmic NRPS synthesis to periplasmic maturation

Recent 2024 work on pyoverdine NRPS organization emphasizes that pyoverdine biosynthesis begins in the cytoplasm with NRPS activity and proceeds to a periplasmic maturation stage; it explicitly references PvdE as the exporter of the acylated ferribactin precursor (manko2024pvdlorchestratesthe pages 1-2). Primary evidence supports that the periplasm contains a distinct precursor form (PVDIp) and that the chromophore maturation is periplasmic (yeterian2010synthesisofthe pages 8-10).

3) Experimental evidence and quantitative data supporting PvdE function

3.1 pvdE is essential for pyoverdine production in a mechanistically characterized ortholog system

In P. aeruginosa, mutation of pvdE almost completely prevented pyoverdine production, and complementation restored production, consistent with PvdE being required for an obligate step in synthesis/export (yeterian2010synthesisofthe pages 6-8, yeterian2010synthesisofthe pages 2-4). Fluorescence microscopy showed no periplasmic fluorescence in the pvdE mutant, while complemented strains restored periplasmic fluorescence similar to wild type (yeterian2010synthesisofthe pages 6-8).

3.2 Subcellular fractionation: periplasmic accumulation depends on PvdE

Fractionation experiments showed a ~5-fold higher fluorescence in the periplasmic fraction of wild type relative to the pvdE mutant, with restoration upon complementation. Fluorescence was not detected in cytoplasm or membrane fractions in those preparations, supporting periplasmic accumulation/maturation after export (yeterian2010synthesisofthe pages 6-8).

3.3 Evidence for a distinct exported/matured intermediate (PVDIp vs PVDI)

A periplasmic fluorescent intermediate PVDIp differs chemically from mature secreted pyoverdine PVDI:
- Mass spectrometry: PVDIp m/z 1301.40 vs PVDI m/z 1334.60 (yeterian2010synthesisofthe pages 8-10).
- Electrophoretic behavior differs on cellulose acetate membranes (yeterian2010synthesisofthe pages 8-10).
These differences support that PvdE exports a precursor that undergoes additional periplasmic processing before secretion.

3.4 Downstream periplasmic maturation steps influence final yield (context for PvdE role)

In the same framework, periplasmic maturation enzymes strongly affect final output: a pvdQ mutant showed only a 17% decrease in chromophore-formation kinetics but a much larger ~70% reduction in mature pyoverdine production after overnight culture, highlighting that periplasmic steps downstream of PvdE substantially shape final product release (yeterian2010synthesisofthe pages 8-10).

4) Pathway integration in P. putida KT2440: PvdE vs downstream secretion systems

A key nuance for KT2440 annotation is that PvdE performs an inner-membrane cytoplasm→periplasm export step, whereas export of mature pyoverdine from the periplasm to the extracellular environment is mediated by distinct tripartite efflux pumps.

4.1 KT2440 efflux network for pyoverdine secretion (downstream of PvdE)

KT2440 uses overlapping tripartite systems for extracellular secretion of pyoverdine:
- PvdRT-OpmQ (ABC-type tripartite efflux) and MdtABC-OpmB (RND-type tripartite efflux) are involved in pyoverdine secretion; double deletion strongly affects growth under iron limitation and shifts pyoverdine distribution (less in medium, more in periplasm) (henriquez2019pvdrt‐opmqandmdtabc‐opmb pages 1-5).
- A 2023 study identifies ParXY as influencing pyoverdine production/secretion in a background where other secretion systems are inactive, demonstrating redundancy/overlap among efflux systems under iron limitation (stein2023therndefflux pages 1-2).
This KT2440 work supports a compartmental model where PvdE is upstream of these tripartite pumps: PvdE delivers the precursor to the periplasm for maturation, and tripartite pumps then secrete mature siderophore outward.

4.2 Quantitative assay conditions used in recent KT2440 secretion research

The 2023 KT2440 efflux work provides recent quantitative experimental context relevant to real-world implementation/testing:
- Iron limitation often imposed with 1 mM 2,2′-bipyridyl (stein2023therndefflux pages 1-2).
- Rescue experiments supplemented purified pyoverdine at 10 µM, and FeCl3/CuSO4 at 1 µM in iron-limited CAA medium; additional FeCl3 rescue range 100–400 µM in KB + bipyridyl conditions (stein2023therndefflux pages 13-15).

5) Regulation and systems-level context (recent understanding)

Iron acquisition in Pseudomonas is controlled by regulatory networks including Fur and extra-cytoplasmic function sigma factors and cell-surface signaling cascades; recent reviews emphasize that iron homeostasis is tightly integrated with virulence and stress responses in Pseudomonas aeruginosa (sanchez‐jimenez2023mechanismsofiron pages 8-10). While that review is focused on P. aeruginosa, it provides authoritative modern framing of why siderophore pathways (including biosynthesis and transport steps) are high-value intervention points.

6) Current applications and real-world implementations connected to PvdE/pyoverdine pathways

Direct PvdE-targeted applications are uncommon in the accessible literature; most implementations target the pyoverdine pathway as a system.

6.1 Biotechnology/diagnostics: pyoverdine as a readout and chemical tool

A KT2440-focused 2023 dissertation overview highlights:
- “Siderotyping”: using isoelectric focusing (IEF) patterns of pyoverdines to help classify Pseudomonas strains.
- Biosensing: a pyoverdine-based biosensor concept where pyoverdine quenches fluorescence of the pollutant furazolidone, enabling rapid detection (stein2023navigatingpyoverdineand pages 22-25).
Because PvdE is required to generate mature exported pyoverdine (via precursor export and enabling periplasmic maturation), it is indirectly critical to any pyoverdine-based detection or production process.

6.2 Anti-virulence and therapeutic strategies (pathogen-focused)

A 2023 review on iron homeostasis in P. aeruginosa highlights therapeutic strategies that target iron acquisition mechanisms—including disruption of siderophore production and uptake, “Trojan-horse” approaches exploiting siderophore uptake systems, and the idea that siderophore transporters can be vaccine targets (sanchez‐jimenez2023mechanismsofiron pages 8-10). Although this is not KT2440-specific, it represents authoritative expert opinion on how siderophore pathways are translated into interventions.

6.3 Ecological and host-interaction roles (2024 perspective)

A 2024 review emphasizes expanding “non-classical” roles of siderophores (binding non-iron metals, contributing to stress tolerance, signaling), reinforcing that siderophore pathways have consequences beyond iron uptake alone (arnold2024nonclassicalrolesof pages 1-2). This broadens how PvdE-supported pyoverdine production could matter in complex environments.

7) Relevant statistics and comparative constants (recently summarized)

Recent KT2440-focused work reports a very high pyoverdine affinity for Fe3+ with an example kD ~10^-32 M^-1, and also summarizes siderophore binding strengths more broadly (10^17–10^43 M^-1) (stein2023therndefflux pages 1-2, stein2023navigatingpyoverdineand pages 22-25). It also provides comparator host-protein constants (e.g., transferrin kD ~10^-22 M; albumin kD ~10^-5 M; siderocalin kD ~4×10^-10 M; FepA affinity for Fe-enterobactin ~2×10^-10 M), illustrating why siderophores can outcompete host iron-binding proteins in relevant contexts (stein2023navigatingpyoverdineand pages 22-25).

8) Evidence grading and organism-specific caveats

  • High-confidence annotation for KT2440 PvdE identity, localization (inner membrane), and pathway placement comes from KT2440-specific sources mapping pvdE to PP_4216 and describing cytoplasm→periplasm export (stein2023navigatingpyoverdineand pages 22-25).
  • Strong mechanistic and quantitative evidence for the role of PvdE in precursor export and periplasmic maturation comes from P. aeruginosa primary studies (yeterian2010synthesisofthe pages 6-8, yeterian2010synthesisofthe pages 8-10).
  • Gap: direct, KT2440-specific biochemical transport assays of PvdE (e.g., reconstituted transport, substrate specificity mapping) were not found in the retrieved full-text set; KT2440 recent work is richer on downstream secretion efflux systems rather than the PvdE step itself (henriquez2019pvdrt‐opmqandmdtabc‐opmb pages 1-5, stein2023therndefflux pages 1-2).

Recent KT2440 study and dissertation context (stein2023therndefflux pages 1-2, stein2023navigatingpyoverdineand pages 22-25)
Recent P. putida secretion context downstream of PvdE After periplasmic maturation, pyoverdine secretion to the exterior is mediated by overlapping tripartite efflux systems. In KT2440, PvdRT-OpmQ and MdtABC-OpmB contribute to secretion, and ParXY further influences pyoverdine production/secretion when other systems are inactive. These systems act downstream of PvdE, which handles the earlier inner-membrane export step. KT2440 secretion studies and recent 2023 paper (henriquez2019pvdrt‐opmqandmdtabc‐opmb pages 1-5, henriquez2019pvdrt‐opmqandmdtabc‐opmb pages 12-15, stein2023therndefflux pages 1-2, stein2023therndefflux pages 13-15)

Experimental conditions from recent KT2440 secretion study (stein2023therndefflux pages 13-15)
Evidence strength / annotation confidence High confidence for assignment as a pyoverdine-precursor exporter because KT2440 gene identity and pathway placement match direct ortholog experiments in P. aeruginosa; direct KT2440 biochemical transport assays for PvdE itself remain limited relative to downstream secretion-system studies. Organism-specific annotation plus orthologous primary evidence (stein2023navigatingpyoverdineand pages 22-25, yeterian2010synthesisofthe pages 6-8, henriquez2019pvdrt‐opmqandmdtabc‐opmb pages 1-5)

pvdE Notes

Identity and basic architecture

  • pvdE in Pseudomonas putida KT2440 corresponds to UniProt Q88F82 / locus PP_4216 and is annotated as a "Pyoverdine ABC export system, fused ATPase and permease components" [file:PSEPK/pvdE/pvdE-uniprot.txt, "SubName: Full=Pyoverdine ABC export system, fused ATPase and permease components"].
  • PvdE is a 552 aa fused ABC exporter with an N-terminal multi-pass membrane domain and a C-terminal nucleotide-binding domain [file:PSEPK/pvdE/pvdE-uniprot.txt, "DOMAIN 12..299"] [file:PSEPK/pvdE/pvdE-uniprot.txt, "DOMAIN 336..552"].
  • UniProt predicts cell membrane localization and multiple transmembrane helices, consistent with an inner membrane transporter [file:PSEPK/pvdE/pvdE-uniprot.txt, "SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell membrane"] [file:PSEPK/pvdE/pvdE-uniprot.txt, "Multi-pass membrane protein"].

Function inference

  • P. putida KT2440 produces a characterized pyoverdine siderophore, so pvdE fits an intact pyoverdine biosynthetic/export module in this strain [PMID:19459056 Siderophore-mediated iron acquisition in the entomopathogenic bacterium Pseudomonas entomophila L48 and its close relative Pseudomonas putida KT2440, "Structural analysis of the pyoverdine produced by the closely related P. putida KT2440 showed that this strain produces an already characterised pyoverdine"].
  • In experimentally dissected pyoverdine biosynthesis in Pseudomonas aeruginosa, PvdE transports the precursor PVDIq to the periplasm before downstream maturation steps [PMID:24816606 PvdP is a tyrosinase that drives maturation of the pyoverdine chromophore in Pseudomonas aeruginosa, "PvdE transports PVDIq to the periplasm"].
  • The best-supported specific role is ATP-dependent export of the pyoverdine precursor PVDIq from the cytoplasmic side of the inner membrane to the periplasm [PMID:24816606 PvdP is a tyrosinase that drives maturation of the pyoverdine chromophore in Pseudomonas aeruginosa, "PvdE transports PVDIq to the periplasm"].
  • The specific substrate assignment is based on experimental work in Pseudomonas aeruginosa and on strong orthology/domain conservation, not on a direct biochemical assay of the KT2440 protein.

GO review implications

  • GO:0005886 plasma membrane is appropriate for this protein because the UniProt entry calls it a cell membrane multi-pass protein and the topology is transporter-like.
  • GO:0140359 ABC-type transporter activity is the most reliable current molecular function term for the KT2440 protein.
  • GO:1904680 peptide transmembrane transporter activity is plausible because the transported cargo is a peptidyl pyoverdine precursor, but the term is still imperfect because it does not convey siderophore-precursor specificity.
  • GO:0034040 ATPase-coupled lipid transmembrane transporter activity is not supported; the inferred cargo is a pyoverdine precursor rather than a lipid.
  • GO:0015891 siderophore transport is a better biological process term than generic transmembrane transport for this protein.
  • GO:0019290 siderophore biosynthetic process is justified because PvdE acts within the pyoverdine maturation pathway rather than in general peptide transport.

Term gap

  • Current GO molecular function terms do not capture ATP-driven export of a pyoverdine precursor from the cytoplasmic membrane to the periplasm.
  • A useful new term would be "pyoverdine precursor transmembrane transporter activity".
