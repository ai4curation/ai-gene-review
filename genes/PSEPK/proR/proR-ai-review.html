Gene Description

proR encodes the experimentally characterized 4-hydroxyproline 2-epimerase of Pseudomonas putida KT2440. It reversibly interconverts cis-4-hydroxy-D-proline and trans-4-hydroxy-L-proline and supports hydroxyproline utilization.

Research report: proR (PP_1258; UniProt Q88NF3) in Pseudomonas putida KT2440

0) Target verification (mandatory disambiguation)

The gene symbol proR is ambiguous across bacteria (often used for “proline racemase”-like annotations). Here, the relevant target is proR = PP_1258 from Pseudomonas putida KT2440, which is experimentally characterized as a 4-hydroxyproline epimerase / proline racemase-family enzyme acting preferentially on 4-hydroxyproline (Hyp) stereoisomers, consistent with the UniProt description for Q88NF3. (radkov2013aminoacidracemization pages 4-5, radkov2013aminoacidracemization pages 3-4, radkov2013aminoacidracemization pages 7-8)

1) Key concepts and definitions (current understanding)

1.1 4-hydroxyproline 2-epimerase (EC 5.1.1.8)

A 4-hydroxyproline 2-epimerase (also referred to in pseudomonads as HyPRE/HypE) catalyzes stereoinversion at the α-carbon (C2) of 4-hydroxyproline, interconverting L- and D- forms/epimers of 4-hydroxyproline. In hydroxyproline utilization pathways, this epimerization serves as a gateway step that converts an imported hydroxyproline stereoisomer to the stereochemical form required for downstream oxidative steps. (radkov2013aminoacidracemization pages 5-7, radkov2013aminoacidracemization pages 7-8, leon2024integratinggenomicevidence pages 7-11)

1.2 Proline racemase family enzymes in bacteria

In P. putida KT2440, ProR (PP_1258) was initially annotated as a “proline racemase,” but experimental profiling shows it is not a broad-spectrum amino-acid racemase for canonical proteinogenic amino acids; rather, it behaves primarily as a hydroxyproline epimerase in vitro. (radkov2013aminoacidracemization pages 1-2, radkov2013aminoacidracemization pages 5-7)

1.3 Physiological context: hydroxyproline as a nutrient and ecological metabolite

A canonical bacterial hydroxyproline degradation pathway begins with L-hydroxyproline 2-epimerase (EC 5.1.1.8) and ultimately yields α-ketoglutarate through oxidative and deamination steps. This is discussed as a conserved capability in diverse bacteria, including pseudomonads, and in 2024 work it is also framed as part of metabolic versatility in saline-adapted organisms. (radkov2013aminoacidracemization pages 7-8, leon2024integratinggenomicevidence pages 7-11)

2) Primary function of P. putida ProR (reaction, substrate specificity, and quantitative kinetics)

2.1 Reaction assignment and substrate specificity

Biochemical assays of recombinant P. putida KT2440 ProR (PP_1258) across many amino-acid stereoisomers found strong preference for 4-hydroxyproline stereoisomers, with highest activity on cis-D-hydroxyproline and measurable activity on trans-L-, cis-L-, and trans-D-hydroxyproline. (radkov2013aminoacidracemization pages 3-4)

Normalized activities (under the reported assay conditions) were: cis-D-HyPro 100.00 ± 0.00; trans-L-HyPro 32.34 ± 0.82; cis-L-HyPro 32.23 ± 1.82; trans-D-HyPro 22.73 ± 0.62, supporting annotation as a hydroxyproline epimerase rather than a canonical proline racemase. (radkov2013aminoacidracemization pages 3-4)

2.2 Kinetics and directionality (quantitative data)

Radkov & Moe (2013; Journal of Bacteriology; publication month in metadata Nov 2013; URL https://doi.org/10.1128/jb.00761-13) reported Michaelis–Menten kinetics for hydroxyproline epimerization:

  • Km (D→L) = 5.26 ± 1.12 mM
  • Km (L→D) = 15.04 ± 4.19 mM
  • kcat (D→L) = 69.63 ± 8.60 s⁻1
  • kcat (L→D) = 7.74 ± 0.00 s⁻1
  • kcat/Km ≈ 1.32×10⁴ M⁻1 s⁻1 (D→L) vs 5.15×10² M⁻1 s⁻1 (L→D)

This indicates a strong catalytic preference for cis-D-HyPro → trans-L-HyPro (∼26-fold higher kcat/Km than the reverse), even though the physiological pathway context frequently describes trans-L-HyPro → cis-D-HyPro as the entry direction for catabolism (consistent with reversibility plus cellular metabolite flows). (radkov2013aminoacidracemization pages 4-5, radkov2013aminoacidracemization pages 7-8)

2.3 Cofactor dependence and mechanism (authoritative experimental inference)

The ProR assays were performed without adding PLP, consistent with a PLP-independent mechanism in this enzyme class. (radkov2013aminoacidracemization pages 2-2)

Mechanistic insight for pseudomonad HyPRE-class enzymes is provided by a dedicated enzyme-assay/method paper (Gavina et al., 2010; ELECTROPHORESIS; Aug 2010; URL https://doi.org/10.1002/elps.201000187), which describes Pseudomonas aeruginosa HyPRE as a 36-kDa PLP-independent epimerase that stereoinverts the α-carbon via a dual acid/base mechanism involving catalytic thiol sulfhydryls, and notes structural homology to proline racemase (PRAC). While this is not the P. putida enzyme, it supports a family-consistent catalytic logic for ProR/HypE-like proteins in pseudomonads. (gavina2010determinationof4‐hydroxyproline‐2‐epimerase pages 5-6)

3) Biological processes and pathway integration

3.1 Hydroxyproline catabolism pathway position

In the pathway framing cited and discussed for Pseudomonas spp., trans-L-4-hydroxyproline is epimerized to cis-D-4-hydroxyproline by a 4-hydroxyproline epimerase, and the resulting D-form is further oxidatively metabolized through multiple steps to yield α-ketoglutarate. ProR in P. putida KT2440 is experimentally linked to this initial epimerization step, and the authors explicitly propose renaming the enzyme from “proline racemase” to 4-hydroxyproline epimerase. (radkov2013aminoacidracemization pages 7-8, radkov2013aminoacidracemization pages 5-7)

A 2024 comparative-genomics study in Spiribacter provides a contemporary depiction of the canonical pathway and intermediate names/enzymes (epimerase → D-Hyp dehydrogenase → spontaneous hydrolysis → deaminase → dehydrogenase) culminating in α-ketoglutarate, reinforcing the accepted biochemical route used to annotate EC 5.1.1.8 proteins. (leon2024integratinggenomicevidence pages 7-11)

3.2 Physiological evidence consistent with pathway involvement

Growth experiments reported in the P. putida KT2440 study indicate the organism can grow on either epimer of 4-hydroxyproline (cis-D and trans-L), consistent with the presence of an epimerase step that enables utilization of available stereoisomers. (radkov2013aminoacidracemization pages 2-3)
