# PP_4545 notes

PP_4545 is FabH2, one of two conventional KAS-III initiation enzymes in
Pseudomonas putida KT2440. It preferentially accepts medium-chain acyl-CoA
primers, in contrast to the short-chain preference of FabH1
[PMID:36796578 "The first two routes employ conventional β-ketoacyl-ACP
synthase III enzymes, FabH1 and FabH2, that accept short- and
medium-chain-length acyl-CoAs, respectively."].

The UniProt EC mapping is more specific than the broad KAS activity: GO:0061990
describes octanoyl-CoA plus malonyl-ACP forming 3-oxodecanoyl-ACP. The
TreeGrafter secondary-metabolite process annotation has no direct support in the
KT2440 initiation study.
