# vanA notes

- VanA (PP_3736; UniProt Q88GI6) is annotated in UniProt as the vanillate O-demethylase oxygenase subunit with EC 1.14.13.82. The sequence has an N-terminal Rieske [2Fe-2S] domain and a C-terminal VanA_C catalytic domain, consistent with the oxygenase component of a two-component aromatic O-demethylase.

- VanA is the oxygenase/Rieske subunit of the two-component VanAB vanillate O-demethylase in KT2440. The 2024 KT2440 O-demethylation engineering paper describes VanAB as the native Rieske non-heme iron monooxygenase and uses vanillate as the model substrate for pathway optimization. [PMID:38936762 "The native Rieske non-heme iron monooxygenase (VanAB)"]

- VanAB-overexpressing KT2440 strains showed faster vanillate utilization, and the same study identifies VanB as the reductase partner for vanillate O-demethylase. This supports vanA as part of the core vanillate catabolic module rather than an incidental side activity. [PMID:38936762 "The VanAB-overexpressing strains contained the most impactful mutations, including those in VanB, the reductase for vanillate O-demethylase"]

- VanAB also accepts syringate when overexpressed. A 2021 KT2440 study reports that syringate was O-demethylated to gallate by VanAB and that the specificity of VanAB for syringate was within 25% of that for vanillate. This broadens substrate scope but still points to vanillate O-demethylation as the native, core activity. [PMID:33741529 "syringate was found to be O-demethylated to gallate by VanAB, a two-component monooxygenase"] [PMID:33741529 "the specificity (kcat/KM) of VanAB for syringate was within 25% that for vanillate"]

- Disrupting vanAB blocks downstream vanillic acid metabolism in KT2440. In an engineered strain for vanillic acid production, a vanAB nonfunctional mutant was explicitly selected because it was defective in vanillic acid metabolism. [PMID:31809239 "The strain was engineered by rendering the van AB gene nonfunctional and obtaining the mutant defective in vanillic acid metabolism"]

- KT2440 natively grows on vanillate, and pathway summaries for lignin model compound conversion identify VanAB as the vanillate O-demethylase complex in the upper aromatic pathway. [PMID:28299400 "The specific growth rates on benzoate, p-coumarate, and 4-hydroxybenzoate were considerably higher (0.26-0.27 h^{-1}) than those on ferulate and vanillate"] [PMID:28299400 "VanAB vanillate O-demethylase complex"]

- VanAB abundance is also influenced by catabolite repression. Proteomics in an engineered KT2440 strain identified VanAB as a target of the global Crc regulator, so regulation can modulate flux through vanillate demethylation without changing the underlying catalytic role of VanA. [PMID:29188181 "we have identified the 4-hydroxybenzoate hydroxylase, PobA, and the vanillate demethylase, VanAB, as targets of the Catabolite Repression Control (Crc) protein"]
