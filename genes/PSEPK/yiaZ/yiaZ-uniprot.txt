CC   -!- FUNCTION: Sensor histidine kinase required for transcriptional response
CC       to isoprenol. Member of a two-component regulatory system YiaZ/PP_2665
CC       that responds to isoprenol and some other alcohols. Either directly or
CC       indirectly phosphorylates and activates response regulator PP_2665,
CC       which binds to and drives gene expression from the pedF (and other)
CC       promoters (Probable). Alcohol dehydrogenase YiaY is also required for
CC       activation. YiaY may modify the signaling alcohol and/or stabilize the
CC       alcohol ligand for binding by this protein (Probable). The ability of
CC       YiaZ to form heterodimers with histidine kinase PP_2664 modifies the
CC       response to alcohols; the YiaZ homodimer responds to isoprenol but
CC       poorly to 3-methyl-1-pentanol for example. The PP_2664 homodimer is not
CC       activated by isoprenol. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:41134890,
CC       ECO:0000305|PubMed:41134890}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY:
CC       Reaction=ATP + protein L-histidine = ADP + protein N-phospho-L-
CC         histidine.; EC=2.7.13.3; Evidence={ECO:0000305};
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homodimer (Probable). Forms a 1:1 complex with alcohol
CC       dehyrogenase YiaY. Forms a complex with histidine kinase PP_2664 that
CC       is probably a heterodimer. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:41134890,
CC       ECO:0000305|PubMed:41134890}.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: Cells no longer respond transcriptionally to
CC       isoprenol; complementation requires both this protein and the protein
CC       encoded by overlapping upstream gene yiaY.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:41134890}.
CC   -!- BIOTECHNOLOGY: Component of an isoprenol-responsive two-component
CC       system used as a transcriptional biosensor for growth-coupled selection
CC       and high-throughput screening to improve isoprenol production in
CC       P.putida KT2440. Created to optimize production of the aviation biofuel
CC       precursor isoprenol. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:41134890}.
