Gene Description

pobA encodes an FAD-dependent 4-hydroxybenzoate 3-monooxygenase that uses NADPH and molecular oxygen to hydroxylate 4-hydroxybenzoate to 3,4-dihydroxybenzoate (protocatechuate). This is a peripheral aromatic-catabolic step that feeds the protocatechuate branch of aromatic degradation rather than the unsubstituted benzoate-to-catechol branch.

Research report: Functional annotation of pobA (UniProt Q88H28, locus PP_3537) in Pseudomonas putida KT2440

0) Mandatory identity verification (critical disambiguation)

The UniProt entry Q88H28 corresponds to Pseudomonas putida strain KT2440 locus PP_3537 annotated as 4-hydroxybenzoate 3-monooxygenase (also called p-hydroxybenzoate hydroxylase; PHBH) and is placed in the aromatic “upper” pathway that converts 4-hydroxybenzoate (4-HBA) to protocatechuate (PCA; 3,4-dihydroxybenzoate). This identity is consistent with KT2440 pathway mapping and with PHBH/PobA family literature that uses “pobA/PHBH” for the same enzymatic step (ravi2017conversionoflignin pages 7-9, westphal2018pyridinenucleotidecoenzyme pages 15-15).

1) Key concepts and definitions (current understanding)

1.1 What PobA is

PobA (4-hydroxybenzoate 3-monooxygenase; EC 1.14.13.2) is a flavin-dependent monooxygenase that performs an ortho-hydroxylation of 4-HBA to form PCA, an essential “funneling” intermediate of bacterial aromatic metabolism (ravi2017conversionoflignin pages 7-9, tsagogiannis2024elucidationof4hydroxybenzoic pages 1-2).

1.2 Core biochemical reaction and stoichiometry

In P. putida KT2440, PobA is mapped to the step:

  • Substrate: 4-hydroxybenzoic acid (4-HBA)
  • Product: protocatechuic acid (PCA; 3,4-dihydroxybenzoate)
  • Co-substrates/cofactors (as shown in a KT2440 pathway diagram): NADPH + O2 + H+ → NADP+ + H2O (ravi2017conversionoflignin media 4266a7b4, ravi2017conversionoflignin pages 7-9).

This reaction definition matches the general description of PHBH/PobA enzymes as flavoprotein monooxygenases that consume reducing equivalents and oxygen to hydroxylate aromatic substrates (westphal2018pyridinenucleotidecoenzyme pages 15-15, tsagogiannis2024elucidationof4hydroxybenzoic pages 1-2).

1.3 Pathway role: β-ketoadipate “protocatechuate branch”

PCA produced by PobA is a central intermediate that can be cleaved by ring-cleavage dioxygenases and funneled into central metabolism (TCA cycle intermediates) via the β-ketoadipate pathway and related branches (tsagogiannis2024elucidationof4hydroxybenzoic pages 1-2, ravi2017conversionoflignin pages 7-9).

A 2024 systems-level analysis in Comamonas testosteroni (a different but relevant aromatic-degrading bacterium) summarizes the general logic: diverse aromatics (including 4-HBA) are funneled through PCA and then routed via alternative ring-cleavage routes (ortho and meta) with strong dependence on cellular redox (NAD(P)H) and multilevel regulation of flux (wilkes2023complexregulationin pages 1-2). This provides a modern conceptual framing for why the 4-HBA→PCA “gateway” step is often critical in aromatic metabolism engineering.

2) Molecular function: substrate specificity, cofactors, and enzyme family insights

2.1 Enzyme family and coenzyme specificity (NADPH vs NADH)

PHBH/PobA enzymes are classically described as flavoprotein monooxygenases that do not carry a canonical nucleotide-binding Rossmann domain, and coenzyme specificity varies by clade (NADPH-specific, NAD(P)H-dependent, or NADH-preferring) (westphal2018pyridinenucleotidecoenzyme pages 15-15).

Direct kinetic constants for KT2440 PP_3537 (Q88H28) were not recovered in the retrieved full text set, so KT2440-specific NADPH vs NADH preference cannot be asserted here beyond the pathway depiction that uses NADPH (ravi2017conversionoflignin media 4266a7b4). However, biochemical work on a PobA homolog from Agrobacterium tumefaciens demonstrates that PobA can utilize either NADH or NADPH, with higher activity on NADPH (4.53 U/mg vs 1.96 U/mg, in vitro; FAD-dependent), illustrating the plausible coenzyme range across homologs (xu2025functionandregulation pages 2-5).

2.2 Substrate specificity and engineered variants

A 2024 review of microbial lignin valorization highlights engineered PobA variants (e.g., PobAY385F) that alter/improve substrate usage toward PCA-related chemistry, indicating that PobA’s active site can be tuned for different aromatic hydroxylation/oxidation objectives (zhang2024tappingintothe pages 6-9). Consistent with this, a 2023 KT2440 metabolic-engineering study used a heterologous PobA* (Y385F) to push conversion from PCA toward gallic acid as part of a synthetic operon, underscoring the practical relevance of PobA mutability for product-directed biocatalysis (dias2023fromdegraderto pages 2-4).

3) Biological process context in Pseudomonas putida KT2440

3.1 Role in lignin-related aromatic funneling

Aromatic monomers from lignin (and model lignin compounds) are often converted into a smaller set of “central intermediates” such as PCA. In KT2440, a pathway map explicitly places PobA at 4-HBA→PCA, upstream of PCA ring cleavage, defining it as an upper funneling enzyme (ravi2017conversionoflignin media 4266a7b4, ravi2017conversionoflignin pages 7-9).

Physiologically, KT2440 grows efficiently on 4-HBA among lignin model compounds (reported specific growth rates ~0.26–0.27 h−1 on benzoate, p-coumarate, and 4-HBA under the tested conditions), consistent with an effective upper pathway that includes PobA (ravi2017conversionoflignin pages 7-9).

3.2 Regulation: carbon catabolite repression impacts PobA usage

A key KT2440-specific regulatory point retrieved here is that the global carbon catabolite repression regulator Crc (working with Hfq) targets translation of multiple aromatic-catabolic proteins; proteomics identified PobA among Crc targets. Deletion of crc in an engineered KT2440 background reduced accumulation of aromatic intermediates (including 4-HBA) and improved downstream product yields, consistent with derepression of the upper pathway that includes PobA (johnson2017eliminatingaglobal pages 1-3).

4) Cellular localization (evidence and inference)

No KT2440-specific experimental localization (e.g., fractionation, fluorescence tagging) for Q88H28/PP_3537 was present in the retrieved full text. Based on (i) its assignment to a soluble flavin monooxygenase family and (ii) its role in intracellular aromatic intermediate metabolism after substrate uptake, the most plausible localization is cytosolic (soluble). This statement should be treated as inference, not direct KT2440 evidence (westphal2018pyridinenucleotidecoenzyme pages 15-15, ravi2017conversionoflignin pages 7-9).

A broader 2024 review notes that P. putida KT2440 can secrete outer membrane vesicles and has an extracellular proteome enriched in aromatic-catabolism enzymes in lignin-rich contexts, but this does not directly demonstrate PobA secretion/periplasmic localization, and should not be interpreted as localization evidence for PobA itself (zhang2024tappingintothe pages 6-9).

5) Recent developments (prioritizing 2023–2024) and latest research themes

5.1 2023: multilevel regulation and redox constraints in aromatic funneling (generalizable insight)

Wilkes et al. (Nature Chemical Biology, 2023-02, https://doi.org/10.1038/s41589-022-01237-7) demonstrated in Comamonas testosteroni that aromatic catabolism through PCA is controlled by transcriptional activation plus metabolite-level thermodynamic regulation, and that 4-HBA→PCA requires reducing power (NADPH or NADH). Quantitatively, during growth on 4-HBA, secreted metabolites represented 28% of 4-HBA-derived carbon, with about 60% of carbon loss attributable to secretion of PCA/PDC before entry to central metabolism (wilkes2023complexregulationin pages 1-2). While not a P. putida KT2440 study, this is a high-authority 2023 reference framing why the 4-HBA→PCA gateway step and NAD(P)H supply are central design constraints in aromatic valorization.

5.2 2024: authoritative review synthesis for lignin valorization

Zhang et al. (Green Chemistry, 2024-01, https://doi.org/10.1039/d4gc03567d) synthesize microbial lignin valorization strategies and explicitly cite PobA as a key hydroxylation node in aromatic funneling, including discussion of engineered PobA variants (e.g., Y385F) used to redirect aromatic intermediates toward value-added products (zhang2024tappingintothe pages 6-9).

5.3 2024: near-theoretical-yield production regimes using P. putida (pobA deletion as a design lever)

Bruinsma et al. (bioRxiv, 2024-07, https://doi.org/10.1101/2024.07.06.602327) reprogrammed P. putida metabolism to make shikimate-pathway-dependent catabolism and used pobA deletion as part of a strategy to enable 4-HBA accumulation/excretion rather than catabolism. Flux balance analysis predicted a maximum 4-HBA yield of 0.39 mol/mol glycerol; engineered strains achieved 0.35 mol/mol glycerol (0.52 g/g), corresponding to ~89.2% of that predicted maximum (bruinsma2024shikimatepathwaydependentcatabolism pages 10-13, bruinsma2024shikimatepathwaydependentcatabolism pages 1-4). This modern study highlights how removing PobA activity can be beneficial when the engineering objective is 4-HBA production rather than 4-HBA utilization.

5.4 2024: updated multi-omics pathway elucidation for 4-HBA biodegradation (environmental/bioremediation relevance)

Tsagogiannis et al. (IJMS, 2024-01, https://doi.org/10.3390/ijms25020843) provides a recent, clear definition of the pobA step (4-HBA hydroxylation to PCA by a class A flavin monooxygenase) and shows, in a soil actinobacterium model, that downstream metabolism can proceed through multiple cleavage routes; it also provides quantitative biodegradation phenotypes (e.g., ~92% 4-HBA consumed by 12 h in their system) (tsagogiannis2024elucidationof4hydroxybenzoic pages 1-2, tsagogiannis2024elucidationof4hydroxybenzoic pages 2-4). This supports the current understanding that 4-HBA→PCA is a conserved entry step to diverse aromatic assimilation pathways.

6) Current applications and real-world implementations

6.1 Lignin valorization and platform chemical production in KT2440 (muconate)

In KT2440 engineered for muconate production from lignin-related aromatics, derepressing aromatic uptake/upper catabolism (including PobA as a Crc target) improves overall conversion. Johnson et al. (Metabolic Engineering Communications, 2017-12, https://doi.org/10.1016/j.meteno.2017.05.002) reported that crc deletion improved muconate yields (mol/mol) on glucose:
- From p-coumarate: 56.0 ± 3.0% → 94.6 ± 0.6% (36 h)
- From ferulate: 12.0 ± 2.3% → 28.3 ± 3.3% (72 h)
with improvements also under acetate co-feeding (johnson2017eliminatingaglobal pages 1-3).

Although the study optimizes a pathway-level objective and does not isolate PobA kinetics, it is strong evidence that PobA-linked upper pathway capacity is a practical bottleneck/lever in KT2440 aromatic bioconversion (johnson2017eliminatingaglobal pages 1-3).

6.2 Production of gallic acid in KT2440 using PobA variants

Dias et al. (International Microbiology, 2023-11, https://doi.org/10.1007/s10123-022-00282-5) demonstrate engineering of KT2440 to produce gallic acid from glycerol, using a synthetic operon incorporating a PobA* (Y385F) variant (heterologous) and deletions blocking degradation of PCA/GA. The reported final gallic acid concentration was 346.7 ± 0.004 mg/L after 72 h (dias2023fromdegraderto pages 1-2, dias2023fromdegraderto pages 2-4).

6.3 Transferability of PobA as a modular enzyme in heterologous hosts

A P. putida pobA expressed in E. coli supported in vivo conversion of 4-HBA to PCA (PCA detection), enabling downstream synthetic routes (e.g., muconate pathway builds) (sengupta2015metabolicengineeringof pages 13-17). This illustrates real-world synthetic biology usage: PobA provides a modular “entry” reaction from 4-HBA into PCA-centered chemistry.

7) Expert synthesis and analysis (authoritative perspectives)

  1. PobA is a gateway enzyme: modern lignin valorization frameworks repeatedly identify hydroxylation steps producing PCA as central “funneling” nodes, and PobA is the canonical 4-HBA→PCA entry point (zhang2024tappingintothe pages 6-9, ravi2017conversionoflignin media 4266a7b4).
  2. Redox supply is often the hidden constraint: 4-HBA hydroxylation consumes reducing power, and recent quantitative work shows that even when catabolic enzymes are induced, metabolite-level thermodynamic/redox constraints can dominate aromatic flux outcomes—important for designing strains where PobA flux must be high (wilkes2023complexregulationin pages 1-2).
  3. Engineering direction determines whether PobA is helpful or harmful:
  4. For utilization of 4-HBA (e.g., lignin streams), derepression/robust expression of PobA can improve downstream conversion and yields (johnson2017eliminatingaglobal pages 1-3).
  5. For production of 4-HBA as a product, disabling pobA prevents the cell from consuming the product and can enable near-theoretical yields in engineered regimes (bruinsma2024shikimatepathwaydependentcatabolism pages 10-13).

8) Quantitative data highlights (recent and foundational)

  • KT2440 aromatic assimilation: growth rates on 4-HBA reported around 0.26–0.27 h−1 under tested conditions (ravi2017conversionoflignin pages 7-9).
  • KT2440 pathway-level outcome: crc deletion improves muconate yield from lignin aromatics (up to ~94.6% mol/mol from p-coumarate on glucose) (johnson2017eliminatingaglobal pages 1-3).
  • KT2440 engineered product: 346.7 mg/L gallic acid at 72 h using a PobA* variant pathway (dias2023fromdegraderto pages 1-2).
  • 2024 high-yield production regime: 0.35 mol 4-HBA/mol glycerol (0.52 g/g), ~89.2% of predicted maximum (0.39 mol/mol) in engineered P. putida strains where pobA is deleted to avoid 4-HBA consumption (bruinsma2024shikimatepathwaydependentcatabolism pages 10-13).
  • 2023 systems context (Comamonas): 28% of 4-HBA-derived carbon secreted as metabolites; ~60% of carbon loss attributable to PCA/PDC secretion prior to central metabolism (wilkes2023complexregulationin pages 1-2).

10) Summary of function for annotation (KT2440 Q88H28)

pobA (PP_3537; UniProt Q88H28) encodes a cytosolic flavin-dependent monooxygenase (PHBH-like; EC 1.14.13.2) that hydroxylates 4-hydroxybenzoate to protocatechuate, consuming reducing equivalents and oxygen, thereby funneling lignin- and plant-derived aromatics into the protocatechuate branch of aromatic ring cleavage/β-ketoadipate metabolism. (ravi2017conversionoflignin media 4266a7b4, ravi2017conversionoflignin pages 7-9, westphal2018pyridinenucleotidecoenzyme pages 15-15)

pobA Notes

  • pobA in Pseudomonas putida KT2440 corresponds to UniProt Q88H28 / locus PP_3537 and is a 395 aa protein annotated as 4-hydroxybenzoate 3-monooxygenase with EC 1.14.13.2 [file:PSEPK/pobA/pobA-uniprot.txt "RecName: Full=4-hydroxybenzoate 3-monooxygenase"; "OrderedLocusNames=PP_3537"; "395 AA"].
  • The UniProt entry includes PDB 6DLL and explicit FAD-binding features, supporting a flavin-dependent monooxygenase mechanism rather than a generic oxidoreductase-only description [file:PSEPK/pobA/pobA-uniprot.txt "X-RAY CRYSTALLOGRAPHY (2.20 ANGSTROMS) IN COMPLEX WITH FAD"; "BINDING 13 /ligand=\"FAD\""].
  • Falcon deep research for KT2440 converged on the same pathway placement: PobA is the 4-HBA -> PCA gateway enzyme in the protocatechuate branch of aromatic degradation, with inferred cytosolic localization and NADPH/O2-dependent monooxygenase chemistry [file:PSEPK/pobA/pobA-deep-research-falcon.md "PobA catalyzing 4-HBA -> PCA with NADPH and O2"; "encodes a cytosolic flavin-dependent monooxygenase"].
  • KT2440 PobA is described as converting 4-hydroxybenzoic acid (4-HBA) to 3,4-dihydroxybenzoic acid (protocatechuate) using oxygen, FAD, and NADPH, which supports GO:0018659 4-hydroxybenzoate 3-monooxygenase activity as the core MF [DOI:10.1016/j.ymben.2021.12.010].
  • The automated GO BP annotations to benzoate metabolic process and benzoate catabolic process look overgeneralized for this gene because PobA acts on hydroxy-substituted benzoate and feeds the protocatechuate branch, not the unsubstituted benzoate-to-catechol branch [file:PSEPK/pobA/pobA-goa.tsv "GO:0018874 benzoate metabolic process"; "GO:0043639 benzoate catabolic process"].
  • GO:0050660 flavin adenine dinucleotide binding is the better cofactor-binding term to keep; GO:0071949 FAD binding is effectively redundant with it and is removed as a duplicate in other reviews in this repository [file:PSEPK/pobA/pobA-goa.tsv "GO:0050660 flavin adenine dinucleotide binding"; "GO:0071949 FAD binding"].
