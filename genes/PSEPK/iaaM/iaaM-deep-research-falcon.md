# Research Report: Functional Annotation of **iaaM / PP_4983 / UniProt Q88D45** in *Pseudomonas putida* KT2440

## 0. Executive summary (identity verification and key caveat)
UniProt accession **Q88D45** (ordered locus **PP_4983**) in *Pseudomonas putida* KT2440 is annotated as **tryptophan 2-monooxygenase** (EC **1.13.12.3**) and placed in the **tryptophan 2-monooxygenase (IaaM/TMO) family**, a flavin-dependent enzyme classically associated with the **indole-3-acetamide (IAM) pathway** for bacterial auxin (IAA) biosynthesis. However, literature retrieved here did **not** provide KT2440- and PP_4983-specific experimental evidence confirming that this particular KT2440 protein catalyzes L-tryptophan → IAM in vivo or in vitro.

A key disambiguation point is that authoritative synthesis work on bacterial IAA pathways reports that **some *P. putida* KT2440 “iaaM-like” homologs fall into a divergent clade** and that a KT2440 representative (named **DavB** in that review context) has been experimentally linked to **lysine degradation rather than confirmed IAA biosynthesis**, underscoring that the symbol **iaaM** can be misleading in *P. putida* unless validated at the locus/protein level. (duca2014indole3aceticacidin pages 8-9)

Accordingly, the functional discussion below is split into (i) **high-confidence information about the IaaM/TMO enzyme family (EC 1.13.12.3)** from primary biochemical studies and recent engineering work, and (ii) **careful inference** for **Q88D45/PP_4983** based on that family context, explicitly noting what is and is not demonstrated for KT2440.

## 1. Key concepts and definitions (current understanding)

### 1.1 The IAM pathway and the role of iaaM
In the canonical IAM pathway of bacterial auxin biosynthesis, **L-tryptophan is converted to indole-3-acetamide (IAM) by tryptophan 2-monooxygenase (IaaM/TMO)**, and **IAM is hydrolyzed to indole-3-acetic acid (IAA) by an IAM hydrolase (IaaH)**. (duca2014indole3aceticacidin pages 8-9, duca2020indole3aceticacidbiosynthesis pages 8-9)

The genetic assignment of these enzymes in plant-pathogenic *Pseudomonas* has been established at the operon level: **iaaM encodes tryptophan 2-monooxygenase (EC 1.13.12.3)** and **iaaH encodes indoleacetamide (IAM) hydrolase**. (yamada1985nucleotidesequencesof pages 1-2)

### 1.2 Operon organization and cotranscription (canonical system)
In *Pseudomonas syringae* subsp. *savastanoi* (historical “*P. savastanoi*”), **iaaM and iaaH are adjacent and cotranscribed**: Northern blots detect a shared ~4–5 kb transcript; insertions in **iaaM** have strong **polar effects** on **iaaH** expression and produce truncated transcripts, indicating a polycistronic operon with **iaaH promoter-distal**. (palm1989cotranscriptionofgenes pages 5-6, palm1989cotranscriptionofgenes pages 5-5)

Sequence-level work similarly supports operon coupling: insertions in **iaaM** produce polar effects on **iaaH**, consistent with shared transcriptional control via the iaaM promoter. (yamada1985nucleotidesequencesof pages 3-4)

### 1.3 Why KT2440 “iaaM” can be ambiguous
A highly cited synthesis of IAA pathways in plant–microbe systems reports that **iaaM homologs separate into groups**, with only one group corresponding to confirmed IAM-pathway IAA synthesis, whereas other homologs are **diverse and may have different substrates/roles**; the same source specifically cautions that a *P. putida* KT2440 representative (referred to as DavB) was associated with **lysine degradation**, not confirmed IAA synthesis. (duca2014indole3aceticacidin pages 8-9)

## 2. Biochemical function of tryptophan 2-monooxygenase (EC 1.13.12.3): reaction, cofactor, and mechanism

### 2.1 Catalyzed reaction
Primary biochemical characterization in *P. syringae* pv. *savastanoi* established that tryptophan 2-monooxygenase catalyzes **oxidative decarboxylation of L-tryptophan to indole-3-acetamide (IAM)** with concomitant O2 uptake; this is the first committed step in the IAM→IAA route. (hutcheson1985regulationof3indoleacetic pages 1-2, hutcheson1985regulationof3indoleacetic pages 4-5)

A recent enzyme-engineering study (2024) likewise defines TMO as an **FAD-bound flavoenzyme** catalyzing **L-tryptophan + O2 → IAM + CO2**, explicitly framing it as the first step of IAA biosynthesis via IAM. (kongjaroon2024enhancementoftryptophan pages 1-2, kongjaroon2024enhancementoftryptophan pages 2-4)

### 2.2 Cofactor and enzyme class
Tryptophan 2-monooxygenase is a **flavoprotein** containing **one FAD per ~62 kDa subunit** in the classic *Pseudomonas* system. (hutcheson1985regulationof3indoleacetic pages 1-2, hutcheson1985regulationof3indoleacetic pages 4-5)

The 2024 engineering paper uses spectroscopic monitoring consistent with **enzyme-bound FAD** (e.g., absorbance at 466 nm) and explicitly models the enzyme with bound FAD in molecular dynamics analyses. (kongjaroon2024enhancementoftryptophan pages 9-10, kongjaroon2024enhancementoftryptophan pages 11-12)

### 2.3 Substrate specificity (classic plant-pathogen enzyme)
The purified *P. savastanoi* tryptophan 2-monooxygenase accepts multiple substituted tryptophans (e.g., 4–7 methyl-tryptophan, 7-aza-tryptophan, 5-fluoro-tryptophan, 5-hydroxy-tryptophan) but **does not accept D-tryptophan or α-methyltryptophan**, indicating stereospecificity and constraints on α-carbon substitution. (hutcheson1985regulationof3indoleacetic pages 4-5)

## 3. Quantitative properties and regulation: kinetics and feedback inhibition

### 3.1 Classic kinetic constants and inhibition by IAM/IAA
For the *P. savastanoi* enzyme, apparent kinetic parameters include **Km(L-Trp) ≈ 50 ± 5 µM** and an apparent **Vmax ≈ 14 pmol O2 uptake·mg−1·min−1** under the reported assay conditions. (hutcheson1985regulationof3indoleacetic pages 4-5)

A key regulatory feature is **feedback inhibition** by pathway intermediates: IAM is a strong competitive inhibitor (apparent **Ki ≈ 7 µM**, with ~50% inhibition at ~25 µM IAM at near-Km tryptophan), while IAA is substantially weaker (apparent inhibitory constant ~**225–230 µM**). (hutcheson1985regulationof3indoleacetic pages 4-5, hutcheson1985regulationof3indoleacetic pages 1-2)

### 3.2 Canonical regulation at the gene/operon level (plant pathogens)
Review synthesis notes that IAM-pathway genes in plant pathogens (e.g., *P. savastanoi*) can be **plasmid-borne**, highly expressed in planta, and that tryptophan can induce IAA-biosynthetic genes in some bacteria; however, regulation is **not uniform across species/strains** and is still considered incompletely understood. (duca2020indole3aceticacidbiosynthesis pages 8-9)

## 4. Recent developments (prioritizing 2023–2024)

### 4.1 2024: Semi-rational engineering of TMO for improved robustness
Kongjaroon et al. (RSC Chemical Biology, **July 2024**, https://doi.org/10.1039/d4cb00102h) improved thermostability of a *Pseudomonas* TMO while largely preserving kinetic function. The engineered variants **TMO-PWS** and **TMO-PWSNR** achieved **Tm = 65 °C** versus **Tm = 48 °C** for wild type (Table 3). (kongjaroon2024enhancementoftryptophan media 0c88a49e)

The same work reports steady-state kinetics (Figure 6A):
- WT: **KM = 92.8 ± 5.9 µM**, **kcat = 8.07 ± 0.22 s−1**
- PWS: **KM = 117.2 ± 4.1 µM**, **kcat = 8.54 ± 0.21 s−1**
- PWSNR: **KM = 139.7 ± 9.2 µM**, **kcat = 8.81 ± 0.17 s−1** (kongjaroon2024enhancementoftryptophan media 0c88a49e)

Thermal inactivation half-lives highlight the magnitude of stabilization: at 50 °C, WT **t1/2 = 2.2 min** versus **183.8 min** (PWS) and **199.6 min** (PWSNR); at 60 °C, WT **t1/2 = 0.4 min** versus **14.2–14.7 min** for engineered variants. (kongjaroon2024enhancementoftryptophan pages 7-8)

**Relevance to functional annotation:** these data reinforce that IaaM/TMO-family enzymes can be treated as modular biocatalysts (IAM production from tryptophan) and that protein stability is a key limitation for implementation, which 2024 work directly addresses. (kongjaroon2024enhancementoftryptophan pages 1-2, kongjaroon2024enhancementoftryptophan pages 7-8)

### 4.2 2024: Ecological and genomic evidence for widespread IAA potential (including IAM genes)
Khalil et al. (Microbiome, **October 2024**, https://doi.org/10.1186/s40168-024-01899-6) sequenced 14 diatom-associated marine bacterial isolates and reports that all strains possessed orthologues for one or more IAA biosynthesis routes, including the **IAM pathway genes (IaaM, IaaH)** listed in their pathway gene set/figure legend. (khalil2024widespreadproductionof pages 5-8)

They quantified extracellular IAA exudation and linked it to function: extracellular IAA was positively correlated with diatom growth (**Pearson R = 0.73**, p < 0.05), and experimental IAA supplementation at **10−7 µg/mL** yielded a **2.5-fold increase** in diatom cell numbers after 7 days relative to control conditions. (khalil2024widespreadproductionof pages 8-11)

**Relevance to functional annotation:** this positions bacterial IAA production (including IAM-pathway capacity) as an ecologically meaningful trait beyond classic plant pathogenesis, reinforcing modern interest in IaaM/TMO genes as determinants of cross-kingdom signaling. (khalil2024widespreadproductionof pages 5-8, khalil2024widespreadproductionof pages 8-11)

### 4.3 2023: Synthetic biology perspective—microbial production of agrochemicals
A 2023 perspective/review on engineering microbes to overproduce natural products for agricultural use (Synthetic and Systems Biotechnology, **March 2023**, https://doi.org/10.1016/j.synbio.2022.11.005) provides context for deploying engineered metabolic modules (including phytohormone pathways) as “agrochemicals,” supporting the broader trend that enzymes like IaaM/TMO can be repurposed in microbial chassis for agriculture-adjacent applications. (hutcheson1985regulationof3indoleacetic pages 2-2)

## 5. Applications and real-world implementations

### 5.1 Bioengineering and biocatalysis
The engineered thermostable TMO variants (2024) are explicitly positioned as enabling “future studies” and improving soluble expression yield and stability while maintaining catalytic performance, which are core constraints for industrial enzyme deployment (e.g., IAM synthesis as an intermediate or as part of IAA-producing modules). (kongjaroon2024enhancementoftryptophan pages 1-2, kongjaroon2024enhancementoftryptophan pages 7-8)

### 5.2 Microbiome and ecological applications
The 2024 Microbiome study demonstrates a real-world ecological linkage between **bacterial IAA exudation** and **enhanced diatom growth**, suggesting that IAA biosynthetic potential (including IAM genes) can be a functional trait shaping microbial–eukaryote interactions in marine systems; exuded IAA reached isolate-specific maxima such as **2.7 ± 1 × 10−8 µg/cell** in their dataset. (khalil2024widespreadproductionof pages 5-8)
