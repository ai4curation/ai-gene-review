Gene Description

benC encodes the electron-transfer component of the BenABC benzoate 1,2-dioxygenase system. It is a ferredoxin-NAD+ reductase/[2Fe-2S] protein that supplies reducing equivalents to the terminal dioxygenase components, enabling the first hydroxylating step of benzoate catabolism in KT2440.

Research report: benC (PP_3163; UniProt Q88I38) in Pseudomonas putida KT2440

Executive summary (identity verification)

The UniProt target Q88I38 corresponds to benC / PP_3163 in Pseudomonas putida strain KT2440 and is functionally associated with the benzoate 1,2-dioxygenase (Ben) peripheral pathway that converts benzoate to catechol. Multiple independent sources converge on the same functional identity: BenC is the electron-transfer (reductase) component of the multicomponent benzoate 1,2-dioxygenase system encoded in the ben gene cluster. (jeffrey1992characterizationofpseudomonas pages 1-1, perezpantoja2008metabolicreconstructionof pages 5-5, zavalameneses2024proteogenomiccharacterizationof pages 8-9)

1) Key concepts and definitions (current understanding)

1.1 Benzoate 1,2-dioxygenase (BenDO) and “electron transfer component”

Aerobic benzoate utilization in many bacteria begins with a ring-hydroxylating dioxygenase that catalyzes dihydroxylation of the aromatic ring to form a non-aromatic cis-dihydrodiol (“benzoate diol”). In Pseudomonas benzoate metabolism, the structural genes benABC encode the initiating benzoate dioxygenase system responsible for benzoate → benzoate diol conversion. (jeffrey1992characterizationofpseudomonas pages 1-1, beharry2002characterizationofanthranilate pages 65-72)

In this system, BenC is the electron transfer component (reductase) that delivers reducing equivalents required for oxygen activation and substrate dihydroxylation by the oxygenase component. (perezpantoja2008metabolicreconstructionof pages 5-5, beharry2002characterizationofanthranilate pages 65-72)

1.2 Peripheral pathway to catechol and entry into central metabolism

A key concept in aromatic catabolism is modularity: a “peripheral” pathway transforms a specific aromatic compound into a few “central” intermediates (e.g., catechol), which then enter broad central routes (e.g., catechol ortho-cleavage). In P. putida, benD encodes a benzoate diol dehydrogenase that converts the benzoate cis-dihydrodiol product into catechol. (jeffrey1992characterizationofpseudomonas pages 1-1, perezpantoja2008metabolicreconstructionof pages 5-5)

2) Function, mechanism, and substrate specificity

2.1 Primary biochemical function of BenC (what it does)

BenC functions as the reductase/electron-transfer partner of benzoate 1,2-dioxygenase, providing electrons (ultimately from NADH/NAD(P)H) to drive the oxygenation of benzoate by the oxygenase component (BenAB in many naming schemes; benABC in the Pseudomonas locus context). (beharry2002characterizationofanthranilate pages 65-72, jeffrey1992characterizationofpseudomonas pages 1-1)

2.2 Enzyme system architecture and cofactors

Biochemical characterization of Pseudomonas benzoate 1,2-dioxygenase indicates a multicomponent system in which the oxygenase is an α3β3 complex containing a Rieske-type [2Fe-2S] center and mononuclear iron, while the associated reductase component is monomeric and contains FAD and a plant-type [2Fe-2S] cluster—i.e., an electron-transfer module consistent with BenC’s role. (beharry2002characterizationofanthranilate pages 65-72)

Direct structural/biochemical data for KT2440 Q88I38 itself were not retrieved here; however, a close homolog (Acinetobacter ADP1 BenC) has been structurally and biochemically characterized and exhibits a domain architecture that aligns with UniProt/InterPro expectations for Q88I38: an N-terminal [2Fe-2S] ferredoxin-like domain, a FAD-binding domain, and a C-terminal NAD(H)-binding domain. (beharry2002characterizationofanthranilate pages 99-106, beharry2002characterizationofanthranilate pages 86-93, beharry2002characterizationofanthranilate pages 93-99)

2.3 Reaction coupling and substrate preference (system-level properties)

For purified Pseudomonas BenDO activity, the reported stoichiometry for benzoate conversion is NADH:O2:benzoate:benzoate diol = 1:1:1:1, consistent with tightly coupled dioxygenation. (beharry2002characterizationofanthranilate pages 65-72)

Substrate preference described for Pseudomonas benzoate 1,2-dioxygenase includes a preference for meta-substituted benzoates, indicating that while benzoate is the canonical substrate, the enzyme system can accept substituted benzoates with some specificity constraints (system-level behavior, not necessarily unique to BenC). (beharry2002characterizationofanthranilate pages 65-72)

3) Pathway context in P. putida KT2440 (benzoate → catechol → catechol cleavage)

3.1 Genetic evidence for pathway function

Classic genetic/physiological work in Pseudomonas putida identified mutants unable to catabolize benzoate to catechol, including strains accumulating the cis-diol intermediate. Cloned chromosomal fragments containing benD, benABC, and a regulatory region (benR) complemented benzoate-negative phenotypes and enabled benzoate removal and catechol production, supporting the in vivo role of the ben genes in benzoate-to-catechol conversion. (jeffrey1992characterizationofpseudomonas pages 1-1)

3.2 Operon/cluster organization and linkage to catechol metabolism

A recent comparative genomics figure (2024) explicitly annotates benA, benB, benC as benzoate 1,2-dioxygenase components (with benC = ferredoxin reductase component) and places these near a catA-II locus in P. putida KT2440, reinforcing the functional coupling between benzoate activation and catechol-processing genes. (zavalameneses2024proteogenomiccharacterizationof media b208bc8e, zavalameneses2024proteogenomiccharacterizationof media 9ae478a3)

4) Cellular localization (what is supported vs inferred)

No direct subcellular localization experiment for BenC (Q88I38) in KT2440 was retrieved in the evidence assembled here. Based on its role as a soluble reductase/electron-transfer protein supplying electrons to an intracellular dioxygenase system, BenC is most consistent with a cytosolic (intracellular) enzyme associated functionally with the benzoate dioxygenase complex; however, this remains an inference from enzymology and gene-cluster context rather than direct localization evidence. (beharry2002characterizationofanthranilate pages 86-93, beharry2002characterizationofanthranilate pages 65-72, zavalameneses2024proteogenomiccharacterizationof pages 8-9)

5) Regulation and signaling/control of the ben pathway (expert-level mechanistic model)

5.1 BenR is the pathway-specific activator for benABCD

In P. putida, BenR is the transcriptional activator required for expression of the benABCD benzoate peripheral pathway genes. (moreno2008thetargetfor pages 1-2, jeffrey1992characterizationofpseudomonas pages 1-1)

5.2 Global catabolite repression via Crc targets benR translation

A detailed mechanistic study demonstrated that the Crc global regulator controls the benzoate pathway primarily by post-transcriptional repression of benR translation: Crc binds the 5′ region of benR mRNA and reduces BenR protein levels, thereby decreasing activation of the benABCD operon (and thus lowering BenC expression as part of the operon output). (moreno2008thetargetfor pages 2-2, moreno2008thetargetfor pages 5-6, moreno2008thetargetfor pages 1-2)

Quantitative regulatory effects (KT2440 context): in LB + benzoate, the presence of functional Crc represses benA ~70-fold, catB ~80-fold, and pcaI ~240-fold (mRNA-level differences), while benR mRNA is not strongly affected—supporting a translational control mechanism for benR. (moreno2008thetargetfor pages 3-4)

Induction by benzoate: addition of benzoate can induce benA up to ~85-fold and catB ~25-fold in the examined conditions, consistent with strong substrate-responsive activation of peripheral and central catabolic genes once repression is relieved and/or in appropriate growth states. (moreno2008thetargetfor pages 4-5)

6) Recent developments (prioritizing 2023–2024) and current applications

6.1 Metabolic engineering and biomanufacturing (2023)

Recent synthetic/systems biotechnology work leverages aromatic catabolism modules (including the BenABC/BenD benzoate→catechol route) to channel aromatic substrates toward industrial products such as cis,cis-muconic acid (ccMA), an important nylon/PET-precursor candidate chemical. In engineered Pseudomonas, pathway interventions (e.g., disabling competing catechol cleavage routes and downstream muconate consumption) are used to accumulate ccMA while using aromatic catabolic steps upstream to supply catechol. (He et al., 2023-09-01, https://doi.org/10.1016/j.synbio.2023.08.001) (he2023microbialproductionof pages 3-4)

While BenC (Q88I38) is not singled out biochemically in this 2023 engineering paper, the work illustrates a key real-world implementation: the benzoate dioxygenase module is treated as a reusable metabolic “input” block to supply catechol for downstream production pathways. (he2023microbialproductionof pages 3-4)

6.2 Comparative genomics/proteogenomics of aromatic degradation (2024)

A 2024 proteogenomic/comparative genomics analysis of Pseudomonas veronii (with explicit comparison panels including P. putida KT2440) highlights the genomic colocalization and modular organization of ben and cat gene clusters, with explicit annotation of benC as the ferredoxin reductase component. Such studies reflect current practice: integrating genome context and proteomics to infer regulatory coordination and biodegradation capabilities in environmental pseudomonads. (Zavala-Meneses et al., 2024-04, https://doi.org/10.3390/microorganisms12040753) (zavalameneses2024proteogenomiccharacterizationof pages 8-9, zavalameneses2024proteogenomiccharacterizationof media 9ae478a3)

benC (Q88I38, PP_3163) in Pseudomonas putida KT2440: The Reductase Component of Benzoate 1,2-Dioxygenase

Summary

benC (UniProt Q88I38; ordered locus PP_3163) in Pseudomonas putida strain KT2440 encodes the soluble, cytoplasmic reductase (electron-transfer) component of benzoate 1,2-dioxygenase. It is a class IB Rieske non-heme iron dioxygenase reductase — a single polypeptide that fuses three redox-cofactor domains (a plant-type [2Fe-2S] ferredoxin domain, an FAD-binding domain, and an NAD(H)-binding domain). Its molecular function is to accept a hydride (two electrons) from NADH, pass those electrons one at a time through its bound FAD flavin and its [2Fe-2S] cluster, and deliver them directly to the terminal oxygenase (the BenA/BenB heterocomplex). The oxygenase then uses those electrons plus molecular O₂ to carry out cis-1,2-dihydroxylation of benzoate, producing benzoate-cis-1,2-dihydrodiol (also called cis-1,2-dihydroxycyclohexa-3,5-diene-1-carboxylate, DHCDC). This is the first committed and rate-setting step of aerobic benzoate catabolism.

The two EC numbers annotated for Q88I38 reflect the same protein viewed at two levels: EC 1.14.12.10 (benzoate 1,2-dioxygenase) describes the overall multi-component reaction that benC participates in, while EC 1.18.1.3 (ferredoxin—NAD(P)⁺ reductase-type activity) describes benC's own intrinsic catalytic activity as an NAD(H)-dependent oxidoreductase. Importantly, benC does not itself bind benzoate and does not determine substrate specificity; specificity is dictated by the oxygenase α-subunit (BenA). benC's role is purely to serve as the electron conduit.

The physiological context is well established in the target organism. In P. putida KT2440, benC is part of the chromosomal benABC operon, which is induced by benzoate through the AraC/XylS-family transcriptional activator BenR. The pathway funnels benzoate → catechol → cis,cis-muconate → β-ketoadipate → succinyl-CoA + acetyl-CoA into the TCA cycle, via the catechol branch of the β-ketoadipate pathway. This report synthesizes 12 confirmed findings drawn from 32 papers, including the definitive 1.5 Å crystal structure of the direct BenC homolog from Acinetobacter sp. ADP1, biochemical reconstitution studies, genetic/regulatory dissection, and the modern systematic classification of Rieske oxygenases.

Identity verification (mandatory)

The gene symbol benC, the operon context (benABC), the organism (P. putida KT2440), the EC numbers, and the InterPro domain complement (2Fe-2S ferredoxin-type IPR001041; BenC-like FAD/NAD-binding IPR047683; beta-grasp IPR012675) are all mutually consistent and match a large, coherent primary literature on benzoate/toluate dioxygenase reductases. There is no ambiguity: this is the reductase (BenC) of benzoate 1,2-dioxygenase.

Key Findings

1. benC is the NADH-dependent reductase component of benzoate 1,2-dioxygenase

The single most definitive piece of evidence comes from the X-ray crystal structure of the direct structural homolog, BenC from Acinetobacter sp. strain ADP1, solved at 1.5 Å resolution (PMID: 12051836). This homolog shares >50% amino-acid identity with Q88I38 and comes from an identical benABC operon architecture. The authors state plainly that benzoate dioxygenase "contains a reductase component, BenC, that is responsible for the two-electron transfer from NADH via FAD and an iron-sulfur cluster to the terminal oxygenase component." This directly defines the molecular function of benC: it is the entry point for reducing equivalents into the dioxygenase system.

The structure reveals three domains, each binding a redox cofactor: iron-sulfur, FAD, and NADH, respectively. The [2Fe-2S] domain resembles that of plant ferredoxins, while the FAD and NADH domains belong to the ferredoxin:NADPH reductase (FNR) superfamily. This three-domain cofactor architecture is exactly mirrored by the InterPro domain annotations for Q88I38: the 2Fe-2S ferredoxin-type domain (IPR001041), the BenC-like FAD/NAD-binding domain (IPR047683), and the beta-grasp domain (IPR012675). The structure also shows that the iron-sulfur center and the flavin sit only ~9 Å apart, a geometry that "allows a fast electron transfer" — providing the physical basis for rapid intramolecular electron relay within the protein.

2. benC is a class IB Rieske dioxygenase reductase that transfers electrons directly to the oxygenase

The BenC structure was, at the time of publication, "the first determined for a reductase from the class IB Rieske dioxygenases, whose reductases transfer electrons directly to their oxygenase components" (PMID: 12051836). This is a mechanistically important distinction. In three-component Rieske systems (e.g., toluene dioxygenase, carbazole dioxygenase), a separate small ferredoxin protein shuttles electrons between the reductase and the oxygenase. In class IB systems like benzoate dioxygenase, there is no discrete ferredoxin gene in the operon; instead, benC itself carries the plant-type [2Fe-2S] domain fused to the FAD/NAD reductase module, so the electron chain collapses into a two-component system: oxygenase (benAB) + reductase (benC).

General studies of Rieske reductases contextualize benC's function. The reductase's task is to "shuttle electrons from NADH to the ferredoxin, a reaction the enzyme has to catalyze in the presence of dioxygen" (PMID: 22992736) — in benC's case, the [2Fe-2S] acceptor is fused rather than a separate protein, but the principle of managing electron flow in an O₂-rich environment is the same.

3. benC belongs to a two-component RHO system with a fused-domain FNR-type reductase

The authoritative Kweon et al. (2008) reclassification analyzed 130 bacterial Rieske non-heme iron aromatic ring-hydroxylating oxygenases (RHOs) and organized them into 5 types based on their electron-transport-chain (ETC) components (PMID: 18387195). "Type I represents two-component RHO systems that consist of an oxygenase and an FNRC-type reductase." Benzoate 1,2-dioxygenase — oxygenase BenAB plus reductase BenC (with its fused plant-type [2Fe-2S] domain and FAD/NAD FNR-like domains) — is precisely such a two-component system. This modern classification is fully consistent with the earlier "class IB" designation from the BenC crystal structure paper. The same review establishes that RHOs "are multi-component enzyme systems that are remarkably diverse in bacteria isolated from diverse habitats," placing benC firmly within this large, functionally coherent enzyme family.

4. benC carries ~1 FAD + 1 [2Fe-2S] per monomer and couples NADH oxidation stoichiometrically to O₂ consumption

Biochemical characterization of the closely related two-component anthranilate/benzoate dioxygenase system from Acinetobacter ADP1 (same enzyme family, benC-type reductase) established the cofactor stoichiometry (PMID: 11114907). The reductase component "was found to contain approximately one flavin adenine dinucleotide and one ferredoxin-type [2Fe-2S] center per 39-kDa monomer." The complete dioxygenase converts substrate "to catechol with insertion of both atoms of O₂ and consumption of one NADH," and this conversion is "stoichiometrically coupled to NADH oxidation and O₂ consumption."

Critically, this same enzyme, when tested with benzoate, "was converted to a nonaromatic benzoate 1,2-diol with similarly tight coupling. This latter activity is identical to that of the related benzoate 1,2-dioxygenase." This directly links benC-supplied electron flow to benzoate cis-diol formation with tight NADH/O₂ coupling — a hallmark of a well-behaved, non-leaky electron-transfer chain.

Reaction (overall system, EC 1.14.12.10):

benzoate + NADH + H⁺ + O₂ → benzoate-cis-1,2-dihydrodiol + NAD⁺

benC's intrinsic half-reaction (EC 1.18.1.3):

NADH + H⁺ + 2 oxidized acceptor → NAD⁺ + 2 reduced acceptor (electrons passed onward to the oxygenase's Rieske / mononuclear-Fe centers).

5. The flavin acts as a 2-electron/1-electron switch

benC solves a fundamental chemical problem: NADH is an obligatory two-electron donor, while the [2Fe-2S] cluster and the downstream oxygenase iron centers are obligatory one-electron carriers. The paradigm enzyme for understanding this switch is phthalate dioxygenase reductase (PDR), a structurally homologous three-domain iron-sulfur flavoprotein (PMID: 7589982). As the authors state, "Transfer of electrons between pyridine nucleotides (obligatory two-electron carriers) and hemes or [2Fe-2S] centers (obligatory one-electron carriers) is an essential step mediated by flavins in respiration, photosynthesis, and many oxygenase systems."

The mechanism proceeds in defined sequential steps: "Kinetic studies have identified sequential steps in the reaction of PDR with NADH that involve pyridine nucleotide binding, hydride transfer to FMN, and intramolecular electron transfer from the reduced flavin to the [2Fe-2S] cluster" (PMID: 7589982). In benC, the analogous flavin is FAD (rather than PDR's FMN), and the ~9 Å flavin-to-[2Fe-2S] distance enables fast intramolecular transfer. A related concern — avoiding wasteful reaction of the reduced flavin with O₂ — is addressed in the homologous toluene dioxygenase reductase, whose structure shows "the complex between NAD(+) and reduced reductase, which shows that the nicotinamide ring and the protein matrix shield the reactive C4a position of the isoalloxazine ring" (PMID: 22992736), suppressing unwanted flavin-O₂ side reactions during electron shuttling.

6. Substrate specificity resides in the oxygenase α-subunit, not in benC

A crucial point for functional annotation: benC does not confer substrate specificity. Reconstitution and hybrid-oxygenase experiments with benzoate dioxygenase (BADO) from Acinetobacter ADP1 showed that "Reconstituted BADO(ADP1) utilized four of the seven tested benzoates in the following order of apparent specificity: benzoate > 3-methylbenzoate > 3-chlorobenzoate > 2-methylbenzoate" (PMID: 12949084) — a narrower specificity than the related toluate dioxygenase. By constructing hybrid oxygenases, the authors demonstrated that "The apparent substrate specificity of the alphaBbetaT hybrid oxygenase for these benzoates corresponded to that of BADO(ADP1), the parent from which the alpha subunit originated." In other words, swapping the α-subunit swaps the specificity; the reductase (benC) is functionally interchangeable and does not select substrates. This confirms benzoate as the preferred physiological substrate and localizes specificity determinants to BenA, not benC.

7. benC is evolutionarily homologous to related aromatic-ring dioxygenase reductases

benC belongs to a family of closely related reductases. Sequence alignment of the toluate 1,2-dioxygenase reductase XylZ (TOL plasmid pWW0) with BenC (benzoate dioxygenase reductase) shows that "comparison of aligned XylX-BenA, XylY-BenB, and XylZ-BenC amino acid sequences revealed respective identities of 58.3, 61.3, and 53%" (PMID: 1938949). The 2-halobenzoate 1,2-dioxygenase operon cbdABC from Burkholderia/Pseudomonas cepacia 2CBS shows ~52% identity to benABC, and its reductase CbdC has "a putative binding site for a chloroplast-type [2Fe-2S] center and possible flavin adenine dinucleotide- and NAD-binding domains" (PMID: 7530709) — the same domain architecture as benC. This conserved reductase design across multiple aromatic-acid dioxygenases reinforces the functional assignment of benC.

8. Expression of benABC (including benC) is benzoate-inducible via the activator BenR

In P. putida, the benABC operon is transcriptionally controlled by BenR, an AraC/XylS-family regulator whose deduced sequence is 62% identical to XylS. Genetic evidence is definitive: "A benR mutant isolated by random transposon mutagenesis was unable to grow on benzoate," and "benR activated expression of a benA-lacZ reporter fusion in response to benzoate" (PMID: 11053377). The same study confirms operon assignment: "The benABC genes likely encode benzoate dioxygenase, and benD likely encodes 2-hydro-1,2-dihydroxybenzoate dehydrogenase." The broader ben gene cluster comprises benABC (dioxygenase), benD (dihydrodiol dehydrogenase), benK (benzoate permease), benF (porin), and benE, with benR as the adjacent regulator.

9. benABC is a chromosomal peripheral pathway feeding the β-ketoadipate pathway in KT2440

Genomic analysis of KT2440 mapped the benzoate (ben) peripheral pathway on the chromosome, feeding into the catechol branch (cat genes) of the β-ketoadipate pathway. The KT2440 genome contains "the genes encoding the peripheral pathways for the catabolism of p-hydroxybenzoate (pob), benzoate (ben), quinate (qui)" (PMID: 12534466), which converge on "the protocatechuate (pca genes) and catechol (cat genes) branches of the beta-ketoadipate pathway." Proteomics of benzoate-grown KT2440 provided direct expression evidence: "Benzoate dioxygenase (BenA, BenD) and catechol 1,2-dioxygenase (CatA) were induced by benzoate" (PMID: 16470664).

Functional evidence that benABC catalyzes the first (dihydroxylation) step comes from an overexpression experiment: "Genes benABC encoding benzoate dioxygenase were cloned into plasmid pSYM01 and overexpressed in P. putida KTSY01. The recombinant bacteria P. putida KTSY01 (pSYM01) showed strong ability to transform benzoate to DHCDC" (PMID: 18679676) — confirming that benABC (including benC) converts benzoate to the cis-dihydrodiol product.

10. benC initiates catabolic flux from benzoate into central metabolism

The benABC/benD-initiated pathway converts benzoate to catechol, which is processed by the catechol branch (catechol → cis,cis-muconate → ... → β-ketoadipate → succinyl-CoA + acetyl-CoA → TCA cycle). This in vivo flux is exploited biotechnologically: engineered P. putida KT2440 produces cis,cis-muconic acid "in a 42.5 L fermenter using glucose and benzoate as carbon substrates" (PMID: 32022244), demonstrating substantial catabolic throughput from benzoate through the benABC-initiated catechol pathway.

11. benC's pathway is directly studied in KT2440 — the target strain

This is not merely inference from homologs. The benzoate route is a strain-native, active pathway in KT2440: soil microorganisms "mineralize lignin-derived aromatic carbon sources using oxidative catabolic pathways, such as the β-ketoadipate pathway" (PMID: 29468117), and the dynamics of benzoate metabolism have been explicitly modeled in P. putida KT2440, integrating the benzoate pathway and its aromatic-carbon-source regulation into the KT2440 metabolic network.

12. benC is a soluble, cytoplasmic protein

All evidence points to a soluble cytoplasmic localization. The paradigm class member, phthalate dioxygenase reductase, is explicitly "a soluble iron-sulfur flavoprotein from Pseudomonas cepacia" (PMID: 7589982). The BenC homolog from Acinetobacter ADP1 was purified as a soluble recombinant protein and crystallized ("Here, we present the structure of BenC from Acinetobacter sp. strain ADP1 at 1.5 A resolution," PMID: 12051836). benC's InterPro domains are all soluble cofactor-binding folds (2Fe-2S ferredoxin-like, FAD/NAD-binding, beta-grasp), with no signal peptide or transmembrane segment. Consistent with cytoplasmic action, benzoate must first be imported (via the BenK permease/BenF porin) before intracellular oxidation.

Mechanistic Model / Interpretation

The electron-transfer chain

benC sits at the head of a short, fast electron-transfer chain that powers benzoate dihydroxylation:

                  benC (reductase, PP_3163)
          ┌───────────────────────────────────┐
   NADH  ──(hydride, 2e⁻)──▶  FAD  ──(1e⁻ each)──▶  [2Fe-2S]   │
   NAD⁺ ◀────────────────────┘                        │        │
          └────────────────────────────┼───────┘
                                        │ (1e⁻ each, direct)
                                        ▼
              BenAB terminal oxygenase (Rieske [2Fe-2S] + mononuclear Fe)
                                        │
                          benzoate + O₂ │
                                        ▼
                   benzoate-cis-1,2-dihydrodiol (DHCDC)
                                        │  BenD (dihydrodiol dehydrogenase)
                                        ▼
                                    catechol
                                        │  CatA (catechol 1,2-dioxygenase)
                                        ▼
                               cis,cis-muconate
                                        │  β-ketoadipate pathway (cat branch)
                                        ▼
              β-ketoadipate → succinyl-CoA + acetyl-CoA → TCA cycle

benC is a modular, single-polypeptide electron relay. NADH binds and delivers a hydride to FAD (a 2-electron event). The reduced flavin then passes electrons one at a time — first, intramolecularly, to the closely juxtaposed (~9 Å) [2Fe-2S] cluster, and then to the terminal oxygenase's Rieske center. Because benzoate dioxygenase is a class IB / Type I system, there is no intervening ferredoxin: benC's own [2Fe-2S] domain performs the role that a separate ferredoxin plays in three-component systems.

Division of labor within the enzyme complex

Component Gene Cofactors Role
Reductase benC (PP_3163, Q88I38) FAD + plant-type [2Fe-2S] Accept 2e⁻ from NADH; relay 1e⁻ at a time to oxygenase
Oxygenase α benA Rieske [2Fe-2S] + mononuclear Fe O₂ activation; substrate binding & specificity; catalysis
Oxygenase β benB (structural) Structural subunit of oxygenase

The key conceptual takeaway is a clean separation of function: benC handles electrons; BenA handles substrate and chemistry. Hybrid-oxygenase experiments proved that specificity travels with the α-subunit, not the reductase (PMID: 12949084).

The two EC numbers, reconciled

  • EC 1.14.12.10 (benzoate 1,2-dioxygenase): the overall reaction of the multi-component enzyme system that benC is a part of — benzoate + NADH + H⁺ + O₂ → benzoate-cis-dihydrodiol + NAD⁺.
  • EC 1.18.1.3 (ferredoxin—NAD(P)⁺ oxidoreductase-type activity): benC's own intrinsic catalytic activity — oxidation of NADH with transfer of electrons to a ferredoxin-type acceptor (here, its fused [2Fe-2S] and, downstream, the oxygenase).

Both annotations describe the same protein; benC is the reductase that makes the EC 1.14.12.10 reaction possible via its EC 1.18.1.3 chemistry.

Physiological role

benC enables P. putida KT2440 to use benzoate — and, through the same pathway, various lignin-derived aromatic acids — as a carbon and energy source. The operon is switched on only when benzoate is present (BenR-mediated induction), reflecting an economical, substrate-inducible catabolic strategy. The pathway's central-metabolic connection (via catechol → β-ketoadipate → TCA cycle) explains why KT2440 is a favored chassis for producing cis,cis-muconate and adipic acid precursors from aromatic feedstocks.

Supported:
- benC is the reductase/electron-transfer component of benzoate 1,2-dioxygenase (NADH → FAD → [2Fe-2S] → oxygenase). ✔ (PMID: 12051836, PMID: 11114907)

Refuted / excluded:
- benC is NOT the substrate-binding hydroxylase (that is BenAB). ✘
- benC does NOT determine substrate specificity. ✘
- benC does NOT require a separate ferredoxin partner (unlike class III systems). ✘

A note on evidence strength

The functional assignment of benC rests primarily on the 1.5 Å crystal structure of the direct Acinetobacter BenC homolog (>50% identity, identical operon), combined with biochemical reconstitution of the closely related ADP1 dioxygenase and detailed mechanistic work on the paradigm reductases PDR and toluene dioxygenase reductase. For the target organism P. putida KT2440 specifically, we have strong genetic (BenR regulation), genomic (operon mapping), and proteomic (benzoate induction) evidence that the pathway is native and active, plus functional overexpression evidence that benABC dihydroxylates benzoate. This is a well-supported annotation.

Limitations and Knowledge Gaps

  1. No direct crystal structure or enzymatic assay of Q88I38 itself. The definitive structural and biochemical characterization is of the Acinetobacter sp. ADP1 BenC homolog (>50% identity) and the ADP1 anthranilate/benzoate dioxygenase system, not of the P. putida KT2440 protein directly. Functional equivalence is strongly inferred from high sequence identity and identical operon architecture, but the KT2440 protein's specific kinetic parameters (Km for NADH, kcat, coupling efficiency, midpoint potentials) have not been measured in the reviewed literature.

  2. NADH vs. NADPH preference not experimentally pinned down for benC. By homology to class IB Rieske reductases, NADH is expected, and the ADP1 system uses NADH; however, the exact pyridine-nucleotide preference and its Km for Q88I38 were not directly quantified in the sources reviewed.

  3. Localization is inferred, not directly demonstrated for Q88I38. Cytoplasmic localization is a robust inference (soluble domains, no signal peptide, soluble homologs crystallized), but no direct fractionation/localization experiment on the KT2440 protein was found.

  4. Cofactor occupancy assumed by homology. The 1 FAD + 1 [2Fe-2S] stoichiometry comes from the ADP1 system; it is expected but not independently measured for the KT2440 protein.

  5. Quaternary structure and reductase–oxygenase docking geometry. How benC physically docks onto the BenAB oxygenase to deliver electrons, and whether a transient complex forms, remains less characterized for this specific system than for some three-component Rieske systems where ferredoxin–oxygenase interfaces have been mapped.

Proposed Follow-up Experiments / Actions

  1. Heterologous expression and biochemical characterization of Q88I38 (KT2440 benC). Purify the KT2440 protein and directly measure: cofactor content (FAD, [2Fe-2S] by UV-vis/EPR/iron & acid-labile sulfide assays), NADH vs. NADPH preference and Km, and diaphorase (cytochrome c / ferricyanide reduction) activity — to confirm the intrinsic EC 1.18.1.3 chemistry.

  2. Reconstitute the full KT2440 benzoate 1,2-dioxygenase in vitro (benC + BenAB) and verify stoichiometric, tightly coupled conversion of benzoate to benzoate-cis-1,2-dihydrodiol with 1:1 NADH:O₂:product ratios, replicating the ADP1 result for the target organism.

  3. Solve or model the KT2440 benC structure (an AlphaFold model is readily obtainable from the domain composition; an experimental structure would confirm the ~9 Å flavin–[2Fe-2S] geometry and validate the electron-transfer distance).

  4. Cross-complementation / interchangeability tests. Test whether benC can substitute for related reductases (XylZ, CbdC) and vice versa in reconstituted systems, to formally confirm that the reductase does not confer substrate specificity in the KT2440 context.

  5. Subcellular localization confirmation. Cell fractionation or fluorescent-tag microscopy of benC in KT2440 to experimentally confirm cytoplasmic localization.

  6. Targeted benC deletion in KT2440. A clean ΔbenC mutant should abolish benzoate-supported growth (or shift it to alternative routes), providing direct genetic proof of benC's essentiality for benzoate dihydroxylation in the native strain.

Conclusion

benC (Q88I38, PP_3163) is the soluble, cytoplasmic reductase component of benzoate 1,2-dioxygenase in Pseudomonas putida KT2440. As a class IB / Type I Rieske dioxygenase reductase, it uses a bound FAD and a plant-type [2Fe-2S] cluster to accept two electrons from NADH and relay them, one at a time, directly to the terminal oxygenase BenAB. Those electrons and O₂ drive cis-1,2-dihydroxylation of benzoate to benzoate-cis-dihydrodiol — the first committed step of aerobic benzoate catabolism, which funnels through catechol and the β-ketoadipate pathway into central metabolism. benC supplies reducing power but does not bind benzoate or set substrate specificity; its benABC operon is benzoate-inducible via the AraC/XylS regulator BenR.
