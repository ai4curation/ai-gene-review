# fleQ Gene Review Notes

## Gene identity and architecture

- `fleQ` in *Pseudomonas putida* KT2440 encodes the conserved FleQ-family enhancer-binding regulator (UniProt `Q88ET0`, locus tag `PP_4373`) with the expected AAA+ ATPase, sigma-54 interaction, and HTH DNA-binding architecture from UniProt/InterPro.
- FleQ is described in KT2440 as an atypical NtrC-family enhancer-binding protein with a central AAA+/ATPase `sigma54`-interaction domain and a C-terminal DNA-binding domain [PMID:30177764 Genome-wide analysis of the FleQ direct regulon in *Pseudomonas fluorescens* F113 and *Pseudomonas putida* KT2440, "FleQ is an atypical enhancer binding protein (EBP) from the NtrC family of bacterial transcription factors"].

## Core role: master regulator of flagellar biogenesis and motility

- Loss of `fleQ` causes a strong motility phenotype in *P. putida* and directly links FleQ to the flagellar lifestyle switch [PMID:27636892 Complex Interplay between FleQ, Cyclic Diguanylate and Multiple σ Factors Coordinately Regulates Flagellar Motility and Biofilm Development in *Pseudomonas putida*, "Disruption of fleQ caused strong defects in flagellar motility"].
- Under low c-di-GMP, FleQ preferentially activates the sigma-N-dependent flagellar cascade [PMID:27636892 Complex Interplay between FleQ, Cyclic Diguanylate and Multiple σ Factors Coordinately Regulates Flagellar Motility and Biofilm Development in *Pseudomonas putida*, "Low cyclic diguanylate levels favor FleQ interaction with σN-dependent promoters to activate the flagellar cascade"].
- ChIP-seq in KT2440 identified 103 FleQ binding sites, with strong enrichment at flagellar and motility-associated loci [PMID:30177764 Genome-wide analysis of the FleQ direct regulon in *Pseudomonas fluorescens* F113 and *Pseudomonas putida* KT2440, "103 FleQ putative binding sites in P. putida KT2440"; "Putative genes regulated by FleQ included, as expected, flagellar and motility-related genes"].
- The direct regulon paper also states that FleQ is the master regulator of flagella synthesis in pseudomonads and that `fleQ` mutants are non-motile and aflagellated [PMID:30177764 Genome-wide analysis of the FleQ direct regulon in *Pseudomonas fluorescens* F113 and *Pseudomonas putida* KT2440, "mutations in the fleQ gene result in non-motile, aflagellated bacteria"].
- An independent KT2442 study reached the same directional conclusion, noting that FleQ positively regulates flagella biosynthesis [PMID:28968917 FleQ regulates both the type VI secretion system and flagella in *Pseudomonas putida*, "FleQ positively regulates flagella biosynthesis"].

## c-di-GMP-responsive control of biofilm and surface colonization genes

- FleQ is also central to the sessile branch of the lifestyle switch: `fleQ` disruption reduces biofilm formation and surface attachment, and suppresses several high-c-di-GMP biofilm phenotypes [PMID:27636892 Complex Interplay between FleQ, Cyclic Diguanylate and Multiple σ Factors Coordinately Regulates Flagellar Motility and Biofilm Development in *Pseudomonas putida*, "Disruption of fleQ caused strong defects in ... biofilm formation and surface attachment"; "FleQ mediates cyclic diguanylate signaling critical to biofilm growth"].
- FleQ directly binds the `lapA` and `bcs` promoters in KT2440 and regulates them in opposite directions under baseline conditions [PMID:27120564 C-di-GMP regulates the expression of `lapA` and `bcs` operons via FleQ in *Pseudomonas putida* KT2440, "FleQ acts as an activator of lapA, but a repressor of bcs"; "FleQ binds to lapA and bcs promoter DNA"].
- c-di-GMP changes the promoter response rather than simply turning FleQ on or off: binding to `lapA` is slightly promoted by c-di-GMP, whereas binding to `bcs` is inhibited [PMID:27120564 C-di-GMP regulates the expression of `lapA` and `bcs` operons via FleQ in *Pseudomonas putida* KT2440, "The binding to lapA promoter was slightly promoted by c-di-GMP, while binding to bcs promoter was inhibited by c-di-GMP"].
- FleQ itself is a c-di-GMP-binding regulator in KT2440 and directly binds promoters of flagellar and EPS genes [PMID:27503246 FleQ of *Pseudomonas putida* KT2440 is a multimeric cyclic diguanylate binding protein that differentially regulates expression of biofilm matrix components, "FleQ of P. putida interacts with c-di-GMP and directly binds the promoter regions of flagellar and EPS genes"].
- FleQ adopts multiple oligomeric states in solution, consistent with its mechanistically flexible promoter control [PMID:27503246 FleQ of *Pseudomonas putida* KT2440 is a multimeric cyclic diguanylate binding protein that differentially regulates expression of biofilm matrix components, "FleQ is in multiple oligomeric states in solution (dimers, tetramers and hexamers)"].
- FleN is not simply an antagonist in KT2440 biofilm regulation; instead it synergizes with FleQ at `lapA` and `bcs` promoters [PMID:28517238 FleN and FleQ play a synergistic role in regulating `lapA` and `bcs` operons in *Pseudomonas putida* KT2440, "FleN and FleQ play a synergistic role in regulating two biofilm matrix coding operons, lapA and bcs"].
- Later work expanded the direct c-di-GMP/FleQ target set to include `lapE` and `cyaA`, showing that FleQ directly binds these promoters and that LapE-dependent LapA secretion is an important downstream mechanism [PMID:33975969 Identification of c-di-GMP/FleQ-Regulated New Target Genes, Including `cyaA`, Encoding Adenylate Cyclase, in *Pseudomonas putida*, "Both in vitro and in vivo assays show that FleQ binds directly to promoters of the five genes"; "LapE plays a central role ... in the secretion of adhesin LapA"].
- The same 2021 study concludes that FleQ is essential for the c-di-GMP-dependent biofilm branch because `fleQ` mutants lose normal c-di-GMP-mediated LapA secretion and biofilm output [PMID:33975969 Identification of c-di-GMP/FleQ-Regulated New Target Genes, Including `cyaA`, Encoding Adenylate Cyclase, in *Pseudomonas putida*, "FleQ was essential for c-di-GMP-mediated biofilm formation and LapA secretion"].

## Additional direct or proposed regulon branches

- The 2018 ChIP-seq study adds a broader direct regulon beyond motility and matrix genes, including adhesion, exopolysaccharide, and iron-homeostasis genes [PMID:30177764 Genome-wide analysis of the FleQ direct regulon in *Pseudomonas fluorescens* F113 and *Pseudomonas putida* KT2440, "others involved in adhesion and exopolysaccharide production"; "identified iron homeostasis-related genes for which positive regulation was shown by RT-qPCR"].
- A separate transcriptomic study indicates that FleQ also negatively regulates type VI secretion genes in KT2442 [PMID:28968917 FleQ regulates both the type VI secretion system and flagella in *Pseudomonas putida*, "FleQ positively regulates flagella biosynthesis but negatively regulates the type VI secretion system"].

## Curation takeaways for GO review

- `GO:0043565` sequence-specific DNA binding is core and directly supported by promoter-binding data.
- `GO:0005524` ATP binding is a defensible core mechanistic function because FleQ is an AAA+ enhancer-binding regulator, but the current cached KT2440 literature is stronger for ATPase-domain architecture than for a standalone ATP-binding assay in this strain.
- `GO:0006355` regulation of DNA-templated transcription is correct but too broad to represent FleQ biology on its own; the review should add more specific terms for flagellar regulation and c-di-GMP-responsive biofilm regulation.
- Iron-homeostasis and type VI secretion effects are real enough to mention in references and questions, but they are not the safest core GO additions for this first-pass review.
