Gene Description

ftsZ encodes the essential bacterial cell division protein FtsZ, a tubulin homolog and GTPase. FtsZ binds GTP and, in a strictly GTP-dependent manner, polymerizes into protofilaments that assemble into a dynamic contractile ring structure (the Z ring) at the cytoplasmic face of the inner membrane at the future division site (midcell). The Z ring marks the division plane and serves as the scaffold that recruits the downstream divisome proteins to the septum, driving constriction and synthesis of new cell wall (septum) to separate the two daughter cells. GTP hydrolysis controls the turnover and remodeling of the filaments, regulating the timing and positioning of cytokinesis.

In contrast, for non-model organisms such as those in the Ag100Pest Initiative, most, if not all, functional annotation is performed computationally, as (1) gene function in very few genes have been established experimentally for these non-model species, and (2) the capacity for literature-based curation of gene function does not yet exist for these species.
