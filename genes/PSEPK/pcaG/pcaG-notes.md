# pcaG notes

## 2026-03-20

- Identity check: UniProt Q88E13 is `pcaG` / `PP_4655` in *Pseudomonas putida* KT2440 and is annotated as the protocatechuate 3,4-dioxygenase alpha chain. The classical *Pseudomonas putida* cloning paper states that the cloned fragment contained `pcaH` and `pcaG`, "corresponding to the beta and alpha subunits, respectively, of 3,4-PCD" [PMID:8407791 "contain two successive open reading frames, designated pcaH and pcaG, corresponding to the beta and alpha subunits, respectively, of 3,4-PCD"].
- Complex architecture: *P. putida* protocatechuate 3,4-dioxygenase is an `alpha4beta4` enzyme with one ferric iron per `alpha beta` catalytic unit [PMID:6273403 "contains 4 alpha subunits of 23,000 daltons, 4 beta subunits of 26,500 daltons, and 4 ferric irons suggesting that the enzyme is a tetramer of (alpha beta Fe+3) catalytic units"].
- KT2440-specific evidence: the 2018 KT2440 biosensor paper explicitly deleted `pcaH`/`pcaG` and describes them as the two-subunit protocatechuate 3,4-dioxygenase [PMID:29765865 "deleting the genes pcaH and pcaG, which encode the two-subunit protocatechuate 3,4-dioxygenase"]. In that study, WT KT2440 metabolized PCA further through the beta-ketoadipate pathway whereas the `pcaHG` deletion strain accumulated PCA [PMID:29765865 "native (in which PCA is metabolized via the β-ketoadipate pathway)" ].
- Key curation point: the seeded GOA iron-binding terms appear wrong for `pcaG` specifically. Structural work places the Fe3+ ligands on beta-subunit residues: "Tyr447 (147beta) and His462 (162beta), and equatorial ligands, Tyr408 (108beta), His460 (160beta)" [PMID:9485360 "is bound by axial ligands, Tyr447 (147beta) and His462 (162beta), and equatorial ligands, Tyr408 (108beta), His460 (160beta)"]. A separate structure paper shows the alpha subunit contributes Tyr16 to the active-site environment after substrate binding: "Tyr447 is stabilized by hydrogen bonding to Tyr16 (16alpha) and Asp413 (113beta)" [PMID:9254600 "Tyr447 is stabilized by hydrogen bonding to Tyr16 (16alpha) and Asp413 (113beta)"].
- Interpretation: keep the specific dioxygenase activity term for `pcaG`, but treat it as a complex-mediated activity of the alpha chain rather than evidence that the alpha monomer independently binds ferric iron.
- Pathway context: *pca* genes are part of the central aromatic-catabolic branch. The Crc/BenR paper describes "the cat and pca genes of the central catechol and beta-ketoadipate pathways" [PMID:18156252 "the cat and pca genes of the central catechol and beta-ketoadipate pathways"].
- Conserved ortho-cleavage chemistry is also supported by a homologous *Pseudomonas* system: in strain HR199, `pcaG/pcaH` encode protocatechuate 3,4-dioxygenase homologs and "vanillin is degraded through the ortho-cleavage pathway" [PMID:10049847 "revealed high degrees of homology to pcaG and pcaH, encoding the two subunits of protocatechuate 3,4-dioxygenase"] [PMID:10049847 "vanillin is degraded through the ortho-cleavage pathway in Pseudomonas sp. strain HR199"].
