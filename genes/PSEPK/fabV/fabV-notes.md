# fabV curation notes

- Reviewed UniProt accession Q88E33 identifies FabV as the NADH-dependent
  final reductase of the FAS-II elongation cycle
  [file:PSEPK/fabV/fabV-uniprot.txt,
  "Involved in the final reduction of the elongation cycle of"].
- The exact enoyl-ACP reductase activity and fatty-acid biosynthesis are
  accepted; generic oxidoreductase activity is replaced by the exact term.
- `trans-2-enoyl-CoA reductase (NADH) activity` is removed because FabV uses an
  ACP-linked, not CoA-linked, substrate.
- NAD binding is retained as ancillary.
