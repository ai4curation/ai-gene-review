## Research report: **algA** (UniProt **Q88ND5**; locus **PP_1277**) in *Pseudomonas putida* KT2440

### 1) Target verification (gene/protein identity and disambiguation)
The KT2440 genome analysis explicitly identifies **PP1277** as a **homologue of algA** encoding a **bifunctional phosphomannose isomerase (PMI) and GDP-mannose pyrophosphorylase** enzyme, linking it to synthesis of the sugar-nucleotide precursor **GDP-D-mannose** from **fructose-6-phosphate**. This matches the UniProt Q88ND5 description (AlgA; PMI + GMPP) and supports that the correct target is the **algA-like bifunctional enzyme in *P. putida* KT2440**, not an unrelated “algA” from another organism. (santos2004insightsintothe pages 11-12)

### 2) Key concepts and current understanding (functional definition)

#### 2.1 Core biochemical function
**AlgA is a bifunctional cytosolic enzyme** that supplies two enzymatic steps in the canonical bacterial alginate precursor pathway:

1) **PMI activity (EC 5.3.1.8)**: interconverts **fructose-6-phosphate (F6P)** and **mannose-6-phosphate (M6P)** (often discussed as F6P → M6P in the forward direction toward alginate precursors). (gheorghita2023pseudomonasaeruginosabiofilm pages 4-5, gheorghita2023pseudomonasaeruginosabiofilm pages 5-7, serrato2024bacterialalginatebiosynthesis pages 6-8)

2) **GDP-mannose pyrophosphorylase / mannose-1-phosphate guanylyltransferase activity (EC 2.7.7.13)**: converts **mannose-1-phosphate (M1P) + GTP** into **GDP-mannose + PPi**. (keiski2010structuralandfunctional pages 36-42, serrato2024bacterialalginatebiosynthesis pages 6-8)

The mechanistic interpretation (two independent catalytic domains mediating the two activities) has strong biochemical support in **Pseudomonas AlgA homologs** (not KT2440-specific in the retrieved evidence), including **mutagenesis and limited proteolysis** consistent with domain separation and bifunctionality. (keiski2010structuralandfunctional pages 36-42, keiski2010structuralandfunctional pages 171-173)

#### 2.2 Pathway placement (alginate precursor synthesis)
In alginate-producing pseudomonads, **AlgA works with AlgC and AlgD** to generate **GDP-mannuronic acid (GDP-mannuronate; also described as GDP-mannuronic acid/GDP-ManA)**, the activated monomer precursor for alginate polymerization:

- **AlgA(PMI)**: F6P → M6P
- **AlgC**: M6P → M1P
- **AlgA(GMPP)**: M1P + GTP → GDP-mannose
- **AlgD**: GDP-mannose → GDP-mannuronic acid

This upstream precursor module is described as **cytoplasmic**, preceding the membrane-spanning polymerization/export machinery. (gheorghita2023pseudomonasaeruginosabiofilm pages 4-5, gheorghita2023pseudomonasaeruginosabiofilm pages 5-7, serrato2024bacterialalginatebiosynthesis pages 6-8)

The KT2440 genome review connects PP1277/algA specifically to formation of **GDP-D-mannose from fructose-6-phosphate**, aligning KT2440 algA with this precursor logic even when downstream alginate expression may differ by strain/condition. (santos2004insightsintothe pages 11-12)

#### 2.3 Substrate specificity and reaction directionality
Across the reviewed alginate pathway descriptions, the PMI step is consistently presented as **F6P → M6P** in the biosynthetic direction and the GMPP step as **M1P + GTP → GDP-mannose + PPi**. (keiski2010structuralandfunctional pages 36-42, gheorghita2023pseudomonasaeruginosabiofilm pages 4-5, serrato2024bacterialalginatebiosynthesis pages 6-8)

**Limitations:** the retrieved evidence did not include KT2440-specific enzymatic assays (Km/kcat, metal/ligand requirements, or directionality under physiological conditions). The most direct biochemical evidence in this corpus is from **Pseudomonas homolog enzymology**, which is appropriate for functional inference but not a substitute for KT2440-specific kinetics. (keiski2010structuralandfunctional pages 36-42, keiski2010structuralandfunctional pages 171-173)

### 3) Protein architecture and inferred cellular localization

#### 3.1 Domain architecture and evolutionary/structural inferences (2023 synthesis)
A 2023 authoritative review synthesizing recent structural modeling for *Pseudomonas* exopolysaccharide pathways describes AlgA as a **multidomain** enzyme. In *P. aeruginosa* numbering used in that review, AlgA includes:
- an **N-terminal Rossmann-like fold** typical of sugar-nucleotide-binding enzymes,
- a **left-handed parallel β-helix region**, and
- a **C-terminal cupin domain** predicted to support PMI activity.
These assignments are based on **AlphaFold modeling and structural homology comparisons**, because an experimentally determined AlgA structure was not available in that review’s summary. (gheorghita2023pseudomonasaeruginosabiofilm pages 7-8, gheorghita2023pseudomonasaeruginosabiofilm pages 4-5)

#### 3.2 Localization
AlgA is described as a **cytoplasmic enzyme** responsible for producing the activated sugar-nucleotide precursor used for alginate biosynthesis, consistent with its role in central sugar-phosphate/sugar-nucleotide metabolism and the cytoplasmic positioning of AlgA/AlgC/AlgD upstream of periplasmic modification and outer-membrane export. (gheorghita2023pseudomonasaeruginosabiofilm pages 4-5, ponce2025advancesinalginate pages 4-6, serrato2024bacterialalginatebiosynthesis pages 6-8)

### 4) Recent developments (prioritizing 2023–2024)

#### 4.1 2023: pathway organization hypotheses and structural modeling emphasis
The 2023 FEMS Microbiology Reviews synthesis emphasizes that (i) AlgA is bifunctional (PMI+GMPP), (ii) it is part of the cytoplasmic precursor module with AlgC and AlgD, and (iii) modern structural prediction/homology tools enable domain-level mechanistic hypotheses (e.g., potential precursor channeling or subcomplex formation), though some of these organizational ideas remain to be experimentally validated. (gheorghita2023pseudomonasaeruginosabiofilm pages 7-8, gheorghita2023pseudomonasaeruginosabiofilm pages 4-5)

#### 4.2 2024: renewed focus on tunability of bacterial alginate biosynthesis
A 2024 overview highlights that bacterial alginate biosynthesis can be tuned by **changing culture conditions and manipulating genes/proteins**, enabling production of polymers with altered molecular weight and composition; this context reinforces why enzymes like AlgA (precursor supply) remain key targets in pathway engineering and systems analysis. (serrato2024bacterialalginatebiosynthesis pages 6-8)

### 5) Current applications and real-world implementations (with quantitative metrics)

#### 5.1 Bioprocessing application: reducing biofilms/foam for fermentation robustness (2024)
A 2024 fermentation-focused study engineered *P. putida* strains by deleting **algA** (described there as involved in alginate accumulation) alongside motility/pilus genes (flhA, pilQ) to reduce biofilm formation and improve bioreactor performance characteristics. Key quantitative outcomes include:

- **Biofilm reduction:** deletion strains showed ~**20–30%** lower biofilm vs wild type at 24 h; the triple-deletion strain **LN6160 (ΔalgA ΔflhA ΔpilQ)** showed **33.3% lower** biofilm at 48 h and **40% lower** at 72 h. (frolov2024constructionofthe pages 5-8, frolov2024constructionofthe media 54846324)

- **Motility:** an **algA deletion alone** did **not** meaningfully reduce motility (spread diameter **28 ± 6 mm** vs wild type **32 ± 2 mm**), while strong motility loss required combined deletions with flhA (e.g., **5 ± 1 mm**). (frolov2024constructionofthe pages 5-8)

- **Growth in microplate assay:** final OD600 was similar between triple mutant and wild type (**2.005 ± 0.02** vs **1.98 ± 0.03**), with similar growth parameters (μ **0.79 ± 0.03** vs **0.74 ± 0.04**; doubling time **0.90 ± 0.04 h** vs **0.94 ± 0.05 h**). (frolov2024constructionofthe pages 5-8, frolov2024constructionofthe media f6ebb4bb)

- **Bioreactor-like growth (CFU):** after 24 h in rich medium, LN6160 reached **1.39 × 10^10 CFU/mL** vs **6.4 × 10^9 CFU/mL** for wild type; in mineral medium after 24 h, LN6160 reached **6.11 × 10^9 CFU/mL** vs **1.36 × 10^9 CFU/mL**. Reduced foaming was also noted. (frolov2024constructionofthe pages 8-10, frolov2024constructionofthe media b819b3ae)

**Interpretation/limitations:** these are *P. putida* data but not KT2440 specifically (the study uses *P. putida* PCL1760 derivatives). Also, many benefits are for combined deletions, so phenotypes cannot be attributed uniquely to algA loss. Nevertheless, the work illustrates a current, practical rationale for targeting alginate/EPS genes (including algA) to improve fermentation operability. (frolov2024constructionofthe pages 5-8, frolov2024constructionofthe pages 8-10)

### 6) Expert opinions and authoritative synthesis
The 2023 FEMS Microbiology Reviews article functions as a domain-authoritative synthesis that integrates genetics, biochemistry, and structural modeling to describe AlgA as a **cytoplasmic, bifunctional PMI/GMPP enzyme** providing activated precursors for alginate biosynthesis and to highlight where the field is transitioning from gene lists to structural/compositional mechanisms. (gheorghita2023pseudomonasaeruginosabiofilm pages 7-8, gheorghita2023pseudomonasaeruginosabiofilm pages 4-5)

### 9) Gaps and recommendations for KT2440-specific functional annotation
The retrieved literature strongly supports **identity and pathway function** of KT2440 AlgA (PP1277/Q88ND5) as a bifunctional PMI/GMPP enzyme (genomic annotation plus cross-Pseudomonas biochemical consensus), but does **not** provide KT2440-specific enzymatic constants, substrate-range testing, or direct localization experiments.
