## Research report: *oprF* (UniProt Q88L46; PP_2089) in *Pseudomonas putida* KT2440

### 1) Gene/protein identity verification (mandatory)

**Target identity confirmed.** In *Pseudomonas putida* KT2440, multiple KT2440-focused studies explicitly refer to **OprF as the major/general (non-specific) outer-membrane porin encoded by PP_2089**, matching the UniProt accession **Q88L46** and gene symbol **oprF** provided in the prompt (pobletecastro2020engineeringtheosmotic pages 2-4, pobletecastro2020engineeringtheosmotic pages 1-2). This is important because “OprF” is heavily studied in *P. aeruginosa*; in this report, cross-species claims are labeled as such.

### 2) Key concepts, definitions, and current understanding

#### 2.1 What is OprF?
OprF is described in KT2440 literature as the **major non-specific outer membrane porin** and a **pleiotropic trans–outer membrane protein** implicated in **outer membrane permeability and integrity** and in **maintenance of cell shape** (pobletecastro2020engineeringtheosmotic pages 1-2). It is part of the broader **OmpA/OprF family**, which typically combines a **β-barrel outer membrane domain** with a **periplasmic OmpA-like domain** that can interact with peptidoglycan (pobletecastro2020engineeringtheosmotic pages 1-2, romarodrigues2010responseofpseudomonas pages 17-18).

#### 2.2 Subcellular localization
OprF is consistently treated as an **outer membrane protein/porin** in KT2440 engineering work (pobletecastro2020engineeringtheosmotic pages 2-4, pobletecastro2020engineeringtheosmotic pages 1-2). Its detection among outer membrane proteins in KT2440 contexts supports this localization (pobletecastro2020engineeringtheosmotic pages 2-4, pobletecastro2020engineeringtheosmotic pages 1-2).

#### 2.3 Structural model and domains
A KT2440 membrane-proteomics study summarizes a widely cited **two-conformer model** for OprF-like proteins: 
- a predominant **closed-channel conformer (~95%)** consisting of an **N-terminal β-barrel plus a C-terminal periplasmic α-helix-containing region**, linked to **cell envelope stabilization**, and 
- a minority **open, pore-active conformer (~5%)** described as a **single larger β-barrel with more β-strands** (romarodrigues2010responseofpseudomonas pages 14-15).

An independent KT2440-focused engineering paper provides a consistent architectural description: OprF “encloses a large N-terminal beta-barrel channel” and has a “periplasmic C-terminal domain rich in alpha-helices” (pobletecastro2020engineeringtheosmotic pages 1-2). 

**Evidence strength note:** the 95%/5% conformer ratio is a mechanistic framework drawn from the broader OprF literature and summarized in a KT2440 study; it is best treated as conserved inference rather than a KT2440-specific structural determination (romarodrigues2010responseofpseudomonas pages 14-15).

#### 2.4 Primary molecular function
**Primary function:** OprF is best annotated as a **general diffusion porin and envelope integrity factor**.

Functional statements in KT2440 engineering work attribute to OprF: 
- “cell wall permeability and integrity” and cell-shape contributions (pobletecastro2020engineeringtheosmotic pages 1-2),
- and uptake of diverse low-molecular-weight solutes (reported list includes **ions (weakly cation-biased), low-molecular-mass sugars, toluene, nitrate/nitrite**) (pobletecastro2020engineeringtheosmotic pages 1-2).

**Interpretation:** For functional annotation purposes, the strongest supported molecular role is **non-specific outer membrane permeability** coupled to a **structural role in envelope stabilization** (pobletecastro2020engineeringtheosmotic pages 1-2, romarodrigues2010responseofpseudomonas pages 14-15).

### 3) Recent developments (prioritizing 2023–2024)

#### 3.1 OprF in rhizosphere/root-associated adaptation (2024)
A 2024 membrane-proteomics study in *P. putida* (isolate AA7, not KT2440) reports that **OprF (annotated as an OmpA-family protein)** is **significantly upregulated in cells near maize roots** (venkataraman2024anovelmembraneassociated pages 5-8). The study’s differential-abundance threshold was **≥2-fold change with P < 0.05** (venkataraman2024anovelmembraneassociated pages 5-8). The authors also note prior literature implicating OprF in root attachment in *P. fluorescens*, using this as biological rationale (venkataraman2024anovelmembraneassociated pages 5-8).

**Species-specificity note:** this provides *P. putida* ecological context but is not a KT2440-specific experiment (venkataraman2024anovelmembraneassociated pages 5-8).

#### 3.2 OprF as an engineering target to increase outer membrane permeability for electron-mediator flux (2024)
A 2024 systems metabolic engineering study in *P. putida* KT2440 cloned **oprF (PP_2089)** into an IPTG-inducible vector (pSEVA234-oprF) and reported that OprF overexpression measurably increases outer membrane permeability, quantified by **NPN uptake fluorescence**. Reported effects were:
- **3.6-fold** increase in NPN fluorescence in KT/234-oprF,
- **1.8-fold** increase in a ΔTonB background strain,
and functionally a **~3-fold increase in ferricyanide reduction rate** (1 mM ferricyanide) in anaerobic serum flask assays, supporting OprF involvement in mediator transport and suggesting it as a target to accelerate extracellular electron transfer (weimer2024systemsmetabolicengineering pages 97-101, weimer2024systemsmetabolicengineeringa pages 97-101).

### 4) Established experimental evidence in KT2440 (pre-2023 but still central)

#### 4.1 Solvent/phenol stress alters OprF proteoforms (2010)
In *P. putida* KT2440 exposed to **800 mg/L phenol**, OprF appears in multiple electrophoretic forms; after phenol exposure, a **higher-molecular-weight OprF form increased**, while lower-molecular-weight forms were strongly reduced (romarodrigues2010responseofpseudomonas pages 14-15, romarodrigues2010responseofpseudomonas pages 7-14). The authors interpret this pattern as consistent with changes in folding/proteoform or conformer distributions that may reduce permeability (hypothesized as adaptive), while noting this requires further study (romarodrigues2010responseofpseudomonas pages 14-15).

#### 4.2 Membrane/osmotic engineering and downstream processing application (2020)
A KT2440 bioprocess engineering study overproduced OprF (and OprE) during PHA production and found:
- **No impairment** of growth rate, biomass, or PHA yield after **48 h** for strains overproducing each porin individually,
- **~10% biomass reduction** but higher PHA accumulation when expressing both porins in tandem in a ΔmscL background,
- and after osmotic upshift then hypotonic shock, **>95% cell disruption within 3 h** and **94.2% recovery** of biosynthesized PHA (pobletecastro2020engineeringtheosmotic pages 2-4, pobletecastro2020engineeringtheosmotic pages 1-2).

These findings support a practical interpretation of OprF as a controllable determinant of **outer membrane permeability and mechanical/osmotic robustness** (pobletecastro2020engineeringtheosmotic pages 1-2).

### 5) Applications and real-world implementations

1. **Bioprocessing/downstream recovery of intracellular bioproducts (PHA):** OprF (with OprE) overproduction in KT2440 can be used as part of an engineered “osmotic fragility” platform enabling **rapid lysis (>95% in 3 h)** and high **PHA recovery (94.2%)** (pobletecastro2020engineeringtheosmotic pages 2-4, pobletecastro2020engineeringtheosmotic pages 1-2).

2. **Bioelectrochemical/electrogenic engineering:** OprF overexpression can increase OM permeability (NPN uptake **3.6-fold**) and enhance mediator reduction (**~3-fold** ferricyanide reduction rate), supporting its utility for increasing mediator flux (weimer2024systemsmetabolicengineering pages 97-101, weimer2024systemsmetabolicengineeringa pages 97-101).

3. **Rhizosphere persistence/colonization (contextual):** root-proximal upregulation of OprF in *P. putida* AA7 implicates OM remodeling and porin regulation during plant association (venkataraman2024anovelmembraneassociated pages 5-8).

4. **Membrane-stress and permeability tuning under solvent challenge:** OprF proteoform shifts under phenol suggest an envelope-level adaptation mechanism potentially relevant to industrial solvent tolerance (romarodrigues2010responseofpseudomonas pages 14-15, romarodrigues2010responseofpseudomonas pages 7-14).

### 6) Quantitative statistics and data highlights (recent and/or high-confidence)

- PHA downstream processing engineering (KT2440): **>95% cell disruption within 3 h**; **94.2% PHA recovery**; **~10% biomass reduction** for combined oprF+oprE expression in ΔmscL background (published **05 Mar 2020**, *Frontiers in Bioengineering and Biotechnology*, https://doi.org/10.3389/fbioe.2020.00161) (pobletecastro2020engineeringtheosmotic pages 2-4, pobletecastro2020engineeringtheosmotic pages 1-2).
- Electrogenic/mediator transport engineering (KT2440): **3.6-fold** NPN permeability increase and **~3-fold** ferricyanide reduction rate increase with oprF overexpression; **1.8-fold** NPN increase in ΔTonB background (2024; DOI/URL not available in the retrieved excerpt) (weimer2024systemsmetabolicengineering pages 97-101, weimer2024systemsmetabolicengineeringa pages 97-101).
- Root-associated proteomics (AA7): OprF significantly upregulated using **≥2-fold** and **P < 0.05** criteria (published **Dec 2024**, *ACS Synthetic Biology*, https://doi.org/10.1021/acssynbio.4c00489) (venkataraman2024anovelmembraneassociated pages 5-8).
- Phenol stress (KT2440): exposure condition **800 mg/L phenol** with OprF proteoform redistribution (published **Jun 2010**, *Journal of Proteomics*, https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jprot.2010.02.003) (romarodrigues2010responseofpseudomonas pages 14-15, romarodrigues2010responseofpseudomonas pages 7-14).

### 7) High-level expert analysis and consensus interpretation (evidence-weighted)

**Primary functional annotation recommendation (KT2440; UniProt Q88L46):**
- **Protein type:** outer membrane **general diffusion porin** of the OmpA/OprF family.
- **Localization:** outer membrane.
- **Mechanistic role:** contributes to **outer membrane permeability** (diffusion of small solutes) and **envelope integrity/cell-shape maintenance**, consistent with a β-barrel (transport) plus periplasmic OmpA-like domain (structural/peptidoglycan interaction) model (pobletecastro2020engineeringtheosmotic pages 1-2, romarodrigues2010responseofpseudomonas pages 14-15, romarodrigues2010responseofpseudomonas pages 17-18).

**Why this is the “primary function”:** Direct engineering and permeability assays show that modulating OprF expression changes OM permeability and downstream phenotypes (osmotic fragility, mediator flux), which are most parsimoniously explained by a porin/integrity function rather than a specific enzyme-like activity (pobletecastro2020engineeringtheosmotic pages 1-2, weimer2024systemsmetabolicengineering pages 97-101, weimer2024systemsmetabolicengineeringa pages 97-101).

### 10) Summary statement for functional annotation

**OprF (UniProt Q88L46; oprF/PP_2089) in *Pseudomonas putida* KT2440 is a major outer membrane OmpA-family porin that modulates general outer membrane permeability and contributes to envelope integrity and cellular robustness.** Experimental manipulation in KT2440 demonstrates that changing OprF expression affects permeability-dependent phenotypes (osmotic lysis for PHA recovery; mediator flux for electrogenic metabolism), and stress proteomics indicates OprF proteoform/conformer distributions are responsive to solvent stress (phenol), consistent with an adaptive permeability-control role (pobletecastro2020engineeringtheosmotic pages 2-4, pobletecastro2020engineeringtheosmotic pages 1-2, romarodrigues2010responseofpseudomonas pages 14-15, weimer2024systemsmetabolicengineering pages 97-101, weimer2024systemsmetabolicengineeringa pages 97-101).
