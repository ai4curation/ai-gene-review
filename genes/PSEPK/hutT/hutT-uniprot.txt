CC   -!- FUNCTION: Major high-affinity histidine transporter (PubMed:34245008).
CC       Binds and catalyzes the uptake of histidine into the cell
CC       (PubMed:34245008). Functions as an histidine:proton symporter with high
CC       specificity for histidine (PubMed:34245008).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:34245008}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY:
CC       Reaction=L-histidine(out) + n H(+)(out) = L-histidine(in) + n H(+)(in);
CC         Xref=Rhea:RHEA:76379, ChEBI:CHEBI:15378, ChEBI:CHEBI:57595;
CC         Evidence={ECO:0000269|PubMed:34245008};
CC       PhysiologicalDirection=left-to-right; Xref=Rhea:RHEA:76380;
CC         Evidence={ECO:0000269|PubMed:34245008};
CC   -!- ACTIVITY REGULATION: Transport activity is inhibited by the proton
CC       ionophores carbonyl cyanide m-chlorophenyl hydrazine (CCCP) and 2,4-
CC       dinitrophenol (DNP), but not by valinomycin, nigericin and nonactin
CC       (PubMed:34245008). Uptake is reduced in the presence of the sulfhydryl
CC       reagent N-ethylmaleimide (NEM) (PubMed:34245008). Uptake is not
CC       affected by arginine, lysine, proline or compounds structurally related
CC       to histidine such as imidazole, 3-amino-1,2,4-triazole and urocanate
CC       (PubMed:34245008). Only 1,2,4-triazolyl-3-alanine reduces the rate of
CC       L-histidine uptake significantly (PubMed:34245008).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:34245008}.
