# pqqD2 curation notes

## 2026-07-20 OpenScientist assessment

- UniProt and PqqD-family evidence support the conserved PqqA-binding,
  PqqE-presentation role. This is sufficient for the PQQ-biosynthesis process
  annotation but does not resolve paralog specialization.
- OpenScientist places PP_2681 outside the canonical pqq operon and near the Ped
  alcohol-oxidation locus. It proposes that PqqD2 is a co-regulated backup that
  preferentially supplies PQQ to PedE/PedH.
- The same report states that no pqqD2-specific knockout, expression study, or
  direct PqqA/PqqE assay establishes that specialization. The Ped-coupling model
  is therefore retained as a testable hypothesis rather than a core-function
  claim.
- The report's inference that a single PqqA gene means both PqqD paralogs must
  act on that precursor is not decisive evidence of PqqD2 substrate use or
  physiological specialization. It also reports exact pairwise alignment and
  STRING scores without retaining reproducible artifacts. Those quantitative
  claims are not used in the review.
- GO:0048038 quinone binding was removed because PqqD proteins bind the PqqA
  peptide precursor; pathway membership does not demonstrate binding of mature
  quinone.
