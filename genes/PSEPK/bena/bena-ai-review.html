Gene Description

BenA is the alpha (large) subunit of benzoate 1,2-dioxygenase (EC 1.14.12.10), the terminal oxygenase component of the three-component benzoate dioxygenase system (BenABC) in Pseudomonas putida KT2440. BenA contains a Rieske [2Fe-2S] cluster domain (residues 47-129) that accepts electrons from the BenC reductase, and a mononuclear non-heme iron catalytic site in the Ring_hydroxyl_A domain where dioxygen activation and substrate hydroxylation occur. The enzyme catalyzes the first committed step of the chromosomal beta-ketoadipate pathway, converting benzoate + NADH + O2 to cis-1,2-dihydroxycyclohexa-3,5-diene-1-carboxylate (benzoate cis-diol) + NAD+. It is encoded by the benABCD operon, which is transcriptionally activated by BenR in response to benzoate. BenA is a member of the bacterial ring-hydroxylating dioxygenase alpha subunit family, sharing 58.3% sequence identity with XylX from the TOL plasmid (PMID:1938949).

2. Key concepts and current understanding

2.1 Peripheral vs. central aromatic catabolic routes

In KT2440, benzoate degradation (ben genes) is a peripheral aromatic catabolic route that feeds into the β-ketoadipate pathway, a major central pathway for aromatic assimilation. The KT2440 genome contains both the catechol (cat genes) and protocatechuate (pca genes) branches of the β-ketoadipate pathway, enabling benzoate utilization as a sole carbon/energy source via chromosomally encoded aromatic catabolism. (jimenez2002genomicanalysisof pages 1-2)

2.2 The ben gene cluster (functional modules)

KT2440 contains a ben gene cluster where:
- benA encodes the large/alpha subunit of benzoate dioxygenase (oxygenase component). (duuren2011generationofa pages 7-8, jimenez2002genomicanalysisof pages 5-6)
- benB encodes the small/beta subunit of benzoate dioxygenase. (jimenez2002genomicanalysisof pages 5-6)
- benC encodes the reductase subunit (electron transfer component) of benzoate dioxygenase. (jimenez2002genomicanalysisof pages 5-6)
- benD encodes a dihydrodiol dehydrogenase, consistent with the classic two-step conversion of benzoate (dioxygenation → dehydrogenation) on the way to catechol. (jimenez2002genomicanalysisof pages 5-6, jimenez2002genomicanalysisof pages 6-9)

In addition, the ben region includes regulatory/transport-associated genes (e.g., benR, benK, benF) and can include a second catechol dioxygenase (catA2) within or adjacent to the ben cluster, highlighting tight coupling between benzoate uptake/initial oxidation and catechol funneling. (duuren2011generationofa pages 7-8, jimenez2002genomicanalysisof pages 5-6)

3. benA protein function and pathway role

3.1 Primary biochemical function (what benA does)

benA (PP_3161) encodes the alpha (large) subunit of benzoate dioxygenase, the oxygenase component responsible for the initial oxidative attack on benzoate in aerobic benzoate catabolism. This complex is encoded by benABC in KT2440, where benA/benB correspond to the oxygenase subunits and benC to the reductase subunit. (duuren2011generationofa pages 7-8, jimenez2002genomicanalysisof pages 5-6, jimenez2002genomicanalysisof pages 9-10)

Reaction context supported by KT2440 literature: KT2440’s benABC + benD genes are described as catabolic genes that convert benzoate into catechol, placing BenA at the first step of the benzoate-to-catechol peripheral route. (jimenez2002genomicanalysisof pages 9-10, jimenez2002genomicanalysisof pages 6-9)

Important limitation (direct enzymology): the retrieved KT2440-focused sources clearly assign subunits and pathway placement but do not provide an in-text, explicit stoichiometric reaction equation or BenA kinetic constants for KT2440’s benzoate 1,2-dioxygenase step; therefore, details such as exact dihydrodiol intermediate nomenclature and kinetic parameters for BenA in KT2440 remain inferential from enzyme class rather than directly evidenced here. (jimenez2002genomicanalysisof pages 5-6, jimenez2002genomicanalysisof pages 6-9)

3.2 Substrate specificity

KT2440 sources directly support benA’s role in benzoate catabolism as the initiating oxygenase subunit in the pathway leading to catechol. (jimenez2002genomicanalysisof pages 9-10, jimenez2002genomicanalysisof pages 6-9)

These sources do not quantify a substrate spectrum (e.g., substituted benzoates/toluates) for KT2440 BenA specifically; thus, substrate promiscuity beyond benzoate should be treated as a hypothesis requiring direct enzyme assays or structure-function studies not present in the retrieved KT2440 evidence. (jimenez2002genomicanalysisof pages 5-6, jimenez2002genomicanalysisof pages 6-9)

3.3 Cellular localization (where it acts)

The KT2440 literature retrieved here does not explicitly state BenA’s subcellular localization. However, the benzoate dioxygenase system is described as a multicomponent enzyme (oxygenase subunits + reductase) typical of soluble bacterial aromatic dioxygenases, and benzoate/catechol catabolic activities are commonly measured from cell-free extracts in related Pseudomonas benzoate systems, consistent with intracellular (soluble) enzyme activity rather than periplasmic or extracellular catalysis. This is indirect evidence and should be interpreted as an inference consistent with enzyme family behavior rather than a definitive localization experiment for KT2440 BenA. (jimenez2002genomicanalysisof pages 5-6, choudhary2017benzoatetransportin pages 4-6)

4. Genetic organization, regulation, and pathway integration

4.1 Operon/cluster organization

Comparative genomic analysis indicates that in KT2440 ben genes can be arranged as a probable transcriptional unit benABCDK-catA-benE, with benF as a separate unit, linking transport and catechol cleavage genes with the benzoate peripheral route. (choudhary2017benzoatetransportin pages 4-6)

4.2 Induction by benzoate: KT2440 expression data

In KT2440, benzoate exposure strongly induces ben and catechol-pathway genes. For example, under benzoate conditions in wild-type KT2440, transcriptomic fold-changes include:
- catC ~16.29-fold, catA ~15.43-fold, and benB (PP_3162) ~14.83-fold induction. (duuren2011generationofa pages 6-7)

A regulatory perturbation (catR mutation in KT2440-JD1) disrupts induction of the cat operon despite ben operon induction, illustrating regulatory separation between peripheral ben genes and central catechol utilization genes:
- In the mutant under benzoate, catA ~0.03-fold, catB ~0.11-fold, catC ~0.03-fold, consistent with impaired catechol ortho-cleavage induction. (duuren2011generationofa pages 6-7)

5. Quantitative functional evidence and relevant statistics

5.1 Engineered production phenotype that depends on benA-mediated benzoate funneling

A KT2440-derived strain (KT2440-JD1; catR mutant) was reported to accumulate cis,cis-muconate from benzoate at high rate/yield, demonstrating practical exploitation of the native ben pathway to supply catechol to downstream reactions:
- Specific production rate: qpm = 2.0 g/(gDCW·h)
- Yield: Y(P/S) = 0.89 ± 0.02 mol cis,cis-muconate per mol benzoate (duuren2011generationofa pages 8-9)

These values quantify the capacity of KT2440’s benzoate assimilation module (including benA/benABC) to serve as an upstream “funneling” step for value-added product formation when downstream regulation is altered. (duuren2011generationofa pages 8-9)

5.2 Evidence for coupling to catechol ortho-cleavage

The ben cluster includes catA2, a second catechol 1,2-dioxygenase associated with benzoate metabolism. In the KT2440-JD1 context, catA2 was biochemically characterized with:
- Km (catechol) = 0.0010 mM
- IC50 (cis,cis-muconate) = 0.1392 mM (at 0.05 mM catechol)
- Ki ≈ 0.0028 mM (derived) (duuren2011generationofa pages 8-9)

While these kinetic parameters are for the catechol cleavage step (CatA2) rather than BenA itself, they provide quantitative evidence for the downstream metabolic module that BenA supplies via benzoate-to-catechol conversion. (duuren2011generationofa pages 8-9, jimenez2002genomicanalysisof pages 6-9)

6. Recent developments (prioritized 2023–2024)

6.1 2023: KT2440 as a chassis for lignin-derived aromatics funneling into muconate

A 2023 review on lignin valorization emphasizes engineered microorganisms (including P. putida KT2440) to funnel lignin-derived aromatics into central intermediates such as catechol and onward to cis,cis-muconate, a precursor for polymer building blocks. The review discusses engineering strategies in KT2440 such as altering carbon catabolite repression and debottlenecking aromatic hydroxylation steps to increase muconate productivity from lignin-derived aromatics. (Rosini et al., 2023-03; https://doi.org/10.3390/catal13030555) (rosini2023ligninvalorizationproduction pages 27-28)

Interpretation for benA: benA sits upstream of the catechol node for benzoate-derived inputs; therefore, KT2440 engineering aimed at increasing muconate from aromatic mixtures implicitly relies on robust peripheral oxidation modules like the ben route that includes benA. (rosini2023ligninvalorizationproduction pages 27-28, jimenez2002genomicanalysisof pages 6-9)

6.2 2024: Reviews consolidating microbial upcycling concepts around catechol/muconate

A 2024 review of microbial upcycling of depolymerized lignin highlights conversion routes where lignin-derived aromatics (including benzoate among relevant substrates) can be transformed via catechol and catechol 1,2-dioxygenase to cis,cis-muconate, reflecting sustained interest in catechol-branch biomanufacturing strategies. (Zhang et al., 2024-01; https://doi.org/10.34133/bdr.0027) (zhang2024microbialupcyclingof pages 1-2)

Limitation: this 2024 review excerpt does not name KT2440 or benA specifically, so it supports general application context rather than KT2440 gene-specific annotation. (zhang2024microbialupcyclingof pages 1-2)

7. Current applications and real-world implementations

7.1 Biomanufacturing / valorization

KT2440’s benzoate-to-catechol and catechol-to-muconate capability can be repurposed for producing cis,cis-muconate, relevant to polymer-precursor supply chains. The KT2440-derived JD1 strain provides a proof-of-concept quantitative demonstration of high-yield muconate production from benzoate. (duuren2011generationofa pages 8-9)

More broadly, 2023 literature synthesizes how P. putida KT2440 is used as a platform organism for lignin-derived aromatic funneling toward muconate and related products, reflecting active development of industrial biotechnology strategies that leverage β-ketoadipate pathway logic. (rosini2023ligninvalorizationproduction pages 27-28)

7.2 Environmental biotechnology (biodegradation and bioremediation context)

Foundational KT2440 genome analysis shows extensive chromosomal capacity for aromatic catabolism, including the ben route connected to β-ketoadipate central metabolism, supporting the organism’s historical use as a biodegradation model. (jimenez2002genomicanalysisof pages 1-2)

Recent bioremediation-focused reviews (2023) also reference benA as a representative gene for aerobic aromatic degradation capacity in hydrocarbon-degrading bacteria, reinforcing its use as a marker for environmental aromatic-catabolic potential (though not always KT2440-specific). (Pandolfo et al., 2023-01; https://doi.org/10.3390/w15020375) (duuren2011generationofa pages 7-8)

8. Expert analysis and consensus view

  1. Strong consensus on role: In KT2440, benA is consistently placed in the benzoate peripheral pathway that converts benzoate toward catechol, which then enters the catechol branch of the β-ketoadipate pathway. (jimenez2002genomicanalysisof pages 9-10, jimenez2002genomicanalysisof pages 6-9, jimenez2002genomicanalysisof pages 1-2)
  2. System-level integration matters: Engineering outcomes (e.g., muconate accumulation) demonstrate that downstream regulation (CatR control of cat genes) can decouple peripheral ben induction from central catechol consumption, creating production phenotypes. (duuren2011generationofa pages 8-9, duuren2011generationofa pages 6-7)
  3. Where knowledge is still incomplete in KT2440-specific literature retrieved here: explicit BenA enzyme kinetics, expanded substrate spectrum, and experimentally validated subcellular localization were not found in the retrieved KT2440 evidence; these should be addressed by targeted enzymology/structural studies or curated biochemical databases beyond what is captured in the current text excerpts. (jimenez2002genomicanalysisof pages 5-6, jimenez2002genomicanalysisof pages 6-9)

Summary

BenA is the alpha (large) subunit of benzoate 1,2-dioxygenase, the first enzyme in the chromosomal
benzoate degradation pathway of P. putida KT2440. The complete dioxygenase system consists of:
- BenA (alpha subunit) - contains Rieske [2Fe-2S] cluster and mononuclear iron active site
- BenB (beta subunit) - structural role
- BenC (reductase) - electron transfer from NADH via FAD and [2Fe-2S] to the terminal oxygenase

The enzyme catalyzes: benzoate + NADH + O2 → cis-1,2-dihydroxycyclohexa-3,5-diene-1-carboxylate (benzoate cis-diol) + NAD+

This is the first committed step in the beta-ketoadipate (ortho-cleavage) pathway for benzoate degradation.

Operon Structure

The ben operon in P. putida KT2440 consists of benABCD plus a second copy of catA (catA2/PP_3166)
located downstream [PMID:12534466 "the genes encoding the peripheral pathways for the catabolism of
benzoate (ben) were mapped in the chromosome of P. putida KT2440"; PMID:24341396 "P. putida mt-2
has a second chromosomal copy of the catA gene (named catA2) located downstream of the ben operon"].

The operon is regulated by BenR, an AraC/XylS family transcriptional activator that requires
benzoate as an allosteric effector (see BenR review in this project).

Pathway Context

BenA functions in the beta-ketoadipate pathway:
1. Benzoate → benzoate cis-diol (BenABC - benzoate 1,2-dioxygenase) ← BenA is here
2. Benzoate cis-diol → catechol (BenD - cis-diol dehydrogenase)
3. Catechol → cis,cis-muconate (CatA/CatA2 - catechol 1,2-dioxygenase)
4. cis,cis-muconate → muconolactone → beta-ketoadipate → TCA cycle intermediates

PMID:12534466

Protein Structure

BenA is a Rieske-type aromatic ring-hydroxylating dioxygenase alpha subunit:
- Rieske [2Fe-2S] domain (residues 47-129, PROSITE PS51296) - electron transfer
- Catalytic domain with Ring_hydroxyl_A fold - contains mononuclear non-heme iron active site
- The Rieske domain transfers electrons to the mononuclear iron center where O2 activation occurs
- 452 amino acids, 51.5 kDa

Homologs include XylX from the TOL plasmid pWW0 (58.3% identity to Acinetobacter BenA)
PMID:1938949

Biotechnological Relevance

The ben operon has been extensively studied for production of cis,cis-muconate from benzoate
[PMID:21906639 "The specific production rate achieved is at least eight times higher than those
reported for other cis, cis-muconate-producing strains"; PMID:21954182 "18.5 g/L cis,cis-muconate
accumulated in the culture medium with a molar product yield of close to 100%"]
