CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: Cells lacking this gene grow efficiently with
CC       ethanol, 1-butanol, and 2-phenylethanol in the absence of La(3+), like
CC       wild-type strain, and the addition of 20 uM La(3+) to the agar medium
CC       restricts the growth of the mutant strain. Cells lacking both pedE and
CC       pedH show no growth under both conditions.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:28655819}.
CC   -!- BIOTECHNOLOGY: Is an attractive biocatalyst for the oxidation of 5-
CC       (hydroxymethyl)furoic acid (HMFA) into 5-formylfuroic acid (FFA), a key
CC       reaction within the biocatalytic route toward furan-2,5-dicarboxylic
CC       acid (FDCA) production. FDCA is a bio-based platform chemical with the
CC       potential to replace terephthalic acid in the production of polymers.
CC       {ECO:0000269|Ref.3}.
CC   -!- MISCELLANEOUS: The soil-dwelling organism P.putida KT2440 produces 2
CC       PQQ-ADHs, namely, PedE, which is Ca(2+)-dependent, and PedH, which is
CC       lanthanide-dependent. These enzymes are crucial for efficient bacterial
CC       growth with a variety of volatile alcohols. The functional redundancy
CC       of the PQQ-ADHs and inverse transcriptional regulation of PedE and PedH
CC       represent an adaptive strategy of P.putida KT2440 to optimize growth
CC       with volatile alcohols and aldehyde substrates in response to the
CC       availability of different lanthanides in the natural environment of the
CC       bacterium. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:28655819}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the bacterial PQQ dehydrogenase family.
