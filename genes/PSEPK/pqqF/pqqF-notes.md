# pqqF curation notes

## 2026-07-20 OpenScientist assessment

The OpenScientist report is useful for retrieving the KT2440 locus, the M16
active-site architecture, and the PQQ literature, but its target-level
quaternary-structure conclusion is not accepted. Q88QV3 is a 766-residue
single-chain M16A-like PqqF. PMID:31427437 characterizes a two-component M16B
PqqF/PqqG protease from *Methylorubrum extorquens*; that architecture is a
separate pathway realization and does not show that KT2440 Q88QV3 is one
subunit of the same kind of heterodimer.

The report also conflates the M16B partner with PP_0375, the adjacent KT2440
protein called a putative `pqqG` in PMID:27287323. PP_0375 is an S9-family
peptidase and is not homologous to the M16B PqqG characterized in
PMID:31427437. The reusable module therefore keeps three distinct proteolysis
realizations: single-chain M16A-like PqqF, two-component M16B PqqF/PqqG, and an
alternative cellular-protease route. PP_0375 remains a KT2440 follow-up
candidate rather than a required PqqF partner.

No direct KT2440 Q88QV3 knockout, purified-enzyme assay, cleavage-site map, or
complex assay was retrieved. The accepted gene-level claim remains the
conservative one: Q88QV3 is an M16 zinc metalloendopeptidase associated with
PQQ biosynthesis and is inferred to process a PqqA-derived intermediate.
