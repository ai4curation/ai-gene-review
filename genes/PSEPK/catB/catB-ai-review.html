Gene Description

CatB is the cytosolic Mn(2+)-dependent muconate cycloisomerase of the catechol ortho-cleavage branch of aromatic compound catabolism in Pseudomonas putida KT2440. It catalyzes the reversible conversion of cis,cis-muconate to (+)-muconolactone downstream of catechol 1,2-dioxygenase and functions in the CatR-regulated catBCA module of the beta-ketoadipate pathway.

Research Report: Functional Annotation of catB (PP_3715; UniProt Q88GK6) in Pseudomonas putida KT2440

1. Target verification (mandatory identity checks)

1.1 Confirming the correct gene/protein

The literature evidence supports that catB in P. putida KT2440 corresponds to locus tag PP_3715 and encodes a muconate (and chloromuconate) cycloisomerase in the catechol ortho-cleavage (β-ketoadipate) pathway (duuren2011generationofa pages 7-8, duuren2011generationofa pages 6-7). This matches the UniProt target identity provided (UniProt Q88GK6; “Muconate cycloisomerase 1”; EC 5.5.1.1).

A genomic survey of KT2440 aromatic catabolism genes also places the catechol-branch cluster in the conserved order catR–catBCA (jimenez2002genomicanalysisof pages 5-6), consistent with the operon context expected for CatB enzymes.

1.2 Ambiguity control: avoiding misattribution to other “catB” genes

Recent engineering literature sometimes discusses catB deletions in other Pseudomonas strains/species (e.g., P. putida B6-2) for muconate production. These must not be conflated with KT2440 CatB/PP_3715. He et al. (2023) explicitly used P. putida B6-2 (not KT2440) and P. brassicacearum MPDS (he2023microbialproductionof pages 1-2, he2023microbialproductionof pages 6-7). This report distinguishes KT2440-specific evidence from non-KT2440 evidence.

2. Key concepts and definitions (current understanding)

2.1 The β-ketoadipate pathway and catechol ortho-cleavage

The β-ketoadipate pathway is a central aerobic aromatic catabolic route. In KT2440, aromatic compounds such as benzoate can be converted to catechol, which is cleaved by catechol 1,2-dioxygenase (CatA) to form cis,cis-muconate; this intermediate is then processed by CatB and CatC and ultimately funneled toward central metabolism (duuren2011generationofa pages 1-2, jimenez2002genomicanalysisof pages 5-6).

2.2 Definition: muconate cycloisomerase (CatB; EC 5.5.1.1)

Muconate cycloisomerase (also described as cis,cis-muconate lactonizing enzyme) catalyzes the cycloisomerization/lactonization of cis,cis-muconate to (+)-muconolactone, a key step immediately downstream of catechol ring cleavage in the ortho-cleavage branch (ngai1983enzymesofthe pages 3-4, ngai1983enzymesofthe pages 1-2).

3. Gene/protein function in KT2440: reaction, substrates, and pathway role

3.1 Primary catalytic reaction and substrate specificity

Biochemical characterization in Pseudomonas putida established that cis,cis-muconate cycloisomerase catalyzes the reversible interconversion:

  • cis,cis-muconate ⇌ (+)-muconolactone (ngai1983enzymesofthe pages 2-3, ngai1983enzymesofthe pages 3-4, ngai1983enzymesofthe pages 1-2).

In KT2440, PP_3715 is annotated as CatB in the cat operon alongside CatA (PP_3713) and CatC (PP_3714), supporting that this gene product performs the canonical catechol-branch cycloisomerization step (duuren2011generationofa pages 6-7).

3.2 Biological process and pathway placement

catB is part of the catechol ortho-cleavage gene cluster. Comparative/genomic evidence describes this cluster as commonly organized as catR–catBCA, linking catB to coordinated expression with catA and catC and pathway-level regulation (jimenez2002genomicanalysisof pages 5-6).

4. Regulation and operon context (expert-level annotation)

4.1 Operon structure and CatR regulation

In KT2440, catB resides in the cat operon with catA and catC, and expression is controlled by the LysR-type regulator CatR. Experimental evidence from a KT2440-derived mutant (KT2440-JD1) shows that a single point mutation in catR (Arg50→Cys50) prevents normal induction of the cat operon in response to benzoate/cis,cis-muconate, resulting in reduced CatA/CatB protein levels and impaired growth on benzoate unless complemented with wild-type catR (duuren2011generationofa pages 8-9).

Transcriptomic measurements in that study show strong induction of cat genes in wild-type KT2440 with benzoate (e.g., catA ~15.43-fold; catC ~16.29-fold in one comparison), whereas the JD1 mutant shows very low cat gene expression when benzoate is present (e.g., catB ~0.11; catA ~0.03; catC ~0.03, in the JD1 vs WT comparison under benzoate) (duuren2011generationofa pages 6-7). A supporting table/figure crop documenting these cat operon values and gene locus tags is available (duuren2011generationofa media 5142f029).

5. Subcellular localization and physiological context

5.1 Cellular compartment: cytosolic/soluble enzyme

Proteomic analyses of KT2440 (or KT2440 derivatives) focusing on cytosolic/soluble fractions detect Cat pathway enzymes, including cis,cis-muconate cycloisomerase as part of the “Cat proteins” set, supporting a cytosolic/soluble localization (kurbatov2006analysisofthe pages 1-2, yun2011proteomiccharacterizationof pages 2-4).

5.2 Carbon catabolite repression and environmental responsiveness

KT2440 exhibits strong carbon-source regulation over aromatic catabolism.

  • In cytosolic proteome experiments, translation of Cat proteins (including CatB) was repressed immediately upon succinate addition, with CatA/B/C synthesis changes detectable within ~15 minutes, consistent with rapid catabolite repression of aromatic utilization when preferred carbon sources are available (kurbatov2006analysisofthe pages 1-2, kurbatov2006analysisofthe pages 4-5).

This physiological pattern supports the interpretation that CatB participates primarily during growth on aromatics (or aromatic-generating substrates such as phenol/benzoate), and its expression is tightly controlled to minimize unnecessary catabolic burden.

6. Enzyme mechanism and quantitative biochemistry (key statistics)

Classic enzymology work in P. putida provides quantitative parameters that are directly useful for functional annotation.

6.1 Quaternary structure and metal dependence

The purified P. putida cis,cis-muconate cycloisomerase is reported as a hexamer of identical ~40 kDa subunits with one active site per subunit and a requirement for a divalent metal ion (Mn²⁺ commonly used); EDTA treatment abolishes activity (ngai1983enzymesofthe pages 2-3).

6.2 Steady-state kinetic constants

Reported steady-state parameters include:

  • Forward reaction (cis,cis-muconate → muconolactone): kcat ≈ 13.9 s⁻¹ and Km (cis,cis-muconate) ≈ 105 µM (ngai1983enzymesofthe pages 4-5, ngai1983enzymesofthe pages 6-7).
  • Reverse reaction (muconolactone → muconate): kcat ≈ 1.4 s⁻¹ and Km (muconolactone) ≈ 424–500 µM (ngai1983enzymesofthe pages 4-5, ngai1983enzymesofthe pages 6-7).

6.3 Assay methods and optical parameters

Activity assays followed substrate/product absorbance:

  • cis,cis-muconate monitored at 260 nm, with molar absorptivity 1.72 × 10⁴ M⁻¹ cm⁻¹ (260 nm).
  • (+)-muconolactone absorbance referenced at 230 nm, with molar absorptivity 1.52 × 10³ M⁻¹ cm⁻¹ (230 nm).
  • Example assay conditions included 33 mM Tris-HCl pH 8.0, 25 °C, 100 µM cis,cis-muconate, 1 mM MnCl₂ (ngai1983enzymesofthe pages 2-3).

6.4 Mechanistic interpretation and pH dependence

The reaction equilibrium strongly depends on pH (Kapp decreasing with increasing pH), and pH profiles of catalytic efficiency suggest apparent free-enzyme pKa values ~6.2 and ~7.4, consistent with acid–base catalysis (ngai1983enzymesofthe pages 3-4, ngai1983enzymesofthe pages 1-2). NMR evidence indicates stereospecific solvent hydrogen exchange at the 5R position of muconolactone, helping define the reaction stereochemistry (ngai1983enzymesofthe pages 1-2).

7. Recent developments (prioritizing 2023–2024)

7.1 2024: Functional genomics and annotation acceleration in KT2440

Borchert et al. (published March 2024, URL: https://doi.org/10.1128/msystems.00942-23) applied independent component analysis (ICA) to a large RB-TnSeq fitness compendium in P. putida KT2440: 332 samples, 179 unique conditions, and fitness data for 4,732 of 5,564 protein-coding genes, yielding 84 functional modules (fModules) that explain 83% of fitness variance (borchert2024machinelearninganalysis pages 2-4, borchert2024machinelearninganalysis pages 1-2).

Critically for CatB annotation context, the study identifies multiple modules connected to aromatic catabolism and β-ketoadipate-related functions/stress, including modules annotated for benzoate catabolism and “β-ketoadipate and levulinic acid stress,” with module sizes and explained-variance values quantified (borchert2024machinelearninganalysis pages 4-6, borchert2024machinelearninganalysis pages 6-7). Although catB itself is not explicitly highlighted in the excerpts retrieved here, the work represents a 2024 state-of-the-art approach for inferring and validating functional gene networks that include aromatic funneling routes convergent on β-ketoadipate metabolism (borchert2024machinelearninganalysis pages 13-15).

7.2 2023: Lignin-related aromatic funneling to β-ketoadipate in KT2440 (industrial relevance)

Werner et al. (published September 2023, URL: https://doi.org/10.1126/sciadv.adj0053) engineered P. putida KT2440 for conversion of lignin-related aromatics (e.g., p-coumarate, ferulate) to β-ketoadipic acid with bioprocess metrics approaching economic relevance.

Key performance statistics reported include:

  • β-ketoadipate titer 44.5 g/L (model lignin-related compounds) and 25 g/L (corn stover-derived lignin-related compounds).
  • Productivities 1.15 g·L⁻¹·h⁻¹ (model) and 0.66 g·L⁻¹·h⁻¹ (corn stover-derived), and a yield of 0.10 g/g from corn stover lignin.
  • Process comparisons included DO-stat and constant fed-batch modes achieving 43.5 ± 1.6 g/L at 0.55 ± 0.02 g/L·h⁻¹ and 44.5 ± 1.85 g/L at 0.85 ± 0.04 g/L·h⁻¹, respectively (werner2023ligninconversionto pages 2-4, werner2023ligninconversionto pages 1-2).

While this work does not specifically engineer catB, it underscores the modern (2023) application context for β-ketoadipate pathway enzymes in KT2440, where CatB sits at the catechol-branch node that is often engineered to redirect flux toward platform chemicals.

7.3 2023: CatB as an engineering “valve” for muconate accumulation (disambiguated strain context)

He et al. (published September 2023, URL: https://doi.org/10.1016/j.synbio.2023.08.001) demonstrate the general engineering principle that deleting catB prevents muconate consumption and allows cis,cis-muconic acid (ccMA) accumulation (he2023microbialproductionof pages 2-3). However, their main experimental strains were not KT2440; they used P. putida B6-2 (e.g., B6-2ΔcatBΔsalC) and P. brassicacearum MPDS (he2023microbialproductionof pages 1-2).

Quantitative results from their system include:

  • B6-2ΔcatBΔsalC produced 19.0 mM ccMA from biphenyl after 120 h (OD600 ~0.4).
  • MPDS-based strains produced ~5.1 mM ccMA from naphthalene.
  • Reported molar conversion efficiencies reached 95.3% (ccMA/biphenyl) and 100% (ccMA/naphthalene), and a staged consortium reached 85.2% maximum molar conversion (he2023microbialproductionof pages 1-2, he2023microbialproductionof pages 6-7).

These results strongly support CatB’s functional role (muconate consumption) by showing the predictable phenotype (muconate accumulation) upon catB deletion, while also reinforcing the need to avoid misattribution across Pseudomonas strains.

8. Current applications and real-world implementations

8.1 Biorefinery/industrial biotechnology: aromatic funneling to platform chemicals

The 2023 KT2440 work of Werner et al. demonstrates that P. putida can be engineered and run in controlled fed-batch processes to produce β-ketoadipate at tens of g/L titers with productivities >0.5 g/L·h, alongside technoeconomic analysis suggesting potential competitiveness (werner2023ligninconversionto pages 2-4, werner2023ligninconversionto pages 1-2). Because CatB natively channels muconate toward central metabolism, CatB’s node is conceptually central to deciding whether carbon is assimilated (CatB active) or accumulated as muconate (CatB blocked).

8.2 Functional genomics for pathway-informed strain design

The 2024 RB-TnSeq/ICA approach provides a scalable strategy to detect genetic modules linked to aromatic catabolism and stress, allowing engineers to identify additional genes that modulate performance on aromatic feedstocks, potentially including accessory functions (cofactor supply, detoxification) that influence Cat pathway throughput (borchert2024machinelearninganalysis pages 2-4, borchert2024machinelearninganalysis pages 6-7).

9. Expert synthesis (authoritative interpretation grounded in evidence)

  1. Primary function (enzyme definition): CatB (PP_3715; UniProt Q88GK6) is best annotated as a cytosolic, Mn²⁺-dependent cis,cis-muconate cycloisomerase (EC 5.5.1.1) catalyzing cis,cis-muconate ⇌ (+)-muconolactone, with well-defined kinetics (kcat ~13.9 s⁻¹; Km ~105 µM) and spectrophotometric assays (260 nm) in Pseudomonas putida (duuren2011generationofa pages 6-7, ngai1983enzymesofthe pages 4-5, ngai1983enzymesofthe pages 2-3).

  2. Pathway integration: The cat gene cluster’s conserved organization and CatR-controlled induction provide strong evidence that CatB functions in a regulated aromatic catabolic module whose expression is tuned to environmental aromatics and repressed by preferred carbon sources (jimenez2002genomicanalysisof pages 5-6, kurbatov2006analysisofthe pages 1-2).

  3. Physiological role in KT2440: Genetic disruption of CatR in KT2440 prevents induction of cat genes under benzoate, reducing CatA/CatB levels and causing cis,cis-muconate accumulation, demonstrating that CatB activity is physiologically important for growth on benzoate-derived catechol carbon (duuren2011generationofa pages 8-9).

  4. Engineering significance: Across the field, CatB is widely treated as a metabolic “drain” on muconate; blocking catB (often together with catC) is a rational strategy to accumulate muconate (ccMA) as a product, while maintaining CatB supports complete assimilation to central metabolites—two opposing goals relevant to industrial biotechnology (he2023microbialproductionof pages 2-3).
