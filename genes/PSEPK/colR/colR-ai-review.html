Gene Description

ColR is the response-regulator output of the ColRS two-component system in Pseudomonas putida KT2440. It is an OmpR/PhoB-family phosphorelay regulator that binds a conserved promoter motif and modulates expression of membrane- and envelope-associated genes. Across genetic, binding-site, and proteome studies, ColR is best understood as a dual transcription factor that preserves envelope homeostasis during phenol stress, glucose-limitation hunger response, and metal excess, with especially strong roles in zinc and iron tolerance.

colR deep research summary

Manual fallback summary prepared on 2026-03-21 after just deep-research-falcon PSEPK colR was attempted but did not return output in this environment.

Molecular role

  • ColR is an OmpR/PhoB-family response regulator with a receiver-domain phosphoacceptor and a C-terminal winged-helix DNA-binding domain.
  • ColR and ColS form a functional phosphorelay, and Asp51 of ColR is required for phosphotransfer-dependent output.
  • ColR directly binds a 16-bp consensus motif and CollecTF curates seven binding sites in P. putida KT2440.
  • In the curated binding dataset, most direct ColR targets are activated and a smaller subset are repressed.
  • ColR functions mainly as an activator but also directly represses at least oprQ, algD, and PP_0737.

Biological role

  • Most validated ColR targets encode envelope or membrane-associated functions, supporting ColR as an envelope homeostasis regulator.
  • colR mutants are hypersensitive to phenol, and phenol stress magnifies the promoter defects of ColR-regulated targets.
  • On solid glucose medium, a subpopulation of colR mutants lyses during hunger response; lowering OprB1, OprF, or SecB-dependent secretion suppresses the defect.
  • ColRS is required for tolerance to excess iron and zinc and contributes to manganese and cadmium tolerance.
  • Under zinc excess, loss of colR triggers broad envelope-stress remodeling and strong induction of AlgU-dependent alginate biosynthesis proteins.

Direct regulon highlights

  • Directly discussed ColR targets include oprQ, algD, PP_0737, the PP_0900-PP_0905 locus, the PP_0033-PP_0036 locus, the PP_2558-hasE-PP_2561 region, and the gloA/PP_3767 region.
  • The regulon architecture links promoter binding to outer membrane composition, periplasmic stress control, and nutrient-stress survival.
