Gene Description

Tryptophan 2,3-dioxygenase (TDO) is a heme-dependent oxidoreductase that catalyzes the first committed step in ommochrome pigment biosynthesis in D. pealeii, converting L-tryptophan to N-formylkynurenine via oxidative cleavage of the indole ring. TDO is expressed in the retina and chromatophore layer of the skin, the two principal ommochrome-pigmented tissues. Crawford et al. (2020) demonstrated that CRISPR-Cas9 knockout of TDO in D. pealeii embryos efficiently eliminated pigmentation in both eyes and chromatophores with >90% disruption efficiency, while ink sac melanin pigmentation was unaffected. This was the first gene knockout achieved in any cephalopod. TDO inhibition with 680C91 also completely blocked pigmentation, confirming the enzyme is required for ommochrome synthesis. Notably, D. pealeii lacks an IDO (indoleamine 2,3-dioxygenase) ortholog, unlike Euprymna berryi where IDO can compensate for TDO loss. TDO belongs to the PANTHER family PTHR10138 (Tryptophan 2,3-dioxygenase), is predicted to function as a homotetramer (dimer of dimers), and contains a single heme cofactor per subunit. The gene spans 13 exons over 120 kb of genomic DNA.

Core Functions

TDO catalyzes the oxidative cleavage of L-tryptophan to N-formylkynurenine, the first committed step in the kynurenine pathway and ommochrome biosynthesis in D. pealeii. CRISPR-Cas9 knockout eliminated ommochrome pigmentation in both eyes and chromatophores with >90% efficiency, and pharmacological inhibition with 680C91 confirmed the enzyme is required. D. pealeii lacks an IDO ortholog, making TDO the sole enzyme for this reaction. TDO is a heme-dependent dioxygenase that binds one heme group per subunit. The downstream kynurenine pathway intermediates 3-hydroxykynurenine and xanthurenic acid serve as precursors for xanthommatin and related ommatin pigments deposited in specialized ommochromasomes within chromatophore and retinal pigment cells (PMID:29989284). The TDO-selective inhibitor 680C91 at 3.15 mM from stage 20-27 phenocopied the CRISPR knockout, confirming enzymatic activity is required upstream of ommochrome deposition. Genotyping of CRISPR-edited embryos showed a mean of 38 +/- 13 distinct indel events per animal, with no dominant allele, yet produced near-complete pigmentation loss, demonstrating the strong genotype-phenotype link. In E. berryi, TDO knockout alone produced only slightly delayed pigmentation due to IDO compensation, but TDO+IDO double knockout yielded complete albinism enabling the first in vivo calcium imaging of neural activity in a cephalopod (PMID:37343558).

Research Report: Functional Annotation of TDO (UniProt A0A7G4RN94) in Doryteuthis pealeii (Longfin inshore squid)

1. Target verification (critical disambiguation)

The gene symbol TDO is ambiguous across taxa (e.g., vertebrate TDO2), but the Doryteuthis pealeii target relevant to UniProt A0A7G4RN94 is strongly supported as a tryptophan 2,3-dioxygenase family enzyme by: (i) a squid-specific locus identified by BLAST against known TDO/IDO sequences and placed in a phylogeny to distinguish TDO from related dioxygenases (e.g., IDO), (ii) mapping to a single genomic locus reported as 13 exons spanning >120 kb and deposited as GenBank MT648678, and (iii) a causal pigmentation phenotype following inhibition or CRISPR disruption of this locus in D. pealeii embryos/hatchlings. (crawford2020highlyefficientknockout pages 8-10, crawford2020highlyefficientknockout pages 1-3)

2. Key concepts and current biochemical understanding

2.1 Definition and catalytic reaction

Tryptophan 2,3-dioxygenase (TDO; EC 1.13.11.11) is a heme-containing dioxygenase that catalyzes oxidative cleavage of the indole ring of L-tryptophan to yield N-formyl-L-kynurenine (NFK)—the first (often rate-limiting) committed step of the kynurenine pathway (KP). (alves2024thebiologyand pages 2-5)

Although TDO and indoleamine 2,3-dioxygenases (IDO1/IDO2) perform the same overall initial KP chemistry, they are described as differing in structure, substrate specificity, and tissue distribution, which affects biological roles across organisms. (alves2024thebiologyand pages 2-5)

2.2 Substrate specificity (inference relevant to squid)

A 2024 KP-focused review summarizes that (for human TDO as a well-studied reference) TDO shows high physiological specificity for L-tryptophan (with much lower activity on D-tryptophan), supporting the common annotation of TDO enzymes as primarily L-Trp oxygenases. (alves2024thebiologyand pages 5-6)

Given that D. pealeii TDO is experimentally tied to ommochrome pigmentation derived from tryptophan catabolism (Section 3), the most parsimonious functional annotation for UniProt A0A7G4RN94 is an L-tryptophan dioxygenase entering the kynurenine/ommochrome branch. (crawford2020highlyefficientknockout pages 1-3, alves2024thebiologyand pages 2-5)

2.3 Pathway context: kynurenine pathway → ommochrome pigments

In invertebrates, tryptophan catabolism through the Tryptophan → Ommochrome pathway produces ommochrome pigments (e.g., xanthommatin/ommatins) used in coloration and vision-related tissues. An authoritative synthesis in Biological Reviews identifies TDO (EC 1.13.11.11) as the enzyme catalyzing the first step of this pathway and lists heme as its cofactor in ommochrome biosynthesis contexts. (figon2019ommochromesininvertebrates pages 8-9, figon2019ommochromesininvertebrates pages 9-10)

3. Organism-specific functional evidence in Doryteuthis pealeii

3.1 Expression/localization: pigment-producing tissues

In D. pealeii embryos, fluorescent in situ hybridization detected TDO mRNA in the developing eyes/retina and in punctate signals consistent with developing chromatophores on the mantle/arms. (crawford2020highlyefficientknockout pages 3-5, crawford2020highlyefficientknockout media 7a0753cd)

Complementing embryo spatial expression, adult tissue RNA-seq (as analyzed in the same study) indicated highest expression in retina and in the chromatophore layer of the skin, consistent with a role in local pigment precursor production. (crawford2020highlyefficientknockout pages 3-5)

3.2 Pharmacological perturbation supports enzymatic requirement

Treatment of embryos with the TDO-selective inhibitor 680C91 (reported regimen: 3.15 mM, from stage 20–27) “clearly blocked pigmentation” in both eyes and chromatophores, supporting that enzymatic TDO activity is upstream of ommochrome deposition in these tissues. (crawford2020highlyefficientknockout pages 1-3, crawford2020highlyefficientknockout pages 10-12)

3.3 Genetic perturbation (CRISPR-Cas9) establishes causality

CRISPR-Cas9 disruption of D. pealeii tdo produced a strong and reproducible loss-of-pigmentation phenotype in hatchlings, with near-complete loss of pigmentation in eyes and chromatophores, while the ink sac (melanin-based pigment) was unaffected—supporting specificity for tryptophan-derived ommochromes rather than melanin. (crawford2020highlyefficientknockout pages 1-3, crawford2020highlyefficientknockout pages 3-5, crawford2020highlyefficientknockout media 7a0753cd)

A peer-reviewed research highlight independently summarizes the same core observation: CRISPR targeting of TDO in D. pealeii yields marked loss/reduction of pigment in eyes and chromatophores, with reported knockout efficiencies >90% in genotyped embryos. (neff2020crisprincephalopods pages 1-1)

4. Cellular compartment and subcellular localization (what can be concluded)

Direct subcellular localization of squid TDO protein (e.g., cytosolic vs organellar) was not established by protein-level localization in the retrieved D. pealeii sources; however, within the ommochrome pathway literature (in insects as the best-established reference), TDO is discussed as acting early in tryptophan catabolism and is contrasted with downstream steps having mitochondrial association (e.g., kynurenine monooxygenase in some systems). (cordell2021biosynthesisofthe pages 4-6)

Therefore, for UniProt A0A7G4RN94, the most supportable localization statement from available evidence is tissue-level localization to retina and chromatophore-bearing skin, with subcellular localization remaining an inference rather than squid-specific experimental fact. (crawford2020highlyefficientknockout pages 3-5, crawford2020highlyefficientknockout media 7a0753cd)

core_functions:
- description: >-
    TDO catalyzes the oxidative cleavage of L-tryptophan to N-formylkynurenine,
    the first committed step in the kynurenine pathway and ommochrome biosynthesis
    in D. pealeii. CRISPR-Cas9 knockout eliminated ommochrome pigmentation in both
    eyes and chromatophores with >90% efficiency, and pharmacological inhibition
    with 680C91 confirmed the enzyme is required. D. pealeii lacks an IDO ortholog,
    making TDO the sole enzyme for this reaction. TDO is a heme-dependent dioxygenase
    that binds one heme group per subunit. The downstream kynurenine pathway
    intermediates 3-hydroxykynurenine and xanthurenic acid serve as precursors for
    xanthommatin and related ommatin pigments deposited in specialized ommochromasomes
    within chromatophore and retinal pigment cells (PMID:29989284). The TDO-selective
    inhibitor 680C91 at 3.15 mM from stage 20-27 phenocopied the CRISPR knockout,
    confirming enzymatic activity is required upstream of ommochrome deposition.
    Genotyping of CRISPR-edited embryos showed a mean of 38 +/- 13 distinct indel
    events per animal, with no dominant allele, yet produced near-complete pigmentation
    loss, demonstrating the strong genotype-phenotype link. In E. berryi, TDO knockout
    alone produced only slightly delayed pigmentation due to IDO compensation, but
    TDO+IDO double knockout yielded complete albinism enabling the first in vivo
    calcium imaging of neural activity in a cephalopod (PMID:37343558).
