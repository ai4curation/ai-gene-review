Gene Description

Tryptophan 2,3-dioxygenase (TDO) is a heme-dependent oxidoreductase that catalyzes the first committed step in ommochrome pigment biosynthesis in D. pealeii, converting L-tryptophan to N-formylkynurenine via oxidative cleavage of the indole ring. TDO is expressed in the retina and chromatophore layer of the skin, the two principal ommochrome-pigmented tissues. Crawford et al. (2020) demonstrated that CRISPR-Cas9 knockout of TDO in D. pealeii embryos efficiently eliminated pigmentation in both eyes and chromatophores with >90% disruption efficiency, while ink sac melanin pigmentation was unaffected. This was the first gene knockout achieved in any cephalopod. TDO inhibition with 680C91 also completely blocked pigmentation, confirming the enzyme is required for ommochrome synthesis. Notably, D. pealeii lacks an IDO (indoleamine 2,3-dioxygenase) ortholog, unlike Euprymna berryi where IDO can compensate for TDO loss. TDO belongs to the PANTHER family PTHR10138 (Tryptophan 2,3-dioxygenase), is predicted to function as a homotetramer (dimer of dimers), and contains a single heme cofactor per subunit. The gene spans 13 exons over 120 kb of genomic DNA.

Core Functions

TDO catalyzes the oxidative cleavage of L-tryptophan to N-formylkynurenine, the first committed step in the kynurenine pathway and ommochrome biosynthesis in D. pealeii. CRISPR-Cas9 knockout eliminated ommochrome pigmentation in both eyes and chromatophores with >90% efficiency, and pharmacological inhibition with 680C91 confirmed the enzyme is required. D. pealeii lacks an IDO ortholog, making TDO the sole enzyme for this reaction. TDO is a heme-dependent dioxygenase that binds one heme group per subunit.

Highly Efficient Knockout of a Squid Pigmentation Gene
  • TDO was selected as a knockout target because ommochromes pigment cephalopod eyes and chromatophores, and TDO catalyzes the first committed step in their biosynthesis.
  • A single TDO gene was identified in the D. pealeii genome, spanning 13 exons over 120 kb. Phylogenetic analysis confirmed the gene as a bona fide TDO distinct from IDO.
  • In situ hybridization showed TDO expression in chromatophore precursors on arms and mantle, and in eyes; RNAseq confirmed highest expression in retina and chromatophore layer of skin.
  • TDO-selective inhibitor 680C91 completely blocked pigmentation in both eyes and chromatophores, with animals developing normally otherwise.
  • CRISPR-Cas9 knockout with two sgRNAs targeting exons 1 and 2 eliminated pigmentation with >90% disruption efficiency. Ink sac melanin was unaffected, confirming specificity for the ommochrome pathway.
  • This was the first gene knockout achieved in any cephalopod.
PMID:37343558
Creation of an albino squid line by CRISPR-Cas9 and its application for in vivo functional imaging of neural activity
  • In E. berryi, TDO knockout alone did not eliminate pigmentation despite >90% disruption efficiency, due to compensation by IDO. D. pealeii genome lacks IDO orthologs, explaining why TDO knockout alone is sufficient in that species.
  • E. berryi homozygous TDO knockout showed slightly delayed pigmentation during development. TDO+IDO double knockout produced complete albinism.
  • TDO expression in E. berryi is approximately 20-fold higher than IDO in stage 24 embryos. TDO is expressed in eyes and chromatophores, similar to D. pealeii.
  • Albino E. berryi enabled the first in vivo calcium imaging of neural activity in a cephalopod using two-photon microscopy.

Experiment: Use single-cell RNA-seq to map TDO expression across chromatophore subtypes and developmental stages in D. pealeii embryos.

Type: transcriptomics

Experiment: Measure kynurenine pathway metabolite levels (N-formylkynurenine, kynurenine, 3-hydroxykynurenine, xanthurenic acid) in TDO knockout vs wildtype D. pealeii embryos by LC-MS/MS.

Hypothesis: TDO knockout eliminates kynurenine pathway flux entirely in D. pealeii due to the absence of IDO, unlike in E. berryi where IDO provides compensation.

Type: metabolomics

Experiment: Test whether partial TDO knockout in adult squid affects chromatophore expansion dynamics and camouflage behavior using video analysis.

Hypothesis: Reduced ommochrome content from partial TDO loss impairs chromatophore opacity and dynamic range of color change.
