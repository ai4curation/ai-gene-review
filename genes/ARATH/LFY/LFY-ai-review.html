Gene Description

LEAFY (LFY; At5g61850) is a plant-specific nuclear FLO/LFY-family DNA-binding transcription factor that commits Arabidopsis primordia to floral fate. Its core activity is sequence-specific binding to cis-regulatory DNA at floral meristem and floral organ identity loci, including AP1, AP3, AG and CAL, followed by transcriptional activation. Recent mechanistic work synthesized in the Falcon research file supports LFY as a pioneer transcription factor: LFY binds nucleosome-occupied AP1 regulatory DNA, displaces linker histone H1, recruits SWI/SNF chromatin remodelers, and licenses chromatin for floral fate reprogramming. Its target selection is modulated by oligomerization through an N-terminal SAM domain and by cofactors such as UFO, LMI2, SYD and BRM.

Proposed New Ontology Terms

Definition: A DNA-binding transcription factor activity in which a gene product binds cognate cis-regulatory DNA in nucleosome-occupied or otherwise closed chromatin and initiates local chromatin opening to enable subsequent transcriptional activation.

Justification: Existing LFY annotations capture DNA-binding transcription factor activity and chromatin DNA binding separately, but not the experimentally supported pioneer activity that couples nucleosomal DNA binding to H1 displacement, SWI/SNF recruitment and competence for floral fate reprogramming.

Research Report: Functional Annotation of LEAFY (LFY) in Arabidopsis thaliana (UniProt Q00958, locus At5g61850)

1) Target verification (critical disambiguation)

The literature synthesized below explicitly concerns Arabidopsis thaliana LEAFY (LFY), a plant-specific FLO/LFY-family master regulator of floral development described as a helix–turn–helix (HTH) DNA-binding transcription factor and pioneer factor that activates APETALA1 (AP1) and other floral regulators (yamaguchi2021leafyapioneer pages 3-6, yamaguchi2021leafyapioneer pages 1-2, jin2021leafyisa pages 4-6). These defining properties align with the UniProt identity you provided for Q00958 (“Protein LEAFY”, gene LFY, Arabidopsis), and no evidence in the retrieved corpus indicates conflation with a different gene sharing the symbol LFY.

2) Key concepts and current understanding (definitions and primary function)

2.1. What LFY is (molecular function)

LFY is a sequence-specific DNA-binding transcription factor that recognizes semi-palindromic DNA elements (notably described as 19-bp elements) using a conserved C-terminal DNA-binding domain with an HTH-type fold (sayou2016asamoligomerization pages 2-3). Functionally, LFY is a “master regulator” that can trigger early flowering and ectopic flower formation when ectopically expressed, consistent with a primary role in reprogramming developmental fate rather than catalysis/transport (sayou2016asamoligomerization pages 1-2, sayou2016asamoligomerization pages 3-4).

2.2. Domain architecture concepts relevant to function

A central modern concept is that LFY’s genomic activity is not determined solely by its DNA-binding domain; an N-terminal SAM (Sterile Alpha Motif) oligomerization domain strongly shapes the set of genomic loci that LFY can access, particularly those with low-affinity binding sites and/or repressive chromatin (sayou2016asamoligomerization pages 1-2, sayou2016asamoligomerization pages 2-3).

Quantitative structural/biophysical evidence (domain function):
* The SAM domain crystal structure was solved at 2.3 Å resolution, and SEC-MALLS showed concentration-dependent oligomers of up to 8 LFY-SAM monomers in solution; key interface mutations (e.g., T75E, R112E) abolish oligomerization (sayou2016asamoligomerization pages 1-2, sayou2016asamoligomerization pages 3-4).

2.3. “Pioneer transcription factor” definition as applied to LFY

Pioneer transcription factors are typically defined as TFs that can bind their motifs on nucleosomal/closed chromatin, initiate local chromatin opening, and thereby enable recruitment of additional factors to activate lineage programs. LFY meets these criteria in plants: it binds nucleosome-occupied DNA at key loci (e.g., AP1), locally unlocks chromatin by displacing linker histone H1 and recruiting SWI/SNF remodelers, and licenses floral fate (jin2021leafyisa pages 4-6, jin2021leafyisa pages 6-8, yamaguchi2021leafyapioneer pages 1-2).

3) Core biological role and pathways

3.1. Pathway position: from florigen inputs to floral fate outputs

A widely used current model positions LFY as a floral fate switch controlled upstream by florigen/anti-florigen signaling and downstream by direct activation of floral meristem identity and floral organ identity programs.

Upstream, an expert mini-review describes repression of LFY during vegetative growth by the TFL1–FD complex (“anti-florigen”), and activation when FT competes with TFL1 to prevent FD-mediated repression; in addition, auxin signaling (ARF5/MONOPTEROS) and SWI/SNF ATPases (SYD/BRM) are described as contributing to LFY locus activation via promoter elements (yamaguchi2021leafyapioneer pages 3-6, yamaguchi2021leafyapioneer pages 1-2).

Downstream, LFY directly promotes AP1, and cooperates with cofactors to activate organ identity genes such as AP3 (jin2021leafyisa pages 9-10, rieu2023thefboxprotein pages 6-10).

3.2. Direct mechanistic link to AP1 activation (pioneer activity at a key locus)

The best-supported mechanistic pathway for LFY’s primary function is:
1) LFY binds the AP1 regulatory region even when assembled into a nucleosome;
2) LFY locally reduces linker histone H1 occupancy and recruits SWI/SNF components;
3) chromatin accessibility changes broaden later, coincident with additional cofactor recruitment and stronger AP1 induction.

Key quantitative findings (Jin et al., 2021):
* In vitro, LFY bound a nucleosomal AP1 fragment with apparent K_D ~5–10 nM, and binding was lost when the LFY motif was mutated (jin2021leafyisa pages 1-2).
* In vivo, most LFY binding sites were nucleosome occupied: 68% in root explants and ~60% of peaks in inflorescences overlapped nucleosomes (jin2021leafyisa pages 4-6).
* A time-course showed increasing numbers of LFY-bound differentially expressed genes: 5 (1 h), 33 (6 h), 302 (24 h); LFY-regulated genes included 202 nucleosomal versus 101 nucleosome-free genes (jin2021leafyisa pages 6-8).
* At AP1, H1 ChIP-qPCR showed reduced linker histone H1 occupancy and SWI/SNF component recruitment (SWI3B) at 24 h post LFY activation (jin2021leafyisa pages 6-8).

4) Subcellular localization and site of action

LFY’s biochemical function is exerted at chromatin, consistent with nuclear localization as a DNA-binding transcription factor. The primary evidence is functional: LFY binds genomic loci in vivo (ChIP-seq) and binds nucleosome-occupied DNA to initiate chromatin changes at AP1 and other targets (jin2021leafyisa pages 4-6, jin2021leafyisa pages 6-8). In addition, the UFO study demonstrates formation of a DNA-bound LFY-containing complex on defined cis-elements, also consistent with chromatin/nuclear action (rieu2023thefboxprotein pages 6-10).

5) Recent developments and latest research (prioritizing 2023–2024)

5.1. 2023: UFO redirects LFY to new cis-elements (mechanistic “retargeting” model)

A major recent advance is the 2023 Nature Plants study showing that the F-box protein UNUSUAL FLORAL ORGANS (UFO) is not merely an accessory regulator but can redirect LFY binding to novel cis-elements (LUBS), thereby changing LFY’s target selection and enabling activation of specific genes (notably AP3).

Key mechanistic findings include:
* Functional specificity: In transient assays, AP3 and RBE promoters showed strong activation by LFY+UFO, while LFY-driven activation of AP1 and AG was relatively insensitive to UFO (rieu2023thefboxprotein pages 6-10).
* F-box dispensability: A truncated UFO lacking the F-box (UFOΔFbox) could activate pAP3 similarly to full-length UFO and complement a ufo mutant, indicating the canonical ubiquitination link is largely dispensable for the LFY–UFO synergy (rieu2023thefboxprotein pages 6-10).
* Defined DNA element and complex stoichiometry: A 20-bp LFY–UFO-dependent element (LUBS0) near AP3 regulatory sequences was identified, and biochemical reconstitution supported an ASK1–UFO–LFY DNA-bound complex with SEC-MALS mass 102 ± 3.3 kDa, consistent with 1:1:1:1 stoichiometry (rieu2023thefboxprotein pages 6-10).
* Genome-scale binding effect: ampDAP-seq identified numerous regions with UFO-enhanced LFY binding; the top 600 had coverage fold change > 4.7 (rieu2023thefboxprotein pages 6-10).
* Motif logic: LUBS motifs comprise a 6-bp URM (RRNRCA) + 4-bp spacer + a monomeric/dimeric LFY-like site; these LFY-like sites are typically lower affinity than LFY-alone sites, implying UFO expands LFY’s usable cis-regulatory space (rieu2023thefboxprotein pages 6-10).

These findings refine “LFY cofactor biology” from a general synergy concept to a concrete structural/biochemical model in which UFO contributes direct DNA contacts and alters the effective binding landscape.

5.2. 2024: Trans-kingdom pioneer remodeling mechanisms (LFY in vertebrate chromatin)

A 2024 bioRxiv preprint reports that Arabidopsis LFY can recruit vertebrate SWI/SNF remodelers and confer accessibility/activation to a plant promoter reporter in zebrafish embryos/cells, suggesting that LFY’s pioneering logic (H1 displacement and SWI/SNF recruitment) can be deployed in a heterologous chromatin environment.

Key quantitative findings:
* Reporter activation fractions: 6.5% GFP-positive embryos (n=1025) and 10.2% (n=974) at two LFY mRNA doses, versus 0% in controls (ribeiro2024theplantpioneer pages 1-4).
* ATAC-seq motif enrichment: LFY-exclusive accessible regions were enriched for LFY motifs (4.60%) relative to shared accessible regions (4.10%; p < 0.0001, Fisher exact test) (ribeiro2024theplantpioneer pages 4-7).

As a 2024 preprint, these results should be interpreted cautiously pending peer review, but they represent a notable conceptual extension of LFY pioneer activity.

A 2023 Frontiers review highlights how 3D gene expression atlases and high-resolution TF–chromatin interaction studies are revising “classic” floral meristem models, including the importance of chromatin environment and temporally staged regulatory events (sayou2016asamoligomerization pages 2-3).

LFY is frequently used as an engineering handle to accelerate flowering and to reprogram tissues toward reproductive fate, with practical implications for breeding cycle time and tissue culture workflows.

  • Ectopic flower induction / developmental reprogramming: Constitutive LFY expression can trigger early flowering and ectopic flowers (including in non-floral tissues), supporting its use in experimental reprogramming strategies (sayou2016asamoligomerization pages 1-2, sayou2016asamoligomerization pages 3-4).
  • In vitro flower organogenesis concepts: A recent review on “de novo flower organogenesis” frames LFY among core floral meristem identity genes (with AP1) whose coordinated induction may help bypass prolonged vegetative phases in tissue culture; the review proposes that coordinated expression of UFO and LFY may be required for full activation of floral identity programs in vitro (peeters2026integratingmeristeminitiation pages 1-2; not yet extracted as evidence snippets in this run, so not cited here).

9) Summary functional annotation (evidence-weighted)

LFY (UniProt Q00958; A. thaliana At5g61850) is a nuclear, DNA-binding transcription factor and pioneer factor that drives floral fate primarily by activating AP1 and related floral regulators. Its ability to engage nucleosome-occupied targets and locally unlock chromatin is supported by quantitative nucleosome overlap statistics (68% root; ~60% inflorescence), nucleosomal binding affinity (K_D ~5–10 nM at AP1), and ChIP-qPCR evidence for H1 displacement and SWI/SNF recruitment at AP1 (jin2021leafyisa pages 4-6, jin2021leafyisa pages 1-2, jin2021leafyisa pages 6-8).
