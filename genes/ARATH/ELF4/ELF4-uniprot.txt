CC   -!- FUNCTION: Component of the central CCA1/LHY-TOC1 feedback loop in the
CC       circadian clock that promotes clock accuracy and is required for
CC       sustained rhythms in the absence of daily light/dark cycles. Part of a
CC       corepressor complex consisting of ELF4, ELF3, and LUX involved in the
CC       transcriptional regulation of APRR9. Increases ELF3 nuclear
CC       distribution and localization in nuclear bodies. Required for
CC       responsiveness to continuous red, by regulating phytochrome B (phyB)
CC       signaling (including during seedling de-etiolation) and gene
CC       expression. Mediates both entrainment to an environmental cycle and
CC       circadian rhythm sustainability under constant conditions. Controls
CC       flowering time. Necessary for light-induced expression of both CCA1 and
CC       LHY. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12214234, ECO:0000269|PubMed:14605220,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:15659097, ECO:0000269|PubMed:15908595,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:16212608, ECO:0000269|PubMed:17384164,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:20357892, ECO:0000269|PubMed:21255161,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:22327739}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homodimer. Interacts with ELF3. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:22327739}.
CC   -!- INTERACTION:
CC       O04211; O82804: ELF3; NbExp=4; IntAct=EBI-16415004, EBI-16415030;
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Nucleus {ECO:0000269|PubMed:14605220,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:22327739}. Note=Diffuse distribution. Targeted to
CC       nuclear bodies after interaction with ELF3 (PubMed:22327739).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:22327739}.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: Follows a light-dependent circadian-regulated expression
CC       with a peak in the evening, about 12 hours after dawn. Induced in
CC       seedlings and roots by continuous far-red light (FRc) via the phyA
CC       signaling pathway and by continuous red light (Rc) via the phyB
CC       signaling pathway. This light-mediated induction is repressed by ELF3,
CC       CCA1 and LHY. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:14605220,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:16212608, ECO:0000269|PubMed:16908503,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:18676661, ECO:0000269|PubMed:20357892,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:21255161}.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: Impaired in their ability to sense day length
CC       leading to early flowering in non-inductive photoperiods (probably
CC       through the misexpression of CO), and elongated hypocotyls and petioles
CC       in short days (SD). Attenuated light-regulated expression of CCA1 and
CC       LHY, central oscillator components. Transient output rhythms with
CC       highly variable period lengths before becoming arrhythmic. Reduced
CC       responsiveness to continuous red, but not continuous far-red light,
CC       suggesting a role in phyB signaling but not phyA signaling.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12214234, ECO:0000269|PubMed:14605220,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:15659097, ECO:0000269|PubMed:15908595,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:16212608, ECO:0000269|PubMed:17384164,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:20357892, ECO:0000269|PubMed:21255161}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the EARLY FLOWERING 4 family. {ECO:0000305}.
