CC   -!- FUNCTION: Transcription activator active in flowering time control. May
CC       integrate signals from the photoperiod, vernalization and autonomous
CC       floral induction pathways. Can modulate class B and C homeotic genes
CC       expression. When associated with AGL24, mediates effect of gibberellins
CC       on flowering under short-day conditions, and regulates the expression
CC       of LEAFY (LFY), which links floral induction and floral development.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10995392, ECO:0000269|PubMed:18339670,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:18466303, ECO:0000269|PubMed:19656343}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Forms a heterodimer with AGL24 through MADS-box domain.
CC       Interacts with AGL15, AGL16 and AGL19 (PubMed:15805477,
CC       PubMed:18466303). Interacts with OXS3 in the nucleus (PubMed:31540691).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15805477, ECO:0000269|PubMed:18466303,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:31540691}.

CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:18466303, ECO:0000269|PubMed:31540691}. Cytoplasm
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:18466303}. Note=Translocation from the cytoplasm to
CC       the nucleus in the presence of AGL24.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Widely expressed. Not found in the apical meristem
CC       of short-day grown plants in vegetative stage.
CC   -!- DEVELOPMENTAL STAGE: Rapidly up-regulated in apical meristems during
CC       the transition to flowering. Transiently expressed in inflorescence
CC       meristem. Re-appears in stage 3 flowers, in the central dome that later
CC       will develop into stamens and carpels. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:19656343}.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: Up-regulated by gibberellins, vernalization and under long-
CC       day conditions. Gradual increase during vegetative growth. Induced by
CC       AGL24 at the shoot apex at the floral transitional stage. Repressed by
CC       SVP during the early stages of flower development. Inhibited by AP1 in
CC       emerging floral meristems (PubMed:17428825, PubMed:18339670,
CC       PubMed:19656343). Repressed by SHL to prevent flowering
CC       (PubMed:25281686). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:17428825,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:18339670, ECO:0000269|PubMed:19656343,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:25281686}.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: Plants are late-flowering.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11123798}.
