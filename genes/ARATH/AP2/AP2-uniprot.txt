CC   -!- FUNCTION: Probable transcriptional activator that promotes early floral
CC       meristem identity (PubMed:7919989). Is required subsequently for the
CC       transition of an inflorescence meristem into a floral meristem
CC       (PubMed:1675158). Plays a central role in the specification of floral
CC       identity, particularly for the normal development of sepals and petals
CC       in the wild-type flower, by spatially controlling the expression
CC       domains of multiple floral organ identity genes (PubMed:1675158,
CC       PubMed:23034631). Acts as A class cadastral protein by repressing the C
CC       class floral homeotic gene AGAMOUS in association with other repressors
CC       like LEUNIG and SEUSS (PubMed:1675158). Directly represses AGAMOUS by
CC       recruiting the transcriptional corepressor TOPLESS and the histone
CC       deacetylase HDA19 (PubMed:23034631). It is also required during seed
CC       development (PubMed:1675158). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:1675158,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:23034631, ECO:0000269|PubMed:7919989}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: May form homodimer. Interacts with HDA19 and with TPL in an
CC       EAR-motif dependent manner (PubMed:23034631).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:23034631}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Nucleus {ECO:0000255|PROSITE-ProRule:PRU00366}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Sepals, petals, stamens, carpels, developing
CC       ovules, inflorescence stem, leaf and stem.
CC   -!- DEVELOPMENTAL STAGE: It is detectable at low levels throughout the
CC       shoot apex and at enhanced levels in the inflorescence meristem, young
CC       floral buds and throughout the early stages of flower development and
CC       organogenesis. During floral organ differentiation it becomes spatially
CC       restricted to specific organ, tissue and cell types within the flower.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: Negatively regulated by the C class floral homeotic protein
CC       AGAMOUS in stamens and carpels. MicroRNA 172 (miRNA172) negatively
CC       regulates APETALA2 at the translational level and may modulate its
CC       expression pattern. Does not seem to be influenced by jasmonate and
CC       Alternaria brassicicola. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12805630,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:12893888, ECO:0000269|PubMed:14555699}.
CC   -!- MISCELLANEOUS: Mutations in the APETALA2 gene result in the ectopic
CC       expression of AGAMOUS, leading to the replacement of sepals by carpels
CC       and stamens and of petals by stamens.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the AP2/ERF transcription factor family. AP2
CC       subfamily. {ECO:0000305}.
