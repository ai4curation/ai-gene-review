CC   -!- FUNCTION: Transcription factor acting as a positive regulator in the
CC       ethylene response pathway, by promoting histone acetylation in an
CC       ENAP1-dependent manner, thus accelerating the expression of ethylene-
CC       responsive genes (PubMed:28874528). Binds DNA (PubMed:26352699). Is
CC       required for ethylene responsiveness in adult plant tissues. Binds a
CC       primary ethylene response element present in the ETHYLENE-RESPONSE-
CC       FACTOR1 promoter with consequence to activate the transcription of this
CC       gene. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:26352699, ECO:0000269|PubMed:28874528,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:9215635, ECO:0000269|PubMed:9851977}.
CC   -!- ACTIVITY REGULATION: Activated by phosphorylation by MPK3 and MPK6
CC       (PubMed:18273012). Down-regulated by KIN10 that controls its protein
CC       stability under a phosphorylation-dependent manner (PubMed:28600557).
CC       Satnilitzed during hypoxia (e.g. submergences) via a ceramides-
CC       triggered and CTR1-dependent manner (PubMed:25822663).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:18273012, ECO:0000269|PubMed:25822663,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:28600557}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Acts as a homodimer to bind the primary ethylene response
CC       element. Interacts with TAF12B. Interacts with KIN10 (PubMed:28600557).
CC       Binds to ENAP1 in the presence of ethylene; this reaction facilitates
CC       its association with histone (PubMed:27694846, PubMed:28874528).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:17526916, ECO:0000269|PubMed:27694846,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:28600557, ECO:0000269|PubMed:28874528}.
