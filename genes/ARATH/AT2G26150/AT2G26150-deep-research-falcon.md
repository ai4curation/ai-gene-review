## Research report: Functional annotation of **Arabidopsis thaliana HSFA2** (AT2G26150; UniProt **O80982**)

### Executive summary
**HSFA2 (AtHsfA2)** is a class A **heat shock transcription factor (HSF)** in *Arabidopsis thaliana* that functions as a **sequence-specific transcriptional activator** of heat-stress response genes and, critically, as a **core regulator of heat-stress–induced transcriptional memory (thermomemory)**. Its best-supported primary role is to **bind heat shock elements (HSEs)** in promoters of “memory genes” (e.g., **HSP22, HSP18.2, HSA32, HSP21, APX2**) and sustain their transcription during the recovery period after heat stress; this is coupled to persistent chromatin marking (notably **H3K4me3**) and depends on **heteromeric complexes with HSFA3 and other HSFs**. HSFA2 activity is regulated by hierarchical induction downstream of **HSFA1** factors, complex assembly, nucleocytoplasmic trafficking, alternative splicing, and post-translational modifications including **MPK6-dependent phosphorylation** and **SUMO1-mediated SUMOylation at Lys315**. (friedrich2021heteromerichsfa2hsfa3complexes pages 6-7, friedrich2021heteromerichsfa2hsfa3complexes pages 10-11, kappel2023genomicandepigenomic pages 1-3, andrasi2021diversityofplant pages 14-15, bakery2024heatstresstranscription pages 6-7)

### 1) Key concepts and definitions (current understanding)

#### 1.1 Heat shock transcription factors (HSFs) and heat shock elements (HSEs)
Plant HSFs are transcription factors that bind promoter **HSEs**, which are composed of inverted **5′-nGAAn-3′ repeats**, to activate heat-stress response programs. Class A HSFs (including HSFA2) generally function as transcriptional activators, in part because they contain activation features (e.g., AHA motifs, discussed in reviews), and they participate in homo-/hetero-oligomeric assemblies that support DNA binding and activation. (li2025heatshocktranscription pages 2-4, kappel2023genomicandepigenomic pages 1-3)

#### 1.2 Thermomemory / heat-stress transcriptional memory
**Heat-stress transcriptional memory** refers to the sustained induction (or enhanced re-induction) of a subset of heat-inducible genes after the initial stress has ended, enabling improved responses to recurrent heat episodes. A major recent framework is that **HSFA2/HSFA3-dependent promoter binding** and **chromatin features** (sequence elements + accessibility + histone marks) distinguish memory genes from non-memory genes. (kappel2023genomicandepigenomic pages 1-3, friedrich2021heteromerichsfa2hsfa3complexes pages 10-11)

### 2) Recent developments and latest research (prioritizing 2023–2024)

#### 2.1 Genome-scale identification of HSFA2/HSFA3 memory targets and determinants (2023)
A 2023 *Genome Biology* study used **time-course ChIP-seq** to define global binding of two “memory” HSFs, **HSFA2 and HSFA3**, reporting that they show **near-identical binding patterns** and that in vitro vs in vivo binding strengths correlate strongly—supporting the conclusion that **DNA sequence elements are major determinants** of binding. Importantly, **memory genes** were found to be strongly enriched for a **tripartite HSE**, tend to have **low basal expression**, sit in an **accessible chromatin environment**, and gain **heat-induced H3K4me3**. These results provide a predictive basis for identifying and potentially engineering transcriptional memory. 

Publication details: Kappel et al., 2023-05 (Genome Biology). URL: https://doi.org/10.1186/s13059-023-02970-5. (kappel2023genomicandepigenomic pages 1-3)

#### 2.2 Updated regulatory models emphasizing dynamic tuning (“rheostat”) and recovery/reset (2024)
A 2024 *New Phytologist* review frames HSFs as a **molecular rheostat** that tunes response intensity and supports recovery. In this model, HSFA2 contributes to transcriptional memory (including via HSFA2–HSFA3 complexes and H3K4 methylation at memory loci), and memory strength can be modulated by protein-quality-control and autophagy-linked processes that affect HSFA2-associated complexes. The review also emphasizes **HSFA2 alternative splicing**, yielding isoforms with different NES features that alter nuclear retention and stability, suggesting an additional layer by which plants tune immediate vs sustained responses under differing heat regimes. 

Publication details: Bakery et al., 2024-07 (New Phytologist). URL: https://doi.org/10.1111/nph.20017. (bakery2024heatstresstranscription pages 6-7)

#### 2.3 Maintenance of abiotic stress memory framework (2023)
A 2023 *Plant Cell* review on maintenance of abiotic stress memory uses heat acclimation genetics as a model and explicitly points to HSFA2 as a priming-induced regulator of memory outputs (including differential control of memory genes vs non-memory genes, such as APX2 vs other heat-induced loci), framing HSFA2 as part of regulatory circuits that maintain stress memory. 

Publication details: Charng et al., 2023-10 (The Plant Cell). URL: https://doi.org/10.1093/plcell/koac313. (wang2023transcriptionalregulatorsof pages 13-14)

### 3) Molecular function and mechanism

#### 3.1 Primary molecular function: sequence-specific transcriptional activation at HSEs
HSFA2 functions as a **DNA-binding transcription factor** that recognizes HSEs and activates expression of heat-response genes, with prominent influence during **prolonged/repeated stress** and the **recovery/memory** phase. HSFA2 is positioned downstream of HSFA1 “master regulators,” consistent with hierarchical heat-response cascades. (kappel2023genomicandepigenomic pages 1-3, wang2023transcriptionalregulatorsof pages 2-4, guo2016theplantheat pages 6-7)

#### 3.2 Target genes: memory gene set includes HSPs and redox-related genes
In a prominent experimental system for thermomemory, HSFA2 (often together with HSFA3) supports sustained induction of classic memory genes including **HSA32, HSP18.2, HSP22, HSP21**, and regulates the transcriptional memory output gene **APX2** (ascorbate peroxidase 2; a ROS-detox enzyme frequently used as a memory readout). (friedrich2021heteromerichsfa2hsfa3complexes pages 6-7, friedrich2021heteromerichsfa2hsfa3complexes pages 10-11)

#### 3.3 Complexes: HSFA2–HSFA3 heteromers drive transcriptional memory
A 2021 *Nature Communications* study provided mechanistic evidence that **HSFA3 binds HSFA2** and that in vivo these proteins form **heteromeric complexes** with additional HSFs (including HSFA1A/B/D and HSFA7A/B). Time-course ChIP evidence shows heat-dependent enrichment of HSFA3 (and HSFA2) at HSE-containing promoters of memory genes (including **HSP22, HSP18.2, HSA32, APX2**), with binding dynamics during recovery consistent with sustained memory-phase activation. Importantly, the study supports the model that **complexes containing both HSFA2 and HSFA3** are especially effective in promoting transcriptional memory and associated chromatin changes. 

Publication details: Friedrich et al., 2021-06 (Nature Communications). URL: https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-021-23786-6. (friedrich2021heteromerichsfa2hsfa3complexes pages 6-7, friedrich2021heteromerichsfa2hsfa3complexes pages 10-11, friedrich2021heteromerichsfa2hsfa3complexes media e8cfc4fb)

### 4) Biological roles (processes and pathways)

#### 4.1 Heat stress acclimation and acquired thermotolerance
HSFA2 accumulates strongly during prolonged or repeated heat stress and functions as a high-potency activator within heat-response gene networks. Overexpression evidence compiled in authoritative reviews indicates that AtHSFA2 overexpression can enhance thermotolerance and can partially rescue thermotolerance defects even when HSFA1 function is compromised, highlighting HSFA2’s strong activating capacity within the heat response. (guo2016theplantheat pages 6-7)

#### 4.2 Thermomemory and chromatin coupling (H3K4me3)
During thermomemory, HSFA2 and HSFA3 maintain elevated expression of memory genes over days after priming. Mechanistically, HSFA2/HSFA3 promoter occupancy during recovery is associated with sustained **H3K4 hypermethylation** at memory loci such as **HSP22** and **APX2**, and genome-scale analyses show that heat-induced memory is associated with **H3K4me3 accumulation** and specific HSE architecture (tripartite HSEs). (friedrich2021heteromerichsfa2hsfa3complexes pages 10-11, kappel2023genomicandepigenomic pages 1-3, friedrich2021heteromerichsfa2hsfa3complexes media e8cfc4fb)

#### 4.3 ROS/oxidative stress links
While the current evidence set is strongest for thermomemory, HSFA2 is also discussed in the context of broader stress networks involving antioxidant components (including APX-type enzymes) and redox homeostasis in recent synthesis papers that connect HSFA2-regulated programs to antioxidant gene classes. (li2025heatshocktranscription pages 6-7)

### 5) Regulation, localization, and protein-level control

#### 5.1 Hierarchical activation downstream of HSFA1
In current models, **HSFA1A/B/D** act early and upstream as major positive regulators in heat responses, inducing downstream HSFs including HSFA2; HSFA2 then amplifies and sustains longer-term programs (including memory). (kappel2023genomicandepigenomic pages 1-3, wang2023transcriptionalregulatorsof pages 2-4)

#### 5.2 Post-translational modifications (PTMs)
Evidence synthesized in authoritative reviews indicates that HSFA2 is regulated by:
- **MPK6-dependent phosphorylation**, which facilitates nuclear transfer/activation during heat stress. (andrasi2021diversityofplant pages 14-15)
- **SUMO1-mediated SUMOylation at Lys315**, which reduces HSFA2’s capacity to activate HSP genes; SUMO1 overexpression is reported to phenocopy hsfa2 with impaired acquired thermotolerance in the cited synthesis. (andrasi2021diversityofplant pages 14-15)
Additionally, review evidence notes phosphorylation sites (e.g., Ser309) in HSFA2 regulatory discussions. (guo2016theplantheat pages 6-7)

#### 5.3 Nucleocytoplasmic trafficking and alternative splicing
HSFs (including HSFA2) undergo nucleocytoplasmic shuttling via NLS/NES features, enabling stress-responsive nuclear accumulation. Recent synthesis emphasizes that **HSFA2 alternative splicing** can generate isoforms with distinct nuclear export behavior (NES-containing versus NES-lacking), which influences nuclear retention, stability, and potentially the balance between transient initial response and sustained memory programs under different heat severities. (wang2023transcriptionalregulatorsof pages 2-4, bakery2024heatstresstranscription pages 6-7)

### 7) Current applications and real-world implementations

#### 7.1 Crop improvement and engineering of heat resilience
Recent literature explicitly links HSFA2-centered thermomemory biology to **breeding and engineering goals**. The 2023 genome/epigenome determinants study frames prediction/engineering of transcriptional memory as a route toward improved heat tolerance, while the 2024 rheostat review discusses how dynamic tuning of HSF pathways—including memory establishment and resetting—could be leveraged to optimize survival and recovery under heat waves. These sources collectively support HSFA2 as a high-priority node for engineering strategies (e.g., manipulating HSF expression timing, memory-gene promoter architecture, or chromatin features). (kappel2023genomicandepigenomic pages 1-3, bakery2024heatstresstranscription pages 6-7)

### 8) Expert opinion and synthesis (authoritative perspectives)
Expert synthesis across recent reviews converges on a consensus that HSFA2 is not merely a heat-response amplifier, but a **core coordinator of sustained post-stress transcriptional states** via **HSFA2/HSFA3 complexes** and chromatin coupling (H3K4 methylation). The “rheostat” framing further emphasizes the importance of balancing HSFA2-driven protection with controlled attenuation/reset to support growth and reproduction after stress. (kappel2023genomicandepigenomic pages 1-3, bakery2024heatstresstranscription pages 6-7)
