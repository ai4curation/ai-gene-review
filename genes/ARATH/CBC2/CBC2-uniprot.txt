CC   -!- FUNCTION: Serine/threonine protein kinase that phosphorylates proteins
CC       on serine and threonine residues (PubMed:29101334). Collectively with
CC       CBC1, acts as a negative regulator of stomatal opening, probably via
CC       the inhibition of plasma membrane-type ATPases (AHA1 and AHA2) activity
CC       in guard cells, but in an abscisic acid (ABA)-independent manner
CC       (PubMed:31904040). However, at low concentrations of CO(2), together
CC       with CBC1, stimulates stomatal opening via the inhibition of S-type
CC       anion channels in response to blue light (BL) and red light (RL), thus
CC       being a key component to maximize photosynthesis in the light under low
CC       CO(2) conditions (PubMed:29101334, PubMed:31904040). Required for
CC       temperature decrease in leaves (PubMed:29101334). Downstream target of
CC       HIGH LEAF TEMPERATURE1 (HT1) during low CO(2)-induced stomatal opening
CC       (PubMed:29101334). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:29101334,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:31904040}.

CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:29101334, ECO:0000269|PubMed:31904040}.
CC   -!- PTM: Autophosphorylated (PubMed:29101334). Phosphorylated by HT1 in
CC       response to low CO(2) concentrations (PubMed:29101334).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:29101334}.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: Partial impairment in temperature decrease and
CC       slightly impaired stomatal opening triggered by blue light (BL)
CC       (PubMed:29101334). The double mutant cbc1 cbc2 exhibits a reduced
CC       voltage-dependent inward-rectifying K(+) current and suppressed low
CC       CO(2), red and blue light-induced stomatal opening leading to a
CC       severely impaired temperature decrease (PubMed:29101334). Larger
CC       stomatal opening under both dark and blue light conditions in cbc1 cbc2
CC       plants, and associated with increased phosphorylation of C-terminal Thr
CC       in plasma membrane-type ATPases (AHA1 and AHA2) in guard cells
CC       (PubMed:31904040). Altered CO(2)-triggered stomatal closure
CC       (PubMed:29101334). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:29101334,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:31904040}.
