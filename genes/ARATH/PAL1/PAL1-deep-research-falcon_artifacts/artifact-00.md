| Identity | The target is correctly identified as **Arabidopsis thaliana PAL1**, locus **At2g37040**, UniProt **P35510**, one of four Arabidopsis PAL genes; evidence in the corpus explicitly maps AT2G37040 to PAL1 and P35510. | (pqac-00000000, pqac-00000002, pqac-00000004) |
| Reaction | PAL1 is a **phenylalanine ammonia-lyase** that catalyzes the **non-oxidative deamination of L-phenylalanine to trans-cinnamic acid and NH3**, the committed entry step into phenylpropanoid metabolism. | (pqac-00000003, pqac-00000004) |
| Substrate specificity | Direct Arabidopsis family evidence in the corpus indicates **AtPAL1, AtPAL2, and AtPAL4 show robust in vitro phenylalanine deamination**, whereas AtPAL3 is weak. The broader PAL literature in the corpus supports PAL enzymes as MIO-dependent aromatic lyases; however, **direct 2023-2024 AtPAL1-specific kinetic constants/substrate comparisons are not present in our corpus**. | (pqac-00000004, pqac-00000002) |
| Pathway roles | PAL1 functions at the entry to the **phenylpropanoid pathway**, supplying precursors for **lignin**, **flavonoids/anthocyanins**, and contributing to **salicylic acid (SA)** biosynthesis. Recent mechanistic studies in the corpus link PAL1 transcription to induced **lignin and SA accumulation** during defense signaling. | (pqac-00000003, pqac-00000018, pqac-00000020) |
| Localization | The corpus supports PAL1 acting in the **cytosolic PAL pathway** for SA biosynthesis, but it does **not contain a direct PAL1 protein subcellular localization experiment** for At2g37040 itself. Tissue expression is strongest in **roots and stems**, especially **inflorescence stems/vascular tissues** during lignification. | (pqac-00000020, pqac-00000015, pqac-00000016, pqac-00000017, pqac-00000019) |
| Regulation | PAL1 is regulated transcriptionally and post-translationally. In the corpus, **ERF114 directly binds the PAL1 promoter** and activates its expression in pathogen/elicitor-triggered defense; **WHY1 represses PAL1 indirectly via MYB15** in developmental SA control; **KFB F-box proteins mediate proteasome-dependent PAL degradation**, linking PAL abundance to glucosinolate crosstalk and carbon signaling. | (pqac-00000018, pqac-00000020, pqac-00000008) |
| Mutant phenotype | **pal1** single mutants reduce stem PAL activity without major gross visible defects; **pal1 pal2** double mutants show strong metabolic and reproductive phenotypes, including reduced lignification, altered cell-wall ultrastructure, sterility, and loss of multiple soluble phenylpropanoids/flavonoids. PAL1 appears more important than PAL2 for phenylpropanoid generation. | (pqac-00000003, pqac-00000006, pqac-00000016) |
| Quantitative data | Specific values captured in the corpus include: **pal1 pal2 siliques ~3–4 mm vs ~12 mm in wild type**; the **double mutant retains ~25% residual PAL activity** in inflorescence stems (likely from PAL4); leaf PAL activity is reported as **~50% reduced** across genotypes in one assay context; the cytosolic PAL pathway contributes **~10% of defense-related SA** in Arabidopsis. | (pqac-00000006, pqac-00000020) |
| Recent developments/applications | Direct **2023-2024 primary Arabidopsis PAL1 papers are limited in our corpus**; newer relevant corpus items are mainly dissertations/theses or broader Arabidopsis studies rather than PAL1-focused primary enzymology. Still, recent work reinforces PAL1 as a regulatory node for **defense-induced lignin/SA** and as a control point for **phenylpropanoid flux engineering** via KFB/SnRK1/MED5-linked PAL turnover. | (pqac-00000001, pqac-00000008, pqac-00000018) |
