Gene Description

PHYB (PHYTOCHROME B, also known as HY3/OOP1) is the predominant red/far-red light photoreceptor in Arabidopsis that functions as a master regulator of photomorphogenesis, thermomorphogenesis, and circadian entrainment. The protein exists as a homodimer with a modular architecture comprising an N-terminal photosensory module (PSM) containing PAS, GAF, and PHY domains, and a C-terminal output module (OPM) with histidine kinase-like activity. PHYB undergoes reversible photoconversion between the inactive cytoplasmic Pr form (red light-absorbing) and the active nuclear Pfr form (far-red light-absorbing) through conformational changes in its covalently-attached linear tetrapyrrole chromophore. Upon red light activation, the Pfr form rapidly translocates to the nucleus where it assembles into discrete nuclear photobodies and interacts with Phytochrome Interacting Factors (PIFs) including PIF3, PIF4, PIF5, and PIF8 to regulate gene expression. Key structural features include an N-terminal extension (NTE) essential for nuclear localization and transcriptional activity, and a light-sensing knot crucial for PIF binding specificity. PHYB functions as a thermal integrator, associating with target gene promoters in a temperature-dependent manner to control thermomorphogenic responses through PIF4-mediated transcriptional repression. The protein exhibits negative autoregulation of its own expression and controls diverse developmental processes including de-etiolation, hypocotyl elongation, flowering time, stomatal regulation, and circadian phase control. Additionally, PHYB destabilizes specific PIFs (like UNE10/PIF8) in response to red light and coordinates complex signaling networks through interactions with co-regulators, chromatin remodeling factors, and post-translational modification enzymes.
