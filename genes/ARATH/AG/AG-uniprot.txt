CC   -!- FUNCTION: Probable transcription factor involved in the control of
CC       organ identity during the early development of flowers. Is required for
CC       normal development of stamens and carpels in the wild-type flower.
CC       Plays a role in maintaining the determinacy of the floral meristem.
CC       Acts as C class cadastral protein by repressing the A class floral
CC       homeotic genes like APETALA1. Forms a heterodimer via the K-box domain
CC       with either SEPALATTA1/AGL2, SEPALATTA2/AGL4, SEPALLATA3/AGL9 or AGL6
CC       that could be involved in genes regulation during floral meristem
CC       development. Controls AHL21/GIK, a multifunctional chromatin modifier
CC       in reproductive organ patterning and differentiation (PubMed:19956801).
CC       Induces microsporogenesis through the activation of SPL/NZZ
CC       (PubMed:15254538). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15254538,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:19956801}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homodimer, capable of binding to CArG-box sequences. Forms a
CC       heterodimer via the K-box domain with either SEPALATTA1/AGL2,
CC       SEPALATTA2/AGL4, SEPALLATA3/AGL9 or AGL6. Heterodimerization also seen
CC       with some other Agamous-like MADS-box proteins. Interacts with AGL15
CC       and AGL16. Component of a complex made of FLOR1, VSP1 and AGAMOUS (AG).
CC       Binds directly with FLR1 (PubMed:11689012).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11689012, ECO:0000269|PubMed:15805477}.
