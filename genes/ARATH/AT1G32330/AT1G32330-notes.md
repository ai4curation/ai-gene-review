# HSFA1D (AT1G32330) Curation Notes

## Current Review Position

HSFA1D is a class A heat shock transcription factor with conserved HSE-binding
transcriptional activator function. The final YAML focuses on the locally supported
GO conclusions: RNA polymerase II-specific DNA-binding transcription activator
activity, nuclear/cytoplasmic localization, broad heat-response involvement, and
HSP90-associated protein binding.

## Supported Core Function

- HSFA1D binds heat shock elements in promoters and acts as a transcriptional
  activator in the Arabidopsis heat-shock response.
- It is localized to cytoplasm and nucleus, consistent with regulated HSF activation
  and nuclear transcriptional function.
- PMID:19704818 and UniProt support a specific HSP90-2 interaction; the review
  recommends replacing generic `protein binding` with `GO:0051879 Hsp90 protein
  binding`.

## Non-Retained Draft Claims

Earlier drafts and generated research notes discussed blue light signaling,
circadian gating, thermomorphogenesis, brassinosteroid signaling, chilling, and
salt/osmotic stress. Those topics are not retained as new GO annotations or as
core functions in this review because the local cached evidence was not strong
enough for HSFA1D-specific curation actions in this batch.

## Review Scope

The current review accepts or modifies only the existing GOA-supported annotations
and does not propose new annotations for light, circadian, cold, osmotic, salt, or
thermomorphogenesis processes.
