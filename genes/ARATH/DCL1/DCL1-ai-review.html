Gene Description

DCL1 is the Arabidopsis nuclear Dicer-like RNase III enzyme that processes structured pri- and pre-miRNA hairpins into miRNA/miRNA-star duplexes. It works with DRB1/HYL1, SERRATE, and other nuclear microprocessor factors in D-bodies to control the precision and efficiency of plant miRNA biogenesis. Developmental, antiviral, lsiRNA, ta-siRNA, and seed/flower phenotypes arise mainly from this nuclear small-RNA processing role rather than from separate primary functions.

Research report: Arabidopsis thaliana DCL1 (UniProt Q9SP32; At1g01040) — functional annotation (2023–2024 emphasis)

1) Target verification and scope

All evidence synthesized here refers to Arabidopsis thaliana DICER-LIKE 1 (DCL1), the nuclear RNase III enzyme that catalyzes microRNA (miRNA) precursor processing in plants, functioning with HYL1/DRB1 and SERRATE (SE) in nuclear miRNA-processing foci often termed D-bodies. Key sources explicitly state this Arabidopsis DCL1 context and microprocessor composition (ding2023micrornaproductionin pages 1-2, zhou2024thespliceosomeassociatedprotein pages 1-2, rosatti2024principlesofmirnamirna* pages 1-2).

2) Key concepts and definitions (current understanding)

2.1 DCL1 and the plant “microprocessor”

In Arabidopsis, miRNA genes (MIRs) are transcribed by RNA polymerase II to produce pri-miRNAs, and DCL1 is the central RNase III enzyme that processes pri-miRNAs in the nucleus to release a miRNA/miRNA duplex (ding2023micrornaproductionin pages 1-2, zhou2024thespliceosomeassociatedprotein pages 1-2, rosatti2024principlesofmirnamirna pages 1-2). Core “microprocessor” (also described as the D-body/dicing complex) components are:
- DCL1 (RNase III endonuclease)
- HYL1/DRB1 (double-stranded RNA-binding protein that improves accuracy/efficiency)
- SERRATE (SE) (zinc-finger protein/scaffold; also linked to phase separation)
(ding2023micrornaproductionin pages 4-5, zhou2024thespliceosomeassociatedprotein pages 1-2, ding2023micrornaproductionin pages 5-6).

Recent work also places TOUGH (TGH) in the nuclear DCL1 complex in Arabidopsis and emphasizes that multiple factors dynamically associate with the microprocessor to tune assembly and activity (li2024janusaspliceosomeassociated pages 1-2).

2.2 What reaction does DCL1 catalyze? Substrates and products

Reaction (functional description): DCL1 catalyzes endonucleolytic cleavage(s) of fold-back pri-/pre-miRNA hairpins to generate a ~21–22 nt miRNA/miRNA duplex (Arabidopsis typical size), generally with 2-nt 3′ overhangs, consistent with RNase III products (ding2023micrornaproductionin pages 1-2, rosatti2024principlesofmirnamirna pages 1-2). A 2024 microprocessor paper similarly describes miRNAs as ~21 nt and states DCL1 releases the duplex co-transcriptionally in the nucleus (zhou2024thespliceosomeassociatedprotein pages 1-2).

Downstream steps (context for functional annotation): After DCL1 cleavage, the duplex is 2′-O-methylated by HEN1 and mature miRNA is sorted/loaded into AGO1 (nuclear loading emphasized), then exported to regulate targets (ding2023micrornaproductionin pages 1-2, li2024janusaspliceosomeassociated pages 1-2, zhou2024thespliceosomeassociatedprotein pages 1-2).

2.3 Processing geometry and modes (how DCL1 measures cleavage position)

DCL1 is described as a “molecular ruler” that measures and cleaves pri-miRNAs into 21-nt duplexes (ding2023micrornaproductionin pages 4-5). The canonical initial cleavage position is frequently described as being ~15–17 nt from a structural reference (e.g., an ssRNA–dsRNA junction/base of stem) (ding2023micrornaproductionin pages 4-5, yan2024paralleldegradomeseqand pages 1-3). Plant pri-miRNAs can be processed in different directional modes (e.g., base-to-loop, loop-to-base, and in some cases bidirectional or sequential multi-cut modes), with subsequent cuts typically spaced by ~21 nt (ding2023micrornaproductionin pages 4-5, yan2024paralleldegradomeseqand pages 1-3, rosatti2024principlesofmirnamirna* pages 1-2).

3) Subcellular localization and pathway placement

3.1 Nuclear localization and D-bodies

Multiple sources explicitly place DCL1-mediated pri-miRNA processing in the nucleus (ding2023micrornaproductionin pages 1-2, zhou2024thespliceosomeassociatedprotein pages 1-2, rosatti2024principlesofmirnamirna pages 1-2). DCL1, HYL1, and SE are described as forming a complex in nuclear foci termed D-bodies* (also called Dicing bodies), which support efficient/accurate pri-miRNA processing (zhou2024thespliceosomeassociatedprotein pages 1-2, ding2023micrornaproductionin pages 5-6).

A 2023 review summarizes quantitative descriptors of D-bodies as droplets ~0.2–0.8 (as reported) in diameter and typically 2–4 per nucleus in Arabidopsis, linking these structures to SE-mediated phase separation and microprocessor assembly (ding2023micrornaproductionin pages 5-6).

3.2 Partners and accessory regulators in Arabidopsis

Core and recent regulatory partners supported by 2023–2024 evidence:
- HYL1: improves efficiency/precision; binds pri-miRNA stems; interacts with DCL1; contributes to AGO1 loading context; nuclear import via KETCH1 is noted in a 2023 review (ding2023micrornaproductionin pages 5-6).
- SE: scaffold for DCL1 and HYL1; phase-separation-driven D-body assembly; phosphorylation/turnover affects function (ding2023micrornaproductionin pages 5-6, zhou2024thespliceosomeassociatedprotein pages 1-2).
- TGH: included with DCL1/SE/HYL1 in a nuclear co-transcriptional microprocessor description (li2024janusaspliceosomeassociated pages 1-2).
- DAWDLE (DDL): described as interacting with DCL1 and pri-miRNAs to stabilize pri-miRNAs, and DCL1 phosphorylation is linked to DDL in a 2023 review (ding2023micrornaproductionin pages 4-5).

4) Recent developments and latest research (prioritizing 2023–2024)

4.1 2024: Revising the Arabidopsis miRNA biogenesis atlas with in vivo structure and cleavage mapping (Nature Plants)

Yan et al. (publication date: Jun 2024) combined degradome-seq and DMS-MaPseq with semi-active DCL1 mutants to map pri-miRNA processing in vivo and revise the “atlas” of miRNA processing (https://doi.org/10.1038/s41477-024-01725-9) (yan2024paralleldegradomeseqand pages 1-3). Key quantitative and mechanistic outputs:
- Identified initial processing sites for 147 of 326 annotated Arabidopsis miRNAs (yan2024paralleldegradomeseqand pages 1-3).
- Determined in vivo secondary structures for 73 pri-miRNAs, and reported that ~95% of measured structures deviated from in silico predictions (yan2024paralleldegradomeseqand pages 1-3).
- Used semi-active DCL1 mutants implicating critical RNase III residues (including Glu1507 and Glu1696) and distinguished contributions of the two RNase III domains (yan2024paralleldegradomeseqand pages 1-3).
- Confirmed processing-rule concepts such as the 15–17 nt positioning relative to structural junctions and diversity of processing modes (yan2024paralleldegradomeseqand pages 1-3).

Interpretation: This work strengthens the current view that DCL1 cleavage behavior is strongly constrained by in vivo RNA structure, and that computationally predicted pri-miRNA folds can be misleading at genome scale (yan2024paralleldegradomeseqand pages 1-3).

4.2 2024: Structural determinants of processing precision — PAZ pocket, 3′ overhang recognition, and duplex features (The Plant Journal; Nucleic Acids Research)

Zhang & Li (Aug 2024, The Plant Journal) (https://doi.org/10.1111/tpj.16974) used engineered precursor features and reconstitution to dissect precision and efficiency determinants (zhang2024structuraldeterminantsin pages 1-2, zhang2024structuraldeterminantsin pages 11-12). Notable advances:
- Reported mechanistic detail for two successive cleavages and specific cleavage offsets (e.g., first cut occurring at 17 and 15 nt on the 5′ and 3′ arms relative to a junction in their described framework) (zhang2024structuraldeterminantsin pages 11-12).
- Demonstrated that the DCL1 PAZ domain interaction with a 2-nt 3′ overhang is critical for precision of the second cleavage across many miRNAs; weakening this interaction caused 1–2 nt shifts in miRNA ends and reduced second-cleavage accuracy (zhang2024structuraldeterminantsin pages 8-8).
- In complementation/processing analyses, 16 of 25 analyzed miRNAs showed altered mature-miRNA levels associated with second-cleavage variation in a PAZ-pocket mutant background (zhang2024structuraldeterminantsin pages 9-10).
- Implemented a notable methodological development: reconstitution of Arabidopsis DCL1/SE/HYL1 activity in Saccharomyces cerevisiae, helping separate cleavage from downstream steps like HEN1 methylation and RISC assembly (zhang2024structuraldeterminantsin pages 11-12, zhang2024structuraldeterminantsin pages 1-2).

Rosatti et al. (Jun 2024, Nucleic Acids Research) (https://doi.org/10.1093/nar/gkae458) emphasize that endogenous mismatches in the miRNA/miRNA duplex can tune processing outcomes; DCL1 with HYL1/SE generates ~21-nt duplexes with ~21-nt spaced cuts, and duplex structural features can cause suboptimal processing (rosatti2024principlesofmirnamirna pages 1-2).

Interpretation: Collectively, these 2024 results reinforce a model where DCL1 precision is jointly governed by (i) end recognition (PAZ pocket/3′ overhang), (ii) internal duplex thermodynamics/mismatches, and (iii) processing-direction logic (two-step vs sequential precursors) (zhang2024structuraldeterminantsin pages 8-8, rosatti2024principlesofmirnamirna* pages 1-2).

4.3 2024: New regulators linking splicing machinery and microprocessor assembly/activity (Nature Communications; Nucleic Acids Research)

CWC15 (Mar 2024, Nature Communications) (https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-024-46676-z) connects spliceosome-associated proteins to nuclear miRNA biogenesis. The paper explicitly defines DCL1/HYL1/SE as a nuclear D-body complex, with SE IDRs driving phase separation and notes some processing occurs in the nucleoplasm (zhou2024thespliceosomeassociatedprotein pages 1-2). CWC15 binds MIR promoters, increases Pol II occupancy, interacts with SE and HYL1, supports HYL1–pri-miRNA interaction, and can promote cleavage activity of the DCL1 complex in vitro despite not co-IP’ing with DCL1 in the cited excerpt (zhou2024thespliceosomeassociatedprotein pages 1-2, zhou2024thespliceosomeassociatedprotein pages 2-3).

JANUS (Nov 2024, Nucleic Acids Research) (https://doi.org/10.1093/nar/gkad1105) similarly adds a spliceosome-associated factor that associates with DCL1 and SE, binds pri-miRNA stem-loops, and promotes microprocessor assembly and activity; the authors report that in a hypomorphic janus mutant, DCL1 activity and microprocessor number/interactions are reduced (li2024janusaspliceosomeassociated pages 1-2).

Interpretation: These studies represent a strong 2024 theme: miRNA biogenesis is not an isolated enzymatic step but is coupled to co-transcriptional processes, promoter occupancy, and general RNA-processing machinery in the nucleus (zhou2024thespliceosomeassociatedprotein pages 1-2, li2024janusaspliceosomeassociated pages 1-2).

4.4 2024: Phase separation as organizing principle for nuclear miRNA processing

Multiple 2024 sources highlight SE as an intrinsically disordered protein that undergoes liquid–liquid phase separation (LLPS), recruiting DCL1/HYL1 and cofactors into nuclear condensates/D-bodies and tuning processing outcomes (shang2024howintrinsicallydisordered pages 8-10, zhou2024thespliceosomeassociatedprotein pages 1-2). A higher-level mechanistic development in 2024 is that SE LLPS is also described as coordinating miRNA biogenesis with m6A RNA methylation machinery.

  • Bai et al. (Aug 2024, Journal of Integrative Plant Biology) (https://doi.org/10.1111/jipb.13770) reports that the m6A methyltransferase complex associates with the microprocessor through an MTB–SE module, and that MTB knockdown diminishes microprocessor binding to pri-miRNAs and MIR loci; the paper also links SE-enhanced LLPS and FRAP evidence to MTC solubility/behavior, indicating reciprocal regulation between m6A and microprocessor pathways (bai2024themethyltransferaseb–serrate pages 1-2).
  • Zhong et al. (Oct 2024, Nature Cell Biology) (https://doi.org/10.1038/s41556-024-01530-8) describes SE LLPS as facilitating pri-miRNA processing and coordinating with m6A modification machinery; it provides quantitative m6A peak counts (e.g., 11,487 HC-m6A peaks in Col-0 in the excerpt) and extensive transcriptome-wide hypomethylation in se-2, supporting global coupling between SE-driven condensates and RNA-modification state (zhong2024serratedrivesphase pages 2-4, zhong2024serratedrivesphase pages 1-2).

6) Current applications and real-world implementations

Although DCL1 itself is not typically a direct engineering target (because it is core to endogenous miRNA homeostasis), DCL1’s catalytic role is central to practical technologies that rely on miRNA/amiRNA biogenesis.

6.1 Artificial miRNAs and RNAi-based crop protection/breeding

A 2024 applied review (Sep 2024, Genes) explicitly restates the DCL1/HYL1/SE nuclear processing step and discusses applied directions in breeding and pest control (https://doi.org/10.3390/genes15091200) (ma2024researchprogresson pages 1-3). It also describes two main implementation strategies:
- HIGS (host-induced gene silencing): plants engineered (transgenic or edited) to express silencing RNAs.
- SIGS (spray-induced gene silencing): exogenous application of synthesized RNAs.
(ma2024researchprogresson pages 10-12).

The same review cites multiple applied demonstrations (e.g., amiRNA-mediated resistance to diverse plant viruses; insect resistance traits; reversible male sterility engineering) and notes practical constraints such as pathogen escape/evolution, cost, and field-stability challenges (ma2024researchprogresson pages 16-16, ma2024researchprogresson pages 10-12).

6.2 Delivery technologies for field-relevant RNA silencing

The 2024 applications review highlights emerging delivery approaches aimed at improving RNA stability/bioavailability, including nanotechnology-based carriers (carbon dots/quantum dots; carbon nanotubes) used to deliver siRNAs or DNA without stable integration in experimental contexts (ma2024researchprogresson pages 10-12). While these approaches are not DCL1-specific, they functionally depend on the host’s RNA silencing machinery, including DCL/AGO pathways.

7) Expert synthesis and authoritative perspectives (2023–2024)

Across 2023–2024 reviews and primary studies, an integrated model emerges:
1. DCL1 is the enzymatic core of plant miRNA biogenesis, generating ~21–22 nt duplexes from structured pri-miRNAs in the nucleus (ding2023micrornaproductionin pages 1-2, zhou2024thespliceosomeassociatedprotein pages 1-2).
2. Precision is emergent, resulting from DCL1 domain logic (including PAZ 3′ overhang recognition) plus duplex structural determinants and cofactors HYL1/SE (zhang2024structuraldeterminantsin pages 8-8, rosatti2024principlesofmirnamirna pages 1-2).
3. Cell biology matters: microprocessor activity is organized in nuclear condensates (D-bodies) whose formation and composition are strongly influenced by SE-driven LLPS and by post-translational control (phosphorylation, proteasome-linked turnover), and is further coupled to transcription and splicing-associated factors (CWC15, JANUS) (zhou2024thespliceosomeassociatedprotein pages 1-2, li2024janusaspliceosomeassociated pages 1-2).
4. In vivo structure is central:* high-throughput structural mapping suggests most pri-miRNA folds differ from in silico predictions, implying that functional annotation and engineering should be guided by experimentally constrained structures when possible (yan2024paralleldegradomeseqand pages 1-3).
