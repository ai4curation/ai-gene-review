CC   -!- FUNCTION: Putative transcription factor that seems to play a central
CC       role in the regulation of flowering time in the late-flowering
CC       phenotype by interacting with 'FRIGIDA', the autonomous and the
CC       vernalization flowering pathways. Inhibits flowering by repressing
CC       'SUPPRESSOR OF OVEREXPRESSION OF CONSTANS 1'. At elevated temperatures
CC       (e.g. 29 degrees Celsius), maintained at high levels in a JMJ30/JMJ32-
CC       dependent manner to prevent extreme precocious flowering
CC       (PubMed:25267112). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10716723,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:11283346, ECO:0000269|PubMed:19121105,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:25267112}.
CC   -!- INTERACTION:
CC       Q9S7Q7; Q9FVC1: SVP; NbExp=4; IntAct=EBI-2127872, EBI-592058;

CC       ECO:0000255|PROSITE-ProRule:PRU00768}.

CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: High expression in the vegetative apex and in root
CC       tissue and lower expression in leaves and stems. Not detected in young
CC       tissues of the inflorescence. Before fertilization, expressed in
CC       ovules, but not in pollen or stamens, of non-vernalized plants. After
CC       vernalization, not detected in ovules. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:19121105}.
CC   -!- DEVELOPMENTAL STAGE: Found in shoots of non-flowering plants grown
CC       under long-day conditions at days 4 to 15, and in shoots of plants
CC       grown under short-day conditions at days 4 to 11 after germination
CC       (PubMed:19121105). Expressed in embryos from the early globular stage
CC       (PubMed:19121105). FLC is not imprinted and both parental alleles
CC       contribute equally to expression in embryos (PubMed:19121105).
CC       Expression is repressed during gametogenesis, and is then reactivated
CC       after fertilization in embryos (PubMed:19121105). In seedlings,
CC       observed in the vasculature of the cotyledons, hypocotyls, and the
CC       first pair of leaves (PubMed:18375656). Just prior to flowering, a
CC       strong reduction in expression levels occurs in the vasculature
CC       (PubMed:18375656). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:18375656,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:19121105}.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: Epigenetically down-regulated by vernalization.
CC       Vernalization repression is initiated by VIN3. Repressed by silencing
CC       mediated by polycomb group (PcG) protein complex containing EMF1 and
CC       EMF2. Up-regulated by HUA2. Down-regulated by VOZ1 and/or VOZ2. Down-
CC       regulated by RBG7. Accumulates at elevated temperatures via a
CC       JMJ30/JMJ32-mediated epigenetic regulation (e.g. removal of the
CC       repressive histone modification H3 lysine 27 trimethylation (H3K27me3))
CC       (PubMed:25267112). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:14712276,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:15659097, ECO:0000269|PubMed:18573194,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:19783648, ECO:0000269|PubMed:22904146,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:25267112}.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: Early-flowering phenotype at elevated
CC       temperatures (e.g. 29 degrees Celsius) but not at room temperature
CC       (e.g. 22 degrees Celsius). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:25267112}.
CC   -!- MISCELLANEOUS: Prevention of early flowering by expression of FLC
CC       requires methylation of 'Lys-36' of histone H3. Repression of FLC is
CC       dependent on histone H3 'Lys-4' methylation which is partly controlled
CC       by the lysine-specific demthylase 1 (LSD1) homologs, LDL1, LDL2 and
CC       FLD. May also be regulated by the level of histone acetylation.
