Gene Description

MYC2/JIN1 is an Arabidopsis nuclear basic helix-loop-helix transcription factor and central regulator of jasmonate responses. Its core molecular role is sequence-specific cis-regulatory DNA binding and transcriptional regulation of jasmonate-responsive genes, with protein interactions such as JAZ repressors, MED25, DELLA, and MAPK pathway components modulating activity. Phenotypes in wounding, herbivory, glucosinolate and flavonoid metabolism, root development, stomatal development, and secondary cell wall regulation are downstream transcriptional outputs rather than distinct biochemical functions.

Research report: Functional annotation of Arabidopsis thaliana MYC2 (UniProt Q39204; locus At1g32640)

0) Target verification and scope control

The literature synthesized here is restricted to Arabidopsis thaliana MYC2 (At1g32640; UniProt Q39204), described as a basic helix–loop–helix (bHLH) transcription factor and a central regulator of jasmonate (JA) responses. When papers discuss “MYC2” from other plant species, they are used only as comparative context and not as a substitute for the Arabidopsis gene/protein. Arabidopsis-focused sources explicitly treat MYC2 as a jasmonate-signaling “master regulator” and/or a bHLH transcription factor with the expected DNA-binding and repressor-interaction features (luo2023myc2amaster pages 2-3, im2024jasmonateactivatessecondary pages 1-2).

1) Key concepts and definitions (current understanding)

1.1 Jasmonate signaling core module (JA–JAZ–MYC2)

A widely accepted core model is that bioactive jasmonate JA-Ile is perceived by the F-box protein COI1 as part of the SCFCOI1 complex, which promotes ubiquitination and 26S proteasome-dependent degradation of JAZ repressors. In the absence of JA-Ile, JAZ proteins bind MYC2 and repress its transcriptional activity; JA-Ile–triggered JAZ destruction derepresses MYC2, enabling transcriptional activation of JA-responsive genes (luo2023myc2amaster pages 2-3, im2024jasmonateactivatessecondary pages 1-2, takaoka2024astructureredesignedintrinsically pages 1-2).

1.2 MYC2 as a transcription factor: domains and DNA binding

Arabidopsis MYC2 is a bHLH transcription factor with a C-terminal bHLH DNA-binding region and N-terminal regions important for regulation and protein–protein interactions. Mechanistically, MYC2 binds G-box sequences (notably 5′-CACGTG-3′) in target promoters and can form homo-/hetero-oligomers with related MYC factors (luo2023myc2amaster pages 2-3, zeng2025theroleof pages 1-3).

2) Molecular function, mechanism, and pathways (functional annotation)

2.1 Primary molecular function

MYC2’s primary function is sequence-specific transcriptional regulation downstream of jasmonate perception: when derepressed, MYC2 binds G-box–type promoter elements and coordinates recruitment of transcriptional machinery and chromatin regulators, thereby reprogramming gene expression for defense, stress responses, and developmental tradeoffs (luo2023myc2amaster pages 2-3, im2024jasmonateactivatessecondary pages 1-2).

2.2 Repression and activation logic: JAZ, NINJA/TPL, and chromatin state

A mechanistic basis for MYC2 repression is direct binding of JAZ proteins to MYC2; the JA system converts this into a switch-like response via COI1-dependent JAZ turnover, freeing MYC2 to activate transcription. The MYC2 repression machinery is commonly described as involving JAZ-associated corepressors and chromatin closure (e.g., via NINJA/TPL and histone deacetylation in the repressed state) with activation following JAZ degradation (luo2023myc2amaster pages 2-3).

2.3 Coactivators and mediator: MED25 and histone acetyltransferases

A recurring mechanistic feature in Arabidopsis is that active MYC2 interacts with MED25 (Mediator subunit) to form an activation complex and promote JA-triggered transcriptional reprogramming. This coupling connects MYC2 promoter binding to recruitment of chromatin/coactivator functions (e.g., histone acetyltransferase activities) and RNA polymerase II engagement (luo2023myc2amaster pages 2-3, im2024jasmonateactivatessecondary pages 1-2).

2.4 Post-translational regulation (PTMs) and proteostasis

Phosphorylation and ubiquitin signaling tune MYC2 activity and stability:
- CK2-dependent phosphorylation of MYC2 is summarized as promoting JA signaling outputs (cited within recent reviews) (zeng2025theroleof pages 15-17).
- A 2024 review of plant deubiquitinases reports that UBP12 and UBP13 physically interact with MYC2 and remove K63-linked polyubiquitin chains, and this deubiquitination positively regulates JA-mediated responses (wang2024aninsightinto pages 10-12).
These controls support a model where MYC2 is not merely derepressed by JAZ degradation, but also quantitatively tuned by PTMs and ubiquitin editing (wang2024aninsightinto pages 10-12).

3) Subcellular localization and where MYC2 acts

MYC2 functions as a transcription factor and therefore acts in the nucleus as part of promoter-bound regulatory complexes. While not every Arabidopsis excerpt explicitly states localization, nuclear action is directly supported by descriptions of promoter binding, Mediator recruitment, and assays using tagged MYC2 in plant cells (luo2023myc2amaster pages 2-3, im2024jasmonateactivatessecondary pages 13-13). In the 2024 secondary cell wall study, MYC2 is used as a promoter-binding regulator (including ChIP-qPCR methods using YFP-tagged MYC2), consistent with nuclear localization and function (im2024jasmonateactivatessecondary pages 13-13).

4) Recent developments and latest research (prioritizing 2023–2024)

4.1 2024: MYC2–MYB46 module links jasmonate to secondary cell wall (SCW) biosynthesis

A key 2024 Arabidopsis advance is experimental support for a MYC2→MYB46 regulatory module connecting JA signaling to secondary cell wall formation/lignification. Im et al. (Plant Journal; published 2024-11; https://doi.org/10.1111/tpj.16541) report that MYC2 (described as a JA master regulator) binds the G-box in the MYB46 promoter and that MYC2-mediated MYB46 activation increases SCW biosynthetic gene expression and lignification phenotypes (im2024jasmonateactivatessecondary pages 11-12).

Quantitative/statistical reporting and experimental conditions (useful for annotation confidence) include:
- 7-day seedlings treated with 50 µM methyl jasmonate (MJA) for 24 h for MYB46 protein blots and RT-qPCR (im2024jasmonateactivatessecondary pages 8-9).
- Root lignification assays using phloroglucinol-HCl staining after 36 h MJA treatment (im2024jasmonateactivatessecondary pages 8-9).
- Mature stem assays: 32-day plants sprayed with 50 µM MJA every 2 days, and gene expression measured after treatments (im2024jasmonateactivatessecondary pages 8-9, im2024jasmonateactivatessecondary pages 12-12).

- JA-induced SCW formation is reported as “mediated by MYC2”* and MYC2 loss compromises JA induction of MYB46 outputs (im2024jasmonateactivatessecondary pages 8-9).

4.2 2024: Chemical biology control—selective peptide inhibitor of MYC TFs in JA signaling

A 2024 development in real-world experimental control is a selective peptide inhibitor strategy. Takaoka et al. (PNAS Nexus; published 2024-07; https://doi.org/10.1093/pnasnexus/pgae312) designed inhibitors based on an intrinsically disordered MYC-binding region derived from a JAZ protein domain and report selective inhibition of MYC-family TF activity (MYC2/3/4 family context) to uncouple JA–ethylene crosstalk in Arabidopsis without genetic modification (takaoka2024astructureredesignedintrinsically pages 1-2, takaoka2024astructureredesignedintrinsically pages 2-3).

5) Biological roles and processes (selected experimentally supported outputs)

MYC2 is portrayed as a hub integrating jasmonate-mediated defense/stress responses with growth and development. The 2023 review synthesizes evidence that MYC2 regulates multiple developmental and stress-related processes, often by direct promoter binding to downstream regulators (e.g., root developmental regulators and senescence-associated genes) (luo2023myc2amaster pages 7-8). The same review frames MYC2 as a “master switch” for a wide set of physiological processes and specialized metabolite regulation, emphasizing that MYC2 is central to JA signaling but also participates in multi-hormone crosstalk networks (luo2023myc2amaster pages 2-3, luo2023myc2amaster pages 7-8).

6) Current applications and real-world implementations

6.1 Synthetic biology and metabolic engineering concept: inducible MYC2 switch

A major translational concept is using the inducible JA–JAZ–MYC2 pathway as a controllable regulatory switch to drive production of specialized metabolites. The 2023 review explicitly proposes leveraging MYC2-driven chassis cells and notes plant chassis systems (e.g., Nicotiana benthamiana) and alternative chassis concepts (e.g., microalgae), where inducible control may reduce metabolic burden compared with constitutive promoters (luo2023myc2amaster pages 11-13, luo2023myc2amaster pages 1-2).

6.2 Chemical perturbation tool: MYC-selective inhibitory peptide (2024)

The 2024 peptide inhibitor work provides a concrete implementation pathway for non-genetic perturbation of MYC signaling, potentially accelerating pathway dissection in plants with genetic redundancy among MYC factors and enabling cross-species probing of MYC orthologs (takaoka2024astructureredesignedintrinsically pages 1-2).

6.3 Engineering SCW/lignification responses via MYC2–MYB46 (2024)

The MYC2→MYB46 module offers a mechanistic lever for tuning SCW formation and lignification in response to jasmonate, with clearly defined treatment regimes and phenotype assays (protein, RT-qPCR, staining, ABSL lignin). This positions MYC2 as an upstream regulatory node for SCW traits connected to stress/wounding signaling (im2024jasmonateactivatessecondary pages 8-9, im2024jasmonateactivatessecondary pages 11-12).

7) Relevant statistics and data (from recent studies)

Accessible quantitative/statistical details from 2024 Arabidopsis primary research include treatment dose/regimens and replication/statistical testing (rather than numeric fold-change values in the extracted captions):

8) Expert synthesis and analysis (authoritative interpretation)

Across recent reviews and primary studies, MYC2 is consistently treated as a central integrator that converts jasmonate perception into transcriptional reprogramming via (i) JAZ-mediated repression/derepression, (ii) Mediator/chromatin coactivator recruitment for productive transcription, and (iii) multi-layer PTM/proteostasis tuning of MYC2 activity/stability. The 2024 SCW study extends MYC2’s functional annotation beyond classical defense outputs by establishing a direct mechanistic link to a major developmental/metabolic program (secondary cell wall biosynthesis) through the MYC2–MYB46 module, consistent with MYC2’s broader role in coordinating growth–defense tradeoffs (im2024jasmonateactivatessecondary pages 11-12, luo2023myc2amaster pages 7-8). The 2024 chemical inhibitor study underscores that the MYC module is sufficiently well-characterized structurally and mechanistically to support rational inhibitor design, strengthening its status as a tractable node for both basic biology and applied manipulation (takaoka2024astructureredesignedintrinsically pages 1-2).
