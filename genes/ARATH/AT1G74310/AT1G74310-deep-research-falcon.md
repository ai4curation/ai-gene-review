## Research report: Arabidopsis thaliana **CLPB1 / HSP101 / HOT1** (AT1G74310; UniProt P42730)

### 0) Target verification and disambiguation (critical)
The UniProt accession **P42730** corresponds to **Arabidopsis thaliana** gene **AT1G74310**, widely referred to as **HSP101** and also known as **HOT1** and **CLPB1** (and functionally connected to **DLT1** alleles in later work). This protein is the **cytosolic/nuclear** member of the Arabidopsis ClpB/Hsp100 family, and is explicitly distinguished from organellar ClpB homologs (plastid- and mitochondria-targeted ClpB proteins) in family-level analyses. (lee2007thearabidopsisclpbhsp100 pages 1-2, lee2007thearabidopsisclpbhsp100 pages 4-6)

### 1) Key concepts and definitions (current understanding)

#### 1.1 What CLPB1/HSP101 is
HSP101 is a **class I ClpB/Hsp100 AAA+ ATPase** (two AAA/NBD modules) that uses ATP to remodel protein substrates. Within the Arabidopsis Clp/Hsp100 superfamily, it is the **only cytosolic/nuclear** ClpB/Hsp100 member emphasized in the family analysis, whereas other ClpB homologs are targeted to plastids or mitochondria. (lee2007thearabidopsisclpbhsp100 pages 1-2, lee2007thearabidopsisclpbhsp100 pages 4-6)

#### 1.2 Core molecular function: ATP-dependent protein disaggregation (disaggregase)
The most strongly supported primary biochemical role of Arabidopsis HSP101 is as a **protein disaggregase/remodeling chaperone** that promotes recovery from heat damage. Mechanistic models (inferred from close homology and conserved thermotolerance phenotypes with yeast Hsp104 and bacterial ClpB systems) posit that HSP101 participates in a stepwise process in which aggregates are first remodeled/dissociated by HSP101/ClpB-type activity, enabling Hsp70/Hsp40-type chaperones to complete refolding/reactivation. (queitsch2000heatshockprotein pages 1-2, queitsch2000heatshockprotein pages 9-10, katiyaragarwal2001searchforthe pages 12-13)

#### 1.3 Thermotolerance terminology used in the literature
* **Basal thermotolerance (BT):** ability to survive a severe heat shock without prior acclimation.
* **Acquired thermotolerance (AT):** enhanced tolerance induced by a prior nonlethal heat pretreatment (“conditioning”/“priming”).
* **Heat acclimation memory / long-term acquired thermotolerance (LAT):** persistence of acclimation benefits during recovery (“memory phase”), beyond short-term AT. (mitra2024stabilizationofhsa32 pages 1-4, wu2013interplaybetweenheat pages 6-8)

### 2) Experimentally supported biological roles in Arabidopsis

#### 2.1 Essential role in acquired thermotolerance
Queitsch et al. (Plant Cell, **April 2000**, https://doi.org/10.1105/tpc.12.4.479) provided foundational evidence that manipulating HSP101 levels causally changes thermotolerance.

* Plants with **reduced HSP101** due to **antisense** or **cosuppression** retained normal growth under non-stress conditions but showed a **severely diminished capacity to acquire thermotolerance** following conditioning pretreatments. (queitsch2000heatshockprotein pages 1-2)
* Conversely, plants with **constitutive HSP101 expression** better tolerated severe heat shifts and could gain a growth advantage after heat shock. (queitsch2000heatshockprotein pages 1-2, queitsch2000heatshockprotein pages 10-11)

These qualitative conclusions are reinforced visually by the paper’s **western blots** and **thermotolerance assays** (e.g., representative acquired/basal thermotolerance plates and growth outcomes), which were retrieved from the publication figures. (queitsch2000heatshockprotein media f7c03d59, queitsch2000heatshockprotein media 500f4ed1)

#### 2.2 Role in basal thermotolerance in seeds and seedlings
HSP101 is developmentally induced in seeds and becomes strongly heat-inducible in vegetative tissues; disrupting its accumulation can reduce basal tolerance during germination in conditions where seeds normally show high baseline heat tolerance. (queitsch2000heatshockprotein pages 1-2, queitsch2000heatshockprotein pages 9-10, lee2007thearabidopsisclpbhsp100 pages 1-2)

#### 2.3 A dual role in (i) thermotolerance and (ii) heat-acclimation memory via HSA32
A key extension of HSP101 function in Arabidopsis is its **post-transcriptional protein-level coupling** with the plant protein **HSA32**.

* Wu et al. (Plant Physiology, **Feb 2013**, https://doi.org/10.1104/pp.112.212589) identified **dlt1** missense alleles in HSP101 that decouple thermotolerance from regulation of HSA32 accumulation, supporting a **dual function** model for HSP101. (wu2013interplaybetweenheat pages 6-8)
* A mechanistic and cell-biological update is provided by Mitra et al. (bioRxiv preprint, **Oct 2024**, https://doi.org/10.1101/2024.10.15.618377), which concludes that HSP101 stabilizes HSA32 during the memory phase and that HSP101–HSA32 interplay is critical for maintaining acquired thermotolerance. (mitra2024stabilizationofhsa32 pages 1-4)

### 3) Cellular localization and where the protein acts

#### 3.1 Localization of HSP101 (CLPB1)
HSP101 is described as localized to the **cytosol/nucleus** in Arabidopsis family analyses, and recent fractionation work in the 2024 preprint context supports that HSP101 is primarily in the **cytosolic fraction** after heat acclimation (with similar cytosolic distribution to HSA32). (lee2007thearabidopsisclpbhsp100 pages 1-2, mitra2024stabilizationofhsa32 pages 10-12)

#### 3.2 Cytosolic location of HSA32 and heat-induced condensates (relevant to the HSP101 module)
Mitra et al. (2024 preprint) directly tested HSA32 localization: HSA32 was **barely detectable in the nuclear fraction** and **mainly cytosolic** after heat acclimation, with a similar pattern observed for HSP101. In vivo imaging with GFP fusions showed **heat-induced cytosolic foci/condensates** appearing after ~30 min of heat treatment at 32°C and then gradually disappearing during recovery; foci formation required new protein synthesis (blocked by cycloheximide). (mitra2024stabilizationofhsa32 pages 10-12)

### 4) Pathways and systems context

#### 4.1 Heat shock response (HSR) transcriptional control upstream of HSP101
A synthesis of regulatory evidence indicates that **HsfA1-class heat shock factors** are central regulators of the heat shock response and that HSP101 is a key target. Review synthesis notes that Arabidopsis has **21 Hsfs**, and that HSP101 transcript/protein is strongly affected in **HsfA1 quadruple mutants** and can be restored via HsfA2 in those backgrounds (as summarized). (kumar2020athsp101researchsets pages 7-8)

#### 4.2 Proteostasis and recovery: stress granules, translation recovery, and proteasome association
A detailed review focused on AtHsp101 positions the protein as a major node in **proteostasis recovery** after heat stress, including roles in **stress granule (SG) dissociation** and **translation recovery**, and reports affinity isolation recovering multiple proteasome subunits consistent with a link to clearance of ubiquitin-labeled aggregates; these points are currently best treated as synthesis/hypothesis-backed by cited studies in that review rather than a single primary dataset analyzed here. (kumar2020athsp101researchsets pages 7-8)

### 5) Quantitative/statistical highlights from the retrieved literature

* **Heat induction condition:** HSP101 was **undetectable** in plants grown at **22°C** and was **strongly induced** by heat treatment at **38°C for 90 min** in the classic transgenic study. (queitsch2000heatshockprotein pages 1-2)
* **Transformant statistics (Queitsch 2000):**
  * In one transformation set, **12 of 27** No-0 transformants had strongly diminished HSP101 expression.
  * In another, **17 of 25** Col-0 transformants showed markedly less HSP101 after heat stress.
  * Constitutive-expression lines were also obtained (rare events relative to total). (queitsch2000heatshockprotein pages 1-2)
* **Cross-kingdom functional conservation:** Arabidopsis Hsp100/ClpB cDNA enhanced survival of Hsp104-null yeast by approximately **100-fold** at extreme temperatures in heterologous complementation assays (reviewed evidence). (katiyaragarwal2001searchforthe pages 12-13)
* **Regulatory DNA feature (reviewed):** a **1328 bp upstream region** has been described as a heat-inducible bidirectional promoter region for AtHsp101 and a neighboring gene (summary in review). (kumar2020athsp101researchsets pages 7-8)

### 6) Recent developments (prioritizing 2023–2024)

#### 6.1 2024 mechanistic advance: HSP101 stabilizes an aggregation-prone “memory” protein (HSA32)
The 2024 bioRxiv preprint provides several recent mechanistic insights:

* **Subcellular fractionation:** HSA32 and HSP101 are mainly **cytosolic** after heat acclimation. (mitra2024stabilizationofhsa32 pages 10-12)
* **Condensate biology:** HSA32 forms **heat-induced liquid-like condensates** (FRAP recovery), distinct from canonical stress granules induced at higher temperatures, implying specialized condensate biology in the heat memory pathway. (mitra2024stabilizationofhsa32 pages 10-12)
* **Stabilization mechanism:** Without HSP101, nascent HSA32 is rapidly degraded; inhibition of degradation by proteasome inhibitors (and analysis of proteasome-related mutants) is consistent with proteasome involvement in HSA32 turnover, while HSP101 levels were not affected in the tested proteasome subunit mutant background. (mitra2024stabilizationofhsa32 pages 1-4, mitra2024stabilizationofhsa32 pages 10-12)

#### 6.2 2023–2024 translational/crop engineering direction (evidence limits in this run)
The retrieved corpus includes broader plant/crop-oriented work and reviews that emphasize HSP101/ClpB proteins as genetic engineering targets for heat tolerance, but a specifically mentioned **May 2023 Plant Science** paper on Arabidopsis overexpression of additional HSP101 copies was not obtainable in this run; therefore, I do not report its quantitative outcomes here. (kumar2020athsp101researchsets pages 7-8)

### 7) Current applications and real-world implementations

#### 7.1 Engineering thermotolerance
The classic Arabidopsis evidence that constitutive HSP101 expression can enhance heat tolerance without overt growth penalties under normal temperatures has been repeatedly framed as an enabling result for engineering heat resilience in plants. (queitsch2000heatshockprotein pages 10-11, queitsch2000heatshockprotein pages 1-2)

At a practical level, proposed engineering strategies summarized in the AtHsp101-focused review include: (i) **overproducing HSP101** using constitutive or heat-inducible promoters and (ii) manipulating upstream regulators or negative regulators (e.g., HSF/Hsc70 circuitry) to tune the heat response. (kumar2020athsp101researchsets pages 7-8)

#### 7.2 Biomarker and pathway node
HSP101 abundance and/or inducibility is used experimentally as a marker for the strength of the heat shock response and is also used to interpret phenotypes in mutants affecting acclimation, oxidative stress signaling, or translation recovery (as synthesized). (kumar2020athsp101researchsets pages 7-8)

### 8) Expert opinions / authoritative synthesis
A key authoritative synthesis available in the retrieved set (Kumar et al., 2020; Journal of Plant Biochemistry and Biotechnology, **Oct 2020**, https://doi.org/10.1007/s13562-020-00624-2) positions AtHsp101 as a central “course-of-action” target for thermotolerance engineering, integrating upstream transcriptional regulation (HSFs), post-transcriptional/protein stability mechanisms (HSA32), and cellular recovery processes (stress granules/translation). (kumar2020athsp101researchsets pages 7-8)

### 9) Functional annotation summary (evidence-weighted)

**Primary molecular function:** ATP-dependent protein disaggregase/remodeling chaperone (AAA+ class I ClpB/Hsp100) that enables recovery from heat-induced protein aggregation and supports cellular proteostasis recovery. (queitsch2000heatshockprotein pages 9-10, queitsch2000heatshockprotein pages 1-2, katiyaragarwal2001searchforthe pages 12-13)

**Primary biological process:** acquired thermotolerance (and, in specific developmental contexts, basal thermotolerance), including longer-term heat acclimation memory via interaction with the HSA32 module. (queitsch2000heatshockprotein pages 1-2, wu2013interplaybetweenheat pages 6-8, mitra2024stabilizationofhsa32 pages 1-4)

**Primary cellular site of action:** cytosol (with cytosolic/nuclear designation in family analyses; fractionation-based support for cytosolic enrichment after acclimation). (lee2007thearabidopsisclpbhsp100 pages 1-2, mitra2024stabilizationofhsa32 pages 10-12)
