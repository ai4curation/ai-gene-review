CC   -!- FUNCTION: Transcription factor that plays a central role during early
CC       embryogenesis, oogenesis and flowering, probably by regulating
CC       expression of specific genes. Required to specify stem cell identity in
CC       meristems, such as shoot apical meristem (SAM). May induce shoot stem
CC       cells activity in order to maintain the stem cell identity. Involved in
CC       the developmental root meristem. In shoot apices, it is sufficient to
CC       induce the expression of CLV3, a putative ligand of the CLV signaling
CC       pathway. Also required to sustain organogenesis in the floral meristem
CC       by contributing to the expression of its own repressor, the AGAMOUS
CC       (AG) gene at the end of flower development. Binds directly to the 5'-
CC       TTAAT[GC][GC]-3' DNA sequence in the regulatory sequence of AG and
CC       activates its expression directly. Regulates one important step in
CC       ovule development to induce integument formation from the underlying
CC       chalazal domain. Participates in the promotion of vegetative to
CC       embryonic transition. Required to repress LEC1 expression.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10761929, ECO:0000269|PubMed:11440721,

CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:15004006, ECO:0000269|PubMed:9865698}.

CC       expressed in a small cell group underneath the presume position of stem

CC       of ovule primordia. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12000795,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:9865698}.
CC   -!- DEVELOPMENTAL STAGE: Expressed from 16 cell embryo stage, long before
CC       shoot meristem is evident, and gradually become restricted to the
CC       center of shoot meristem primordium. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:9865698}.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: Repressed by the CLV (CLV1, CLV2 and CLV3) proteins,
CC       possibly to rapidly down-regulate WUS expression in apical daughter
CC       cells after cell divisions, suggesting the existence of a feedback
CC       loop. Repressed by AG at the end of floral development. Down-regulated
CC       by ULT1, probably to establish floral meristem determinacy.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10761929, ECO:0000269|PubMed:11440721,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:11440722, ECO:0000269|PubMed:15342527}.
