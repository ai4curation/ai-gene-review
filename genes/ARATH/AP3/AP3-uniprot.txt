CC   -!- FUNCTION: Probable transcription factor involved in the genetic control
CC       of flower development. Is required for normal development of petals and
CC       stamens in the wild-type flower. Forms a heterodimer with PISTILLATA
CC       that is required for autoregulation of both AP3 and PI genes. AP3/PI
CC       heterodimer interacts with APETALA1 or SEPALLATA3 to form a ternary
CC       complex that could be responsible for the regulation of the genes
CC       involved in the flower development. AP3/PI heterodimer activates the
CC       expression of NAP. AP3/PI prevents GATA22/GNL and GATA21/GNC expression
CC       (PubMed:18417639). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:18417639,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:8565821, ECO:0000269|PubMed:9489703}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Forms a heterodimer with PISTILLATA, capable of binding to
CC       CArG-box sequences. AP3/PI heterodimer binds AP1 or SEP3 to form
CC       complexes.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Nucleus.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Expressed in petals and stamens.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: Positively regulated by the meristem identity proteins
CC       APETALA1 and LEAFY with the cooperation of UFO. Repressed by silencing
CC       mediated by polycomb group (PcG) protein complex containing EMF1 and
CC       EMF2. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11283333, ECO:0000269|PubMed:19783648}.
CC   -!- MISCELLANEOUS: Mutations in AP3 cause transformation of petals into
CC       sepals and stamina into carpels.
