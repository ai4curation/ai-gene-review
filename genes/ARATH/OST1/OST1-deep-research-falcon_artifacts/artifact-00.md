| Category | Summary |
|---|---|
| Identity | Arabidopsis thaliana OST1 is the same gene/protein as SRK2E and SnRK2.6; primary literature explicitly identifies OST1 as identical to SRK2E (At4g33950), matching the UniProt target Q940H6 and placing it in subclass III SnRK2 ABA/stress kinases (pqac-00000008, pqac-00000014) |
| Enzymatic function | OST1/SRK2E/SnRK2.6 is an ABA-activated Ser/Thr protein kinase that transfers phosphate from ATP to serine/threonine residues on downstream effectors in ABA, osmotic-stress, and guard-cell signaling pathways; PP2C phosphatases antagonize this by dephosphorylation (pqac-00000008, pqac-00000012, pqac-00000030) |
| Key upstream regulators | Major regulators include clade A PP2Cs such as ABI1 and PP2CA that bind/inhibit OST1; ABA-bound PYR/PYL receptors relieve PP2C repression; upstream kinases include B2/B3 RAFs and other kinases such as BAK1, BIK1, CPKs, and HT1 that modulate OST1 phosphorylation and activity in context-dependent ways (pqac-00000008, pqac-00000010, pqac-00000013, pqac-00000018, pqac-00000022) |
| Key direct substrates/targets | Best-established direct target is the guard-cell S-type anion channel SLAC1, activated by OST1-dependent phosphorylation; key functionally supported SLAC1 residue includes S120. Additional reported direct targets include RBOHF (Ser13, Ser174), RBOHD (Ser343, Ser347; also listed Ser163), CNGC5/6/9/12, KAT1, QUAC1, PIP2;1, BRM, ICE1, PUB25/26, AtANN1, and other transcriptional/metabolic regulators (pqac-00000009, pqac-00000012, pqac-00000017, pqac-00000026, pqac-00000028, pqac-00000029, pqac-00000032, pqac-00000033) |
| Cellular localization/compartments | Functional evidence places OST1 in guard cells at plasma-membrane-proximal signaling complexes with SLAC1, ABI1/PP2CA, BAK1, RBOHs, and CNGCs, while additional targets such as transcription factors/chromatin regulators indicate nuclear and cytosolic activity; related SnRK2 studies emphasize nucleus-to-peroxisome relocalization for SnRK2.2/2.3 during prolonged ABA, highlighting compartmentalized SnRK2 signaling even if that long-term relocalization is not yet directly assigned to OST1 (pqac-00000009, pqac-00000015, pqac-00000021, pqac-00000026, pqac-00000027) |
| Physiological roles | OST1 is a core positive regulator of ABA-induced stomatal closure, drought and osmotic-stress responses, ROS production in guard cells, and broader stress signaling; loss-of-function ost1 mutants are ABA-insensitive in stomata, fail to close properly, lose more water, and are drought sensitive (pqac-00000009, pqac-00000013, pqac-00000015) |
| Recent 2023-2024 developments | Recent work expanded SnRK2/OST1 biology beyond the canonical PYL-PP2C-SnRK2 pathway: new upstream kinase layers (RAF/BIK1/CPKs), new PTM control (persulfidation, S-nitrosylation, ubiquitination, SUMOylation), new Ca2+ entry targets (CNGCs), and broader network integration with metabolism/cytoskeleton. A major 2024 advance showed prolonged ABA signaling can drive N-glycosylation-dependent relocalization of related SnRK2s to peroxisomes to control NADPH/H2O2 homeostasis, reframing SnRK2 signaling as temporally compartmentalized rather than only acute and nuclear/PM-centered (pqac-00000017, pqac-00000018, pqac-00000020, pqac-00000021, pqac-00000022) |
| Quantitative data points | In transcriptome time courses under ABA, 8/20 clusters (12,337 genes) showed biphasic patterns and 5,271 ABA-upregulated genes were identified; in glycosylation mutant stt3a-2, the desensitization switch of 1,632/5,271 genes was delayed. Guard-cell phosphoproteomics found 166 peptides increased and 117 decreased after ABA in WT, with 84 of the 166 ABA-induced phosphopeptides (58 proteins) reduced or lost in snrk2.2/2.3/2.6 mutants. Pharmacology in prolonged ABA signaling used 10 μM polydatin or 3 μM GN6P to block late NADPH accumulation (pqac-00000017, pqac-00000020, pqac-00000021) |
| Applications | OST1 is a leading target for improving drought resilience, transpiration efficiency, stomatal control, and possibly productivity by engineering ABA signaling or guard-cell metabolism. Recent reviews explicitly frame activation of ABA signaling and manipulation of guard-cell pathways, including OST1-linked starch dynamics and ion-channel regulation, as practical strategies for enhancing stress tolerance and crop performance under changing climates (pqac-00000023, pqac-00000025, pqac-00000034) |
