## Research report: Arabidopsis thaliana **GL3 / GLABRA3** (UniProt **Q9FN69**, locus **At5g41315**)

### 1) Key concepts and definitions (current understanding)

**Gene/protein identity (verified).** Arabidopsis **GL3 (GLABRA3)** encodes a **basic helix–loop–helix (bHLH) transcription factor** that is a core component of the epidermal fate regulatory network. It acts within the canonical **MYB–bHLH–WD40 (MBW)** transcriptional regulatory cassette, pairing with an R2R3-MYB (e.g., **GL1** in shoots; **WER** in roots) and the WD40 protein **TTG1** to regulate epidermal differentiation programs and specialized metabolism outputs (morohashi2007participationofthe pages 1-2, pesch2015transparenttestaglabra1 pages 1-4, chen2019glabra2acommon pages 1-3).

**MBW complex as a regulatory “module.”** In Arabidopsis, MBW modules are reused across tissues to control: (i) **trichome initiation/patterning**, (ii) **root epidermal (root hair vs non-hair) patterning**, and (iii) **flavonoid/anthocyanin** (and in some contexts proanthocyanidin) biosynthetic gene expression, with specificity determined by which MYB/bHLH paralogues are expressed and how they assemble (wei2019genomewideidentificationof pages 1-3, pesch2015transparenttestaglabra1 pages 1-4, chen2019glabra2acommon pages 3-5).

**Lateral inhibition and mobile repressors.** A defining concept in trichome/root epidermal patterning is that MBW activity induces single-repeat **R3-MYB repressors** (e.g., **CPC**, **TRY**, **ETC1**) that can **compete** for bHLH binding and (for some members) move between cells, implementing negative feedback and spacing (lateral inhibition) (wei2019genomewideidentificationof pages 1-3, morohashi2007participationofthe pages 1-2, chen2019glabra2acommon pages 3-5).

### 2) Primary function, mechanisms, pathways, and localization

#### 2.1 Molecular function and subcellular localization

**Nuclear transcription factor action (functional evidence).** Direct GL3 nuclear action is strongly supported by steroid-inducible **GL3–GR** constructs: dexamethasone-dependent activation rapidly triggers transcriptional changes and GL3–GR promoter occupancy detectable by ChIP, consistent with ligand-controlled nuclear entry and transcription-factor function (morohashi2007participationofthe pages 3-4, morohashi2007participationofthe pages 4-6).

**DNA binding and promoter context.** GL3 directly occupies target promoters in vivo; the **GL2** promoter is reported to contain **E-boxes** (bHLH-type cis-elements) as well as MYB sites, consistent with combinatorial regulation by bHLH+MYB partners (morohashi2007participationofthe pages 8-9, chen2019glabra2acommon pages 3-5).

#### 2.2 Direct targets and regulatory circuitry

**Early direct targets in trichome initiation.** Morohashi et al. (2007; publication date: **Sep 2007**; URL: https://doi.org/10.1104/pp.107.104521) demonstrated that induction of GL3 triggers early trichome initiation and that GL3 binds the promoters of **GL2**, **CPC**, and **ETC1** in vivo and activates their transcription; cycloheximide co-treatment supports these as **immediate direct targets** (morohashi2007participationofthe pages 3-4, morohashi2007participationofthe pages 4-6).

**GL1-dependent vs GL1-independent promoter occupancy.** Promoter recruitment is target-dependent: GL3 recruitment to **GL2** and **CPC** was found to require **GL1**, supporting a GL3–GL1 enhanceosome model for some targets, while GL3 binding to its own promoter can occur **GL1-independently** and reduces GL3 expression (negative autoregulation) (morohashi2007participationofthe pages 6-8, morohashi2007participationofthe pages 1-2).

**Autoregulation.** GL3 can be recruited to its own promoter and reduce its own transcript abundance, supporting a **negative feedback** loop that can stabilize patterning outcomes (morohashi2007participationofthe pages 1-2, morohashi2007participationofthe pages 4-6).

#### 2.3 Protein–protein interactions and MBW architecture

**Competitive binding and alternative complexes.** Rather than a single obligate, stable trimeric MBW complex, experimental evidence indicates **GL1 and TTG1 compete for binding to GL3** and that GL3 can exist in alternative dimeric configurations (GL1–GL3 vs GL3–TTG1), with promoter-specific consequences (publication date: **Apr 2015**; URL: https://doi.org/10.1104/pp.15.00328) (pesch2015transparenttestaglabra1 pages 1-4).

### 3) Biological roles (process-level functional annotation)

#### 3.1 Trichome initiation and patterning (shoot epidermis)

**Core positive regulator of trichome initiation.** GL3 activity can trigger trichome initiation within hours, and GL3 acts upstream of both differentiation drivers (**GL2**) and inhibitory spacing components (**CPC/ETC1**) (morohashi2007participationofthe pages 3-4, morohashi2007participationofthe pages 1-2).

**Redundancy with EGL3.** GL3 and the related bHLH **EGL3** are partially redundant; double mutant phenotypes support that combined bHLH activity is essential for robust epidermal patterning (morohashi2007participationofthe pages 1-2, morohashi2007participationofthe pages 2-3).

#### 3.2 Root epidermal patterning (root hair vs non-hair)

In the root, analogous MBW logic couples MYB partners (e.g., WER) with GL3/EGL3-type bHLHs and TTG1 to activate **GL2**, promoting non-hair fate and restricting root hair formation, while R3 MYBs antagonize complex formation by competing for the bHLH (chen2019glabra2acommon pages 1-3, chen2019glabra2acommon pages 5-7).

#### 3.3 Flavonoid/anthocyanin pathway regulation

**MBW as central regulator of anthocyanin biosynthesis.** Reviews and MBW-target studies place GL3 among the bHLH components (GL3/EGL3/TT8) that participate in MBW-mediated control of late flavonoid/anthocyanin genes. A TTG1-dependent MBW module directly activates a set of flavonoid-related structural and regulatory genes in vegetative tissues, supporting the MBW framework in which GL3-family bHLHs function (publication date: **Oct 2019**; URL: https://doi.org/10.3390/ijms20205014) (wei2019genomewideidentificationof pages 1-3, wei2019genomewideidentificationof pages 8-10, wei2019genomewideidentificationof pages 4-8).

### 4) Recent developments (prioritizing 2023–2024)

#### 4.1 2024: Quantitative biophysics of GL3-centered complex formation and patterning robustness

Zhang et al. (publication date: **Mar 2024**; URL: https://doi.org/10.3389/fpls.2024.1331156) provided **quantitative dissociation constants** and model-based predictions for ensemble MBW complex composition in trichome patterning.

Key quantitative findings (relative, dimensionless Kd scale) include:
- **GL1–GL3 Kd ≈ 0.5** and **TTG1–GL3 Kd ≈ 1** (similar range) (zhang2024quantitativeanalysisof pages 6-8, zhang2024quantitativeanalysisof media 5862c7e2).
- **GL3–GL3 (homodimerization) Kd ≈ 0.5**, supporting a plausible route to higher-order assemblies (zhang2024quantitativeanalysisof pages 6-8, zhang2024quantitativeanalysisof media 5862c7e2).
- Inhibitor binding is weaker: **TRY–GL3 Kd ≈ 2.7** and **CPC–GL3 Kd ≈ 2.3**, implying inhibitors must reach higher effective concentrations to displace activators (zhang2024quantitativeanalysisof pages 11-13, zhang2024quantitativeanalysisof media 5862c7e2).
- **Negative cooperativity** between GL1 and TTG1 binding to GL3 (α ≈ 0.2; reported ~0.4 under higher-order assumptions) indicates canonical trimer formation is disfavored unless stoichiometry or GL3 dimerization compensates (zhang2024quantitativeanalysisof pages 4-6, zhang2024quantitativeanalysisof media 5862c7e2).
- Model predictions suggest the classic **GL1–GL3–TTG1** trimer may be a **low-fraction species (~5%)** under equimolar assumptions, and that overall regulation arises from an **ensemble** of complexes (including inhibited complexes) whose fractions vary with component abundance (zhang2024quantitativeanalysisof pages 10-11, zhang2024quantitativeanalysisof media 5862c7e2).

These results materially shift “current understanding” from a qualitative MBW-trimer cartoon to a quantitatively constrained, stoichiometry-sensitive ensemble view of GL3-centered regulation (zhang2024quantitativeanalysisof pages 10-11).

#### 4.2 2023: Evolutionary and tissue-specific roles of GL3/EGL3 vs TT8 in seeds

Zumajo‑Cardona et al. (publication date: **Jul 2023**; URL: https://doi.org/10.1093/aob/mcad097) reviewed functional evolution among MBW bHLHs and emphasized expression-domain and function partitioning: the **EGL3/GL3 clade** is canonically linked to trichome/root hair regulation but shows additional roles and expression outside trichomes, including seed-related contexts such as outer seed-coat layers and mucilage-associated programs in some datasets, while **TT8** is conserved in endothelium-linked proanthocyanidin regulation (zumajocardona2023evolutionarystudiesof pages 11-13, zumajocardona2023evolutionarystudiesof pages 10-11).

#### 4.3 2023: GL3 in light-regulated phenolic/anthocyanin control networks

Liu et al. (publication date: **Oct 2023**; URL: https://doi.org/10.1038/s42003-023-05435-4) synthesized how light signaling regulates MBW outputs. In this framework, bHLH partners including **GL3/EGL3/TT8** participate in MBW control of anthocyanin genes, while upstream photoreceptor-mediated inhibition of COP1/SPA/DET1 stabilizes **HY5** and alters PIF activity, thereby changing transcription of MBW regulators and potentially MBW component stability via post-translational modifications (liu2023lightregulationof pages 1-2, liu2023lightregulationof pages 4-5).

### 5) Current applications and real-world implementations

**Engineering epidermal traits and specialized metabolism (translational logic).** While Arabidopsis GL3 itself is primarily a model-system gene, the MBW module it participates in is broadly conserved and is used as a conceptual and practical scaffold for engineering:
- **Epidermal outgrowth traits** (trichomes/fiber-like structures) by tuning MBW component expression and inhibitor strength; the 2024 quantitative parameters provide a blueprint for how stoichiometry and binding strengths constrain feasible engineering designs (zhang2024quantitativeanalysisof pages 10-11, zhang2024quantitativeanalysisof media 5862c7e2).
- **Anthocyanin/phenolic accumulation** under environmental control (e.g., light regimes) by targeting upstream regulators (HY5/PIF/BBX) that regulate MBW regulators and by stabilizing MBW components via post-translational modifications described in recent reviews (liu2023lightregulationof pages 1-2, liu2023lightregulationof pages 10-11).

### 6) Expert opinion and analysis (authoritative synthesis)

**Ensemble vs single-complex view.** A key emerging expert conclusion from 2024 quantitative work is that MBW regulation should be conceptualized as an **ensemble of competing complexes** centered on GL3 (including GL3 homodimers and inhibited complexes), rather than a single dominant GL1–GL3–TTG1 trimer. This re-frames how robustness and spacing can arise: patterning behavior can be stabilized by GL3 dimerization and by concentration gradients of inhibitors, despite relatively weak inhibitor binding (zhang2024quantitativeanalysisof pages 10-11, zhang2024quantitativeanalysisof media 5862c7e2).

**Context dependence across tissues.** Reviews emphasize that “GL3 function” cannot be reduced to a single output; instead, GL3 participates in a modular network whose tissue specificity depends on the available MYB/bHLH/WD40 paralogues and regulatory environment (seed coat vs epidermis; light vs dark) (zumajocardona2023evolutionarystudiesof pages 11-13, liu2023lightregulationof pages 1-2).

### 7) Recent statistics and data highlights

Quantitative statistics from 2024 (Zhang et al.) relevant for functional annotation and modeling include (relative scale): **Kd(GL1–GL3) ≈ 0.5; Kd(TTG1–GL3) ≈ 1; Kd(GL3–GL3) ≈ 0.5; Kd(TRY–GL3) ≈ 2.7; Kd(CPC–GL3) ≈ 2.3; cooperativity α ≈ 0.2**; and predicted **~5%** fraction for the canonical GL1–GL3–TTG1 trimer under equimolar assumptions (zhang2024quantitativeanalysisof pages 6-8, zhang2024quantitativeanalysisof pages 10-11, zhang2024quantitativeanalysisof pages 4-6, zhang2024quantitativeanalysisof media 5862c7e2).
