CC   -!- FUNCTION: Acts as a component of the auxin efflux carrier; this
CC       activity is enhanced when activated by D6PK-mediated phosphorylation
CC       (PubMed:35917925). Binds auxins including indole-3-acetic acid (IAA),
CC       indole-3-butyric acid (IBA), indole-3-propionic acid (IPA) and 4-
CC       chloroindole-3-acetic acid (4-Cl-IAA) (PubMed:35917925). Seems to be
CC       involved in the basipetal auxin transport. Mediates the formation of
CC       auxin gradient which is required to ensure correct organogenesis.
CC       Coordinated polar localization of PIN1 is directly regulated by the
CC       vesicle trafficking process and apical-basal PIN1 polarity also depends
CC       on the phosphorylation of conserved serine residues by PID kinase. The
CC       ARF-GEF protein GNOM is required for the correct recycling of PIN1
CC       between the plasma membrane and endosomal compartments. Recrutes NPY
CC       proteins (e.g. NPY1/MAB4 and NPY5/MEL1) to the plasma membrane in a
CC       polar basal localization in root epidermis; this activity is optimized
CC       by AGC kinases-mediated (e.g. D6PK and PID) phosphorylation that limits
CC       their lateral diffusion-based escape (PubMed:33705718).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:14651850, ECO:0000269|PubMed:20407025,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:20439545, ECO:0000269|PubMed:33705718,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:35917925}.
CC   -!- ACTIVITY REGULATION: Auxin efflux carrier activity is competitively
CC       inhibited by naptalamate (N-1-naphthylphthalamic acid, NPA) but
CC       activated by D6PK-mediated phosphorylation.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:35917925}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homodimer (PubMed:35917925). Interacts with TOPP4
CC       (PubMed:11574889). Interacts with FYPP1 and FYPP3 (PubMed:22715043).
CC       Component of a complex made of PINs (e.g. PIN1 and PIN2), MAB4/MELs
CC       (e.g. NPY1/MAB4 and NPY5/MEL1) and AGC kinases (e.g. D6PK and PID) at
CC       the plasma membrane (PubMed:33705718). Binds directly to NPY5/MEL1
CC       (PubMed:33705718). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11574889,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:22715043, ECO:0000269|PubMed:33705718,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:35917925}.

CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell membrane {ECO:0000269|PubMed:20439545,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:33705718}; Multi-pass membrane protein

CC       localization. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:33705718}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Expressed at the basal side of elongated
CC       parenchymatous xylem cells.
CC   -!- DEVELOPMENTAL STAGE: Expressed during embryogenesis. Already detected
CC       in the 8-cell stage at the inner cell boundaries. Later, polarity is
CC       gradually established at the basal side of provascular cells then in
CC       epidermis cells. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:14614497}.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: Down-regulated by endoplasmic reticulum stress treatment.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:24180465}.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: Plants exhibit developed naked, pin-shaped
CC       inflorescences and abnormalities in the number, size, shape, and
CC       position of lateral organs. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:9856939}.
