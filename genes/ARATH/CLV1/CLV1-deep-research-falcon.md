## Research Report: Arabidopsis thaliana CLAVATA1 (CLV1; UniProt Q9SYQ8)

### 1) Target identity verification (mandatory disambiguation)
The target protein (UniProt Q9SYQ8) is **Arabidopsis thaliana CLAVATA1 (CLV1)**, a **leucine-rich repeat receptor-like kinase (LRR-RLK)** functioning in the canonical **CLAVATA–WUSCHEL (CLV–WUS) feedback loop** that maintains shoot apical meristem (SAM) stem-cell homeostasis. Multiple recent authoritative sources describe Arabidopsis **CLV1** specifically as a plasma-membrane LRR-RLK receptor for CLE peptides that restricts WUS activity, matching the UniProt description (extracellular LRRs, single-pass membrane receptor kinase precursor). (hong2023stemcellsengines pages 9-10, demesaarevalo2024intercellularcommunicationin pages 15-17, bashyal2024clavatasignalingin pages 1-2, john2023anetworkof pages 1-2)

**Symbol ambiguity note:** “CLV1” can refer to CLV1 orthologs/paralogs in other plant species and to closely related Arabidopsis LRR-RLKs such as **BAM** receptors (“CLV1/BAMs”), so species context is essential; recent reviews explicitly discuss CLV1-like genes across land plants and receptor redundancy/compensation. (demesaarevalo2024intercellularcommunicationin pages 15-17, john2023anetworkof pages 1-2)

### 2) Key concepts and definitions (current understanding)
**CLV1 receptor kinase module.** CLV1 is part of the plant receptor kinase signaling paradigm in which extracellular peptides (ligands) are perceived by plasma-membrane receptors that trigger intracellular phosphorylation cascades. In Arabidopsis meristems, CLV3-family CLE peptides are secreted and perceived by membrane receptor systems that include CLV1 and additional CLAVATA receptors. (bashyal2024clavatasignalingin pages 1-2, hong2023stemcellsengines pages 9-10)

**CLV–WUS feedback loop.** The CLV pathway restricts stem-cell proliferation by repressing **WUSCHEL (WUS)** activity; WUS supports stem-cell identity, while CLV signaling constrains WUS output to maintain a stable meristem size. (hong2023stemcellsengines pages 9-10, demesaarevalo2024intercellularcommunicationin pages 15-17, john2023anetworkof pages 1-2)

**Receptor network and buffering.** Rather than a single linear pathway, meristem homeostasis is buffered by a **network** of CLAVATA receptors (CLV1, BAM-family RLKs, CLV2/CRN module, and other RLKs) that can overlap and compensate. This buffering is emphasized in 2023–2024 syntheses of meristem intercellular communication and CLAVATA signaling. (demesaarevalo2024intercellularcommunicationin pages 15-17, bashyal2024clavatasignalingin pages 1-2, john2023anetworkof pages 1-2)

### 3) Molecular function and mechanism (functional annotation)
#### 3.1 Biochemical activity
CLV1 is described as an **active receptor-like kinase** that transduces CLE-peptide perception into intracellular phosphorylation events, consistent with its classification as an LRR-RLK. (hong2023stemcellsengines pages 9-10, bashyal2024clavatasignalingin pages 1-2)

#### 3.2 Ligands and upstream activation
In the SAM, **CLV3 peptide** produced in the central zone acts non-cell-autonomously and is perceived via CLV1 (and parallel receptor systems) to repress WUS expression in the organizing center and regulate stem-cell proliferation. (john2023anetworkof pages 1-2, demesaarevalo2024intercellularcommunicationin pages 15-17)

#### 3.3 Receptor complexes and co-receptors
Recent reviews and mechanistic summaries describe CLV1 as operating within **multi-protein receptor complexes**, including functional association with **BAM receptors** and requirement for **CIK family co-receptor kinases** to transmit CLE/CLV signals, while the **CLV2–CRN** module acts in parallel. (hong2023stemcellsengines pages 9-10, john2023anetworkof pages 1-2)

#### 3.4 Downstream signaling partners and regulation
A 2023 stem-cell review synthesizes a mechanistic model in which **RLCKs (PBL34/35/36)** function downstream of CLV1/BAM receptors, physically associating with CLV1/BAM and CIK co-receptors and being phosphorylated by CLV1/BAM; this connects CLV1 activation to cytosolic kinase signaling. (hong2023stemcellsengines pages 9-10)

The same synthesis describes key negative/attenuating regulators: **KAPP** associates with CLV1 and dephosphorylates its kinase domain (negative regulation), while **POL and PLL1** (PP2C phosphatases) act downstream and are regulated by phosphorylation such that CLV3 perception promotes signaling competence. (hong2023stemcellsengines pages 9-10)

MAPK involvement downstream of CLV signaling (e.g., MPK3/MPK6 phosphorylation responsiveness to CLV3 via CLV1/BAM) is summarized in recent reviews, positioning CLV1 within broader receptor kinase signaling paradigms. (hong2023stemcellsengines pages 9-10)

### 4) Subcellular localization and where CLV1 acts
CLV1 functions at the **plasma membrane** of meristem cells as a CLE peptide receptor, and receptor activation is coupled to **dynamic trafficking/endocytosis** that shapes signal output. (bashyal2024clavatasignalingin pages 1-2, wang2023tplatecomplex‐dependentendocytosis pages 4-6)

A key 2023 primary study in *EMBO Reports* demonstrated that CLV1 undergoes **CLV3-triggered internalization** and that this process requires **TPLATE-complex (TPC)-dependent clathrin-mediated endocytosis**; impairing this endocytosis increases CLV1 abundance at the plasma membrane and alters signaling sensitivity. (wang2023tplatecomplex‐dependentendocytosis pages 4-6, wang2023tplatecomplex‐dependentendocytosis pages 2-4)

**Image-based evidence:** In Wang et al. (2023), microscopy and quantification show **higher plasma-membrane CLV1-GFP** in an endocytosis-impaired background (WDXM2) and reduced CLV3-induced internalization relative to control, consistent with endocytosis-mediated attenuation of CLV1 signaling. (wang2023tplatecomplex‐dependentendocytosis media c2285e87)

### 5) Recent developments (prioritizing 2023–2024)
#### 5.1 Endocytosis as a quantitative “signal dampener” (2023)
Wang et al. (*EMBO Reports*, published July 2023; https://doi.org/10.15252/embr.202254709) reported that reduced TPC-dependent endocytosis causes **hypersensitivity** to exogenous CLV3/CLE peptides and correlates this with increased CLV1 at the plasma membrane. They tested CLV3 doses including **10 nM, 100 nM, and 1 µM**, observing dose-dependent effects on meristem maintenance and WUS reporter behavior in endocytosis-impaired lines. (wang2023tplatecomplex‐dependentendocytosis pages 2-4, wang2023tplatecomplex‐dependentendocytosis pages 4-6)

Quantitatively, CLV1-GFP plasma-membrane signal differences between backgrounds were statistically significant (reported as **P < 0.001** for fluorescence intensity comparisons), strengthening the conclusion that receptor abundance/trafficking modulates signaling strength. (wang2023tplatecomplex‐dependentendocytosis pages 4-6)

#### 5.2 Environmental integration and broader physiology (2024 review perspective)
A 2024 *Plant Physiology* review (published Nov 2024; https://doi.org/10.1093/plphys/kiad591) emphasizes that CLAVATA-type receptors, often including **CLV1**, extend beyond classical meristem maintenance into **plant–environment interactions**, where environment-induced CLE peptides can trigger cell-type-specific responses through CLV1-containing receptor complexes and downstream signaling modules (phosphatases, MAPKs, etc.). (bashyal2024clavatasignalingin pages 1-2)

#### 5.3 Meristem communication frameworks (2024 review perspective)
A 2024 *Annual Review of Plant Biology* article (online May 2024 / issue July 2024; https://doi.org/10.1146/annurev-arplant-070523-035342) situates CLV1 within the broader framework of **intercellular communication** (mobile peptides, transcription factors, hormones) that coordinates meristem domain behavior, highlighting that CLV modules are deeply conserved and are a central “output” regulating meristem stability. (demesaarevalo2024intercellularcommunicationin pages 15-17)

### 6) Current applications and real-world implementations
Although CLV1 functional annotation is rooted in Arabidopsis developmental genetics, **CLV1-type signaling is actively leveraged for crop trait engineering**, especially via genome editing of CLE/CLV modules to tune meristem size and inflorescence architecture.

**Expert consensus (authoritative review framing):** The conserved CLV–WUS module has been exploited in domestication and is positioned as a tractable target to improve productivity by increasing meristem size and organ number (while avoiding severe fasciation), with CRISPR/Cas strategies highlighted in recent meristem-communication reviews. (demesaarevalo2024intercellularcommunicationin pages 17-18)

**Solanaceae (tomato/groundcherry) CRISPR evidence:** A 2024 *Horticulture Research* study (published May 2024; https://doi.org/10.1093/hr/uhae126) used CRISPR-Cas9 receptor mutants to show that **BAM paralogs compensate** for CLV1 perturbations, an important practical consideration for designing edits with predictable phenotypic magnitude. (seo2024evolutionaryconservationof pages 1-2)

**Barley CLV1 ortholog as an engineering target (quantitative data):** A 2025 *Nature Communications* study (published Apr 2025; https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-025-59330-z) identified a barley module involving **HvCLV1** and a CLE ligand (**HvFCP1**) that controls inflorescence and rachilla meristem determinacy. Quantitative outcomes include:
- multi-floret spikelets in **12/35** Hvfcp1-1 inflorescences, and
- transcriptome shifts in Hvclv1 vs WT of **1,208 genes upregulated and 1,197 downregulated** (plus additional differential-expression counts in other comparisons),
supporting that CLV1-type signaling is a major lever on meristem activity and downstream metabolic programs relevant to yield components. (vardanega2025clavatasignallingshapes pages 6-7)

### 7) Statistics and data points (recent studies)
Key quantitative findings directly tied to CLV1 signaling regulation include:
- **Dose-response experiments** with CLV3 peptide (e.g., 10 nM vs 100 nM vs 1 µM) demonstrating that impaired endocytosis causes **hypersensitive** phenotypes and stronger repression of WUS reporter output at higher doses in endocytosis-impaired lines. (wang2023tplatecomplex‐dependentendocytosis pages 2-4)
- **Microscopy quantification** showing significantly increased CLV1-GFP plasma-membrane signal in endocytosis-impaired WDXM2 relative to control, with **P < 0.001** in reported comparisons, and impaired internalization after 100 nM CLV3. (wang2023tplatecomplex‐dependentendocytosis pages 4-6, wang2023tplatecomplex‐dependentendocytosis media c2285e87)
- Crop-relevant quantitative summaries for CLV1-type pathways in barley: **12/35** multi-floret spikelet incidence and thousands of DE genes in Hvclv1 mutants. (vardanega2025clavatasignallingshapes pages 6-7)

### 8) Consolidated functional summary (for annotation)
CLV1 (Q9SYQ8) is best annotated as a **plasma-membrane LRR receptor Ser/Thr kinase** that perceives **CLV3-family CLE peptides** and activates intracellular phosphorylation pathways (via co-receptors and RLCKs) to repress WUS activity and thereby constrain stem-cell proliferation in shoot and floral meristems; signal strength is tuned by **negative regulators (KAPP; POL/PLL1)** and by **ligand-triggered endocytosis** that removes CLV1 from the plasma membrane to attenuate signaling. (hong2023stemcellsengines pages 9-10, john2023anetworkof pages 1-2, bashyal2024clavatasignalingin pages 1-2, wang2023tplatecomplex‐dependentendocytosis pages 4-6)
