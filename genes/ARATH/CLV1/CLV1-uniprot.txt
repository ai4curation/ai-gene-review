CC   -!- FUNCTION: LRR-rich receptor-like kinase (LRR-RLK) that, similarly to
CC       BAM1 and BAM3, participates in the detection of CLAVATA-family peptides
CC       (e.g. CLV3, CLE25, CLE26 and CLE45), and to the regulation of the
CC       triggered signaling required for protophloem differentiation and
CC       meristems maintenance through the phosphorylation of CIK proteins
CC       (CIKs) and of PBL34, PBL35 and PBL36 (PubMed:19933383, PubMed:29581511,
CC       PubMed:34651321, PubMed:34935965). Acts with CLV3p as a ligand-receptor
CC       pair in a signal transduction pathway coordinating growth between
CC       adjacent meristematic regions and controlling the balance between
CC       meristem cell proliferation and differentiation (PubMed:19933383).
CC       Together with receptor-like cytoplasmic kinases such as
CC       CARK5/MAZ/PTI13, modulates developmental processes, including stomatal
CC       patterning and spacing, as well as root development, via the perception
CC       of CLE peptides (e.g. CLE40) (PubMed:33909893).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:19933383, ECO:0000269|PubMed:29581511,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:33909893, ECO:0000269|PubMed:34651321,

CC   -!- DEVELOPMENTAL STAGE: Accumulates in shoot meristems, companion cells of

CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: Ectopic fruit organ initiation after floral
CC       meristem termination (PubMed:21705761). Enhanced disease resistance
CC       response to the bacterial pathogen Ralstonia solanacearum and to the
CC       biotrophic oomycete pathogen Hyaloperonospora arabidopsidis
CC       (PubMed:26990325). Increased columella stem cell (CSC) number due to
CC       reduced CLE40 signaling (PubMed:33909893). Abnormal stomatal patterning
CC       leading to clusters formation in plants lacking both CLV1 and
CC       CARK5/MAZ/PTI13 (PubMed:33909893). Impaired CIK1 phosphorylation upon
CC       CLV3 treatment in the triple mutant clv1 bam1 bam2 (PubMed:29581511).
CC       The triple mutant clv1 bam1 bam3 is insensitive to CLV3-like (e.g.
CC       CLE25, CLE26 and CLE45) peptides-triggered protophloem differentiation
CC       and proximal root meristem consumption, thus leading to the formation
CC       of premature protophloems (PubMed:34651321).
