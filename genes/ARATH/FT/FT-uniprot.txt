CC   -!- FUNCTION: Component of the mobile flower-promoting signal (floral
CC       stimulus or florigen). Promotes the transition from vegetative growth
CC       to flowering. Required for 'SEPALLATA3' (SEP3) and 'FRUITFULL' (FUL)
CC       accumulation in mature rosette leaves. Seems to acts in parallel with
CC       'LEAFY' to induce flowering by regulating 'APETALA1'. Translated in
CC       leaves and then transported to the shoot apical meristem where it
CC       activates the transcription of several floral meristem identity genes.
CC       May play a role in both the autonomous and the long-day flowering
CC       pathways. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:16099979, ECO:0000269|PubMed:16099980,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:16155177}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Interacts with FD/BZIP14 and FDP/BZIP27 (PubMed:16099979,
CC       PubMed:16099980). Interacts with FTIP1/MCTP1 in phloem companion cells
CC       (PubMed:22529749, PubMed:29259105). Interacts with NAKR1
CC       (PubMed:27255839). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:16099979,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:16099980, ECO:0000269|PubMed:22529749,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:27255839, ECO:0000269|PubMed:29259105}.

CC   -!- DEVELOPMENTAL STAGE: Expression gradually increases with time under
CC       both long-day (LD) and short-day (SD) photoperiods. Under LD
CC       conditions, expression is first detected on day 4 and plateaued around
CC       day 6, preceeding floral commitment around days 9 and 10.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: Levels follow a circadian cycle with a progressive
CC       accumulation during the day time (PubMed:25343985). By light.
CC       Expression is delayed and reduced under SD conditions. Repressed by
CC       FLC. Up-Regulated by VOZ1 and/or VOZ2 (PubMed:22904146). Up-regulated
CC       by APL/FE (PubMed:26239308). Down-regulated by the H3K36me2
CC       modification at the FT locus produced by the interaction between EFM
CC       and JMJ30 (PubMed:25132385). Repressed by MYB56 (PubMed:25343985).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:16099980, ECO:0000269|PubMed:22904146,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:25132385, ECO:0000269|PubMed:25343985,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:26239308}.
CC   -!- MISCELLANEOUS: Mutagenesis of Tyr-85 to His converts the activator of
CC       flowering 'FLOWERING LOCUS T' into a 'TERMINAL FLOWER 1'-like repressor
CC       of flowering.
