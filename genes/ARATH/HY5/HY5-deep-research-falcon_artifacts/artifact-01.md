> - **Light inputs upstream of HY5:** HY5 functions downstream of multiple photoreceptor pathways that sense red/far-red, blue, and UV-B light; recent synthesis and shade studies place HY5 in phytochrome-, cryptochrome-, and UVR8-linked transcriptional networks that couple light quality to developmental and metabolic outputs (pqac-00000000, pqac-00000001, pqac-00000018).
> - **Dark-state repression:** In darkness, the **COP1/SPA** complex represses photomorphogenesis by promoting HY5 destabilization through ubiquitin/proteasome control; COP1 accumulates in the nucleus in the dark and targets HY5 and related positive regulators of light responses (pqac-00000003, pqac-00000004, pqac-00000009, pqac-00000011).
> - **Light-state activation:** Upon illumination, photoreceptor signaling inhibits or reorganizes COP1/SPA activity, allowing HY5 accumulation and transcriptional activity; HY5 abundance increases in light, and its unphosphorylated form is described as the more active promoter-binding species (pqac-00000009, pqac-00000010, pqac-00000011).
> - **Core TF mechanism:** HY5 is a **bZIP transcription factor** that can homo/heterodimerize with related factors such as HYH and bind light-responsive cis-elements including **G-box, C-box, ACE-box**, and hybrid C/G- or C/A-box motifs, thereby converting photoreceptor information into gene-expression outputs (pqac-00000004, pqac-00000008, pqac-00000015).
> - **Primary developmental output:** Canonically, HY5 promotes **photomorphogenesis** by suppressing hypocotyl elongation and activating light-growth programs; transcriptomic and genetic evidence place HY5 as a major positive regulator of seedling de-etiolation and light-dependent development (pqac-00000000, pqac-00000005, pqac-00000015).
> - **Photosynthesis and chlorophyll programs:** HY5 positively regulates genes associated with **chlorophyll biosynthesis, photosynthesis, and chloroplast development**; in 2024 organ-specific shade transcriptomics, loss of HY5/HFR1 reduced expression of light-response and chlorophyll-related programs, especially in hypocotyls and cotyledons (pqac-00000005, pqac-00000006, pqac-00000016, pqac-00000038).
> - **Shade avoidance branch:** In shade, HY5 operates with **HFR1** as a negative regulator of shade-avoidance responses; the HFR1-HY5 module co-regulates organ-specific gene sets enriched for cell-wall remodeling, chlorophyll biosynthesis, and hormone responses, while mutant combinations show additive hypocotyl elongation phenotypes (pqac-00000005, pqac-00000006, pqac-00000037, pqac-00000039).
> - **Auxin-metabolism interface:** The HFR1-HY5 module suppresses shade-associated **auxin biosynthesis/conjugation** genes in cotyledons and hypocotyls; recent RNA-seq identified many HY5-targeted shade DEGs, including regulation of TAA1/YUC- and NIT2-associated auxin pathways linked to hypocotyl elongation control (pqac-00000006, pqac-00000037).
> - **Phenylpropanoid / anthocyanin branch:** HY5 is a central activator of **phenylpropanoid, flavonoid, and anthocyanin** gene programs, acting through targets such as MYB12, CHS, DFR, LDOX, and related biosynthetic regulators; UV-B signaling can increase HY5 transcription via UVR8-mediated displacement of WRKY36, linking photoperception directly to phenolic accumulation (pqac-00000002, pqac-00000016, pqac-00000018).
> - **Nutrient-assimilation branch:** HY5 links shoot light status to **nutrient uptake and assimilation**, with curated target sets including nitrate/phosphate-associated genes such as **NIA2, PHR1**, and regulation of transport/signaling genes such as **NPF6.3/NRT1.1**; broader HY5 target compilations also include NIR1, NRT2.1, sulfur-assimilation genes, and copper-related MIR408/SPL7 modules (pqac-00000016, pqac-00000019, pqac-00000021, pqac-00000032).
> - **Metabolic extension in 2024 work:** A 2024 Arabidopsis study further expands the HY5 network by showing direct HY5 binding to the **AtSQS1** promoter and negative regulation of **squalene/sterol biosynthesis**, indicating that HY5 coordinates not only classical light development but also terpenoid-related metabolic allocation (pqac-00000011, pqac-00000012).
> - **Application-relevant levers:** Evidence from 2024 bZIP application reviews highlights two practical HY5 levers: **light environment control** (including LED/white-light regulation of HY5 homologs affecting ripening/ethylene pathways) and **engineering HY5 or HY5 stability/activity** through chimeric TF design, modulation of COP1/SPA-dependent turnover, or broader bZIP editing/overexpression strategies for stress resilience and quality traits (pqac-00000025, pqac-00000026, pqac-00000028, pqac-00000029, pqac-00000031).
