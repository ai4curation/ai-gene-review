Gene Description

HY5 (ELONGATED HYPOCOTYL 5) is a basic leucine zipper (bZIP) transcription factor that acts as a master regulator of photomorphogenesis and light signaling in Arabidopsis thaliana. The protein contains a highly conserved basic region for DNA binding and a leucine zipper domain for dimerization, enabling it to function as a homodimer or form heterodimers with other bZIP factors. HY5 directly binds to G-box (CACGTG), Z-box (ATACGTGT), and other light-responsive cis-regulatory elements to activate transcription of hundreds of target genes involved in photosynthesis, chloroplast biogenesis, anthocyanin biosynthesis, and metabolic reprogramming. As a central hub in light signaling networks, HY5 integrates signals from multiple photoreceptor pathways including phytochromes (red/far-red light), cryptochromes (blue light), phototropins, and UVR8 (UV-B), coordinating photomorphogenic development with circadian rhythms and hormone signaling pathways (ABA, auxin, cytokinin, gibberellin). The protein undergoes light-dependent post-translational regulation through phosphorylation by multiple kinases and is targeted for ubiquitin-mediated proteasomal degradation by the COP1/SPA E3 ligase complex in darkness, with light exposure leading to COP1 inactivation and HY5 accumulation. HY5 also interacts with chromatin remodeling complexes and histone-modifying enzymes to regulate target gene expression at the chromatin level, establishing it as a key coordinator between light perception and genome-wide transcriptional reprogramming during photomorphogenesis.
