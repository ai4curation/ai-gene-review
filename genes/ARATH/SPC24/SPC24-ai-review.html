Gene Description

SPC24 (also MUN, MERISTEM UNSTRUCTURED; locus At3g08880) is the Arabidopsis thaliana homolog of kinetochore protein SPC24, one of the four subunits of the NDC80 outer-kinetochore complex (NDC80, NUF2, SPC24, SPC25). It is a small (201 aa) protein with a central coiled-coil and a C-terminal RWD-like globular domain; together with SPC25 it forms the inner-kinetochore-anchoring module of the NDC80 complex, while NDC80 and NUF2 form the microtubule-binding module. The NDC80 complex localizes to the outer kinetochore at centromeres and provides the principal end-on attachment site coupling spindle microtubules to chromosomes, and is required for accurate chromosome segregation and spindle-checkpoint signaling during cell division. AtSPC24/MUN localizes to the centromere/kinetochore, colocalizing with the centromeric histone variant CENH3, and physically interacts with the other three NDC80-complex subunits (AtNDC80, AtNUF2, AtSPC25). It is highly expressed in actively dividing tissues (shoot and root apical meristems, vasculature, emerging leaves). Loss of function is essential: null mun mutants arrest in early embryogenesis (division arrest before the globular stage), while weak alleles show stunted growth, DNA aneuploidy, reduced cell-division rate and chromosome-segregation defects, with secondary disorganization of the shoot apical meristem (ectopic WUSCHEL expression).

Structural subunit of the NDC80 outer-kinetochore complex (with NDC80, NUF2 and SPC25); SPC24 pairs with SPC25 to anchor the microtubule-binding NDC80-NUF2 module to the inner kinetochore, demonstrated by physical interaction with all three other Arabidopsis subunits.

Suggested Questions for Experts

Q: Does AtSPC24/MUN contribute to spindle-assembly-checkpoint signaling in Arabidopsis, as its NDC80-complex orthologs do in other eukaryotes, or is its role limited to kinetochore-microtubule attachment?

Q: Is the meristem disorganization in mun mutants entirely a downstream consequence of impaired cell division, or does ectopic WUSCHEL expression reflect a more direct link between kinetochore function and stem-cell-niche patterning?

Suggested Experiments

Experiment: Map the AtSPC24-AtSPC25 and AtSPC24-(NDC80/NUF2) binding interfaces by truncation/point-mutation interaction assays and, if feasible, reconstitute the recombinant Arabidopsis NDC80 complex to test microtubule binding.

Hypothesis: AtSPC24 binds SPC25 via its C-terminal RWD domain to form the inner-kinetochore-anchoring module, while microtubule binding is contributed by the NDC80-NUF2 module.

Type: protein interaction / in vitro reconstitution

Experiment: Use live-cell imaging of fluorescently tagged AtSPC24/MUN through the cell cycle to quantify kinetochore loading dynamics and test for spindle-checkpoint-dependent behavior under spindle poisons.

Hypothesis: AtSPC24 occupies kinetochores throughout mitosis and participates in checkpoint-controlled kinetochore-microtubule attachment.

Type: live-cell imaging / cell biology
