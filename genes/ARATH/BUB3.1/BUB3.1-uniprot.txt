CC   -!- FUNCTION: Has a dual function in spindle-assembly checkpoint signaling
CC       and in promoting the establishment of correct kinetochore-microtubule
CC       (K-MT) attachments. Promotes the formation of stable end-on bipolar
CC       attachments. Necessary for kinetochore localization of BUB1. The
CC       BUB1/BUB3 complex plays a role in the inhibition of anaphase-promoting
CC       complex or cyclosome (APC/C) when spindle-assembly checkpoint is
CC       activated and inhibits the ubiquitin ligase activity of APC/C by
CC       phosphorylating its activator CDC20 (By similarity). Essential for
CC       gametophyte development. {ECO:0000250, ECO:0000269|PubMed:18508582,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:19710914}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Part of the mitotic checkpoint complex (MCC); interacts with
CC       CDC20-1 and CDC20-2. Interacts with MAD2 and BUBR1.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:19710914, ECO:0000269|PubMed:21687678}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Nucleus. Chromosome, centromere, kinetochore.
CC       Cytoplasm, cytoskeleton, phragmoplast. Cytoplasm, cytoskeleton,
CC       spindle. Note=Accumulates onto both kinetochores and the spindle
CC       microtubules in cell arrested in metaphase. Starts to localize at
CC       kinetochores in prometaphase I (Pro-MI) stage and maintains the
CC       localization until the metaphase I-anaphase I (MI-AI) transition.
CC       Associates with unattached kinetochores upon spindle assembly
CC       checkpoint (SAC) activation. Present in the phragmoplast midline during
CC       the final step of cell division.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Expressed in actively dividing tissues, early in
CC       organ development, in young leaves, lateral root primordia and root
CC       meristems, flower buds, flowers and siliques.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:18508582, ECO:0000269|PubMed:19710914}.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: Cell cycle regulated expression with a distinct expression
CC       peak at the G2/M boundary. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:18508582,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:19710914}.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: Survives only in heterozygous state. Displays
CC       defects in development of male and female gametophytes.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:18508582}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the WD repeat BUB3 family. {ECO:0000305}.
