CC   -!- FUNCTION: Auxin response factors (ARFs) are transcriptional factors
CC       that bind specifically to the DNA sequence 5'-TGTCTC-3' found in the
CC       auxin-responsive promoter elements (AuxREs). Seems to act as
CC       transcriptional activator. Formation of heterodimers with Aux/IAA
CC       proteins may alter their ability to modulate early auxin response genes
CC       expression. Mediates embryo axis formation and vascular tissues
CC       differentiation. Functionally redundant with ARF7. May be necessary to
CC       counteract AMP1 activity. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12036261,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:14973283, ECO:0000269|PubMed:17553903}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homodimers and heterodimers. Interacts with BRX and the auxin-
CC       responsive proteins IAA1, IAA12 (BODENLOS), IAA17 and ARF7.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12101120, ECO:0000269|PubMed:14973283,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:19465596}.

CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Nucleus.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Expressed in the whole plant with a lower
CC       expression in leaves. Detected in embryo axis, provascular tissues,
CC       procambium and some differentiated vascular regions of mature organs.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10476078}.
CC   -!- DEVELOPMENTAL STAGE: In early embryo and during organ development.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: Interactions between auxin response factors (ARFs) and Aux/IAA
