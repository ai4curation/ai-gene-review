CC   -!- FUNCTION: Transcription factor that promotes early floral meristem
CC       identity in synergy with LEAFY. Is required subsequently for the
CC       transition of an inflorescence meristem into a floral meristem. Is
CC       indispensable for normal development of sepals and petals in flowers.
CC       Positively regulates the B class homeotic proteins APETALA3 and
CC       PISTILLATA with the cooperation of LEAFY and UFO. Interacts with
CC       SEPALLATA3 or AP3/PI heterodimer to form complexes that could be
CC       involved in genes regulation during floral meristem development.
CC       Positively regulates AGAMOUS in cooperation with LEAFY. Displays a
CC       redundant function with CAULIFLOWER in the up-regulation of LEAFY.
CC       Together with AGL24 and SVP, controls the identity of the floral
CC       meristem and regulates expression of class B, C and E genes. Represses
CC       flowering time genes AGL24, SVP and SOC1 in emerging floral meristems.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11283333, ECO:0000269|PubMed:17428825,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:17794879, ECO:0000269|PubMed:19656343,
CC       ECO:0000269|Ref.8}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homodimer capable of binding to CArG-box sequences.
CC       Heterodimer with SEP3, AP1 and SVP. Binds AP3/PI to form a ternary
CC       complex. Interacts with the SEU-LUG corepressor complex when complexed
CC       to AGL24 or SVP. Interacts with AGL15 and AGL16 (PubMed:15805477,
CC       PubMed:16679456). Interacts with TT16/AGL32 (PubMed:16080001).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15805477, ECO:0000269|PubMed:16080001,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:16679456}.
