CC   -!- FUNCTION: Plays a central role in immune responses (PubMed:20413097,
CC       PubMed:32846426, PubMed:29649442). Required to activate the resistance
CC       responses to necrotrophic pathogens, including the regulation of
CC       defense hormone expression (e.g. jasmonic acid (JA) and salicylic acid
CC       (SA)) (PubMed:16339855, PubMed:29649442). Phosphorylates FLS2 and BAK1
CC       (PubMed:20404519, PubMed:24104392). Involved in pathogen-associated
CC       molecular pattern (PAMP)-triggered immunity (PTI) signaling, including
CC       calcium signaling, and defense responses downstream of FLS2; upon PAMP
CC       recognition, first phosphorylated by FLS2 and SIK1 prior to being
CC       monoubiquitinated by ATL44/RHA3A and ATL45/RHA3B at the plasma
CC       membrane, then internalized dynamically into endocytic compartments
CC       together with FLS2 (PubMed:20413097, PubMed:25522736, PubMed:30212650,
CC       PubMed:32404997). Acts additively with PBL1 in PTI defenses
CC       (PubMed:20413097). Acts as a positive regulator of the PAMP flg22-
CC       induced increase of cytosolic calcium (PubMed:24629339,
CC       PubMed:32846426). Upon flg22 perception, phosphorylates and activates
CC       the calcium-permeable channel OSCA1.3, promoting stomatal closure
CC       (PubMed:32846426). Phosphorylates the NADPH oxidase RBOHD at specific
CC       sites in a calcium-independent manner to enhance reactive oxygen
CC       species (ROS) generation upon flg22 perception (PubMed:24629339). ROS
CC       production in response to flg22 controls stomatal movement and
CC       restriction of bacterial entry into leaf tissues (PubMed:24629339).
CC       Seems not required for flg22-induced MAPK activation (PubMed:22504181).
CC       Required for Pep1-induced defenses. Pep1 is an endogenous elicitor that
CC       potentiates PAMP-inducible plant responses. In association with PEPR1,
CC       acts downstream of the canonical ethylene signaling cascade to regulate
CC       ethylene responses (PubMed:23431184). Involved in ethylene signaling.
CC       Destabilizes EIN3, the key transcription activator in ethylene
CC       signaling, and represses EIN3-dependent transcription
CC       (PubMed:26021844). Acts as a negative regulator in brassinosteroid (BR)
CC       signaling. Functions in BR signaling by direct interaction with the BR
CC       receptor BRI1 cytosolic kinase domain (PubMed:23818580). Required
CC       during SCOOP small peptides (e.g. SCOOP10 and SCOOP12) perception and
CC       signaling; receptor-like cytosolic kinases (RLCK) activated by
CC       BAK1/SERK3 and SERK4 coreceptors when associated with MIK2 upon the
CC       perception of SCOOP peptides (PubMed:34535661).
