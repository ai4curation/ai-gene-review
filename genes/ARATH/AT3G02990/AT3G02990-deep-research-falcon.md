## Research report: Arabidopsis thaliana **HSFA1E** (AT3G02990; UniProt Q9SCW5)

### 0) Target verification (critical disambiguation)
The target protein is **Arabidopsis thaliana heat stress transcription factor A‑1e (HSFA1E; AtHsfA1e)**, a **class A1 heat shock transcription factor (HSF)**. Multiple Arabidopsis-focused sources explicitly state that the Arabidopsis class A1 group comprises **HSFA1a, HSFA1b, HSFA1d, and HSFA1e**, which function redundantly as master regulators of the heat-stress response (HSR). (liu2013commonanddistinct pages 1-2, liu2011theroleof pages 1-2)

Because “HSF” gene names are used across many plant species, the literature must be interpreted carefully: many papers discuss **HSFA1** functions as a *group* (A1a/b/d/e) or in other species, and HSFA1E-specific evidence is often embedded in multi-mutant comparisons rather than isolated single-mutant studies. (liu2013commonanddistinct pages 1-2, liu2013commonanddistinct pages 6-8)

### 1) Key concepts and definitions (current understanding)

#### 1.1 Heat shock transcription factors (HSFs) and heat shock elements (HSEs)
Plant **HSFs** are transcription factors that bind **heat shock elements (HSEs)** in promoters to activate stress-responsive transcription programs, including the induction of **heat shock proteins (HSPs)** and additional transcription factors. (guo2016theplantheat pages 1-2)

HSFs are modular proteins with conserved **N-terminal DNA-binding and oligomerization domains** and more divergent C-terminal regulatory/activation regions. Trimerization is generally required for high-affinity binding to HSEs; an HSE consensus is described as **nGAAnnTTCn** (or the inverse orientation) in Arabidopsis mechanistic work. (ohama2016thetranscriptionalcascade pages 1-5)

#### 1.2 Domain architecture and class A specificity (AHA motifs)
A commonly used plant HSF classification (A/B/C) reflects structural differences in the oligomerization domain region (HR‑A/B) and C‑terminal regulation. For **class A HSFs** (including HSFA1E), the C‑terminal activation region contains **AHA motifs** (aromatic/large hydrophobic/acidic residues) that are important for transactivation and are absent from class B/C HSFs. (guo2016theplantheat pages 1-2, fragkostefanakis2015prospectsofengineering pages 4-6)

#### 1.3 “Basal” vs “acquired” thermotolerance
*Basal thermotolerance* refers to survival/fitness during heat without prior acclimation, while *acquired thermotolerance* refers to improved survival after a nonlethal priming treatment. Arabidopsis **HSFA1s** collectively are required for robust heat-inducible gene expression and both basal and acquired thermotolerance at the organismal level; strong phenotypes emerge most clearly in high-order mutants due to redundancy among HSFA1 paralogs. (liu2011theroleof pages 1-2)

#### 1.4 HSFA1s as “master regulators” and transcriptional cascades
Arabidopsis **HSFA1s (A1a/b/d/e)** sit at the top of a transcriptional hierarchy. Mechanistic work describes HSFA1s initiating a cascade that induces not only HSPs but also downstream TFs such as **HSFA2/HSFA3/HSFA7s, HSFBs, DREB2A**, and **MBF1c**, and identifies amplification motifs (e.g., HSFA1 → DREB2A → HSFA3) that can sustain response dynamics beyond the immediate heat shock. (ohama2016thetranscriptionalcascade pages 1-5, andrasi2021diversityofplant pages 5-6)

### 2) Functional annotation of HSFA1E (primary function, processes, localization)

#### 2.1 Primary molecular function: sequence-specific transcriptional activation
HSFA1E is not an enzyme or transporter; its primary function is **DNA-binding transcription factor activity**. It is expected (by HSF family structure/function and class A features) to bind **HSEs** in target promoters and activate transcription of heat- and stress-responsive genes. (guo2016theplantheat pages 1-2, ohama2016thetranscriptionalcascade pages 1-5)

#### 2.2 Biological processes and pathway placement
**Heat stress response:** HSFA1 factors are master regulators of the Arabidopsis HSR and are necessary for induction of a large fraction of heat-upregulated genes; transcriptome analysis summarized in a key review reports that **>65%** of heat stress–upregulated genes are HSFA1-dependent (measured in a quadruple mutant genetic framework). (liu2011theroleof pages 1-2)

**Broader abiotic stress integration:** The HSFA1 group regulates gene expression programs that overlap with oxidative, osmotic, and salt stress responses, and high-order hsfa1 mutants show sensitivity to **H2O2**, **mannitol**, and **NaCl** challenges, indicating functional cross-talk beyond canonical heat stress. (liu2011theroleof pages 1-2, liu2013commonanddistinct pages 6-8)

#### 2.3 Subcellular localization and activation cycle (current model)
HSFA1-type factors are often described as cycling between inactive and active states regulated by chaperones. A major model is that inactive HSFs are restrained via associations with **HSP70/HSP90** under non-stress conditions, and heat-induced protein misfolding titrates chaperones, releasing HSFs to enable nuclear accumulation, trimerization, and transcriptional activation. (andrasi2021diversityofplant pages 5-6)

A detailed mechanistic analysis in Arabidopsis identifies a repression region in HSFA1d that mediates **HSP70/HSP90 repression** and dissociation upon heat, situating HSFA1 proteins at the interface of proteostasis and transcriptional reprogramming; this provides the best mechanistic framework for placing HSFA1E within an analogous regulatory architecture. (ohama2016thetranscriptionalcascade pages 1-5)

**Visual evidence:** a working model summarizing the chaperone-regulated HSFA1 activation and transcriptional cascade is shown in Figure 9 of Ohama et al. (Plant Cell, 2016). (ohama2016thetranscriptionalcascade media 311e6a00)

#### 2.4 HSFA1E-specific experimental evidence (what is known)
Because HSFA1 paralogs are partly redundant, HSFA1E-specific evidence is often derived from **comparative mutant sets** rather than single-gene analyses.

**(i) Cytosolic protein interaction with Hsc70-1 (non-heat conditions):** In Arabidopsis, Hsc70-1 physically interacts with **HsfA1d and HsfA1e in the cytosol under non-heat stress**, consistent with chaperone-mediated regulation occurring before stress. (tiwari2020athsc70‐1negativelyregulates pages 1-6)

**(ii) Transactivation of the Hsp101 promoter:** In transient reporter assays, **HsfA1e** (together with HsfA1d and HsfA2) shows transcriptional activation of the **Hsp101** promoter. This links HSFA1E to regulation of a major chaperone effector implicated in thermotolerance. (tiwari2020athsc70‐1negativelyregulates pages 1-6)

**(iii) Regulation of downstream TFs (DREB2A and HSFA2):** Mutant comparisons implicate HsfA1e in regulation of the broader TF cascade.
- In an HSFA1e-linked mutant background (eTK), **thermotolerance decreases**, and **heat-inducible expression of DREB2A is suppressed**, suggesting a specific role for HSFA1E in DREB2A-associated signaling under certain contexts. (yabuta2016functionsofheat pages 3-4)
- Independent work indicates HsfA1d/e are involved in the transcriptional regulation of **HsfA2**; HsfA2 expression is reduced in hsfa1d/e-related genetic backgrounds. (tiwari2020athsc70‐1negativelyregulates pages 1-6)

**(iv) Stress phenotype partitioning across HSFA1 paralogs:** A systematic comparison of HSFA1 triple-mutant combinations indicates that HSFA1a/1b/1d are more pivotal than HSFA1e for some outputs (e.g., chronic thermotolerance at ~27–29°C), while HSFA1e contributes variably to oxidative stress tolerance. (liu2013commonanddistinct pages 6-8)

**(v) Warm-temperature development (thermomorphogenesis) and a recent (2023) update:** Warm daytime temperature responses involve HSFA1 accumulation/nuclear translocation and interaction with PIF4 to stabilize PIF4; however, a 2023 Science Advances study reports that PIF4 interacts with some HSFA1 factors (HSFA1a/HSFA1b) **but not HSFA1e**, and notes HSFA1e is less important for “heat response” in that context. This points to **subfunctionalization** among A1 paralogs and suggests HSFA1E is not a central component of the HSFA1–PIF4 thermomorphogenesis module. (tan2023theheatresponse pages 1-2)

### 3) Recent developments (prioritizing 2023–2024)

#### 3.1 HSFA1s in thermomorphogenesis (2023)
The 2023 Science Advances work expands HSFA1 biology from heat-shock survival to **daytime thermomorphogenesis**, where HSFA1s act in warm daytime conditions to stabilize PIF4 by disrupting phyB–PIF4 interactions and promoting nuclear accumulation of HSFA1d under BIN2 control. HSFA1E appears to be **non-interacting with PIF4** in the reported interaction tests, highlighting functional divergence within the HSFA1 clade. (tan2023theheatresponse pages 1-2)

#### 3.2 HSFs as a “molecular rheostat” (2024 review synthesis)
A 2024 New Phytologist review updates the conceptual framing of plant HSFs as a dynamic “**molecular rheostat**” that tunes response intensity and recovery rather than a simple ON/OFF switch. It highlights regulatory layers that are directly relevant for functional annotation and experimental design: post-translational modifications (e.g., phosphorylation), alternative splicing (including unproductive isoforms regulated by NMD), and cofactor/chaperone complexes (e.g., ROF1–HSP90 with HSFA2). These mechanisms contextualize how HSFA1-class factors might integrate proteostasis state with transcriptional outputs. (bakery2024heatstresstranscription pages 5-6)

### 4) Current applications and real-world implementations

#### 4.1 Engineering stress tolerance via HSFA1 networks (translational rationale)
Engineering thermotolerance has been proposed through modulation of HSF/HSP networks, leveraging the master-regulator position of HsfA1 and the cascade it triggers. This includes strategies based on manipulating A1 and A2 HSF activities and their complexes. (fragkostefanakis2015prospectsofengineering pages 4-6)

#### 4.2 Concrete implementation examples (A1 paralog, Arabidopsis)
While HSFA1E itself is less frequently used as a transgene target, **A1 paralog manipulation** provides proof-of-principle for the class.

**HSFA1b overexpression and productivity traits:** Overexpression of Arabidopsis **HSFA1b** (a close paralog in the same A1 clade as HSFA1E) enhances **water productivity and harvest index** under well-watered and water-limiting conditions, and increases resistance to drought and infection, illustrating that A1 HSF manipulation can influence whole-plant performance traits relevant to agriculture. In this study, **509 genes** were differentially expressed in non-stressed HSFA1b-OE plants; **55/509** had an HSE variant (HSE1b) in promoters, and in vivo binding to HSE1b was confirmed. (bechtold2013arabidopsisheatshock pages 1-2)

**HSFA1b regulatory network quantification:** A follow-up multi-omics analysis identified **952 differentially expressed genes** that were also HSFA1b targets (ChIP/RNA-seq integration), plus **1780** differentially expressed genes not directly bound (indirect regulation), and **480** bound cisNAT genes, indicating the breadth of direct and indirect control exerted by A1 HSFs. (albihlal2018arabidopsisheatshock pages 1-4)

These A1-paralog results are relevant to HSFA1E annotation because they support a general principle: **class A1 HSFs function as transcriptional hubs** whose manipulation can reprogram stress and development networks; however, subfunctionalization (e.g., limited role of HSFA1E in some heat outputs) suggests paralog choice matters for applications. (liu2013commonanddistinct pages 1-2, tan2023theheatresponse pages 1-2)

### 5) Relevant statistics and quantitative data (from recent and foundational studies)

- **>65% of heat stress–upregulated genes** are **HSFA1-dependent** based on Arabidopsis transcriptome analyses summarized in a highly cited HSFA1 review (quadruple mutant genetic framework). (liu2011theroleof pages 1-2)
- Stress assay conditions used to dissect HSFA1 contributions include **125 mM NaCl**, **300 mM mannitol**, and **5 mM H2O2** during seedling establishment; QK mutants show substantially increased severe-stunting frequency under these stresses. (liu2013commonanddistinct pages 6-8)
- HSFA1b overexpression alters **509 genes** (non-stress); **55** of these contain an HSE variant (HSE1b). (bechtold2013arabidopsisheatshock pages 1-2)
- HSFA1b network integration identified **952** differentially expressed HSFA1b-targeted genes, **1780** additional indirectly affected genes, and **480** bound cisNAT genes. (albihlal2018arabidopsisheatshock pages 1-4)

### 6) Expert opinion and analysis (authoritative synthesis)

1. **Redundancy and subfunctionalization are central to interpreting HSFA1E function.** High-order genetics show HSFA1s are essential master regulators, but comparative mutant sets and newer functional studies indicate HSFA1E can be less central for some traits (e.g., chronic thermotolerance and PIF4-linked thermomorphogenesis), suggesting functional partitioning across A1 paralogs. (liu2013commonanddistinct pages 6-8, tan2023theheatresponse pages 1-2)

2. **HSFA1E is best annotated as an upstream transcriptional activator that contributes to proteostasis-linked transcriptional control,** including regulation of major chaperone systems and second-wave TF cascades (HsfA2, DREB2A), with regulation constrained by chaperone associations in the cytosol and activation upon stress. The strongest HSFA1E-specific mechanistic hooks in the retrieved corpus are its **Hsc70-1 interaction** and its **ability to activate the Hsp101 promoter**. (tiwari2020athsc70‐1negativelyregulates pages 1-6, ohama2016thetranscriptionalcascade media 311e6a00)

3. **Modern (2024) models emphasize dynamic tuning and recovery.** The “molecular rheostat” framing highlights the need to capture both activation and attenuation mechanisms (PTMs, splicing, cofactor complexes) when designing experiments or crop-engineering interventions involving HSFA1-family regulators. (bakery2024heatstresstranscription pages 5-6)

### 7) Summary functional annotation (recommended)

**Gene/protein:** HSFA1E / AtHsfA1e (Arabidopsis thaliana; class A1 HSF). (liu2013commonanddistinct pages 1-2, liu2011theroleof pages 1-2)

**Primary function:** Sequence-specific **transcriptional activator** that binds HSEs and initiates stress-response transcriptional programs; part of the HSFA1 master-regulator set. (guo2016theplantheat pages 1-2, liu2011theroleof pages 1-2)

**Key pathways/processes:** Heat stress response (basal and acquired thermotolerance), oxidative/osmotic/salt stress cross-talk, induction of downstream TF cascades (notably DREB2A and HSFA2), and regulation of thermotolerance effectors such as HSP101. (tiwari2020athsc70‐1negativelyregulates pages 1-6, yabuta2016functionsofheat pages 3-4, liu2011theroleof pages 1-2)

**Subcellular site of action:** Ultimately the **nucleus** as a transcription factor; evidence indicates cytosolic association with chaperone systems (e.g., Hsc70-1 interaction) under non-stress and stress-triggered activation consistent with HSFA1 chaperone-release models. (tiwari2020athsc70‐1negativelyregulates pages 1-6, andrasi2021diversityofplant pages 5-6)

**Notable specialization:** HSFA1E appears **less important** than other A1 paralogs for certain outputs such as chronic thermotolerance and PIF4-mediated thermomorphogenesis; PIF4 interaction tests report no interaction with HSFA1E. (liu2013commonanddistinct pages 6-8, tan2023theheatresponse pages 1-2)

### Limitations of the current evidence base for HSFA1E specifically
Most mechanistic and systems-level datasets in the retrieved corpus focus on HSFA1s collectively or on other A1 paralogs (notably HSFA1b, HSFA1d, and HSFA1a). HSFA1E-specific functional dissection is therefore best supported here by: (i) mutant-comparison evidence (eTK; hsfa1d/e), and (ii) chaperone-interaction/regulation studies that explicitly include HsfA1e.
