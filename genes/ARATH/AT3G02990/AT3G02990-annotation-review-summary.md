# HSFA1E (AT3G02990) GO Annotation Review Summary

## Current Review Position

HSFA1E is reviewed conservatively as a class A heat shock transcription factor with
supported DNA-binding transcription factor activity, nuclear/cytoplasmic localization,
and a broad non-core heat-response annotation. The current YAML does not propose new
GO annotations for osmotic, salt, oxidative, or cold stress because the local evidence
does not support HSFA1E-specific additions at that strength.

## Existing Annotation Decisions

- DNA-binding and transcription factor annotations are retained as core molecular
  functions, supported by the conserved HSF DNA-binding domain, UniProt functional
  summary, IBA/IEA/ISS annotations, and Falcon synthesis.
- Nuclear and cytoplasmic localization annotations are retained. PMID:21931939 is
  treated as group-level HsfA1 support for nuclear accumulation rather than as
  uniquely strong HSFA1E-specific localization evidence.
- `GO:0034605 cellular response to heat` is retained as non-core. HSFA1E belongs to
  the HsfA1 family and can contribute to heat-responsive transcription, but available
  evidence supports HSFA1A/B/D as the main thermotolerance regulators.

## Proposed New Terms

No new GO terms are proposed in the current review. Earlier draft claims that HSFA1E
has primary osmotic or salt stress functions, or secondary oxidative/cold stress
functions, were not retained because they were not supported well enough by the
available gene-specific evidence.

## Core Function

The core function represented in the YAML is DNA-binding transcription factor activity
from the nucleus, with regulation of stress-responsive transcription as the biological
process context. Broader stress phenotypes remain questions for future expert review
unless additional HSFA1E-specific experimental evidence is added.
