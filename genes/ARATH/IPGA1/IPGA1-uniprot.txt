CC   -!- FUNCTION: Microtubule-associated protein involved in the regulation of
CC       anisotropic petal and cotyledons growth and shape by affecting cortical
CC       microtubule organization (PubMed:31198941, PubMed:31623377,
CC       PubMed:35975703). Prevents cortical microtubules organization into
CC       parallel arrays oriented perpendicular to the axis of cell elongation
CC       thus limiting anisotropic cell growth in the late phases of petal
CC       development (PubMed:31198941, PubMed:31623377, PubMed:35975703).
CC       Cooperatively with ANGUSTIFOLIA (AN), negatively regulates cortical
CC       microtubules (CMTs) organization in response to mechanical stress and
CC       modulates pavement cells morphogenesis leading to puzzle shape,
CC       probably in an AAA1/KTN1-dependent manner (PubMed:35975703).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:31198941, ECO:0000269|PubMed:31623377,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:35975703}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Associates to cortical microtubules via its N-terminal region
CC       (PubMed:31198941, PubMed:35975703). Interacts with ANGUSTIFOLIA (AN) on
CC       microtubule upon mechanical stress to regulate microtubule organization
CC       (PubMed:35975703). Binds to the microtubule-severing enzyme KATANIN
CC       (KTN1) (PubMed:35975703). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:31198941,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:35975703}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cytoplasm, cytoskeleton
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:31198941, ECO:0000269|PubMed:35975703}. Cytoplasm,
CC       cytosol {ECO:0000269|PubMed:35975703}. Cell membrane
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:35975703}; Peripheral membrane protein
CC       {ECO:0000305}; Cytoplasmic side {ECO:0000305}. Note=Colocalizes with
CC       cortical microtubules and binds directly to microtubules
CC       (PubMed:31198941, PubMed:35975703). In young pavement cells, observed
CC       as granules in the cytosol and at the cell periphery (PubMed:35975703).
CC       Formation to granules on microtubules is promoted by mechanical
CC       perturbations (PubMed:35975703). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:31198941,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:35975703}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Expressed ubiquitously at all development stages,
CC       with highest in developing petals (PubMed:31198941). During mechanical
CC       stress, accumulates in granules on microtubules (PubMed:35975703).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:31198941, ECO:0000269|PubMed:35975703}.
CC   -!- DEVELOPMENTAL STAGE: Highest levels observed in petals during late
CC       developmental stages. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:31198941}.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: Reduced interdigitated growth of pavement cells,
CC       with smooth shape (PubMed:35975703). Long and narrow petals and
CC       cotyledons, as well as increased anisotropic cell expansion of petal
CC       epidermis in the late phases of flower development, as a result of
CC       increased microtubule ordering in petal abaxial epidermal cells and in
CC       cotyledons pavement cells (PubMed:31198941, PubMed:31623377,
CC       PubMed:35975703). Enhanced cortical microtubules (CMTs) response to
CC       mechanical stress leading to aligned arrays (PubMed:35975703). The an-
CC       t1 ipga1-2 double mutant exhibits stronger phenotypes than single
CC       mutants in term of longer and narrower cotyledons and leaves
CC       (PubMed:35975703). The ipga1-2 ktn1-4 double mutant has an enhanced
CC       phenotype of pavement cells, resembling soap bubbles and no lobe
CC       formation (PubMed:35975703). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:31198941,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:31623377, ECO:0000269|PubMed:35975703}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the IPGA1 family. {ECO:0000305}.
