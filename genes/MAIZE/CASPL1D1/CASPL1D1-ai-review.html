Gene Description

CASP-like protein 1D1 (ZmCASPL1D1) is a small tetraspan plasma-membrane protein of the CASP-like (CASPL) subfamily (CASPL1B/1D clade) of the MARVEL-related Casparian strip membrane domain protein family. Arabidopsis members of this clade (including AtCASPL1D1/1D2) are expressed in suberized endodermal cells, interact with the aquaporin PIP2;1, and weakly modulate suberization; ZmCASPL1D1 itself is uncharacterized.

Comprehensive Research Report: Maize CASP-like protein (UniProt B6U361; reported as ZmCASPL1D1)

0) Executive summary

The maize protein designated by UniProt accession B6U361 is annotated by UniProt (per user-provided context) as “CASP-like protein 1D1 (ZmCASPL1D1)” and belongs to the Casparian strip membrane domain protein (CASP) / CASP-like (CASPL) family. The most strongly supported, evidence-based functional annotation for B6U361—based on recent plant CASP/CASPL literature and maize family genomics—is that it is a multi-pass plasma-membrane scaffold/organizer protein likely involved in lignified apoplastic barrier formation (Casparian-strip–type membrane domains) and associated ion/nutrient homeostasis and stress responses, rather than an enzyme or transporter with a defined substrate. However, direct gene-specific experimental evidence for B6U361 itself (mutants, localization imaging, biochemical mechanism) was not retrieved in the accessible corpus; thus, annotation must rely primarily on family-level mechanistic evidence and maize CASPL-family expression/stress datasets from 2024. (xue2024genomewideidentificationand pages 1-2, xue2024genomewideidentificationand pages 6-9, xue2024comparativeanalysisof pages 1-2)

A critical complication is that an independently retrieved cross-species CASPL listing mapped B6U361 to “ZmCASPL1D2”, not “ZmCASPL1D1,” indicating symbol ambiguity in the literature-derived naming. This report therefore treats B6U361 as the primary identity anchor and flags the gene symbol as potentially inconsistent across sources. (venado2022lotusjaponicusnatural pages 131-133)

2) Key concepts and definitions (current understanding)

2.1 Casparian strip (CS) and diffusion barriers

The Casparian strip is a lignin-based apoplastic diffusion barrier deposited between root endodermal cells that regulates radial movement of water and ions. CASP/CASPL proteins are central to organizing the specialized membrane domain associated with this barrier. (xue2024genomewideidentificationand pages 1-2, xue2024comparativeanalysisof pages 1-2)

2.2 CASP vs CASPL proteins

  • CASPs (canonical) were initially characterized in Arabidopsis as a small, partially redundant set (e.g., CASP1–CASP5) involved in CS formation. (xue2024genomewideidentificationand pages 2-4)
  • CASPLs (CASP-like proteins) form larger families across plants and are often discussed together with CASPs because they share the defining CASP/DUF588 features and membrane topology; they can participate in CS-like barrier processes or other cell-wall domain specializations. (xue2024genomewideidentificationand pages 1-2, xue2024comparativeanalysisof pages 1-2)

2.3 Protein architecture/topology and what it implies

CASP/CASPL proteins are described as having four transmembrane domains, two intracellular loops, one extracellular loop, and cytosolic N- and C-termini. Such architecture is most consistent with a scaffold/organizer function in membrane domain assembly (not an enzyme active site or a transporter pore with defined substrate specificity). (xue2024genomewideidentificationand pages 1-2, venado2022lotusjaponicusnatural pages 91-94)

2.4 Mechanistic role in lignin polymerization machinery (family-level)

Recent comparative analyses summarize that CASP-family proteins help recruit and organize lignin polymerization and CS assembly components, including RBOHF, ESB1, PER64, and UCC1, and that MYB36 is a key transcriptional regulator connected to CS gene programs (including CASP/CASPL genes and lignin polymerization factors). (xue2024comparativeanalysisof pages 1-2, xue2024genomewideidentificationand pages 1-2)

3) What is known specifically in maize (2023–2024 prioritized)

3.1 Most relevant recent maize source: genome-wide CASPL family analysis (2024)

Xue et al., 2024 (Frontiers in Plant Science; published Oct 2024) performed a whole-genome identification and expression analysis of the maize CASPL family. URL: https://doi.org/10.3389/fpls.2024.1477383. (xue2024genomewideidentificationand pages 1-2)

Key results relevant to annotating maize CASPL proteins (including B6U361 by inference):
- Family size: 47 maize CASPL genes (ZmCASPL1–ZmCASPL47) were identified and grouped into six clades, with expansion driven by whole-genome duplication and tandem duplication. (xue2024genomewideidentificationand pages 1-2, xue2024genomewideidentificationand pages 15-16)
- Domain/motif features: a large majority (~72%) of maize CASPL proteins contain CASP-domain features; several include MARVEL-like domains; and one member includes CASP plus another domain (PHA00427). (xue2024genomewideidentificationand pages 4-6)
- Organ expression: RNA-seq across nine organs/tissues indicated that 43/47 ZmCASPL genes are expressed in roots, consistent with barrier-related functions being prominent in roots, while other members show expression in anther/ear/leaf tip and some were not detected in the surveyed tissues. (xue2024genomewideidentificationand pages 6-9)

3.2 Maize expression statistics and stress responsiveness (quantitative highlights)

From the 2024 maize analysis:
- Root expression prevalence: 43 of 47 ZmCASPL genes were expressed in roots, and 14 had high root expression; ZmCASPL21 and ZmCASPL47 were described as particularly root-specific/high. (xue2024genomewideidentificationand pages 6-9)
- Drought response: multiple ZmCASPL genes were drought-responsive; RT-qPCR validation reported that ZmCASPL13/25/44 showed ~2.46–4.54-fold induction under drought-mimic treatment (PEG). (xue2024genomewideidentificationand pages 6-9, xue2024genomewideidentificationand pages 9-13)
- Salt response: under NaCl, RT-qPCR validation indicated ZmCASPL13 and ZmCASPL25 were up-regulated, whereas ZmCASPL5 and ZmCASPL44 were down-regulated. (xue2024genomewideidentificationand pages 9-13)
- Biotic stress patterns: time-varying responses were observed under fungal and viral challenges, and some genes showed opposite regulation patterns under different pathogens. (xue2024genomewideidentificationand pages 9-13)

Important limitation: these maize statistics are for numbered ZmCASPL genes; due to absent mapping, they cannot be directly assigned to B6U361 without additional identifier cross-reference. (xue2024genomewideidentificationand pages 2-4, xue2024genomewideidentificationand pages 15-16)

4) Inferred functional annotation for B6U361 (CASPL1D-type)

4.1 Primary molecular function (best-supported)

No evidence in the retrieved corpus supports that B6U361 catalyzes a reaction (enzyme) or transports a defined substrate (transporter). Based on CASP/CASPL family knowledge, the most defensible primary function is:
- Plasma membrane scaffold/organizer of Casparian-strip–type membrane domains that facilitate localized lignin deposition and formation of a lignin-based diffusion barrier, impacting nutrient/ion homeostasis. (xue2024comparativeanalysisof pages 1-2, xue2024genomewideidentificationand pages 1-2)

4.2 Subcellular localization (best-supported)

Family-level evidence places CASP/CASPL proteins at the plasma membrane, specifically within the Casparian strip membrane domain of endodermal cells. While some CASPLs may have broader plasma membrane localization or mislocalize depending on conserved residues, the dominant model is plasma membrane–domain localization associated with barrier formation. (xue2024comparativeanalysisof pages 1-2, venado2022lotusjaponicusnatural pages 91-94)

4.3 Biological processes and pathways

The relevant pathway context includes:
- Endodermal differentiation and barrier formation programs regulated by transcriptional networks (e.g., MYB36) that coordinate CASP/CASPL expression with lignin polymerization machinery. (xue2024comparativeanalysisof pages 1-2, xue2024genomewideidentificationand pages 1-2)
- Abiotic and biotic stress adaptation via modulation of barrier properties, which can affect ion exclusion/uptake and pathogen restriction; maize CASPL family expression data show broad responsiveness to drought, salinity, nutrient stress, and pathogens. (xue2024genomewideidentificationand pages 6-9, xue2024genomewideidentificationand pages 9-13)

5) Recent developments and latest research (2023–2024 emphasis)

5.1 2024: Maize CASPL family becomes systematically cataloged

The 2024 Frontiers in Plant Science study provides the first maize-focused, systematic CASPL family catalog with organ-level expression, stress responsiveness, promoter cis-element predictions (including MYB-binding motifs), and co-expression analysis—creating a foundation to prioritize specific maize CASPL targets for functional genetics and breeding-oriented studies. (xue2024genomewideidentificationand pages 1-2, xue2024genomewideidentificationand pages 6-9, xue2024genomewideidentificationand pages 9-13)

5.2 2024: Comparative CASP/CASPL synthesis across species

A 2024 comparative analysis in International Journal of Molecular Sciences reinforces a current consensus model in which CASP/CASPL proteins are integral components of Casparian strip membrane domains that help recruit lignin-polymerization machinery and regulate ion uptake/homeostasis; it highlights DUF588/PF04535 as a key diagnostic signature and emphasizes root/endodermis enrichment patterns across species. URL: https://doi.org/10.3390/ijms25189858 (published Sep 2024). (xue2024comparativeanalysisof pages 1-2)

6) Applications and real-world implementations

Although no application is reported specifically for B6U361, CASP/CASPL biology has clear translational relevance in crops:
- Stress resilience and nutrient use efficiency: Because the Casparian strip and related apoplastic barriers control radial transport of water and ions, manipulating barrier formation (including CASP/CASPL pathways) is a plausible strategy to alter nutrient homeostasis and stress tolerance. The maize CASPL family’s stress-responsive expression (drought/salt/nutrient deficiency/pathogens) supports its relevance to agronomic traits and suggests candidate genes for functional testing and breeding. (xue2024genomewideidentificationand pages 6-9, xue2024genomewideidentificationand pages 9-13)

7) Expert opinion and authoritative analysis (evidence-based synthesis)

Recent authoritative syntheses converge on the view that CASP/CASPL proteins are not lignin biosynthetic enzymes themselves, but rather membrane-domain organizers that spatially constrain and coordinate the cell-wall polymerization machinery needed for lignin-based diffusion barriers (Casparian strip and CS-like structures). This “membrane scaffold guiding localized wall polymerization” model is consistent across the 2024 maize family analysis (conceptual framing) and the 2024 comparative CASP-family synthesis (mechanistic component lists and regulatory context). (xue2024genomewideidentificationand pages 1-2, xue2024comparativeanalysisof pages 1-2)
