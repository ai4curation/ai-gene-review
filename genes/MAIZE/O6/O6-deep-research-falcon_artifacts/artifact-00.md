| Names / aliases | Target identity matches **Zea mays** endosperm RIP known as **albumin b-32**, **b-32**, **RIP1**, **proRIP1**, and literature links it with the **opaque-6 (O6)** context; UniProt P10593 description is therefore consistent with maize b-32/opaque-6 literature rather than an unrelated O6 symbol. | (pqac-00000000, pqac-00000002, pqac-00000006) |
| Protein family / type | Member of the **ribosome-inactivating protein (RIP)** family. More specifically, maize b-32 is a **cereal type-3 RIP / proenzyme-like RIP**, because it is synthesized as an inactive precursor that requires proteolytic activation, unlike canonical constitutively active type-1 RIPs. | (pqac-00000000, pqac-00000006, pqac-00000011) |
| Catalytic activity & reaction | Function is **rRNA N-glycosidase / adenine polynucleotide glycosylase** activity: RIPs hydrolyze the N-glycosidic bond of a conserved adenine in large-subunit rRNA, irreversibly inactivating ribosomes and blocking translation factor function. Recent reviews summarize the reaction as depurination of the sarcin-ricin loop adenine (A4324 numbering in mammalian 28S rRNA). Reviews: Zaeytijd & Van Damme 2017, *Toxins*, DOI: https://doi.org/10.3390/toxins9040123; Kocyigit et al. 2023, *Toxins*, DOI: https://doi.org/10.3390/toxins15060356; Iglesias et al. 2024, *Toxins*, DOI: https://doi.org/10.3390/toxins16040192. | (pqac-00000001, pqac-00000010, pqac-00000011, pqac-00000013) |
| Target site / substrate specificity | Primary target is the **sarcin-ricin loop (SRL)** in large-subunit rRNA; this disables GTPase-associated translation steps. Cereal RIPs, including maize RIP, show unusual specificity: reviews note **weak or absent activity on plant/maize ribosomes in some assays**, while remaining potent on heterologous eukaryotic systems, consistent with a defense-oriented or highly regulated substrate profile. | (pqac-00000001, pqac-00000002, pqac-00000010) |
| Activation mechanism | Native maize b-32 accumulates as an inactive **single-chain zymogen**. During germination, endogenous proteases remove an **N-terminal segment (~16 aa), an internal acidic/inactivation region (~25 aa), and a C-terminal segment (~12-14 aa)**, producing two non-covalently associated active chains (~16.5 and ~8.5 kDa). The internal region is the main suppressor of activity; papain/subtilisin can mimic activation in vitro. | (pqac-00000000, pqac-00000006, pqac-00000015, pqac-00000017) |
| Subcellular localization | Evidence supports a **cytosolic, non-secretory** localization in maize endosperm: b-32 is described as a **cytosolic albumin**, “not secreted via the endoplasmic reticulum,” and cereal RIPs generally **lack signal peptides**, implying cytosolic residence. No strong evidence for a specific organellar targeting route in native maize endosperm RIP1. | (pqac-00000015, pqac-00000016, pqac-00000018, pqac-00000021) |
| Expression context | Expressed predominantly in **maize endosperm/seed**, coordinated with storage-protein deposition, and can reach **~1% of total seed proteins**. Expression is under the control of the **Opaque-2 (O2)** transcriptional program; b-32 levels are greatly reduced in **o2** mutants. Maize also has at least one additional non-allelic RIP form expressed in leaves. | (pqac-00000000, pqac-00000001, pqac-00000002, pqac-00000015) |
| Biological roles | Best-supported roles are **seed defense** and **seed biology/storage-associated function**. Loss of b-32 in opaque mutants correlates with higher susceptibility to fungal attack and insect feeding; reviews also place cereal RIPs in broader roles including defense against fungi/herbivores, storage, senescence, stress adaptation, and translational reprogramming. | (pqac-00000000, pqac-00000003, pqac-00000004, pqac-00000013) |
| Quantitative activity | Activated αβ-RIP inhibits translation in rabbit reticulocyte lysate with **IC50 ~28–66 pM** and is **~10,000-fold more active** than proRIP; engineered removal of the internal inactivation region yields a **~650-fold** activity increase versus proRIP. Reviews on RIPs more broadly report cell-free translation IC50 values around **0.015–3.5 nM** and much higher toxicity for type-2 RIPs than type-1/type-3 RIPs. | (pqac-00000000, pqac-00000006, pqac-00000008, pqac-00000017) |
| Transgenic / biotech applications | Maize b-32 has been used experimentally for **crop protection**: transgenic tobacco showed increased tolerance to **Rhizoctonia solani**; transgenic wheat expressing b-32 showed reduced **Fusarium head blight** and damaged kernels; ectopic maize expression reduced **Fusarium verticillioides** symptoms in leaf assays. Recent RIP reviews highlight broader use of RIPs in pathogen-resistant transgenic crops and as payloads for biomedical conjugates. Reviews: Citores & Ferreras 2023, *Toxins*, DOI: https://doi.org/10.3390/toxins15010035; Iglesias et al. 2024, *Toxins*, DOI: https://doi.org/10.3390/toxins16040192. | (pqac-00000001, pqac-00000004, pqac-00000008, pqac-00000012) |
| Recent developments (2023-2024) | No major 2023-2024 primary paper appears specific to **maize O6/b-32** itself in the retrieved evidence, but recent authoritative reviews updated the field: RIPs are being evaluated for **antifungal crop protection**, **transgenic resistance engineering**, and **therapeutic/immunotoxin** applications. A 2023 antiviral study showed type-1 RIPs MAP30/Momordin inhibited SARS-CoV-2 with **IC50 ~0.2 μM** and **CC50 ~2 μM**, illustrating current biomedical interest in RIP catalytic scaffolds. Reviews/primary papers: Citores & Ferreras 2023, *Toxins*, https://doi.org/10.3390/toxins15010035; Kocyigit et al. 2023, *Toxins*, https://doi.org/10.3390/toxins15060356; Iglesias et al. 2024, *Toxins*, https://doi.org/10.3390/toxins16040192; Watts et al. 2023, *PLOS ONE*, https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pone.0286370. | (pqac-00000008, pqac-00000011, pqac-00000012, pqac-00000013) |
| Key caveats / gaps | Gene-specific literature for **O6** is limited and often embedded within older **b-32/RIP1** or opaque-mutant studies rather than modern gene-centric work. Much mechanistic detail comes from classic maize RIP studies and later reviews; recent 2023-2024 sources are mostly **RIP-family reviews**, not new O6-focused experiments. Substrate specificity toward native maize ribosomes remains context-dependent across assays, and some defense effects may depend on **proteolytic activation state** and tissue context. | (pqac-00000002, pqac-00000010, pqac-00000018) |
