Gene Description

GlcE encodes a subunit of the glycolate dehydrogenase complex (EC 1.1.99.14) in Cupriavidus necator, a chemolithoautotrophic bacterium. This enzyme catalyzes the initial oxidation of glycolate to glyoxylate, which is central to glycolate metabolism and phosphoglycolate salvage pathways. The protein functions as part of the glcDEF operon, where it operates in concert with other subunits to form a multi-component enzyme complex essential for carbon assimilation under autotrophic conditions. Deletion studies demonstrate that glcE is critical for efficient growth on glycolate-based substrates and maintaining metabolic homeostasis during carbon utilization.
