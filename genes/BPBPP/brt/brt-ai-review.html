Gene Description

Brt encodes a reverse transcriptase that drives the diversity-generating retroelement (DGR) system in Bordetella phage BPP-1. This RT catalyzes targeted hypermutation during tropism switching, enabling the phage to alter its host receptor specificity. Through target-primed reverse transcription with adenine-specific mutagenesis, Brt generates extraordinary sequence diversity (up to 10^12 variants) in the major tropism determinant (Mtd) protein, the phage tail fiber receptor-binding protein. This mutagenic homing mechanism allows rapid adaptation to different Bordetella host phases regulated by the BvgAS system, representing a unique retroelement function distinct from viral replication or retrotransposition.
