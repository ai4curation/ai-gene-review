Gene Description

A0A6I8TLE4 is a predicted Ras GTPase-activating protein (RasGAP) in Aedes aegypti, classified by PANTHER as an ortholog of the Drosophila raskol gene (PTHR10194:SF60). The protein is 1442 amino acids and contains a conserved multi-domain architecture characteristic of the DAB2IP/SynGAP-like RasGAP subfamily: a RasGAP catalytic domain (IPR001936), a C2 domain (IPR000008, SynGAP-like subtype per CDD cd04013), a PH domain (IPR001849, SynGAP-like subtype per CDD cd13262), and a DAB2P C-terminal domain (IPR021887). The RasGAP domain catalyzes stimulation of the intrinsic GTPase activity of Ras-family small GTPases, converting them from the active GTP-bound state to the inactive GDP-bound state, thereby functioning as a negative regulator of Ras signaling. The C2 and PH domains are predicted to mediate calcium-dependent and phosphoinositide-dependent membrane targeting, respectively, enabling recruitment to specific membrane compartments where Ras signaling occurs. The DAB2P_C domain is associated with scaffold/adaptor functions in related proteins such as human DAB2IP. No direct experimental characterization of this specific gene product has been published; functional annotation is based on conserved domain architecture, PANTHER subfamily classification, and orthology to characterized RasGAPs.

Core Functions

Predicted Ras GTPase-activating protein that stimulates the intrinsic GTPase activity of Ras-family small GTPases, accelerating hydrolysis of GTP to GDP and thereby inactivating Ras signaling. This is the canonical function of all RasGAP domain-containing proteins and is strongly supported by the conserved RasGAP domain (IPR001936, Pfam PF00616), the RasGAP catalytic signature (PROSITE PS00509), and the PANTHER subfamily assignment to raskol (PTHR10194:SF60). The CDD match to cd05136 (RasGAP_DAB2IP) further specifies this as a DAB2IP-subfamily RasGAP.
