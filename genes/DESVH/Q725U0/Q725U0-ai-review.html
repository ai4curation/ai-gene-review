Gene Description

DVU_3335 (Q725U0) is a membrane-associated two-component system (TCS) sensor histidine kinase in Desulfovibrio vulgaris Hildenborough (now Nitratidesulfovibrio vulgaris). The protein contains two predicted transmembrane helices (residues 14-36 and 148-170), a PAS sensory domain (residues 238-283), and a C-terminal histidine kinase catalytic core comprising a dimerization/histidine phosphotransfer (DHp/HisKA) domain and a HATPase_C domain (residues 363-578). It catalyzes ATP-dependent autophosphorylation on a conserved histidine residue and subsequent phosphotransfer to a cognate response regulator. The specific environmental signal detected and the cognate response regulator remain uncharacterized. Based on domain architecture and organism-level TCS studies, it likely participates in adaptive regulation tied to nutrient availability, stress sensing, or biofilm-related behaviors.

Key concepts and definitions
- Two-component histidine kinase (HK): membrane-associated or cytosolic sensor enzymes that autophosphorylate a conserved histidine residue using ATP and transfer the phosphoryl group to an aspartate on a cognate response regulator (RR), modulating transcription or behavior. The catalytic core typically comprises a dimerization and histidine phosphotransfer (DHp/HisKA) domain and a C-terminal HATPase (H-box/N/G motifs) domain.
