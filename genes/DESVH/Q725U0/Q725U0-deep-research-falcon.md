Key concepts and definitions
- Two-component histidine kinase (HK): membrane-associated or cytosolic sensor enzymes that autophosphorylate a conserved histidine residue using ATP and transfer the phosphoryl group to an aspartate on a cognate response regulator (RR), modulating transcription or behavior. The catalytic core typically comprises a dimerization and histidine phosphotransfer (DHp/HisKA) domain and a C-terminal HATPase (H-box/N/G motifs) domain. Some HK-like systems show domain variations (e.g., GHKL-family HATPase_c_3 fusions) that broaden architecture diversity (bellienyrabelo2021noveltwocomponentsystemlike pages 10-11).
- Reaction chemistry (EC 2.7.13.3): ATP-dependent autophosphorylation on histidine followed by phosphotransfer to RR Asp; regulates adaptive responses to environmental cues.
