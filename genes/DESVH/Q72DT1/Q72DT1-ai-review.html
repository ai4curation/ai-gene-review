Gene Description

DVU_0848 (Q72DT1) encodes an HdrA-like flavoprotein that is part of the cytosolic FlxABCD-HdrABC complex in Desulfovibrio vulgaris Hildenborough. This protein contains FAD/NAD-binding domains and 4Fe-4S clusters, functioning as the catalytic bifurcating/confurcating subunit of the Hdr-like complex. The complex couples the exergonic reduction of a heterodisulfide analog (likely involving DsrC) to drive endergonic ferredoxin reduction using NADH (electron bifurcation), or operates in reverse to combine electrons from reduced ferredoxin and NADH (confurcation). This redox-balancing activity is essential for ethanol metabolism and supports sulfate respiration in anaerobic conditions. Note: Despite superficial similarity in name, this is NOT QmoA (which is DVU_0851); this is an HdrA-like protein with distinct function in electron bifurcation.

Proposed New Ontology Terms

electron bifurcating oxidoreductase activity

Definition: Catalysis of an electron bifurcation reaction in which electrons from a mid-potential donor (e.g., NADH) are split such that one electron reduces a high-potential acceptor while the other reduces a low-potential acceptor (e.g., ferredoxin), coupling thermodynamically favorable and unfavorable half-reactions.

Justification: Electron bifurcation is a distinct mechanism of energy conservation in anaerobic microorganisms that is not captured by existing GO terms. HdrA-like proteins are paradigmatic examples of this enzymatic class.

Parent term: oxidoreductase activity
