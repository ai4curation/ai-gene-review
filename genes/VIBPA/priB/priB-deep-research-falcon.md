## Research report: priB (UniProt Q87L73; locus VP2739) — Replication restart protein PriB in *Vibrio parahaemolyticus* RIMD 2210633

### 0) Target verification and scope limitations
The UniProt entry provided (Q87L73) describes a bacterial **replication restart protein PriB** ("primosomal replication protein N") encoded by **priB** in *Vibrio parahaemolyticus* serotype O3:K6 strain RIMD 2210633. In the scientific literature, **PriB refers to an accessory factor of the PriA-dependent replication restart primosome** and is **structurally an OB-fold ssDNA-binding protein**, often compared to bacterial SSB. The evidence base available here is dominated by *E. coli* and other bacteria (structural and biochemical studies), plus a 2024 comparative genomics study in Vibrionaceae.

Despite targeted searches, **no primary paper retrieved in this run directly characterizes priB/VP2739/Q87L73 in *V. parahaemolyticus* RIMD 2210633** (e.g., mutant phenotypes, essentiality, expression, or localization for this exact strain). Consequently, **organism-specific statements for *V. parahaemolyticus* are limited to family-based inference** and **Vibrionaceae comparative genomics**; claims about PriB biochemical mechanisms are supported by PriB-family experimental studies in other bacteria. (lopper2004crystalstructureof pages 1-2, tominaga2024frequentnonhomologousreplacement pages 7-8)

### 1) Key concepts and definitions (current understanding)

#### 1.1 Replication restart and the restart primosome
Bacterial chromosome replication frequently encounters impediments that can stall or collapse replisomes; restart pathways **reload the replicative helicase (DnaB in *E. coli*) and rebuild replisomes at abandoned forks** to ensure genome duplication completes. A canonical *E. coli* restart primosome is described as comprising **PriA helicase, PriB, PriC, DnaT, DnaC, DnaB, and DnaG primase**, with assembly occurring on forked DNA substrates. (huang2014structuralinsightinto pages 9-10, michel2017replicationrestartin pages 4-6)

PriB is positioned in this framework as a **PriA partner** that binds ssDNA and promotes stable pre-primosome formation; PriB’s binding to ssDNA and/or fork-associated ssDNA intermediates is a key mechanistic feature. (huang2006complexedcrystalstructure pages 1-1, michel2017replicationrestartin pages 4-6)

#### 1.2 What PriB is (molecular identity)
PriB is an **~11 kDa homodimeric single-stranded nucleic acid-binding protein** with structural similarity to SSB proteins, consistent with proposals that PriB may have originated from an SSB-like ancestor. (bonde2024molecularinsightsinto pages 11-12)

Primary structure/structural studies define PriB as a **homodimer** with each monomer adopting an **oligonucleotide/oligosaccharide-binding (OB) fold** and having prominent flexible loops (e.g., L45) important for ssDNA binding. (huang2006complexedcrystalstructure pages 1-1, lopper2004crystalstructureof pages 1-2)

#### 1.3 PriB’s core function in replication restart
Experimental and review evidence converges on a model in which **PriB binds ssDNA and joins the PriA–DNA complex**, thereby stabilizing PriA at stalled/abandoned forks and facilitating recruitment of downstream factors (notably DnaT), ultimately enabling helicase reloading. (huang2006complexedcrystalstructure pages 1-1, lopper2004crystalstructureof pages 1-2, michel2017replicationrestartin pages 4-6)

### 2) Mechanistic and structural biology details

#### 2.1 PriB structure: OB-fold dimer and ssDNA-binding surface
Crystal structures show E. coli PriB as a **dimer** comprising two OB folds (one per monomer), forming a single-domain architecture. (lopper2004crystalstructureof pages 1-2)

In PriB–ssDNA complex structures, PriB uses its OB-fold surface in a binding mode **distinct from tetrameric EcoSSB**, supporting the view that OB-fold proteins can diversify binding geometries even when fold is conserved. (huang2006complexedcrystalstructure pages 1-1)

#### 2.2 ssDNA binding properties: cooperativity and salt sensitivity
Filter-binding assays and structural interpretation support that PriB–ssDNA binding can be **cooperative and salt-sensitive**, and loop elements (notably **L45**) play a key role. (huang2006complexedcrystalstructure pages 1-1)

One proposed assembly/stabilization mechanism from PriB–ssDNA complex analysis is that **binding of a second PriB dimer** may be important for stable PriB–ssDNA complex formation, consistent with cooperative behavior and potentially explaining instability of partially occupied complexes. (huang2006complexedcrystalstructure pages 8-9)

#### 2.3 Quantitative binding data and key residues
A fluorescence anisotropy analysis of E. coli PriB binding to a 30-nt ssDNA reported **WT Kd = 34.6 ± 7.7 nM** and showed that mutations at residues implicated in ssDNA binding reduce affinity: **F42A Kd = 72.8 ± 11.5 nM**, **W47A Kd = 112.3 ± 7.2 nM**, **K82A Kd = 218.2 ± 9.4 nM**, and **W47A/K82A Kd = 426.6 ± 52.3 nM**. These data support roles for **Phe42, Trp47, and Lys82** in ssDNA binding; Lys82 lies at the base of the L45 loop and contributes to an electropositive binding pocket. (lopper2004crystalstructureof pages 6-7)

Reviews and interaction mapping further propose that **K82/K84/K89 in the L45 loop** may contribute to **PriB interaction with SSB** (in addition to roles in ssDNA binding), aligning with the functional centrality of the L45 region. (bonde2024molecularinsightsinto pages 11-12)

#### 2.4 Protein–protein interactions and pathway position
Across biochemical/interaction studies summarized in the SSB-focused 2024 review, PriB is supported to interact with **PriA, DnaT, itself, and SSB**. (bonde2024molecularinsightsinto pages 11-12)

In mechanistic models of PriA-dependent primosome assembly, PriA binds first to a restart substrate; **PriB stabilizes PriA binding**, and DnaT promotes subsequent loading steps. In vitro models described in a replication-restart review note that high levels of DnaT can partially bypass the need for PriB, underscoring PriB’s role as a stabilizer/adapter in the assembly process. (michel2017replicationrestartin pages 4-6)

### 3) Recent developments (prioritizing 2023–2024)

#### 3.1 2023: Cryo-EM structure reveals a switch-like PriA→PriB recruitment mechanism
A major advance is a cryo-EM structure of an **E. coli PriA/PriB/replication fork complex** (Nature Communications, May 2023; https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-023-38144-x). The study reports a **~3.2 Å global-resolution map** from a major class representing **~80% of particles**, and shows that fork binding triggers large PriA conformational changes including an **~85° pivot** of the PriA cysteine-rich region (CRR). This restructuring creates a **~25 Å long pore** that encircles lagging-strand ssDNA; density supports **8–9 nucleotides** threaded through the pore, with a **~15 Å width** consistent with excluding dsDNA. The authors report **42 observed PriA/lagging-strand contacts** and propose that these structural rearrangements expose the PriB docking site, explaining biochemical observations that PriB binds far more tightly to fork-bound PriA than to PriA alone and enabling high-fidelity restart initiation. (duckworth2023replicationforkbinding pages 1-2)

Key structural panels and a mechanistic schematic are visible in the retrieved figure crops (Figures 1 and 7). (duckworth2023replicationforkbinding media 6bcbc8bc, duckworth2023replicationforkbinding media 11df83ca)

#### 3.2 2024: SSB architecture (IDL length) modulates replication restart pathways involving PriA–PriB
A 2024 Journal of Bacteriology study (Apr 2024; https://doi.org/10.1128/jb.00330-23) examined how deletions/insertions in the **intrinsically disordered linker (IDL)** of SSB affect genome maintenance. The work found that only large IDL deletions or an IDL insertion (ssb::mTur2) produce sensitivity in specific pathways and that **PriA–PriB1 and PriA–PriC** restart pathways display synthetic lethalities or growth defects with the largest IDL perturbations. This supports a current view that **SSB’s disordered architecture is functionally important** for proper operation of PriA-dependent restart networks (including PriB-dependent routes), likely by influencing cooperativity, spacing, or dynamic recruitment at ssDNA intermediates. (sandler2024theintrinsicallydisordered pages 1-2)

#### 3.3 2024: Vibrionaceae comparative genomics links priB loss to viral helicase loader acquisition
A 2024 PNAS comparative genomics study (Apr 2024; https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.2317954121) reports that within Vibrionaceae, **priB is frequently absent** in genomes that have acquired virus-derived helicase loader genes (vdhL1/vdhL2), with a strong statistical association (**q-value < 10−15**). The authors describe priB as encoding primosomal replication protein N involved in stalled fork restart with PriA/PriC and replisome proteins and also note prior transposon-insertion sequencing evidence that priB is essential in *Vibrio cholerae* C6706. Together, these findings suggest that in some Vibrionaceae lineages, viral nonhomologous helicase loaders can replace or restructure parts of the host helicase-loading/restart machinery such that priB is selected against and may be lost. (tominaga2024frequentnonhomologousreplacement pages 7-8)

### 4) Current applications and real-world implementations

1. **Genome maintenance as an antimicrobial vulnerability (conceptual application).** Replication restart proteins (PriA/PriB-centered networks) are widely viewed as critical for bacterial survival under replication stress; structural definition of the PriA/PriB/fork interface and the ssDNA pore provides a concrete framework for inhibiting restart initiation by disrupting (i) PriA restructuring, (ii) PriA–PriB docking, or (iii) ssDNA threading/recognition. While no PriB-targeting drug implementation is supported by the retrieved sources, the 2023 structure provides a mechanistic starting point for rational inhibitor design against restart initiation surfaces. (duckworth2023replicationforkbinding pages 1-2, duckworth2023replicationforkbinding media 6bcbc8bc)

2. **Comparative genomics for functional inference in Vibrionaceae (real-world genomics pipelines).** The 2024 Vibrionaceae study demonstrates a practical computational strategy (“contrapositive genetics”) integrating evolutionary exclusivity signals with structure prediction and assays to infer replication gene functions, and uses priB presence/absence patterns as part of replication system inference across many genomes. This kind of pipeline is directly applicable to annotate less-characterized genes in Vibrio species, including *V. parahaemolyticus* lineages where priB may be retained or lost depending on replication-module evolution. (tominaga2024frequentnonhomologousreplacement pages 7-8)

### 5) Expert opinions and authoritative synthesis

**Mechanistic consensus from reviews and structures:** Reviews and structural work converge on PriB as an **OB-fold ssDNA-binding adapter** that (a) stabilizes PriA at restart substrates, (b) supports recruitment of DnaT (and thus helicase loading factors), and (c) operates in a dynamic protein–DNA assembly where binding sites for ssDNA and partner proteins can overlap, implying regulated handoffs. (bonde2024molecularinsightsinto pages 11-12, huang2014structuralinsightinto pages 9-10, michel2017replicationrestartin pages 4-6)

**Species specialization:** Structural comparison of PriB homologs (e.g., *Neisseria* vs *E. coli*) supports an expert view that although the PriB dimeric OB-fold scaffold is conserved, **surface residue variation correlates with differences in affinity for ssDNA and PriA**, implying that replication restart pathways can be tuned to species-specific DNA metabolism needs. This underscores caution when transferring detailed quantitative parameters from *E. coli* to Vibrio proteins, while still supporting overall functional annotation by homology. (dong2010thecrystalstructure pages 1-2)

### 6) Functional annotation for *V. parahaemolyticus* PriB (Q87L73): best-supported conclusions

#### 6.1 Primary function
The most evidence-supported functional annotation for the *V. parahaemolyticus* priB product (Q87L73) is:
- **A cytosolic/nucleoid-associated replication restart primosome component**
- **An OB-fold ssDNA-binding protein (dimeric)**
- **An accessory factor for PriA-dependent replication restart**, stabilizing PriA at stalled/abandoned replication forks and facilitating recruitment/assembly of downstream primosome/helicase-loading factors (e.g., DnaT and helicase loader systems), leading to reloading of replicative helicase and resumption of replication. (huang2006complexedcrystalstructure pages 1-1, lopper2004crystalstructureof pages 1-2, michel2017replicationrestartin pages 4-6, duckworth2023replicationforkbinding pages 1-2)

PriB is not an enzyme in the sense of catalyzing a chemical transformation; rather, its “activity” is **ssDNA binding and scaffolding/stabilizing protein–DNA assemblies** for restart. (huang2006complexedcrystalstructure pages 1-1, lopper2004crystalstructureof pages 1-2)

#### 6.2 Biological process and pathway placement
PriB acts within the **PriA-dependent replication restart pathway** and is part of the early pre-primosome assembly sequence (PriA first, PriB second, followed by DnaT recruitment and helicase loading). (huang2006complexedcrystalstructure pages 1-1, michel2017replicationrestartin pages 4-6, duckworth2023replicationforkbinding pages 1-2)

#### 6.3 Cellular localization
No *V. parahaemolyticus*-specific localization data were retrieved. By function, PriB is expected to be **intracellular**, acting at **chromosomal replication forks and ssDNA intermediates** (nucleoid region) rather than being secreted or membrane-localized. This inference is supported by its direct role in fork-bound PriA/PriB complexes and its ssDNA binding function. (duckworth2023replicationforkbinding pages 1-2)

#### 6.4 Vibrionaceae-/Vibrio-relevant notes
Comparative genomics in Vibrionaceae shows that priB can be **retained as an essential replication restart gene** in some vibrios (reported for *V. cholerae* C6706) yet can also be **lost** in lineages with viral helicase loader acquisition, suggesting that *priB*’s indispensability can depend on the broader helicase-loading module encoded in a given genome. This is important context when annotating priB in *V. parahaemolyticus* genomes: its role is core to canonical restart, but the replication module can evolve. (tominaga2024frequentnonhomologousreplacement pages 7-8)
