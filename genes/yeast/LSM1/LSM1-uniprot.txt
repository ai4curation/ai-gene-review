CC   -!- FUNCTION: Component of the cytoplasmic LSM1-LSM7 complex which is
CC       involved in mRNA degradation by activating the decapping step
CC       (PubMed:10747033, PubMed:10913177, PubMed:10761922, PubMed:24513854).
CC       Together with PAT1, the LSM1-LSM7 complex binds to osmotic stress-
CC       activated mRNAs to attenuate the osmotic stress response, probably by
CC       limiting ribosome access to the mRNA and consequently translation
CC       (PubMed:30059503). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10747033,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:10761922, ECO:0000269|PubMed:10913177,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:24513854, ECO:0000269|PubMed:30059503}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Component of the heptameric LSM1-LSM7 complex that forms a
CC       seven-membered ring structure with a donut shape (PubMed:10747033,
CC       PubMed:24139796, PubMed:24513854). The LSm subunits are arranged in the
CC       order LSM1, LSM2, LSM3, LSM6, LSM5, LSM7 and LSM4 (PubMed:24139796).
CC       Except for LSM1, where a C-terminal helix crosses the ring structure to
CC       form additional interactions with LSM3 and LSM6, each subunit interacts
CC       only with its two neighboring subunits (PubMed:24139796). The LSM1-LSM7
CC       complex interacts with PAT1; within the complex PAT1 has direct
CC       interactions with LSM2 and LSM3 (PubMed:10747033, PubMed:10913177,
CC       PubMed:10761922, PubMed:24139796). The LSM1-LSM7 complex interacts with
CC       XRN1 (PubMed:10747033). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10747033,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:10761922, ECO:0000269|PubMed:10913177,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:24139796, ECO:0000269|PubMed:24513854}.
