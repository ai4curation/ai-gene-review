Gene Description

KAR2 encodes the essential ER-resident Hsp70 chaperone BiP in S. cerevisiae. It is the yeast homolog of mammalian GRP78/BiP. KAR2/BiP functions as an ATP-dependent protein folding chaperone in the ER lumen, is required for both co- and post-translational protein translocation across the ER membrane (acting as a molecular ratchet via the Sec61/Sec63 translocon), participates in ER-associated degradation (ERAD) by maintaining substrate solubility, and plays a key role in the unfolded protein response (UPR) by regulating the Ire1 kinase. It also participates in karyogamy during mating. KAR2 is essential for viability and is one of the most abundant ER proteins (337,000 molecules/cell). Its chaperone cycle is regulated by J-domain co-chaperones (Sec63, Scj1, Jem1) that stimulate ATP hydrolysis, and by nucleotide exchange factors (Lhs1, Sil1).

  KAR2 encodes the essential ER-resident Hsp70 chaperone BiP in S. cerevisiae. It is the yeast
  homolog of mammalian GRP78/BiP. KAR2/BiP functions as an ATP-dependent protein folding chaperone
  in the ER lumen, is required for both co- and post-translational protein translocation across
  the ER membrane (acting as a molecular ratchet via the Sec61/Sec63 translocon), participates
  in ER-associated degradation (ERAD) by maintaining substrate solubility, and plays a key role
  in the unfolded protein response (UPR) by regulating the Ire1 kinase. It also participates
  in karyogamy during mating. KAR2 is essential for viability and is one of the most abundant
  ER proteins (337,000 molecules/cell). Its chaperone cycle is regulated by J-domain co-chaperones
  (Sec63, Scj1, Jem1) that stimulate ATP hydrolysis, and by nucleotide exchange factors (Lhs1, Sil1).
