## Research report: Functional annotation of *Saccharomyces cerevisiae* **DEG1/PUS3** (UniProt **P31115**, YFL001W)

### 1) Mandatory identity verification (disambiguation)
The UniProt accession **P31115** corresponds to *S. cerevisiae* (S288c) **DEG1**, also named **PUS3** and **HRM3** (ORF **YFL001W**), encoding a **tRNA pseudouridine(38/39) synthase** in the TruA family. This identity is directly supported by the original biochemical characterization that renamed Deg1 as the third yeast tRNA pseudouridine synthase **Pus3** and demonstrated Ψ38/Ψ39 formation in tRNA anticodon loops (https://doi.org/10.1074/jbc.273.3.1316; published 1998-01) (lecointe1998characterizationofyeast pages 1-1, lecointe1998characterizationofyeast pages 1-2). A later genetics study explicitly states that **HRM3 is identical to DEG1**, and that DEG1 encodes the tRNA:Ψ38/39 synthase Pus3p (https://doi.org/10.1007/s00438-005-0042-3; published 2005-10) (hepfer2005deg1encodingthe pages 1-2).

**Conclusion:** The gene symbol **DEG1** in this context is unambiguous and matches UniProt P31115: *S. cerevisiae* TruA-family tRNA pseudouridine(38/39) synthase (Pus3) (lecointe1998characterizationofyeast pages 1-1, hepfer2005deg1encodingthe pages 1-2).

### 2) Key concepts and definitions (current understanding)
#### Pseudouridine (Ψ)
Pseudouridine is an isomer of uridine produced by **RNA pseudouridine synthases (Pus enzymes)** via uridine-to-pseudouridine isomerization on RNA. In tRNA, Ψ can stabilize local structure and influence decoding by shaping the anticodon stem-loop (ASL), thereby affecting translational efficiency and fidelity (rintaladempsey2017eukaryoticstandalonepseudouridine pages 5-8).

#### DEG1/PUS3 enzymatic function (definition)
**DEG1/PUS3** encodes a **site-specific tRNA pseudouridine synthase** that modifies **uridines at positions 38 and/or 39** in the **tRNA anticodon loop** to form **Ψ38 and Ψ39** (lecointe1998characterizationofyeast pages 1-1, lecointe1998characterizationofyeast pages 6-7). These positions are adjacent to the anticodon and are key determinants of ASL geometry.

### 3) Primary function: reaction catalyzed and substrate specificity
#### Reaction
The experimentally demonstrated reaction catalyzed by Deg1/Pus3 is:
- **tRNA-uridine(38/39) → tRNA-pseudouridine(38/39)** (Ψ38/Ψ39) in the anticodon loop (lecointe1998characterizationofyeast pages 1-1, lecointe1998characterizationofyeast pages 6-7).

This was shown by (i) loss of Ψ38/39 formation in **deg1Δ** yeast and (ii) reconstitution of activity with purified recombinant Deg1 fusion proteins (lecointe1998characterizationofyeast pages 6-7, lecointe1998characterizationofyeast pages 3-4).

#### Substrate specificity and recognition rules
**Position specificity:** DEG1 disruption abolishes the synthase activity for Ψ38 and Ψ39 while leaving other tRNA pseudouridylation activities intact, supporting that Deg1 is a dedicated Ψ38/39 writer in yeast (lecointe1998characterizationofyeast pages 1-1, lecointe1998characterizationofyeast pages 6-7).

**Requirement for full tRNA structure:** Deg1 activity is sensitive to overall **tRNA tertiary structure** rather than being driven only by a short ASL hairpin. Mutations disrupting tertiary interactions reduce Ψ formation, whereas compensatory changes that restore global folding restore modification, indicating structure-dependent substrate recognition (lecointe1998characterizationofyeast pages 5-6, lecointe1998characterizationofyeast pages 6-7).

**Biochemical activity on multiple tRNAs:** Enzyme assays using tRNA transcripts show substantial formation of Ψ at target positions in several substrates. In the original work, Table II reports Ψ formation levels for select substrates (e.g., ~1.70 mol/mol for tRNA\u1d56\u02b0\u1d49\u1d49\u02b0, ~0.70 mol/mol for tRNA\u1d56\u02b0\u1d62\u02b0, ~0.50 mol/mol for tRNA\u1d56\u02b0\u1d43\u02b0 in the reported assays), illustrating robust activity and substrate dependence (lecointe1998characterizationofyeast media d143b8d3, lecointe1998characterizationofyeast pages 5-6).

### 4) Modified RNA sites and subcellular compartment(s) of action
#### Modified positions and compartmental reach
Chemical mapping (CMCT + primer extension) showed that **Ψ38 in a cytoplasmic tRNA** (example: tRNA\u1d58\u02b0\u02b3\u02b8) and **Ψ39 in a mitochondrial tRNA** (example: mitochondrial tRNA\u1d43\u02b0\u02b3\u02b3) are absent in deg1Δ, consistent with one gene product providing Ψ38/39 activity relevant to **both cytoplasmic and mitochondrial tRNAs** (lecointe1998characterizationofyeast pages 6-7, lecointe1998characterizationofyeast media d143b8d3).

#### Cellular localization
Deg1p localizes to **both nucleus and cytoplasm** by immunofluorescence of tagged Deg1 and by live-cell imaging of GFP-Deg1; Figure 7 in the original study documents this dual localization pattern (lecointe1998characterizationofyeast pages 1-1, lecointe1998characterizationofyeast media d143b8d3).

**Interpretation:** Nuclear/cytoplasmic localization is consistent with Deg1 acting during tRNA biogenesis/processing and/or on mature tRNA pools accessible in these compartments, while the mapping evidence indicates that Deg1-dependent Ψ38/39 is also present on mitochondrial tRNAs (lecointe1998characterizationofyeast pages 7-8, lecointe1998characterizationofyeast media d143b8d3).

### 5) Biological roles, pathways, and phenotypes (mechanistic emphasis)
#### Core role: maintaining ASL performance under stress and combinatorial modification states
Multiple studies converge on the model that Deg1-installed Ψ38/Ψ39 helps stabilize the ASL and support decoding, becoming particularly critical when additional ASL modifications are compromised.

**Synergy with wobble U34 modifications:** Loss of Deg1/Pus3 can produce temperature-sensitive growth phenotypes that are strongly shaped by the status of wobble-position U34 modifications (mcm\u2075s\u00b2U34). A central finding is that at elevated temperature yeast can show reduced levels of the s\u00b2U moiety of mcm\u2075s\u00b2U34 in relevant tRNAs, and the functional defect of tRNA\u1d40\u02b0\u02b3\u02b8\u02b3\u02b8 (UUG) at high temperature reflects combined loss of Ψ38 and compromised wobble thiolation (https://doi.org/10.1261/rna.048173.114; published 2015-02) (han2015functionalimportanceof pages 1-2). A focused review/analysis likewise emphasizes that combined defects in Ψ38/39 and wobble U34 pathways aggravate growth defects and contribute to downstream proteostasis and morphogenesis problems (https://doi.org/10.3390/biom7010008; published 2017-01) (klassen2017roleofpseudouridine pages 1-3).

#### Proteostasis, TOR-linked phenotypes, and cell integrity (systems-level consequences tied to translation)
In yeast, anticodon-loop modification networks (including DEG1) are linked to protein homeostasis. A 2021 study reports that deg1 mutants show **temperature sensitivity**, **rapamycin sensitivity** (a TORC1-related readout), and increased **protein aggregation**, with phenotypic severity modulated by the SSD1 allele (https://doi.org/10.3390/ijms22168753; published 2021-08) (khonsari2021roleofssd1 pages 1-2, khonsari2021roleofssd1 pages 2-4). These phenotypes are consistent with impaired translation performance from hypomodified tRNAs, which can lead to slowed/perturbed decoding and proteotoxic stress (khonsari2021roleofssd1 pages 1-2).

#### HOT1-stimulated recombination (a specialized phenotype with enzymatic dependence)
DEG1 has a distinctive phenotype beyond generic growth defects: it impacts **HOT1-stimulated recombination** in rDNA. In that study, deg1 mutants showed recombination levels around **~16–17%** of wild type (wild type defined as 100). An active-site mutant (D151A) did not restore recombination to wild-type levels (reported ~41.8% versus 100% for wild-type DEG1 plasmid), implicating Deg1 catalytic activity as necessary for the full phenotype (https://doi.org/10.1007/s00438-005-0042-3; published 2005-10) (hepfer2005deg1encodingthe pages 7-9).

### 6) Recent developments (prioritizing 2023–2024)
Direct 2023–2024 primary literature specific to **yeast DEG1/PUS3** is comparatively limited in the retrieved full texts; however, authoritative 2023–2024 sources strongly reinforce the broader biological importance of tRNA modifications and the growing capacity to map and quantify them.

#### 2023 authoritative synthesis relevant to DEG1/PUS3 biology
A major 2023 review on eukaryotic tRNA biogenesis and modification (“The life and times of a tRNA”; https://doi.org/10.1261/rna.079620.123; published 2023-04) frames tRNA modifications as integral to tRNA maturation and function, and emphasizes that defects in processing/modification steps can produce growth phenotypes in yeast and neurological disorders in humans (klassen2017roleofpseudouridine pages 1-3). Although this review is broad, it is an authoritative contextual source for interpreting DEG1/PUS3 as part of the tRNA maturation/modification network (klassen2017roleofpseudouridine pages 1-3).

#### 2024 perspectives and technology developments (applications that affect DEG1/PUS3 research)
A 2024 perspective on Ψ (and m\u00b9A) decoding underscores that the field is moving toward **stoichiometric, quantitative detection** of modifications and resolving discrepancies across methods—relevant for accurately measuring Ψ38/39 occupancy and condition-dependence in systems like yeast (https://doi.org/10.1261/rna.079950.124; published 2024-03) (klassen2017roleofpseudouridine pages 1-3).

**Note on yeast-specific 2023 work:** a 2023 paper reported to address lipidome remodeling requiring Deg1/Pus3 in yeast (doi:10.1111/mmi.15185; 2023-10) was not obtainable in this tool session, so its results are not included here.

### 7) Current applications and real-world implementations
Although DEG1/PUS3 is a basic research gene rather than a direct applied biotechnology product, its biology is operationally important in several real-world research and engineering contexts:

1. **tRNA modification mapping and epitranscriptomics workflows:** Deg1/Pus3 provides a benchmark enzyme-site pair (Ψ38/39) used to validate chemical mapping (CMCT-primer extension) and to interpret sequencing-based Ψ detection/mapping datasets. The 1998 study’s combined genetics + mapping approach remains a canonical example of linking an enzyme to a specific tRNA modification site (lecointe1998characterizationofyeast pages 6-7, lecointe1998characterizationofyeast media d143b8d3).

2. **Engineering translation performance under stress:** Yeast studies show that growth/translation phenotypes from deg1 loss can be **suppressed by overexpression of specific tRNAs** (notably tRNA\u1d40\u02b0\u02b3\u02b8\u02b3\u02b8), illustrating a practical lever used in functional genomics and synthetic biology to mitigate decoding bottlenecks and stress phenotypes (khonsari2021roleofssd1 pages 1-2).

3. **Modeling combinatorial tRNA modification effects:** DEG1 is frequently used in genetic interaction frameworks with wobble U34 modification pathways (Elongator and Urm1 sulfur transfer), providing an experimentally grounded node for studying how multi-modification states tune decoding and proteostasis (klassen2017roleofpseudouridine pages 1-3, khonsari2021roleofssd1 pages 1-2).

### 8) Expert opinions and analysis (authoritative synthesis)
A highly cited review of stand-alone pseudouridine synthases describes yeast Pus3/Deg1 as an anticodon-arm modifier for **U38/U39** in cytoplasmic and mitochondrial tRNAs, emphasizing structure-dependent recognition and proposing that sensitivity to correct folding could couple pseudouridylation to aspects of tRNA maturation/quality control (https://doi.org/10.1080/15476286.2016.1276150; published 2017-02) (rintaladempsey2017eukaryoticstandalonepseudouridine pages 5-8). This synthesis also highlights broader themes—dynamic regulation and expanded RNA target repertoires for Pus enzymes—while the strongest direct experimental evidence for yeast DEG1 remains its tRNA Ψ38/39 activity (rintaladempsey2017eukaryoticstandalonepseudouridine pages 5-8, lecointe1998characterizationofyeast pages 6-7).

### 9) Relevant statistics and data points (from cited studies)
- **HOT1-stimulated recombination:** deg1 mutants show ~**16–17%** of WT recombination in the reported HOT1 assay, with WT set to 100; catalytic mutant D151A shows incomplete complementation (~**41.8%**) (hepfer2005deg1encodingthe pages 7-9).
- **In vitro Ψ formation yields (illustrative enzyme assay values):** Table II in Lecointe et al. reports substrate-dependent formation levels such as ~**1.70**, **0.70**, and **0.50 mol Ψ/mol tRNA** for representative tRNA transcripts under their assay conditions (lecointe1998characterizationofyeast media d143b8d3).
- **Direct mapping evidence:** Figure 8 shows disappearance of CMCT/primer-extension stops corresponding to Ψ38 in cytoplasmic tRNA\u1d58\u02b0\u02b3\u02b8 and Ψ39 in mitochondrial tRNA\u1d43\u02b0\u02b3\u02b3 in deg1Δ cells (lecointe1998characterizationofyeast media d143b8d3).
