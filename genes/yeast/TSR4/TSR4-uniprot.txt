CC   -!- FUNCTION: Cytoplasmic chaperone for ribosomal protein uS5;
CC       cotranslationally associates with uS5 to facilitate its expression and
CC       solubility and is released prior to nuclear import of uS5
CC       (PubMed:31062022). Required for processing of the 20S pre-rRNA at site
CC       D to generate mature 18S rRNA (PubMed:19806183).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:19806183, ECO:0000269|PubMed:31062022}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Interacts with small ribosomal subunit protein RPS2/uS5 (via
CC       N-terminus); the interaction is direct, occurs cotranslationally, and
CC       serves to chaperone uS5 prior to its nuclear import.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:31062022}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cytoplasm, cytosol {ECO:0000269|PubMed:14562095,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:31062022}.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: Does not disrupt import of RPS2 into the nucleus
CC       but does not allow its efficient assembly into pre-ribosomes
CC       (PubMed:31062022). Strongly reduces cell population growth; leads to
CC       morphological defects such as enlarged cells, enlarged vacuoles, and
CC       cells with budding defects (PubMed:31062022).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:31062022}.
CC   -!- MISCELLANEOUS: Present with 125 molecules/cell in log phase SD medium.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:14562106}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the TSR4 family. {ECO:0000305}.
