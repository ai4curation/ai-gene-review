CC   -!- FUNCTION: Ceramide-binding protein that may transfer ceramides from the
CC       endoplasmic reticulum membrane to the cis-Golgi network membrane, and
CC       is thereby required for the biosynthesis of complex sphingolipids
CC       (PubMed:36897280). Required for survival in response to oxidative
CC       stress (PubMed:16034825). Involved in the diauxic shift
CC       (PubMed:12244097). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12244097,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:16034825, ECO:0000269|PubMed:36897280}.

CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Golgi apparatus, cis-Golgi network membrane
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:36897280}; Peripheral membrane protein
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:36897280}. Endoplasmic reticulum membrane
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:36897280}; Peripheral membrane protein
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:36897280}. Cytoplasm {ECO:0000269|PubMed:14562095,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:36897280}. Nucleus {ECO:0000269|PubMed:14562095}.
CC       Note=Localizes to the interface between the cis-Golgi network and
CC       endoplasmic reticulum exit sites. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:36897280}.
CC   -!- PTM: Acetylated at the N-terminus in a NatC complex-dependent manner,
CC       which is required for membrane targeting.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:36897280}.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: Leads to an increase in cellular ceramide levels
CC       and a decrease in complex sphingolipid levels (PubMed:36897280). Mildly
CC       decreases cellular phytosphingosine (PHS) levels, with no increase in
CC       dihydrosphingosine (DHS) levels (PubMed:36897280). Sensitive to
CC       Auroebasidin A (IPC synthase inhibitor) (PubMed:36897280). Simultaneous
CC       knockout of NVJ2 to leads to myriocin sensitivity (sphingosine
CC       biosynthesis inhibitor) (PubMed:36897280). Simultaneous knockout of
CC       SUR2 leads to a growth defect (PubMed:36897280).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:36897280}.
CC   -!- MISCELLANEOUS: Present with 45200 molecules/cell in log phase SD
CC       medium. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:14562106}.
CC   -!- MISCELLANEOUS: Expression in mammalian cells increases survival under
CC       conditions inducing apoptosis.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the SVF1 family. {ECO:0000305}.
