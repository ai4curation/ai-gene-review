CC   -!- FUNCTION: Histone acetyltransferase that acetylates histone H2B to form
CC       H2BK11ac and H2BK16ac, histone H3 to form H3K9ac, H3K14ac, H3K18ac,
CC       H3K23ac, H3K27ac and H3K36ac, with a lower preference histone H4 to
CC       form H4K8ac and H4K16ac, and contributes to H2A.Z acetylation.
CC       Acetylation of histones gives a specific tag for epigenetic
CC       transcription activation and elongation. Operates in concert with
CC       certain DNA-binding transcriptional activators such as GCN4 or
CC       HAP2/3/4. Its acetyltransferase activity seems to be dependent on the
CC       association in different multisubunit complexes (PubMed:10026213,
CC       PubMed:11545749, PubMed:16543222, PubMed:16543223, PubMed:17189264,
CC       PubMed:19822662). Component of the transcription coactivator SAGA
CC       complex. SAGA acts as a general cofactor required for essentially all
CC       RNA polymerase II transcription (PubMed:10864329, PubMed:25216679,
CC       PubMed:28918903). At the promoters, SAGA is required for transcription
CC       pre-initiation complex (PIC) recruitment. It influences RNA polymerase
CC       II transcriptional activity through different activities such as TBP
CC       interaction (via core/TAF module) and promoter selectivity, interaction
CC       with transcription activators (via Tra1/SPT module), and chromatin
CC       modification through histone acetylation (via HAT module) and
CC       deubiquitination (via DUB module) (PubMed:25216679). SAGA
CC       preferentially acetylates histones H3 (to form H3K9ac, H3K14ac, H3K18ac
CC       and H3K23ac) and H2B and deubiquitinates histone H2B (PubMed:10026213,
CC       PubMed:18458063). SAGA interacts with DNA via upstream activating
CC       sequences (UASs) (PubMed:28918903). Also identified in a modified
CC       version of SAGA named SALSA or SLIK (PubMed:12186975, PubMed:12446794).
CC       The cleavage of SPT7 and the absence of the SPT8 subunit in SLIK
CC       neither drive any major conformational differences in its structure
CC       compared with SAGA, nor significantly affect HAT, DUB, or DNA-binding
CC       activities (PubMed:33864814). Component of the ADA histone
CC       acetyltransferase complex, which preferentially acetylates nucleosomal
CC       histones H3 (to form H3K14ac and H3K18ac) and H2B (PubMed:10026213,
CC       PubMed:9224714). In addition to histone acetyltransferase, can use
CC       different acyl-CoA substrates, such as (2E)-butenoyl-CoA (crotonyl-CoA)
CC       and is able to mediate histone crotonylation (PubMed:31699900).
CC       Controls the metaphase-to-anaphase transition and is required for
CC       correct chromosome segregation and centromere/kinetochore function in
CC       mitosis (PubMed:18039853). May be involved in response to DNA damage by
CC       genotoxic agents (PubMed:15340070). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10026213,
