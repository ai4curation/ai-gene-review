CC   -!- FUNCTION: Dual chitinase/transglycosylase that plays a role in cell
CC       wall architecture (PubMed:18694928, PubMed:19734368, PubMed:23919454).
CC       Chitinase and transglycosylase activities are coupled (By similarity).
CC       Required for the polysaccharide cross-linking at the septa and the cell
CC       wall (PubMed:18694928, PubMed:19734368, PubMed:23919454). More
CC       specifically, transfers chitin to both beta(1-3)- and beta(1-6)glucan
CC       in the cell wall (PubMed:18694928, PubMed:19734368, PubMed:23919454).
CC       The minimal number of intact hexopyranose units required in the
CC       molecule of the acceptor oligosaccharide is two and the effectivity of
CC       the acceptor increased with the increasing length of its
CC       oligosaccharide chain (PubMed:23919454). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8J0P4,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:18694928, ECO:0000269|PubMed:19734368,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:23919454}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY:
CC       Reaction=Random endo-hydrolysis of N-acetyl-beta-D-glucosaminide
CC         (1->4)-beta-linkages in chitin and chitodextrins.; EC=3.2.1.14;
CC         Evidence={ECO:0000269|PubMed:23919454};
CC   -!- BIOPHYSICOCHEMICAL PROPERTIES:
CC       pH dependence:
CC         Optimum pH is 3.5. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:23919454};
CC       Temperature dependence:

CC         {ECO:0000269|PubMed:23919454};
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Secreted, cell wall
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:17302808}. Membrane {ECO:0000255}; Lipid-anchor,
CC       GPI-anchor {ECO:0000255}. Note=Covalently-linked GPI-modified cell wall
CC       protein (GPI-CWP), localized particularly in chitin-rich areas. Found
CC       at the incipient bud site, around the septum area in later stages of
CC       budding, and in ascospore envelopes. Redistributes uniformly over the
CC       cell cortex upon heat stress. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:17302808}.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: Positively regulated by cell integrity signaling through
CC       MPK1 in response to cell wall perturbation. Induced by heat stress.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10594829, ECO:0000269|PubMed:11016834,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:17302808}.
CC   -!- PTM: The GPI-anchor is attached to the protein in the endoplasmic
CC       reticulum and serves to target the protein to the cell surface. There,
CC       the glucosamine-inositol phospholipid moiety is cleaved off and the
CC       GPI-modified mannoprotein is covalently attached via its lipidless GPI
CC       glycan remnant to the 1,6-beta-glucan of the outer cell wall layer.
CC       {ECO:0000305}.
CC   -!- MISCELLANEOUS: Present with 44000 wall-bound molecules/cell in log
CC       phase YPD medium. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:17617218}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the glycosyl hydrolase 16 family. CRH1
CC       subfamily. {ECO:0000305}.
