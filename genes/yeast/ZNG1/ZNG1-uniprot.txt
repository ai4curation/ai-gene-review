CC   -!- FUNCTION: Zinc chaperone that directly transfers zinc cofactor to
CC       target metalloproteins, thereby activating them (PubMed:35584675).
CC       Catalyzes zinc insertion into the active site of methionine
CC       aminopeptidase MAP1, which function to cleave the initiator methionine
CC       from polypeptides during or after protein translation
CC       (PubMed:35584675). Mechanistically, the N-terminal psi-PxLVp motif
CC       binds to the C6H2-type zinc finger of inactive form of MAP1
CC       (PubMed:35584675). After formation of the docked complex, zinc is
CC       transferred from the CXCC motif in the GTPase domain of ZNG1 to the
CC       zinc binding site in the peptidase domain of MAP1 in a process
CC       requiring GTP hydrolysis (PubMed:35584675). GTP/GDP exchange is
CC       required for release of active MAP1 (PubMed:35584675).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:35584675}.
