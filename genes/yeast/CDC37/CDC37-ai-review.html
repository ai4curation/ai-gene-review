Gene Description

CDC37 encodes an essential cytosolic Hsp90 co-chaperone that specializes the Hsp90 system for protein kinase clients. Cdc37 binds protein kinases and Hsp90, promotes Hsp90-kinase complex formation, protects nascent kinase chains from rapid degradation, and supports post-translational kinase maturation. Its client effects explain downstream roles in Cdc28/Cak1-dependent cell-cycle progression, MAP kinase signaling, osmotic and cell wall stress responses, and spindle pole body duplication, but the core function is kinase-specific chaperone/adaptor activity rather than those downstream pathways themselves.

Core Functions

Cdc37 is the kinase-targeting adaptor of the cytosolic Hsp90 chaperone system. It binds nascent or unstable protein kinase clients, protects many kinases from rapid degradation, and promotes subsequent Hsp90-dependent maturation. This client-specific chaperone role explains broad effects on Cdc28/Cak1 cell-cycle control, MAPK signaling, and spindle-pole-body duplication without making those downstream pathways the core function.

Research Report: Functional Annotation of Saccharomyces cerevisiae CDC37 (UniProt P06101)

0. Target verification (critical identity checks)

The provided UniProt record describes Cdc37, an Hsp90 co-chaperone (also called the kinase-targeting subunit/p50) from Saccharomyces cerevisiae S288c, encoded by CDC37 (systematic locus YDR168W). The retrieved yeast primary literature consistently describes S. cerevisiae Cdc37 as an essential factor for protein-kinase stability/maturation that functions with Hsp90 and is regulated by CK2-dependent phosphorylation at conserved N-terminal serines (Ser14/Ser17 in yeast), matching the functional and regulatory features in the UniProt description. This corpus did not contain an explicit statement linking the accession number P06101 to CDC37/YDR168W, so accession-level mapping is not independently cross-validated here; the functional identity and organism match are strong and yeast-specific. (farrell2000cdc37promotesthe pages 1-2, mandal2007cdc37hasdistinct pages 1-3, miyata2004ck2controlsmultiple pages 1-2, millson2014cdc37engagesin pages 1-2)

1. Key concepts and definitions (current understanding)

1.1 What Cdc37 is (definition)

Cdc37 is a protein-kinase–directed co-chaperone/adaptor in the cytosolic Hsp90 system. Rather than catalyzing a chemical reaction, Cdc37 selectively recognizes kinase clients and promotes their stabilization and productive engagement with Hsp90 to achieve folding/maturation and functional activation. (mandal2007cdc37hasdistinct pages 1-3, miyata2004ck2controlsmultiple pages 1-2)

1.2 Domain architecture and how domains map to function

A widely used mechanistic model assigns separable roles to Cdc37 domains:
- N-terminal domain/region: primary kinase-binding module; binds determinants in the kinase catalytic domain (often mapped to the N-lobe of kinases). (mandal2007cdc37hasdistinct pages 1-3, verba2017howhsp90and pages 4-6)
- Middle domain: major Hsp90-binding interface (binding Hsp90 N-terminal and/or middle domain depending on cycle state), helping recruit kinases into the Hsp90 cycle. (verba2017howhsp90and pages 4-6)
- C-terminal region: contributes to client interactions in some species/contexts; in yeast it can be partly dispensable in some experimental settings discussed in review literature. (verba2017howhsp90and pages 4-6)

A structural feature highlighted in expert review is that a conserved Cdc37 motif can mimic part of the kinase αC–β4 loop, stabilizing a partially unfolded kinase conformation that can be further remodeled by Hsp90, providing a physical rationale for kinase selectivity. (verba2017howhsp90and pages 4-6)

Supporting visual evidence: Verba & Agard (2017) provide a schematic of Cdc37 domain architecture and a model of the Hsp90–Cdc37–kinase cycle. (verba2017howhsp90and media 39c974c8, verba2017howhsp90and media fe609c0f)

1.3 Mechanistic role in the Hsp90 cycle (“client loading” and ATPase tuning)

A consistent mechanistic theme is that Cdc37 helps load kinases onto Hsp90 and can inhibit/arrest Hsp90 ATPase-coupled conformational progression during client loading (e.g., by blocking lid closure/NTD dimerization), thereby stabilizing intermediate complexes until the client is ready for productive folding steps. (guimaraes2022developmentofa pages 20-25, verba2017howhsp90and pages 4-6)

2. Primary molecular function in yeast

2.1 Core function: kinase stability and cell-cycle control through Cdc28 and Cak1

In budding yeast, CDC37 is essential for G1/Start progression and for activation of the major cyclin-dependent kinase Cdc28 (Cdk1). In the temperature-sensitive cdc37-1 mutant, Cdc28 shows multiple defects including reduced total protein and reduced activity; importantly, Cdc28 protein levels were reported as ~3–5-fold lower than wild type in cdc37-1. (farrell2000cdc37promotesthe pages 1-2)

Cdc37 also supports the stability/activity of the CDK-activating kinase Cak1. The same primary study reported that Cak1 levels and activity are reduced in cdc37-1, supporting the view that Cdc37 is a general kinase biogenesis factor with particularly strong effects on key cell-cycle kinases. (farrell2000cdc37promotesthe pages 1-2, farrell2000cdc37promotesthe pages 5-6)

2.2 Kinome-wide role (“kinome biogenesis”)

A large-scale analysis (sampling 65 yeast protein kinases, ~50% of the kinome) found that 51 kinases had decreased abundance in a cdc37 mutant background, supporting a broad role for Cdc37 in kinase quality control and kinome stability. The same work proposed a two-step model: (i) protection of newly synthesized kinases from rapid degradation and (ii) post-translational maturation with Hsp90. (mandal2007cdc37hasdistinct pages 1-3)

Independent yeast genetic/interaction work further supports broad kinase dependence on Cdc37 phosphorylation state: the nonphosphorylatable cdc37(S14A) allele is temperature sensitive and was reported to destabilize ~70% of the kinome (as summarized in yeast-focused work on Cak1–Cdc37 association). (millson2014cdc37engagesin pages 1-2)

3. Regulation and pathway integration

3.1 Phosphoregulation by CK2 and coupling to the Hsp90 cycle

A central regulatory input is casein kinase 2 (CK2)-dependent phosphorylation of Cdc37 at a conserved N-terminal serine (Ser13 in mammals; Ser14/Ser17 in yeast numbering discussed in yeast literature). CK2-dependent phosphorylation is described as essential for optimal engagement of Cdc37 with a wide set of protein kinases and for productive Hsp90–kinase complex formation, while certain Cdc37 mutants can retain Hsp90 binding but lose effective kinase handling. (miyata2004ck2controlsmultiple pages 1-2, millson2014cdc37engagesin pages 1-2)

This establishes CDC37 as a node by which CK2 can coordinately influence the stability and activity of many kinases through a chaperone-mediated mechanism. (miyata2004ck2controlsmultiple pages 1-2)

3.2 Osmoadaptation and MAPK pathway cross-talk (Kss1)

Beyond core cell-cycle kinases, CDC37 connects to signaling. In a study of osmoadaptation, the cdc37-34 mutant showed osmosensitivity not explained by canonical HOG signaling defects; instead, the filamentous growth MAPK module (Ste11/Ste7/Kss1) was implicated. Multicopy suppression identified KSS1 as a suppressor and Kss1 was shown to physically interact with Cdc37, supporting a model where Cdc37 modulates osmoadaptation via MAPK signaling and downstream cell-wall organization. (yang2007cdc37pisinvolved pages 1-2)

4. Subcellular localization and complexes (where the protein acts)

Cdc37’s functional locale is best described as the cytosolic chaperone machinery, where it forms transient Cdc37–kinase and Hsp90–Cdc37–kinase complexes. A notable mechanistic detail is that Cdc37 can be dimeric in solution but is often described as monomeric within the ternary Hsp90–Cdc37–client complex, consistent with a dynamic adaptor role during client processing. (mandal2007cdc37hasdistinct pages 1-3, verba2017howhsp90and pages 4-6)

5. Recent developments (prioritizing 2023–2024) and current applications

5.1 2023–2024 research directions in yeast Hsp90/co-chaperone biology

A 2023 review emphasizes S. cerevisiae as a premier system for dissecting the Hsp90/co-chaperone network using genetic tools, systematic interaction mapping, and proteomics, enabling inference of client dependencies and pathway integration for components such as Cdc37. This reflects current practice: using yeast to connect chaperone/co-chaperone perturbations to signaling outputs and drug responses. (Backe et al., 2023-09-01, https://doi.org/10.1042/ebc20220224) (backe2023saccharomycescerevisiaeas pages 12-13)

5.2 Real-world implementations: yeast as a screening platform for Hsp90–Cdc37 pathway inhibitors

Yeast systems are also used as functional screening platforms to identify small molecules that perturb the Hsp90–Cdc37 pathway, commonly employing Cdc37-dependent kinase clients (e.g., sensitized Cdc28 backgrounds) as readouts and orthogonal assays for PPI disruption versus direct Hsp90 inhibition. This illustrates translational relevance (tool compounds, antifungal/anticancer target discovery) while leveraging yeast genetics for mechanism-of-action deconvolution. (guimaraes2022developmentofa pages 1-8, guimaraes2022developmentofa pages 63-66)

6. Expert opinion and authoritative synthesis

A highly cited expert review frames the Hsp90–Cdc37 system as enabling kinases to traverse conformational/energetic barriers (e.g., maintaining partially unfolded, activation-competent intermediates) and highlights open mechanistic questions such as how remodeling steps are coordinated with ATPase timing and co-chaperone exchange. This perspective supports interpreting yeast CDC37 phenotypes as resulting from failures in client loading, conformational stabilization, and maturation timing, rather than a single static binding defect. (Verba & Agard, 2017-10-01, https://doi.org/10.1016/j.tibs.2017.07.002) (verba2017howhsp90and pages 4-6)

7. Key statistics and data points (from recent and classic studies)

  • Cdc28 abundance defect: Cdc28 protein levels are ~3–5× lower in the budding-yeast cdc37-1 mutant (linked to Start arrest and low Cdc28 activity). (Farrell & Morgan, 2000-02-01, https://doi.org/10.1128/mcb.20.3.749-754.2000) (farrell2000cdc37promotesthe pages 1-2)
  • Kinome-scale dependence: in a panel of 65 kinases (~50% of the yeast kinome), 51 showed decreased abundance in a cdc37 mutant. (Mandal et al., 2007-01-29, https://doi.org/10.1083/jcb.200604106) (mandal2007cdc37hasdistinct pages 1-3)
  • Phospho-mutant broad impact (yeast): cdc37(S14A) associated with destabilization of ~70% of the kinome (reported in yeast-focused work analyzing kinase interactions and stability). (millson2014cdc37engagesin pages 1-2)

8. Concise functional annotation statement (yeast CDC37 / UniProt P06101)

CDC37 encodes an essential cytosolic Hsp90 co-chaperone that acts as a kinase-targeting adaptor. Its primary molecular role is to protect newly synthesized kinases from degradation and promote their Hsp90-dependent maturation, thereby maintaining cellular kinase abundance and enabling signaling and cell-cycle progression (notably via Cdc28 and Cak1). CDC37 function is regulated by CK2-dependent phosphorylation of conserved N-terminal serines (Ser14/Ser17 in yeast), and CDC37 also contributes to stress and osmoadaptation signaling (e.g., via Kss1-associated MAPK module crosstalk). (farrell2000cdc37promotesthe pages 1-2, mandal2007cdc37hasdistinct pages 1-3, miyata2004ck2controlsmultiple pages 1-2, yang2007cdc37pisinvolved pages 1-2)

BioReason-Pro RL Review: CDC37 (S. cerevisiae)

Source: CDC37-deep-research-bioreason-rl.md

  • Correctness: 4/5
  • Completeness: 3/5

Functional Summary Review

The BioReason summary states:

A soluble co-chaperone that assembles with the Hsp90 system to stabilize and mature metastable client proteins -- especially kinase-class substrates -- in baker's yeast.

This is accurate. The curated review confirms CDC37 is an Hsp90 co-chaperone that promotes maturation of specific Hsp90 client proteins, with kinase specificity well documented (GO:0019901 protein kinase binding from GO_REF:0000002; protein stabilization GO:0050821).

Its N-terminal module captures labile clients, a central region docks Hsp90 to drive ATP-dependent folding cycles, and the C-terminal domain consolidates the assembly.

This domain-level description correctly maps to the three known CDC37 domains (IPR013855, IPR013874, IPR013873) and their functional roles.

By orchestrating chaperone-mediated folding in the cytosol, it promotes the attainment and maintenance of functional conformations central to cellular signaling and proteostasis.

Cytosolic localization is correct (multiple lines of evidence in curated review: IBA, IEA, HDA). The connection to protein folding (GO:0006457) and protein stabilization (GO:0050821) is accurate.

The summary correctly captures protein folding and protein stabilization as core functions. However, it misses several important non-core but documented functions: the requirement for passage through START in the cell cycle (GO:0051726), involvement in HOG and PKC MAP kinase signaling cascades (GO:0043410, GO:0038066), spindle pole body duplication (GO:0030474), and the cellular hyperosmotic response (GO:0071474). These are context-dependent functions documented by IMP evidence (PMID:17220467, PMID:9060463, PMID:7753858).

The molecular function is described as "selective protein binding," which the curated review marks as over-annotated when generic (GO:0005515). The more informative terms are heat shock protein binding (GO:0031072), protein-folding chaperone binding (GO:0051087), and protein kinase binding (GO:0019901).

Comparison with interpro2go:

The interpro2go annotation (GO_REF:0000002) provides protein kinase binding (GO:0019901), which is a specific and informative molecular function term. BioReason's summary describes kinase-client binding in prose but does not reach this level of specificity in its GO term predictions -- it outputs only protein binding (GO:0005515) and unfolded protein binding (GO:0051082) as MF terms, both of which the curated review marks as over-annotated. BioReason adds narrative value about the tri-partite domain architecture and mechanism but does not improve on interpro2go for functional specificity.

Notes on thinking trace

The trace correctly identifies the three-domain architecture and its functional implications. The reasoning from domain structure to co-chaperone function is sound. The prediction of Hsp70 system cooperation is reasonable but the specific downstream signaling roles (MAPK cascades, cell cycle) are entirely absent from the reasoning, which stays at the level of generic chaperone biology.
