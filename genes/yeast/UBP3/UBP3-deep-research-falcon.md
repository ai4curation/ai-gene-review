## Research Report: Functional Annotation of *Saccharomyces cerevisiae* **UBP3** (UniProt **Q01477**, ORF **YER151C**)

### 0) Target verification (critical identity checks)
The evidence assembled here consistently refers to **Ubp3** from *Saccharomyces cerevisiae* (budding yeast; strain background varies by study), a **deubiquitinase (DUB)** that functions in a stable complex with **Bre5** and is experimentally linked to deubiquitination of trafficking and ribosomal substrates (e.g., Sec23; ribosomal eS7A) and to processes including ER–Golgi trafficking, selective autophagy, and UPR-linked translational control (cohen2003deubiquitinationanew pages 1-1, 稲田2024grr1mediatedubp3degradation pages 1-5). This matches the UniProt-provided protein description “ubiquitin carboxyl-terminal hydrolase 3 / ubiquitin-specific-processing protease 3” for **UniProt Q01477**.

---

### 1) Key concepts and definitions (current understanding)

#### 1.1 Deubiquitinases (DUBs) and Ubp3’s enzymatic role
DUBs remove ubiquitin from ubiquitinated proteins, thereby reversing or editing ubiquitin signals that control protein fate, localization, trafficking, and pathway signaling. A yeast-focused review synthesizes Ubp3 as an **active DUB** with multiple experimentally supported roles and emphasizes that Ubp3 activity is typically deployed via **recruitment and cofactor control** rather than acting as a constitutive “housekeeping” enzyme (suresh2020thestructureand pages 17-18).

#### 1.2 Ubp3–Bre5 as a functional DUB complex
A core principle for Ubp3 biology is that it acts in a **Ubp3·Bre5 complex**. In the ER–Golgi trafficking context, Bre5 is described as an **essential positive regulator** of Ubp3’s activity (not merely a passive binding partner), consistent with a model where cofactors tune DUB substrate specificity and/or activity (cohen2003deubiquitinationanew pages 1-1).

---

### 2) Molecular function: reaction catalyzed and substrate specificity

#### 2.1 Reaction catalyzed
Ubp3 catalyzes **deubiquitination**—hydrolysis of the isopeptide bond linking ubiquitin to a substrate lysine (or the corresponding bond types on ubiquitin chains)—thereby removing ubiquitin from target proteins (suresh2020thestructureand pages 17-18).

#### 2.2 Experimentally supported substrates and substrate contexts
**(A) COPII coat subunit Sec23 (ER-to-Golgi trafficking substrate).**
A primary study reports that Ubp3, with Bre5, specifically deubiquitinates the COPII subunit **Sec23**, and in that report Ubp3 is described as **the only yeast Ubp** able to catalyze Sec23 deubiquitination (cohen2003deubiquitinationanew pages 1-1). This directly anchors Ubp3 in the COPII pathway and connects its enzymatic activity to vesicle-coat regulation at the ER.

**(B) COPI subunit (Golgi-to-ER retrograde trafficking substrate).**
The same work identifies a COPI subunit (β′-COP noted as a substrate) as another Ubp3·Bre5 target, consistent with Ubp3 coordinating both anterograde and retrograde trafficking between ER and Golgi (cohen2003deubiquitinationanew pages 1-1).

**(C) Ribosomal ubiquitination targets: eS7A in UPR-linked translation control.**
A 2024 preprint presents Ubp3 as the deubiquitinase that counteracts ubiquitination of ribosomal protein **eS7A**, placing Ubp3 activity directly in **ribosome ubiquitination cycles** that regulate stress-responsive translation (稲田2024grr1mediatedubp3degradation pages 1-5).

**(D) Ribosome/60S-associated ubiquitin signals in ribophagy.**
Ribophagy (selective autophagy of ribosomes) requires Ubp3–Bre5; Ubp3 is positioned as a DUB that removes an inhibitory ubiquitin signal from ribosomal components to permit delivery to the vacuole during starvation (ossarehnazari2014ubiquitylationbythe pages 9-9).

**(E) Additional functional substrate areas supported by literature synthesis.**
A yeast DUB review and a JBC study summarizing Ubp3 literature link Ubp3 to additional substrates/contexts (e.g., RNA polymerase II deubiquitination; proteasome-linked degradation control) while emphasizing Bre5 and other partners that tune Ubp3 function across pathways (suresh2020thestructureand pages 17-18, lan2021deubiquitinaseubp3enhances pages 13-13).

---

### 3) Key binding partners and regulators

#### 3.1 Bre5 (core cofactor)
Bre5 is repeatedly supported as Ubp3’s key partner required for specific deubiquitination events, including Sec23/COPII regulation and ribosome-linked pathways (cohen2003deubiquitinationanew pages 1-1, 稲田2024grr1mediatedubp3degradation pages 1-5).

#### 3.2 Grr1 (E3 ligase component regulating Ubp3 abundance during UPR; 2024)
A 2024 preprint identifies **Grr1**, an F-box component of the SCF ubiquitin ligase complex, as an E3 factor required for **Ubp3 proteasomal degradation** during ER stress, which increases eS7A monoubiquitination and enables robust translation of the spliced **HAC1i** mRNA (稲田2024grr1mediatedubp3degradation pages 1-5, 稲田2024grr1mediatedubp3degradation pages 5-7).

#### 3.3 Not4 (E3 ligase for eS7A ubiquitination)
The same 2024 preprint positions Not4 as the E3 ligase that monoubiquitinates eS7A, with Ubp3 acting in opposition (as the DUB) and Grr1 acting upstream to reduce Ubp3 levels under UPR (稲田2024grr1mediatedubp3degradation pages 1-5).

#### 3.4 Antagonistic ubiquitin-system components in DNA damage responses
In the DNA damage context, genetic evidence supports an antagonistic relationship between Ubp3/Bre5 and the **Rsp5/Bul1** ligase pathway with respect to phleomycin sensitivity and NHEJ (bilsland2007thebre5ubp3ubiquitin pages 11-12, bilsland2007thebre5ubp3ubiquitin pages 9-11).

#### 4.1 ER–Golgi trafficking (COPII/COPI coat regulation)
Ubp3·Bre5 deubiquitinates **Sec23 (COPII)** and a **COPI subunit**, and Bre5 disruption produces a Golgi-to-ER retrograde transport defect, implying that Ubp3 functionally operates at ER exit/trafficking interfaces and in ER–Golgi bidirectional transport regulation (cohen2003deubiquitinationanew pages 1-1). These substrates provide the strongest direct anchoring of Ubp3 to a specific subcellular “worksite” (ER–Golgi transport machinery).

#### 4.2 Ribophagy (selective autophagy of ribosomes)
Ubp3–Bre5 is required for starvation-induced ribophagy; ubiquitination of ribosomes inhibits ribophagy, and Ubp3–Bre5 deubiquitination is required to set the effective rate of starvation-induced ribosomal protein degradation via ribophagy (ossarehnazari2014ubiquitylationbythe pages 9-9). This places Ubp3 function in a cytosolic-to-vacuolar selective autophagy pathway.

#### 4.3 Mitophagy (negative regulator)
A genome-wide screen-based study identifies Ubp3–Bre5 as a **negative regulator of rapamycin-induced mitophagy**, while also connecting the complex to bulk autophagy and other autophagic routes (muller2015syntheticquantitativearray pages 9-10). This supports a model where Ubp3–Bre5 edits ubiquitin signals that, depending on context and substrate, can either permit or restrain selective organelle turnover.

#### 4.4 DNA damage response: double-strand break repair by NHEJ
A primary DNA repair study reports that **ubp3** and **bre5** mutants are sensitive to the DSB-inducing drug **phleomycin** and display NHEJ defects; importantly, Ubp3 catalytic activity is required (a catalytic point mutant cannot complement), connecting enzymatic activity to the phenotype (bilsland2007thebre5ubp3ubiquitin pages 9-11). The work also supports pathway-level antagonism between Ubp3/Bre5 and Bul1/Rsp5 (bilsland2007thebre5ubp3ubiquitin pages 11-12).

#### 4.5 Unfolded protein response (UPR): translational control via ribosome ubiquitination (2024)
A 2024 preprint proposes a detailed regulatory model: during UPR, **SCF^Grr1 promotes Ubp3 degradation**, raising **monoubiquitinated eS7A** (produced by Not4), which facilitates efficient translation of **HAC1i** mRNA and thus UPR output (稲田2024grr1mediatedubp3degradation pages 1-5, 稲田2024grr1mediatedubp3degradation pages 5-7). This provides a direct mechanistic role for Ubp3 in an ER-stress signaling pathway at the level of **translation**.

#### 4.6 Glucose repression/mitochondrial repression via phosphate budgeting (eLife 2024)
A 2024 *eLife* study identifies Ubp3 in a DUB knockout screen as necessary for glucose-mediated mitochondrial repression (Crabtree-like behavior). Loss of Ubp3 rewires metabolism (lower Pfk1 and GAPDH/Tdh2-Tdh3; increased trehalose flux) and changes **inorganic phosphate (Pi) budgeting** between glycolysis and mitochondria, enabling mitochondrial activation despite high glucose (vengayil2024thedeubiquitinaseubp3usp10 pages 1-2, vengayil2024thedeubiquitinaseubp3usp10 pages 6-7).

### 5) Recent developments and latest research (prioritizing 2023–2024)

#### 5.1 Ubp3 as a regulator of glucose-mediated mitochondrial repression (publication date: May 2024)
Vengayil et al. (*eLife*, 2024-05; https://doi.org/10.7554/elife.90293) report that Ubp3 catalytic activity is required for mitochondrial repression in high glucose (supported by a catalytic mutant analysis) and propose that Ubp3 controls mitochondrial activity indirectly via **Pi availability** by governing glycolytic flux and trehalose synthesis (vengayil2024thedeubiquitinaseubp3usp10 pages 3-4, vengayil2024thedeubiquitinaseubp3usp10 pages 17-18). The study further reports this phenotype is reproducible across multiple yeast genetic backgrounds (vengayil2024thedeubiquitinaseubp3usp10 pages 15-17).

#### 5.2 Ubp3 degradation as a UPR control point (publication date: Aug 2024 preprint)
Inada et al. (preprint, 2024-08; https://doi.org/10.21203/rs.3.rs-4865151/v1) introduce a regulatory axis in which the E3 ligase component **Grr1** promotes Ubp3 degradation during ER stress to increase ribosome monoubiquitination (eS7A) and enable translation of the spliced HAC1 mRNA (稲田2024grr1mediatedubp3degradation pages 5-7, 稲田2024grr1mediatedubp3degradation pages 18-24). This reframes Ubp3 not only as an enzyme acting on substrates but also as a **regulated node** whose abundance is dynamically tuned to remodel translation under stress.

#### 5.3 2023 pathway framing: ubiquitin-dependent selective autophagy in yeast
While not Ubp3-centric, 2023 commentary/review literature on ubiquitin-binding autophagy receptors in yeast emphasizes that ubiquitin can drive selective autophagy through multiple receptor systems, motivating ongoing exploration of how ubiquitin editing enzymes (including DUBs) determine whether ubiquitinated cargo is degraded by proteasome or autophagy (Mensah et al., 2023; see retrieved paper metadata). In the current evidence base, Ubp3’s direct involvement is best supported for ribophagy/mitophagy and not yet mapped to specific ubiquitin-binding receptors (context not directly extracted here).

### 6) Subcellular localization: where Ubp3 acts
No single dedicated localization study was retrieved in the current evidence set; however, **substrate- and pathway-anchored localization** can be inferred with high confidence:

* **ER/Golgi trafficking interfaces**: Ubp3 acts on **COPII Sec23** and a **COPI subunit**, placing its action at vesicle coat/trafficking machinery between ER and Golgi (cohen2003deubiquitinationanew pages 1-1).
* **Cytosolic ribosome-associated contexts**: Ubp3–Bre5 acts in ribophagy and ribosome ubiquitination cycles (e.g., eS7A), consistent with action on ribosomes prior to vacuolar delivery or during translational control (ossarehnazari2014ubiquitylationbythe pages 9-9, 稲田2024grr1mediatedubp3degradation pages 1-5).
* **Mitochondria-linked regulation**: Ubp3 impacts mitochondrial respiration under high glucose through metabolic/Pi budgeting mechanisms; while not proving mitochondrial residency of Ubp3, the functional readouts are mitochondrial (OCR, Cox2, membrane potential) (vengayil2024thedeubiquitinaseubp3usp10 pages 3-4).

### 7) Expert opinions and analysis (authoritative synthesis)
A yeast-centered DUB review highlights Ubp3 as an example of a multifunctional DUB whose pathway outputs (ribophagy, stress granules, trafficking) depend on **cofactor recruitment (Bre5)** and context-specific deployment, aligning with a broader principle that DUB specificity is often “built” by interaction networks rather than by catalytic domain sequence alone (suresh2020thestructureand pages 17-18). The early trafficking-focused primary work similarly emphasizes Bre5 as an essential positive regulator of Ubp3 activity, reinforcing this mechanistic concept (cohen2003deubiquitinationanew pages 1-1).

### 8) Statistics, data, and quantitative findings (recent and/or mechanistically informative)

#### 8.1 UPR translation control (2024 preprint)
* **HAC1 translation efficiency (TE)** is reported to be **~4-fold lower** in **grr1Δ** versus WT, linking Grr1-dependent Ubp3 regulation to translational output (稲田2024grr1mediatedubp3degradation pages 7-10, 稲田2024grr1mediatedubp3degradation pages 5-7).
* Under ER stress, Ubp3 stability changes: CHX chase estimates an Ubp3 half-life of **~80 min with stress vs ~120 min without**, and Ubp3 stabilization is observed with MG132 or in grr1Δ, consistent with proteasomal degradation (稲田2024grr1mediatedubp3degradation pages 5-7).

#### 8.2 Glucose/mitochondrial repression via Pi budgeting (eLife 2024)
* ubp3Δ increases basal respiration and mitochondrial activation measures; quantitative reporting includes replicate counts and statistical thresholds (often **n=3** biological replicates; significance annotations *p*<0.05 to ***p*<0.001) (vengayil2024thedeubiquitinaseubp3usp10 pages 15-17, vengayil2024thedeubiquitinaseubp3usp10 pages 6-7).
* ATP partitioning experiment: after sodium azide treatment, ATP levels correspond to **~60%** (WT) vs **~40%** (ubp3Δ) of total ATP, interpreted as a higher mitochondrial contribution in ubp3Δ (vengayil2024thedeubiquitinaseubp3usp10 pages 3-4).

#### 8.3 DNA damage/NHEJ studies (2007)
Quantitative experimental design is described for NHEJ/plasmid repair assays (e.g., WT set to 100% repaired; multiple replicates), though explicit numeric survival values were not extractable from the provided excerpt (bilsland2007thebre5ubp3ubiquitin pages 1-2).

### 9) Current applications and real-world implementations

1. **Yeast as a model for conserved deubiquitinase biology.** Ubp3 is discussed as orthologous to mammalian **USP10** in a 2024 metabolic repression study, enabling cross-species hypothesis generation about DUB control of metabolism and mitochondrial activity (Vengayil et al., *eLife*, 2024-05; https://doi.org/10.7554/elife.90293) (vengayil2024thedeubiquitinaseubp3usp10 pages 1-2).
2. **Mechanistic dissection of stress responses relevant to ER stress and proteostasis.** The Ubp3→eS7A ubiquitination→HAC1 translation axis provides a genetically tractable framework for studying how ubiquitin modifications on ribosomes reshape translation during UPR (Inada et al., 2024-08 preprint; https://doi.org/10.21203/rs.3.rs-4865151/v1) (稲田2024grr1mediatedubp3degradation pages 1-5, 稲田2024grr1mediatedubp3degradation pages 5-7).
3. **Trafficking/secretory pathway regulation.** Ubp3–Bre5 modulation of COPII/COPI coat ubiquitination provides an experimental handle for probing how ubiquitin editing controls vesicle formation and ER–Golgi homeostasis—important both for fundamental cell biology and for optimizing secretion-related phenotypes in yeast-based production systems (cohen2003deubiquitinationanew pages 1-1).

### 10) Consolidated functional annotation (high-confidence statements)
* **Primary biochemical function:** Ubp3 is a **deubiquitinase** that removes ubiquitin from protein substrates (suresh2020thestructureand pages 17-18).
* **Core complex and specificity determinant:** Ubp3 functions with cofactor **Bre5**, an essential positive regulator required for key substrate deubiquitination (cohen2003deubiquitinationanew pages 1-1).
* **Best-supported direct substrates:** COPII **Sec23** (and a COPI subunit in retrograde trafficking) (cohen2003deubiquitinationanew pages 1-1) and ribosomal protein **eS7A** in UPR-linked translation control (稲田2024grr1mediatedubp3degradation pages 1-5).
* **Best-supported processes:** ER–Golgi trafficking regulation (cohen2003deubiquitinationanew pages 1-1), ribophagy (ossarehnazari2014ubiquitylationbythe pages 9-9), mitophagy modulation (muller2015syntheticquantitativearray pages 9-10), DNA DSB repair via NHEJ (bilsland2007thebre5ubp3ubiquitin pages 9-11), and glucose-mediated mitochondrial repression via Pi budgeting (vengayil2024thedeubiquitinaseubp3usp10 pages 3-4).
