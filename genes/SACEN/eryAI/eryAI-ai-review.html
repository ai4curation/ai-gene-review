Gene Description

EryAI (DEBS1) is the first subunit of the 6-deoxyerythronolide B synthase (DEBS), the modular type I polyketide synthase that assembles the macrolactone core of erythromycin in Saccharopolyspora erythraea (EC 2.3.1.94). DEBS comprises three giant multimodular polypeptides (EryAI/AII/AIII); EryAI contains the loading didomain plus extension modules 1 and 2. Each module is a set of catalytic domains - ketosynthase (KS), acyltransferase (AT, which selects the (2S)-methylmalonyl-CoA extender), ketoreductase (KR), and acyl carrier protein (ACP, phosphopantetheinylated) - that carry out one round of FAS-like decarboxylative chain extension and reduction, building two of the six extension cycles that yield 6-deoxyerythronolide B. As the chain-initiating subunit, EryAI sits at the entry point of the DEBS assembly line, the textbook example of modular polyketide biosynthesis.

Core Functions

First subunit (DEBS1) of the modular type I polyketide synthase 6-deoxyerythronolide B synthase (EC 2.3.1.94); contains the loading didomain and extension modules 1-2 (KS/AT/KR/ACP), carrying out the first chain-extension cycles that build the erythromycin macrolactone. Component activities include polyketide ketosynthase, (methyl)malonyl-CoA acyltransferase (AT), ketoreductase, and ACP phosphopantetheine carrier.
