Gene Description

LOC100829928 encodes a predicted phenylalanine ammonia-lyase, EC 4.3.1.24, in Brachypodium distachyon. The enzyme converts L-phenylalanine to trans-cinnamate, the entry reaction into phenylpropanoid metabolism.

Evidence synthesis

LOC100829928 is annotated as phenylalanine ammonia-lyase with EC 4.3.1.24 [file:BRADI/LOC100829928/LOC100829928-uniprot.txt "RecName: Full=Phenylalanine ammonia-lyase; EC=4.3.1.24"]. UniProt gives the reaction L-phenylalanine to trans-cinnamate plus ammonium and maps the protein to trans-cinnamate biosynthesis in phenylpropanoid metabolism [file:BRADI/LOC100829928/LOC100829928-uniprot.txt "L-phenylalanine = (E)-cinnamate + NH4(+)"; "PATHWAY: Phenylpropanoid metabolism; trans-cinnamate biosynthesis; (E)-cinnamate from L-phenylalanine"].

The family evidence supports PAL rather than only a generic ammonia-lyase call: InterPro includes Phe_NH3-lyase and NCBIfam TIGR01226 phe_am_lyase, while the broader PAL/histidase/PANTHER histidine ammonia-lyase relationship explains evolutionary homology among aromatic ammonia-lyases [file:BRADI/LOC100829928/LOC100829928-uniprot.txt "InterPro; IPR005922; Phe_NH3-lyase"; "NCBIfam; TIGR01226; phe_am_lyase; 1"; "PANTHER; PTHR10362; HISTIDINE AMMONIA-LYASE; 1"].

Curation consequence

Accept phenylalanine ammonia-lyase activity, cinnamic acid biosynthetic process, and phenylpropanoid biosynthetic process. Keep generic catalytic and ammonia-lyase parent terms as non-core.
