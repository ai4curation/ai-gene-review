CC   -!- FUNCTION: Inorganic ion transporter that mediates phosphate ion export
CC       across the cell membrane (PubMed:37138087). Plays a major role in
CC       phosphate homeostasis, preventing intracellular phosphate accumulation
CC       and possible calcium phosphate precipitation, ultimately preserving
CC       calcium signaling (PubMed:37138087). Binds inositol hexakisphosphate
CC       (Ins6P) and similar inositol polyphosphates, such as 5-diphospho-
CC       inositol pentakisphosphate (5-InsP7), which are important intracellular
CC       signaling molecules involved in regulation of phosphate flux (By
CC       similarity). In enterocytes and differentiating progenitors of the gut,
CC       promotes the biogenesis and maintenance of organelles called PXo bodies
CC       that store intracellular inorganic phosphate (Pi), and also regulates
CC       Cka-JNK mediated tissue homeostasis in response to Pi availability in
CC       these tissues (PubMed:37138087). Under conditions of adequate Pi,
CC       transports Pi into PXo bodies which convert and store the Pi in the
CC       form of phospholipids (PubMed:37138087). It also inhibits Cka at the
CC       post-transcriptional level to prevent Cka-bsk/JNK mediated cell
CC       proliferation (PubMed:37138087). Upon Pi starvation, Pxo expression is
CC       down-regulated resulting in the PXo bodies decreasing in phospholipid
CC       content until they undergo lysosomal/autophagosomal degradation and
CC       release the stored Pi back into the cytosol for use by the cell
CC       (PubMed:37138087). Decrease in Pxo expression also activates the Cka
CC       protein, which moves to the nucleus to activate bsk/JNK which then
CC       induces nearby progenitor cells to proliferate and form new absorptive
CC       cells, probably helping the organism to cope with the nutrient
CC       deficiency by maximizing absorption of dietary Pi (PubMed:37138087).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9UBH6, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9Z0U0,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:37138087}.

CC       pass membrane protein {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9UBH6}. Note=Localizes to
CC       the membrane of multi-membrane-layered organelles called PXo bodies,
CC       that function as intracellular reserves of inorganic phosphate stored
CC       in the form of phospholipids. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:37138087}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Detected in PXo bodies found in the enterocytes and
CC       progenitors of the midgut and in the hindgut, but rarely occur in the
CC       Malpighian tubules, crop, brain, muscles and germlines (at protein
CC       level). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:37138087}.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: Down-regulated in the midgut following inorganic phosphate
CC       starvation. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:37138087}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The SPX domain plays a role in the regulation of phosphate flux
CC       (By similarity). Inositol hexakisphosphate (Ins6P) is bound between two
CC       SPX domains of the homodimer (By similarity). The SPX domain has high
CC       affinity for inositol polyphosphates and its affinity for inorganic
CC       phosphate is two to three orders of magnitude lower (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9UBH6}.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: RNAi-mediated knockdown results in various
CC       phenotypes that also occur in flies undergoing inorganic phosphate
CC       starvation, including activation of midgut hyperproliferation and
CC       activation of cell mitosis (PubMed:37138087). RNAi-mediated knockdown
CC       in either enterocytes or progenitors, induces mitosis under both under
CC       normal conditions and with bleomycin-induced tissue damage
CC       (PubMed:37138087). Enterocytes but not progenitors, display increased
CC       levels of cytosolic inorganic phosphate (PubMed:37138087). RNAi-
CC       mediated knockdown in enterocytes does not result in an increase in
CC       apoptosis (PubMed:37138087). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:37138087}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the SYG1 (TC 2.A.94) family. {ECO:0000305}.
