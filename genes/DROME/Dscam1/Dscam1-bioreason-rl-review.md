## Functional Summary Review

The BioReason summary states:

> A cell-surface adhesion receptor in fruit fly that presents a long extracellular array of immunoglobulin and fibronectin type III modules to mediate high-specificity cell-cell adhesion and recognition. Its architecture supports homophilic binding and clustering at the cell surface, while a specialized cytoplasmic tail couples adhesion to intracellular signaling and actin remodeling. Together these features position the protein at the plasma membrane where it stabilizes cell contacts and coordinates adhesion-dependent morphogenetic programs.

While the structural description is reasonable, the functional interpretation fundamentally mischaracterizes Dscam1 biology:

1. **Adhesion vs. self-avoidance**: The summary frames Dscam1 as a protein that "stabilizes cell contacts" and coordinates "adhesion-dependent morphogenetic programs." The curated review is explicit that Dscam1's "primary function is actually in homophilic repulsion and self-avoidance rather than adhesion." Isoform-specific homophilic binding leads to **repulsion** between sister dendrites and axons, not stable adhesion. The curated core functions list dendrite self-avoidance (GO:0070593) as a primary process.

2. **Extraordinary molecular diversity omitted**: Dscam1's defining feature -- 38,016 potential isoforms generated by alternative splicing, enabling molecular self-recognition -- is completely absent from the summary. The curated review states: "Cell adhesion molecule with extraordinary molecular diversity (38,016 potential isoforms) that mediates isoform-specific homophilic binding for self-recognition."

3. **Immune function absent**: The curated review documents that Dscam1 has secreted isoforms that function in immune recognition by binding bacteria in the hemolymph (PMID:16109846). BioReason mentions only the cell-surface form.

4. **Axon guidance receptor activity missing**: The curated core functions include axon guidance receptor activity (GO:0008046), with signaling via the Dock/Pak pathway. BioReason vaguely mentions "actin remodeling" but does not identify axon guidance.

Comparison with interpro2go:

The ai-review.yaml does not contain GO_REF:0000002 annotations for Dscam1. BioReason's reasoning from Ig/FNIII/DSCAM domains correctly identifies the cell-surface receptor topology and homophilic binding capability, but then defaults to a generic "adhesion" interpretation. This is the same error pattern as the IEA annotation for cell adhesion (GO:0007155) that the curated review flags as needing modification -- interpro2go-level reasoning correctly identifies adhesion-related domains but cannot distinguish adhesion from repulsion/self-avoidance.
