Gene Description

Buffy is a Drosophila melanogaster Bcl-2 family protein that functions primarily as an anti-apoptotic regulator. Unlike its paralog Debcl (the pro-apoptotic Bcl-2 family member), Buffy predominantly localizes to the endoplasmic reticulum via a C-terminal membrane anchor and antagonizes Debcl-driven mitochondrial apoptosis. Buffy modulates the Debcl-Drp1 axis that regulates mitochondrial fission, ROS generation, and JNK-mediated caspase activation. While primarily pro-survival in vivo, Buffy exhibits context-dependent activity and can promote cell death in specific contexts such as microchaete glial cell death. Buffy also participates in autophagy regulation during starvation responses.
