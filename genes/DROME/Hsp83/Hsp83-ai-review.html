Gene Description

Hsp83 is the sole Drosophila melanogaster member of the HSP90 family of ATP-dependent molecular chaperones. It promotes the maturation, structural maintenance, and proper regulation of specific client proteins involved in cell cycle control, signal transduction, piRNA biogenesis, and centrosome function. Hsp83 undergoes a functional ATPase cycle that induces conformational changes in client proteins, thereby causing their activation. It interacts dynamically with various co-chaperones (Hop, Shu, Dpit47, Morgana) that modulate its substrate recognition, ATPase cycle, and chaperone function. In addition to its canonical chaperone role, Hsp83 has a non-chaperone membrane-deforming function mediated by an amphipathic helix that promotes exosome release. Hsp83 is constitutively expressed and further induced by heat stress; it is also expressed during oogenesis and plays roles in developmental processes including centrosome cycle, cell cycle exit, mRNA localization, and sleep regulation.

Core Functions

The primary molecular function of Hsp83. As a member of the HSP90 family, Hsp83 binds near-native client proteins and uses its intrinsic ATPase activity to drive conformational changes that promote client maturation, activation, and stabilization. Known clients include kinases (insulin receptor, LKB1), transcription factors, Argonaute, APC/C components (Cdh1/Fzr), and centrosomal proteins.
