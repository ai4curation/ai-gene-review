CC   -!- FUNCTION: Blue light-dependent regulator that is the input of the
CC       circadian feedback loop (PubMed:10063806, PubMed:10233998,
CC       PubMed:10417378, PubMed:16527739, PubMed:17298948, PubMed:18597555,
CC       PubMed:36994075, PubMed:9845369). Has no photolyase activity for
CC       cyclobutane pyrimidine dimers or 6-4 photoproducts (PubMed:10063806).
CC       Regulation of expression by light suggests a role in photoreception for
CC       locomotor activity rhythms (PubMed:10063806, PubMed:10233998,
CC       PubMed:9845369). Functions, together with per, as a transcriptional
CC       repressor required for the oscillation of peripheral circadian clocks
CC       and for the correct specification of clock cells (PubMed:10417378,
CC       PubMed:16527739, PubMed:36994075, PubMed:9845370). Genes directly
CC       activated by the transcription factors Clock (Clk) and cycle (cyc) are
CC       repressed by cry (PubMed:10417378, PubMed:16527739). Necessary for
CC       light-dependent magnetosensitivity, an intact circadian system is not
CC       required for the magnetoreception mechanism to operate
CC       (PubMed:18641630). Required for both the naive and trained responses to
CC       magnetic field, consistent with the notion that cry is in the input
CC       pathway of magnetic sensing (PubMed:18641630).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10063806, ECO:0000269|PubMed:10233998,

CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Expressed at higher levels in the head than in body
CC       and it is more expressed in antennae than in legs, wings and mouth
CC       appendages. Prominent expression is seen in cells of the lateral brain,
CC       which are close to or coincident with the clock neurons. Abundance
CC       oscillates in a circadian manner. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10063806,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:10233998, ECO:0000269|PubMed:9845369}.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: Expression is regulated by light and circadian rhythms.
CC       Under circadian regulation, expression is influenced by the clock
CC       pacemaker genes period, timeless, Clock and cycle.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:9845369}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: FAD-binding region regulates cry stability, cry-tim
CC       interaction, and circadian photosensitivity.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15258584}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: Photolyase/cryptochrome alpha/beta domain is sufficient for
CC       light detection and phototransduction.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: Flies exhibit poor synchronization to light-dark

CC       not within certain pacemaker neurons of adult brain.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:9845370}.
CC   -!- MISCELLANEOUS: Unstable upon light exposure. Light induces the
