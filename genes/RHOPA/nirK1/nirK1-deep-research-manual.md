# Manual Deep Research: nirK1

Automated Falcon and OpenScientist runs did not complete for nirK1, so this is a manual synthesis from the fetched UniProt record and the completed multicopper-oxidase family research.

## Evidence synthesis

nirK1 is annotated in UniProt as a copper-containing nitrite reductase with EC 1.7.2.1 [file:RHOPA/nirK1/nirK1-uniprot.txt "RecName: Full=Copper-containing nitrite reductase; EC=1.7.2.1"]. The record places the protein in nitrate reduction by denitrification, step 2 of 4, which is the nitrite-to-nitric-oxide step [file:RHOPA/nirK1/nirK1-uniprot.txt "PATHWAY: Nitrogen metabolism; nitrate reduction (denitrification); dinitrogen from nitrate: step 2/4"].

Domain and family evidence supports the same molecular interpretation: the protein has NO2-reductase_Cu, cupredoxin, and TIGR02376 copper nitrite reductase signatures [file:RHOPA/nirK1/nirK1-uniprot.txt "InterPro; IPR001287; NO2-reductase_Cu"; "NCBIfam; TIGR02376; Cu_nitrite_red; 1"]. The multicopper-oxidase family is broad, so the activity should not be inferred from multicopper membership alone; for nirK1, the specific EC and NO2-reductase_Cu/TIGR evidence make the NO-forming nitrite reductase call strong.

## Curation consequence

Accept NO-forming nitrite reductase activity and denitrification pathway for nirK1. The evidence is conserved enzyme/family inference and UniProt pathway mapping, not direct strain-specific biochemistry.
