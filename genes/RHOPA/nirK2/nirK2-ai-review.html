Research Report: Functional Annotation of nirK2 (UniProt Q6N2A5; locus RPA4145) in Rhodopseudomonas palustris CGA009

1. Target verification (gene/protein identity)

The research target is nirK2 / RPA4145 from Rhodopseudomonas palustris strain CGA009, corresponding to a copper-containing nitrite reductase (NirK; EC 1.7.2.1) as described in UniProt. Independent literature supports that R. palustris CGA009 encodes a nitrite reductase and that its NirK sequence is included in comparative NirK analyses with conserved copper-nitrite-reductase motifs, consistent with a multicopper oxidase-family CuNiR. (lee2002taxisresponseof pages 2-2, lee2002taxisresponseof pages 4-5, cantera2007moleculardiversityof pages 4-8)

2. Key concepts and definitions (current understanding)

2.1 Denitrification step catalyzed by NirK

Copper-containing nitrite reductase NirK (CuNiR/Cu-NIR) catalyzes the key denitrification transformation nitrite (NO2−) → nitric oxide (NO), a central NO-forming step in nitrogen-oxide respiration. (cua2010expressionofgenes pages 37-42)

2.2 Enzyme class and redundancy with NirS

Two evolutionarily unrelated enzymes can perform NO-forming nitrite reduction: NirK (multicopper oxidase-family) and NirS (cytochrome cd1). They are often treated as functionally redundant for denitrification, and are rarely encoded together in the same genome. (pold2024phylogeneticsandenvironmental pages 1-2, cua2010expressionofgenes pages 37-42)

2.3 Structural organization, cofactors, and active site architecture

CuNiRs are homotrimeric enzymes; each monomer contains two copper centers: a type 1 copper (T1Cu) redox center that accepts electrons from a partner protein, and a type 2 copper (T2Cu) catalytic center where nitrite binds and is reduced to NO. (rose2023spectroscopicallyvalidatedmultiple pages 1-3, cua2010expressionofgenes pages 37-42)

2.4 Mechanistic model: electron and proton transfer

High-resolution structural/spectroscopic work supports a mechanism in which nitrite binds at T2Cu (often described as a side-on binding mode), and reduction proceeds via proton-coupled electron transfer (PCET) from T1Cu to T2Cu through a Cys–His bridge, with catalytic residues (including conserved His/Asp) supporting proton delivery and gating. (rose2023spectroscopicallyvalidatedmultiple pages 1-3, rose2023spectroscopicallyvalidatedmultiple pages 3-5, rose2023spectroscopicallyvalidatedmultiple pages 12-14)

2.5 Cellular localization

A widely cited property of Cu-NIR/NirK is periplasmic localization in Gram-negative bacteria (extracytoplasmic nitrite reduction coupled to periplasmic electron transfer chains). The most defensible localization for R. palustris CGA009 NirK2 is therefore periplasmic, although a strain-specific localization experiment for RPA4145 was not retrieved in the available sources. (cua2010expressionofgenes pages 37-42)

3. Organism-specific functional context in R. palustris CGA009

3.1 Evidence for nitrite-reductase activity and associated NOx metabolism potential

In a study explicitly using R. palustris CGA009, genome-sequencing information was reported to indicate that this strain encodes nitrite reductase and genes necessary for expression of nitric oxide reductase, but has no obvious ortholog of a dissimilatory nitrate reductase. (lee2002taxisresponseof pages 2-2)

Consistent with this genetic potential, R. palustris CGA009 cells grown microaerobically in the presence of nitrite showed detectable nitrite reductase activity and exhibited taxis toward nitrite (behavior interpreted as accumulation in regions permissive for nitrite respiration). (lee2002taxisresponseof pages 4-5, lee2002taxisresponseof pages 5-7)

Functional implication for nirK2/Q6N2A5: the strongest organism-specific evidence supports physiological nitrite reduction capacity in CGA009 and positions NirK-type nitrite reductase activity as a likely contributor to NO formation and NOx-responsive physiology under low-oxygen + nitrite conditions. (lee2002taxisresponseof pages 4-5, lee2002taxisresponseof pages 2-2)

3.2 Substrate specificity

The enzyme class definition and mechanistic evidence support nitrite (NO2−) as the substrate at the catalytic T2Cu site, with product NO. No evidence in the retrieved CGA009-specific literature suggested an alternative substrate specificity for NirK2 beyond canonical nitrite reduction. (rose2023spectroscopicallyvalidatedmultiple pages 1-3, cua2010expressionofgenes pages 37-42, lee2002taxisresponseof pages 4-5)

4. Regulation and pathway integration (expert synthesis)

4.1 NO-responsive transcriptional control context

Comparative promoter analyses of nirK emphasize that Alphaproteobacterial nirK genes frequently show motifs associated with NnrR-family NO-responsive regulation, and note that in Rhodobacter sphaeroides NnrR requires NO as an effector to induce nirK expression. (cantera2007moleculardiversityof pages 9-10, cantera2007moleculardiversityof pages 4-8)

Interpretation for R. palustris nirK2: while direct promoter mapping/TF-binding experiments for RPA4145 were not retrieved here, the combination of (i) CGA009 encoding nitrite reductase and NO reductase genes and (ii) the broader Alphaproteobacterial association of nirK with NnrR-like NO-responsive control supports the working hypothesis that nirK2 participates in an NOx-responsive regulatory module (often centered around NnrR-like transcription factors) that couples nitrite reduction and downstream NO metabolism. (lee2002taxisresponseof pages 2-2, cantera2007moleculardiversityof pages 9-10)

4.2 Genomic neighborhood (limits of current evidence)

Across bacteria, nirK genes can occur in operonic contexts that include cytochrome c and c-type cytochrome biogenesis genes or other redox partners, consistent with periplasmic electron transfer requirements. However, a direct operon map for RPA4145 was not recoverable from the available excerpts; therefore, neighborhood statements for R. palustris nirK2 should be treated as inference from common NirK contexts rather than demonstrated facts for this locus. (cantera2007moleculardiversityof pages 8-9, starkenburg2008aninvestigationof pages 31-34)

5. Recent developments (prioritizing 2023–2024)

5.1 Structural and mechanistic advances (2023)

A 2023 crystallography/spectroscopy study used MSOX serial crystallography with in-crystal optical spectroscopy (and referenced damage-reduced/XFEL models) to connect T1Cu redox changes to catalytic chemistry at T2Cu. Key recent mechanistic insights include:
- Explicit two-copper-site description (T1Cu electron-entry; T2Cu nitrite-binding/catalytic center). (rose2023spectroscopicallyvalidatedmultiple pages 1-3)
- pH dependence of activity via proton availability and PCET efficiency, including changes in proton-channel water ordering and catalytic Asp behavior. (rose2023spectroscopicallyvalidatedmultiple pages 3-5, rose2023spectroscopicallyvalidatedmultiple pages 12-14)
These results refine contemporary understanding of how CuNiRs couple electron transfer to proton delivery during nitrite-to-NO reduction. (rose2023spectroscopicallyvalidatedmultiple pages 1-3, rose2023spectroscopicallyvalidatedmultiple pages 12-14)

Publication: Rose et al., 2023-08, Acta Crystallographica Section A, DOI/URL: https://doi.org/10.1107/S2053273323094627. (rose2023spectroscopicallyvalidatedmultiple pages 1-3)

5.2 Ecological/phylogenomic reframing of NirK vs NirS (2024)

A 2024 study built robust phylogenies of NirK and NirS from 6,973 isolate and metagenome-assembled genomes, identified 32 clades of structurally distinct lineages, and mapped relative abundances across 4,082 metagenomes from diverse biomes. This work shows that:
- Nir phylogeny can reflect ecology and functional gene associations differently from organismal phylogeny, suggesting convergent/independent niche associations for nitrite reduction capacity. (pold2024phylogeneticsandenvironmental pages 1-2)
- NirK lineages exhibit clade-specific associations and biome enrichments (e.g., some clades enriched in nitrate-rich oxygen-poor waters; others in sewage), supporting the idea that the “nirK gene” label hides substantial functional/ecological diversity. (pold2024phylogeneticsandenvironmental pages 5-8)

Publication: Pold et al., 2024-01, ISME Communications, DOI/URL: https://doi.org/10.1093/ismeco/ycae020. (pold2024phylogeneticsandenvironmental pages 1-2)

6. Current applications and real-world implementations (with data)

6.1 nirK as a biomarker in wastewater treatment plant (WWTP) monitoring

In activated sludge across four WWTPs, GeoChip 4.2 detected 2,055 nitrogen-cycling genes and specifically detected 184 nirK genes, with 62 nirK genes present in all samples. nirK abundance correlated positively with influent nitrogen variables (influent TN: r = 0.421, P < 0.01; ammonia: r = 0.358, P < 0.05), illustrating routine real-world use of nirK as a functional marker linking community potential to operational chemistry. (wang2014microbialcommunityfunctional pages 3-5)

Publication: Wang et al., 2014-03, PLoS ONE, DOI/URL: https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pone.0093422. (wang2014microbialcommunityfunctional pages 3-5)

6.2 nirK detection and activity inference in stratified freshwater systems

In a subtropical deep reservoir oxygen-minimum zone study, nirK was assessed via clone libraries and qPCR/RT-qPCR approaches. The authors report 69 nirK clones forming 24 OTUs (97% similarity), and infer low activity via an RNA:DNA ratio for nirK of 0.0076 ± 0.001, with gene abundance/activity ranking narG > nirS > nirK > nosZ. This exemplifies common field implementation of nirK to track denitrifier/nitrite-reducer community structure and potential activity. (yu2014denitrifiercommunityin pages 3-4)

Publication: Yu et al., 2014-03, PLoS ONE, DOI/URL: https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pone.0092055. (yu2014denitrifiercommunityin pages 3-4)

7. Expert synthesis for functional annotation of R. palustris CGA009 NirK2 (Q6N2A5)

Primary molecular function: copper-containing nitrite reductase (EC 1.7.2.1) that reduces NO2− to NO at a T2Cu catalytic site, receiving electrons via a T1Cu redox site and coupling electron transfer to proton delivery (PCET). (rose2023spectroscopicallyvalidatedmultiple pages 1-3, cua2010expressionofgenes pages 37-42)

Biological process / pathway: nitrogen-oxide respiration/denitrification segment centered on NO formation from nitrite; in CGA009, available evidence supports nitrite reduction capability and suggests downstream NO metabolism potential (NO reductase genes reported), even though a canonical dissimilatory nitrate reductase ortholog was not evident in the referenced genome survey. (lee2002taxisresponseof pages 2-2, lee2002taxisresponseof pages 4-5)

Cellular localization: most consistent with periplasmic CuNiR localization and periplasmic electron-transfer coupling; direct localization data for RPA4145 were not retrieved and should be treated as an evidence gap. (cua2010expressionofgenes pages 37-42)

Regulation: likely embedded in NO-responsive regulatory logic characteristic of Alphaproteobacterial nirK systems (NnrR-family associations), but experimental validation of RPA4145-specific regulation remains limited in the retrieved corpus. (cantera2007moleculardiversityof pages 9-10)
