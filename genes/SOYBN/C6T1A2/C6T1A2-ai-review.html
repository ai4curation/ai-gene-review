Gene Description

C6T1A2 (Glyma17g18110.1) is a 210-residue unreviewed (TrEMBL) protein from soybean (Glycine max) that contains a single C2H2-type zinc finger domain (residues 79-106). It was cloned as part of a soybean transcription factor ORFeome effort that identified drought-resistance-associated transcription factors. InterPro classifies it in the Zinc_finger_protein_7 family (IPR053266) and in the C2H2-type zinc finger superfamily (IPR036236, IPR013087), while PANTHER places it in the ZINC FINGER PROTEIN 4-LIKE family (PTHR47593). The protein has an N-terminal acidic disordered region (residues 1-31) and a second disordered region (residues 95-116) flanking the zinc finger domain. Based on its domain architecture, C6T1A2 is predicted to function as a DNA-binding transcription factor localized to the nucleus, with zinc ion binding activity mediated by the C2H2 zinc finger. No gene-specific experimental studies have been published for this protein; all functional inferences derive from domain architecture, family membership, and analogy to characterized C2H2 zinc finger proteins in Arabidopsis and other plants. The closest characterized relative identified by phmmer is Arabidopsis ZFP7 (Q39266), which shares the same single-C2H2 domain architecture and IPR053266 family membership and has been shown to negatively regulate ABA signaling during germination, though whether this specific function is conserved in the soybean ortholog remains unknown.

No experimental evidence directly demonstrates transcription factor activity for this specific protein; the annotation is inferred from domain architecture and family membership. The protein coordinates a zinc ion via the C2H2 zinc finger motif to stabilize the DNA-binding fold.
