Gene Description

Response regulator (MxaB) that functions as additional regulator required for mxa operon expression alongside the MxcQE and MxbDM two-component systems. Contains response regulatory domain (residues 17-134) with conserved aspartate phosphorylation site (D67) and HTH luxR-type DNA-binding domain (residues 156-221). Part of complex regulatory network controlling lanthanide-dependent metal switch between calcium-dependent (MxaFI) and lanthanide-dependent (XoxF) methanol dehydrogenases. MxaB is required for expression of calcium-dependent methanol oxidation system.

Core Functions

MxaB functions as a response regulator essential for transcriptional control of the mxa operon encoding the calcium-dependent methanol dehydrogenase system. It contains an N-terminal response regulatory domain (residues 17-134) with a conserved aspartate phosphorylation site (D67) and a C-terminal HTH luxR-type DNA-binding domain (residues 156-221). MxaB is part of the complex regulatory network controlling the lanthanide-dependent metal switch between Ca2+-dependent (MxaFI) and Ln3+-dependent (XoxF) methanol oxidation systems. As an "orphan" response regulator (lacking a cognate sensor kinase), MxaB likely receives phosphorylation signals from other components in the regulatory cascade, particularly the MxcQE and MxbDM two-component systems. MxaB is absolutely required for expression of the mxa operon and thus for calcium-dependent methanol oxidation.
