Gene Description

mxaK encodes a membrane protein of 208 amino acids that is part of the mxa operon involved in calcium-dependent methanol oxidation in Methylorubrum extorquens AM1. The protein contains a single transmembrane helix (residues 32-52). MxaK is one of three genes (mxaA, mxaK, mxaL) involved in incorporation of calcium into methanol dehydrogenase (MDH). Mutant studies demonstrate that mxaK is essential for production of active MDH - MDH from mxaK deletion mutants is inactive but can be reconstituted in vitro by incubation with Ca2+ at pH 9.5. MxaK is proposed to function as a molecular chaperone that stabilizes a configuration of MDH which permits incorporation of Ca2+ into the enzyme at physiological concentrations. Without MxaK (and MxaA/MxaL), MDH cannot properly incorporate the essential calcium cofactor required for methanol oxidation activity.

Core Functions

MxaK functions as a molecular chaperone required for calcium incorporation into methanol dehydrogenase. It stabilizes a configuration of MDH that permits Ca2+ insertion at physiological concentrations. MxaK is essential for producing catalytically active MDH - deletion mutants produce inactive enzyme that can only be reconstituted by high pH calcium incubation in vitro. The protein is a single-pass transmembrane protein, likely facilitating Ca2+ delivery or MDH assembly at the membrane-periplasm interface during methanol oxidation.
