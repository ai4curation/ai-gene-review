CC   -!- FUNCTION: Catalyzes the oxidation of methanol to formaldehyde, but only
CC       in the presence of lanthanides (Ln). Contributes to methanol metabolism
CC       when La(3+) is present in the natural environment of the bacterium,
CC       allowing bacterial growth with methanol as carbon and energy source.
CC       Thereby is an essential enzyme for Ln-dependent methylotrophy
CC       (PubMed:23209751, PubMed:30862918, PubMed:32366463). Uses a specific
CC       cytochrome cL (XoxG), encoded by the adjacent gene in the locus, as
CC       electron acceptor (Probable). Also plays a role in the transcriptional
CC       regulation of the mxa and xox1 operons, most likely acting as a
CC       lanthanide sensory module (PubMed:32366463). Is also able to oxidize
CC       formaldehyde to formate in vitro, but this activity does not occur in
CC       vivo (PubMed:30862918). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:23209751,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:30862918, ECO:0000269|PubMed:32366463,
CC       ECO:0000305|PubMed:30862918, ECO:0000305|PubMed:32366463}.
