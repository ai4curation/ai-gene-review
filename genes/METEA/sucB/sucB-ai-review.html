Gene Description

Dihydrolipoyllysine-residue succinyltransferase (E2 component) of the 2-oxoglutarate dehydrogenase (OGDH) complex. This enzyme catalyzes the second step in the conversion of 2-oxoglutarate to succinyl-CoA and CO2, transferring a succinyl group from succinyl-dihydrolipoamide to coenzyme A. The E2 subunit forms a 24-polypeptide structural core with octahedral symmetry, onto which the E1 (2-oxoglutarate dehydrogenase) and E3 (dihydrolipoamide dehydrogenase) components assemble. Contains a lipoyl-binding domain (residues 2-77) and a peripheral subunit-binding domain (PSBD, residues 143-180). This is a core TCA cycle enzyme with EC 2.3.1.61. Note: The iRP911 GEM (2011) had this gene with noEC, but UniProt now correctly assigns EC 2.3.1.61, representing a GEM_MISSING_EC case that should be updated.

1) Key concepts and definitions
- Gene/protein identity and family
‚Ä¢ sucB encodes the E2 dihydrolipoyllysine-residue succinyltransferase (also termed DLST or E2o) subunit of the 2-oxoglutarate dehydrogenase complex (OGDHc), a member of the 2-oxoacid dehydrogenase superfamily. In bacteria, the OGDHc is typically encoded by sucA (E1o, 2-oxoglutarate dehydrogenase), sucB (E2o), and lpd (E3, dihydrolipoamide dehydrogenase). This organization and naming are demonstrated experimentally in closely related Alphaproteobacteria (e.g., Sinorhizobium meliloti), providing strong orthology-based confirmation of sucB‚Äôs identity and role as E2o in M. extorquens AM1 (OGDHc core-forming E2, lipoyl-lysine catalytic arm) (URL: n/a, thesis; 2014) (meek2014thebiochemicaland pages 101-106, meek2014thebiochemicaland pages 91-101) (maleh2012geneticanalysisof pages 13-19).

  • Catalyzed reaction (EC 2.3.1.61) and mechanism
    ‚Ä¢ The OGDHc converts 2-oxoglutarate + CoA + NAD+ ‚Üí succinyl‚ÄëCoA + CO2 + NADH.
