CC   -!- FUNCTION: In vivo may favor replication restart by preventing RecA from
CC       binding to blocked replication forks, avoiding unnecessary
CC       recombination during replication restart. Acts as a negative modulator
CC       of the RecA-ssDNA filament, may disassemble RecA threads, can act as
CC       both a positive and negative regulator of strand exchange
CC       (PubMed:35234892). Probably stabilizes or aids normal replication fork
CC       progression, is important for survival after treatment with DNA-
CC       damaging agents that can result in replication fork stress
CC       (PubMed:24443534). Overcomes the inhibition of replication restart by
CC       RecA/RecO, probably by displacing RecA (PubMed:35234892). Increasing
CC       levels inhibit PriA-dependent DNA replication initiation (but have
CC       little effect on ongoing replication) in vitro; may act by disturbing
CC       SsbA assembly (PubMed:35234892). Probably has a role in recombinational
CC       DNA repair (Probable) (PubMed:28527403). Does not seem to contribute to
CC       mismatch repair (PubMed:24443534). Has 5'-3' helicase activity that is
CC       probably ATP-dependent (PubMed:24443534). {ECO:0000255|HAMAP-
CC       Rule:MF_01488, ECO:0000269|PubMed:24443534,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:35234892, ECO:0000305|PubMed:28527403}.

CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:21170359}. Note=Localizes in tight foci on the

CC   -!- INDUCTION: Approximately 25 monomers per cell during exponential growth
CC       (at protein level) (PubMed:35234892). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:35234892}.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: Replication forks collapse more frequently
CC       (PubMed:24443534). A modest (2.8- to 3.6-fold) increase in spontaneous
CC       mutation; the mutations observed are not consistent with a mismatch
CC       repair defect (PubMed:24443534). Increased sensitivity to mitomycin C
CC       (blocks replication by adding bulky N2 adducts), single- and double-
CC       stranded breaks generated by phleomycin, alkylating agent methyl
CC       methane sulfonate (MMS) and UV damage (PubMed:24443534). Increased
CC       sensitivity to MMS (PubMed:28527403, PubMed:35234892). Increased
CC       sensitivity to H2O2 (PubMed:28527403). RecD2 is synthetically lethal
CC       with recG and with ruvAB (PubMed:28527403). RecA threads persist for a
CC       longer time after induction of DNA damage (PubMed:35234892).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:24443534, ECO:0000269|PubMed:28527403,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:35234892}.
