Gene Description

ComGA is an AAA+ ATPase that powers the assembly of the ComG competence pseudopilus in Bacillus subtilis. The protein contains a P-loop NTPase domain homologous to type II secretion system ATPases (GspE/T2SS-E family). ComGA is essential for DNA binding at the cell surface during natural genetic transformation, as it drives the assembly of competence pili composed primarily of ComGC pilin subunits. The protein localizes to cell poles through diffusion-capture mediated by ComEB, where it forms foci coincident with the DNA uptake machinery. ATP hydrolysis by ComGA is required for function, as Walker B motif mutations abolish both DNA binding and transformation competence while still allowing polar localization. During competence development, ComGA dynamics increase upon addition of extracellular DNA, consistent with pilus activity during substrate engagement.

1) Key concepts and definitions
- Identity and family: comGA encodes the competence-associated secretion/assembly ATPase of the ComG pseudopilus machinery in Bacillus subtilis. ComGA is an AAA+ P-loop NTPase homologous to type II secretion ATPases (GspE/T2SS-E). Functional Walker A/B motifs are present, and ATP hydrolysis is required for DNA binding and competence function (Jan 2020; Molecular Microbiology; https://doi.org/10.1111/mmi.14457) (burghard‚Äêschrod2020thebacillussubtilis pages 1-2).
- Primary role in DNA uptake: ComGA powers assembly of the ComG pilus required for transport of environmental DNA across the Gram-positive cell wall. Deletion of comGA abolishes surface-exposed pilus structures and transformation-associated DNA binding/uptake (Oct 10, 2023; PLOS Genetics; https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pgen.1010696) (kilb2023uptakeofenvironmental pages 2-4, kilb2023uptakeofenvironmental pages 8-10).
- Uptake pathway context: Recent imaging supports a two-tier uptake model in B. subtilis: dynamic ComG pili capture dsDNA across the wall all over the cell surface; DNA then accumulates in the periplasm (where ComEA can bind), with subsequent entry of ssDNA through the membrane channel ComEC, which is enriched at a single pole (Oct 10, 2023; PLOS Genetics; https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pgen.1010696) (kilb2023uptakeofenvironmental pages 4-5, kilb2023uptakeofenvironmental pages 16-19).

- Pilus lengths: Structured illumination microscopy reported pilus lengths of ~505 ¬± 155 nm (n=35) under full induction and ~539 ¬± 40 nm (n=40) at low induction (Oct 10, 2023; PLOS Genetics; https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pgen.1010696) (kilb2023uptakeofenvironmental pages 8-10).

- ComGA turnover and localization shift: ComGA turnover rate at poles increased from 0.076 ¬± 0.012 s‚àí1 to 0.131 ¬± 0.007 s‚àí1 upon DNA addition, with polar localization probability shifting from ~49.9% to ~59.2% (Jan 2020; Molecular Microbiology; https://doi.org/10.1111/mmi.14457) (burghard‚Äêschrod2020thebacillussubtilis pages 10-12).
- Retraction speeds: Median slow ~3 nm/s; median fast ~12 nm/s for competence pili, consistent with mixed spontaneous and ATPase-driven retraction (2024; details as reported) (zuke2024productiondynamicsanda pages 72-77, zuke2024productiondynamicsanda pages 68-72).

Functional and mechanistic narrative
- Primary molecular function: ComGA is an AAA+ ATPase that powers ComG pseudopilus assembly, a requirement for DNA binding at the cell surface and for subsequent uptake. Mutation of the Walker B motif abrogates DNA binding, implicating ATP hydrolysis in function. Deletion of comGA abolishes formation of surface-exposed pilus structures, establishing its essential role in pilus biogenesis (Jan 2020; Molecular Microbiology; Oct 10, 2023; PLOS Genetics; https://doi.org/10.1111/mmi.14457; https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pgen.1010696) (burghard‚Äêschrod2020thebacillussubtilis pages 1-2, kilb2023uptakeofenvironmental pages 8-10, kilb2023uptakeofenvironmental pages 2-4).
- Localization and dynamics: ComGA accumulates at a single cell pole through diffusion‚Äìcapture, recruited by ComEB; DNA addition increases ComGA turnover and enhances polar localization. However, imaging shows competence pili can assemble all over the cell surface, retract dynamically, and capture dsDNA across the wall; periplasmic DNA is then directed toward the pole for translocation into the cytosol via ComEC (Jan 2020; Molecular Microbiology; Oct 10, 2023; PLOS Genetics; https://doi.org/10.1111/mmi.14457; https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pgen.1010696) (burghard‚Äêschrod2020thebacillussubtilis pages 10-12, kilb2023uptakeofenvironmental pages 4-5, kilb2023uptakeofenvironmental pages 16-19).
- Interactions and dependencies: The ComG machinery comprises the platform (ComGB), major pilin (ComGC), and minor pilins (e.g., ComGD/GE/GG), with ComC prepilin processing. ComGA provides the energy for assembly and likely contributes to retraction; loss of ComGA eliminates pili. Uptake into the periplasm depends on the pilus and ComEA‚Äôs DNA-binding, while entry into the cytosol depends on ComEC at the pole (Oct 10, 2023; PLOS Genetics; https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pgen.1010696) (kilb2023uptakeofenvironmental pages 2-4, kilb2023uptakeofenvironmental pages 4-5, kilb2023uptakeofenvironmental pages 16-19).
- Regulation/physiology: ComEB functions as a polar localization factor for ComGA, acting on millisecond timescales and enabling efficient assembly/activity of the uptake complex; comEB deletion impairs competence. DNA serves as an activating signal that increases ComGA exchange at the pole, consistent with enhanced pilus activity during substrate engagement (Jan 2020; Molecular Microbiology; https://doi.org/10.1111/mmi.14457) (burghard‚Äêschrod2020thebacillussubtilis pages 1-2, burghard‚Äêschrod2020thebacillussubtilis pages 10-12).

Verification of identity and domain alignment
- Gene symbol and organism: The studies above explicitly analyze the B. subtilis competence ATPase ComGA within the ComG operon, consistent with the UniProt P25953 annotation for Bacillus subtilis 168 (Jan 2020; Molecular Microbiology; Oct 10, 2023; PLOS Genetics; https://doi.org/10.1111/mmi.14457; https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pgen.1010696) (burghard‚Äêschrod2020thebacillussubtilis pages 1-2, kilb2023uptakeofenvironmental pages 2-4).
- Domains/family: ComGA bears AAA+ P-loop NTPase motifs and is functionally homologous to T2SS E/GspE ATPases required for pseudopilus assembly, aligning with UniProt domain annotations (Jan 2020; Molecular Microbiology; https://doi.org/10.1111/mmi.14457) (burghard‚Äêschrod2020thebacillussubtilis pages 1-2).

Limitations and open questions
- Retraction ATPase identity: While retraction dynamics are observed and a fast retraction class is consistent with ATPase-driven activity, whether ComGA alone powers retraction or a second factor contributes remains under investigation (Oct 10, 2023; PLOS Genetics; https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pgen.1010696; 2024 report) (kilb2023uptakeofenvironmental pages 16-19, zuke2024productiondynamicsanda pages 72-77, zuke2024productiondynamicsanda pages 68-72).
- Spatial coupling: The extent to which lateral wall-associated pili channel DNA exclusively to the polar ComEC or whether additional membrane translocation sites contribute remains an active area of study (Oct 10, 2023; PLOS Genetics; https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pgen.1010696) (kilb2023uptakeofenvironmental pages 4-5, kilb2023uptakeofenvironmental pages 16-19).

Conclusion
Collectively, recent live-cell imaging and single-molecule studies consolidate ComGA (P25953) as the AAA+ assembly ATPase of the B. subtilis ComG system, essential for competence pilus formation and DNA binding, dynamically recruited to the pole by ComEB, and responsive to extracellular DNA. Competence pili act broadly over the cell surface to introduce dsDNA into the periplasm, with polar ComEC mediating cytosolic entry. Quantitative parameters of pilus length, population partitioning of ComGC, and DNA-activated ComGA turnover provide a calibrated framework for functional annotation and modeling of the competence machinery (Oct 10, 2023; Jan 2020; 2024) (kilb2023uptakeofenvironmental pages 2-4, burghard‚Äêschrod2020thebacillussubtilis pages 1-2, kilb2023uptakeofenvironmental pages 4-5, kilb2023uptakeofenvironmental pages 8-10, burghard‚Äêschrod2020thebacillussubtilis pages 10-12, zuke2024productiondynamicsanda pages 68-72, kilb2023uptakeofenvironmental pages 16-19).
