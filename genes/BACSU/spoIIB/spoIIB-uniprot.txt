CC   -!- FUNCTION: Facilitates the rapid and spatially regulated dissolution of
CC       septal peptidoglycan. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10648537}.
CC   -!- ACTIVITY REGULATION: Appears to be degraded early in engulfment, in
CC       correlation with its loss from polar septa.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10648537}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell membrane {ECO:0000305}; Single-pass membrane
CC       protein {ECO:0000255}. Note=Colocalizes with FtsZ at sites of polar
CC       septation, either shortly before or after the onset of septal
CC       biogenesis. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10648537}.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: Transcription is under sporulation control.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:8419299}.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: Deletion of the gene causes little impairment of
CC       spore formation (PubMed:8419299). Mutant sporangia display a transient
CC       engulfment defect in which the forespore pushes through the septum and
CC       bulges into the mother cell (PubMed:10648537). Ultimately, however, the
CC       mutant is able to complete engulfment (PubMed:10648537). A spoIIB spoVG
CC       double mutant is blocked at the stage of sporulation septum formation
CC       and displays a strong impairment of engulfment (PubMed:10648537,
CC       PubMed:8419299). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10648537,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:8419299}.
