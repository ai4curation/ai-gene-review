CC   -!- FUNCTION: Calcium channel that probably plays a role in the regulation
CC       of calcium homeostasis (PubMed:30930064, PubMed:33208533,
CC       PubMed:36948481). Uptakes calcium ions and mediates calcium flux in
CC       proteoliposomes in a pH-dependent manner (PubMed:24904158,
CC       PubMed:30930064). When expressed in E.coli in the presence of high
CC       extracellular calcium concentrations, shows calcium-leak activity,
CC       increasing intracellular calcium concentration (PubMed:24904158). It
CC       can also mediate Ca(2+) flux from the endoplamic reticulum (ER) when
CC       expressed in permeabilized mammalian cells (PubMed:30930064). Calcium
CC       transport activity is also detected in ER-like lipid vesicles
CC       (PubMed:36948481). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:24904158,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:30930064, ECO:0000269|PubMed:33208533,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:36948481}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY:
CC       Reaction=Ca(2+)(in) = Ca(2+)(out); Xref=Rhea:RHEA:29671,
CC         ChEBI:CHEBI:29108; Evidence={ECO:0000305|PubMed:24904158,
CC         ECO:0000305|PubMed:30930064, ECO:0000305|PubMed:36948481};
CC   -!- ACTIVITY REGULATION: The calcium-release activity is mediated by two
CC       factors: pH and transmembrane ion gradient (PubMed:33208533). It was
CC       proposed, based on an MD simulation, that the conserved aspartyl dyad
CC       (Asp-171-Asp-195) regulates Ca(2+) binding, pH sensing, and the channel
CC       pore opening and closing, and that protonation of Asp-171 probably
CC       weakens its interaction with Arg-60, facilitating the opening of the
CC       channel (PubMed:30930064). Another study using nanodiscs suggests that
CC       Asp-171 is not a pH sensor regulating the pore dynamics; instead, it is
CC       only involved in the gating of calcium ions (PubMed:33208533). When
CC       crystallized in detergents at different pH conditions, the transition
CC       between open and closed conformations is regulated by pH
CC       (PubMed:24904158). Ca(2+) binding is inhibited by Na(+), K(+), Li(+),
CC       Yb(3+) and Lu(3+), but not by Mg(2+) and Mn(2+) (PubMed:30930064).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:24904158, ECO:0000269|PubMed:30930064,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:33208533}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Monomer. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:24904158,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:25286840}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell membrane {ECO:0000305}; Multi-pass membrane
CC       protein {ECO:0000269|PubMed:24904158, ECO:0000269|PubMed:30930064,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:33208533}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: BsYetJ in nanodiscs is structurally different from those
CC       crystallized in detergents (PubMed:33208533). In lipid nanodiscs,
CC       BsYetJ conformation is pH-sensitive in apo state (lacking calcium),
CC       whereas in a calcium-containing solution it is locked in an
CC       intermediate state, inert to pH changes (PubMed:33208533). When
CC       crystallized in detergents it shows a pH-sensitive conformational
CC       equilibrium between a rather flexible pore-open state seen at lower pH
CC       and a pore-closed state seen at higher pH (PubMed:24904158). BsYetJ
CC       appears to be a lipid-sensitive membrane protein: differences in
CC       membrane lipid composition lead to changes in functional sites
CC       (PubMed:36948481). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:24904158,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:33208533, ECO:0000269|PubMed:36948481}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the BI1 family. {ECO:0000305}.
