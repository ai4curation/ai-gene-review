Gene Description

FliH is a cytosolic component of the flagellar type III secretion system (fT3SS) export apparatus. It forms a complex with the FliI ATPase (FliH2-FliI1 stoichiometry) and acts as a negative regulator, inhibiting FliI ATPase activity until productive engagement with the export gate (FlhA/FlhB/FliPQR). FliH helps coordinate ATP hydrolysis with PMF-driven translocation of flagellar substrates and is essential for efficient and ordered export of flagellar proteins. Based primarily on conserved mechanism studies in Salmonella, with B. subtilis fliH expected to function similarly due to conserved architecture.
