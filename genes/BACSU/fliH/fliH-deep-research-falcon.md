1) Key concepts and definitions
- FliH definition and position in the fT3SS: FliH is one of three soluble cytoplasmic components (FliH, FliI, FliJ) that form the ATPase-associated module of the flagellar export apparatus. This module associates beneath the MS/C rings at the base of the basal body, adjacent to the transmembrane export gate (FlhA/FlhB/FliPQR). Its functions are to dock export substrates (often as chaperone–substrate complexes), regulate FliI ATPase activity, and couple ATPase activity to proton-motive-force (PMF)-driven translocation through the export gate. URL: https://doi.org/10.3390/biom11020186 (Jan 2021) (halte2021proteinexportvia pages 1-3)
- Primary biochemical role: FliH forms a complex with the FliI ATPase (classically FliH2–FliI1), restrains FliI ATP hydrolysis until productive engagement with the export machinery, and organizes substrate delivery. In Salmonella, purified FliH inhibits FliI ATPase activity; this supports a conserved role for FliH as a negative regulator and organizer of the ATPase until the export gate is competent. URL: https://doi.org/10.1046/j.1365-2958.2000.02106.x (Sep 2000) (cairns2014probingtheassembly pages 39-43)
- Coupling to the membrane export gate: The cytosolic C-terminal domain of FlhA (FlhAC) oligomerizes into a nonameric ring that engages FliH/FliI/FliJ and chaperone–substrate complexes. FliH and FliI promote FlhA interactions with FliJ and substrates, thereby coupling ATPase-driven steps to PMF-driven translocation. URL: https://doi.org/10.3390/biom11020186 (Jan 2021) (halte2021proteinexportvia pages 7-8)
- Localization: The FliHIJ complex localizes to the cytoplasmic face of the basal body at the C-ring/MS-ring interface as part of the export apparatus. URL: https://doi.org/10.3390/biom11020186 (Jan 2021) (halte2021proteinexportvia pages 1-3)

2) Recent developments and latest research (2023–2024 prioritized)
- Ordered export requires FliH/FliI support of FlhA conformational switching. In Salmonella, the conserved GYXLI motif in FlhAC orchestrates substrate specificity switching (rod/hook to filament). 2024 work shows FliH and FliI are required to help FlhA maintain strict export order and correct substrate-recognition errors; mutations in FlhA’s GYXLI motif alter FlhAC dynamics and dependence on FliH/FliI, sometimes partially bypassing their absence. This provides direct mechanistic evidence that the FliHI ATPase module stabilizes productive FlhA conformations to impose ordered export. URL: https://doi.org/10.1038/s42003-024-06081-0 (Mar 2024) (kinoshita2024flihandflii pages 1-2, kinoshita2024flihandflii pages 7-8)
- Broader 2024 synthesis: A 2024 review of flagellar motor structure and dynamics highlights the ATPase–FlhA coupling paradigm and cites the above 2024 advance that “FliH and FliI help FlhA bring strict order to flagellar protein export,” consolidating the role of FliHIJ in export ordering. URL: https://doi.org/10.3390/biom14121488 (Nov 2024) (nakamura2024structureanddynamics pages 18-19)
- Energy transduction and ATPase involvement: While PMF can drive secretion, ATPase activity and the FliH–FliI module elevate efficiency and enable robust export under typical intracellular substrate concentrations; increasing PMF and substrate levels can bypass an ATPase requirement in Salmonella, underscoring that FliH/FliI serve as efficiency enhancers and organizers rather than the sole energy source. URL: https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pgen.1004800 (Nov 2014) (kamble2019bioengineeringofescherichia pages 47-52)
- 2023 authoritative overview: A comprehensive 2023 review details architectures of type III/IV systems and ATPase similarities, reinforcing the conserved layout in which FliHIJ dock to FlhA/FlhB/FliPQR and coordinate ATP hydrolysis with substrate delivery. URL: https://doi.org/10.3389/fcimb.2023.1255852 (Nov 2023) (nakamura2024structureanddynamics pages 18-19)

Organismal generality vs. Bacillus subtilis applicability
- Most precise mechanistic studies (inhibition of FliI by FliH; FlhA conformational control by FliH/FliI; ATPase bypass by elevated PMF) are in Salmonella/Enterobacteriaceae. However, the core components (FliH/I/J with FlhA/B and FliPQR) and their modular roles are conserved across bacterial flagellar systems, including Gram-positive bacteria. B. subtilis possesses the same core fT3SS architecture, and thus the FliH functions are inferred to be conserved in B. subtilis (strain 168), although direct strain-specific phenotypes for fliH deletion have not been exhaustively reported. URLs: https://doi.org/10.3390/biom11020186 (Jan 2021); https://doi.org/10.1128/ecosalplus.esp-0011-2023 (Dec 2023) (halte2021proteinexportvia pages 1-3, nakamura2024structureanddynamics pages 18-19)

3) Current applications and real-world implementations
- Synthetic biology and secretion engineering: The flagellar T3SS has been repurposed for heterologous protein export. Engineering efforts organize the FliHIJ ATPase complex and the FlhA/FlhB/FliPQR gate to improve secretion yields and substrate recognition rules. While this body of work is largely in E. coli/Salmonella, it illustrates how manipulating the FliH–FliI–FlhA interface can tune secretion efficiency and specificity. URL: https://hdl.handle.net/1842/31609 or institutional repositories; summarized background in 2019 thesis outlines canonical FliHIJ–FlhA coupling. (2019) (kamble2019bioengineeringofescherichia pages 47-52)
- Fermentation/process improvement in B. subtilis: Disabling flagellar systems can reallocate resources to product synthesis. In a B. subtilis 168-derived amylase producer, CRISPRi knockdowns in the flagellar operon or deletion of flgE increased alpha-amylase yields up to about two- to threefold, illustrating industrial relevance of modulating flagellar biogenesis, albeit not specifically via fliH. URL: https://doi.org/10.1186/s12934-022-01861-x (Jul 2022) (kamble2019bioengineeringofescherichia pages 47-52)

4) Expert opinions and authoritative analyses
- Minamino and colleagues emphasize that FliH restrains and organizes FliI, delivering substrates and coordinating ATPase activity with the export gate; FlhAC serves as the central hub. This model, now bolstered by 2024 structural/functional data, frames FliH as essential for “ordered” export logic via FlhA. URLs: https://doi.org/10.3390/biom14121488 (Nov 2024); https://doi.org/10.1038/s42003-024-06081-0 (Mar 2024) (nakamura2024structureanddynamics pages 18-19, kinoshita2024flihandflii pages 1-2, kinoshita2024flihandflii pages 7-8)
- Foundational biochemical opinion: FliH acts as a negative regulator to prevent wasteful ATP hydrolysis by FliI until docking/productive interactions are made, consistent with an energy-coupling checkpoint role. URL: https://doi.org/10.1046/j.1365-2958.2000.02106.x (Sep 2000) (cairns2014probingtheassembly pages 39-43)

5) Relevant statistics and data
- Structural state/order control: The FlhAC ring’s conformational states (rod/hook “RH” vs filament “F”) and the conserved GYXLI motif control substrate ordering; perturbations to GYXLI increase export errors, and dependence on FliH/FliI increases. The 2024 study quantifies altered affinities and genetic bypass of FliH/FliI in FlhA-variant backgrounds, demonstrating that FliH/FliI support timely switching and error correction during export. URL: https://doi.org/10.1038/s42003-024-06081-0 (Mar 2024) (kinoshita2024flihandflii pages 1-2, kinoshita2024flihandflii pages 7-8)
- ATPase vs. PMF: Experiments show successful flagella assembly even without ATPase activity if PMF and substrate levels are elevated; conversely, typical conditions require FliHIJ for efficient throughput. These findings quantify the organizational rather than strictly energetic necessity for FliHIJ. URL: https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pgen.1004800 (Nov 2014) (kamble2019bioengineeringofescherichia pages 47-52)

Functional annotation for B. subtilis fliH (P23449)
- Primary function: Cytosolic component of the fT3SS export apparatus that forms a complex with FliI (and FliJ) to regulate ATPase activity, dock/export chaperone–substrate complexes, and couple ATP hydrolysis to PMF-driven translocation through FlhA/FlhB/FliPQR. Inhibits FliI ATPase inappropriately and promotes productive export when the gate is ready. URLs: https://doi.org/10.3390/biom11020186 (Jan 2021); https://doi.org/10.1046/j.1365-2958.2000.02106.x (Sep 2000) (halte2021proteinexportvia pages 1-3, cairns2014probingtheassembly pages 39-43)
- Molecular interactions: FliH directly binds FliI, forming FliH2–FliI and larger ATPase assemblies; it promotes interactions between FliI/FliJ and FlhA’s cytoplasmic domain, which is the substrate-docking/nonameric hub. URL: https://doi.org/10.3390/biom11020186 (Jan 2021) (halte2021proteinexportvia pages 7-8, halte2021proteinexportvia pages 1-3)
- Localization: Cytosolic face of the basal body (C-ring/MS ring region) as part of the export machinery. URL: https://doi.org/10.3390/biom11020186 (Jan 2021) (halte2021proteinexportvia pages 1-3)
- Genetic/phenotypic consequences in Bacillus/Gram-positives: Direct B. subtilis fliH-mutant phenotypes are not comprehensively documented in the sources retrieved here; based on conserved architecture and mechanistic studies, loss of fliH would be expected to reduce the efficiency and ordering of export, leading to severe motility defects. In Gram-negative models, ATPase/FliH impairment reduces export efficiency and order, with PMF elevation partially compensating. URLs: https://doi.org/10.3390/biom11020186 (Jan 2021); https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pgen.1004800 (Nov 2014); https://doi.org/10.1038/s42003-024-06081-0 (Mar 2024) (halte2021proteinexportvia pages 1-3, kamble2019bioengineeringofescherichia pages 47-52, kinoshita2024flihandflii pages 1-2, kinoshita2024flihandflii pages 7-8)
