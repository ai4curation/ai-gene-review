CC   -!- FUNCTION: A master regulator required for the expression of late
CC       competence genes including comC, comE, comG and the bdbDC operon
CC       (PubMed:11744713, PubMed:11918817, PubMed:11948146, PubMed:7783616,
CC       PubMed:8196543). Receives signals from SrfA, and possibly other
CC       regulatory COM genes, and transduces these signals to the late COM
CC       genes (PubMed:7783616, PubMed:8196543). Represses transcription of rok
CC       (PubMed:11849533). May repress expression of a few genes
CC       (PubMed:7783616). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11744713,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:11849533, ECO:0000269|PubMed:11918817,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:11948146, ECO:0000269|PubMed:7783616,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:8196543}.
CC   -!- DEVELOPMENTAL STAGE: Expressed in cells competent for DNA
CC       transformation; that is 5-10% of the population (PubMed:11918817).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11918817}.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: Positively regulates its own expression (PubMed:7783616).
CC       Expressed only in glucose-based minimal medium during the transition to
CC       stationary phase (PubMed:8196543). Expression is initiated at the time
CC       of transition to the post-exponential state, and exclusively in
CC       competence-stimulating medium. Repressed by AbrB and Rok
CC       (PubMed:11849533). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11849533,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:7783616, ECO:0000269|PubMed:8196543}.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: Transformation deficient (PubMed:8196543). Loss
CC       of expression of at least 165 genes (PubMed:11918817, PubMed:11948146).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11918817, ECO:0000269|PubMed:11948146,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:8196543}.
