Gene Description

ComK is the master transcriptional regulator of genetic competence in Bacillus subtilis, functioning as a DNA-binding transcription activator that controls the expression of late competence genes required for DNA uptake, processing, and recombination. ComK binds to specific AT-rich motifs called K-boxes (consensus AAAA-N5-TTTT) in promoter regions as a tetramer (dimer-of-dimers) to activate transcription of target genes including the comC, comE, comF, comG operons encoding DNA uptake machinery, as well as recA and nucA involved in DNA processing. A critical feature of ComK regulation is its positive autoregulation at its own promoter (PcomK), which creates a bistable switch leading to bimodal population behavior where only 5-10% of cells enter the competent K-state. ComK protein levels are tightly controlled through post-translational degradation by the MecA-ClpC-ClpP protease system; the adaptor protein MecA targets ComK to the ClpC-ClpP protease for degradation. This proteolytic control is relieved by ComS, a small protein produced from the quorum-sensing regulated srf operon, which competes with ComK for MecA binding and thereby stabilizes ComK. ComK expression is also negatively regulated at the transcriptional level by multiple repressors including AbrB, Rok, and CodY, with Spo0A-P relieving AbrB repression during transition to stationary phase. The ComK regulon encompasses at least 165 genes, making it a central hub connecting quorum sensing, nutritional status, and stress responses to the developmental decision to enter the competent state.

- ComK binds K-boxes as a tetramer (dimer-of-dimers) and directly activates promoters of late competence genes (e.g., comG) and PcomK, producing positive feedback and bistability (maier2020competenceandtransformation pages 3-5, berka2002microarrayanalysisof pages 1-2). URL and dates: Maier 2020-01, https://doi.org/10.21775/cimb.037.057; Berka 2002-03, https://doi.org/10.1046/j.1365-2958.2002.02833.x.
- Upstream signals flow through ComX/ComP/ComA to induce ComS (srf), which titrates MecA and reduces ComK degradation by ClpC/ClpP, enabling competence entry; repression by Rok, CodY, AbrB is relieved in specific states (e.g., Spo0A~P lowering AbrB), with DegU priming effects at low ComK; stress-activated SigB antisense as-comK shuts competence under acute stress (rahmer2015constructionofa pages 1-2, harms2024molecularmechanismsofa pages 51-54, harms2024molecularmechanismsof pages 75-78). URLs and dates: Rahmer 2015-12, https://doi.org/10.3389/fmicb.2015.01431; Harms 2024.
- The regulon encompasses DNA uptake (comCDEFG, comFA, comEA), DNA processing (nucA, recA), and additional genes revealed by high-resolution transcriptomics and proteomics, while competence imposes a growth-arrested physiological state (berka2002microarrayanalysisof pages 1-2, boonstra2020analysesofcompetent pages 1-2). URLs and dates: Berka 2002-03, https://doi.org/10.1046/j.1365-2958.2002.02833.x; Boonstra 2020-04, https://doi.org/10.1111/1462-2920.15005.

- While foundational mechanisms of ComK and competence are established, newer layers involving antisense RNAs (SigB‚Üías-comK), transcription termination (Rho), and proteostasis remodeling during stress continue to refine models of when and how competence is permitted or repressed in dynamic environments (harms2024molecularmechanismsof pages 75-78, harms2024molecularmechanismsofa pages 51-54, harms2024molecularmechanismsof pages 51-54). URLs and dates: Harms 2024; Bidnenko 2023-02, https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pgen.1010618; Ishikawa 2024-07, https://doi.org/10.1111/gtc.13141.

description: ComK is the master transcriptional regulator of genetic competence in
  Bacillus subtilis, functioning as a DNA-binding transcription activator that controls
  the expression of late competence genes required for DNA uptake, processing, and
  recombination. ComK binds to specific AT-rich motifs called K-boxes (consensus AAAA-N5-TTTT)
  in promoter regions as a tetramer (dimer-of-dimers) to activate transcription of
  target genes including the comC, comE, comF, comG operons encoding DNA uptake machinery,
  as well as recA and nucA involved in DNA processing. A critical feature of ComK
  regulation is its positive autoregulation at its own promoter (PcomK), which creates
  a bistable switch leading to bimodal population behavior where only 5-10% of cells
  enter the competent K-state. ComK protein levels are tightly controlled through
  post-translational degradation by the MecA-ClpC-ClpP protease system; the adaptor
  protein MecA targets ComK to the ClpC-ClpP protease for degradation. This proteolytic
  control is relieved by ComS, a small protein produced from the quorum-sensing regulated
  srf operon, which competes with ComK for MecA binding and thereby stabilizes ComK.
  ComK expression is also negatively regulated at the transcriptional level by multiple
  repressors including AbrB, Rok, and CodY, with Spo0A-P relieving AbrB repression
  during transition to stationary phase. The ComK regulon encompasses at least 165
  genes, making it a central hub connecting quorum sensing, nutritional status, and
  stress responses to the developmental decision to enter the competent state.
