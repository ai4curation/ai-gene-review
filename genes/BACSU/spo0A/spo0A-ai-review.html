Gene Description

Spo0A is the master transcriptional regulator that controls entry into sporulation in Bacillus subtilis. It functions as the terminal response regulator in the KinA/KinB -> Spo0F -> Spo0B -> Spo0A phosphorelay signaling system. The protein contains an N-terminal CheY-like receiver domain (residues 5-123) that accepts a phosphoryl group on Asp-56, and a C-terminal DNA-binding effector domain with an HTH motif (residues 199-218) that recognizes the 0A-box consensus sequence 5'-TGNCGAA-3'. Upon phosphorylation, Spo0A~P dimerizes and binds to promoter DNA, functioning as both an activator (e.g., spoIIa operon) and repressor (e.g., abrB). The level of Spo0A~P determines cell fate in a graded manner: low/moderate levels activate biofilm and competence pathways, while high levels commit cells to sporulation. Spo0A is essential for asymmetric septum formation during sporulation initiation, and also plays critical roles in biofilm formation and fruiting body development in natural isolates.
