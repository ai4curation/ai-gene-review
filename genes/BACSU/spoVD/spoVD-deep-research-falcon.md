Comprehensive research report on spoVD (UniProt Q03524) in Bacillus subtilis 168

1) Key concepts and definitions with current understanding
- Molecular identity and domains: SpoVD is a sporulation-specific class B, high molecular weight PBP with an N‑terminal transmembrane helix, a PBP dimerization domain, a catalytic transpeptidase domain containing the conserved active-site serine, and a C‑terminal PASTA domain; it belongs to the transpeptidase family (UniProt Q03524; literature consensus). Its cognate SEDS partner is SpoVE, forming a dedicated complex for cortex PG synthesis during sporulation (Fay et al., 2010, doi:10.1016/j.jmb.2010.04.036; Alabdali, 2017, doi:10.13140/rg.2.2.23964.92803) (fay2010interactionsbetweenlateacting pages 1-2, alabdali2017characterisationofantibiotic pages 92-97).
- Primary biochemical function: SpoVD is a D,D‑transpeptidase that cross-links peptide stems in the nascent cortex PG; catalytic mutants abolish heat-resistant spore formation, demonstrating transpeptidase activity is essential for cortex assembly (Fay et al., 2010, doi:10.1016/j.jmb.2010.04.036) (fay2010interactionsbetweenlateacting pages 7-8).
- Spatial and temporal localization: SpoVD accumulates at the outer forespore membrane during the defined window of cortex synthesis (T2.5–T4.5), with quantified forespore enrichment (GFP-SpoVD forespore/mother cell fluorescence ratio 3.27 ± 0.76). Recruitment of SpoVD to the forespore requires SpoVE (Fay et al., 2010, doi:10.1016/j.jmb.2010.04.036) (fay2010interactionsbetweenlateacting pages 2-4).
- Genetic regulation and genomic context: spoVD lies in a locus with PG biosynthesis genes and is transcriptionally activated by σE and repressed by SpoIIID, consistent with late sporulation control (Alabdali, 2017; Fay et al., 2010) (alabdali2017characterisationofantibiotic pages 92-97, fay2010interactionsbetweenlateacting pages 2-4).

2) Recent developments and latest research (emphasis 2023–2024)
- Specialization of SEDS–bPBP systems for sporulation: Comparative phylogenomics and cell biology reveal that in endospore-forming Firmicutes, SEDS–bPBP pairs have specialized. In Clostridioides difficile, dcw-encoded PG synthases are repurposed for sporulation-specific roles; vegetative division instead relies on a class A PBP, highlighting diversification. The SpoVE–SpoVD pair contributes to asymmetric division and cortex synthesis during sporulation and forms a polar divisome-like subcomplex with FtsL/Q/B (Shrestha et al., 2023, Nat Commun, doi:10.1038/s41467-023-43595-3; published Dec 2023) (shrestha2023diversificationofdivision pages 1-2, shrestha2023diversificationofdivision pages 6-8).
- Functional redundancy across PBPs during asymmetric division: Recent mechanistic studies in C. difficile show SpoVD catalytic activity is essential for cortex but partially dispensable for initial asymmetric septation when SpoVE is present; a sporulation-induced bPBP (PBP3) can partially compensate, suggesting bPBP redundancy in the polar divisome. Quantification shows elevated progression through asymmetric division with catalytically inactive SpoVD compared to deletion, and strong dependence on SpoVE; experiments used ≥3 biological replicates with >1,000 total cells and >100 sporulating cells per sample (preprint posted Sep 2025; study period 2023–2024) (bioRxiv doi:10.1101/2024.09.26.615255) (shrestha2025functionalredundancybetween pages 8-10, shrestha2025functionalredundancybetween pages 16-19, shrestha2025functionalredundancybetween pages 5-8).

3) Current applications and real-world implementations
- Targeting sporulation-specific PG assembly: Because SpoVD is essential for B. subtilis cortex assembly and its homologs are required for sporulation in pathogens, the SpoVE–SpoVD complex is a candidate target for strategies aiming to block spore formation. Foundational work demonstrates the specificity and interdependence of this pair, including functional SpoVE–SpoVD fusions and direct physical interactions, indicating a tractable interface for inhibition (Fay et al., 2010, doi:10.1016/j.jmb.2010.04.036) (fay2010interactionsbetweenlateacting pages 7-8, fay2010interactionsbetweenlateacting pages 5-7, fay2010interactionsbetweenlateacting pages 1-2).
- Antibiotic sensitivity and resistance context in C. difficile: A cortex-specific SpoVD homolog contributes to cephalosporin resistance; spoVD deletion or catalytic inactivation increases cephalosporin susceptibility (~4‑fold lower MIC for cefoxitin and ceftazidime in one study), linking cortex-associated PBPs to clinical antibiotic responses in spore-forming pathogens (Alabdali et al., 2019, bioRxiv doi:10.1101/715458) (alabdali2019acortexspecificpbp pages 8-11).

4) Expert opinions and analysis from authoritative sources
- Mechanistic paradigm: SpoVD exemplifies the conserved model in which a SEDS-family glycosyltransferase and a class B PBP function as a cognate pair for PG polymerization and crosslinking in specialized contexts. In vivo evidence from B. subtilis shows direct SpoVE–SpoVD interaction, reciprocal stability effects, and the need for both activities for cortex assembly; specificity cannot be bypassed by simply co-localizing a noncognate bPBP (Fay et al., 2010) (fay2010interactionsbetweenlateacting pages 1-2, fay2010interactionsbetweenlateacting pages 5-7, fay2010interactionsbetweenlateacting pages 7-8).
- Evolutionary and systems perspective (2023): The emergence of endosporulation facilitated repurposing of cell division factors, yielding sporulation-specialized SEDS–bPBP modules. In C. difficile, divisome-associated factors (FtsLQ) are dispensable for vegetative division but required for sporulation, where they operate with SpoVE/SpoVD, supporting a dedicated polar divisome program during sporulation (Shrestha et al., 2023, Nat Commun) (shrestha2023diversificationofdivision pages 6-8, shrestha2023diversificationofdivision pages 1-2).

5) Relevant statistics and data from recent and foundational studies
- Localization quantitation: GFP-SpoVD enrichment at the forespore over the mother cell is 3.27 ± 0.76; SpoVE loss disrupts this enrichment (ratio ~1.99 ± 0.26; p < 0.0001), establishing a dependency of SpoVD recruitment on SpoVE (Fay et al., 2010) (fay2010interactionsbetweenlateacting pages 2-4).
- Functional interaction metrics: FRET between SpoVE-CFP and YFP-SpoVD is detected during T2.5–T4.5 (mean −5.8 ± 6.9% CFP difference), consistent with close proximity during cortex synthesis; functional SpoVE–SpoVD fusion complements ΔspoVE ΔspoVD (Fay et al., 2010) (fay2010interactionsbetweenlateacting pages 5-7).
- Mutant phenotypes: Catalytic mutations in SpoVD abolish heat-resistant spore formation (~0% heat resistance) while still allowing localization, demonstrating that local recruitment is necessary but insufficient without transpeptidase activity (Fay et al., 2010) (fay2010interactionsbetweenlateacting pages 7-8).
- 2023 study design statistics: Endospore-former specialization analyses in C. difficile used rigorous sampling (>1,000 cells, >100 sporulating cells per sample; three biological replicates), reinforcing conclusions about divisome specialization and SpoVE/SpoVD roles (Shrestha et al., 2023) (shrestha2023diversificationofdivision pages 6-8).
- Antibiotic susceptibility statistics: Loss of SpoVD function reduced cephalosporin MICs by approximately four-fold in C. difficile (cefoxitin and ceftazidime), indicating a measurable role in resistance phenotypes (Alabdali et al., 2019) (alabdali2019acortexspecificpbp pages 8-11).

Mechanistic synthesis: pathway and localization
- SpoVD operates at the forespore outer membrane during late sporulation to crosslink glycan strands supplied by SpoVE, a SEDS-family synthase. Multiple lines of evidence demonstrate a direct SpoVE–SpoVD complex, reciprocal stability effects, specific FRET during cortex synthesis, and functional rescue by a SpoVE–SpoVD fusion, all consistent with a cognate, co-dependent pair assembling cortex PG at stage V of sporulation (Fay et al., 2010) (fay2010interactionsbetweenlateacting pages 1-2, fay2010interactionsbetweenlateacting pages 5-7, fay2010interactionsbetweenlateacting pages 7-8).
- Regulatory integration coordinates spoVD expression with sporulation programs via σE activation and SpoIIID repression, ensuring timing coincident with cortex synthesis. Disruption of spoVD or its catalytic activity prevents cortex formation and heat-resistant spore production, consistent with its specific role in the cortex layer and not vegetative PG (Fay et al., 2010; Alabdali, 2017) (fay2010interactionsbetweenlateacting pages 2-4, alabdali2017characterisationofantibiotic pages 92-97).

Conservation and organismal variation
- The SpoVE–SpoVD module is conserved across endospore-formers with evidence for specialization versus vegetative cell-wall systems. In C. difficile, the dcw SEDS–bPBP set is dedicated to sporulation; vegetative division uses a class A PBP, and SpoVD homologs contribute to spore cortex synthesis and to cephalosporin resistance (Shrestha et al., 2023; Alabdali et al., 2019) (shrestha2023diversificationofdivision pages 1-2, shrestha2023diversificationofdivision pages 6-8, alabdali2019acortexspecificpbp pages 8-11).
