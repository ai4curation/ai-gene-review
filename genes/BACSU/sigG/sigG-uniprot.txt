CC   -!- FUNCTION: Sigma factors are initiation factors that promote the
CC       attachment of RNA polymerase to specific initiation sites and are then
CC       released (PubMed:18208527). This sigma factor is responsible for the
CC       expression of sporulation specific genes in the forespore
CC       (PubMed:18208527). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:18208527}.
CC   -!- ACTIVITY REGULATION: Activity repressed by anti-sigma-G factor Gin
CC       (csfB) and Lon protease during the early stages of forespore
CC       development (PubMed:17921305). When both Gin and sigma-G are expressed
CC       in E.coli Gin inhibits sigma-G activity, strongly suggesting Gin
CC       inhibits by direct physical interaction (PubMed:19497328).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:17921305, ECO:0000269|PubMed:19497328}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Interacts with anti-sigma-G factor Gin (csfB)
CC       (PubMed:18208527, PubMed:19497328). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:18208527,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:19497328}.
CC   -!- DEVELOPMENTAL STAGE: Active only in the forespore (PubMed:18208527).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:18208527}.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: Expressed and active 2 hours after sporulation starts (at
CC       protein level); stimulates its own transcription (PubMed:18208527).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:18208527}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: Recognition of anti-sigma-G factor Gin (csfB) occurs via the
CC       first 71 residues (PubMed:18208527). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:18208527}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the sigma-70 factor family. {ECO:0000305}.
