Gene Description

FliY is a bifunctional flagellar motor switch protein that acts as a CheY-P phosphatase localized at the flagellar C-ring. It contains three functional domains: an N-terminal CheY-binding region homologous to FliM residues 6-15, a middle CheC-like phosphatase domain with conserved Glu/Asn catalytic residues, and a C-terminal FliN-like domain that integrates it into the flagellar switch complex. FliY catalyzes the dephosphorylation of the phosphorylated response regulator CheY-P at the site of its action (the flagellar motor), thereby contributing to signal termination in the chemotaxis pathway. B. subtilis uses a three-phosphatase system (CheC, FliY, CheX) instead of the CheZ found in E. coli. FliY functions constitutively to maintain optimal CheY-P levels near the motor, while CheC responds to attractant stimuli. FliY can complement Salmonella fliN mutants, confirming its structural role in the C-ring.
