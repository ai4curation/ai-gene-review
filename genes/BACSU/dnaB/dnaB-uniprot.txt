CC   -!- FUNCTION: Helps DnaI load the DnaC replicative helicase onto single-
CC       stranded (ss)DNA (PubMed:12718886). During DNA replication from the
CC       origin of replication (oriC) in the DNA replisome, DnaD is required
CC       after DnaA, before DnaB and before subsequent helicase DnaC loading
CC       (PubMed:15186423, PubMed:19968790). Component of the replication
CC       restart primosome, which reloads the replicative helicase on sites
CC       other than oriC (PubMed:11585815). DnaB, DnaD and DnaI may also be
CC       required for a PriA-independent pathway of replication fork restart
CC       (PubMed:11679082). DnaB and DnaD work together to allow DnaB access to
CC       ssDNA (PubMed:15686560). DNA replication at oriC might originate on the
CC       inner face of the cell membrane; DnaB is essential for both replication
CC       initiation and cell membrane attachment of the origin region of the
CC       chromosome and plasmids (PubMed:6771760, PubMed:3027671,
CC       PubMed:3027697). Weakly binds ssDNA (PubMed:11585815, PubMed:15686560,
CC       PubMed:32817095), preferentially binds double-stranded (ds)DNA
CC       (PubMed:32817095), and replication fork-like substrates
CC       (PubMed:11585815). Remodels DNA, laterally compacts supercoiled plasmid
CC       and linear DNA, forms beads along the dsDNA (PubMed:16002087). Together
CC       DnaB and DnaD form bipolar complexes on plasmid DNA (PubMed:16002087).
CC       DnaB and DnaD are also required to load helicase on the repN plasmid
CC       origin of replication (oriN) (PubMed:36416272).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11679082, ECO:0000269|PubMed:12718886,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:15186423, ECO:0000269|PubMed:16002087,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:19968790, ECO:0000269|PubMed:3027671,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:3027697, ECO:0000269|PubMed:32817095,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:36416272, ECO:0000269|PubMed:6771760}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homotetramer (PubMed:11585815, PubMed:12718886,
CC       PubMed:16002087, PubMed:20071750, PubMed:32817095). Also forms higher-
CC       order oligomers, can be induced by some ssDNA (PubMed:16002087,
CC       PubMed:32817095). The DNA replisome assembles sequentially on oriC in
CC       this order; DnaA, DnaD, DnaB, DnaI-DnaC helicase (PubMed:19968790). In
CC       atomic force microscopy forms a square with a small central hole
CC       (PubMed:16002087). Part of the replication restart primosome which
CC       assembles in this order; PriA, DnaD then DnaB. The preferred DNA
CC       substrate mimics an arrested DNA replication fork with unreplicated
CC       lagging strand (PubMed:11585815). Interacts with DnaC, but probably not
CC       as a tetramer (PubMed:12718886). Interacts with DnaD (PubMed:15186423,
CC       PubMed:15686560, PubMed:36416272) but no interaction with PriA was seen
CC       (PubMed:15686560). Interacts with cell cycle regulator CcrZ
CC       (PubMed:35576203). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11585815,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:12718886, ECO:0000269|PubMed:15686560,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:16002087, ECO:0000269|PubMed:19968790,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:20071750, ECO:0000269|PubMed:32817095,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:35576203, ECO:0000269|PubMed:36416272}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cytoplasm {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10844689,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:20071750}. Cell membrane
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15186423, ECO:0000269|PubMed:20071750}. Note=Forms
CC       foci near both cell poles and in the middle of cells (PubMed:10844689).
CC       DnaB interacts with oriC downstream of dnaA, truncated DnaB is
CC       specifically depleted at oriC and not other sites in the genome
CC       (PubMed:20071750). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10844689,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:20071750}.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: Transcribed at constant levels during exponential growth
CC       (PubMed:20071750). Present in all growth phases (at protein level)
CC       (PubMed:20071750). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:20071750}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: Bind ssDNA via the N-terminus (residues 1-300), which also
CC       mediates tetramerization (PubMed:20071750). A region in the C-terminus
CC       (residues 365-428) also binds ssDNA, dsDNA and is responsible for DNA-
CC       mediated oligomerization (PubMed:20071750). Has 3 domains with homology
CC       to DnaD called DDBH1 and DDBH2 (DnaD DnaB Homology 1 and 2) followed by
CC       a short positively charged region and a probable C-terminal alpha-helix
CC       (PubMed:20587500). Removal of residues 429-472 increases dsDNA and some
CC       ssDNA binding, while removal of 312-472 prevents all DNA binding
CC       (PubMed:32817095). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:20071750,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:32817095, ECO:0000305|PubMed:20587500}.
CC   -!- PTM: In early growth phase only full-length protein is detected, during
CC       late growth and stationary phase full-length and C-terminally truncated
CC       proteins are seen (at protein level) (PubMed:20071750). Truncated
CC       protein is only seen in cytoplasmic fractions (PubMed:20071750).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:20071750}.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: Essential, it cannot be deleted; in depletion
CC       experiments DNA replication slows as soon as the protein is degraded
CC       and replication stops by 1 hour (PubMed:19968790).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:19968790}.
CC   -!- MISCELLANEOUS: The dna-1 (dnaBI) and dnaB-19 (dnaBII) mutants show
CC       different characteristics for replication and membrane binding of
CC       plasmid pUB110. DnaBI is essential for both chromosome and pUB110
CC       replication, whereas dnaBII is necessary only for chromosome
CC       replication. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:3027671}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the DnaB/DnaD family.
CC       {ECO:0000305|PubMed:20587500}.
