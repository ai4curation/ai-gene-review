CC   -!- FUNCTION: Plays a role in cell cycle regulation and chromosome
CC       integrity. Activates DnaA-dependent chromosomal DNA replication
CC       initiation ensuring that the chromosome is replicated at the right time
CC       during the cell cycle (By similarity). May regulate replication
CC       initiation through phosphorylation of a possible second messenger or
CC       metabolite, and by interacting with replication initiation proteins.
CC       Has ATPase activity with D-ribose and 2-deoxy-D-ribose in vitro, but
CC       not with choline. Involved in DNA damage response (PubMed:35576203).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:A0A0H2ZQL5, ECO:0000269|PubMed:35576203}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY:
CC       Reaction=D-ribose + ATP = D-ribose 5-phosphate + ADP + H(+);
CC         Xref=Rhea:RHEA:13697, ChEBI:CHEBI:15378, ChEBI:CHEBI:30616,
CC         ChEBI:CHEBI:47013, ChEBI:CHEBI:78346, ChEBI:CHEBI:456216;
CC         EC=2.7.1.15; Evidence={ECO:0000269|PubMed:35576203};
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY:
CC       Reaction=2-deoxy-D-ribose + ATP = 2-deoxy-D-ribose 5-phosphate + ADP +

CC         EC=2.7.1.229; Evidence={ECO:0000269|PubMed:35576203};
CC   -!- ACTIVITY REGULATION: Activated by D-ribose and 2-deoxy-D-ribose.
CC       Slightly activated by kanamycin and gentamicin.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:35576203}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Monomer in solution. Interacts with DnaA (via domains I (1-82)
CC       and III (111-326)). Interacts with DnaB (PubMed:35576203). Interacts
CC       with FtsZ (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:A0A0H2ZQL5,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:35576203}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cytoplasm {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:A0A0H2ZQL5}.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: Cells lacking this gene are slightly thinner and
CC       longer, but no anucleate cells are present and they have a growth rate
CC       similar to wild-type cells. Significantly decreased DNA replication
CC       initiation rate (PubMed:34373624). Suppresses the synthetic lethality
CC       of a dnaA1-yabA deletion for growth on rich me (PubMed:36053906).
CC       Susceptible to a broad range of DNA damage including mitomycin C (MMC),
CC       methyl methanesulfonate (MMS), ciprofloxacin, phleomycin and UV
CC       irradiation. Significant induction of the DNA damage response (SOS).
CC       Not sensitive to kanamycin or gentamicin (PubMed:35576203).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:34373624, ECO:0000269|PubMed:35576203,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:36053906}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the aminoglycoside phosphotransferase family.
CC       {ECO:0000305}.
