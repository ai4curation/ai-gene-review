CC   -!- FUNCTION: Plays an essential role in the initiation and regulation of
CC       chromosomal replication (PubMed:2167836). ATP-DnaA binds to the origin
CC       of replication (oriC) to initiate formation of the DNA replication
CC       initiation complex once per cell cycle. Binds directly to oriC at a 9
CC       bp consensus (DnaA box): 5'-TTATCCACA-3' and separates the double-
CC       stranded (ds)DNA (PubMed:2168872, PubMed:21895792, PubMed:28166228,
CC       PubMed:34197592, PubMed:38097584). Forms a right-handed helical
CC       filament on oriC DNA; dsDNA binds to the exterior of the filament while
CC       single-stranded (ss)DNA is stabilized in the filament's interior
CC       (PubMed:38097584). The ATP-DnaA-oriC complex binds and stabilizes one
CC       strand of the AT-rich DNA unwinding element (DUE or basal unwinding
CC       system, BUS), permitting loading of DNA polymerase (PubMed:38097584).
CC       Binds ATP with high affinity, ADP with lower affinity, but not AMP,
CC       cAMP or cGMP; ATP stimulates binding to DnaA boxes (PubMed:2168872,
CC       PubMed:21895792, PubMed:28166228). Once bound promotes sequence-
CC       specific strand separation of DnaA-trios (3'-GAT-5' consensus) adjacent
CC       to oriC in the presence of ATP but not ADP (PubMed:34197592,
CC       PubMed:38097584). Domains III and IV are sufficient to separate dsDNA
CC       strands (PubMed:38097584). The 'initiator specific motif' (ISM) of
CC       domain III contacts the middle adenine residue of the DnaA-trio
CC       probably stretching and stabilizing ssDNA (PubMed:38097584). DnaA-trio
CC       recognition is co-operative and depends on DnaA self-assembly
CC       (PubMed:38097584). The ssDNA serves as an assembly region for the
CC       replication machinery. Tethered to DnaN (beta sliding clamp subunit of
CC       DNA polymerase) and thus replication forks by YabA (PubMed:19081080).
CC       During replication initiation DnaA-ATP binds cooperatively to sequences
CC       in oriC (PubMed:21895792, PubMed:22286949). YabA prevents this
CC       cooperative binding while still allowing DnaA to bind DNA
CC       (PubMed:21895792). During the cell cycle an initial phase occurs in
CC       which DnaA is associated with origin regions, then the origin regions
CC       become spatially separate from the centrally sequestered DnaA
CC       molecules, and most DnaA molecules are unable to reassociate with
CC       origin regions (PubMed:19081080). Does not require YabA to bind DNA
CC       (PubMed:18506095). During sporulation SirA prevents DnaA association
CC       with the replication origin to prevent excessive chromosome replication
CC       (PubMed:21239581). Overexpression induces the SOS response; increasing
CC       expression of downstream dnaN blocks this induction (PubMed:11395445).
CC       Over-initiation of DNA replication is very deleterious; isolated
CC       suppressors in relA, ndrR, dnaC, cshA and crrZ increase replication
CC       elongation, decrease replication inititation or lead to a decrease in
CC       the replicative DNA helicase (PubMed:36053906). Binds acidic
CC       phospholipids (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P03004,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:11395445, ECO:0000269|PubMed:18506095,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:19081080, ECO:0000269|PubMed:21239581,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:2167836, ECO:0000269|PubMed:2168872,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:21895792, ECO:0000269|PubMed:28166228,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:34197592, ECO:0000269|PubMed:36053906,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:38097584}.
CC   -!- FUNCTION: The half-life of ADP-DnaA is 1.5 minutes, of ATP-DnaA is 5
CC       minutes at 37 degrees Celsius; in E.coli the half-life of ADP-DnaA is
CC       about 45 minutes (PubMed:19841480). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:19841480}.
CC   -!- FUNCTION: Also acts as a transcriptional regulator. DnaA inhibits its
CC       own gene expression (PubMed:11395445). DnaA binds specifically to the
CC       promoter regions of at least 20 operons (56 genes), including itself,
CC       sda and dnaB, and probably controls their expression in response to DNA
CC       replication inhibition (PubMed:16120674). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11395445,
CC       ECO:0000305|PubMed:16120674}.

CC   -!- ACTIVITY REGULATION: Oligomerization of DnaA can be controlled by Soj;
CC       monomeric ADP-Soj inhibits formation of the DnaA helix
CC       (PubMed:18854156, PubMed:22286949). YabA prevents the cooperative
CC       binding of DnaA-ATP to oriC-containing sequences; increased levels of
CC       DnaN (beta sliding clamp subunit of DNA polymerase) removes YabA from
CC       association with DnaA on the chromosome, enabling increased association
CC       of DnaA with its chromosomal binding sites (PubMed:21895792). Both Soj
CC       and YabA chase DnaA from oriC site, YabA tethers DnaA to the DNA
CC       replication fork via the beta sliding clamp subunit DnaN
CC       (PubMed:28166228). SirA antagonizes the ability of DnaA to bind to the
CC       replication origin, and thus decreases replication inititation during
CC       sporulation (PubMed:21239581). Small protein YqaH, part of the skin
CC       prophage-like element, binds to DnaA and antagonizes its replication
CC       initiation and transcriptional regulation activities (PubMed:36748575).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:18854156, ECO:0000269|PubMed:21239581,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:21895792, ECO:0000269|PubMed:28166228,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:36748575}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: The DNA replisome assembles sequentially on oriC in this
CC       order; DnaA, DnaD, DnaB, DnaI-DnaC helicase (PubMed:19968790).
CC       Oligomerizes as a right-handed, spiral filament on DNA at oriC
CC       (PubMed:38097584). Forms an ATP-dependent helix on DNA at oriC; both
CC       DnaD and YabA inhibit formation of the DnaA helix (PubMed:23909787).
CC       Forms an ATP-dependent oligomer, formation is stimulated by ds- and
CC       ssDNA; monomeric ADP-Soj inhibits oligomer formation (PubMed:22286949).
CC       Interacts with DnaD (PubMed:36416272). Interacts with YabA
CC       (PubMed:12060778, PubMed:16461910, PubMed:23909787), and via YabA, with
CC       the replication machinery subunit beta sliding clamp DnaN
CC       (PubMed:12060778, PubMed:16461910). Interacts with YabA via domain IIIa
CC       (residues 109-275) (PubMed:18506095). Isolated domain I forms a 1:1
CC       complex with SirA (PubMed:19682252, PubMed:21239581, PubMed:25041308).
CC       Interacts with Soj, probably via domain III (PubMed:18854156,
