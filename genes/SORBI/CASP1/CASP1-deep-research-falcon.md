### 1. Key concepts and definitions (current understanding)

#### 1.1 Casparian strip and the Casparian strip membrane domain (CSD)
The **Casparian strip (CS)** is a localized cell-wall lignin barrier in the root endodermis that restricts **apoplastic diffusion** into the stele; at the plasma membrane adjacent to the CS, plants form a specialized membrane region termed the **Casparian strip membrane domain (CSD)**. CASP proteins accumulate in this domain and are a defining molecular feature of CSD organization. (roppolo2014functionalandevolutionary pages 1-2)

#### 1.2 CASP proteins (including CASP1): definition, topology, and “membrane scaffold” concept
CASPARIAN STRIP MEMBRANE DOMAIN PROTEINS (CASPs) are described as **four-transmembrane (4TM) proteins** that mediate CS deposition by **recruiting lignin polymerization machinery**, and their membrane domain is unusually stable, consistent with a **membrane scaffold/fence** that defines a restricted domain (CSD). CASPs have **cytosolic N- and C-termini**, with a short intracellular loop, and show **very low turnover** once localized to the CSD; they are initially present more broadly but become restricted to the CSD. (roppolo2014functionalandevolutionary pages 1-2, roppolo2014functionalandevolutionary pages 12-13)

Given that SbCASP1 (C5YAP3) is annotated as a CASP-family member (UniProt context) and CASPs share conserved 4TM architecture, the most parsimonious functional definition for SbCASP1 is that it is a **CSD-localizing 4TM scaffold protein** involved in endodermal barrier formation (inference supported by family mechanism). (roppolo2014functionalandevolutionary pages 1-2)

#### 1.3 CASP/CASPL domains: PF04535/DUF588 and relationship to MARVEL proteins
Recent gene-family identification work in rice and Arabidopsis uses a hidden Markov model for **DUF588 (PF04535)** to define CASP family members, consistent with the CASP/CASPL domain assignment in UniProt/InterPro for SbCASP1. (xue2024comparativeanalysisof pages 1-2)

Evolutionary analysis indicates that CASP-like proteins (CASPLs) occur across land plants and green algae, and homologs outside plants map to the **MARVEL** protein family; some sequences are annotated as MARVEL-like (InterPro) and comparative analyses explicitly connect plant CASPLs with MARVEL domains (Pfam PF01284). (roppolo2014functionalandevolutionary pages 1-2, roppolo2014functionalandevolutionary pages 14-17)

### 2. Molecular function, biological process, and subcellular localization of SbCASP1 (best-supported annotation)

#### 2.1 Likely molecular function: scaffold to organize lignin polymerization at the Casparian strip
The best-supported primary molecular role for CASP-family proteins is **organizational rather than enzymatic**: CASPs are described as a **membrane scaffold/fence** at the CSD that **recruits/coordinates the lignin polymerization machinery** for CS formation. (roppolo2014functionalandevolutionary pages 1-2, roppolo2014functionalandevolutionary pages 1-1)

A recent 2024 synthesis further describes CASPs as localizing to the CS membrane domain and recruiting enzymes implicated in lignin polymerization and strip assembly, including **RBOHF, ESB1, PER64, and UCC1**. (xue2024comparativeanalysisof pages 1-2)

**Implication for SbCASP1:** SbCASP1 is therefore most likely a **plasma membrane structural organizer** that helps establish a localized lignin impregnation in the endodermal anticlinal wall (Casparian strip), shaping solute flow into the stele (inference from family mechanism). (roppolo2014functionalandevolutionary pages 1-2, xue2024comparativeanalysisof pages 1-2)

#### 2.2 Subcellular localization: plasma membrane; enrichment at the Casparian strip domain (CSD)
CASP-family proteins are membrane proteins; sorghum homolog evidence supports plasma membrane/Casparian strip association. In sweet sorghum, **SbCASP4** (a sorghum CASP homolog) was experimentally localized to the **plasma membrane** and expressed in root endodermal/Casparian-strip regions via in situ hybridization and reporter constructs; in Arabidopsis, SbCASP4-GFP showed enrichment in endodermal cells, especially the Casparian strip. (wei2021sbcasp4improvessalt pages 3-6, wei2021sbcasp4improvessalt pages 6-9, wei2021sbcasp4improvessalt pages 9-12)

**Implication for SbCASP1:** given shared CASP family topology and CSD targeting behavior, SbCASP1 is most likely a **plasma membrane protein enriched at the CSD in root endodermal cells** (inference supported by foundational CASP localization behavior and sorghum homolog experiments). (roppolo2014functionalandevolutionary pages 1-2, wei2021sbcasp4improvessalt pages 9-12)

#### 2.3 Pathway context: endodermal differentiation and barrier surveillance signaling
A key transcriptional regulator of endodermal barrier formation is **MYB36**, which activates CASP-related genes (including **CASP1**) and other CS polymerization components (e.g., PER64, ESB1). (manzano2025regulationandfunction pages 1-3)

CASP-mediated barrier formation is monitored by the **CIF peptide–SGN receptor/kinase pathway** (“SCHENGEN” surveillance): comparative and mechanistic syntheses emphasize CIF1/2 peptide signaling through **SGN3** and **SGN1** to ensure barrier integrity and to trigger compensatory lignification/suberization when defects are detected. (manzano2025regulationandfunction pages 1-3, xue2024comparativeanalysisof pages 1-2)

In a sorghum-homolog transgenic context, SbCASP4 expression correlated with elevated expression of **AtMYB36** and barrier-related genes including **AtSGN1** under salt stress, consistent with CASP proteins functioning within/alongside this regulatory network rather than as classic ion transporters. (wei2021sbcasp4improvessalt pages 9-12)

### 3. Recent developments and latest research (prioritizing 2023–2024)

#### 3.1 2024: comparative genomics and stress/nutrient-responsive CASP biology in cereals
A 2024 comparative analysis in **rice (Oryza sativa)** and **Arabidopsis** expanded CASP family catalogs using the **PF04535 (DUF588)** model and reported that most CASP genes are **highly expressed in roots/endodermis** and respond to abiotic stresses. The study highlights crop-relevant phenotypes previously reported for **OsCASP1**, including roles in CS formation and mineral/nutrient homeostasis (e.g., altered tolerance to ion deficiencies), and it presents nutrient-deficiency responsive expression patterns with statistical testing (Student’s t-test; **p < 0.01**, ***p < 0.001). (xue2024comparativeanalysisof pages 13-15, xue2024comparativeanalysisof pages 17-18)

This supports a current view that CASP-family proteins are not only developmental barrier components but also participate in **adaptive remodeling** of root diffusion barriers under nutrient and stress conditions—an important framing for crop functional annotation. (xue2024comparativeanalysisof pages 13-15)

#### 3.2 2023: sorghum CASP-like protein engineering for salt exclusion (directly relevant to sorghum CASP function)
A 2023 study reported that a sweet sorghum CASP-like protein (**SbCASP‑LP1C1**) can **improve salt exclusion by enhancing the root apoplastic barrier** when expressed in Arabidopsis, reducing Na+ accumulation and decreasing stele permeability to propidium iodide under salinity. The same study framed these results in an application context for crop salt tolerance improvement and cited global salinization pressure (~**1.125 billion hectares** of salinized land). (liu2023sbcasplp1c1improvessalt pages 1-2)

Although this is not SbCASP1 (C5YAP3), it provides **direct, recent (2023) sorghum CASP-family functional evidence** supporting the inference that sorghum CASP proteins can be leveraged to strengthen endodermal/apoplastic barriers. (liu2023sbcasplp1c1improvessalt pages 1-2)

### 4. Current applications and real-world implementations

#### 4.1 Transgenic/heterologous expression as a proof-of-concept implementation
A clear real-world implementation pattern in this literature is **engineering/expressing CASP-family genes** to modulate the root apoplastic barrier and thereby improve salt tolerance.

In sorghum-derived gene experiments, **SbCASP4** expressed in Arabidopsis increased salt tolerance and salt exclusion phenotypes under **50 mM NaCl**, with multiple physiological readouts including ion accumulation, reactive oxygen species (ROS) measures, and propidium iodide exclusion from the stele (barrier integrity). (wei2021sbcasp4improvessalt pages 9-12)

In addition, SbCASP4 overexpression yielded a higher computed **salinity threshold** (external NaCl concentration reducing fresh weight to 50%) of **125 mM NaCl** compared with **115 mM** for wild type and **94 mM** for a casp5 mutant, providing a quantitative metric for barrier-mediated salt tolerance improvement. (wei2021sbcasp4improvessalt pages 6-9)

#### 4.2 Crop improvement rationale: barrier traits affect ion homeostasis and stress resilience
The agricultural rationale is that the endodermal diffusion barrier influences **Na+ exclusion** (salt tolerance) and **selective nutrient uptake/homeostasis**. The 2024 rice/Arabidopsis CASP family analysis explicitly connects CASP genes (including OsCASP1 evidence cited therein) to selective mineral uptake and nutrient stress phenotypes, supporting the notion that tuning CASP-mediated barriers can shape ion homeostasis in crops. (xue2024comparativeanalysisof pages 17-18, xue2024comparativeanalysisof pages 13-15)

### 7. Functional annotation for SbCASP1 (C5YAP3): best-supported statements (with explicit evidence level)

**Most defensible primary function (inference from conserved CASP mechanism):**
SbCASP1 (C5YAP3) is most likely a **4TM plasma membrane scaffold protein** that localizes to the **Casparian strip membrane domain (CSD)** in root endodermal cells, where it helps organize a membrane microdomain and **recruits/positions lignin polymerization machinery** to build the Casparian strip, thereby contributing to the **endodermal apoplastic diffusion barrier** and influencing ion/water movement into the stele. **Pathway/regulation (inference from conserved endodermal programs):**
