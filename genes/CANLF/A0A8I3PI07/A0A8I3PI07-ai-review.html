Gene Description

CNNM4 (Cyclin and CBS Domain Divalent Metal Cation Transport Mediator 4) is a multi-pass transmembrane protein belonging to the ancient conserved domain protein (ACDP) family. The 796-amino-acid protein contains a signal peptide, an N-terminal immunoglobulin-like domain, a CNNM transmembrane domain with four transmembrane helices, a cystathionine-beta-synthase (CBS) domain pair that binds Mg2+-ATP, and a C-terminal cyclic nucleotide binding homology (CNBH) domain. CNNM4 functions as a magnesium efflux transporter at the basolateral membrane of epithelial cells, mediating Mg2+ extrusion from cells. In mammals, CNNM4 is expressed in intestinal epithelial cells where it is critical for magnesium homeostasis. The protein has also been reported to have sodium ion transmembrane transporter activity. Mutations in the human ortholog cause Jalili syndrome, characterized by cone-rod dystrophy and amelogenesis imperfecta. CNNM4 forms oligomeric complexes and its transport activity is regulated by intracellular Mg2+-ATP binding to the CBS domain pair.

Core Functions

CNNM4 is a magnesium efflux transporter that mediates Mg2+ extrusion across the basolateral plasma membrane of epithelial cells. The protein contains a CNNM transmembrane domain with four transmembrane helices that forms the transport pathway, and a cytoplasmic CBS domain pair that binds Mg2+-ATP to regulate transport activity. CNNM4 localizes to the basolateral membrane where it functions in transcellular magnesium absorption, particularly in intestinal epithelium. Loss of function causes Jalili syndrome (cone-rod dystrophy with amelogenesis imperfecta) in humans, highlighting its essential role in magnesium homeostasis for eye and tooth development. CNNM4 forms oligomeric complexes and interacts with PRL (PTP4A) family phosphatases which regulate its activity.

core_functions:
  - description: >-
      CNNM4 is a magnesium efflux transporter that mediates Mg2+ extrusion across the
      basolateral plasma membrane of epithelial cells. The protein contains a CNNM
      transmembrane domain with four transmembrane helices that forms the transport
      pathway, and a cytoplasmic CBS domain pair that binds Mg2+-ATP to regulate transport
      activity. CNNM4 localizes to the basolateral membrane where it functions in
      transcellular magnesium absorption, particularly in intestinal epithelium. Loss
      of function causes Jalili syndrome (cone-rod dystrophy with amelogenesis imperfecta)
      in humans, highlighting its essential role in magnesium homeostasis for eye and
      tooth development. CNNM4 forms oligomeric complexes and interacts with PRL (PTP4A)
      family phosphatases which regulate its activity.
