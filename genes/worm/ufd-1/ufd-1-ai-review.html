Gene Description

UFD-1 (Ubiquitin Fusion Degradation protein 1) is an essential cofactor of the CDC-48/p97 AAA ATPase segregase complex in C. elegans. Together with NPL-4, UFD-1 forms a heterodimeric cofactor that directs CDC-48 activity toward polyubiquitinated substrates. The CDC-48/UFD-1/NPL-4 complex plays critical roles in two major cellular processes: (1) ER-associated degradation (ERAD), where it extracts misfolded ubiquitinated proteins from the ER membrane for proteasomal degradation, and (2) DNA replication regulation, where it coordinates the degradation of replication licensing factors and the disassembly of replisome components. UFD-1 is essential for embryonic development, with RNAi knockdown causing embryonic lethality due to S-phase progression defects and activation of the DNA replication checkpoint. In adults, UFD-1 depletion causes germline developmental defects and accumulation of misfolded proteins in the ER.

Summary
C. elegans ufd-1 encodes a conserved UFD1-family cofactor that heterodimerizes with NPL-4 to form the principal ubiquitin receptor module for the CDC-48/p97 segregase. In worms, this complex executes ERAD and, crucially, chromatin-associated degradation during DNA replication, ensuring turnover of licensing factors (e.g., CDT-1) and promoting removal/disassembly of CDC-45/GINS/CMG to preserve fork progression and genome stability. Nuclear accumulation of UFD-1/NPL-4 post-mitosis aligns with S-phase functions. Depletion produces embryonic lethality, replication stress, RAD-51 foci, and germline defects; recent preprint work links UFD-1‚ÄìNPL-4 inhibition to an ELT-2‚Äìmediated immune-like state. These functions are mechanistically underpinned by UFD-1‚ÄìNPL-4‚Äôs ubiquitin-binding and client recruitment to CDC-48, a paradigm consolidated by 2016 worm studies and 2024 mechanistic syntheses (mouysset2008cellcycleprogression pages 1-2, franz2016chromatinassociateddegradationis pages 10-11, dressler2016theaaaatpasep97 pages 31-34, mirzadeh2024molecularmechanismof pages 18-19, rao2025inhibitionofthe pages 1-4, franz2016ringofchange pages 4-5).
