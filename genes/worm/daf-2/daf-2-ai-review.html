Gene Description

DAF-2 is the C. elegans insulin/IGF-1 receptor ortholog that functions as a receptor tyrosine kinase to regulate metabolism, longevity, dauer diapause formation, and stress resistance. DAF-2 signals through a conserved PI3K/AKT pathway (via AGE-1/AKT-1/AKT-2) to negatively regulate the FOXO transcription factor DAF-16 and the Nrf transcription factor SKN-1. Reduced DAF-2 signaling leads to DAF-16 nuclear localization, activation of longevity genes, enhanced stress resistance, increased fat storage, and entry into the dauer diapause stage. DAF-2 is expressed in neurons, intestine, and other tissues, and functions cell-nonautonomously to coordinate organismal growth, metabolism, and aging through secondary signaling cascades.
