Gene Description

ALX-1 is the sole C. elegans ortholog of mammalian ALIX (PDCD6IP/AIP1) and yeast Bro1p, functioning as a Bro1/ALIX-family ESCRT accessory protein. It contains an N-terminal Bro1 domain (binds ESCRT-III CHMP4/Snf7), a central V (ALIX_V) domain (mediates YPXnL/LYPXL late-domain interactions), and a C-terminal proline-rich domain (PRD) that scaffolds regulatory and ESCRT-I contacts. ALX-1 has dual functions in endocytic recycling (via physical interaction with RME-1) and in MVB/late endosome cargo sorting for lysosomal degradation. It is required for post-internalization trafficking and degradation of LIN-12/Notch in vulval precursor cells.

Key concepts and current understanding
- Molecular role as ESCRT accessory: ALX-1/ALIX promotes recruitment and activation (closed-to-open transition) of ESCRT-III/CHMP4 polymerization via Bro1‚ÄìCHMP4 binding, providing an ESCRT-0/‚ÄìI/‚ÄìII-independent entry point under some contexts. Mechanistically, ALIX can drive CHMP4 activation and organization at scission sites; NMR-guided analyses show ALIX triggers CHMP4 activation that is required for abscission.
