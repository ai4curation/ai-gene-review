CC   -!- FUNCTION: Catalyzes the hydroxylation of the 5-methoxy-2-methyl-3-(all-
CC       trans-polyprenyl)benzoquinone at the C6 position and participates in
CC       the biosynthesis of ubiquinone. Catalyzes the reaction through a
CC       substrate-mediated reduction pathway, whereby NADH shuttles electrons
CC       to 5-methoxy-2-methyl-3-(all-trans-decaprenyl)benzoquinone, which then
CC       transfers the electrons to the two Fe(3+) centers. The binding of 5-
CC       methoxy-2-methyl-3-(all-trans-polyprenyl)benzoquinone (DMQn) mediates
CC       reduction of the diiron center by nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide
CC       (NADH) and initiates oxygen activation for subsequent DMQ hydroxylation
CC       (By similarity). Also has a structural role in the COQ enzyme complex,
CC       stabilizing other COQ polypeptides (By similarity). Involved in
CC       lifespan determination in a ubiquinone-independent manner
CC       (PubMed:25961505, PubMed:9020081). Plays a role in modulating
CC       mitochondrial stress responses, acting in the nucleus, perhaps via
CC       regulating gene expression, independent of its characterized
CC       mitochondrial function in ubiquinone biosynthesis (PubMed:25961505).
CC       Plays a role in modulating polyribosome formation (PubMed:30198021).
CC       {ECO:0000255|HAMAP-Rule:MF_03194, ECO:0000269|PubMed:25961505,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:30198021, ECO:0000269|PubMed:9020081}.
