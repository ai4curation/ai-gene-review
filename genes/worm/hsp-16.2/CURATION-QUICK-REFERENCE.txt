================================================================================
                            KEY EVIDENCE SUMMARY
================================================================================

PRIMARY FUNCTION: ATP-independent holdase chaperone
  - Binds unfolded/misfolded proteins
  - Prevents aggregation via hydrophobic interactions
  - Maintains substrates in refolding-competent state
  - Does NOT directly catalyze refolding

HEAT SHOCK RESPONSE (Core Function):
  - Regulated by HSF-1 (heat shock factor)
  - >1000-fold induction following heat stress
  - Tissue-specific expression (intestine, pharynx, muscle, hypodermis)
  - Heat shock elements in promoter regulate transcription
  - Expression is developmentally restricted

LOCALIZATION:
  - Primarily cytoplasmic (well-established)
  - Possible nuclear translocation under stress (phylogenetic evidence)
  - Promoter relocates to nuclear pores during heat shock

PROTEIN STRUCTURE:
  - Small heat shock protein (sHSP/HSP20 family)
  - ~145 amino acids (~16 kDa)
  - Alpha-crystallin domain (ACD) - conserved core structure
  - I-X-I/V motif in C-terminal - oligomerization
  - Four related hsp16 genes in C. elegans at two loci

REGULATORY PATHWAYS:
  - Heat shock response: HSF-1 (primary)
  - Insulin/IGF-1 signaling: IIS/DAF-16 (longevity pathway)
  - MAPK pathway: PMK-1/p38 (stress support)
  - Non-cell-autonomous: Neuronal GPCR signaling
  - Chromatin organization: Promoter repositioning to nuclear pores

                         CRITICAL ANNOTATIONS
================================================================================

ANNOTATION ERROR:
  GO:0042026 "protein refolding" is FUNCTIONALLY INACCURATE

  Problem: Implies sHSPs catalyze refolding reaction
  Reality: sHSPs are ATP-independent holdases that:
           - Bind to unfolded proteins
           - Prevent aggregation
           - Maintain refolding-competent state
           - Require ATP-dependent HSP70 for actual refolding

  Supporting Quote (Deep Research):
    "As a member of the sHSP family, HSP-16.2 functions primarily as a
     holdase chaperone buffering proteotoxic stress. sHSPs are ATP-independent
     chaperones that prevent irreversible aggregation of misfolded proteins
     (holdase function)." - Bushman 2023

CORRECTIVE ACTION:
  Change to GO:0036506 "maintenance of unfolded protein"
  This accurately describes the holdase mechanism
