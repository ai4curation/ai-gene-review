CC   -!- FUNCTION: Transcription factor that binds to the promoter and the
CC       enhancer regions of target genes (By similarity). May act together with
CC       the bZIP transcription factor, cebp-2 (PubMed:26876169). Involved in
CC       responding to mitochondrial damage (PubMed:28662060, PubMed:32350153).
CC       Plays a role in the delay of age-associated mitochondrial fragmentation
CC       and muscle decline (PubMed:32350153). Has a protective role in response
CC       to infection by the Gram-negative bacterium P.aeruginosa
CC       (PubMed:20133860, PubMed:22520465, PubMed:26876169, PubMed:28662060).
CC       Required to prevent P.aeruginosa ToxA-mediated lethality
CC       (PubMed:22520464, PubMed:22520465). Required for the activation of
CC       several infection response genes including irg-1 and irg-2 following
CC       P.aeruginosa infection; target gene activation may involve effects of
CC       the bacterial toxin, ToxA, and perhaps other toxins (PubMed:20133860,
CC       PubMed:22520465). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P53567,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:20133860, ECO:0000269|PubMed:22520464,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:22520465, ECO:0000269|PubMed:26876169,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:28662060, ECO:0000269|PubMed:32350153}.
CC   -!- INTERACTION:

CC       Q21148; Q8IG69: cebp-2; NbExp=3; IntAct=EBI-6731556, EBI-2914231;
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Nucleus {ECO:0000269|PubMed:22520465,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:26876169}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Expressed in the pharynx and throughout the
CC       intestine. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:20133860, ECO:0000269|PubMed:22520465}.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: Induced by P.aeruginosa infection (PubMed:20133860,
CC       PubMed:22520464, PubMed:22520465, PubMed:26876169). Induced by
CC       translational inhibitors such as cycloheximide and exogenous or
CC       P.aeruginosa-derived ToxA (PubMed:22520465).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:20133860, ECO:0000269|PubMed:22520464,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:22520465, ECO:0000269|PubMed:26876169}.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: RNAi-mediated knockdown increases the proportion
CC       of aged worms with fragmented mitochondria from 5% to 59% and causes a
CC       30% decrease in cellular ATP levels at day 8 in adult
CC       (PubMed:32350153). RNAi-mediated knockdown decreases survival upon
CC       infection with P.aeruginosa (PubMed:20133860, PubMed:28662060). RNAi-
CC       mediated knockdown reduces induction of expression of infection
CC       response gene, irg-1, in response to P.aeruginosa (PubMed:20133860,
CC       PubMed:26876169). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:20133860,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:26876169, ECO:0000269|PubMed:28662060,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:32350153}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the bZIP family. C/EBP subfamily. {ECO:0000305}.
