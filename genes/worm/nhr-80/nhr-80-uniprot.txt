CC   -!- FUNCTION: Transcription factor (PubMed:16839188, PubMed:35021096).
CC       Binds to regulatory elements and regulates transcription of target
CC       genes, including acyltransferase dgat-2 (PubMed:35021096). As part of a
CC       lysosome-to-nucleus retrograde lipid signaling pathway, acts as a
CC       direct nuclear receptor of oleoylethanolamide (OEA) and, acting in
CC       concert with nuclear hormone receptor nhr-49, activates the
CC       transcription of genes promoting longevity and mitochondrial beta-
CC       oxidation (PubMed:25554789, PubMed:26671266). Required to modulate
CC       expression of delta-9 fatty acid desaturases, thereby regulating lipid
CC       metabolism; in some contexts, acting in concert with nhr-49
CC       (PubMed:16839188, PubMed:21423649, PubMed:22511885). Involved in
CC       modulation of lipid metabolism in response to the citrate-induced
CC       mitochondrial unfolded protein response (mtUPR), acting downstream of
CC       transcription factor dve-1 and ubiquitin-like protein 5
CC       (PubMed:35021096). Plays a role in modulating mitochondrial morphology
CC       and function (PubMed:22511885). Involved in positively modulating life-
CC       span in a germline-dependent manner, acting in concert with nuclear
CC       hormone receptor daf-12 (PubMed:21423649). Plays a role in
CC       transgenerational lipid accumulation in response to a high-fat diet
CC       (PubMed:35140229). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:16839188,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:21423649, ECO:0000269|PubMed:22511885,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:25554789, ECO:0000269|PubMed:26671266,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:35021096, ECO:0000269|PubMed:35140229}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Interacts with nuclear hormone receptor nhr-49; the
CC       interaction is direct. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:22511885}.

CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: RNAi-mediated knockdown causes a change in fatty
CC       acid composition, including an increase in saturated fatty acid 18:0
CC       and decrease in unsaturated 18:1 (PubMed:16839188). In a fatty acid
CC       desaturase fat-6 mutant background, become thin, slow growing,
CC       reproductively inviable after 4 days, and accumulate very high levels
CC       of 18:0 saturated fatty acid (PubMed:16839188). In a fat-6 mutant
CC       background, also drastically reduces expression of fat-7
CC       (PubMed:16839188). In either a fat-5 or fat-7 mutant background,
CC       accumulates high levels of 18:0 saturated fatty acid, but less so than
CC       in the fat-6 mutant background (PubMed:16839188). Significantly reduced
CC       lifespan in a glp-1 mutant background, but does not affect lifespan of
CC       wild type (PubMed:21423649). Knockdown in the first generation
CC       offspring (F1) of adults fed a high-fat diet prevents lipid
CC       accumulation (PubMed:35140229). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:16839188,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:21423649, ECO:0000269|PubMed:35140229}.
