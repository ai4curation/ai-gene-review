Gene Description

OCR-2 (OSM-9 and Capsaicin Receptor-related 2) is a TRPV (Transient Receptor Potential Vanilloid) family cation channel subunit that functions as part of a heteromeric complex with OSM-9 in C. elegans sensory neurons. The OCR-2/OSM-9 heteromeric channel is essential for multiple sensory modalities including thermosensation, chemosensation, osmosensation, and nociception. The channel localizes to the sensory cilia of amphid neurons (including ADL, ASH, AWA, ADF) where it mediates sensory transduction. OCR-2/OSM-9 is directly activated by warming stimuli and functions as an accessorial warm receptor contributing to temperature acclimatization. The channel also regulates serotonin biosynthesis in ADF neurons. Neither OCR-2 nor OSM-9 alone is sufficient for heat-evoked currents; functional channels require co-expression of both subunits. The channel displays outward-rectifying current-voltage characteristics typical of TRPV channels.

We verified that the target is Caenorhabditis elegans ocr-2, a TRPV-subfamily ion channel subunit that functions with OSM-9; the organism and functional identity are consistent with experimental studies demonstrating OSM-9/OCR-2 as a heteromeric, warmth-responsive TRPV channel in C. elegans sensory neurons (particularly ADL) (ohnishi2020osm9andocr2 pages 1-2, ohnishi2020osm9andocr2 pages 8-9). The literature below focuses strictly on C. elegans OCR-2/OSM-9 biology per the mandate to avoid cross-organism symbol ambiguity.

1) Key concepts and definitions
- Molecular identity and family: ocr-2 encodes a transient receptor potential vanilloid (TRPV)-like channel subunit. Together with OSM-9, it forms a functional TRPV channel required for thermosensory signaling and multiple chemosensory modalities in C. elegans (ohnishi2020osm9andocr2 pages 1-2, ohnishi2020osm9andocr2 pages 8-9).
- Channel assembly: OSM-9 and OCR-2 act cooperatively; single subunits alone do not confer warming-evoked currents, whereas co-expression yields a functional, heat-responsive channel, consistent with heteromeric TRPV assembly (ohnishi2020osm9andocr2 pages 7-8, ohnishi2020osm9andocr2 pages 11-12).
- Sensory cilia context: OCR-2/OSM-9 signals in sensory cilia. TRPV-family channels require intraflagellar transport (IFT) for vectorial movement within cilia, and the ciliary transition zone (TZ) functions as a diffusion/gating barrier controlling membrane protein entry/exit (burght2019signalingcompartmentat pages 19-22).

2) Recent developments and latest research (emphasis 2023‚Äì2024)
- Cilia trafficking controls response dynamics: A 2024 study dissected the roles of cilia structure and IFT in shaping chemosensory responses. Acute or prolonged IFT inhibition can generate ectopic periciliary membrane compartment (PCMC) branches that mislocalize ciliary signaling proteins including OSM-9, altering odorant response desensitization despite preserved primary responses in some contexts (AWA). Detailed functional annotation of ocr-2
Primary molecular function and substrates
- Channel class: OCR-2 is a TRPV-subfamily ion channel subunit. In heteromer with OSM-9, it forms an ion channel that is directly heat-activated in both heterologous expression (oocytes) and in vivo reconstitution, with properties resembling vertebrate TRPVs (outward-rectifying I‚ÄìV) (ohnishi2020osm9andocr2 pages 7-8, ohnishi2020osm9andocr2 pages 11-12).
- Stimuli: Heat is a direct stimulus for the OSM-9/OCR-2 channel; OSM-9/OCR-2 acts as an accessorial warmth receptor in ADL neurons contributing to temperature acclimatisation (ohnishi2020osm9andocr2 pages 1-2, ohnishi2020osm9andocr2 pages 8-9). Genetic literature also supports roles in chemosensation and mechanosensation across neuron types, consistent with polymodal TRPV function (ohnishi2020osm9andocr2 pages 8-9).
- Ion selectivity: The oocyte recordings demonstrate macroscopic currents and outward rectification; specific ionic selectivity ratios were not provided in the excerpts, but the behavior and I‚ÄìV features are consistent with cation-permeable TRPV channels (ohnishi2020osm9andocr2 pages 7-8, ohnishi2020osm9andocr2 pages 11-12).

Subunit composition and codependence
- Heteromerization: Functional heat responses require co-expression of OSM-9 and OCR-2; each alone is insufficient. In vivo, ciliary colocalization is reported to be codependent, and single-mutant phenotypes/rescues support heteromeric channel function (ohnishi2020osm9andocr2 pages 8-9, ohnishi2020osm9andocr2 pages 5-6).

Cellular/subcellular localization and trafficking
- Sensory cilia localization: OCR-2/OSM-9 localize to sensory cilia, where TRPV channels are trafficked by IFT; a transition zone barrier modulates ciliary entry/exit (burght2019signalingcompartmentat pages 19-22).
- IFT dependence and mislocalization under trafficking stress: Conditional IFT inhibition or IFT mutants cause formation of PCMC branches and mislocalization of OSM-9 and other ciliary signaling proteins from cilia to PCMC branches in AWA, altering desensitization kinetics while sometimes sparing primary responses, highlighting the importance of precise localization for response dynamics (PLOS Biology, 2024; https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pbio.3002892) (philbrook2024ciliastructureand pages 10-11, philbrook2024ciliastructureand pages 16-18).

Neuronal expression and organismal roles
- Neuron types and behaviors: OCR-2 functions with OSM-9 in multiple sensory neurons. In ADL, OSM-9/OCR-2 mediate warmth responses and contribute to temperature acclimatisation (ohnishi2020osm9andocr2 pages 1-2, ohnishi2020osm9andocr2 pages 5-6). In nociceptive contexts, a heteromeric OSM-9/OCR-2 channel is required for aversive chemical responses such as to 1-octanol, consistent with roles in ASH nociceptors (J. Neurosci., 2019; https://doi.org/10.1523/JNEUROSCI.2371-18.2019) (oakes2019cannabinoidsstimulatethe pages 9-10). More broadly, modeling and synthesis works list OCR-2 as a TRPV nociceptor subunit with roles in chemosensation and thermosensation across amphid neurons, though some of these are preprints (castano2025modelingtrpvlikereceptors pages 13-17, pieroni2025identificationofmolecular pages 27-29).
- Osmosensation and egg-laying context: Hyperosmotic environments inhibit egg-laying via sensory and mechanical pathways; one 2024 study reported that ocr-2(vs29) mutants remained inhibited under high osmolarity, suggesting that OCR-2 is not alone sufficient to mediate this inhibition (bioRxiv, 2024; https://doi.org/10.1101/2024.12.30.630790) (medrano2024osmolarityregulatesc. pages 28-33).

- Endocannabinoids: 2-AG engages TRP channel‚Äìdependent mechanisms to stimulate serotonin and dopamine release and modulate locomotory behavior. Although this work did not isolate OCR-2 specifically, it implicates TRPV-like channels in cannabinoid-dependent neuromodulation in C. elegans, intersecting with OCR-2/OSM-9‚Äìdependent aversive responses (J. Synthesis and outlook
The strongest evidence defines OCR-2 as a TRPV-family subunit that, together with OSM-9, forms a heat-responsive cation channel in sensory cilia, shaping thermosensory signaling in ADL and contributing to chemosensory and nociceptive behaviors across neuron types. Recent work (2024) emphasizes that continuous IFT and precise spatial organization of ciliary signaling components‚Äîincluding TRPV subunits‚Äîcritically tune sensory response dynamics, particularly desensitization and adaptation. Endocannabinoid-dependent modulation of TRP pathways provides a tractable entry point to dissect neuromodulatory control of TRPV-dependent behaviors in vivo. Open questions include precise ionic selectivity and pharmacology of native OSM-9/OCR-2 channels, neuron-by-neuron contributions in polymodal nociception and osmosensation, and the molecular logic by which the ciliary transition zone and IFT adaptively regulate TRPV trafficking and signaling.
