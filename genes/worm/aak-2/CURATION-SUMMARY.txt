REQUIRED CHANGES
================

1. REMOVE: GO:0050714 - Positive regulation of protein secretion [IMP]
   Evidence: PMID:28128367
   Reason: This annotation is MISLEADING and contradicts GO:0050709
           (negative regulation of protein secretion, also from same paper).

   The PMID:28128367 study shows:
   - AAK-2 keeps CRTC-1 inactive, which restrains FLP-7 secretion
   - Loss of aak-2 increases secretion (loss-of-function phenotype)

   GO annotations should reflect GENE FUNCTION, not loss-of-function phenotypes.
   AAK-2's actual function is NEGATIVE regulation of secretion (GO:0050709).

   The annotation GO:0050714 confuses the consequence of AAK-2 loss with
   what AAK-2 actually does. This should be REMOVED.

2. MODIFY: GO:0005515 - Protein binding [IPI]
   Evidence: PMID:19123269
   Current: Generic protein binding annotation
   Replace with: GO:0031588 - nucleotide-activated protein kinase complex

   Reason: GO curation guidelines strongly discourage generic "protein binding"
           annotations. The specific interaction documented is with AAKB-2
           (the AMPK beta subunit), which is essential complex formation,
           not incidental binding.

           The GO:0031588 annotation already captures AAK-2 as part of the
           AMPK complex, which is the more appropriate way to represent this.

3. MODIFY: GO:0120025 - Plasma membrane bounded cell projection [IEA]
   Evidence: PMID:18408008
   Current: General cellular compartment term
   Replace with: GO:0030424 - axon

   Reason: The experimental evidence specifically shows axonal localization
           (ventral cord neurons), not broader cell projections.
           GO:0030424 is more specific and informative.

           This change improves annotation specificity while remaining
           consistent with the evidence.

CURATION PHILOSOPHY APPLIED
============================

1. GO Annotation Guidelines
   - Removed generic "protein binding" (discouraged per GO standards)
   - Used most specific terms supported by evidence
   - Avoided high-level parent terms when better specificity available

2. Evidence Hierarchy
   - Direct experimental evidence (IDA, IMP) preferred when available
   - IBA used appropriately for conserved, well-characterized functions

4. Core vs. Non-Core Designation
   - Core: conserved AMPK functions (kinase activity, energy sensing, autophagy,
           TORC1 regulation, lifespan)
   - Non-core: C. elegans-specific developmental/behavioral processes
