Gene Description

FNDC-1 is the Caenorhabditis elegans ortholog of mammalian FUNDC1 and a member of the conserved FUN14 family. It is a small (138-residue) multi-pass protein of the mitochondrial outer membrane. FNDC-1 acts as a receptor/adaptor for selective autophagy of mitochondria (mitophagy): loss of fndc-1 impairs mitochondrial clearance in two characterized contexts. First, in the early embryo it contributes to the elimination of sperm-derived (paternal) mitochondria after fertilization, providing a second, ubiquitin-independent route that acts redundantly with, and later than, an initial ubiquitination-dependent mechanism; loss of fndc-1 delays paternal-mitochondria degradation and allows paternal mitochondrial DNA to persist in cross-progeny. Second, in body-wall muscle it mediates mitophagy triggered by hypoxia-reoxygenation, and its loss engages an ATFS-1/UPRmt stress program that is protective against reoxygenation injury. By analogy to mammalian FUNDC1, FNDC-1 is expected to recruit autophagosomal ATG8/LC3 proteins (LGG-1/LGG-2 in the worm) through an LC3-interacting region (LIR) motif, but the LIR and a direct ATG8 interaction have not yet been demonstrated in C. elegans. FNDC-1 thus represents a receptor-mediated, ubiquitin-independent branch of mitochondrial quality control, distinct from the PINK-1/PDR-1 (PINK1/Parkin) pathway.

Core Functions

FNDC-1 functions as a mitophagy receptor/adaptor that bridges mitochondria to autophagosomes. It localizes to the mitochondrial outer membrane, and genetic loss-of-function shows it is required for selective autophagy of mitochondria in two worm contexts: hypoxia-reoxygenation-induced mitophagy in body-wall muscle (PMID:33416042) and paternal-mitochondria elimination after fertilization (PMID:31233739, PMID:31153831). As a FUN14-family protein it is predicted (by orthology to mammalian FUNDC1) to recruit ATG8/LC3 proteins (LGG-1/LGG-2) via an LC3-interacting region (LIR); the worm LIR and a direct ATG8 interaction have not yet been demonstrated experimentally.

Suggested Questions for Experts

Q: What is the precise LIR motif sequence in C. elegans FNDC-1, and how does phosphorylation regulate its interaction with LGG-1/LGG-2 (C. elegans LC3 homologs)? Mammalian FUNDC1 regulation involves phosphorylation of the LIR motif (Tyr18, Ser13, Ser17) by SRC, CK2, ULK1, and dephosphorylation by PGAM5. The regulatory mechanisms for C. elegans FNDC-1 remain to be fully characterized.

Q: Does FNDC-1 localize to mitochondria-ER contact sites (MAMs) in C. elegans, and what is its relationship with DRP-1-mediated mitochondrial fission during mitophagy? Recent preprints suggest FNDC-1 forms puncta at MAMs and recruits DRP-1 during developmental mitophagy at the oocyte-to-zygote transition. This mechanism needs validation in peer-reviewed studies.

Q: What is the relationship between FNDC-1 and other mitophagy pathways (PINK-1/PDR-1, DCT-1/NIX) in C. elegans across different stress conditions and developmental stages? FNDC-1 operates independently of ubiquitination for paternal mitochondria elimination, but the pathway relationships and possible redundancies in different contexts remain unclear.

Suggested Experiments

Experiment: Co-immunoprecipitation or split-GFP complementation assays to detect direct interaction between FNDC-1 and LGG-1/LGG-2 in vivo. While FNDC-1 is functionally characterized as a mitophagy receptor, direct physical interaction with C. elegans ATG8 homologs has not been demonstrated experimentally.

Knowledge Gaps

What is not known — curated, literature-grounded statements of the open unknowns (the inverse of core functions).

Gap: The molecular activity of FNDC-1 as a mitophagy receptor is not experimentally established in C. elegans. Its LC3-interacting region (LIR) motif has not been identified or mutated, and a direct physical interaction between FNDC-1 and the worm ATG8 orthologs LGG-1/LGG-2 has not been demonstrated. Whether receptor/adaptor function requires an intact LIR is therefore inferred from mammalian FUNDC1 rather than shown in worm.

Comprehensive research report: C. elegans fndc-1 (UniProt Q22252)

1) Key concepts and definitions
- Gene/protein identity: fndc-1 (WormBase T06D8.7) encodes the C. elegans ortholog of mammalian FUNDC1, a conserved FUN14-domain protein functioning as a mitophagy receptor. Experimental data support mitochondrial localization and function in selective mitochondrial autophagy (mitophagy) in C. elegans (Lim et al., Autophagy, Jan 2021; https://doi.org/10.1080/15548627.2021.1872885) (lim2021fndc1mediatedmitophagyand pages 2-4, lim2021fndc1mediatedmitophagyand pages 1-2).
- Mitophagy receptor concept: FUNDC1-family receptors reside in the OMM and bind ATG8/LC3 family proteins via an LC3-interacting region (LIR) to target mitochondria for autophagic degradation; in mammals, LIR-dependent LC3 binding is regulated by phosphorylation and ubiquitination, which modulate receptor activity (Choubey et al., Cells, Dec 2021; https://doi.org/10.3390/cells11010038; Liu et al., Antioxidants & Redox Signaling, Apr 2020; https://doi.org/10.1089/ars.2019.8013) (choubey2021molecularmechanismsand pages 16-17, liu2020mitophagyandits pages 6-8).
- Domain/family alignment: The C. elegans protein belongs to the FUN14 family, consistent with conserved receptor features (LIR, OMM localization, ER–mitochondria contact enrichment) that are well established for mammalian FUNDC1 and are observed for FNDC-1 in worm developmental contexts (choubey2021molecularmechanismsand pages 16-17, liu2020mitophagyandits pages 6-8, sherwood2025programmedmitophagyat pages 11-15).

2) Recent developments and latest research (priority 2023–2024, plus 2025 preprints as preliminary)
- 2024 expert review (C. elegans): A recent review of mitochondrial quality control in Alzheimer’s disease models highlights fndc-1 as one of the conserved mitophagy receptors in C. elegans, summarizing that loss of fndc-1 reduces mitophagy and emphasizing use of mt-Keima/MitoTimer reporters to quantify flux in vivo (Ganguly et al., Antioxidants, Nov 2024; https://doi.org/10.3390/antiox13111343) (ganguly2024mitochondrialqualitycontrol pages 32-33).
- 2025 preprint (developmental mitophagy): Two related preprints report a developmentally programmed mitophagy event at the oocyte-to-zygote transition (MOZT) in C. elegans and identify FNDC-1 as the required receptor. 3) Molecular function, localization, pathways, and phenotypes
- Function as mitophagy receptor under hypoxia–reoxygenation (HR): In body wall muscle, FNDC-1 mediates mitophagy after short HR exposure; loss of fndc-1 delays HR-induced mitophagy measured by mito-mKeima, whereas prolonged HR reveals paradoxical protection in fndc-1 loss-of-function animals through compensatory adaptation (Lim et al., Autophagy, Jan 2021; https://doi.org/10.1080/15548627.2021.1872885) (lim2021fndc1mediatedmitophagyand pages 2-4, lim2021fndc1mediatedmitophagyand pages 1-2).
- Subcellular localization: Biochemical fractionation and proteinase K accessibility indicate FNDC-1 is on the OMM. Single-copy mRuby3::FNDC-1 partially co-localizes with mitochondria in muscle and decorates mitochondrial ends; overexpression promotes mitochondrial fragmentation, consistent with receptor activity (Lim et al., Autophagy, 2021; https://doi.org/10.1080/15548627.2021.1872885) (lim2021fndc1mediatedmitophagyand pages 2-4).
- Developmental mitophagy during oocyte-to-zygote transition (MOZT): FNDC-1 forms puncta at mitochondria-associated membranes (MAMs), recruits DRP-1, and is necessary for mitochondrial fragmentation and mitophagy surrounding fertilization. Quantitative imaging shows that ~70% of FNDC-1 puncta are short-lived (10 s–2 min), while ~30% are long-lived (4–8.6 min) and consistently trigger fragmentation; co-localization with mitochondrial foci increases from ~35% in -2 oocytes to ~60% in -1 oocytes. Mitochondrial volume in the oocyte is reduced (~2.0 µm³ to ~0.3 µm³) during MOZT, and this reduction is abrogated by RNAi of autophagy genes (vps-34, atg-16.1/2, vps-41, vha-2/9/12) or drp-1. These findings support FNDC-1 as the receptor executing a developmentally programmed mitophagy independent of PINK1/Parkin in this context (bioRxiv Feb 2025; https://doi.org/10.1101/2025.02.01.636045; Research Square Apr 2025; https://doi.org/10.21203/rs.3.rs-6330979/v1) (thendral2025mitophagyatthe pages 6-10, sherwood2025programmedmitophagyat pages 11-15).
- Paternal mitochondria elimination after fertilization: Genetic studies indicate fndc-1 contributes to removal of paternal mitochondria, acting as one of at least two temporally distinct mechanisms (with a ubiquitination-dependent pathway) to target paternal organelles for degradation in the embryo (summarized in PLOS Genetics review and C. elegans MQC review). This situates FNDC-1 in conserved mitochondrial purifying mechanisms at fertilization (Haeussler et al., PLOS Genetics, Mar 2020; https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pgen.1008638; Ganguly et al., Antioxidants, Nov 2024; https://doi.org/10.3390/antiox13111343) (haeussler2020autophagycompensatesfor pages 34-35, ganguly2024mitochondrialqualitycontrol pages 32-33).
- Pathway crosstalk and dependencies: Under HR, loss of fndc-1 triggers ATFS-1–dependent UPRmt programs, providing prophylactic protection against subsequent injury, highlighting crosstalk between receptor-mediated mitophagy and mitochondrial stress adaptation (Lim et al., Autophagy, 2021; https://doi.org/10.1080/15548627.2021.1872885) (lim2021fndc1mediatedmitophagyand pages 1-2). During acute heat stress, autophagy facilitates mitochondrial rebuilding through a DRP-1–dependent process that does not require PINK-1/pdr-1 (Parkin) or fndc-1, suggesting condition-specific redundancy or alternative mitophagy/bulk autophagy pathways (Chen et al., J Cell Biol., Mar 2021; https://doi.org/10.1083/jcb.201909139) (lim2021fndc1mediatedmitophagyand pages 2-4).
- Regulatory mechanisms: Direct C. elegans post-translational regulation motifs for FNDC-1 remain incompletely defined in the available worm literature; however, mammalian FUNDC1 regulation provides a strong framework: LIR-dependent LC3 binding is inhibited by phosphorylation at Tyr18 and Ser13 (SRC/CK2) and enhanced by ULK1 phosphorylation (Ser17) and PGAM5-mediated dephosphorylation under hypoxia/depolarization; MARCH5-mediated ubiquitination limits receptor abundance early during hypoxia; FUNDC1 interacts with DRP1 and is enriched at MAMs (Choubey et al., Cells, Dec 2021; https://doi.org/10.3390/cells11010038; Liu et al., Antioxidants & Redox Signaling, Apr 2020; https://doi.org/10.1089/ars.2019.8013). These mechanisms are plausible and partially mirrored by FNDC-1’s DRP-1 recruitment and MAM localization observed in C. elegans development (choubey2021molecularmechanismsand pages 16-17, liu2020mitophagyandits pages 6-8, sherwood2025programmedmitophagyat pages 11-15).

4) Quantitative statistics and data highlights
- HR-induced mitophagy (muscle): fndc-1 loss-of-function significantly reduced the percentage of mitochondria undergoing mitophagy and the number of mitolysosomes (objects >10 pixels) by mito-mKeima imaging at early timepoints; example statistics include P < 0.0002 comparing wild type vs fndc-1[rny14] by two-way ANOVA (Lim et al., Autophagy, 2021; https://doi.org/10.1080/15548627.2021.1872885) (lim2021fndc1mediatedmitophagyand pages 2-4).
- Developmental MOZT (oocyte): mitochondrial volume reduced from ~2.0 µm³ to ~0.3 µm³ at -1 oocyte; GFP::DRP-1 puncta increased ~2-fold in number and ~1.5-fold in size; FNDC-1 puncta lifetimes showed two classes (~70% short-lived 10 s–2 min; ~30% long-lived 4–8.6 min) with the latter consistently triggering mitochondrial fragmentation; FNDC-1/mitochondria co-localization rose from ~35% (-2 oocytes) to ~60% (-1 oocytes). Autophagy gene RNAi and drp-1 RNAi blocked mitochondrial reduction and mitophagy (bioRxiv Feb 2025; https://doi.org/10.1101/2025.02.01.636045; Research Square Apr 2025; https://doi.org/10.21203/rs.3.rs-6330979/v1) (thendral2025mitophagyatthe pages 6-10, sherwood2025programmedmitophagyat pages 11-15).
- Paternal mitochondria elimination: Loss of fndc-1 delays paternal organelle removal, acting alongside ubiquitin-dependent targeting; the redundancy highlights multiple quality control layers around fertilization (Haeussler et al., PLOS Genetics, 2020; https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pgen.1008638) (haeussler2020autophagycompensatesfor pages 34-35).

5) Current applications and real-world implementations
- C. elegans as an in vivo platform to quantify mitophagy receptor function: FNDC-1 studies leverage mito-mKeima, MitoTimer, and endogenous reporters (mRuby3::FNDC-1, GFP::DRP-1) to measure mitophagy flux and receptor dynamics in muscle and germline. These assays are applicable to genetic and pharmacologic screening for mitophagy modulators with translational potential for neurodegeneration and ischemia-reperfusion injury (Ganguly et al., Antioxidants, Nov 2024; https://doi.org/10.3390/antiox13111343; Lim et al., Autophagy, 2021; https://doi.org/10.1080/15548627.2021.1872885) (ganguly2024mitochondrialqualitycontrol pages 32-33, lim2021fndc1mediatedmitophagyand pages 2-4, lim2021fndc1mediatedmitophagyand pages 1-2).
- Translational inference from mammalian FUNDC1: Regulatory axes (ULK1–PGAM5–SRC/CK2–MARCH5) and MAM enrichment suggest druggable nodes to modulate receptor activity; these are being explored preclinically in cardiovascular and neurodegenerative disease contexts and provide hypotheses to test in worm for conserved intervention points (Choubey et al., Cells, Dec 2021; https://doi.org/10.3390/cells11010038; Liu et al., Antioxidants & Redox Signaling, Apr 2020; https://doi.org/10.1089/ars.2019.8013) (choubey2021molecularmechanismsand pages 16-17, liu2020mitophagyandits pages 6-8).

6) Expert opinions and analysis
- Context-dependent roles and compensations: C. elegans studies emphasize that mitophagy is not monolithic. Under heat stress, autophagy-mediated mitochondrial rebuilding is DRP-1–dependent yet independent of fndc-1, pink-1, and pdr-1, indicating alternative or bulk autophagy routes can dominate conditionally. Under HR, receptor-mediated mitophagy via FNDC-1 is engaged early, but chronic stress induces adaptive UPRmt programs that can paradoxically protect in fndc-1 mutants. Together, these underscore the need to interrogate stress-, tissue-, and developmental context when evaluating FNDC-1 function (Chen et al., J Cell Biol., Mar 2021; https://doi.org/10.1083/jcb.201909139; Lim et al., Autophagy, 2021; https://doi.org/10.1080/15548627.2021.1872885) (lim2021fndc1mediatedmitophagyand pages 2-4, lim2021fndc1mediatedmitophagyand pages 1-2).
- Developmental mitophagy as quality control: The MOZT findings suggest receptor-driven purifying selection of mitochondria at fertilization, aligning with prior observations of paternal mitochondria elimination and extending them to a broader programmed germline event mediated by FNDC-1. If validated, these data position FNDC-1 as a central developmental mitophagy receptor in nematodes (bioRxiv Feb 2025; https://doi.org/10.1101/2025.02.01.636045; Research Square Apr 2025; https://doi.org/10.21203/rs.3.rs-6330979/v1) (thendral2025mitophagyatthe pages 6-10, sherwood2025programmedmitophagyat pages 11-15).

7) Limitations and open questions
- Direct phosphorylation/ubiquitination mapping and LIR validation for C. elegans FNDC-1 remain to be fully elucidated; mammalian data provide strong mechanistic hypotheses (PGAM5/ULK1/SRC/CK2/MARCH5) to test in worm. Definitive pathway placement relative to PINK1–PDR-1 (Parkin) and DCT-1/NIX across tissues and stresses requires further genetic epistasis and biochemical work. The MOZT studies are preprints and await peer-reviewed confirmation (choubey2021molecularmechanismsand pages 16-17, liu2020mitophagyandits pages 6-8, thendral2025mitophagyatthe pages 6-10, sherwood2025programmedmitophagyat pages 11-15).

  FNDC-1 is the Caenorhabditis elegans ortholog of mammalian FUNDC1 and a member of the
  conserved FUN14 family. It is a small (138-residue) multi-pass protein of the
  mitochondrial outer membrane. FNDC-1 acts as a receptor/adaptor for selective autophagy
  of mitochondria (mitophagy): loss of fndc-1 impairs mitochondrial clearance in two
  characterized contexts. First, in the early embryo it contributes to the elimination of
  sperm-derived (paternal) mitochondria after fertilization, providing a second,
  ubiquitin-independent route that acts redundantly with, and later than, an initial
  ubiquitination-dependent mechanism; loss of fndc-1 delays paternal-mitochondria
  degradation and allows paternal mitochondrial DNA to persist in cross-progeny. Second,
  in body-wall muscle it mediates mitophagy triggered by hypoxia-reoxygenation, and its
  loss engages an ATFS-1/UPRmt stress program that is protective against reoxygenation
  injury. By analogy to mammalian FUNDC1, FNDC-1 is expected to recruit autophagosomal
  ATG8/LC3 proteins (LGG-1/LGG-2 in the worm) through an LC3-interacting region (LIR)
  motif, but the LIR and a direct ATG8 interaction have not yet been demonstrated in
  C. elegans. FNDC-1 thus represents a receptor-mediated, ubiquitin-independent branch of
  mitochondrial quality control, distinct from the PINK-1/PDR-1 (PINK1/Parkin) pathway.
