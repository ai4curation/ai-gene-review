Gene Description

FNDC-1 is the C. elegans ortholog of mammalian FUNDC1, a mitophagy receptor that resides on the mitochondrial outer membrane and mediates selective autophagy of mitochondria. FNDC-1 belongs to the conserved FUN14 family characterized by an LC3-interacting region (LIR) motif that enables binding to ATG8/LC3 family proteins to target mitochondria for autophagic degradation. FNDC-1 functions in multiple physiological contexts: (1) it mediates hypoxia-reoxygenation-induced mitophagy in somatic tissues such as body wall muscle (PMID:33416042), (2) it contributes to paternal mitochondria elimination after fertilization as a ubiquitin-independent mechanism (PMID:31233739, PMID:31153831), and (3) recent studies suggest it is required for developmentally programmed mitophagy at the oocyte-to-zygote transition. FNDC-1 localizes to the mitochondrial outer membrane and can be found at mitochondria-associated membrane (MAM) contact sites where it recruits the fission factor DRP-1. Unlike the PINK1/Parkin pathway, FNDC-1 represents a receptor-mediated, ubiquitin-independent mitophagy mechanism. The deep research report (fndc-1-deep-research-falcon.md) provides comprehensive literature evidence supporting these functions.

Suggested Questions for Experts

Q: What is the precise LIR motif sequence in C. elegans FNDC-1, and how does phosphorylation regulate its interaction with LGG-1/LGG-2 (C. elegans LC3 homologs)? Mammalian FUNDC1 regulation involves phosphorylation of the LIR motif (Tyr18, Ser13, Ser17) by SRC, CK2, ULK1, and dephosphorylation by PGAM5. The regulatory mechanisms for C. elegans FNDC-1 remain to be fully characterized.

Q: Does FNDC-1 localize to mitochondria-ER contact sites (MAMs) in C. elegans, and what is its relationship with DRP-1-mediated mitochondrial fission during mitophagy? Recent preprints suggest FNDC-1 forms puncta at MAMs and recruits DRP-1 during developmental mitophagy at the oocyte-to-zygote transition. This mechanism needs validation in peer-reviewed studies.

Q: What is the relationship between FNDC-1 and other mitophagy pathways (PINK-1/PDR-1, DCT-1/NIX) in C. elegans across different stress conditions and developmental stages? FNDC-1 operates independently of ubiquitination for paternal mitochondria elimination, but the pathway relationships and possible redundancies in different contexts remain unclear.

Experiment: Co-immunoprecipitation or split-GFP complementation assays to detect direct interaction between FNDC-1 and LGG-1/LGG-2 in vivo. While FNDC-1 is functionally characterized as a mitophagy receptor, direct physical interaction with C. elegans ATG8 homologs has not been demonstrated experimentally.

Single-copy mRuby3::FNDC-1 partially co-localizes with mitochondria in muscle and decorates mitochondrial ends; overexpression promotes mitochondrial fragmentation, consistent with receptor activity (Lim et al., Autophagy, 2021; https://doi.org/10.1080/15548627.2021.1872885) (lim2021fndc1mediatedmitophagyand pages 2-4).
- Developmental mitophagy during oocyte-to-zygote transition (MOZT): FNDC-1 forms puncta at mitochondria-associated membranes (MAMs), recruits DRP-1, and is necessary for mitochondrial fragmentation and mitophagy surrounding fertilization. Quantitative imaging shows that ~70% of FNDC-1 puncta are short-lived (10 s‚Äì2 min), while ~30% are long-lived (4‚Äì8.6 min) and consistently trigger fragmentation; co-localization with mitochondrial foci increases from ~35% in -2 oocytes to ~60% in -1 oocytes. Mitochondrial volume in the oocyte is reduced (~2.0 ¬µm¬≥ to ~0.3 ¬µm¬≥) during MOZT, and this reduction is abrogated by RNAi of autophagy genes (vps-34, atg-16.1/2, vps-41, vha-2/9/12) or drp-1. These findings support FNDC-1 as the receptor executing a developmentally programmed mitophagy independent of PINK1/Parkin in this context (bioRxiv Feb 2025; https://doi.org/10.1101/2025.02.01.636045; Research Square Apr 2025; https://doi.org/10.21203/rs.3.rs-6330979/v1) (thendral2025mitophagyatthe pages 6-10, sherwood2025programmedmitophagyat pages 11-15).
- Paternal mitochondria elimination after fertilization: Genetic studies indicate fndc-1 contributes to removal of paternal mitochondria, acting as one of at least two temporally distinct mechanisms (with a ubiquitination-dependent pathway) to target paternal organelles for degradation in the embryo (summarized in PLOS Genetics review and C. elegans MQC review). This situates FNDC-1 in conserved mitochondrial purifying mechanisms at fertilization (Haeussler et al., PLOS Genetics, Mar 2020; https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pgen.1008638; Ganguly et al., Antioxidants, Nov 2024; https://doi.org/10.3390/antiox13111343) (haeussler2020autophagycompensatesfor pages 34-35, ganguly2024mitochondrialqualitycontrol pages 32-33).
- Pathway crosstalk and dependencies: Under HR, loss of fndc-1 triggers ATFS-1‚Äìdependent UPRmt programs, providing prophylactic protection against subsequent injury, highlighting crosstalk between receptor-mediated mitophagy and mitochondrial stress adaptation (Lim et al., Autophagy, 2021; https://doi.org/10.1080/15548627.2021.1872885) (lim2021fndc1mediatedmitophagyand pages 1-2). During acute heat stress, autophagy facilitates mitochondrial rebuilding through a DRP-1‚Äìdependent process that does not require PINK-1/pdr-1 (Parkin) or fndc-1, suggesting condition-specific redundancy or alternative mitophagy/bulk autophagy pathways (Chen et al., J Cell Biol., Mar 2021; https://doi.org/10.1083/jcb.201909139) (lim2021fndc1mediatedmitophagyand pages 2-4).
- Regulatory mechanisms: Direct C. elegans post-translational regulation motifs for FNDC-1 remain incompletely defined in the available worm literature; however, mammalian FUNDC1 regulation provides a strong framework: LIR-dependent LC3 binding is inhibited by phosphorylation at Tyr18 and Ser13 (SRC/CK2) and enhanced by ULK1 phosphorylation (Ser17) and PGAM5-mediated dephosphorylation under hypoxia/depolarization; MARCH5-mediated ubiquitination limits receptor abundance early during hypoxia; FUNDC1 interacts with DRP1 and is enriched at MAMs (Choubey et al., Cells, Dec 2021; https://doi.org/10.3390/cells11010038; Liu et al., Antioxidants & Redox Signaling, Apr 2020; https://doi.org/10.1089/ars.2019.8013). These mechanisms are plausible and partially mirrored by FNDC-1‚Äôs DRP-1 recruitment and MAM localization observed in C. elegans development (choubey2021molecularmechanismsand pages 16-17, liu2020mitophagyandits pages 6-8, sherwood2025programmedmitophagyat pages 11-15).

4) Quantitative statistics and data highlights
- HR-induced mitophagy (muscle): fndc-1 loss-of-function significantly reduced the percentage of mitochondria undergoing mitophagy and the number of mitolysosomes (objects >10 pixels) by mito-mKeima imaging at early timepoints; example statistics include P < 0.0002 comparing wild type vs fndc-1[rny14] by two-way ANOVA (Lim et al., Autophagy, 2021; https://doi.org/10.1080/15548627.2021.1872885) (lim2021fndc1mediatedmitophagyand pages 2-4).
- Developmental MOZT (oocyte): mitochondrial volume reduced from ~2.0 ¬µm¬≥ to ~0.3 ¬µm¬≥ at -1 oocyte; GFP::DRP-1 puncta increased ~2-fold in number and ~1.5-fold in size; FNDC-1 puncta lifetimes showed two classes (~70% short-lived 10 s‚Äì2 min; ~30% long-lived 4‚Äì8.6 min) with the latter consistently triggering mitochondrial fragmentation; FNDC-1/mitochondria co-localization rose from ~35% (-2 oocytes) to ~60% (-1 oocytes). Autophagy gene RNAi and drp-1 RNAi blocked mitochondrial reduction and mitophagy (bioRxiv Feb 2025; https://doi.org/10.1101/2025.02.01.636045; Research Square Apr 2025; https://doi.org/10.21203/rs.3.rs-6330979/v1) (thendral2025mitophagyatthe pages 6-10, sherwood2025programmedmitophagyat pages 11-15).
- Paternal mitochondria elimination: Loss of fndc-1 delays paternal organelle removal, acting alongside ubiquitin-dependent targeting; the redundancy highlights multiple quality control layers around fertilization (Haeussler et al., PLOS Genetics, 2020; https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pgen.1008638) (haeussler2020autophagycompensatesfor pages 34-35).

5) Current applications and real-world implementations
- C. elegans as an in vivo platform to quantify mitophagy receptor function: FNDC-1 studies leverage mito-mKeima, MitoTimer, and endogenous reporters (mRuby3::FNDC-1, GFP::DRP-1) to measure mitophagy flux and receptor dynamics in muscle and germline. These assays are applicable to genetic and pharmacologic screening for mitophagy modulators with translational potential for neurodegeneration and ischemia-reperfusion injury (Ganguly et al., Antioxidants, Nov 2024; https://doi.org/10.3390/antiox13111343; Lim et al., Autophagy, 2021; https://doi.org/10.1080/15548627.2021.1872885) (ganguly2024mitochondrialqualitycontrol pages 32-33, lim2021fndc1mediatedmitophagyand pages 2-4, lim2021fndc1mediatedmitophagyand pages 1-2).
- Translational inference from mammalian FUNDC1: Regulatory axes (ULK1‚ÄìPGAM5‚ÄìSRC/CK2‚ÄìMARCH5) and MAM enrichment suggest druggable nodes to modulate receptor activity; these are being explored preclinically in cardiovascular and neurodegenerative disease contexts and provide hypotheses to test in worm for conserved intervention points (Choubey et al., Cells, Dec 2021; https://doi.org/10.3390/cells11010038; Liu et al., Antioxidants & Redox Signaling, Apr 2020; https://doi.org/10.1089/ars.2019.8013) (choubey2021molecularmechanismsand pages 16-17, liu2020mitophagyandits pages 6-8).

6) Expert opinions and analysis
- Context-dependent roles and compensations: C. elegans studies emphasize that mitophagy is not monolithic. Under heat stress, autophagy-mediated mitochondrial rebuilding is DRP-1‚Äìdependent yet independent of fndc-1, pink-1, and pdr-1, indicating alternative or bulk autophagy routes can dominate conditionally. Under HR, receptor-mediated mitophagy via FNDC-1 is engaged early, but chronic stress induces adaptive UPRmt programs that can paradoxically protect in fndc-1 mutants. Together, these underscore the need to interrogate stress-, tissue-, and developmental context when evaluating FNDC-1 function (Chen et al., J Cell Biol., Mar 2021; https://doi.org/10.1083/jcb.201909139; Lim et al., Autophagy, 2021; https://doi.org/10.1080/15548627.2021.1872885) (lim2021fndc1mediatedmitophagyand pages 2-4, lim2021fndc1mediatedmitophagyand pages 1-2).
- Developmental mitophagy as quality control: The MOZT findings suggest receptor-driven purifying selection of mitochondria at fertilization, aligning with prior observations of paternal mitochondria elimination and extending them to a broader programmed germline event mediated by FNDC-1. If validated, these data position FNDC-1 as a central developmental mitophagy receptor in nematodes (bioRxiv Feb 2025; https://doi.org/10.1101/2025.02.01.636045; Research Square Apr 2025; https://doi.org/10.21203/rs.3.rs-6330979/v1) (thendral2025mitophagyatthe pages 6-10, sherwood2025programmedmitophagyat pages 11-15).

7) Limitations and open questions
- Direct phosphorylation/ubiquitination mapping and LIR validation for C. elegans FNDC-1 remain to be fully elucidated; mammalian data provide strong mechanistic hypotheses (PGAM5/ULK1/SRC/CK2/MARCH5) to test in worm. Definitive pathway placement relative to PINK1‚ÄìPDR-1 (Parkin) and DCT-1/NIX across tissues and stresses requires further genetic epistasis and biochemical work. The MOZT studies are preprints and await peer-reviewed confirmation (choubey2021molecularmechanismsand pages 16-17, liu2020mitophagyandits pages 6-8, thendral2025mitophagyatthe pages 6-10, sherwood2025programmedmitophagyat pages 11-15).
