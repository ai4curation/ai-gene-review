Executive verification (MANDATORY)
- Target identifier: UniProt Q09490 corresponds to C. elegans protein LGG-1 (also known as ATG-8.1; gene lgg-1), belonging to the ATG8 ubiquitin-like protein family. The organism and aliases match the user-provided UniProt record. Domain architecture and family placement align with ATG8/LC3/GABARAP features described below. No conflicting gene with symbol lgg-1 in other organisms is used in this report. (Domain/family evidence: ATG8 family review) (varga2022theevolutionaryand pages 2-4)

1) Key concepts and definitions
- LGG-1 is the Caenorhabditis elegans ortholog of the ATG8/LC3/GABARAP family of ubiquitin-like proteins that are covalently conjugated to the autophagosomal membrane lipid phosphatidylethanolamine (PE) during macroautophagy. ATG8 proteins possess an ubiquitin-like fold with an N-terminal helix and interaction surfaces (“W- and L-sites”) that bind LIR/AIM motifs in partner proteins, enabling cargo selection and autophagosome biogenesis functions (Jun 2022; https://doi.org/10.1007/s42977-022-00123-6) (varga2022theevolutionaryand pages 2-4).
- LGG-1 vs LGG-2: C. elegans has two ATG8 paralogs. Comparative analyses indicate LGG-1 is more GABARAP-like and functions primarily in autophagosome formation and selective autophagy, whereas LGG-2 (LC3-like) contributes to later steps including autophagosome acidification and specific developmental/neuronal roles (Jun 2022; https://doi.org/10.1007/s42977-022-00123-6) (varga2022theevolutionaryand pages 2-4). Recent work on corpse processing further highlights distinct deployment of LGG-1 vs LGG-2 on phagolysosomes (Nov 2024; https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pone.0306435) (kolli2024phagolysosomesbreakdown pages 1-2).

2) Biochemical mechanism and primary function
- Processing and conjugation cascade: ATG4 first cleaves the LGG-1 precursor to expose the C-terminal glycine; ATG7 (E1-like) activates ATG8; ATG3 (E2-like) transfers it to PE on isolation membranes, with the ATG12–ATG5–ATG16 complex facilitating lipidation; deconjugation by ATG4 recycles LGG-1 (Jun 2022; https://doi.org/10.1007/s42977-022-00123-6) (varga2022theevolutionaryand pages 2-4). Reporter design and interpretations in C. elegans explicitly rely on this processing (Jul 2024; https://doi.org/10.1080/27694127.2024.2371736) (dawson2024afluorescentreporter pages 1-3).
- Role in autophagosome biogenesis: As with yeast/mammals, ATG8 dosage controls phagophore expansion and autophagosome size; the LGG-1 paralog executes analogous functions in worms to promote autophagosome membrane growth and maturation, and provides LIR-dependent scaffolding for selective autophagy cargo receptors (review synthesis) (varga2022theevolutionaryand pages 2-4).
- Non-canonical/associated functions on single-membrane compartments: In C. elegans corpse processing, ATG7-dependent lipidation of LC3/LGG proteins contributes to breakdown of phagolysosomal corpse membranes; LGG-2 accumulates within polar body phagolysosomes even when canonical autophagosome formation is compromised, highlighting lipidation-dependent but macroautophagy-independent roles. ATG-16.1/ATG-16.2 redundantly promote membrane breakdown, whereas ATG-9 is required for autophagosome formation but dispensable for timely corpse membrane degradation (Nov 2024; https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pone.0306435) (kolli2024phagolysosomesbreakdown pages 1-2).

3) Subcellular localization and dynamics
- LGG-1 cycles between diffuse cytosolic and punctate membrane-associated forms. GFP::LGG-1 puncta mark forming autophagosomes; tandem reporters discriminate maturation: mCherry::GFP::LGG-1 puncta that are GFP+ mCherry+ indicate autophagosomes, while GFP– mCherry+ indicate autolysosomes due to lysosomal quenching of GFP. A 2024 C. elegans-optimized flux reporter, GFP::LGG-1::mKate2 (AFR), is ATG-4 processed in vivo into GFP::LGG-1 and mKate2; the GFP:mKate ratio functions as a quantitative readout of flux in live worms (Jul 2024; https://doi.org/10.1080/27694127.2024.2371736) (dawson2024afluorescentreporter pages 1-3).
- In phagosome/corpse processing, LGG-1 and LGG-2 puncta transiently localize adjacent to phagosomes; marker entry indicates autophagosome–phagosome fusion events and subsequent delivery into phagolysosomes (Nov 2024; https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pone.0306435) (kolli2024phagolysosomesbreakdown pages 1-2).

4) Pathway and selective autophagy roles
- Paternal mitochondrial elimination (PME; selective mitophagy): After fertilization, paternal mitochondria are selectively recognized and engulfed by LGG-1–positive autophagosomes. Structured-illumination microscopy and colocalization analyses in zygotes show paternal mitochondrial clusters (MitoTracker Red) are enclosed or surrounded by LGG-1 autophagosomes; genetic perturbations of mitochondrial fusion (fzo-1) and fission (drp-1) modify the kinetics and specificity of PME by altering competition for the autophagy machinery. Loss of core conjugation (atg-7) permits paternal mtDNA persistence into the F1 generation (Sep 2016; https://doi.org/10.1038/ncomms12569) (wang2016kineticsandspecificity pages 5-7, wang2016kineticsandspecificity pages 10-11).
- Nucleophagy in germline and aging: Autophagic recycling of nuclear envelope-associated and nucleolar components limits nucleolar enlargement and preserves germline immortality under stress. Genetic ablation/knockdown of lgg-1 impairs this autophagy-dependent quality control, resulting in germline defects and accelerated aging phenotypes. LGG-1 reporters (adIs2122 lgg-1p::GFP::lgg-1) were used to visualize autophagy in these contexts in vivo (Dec 2023; https://doi.org/10.1038/s43587-022-00327-4) (papandreou2023nucleophagydelaysaging pages 9-10).
- LC3-associated phagocytosis-like roles: In C. elegans polar body corpse clearance, LGG proteins participate in phagolysosomal membrane breakdown via conjugation machinery, with macroautophagy dispensable in this non-apoptotic context. Distinct deployment of LGG-1 vs LGG-2 underscores specialized functions of ATG8 paralogs at single-membrane compartments (Nov 2024; https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pone.0306435) (kolli2024phagolysosomesbreakdown pages 1-2).

5) Recent developments (2023–2024) and latest research
- New autophagy flux reporter and quantitative profiling: GFP::LGG-1::mKate2 enables rapid, live-animal quantitation of autophagic flux. Developmental profiling showed basal flux is relatively low during larval stages, rises markedly in reproductive adults, and declines with age; autophagic flux trajectories differ among wild-type, dietary-restricted (eat-2), and insulin/IGF signaling mutant (daf-2) animals. The system also supported a high-content small-molecule screen to identify autophagy modulators (Jul 2024; https://doi.org/10.1080/27694127.2024.2371736) (dawson2024afluorescentreporter pages 1-3).
- Refining phagolysosomal roles of LGG paralogs: 2024 work in C. elegans embryos demonstrates ATG7-dependent LGG lipidation promotes corpse membrane breakdown; LGG-2 enrichment in phagolysosomes can occur independent of canonical autophagosome formation, distinguishing non-canonical LGG functions in corpse processing (Nov 2024; https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pone.0306435) (kolli2024phagolysosomesbreakdown pages 1-2).
- Nucleophagy and organismal aging: 2023 Nature Aging study positions LGG-1-dependent autophagy as a determinant of nucleolar homeostasis, stress resistance, and germline immortality, integrating nuclear quality control into the autophagy–aging axis (Dec 2023; https://doi.org/10.1038/s43587-022-00327-4) (papandreou2023nucleophagydelaysaging pages 9-10).

6) Applications and real-world implementations
- Genetic and imaging reporters: C. elegans strains expressing GFP::LGG-1 (e.g., adIs2122) and tandem reporters (mCherry::GFP::LGG-1) are standard in vivo tools to monitor autophagosome formation and autophagic flux across tissues. The 2024 AFR reporter (GFP::LGG-1::mKate2) adds a ratiometric, high-throughput-compatible flux measure, enabling developmental and pharmacological autophagy profiling (Jul 2024; https://doi.org/10.1080/27694127.2024.2371736) (dawson2024afluorescentreporter pages 1-3).
- Embryonic corpse clearance paradigms: Time-resolved imaging of LGG-1/2 dynamics during phagosome maturation and membrane breakdown under genetic perturbations (ATG-7, ATG-16s, ATG-9) provides a tractable model for non-canonical autophagy and LAP-like mechanisms (Nov 2024; https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pone.0306435) (kolli2024phagolysosomesbreakdown pages 1-2).
- PME assays: Colocalization of LGG-1 puncta with paternal mitochondria, plus PCR-based assays of paternal mtDNA persistence, quantify mitophagy efficiency and dissect mitochondrial dynamics–autophagy cross-talk (Sep 2016; https://doi.org/10.1038/ncomms12569) (wang2016kineticsandspecificity pages 5-7, wang2016kineticsandspecificity pages 10-11).

7) Expert opinions and authoritative context
- Evolutionary and mechanistic consensus: The ATG8 conjugation cascade, LIR-based interactions, and functional divergence between LC3-like and GABARAP-like subfamilies are well-supported across metazoans, with C. elegans LGG-1 aligning with GABARAP-like functions in autophagosome biogenesis and selective autophagy (Jun 2022; https://doi.org/10.1007/s42977-022-00123-6) (varga2022theevolutionaryand pages 2-4).
- Corpse processing perspective: Contemporary C. elegans work consolidates the view that ATG8 lipidation machinery can act in single-membrane contexts to facilitate phagolysosomal corpse degradation, complementing canonical macroautophagy pathways (Nov 2024; https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pone.0306435) (kolli2024phagolysosomesbreakdown pages 1-2).

8) Quantitative statistics and selected data
- Developmental flux trajectories: Using the AFR reporter in live C. elegans, basal autophagic flux is “relatively low during larval development,” increases “markedly in reproductive adults,” and then decreases with age; distinct genotype-specific flux profiles were observed for wild-type, eat-2, and daf-2 animals, demonstrating sensitivity to metabolic and insulin/IGF signaling status (Jul 2024; https://doi.org/10.1080/27694127.2024.2371736). The flux metric used was the GFP:mKate ratio derived from endogenous ATG-4 processing of GFP::LGG-1::mKate2 (dawson2024afluorescentreporter pages 1-3).
- PME imaging and counts: In zygotes, structured illumination microscopy quantified enclosure/partial enclosure of paternal mitochondria by LGG-1 autophagosomes, with per-panel mitochondria scored numbering 202, 202, 133, 108, 146, and 130 in the study’s analyses; colocalization was measured using Mander’s overlap coefficient on 15 zygotes per mating (Sep 2016; https://doi.org/10.1038/ncomms12569) (wang2016kineticsandspecificity pages 5-7). Genetic loss of atg-7 allowed persistence of paternal mtDNA into the F1 generation (Sep 2016; https://doi.org/10.1038/ncomms12569) (wang2016kineticsandspecificity pages 10-11).
- Phagolysosomal corpse processing: Depletion of ATG-7 delayed membrane breakdown and cargo disappearance; LGG-2 enrichment in phagolysosomes occurred independent of autophagosome formation, and ATG-16.1/16.2 redundantly promoted membrane breakdown, while ATG-9 was dispensable for timely breakdown in polar body clearance (Nov 2024; https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pone.0306435) (kolli2024phagolysosomesbreakdown pages 1-2).

Limitations and notes
- While classic resources (e.g., protocols/guidelines) standardize interpretations of LGG-1 reporters in C. elegans, the most recent primary, organism-specific advances emphasized here are from 2023–2024, covering nucleophagy, non-canonical corpse processing roles, and a new quantitative flux reporter (papandreou2023nucleophagydelaysaging pages 9-10, kolli2024phagolysosomesbreakdown pages 1-2, dawson2024afluorescentreporter pages 1-3). Broader ATG8 biochemical features are derived from an authoritative 2022 review (varga2022theevolutionaryand pages 2-4).

Conclusions
C. elegans LGG-1 (UniProt Q09490) is an ATG8-family ubiquitin-like protein that is proteolytically activated by ATG4, conjugated to PE via ATG7/ATG3 with ATG12–ATG5–ATG16, and functions at phagophores/autophagosomes to drive membrane expansion, maturation, and selective autophagy. In vivo, LGG-1 localizes as autophagosomal puncta and enables quantitative assessment of autophagy via GFP/mCherry tandem and new GFP::LGG-1::mKate2 reporters. Recent work defines LGG-1’s roles in selective mitophagy during paternal mitochondrial elimination, in nucleophagy underpinning germline immortality and aging resistance, and in non-canonical, lipidation-dependent support of phagolysosomal corpse degradation. Together, these studies refine LGG-1’s mechanistic annotation and provide validated tools and readouts for tissue- and stage-resolved autophagy analysis in the worm (varga2022theevolutionaryand pages 2-4, dawson2024afluorescentreporter pages 1-3, wang2016kineticsandspecificity pages 5-7, wang2016kineticsandspecificity pages 10-11, papandreou2023nucleophagydelaysaging pages 9-10, kolli2024phagolysosomesbreakdown pages 1-2).
