CC   -!- FUNCTION: P granule component, which acts redundantly with P granule
CC       component meg-1 to promote P granule segregation during embryogenesis,
CC       and germ cell proliferation and differentiation in larval stages
CC       (PubMed:18202375, PubMed:21305687, PubMed:25535836). Together with
CC       phosphorylated meg-1, promotes the disassembly of zygotic P granules in
CC       the anterior cytoplasm of pre-gastrulation embryos (PubMed:25535836).
CC       Together with dephosphorylated meg-1, promotes the assembly and
CC       accumulation of zygotic P granules in the posterior cytoplasm of pre-
CC       gastrulation embryos (PubMed:25535836). Required for fertility
CC       (PubMed:18202375, PubMed:21305687). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:18202375,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:21305687, ECO:0000269|PubMed:25535836}.
CC   -!- INTERACTION:
CC       Q21127; O17670: eya-1; NbExp=3; IntAct=EBI-317734, EBI-311862;
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cytoplasmic granule
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:18202375}. Note=Localizes to P granules in
CC       embryogenesis. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:18202375}.
CC   -!- DEVELOPMENTAL STAGE: Expressed in the P lineage during embryogenesis
CC       (PubMed:18202375). Expressed at the 4- to 8-cell stage, and expression
CC       remains throughout the remaining P cell divisions in P2, P3 and P4
CC       cells, and subsequently in primordial germ cells Z2 and Z3
CC       (PubMed:18202375). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:18202375}.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: RNAi-mediated knockdown results in sterility, and
CC       in P granule segregation defects (PubMed:18202375). RNAi-mediated
CC       knockdown increases the sterility phenotype of meg-1 vr10 and meg-1
CC       vr11 mutants from 15 to 100% and from 4 to 93%, respectively, and
CC       results in a larger proportion of under-proliferated gonads than in the
CC       single meg-1 mutants (PubMed:18202375, PubMed:21305687,
CC       PubMed:25535836). Simultaneous RNAi-mediated knockdown of meg-1 and
CC       meg-2 results in defects in P granule disassembly in the anterior
CC       cytoplasm of the P1 blastomere causing some P granules to be mis-
CC       segregated to somatic blastomeres (PubMed:25535836).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:18202375, ECO:0000269|PubMed:21305687,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:25535836}.
