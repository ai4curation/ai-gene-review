Gene Description

LOV-1 (Location Of Vulva defective 1) is the C. elegans ortholog of human PKD1/Polycystin-1. It is a large membrane protein with multiple transmembrane domains, a PLAT domain, and a GPS autoproteolytic cleavage site. LOV-1 functions as part of a mechanosensory receptor complex with PKD-2 (the C. elegans TRPP2/Polycystin-2 ortholog) in male-specific sensory neurons. This complex is required for male mating behaviors, specifically response to hermaphrodite contact and vulva location. LOV-1 localizes to sensory cilia in ray neurons, hook neurons, and cephalic (CEM) neurons of adult males. The protein interacts with ATP-2 (F1-ATP synthase beta subunit) via its PLAT domain, with casein kinase II regulatory subunit KIN-10, and with STAM-1 for endosomal trafficking. LOV-1 and PKD-2 act in the same genetic pathway, as double mutants show no enhancement over single mutants.

Core Functions

LOV-1 functions as a mechanosensory receptor in male sensory cilia, forming a complex with PKD-2 (TRPP2/Polycystin-2) to detect mechanical stimuli during male mating behavior. LOV-1 acts as the receptor component that partners with the ion channel PKD-2.

Key concepts and definitions with current understanding
- Protein identity and domains. LOV-1 is the C. elegans homolog of mammalian polycystin-1 (PC1). It possesses a large extracellular N-terminus with a GAIN domain containing a GPCR proteolysis site (GPS), a PLAT/LH2 domain, and an ~11-transmembrane C-terminal region. LOV-1 undergoes GPS-dependent autoproteolysis to yield an N-terminal (NTM) and a C-terminal membrane-tethered fragment (CTM). GPS point mutation (e.g., C2181S) abolishes proper CTM localization and phenocopies lov-1 null in behavior, demonstrating the functional necessity of cleavage (Dec 2022; PLOS Genetics: https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pgen.1010560) (walsh2022trackingnand pages 6-8, maguire2015myristoylatedcil7is pages 13-17).
- Subcellular localization. LOV-1 is a ciliary protein. Endogenous dual-tagging and live imaging show CTM LOV-1 localizes to the ciliary membrane and tip and is shed on environmentally released ciliary EVs, whereas NTM is enriched at the ciliary base, cilium proper, and extracellular matrix surrounding the cilium, and is excluded from tip EVs (Dec 2022; PLOS Genetics: https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pgen.1010560) (walsh2022trackingnand pages 2-4, walsh2022trackingnand pages 4-6).
- Functional complex with PKD-2. CTM LOV-1 co-traffics and co-localizes with PKD-2 from ER/soma to dendritic transport vesicles, to cilia, and into EVs. PKD-2 is required for CTM‚Äôs somatic exit, ciliary targeting, and EV localization, while PKD-2 can enter cilia/EVs in the absence of LOV-1 (albeit with altered abundance/aggregation), indicating interdependent and independent behaviors within the polycystin pathway (Dec 2022; PLOS Genetics: https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pgen.1010560) (walsh2022trackingnand pages 8-12, walsh2022trackingnand pages 4-6).

Expert opinions and analysis from authoritative sources
- Mechanistic consensus: LOV-1/PKD-2 define a conserved ciliary polycystin pathway that controls sensory signaling and inter-organismal communication via EVs. The 2022 in vivo imaging and the 2025 EV-cargo mapping together support a model in which LOV-1 (via its CTM) scaffolds PKD-2 and recruits additional membrane cargos into specific EV subtypes, with GPS cleavage being essential for proper localization and function (Dec 2022; PLOS Genetics: https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pgen.1010560; Apr 2025; Nature Communications: https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-025-57512-3) (walsh2022trackingnand pages 6-8, walsh2022trackingnand pages 8-12, nikonorova2025polycystinsrecruitcargo pages 1-2, nikonorova2025polycystinsrecruitcargo pages 7-8).
- Ciliary homeostasis and glia interactions. Studies in 2021 show that ectocytosis/EV budding functions as a safeguard pathway to remove excess ciliary cargo, and that glial uptake of ectosomes shapes cilia, emphasizing neuron‚Äìglia regulation of ciliary composition. Biological role, pathways, and localization of LOV-1
- Primary role. LOV-1 functions as the PC1-like scaffold and sensory module in the ciliary membrane of male-specific neurons, where its CTM associates with PKD-2 to form a functional complex; LOV-1 is additionally required independently of PKD-2 for specific chemosensory behaviors (ascaroside #3 detection), pointing to dual polycystin-coupled and polycystin-uncoupled signaling roles (Dec 2022; PLOS Genetics: https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pgen.1010560) (walsh2022trackingnand pages 12-14, walsh2022trackingnand pages 8-12).
- Subcellular site of action. LOV-1 CTM operates at the ciliary membrane, concentrating at the ciliary tip‚Äîan EV-biogenesis zone‚Äîwhere it co-packages with PKD-2 into EVs for environmental release; LOV-1 NTM resides in the cilium and extracellular matrix but is excluded from EVs (Dec 2022; PLOS Genetics: https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pgen.1010560) (walsh2022trackingnand pages 2-4, walsh2022trackingnand pages 8-12).
- EV-mediated signaling. LOV-1 scaffolds cargo-specific EV subtypes: PKD-2+ EVs carry PACL-1, PAML-1/2, and TRF-1/2 in a LOV-1‚Äìdependent manner, potentially creating distinct ‚Äúsignalosomes‚Äù that may mediate inter-organismal communication and mating-related sensory functions (Apr 2025; Nature Communications: https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-025-57512-3; Dec 2022; PLOS Genetics: https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pgen.1010560) (nikonorova2025polycystinsrecruitcargo pages 1-2, nikonorova2025polycystinsrecruitcargo pages 8-9, walsh2022trackingnand pages 12-14).
- Upstream regulation. ECM components and neuron‚Äìglia interactions (phagocytic uptake of ectosomes) modulate ciliary morphology and receptor abundance; IFT dynamics and ectocytosis govern import/removal balance of ciliary cargo including polycystins (Jul 2018; bioRxiv: https://doi.org/10.1101/376152; Sep 2021; eLife: https://doi.org/10.7554/eLife.67670) (walsh2022trackingnand pages 20-21, maguire2015myristoylatedcil7is pages 13-17).

Limitations and open questions
- Biochemical detection of endogenous LOV-1 remains challenging due to low abundance; much of the mechanistic inference relies on endogenous tagging and live-cell imaging rather than biochemical purification (Dec 2022; PLOS Genetics: https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pgen.1010560) (walsh2022trackingnand pages 4-6).
- While the EV cargo code has been expanded (PACL-1, PAMLs, TRF-1/2), how these cargos modulate channel activity and behavior in cell-autonomous versus EV-mediated, non-autonomous contexts remains an active area (Apr 2025; Nature Communications: https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-025-57512-3) (nikonorova2025polycystinsrecruitcargo pages 8-9).
