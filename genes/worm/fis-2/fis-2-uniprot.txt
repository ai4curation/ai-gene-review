CC   -!- FUNCTION: Involved in the fragmentation of the mitochondrial network
CC       (By similarity). Involved in perinuclear clustering of the
CC       mitochondrial network (By similarity). May act, redundantly with fis-1,
CC       downstream of mitochondrial fission, before the fission products
CC       participate in mitochondrial homeostasis, mitophagy, or apoptosis
CC       (PubMed:24196833). Plays a role in apoptosis by promoting mitochondrial
CC       elimination and cell-death execution, acting downstream of caspase ced-
CC       3, and perhaps independently of dynamin GTPase drp-1, caspase ced-9 and
CC       apoptosis-inducing factor AIFM/wah-1 (PubMed:18722182).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9Y3D6, ECO:0000255|PIRNR:PIRNR008835,

CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: Mitochondrial connectivity appears normal and
CC       also normal in a fis-1 mutant background (PubMed:18722182). Abnormal,
CC       but minor, affects on apoptosis, with fewer cell corpses and an
CC       increase in cell numbers in a caspase ced-3, ced-4, or dynamin GTPase
CC       drp-1 mutant background (PubMed:18722182). Increases number of ectopic
CC       cells in a combined ced-3 and drp-1 mutant background
CC       (PubMed:18722182). Increases number of ectopic cells in a combined ced-
CC       3; ced-9 mutant background, which increases further in a ced-3; ced-9;
CC       drp-1 mutant background (PubMed:18722182). Larger number of
CC       mitochondria, and little reduction in mitochondria area index, in cell
CC       corpses in a ced-1 mutant background (PubMed:18722182).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:18722182}.
