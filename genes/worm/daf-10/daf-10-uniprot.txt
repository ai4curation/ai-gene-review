CC   -!- FUNCTION: Component of the IFT complex A (IFT-A), a complex required
CC       for retrograde ciliary transport and entry into cilia of G protein-
CC       coupled receptors (GPCRs) (PubMed:28479320). Plays a role in chemotaxis
CC       and sensory perception (PubMed:7240452, PubMed:7705621). Required for
CC       entry into and exit from the dauer larval stage and this may be
CC       mediated by daf-12, daf-16 and daf-41 (PubMed:23284299,
CC       PubMed:25830239, PubMed:7240452). Controls the behavioral response,
CC       namely the avoidance response, and pathogen-responsive gene expression
CC       in the response to pathogenic bacteria such as E.coli and P.aeruginosa
CC       (PubMed:23284299). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11301258,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:23284299, ECO:0000269|PubMed:25830239,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:28479320, ECO:0000269|PubMed:7240452,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:7705621}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Component of the IFT complex A (IFT-A) composed of at least
CC       che-11, daf-10, dyf-2, ift-139, ift-43 and ifta-1.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:28479320}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell projection, cilium
CC       {ECO:0000305|PubMed:11301258}. Note=Shuttles at the same rate as
CC       intraflagellar transport (IFT) complex B proteins such as osm-1, osm-5
CC       and osm-6 along the cilia of the amphid and phasmid sensory neurons.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11301258}.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: Sterile. Defective chemotaxis with 86% of mutants
CC       exhibiting reduced tracking to the chemoattractant ammonium chloride.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:7705621}.
