CC   -!- FUNCTION: Probable transmembrane GTPase (By similarity). Mediates
CC       mitochondrial fusion (PubMed:18722182, PubMed:19327994,
CC       PubMed:21248201, PubMed:25190516, PubMed:33734301). Fusion of
CC       mitochondria occurs in many cell types and constitutes an important
CC       step in mitochondria morphology, which is balanced between fusion and
CC       fission (By similarity). Dispensable for normal apoptotic processes
CC       during embryonic development (PubMed:18722182).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P38297, ECO:0000269|PubMed:21248201,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:25190516}.

CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: RNAi-mediated knockdown results in fragmented
CC       mitochondria, most likely due to lack of fusion activity
CC       (PubMed:25190516, PubMed:33734301). RNAi-mediated knockdown in a moma-1

CC       (PubMed:21248201). In a drp-1 mutant background, partially restores
CC       some tubular mitochondria (PubMed:33734301). Upon acute heat stress, in
CC       a drp-1 mutant background, autophagosomes clustered at mitochondria are
CC       detected in the epidermis (PubMed:33734301).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:21248201, ECO:0000269|PubMed:25190516}.
