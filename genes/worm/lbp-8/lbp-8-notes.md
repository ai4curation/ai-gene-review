# lbp-8 Research Notes

## Gene Overview
lbp-8 (T22G5.6) encodes a fatty acid-binding protein (FABP) homolog that functions as a lysosomal lipid chaperone in C. elegans. It is a key mediator in the lysosome-to-nucleus retrograde lipid signaling pathway linking lysosomal lipid metabolism to nuclear transcription and longevity.

## Core Pathway: LIPL-4 → LBP-8 → NHR-49/NHR-80

LBP-8 shuttles lipid signals generated by the lysosomal lipase LIPL-4 to the nucleus, where they activate transcription factors NHR-49 and NHR-80 to promote longevity [PMID:25554789 "Lysosomal signaling molecules regulate longevity in Caenorhabditis elegans"].

## Structural Features
- Crystal structure solved at 1.3 Å (PDB: 6C1Z) [PMID:31292465 "Structural characterization of life-extending Caenorhabditis elegans Lipid Binding Protein 8"]
- Adopts typical lipocalin/FABP fold: beta barrel capped by alpha helical lid
- Monomer in solution (16.4 kDa)
- Interior cavity: 825 Å² surface area, 1170 Å³ volume
- Contains structurally conserved nuclear localization signal (NLS) in portal region (K24, R33, K34)
- Classified as a "collisional" FABP - interacts with membranes via hydrophobic patch in alpha helical lid

## Lipid Binding
- Binds oleic acid and OEA with high and similar affinity [PMID:31292465 "both had very similar Ki's, suggesting oleic acid, along with OEA, are high affinity ligands of LBP-8"]
- OEA binding affinity ~3x higher than for C20 fatty acids (AA, DGLA, omega-3 AA) [PMID:25554789]
- Also binds arachidonic acid, DGLA, palmitic acid, linoleic acid, and other long-chain fatty acids [PMID:31292465]
- Does NOT bind bile acids (cholic, taurocholic, glycocholic) despite pocket size similarity to FABP6 [PMID:31292465]
- R132 is the key conserved residue for fatty acid head group interaction [PMID:31292465]
- Oleic acid destabilizes LBP-8 (Tm decreased ~4°C); OEA has no effect on Tm [PMID:31292465]

## Subcellular Localization
- Expressed exclusively in the intestine [PMID:25554789]
- Predominantly localized to intestinal lysosomes [PMID:25554789 "Both FLAG- and mCherry-tagged LBP-8 proteins were predominantly localized to intestinal lysosomes"]
- Partial nuclear localization enhanced in lipl-4 Tg animals [PMID:25554789]
- NLS deletion abolishes nuclear localization and lifespan extension [PMID:25554789]

## Longevity Function
- LBP-8 overexpression extends lifespan by ~30% [PMID:25554789]
- Nuclear localization required for longevity effect (no NLS version shows little extension) [PMID:25554789]
- LBP-8 depletion or deletion (lbp-8(rax1)) suppresses lipl-4 Tg longevity [PMID:25554789]
- Longevity requires NHR-49 and NHR-80 [PMID:25554789]

## Regulation
- lbp-8 is exclusively upregulated (of 9 FABP homologs) in lipl-4 Tg animals [PMID:25554789]
- Not upregulated in lipl-4 Tg no SP strain [PMID:25554789]
- OEA and its analogue induce lbp-8 transcription in wild-type via NHR-49/NHR-80 [PMID:25554789]
- Positive feedback loop: LIPL-4 → OEA → NHR-49/NHR-80 → lbp-8 transcription

## Early Characterization
- Initially characterized as part of a novel class of intracellular FABPs in nematodes [PMID:10693745 "A novel fatty acid binding protein, As-p18, is secreted... at least eight potential homologues of As-p18 have been identified in the Caenorhabditis elegans genome"]
