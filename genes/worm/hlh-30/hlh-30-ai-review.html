Gene Description

HLH-30 is the C. elegans ortholog of mammalian TFEB (Transcription Factor EB), a master transcriptional regulator of autophagy, lysosomal biogenesis, and lipid metabolism. As a basic helix-loop-helix (bHLH) transcription factor of the MiT/TFE family, HLH-30 functions by binding E-box motifs (CACGTG) in promoters of target genes. Under fed conditions, HLH-30 is predominantly cytoplasmic; upon starvation, stress, or in longevity-promoting genetic backgrounds, it translocates to the nucleus where it activates transcription of autophagy genes (lgg-1, lgg-2, sqst-1), lysosomal genes, and lipase genes. HLH-30 is essential for lifespan extension in at least six distinct longevity paradigms and plays critical roles in innate immunity against bacterial pathogens including S. aureus. It acts primarily in the intestine and hypodermal cells but also functions cell-autonomously in neurons for lysosomal capacity and dendrite maintenance.

- Microbiota interventions: Heat-inactivated Lactobacillus fermentum BGHV110 extends lifespan via HLH-30-dependent autophagy, suggesting probiotic components can engage HLH-30-mediated healthspan pathways (Aging, 2021; https://doi.org/10.18632/aging.202885; Mar 2021) (lin2024hlh30tfebmodulatesautophagy pages 4-9).
- Neurodegenerative disease models: HLH-30 activation improves proteostasis in AŒ≤ worms, nominating TFEB/HLH-30 as a target for amyloid clearance strategies (Lin 2024) (lin2024hlh30tfebmodulatesautophagy pages 4-9).

Quantitative statistics and key data points
- Lifespan: HLH-30 overexpression extends mean lifespan by ~15‚Äì20% in worms; hlh-30 is required for lifespan extension in six distinct longevity paradigms (Lapierre 2013) (lapierre2013thetfeborthologue pages 5-7). xpo-1 knockdown or SINE treatment extends lifespan in a manner dependent on hlh-30 (Silvestrini 2018) (silvestrini2018nuclearexportinhibition pages 1-3, silvestrini2018nuclearexportinhibition pages 9-15).
- Autophagy readouts: hlh-30 loss reduces GFP::LGG-1 puncta and increases SQST-1 foci; HLH-30 overexpression increases puncta; sams-1 and set-2 mutants increase autophagic flux measured by GFP::LGG-1::mCherry dual reporter; SAM rescues (Lapierre 2013; Lim 2023) (lapierre2013thetfeborthologue pages 5-7, lim2023sams1coordinateshlh30tfeb pages 2-4, lim2023sams1coordinateshlh30tfeb pages 4-6).
- Neuronal maintenance: HLH-30 loss delays synaptic vesicle protein turnover and accelerates dendrite degeneration with age, supporting a basal neuronal role in lysosomal capacity (Zhong & Richardson 2024 preprint) (zhong2024tfebhlh30mediatedexpansionof pages 1-4).
- AŒ≤ proteostasis: HLH-30 activation elevates lysosomal genes (v-ATPase, cathepsins), increases autophagic flux, reduces ROS, and decreases paralysis rates in AŒ≤ worms (Lin 2024) (lin2024hlh30tfebmodulatesautophagy pages 4-9).

Expert perspectives and analysis
- HLH-30 is a central, conserved node that converts nutrient, stress, and organelle-status inputs into a coordinated autophagy‚Äìlysosome transcriptional program, with demonstrable organismal benefits on stress resistance and longevity; multiple upstream levers (TOR, exportin-1, chaperone phosphorylation, epigenetic SAM/H3K4) provide orthogonal intervention points (Lapierre 2013; Silvestrini 2018; Lim 2023; Yang 2023) (lapierre2013thetfeborthologue pages 14-17, silvestrini2018nuclearexportinhibition pages 1-3, lim2023sams1coordinateshlh30tfeb pages 4-6, yang2023regulationoftfeb pages 13-14). The 2023‚Äì2024 advances particularly underscore epigenetic coordination with PHA-4/FOXA and neuronal homeostatic roles that may not rely on dramatic nuclear translocation, implying tissue-specific regimes of HLH-30 control and dosage-sensitive basal activity (Lim 2023; Zhong & Richardson 2024) (lim2023sams1coordinateshlh30tfeb pages 4-6, zhong2024tfebhlh30mediatedexpansionof pages 1-4).

Notes on gene symbol ambiguity
- The symbol hlh-30 is sometimes used generically for bHLH factors across species; here all functional statements and evidence refer specifically to C. elegans hlh-30 (W02C12.3), consistent with UniProt H2KZZ2 and the MiT/TFE bHLH family identity (Lapierre 2013) (lapierre2013thetfeborthologue pages 1-2, lapierre2013thetfeborthologue pages 2-4).
