Gene Description

IRE-1 is a conserved ER transmembrane stress sensor with dual enzymatic activities: a serine/threonine protein kinase and an endoribonuclease. The core function is sensing unfolded proteins in the ER lumen and transducing this signal to the cytosol through trans-autophosphorylation of its kinase domain, which activates its RNase domain to catalyze unconventional cytoplasmic splicing of xbp-1 mRNA, generating the active XBP-1s transcription factor that induces UPR target genes. IRE-1 also executes Regulated IRE1-Dependent Decay (RIDD) of select mRNAs including daf-7. The protein localizes to the ER membrane as a type I single-pass transmembrane protein with a luminal stress-sensing domain and cytosolic kinase-RNase effector domains. IRE-1 acts in one of three parallel UPR-ER branches (with PEK-1 and ATF-6), showing synthetic lethality with loss of a second branch. Neuronal IRE-1 also mediates cell-nonautonomous signaling including germline apoptosis regulation and anticipatory stress responses.

Key concepts and definitions
- Unfolded Protein Response of the ER (UPR-ER): In metazoa, the UPR-ER is mediated by three branches‚ÄîIRE-1 (ire-1), PERK/PEK-1 (pek-1), and ATF-6 (atf-6). IRE-1 oligomerizes upon accumulation of misfolded proteins in the ER, activating a cytosolic RNase that splices xbp-1 mRNA to generate the active transcription factor XBP-1s, while PERK/PEK-1 phosphorylates eIF2Œ± to attenuate translation, and ATF-6 functions as a transcription factor following ER-to-Golgi trafficking and proteolysis (richardson2011investigatingtherole pages 110-118). IRE-1 also executes RIDD, degrading select mRNAs to reduce ER client load and tune signaling outputs (xu2024theunfoldedprotein pages 1-2, ying2023theriddactivity pages 1-4).
- ire-1 gene product: A type I single-pass ER transmembrane protein with a luminal stress-sensing domain and cytosolic serine/threonine kinase and endoribonuclease domains. In C. elegans and heterologous systems, the kinase activity is required for RNase function, and species-specific linkers modulate activity and temperature dependence (ying2023theriddactivity pages 7-9, ying2023theriddactivity pages 1-4).

- Canonical UPR-ER wiring in C. elegans: In somatic tissues, activation of the UPR-ER reporter hsp-4 under replication stress caused by depletion of DNA primase subunits (pri-1/pri-2) depends completely on ire-1 and xbp-1, whereas embryos exhibit partial rerouting with atf-6-dependent hsp-4 induction. Loss of ire-1 or pek-1 sensitizes animals to hydroxyurea-induced replication stress, indicating functional contributions of both branches to genome integrity under stress (G3, 2024; https://doi.org/10.1093/g3journal/jkae017) (xu2024theunfoldedprotein pages 4-5, xu2024theunfoldedprotein pages 1-2).
- Redundancy and essentiality: Single-branch mutants (ire-1, pek-1, atf-6) are viable but ER-stress sensitive; combining loss of two branches (e.g., ire-1/xbp-1 with atf-6 or pek-1) causes early larval arrest. Conclusions
C. elegans ire-1 (Q09499) encodes a conserved ER stress sensor with dual catalytic activities that orchestrate both the canonical xbp-1 splicing pathway and RIDD. Recent work in 2023‚Äì2024 extends its roles beyond proteostasis into anticipatory neuroendocrine signaling (RIDD of daf-7) and cross-compartment stress coordination (UPR-ER responses to replication stress; allocation between UPR and SKN-1-mediated antioxidant defenses). Tissue- and context-specific wiring is evident: soma relies on ire-1/xbp-1 for UPR induction under replication stress, while embryos utilize atf-6; infection and ERAD perturbations reveal an IRE-1 bottleneck that coordinates stress-response priorities. Together, these studies refine our understanding of IRE-1 as both a proteostasis maintainer and a systems-level regulator of developmental and immune physiology in nematodes (xu2024theunfoldedprotein pages 4-5, gabaldon2024skn1activationduring pages 6-8, richardson2011investigatingtherole pages 110-118, ying2023theriddactivity pages 15-17).
