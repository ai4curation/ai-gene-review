CC   -!- FUNCTION: Component of the trimeric PUCH (precursor of 21U RNA 5'-end
CC       cleavage holoenzyme) complex, that acts as an endoribonuclease
CC       processing the 5'-end of precursor Piwi-interacting RNAs (piRNAs)
CC       (PubMed:37758951). The PUCH complex consists of tofu-1, tofu-2 and
CC       either slfl-3 or slfl-4, with tofu-2 exhibiting endoribonuclease
CC       activity (PubMed:37758951). PUCH-mediated processing strictly requires
CC       a 7-methyl-G cap (m7 G-cap) and an uracil at position three (U3)
CC       (PubMed:37758951). PUCH also exhibits a strict bias for piRNA
CC       precursors with an A or G at position 1 (PubMed:37758951). Mature piRNA
CC       production is enhanced by the interaction of PUCH with the PETISCO
CC       complex, which is stabilizing piRNA precursors and allows their
CC       processing by PUCH (PubMed:37758951). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:37758951}.

CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:37758951};
CC   -!- ACTIVITY REGULATION: Inhibited by ethylenediaminetetraacetic acid
CC       (EDTA). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:37758951}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Component of the trimeric PUCH (precursor of 21U RNA 5'-end
CC       cleavage holoenzyme) complex; consisting of tofu-1, tofu-2 and either
CC       slfl-3 or slfl-4 (PubMed:37758951). Within the complex, interacts (via
CC       N-terminus) with tofu-1 (via N-terminus); the interaction stabilizes
CC       tofu-2 and may form a functional nuclease (PubMed:37758951). Within the
CC       complex, interacts (via N-terminus) with slfl-3 (via N-terminus); the
CC       presence of tofu-1 is required for this interaction (PubMed:37758951).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:37758951}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cytoplasm {ECO:0000269|PubMed:31216475}.
CC       Mitochondrion {ECO:0000269|PubMed:37758951}. Note=The localization to
CC       the mitochondrion is mediated by slfl-3. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:37758951}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Expressed in the germline.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:31216475}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: In mammalian Schlafen proteins, two SLFN-folds come together to
CC       form the nuclease domain. In tofu-1 and tofu-2, only one SLFN-like fold
CC       can be identified, suggesting that the two proteins may interact via
CC       their SLFN-like folds to form a functional nuclease.
CC       {ECO:0000303|PubMed:37758951}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the Schlafen family. {ECO:0000305}.
