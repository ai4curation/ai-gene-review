CC   -!- FUNCTION: Carries out a dual function: signal transduction and
CC       activation of transcription (By similarity). Required, in concert with
CC       transcription factor elt-3, for up-regulation of the vacuolar H(+)-
CC       ATPase and acceleration of lysosome maturation at molt
CC       (PubMed:31735670). As part of the innate immune response to molting and
CC       injury of the adult epidermis, positively regulates the expression of
CC       epidermal antimicrobial peptides, such as nlp-29 (PubMed:21575913,
CC       PubMed:22470487, PubMed:33259791). Through positively modulating the
CC       expression of epidermal antimicrobial peptides, such as nlp-29, plays a
CC       role in resistance to fungal infection and in the response to physical
CC       wounding and phorbol ester PMA treatment (PubMed:21575913,
CC       PubMed:22470487). Functions cell autonomously in the epidermis, in
CC       concert with sodium-dependent transporter snf-12, probably acting at
CC       vesicular membranes, downstream of a p38 MAPK/pmk-1 pathway.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P51692, ECO:0000269|PubMed:21575913,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:22470487, ECO:0000269|PubMed:33259791}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: May interact with sodium-dependent transporter snf-12; the
CC       interaction is probably direct. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:21575913}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cytoplasm {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P51692}. Nucleus
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:21575913}. Vesicle {ECO:0000269|PubMed:21575913}.
CC       Note=Translocated into the nucleus in response to phosphorylation (By
CC       similarity). Localized to vesicles, which may be a type of endosome
CC       (PubMed:21575913). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P51692,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:21575913}.
CC   -!- DEVELOPMENTAL STAGE: Expressed in the epidermis throughout development
CC       (PubMed:21575913). Also expressed in the seam cells, which are
CC       specialized epithelial cells that secrete the cuticle, as well as in
CC       the intestine, the phasmid socket cells, and the excretory duct cell,
CC       as well as cells in the vulva and the dorsal rectal cells
CC       (PubMed:21575913). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:21575913}.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: RNAi-mediated knockdown almost completely
CC       abolishes induction of nlp-29 expression upon infection with Drechmeria
CC       coniospora, or upon injury, or in response to phorbol ester PMA.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:21575913}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the transcription factor STAT family.
CC       {ECO:0000255}.
