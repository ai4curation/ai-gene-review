CC   -!- FUNCTION: Orphan nuclear receptor (PubMed:25981666). Regulates
CC       expression of genes involved in fat metabolism and in maintaining
CC       homeostasis of fatty acid saturation, such as lipid desaturase fat-7,
CC       and acyl-CoA synthetase acs-2 (PubMed:15719061, PubMed:22511885,
CC       PubMed:25981666). May form part of a negative feedback loop with fat-7
CC       to limit mitochondrial beta-oxidation, in which it stimulates
CC       expression of fat-7 and acs-2, and in turn fat-7 indirectly inhibits
CC       acs-2 and other genes also involved in beta-oxidation
CC       (PubMed:15719061). Acts in multiple somatic tissues, probably cell non-
CC       autonomously, to regulate response to a low oxygen environment
CC       (hypoxia) (PubMed:35285794). During hypoxia, activates expression of
CC       acs-2, various autophagy-related genes, and the formation of
CC       autophagosomes, independent of hypoxia-inducible factor hif-1
CC       (PubMed:35285794). Furthermore, activates detoxification gene flavin
CC       mono-oxygenase fmo-2, acting in parallel with hif-1, during hypoxia
CC       (PubMed:35285794). As part of a lysosome-to-nucleus retrograde lipid
CC       signaling pathway, acting in concert with nuclear hormone receptor nhr-
CC       80, activates the transcription of genes promoting longevity and
CC       mitochondrial beta-oxidation (PubMed:25554789, PubMed:26671266). In
CC       concert with nuclear hormone receptor nhr-66, involved in regulating
CC       target genes with roles in sphingolipid breakdown and lipid remodeling
CC       (PubMed:22511885). Also involved in regulating fatty acid desaturase
CC       genes, acting in concert with nuclear hormone receptors nhr-13 and nhr-
CC       80 (PubMed:22511885). Plays a role in modulating mitochondrial
CC       morphology and function (PubMed:22511885). Plays a role in
CC       transgenerational lipid accumulation in response to a high-fat diet
CC       (PubMed:35140229). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15719061,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:25981666, ECO:0000269|PubMed:35285794}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homodimer (PubMed:22511885). Interacts with nuclear hormone
CC       receptors nhr-80 and nhr-66; the interactions are direct
CC       (PubMed:22511885). May interact with nuclear hormone receptors nhr-13,
CC       nhr-22, nhr-79, nhr-105, and nhr-256 (PubMed:22511885).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:22511885}.

CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: Displays mild developmental defect, exhibiting
CC       slower larval growth (PubMed:35285794). In response to hypoxia, only
CC       25% of embryos develop to the L4 larval stage, by comparison with 86%
CC       of wild-type (PubMed:35285794). Exhibits intolerance to hypoxia in hif-
CC       1 mutant background, with less than 2% animal surviving to L4 larval
CC       stage (PubMed:35285794). Reduces the survival rate of hypoxia-exposed
CC       embryos to 25% from 86% in wild-type animals (PubMed:35285794). Reduces
CC       the survival rate of hypoxia-exposed L1 stage larvae to 19% from 95% in
CC       wild-type (PubMed:35285794). Leads to hypoxia-dependent up-regulation
CC       of nuclear hormone receptor nhr-67 (PubMed:35285794). Suppresses the
CC       up-regulation of hypoxia response genes, fmo-2 and acs-2, during
CC       hypoxia (PubMed:35285794). Unaffected by hydrogen sulfide
CC       (PubMed:35285794). Suppresses the hypoxia-dependent increase in the
CC       foci of autophagosome assembly factor lgg-1 in the seam cells
CC       (PubMed:35285794). RNAi-mediated knockdown eliminates the effect of C11
CC       or C12 chain fatty acids in activating fat-7 expression and blocks
CC       overexpression of fat-7 in acdh-11 mutants (PubMed:25981666). Increased
CC       ratio of stearic acid to oleic acid (C18:0/C18:1n9) (PubMed:15719061).
CC       Drastically reduces life span (PubMed:15719061, PubMed:25554789).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15719061, ECO:0000269|PubMed:25981666,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:35285794}.
