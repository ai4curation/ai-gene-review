Gene Description

PEF-1 (Phosphatase with EF-hands 1) is the sole C. elegans member of the RdgC/PPEF family of serine/threonine protein phosphatases, distinguished by C-terminal EF-hand calcium-binding domains. Expression is restricted to sensory neurons including ciliated chemosensory neurons AWB, AWC, ASE, AFD, BAG, and others. The protein localizes to sensory cilia via N-terminal myristoylation (Gly-2) and palmitoylation (Cys-3), where it is enriched in the ciliary axoneme and excluded from the transition zone. PEF-1 requires transition zone proteins (MKS-5, CEP-290) for proper ciliary compartmentalization. Functional studies demonstrate that pef-1 mutants show defects in thermotaxis, salt chemotaxis, CO2 avoidance, and UV light avoidance, while osmosensation remains normal. TEM analysis reveals microtubule abnormalities in pef-1 mutant cilia, suggesting a role in cilium maintenance. The protein binds calcium via its EF-hands and binds manganese as a cofactor for phosphatase activity. This is the first PPEF family member demonstrated to function in cilia; in contrast, the Drosophila ortholog RdgC functions in non-ciliary rhabdomeric photoreceptors.
