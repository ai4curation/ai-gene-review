Research report: Functional annotation of C. elegans pef-1 (UniProt G5EBX9)

Plan and verification summary
- We verified the target is Caenorhabditis elegans pef-1 (also called CePPEF), UniProt G5EBX9, encoding the sole nematode PPEF/rdgC-family serine/threonine phosphatase with EF-hands; domain architecture and N-terminal acylation motif match literature reports. (ramulu2001cellularandsubcellular pages 1-2, sherman1997identificationandcharacterization pages 1-2)

1) Key concepts and definitions
- Gene/protein identity and family: pef-1 encodes a Protein Phosphatase with EF-hands (PPEF) orthologous to Drosophila retinal degeneration C (rdgC), within the PPP family of serine/threonine phosphatases distinguished by a C‑terminal EF‑hand pair. (Sherman et al., PNAS, Oct 1997, https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.94.21.11639) (sherman1997identificationandcharacterization pages 1-2)
- Catalytic class: PPEFs are metallo-dependent PPP phosphatases that dephosphorylate serine/threonine residues; in flies, RDGC dephosphorylates rhodopsin in vivo, establishing the enzymatic class and a canonical substrate family for PPEFs. (Lee and Montell, Neuron, Dec 20, 2001, https://doi.org/10.1016/S0896-6273(01)00538-4) (lee2001regulationofthe pages 1-2)
- Regulatory modules: PPEFs possess a C‑terminal EF‑hand Ca2+-binding region and an N‑terminal extended region that includes an IQ motif (calmodulin-binding) in orthologs; these modules regulate catalytic access in PPEFs. For CePPEF, calcium binding to the EF-hands is experimentally demonstrated. (Ramulu and Nathans, JBC, Jul 2001, https://doi.org/10.1074/jbc.M011712200) (ramulu2001cellularandsubcellular pages 6-7, ramulu2001cellularandsubcellular pages 5-6)
- N‑terminal lipidation: CePPEF carries an MGCGPS motif that supports N‑myristoylation (Gly2) and palmitoylation (Cys3), required for membrane and ciliary localization. (Ramulu and Nathans, JBC, Jul 2001, https://doi.org/10.1074/jbc.M011712200) (ramulu2001cellularandsubcellular pages 4-5, ramulu2001cellularandsubcellular pages 5-6)

2) Recent developments (2023–2024 priority) and latest research
- Discovery of ciliary role for pef-1: A 2024 Scientific Reports study demonstrates PEF-1 is a bona fide ciliary protein whose axonemal compartmentalization depends on the transition zone (TZ) “ciliary gate” (e.g., MKS-5, CEP-290). Loss of PEF-1 causes structural defects in multiple cilium types (including degenerative microtubule phenotypes) and behavioral abnormalities in cilium-dependent modalities (chemical, temperature, light, and CO2 responses). N‑terminal myristoylation/palmitoylation is required for ciliary enrichment and function; acylation-site mutants phenocopy null alleles. (Barbelanne et al., Sci Rep, Nov 2024, https://doi.org/10.1038/s41598-024-79057-z) (barbelanne2024c.elegansppeftype pages 1-3, barbelanne2024c.elegansppeftype pages 11-12, barbelanne2024c.elegansppeftype pages 5-6, barbelanne2024c.elegansppeftype pages 9-11, barbelanne2024c.elegansppeftype pages 9-9, barbelanne2024c.elegansppeftype pages 3-4, barbelanne2024c.elegansppeftype pages 4-5, barbelanne2024c.elegansppeftype pages 6-9)
- Expression pattern refined: The 2024 study reports endogenous PEF-1 expression in several ciliated sensory neurons (e.g., AFD, AWC, ASE; detectable in AWB and phasmids), with enrichment distal to the TZ and presence in the periciliary membrane compartment; ciliary enrichment requires intact TZ scaffolds. (Barbelanne et al., Sci Rep, Nov 2024, https://doi.org/10.1038/s41598-024-79057-z) (barbelanne2024c.elegansppeftype pages 3-4, barbelanne2024c.elegansppeftype pages 4-5)

3) Primary function, biochemical activity, and regulation
- Enzymatic activity: By homology to the PPEF family and RDGC, PEF-1 is a PPP-type serine/threonine phosphatase; definitive substrates in C. elegans remain unidentified in current literature. (Sherman et al., PNAS, Oct 1997, https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.94.21.11639; Barbelanne et al., Sci Rep, Nov 2024, https://doi.org/10.1038/s41598-024-79057-z) (sherman1997identificationandcharacterization pages 1-2, barbelanne2024c.elegansppeftype pages 1-3)
- Calcium binding: The CePPEF C-terminus binds 1–2 mol Ca2+ per mol protein via two EF-hands; mutating predicted liganding residues reduces binding, confirming bona fide EF-hand Ca2+ binding sites. (Ramulu and Nathans, JBC, Jul 2001, https://doi.org/10.1074/jbc.M011712200) (ramulu2001cellularandsubcellular pages 5-6, ramulu2001cellularandsubcellular pages 6-7)
- Calmodulin/IQ motif regulation (from orthologs): RDGC (Drosophila) is a calmodulin-regulated phosphatase; disrupting an IQ motif abrogates RDGC–calmodulin binding and prevents rhodopsin dephosphorylation in vivo, supporting a model in which calmodulin and EF-hands modulate catalytic access. This mechanism is likely conserved within PPEFs, though direct calmodulin regulation has not been demonstrated in C. elegans PEF-1 in the cited texts. (Lee and Montell, Neuron, Dec 20, 2001, https://doi.org/10.1016/S0896-6273(01)00538-4) (lee2001regulationofthe pages 1-2)
- Lipidation-dependent membrane/ciliary targeting: CePPEF is both myristoylated (Gly2) and palmitoylated (Cys3); G2A eliminates both modifications and membrane association; C3S eliminates palmitoylation. A 14-aa N-terminus is necessary and sufficient to confer membrane and ciliary localization to reporters in vivo and in cultured cells. (Ramulu and Nathans, JBC, Jul 2001, https://doi.org/10.1074/jbc.M011712200) (ramulu2001cellularandsubcellular pages 4-5, ramulu2001cellularandsubcellular pages 5-6, ramulu2001cellularandsubcellular pages 2-3)

4) Cellular and subcellular localization
- Neuronal and ciliary enrichment: CePPEF/PEF-1 is expressed in a subset of primary sensory neurons (e.g., AWB, AWC, BAG; variably ASE) and is highly enriched in their sensory cilia; also localized to axons and dendrites. Immunostaining and transgene reporters confirm ciliary enrichment in vivo. (Ramulu and Nathans, JBC, Jul 2001, https://doi.org/10.1074/jbc.M011712200) (ramulu2001cellularandsubcellular pages 3-4, ramulu2001cellularandsubcellular pages 1-2)
- Ciliary compartment: In vivo imaging places PEF-1 in the axoneme distal to the transition zone, with additional signal at the periciliary membrane compartment; a “zone of exclusion” at the TZ is observed. Proper localization depends on TZ scaffold integrity. (Barbelanne et al., Sci Rep, Nov 2024, https://doi.org/10.1038/s41598-024-79057-z) (barbelanne2024c.elegansppeftype pages 3-4, barbelanne2024c.elegansppeftype pages 4-5)

5) Physiological roles and phenotypes in C. elegans
- Cilium structure and maintenance: pef-1 null and acylation-disrupting missense alleles display structural abnormalities across multiple cilia types. TEM indicates compromised distal microtubule organization (e.g., incomplete B-tubules, loss of singlet microtubules; electron-dense material replacing distal axoneme), and altered morphologies (e.g., enlarged AWC/AWB wing endings; shortened ASE; altered AFD endings). Dye-filling defects worsen with development, consistent with progressive/degenerative ciliary changes. (Barbelanne et al., Sci Rep, Nov 2024, https://doi.org/10.1038/s41598-024-79057-z) (barbelanne2024c.elegansppeftype pages 5-6, barbelanne2024c.elegansppeftype pages 9-11, barbelanne2024c.elegansppeftype pages 6-9)
- Sensory behaviors: pef-1 mutants show deficits in cilium-dependent behaviors—reduced chemotaxis to salt (NaCl), defective thermotaxis (resembling tax-4), attenuated photoavoidance, and prolonged high-amplitude turning during CO2 avoidance; some modalities (e.g., isoamyl alcohol chemotaxis, osmotic avoidance) are relatively preserved. (Barbelanne et al., Sci Rep, Nov 2024, https://doi.org/10.1038/s41598-024-79057-z) (barbelanne2024c.elegansppeftype pages 9-9)

6) Pathways and complexes
- Ciliary pathway involvement: PEF-1 localizes to the axoneme and requires a functional transition zone for proper ciliary compartmentalization, implicating PEF-1 in cilium-intrinsic regulation downstream of the TZ “gate.” (Barbelanne et al., Sci Rep, Nov 2024, https://doi.org/10.1038/s41598-024-79057-z) (barbelanne2024c.elegansppeftype pages 3-4, barbelanne2024c.elegansppeftype pages 4-5)
- Signaling context: Although direct substrates are not identified in C. elegans, orthology and function suggest PPEFs may target membrane-associated sensory transduction proteins (e.g., GPCR pathways or ciliary channels), analogous to RDGC action in photoreceptors; the 2024 study discusses GPCRs/TRP/cGMP pathway components as plausible targets by analogy. These are hypotheses awaiting biochemical confirmation in worm. (Barbelanne et al., Sci Rep, Nov 2024, https://doi.org/10.1038/s41598-024-79057-z; Lee and Montell, Neuron, Dec 20, 2001, https://doi.org/10.1016/S0896-6273(01)00538-4) (barbelanne2024c.elegansppeftype pages 11-12, lee2001regulationofthe pages 1-2)

7) Expert opinions, authoritative context, and comparative insights
- Founding identification and sensory neuron specialization: The PPEF family, including a single C. elegans homolog, was identified as a conserved branch of PPP phosphatases with EF-hands and specialized expression in sensory neurons, supporting a conserved role in sensory signaling. (Sherman et al., PNAS, Oct 1997, https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.94.21.11639) (sherman1997identificationandcharacterization pages 1-2)
- Direct evidence for Ca2+ and acylation control in CePPEF: Early mechanistic work established that CePPEF binds Ca2+ via two EF-hands and requires dual N-terminal acylation for membrane/ciliary localization, underpinning a regulatory framework likely shared with other PPEFs. (Ramulu and Nathans, JBC, Jul 2001, https://doi.org/10.1074/jbc.M011712200) (ramulu2001cellularandsubcellular pages 5-6, ramulu2001cellularandsubcellular pages 4-5, ramulu2001cellularandsubcellular pages 2-3)
- Calmodulin regulation paradigm from RDGC: Calmodulin-dependent control of RDGC via an IQ motif regulates substrate access and phototransduction termination, highlighting a general PPEF regulatory logic relevant to PEF-1. (Lee and Montell, Neuron, Dec 20, 2001, https://doi.org/10.1016/S0896-6273(01)00538-4) (lee2001regulationofthe pages 1-2)

8) Relevant statistics and data points
- Ca2+-binding stoichiometry and affinity: CePPEF C-terminus binds between one and two Ca2+ per protein; equilibrium analyses fit two-site models with macroscopic dissociation constants in the micromolar range (reported ~36 µM and ~1.0 µM for the two sites), typical for EF-hands; mutating EF-hand ligands reduces maximal binding to ~0.5–0.85 mol Ca2+/mol, consistent with two bona fide EF-hands. (Ramulu and Nathans, JBC, Jul 2001, https://doi.org/10.1074/jbc.M011712200) (ramulu2001cellularandsubcellular pages 6-7, ramulu2001cellularandsubcellular pages 5-6)
- Lipidation dependence: In cell-based metabolic labeling, wild-type CePPEF incorporates [3H]myristate and [3H]palmitate; G2A prevents both, C3S prevents palmitoylation but retains myristoylation; these mutations abolish plasma membrane association and in vivo ciliary enrichment. (Ramulu and Nathans, JBC, Jul 2001, https://doi.org/10.1074/jbc.M011712200) (ramulu2001cellularandsubcellular pages 5-6, ramulu2001cellularandsubcellular pages 4-5)
- Ciliary ultrastructure and behavior: pef-1 mutants exhibit incomplete axonemal doublets and loss of distal singlets on TEM, and show altered salt chemotaxis, thermotaxis failure, attenuated photoavoidance, and prolonged CO2-evoked turning; dye-filling defects are progressive across development. (Barbelanne et al., Sci Rep, Nov 2024, https://doi.org/10.1038/s41598-024-79057-z) (barbelanne2024c.elegansppeftype pages 5-6, barbelanne2024c.elegansppeftype pages 9-11, barbelanne2024c.elegansppeftype pages 6-9, barbelanne2024c.elegansppeftype pages 9-9)

9) Current applications and real-world implementations
- Ciliopathy candidate inference: The 2024 study argues that because PEF-1 is a ciliary protein required for ciliary structure and sensory behaviors, mammalian PPEF1/2 may have unappreciated ciliary roles and represent candidate genes for ciliopathies; this guides translational gene discovery strategies in human genetics. (Barbelanne et al., Sci Rep, Nov 2024, https://doi.org/10.1038/s41598-024-79057-z) (barbelanne2024c.elegansppeftype pages 1-3)
- Ciliary targeting motif utility: The short myristoylation/palmitoylation motif from PEF-1 is sufficient to target reporters to the ciliary axoneme and exclude them from the TZ, suggesting a practical tool for engineering ciliary localization in vivo. (Barbelanne et al., Sci Rep, Nov 2024, https://doi.org/10.1038/s41598-024-79057-z; Ramulu and Nathans, JBC, Jul 2001, https://doi.org/10.1074/jbc.M011712200) (barbelanne2024c.elegansppeftype pages 4-5, ramulu2001cellularandsubcellular pages 4-5)

10) Open questions and limitations
- Substrate specificity in C. elegans remains unknown; the 2024 study proposes candidates by analogy (e.g., GPCRs, TRP/cGMP pathway components), but biochemical verification is outstanding. (Barbelanne et al., Sci Rep, Nov 2024, https://doi.org/10.1038/s41598-024-79057-z) (barbelanne2024c.elegansppeftype pages 11-12)
- Regulatory partners: While EF-hands and acylation are established for PEF-1, direct evidence for calmodulin binding and auto-inhibitory regulation in C. elegans awaits demonstration; RDGC provides a strong mechanistic precedent. (Lee and Montell, Neuron, Dec 20, 2001, https://doi.org/10.1016/S0896-6273(01)00538-4) (lee2001regulationofthe pages 1-2)

Conclusion
C. elegans pef-1 encodes a cilia-enriched, acylation-dependent, Ca2+-binding PPP-family serine/threonine phosphatase of the PPEF/rdgC lineage. Recent work (2024) establishes a direct role in ciliary structure and multiple cilium-dependent behaviors, with proper ciliary compartmentalization requiring transition zone integrity. While worm substrates are not yet defined, EF-hand Ca2+ binding and likely calmodulin-based regulation (by orthology) frame a model wherein PEF-1 modulates membrane-associated sensory signaling within cilia.
