Gene Description

DAF-16 is a forkhead box transcription factor (FOXO) that is the primary downstream effector of the insulin/IGF-1-like signaling (IIS) pathway in C. elegans. It functions as a key regulator of longevity, stress resistance, dauer larval development, innate immunity, and metabolism. Under conditions of reduced IIS (e.g., daf-2 mutants), DAF-16 translocates from the cytoplasm to the nucleus where it activates transcription of target genes involved in stress response, metabolism, and longevity. DAF-16 is negatively regulated by AKT-1, AKT-2, and SGK-1 kinases, which phosphorylate DAF-16 and promote cytoplasmic retention via 14-3-3 protein binding (FTT-2). Multiple DAF-16 isoforms exist (a, b, c, d, e, f, h) with distinct expression patterns and potentially different functions in lifespan modulation. The intestine is the primary tissue for DAF-16-dependent longevity signaling.

Core Functions

DAF-16 is a forkhead transcription factor that binds DNA through its forkhead domain and regulates transcription of target genes. This is the core molecular function of DAF-16.

Molecular Function:
DNA-binding transcription factor activity, RNA polymerase II-specific
Directly Involved In:
regulation of transcription by RNA polymerase II
Cellular Locations:
nucleus

Longevity regulation is the best-characterized function of DAF-16. It is absolutely required for lifespan extension in daf-2/insulin receptor mutants and many other longevity paradigms. DAF-16 activates genes involved in stress resistance, proteostasis, and metabolism that collectively extend lifespan.

Molecular Function:
DNA-binding transcription factor activity, RNA polymerase II-specific

DAF-16 is the major downstream effector of the insulin/IGF-1-like signaling pathway in C. elegans. It is negatively regulated by AKT kinases downstream of DAF-2 receptor signaling. This is fundamental to DAF-16 biology.

Molecular Function:
DNA-binding transcription factor activity, RNA polymerase II-specific
Directly Involved In:

DAF-16 is essential for dauer larval development, the alternative larval diapause stage in C. elegans. daf-16 mutants are dauer-defective. This represents the developmental function of DAF-16 in the IIS pathway.

DAF-16 is a key mediator of oxidative stress responses. It activates expression of antioxidant genes like sod-3 and is required for resistance to oxidative damage. This is a core stress response function.
