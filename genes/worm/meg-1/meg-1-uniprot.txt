CC   -!- FUNCTION: P granule component, which acts redundantly with P granule
CC       component meg-2 to promote P granule segregation during embryogenesis,
CC       and germ cell proliferation and differentiation in larval stages
CC       (PubMed:18202375, PubMed:21305687, PubMed:25535836). In its
CC       phosphorylated form, and together with meg-2, promotes the disassembly
CC       of zygotic P granules in the anterior cytoplasm of pre-gastrulation
CC       embryos (PubMed:25535836). In its dephosphorylated form, and together
CC       with meg-2, promotes the assembly and accumulation of zygotic P
CC       granules in the posterior cytoplasm of pre-gastrulation embryos
CC       (PubMed:25535836). May function with the nanos family members nos-2 and
CC       nos-3 to promote germ cell proliferation during larval development
CC       (PubMed:21305687). Required for fertility (PubMed:18202375,
CC       PubMed:21305687). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:18202375,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:21305687, ECO:0000269|PubMed:25535836}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Interacts with pptr-1, pptr-2 and pgl-1.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:25535836}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cytoplasmic granule
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:18202375}. Note=Localizes to P granules in
CC       embryogenesis (PubMed:18202375). Localization to P granules may depend
CC       on mes-1 (PubMed:18202375). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:18202375}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Not expressed in the adult germline or in any
CC       somatic tissues. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:18202375}.
CC   -!- DEVELOPMENTAL STAGE: Expressed in the P lineage during embryogenesis
CC       (PubMed:18202375). First expressed at the 4- to 8-cell stage, and
CC       expression remains throughout the remaining P cell divisions in P2, P3
CC       and P4 cells, and subsequently in primordial germ cells Z2 and Z3
CC       (PubMed:18202375). Expression begins to diminish at the 100-cell stage
CC       (PubMed:18202375). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:18202375}.
CC   -!- PTM: Phosphorylated by mbk-2, which promotes the disassembly of zygotic
CC       P granules in the anterior cytoplasm of pre-gastrulation embryos
CC       (PubMed:25535836). Dephosphorylated by a phosphatase complex containing
CC       the PP2A regulatory subunit pptr-1, which promotes the assembly and
CC       accumulation of zygotic P granules in the posterior cytoplasm of pre-
CC       gastrulation embryos (PubMed:25535836). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:25535836}.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: RNAi-mediated knockdown results in sterility, and
CC       in P granule segregation defects (PubMed:18202375). Simultaneous RNAi-
CC       mediated knockdown of meg-1 and meg-2 results in defects in P granule
CC       disassembly in the anterior cytoplasm of the P1 blastomere causing some
CC       P granules to be mis-segregated to somatic blastomeres
CC       (PubMed:25535836). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:18202375,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:25535836}.
