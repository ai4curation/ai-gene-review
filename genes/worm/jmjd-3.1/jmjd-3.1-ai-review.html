Gene Description

JMJD-3.1 is a JmjC domain-containing lysine-specific demethylase belonging to the UTX/KDM6 family that specifically demethylates H3K27me2/me3, marks associated with transcriptional repression. The enzyme requires Fe(II) as a cofactor for its dioxygenase activity. JMJD-3.1 functions in the nucleus where it removes repressive H3K27 methylation marks at specific loci to enable gene activation. Unlike the related UTX-1 which is the dominant global H3K27 demethylase during development, JMJD-3.1 acts more locus-specifically and contributes primarily to stress response pathways including the mitochondrial unfolded protein response (UPRmt) and heat shock response. It is required for the robust transdifferentiation of the Y rectal epithelial cell into the PDA motor neuron during larval development. Overexpression of jmjd-3.1 extends lifespan in a manner dependent on UPRmt components (ATFS-1, UBL-5, CLPP-1).
