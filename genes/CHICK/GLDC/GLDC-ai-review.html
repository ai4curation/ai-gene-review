Gene Description

GLDC encodes the P-protein of the mitochondrial glycine cleavage system (GCS), a pyridoxal 5'-phosphate (PLP)-dependent enzyme (EC 1.4.4.2) that catalyzes the decarboxylation of glycine and transfers the aminomethyl moiety to the lipoylated H-protein (GCSH). The GCS is a multienzyme complex consisting of P (GLDC), H (GCSH), T (AMT), and L (DLD) proteins that functions in the mitochondrial matrix. GLDC provides one-carbon units to folate metabolism via 5,10-methylene-THF, linking glycine catabolism to nucleotide biosynthesis and methylation reactions. The chicken protein was extensively characterized in the seminal work by Hiraga and Kikuchi (1980).

Core Functions

GLDC is the P-protein of the glycine cleavage system, catalyzing the PLP-dependent decarboxylation of glycine and transfer of the aminomethyl moiety to lipoylated H-protein (GCSH), releasing CO2. This provides one-carbon units to folate metabolism.

Key concepts and definitions

  • Molecular identity and localization: GLDC encodes the P-protein of the mitochondrial glycine cleavage system (GCS), a PLP-dependent enzyme (EC 1.4.4.2) localized to the mitochondrial matrix or inner-membrane-associated matrix face, functioning within a multienzyme complex comprising P (GLDC), H (GCSH; lipoyl carrier), T (AMT; aminomethyltransferase), and L (DLD; dihydrolipoamide dehydrogenase) proteins (https://doi.org/10.1038/ncomms7388; 2015; Nature Communications) (pai2015glycinedecarboxylasedeficiency pages 1-2), (https://doi.org/10.1002/jimd.12295; 2020; Journal of Inherited Metabolic Disease) (leung2020regulationofglycine pages 1-2), (https://doi.org/10.3389/fgene.2021.625120; 2021; Frontiers in Genetics) (leung2021glycinecleavagesystem pages 1-2).
  • Catalyzed reaction and stoichiometry: GLDC decarboxylates glycine to release CO2 and transfers the aminomethyl group onto the lipoylated H-protein as an aminomethyl-lipoate intermediate; the T-protein then transfers the one-carbon unit to tetrahydrofolate (THF) forming 5,10-methylene-THF while releasing NH3; the L-protein reoxidizes the dihydrolipoyl moiety using NAD+ to NADH, completing the catalytic cycle (https://doi.org/10.1038/ncomms7388; 2015) (pai2015glycinedecarboxylasedeficiency pages 1-2), (https://doi.org/10.1111/tpj.14773; 2020; The Plant Journal) (wittmiss2020stoichiometryoftwo pages 1-2), (https://doi.org/10.3389/fgene.2021.625120; 2021) (leung2021glycinecleavagesystem pages 1-2).
  • Pathway context: The GCS is a central source of mitochondrial one-carbon units that feed folate-mediated one-carbon metabolism (FOCM), supplying 5,10-methylene-THF and downstream formate for nucleotide biosynthesis and methylation reactions (https://doi.org/10.1038/ncomms7388; 2015) (pai2015glycinedecarboxylasedeficiency pages 1-2), (https://doi.org/10.3389/fgene.2021.625120; 2021) (leung2021glycinecleavagesystem pages 1-2).
  • Protein family and domains: GLDC belongs to the GcvP family of PLP-dependent enzymes and is organized into N- and C-terminal subdomains (GDC_P_N and GDC_P_C) that align with GCS mechanistic roles and the H-protein interaction surface (https://doi.org/10.1111/tpj.14773; 2020) (wittmiss2020stoichiometryoftwo pages 1-2).

Mechanism and structural features of the GCS

  • Lipoyl carrier dynamics: The lipoylated H-protein serves as a swinging arm that accepts the aminomethyl group from GLDC and delivers it to AMT; the reduced lipoyl moiety is then reoxidized by DLD using NAD+, yielding NADH (https://doi.org/10.1111/tpj.14773; 2020) (wittmiss2020stoichiometryoftwo pages 1-2), (https://doi.org/10.3389/fgene.2021.625120; 2021) (leung2021glycinecleavagesystem pages 1-2).
  • Lipoylation biogenesis and H‚ÄìP interface: Lipoate is assembled on the H-protein in mitochondria; LIAS generates lipoyl-H from octanoyl-H, and LIPT1 transfers the lipoyl group to E2 subunits of dehydrogenase complexes. Structural mapping places the H-protein lipoyl loop above the GLDC active-site tunnel, rationalizing disease mechanisms from H-protein variants that destabilize the lipoyl arm or its interaction with GLDC (https://doi.org/10.1093/hmg/ddac246; 2023; Human Molecular Genetics) (arribascarreira2023pathogenicvariantsingcshencoding pages 8-9), (arribascarreira2023pathogenicvariantsingcshencoding pages 2-3).
