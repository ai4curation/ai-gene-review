Gene Description

A2FPI7 (TVAG_233530/TVAG_559790) is a 129-amino-acid KilA-N domain-containing protein from Trichomonas vaginalis. The KilA-N domain (Pfam PF04383) was originally identified in bacteriophage proteins associated with DNA-binding and transcriptional regulatory functions, and is structurally related to the APSES DNA-binding domain found in fungal transcription factors. T. vaginalis harbors numerous laterally transferred genes of viral and bacterial origin in its unusually large genome (~160 Mb). Based on conserved domain architecture, A2FPI7 is most likely a DNA-binding protein with a possible role in transcriptional regulation. No direct experimental characterization of this protein has been reported; the protein existence level is PE4 (Predicted), and no GO annotations are present in GOA.

Core Functions

A2FPI7 is predicted to bind DNA based on the presence of a KilA-N domain (PF04383), which belongs to the KilA-N/APSES helix-turn-helix superfamily. This domain has been experimentally demonstrated to mediate sequence-specific DNA binding in fungal APSES transcription factors and in bacteriophage regulatory proteins. No direct experimental evidence exists for A2FPI7 specifically; this annotation is a domain-based inference.
