> The most likely primary molecular function is endonucleolytic 5′-end maturation of precursor tRNAs: cleavage of the phosphodiester bond separating the 5′ leader from the mature tRNA acceptor stem, an essential step in tRNA biogenesis.

> The strongest mechanistic inference comes from Arabidopsis and broader plant PRORP studies, which show that plant PRORPs recognize mainly the tRNA body rather than extended leader/trailer sequences, with important contacts involving the acceptor stem and TΨC/elbow region. >
