CC   -!- FUNCTION: Substrate-specific adapter of a BCR (BTB-CUL3-RBX1) E3
CC       ubiquitin ligase complex that mediates the ubiquitination of multiple
CC       substrates, leading to either their proteasomal or lysosomal
CC       degradation, or modulating their activity through non-degradative
CC       ubiquitination. Controls endothelial barrier function by mediating
CC       'Lys-63'-linked polyubiquitination of RHOB, promoting its lysosomal
CC       degradation (PubMed:29358211). Mediates polyubiquitination of CEP97,
CC       targeting it for proteasomal degradation. CEP97 degradation upon serum
CC       starvation is essential for primary cilium formation by enabling
CC       removal of the CEP97-CP110 complex from mother centrioles
CC       (PubMed:30404837). Acts as a positive regulator of ferroptosis by
CC       destabilizing SLC7A11. Decreased SLC7A11 levels reduce cystine uptake
CC       and glutathione (GSH) biosynthesis, thereby promoting iron-dependent
CC       lipid peroxidation and ferroptotic cell death (PubMed:38959043). In the
CC       innate immune response, mediates 'Lys-27'-linked polyubiquitination of
CC       TICAM1/TRIF at Lys-523 upon stimulation with LPS. This ubiquitination
CC       is essential for recruitment of TICAM1 to TLR3/4, thereby promoting
CC       TLR3/4-mediated immune signaling (PubMed:31511519). Negatively
CC       regulates KCTD13 stability through the ubiquitin/proteasome degradation
CC       pathway within the developing brain to control neuronal progenitor cell
CC       proliferation and differentiation (By similarity). Acts as a sensor for
CC       co-directional transcription-replication conflicts, where it helps
CC       coordinate the interaction between the replication machinery and RNA
CC       polymerase to allow DNA replication to continue through actively
CC       transcribed regions. Promotes recruitment of ubiquitin ligase complexes
CC       that remove stalled transcription factors, thereby maintaining genome
CC       stability by resolving these conflicts. Ubiquitinates TCEA2 to remodel
CC       the RNAP complex (PubMed:41062692). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q922M3,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:29358211, ECO:0000269|PubMed:30404837,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:31511519, ECO:0000269|PubMed:38959043,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:41062692}.

CC       ubiquitin ligase complex, at least composed of CUL3, KCTD10/BACURD3 and
CC       RBX1 (PubMed:29358211, PubMed:30404837). Interacts with DNA polymerase
