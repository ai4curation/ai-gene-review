CC   -!- FUNCTION: Mitochondrial ornithine-citrulline antiporter (Probable)
CC       (PubMed:12807890, PubMed:22262851). Catalyzes the exchange between
CC       cytosolic ornithine and mitochondrial citrulline plus an H(+), the
CC       proton compensates the positive charge of ornithine thus leading to an
CC       electroneutral transport. Plays a crucial role in the urea cycle, by
CC       connecting the cytosolic and the intramitochondrial reactions of the
CC       urea cycle (Probable) (PubMed:12807890, PubMed:22262851). Lysine and
CC       arginine are also transported by the antiport mechanism (Probable)
CC       (PubMed:12807890). In addition, catalyzes an electroneutral exchange of
CC       ornithine or lysine for H(+), a reaction driven by the pH gradient
CC       across the inner membrane (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:A0A0G2K309, ECO:0000269|PubMed:12807890,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:22262851, ECO:0000305|PubMed:10369256,
CC       ECO:0000305|PubMed:19242930, ECO:0000305|PubMed:24721342}.
