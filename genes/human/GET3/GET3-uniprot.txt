CC   -!- FUNCTION: ATPase required for the post-translational delivery of tail-
CC       anchored (TA) proteins to the endoplasmic reticulum (PubMed:17382883).
CC       Recognizes and selectively binds the transmembrane domain of TA
CC       proteins in the cytosol. This complex then targets to the endoplasmic
CC       reticulum by membrane-bound receptors GET1/WRB and CAMLG/GET2, where
CC       the tail-anchored protein is released for insertion. This process is
CC       regulated by ATP binding and hydrolysis. ATP binding drives the
CC       homodimer towards the closed dimer state, facilitating recognition of
CC       newly synthesized TA membrane proteins. ATP hydrolysis is required for
CC       insertion. Subsequently, the homodimer reverts towards the open dimer
CC       state, lowering its affinity for the GET1-CAMLG receptor, and returning
CC       it to the cytosol to initiate a new round of targeting. May be involved
CC       in insulin signaling. {ECO:0000255|HAMAP-Rule:MF_03112,
