Gene Description

Thrombospondin-2 (THBS2/TSP2, ~145 kDa per monomer), extracellular matricellular glycoprotein that modulates cell-matrix interactions rather than serving as core structural ECM component. Forms homotrimeric complexes secreted into extracellular matrix. Multi-domain architecture: N-terminal heparin-binding domain, procollagen-like region, three type I thrombospondin repeats (TSRs), three type II EGF-like repeats, seven type III calcium-binding repeats, and C-terminal globular domain containing RGD sequence. Functions as regulator of extracellular matrix organization, angiogenesis, and tissue remodeling. Primary role is modulating matrix structure and turnover - influences collagen fibrillogenesis by binding MMP-2 (matrix metalloproteinase-2) and facilitating its endocytic clearance via LRP1 receptor, preventing excessive collagen degradation. Thbs2-null mice show disorganized collagen fibrils, abnormally high MMP-2 levels, connective tissue defects (skin fragility, lax tendons), and increased tissue vascularization. Potent endogenous angiogenesis inhibitor - type I repeats bind CD36 receptor on endothelial cells triggering apoptosis and capillary regression. TSP2-deficient mice exhibit elevated vascular density and excessive blood vessel networks in healing wounds. Binds integrin Œ±vŒ≤3 via RGD sequence, transmitting signals into cells (activates PI3K/Akt and NF-Œ∫B pathways in synovial fibroblasts). Unique among thrombospondins: binds Notch3 receptor and Jagged1 ligand, potentiating Notch3 signal transduction. Dynamically regulated during tissue repair - expression rises in fibroblasts during wound healing, peaks during granulation tissue maturation. Restrains fibrovascular proliferation ensuring proper scar formation. In cartilage, protects from breakdown by suppressing MMP-13 and promoting chondrocyte differentiation. Localizes to interstitial spaces and basement membranes of connective tissues including dermis, cartilage, bone, blood vessel walls. Expression low at baseline but induced during tissue remodeling, injury, inflammation. Implicated in cancer (high TSP2 correlates with suppressed tumor vascularization), fibrotic diseases (modulates TGF-Œ≤ activation), osteoarthritis (upregulated as compensatory response), connective tissue disorders. Serves as extracellular scaffold that orchestrates cell-matrix communication, tissue architecture, and angiogenic balance.
