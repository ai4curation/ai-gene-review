Summary: OVER-ANNOTATION: CCNE1 (Cyclin E1) does NOT catalyze phosphorylation - it is a regulatory subunit that ACTIVATES CDK2, which is the kinase. Cyclins have no kinase activity themselves. PMID:10500095 describes cyclin E being phosphorylated ON threonine 380 (as SUBSTRATE), not catalyzing phosphorylation. The paper discusses "cyclin E-Cdk2 complexes" where CDK2 is the enzyme. The annotation relation is "acts_upstream_of_or_within" which hedges, but the term GO:0006468 still implies direct involvement in the catalytic process. Should be GO:0045859 "regulation of protein kinase activity" or GO:0004693 "cyclin-dependent protein serine/threonine kinase activity" as part of the CDK2 complex.
Proposed replacements: regulation of protein kinase activity
