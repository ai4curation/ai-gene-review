| Identity / synonyms | Human **P3H2** corresponds to **LEPREL1** (also reported as **MLAT4**), encoding **prolyl 3-hydroxylase 2**, a member of the leprecan/prolyl 3-hydroxylase family; distinct from **P3H1/LEPRE1** and **P3H3/LEPREL2**. Family members differ in tissue distribution and substrate specificity; P3H2 is enriched in basement-membrane–rich tissues. (pqac-00000002, pqac-00000001, pqac-00000003) | Gjaltema & Bank 2017, https://doi.org/10.1080/10409238.2016.1269716; Hudson et al. 2015, https://doi.org/10.1074/jbc.M114.634915; Pignata et al. 2021, https://doi.org/10.3390/ijms22083896 |
| Enzyme class / reaction | P3H2 is a **2-oxoglutarate–dependent dioxygenase** (EC **1.14.11.7**) that catalyzes **proline → 3-hydroxyproline (3-Hyp)** in collagen. As a collagen prolyl hydroxylase, it uses the canonical cofactor system of this enzyme class: **Fe2+**, **2-oxoglutarate**, **O2**, and **ascorbate**. (pqac-00000010, pqac-00000014, pqac-00000029) | Gjaltema & Bank 2017, https://doi.org/10.1080/10409238.2016.1269716; Rappu et al. 2019, https://doi.org/10.1042/EBC20180053; Ye et al. 2024, https://doi.org/10.1136/bmjophth-2024-001720 |
| Subcellular localization / site of action | P3H2 acts during **intracellular collagen biosynthesis** before triple-helix maturation, in the **ER-associated secretory pathway**; one report places P3H2 in the **endoplasmic reticulum and Golgi network**. Functional biology is therefore tied to **matrix assembly**, especially basement membrane collagen processing. (pqac-00000003, pqac-00000014) | Pignata et al. 2021, https://doi.org/10.3390/ijms22083896; Rappu et al. 2019, https://doi.org/10.1042/EBC20180053 |
| Main substrates | Best-supported primary substrate is **type IV collagen**; P3H2 also modifies selected sites in **fibrillar collagens I, II, and V/XI**. Unlike P3H1, which is dominant for **Pro986**, P3H2 preferentially targets additional sites such as **Pro707**, **Pro944**, and **C-terminal (GPP)n repeats**. (pqac-00000004, pqac-00000006, pqac-00000008, pqac-00000010) | Montgomery et al. 2018, https://doi.org/10.1074/jbc.RA117.000406; Fernandes et al. 2011, https://doi.org/10.1074/jbc.M111.267906; Gjaltema & Bank 2017, https://doi.org/10.1080/10409238.2016.1269716 |
| Sequence motifs / site preferences | Preferred contexts include **Gly-Pro-Pro (GPP) repeats** and **Gly-Pro-4Hyp**-containing motifs; in collagen IV, P3H2-dependent 3-Hyp is concentrated at multiple repeat sites, including long **(GPP)4** regions. P3H2 is the **major but not sole** collagen IV 3-hydroxylase. (pqac-00000009, pqac-00000004, pqac-00000012) | Montgomery et al. 2018, https://doi.org/10.1074/jbc.RA117.000406 |
| Quantitative PTM data | In type IV collagen, amino-acid analysis showed **~30.6 3-Hyp residues per triple helix in WT** versus **~4.2 in P3H2 knockout**, with >80% 4-Hyp and >90% hydroxylysine occupancy retained, indicating a selective loss of 3-hydroxylation. Site-level occupancy in fibrillar collagens includes **~80% at Pro707** in α2(I)/α2(V) and **13% at Pro470** in α2(V); collagen IV contains **>10 3-Hyp residues per α-chain**. (pqac-00000012, pqac-00000023, pqac-00000008, pqac-00000010) | Montgomery et al. 2018, https://doi.org/10.1074/jbc.RA117.000406; Gjaltema & Bank 2017, https://doi.org/10.1080/10409238.2016.1269716 |
| Regulation / pathway integration | In endothelial cells, **VEGF-A** strongly induces P3H2 through **VEGFR-2 → p38 MAPK** signaling; RNA-seq identified P3H2 as the **second-most upregulated gene, 16.7-fold** after VEGF-A stimulation. Sorafenib blocked induction, and p38 inhibition markedly reduced it. (pqac-00000020, pqac-00000017, pqac-00000018) | Pignata et al. 2021, https://doi.org/10.3390/ijms22083896 |
| Angiogenesis-related functional effects | P3H2 promotes a **pro-angiogenic ECM state**: overexpression increased endothelial migration and capillary sprouting, reorganized collagen IV into condensed structures, and aligned cells along collagen IV bundles. Knockdown reduced **migration by ~50%**, **VEGF-driven spheroid sprouting by ~35%**, and in laser-induced choroidal neovascularization reduced lesion volume by **~40%**; P3H2 protein also **doubled** 5 days after laser injury. (pqac-00000021, pqac-00000016, pqac-00000017) | Pignata et al. 2021, https://doi.org/10.3390/ijms22083896 |
| Biological consequence of collagen IV 3-hydroxylation | P3H2-dependent 3-hydroxylation of type IV collagen changes its binding properties, including interactions with **GPVI** and **nidogens 1/2**. Loss of this modification can alter basement membrane biology and platelet-collagen interactions. (pqac-00000004, pqac-00000007) | Montgomery et al. 2018, https://doi.org/10.1074/jbc.RA117.000406; Pokidysheva et al. 2014, https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.1307597111 |
| Disease / phenotype links | **Human LEPREL1/P3H2 mutations** are linked to **severe/high myopia** and broader ocular phenotypes. In mice, P3h2 deficiency causes abnormal ocular collagen PTMs consistent with a mechanism for myopia; one 2024 trio-WES study reported **2 P3H2 variants** (**c.1865T>C; p.Phe622Ser** and **c.212T>C; p.Leu71Pro**) among **10 variants in 7 genes across 7 families** with early-onset high myopia. Open Targets also links P3H2 most strongly to **rare isolated myopia**. (pqac-00000005, pqac-00000026, pqac-00000027, pqac-00000015) | Hudson et al. 2015, https://doi.org/10.1074/jbc.M114.634915; Ye et al. 2024, https://doi.org/10.1136/bmjophth-2024-001720; Open Targets context (pqac-00000015) |
| Developmental phenotype / expert interpretation | In a key mouse study, **P3H2-null embryos died by E8.5** because non-3-hydroxylated embryonic type IV collagen interacted abnormally with maternal platelet **GPVI**, causing platelet aggregation and thrombosis; rescue by GPVI loss supported a direct mechanism. Expert reviews interpret this as strong evidence that P3H2 evolved a specialized role in **basement membrane collagen IV modification** distinct from P3H1. (pqac-00000007, pqac-00000000) | Pokidysheva et al. 2014, https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.1307597111; Hudson & Eyre 2013, https://doi.org/10.3109/03008207.2013.800867 |
