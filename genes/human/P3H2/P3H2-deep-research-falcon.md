# Research report: Human P3H2 (LEPREL1; UniProt Q8IVL5)

## Executive summary
Prolyl 3-hydroxylase 2 (P3H2), encoded by **LEPREL1** (also reported as **P3H2**; UniProt **Q8IVL5**), is a collagen-modifying **2-oxoglutarate–dependent dioxygenase** that catalyzes formation of **3-hydroxyproline (3-Hyp)** in collagens, with strongest evidence for **type IV collagen** (basement membrane collagen) as its major substrate. Loss of P3H2 activity dramatically reduces collagen IV 3-Hyp content and alters collagen IV–dependent biological interactions (e.g., with GPVI and nidogens), linking P3H2 to basement membrane biology, ocular phenotypes (notably severe/high myopia), and VEGF-driven pathological angiogenesis in preclinical models. Key quantitative findings include ~**30.6 vs 4.2** 3-Hyp residues per collagen IV triple helix (WT vs P3H2 knockout), **16.7-fold** VEGF-A induction of P3H2 transcript in endothelial cells, and ~**40%** suppression of laser-induced choroidal neovascularization after intravitreal siRNA knockdown of P3H2. ## 1. Key concepts, definitions, and current understanding

### 1.1 Verified gene/protein identity (mandatory disambiguation)
P3H2 is the **LEPREL1** gene product, part of the “leprecan”/collagen prolyl-3-hydroxylase family that includes **P3H1 (LEPRE1)** and **P3H3 (LEPREL2)**; these paralogs differ in collagen site specificity, tissue distribution, and (for P3H1) obligate complex formation with CRTAP and cyclophilin B/PPIB. (gjaltema2017molecularinsightsinto pages 4-5, hudson2015posttranslationallyabnormalcollagens pages 1-1)

### 1.2 Enzymatic function: what reaction is catalyzed?
P3H2 catalyzes **hydroxylation of selected proline residues to 3-hydroxyproline (3-Hyp) in collagens**, a post-translational modification that is relatively rare compared with prolyl 4-hydroxylation but can be abundant in collagen IV. (montgomery2018posttranslationalmodificationof pages 3-4, montgomery2018posttranslationalmodificationof pages 2-3, pokidysheva2014biologicalroleof pages 1-2)

P3H enzymes are **2-oxoglutarate–dependent dioxygenases**, i.e., members of the iron/2-oxoglutarate oxygenase superfamily; as such they operate in the canonical biochemical framework requiring **2-oxoglutarate, Fe2+, O2, and ascorbate** (the latter maintaining the catalytic Fe in a reduced state). This cofactor logic is described for collagen prolyl hydroxylases in reviews and is consistent with the family classification of P3H2. (gjaltema2017molecularinsightsinto pages 4-5, rappu2019roleofprolyl pages 1-3)

### 1.3 Substrate specificity and site preferences
**Collagen IV is the best-supported major substrate class** for P3H2, consistent with P3H2 enrichment in basement membrane–rich tissues in expert reviews. (gjaltema2017molecularinsightsinto pages 4-5, pokidysheva2014biologicalroleof pages 1-2)

In fibrillar collagens, P3H2 contributes to 3-hydroxylation at **non-Pro986 sites**, including **Pro707**, **Pro944**, and **C-terminal (GPP)n repeats**; by contrast, the canonical **Pro986** site in collagen I/II/V is primarily P3H1-dependent and remains fully hydroxylated when P3H2 is suppressed. (fernandes2011arolefor pages 1-2, gjaltema2017molecularinsightsinto pages 5-7)

Sequence context preferences for P3H2-supported collagen IV 3-Hyp include **Gly-Pro-Pro (GPP) repeats** and **Gly-Pro-4Hyp**-containing repeat motifs, with evidence that long (GPP)n regions can carry multiple 3-Hyp modifications. (montgomery2018posttranslationalmodificationof pages 3-4)

### 1.4 Cellular localization and where the function occurs
P3H2 functions during **intracellular collagen biosynthesis in the secretory pathway** before collagen triple helix maturation; one experimental report places P3H2 within the **endoplasmic reticulum and Golgi network**, consistent with collagen modification prior to secretion and matrix deposition. (pignata2021prolyl3hydroxylase2 pages 1-2, rappu2019roleofprolyl pages 1-3)

## 2. Experimental evidence defining P3H2 function

### 2.1 Collagen IV modification is highly P3H2-dependent (quantitative)
A key biochemical study of type IV collagen produced by PFHR-9 cells showed that P3H2 knockout caused a near-loss of collagen IV 3-Hyp, with **~30.6 3-Hyp residues per triple helix in wild-type vs ~4.2 in P3H2 knockout**; meanwhile, overall 4-Hyp and hydroxylysine occupancies remained high (>80% and >90%, respectively), indicating a selective loss of 3-hydroxylation rather than generalized collagen processing failure. (montgomery2018posttranslationalmodificationof pages 2-3, montgomery2018posttranslationalmodificationof media 2af7e8d3)

The same study further supports successful genetic disruption by showing a ~**40-fold decrease** in P3H2 mRNA in a knockout clone, along with a **12-fold decrease** in P3H3 transcription (highlighting potential compensatory/linked regulation in that cellular system). (montgomery2018posttranslationalmodificationof pages 2-3)

### 2.2 Functional consequences of collagen IV 3-hydroxylation
P3H2-dependent 3-hydroxylation of type IV collagen affects collagen’s interactions with basement membrane partners (e.g., nidogens) and platelet receptor **GPVI**, indicating that 3-Hyp can modulate molecular recognition rather than (only) collagen stability. (montgomery2018posttranslationalmodificationof pages 3-4, pokidysheva2014biologicalroleof pages 1-2)

In a mouse genetic model, P3H2-null embryos died by **E8.5**, and mechanistic analysis implicated aberrant interaction between **non–3-hydroxylated embryonic collagen IV** and maternal platelet **GPVI**, triggering platelet aggregation/thrombosis; rescue by GPVI loss strongly supports a causal pathway from collagen IV 3-Hyp status to a specific receptor interaction outcome. (pokidysheva2014biologicalroleof pages 1-2)

### 2.3 Distinguishing P3H2 from P3H1/P3H3 (expert synthesis)
A frequently cited expert synthesis emphasizes that P3H family members have evolved distinct substrate-site and tissue specificities: P3H1 is the dominant enzyme for the “A1” **Pro986** site and participates in a CRTAP–CypB/PPIB complex, while P3H2 is more closely tied to basement membrane collagens (especially collagen IV) and to additional sites in clade A fibrillar collagens. (gjaltema2017molecularinsightsinto pages 4-5, gjaltema2017molecularinsightsinto pages 5-7)

## 4. Current applications and real-world implementations

### 4.1 Anti-angiogenic targeting (preclinical proof-of-concept)
In a laser-induced choroidal neovascularization (CNV) model, intravitreal delivery of a **cholesterol-conjugated siRNA targeting P3H2** reduced pathological neovascularization by **~40%** compared with control siRNA; CNV volume quantification was reported as mean ± SEM with **n=10 mice/group** and **p=0.001**, and P3H2 protein levels in RPE/choroid were reported to **double at day 5** post-laser injury. (pignata2021prolyl3hydroxylase2 pages 10-14)

In vitro, P3H2 knockdown reduced VEGF/EGM-stimulated endothelial migration by **~50%** and VEGF-induced spheroid sprouting by **~35%**, supporting a mechanistically coherent translational rationale (targeting a VEGF-induced ECM-modifying enzyme rather than VEGF ligand/receptor directly). (pignata2021prolyl3hydroxylase2 pages 7-10)

### 4.2 Diagnostic genetics usage for ocular disease
The 2024 trio-WES study illustrates real-world implementation as a **gene discovery/diagnostic approach** for early-onset high myopia, explicitly reporting P3H2 variants in affected families and embedding P3H2 into an ocular disease gene set (even though functional validation is still needed). (ye2024triobasedwholeexomesequencing pages 1-1, ye2024triobasedwholeexomesequencing pages 5-6)

## 5. Expert opinions and analysis (authoritative sources)

Expert reviews emphasize that collagen prolyl 3-hydroxylation is a “minor” PTM in abundance compared with prolyl 4-hydroxylation, yet it can have **outsized functional effects** via site-specific and collagen-type-specific patterns, particularly in basement membrane collagen IV (where 3-Hyp content is relatively high). This framing supports interpreting P3H2 as a specialized regulator of collagen IV molecular interactions and matrix properties rather than a global collagen folding enzyme. (pokidysheva2014biologicalroleof pages 1-2, gjaltema2017molecularinsightsinto pages 5-7)

## 6. Key statistics and data points (from recent/primary studies)

* Collagen IV 3-Hyp content: **WT ~30.6 vs P3H2-KO ~4.2 residues per triple helix** (PFHR-9-derived collagen IV). (montgomery2018posttranslationalmodificationof pages 2-3, montgomery2018posttranslationalmodificationof media 2af7e8d3)
* Collagen IV processing controls: >**80% 4-Hyp occupancy** and >**90% hydroxylysine occupancy** in both WT and KO in the same system, consistent with selective 3-Hyp loss. (montgomery2018posttranslationalmodificationof pages 2-3)
* VEGF transcriptional response: P3H2 transcript **16.7-fold upregulated** after VEGF-A stimulation; RNA-seq reported **459** DE genes among ~8000 expressed, with **48** genes >3-fold induced. (pignata2021prolyl3hydroxylase2 pages 2-4)
* Endothelial knockdown efficacy and phenotypes: P3H2 siRNA reduced mRNA by **~35% (12 h)** and **~60% (48 h)**; protein by **~50% (24 h)**; migration reduced **~50%**; VEGF-induced sprouting reduced **~35%**. (pignata2021prolyl3hydroxylase2 pages 7-10)
* In vivo pathological angiogenesis: P3H2 protein **~2×** at day 5 post-laser; CNV volume reduced by **~40%** after intravitreal chol-siP3H2 (**n=10 mice/group; p=0.001**). (pignata2021prolyl3hydroxylase2 pages 10-14)
* 2024 cohort variant summary: **7 genes / 10 variants across 7 families**, including **two P3H2 variants** (c.1865T>C p.Phe622Ser; c.212T>C p.Leu71Pro). (ye2024triobasedwholeexomesequencing pages 1-1)

## 8. Practical functional annotation summary (recommended phrasing)
**Molecular function:** P3H2 (LEPREL1) is a collagen prolyl 3-hydroxylase (2-oxoglutarate–dependent dioxygenase) that generates **3-hydroxyproline** at selected proline residues, with strong preference for **type IV collagen** and additional roles at specific non-Pro986 sites in fibrillar collagens. (fernandes2011arolefor pages 1-2, montgomery2018posttranslationalmodificationof pages 3-4, montgomery2018posttranslationalmodificationof pages 2-3)

**Biological process:** Basement membrane collagen IV maturation and matrix molecular interactions; modulation of angiogenic ECM properties in endothelial cells downstream of VEGF/VEGFR2-p38 signaling. (pignata2021prolyl3hydroxylase2 pages 10-14, pokidysheva2014biologicalroleof pages 1-2)

**Cellular component (working model):** ER/secretory pathway compartments supporting collagen biosynthesis (ER and Golgi reported), consistent with intracellular collagen modification. (pignata2021prolyl3hydroxylase2 pages 1-2, rappu2019roleofprolyl pages 1-3)

**Disease relevance:** Recessive loss-of-function variants in LEPREL1/P3H2 are implicated in severe/high myopia and related ocular pathology; P3H2 is also implicated as a modulator of pathological ocular neovascularization in preclinical models. (hudson2015posttranslationallyabnormalcollagens pages 1-2, ye2024triobasedwholeexomesequencing pages 1-1, pignata2021prolyl3hydroxylase2 pages 10-14)
