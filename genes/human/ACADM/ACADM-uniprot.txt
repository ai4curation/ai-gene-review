CC   -!- FUNCTION: Medium-chain specific acyl-CoA dehydrogenase is one of the
CC       acyl-CoA dehydrogenases that catalyze the first step of mitochondrial
CC       fatty acid beta-oxidation (FAO), breaking down fatty acids into acetyl-
CC       CoA and allowing the production of energy from fats (PubMed:1970566,
CC       PubMed:21237683, PubMed:2251268, PubMed:8823175). The first step of FAO
CC       consists in the proR-proR stereospecific alpha, beta-dehydrogenation of
CC       fatty acyl-CoA thioesters using the electron transfer flavoprotein
CC       (ETF) as their physiologic electron acceptor, resulting in the
CC       formation of trans-2-enoyl-CoA ((2E)-enoyl-CoA) (PubMed:2251268). ETF
CC       is the electron acceptor that transfers electrons to the main
CC       mitochondrial respiratory chain via ETF-ubiquinone oxidoreductase (ETF
CC       dehydrogenase) (PubMed:15159392, PubMed:25416781). Among the different
CC       mitochondrial acyl-CoA dehydrogenases, medium-chain specific acyl-CoA
CC       dehydrogenase has preference for fatty acyl-CoAs with saturated 6 to 12
CC       carbons long primary chains, making it but can also catalyze longer
CC       chains such as C14 and C16 (PubMed:1970566, PubMed:21237683,
CC       PubMed:2251268, PubMed:8823175). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15159392,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:1970566, ECO:0000269|PubMed:21237683,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:2251268, ECO:0000269|PubMed:25416781,
