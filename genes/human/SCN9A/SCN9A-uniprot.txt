CC   -!- FUNCTION: Pore-forming subunit of Nav1.7, a voltage-gated sodium (Nav)
CC       channel that directly mediates the depolarizing phase of action
CC       potentials in excitable membranes (PubMed:38381792, PubMed:40768348).
CC       Navs, also called VGSCs (voltage-gated sodium channels) or VDSCs
CC       (voltage-dependent sodium channels), operate by switching between
CC       closed and open conformations depending on the voltage difference
CC       across the membrane. In the open conformation they allow Na(+) ions to
CC       selectively pass through the pore, along their electrochemical gradient
CC       (PubMed:38381792, PubMed:40768348). The influx of Na(+) ions provokes
CC       membrane depolarization, initiating the propagation of electrical
CC       signals throughout cells and tissues (PubMed:15385606, PubMed:16988069,
CC       PubMed:17145499, PubMed:17167479, PubMed:19369487, PubMed:24311784,
CC       PubMed:25240195, PubMed:26680203, PubMed:7720699). Nav1.7 plays a
CC       crucial role in controlling the excitability and action potential
CC       propagation from nociceptor neurons, thereby contributing to the
CC       sensory perception of pain (PubMed:17145499, PubMed:17167479,
CC       PubMed:19369487, PubMed:24311784). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15178348,
