## 1) Key concepts, definitions, and current understanding

### 1.1 Peroxisomes and peroxisomal matrix protein import (PTS1/PTS2)
Peroxisomes are single-membrane organelles essential for lipid metabolism and redox homeostasis; they lack their own protein synthesis and therefore must import most enzymes post-translationally from the cytosol. A defining feature of peroxisomes is their ability to import **folded proteins and even oligomeric complexes**, which makes their import pathway mechanistically unusual relative to ER/mitochondrial import. (kumar2024theperoxisomean pages 10-11, skowyra2024towardssolvingthe pages 1-3)

Peroxisomal matrix proteins are targeted mainly by:
- **PTS1**: a C-terminal tripeptide (often **SKL**) recognized by the soluble receptor **PEX5** through its C-terminal tetratricopeptide repeat (**TPR**) domain. (gao2022proteinimportinto pages 1-3)
- **PTS2**: an N-terminal nonapeptide recognized by **PEX7**, which in humans typically relies on the long PEX5 isoform (**PEX5L**) as a co-receptor. (rudowitz2023importandquality pages 2-3, gao2022proteinimportinto pages 1-3)

### 1.2 Docking/translocation machinery (PEX13/PEX14) and receptor cycling
Cargo-loaded PEX5 is recruited to the peroxisomal membrane by the **docking/translocation machinery**, which in mammals includes the conserved membrane proteins **PEX13** and **PEX14**. (rudowitz2023importandquality pages 2-3, skowyra2024towardssolvingthe pages 1-3)

A mechanistic framework supported by primary studies and recent reviews is:
1. PEX5 binds PTS1 cargo in the cytosol.
2. PEX5 docks at the peroxisomal membrane via interactions involving **PEX13 and PEX14**.
3. Cargo is delivered to the matrix (models differ in details; see below).
4. PEX5 is **ubiquitinated** and exported/recycled back to the cytosol by the **PEX1–PEX6 AAA ATPase** system; this export step consumes ATP and is central to sustained import. (skowyra2024towardssolvingthe pages 8-9, skowyra2024towardssolvingthe pages 1-3)

### 1.3 What is PEX13’s primary molecular function?
PEX13 is a core element of the peroxisomal docking/translocation apparatus that recruits import receptors and helps create (or organize) the physical conduit allowing receptor–cargo complexes to cross the membrane. Mechanistically, PEX13 contributes both:
- **A selective-phase conduit function** via its YG-rich IDR (nuclear pore-like model), and
- **Protein–protein interaction control** via its SH3 domain and adjacent motifs that regulate receptor docking and docking-complex interactions. (gao2022proteinimportinto pages 1-3, gaussmann2024modulationofperoxisomal pages 1-2)

PEX13 is **not an enzyme**; its primary role is structural/adaptor-like in organizing and regulating receptor docking and translocation.

## 2) PEX13 protein features and mechanistic role in matrix import

### 2.1 Subcellular localization and topology
PEX13 is an **integral peroxisomal membrane protein**. Work supporting the “nuclear pore-like” model indicates that PEX13 oligomerizes in the membrane and that its **YG-rich domain** can form a selective phase that interacts with PEX5, enabling translocation of folded cargo. (gao2022proteinimportinto pages 1-3, kumar2024theperoxisomean pages 10-11)

### 2.2 Domain architecture and key motifs
Recent structural and mechanistic work in 2024 provides detailed functional annotation of PEX13’s C-terminal region:
- **C-terminal SH3 domain**: binds diaromatic peptide motifs (not only canonical SH3 PxxP ligands).
- **Proximal intramolecular FxxxF motif**: forms an autoinhibitory intramolecular interaction with the SH3 domain.
- **N-terminal YG repeats in an IDR**: implicated in selective-phase behavior/hydrogel formation and recruitment/partitioning of PEX5. (gaussmann2024modulationofperoxisomal pages 3-4, gao2022proteinimportinto pages 1-3, kumar2024theperoxisomean pages 10-11)

### 2.3 Interaction partners and functional interfaces

#### PEX5 (matrix import receptor)
PEX5 contains conserved **WxxxF/Y motifs** in its unstructured N-terminus. These motifs participate in docking by binding membrane components.
- PEX13 SH3 binds PEX5 WxxxF/Y motifs and this interaction is regulated by the intramolecular FxxxF–SH3 interaction. (gaussmann2024modulationofperoxisomal pages 1-2)
- The YG-phase model shows PEX5 selectively partitions into purified PEX13 YG hydrogels via these conserved aromatic motifs. (gao2022proteinimportinto pages 1-3)

Classic biochemical work also supports the idea that PEX13’s SH3 contributes to receptor docking/handling: PEX5 binding to PEX13 (via SH3) occurs at lower levels than to PEX14, consistent with PEX14 as a high-affinity receptor-binding hub and PEX13 as an associated regulatory component. (borgia2022genotype–phenotypecorrelationsand pages 1-2)

#### PEX14 (docking complex core)
PEX14 is a central docking component in mammals and provides interaction surfaces for PEX5. A 2024 structural/biophysical study demonstrates that human PEX13 binds PEX14 primarily via the **PEX13 FxxxF motif** engaging the **PEX14 N-terminal domain**, rather than through an SH3:PxxP interaction as in yeast. (gaussmann2024modulationofperoxisomal pages 10-11)

#### PEX7 (PTS2 pathway, via PEX5L)
Reviews and mechanistic work describe that in humans PEX7 uses PEX5 as a co-receptor (PEX5L), and the receptor complex docks via the same peroxisomal membrane apparatus including PEX13/PEX14. (rudowitz2023importandquality pages 2-3, gao2022proteinimportinto pages 1-3)

## 3) Recent developments and latest research (priority 2023–2024)

### 3.1 2024: Structural biology of the human PEX13 SH3 + FxxxF module (Nature Communications)
Gaussmann et al. (published **April 2024**, Nature Communications; URL https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-024-47605-w) provide high-resolution structural and functional evidence that:
- PEX13’s SH3 domain forms an **intramolecular interaction** with a proximal **FxxxF motif**.
- PEX14 NTD binding to the FxxxF motif **releases** this intramolecular interaction, making the SH3 surface available for other ligands.
- The SH3 domain recognizes **FxxxF** and **WxxxF/Y** motifs on a **non-canonical surface**, and PEX13-PEX14 binding in humans is mediated by FxxxF–PEX14 NTD rather than SH3:PxxP binding.
- This creates a **dynamic interaction network** among PEX13, PEX14 and PEX5 that tunes matrix import efficiency. (gaussmann2024modulationofperoxisomal pages 3-4, gaussmann2024modulationofperoxisomal pages 1-2)

These results refine the functional annotation of PEX13 from a generic “docking complex component” to a **motif-regulated interaction hub** controlling access of receptor motifs and docking-complex assembly.

### 3.2 2024: Integration of the YG-phase/nuclear pore-like model and “condensate” ideas (reviews)
A 2024 review (“The peroxisome: an update on mysteries 3.0”, **January 2024**, Histochemistry and Cell Biology; URL https://doi.org/10.1007/s00418-023-02259-5) synthesizes models for the “mysterious pore,” emphasizing:
- PEX13 contains conserved **YG repeats** in an IDR reminiscent of nucleoporin FG repeats.
- Purified YG domains form hydrogels, and PEX5 partitions via **WxxxF/Y** motifs.
- Competing/converging models include a **transient pore** model and a **liquid–liquid phase separation/condensate** model where IDRs from PEX5/PEX13/PEX14 form transient conduits. (kumar2024theperoxisomean pages 10-11)

A 2024 Trends in Cell Biology review (published **May 2024**; URL https://doi.org/10.1016/j.tcb.2023.08.005) frames peroxisomal import as analogous to nuclear transport in the sense that intact receptors can fully traverse the membrane and later return via a separate retrotranslocation system, with PEX13 positioned as conduit-forming machinery working with PEX14. (skowyra2024towardssolvingthe pages 1-3)

### 3.3 2023: PEX13 as a peroxisome quality-control/pexophagy regulator (Autophagy)
Demers et al. (Autophagy; **January 2023** publication with 2022 DOI registration; URL https://doi.org/10.1080/15548627.2022.2160566) report that PEX13 is required to prevent degradation of otherwise healthy peroxisomes. Key findings include:
- Loss of PEX13 leads to accumulation of **ubiquitinated PEX5** on peroxisomes and increased **peroxisome-dependent ROS**, which together promote **pexophagy**.
- PEX13 levels are downregulated during amino acid starvation, supporting regulation of peroxisome turnover.
- In zebrafish, pex13 knockout causes peroxisome loss and lethality, and autophagy inhibition (chloroquine) increases peroxisomal structures, consistent with autophagy-mediated loss.
- Human PEX13-GFP localizes to peroxisomes and partially rescues phenotypes in the animal model, supporting conserved function. (demers2023pex13preventspexophagy pages 1-2, demers2023pex13preventspexophagy pages 6-7)

This expands PEX13 functional annotation beyond import per se to **peroxisome homeostasis/quality control**.

## 4) Current applications and real-world implementations

### 4.1 Clinical genetics: PEX13 in Zellweger spectrum disorders (ZSD)
PEX gene variants are used in clinical workflows to diagnose peroxisome biogenesis disorders/Zellweger spectrum disorders. A PEX13-focused cohort study used whole-exome sequencing and variant filtering against population resources (including **gnomAD** and **UK Biobank**) and validated variants by Sanger sequencing, illustrating real-world diagnostic practice for rare disease. (borgia2022genotype–phenotypecorrelationsand pages 6-9)

### 4.2 Cell-based functional validation of variants and mechanism
Recent mechanistic work uses **cellular import assays** that are widely applicable to functional annotation and variant interpretation:
- PTS1 import reporter **GFP–SKL** localization and biochemical fractionation (cytosol vs organelles).
- PTS2 import readout via **thiolase processing** (precursor to mature forms).
These assays were used to show that perturbing PEX13 (including domain deletions/variants) reduces PTS1 and PTS2 import, tying molecular interactions to cell function. (gaussmann2024modulationofperoxisomal pages 10-11)

### 4.3 Translational/therapeutic relevance
While no PEX13-targeted therapy is established in the retrieved sources, the 2023 pexophagy findings suggest pathways (ubiquitination state of PEX5, peroxisomal ROS) that could be leveraged to modulate peroxisome turnover in disease contexts where peroxisome maintenance is beneficial or harmful. This is presently a mechanistic/target-discovery insight rather than a clinical implementation. (demers2023pex13preventspexophagy pages 1-2)

## 5) Expert opinions and analysis (authoritative sources)

### 5.1 Consensus: PEX13/PEX14 as docking/translocation machinery; unresolved “pore” structure
Authoritative reviews emphasize that peroxisomal import remains mechanistically unusual and incompletely structurally resolved:
- A stable, visually defined pore has not been captured; models invoke transient pores or condensates and/or a selective phase formed by PEX13 IDRs. (kumar2024theperoxisomean pages 10-11)
- Reviews emphasize PEX13/PEX14 as core machinery and receptor recycling as a critical energy-consuming step. (skowyra2024towardssolvingthe pages 1-3, rudowitz2023importandquality pages 2-3)

### 5.2 Mechanistic synthesis (integration)
Taken together, 2022–2024 evidence supports a composite model:
- The **YG-rich IDR** of PEX13 provides the physical basis for a selective phase that can accommodate folded cargo by allowing receptor diffusion (nuclear pore-like). (gao2022proteinimportinto pages 1-3)
- The **C-terminal SH3 and FxxxF module** provides regulatory control over receptor/docking interactions through ligand competition and conformational switching induced by PEX14 binding. (gaussmann2024modulationofperoxisomal pages 3-4)
- Quality control integrates import status with organelle fate decisions (pexophagy) through the ubiquitination state of PEX5 and ROS signaling, with PEX13 acting as a suppressor of inappropriate turnover. (demers2023pex13preventspexophagy pages 1-2)

## 6) Statistics and data highlights from recent studies
