CC   -!- FUNCTION: Stabilizing factor, of the core iron-sulfur cluster (ISC)
CC       assembly complex, that regulates, in association with NDUFAB1, the
CC       stability and the cysteine desulfurase activity of NFS1 and
CC       participates in the [2Fe-2S] clusters assembly on the scaffolding
CC       protein ISCU (PubMed:17331979, PubMed:31664822). The core iron-sulfur
CC       cluster (ISC) assembly complex is involved in the de novo synthesis of
CC       a [2Fe-2S] cluster, the first step of the mitochondrial iron-sulfur
CC       protein biogenesis. This process is initiated by the cysteine
CC       desulfurase complex (NFS1:LYRM4:NDUFAB1) that produces persulfide which
CC       is delivered on the scaffold protein ISCU in a FXN-dependent manner.
CC       Then this complex is stabilized by FDX2 which provides reducing
CC       equivalents to accomplish the [2Fe-2S] cluster assembly. Finally, the
CC       [2Fe-2S] cluster is transferred from ISCU to chaperone proteins,
CC       including HSCB, HSPA9 and GLRX5 (By similarity). May also participates
CC       in the iron-sulfur protein biogenesis in the cytoplasm through its
CC       interaction with the cytoplasmic form of NFS1 (PubMed:19454487).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9H1K1, ECO:0000269|PubMed:17331979,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:19454487, ECO:0000269|PubMed:31664822}.
CC   -!- PATHWAY: Cofactor biosynthesis; iron-sulfur cluster biosynthesis.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:17331979, ECO:0000269|PubMed:31664822}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homodimer (PubMed:29097656, PubMed:31101807). Component of the
CC       mitochondrial core iron-sulfur cluster (ISC) complex composed of NFS1,
CC       LYRM4, NDUFAB1, ISCU, FXN, and FDX2; this complex is a heterohexamer
CC       containing two copies of each monomer (Probable). Component of the
CC       cyteine desulfurase complex composed of NFS1, LYRM4 and NDUFAB1; this
CC       complex contributes to the stability and cysteine desulfurase activity
CC       of NFS1 (PubMed:31664822, PubMed:34824239). Interacts with FXN; this
CC       interaction is nickel-dependent (PubMed:17331979). Forms a complex with
CC       the cytoplasmic form of NFS1; this complex increases the stability and
CC       cysteine desulfurase activity of NFS1 (PubMed:18650437,
CC       PubMed:19454487). Interacts with NFS1 (PubMed:29097656). Component of a
CC       complex composed of FXN, NFS1, LYRM4 and ISCU (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8K215, ECO:0000269|PubMed:17331979,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:18650437, ECO:0000269|PubMed:19454487,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:29097656, ECO:0000269|PubMed:31101807,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:31664822, ECO:0000269|PubMed:34824239,
CC       ECO:0000305|PubMed:29097656, ECO:0000305|PubMed:31101807}.
