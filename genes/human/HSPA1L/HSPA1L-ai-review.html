Gene Description

HSPA1L (Heat shock 70 kDa protein 1-like, also known as HSP70-Hom) is a constitutively expressed member of the HSP70/HSPA family of molecular chaperones. It is approximately 90% identical in sequence to the stress-inducible HSPA1A. Unlike HSPA1A, HSPA1L is not induced by heat shock (PMID:1700760) and is notably enriched in testis/spermatids (PMID:9685725). As an ATP-dependent foldase chaperone, HSPA1L assists in protein folding and refolding through cycles of ATP binding, hydrolysis, and ADP release, mediated by co-chaperones. It has been shown to refold heat-denatured luciferase and to suppress polyglutamine aggregation (PMID:21231916). HSPA1L also functions as a positive regulator of PRKN (Parkin) translocation to damaged mitochondria, requiring both its ATPase and substrate-binding domains as well as its EEVD co-chaperone interaction motif (PMID:24270810). The protein is located in the MHC class III region on chromosome 6.
