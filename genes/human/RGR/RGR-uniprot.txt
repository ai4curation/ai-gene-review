CC   -!- FUNCTION: Non-visual opsin that functions as a light-dependent
CC       facilitator of retinoid isomerization and trafficking in retinal
CC       pigment epithelium (RPE) and Mueller glia (PubMed:18474598).
CC       Participates in a photic visual cycle by complementing the classical
CC       enzymatic RPE65/RDH pathway for chromophore regeneration (By
CC       similarity). Binds all-trans-retinal via a conserved Schiff-base lysine
CC       and, upon illumination, promotes photoisomerization of all-trans-
CC       retinal to 11-cis-retinal required for regeneration of visual pigments
CC       (By similarity). Also mediates light-dependent mobilization of stored
CC       all-trans-retinyl esters from lipid droplets to endoplasmic reticulum
CC       membranes, facilitating their processing into 11-cis-retinoids
CC       (PubMed:18474598). May also modulate isomerohydrolase activity
CC       independent of light (By similarity). Although RGR is structurally a
CC       GPCR-family opsin, there is no consistent evidence that RGR activates G
CC       proteins or initiates a canonical second-messenger signaling cascade
CC       (Probable). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9Z2B3, ECO:0000269|PubMed:18474598,
CC       ECO:0000305}.
