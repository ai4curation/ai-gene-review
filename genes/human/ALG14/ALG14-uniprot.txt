CC   -!- FUNCTION: Part of the UDP-N-acetylglucosamine transferase complex that
CC       operates in the biosynthetic pathway of dolichol-linked
CC       oligosaccharides, the glycan precursors employed in protein asparagine
CC       (N)-glycosylation. The assembly of dolichol-linked oligosaccharides
CC       begins on the cytosolic side of the endoplasmic reticulum membrane and
CC       finishes in its lumen. The sequential addition of sugars to dolichol
CC       pyrophosphate produces dolichol-linked oligosaccharides containing
CC       fourteen sugars, including two GlcNAcs, nine mannoses and three
CC       glucoses. Once assembled, the oligosaccharides are transferred from the
CC       lipid to nascent proteins by oligosaccharyltransferases. Functions as a
CC       protein-membrane adapter recruiting ALG13 at the cytoplasmic face of
CC       the endoplasmic reticulum, where the complex catalyzes the second step
CC       of dolichol pyrophosphate biosynthesis, transferring a beta1,4-linked
CC       N-acetylglucosamine (GlcNAc) from UDP-GlcNAc to GlcNAc-
CC       pyrophosphatedolichol (Gn-PDol) to produce N,N'-diacetylchitobiosyl
CC       diphosphodolichol. N,N'-diacetylchitobiosyl diphosphodolichol is a
CC       substrate for ALG1, the following enzyme in the biosynthetic pathway.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:16100110, ECO:0000269|PubMed:36200043}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Forms with ALG13 the active heterodimeric UDP-N-
CC       acetylglucosamine transferase complex. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:16100110,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:36200043}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Endoplasmic reticulum membrane
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:16100110}; Single-pass membrane protein
CC       {ECO:0000255}.
CC   -!- DISEASE: Myasthenic syndrome, congenital, 15 (CMS15) [MIM:616227]: A
CC       form of congenital myasthenic syndrome, a group of disorders
CC       characterized by failure of neuromuscular transmission, including pre-
CC       synaptic, synaptic, and post-synaptic disorders that are not of
CC       autoimmune origin. Clinical features are easy fatigability and muscle
CC       weakness. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:23404334, ECO:0000269|PubMed:36200043}.
CC       Note=The disease is caused by variants affecting the gene represented
CC       in this entry.
CC   -!- DISEASE: Intellectual developmental disorder with epilepsy, behavioral
CC       abnormalities, and coarse facies (IDDEBF) [MIM:619031]: An autosomal
CC       recessive neurodevelopmental disorder that manifests in early infancy
CC       with infantile spasms and developmental delay. Clinical features
CC       include severely impaired intellectual development, epilepsy, autism,
CC       hyperactivity and other behavioral problems, and coarse facies. Brain
CC       MRI findings may include delayed myelination in the deep parietal
CC       lobes. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:30221345}. Note=The disease may be caused by
CC       variants affecting the gene represented in this entry.
CC   -!- DISEASE: Myopathy, epilepsy, and progressive cerebral atrophy (MEPCA)
CC       [MIM:619036]: An autosomal recessive disorder characterized by severe,
CC       early lethal neurodegeneration, myasthenic and myopathic features,
CC       progressive cerebral atrophy with myelination defects, and intractable
CC       epilepsy. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:28733338, ECO:0000269|PubMed:36200043}.
CC       Note=The disease may be caused by variants affecting the gene
CC       represented in this entry.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the ALG14 family. {ECO:0000305}.
