CC   -!- FUNCTION: Channel-forming protein that forms part of the translocase of
CC       the outer mitochondrial membrane (TOM) complex essential for the
CC       recognition and translocation of cytosolically synthesized
CC       mitochondrial preproteins (PubMed:15644312, PubMed:31206022,
CC       PubMed:40080546). The TOM complex associates with the ion channel VDAC2
CC       and PINK1 kinase at depolarized mitochondria, this interaction
CC       stabilizes PINK1 at the outer mitochondrial membrane and triggers
CC       downstream mitophagy by the recruitment of the E3 ubiquitin ligase PRKN
CC       (PubMed:40080546). Plays a role in the assembly of the mitochondrial
CC       membrane respiratory chain NADH dehydrogenase (Complex I) by forming a
CC       complex with BCAP31 and mediating the translocation of Complex I
CC       components from the cytosol to the mitochondria (PubMed:31206022).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15644312, ECO:0000269|PubMed:31206022,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:40080546}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Part of the translocase of the outer mitochondrial membrane
CC       (TOM) complex consisting of at least TOMM5, TOMM6, TOMM7, TOMM20,
CC       TOMM22 and TOMM40; within the complex there are two copies of TOMM40
CC       and they form the channel for the import of unfolded precursor protein
CC       (PubMed:18331822, PubMed:40080546). TOMM70 may also be found in the TOM
CC       complex (PubMed:18331822). The TOM complex interacts with the VDAC2
CC       homodimer (PubMed:40080546). Upon mitochondrial depolarization, the
CC       TOM-VDAC assembly interacts with PINK1; the interaction stabilizes
CC       PINK1 at the outer mitochondrial membrane and triggers downstream
CC       mitophagy (PubMed:40080546). Interacts with mitochondrial targeting
CC       sequences (PubMed:12198123, PubMed:15644312, PubMed:18331822).
CC       Interacts with TIMM29; linking the TIM22 complex to the TOM complex
CC       (PubMed:27554484). Forms a complex with BCAP31 (via C-terminus) which
CC       mediates the translocation of components of the mitochondrial membrane
CC       respiratory chain NADH dehydrogenase (Complex I) from the cytosol to
CC       the mitochondria (PubMed:31206022). Interacts (via N-terminus) with
CC       CYP1A1 (via mitochondrial targeting signal); this interaction is
CC       required for CYP1A1 translocation across the mitochondrial outer
CC       membrane (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q75Q40,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:12198123, ECO:0000269|PubMed:15644312,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:18331822, ECO:0000269|PubMed:27554484,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:31206022, ECO:0000269|PubMed:40080546}.

CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15644312, ECO:0000269|PubMed:31206022,

CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:40080546}. Note=Associates with the mitochondria-
