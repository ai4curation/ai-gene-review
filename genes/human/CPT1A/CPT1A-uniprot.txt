CC   -!- FUNCTION: Catalyzes the transfer of the acyl group of long-chain fatty
CC       acid-CoA conjugates onto carnitine, an essential step for the
CC       mitochondrial uptake of long-chain fatty acids and their subsequent
CC       beta-oxidation in the mitochondrion (PubMed:11350182, PubMed:14517221,
CC       PubMed:16651524, PubMed:9691089). Also possesses a lysine
CC       succinyltransferase activity that can regulate enzymatic activity of
CC       substrate proteins such as ENO1 and metabolism independent of its
CC       classical carnitine O-palmitoyltransferase activity (PubMed:29425493).
CC       Plays an important role in hepatic triglyceride metabolism (By
CC       similarity). Also plays a role in inducible regulatory T-cell (iTreg)
CC       differentiation once activated by butyryl-CoA that antagonizes malonyl-
CC       CoA-mediated CPT1A repression (By similarity). Sustains the IFN-I
CC       response by recruiting ZDHCC4 to palmitoylate MAVS at the mitochondria
CC       leading to MAVS stabilization and activation (PubMed:38016475).
CC       Promotes ROS-induced oxidative stress in liver injury via modulation of
CC       NFE2L2 and NLRP3-mediated signaling pathways (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P32198, ECO:0000269|PubMed:11350182,
