CC   -!- FUNCTION: Substrate-recognition component of the Cul7-RING(FBXW8)
CC       ubiquitin ligase complex, which mediates the ubiquitination and
CC       subsequent proteasomal degradation of target proteins (PubMed:17205132,
CC       PubMed:18498745, PubMed:21572988, PubMed:24362026, PubMed:35982156).
CC       The Cul7-RING(FBXW8) complex mediates ubiquitination and consequent
CC       degradation of GORASP1, acting as a component of the ubiquitin ligase
CC       pathway that regulates Golgi morphogenesis and dendrite patterning in
CC       brain (PubMed:21572988). Mediates ubiquitination and degradation of
CC       IRS1 in a mTOR-dependent manner: the Cul7-RING(FBXW8) complex
CC       recognizes and binds IRS1 previously phosphorylated by S6 kinase
CC       (RPS6KB1 or RPS6KB2) (PubMed:18498745). The Cul7-RING(FBXW8) complex
CC       also mediates ubiquitination of MAP4K1/HPK1: recognizes and binds
CC       autophosphorylated MAP4K1/HPK1, leading to its degradation, thereby
CC       affecting cell proliferation and differentiation (PubMed:24362026). The
CC       Cul7-RING(FBXW8) complex also mediates ubiquitination of phosphorylated
CC       cyclin-D1 (CCND1) (PubMed:17205132). The Cul7-RING(FBXW8) complex is
CC       however not a major regulator of CCND1 stability during the G1/S
CC       transition (By similarity). Associated component of the 3M complex,
CC       suggesting that it mediates some of 3M complex functions
CC       (PubMed:24793695). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8BIA4,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:17205132, ECO:0000269|PubMed:18498745,

CC       (PubMed:12904573). Interacts with OBSL1, CUL1, CUL2, CCT6B, PFDN5,
