CC   -!- FUNCTION: Mediates sodium- and chloride-dependent transport of taurine,
CC       a key amino acid (PubMed:31345061, PubMed:31903486, PubMed:39837997,
CC       PubMed:40108165, PubMed:40108449, PubMed:40615403, PubMed:40789850,
CC       PubMed:41269860, PubMed:41652173, PubMed:8010975, PubMed:8382624,
CC       PubMed:8654117). Taurine acts as an anti-aging agent by reducing
CC       cellular senescence and combating oxidative stress: levels decline with
CC       age and supplementation has been shown to extend lifespan (By
CC       similarity). In addition to mediate extracellular taurine uptake, also
CC       able to mediate transport of taurine into mitochondria, sustaining
CC       mitochondrial translation (PubMed:41652173). Can also mediate transport
CC       of beta-alanine, hypotaurine and gamma-aminobutyric acid (GABA)
CC       (PubMed:39837997, PubMed:40108165, PubMed:40789850, PubMed:41269860,
CC       PubMed:8010975). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O35316,
