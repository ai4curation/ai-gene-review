# ST6GAL1 (human, P15907) review notes

## Identity and core enzymology

- ST6GAL1 = Beta-galactoside alpha-2,6-sialyltransferase 1 (ST6Gal-I), EC 2.4.3.1, CAZy GT29
  (glycosyltransferase family 29). UniProt P15907, 406 aa, single-pass type II membrane protein
  (cytoplasmic 1-9, TM 10-26, lumenal catalytic 27-406). [UniProt P15907]
- Catalytic reaction (UniProt/Rhea RHEA:52104): "a beta-D-galactoside + CMP-N-acetyl-beta-neuraminate
  = an N-acetyl-alpha-neuraminyl-(2->6)-beta-D-galactosyl derivative + CMP + H(+)". It transfers
  Neu5Ac from CMP-Neu5Ac in alpha-2,6 linkage onto terminal galactose of complex N-glycans, forming
  the Sia-alpha-2,6-Gal epitope. UniProt lists many enzyme-specific RHEA reactions for biantennary
  complex N-glycans. [UniProt P15907]
- Kinetics: KM = 530 uM for CMP-NeuAc; inhibited by CTP. Crystal structure (4JS1/4JS2; PDB 6QVS/6QVT
  of catalytic domain) shows GT-A variant fold, SN2 inversion mechanism, substrate-assisted
  charge-relay catalysis. [PMID:23999306 abstract: "ST6Gal-I adopts a variant of the canonical
  glycosyltransferase A fold"; "support an SN2 mechanism with inversion of configuration"]
- KM/activity measured by phosphatase-coupled assay. [PMID:21081508 "we measured the kinetic
  parameters of human sialyltransferase ST6GAL1"]
- Activity confirmed by multiple groups making the alpha-2,6-sialyl N-glycan epitope, including
  IgG Fc remodeling. [PMID:25733881, PMID:36280670, PMID:37632720 — all EXP/IDA GO:0003835 in GOA]

## Localization

- Golgi apparatus, Golgi stack membrane; membrane-bound form in trans cisternae of Golgi; colocalizes
  with MGAT3 and B4GALT1 in Golgi stacks. Also Secreted (soluble form in body fluids, by similarity to
  rat P13721). [UniProt P15907 SUBCELLULAR LOCATION]
- IDA Golgi membrane localization (live-cell). [PMID:20378551]

## Subunit / dimerization

- Monomer and homodimer. ST6Gal-I forms disulfide-mediated + non-covalent homodimers in live Golgi
  membranes (BiFC). >90% of molecules appear homodimeric. Also forms a trans-Golgi heterodimer with
  B4GALT1 (GalT). C24G TM-cysteine mutation abolishes disulfide dimer but not BiFC homodimerization.
  [PMID:20378551 "over 90% of the SiaT molecules appear to be homodimeric in the Golgi membranes of
  live cells"; "the two trans-Golgi enzymes (GalT and SiaT) ... assemble into Golgi-localized
  heterodimers"] — supports GO:0042803 protein homodimerization activity (IDA).

## CD75 caution

- ST6GAL1 has historically been mis-equated with CD75/CDw75; CD75 is actually a carbohydrate epitope
  (Sia-alpha-2,6-Gal product) generated by ST6GAL1, not the protein itself. Many commercial "ST6GAL1"
  antibodies based on this confusion do not recognize ST6GAL1. [UniProt CAUTION; PMID:35789385
  "incorrect use of CD75 as a synonym for ST6GAL1"; PMID:1547499; PMID:1730763]

## Biology (downstream / context-specific)

- Major enzyme generating alpha-2,6-sialylated N-glycans on serum and cell-surface glycoproteins.
  Soluble form (cleaved by BACE1) is abundant in serum, secreted by liver (acute phase) and platelets,
  mediating "extrinsic" sialylation. [deep-research falcon; UniProt notes Secreted form]
- Sialylates IgG Fc N297 N-glycan, modulating anti-inflammatory IgG effector function. [UniProt
  FUNCTION; PMID:25733881; PMID:27872474]
- B-cell biology: generates CDw75/HB-6/CD76 differentiation antigens; Sia-alpha-2,6-Gal is the ligand
  for CD22/Siglec-2 (B-cell masking/regulation). [PMID:1730763 via UniProt]
- Cancer (EGFR, integrin beta1, TNFR1, Fas, etc.), inflammation (TNFR1/TLR4 sialylation), monocyte/
  macrophage development (extrinsic CSF1R sialylation) — all downstream consequences of the same
  catalytic activity acting on different substrate glycoproteins; not separable core MF.

## GOA annotation assessment summary (35 annotations)

Core molecular function: GO:0003835 beta-galactoside alpha-2,6-sialyltransferase activity. Many
duplicate rows (IBA, IEA, 3x TAS-Reactome, 3x EXP, 1 IDA) — all ACCEPT/KEEP; the EXP/IDA-backed ones
are the strongest. GO:0008373 sialyltransferase activity (parent) is correct but over-general -> MODIFY
to the specific GO:0003835.

Process: GO:0097503 sialylation and GO:0006487 protein N-linked glycosylation are accurate and
specific (alpha-2,6-sialylation is the terminal capping step of complex N-glycan biosynthesis) ->
ACCEPT. GO:0009101 glycoprotein biosynthetic process is a vaguer parent -> KEEP_AS_NON_CORE.
GO:0006054 N-acetylneuraminate metabolic process (IDA, PMID:23999306) -> ACCEPT as a correct, slightly
broad sibling describing sialic-acid utilization.

GO:0016266 protein O-linked glycosylation via N-acetylgalactosamine (TAS Reactome sTn / O-glycan
termination): ST6GAL1 is primarily an N-glycan enzyme but Reactome documents it adding sialic acid to
Tn antigens forming sTn (O-GalNAc) and terminating Core1/2 O-glycans. Keep but as non-core (minor /
context-specific activity relative to the dominant N-glycan role).

GO:0019082 viral protein processing (2x TAS Reactome, SARS-CoV-2 3a/spike sialylation): this is a
generic host-glycosylation step the Reactome curators slotted into viral life-cycle pathways. The MF
(sialylation) is real but "viral protein processing" mis-frames a housekeeping glycosylation step as a
dedicated viral function -> MARK_AS_OVER_ANNOTATED.

Locations: GO:0005794 Golgi apparatus (IBA), GO:0000139 Golgi membrane (IDA PMID:20378551 + 5x TAS),
GO:0032580 Golgi cisterna membrane (IEA) -> ACCEPT (core). GO:0005576 extracellular region (IEA + ISS
from rat ortholog P13721) -> KEEP_AS_NON_CORE (the soluble secreted form is real but its serum/extrinsic
activity is secondary to the Golgi-resident core function).

GO:0042803 protein homodimerization activity (IDA, PMID:20378551) -> ACCEPT (well supported by BiFC;
biologically real, organizes the enzyme in the Golgi). Not the core catalytic MF but a genuine MF.

GO:0005515 protein binding (IPI, PMID:16237761): from a yeast two-hybrid screen for hepatocyte proteins
binding HCV F protein; "1 colony was sialyltransferase" among 36 colonies. Generic, uninformative
protein-binding with weak Y2H evidence and no biological follow-up for ST6GAL1. Per curation guideline
to avoid generic "protein binding" -> REMOVE.
