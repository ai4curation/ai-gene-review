Gene Description

NDUFAF3 (also known as C3orf60) is a nucleus-encoded, mitochondrially imported assembly factor for mitochondrial respiratory chain complex I (NADH:ubiquinone oxidoreductase). It is not a structural subunit of the mature holoenzyme and has no catalytic activity. NDUFAF3 associates with the mitochondrial inner membrane and acts early in complex I biogenesis, tightly interacting with its partner factor NDUFAF4 and with the iron-sulfur (IP) core subunits NDUFS2 and NDUFS3 to help nucleate and stabilize an early membrane-arm assembly intermediate. Like other complex I assembly factors, it associates transiently with assembly intermediates and dissociates from the fully assembled complex. Biallelic loss-of-function variants in NDUFAF3 cause isolated mitochondrial complex I deficiency (nuclear type 18), with phenotypes ranging from fatal neonatal mitochondrial disease to Leigh syndrome.

Core Functions

NDUFAF3 is an assembly factor required for biogenesis of mitochondrial respiratory chain complex I. Acting at the mitochondrial inner membrane, it tightly associates with its partner factor NDUFAF4 and with the iron-sulfur core subunits NDUFS2 and NDUFS3 to nucleate and stabilize an early membrane-arm assembly intermediate. It associates transiently with assembly intermediates and is not a subunit of the mature holoenzyme; loss of function causes isolated complex I deficiency.

NDUFAF3 (C3orf60) — review notes

UniProt: Q9BU61 (NDUF3_HUMAN). HGNC:29918. Gene also known as C3orf60.
184 aa; belongs to the NDUFAF3 family. Two isoforms (a = Q9BU61-1 displayed;
b = Q9BU61-2, missing residues 1-57, VSP_041086).

Core biology

NDUFAF3 is a nucleus-encoded, mitochondrially imported assembly factor for
mitochondrial respiratory chain complex I (NADH:ubiquinone oxidoreductase). It is
NOT a structural subunit of the mature holoenzyme and has NO catalytic activity.

Disease

Mitochondrial complex I deficiency, nuclear type 18 (MC1DN18, MIM:618240),
autosomal recessive. Phenotypes from fatal neonatal mitochondrial disease
(PMID:19463981) to Leigh syndrome (PMID:27986404, variant Val-165 / A165V).
[file:human/NDUFAF3/NDUFAF3-uniprot.txt]

    NDUFAF3 is an assembly factor required for biogenesis of mitochondrial
