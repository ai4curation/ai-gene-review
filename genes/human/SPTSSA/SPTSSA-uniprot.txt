CC   -!- FUNCTION: Component of the serine palmitoyltransferase multisubunit
CC       enzyme (SPT) that catalyzes the initial and rate-limiting step in
CC       sphingolipid biosynthesis by condensing L-serine and activated acyl-CoA
CC       (most commonly palmitoyl-CoA) to form long-chain bases
CC       (PubMed:19416851). The SPT complex is composed of SPTLC1, SPTLC2 or
CC       SPTLC3 and SPTSSA or SPTSSB. Within this complex, the heterodimer
CC       consisting of SPTLC1 and SPTLC2/SPTLC3 forms the catalytic core
CC       (PubMed:19416851). Within the SPT complex, SPTSSA stimulates the
CC       catalytic activity and plays a role in substrate specificity, which
CC       depends upon the overall complex composition (PubMed:19416851,
CC       PubMed:33558761). The SPTLC1-SPTLC2-SPTSSA complex shows a strong
CC       preference for C16-CoA substrate, while the SPTLC1-SPTLC3-SPTSSA
CC       isozyme uses both C14-CoA and C16-CoA as substrates, with a slight
CC       preference for C14-CoA (PubMed:19416851). Independently of its action
CC       as a SPT component, may be involved in MBOAT7 localization to
CC       mitochondria-associated membranes, a membrane bridge between the
CC       endoplasmic reticulum and mitochondria, may hence affect MBOAT7-
CC       catalyzed incorporation of arachidonic acid into phosphatidylinositol
CC       (PubMed:23510452). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:19416851,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:23510452, ECO:0000269|PubMed:33558761}.
CC   -!- PATHWAY: Lipid metabolism; sphingolipid metabolism.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Component of the serine palmitoyltransferase (SPT) complex,
CC       which is composed of SPTLC1, SPTLC2 or SPTLC3 and SPTSSA or SPTSSB
CC       (PubMed:19416851, PubMed:33558761, PubMed:33558762, PubMed:37308477).
CC       The heterodimer consisting of SPTLC1 and SPTLC2/SPTLC3 forms the
CC       catalytic core of the enzyme, while SPTSSA or SPTSSB subunits determine
CC       substrate specificity (PubMed:33558762, PubMed:37308477). SPT also
CC       interacts with ORMDL proteins, especially ORMDL3, which negatively
CC       regulate SPT activity in the presence of ceramides (PubMed:33558762,
CC       PubMed:37308477). Interacts with MBOAT7; the interaction plays a role
CC       in MBOAT7 localization to mitochondria-associated membranes
CC       (PubMed:23510452). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:19416851,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:23510452, ECO:0000269|PubMed:33558761,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:33558762, ECO:0000269|PubMed:37308477}.

CC   -!- DISEASE: Spastic paraplegia 90A, autosomal dominant (SPG90A)
CC       [MIM:620416]: A form of spastic paraplegia, a neurodegenerative
CC       disorder characterized by a slow, gradual, progressive weakness and
CC       spasticity of the lower limbs. Rate of progression and the severity of
CC       symptoms are quite variable. Initial symptoms may include difficulty
CC       with balance, weakness and stiffness in the legs, muscle spasms, and
CC       dragging the toes when walking. In some forms of the disorder, bladder
CC       symptoms (such as incontinence) may appear, or weakness and stiffness
CC       may spread to other parts of the body. SPG90A affected individuals have
CC       motor impairment and progressive lower extremity spasticity as well as
CC       neurologic findings, cognitive impairment, and hearing loss.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:36718090}. Note=The disease may be caused by
CC       variants affecting the gene represented in this entry.
CC   -!- DISEASE: Spastic paraplegia 90B, autosomal recessive (SPG90B)

CC       disorder characterized by a slow, gradual, progressive weakness and
CC       spasticity of the lower limbs. Rate of progression and the severity of
CC       symptoms are quite variable. Initial symptoms may include difficulty
CC       with balance, weakness and stiffness in the legs, muscle spasms, and
CC       dragging the toes when walking. In some forms of the disorder, bladder
CC       symptoms (such as incontinence) may appear, or weakness and stiffness
CC       may spread to other parts of the body. SPG90B is an autosomal recessive
CC       form characterized by motor impairment and progressive lower extremity
CC       spasticity as well as neurologic findings, cognitive impairment, and
CC       hearing loss. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:36718090}. Note=The disease may be
