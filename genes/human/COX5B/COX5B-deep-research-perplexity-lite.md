## Output

The **COX5B gene** (UniProt: P10606) encodes the **cytochrome c oxidase subunit 5B**, a nuclear-encoded component of Complex IV in the mitochondrial electron transport chain. This subunit is essential for mitochondrial oxidative phosphorylation, with roles in energy production, cellular metabolism, and potentially disease processes.

### 1. Key Concepts and Definitions

- **Molecular Function:**  
  COX5B is a structural component of **cytochrome c oxidase (Complex IV)**, the terminal enzyme of the mitochondrial respiratory chain. It facilitates the transfer of electrons from cytochrome c to molecular oxygen, contributing to the generation of a proton gradient used for ATP synthesis[1][3][5][9][14][15].

- **Biological Processes:**  
  - **Oxidative phosphorylation:** Drives ATP production by coupling electron transfer to proton pumping[1][3][5][9][14][15].
  - **Mitochondrial electron transport:** Specifically, the transfer from cytochrome c to oxygen[1][3][5][9][14][15].
  - **Regulation and assembly:** While the core catalytic activity is performed by mitochondrially encoded subunits, COX5B (nuclear-encoded) is thought to play a regulatory or assembly role in the complex[1][3][14][15].

- **Cellular Localization:**  
  - **Mitochondrial inner membrane:** COX5B is localized to the inner membrane of mitochondria, where it is integrated as part of Complex IV[1][2][4][5][8][9][10][14].

### 4. Disease Associations
