Gene Description

RAS p21 protein activator 3, bifunctional GTPase-activating protein for both RAS and RAP1 small GTPases. Member of GAP1 family characterized by two N-terminal C2 domains (Ca2+ and phospholipid binding), central pleckstrin homology (PH) domain, and C-terminal RasGAP catalytic domain. Originally identified as first known receptor for inositol 1,3,4,5-tetrakisphosphate (IP4). Unique among RasGAPs for dual substrate specificity: stimulates GTP hydrolysis on both Ras family GTPases (H-Ras, N-Ras, K-Ras, R-Ras) and Rap1, using arginine-finger mechanism for both substrates. PH domain binds multiple lipids - high affinity for PIP3, PI(4,5)P2, and soluble IP4. Critically, PIP3 binding INHIBITS RASA3's GAP activity, providing negative feedback link between PI3K and Ras/Rap signaling - when PI3K generates PIP3, RASA3 is inactivated, relieving restraint on Ras/Rap1. Cytoplasmic protein with regulated membrane association via C2 and PH domains. Essential non-redundant role in vertebrate hematopoiesis: mouse scat mutant (G125V, disrupts membrane targeting) exhibits severe anemia and thrombocytopenia with ~94% lethality by 30 days due to bone marrow failure from unchecked Ras activity in erythroid cells. In platelets, RASA3 is major Rap1-GAP counterbalancing CalDAG-GEFI activator - loss causes spontaneous platelet activation, aberrant integrin Œ±IIbŒ≤3 activation, and consumptive thrombocytopenia. In T lymphocytes, acts as gatekeeper of activation and adhesion: restrains Rap1-driven LFA-1 integrin activation until proper TCR/costimulation occurs; RASA3-deficient T cells show increased Rap1-GTP, constitutive integrin high-affinity state, impaired egress from lymph nodes, and dysregulated immune responses. Human biallelic RASA3 loss-of-function mutations cause Rasopathy-like syndrome with developmental abnormalities and bone marrow failure. Expression in hematopoietic cells, platelets, megakaryocytes, T cells, and various tissues. Critical negative regulator that sets activation thresholds in signaling pathways to prevent overactivation.

Negative Regulator of Ras‚ÄìMAPK Signaling

As a RasGAP, RASA3 is an important negative regulator of the Ras‚ÄìRAF‚ÄìMEK‚ÄìERK (MAPK) cascade. Regulator of Rap1 and Integrin Signaling in Platelets

One of the most prominent roles of RASA3 is in the regulation of platelet activation and blood clotting, via its action on Rap1. Gatekeeper of T Cell Activation and Adhesion

Beyond the blood production lineages, RASA3 also plays a pivotal role in the immune system, particularly in T lymphocytes. In summary, RASA3 in the immune system functions to set thresholds for cell activation and adhesion, particularly by limiting Rap1-driven integrin activation in T cells (and likely in other leukocytes such as B cells or myeloid cells that also utilize Rap1 for adhesion). This gatekeeper role is crucial to prevent inappropriate immune cell activation and to fine-tune immune responses.

description: 'RAS p21 protein activator 3, bifunctional GTPase-activating protein
  for both RAS and RAP1 small GTPases. Member of GAP1 family characterized by two
  N-terminal C2 domains (Ca2+ and phospholipid binding), central pleckstrin homology
  (PH) domain, and C-terminal RasGAP catalytic domain. Originally identified as first
  known receptor for inositol 1,3,4,5-tetrakisphosphate (IP4). Unique among RasGAPs
  for dual substrate specificity: stimulates GTP hydrolysis on both Ras family GTPases
  (H-Ras, N-Ras, K-Ras, R-Ras) and Rap1, using arginine-finger mechanism for both
  substrates. PH domain binds multiple lipids - high affinity for PIP3, PI(4,5)P2,
  and soluble IP4. Critically, PIP3 binding INHIBITS RASA3''s GAP activity, providing
  negative feedback link between PI3K and Ras/Rap signaling - when PI3K generates
  PIP3, RASA3 is inactivated, relieving restraint on Ras/Rap1. Cytoplasmic protein
  with regulated membrane association via C2 and PH domains. Essential non-redundant
  role in vertebrate hematopoiesis: mouse scat mutant (G125V, disrupts membrane targeting)
  exhibits severe anemia and thrombocytopenia with ~94% lethality by 30 days due to
  bone marrow failure from unchecked Ras activity in erythroid cells. In platelets,
  RASA3 is major Rap1-GAP counterbalancing CalDAG-GEFI activator - loss causes spontaneous
  platelet activation, aberrant integrin Œ±IIbŒ≤3 activation, and consumptive thrombocytopenia.
  In T lymphocytes, acts as gatekeeper of activation and adhesion: restrains Rap1-driven
  LFA-1 integrin activation until proper TCR/costimulation occurs; RASA3-deficient
  T cells show increased Rap1-GTP, constitutive integrin high-affinity state, impaired
  egress from lymph nodes, and dysregulated immune responses. Human biallelic RASA3
  loss-of-function mutations cause Rasopathy-like syndrome with developmental abnormalities
  and bone marrow failure. Expression in hematopoietic cells, platelets, megakaryocytes,
  T cells, and various tissues. Critical negative regulator that sets activation thresholds
  in signaling pathways to prevent overactivation.'
