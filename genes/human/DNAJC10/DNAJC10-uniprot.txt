CC   -!- FUNCTION: Endoplasmic reticulum disulfide reductase that collaborates
CC       directly with the chaperone BIP/HSPA5 (GRP78) to maintain protein
CC       quality control by facilitating either the correct folding or the
CC       targeted degradation of misfolded proteins (PubMed:12411443,
CC       PubMed:23769672, PubMed:37739037). Essential for efficient maturation
CC       of newly synthesized polypeptides in the endoplasmic reticulum, binds
CC       to substrate proteins and specifically catalyzes the reduction and
CC       removal of improper (non-native) disulfide bonds during the folding
CC       process (PubMed:23769672, PubMed:37739037). In endoplasmic reticulum-
CC       associated degradation (ERAD), DNAJC10 reduces incorrect disulfide
CC       bonds specifically in misfolded glycoproteins that have been recognized
CC       by EDEM1, a key component of the ERAD pathway, thereby enabling their
CC       retrotranslocation and degradation (PubMed:18400946). Promotes
CC       apoptotic signaling pathway in response to endoplasmic reticulum stress
CC       (PubMed:19122239). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12411443,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:18400946, ECO:0000269|PubMed:19122239,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:23769672, ECO:0000269|PubMed:37739037}.
