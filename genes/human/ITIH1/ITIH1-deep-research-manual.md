# Manual literature synthesis for human ITIH1 (P19827)

Automated deep-research providers were attempted on 2026-07-18 but were unavailable (Falcon/Edison HTTP 402; Perplexity-lite HTTP 401). This manual synthesis is based on the reviewed UniProtKB record and cached primary publications.

## Biological synthesis

ITIH1 encodes the secreted heavy chain HC1 of plasma inter-alpha-inhibitor. The precursor is processed to a mature heavy chain whose C-terminal aspartate is ester-linked to the chondroitin-sulfate chain of bikunin. Together with ITIH2/HC2 and bikunin, HC1 forms the inter-alpha-inhibitor complex. Biochemical chain separation shows that HC1 and HC2 are noninhibitory; the bikunin light chain supplies the complex's trypsin-inhibitor activity [PMID:2476436, "Inter-alpha-trypsin inhibitor contains noninhibitory heavy chains of 65,000 and 70,000 Da"].

HC1's defining biology is its participation in the hyaluronan-rich extracellular matrix. Serum-derived HA-associated proteins include human HC1, and mapping experiments implicate the C-terminal portion of the heavy chain in HA association [PMID:7504674, "Peptides from X and Y showed a high degree of sequence similarity to the two heavy chains, HC2 and HC1"]. TSG-6-dependent transesterification can move heavy chains from bikunin chondroitin sulfate to HA, and HA-linked heavy chains can subsequently serve as donors in further exchange reactions [PMID:20463016, "HCs transferred to HA may function as HC donors in subsequent transfer reactions"]. HC1 is therefore best understood as a transferable extracellular heavy-chain component that decorates and helps organize HA matrices, not as a protease inhibitor or transferase enzyme.

The HC1 VWFA domain also supplies a metal-dependent recognition surface for TSG-6. Recombinant human HC1 contains a magnesium-bearing MIDAS motif, and mutation of the coordinating Asp298 eliminates metal binding and TSG-6 CUB-domain interaction [PMID:26468290, "mutation of the chelating Asp-298 residue abolishes metal ion binding"]. This direct result supersedes the older cDNA-paper suggestion of merely potential calcium-binding sites.

## Core conclusion

The core function of ITIH1 is to provide the noninhibitory HC1 component of plasma inter-alpha-inhibitor and, after TSG-6-mediated transfer, an HA-associated extracellular-matrix component. Directly supported GO concepts are hyaluronic acid binding, hyaluronan metabolic process, extracellular matrix, extracellular space, and magnesium ion binding. Serine-protease inhibition belongs to bikunin and should not be assigned to ITIH1.

## Evidence limitations

The older HC1, plasma-proteome, blood-microparticle, and covalent-crosslink papers are available only as abstracts in the local cache. The relevant abstracts explicitly establish chain composition or study scope, but they do not support finer kinetic or compartmental claims. Proteomic detection in exosome and microparticle fractions is retained as non-core because a soluble abundant plasma protein can co-purify with those preparations.
