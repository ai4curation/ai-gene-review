Gene Description

NAA40 (N-alpha-acetyltransferase 40; also NatD/Nat4/Nat11) is a highly substrate-specific N-terminal acetyltransferase that acetylates the free alpha-amino group of the N-terminal serine of histones H4 and H2A (which share an identical Ser-Gly-Arg-Gly N-terminal sequence after initiator-methionine removal), producing N-terminally acetylated H4/H2A (EC 2.3.1.257). Unlike the ribosome-associated NatA/NatB/NatC/NatE complexes, NAA40 is a monomeric enzyme with a narrow, sequence-specific selectivity restricted to these histone N-termini and recognizes the substrate independently of the bulk Nt-acetylation machinery. NAA40-mediated N-terminal acetylation of H4 influences chromatin function and crosstalk with adjacent histone marks (e.g. H4R3 methylation), and NAA40 has reported roles in transcriptional regulation, ribosomal RNA expression, cellular metabolism (including hepatic lipid metabolism), and as a negative regulator of apoptosis. NAA40 localizes mainly to the nucleus with a cytoplasmic pool.
