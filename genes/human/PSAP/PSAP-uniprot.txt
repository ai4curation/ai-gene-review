CC   -!- FUNCTION: Saposins are specific low-molecular mass non-enzymatic
CC       glycoproteins that act as activator proteins for lysosomal
CC       sphingolipid-degrading enzymes, facilitating the hydrolysis of
CC       sphingolipids by extracting lipid substrates from membranes and
CC       presenting them to their respective enzymes. They are derived from a
CC       common precursor protein, prosaposin, which is proteolytically cleaved
CC       to yield four homologous proteins (saposin A, B, C, and D), each with
CC       distinct but partially overlapping lipid and enzyme specificities.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:16905746}.
CC   -!- FUNCTION: [Prosaposin]: Behaves as a myelinotrophic and neurotrophic
CC       factor, these effects are mediated by its G protein-coupled receptors,
CC       GPR37 and GPR37L1, undergoing ligand-mediated internalization followed
CC       by ERK phosphorylation signaling. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q61207,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:10383054}.
CC   -!- FUNCTION: [Saposin-A]: acts as an essential lysosomal cofactor that
CC       activates galactocerebrosidase (GALC, EC 3.2.1.46) by facilitating
CC       enzyme access to its membrane-embedded galactosylceramide substrate,
CC       the principal lipid component of myelin. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:16905746}.
CC   -!- FUNCTION: [Saposin-B]: Stimulates the hydrolysis of galacto-cerebroside
CC       sulfate by arylsulfatase A ARSA (EC 3.1.6.8), GM1 gangliosides by beta-
CC       galactosidase (EC 3.2.1.23) and globotriaosylceramide by alpha-
CC       galactosidase A (EC 3.2.1.22). Forms a solubilizing complex with the
CC       substrates of the sphingolipid hydrolases.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:19224915}.
CC   -!- FUNCTION: [Saposin-C]: Acts as an essential lysosomal cofactor that
CC       activates acid beta-glucosylceramidases (EC 3.2.1.45) by altering lipid
CC       bilayer properties to facilitate enzyme access to its membrane-embedded
CC       glucosylceramide substrate, while also protecting glucocerebrosidase
CC       from intralysosomal proteolytic degradation.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12813057, ECO:0000269|PubMed:8262201}.
CC   -!- FUNCTION: [Saposin-D]: Acts as a lysosomal activator protein that
CC       stimulates acid ceramidase (EC 3.5.1.23) by solubilizing and presenting
CC       ceramide substrates at the lipid-water interface, facilitating their
CC       hydrolysis into sphingosine and fatty acids. It also activates acid
CC       sphingomyelinase (EC 3.1.4.12), though ceramide catabolism represents
CC       its primary physiological role. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:2845979,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:8185598, ECO:0000269|PubMed:8203897}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: [Prosaposin]: Prosaposin exists as a roughly half-half mixture
CC       of monomers and disulfide-linked dimers (PubMed:21835174). Monomeric
CC       prosaposin interacts (via C-terminus) with sortilin/SORT1, the
CC       interaction is required for targeting to lysosomes (PubMed:14657016,
CC       PubMed:22431521). Interacts with GRN; facilitates lysosomal delivery of
CC       progranulin from the extracellular space and the biosynthetic pathway
CC       (PubMed:26370502). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:14657016,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:21835174, ECO:0000269|PubMed:22431521,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:26370502}.
