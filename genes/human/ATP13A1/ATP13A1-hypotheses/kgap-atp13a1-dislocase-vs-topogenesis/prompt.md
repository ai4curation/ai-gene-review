# AIGR Gene Hypothesis Deep Research

Evaluate one focused AI Gene Review knowledge-gap hypothesis.

## Target Gene

- Organism code: human
- Taxon: Homo sapiens (NCBITaxon:9606)
- Gene symbol: ATP13A1
- UniProt accession: Q9HD20

## Seed Hypothesis

ATP13A1 is primarily an ER membrane-protein dislocase that extracts misinserted
terminal hydrophobic helices from the ER membrane, rather than a general
productive insertion, secretion, or SEC61 handoff/topogenesis factor.

## Context

- The gene review accepts ATP13A1's core molecular function as ATP-dependent
  extraction of misinserted terminal hydrophobic helices from the ER membrane.
- The unresolved boundary is whether GET3/SEC61-linked findings indicate a
  separable productive routing activity or are downstream consequences of the
  accepted dislocase reaction.
- The review asks for client-specific reporters, reconstituted assays, and
  rescue experiments to test insertion/SEC61 handoff independently of
  dislocation.

## Research Objective

Determine whether the hypothesis is supported, partially supported, unresolved,
or refuted. Focus on:

1. Direct biochemical or cell-based evidence that ATP13A1 extracts/dislocates
   terminal hydrophobic helices from ER membranes.
2. Evidence for or against ATP13A1 directly promoting productive membrane
   insertion, secretion, topogenesis, or SEC61 handoff.
3. Known client classes and selection rules that distinguish ATP13A1 from GET3,
   SEC61, EMC, and general ERAD factors.

Use primary literature and compact computational/domain checks where useful.
Avoid broad exploratory workflows; a concise evidence review plus targeted
sequence/topology/domain interpretation is sufficient.

## Required Output

Include:

- Executive judgment.
- Evidence matrix with citations, evidence type, claim tested, finding, context,
  confidence, and limitations.
- GO curation implications for membrane-protein dislocase activity, ER
  extraction of mislocalized proteins, insertion/topogenesis terms, and
  immune/developmental downstream phenotypes.
- Remaining decisive experiments.
