CC       PubMed:15280030, PubMed:17593962, PubMed:21453287, PubMed:23685396,
CC       PubMed:27927697, PubMed:30902677). Participates in the corticosteroid
CC       receptor-mediated anti-inflammatory response, as well as metabolic and
CC       homeostatic processes (PubMed:10497248, PubMed:12414862,
CC       PubMed:15152005, PubMed:21453287). Plays a role in the secretion of
CC       aqueous humor in the eye, maintaining a normotensive, intraocular
CC       environment (PubMed:11481269). Bidirectional in vitro, predominantly
CC       functions as a reductase in vivo, thereby increasing the concentration
CC       of active glucocorticoids (PubMed:10497248, PubMed:11481269,
CC       PubMed:12414862, PubMed:12460758). It has broad substrate specificity,
CC       besides glucocorticoids, it accepts other steroid and sterol substrates
CC       (PubMed:15095019, PubMed:15152005, PubMed:17593962, PubMed:21453287,

CC       17-one), among others (PubMed:17593962). Catalyzes the stereo-specific

CC       oxocholesterol), into the more polar 7-beta-hydroxycholesterol
CC       metabolite (PubMed:15095019, PubMed:15152005). 7-oxocholesterol is one
CC       of the most important oxysterols, it participates in several events
CC       such as induction of apoptosis, accumulation in atherosclerotic
CC       lesions, lipid peroxidation, and induction of foam cell formation

CC       mainly to chenodeoxycholate, and to a lesser extent to
CC       ursodeoxycholate, both in its free form and when conjugated to glycine
CC       or taurine, providing a link between glucocorticoid activation and bile
