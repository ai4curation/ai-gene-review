# OpenScientist prompt: RAB9A GEF/GAP switch-control gap

Investigate the unresolved regulatory switch machinery for human RAB9A: the specific guanine-nucleotide exchange factor(s) that activate RAB9A on late endosomes and the GTPase-activating protein(s) that inactivate it.

Focus on:

- direct evidence for any RAB9A-specific GEFs or GAPs, including in vitro nucleotide-exchange/GTPase assays, localization, knockdown/knockout rescue, and CI-MPR or endosome-to-TGN trafficking readouts;
- whether DENN-domain or TBC-domain candidates have RAB9A-specific evidence versus broad Rab-family inference;
- how known RAB9A effectors and trafficking pathways constrain plausible regulators;
- whether GO-style curation should add regulator relationships, suggested questions, or leave the GEF/GAP mechanism as an explicit residual knowledge gap.

Please include PMIDs and DOIs, flag uncached or uncertain citations, and distinguish validated RAB9A-specific regulators from candidate screens or paralog extrapolation.
