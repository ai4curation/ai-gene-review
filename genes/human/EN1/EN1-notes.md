# EN1 review notes

## Scope and source availability

- Review initiated from the 30-line human GOA snapshot and reviewed UniProtKB entry Q05925. The configured deep-research providers were quota-blocked for this project, so no provider output was retried or fabricated; this manual journal records the evidence used instead.
- UniProtKB describes a 392-aa engrailed-family homeobox protein, places the homeobox DNA-binding region at residues 303-362, and reports nuclear localization [file:human/EN1/EN1-uniprot.txt, "Homeobox protein engrailed-1"; "DNA_BIND        303..362"; "SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Nucleus"].

## Molecular activity

- Human EN1 directly binds a site in the UTRN promoter in vitro and associates with that promoter in cells. EN1 knockdown increases UTRN mRNA, establishing EN1 as a sequence-specific transcriptional repressor in this context [PMID:21672318, "The human UTRN promoter, which contains two putative binding sites for homeobox protein engrailed-1 (EN1), was analysed. It was found that EN1 binding site 2 in the UTRN gene promoter directly interacted with transcription factor EN1 in vitro."] [PMID:21672318, "Small interfering RNA was used to inhibit EN1 gene expression. Higher utrophin mRNA levels were observed in EN1-inhibited cells compared with controls."]
- A large methylation-sensitive SELEX study assayed DNA-binding specificity across 542 human transcription factors and underlies the direct GO annotation of EN1 to sequence-specific double-stranded DNA binding [PMID:28473536, "By analysis of 542 human TFs with methylation-sensitive SELEX (systematic evolution of ligands by exponential enrichment), we found that there are also many TFs that prefer CpG-methylated sequences."] The cached text does not enumerate EN1-specific motifs, so no motif-level claim is made here.
- EN1 is found in nuclei of differentiated human dopaminergic-neuron cultures [PMID:24399192, "EN1 was expressed in all nuclei (inset) and also in perinuclear areas."].

## Developmental and neuronal roles

- Mouse En1 loss-of-function and lineage tracing show that En1 maintains the midbrain-hindbrain isthmic organizer and represses Otx2, Wnt1, and canonical Wnt signaling in rostral hindbrain [PMID:26879466, "Our work suggests a newly-discovered role for En1: the repression of Otx2, Wnt1 and canonical Wnt-signaling in R1. Overall, our results suggest that En1 is essential for proper IsO maintenance and function."]. This supports midbrain/brain patterning as a developmental consequence of its transcriptional activity.
- Conditional En1/En2 mutants establish late, region-specific control of cerebellar foliation after the major cortical output cells have been specified [PMID:20081196, "Furthermore, En1/En2 continue to regulate foliation after embryonic day 14, at which time Fgf8 isthmic organizer activity is complete and the major output cells of the cerebellar cortex have been specified."] [PMID:20081196, "Thus, the En genes represent a new class of genes that are fundamental to patterning cerebellum foliation throughout the mediolateral axis and that act late in development."].
- En1 expression is developmentally dynamic and occurs in several mouse cerebellar neuronal populations, including Purkinje and deep cerebellar nuclear neurons [PMID:21431469, "DCN neurons and Purkinje cells continue to express En1 at P21, whereas En2 is mainly expressed in granule cells; both En1 and En2 continue to be expressed in mature cerebellar interneurons."]. This paper explicitly cautions that midbrain En1/En2 are associated more clearly with survival and axon guidance than initial dopaminergic specification [PMID:21431469, "Interestingly, En1 and En2 expression in midbrain dopaminergic neurons appears not to be required for their specification, but rather for their continuous survival and axon path finding and targeting"].
- Adult En1-heterozygous mice show progressive loss of substantia nigra and ventral tegmental dopaminergic neurons, supporting a neuronal-survival role and explaining several distal behavioral annotations [PMID:17267560, "These loss and gain of function experiments firmly establish that En1/2 is a true survival factor for DA neurons in vivo."].
- Recent conditional mouse work attributes excitatory cerebellar-neuron differentiation and survival jointly to En1/En2, not uniquely to EN1 [PMID:38912572, "We further reveal a similar function for EN1/2 in mediating TBR2 expression, neuron differentiation and survival in the other excitatory neurons (granule and unipolar brush cells)."].
- Mouse En1 is required for ventral limb patterning and apical ectodermal ridge formation [PMID:8684466, "Loss of Engrailed-1 function in mice results in dorsal transformations of ventral paw structures, and in subtle alterations along the proximal-distal limb axis."] Human upstream deletions that extinguish limb EN1 expression cause a matching congenital limb malformation [PMID:33568816, "Re-engineering of the human deletions in mice resulted in a complete loss of En1 expression in the limb and a double dorsal-limb phenotype that recapitulates the human disease phenotype."].

## Annotation-specific caveats

- PMID:8094370 is a human chromosome-mapping paper. Its abstract supports EN1 localization to 2q13-q21, not an experimental role in anatomical structure morphogenesis; the associated TAS annotation is therefore miscited.
- PMID:24431302 is abstract-only in the cache. Its abstract reviews Wnt signaling in midbrain dopaminergic development but does not state an EN1-specific result. Because the cited full-text passage cannot be checked, the precise `dopaminergic neuron differentiation` annotation remains undecided rather than being rejected.
- Behavioral terms transferred from mouse or rat orthologs are biologically coherent downstream phenotypes of neural-development/survival defects, but they are not EN1's core molecular activity. They are retained as non-core.
- EN1 and EN2 overlap extensively in mouse genetic studies. Statements supported by compound mutants are recorded as joint En1/En2 roles and are not treated as proof that EN1 alone is sufficient.
