Gene Description

GOT1 encodes the cytosolic (soluble) aspartate aminotransferase (cAST / cAspAT / AST / GOT / transaminase A), a homodimeric, pyridoxal-5'-phosphate (PLP)-dependent class-I aminotransferase. Its principal reaction is the reversible transfer of the amino group between L-aspartate and 2-oxoglutarate to form oxaloacetate and L-glutamate (EC 2.6.1.1). The enzyme is broadly expressed (highest in heart and skeletal muscle, liver and erythrocytes) and is the cytosolic transaminase arm of the malate-aspartate shuttle, which transfers reducing equivalents (NADH) from the cytosol into mitochondria. Through interconversion of aspartate, glutamate, oxaloacetate and 2-oxoglutarate it links amino-acid metabolism to the tricarboxylic acid cycle, gluconeogenesis and adipocyte glyceroneogenesis, and helps regulate levels of glutamate, the major excitatory neurotransmitter. As a broad-specificity aminotransferase it also transaminates L-cysteine (cysteine aminotransferase activity, generating 3-mercaptopyruvate, a precursor of hydrogen sulfide), cysteine sulfinate and (2S)-2-aminobutanoate. PLP is covalently bound as a Schiff base to Lys-259. The paralogous mitochondrial isozyme is encoded by a separate gene, GOT2. Serum AST activity, largely of cytosolic origin, is a widely used clinical marker of liver, heart and muscle injury; a rare in-frame p.Asn389del variant that abolishes catalytic activity underlies low serum AST (ASTQTL1).

Core Functions

Cytosolic aspartate aminotransferase activity: reversible, PLP-dependent transfer of the amino group between L-aspartate and 2-oxoglutarate to form oxaloacetate and L-glutamate (EC 2.6.1.1), the defining reaction of GOT1. This interconverts key amino acids and TCA-cycle intermediates and provides the cytosolic transaminase step of the malate-aspartate shuttle.

Identity and core enzymology

GOT1 is the cytosolic (soluble) aspartate aminotransferase (cAST / cAspAT / AST /
GOT / transaminase A), a pyridoxal-5'-phosphate (PLP)-dependent, class-I
aminotransferase. The mitochondrial isozyme is a separate gene, GOT2 (P00505).

    {ECO:0000269|PubMed:21900944}; EC=2.6.1.3 {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P13221}"
    [file:human/GOT1/GOT1-uniprot.txt].
  • Primary reaction (EC 2.6.1.1): "Reaction=L-aspartate + 2-oxoglutarate = oxaloacetate
  • L-glutamate; … EC=2.6.1.1; Evidence={ECO:0000269|PubMed:21900944}"
    [file:human/GOT1/GOT1-uniprot.txt]. This is the reversible transamination that
    defines the enzyme; GO:0004069 (L-aspartate:2-oxoglutarate transaminase activity).

Secondary / promiscuous catalytic activities (same active site, broad specificity)

  • L-cysteine transaminase (cysteine aminotransferase, cCAT; EC 2.6.1.3):
    AltName "Cysteine aminotransferase, cytoplasmic"; reaction "L-cysteine +
    2-oxoglutarate = 2-oxo-3-sulfanylpropanoate (3-mercaptopyruvate) + L-glutamate; …
    EC=2.6.1.3" [file:human/GOT1/GOT1-uniprot.txt]. Physiologically links to H2S
    production: "Using L-cysteine as substrate, regulates levels of mercaptopyruvate,
    an important source of hydrogen sulfide" [file:human/GOT1/GOT1-uniprot.txt].
    -> GO:0047801. Human evidence is ISS (transfer from rat P13221), not direct.
  • (2S)-2-aminobutanoate:2-oxoglutarate transaminase (GO:0120554): UniProt
    "catalyzes (2S)-2-aminobutanoate … {ECO:0000269|PubMed:27827456}"
    [file:human/GOT1/GOT1-uniprot.txt]. PMID:27827456 shows AST generates 2-aminobutyric
    acid (2-AB) from 2-oxobutyric acid, a by-product of cysteine biosynthesis from
    cystathionine: "2-AB is generated by an amino group transfer reaction to 2-oxobutyric
    acid, a byproduct of cysteine biosynthesis from cystathionine" and, in vitro, "2-OBA
    was incubated with glutamic acid and AST. We found that 2-AB was produced"
    PMID:27827456. This assay uses commercial AST and inhibitor (AOA); it is not
    GOT1-isoform-specific, but supports the promiscuous activity for the aspartate
    aminotransferase reaction run in reverse.
  • Cysteine sulfinate (3-sulfino-L-alanine) transaminase: UniProt lists the
    reaction "3-sulfino-L-alanine + 2-oxoglutarate = 3-sulfinopyruvate + L-glutamate …

Physiological roles / pathways

  • UniProt FUNCTION: "Biosynthesis of L-glutamate from L-aspartate or L-cysteine
    (PubMed:21900944). Important regulator of levels of glutamate, the major excitatory
    neurotransmitter … Acts as a scavenger of glutamate in brain neuroprotection. The
    aspartate aminotransferase activity is involved in hepatic glucose synthesis during
    development and in adipocyte glyceroneogenesis." [file:human/GOT1/GOT1-uniprot.txt]
  • Malate-aspartate shuttle (MAS): GOT1 is the cytosolic transaminase arm of the
    MAS, which shuttles reducing equivalents (NADH) into mitochondria. GOA has an IMP to
    GO:0043490 from PMID:37647199 (Broeks et al., Cell Rep 2023), a MAS-component
    knockout study in HEK293 showing MAS is important for de novo serine biosynthesis
    (abstract: "We genetically disrupted each MAS component … MAS-deficient cells have
    reduced serine biosynthesis"). Full text not cached (full_text_available: false);
    GOT1/GOT2 are canonical MAS enzymes, so the annotation is biologically sound even
    though the cached abstract foregrounds MDH1/serine.
  • Reactome pathways (DR lines): Malate-aspartate shuttle (R-HSA-9856872), Aspartate
    and asparagine metabolism (R-HSA-8963693), Methionine salvage pathway
    (R-HSA-1237112) [file:human/GOT1/GOT1-uniprot.txt].

  amino group between L-aspartate and 2-oxoglutarate to form oxaloacetate and
  L-glutamate (EC 2.6.1.1). The enzyme is broadly expressed (highest in heart and
  skeletal muscle, liver and erythrocytes) and is the cytosolic transaminase arm of
  the malate-aspartate shuttle, which transfers reducing equivalents (NADH) from the
  cytosol into mitochondria. Through interconversion of aspartate, glutamate,
  oxaloacetate and 2-oxoglutarate it links amino-acid metabolism to the
  tricarboxylic acid cycle, gluconeogenesis and adipocyte glyceroneogenesis, and
  helps regulate levels of glutamate, the major excitatory neurotransmitter. As a
  broad-specificity aminotransferase it also transaminates L-cysteine (cysteine
  aminotransferase activity, generating 3-mercaptopyruvate, a precursor of hydrogen
  sulfide), cysteine sulfinate and (2S)-2-aminobutanoate. PLP is covalently bound as
  a Schiff base to Lys-259. The paralogous mitochondrial isozyme is encoded by a
  separate gene, GOT2. Serum AST activity, largely of cytosolic origin, is a widely
  used clinical marker of liver, heart and muscle injury; a rare in-frame p.Asn389del
  variant that abolishes catalytic activity underlies low serum AST (ASTQTL1).
