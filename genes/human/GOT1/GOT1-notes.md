# GOT1 (human) — curation notes

UniProt: P17174 (AATC_HUMAN). Gene: GOT1 (HGNC:4432). 413 aa. Reviewed.

## Identity and core enzymology

GOT1 is the **cytosolic (soluble) aspartate aminotransferase (cAST / cAspAT / AST /
GOT / transaminase A)**, a pyridoxal-5'-phosphate (PLP)-dependent, class-I
aminotransferase. The mitochondrial isozyme is a separate gene, GOT2 (P00505).

- RecName / EC: "Aspartate aminotransferase, cytoplasmic … EC=2.6.1.1
  {ECO:0000269|PubMed:21900944}; EC=2.6.1.3 {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P13221}"
  [file:human/GOT1/GOT1-uniprot.txt].
- Primary reaction (EC 2.6.1.1): "Reaction=L-aspartate + 2-oxoglutarate = oxaloacetate
  + L-glutamate; … EC=2.6.1.1; Evidence={ECO:0000269|PubMed:21900944}"
  [file:human/GOT1/GOT1-uniprot.txt]. This is the reversible transamination that
  defines the enzyme; GO:0004069 (L-aspartate:2-oxoglutarate transaminase activity).
- Cofactor: "Name=pyridoxal 5'-phosphate" with PLP covalently bound at Lys-259
  ("MOD_RES 259 … N6-(pyridoxal phosphate)lysine"; and PDB structures solved "IN
  COMPLEX WITH PYRIDOXAL PHOSPHATE … PYRIDOXAL PHOSPHATE AT LYS-259")
  [file:human/GOT1/GOT1-uniprot.txt]. -> GO:0030170 pyridoxal phosphate binding.
- Subunit: "Homodimer. {ECO:0000269|Ref.17}" [file:human/GOT1/GOT1-uniprot.txt].
- Localization: "SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cytoplasm {ECO:0000269|PubMed:1974457}"
  [file:human/GOT1/GOT1-uniprot.txt]. Cytosol/cytoplasm is the core location.

## Secondary / promiscuous catalytic activities (same active site, broad specificity)

- **L-cysteine transaminase (cysteine aminotransferase, cCAT; EC 2.6.1.3):**
  AltName "Cysteine aminotransferase, cytoplasmic"; reaction "L-cysteine +
  2-oxoglutarate = 2-oxo-3-sulfanylpropanoate (3-mercaptopyruvate) + L-glutamate; …
  EC=2.6.1.3" [file:human/GOT1/GOT1-uniprot.txt]. Physiologically links to H2S
  production: "Using L-cysteine as substrate, regulates levels of mercaptopyruvate,
  an important source of hydrogen sulfide" [file:human/GOT1/GOT1-uniprot.txt].
  -> GO:0047801. Human evidence is ISS (transfer from rat P13221), not direct.
- **(2S)-2-aminobutanoate:2-oxoglutarate transaminase (GO:0120554):** UniProt
  "catalyzes (2S)-2-aminobutanoate … {ECO:0000269|PubMed:27827456}"
  [file:human/GOT1/GOT1-uniprot.txt]. PMID:27827456 shows AST generates 2-aminobutyric
  acid (2-AB) from 2-oxobutyric acid, a by-product of cysteine biosynthesis from
  cystathionine: "2-AB is generated by an amino group transfer reaction to 2-oxobutyric
  acid, a byproduct of cysteine biosynthesis from cystathionine" and, in vitro, "2-OBA
  was incubated with glutamic acid and AST. We found that 2-AB was produced"
  [PMID:27827456]. This assay uses commercial AST and inhibitor (AOA); it is not
  GOT1-isoform-specific, but supports the promiscuous activity for the aspartate
  aminotransferase reaction run in reverse.
- **Cysteine sulfinate (3-sulfino-L-alanine) transaminase:** UniProt lists the
  reaction "3-sulfino-L-alanine + 2-oxoglutarate = 3-sulfinopyruvate + L-glutamate …
  {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P13221}" [file:human/GOT1/GOT1-uniprot.txt] (ISS, not in GOA).

## Physiological roles / pathways

- UniProt FUNCTION: "Biosynthesis of L-glutamate from L-aspartate or L-cysteine
  (PubMed:21900944). Important regulator of levels of glutamate, the major excitatory
  neurotransmitter … Acts as a scavenger of glutamate in brain neuroprotection. The
  aspartate aminotransferase activity is involved in hepatic glucose synthesis during
  development and in adipocyte glyceroneogenesis." [file:human/GOT1/GOT1-uniprot.txt]
- **Malate-aspartate shuttle (MAS):** GOT1 is the cytosolic transaminase arm of the
  MAS, which shuttles reducing equivalents (NADH) into mitochondria. GOA has an IMP to
  GO:0043490 from PMID:37647199 (Broeks et al., Cell Rep 2023), a MAS-component
  knockout study in HEK293 showing MAS is important for de novo serine biosynthesis
  (abstract: "We genetically disrupted each MAS component … MAS-deficient cells have
  reduced serine biosynthesis"). Full text not cached (full_text_available: false);
  GOT1/GOT2 are canonical MAS enzymes, so the annotation is biologically sound even
  though the cached abstract foregrounds MDH1/serine.
- Reactome pathways (DR lines): Malate-aspartate shuttle (R-HSA-9856872), Aspartate
  and asparagine metabolism (R-HSA-8963693), Methionine salvage pathway
  (R-HSA-1237112) [file:human/GOT1/GOT1-uniprot.txt].

## Disease / genetics

- **ASTQTL1 (aspartate aminotransferase serum level QTL 1; MIM:614419).** PMID:21900944
  GWAS in Amish: an in-frame p.Asn389del in GOT1 markedly lowers serum AST. "even after
  correcting for AST protein, mutant cAST had markedly decreased activity relative to
  wild-type cAST" [PMID:21900944]. UniProt VARIANT 389 "Missing (results in markedly
  diminished enzymatic activity)". Notably no metabolic phenotype in heterozygous
  carriers ("we did not find any significant differences in a wide variety of metabolic
  traits …"), suggesting one allele suffices for normal metabolism [PMID:21900944].

## Existing-annotation adjudication summary

Core (ACCEPT, representing the gene's core function):
- GO:0004069 L-aspartate:2-oxoglutarate transaminase activity — supported by
  multiple IDA (PMID:2182221, PMID:2241899, PMID:2731362, PMID:6391741), EXP
  (PMID:21900944), and IBA/ISS. This is THE core MF.
- GO:0030170 pyridoxal phosphate binding (IEA/InterPro) — cofactor, structurally
  confirmed (Lys-259 PLP); accepted as core enzymatic feature.
- GO:0005829 cytosol / GO:0005737 cytoplasm — core location (IDA PMID:7060339;
  IBA; UniProt SUBCELL; Reactome TAS).
- Core metabolic BPs: aspartate metabolic process (GO:0006531), glutamate metabolic
  process (GO:0006536), 2-oxoglutarate metabolic process (GO:0006103),
  L-aspartate biosynthetic/catabolic, L-glutamate biosynthetic, malate-aspartate
  shuttle (GO:0043490, IMP).

Secondary activities kept as non-core (real but promiscuous / ISS): GO:0047801
(L-cysteine transaminase, ISS), GO:0120554 (2-aminobutanoate transaminase, IDA on
generic AST).

Over-annotation / generic:
- GO:0003824 catalytic activity, GO:0008483 transaminase activity, GO:0006520 amino
  acid metabolic process — uninformative parents (IEA); MARK_AS_OVER_ANNOTATED.
- GO:0005515 protein binding (IPI; EGFR P00533, BRAF P15056) — bare "protein binding"
  from high-throughput interactome / neo-PPI screens (PMID:20029029, PMID:31980649,
  PMID:35512704). Not an informative MF for a metabolic enzyme; MARK_AS_OVER_ANNOTATED
  (policy: never REMOVE a bare-protein-binding IPI).

IEA over-propagations from the rat ortholog (GO_REF:0000107, Ensembl Compara from
UniProtKB:P13221 / ENSRNOP...) — many are dubious for human and marked
MARK_AS_OVER_ANNOTATED or REMOVE where clearly wrong:
- GO:0004609 phosphatidylserine decarboxylase activity (in UniProt DR line, NOT in
  GOA TSV) would be a clear wrong-branch IEA if present — not in GOA, so no action.
- GO:0043679 axon terminus, GO:0060290 transdifferentiation, GO:0030511 positive
  regulation of TGF-beta receptor signaling, GO:0032966 negative regulation of collagen
  biosynthesis, GO:0051902 negative regulation of mitochondrial depolarization,
  GO:0051481 negative regulation of cytosolic calcium, GO:1990267 response to transition
  metal nanoparticle, GO:0046686 response to cadmium ion, GO:0071260 response to
  mechanical stimulus, GO:0009743 response to carbohydrate, GO:0035902 response to
  immobilization stress, GO:0071320 cellular response to cAMP, GO:0071377 cellular
  response to glucagon — all Ensembl IEA from rat, over-specific rat-physiology
  transfers; KEEP_AS_NON_CORE or MARK_AS_OVER_ANNOTATED (not core to the human enzyme's
  molecular function).
- GO:0031406 carboxylic acid binding (IEA, Ensembl) — trivially true (substrates are
  carboxylic acids) but uninformative; MARK_AS_OVER_ANNOTATED.
- GO:0070062 extracellular exosome (HDA, multiple exosome proteomics PMIDs) and
  GO:0005634 nucleus (HDA, sperm-nucleus proteomics PMID:21630459) — mass-spec
  localization to non-canonical compartments for an abundant cytosolic enzyme; a common

Transcriptional-regulation IEP annotations (PMID:8396422 promoter study): GO:0032869
cellular response to insulin, GO:0051384 response to glucocorticoid — the paper shows
the GOT1 promoter is regulated by these hormones; annotations describe regulation OF the
gene, kept as non-core.
