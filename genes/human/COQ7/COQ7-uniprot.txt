CC       trans-polyprenyl)benzoquinone at the C6 position and participates in
CC       the biosynthesis of ubiquinone (Probable). Catalyzes the reaction
CC       through a substrate-mediated reduction pathway, whereby NADH shuttles
CC       electrons to 5-methoxy-2-methyl-3-(all-trans-decaprenyl)benzoquinone,
CC       which then transfers the electrons to the two Fe(3+) centers
CC       (PubMed:23445365). The binding of 5-methoxy-2-methyl-3-(all-trans-
CC       polyprenyl)benzoquinone (DMQn) mediates reduction of the diiron center
CC       by nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide (NADH) and initiates oxygen
CC       activation for subsequent DMQ hydroxylation (PubMed:23445365). The
CC       physiological substrates are 5-methoxy-2-methyl-3-(all-trans-
CC       nonaprenyl)benzoquinone (DMQ(9)) and 5-methoxy-2-methyl-3-(all-trans-
CC       decaprenyl)benzoquinone (DMQ(10)), however in vitro the enzyme does not
CC       have any specificity concerning the length of the polyprenyl tail, and
CC       accepts tails of various lengths with similar efficiency

CC       lifespan determination in a ubiquinone-independent manner (By
