# R3HDML review notes

## 2026-07-18

- Initialized the review with `just fetch-gene human R3HDML`; the fetched GOA contains two extracellular-region groups and one PAINT molecular-adaptor group.
- `just deep-research-falcon human R3HDML --fallback perplexity-lite` failed because Falcon returned HTTP 402 and Perplexity-lite returned HTTP 401 for exhausted provider quota. I continued from cached primary literature and the reviewed UniProt record in `R3HDML-deep-research-manual.md`; no provider-named report was created.
- R3HDML is not an R3H-domain protein despite its inherited gene name. It is a small CAP-superfamily precursor with a signal peptide and a predicted furin-cleaved propeptide [file:human/R3HDML/R3HDML-uniprot.txt, "Despite its name, it does not contain a R3H domain."; file:human/R3HDML/R3HDML-uniprot.txt, "SIGNAL          1..24"; file:human/R3HDML/R3HDML-uniprot.txt, "PROPEP          25..56"].
- A primary mouse study directly detected processed R3hdml in satellite-cell conditioned medium and showed that extracellular recombinant protein rescues the phenotype of R3hdml-silenced myogenic cells [PMID:31524320, "R3hdml protein was secreted upon satellite cell differentiation."; PMID:31524320, "adding the R3hdml protein to the culture media rescued the phenotypes seen in R3hdml‐silenced C2C12 cells"]. This strongly supports the extracellular annotations and an extracellular mode of action.
- Mouse knockout, knockdown, and rescue evidence supports positive roles in myogenic satellite-cell/myoblast proliferation and differentiation, postnatal skeletal-muscle development, and injury-induced muscle regeneration [PMID:31524320, "Silencing of the R3hdml gene in C2C12 cells attenuated myotube formation, and those phenotypes were rescued in the presence of R3hdml protein."; PMID:31524320, "Knockout of this gene inhibited satellite cell proliferation, skeletal muscle development, and regeneration."]. Proposed human process annotations use ISS because direct functional experiments were performed in mouse cells and mice.
- Traced the PAINT `molecular adaptor activity` IBA in `interpro/panther/PTHR10334/PTHR10334-paint.tsv`: the only seed is mouse Glipr1l1 (MGI:MGI:1916536). The corresponding GO-CAM places Glipr1l1 on the outer acrosomal membrane in protein localization during the acrosome reaction. R3HDML is a different CAP-family paralog, is secreted from satellite cells, and has no demonstrated multimeric-adaptor activity. The IBA is therefore removed as a propagation/context error.
- Peptidase-inhibitor activity remains a structural prediction, not an experimentally established molecular function. The primary investigators explicitly describe receptor activation or extracellular-matrix/protease-inhibitor mechanisms as alternatives [PMID:31524320, "we speculate that it may work through the activation of certain receptors or by regulation of the ECM surrounding satellite cells via the predicted protease inhibitor functions based on R3hdml protein structure"]. No molecular-function term is asserted as core.
- Human expression is intestine-enriched in current atlas metadata, whereas the causal study concerns development- and injury-induced mouse skeletal muscle. Whether the myogenic role and the dominant adult tissue context are conserved in humans remains open.
