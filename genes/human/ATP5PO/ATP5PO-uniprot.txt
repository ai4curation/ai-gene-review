CC   -!- FUNCTION: Subunit OSCP, of the mitochondrial membrane ATP synthase
CC       complex (F(1)F(0) ATP synthase or Complex V) that produces ATP from ADP
CC       in the presence of a proton gradient across the membrane which is
CC       generated by electron transport complexes of the respiratory chain
CC       (PubMed:37244256). ATP synthase complex consist of a soluble F(1) head
CC       domain - the catalytic core - and a membrane F(1) domain - the membrane
CC       proton channel (PubMed:37244256). These two domains are linked by a
CC       central stalk rotating inside the F(1) region and a stationary
CC       peripheral stalk (PubMed:37244256). During catalysis, ATP synthesis in
CC       the catalytic domain of F(1) is coupled via a rotary mechanism of the
CC       central stalk subunits to proton translocation (Probable). In vivo, can
CC       only synthesize ATP although its ATP hydrolase activity can be
CC       activated artificially in vitro (By similarity). Part of the complex
CC       F(0) domain (PubMed:37244256). Part of the complex F(0) domain and the
CC       peripheric stalk, which acts as a stator to hold the catalytic
CC       alpha(3)beta(3) subcomplex and subunit a/ATP6 static relative to the
CC       rotary elements (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P13621,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P19483, ECO:0000269|PubMed:37244256,
CC       ECO:0000305|PubMed:37244256}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Component of the ATP synthase complex composed at least of
CC       ATP5F1A/subunit alpha, ATP5F1B/subunit beta, ATP5MC1/subunit c
CC       (homooctamer), MT-ATP6/subunit a, MT-ATP8/subunit 8, ATP5ME/subunit e,
CC       ATP5MF/subunit f, ATP5MG/subunit g, ATP5MK/subunit k, ATP5MJ/subunit j,
CC       ATP5F1C/subunit gamma, ATP5F1D/subunit delta, ATP5F1E/subunit epsilon,
CC       ATP5PF/subunit F6, ATP5PB/subunit b, ATP5PD/subunit d, ATP5PO/subunit
CC       OSCP (PubMed:37244256). ATP synthase complex consists of a soluble F(1)
CC       head domain (subunits alpha(3) and beta(3)) - the catalytic core - and
CC       a membrane F(0) domain - the membrane proton channel (subunits c, a, 8,
CC       e, f, g, k and j) (PubMed:37244256). These two domains are linked by a
CC       central stalk (subunits gamma, delta, and epsilon) rotating inside the
CC       F1 region and a stationary peripheral stalk (subunits F6, b, d, and
CC       OSCP) (PubMed:37244256). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:37244256}.
