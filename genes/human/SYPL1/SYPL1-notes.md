# SYPL1 review notes

## 2026-07-18

- Initialized the human review with `just fetch-gene human SYPL1`. The generated stub contains 11 collapsed GOA annotation groups.
- `just deep-research-falcon human SYPL1 --fallback perplexity-lite` could not produce a report: Falcon returned HTTP 402 and Perplexity-lite returned HTTP 401 because the configured quotas are exhausted. I continued with a clearly labeled manual synthesis in `SYPL1-deep-research-manual.md`; no provider-named report was created.
- Pantophysin/SYPL1 is a broadly expressed four-pass membrane protein. Direct microscopy and fractionation place it on small cytoplasmic transport vesicles in human pancreas and cultured cells, including vesicles carrying constitutive-secretory and endocytic markers [PMID:8707851, "colocalizing with constitutive secretory and endocytotic vesicle markers in nonneuroendocrine cells"; PMID:8707851, "Pantophysin is therefore a broadly distributed marker of small cytoplasmic transport vesicles independent of their content."].
- The older cloning paper explicitly contrasts ubiquitous pantophysin expression with the restricted expression of synaptophysin and synaptoporin [PMID:8034131, "the synaptophysin-related gene is ubiquitously expressed in vitro and in vivo"]. This makes the PAINT/InterPro synaptic-vesicle annotations too specific; I replace them with cytoplasmic vesicle membrane rather than treating pantophysin as a specialized synaptic-vesicle protein.
- A mouse knockout study establishes a separable germ-cell role: SYPL1 and VAMP3 govern formation and sequestration of Golgi-derived saccules in the sperm cytoplasmic droplet [PMID:37607933, "This process is governed by a transmembrane protein SYPL1 and its interaction with VAMP3."; PMID:37607933, "Genetic ablation of SYPL1 in mice reveals that SYPL1 dictates the formation and accumulation of saccular elements in the forming cytoplasmic droplet."].
- That study also reports severely impaired sperm motility and near-complete male infertility after mouse Sypl1 loss [PMID:37607933, "KO epididymal sperm showed severely impaired motility"; PMID:37607933, "Together, these results indicate that Sypl1 is a critical gene for sperm maturation and male fertility."].
- Human relevance is supported by direct immunodetection of both SYPL1 and VAMP3 in human sperm cytoplasmic droplets [PMID:37607933, "the enrichment of SYPL1 and VAMP3 is conserved, also appearing in the CDs of human spermatozoa"]. The causal process annotations are therefore transferred to human with ISS rather than presented as direct human knockout evidence.
- The melanosome and urinary-exosome detections are retained as non-core proteomic localizations. The ALG3 and reference-interactome `protein binding` annotations are marked as uninformative over-annotations rather than converted into a molecular activity.
- The PMID:8034131 plasma-membrane TAS annotation remains `UNDECIDED`: the cached record is abstract-only and does not expose the localization evidence, so it would be inappropriate to overrule the original curator from incomplete evidence.
- No autonomous molecular activity has been demonstrated for SYPL1. Its defining evidence concerns membrane localization, vesicle organization, and VAMP3-dependent trafficking; generic binding or sequence-family membership does not establish a molecular-function term.
