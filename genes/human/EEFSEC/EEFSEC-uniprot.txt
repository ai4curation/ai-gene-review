CC   -!- FUNCTION: Translation factor required for the incorporation of the rare
CC       amino acid selenocysteine encoded by UGA codons (PubMed:27708257,
CC       PubMed:35709277). Replaces the eRF1-eRF3-GTP ternary complex for the
CC       insertion of selenocysteine directed by the UGA codon (PubMed:27708257,
CC       PubMed:35709277). Insertion of selenocysteine at UGA codons is mediated
CC       by SECISBP2 and EEFSEC: SECISBP2 (1) specifically binds the SECIS
CC       sequence once the 80S ribosome encounters an in-frame UGA codon and (2)
CC       contacts the RPS27A/eS31 of the 40S ribosome before ribosome stalling
CC       (PubMed:35709277). (3) GTP-bound EEFSEC then delivers selenocysteinyl-
CC       tRNA(Sec) to the 80S ribosome and adopts a preaccommodated state
CC       conformation (PubMed:35709277). (4) After GTP hydrolysis,
CC       EEFSEC dissociates from the assembly, selenocysteinyl-tRNA(Sec)
CC       accommodates, and peptide bond synthesis and selenoprotein elongation
CC       occur (PubMed:35709277). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:27708257,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:35709277}.
