## Comprehensive research report: Human **EEFSEC** (UniProt **P57772**) — functional annotation

### 0) Target verification (mandatory)
The UniProt accession **P57772** corresponds to human **EEFSEC**, encoding the **selenocysteine-specific translation elongation factor eEFSec** (SelB-like). eEFSec is a specialized **translational GTPase** that binds **Sec-tRNA\u1d65\u1d65\u1d49\u1d9c** and delivers it to the ribosome for **UGA recoding** during selenoprotein synthesis. Structural studies show eEFSec has a distinctive **four-domain (D1–D4)**, chalice-like architecture (D1–D3 EF-Tu-like; D4 specialized C-terminal domain), consistent with the UniProt family/domain description and with membership in the EF-Tu/translation-factor TRAFAC GTPase class. (doboszbartoszek2016crystalstructuresof pages 2-3, doboszbartoszek2016crystalstructuresof pages 1-2, simonovic2018onelongationfactor pages 6-7)

### 1) Key concepts and definitions (current understanding)

#### 1.1 Selenocysteine (Sec) and UGA recoding
**Selenocysteine (Sec)** is incorporated co-translationally by **recoding an in-frame UGA codon** (normally a stop codon). In eukaryotes, this requires a **SECIS element** (a stem–loop RNA structure) typically located in the **3′ UTR** of selenoprotein mRNAs, plus dedicated trans-acting protein and tRNA factors. (dubey2016theselenocysteinespecificelongation pages 1-2, schmidt2012synthesisanddecoding pages 5-6)

#### 1.2 What EEFSEC/eEFSec is (molecular definition)
**eEFSec** is the **dedicated elongation factor** for selenoprotein translation. It forms a functional complex with **GTP** and **Sec-tRNA\u1d65\u1d65\u1d49\u1d9c** and is positioned near the ribosome through the **SECIS–SBP2** system to deliver Sec-tRNA\u1d65\u1d65\u1d49\u1d9c to the **ribosomal A site** at Sec-UGA codons. Canonical elongation factor **eEF1A** cannot substitute because Sec-tRNA\u1d65\u1d65\u1d49\u1d9c has unusual structural features and modifications. (bulteau2015updateonselenoprotein pages 7-9, dubey2016theselenocysteinespecificelongation pages 1-2, schoenmakers2020humandisordersaffecting pages 3-5)

#### 1.3 Core pathway steps upstream of eEFSec: making Sec-tRNA\u1d65\u1d65\u1d49\u1d9c
A standard mechanistic model for **Sec-tRNA\u1d65\u1d65\u1d49\u1d9c biosynthesis** in eukaryotes is:
1) **SerRS** charges tRNA\u1d65\u1d65\u1d49\u1d9c with serine → Ser-tRNA\u1d65\u1d65\u1d49\u1d9c.
2) **PSTK** phosphorylates Ser-tRNA\u1d65\u1d65\u1d49\u1d9c → Sep-tRNA\u1d65\u1d65\u1d49\u1d9c.
3) **SepSecS** converts Sep-tRNA\u1d65\u1d65\u1d49\u1d9c to Sec-tRNA\u1d65\u1d65\u1d49\u1d9c using **selenophosphate** as selenium donor (made by SPS2/SEPHS2). (schmidt2012synthesisanddecoding pages 5-6, deangelo2023selenoproteinsandtrnasec pages 6-8)

### 2) Molecular function, substrate specificity, and mechanism

#### 2.1 Primary molecular function
EEFSEC/eEFSec’s primary function is **Sec-specific elongation**: it **selectively binds Sec-tRNA\u1d65\u1d65\u1d49\u1d9c** (and not canonical aa-tRNAs or serylated precursor tRNA\u1d65\u1d65\u1d49\u1d9c) and delivers it to the ribosome in a **GTP-dependent** manner to enable **Sec insertion** at UGA codons in SECIS-containing mRNAs. (doboszbartoszek2016crystalstructuresof pages 1-2, gonzalezflores2012theselenocysteinespecificelongation pages 1-2)

#### 2.2 Structural basis and “non-canonical” GTPase mechanism
High-resolution structures show human eEFSec is a four-domain protein with a **chalice-like** overall shape. Unlike EF-Tu/eEF1A, guanine nucleotide exchange does not primarily cause a large rearrangement of domain 1; instead, **domain 4 (D4) swings ~26°** (with >15 Å translation) between nucleotide states. This supports a **non-canonical mechanism** in which **D4** plays a key role in sensing nucleotide state and promoting release of Sec-tRNA\u1d65\u1d65\u1d49\u1d9c during decoding. (doboszbartoszek2016crystalstructuresof pages 1-2, simonovic2018onelongationfactor pages 7-9, doboszbartoszek2016crystalstructuresof media 88c5ecc1)

A proposed mechanistic model is that eEFSec binds Sec-tRNA\u1d65\u1d65\u1d49\u1d9c preferentially in the **GTP-bound state**, is tethered near the ribosome by the **SECIS–SBP2** system, and upon correct codon–anticodon interaction at the decoding center, **GTP hydrolysis** triggers coordinated changes (notably D4 movement) that reduce affinity for Sec-tRNA\u1d65\u1d65\u1d49\u1d9c and allow accommodation of the aminoacyl-tRNA into the A site for peptide bond formation. (simonovic2018onelongationfactor pages 7-9, simonovic2018onelongationfactor pages 21-22)

### 3) Cellular localization: where EEFSEC acts
EEFSEC is functionally required for **cytoplasmic translation** (Sec insertion on cytoplasmic ribosomes), but it also shows notable **nuclear localization**. eEFSec **shuttles between nucleus and cytoplasm**, contains a **bipartite nuclear localization signal (NLS)**, and uses **CRM1/exportin-1-mediated nuclear export** (Leptomycin B-sensitive). Experimental mutagenesis identified eEFSec sequences required for both Sec incorporation activity and nucleocytoplasmic trafficking, and supported a model with **two pools**: a cytoplasmic pool used for Sec insertion and a nuclear pool with an unresolved function. (dubey2016theselenocysteinespecificelongation pages 18-19, dubey2016theselenocysteinespecificelongation pages 15-18, dubey2016theselenocysteinespecificelongation pages 10-12)

Some models propose that SBP2/eEFSec/Sec-tRNA\u1d65\u1d65\u1d49\u1d9c complexes may assemble on SECIS-containing mRNAs in the nucleus and be exported for translation, potentially relating to regulation and avoidance of nonsense-mediated decay; however, eEFSec localization appears largely **independent of SBP2** status in tested systems. (dubey2016theselenocysteinespecificelongation pages 12-15)

### 4) Pathways and interaction network

#### 4.1 Core Sec-insertion machinery and accessory regulators
EEFSEC functions in the **selenocysteine insertion pathway**, centered on: **SECIS element**, **SECISBP2 (SBP2)**, **Sec-tRNA\u1d65\u1d65\u1d49\u1d9c**, and **eEFSec**. In vitro and mechanistic evidence supports that **Sec-tRNA\u1d65\u1d65\u1d49\u1d9c, EEFSEC, and SECISBP2** are sufficient and essential for Sec incorporation in reconstituted settings. Additional regulators described in the literature include **TRNAU1AP/SECp43**, **RPL30**, **EIF4A3**, and **nucleolin**, indicating a larger ribonucleoprotein environment that tunes recoding efficiency. (schoenmakers2020humandisordersaffecting pages 3-5, deangelo2023selenoproteinsandtrnasec pages 6-8)

#### 4.2 Downstream biological roles of the pathway (context)
Because EEFSEC is required for **synthesis of the entire selenoproteome**, its biological impact is typically mediated through altered abundance of antioxidant/redox and other specialized selenoproteins (e.g., GPX enzymes and thioredoxin reductases), which in turn influence oxidative stress responses, redox signaling, thyroid hormone metabolism, and ferroptosis resistance in some contexts. (deangelo2023selenoproteinsandtrnasec pages 6-8, alborzinia2023lrp8‐mediatedselenocysteineuptake pages 1-2)

### 5) Recent developments (prioritizing 2023–2024)

#### 5.1 2023: selenocysteine machinery co-opted for stop-codon readthrough (non-selenoprotein targets)
A 2023 study reported that overexpression of **Seryl-tRNA synthetase (SerRS)** can promote **UGA stop-codon translational readthrough** in a luciferase reporter setting and that this readthrough depends on components of the **selenocysteine incorporation machinery**, including **tRNA\u1d65\u1d65\u1d49\u1d9c** and **eEFSec**. Knockdown of these components abrogated SerRS-driven readthrough, while SBP2 knockdown did not; the effect was **UGA-dependent** (UGA→UAA/UAG substitutions eliminated readthrough). The study reported strong statistical significance for some comparisons (e.g., ****P < 0.0001). This work expands the perceived functional footprint of EEFSEC-linked machinery beyond canonical selenoprotein transcripts, and suggests a translational-control avenue that could be leveraged for nonsense mutation contexts. (liu2023seryltrnasynthetasepromotes pages 6-7)

Publication: Liu et al., *Nucleic Acids Research*, Sep 2023. URL: https://doi.org/10.1093/nar/gkad773 (liu2023seryltrnasynthetasepromotes pages 6-7)

#### 5.2 2023: cancer redox homeostasis/ferroptosis framing highlights EEFSEC as a leverage point
A 2023 *Trends in Cancer* review positioned **EEFSEC** within the multiprotein SECIS-associated complex (SECISBP2, EEFSEC, TRNAU1AP) that places tRNA\u1d65\u1d65\u1d49\u1d9c into the elongating ribosome, and emphasized that selenoprotein synthesis is tightly linked to tumor redox biology and ferroptosis vulnerabilities. The review highlighted evidence from survival analyses across cancers (hazard ratios, p < 0.05) and discussed the translational implications of manipulating selenium/selenoprotein pathways and ferroptosis-inducing strategies, including GPX4-related pharmacology. (deangelo2023selenoproteinsandtrnasec pages 6-8)

Publication: DeAngelo et al., *Trends in Cancer*, Dec 2023. URL: https://doi.org/10.1016/j.trecan.2023.08.003 (deangelo2023selenoproteinsandtrnasec pages 6-8)

#### 5.3 2024: tRNA\u1d65\u1d65\u1d49\u1d9c Um34 methylation regulates Sec insertion and metastasis-related oxidative stress resistance
A 2024 *Nature Cancer* paper identified **FTSJ1** as a methyltransferase responsible for installing **Um34** (2′-O-ribose methylation) on a tRNA\u1d65\u1d65\u1d49\u1d9c isodecoder, and reported that this modification is required for efficient **UGA recoding/Sec insertion**. Loss of Um34 was associated with **ribosomal stalling** and decreased UGA recoding, increased oxidative-stress sensitivity, and decreased melanoma metastatic colonization in xenograft models. The authors contextualize this within the SECIS-associated machinery that includes **EEFSEC**. The study also provides concrete experimental implementations (SECIS/UGA reporter constructs; in vitro methylation assays; LC–MS/MS nucleoside quantification) enabling measurement and perturbation of Sec insertion. (nease2024selenocysteinetrnamethylation pages 1-3, nease2024selenocysteinetrnamethylation pages 16-18)

Publication: Nease et al., *Nature Cancer*, Oct 2024. URL: https://doi.org/10.1038/s43018-024-00844-8 (nease2024selenocysteinetrnamethylation pages 1-3)

### 6) Current applications and real-world implementations

#### 6.1 Functional genomics and cancer vulnerabilities in the selenium/selenoprotein axis
A 2023 EMBO Molecular Medicine study used genome-wide **CRISPR activation** screening in a ferroptosis-hypersensitive MYCN-amplified neuroblastoma line to identify regulators of ferroptosis. The study identified **LRP8** (SELENOP receptor) as a determinant of ferroptosis resistance, linking selenium/selenocysteine supply to translation of the antiferroptotic selenoprotein **GPX4**. It validated this axis in **orthotopic xenografts**, illustrating how selenium trafficking and selenoprotein synthesis constitute actionable biology. While this work did not specifically perturb EEFSEC, it exemplifies a translational setting in which the **downstream products of EEFSEC-dependent selenoprotein synthesis** (e.g., GPX4) become therapeutic levers. (alborzinia2023lrp8‐mediatedselenocysteineuptake pages 1-2, alborzinia2023lrp8‐mediatedselenocysteineuptake pages 14-15)

Publication: Alborzinia et al., *EMBO Molecular Medicine*, Jul 2023. URL: https://doi.org/10.15252/emmm.202318014 (alborzinia2023lrp8‐mediatedselenocysteineuptake pages 1-2)

#### 6.2 Reporter systems and biochemical assays for Sec insertion
Recent work provides practical tools to measure and manipulate UGA recoding/Sec insertion, including **SECIS-dependent GFP reporters** with engineered UGA/UAA/UAG codons and flow cytometry readouts, and biochemical pipelines for tRNA\u1d65\u1d65\u1d49\u1d9c modification analysis (LC–MS/MS). Such tools are directly relevant for functional annotation and for testing how EEFSEC variants or regulatory perturbations change Sec insertion efficiency. (nease2024selenocysteinetrnamethylation pages 16-18)

### 7) Expert synthesis and authoritative interpretation
Mechanistic and structural evidence converges on a model where EEFSEC is not a general elongation factor but a **special-purpose translation factor** whose specificity is conferred by (i) dedicated recognition of Sec-tRNA\u1d65\u1d65\u1d49\u1d9c, (ii) engagement with the SECIS–SBP2 system rather than generic mRNA features, and (iii) a **distinct D4 domain** that undergoes nucleotide-state-dependent movements thought to control release/accommodation during decoding. This non-canonical conformational coupling provides a plausible explanation for how Sec insertion can be controlled without mimicking the EF-Tu/eEF1A conformational cycle. (doboszbartoszek2016crystalstructuresof pages 1-2, simonovic2018onelongationfactor pages 7-9, gonzalezflores2012theselenocysteinespecificelongation pages 1-2)

A complementary cell biology layer is that eEFSec (unlike most translation factors) **shuttles through the nucleus**, implying regulated assembly/trafficking steps for the Sec insertion machinery that could influence selenoprotein hierarchy and stress responses—though the precise nuclear role remains unresolved. (dubey2016theselenocysteinespecificelongation pages 18-19, dubey2016theselenocysteinespecificelongation pages 1-2)

### 10) Summary and functional annotation statement (human EEFSEC / P57772)
EEFSEC encodes a Sec-tRNA\u1d65\u1d65\u1d49\u1d9c-specific, EF-Tu-like **translation elongation GTPase** required for **selenoprotein synthesis** by **UGA recoding** in eukaryotes. It acts primarily in the **cytoplasm on translating ribosomes**, is recruited/positioned via the **SECIS–SBP2** system, and employs a **non-canonical conformational cycle** dominated by domain D4 movement to facilitate Sec-tRNA\u1d65\u1d65\u1d49\u1d9c release and accommodation. eEFSec also exhibits **CRM1-dependent nuclear export** and nuclear/cytoplasmic shuttling, suggesting additional regulatory biology beyond its direct decoding role. Recent (2023–2024) literature connects EEFSEC-containing machinery to broader translational readthrough and cancer redox/ferroptosis biology, and provides experimental tools to interrogate Sec insertion efficiency in cells. (doboszbartoszek2016crystalstructuresof pages 1-2, dubey2016theselenocysteinespecificelongation pages 1-2, gonzalezflores2012theselenocysteinespecificelongation pages 1-2, liu2023seryltrnasynthetasepromotes pages 6-7, deangelo2023selenoproteinsandtrnasec pages 6-8, nease2024selenocysteinetrnamethylation pages 1-3)
