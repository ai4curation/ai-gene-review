Gene Description

DNAJB1 (also known as HSP40, HDJ1) is a class II J-domain protein that functions as a co-chaperone for HSP70 family members. It stimulates the ATPase activity of HSP70 and delivers substrates to the HSP70 chaperone machinery, promoting protein folding (foldase-type activity). DNAJB1 contains an N-terminal J-domain that interacts with HSP70 and C-terminal substrate-binding domains. It also negatively regulates HSF1 transcriptional activity during the attenuation phase of the heat shock response by binding HSF1 in complex with HSP70. DNAJB1 is stress-inducible and translocates from the cytoplasm to the nucleus and nucleolus upon heat shock. Notably, DNAJB1 does not possess independent holdase activity; rather, it contributes to the foldase function of the HSP70 chaperone system.
