CC   -!- FUNCTION: Bifunctional enzyme acting on the peroxisomal fatty acid
CC       beta-oxidation pathway. Catalyzes two of the four reactions in fatty
CC       acid degradation: hydration of 2-enoyl-CoA (trans-2-enoyl-CoA) to
CC       produce (3R)-3-hydroxyacyl-CoA, and dehydrogenation of (3R)-3-
CC       hydroxyacyl-CoA to produce 3-ketoacyl-CoA (3-oxoacyl-CoA), which is
CC       further metabolized by SCPx. Can use straight-chain and branched-chain
CC       fatty acids, as well as bile acid intermediates as substrates. May play
CC       a role in peroxisomal beta-oxidation step in polyunsaturated fatty
CC       acids (PUFAs) biosynthesis. Possibly regulates systemic levels of
CC       docosahexaenoic acid (DHA, C22:6n-3) through a process involving
CC       endoplasmic reticulum desaturation and elongation of alpha-linolenic
CC       acid (ALA, C18:3n-3) to form tetracosahexaenoic acid (THA, C24:6n-3),
CC       which is then beta-oxidized to DHA in peroxisomes.
