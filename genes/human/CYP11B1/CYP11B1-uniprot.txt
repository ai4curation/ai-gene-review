CC   -!- FUNCTION: A cytochrome P450 monooxygenase mainly involved in the
CC       biosynthesis of adrenal corticoids (PubMed:12530636, PubMed:1518866,
CC       PubMed:1775135, PubMed:18215163, PubMed:23322723, PubMed:23685396,
CC       PubMed:29277707). Catalyzes a variety of reactions that are essential
CC       for many species, including detoxification, defense, and the formation
CC       of endogenous chemicals like steroid hormones. Steroid 11beta, 18- and
CC       19-hydroxylase with preferred regioselectivity at 11beta, then 18, and
CC       lastly 19 (By similarity). Catalyzes the hydroxylation of 11-
CC       deoxycortisol and 11-deoxycorticosterone (21-hydroxyprogesterone) at
CC       11beta position, yielding cortisol or corticosterone, respectively, but
CC       cannot produce aldosterone (PubMed:12530636, PubMed:1518866,
CC       PubMed:1775135, PubMed:18215163, PubMed:23322723, PubMed:23685396,
CC       PubMed:29277707). Mechanistically, uses molecular oxygen inserting one
CC       oxygen atom into a substrate for hydroxylation and reducing the second
CC       into a water molecule. Two electrons are provided by NADPH via a two-
CC       protein mitochondrial transfer system comprising flavoprotein FDXR
CC       (adrenodoxin/ferredoxin reductase) and nonheme iron-sulfur protein FDX1
CC       or FDX2 (adrenodoxin/ferredoxin) (PubMed:18215163, PubMed:23685396).
CC       Due to its lack of 18-oxidation activity, it is incapable of generating
CC       aldosterone (PubMed:23322723). Besides its role in the biosynthesis of
CC       mineralocorticoids and glucocorticoids, it can also participate in the
CC       androgen metabolic pathway, converting androst-4-ene-3,17-dione to
CC       11beta-hydroxyandrost-4-ene-3,17-dione, and testosterone (17beta-
CC       hydroxy-4-androsten-3-one) to 11beta,17beta-dihydroxyandrost-4-ene-3-
CC       one (PubMed:23685396, PubMed:29277707). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P15393,
