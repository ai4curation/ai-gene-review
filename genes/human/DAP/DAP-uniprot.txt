CC   -!- FUNCTION: Ribosome-binding protein involved in ribosome hibernation, a
CC       process during which ribosomes are stabilized in an inactive state and
CC       preserved from proteasomal degradation (By similarity). Acts via its
CC       association with eiF5a (EIF5A and EIF5A2) at the polypeptide exit
CC       tunnel of the ribosome, preventing mRNA translation (By similarity).
CC       Involved in ribosome hibernation in the mature oocyte by preventing
CC       mRNA translation, leading to ribosome inactivation (By similarity).
CC       Ribosomes, which are produced in large quantities during oogenesis, are
CC       stored and translationally repressed in the oocyte and early embryo (By
CC       similarity). Also acts as a negative regulator of autophagy
CC       (PubMed:20537536). Involved in mediating interferon-gamma-induced cell
CC       death (PubMed:7828849). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9I9N1,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:20537536, ECO:0000269|PubMed:7828849}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Associates with ribosomes; inhibiting translation (By
CC       similarity). Interacts with eiF5a (EIF5A and EIF5A2); inhibiting
CC       translation (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9I9N1}.
CC   -!- PTM: Phosphorylated. Phosphorylation by MTOR inhibits the suppressive
CC       activity of DAP toward autophagy. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:16602700,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:20537536}.
