Gene Description

RFT1 encodes the Man(5)GlcNAc(2)-PP-dolichol translocation protein, a multi-pass transmembrane protein of the endoplasmic reticulum that functions as a glycolipid flippase. It catalyzes the translocation of the lipid-linked oligosaccharide (LLO) intermediate M5GN2-PP-Dol from the cytosolic to the lumenal leaflet of the ER membrane, an essential step in the N-glycosylation pathway. RFT1 belongs to the MOP (multidrug/oligosaccharidyl-lipid/polysaccharide) transporter superfamily and contains approximately 12-14 transmembrane spans with both N- and C-termini facing the cytosol. The protein shows substrate selectivity for the M5 intermediate over shorter M3 intermediates. Loss-of-function mutations cause RFT1-CDG (OMIM 612015), a severe congenital disorder of glycosylation characterized by neurodevelopmental abnormalities, epilepsy, and sensorineural deafness.
