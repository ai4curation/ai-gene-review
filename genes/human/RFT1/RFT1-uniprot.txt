CC   -!- FUNCTION: Intramembrane glycolipid transporter that operates in the
CC       biosynthetic pathway of dolichol-linked oligosaccharides, the glycan
CC       precursors employed in protein asparagine (N)-glycosylation. The
CC       sequential addition of sugars to dolichol pyrophosphate produces
CC       dolichol-linked oligosaccharides containing fourteen sugars, including
CC       two GlcNAcs, nine mannoses and three glucoses. Once assembled, the
CC       oligosaccharide is transferred from the lipid to nascent proteins by
CC       oligosaccharyltransferases. The assembly of dolichol-linked
CC       oligosaccharides begins on the cytosolic side of the endoplasmic
CC       reticulum membrane and finishes in its lumen. RFT1 could mediate the
CC       translocation of the cytosolically oriented intermediate DolPP-
CC       GlcNAc2Man5, produced by ALG11, into the ER lumen where dolichol-linked
CC       oligosaccharides assembly continues (PubMed:18313027, PubMed:19701946).
CC       However, the intramembrane lipid transporter activity could not be
CC       confirmed in vitro (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P38206,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:18313027, ECO:0000269|PubMed:19701946}.
CC   -!- PATHWAY: Protein modification; protein glycosylation.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:18313027}.

CC   -!- DISEASE: Congenital disorder of glycosylation 1N (CDG1N) [MIM:612015]:
CC       A form of congenital disorder of glycosylation, a multisystem disorder
CC       caused by a defect in glycoprotein biosynthesis and characterized by
CC       under-glycosylated serum glycoproteins. Congenital disorders of
CC       glycosylation result in a wide variety of clinical features, such as
CC       defects in the nervous system development, psychomotor retardation,
CC       dysmorphic features, hypotonia, coagulation disorders, and
CC       immunodeficiency. The broad spectrum of features reflects the critical
CC       role of N-glycoproteins during embryonic development, differentiation,
CC       and maintenance of cell functions. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:18313027,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:19701946}. Note=The disease is caused by variants
CC       affecting the gene represented in this entry.
