Gene Description

SPOCK3 (testican-3) is a secreted extracellular matrix proteoglycan that functions as a tumor-suppressive metalloendopeptidase inhibitor. It inhibits membrane-type matrix metalloproteinases (MT1-MMP and MT3-MMP), thereby preventing pro-MMP-2 activation and blocking tumor invasion. The protein contains a modular architecture including follistatin-like domain, acidic region, EC calcium-binding domains, Kazal-like protease inhibitor domain, and thyroglobulin type-1 domain. SPOCK3 carries chondroitin sulfate and heparan sulfate glycosaminoglycan chains and is highly expressed in normal brain, with expression decreasing in gliomas. A splice variant (N-Tes, isoform 2) lacks the thyroglobulin domain but retains MMP inhibitory activity. Unlike testican-2 (SPOCK2), which counteracts MMP inhibition, SPOCK3 shares with testican-1 (SPOCK1) the key function of suppressing MT-MMP-mediated tumor invasion and cell motility through direct interaction with MT-MMPs.

Overview

SPOCK3 is a secreted extracellular matrix proteoglycan with experimentally validated tumor-suppressive metalloendopeptidase inhibitor activity. It was originally identified through expression cloning as an inhibitor of membrane-type matrix metalloproteinases (MT1-MMP and MT3-MMP), distinguishing it from testican-2 (SPOCK2), which lacks this inhibitory activity and instead counteracts it.
