CC   -!- FUNCTION: Mitochondrial membrane insertase required for the
CC       translocation of the C-terminus of cytochrome c oxidase subunit II (MT-
CC       CO2/COX2) across the mitochondrial inner membrane. Plays a role in MT-
CC       CO2/COX2 maturation following the COX20-mediated stabilization of newly
CC       synthesized MT-CO2/COX2 protein and before the action of the
CC       metallochaperones SCO1/2. Essential for the assembly and stability of
CC       the mitochondrial respiratory chain complex IV (also known as
CC       cytochrome c oxidase). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:16911509,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:28330871}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Found in a complex with TMEM177, COA6, MT-CO2/COX2, COX20,
CC       SCO1 and SCO2 (PubMed:29154948). Interacts transiently with MT-CO2/COX2
CC       during its maturation (PubMed:28330871). Interacts with COX20 in a MT-
CC       CO2/COX2-dependent manner (PubMed:28330871).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:28330871, ECO:0000269|PubMed:29154948}.

CC       characterized by neonatal encephalopathy, hypertrophic cardiomyopathy,

CC   -!- DISEASE: Charcot-Marie-Tooth disease, axonal, type 2MM (CMT2MM)
CC       [MIM:621488]: An autosomal recessive, axonal form of Charcot-Marie-
CC       Tooth disease, a disorder of the peripheral nervous system,
CC       characterized by progressive weakness and atrophy, initially of the
CC       peroneal muscles and later of the distal muscles of the arms. Charcot-
CC       Marie-Tooth disease is classified in two main groups on the basis of
CC       electrophysiologic properties and histopathology: primary peripheral
CC       demyelinating neuropathies (designated CMT1 when they are dominantly
CC       inherited) and primary peripheral axonal neuropathies (CMT2).
CC       Neuropathies of the CMT2 group are characterized by signs of axonal
CC       degeneration in the absence of obvious myelin alterations, normal or
CC       slightly reduced nerve conduction velocities, and progressive distal
CC       muscle weakness and atrophy. CMT2MM is characterized by progressive
CC       axonal sensorimotor peripheral neuropathy with age of onset between the
CC       first and fifth decade. Some patients also exhibit central nervous
CC       system symptoms, such as dystonia and spasticity.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:40830826}. Note=The disease is caused by variants
CC       affecting the gene represented in this entry.
