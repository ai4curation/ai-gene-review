CC   -!- FUNCTION: Subunit delta, of the mitochondrial membrane ATP synthase
CC       complex (F(1)F(0) ATP synthase or Complex V) that produces ATP from ADP
CC       in the presence of a proton gradient across the membrane which is
CC       generated by electron transport complexes of the respiratory chain
CC       (Probable) (PubMed:37244256). ATP synthase complex consist of a soluble
CC       F(1) head domain - the catalytic core - and a membrane F(1) domain
CC       - the membrane proton channel (PubMed:37244256). These two domains are
CC       linked by a central stalk rotating inside the F(1) region and a
CC       stationary peripheral stalk (PubMed:37244256). During catalysis, ATP
CC       synthesis in the catalytic domain of F(1) is coupled via a rotary
CC       mechanism of the central stalk subunits to proton translocation
CC       (Probable). In vivo, can only synthesize ATP although its ATP hydrolase
CC       activity can be activated artificially in vitro (By similarity). With
CC       the central stalk subunit gamma, is essential for the biogenesis of
CC       F(1) catalytic part of the ATP synthase complex namely in the formation
CC       of F1 assembly intermediate (PubMed:29499186).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P19483, ECO:0000269|PubMed:29499186,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:37244256, ECO:0000305|PubMed:29478781,
CC       ECO:0000305|PubMed:37244256}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Component of the ATP synthase complex composed at least of
CC       ATP5F1A/subunit alpha, ATP5F1B/subunit beta, ATP5MC1/subunit c
CC       (homooctamer), MT-ATP6/subunit a, MT-ATP8/subunit 8, ATP5ME/subunit e,
CC       ATP5MF/subunit f, ATP5MG/subunit g, ATP5MK/subunit k, ATP5MJ/subunit j,
CC       ATP5F1C/subunit gamma, ATP5F1D/subunit delta, ATP5F1E/subunit epsilon,
CC       ATP5PF/subunit F6, ATP5PB/subunit b, ATP5PD/subunit d, ATP5PO/subunit
CC       OSCP (PubMed:37244256). ATP synthase complex consists of a soluble F(1)
CC       head domain (subunits alpha(3) and beta(3)) - the catalytic core - and
CC       a membrane F(0) domain - the membrane proton channel (subunits c, a, 8,
CC       e, f, g, k and j) (PubMed:37244256). These two domains are linked by a
CC       central stalk (subunits gamma, delta, and epsilon) rotating inside the
CC       F1 region and a stationary peripheral stalk (subunits F6, b, d, and
CC       OSCP) (PubMed:37244256). Component of a complex composed at least by
CC       ATPIF1, ATP5F1A, ATP5F1B, ATP5F1C AND ATP5F1E (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P05630, ECO:0000269|PubMed:37244256}.
