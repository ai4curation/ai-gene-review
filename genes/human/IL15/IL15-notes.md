# IL15 notes

## Falcon integration, 2026-05-12

- Falcon deep research frames IL15 as a common gamma-chain cytokine that is usually delivered by IL15RA-dependent trans-presentation rather than only as freely soluble cytokine: "an IL-15-presenting cell displays a membrane IL-15/IL-15Ralpha complex that stimulates neighboring lymphocytes through IL2RB/IL2RG" [file:human/IL15/IL15-deep-research-falcon.md].
- The most informative MF term beyond generic cytokine activity is interleukin-15 receptor binding. Generic protein binding annotations remain over-annotated because the curated biology is IL15RA binding plus recruitment of IL2RB/IL2RG [file:human/IL15/IL15-deep-research-falcon.md].
- Added NEW BP terms for NK-cell proliferation/activation, B-cell proliferation/immunoglobulin production, and neutrophil chemotaxis because the existing broad cell proliferation and immune response annotations obscure these assayed cell-type-specific functions [file:human/IL15/IL15-deep-research-falcon.md].
- Retained isoform-specific cellular component interpretation: IL15-S48AA is the secreted form, while IL15-S21AA is intracellular with cytoplasm/nucleus localization, matching the existing UniProt-based review text [UniProtKB:P40933; file:human/IL15/IL15-deep-research-falcon.md].
