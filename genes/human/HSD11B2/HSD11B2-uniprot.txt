CC   -!- FUNCTION: Catalyzes the conversion of biologically active 11beta-
CC       hydroxyglucocorticoids (11beta-hydroxysteroid) such as cortisol, to
CC       inactive 11-ketoglucocorticoids (11-oxosteroid) such as cortisone, in
CC       the presence of NAD(+) (PubMed:10497248, PubMed:12788846,
CC       PubMed:17314322, PubMed:22796344, PubMed:23685396, PubMed:27927697,
CC       PubMed:30902677, PubMed:33387577, PubMed:7859916, PubMed:8538347).
CC       Functions as a dehydrogenase (oxidase), thereby decreasing the
CC       concentration of active glucocorticoids, thus protecting the
CC       nonselective mineralocorticoid receptor from occupation by
CC       glucocorticoids (PubMed:10497248, PubMed:12788846, PubMed:17314322,
CC       PubMed:33387577, PubMed:7859916). Plays an important role in
CC       maintaining glucocorticoids balance during preimplantation and protects
CC       the fetus from excessive maternal corticosterone exposure (By
CC       similarity). Catalyzes the oxidation of 11beta-hydroxytestosterone
CC       (11beta,17beta-dihydroxyandrost-4-ene-3-one) to 11-ketotestosterone
CC       (17beta-hydroxyandrost-4-ene-3,11-dione), a major bioactive androgen
CC       (PubMed:22796344, PubMed:27927697). Catalyzes the conversion of 11beta-
CC       hydroxyandrostenedione (11beta-hydroxyandrost-4-ene-3,17-dione) to 11-
CC       ketoandrostenedione (androst-4-ene-3,11,17-trione), which can be
CC       further metabolized to 11-ketotestosterone (PubMed:23685396,
CC       PubMed:27927697). Converts 7-beta-25-dihydroxycholesterol to 7-oxo-25-
CC       hydroxycholesterol in vitro (PubMed:30902677). 7-beta-25-
CC       dihydroxycholesterol (not 7-oxo-25-hydroxycholesterol) acts as a ligand
CC       for the G protein-coupled receptor (GPCR) Epstein-Barr virus-induced
CC       gene 2 (EBI2) and may thereby regulate immune cell migration
CC       (PubMed:30902677). May protect ovulating oocytes and fertilizing
CC       spermatozoa from the adverse effects of cortisol (By similarity).
CC       Catalyzes the conversion of 7-beta-hydroxycholesterol into 7-
CC       ketocholesterol (7-oxocholesterol), a precursor of 7-keto,27-
CC       hydroxycholesterol, which activates smoothened (SMO) and the smoothened
CC       signaling pathway (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O77667,
