Summary: MISANNOTATION: MAML1 is a transcriptional coactivator, NOT a kinase. The cited paper PMID:16510869 describes MAML1 as a "coactivator for MEF2C-mediated transcription" and "required for normal myogenesis" - no phosphorylation activity is described. PMID:15546612 shows MAML1 "recruits CycC:CDK8 to phosphorylate the Notch ICD" - meaning MAML1 brings the kinase to its substrate, it doesn't perform phosphorylation itself. UniProt also shows MAML1 is phosphorylated at Ser-314 and Ser-360 (substrate). MAML1's role in phosphorylation is regulatory (recruiting kinases), not catalytic.
Proposed replacements: regulation of protein kinase activity
