CC   -!- FUNCTION: Central receptor component of the translocase of the outer
CC       membrane of mitochondria (TOM) complex essential for the recognition
CC       and translocation of cytosolically synthesized mitochondrial
CC       preproteins (PubMed:40080546). Together with the peripheral receptor
CC       TOMM20, functions as the transit peptide receptor and facilitates the
CC       movement of preproteins into the translocation pore (PubMed:10982837).
CC       The TOM complex associates with the ion channel VDAC2 and PINK1 kinase
CC       at depolarized mitochondria, this interaction stabilizes PINK1 at the
CC       outer mitochondrial membrane and triggers downstream mitophagy by the
CC       recruitment of the E3 ubiquitin ligase PRKN (PubMed:40080546). Required
CC       for the translocation across the mitochondrial outer membrane of
CC       cytochrome P450 monooxygenases (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q75Q41, ECO:0000269|PubMed:10982837,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:40080546}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Part of the translocase of the outer mitochondrial membrane
CC       (TOM complex) consisting of at least TOMM5, TOMM6, TOMM7, TOMM20,
CC       TOMM22 and TOMM40 (PubMed:18331822, PubMed:40080546). TOMM70 may also
CC       be found in the TOM complex (PubMed:18331822). The TOM complex
CC       interacts with the VDAC2 homodimer (PubMed:40080546). Upon
CC       mitochondrial depolarization, the TOM-VDAC assembly interacts with
CC       PINK1; the interaction stabilizes PINK1 at the outer mitochondrial
CC       membrane and triggers downstream mitophagy (PubMed:40080546). Interacts
CC       with PPP2R2B (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q75Q41,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:18331822, ECO:0000269|PubMed:40080546}.

CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Ubiquitous.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: Requires the transmembrane domain (TMD), a short segment (the
CC       import sequence) in the cytoplasmic domain localizing separately from
CC       the TMD and the C-tail signal in the C-terminal domain for efficient

CC       unfolded mature imported proteins. Residues (49-71) of the cytoplasmic
CC       domain interacts with TOMM20 while the C-terminal segment (residues 63-
CC       82) binds presequence of preproteins. {ECO:0000250}.
