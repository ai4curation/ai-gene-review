Gene Description

SPDL1 (Spindly/CCDC99) encodes a coiled-coil protein that functions as a kinetochore-specific adaptor for cytoplasmic dynein during mitosis. It is recruited to the outer kinetochore via the RZZ complex (Rod/ZW10/Zwilch) in an Aurora B-regulated manner, and its C-terminal farnesylation is required for kinetochore targeting. At kinetochores, Spindly recruits the dynein-dynactin complex, which is essential for initial lateral kinetochore-microtubule interactions, chromosome congression, and correct spindle orientation. Spindly also drives kinetochore corona expansion by promoting oligomerization of RZZ-Spindly complexes, independent of dynein. Following microtubule attachment, dynein-mediated removal of Spindly from kinetochores is the critical event for spindle assembly checkpoint silencing, though in human cells (unlike Drosophila) Spindly is not absolutely required for checkpoint protein stripping. Spindly also functions as a dynein-dynactin cargo adaptor that converts dynein from a non-processive to a highly processive motor. Outside mitosis, Spindly interacts with the deubiquitinase USP45 and plays a role in cell migration. Spindly is monoubiquitinated and deubiquitinated by USP45.

## Functional Summary

A dynein-recruiting kinetochore adaptor that assembles a motor–regulatory module to capture and load dynein–dynactin at kinetochores and spindle poles.
