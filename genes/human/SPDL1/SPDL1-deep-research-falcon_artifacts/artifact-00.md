| Verified identity | **Human SPDL1** encodes **Spindly** (alias **CCDC99**), matching UniProt **Q96EA4**; a coiled-coil, kinetochore-localized **dynein/dynactin adaptor** in the **Spindly family**, functionally linked to the fibrous corona and spindle assembly checkpoint (SAC), not a catalytic enzyme or transporter (pqac-00000002, pqac-00000003, pqac-00000004) |
| Aliases / nomenclature | Common names in the literature include **Spindly**, **hSpindly**, **CCDC99**, and **spindle apparatus coiled-coil domain-containing protein 1**; older papers often use **Spindly/CCDC99** interchangeably (pqac-00000002, pqac-00000004, pqac-00000005) |
| Subcellular localization across cell cycle | **Interphase:** predominantly nuclear. **Prometaphase:** enriched at unattached kinetochores and spindle poles; in the absence of microtubules, expands into a **crescent/fibrous corona** configuration. **Metaphase/alignment:** normally removed from kinetochores as end-on microtubule attachment forms; persistent kinetochore retention is seen with dynein-recruitment-defective mutants (pqac-00000004, pqac-00000009, pqac-00000012) |
| Primary molecular function | SPDL1’s principal role is to **recruit and help activate dynein–dynactin at kinetochores/corona**, thereby promoting **chromosome congression**, **outer-kinetochore/corona remodeling**, and **SAC silencing** after microtubule attachment. It functions as a structural/transport adaptor rather than an enzyme (pqac-00000006, pqac-00000008, pqac-00000009, pqac-00000015) |
| Core mechanistic pathway | The pathway is typically **KMN/Ndc80 → RZZ recruitment → Spindly recruitment → dynein/dynactin loading → corona shedding / checkpoint-protein removal**. SPDL1 acts downstream of **RZZ** and couples kinetochore cargo to minus-end-directed transport and checkpoint shutdown (pqac-00000003, pqac-00000004, pqac-00000012, pqac-00000037) |
| Key complexes / partners: RZZ | SPDL1 binds the **ROD–ZW10–Zwilch (RZZ)** complex and forms the **RZZ–Spindly (RZZS)** building block of the fibrous corona. RZZ is required for efficient kinetochore localization of Spindly, while Spindly contributes to RZZ dynamics, corona expansion, and downstream dynein recruitment (pqac-00000000, pqac-00000007, pqac-00000016, pqac-00000019) |
| Key complexes / partners: dynein–dynactin | Spindly contains conserved motifs that engage **dynein LIC/heavy chain** and the **dynactin pointed-end complex** to assemble an active **dynein–dynactin–Spindly (DDS)** complex. Loss of SPDL1 prevents kinetochore localization of both dynein and dynactin (pqac-00000004, pqac-00000006, pqac-00000015, pqac-00000027) |
| Key complexes / partners: SAC effectors | SPDL1-dependent dynein recruitment enables removal/eviction of **Mad1/Mad2**, **Zwilch**, and **CENP-E** from attached kinetochores/corona; Spindly mutants defective in dynein recruitment retain checkpoint proteins and sustain checkpoint signaling (pqac-00000009, pqac-00000010, pqac-00000013) |
| Key complexes / partners: CENP-E and integrated corona platform | Recent work shows **CENP-E** and **RZZS** form an integrated platform for physiological dynein–dynactin accumulation at the corona; with active MPS1, CENP-E is dispensable for gross corona expansion but required for normal dynein loading, linking opposite-polarity motors on the corona scaffold (pqac-00000017, pqac-00000019, pqac-00000022) |
| Regulatory feature: farnesylation / CAAX | Human Spindly is **C-terminally farnesylated** via a **CAAX motif**; the ROD β-propeller contains a **farnesyl-binding site** needed for Spindly binding. Farnesylated Spindly is important for RZZ interaction and RZZS polymerization/corona assembly (pqac-00000016, pqac-00000020, pqac-00000023) |
| Regulatory feature: autoinhibition and opening | SPDL1 is **autoinhibited** in a closed conformation in which the **CC1 box** and **Spindly motif** are masked by intramolecular contacts involving residues ~**276–309**; opening exposes dynein/dynactin-binding surfaces. **RZZ binding alone is insufficient**, supporting a model in which kinetochores provide an additional activation cue/receptor (pqac-00000028, pqac-00000029, pqac-00000030, pqac-00000033) |
| Regulatory feature: MPS1 and corona assembly | **MPS1 (TTK)** promotes corona assembly mainly by **phosphorylating ROD**, stimulating RZZS polymerization and expansion; phosphomimetic ROD can bypass some MPS1 dependence. MPS1 also supports RZZS interactions with kinetochore receptors, integrating signaling with corona architecture (pqac-00000016, pqac-00000018, pqac-00000019, pqac-00000021) |
| Updated 2023–2024 mechanistic view | Expert synthesis now favors a model where kinetochore dynein/Spindly is **especially important for fibrous corona disassembly**, while phosphatase-dependent mechanisms clear outer-kinetochore SAC pools. Thus, SPDL1-dependent dynein function is increasingly viewed as **specialized and corona-centric**, not the sole SAC-silencing mechanism (pqac-00000035, pqac-00000038, pqac-00000040) |
| Cellular/experimental phenotypes when perturbed | SPDL1 depletion causes **prometaphase arrest**, delayed chromosome congression, spindle abnormalities, reduced kinetochore dynein, and defective checkpoint silencing; dominant-negative or dynein-recruitment-defective mutants remain kinetochore-bound and block removal of RZZ/MAD2 after biorientation (pqac-00000002, pqac-00000008, pqac-00000012, pqac-00000013) |
| Representative high-authority references | **Barisic et al., 2010, Mol Biol Cell** — https://doi.org/10.1091/mbc.e09-04-0356; **Gassmann et al., 2010, Genes Dev** — https://doi.org/10.1101/gad.1886810; **Raisch et al., 2022, EMBO J** — https://doi.org/10.15252/embj.2021110411; **d’Amico et al., 2022, J Cell Biol** — https://doi.org/10.1083/jcb.202206131; **Gassmann, 2023, J Cell Sci** — https://doi.org/10.1242/jcs.220269; **Cmentowski et al., 2023, EMBO J** — https://doi.org/10.15252/embj.2023114838; **Ide et al., 2023, Mol Biol Cell** — https://doi.org/10.1091/mbc.e23-04-0130 (pqac-00000002, pqac-00000004, pqac-00000016, pqac-00000029, pqac-00000037, pqac-00000019, pqac-00000035) |
| Human genetics / disease association highlight | A rare missense variant, **rs116483731 / p.Arg20Gln (Arg20Gln)** in **SPDL1**, is associated with **idiopathic pulmonary fibrosis (IPF)** risk. In discovery sequencing of **752 IPF cases vs 119,055 controls**, **OR 2.87** (95% CI **2.03–4.07**), **p = 2.4 × 10⁻⁷**; replicated in **FinnGen (1,028 cases, 196,986 controls)** with combined **p = 2.2 × 10⁻²⁰**. Reported allele frequencies were ~**2.2% vs 0.78%** in discovery and **6.9% vs 3.0%** in FinnGen cases vs controls (pqac-00000024, pqac-00000025, pqac-00000026) |
| Clinical/real-world interpretation of genetics | The IPF signal implicates **mitotic spindle / chromosome-segregation biology** in fibrosis susceptibility. A small clinical cohort study reported **no distinct phenotype** among SPDL1-variant carriers compared with other IPF patients, so current relevance is strongest for **risk biology and gene discovery**, not yet routine SPDL1-specific clinical management (pqac-00000025, pqac-00000026) |
