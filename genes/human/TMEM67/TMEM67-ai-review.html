Gene Description

TMEM67 (meckelin) is a transmembrane protein that localizes to the ciliary transition zone and is a component of the MKS complex (Meckel syndrome complex). It is essential for primary cilium formation and structure, acting in centrosome migration to the apical cell surface during early ciliogenesis. TMEM67 functions as a receptor or co-receptor in non-canonical Wnt signaling (interacting with WNT5A and ROR2), and plays a role in epithelial branching morphogenesis. It also participates in endoplasmic reticulum-associated degradation (ERAD) of misfolded surfactant protein C (SFTPC), where it acts as an adaptor linking the ER lumenal quality control machinery with the cytosolic degradation apparatus. Mutations in TMEM67 cause a spectrum of ciliopathies including Meckel-Gruber syndrome (MKS3), Joubert syndrome (JBTS6), COACH syndrome, nephronophthisis (NPHP11), Bardet-Biedl syndrome (BBS14), and RHYNS syndrome.
