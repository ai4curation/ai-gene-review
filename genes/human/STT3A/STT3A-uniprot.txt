CC   -!- FUNCTION: Catalytic subunit of the oligosaccharyl transferase (OST)
CC       complex that catalyzes the initial transfer of a defined glycan
CC       (Glc(3)Man(9)GlcNAc(2) in eukaryotes) from the lipid carrier dolichol-
CC       pyrophosphate to an asparagine residue within an Asn-X-Ser/Thr
CC       consensus motif in nascent polypeptide chains, the first step in
CC       protein N-glycosylation (PubMed:19167329, PubMed:31296534,
CC       PubMed:31831667, PubMed:34653363, PubMed:38670073, PubMed:39509507). N-
CC       glycosylation occurs cotranslationally and the complex associates with
CC       the Sec61 complex at the channel-forming translocon complex that
CC       mediates protein translocation across the endoplasmic reticulum (ER)
CC       (PubMed:19167329, PubMed:31296534, PubMed:31831667, PubMed:34653363,
CC       PubMed:38670073, PubMed:39509507). All subunits are required for a
CC       maximal enzyme activity (PubMed:19167329, PubMed:31831667,
CC       PubMed:34653363). This subunit contains the active site and the
CC       acceptor peptide and donor lipid-linked oligosaccharide (LLO) binding
CC       pockets (PubMed:19167329). STT3A is present in the majority of OST
CC       complexes and mediates cotranslational N-glycosylation of most sites on
CC       target proteins, while STT3B-containing complexes are required for
CC       efficient post-translational glycosylation and mediate glycosylation of
CC       sites that have been skipped by STT3A (PubMed:19167329,
CC       PubMed:38670073, PubMed:39509507). STT3A-containing OST-A complex is
CC       also required to prevent hyperglycosylation of some target proteins by
CC       preventing glycosylation of facultative sites before folding of target
CC       proteins is completed (PubMed:39509507). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:19167329,
