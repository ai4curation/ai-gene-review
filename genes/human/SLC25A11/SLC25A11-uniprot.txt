CC   -!- FUNCTION: Catalyzes the transport of 2-oxoglutarate (alpha-
CC       oxoglutarate) across the inner mitochondrial membrane in an
CC       electroneutral exchange for malate (PubMed:25637873, PubMed:38937634).
CC       Can also exchange 2-oxoglutarate for other dicarboxylic acids such as
CC       malonate, succinate, maleate and oxaloacetate, although with lower
CC       affinity (By similarity). Substrate exchange across the membrane occurs
CC       consecutively with one substrate being transported first, then
CC       dissociating from the substrate binding site before the second
CC       substrate binds for transport in the opposite direction
CC       (PubMed:38937634). Does not transport glutathione (PubMed:25637873). In
CC       addition can facilitate proton transport in the presence of
CC       protonophores such as long-chain fatty acids, contributing to
CC       mitochondrial uncoupling and regulation of mitochondrial energetic
CC       efficiency (By similarity). Contributes to several metabolic processes,
CC       including the malate-aspartate shuttle, the oxoglutarate/isocitrate
CC       shuttle, gluconeogenesis from lactate, and nitrogen metabolism (By
CC       similarity). Maintains mitochondrial fusion and fission events, and the
CC       organization and morphology of cristae (PubMed:21448454). Involved in
CC       the regulation of apoptosis (By similarity).
