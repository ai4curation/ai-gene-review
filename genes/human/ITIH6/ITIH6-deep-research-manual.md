# Manual literature synthesis for human ITIH6 (Q6UXX5)

## Scope

The configured automated deep-research providers were unavailable on 2026-07-18 (Falcon/Edison HTTP 402; Perplexity-lite HTTP 401). This synthesis therefore uses the reviewed human UniProt record, the local GOA export, cached primary publications, and the open-access full text of the first ITIH6-focused study (PMID:41109368; DOI:10.1016/j.ijbiomac.2025.148360).

## What is established

ITIH6 encodes a 1,313-amino-acid ITIH-family precursor. Its N terminus contains a predicted signal peptide followed by the characteristic VIT and vWA domains, while an ITI-heavy-chain C-terminal domain lies near the far C terminus. The 2025 study confirms the family placement and shows that ITIH6 is closest to ITIH5, but it also identifies a uniquely expanded central region between the vWA and C-terminal domains. [PMID:41109368, "ITIH6 contains the two canonical domains characteristic of ITIH proteins (VIT, vWA), but also harbors a large central region between the vWA domain and the C-terminus that is unique to ITIH6."]

The same study experimentally profiles ITIH6 expression in human tissues, with abundant protein in salivary gland, kidney, colon, and stomach and enrichment in glandular epithelial structures. [PMID:41109368, "Protein expression profiling of human tissue samples demonstrated that ITIH6 is expressed in a tissue-specific manner, with abundant expression in salivary glands, kidney, colon, and stomach, particularly within glandular structures of the digestive tract."] The study also associates higher tumor expression with favorable cholangiocarcinoma features, but it does not test a causal tumor-suppressor mechanism.

## Heavy-chain processing and hyaluronan boundary

Full-text sequence analysis identifies a conserved candidate cleavage motif (`DPHFVI`, residues 1,097–1,102) immediately upstream of the ITI_HC_C region. This is compatible with the processing logic of other ITIH precursors. It is not equivalent to evidence that ITIH6 is processed at that site or covalently coupled to bikunin.

No experiment in the 2025 paper demonstrates ITIH6 secretion, incorporation into a bikunin-containing complex, covalent linkage to chondroitin sulfate, transfer to hyaluronan, or hyaluronan binding. The discussion explicitly treats ITIH6 interaction with HA/PTX3 and ECM stabilization as a structurally motivated hypothesis and calls for future functional studies. Thus `hyaluronan metabolic process` remains plausible but unverified for ITIH6 and should not be promoted into a positive core function.

## Protease inhibition boundary

The protease-inhibitor activity of classical inter-alpha-inhibitor resides in the bikunin light chain, not in the homologous heavy chains. Direct chain analysis found a single 30-kDa inhibitory chain and noninhibitory heavy chains. [PMID:2476436, "Inter-alpha-trypsin inhibitor contains noninhibitory heavy chains of 65,000 and 70,000 Da"] ITIH6 itself has neither a bikunin/Kunitz inhibitor domain nor a direct inhibition assay. Its `serine-type endopeptidase inhibitor activity` IEA is therefore a complex-to-component or family-name overextension and should be removed.

## Interactions and localization

HuRI reports eight binary Y2H partners for ITIH6. The study is a high-quality systematic interaction map, but none of those pairs has been connected to an endogenous ITIH6 mechanism, and the generic `protein binding` term conveys no specific activity. The annotation is best retained only as over-annotated high-throughput evidence.

A clear signal peptide and reviewed UniProt secretion inference support broad extracellular localization. The exact extracellular compartment, mature processed form, binding partners, and whether the protein is released or matrix-associated remain unresolved.

## Synthesis

ITIH6 is a predicted secretory, tissue-restricted ITIH-family protein with canonical N-terminal family domains, a uniquely expanded central region, and a plausible heavy-chain cleavage motif. Its specific molecular activity and biological process are still unknown. The decisive missing experiments are endogenous secretion/processing analysis, direct bikunin or chondroitin-sulfate linkage mapping, and TSG-6-dependent hyaluronan-transfer or binding assays.
