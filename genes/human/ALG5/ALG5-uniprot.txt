CC   -!- FUNCTION: Dolichyl-phosphate beta-glucosyltransferase that operates in
CC       the biosynthetic pathway of dolichol-linked oligosaccharides, the
CC       glycan precursors employed in protein asparagine (N)-glycosylation. The
CC       assembly of dolichol-linked oligosaccharides begins on the cytosolic
CC       side of the endoplasmic reticulum membrane and finishes in its lumen.
CC       The sequential addition of sugars to dolichol pyrophosphate produces
CC       dolichol-linked oligosaccharides containing fourteen sugars, including
CC       two GlcNAcs, nine mannoses and three glucoses. Once assembled, the
CC       oligosaccharide is transferred from the lipid to nascent proteins by
CC       oligosaccharyltransferases. Dolichyl-phosphate beta-glucosyltransferase
CC       produces dolichyl beta-D-glucosyl phosphate/Dol-P-Glc, the glucose
CC       donor substrate used sequentially by ALG6, ALG8 and ALG10 to add
CC       glucose residues on top of the Man(9)GlcNAc(2)-PP-Dol structure. These
CC       are the three last steps in the biosynthetic pathway of dolichol-linked
CC       oligosaccharides to produce Glc(3)Man(9)GlcNAc(2)-PP-Dol. The enzyme is
CC       most probably active on the cytoplasmic side of the endoplasmic
CC       reticulum while its product Dol-P-Glc is the substrate for ALG6, ALG8
CC       and ALG11 in the lumen of the endoplasmic reticulum.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10359825, ECO:0000269|PubMed:35896117}.
