CC   -!- FUNCTION: Catalytic subunit of the glycosylphosphatidylinositol-anchor
CC       (GPI-anchor) transamidase (GPI-T) complex that catalyzes the formation
CC       of the linkage between a proprotein and a GPI-anchor and participates
CC       in GPI anchored protein biosynthesis (PubMed:10793132, PubMed:11483512,
CC       PubMed:12582175, PubMed:34576938, PubMed:35165458, PubMed:35551457,
CC       PubMed:37684232, PubMed:9356492). Recognizes diverse proproteins at a
CC       C-terminal signal peptide (CSP) region that lacks consensus sequence
CC       and replaces it with a GPI-anchor via a transamidation reaction
CC       (PubMed:35165458, PubMed:35551457, PubMed:37684232). Transamidation
CC       catalysis reaction follows a two-phase mechanism (PubMed:37684232). In
CC       the acyl-enzyme phase, the carbonyl group of the proproteins's omega-
CC       site undergoes a nucleophilic attack forming an enzyme-substrate
CC       thioester bond (PubMed:37684232). Followed by a general acid catalysis
CC       that allows CSP releasing, regenerating the carbonyl, and forming the
CC       acyl-enzyme intermediate (PubMed:37684232). In the GPI-anchor
CC       attachment phase, the amino group of the GPI-anchor's ethanolamine
CC       phosphate, the one on third mannose (EtNP3), mediates a nucleophilic
CC       attack on the carbonyl of the acyl-enzyme intermediate, replacing the
CC       CSP, allowing GPI-anchor attachment to the omega-residue, therefore
CC       forming the product and freeing the enzyme (PubMed:37684232).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10793132, ECO:0000269|PubMed:11483512,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:12582175, ECO:0000269|PubMed:34576938,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:35165458, ECO:0000269|PubMed:35551457,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:37684232, ECO:0000269|PubMed:9356492}.

CC   -!- ACTIVITY REGULATION: In the absence of proproteins substrates, exists
CC       in an inactive state with a disrupted catalytic site by an
CC       autoinhibitory loop (PubMed:37684232). The binding of proprotein
CC       substrates, particularly the CSP region, to GPI-T triggers concerted
CC       conformational changes that alleviate the inhibition by the
CC       autoinhibitory loop (PubMed:37684232). Meanwhile, proprotein residues
CC       near the omega- site induce the formation of a catalytic cleft for
CC       catalysis, following which the products are released and GPI-T reverts
CC       to the inactive state (PubMed:37684232). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:37684232}.
