Gene Description

Activator of 90 kDa heat shock protein ATPase homolog 1 (AHA1/AHSA1) is an HSP90 co-chaperone that stimulates the ATPase activity of HSP90, thereby accelerating its chaperone cycle. AHSA1 competes with inhibitory co-chaperones FNIP1 and TSC1 for HSP90 binding, providing reciprocal regulation of client protein chaperoning. Its N-terminal domain binds the middle domain of HSP90 while the C-terminal domain stabilizes the dimerized HSP90 N-terminal domains. Recent in vitro studies suggest that the N-terminal extension (M1-R16), unique to higher eukaryote Aha1 proteins, confers an autonomous holdase-like chaperone activity independent of HSP90, though the in vivo significance of this remains to be established.

AHSA1 (AHA1) is an HSP90 co-chaperone that stimulates the ATPase activity of HSP90, accelerating the HSP90 chaperone cycle.
