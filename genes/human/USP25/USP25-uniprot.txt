CC   -!- FUNCTION: Deubiquitinating enzyme that hydrolyzes ubiquitin moieties
CC       conjugated to substrates and thus, functions in various biological
CC       processes including inflammation and immune response (PubMed:29518389,
CC       PubMed:37683630). Modulates the Wnt/beta-catenin pathway by
CC       deubiquitinating and stabilizing tankyrases TNKS1 and TNKS2
CC       (PubMed:28619731, PubMed:30926243, PubMed:38875478). Regulates KEAP1-
CC       NRF2 axis in the defense against oxidative assaults by deubiquitinating
CC       KEAP1 and protecting it from degradation leading to degradation of the
CC       NRF2 transcription factor that is responsible for mounting an anti-
CC       oxidation gene expression program (PubMed:37339955). Positively
CC       regulates RNA virus-induced innate signaling by interacting with and
CC       deubiquitinating ERLIN1 and ERLIN2 (PubMed:37683630). In turn,
CC       restricts virus production by regulating cholesterol biosynthetic flux
CC       (PubMed:37683630). Acts as a negative regulator of interleukin-17-
CC       mediated signaling and inflammation through the removal of 'Lys-63'-
CC       linked ubiquitination of TRAF5 and TRAF6 (PubMed:23042150). Prevents
CC       the ubiquitination and degradation of TRAF3 to reduce the
CC       phosphorylation levels of JNK and P38, the secretion of IL-1B and to
CC       induce endotoxin tolerance (PubMed:30579117).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:23042150, ECO:0000269|PubMed:28619731,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:29518389, ECO:0000269|PubMed:30579117,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:30926243, ECO:0000269|PubMed:37339955,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:37683630, ECO:0000269|PubMed:38875478}.
