Gene Description

GDPD2 (Glycerophosphoinositol inositolphosphodiesterase GDPD2) is a membrane-bound enzyme that specifically hydrolyzes glycerophosphoinositol (GroPIns) into glycerol and inositol 1-phosphate. It functions as an ecto-enzyme with its catalytic domain oriented extracellularly, requiring millimolar Ca2+ as a cofactor. GDPD2 plays a critical role in promoting osteoblast differentiation and serves as a marker of osteoblast maturation. The protein localizes to the plasma membrane as a multi-pass membrane protein and colocalizes with actin filaments, suggesting involvement in cytoskeletal remodeling during cell differentiation.

Gene Function and Molecular Mechanisms

GDPD2 (glycerophosphodiester phosphodiesterase domain-containing protein 2), also known as GDE3, encodes an enzyme with glycerophosphoinositol inositolphosphodiesterase activity (EC 3.1.4.43) (lipidmaps.org) (pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov). This enzyme specifically hydrolyzes glycerophosphoinositol (GroPIns) into glycerol and inositol 1-phosphate, and notably does not act on related glycerophosphodiesters such as glycerophosphoinositol-4-phosphate, glycerophosphocholine, -ethanolamine, or -serine (lipidmaps.org) (pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov). GDPD2 functions as an ecto-enzyme, requiring millimolar Ca2+ as a cofactor, and its catalytic domain is oriented extracellularly (lipidmaps.org) (pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov). As a result, it hydrolyzes extracellular GroPIns without altering the intracellular pool (lipidmaps.org). A critical arginine residue (Arg-231) in the catalytic GP-PDE domain is required for activity, as mutation of this residue abolishes enzymatic function (pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov). Through its enzymatic action, GDPD2 is thought to modulate signaling pathways involving GroPIns, which has been implicated in cell growth and differentiation signals (pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov) (pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov). Consistently, GDPD2 activity has been linked to changes in cell morphology, suggesting downstream effects on cytoskeletal or membrane dynamics (pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov).

Cellular Localization and Subcellular Components

GDPD2 is a multi-pass membrane protein predominantly localized at the plasma membrane (lipidmaps.org). Topology studies indicate that it spans the membrane multiple times, anchoring the protein at the cell surface (lipidmaps.org). The catalytic domain resides on the extracellular side of the membrane, consistent with the finding that glycerophosphoinositol is hydrolyzed in the extracellular medium by overexpressed GDPD2 (lipidmaps.org) (pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov). In addition to the cell surface, GDPD2 has been observed in the cytoplasm associated with the cytoskeleton, co-localizing with actin filaments (lipidmaps.org). This suggests that GDPD2 may concentrate at sites of actin-rich membrane structures (e.g. ruffles or protrusions), potentially linking its enzymatic activity to cytoskeletal remodeling. Characteristic of this family, GDPD2 has several hydrophobic transmembrane segments near its N-terminus that anchor the protein in the membrane, with computational predictions and experimental data indicating multiple transmembrane passes (multi-pass membrane protein architecture) (lipidmaps.org). The N-terminus likely includes a signal peptide or leader sequence targeting the protein to the secretory pathway, ensuring the catalytic domain is presented in the luminal/extracellular space (lipidmaps.org). The active site of the GP-PDE domain requires divalent cations for catalysis – notably Ca2+ acts as a cofactor to facilitate bond cleavage (lipidmaps.org). An invariant arginine (Arg-231 in human GDPD2) within the domain is critical for catalysis (pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov), reflecting a conserved active-site motif important for substrate binding or phosphodiester bond breakage. Three isoforms of GDPD2 have been reported due to alternative splicing (590 amino acids in isoform 1; 539 aa in isoform 2; 460 aa in isoform 3) (lipidmaps.org). Metal-binding sites (for Ca2+) and conserved catalytic residues within the GP-PDE domain define the molecular function of GDPD2 (lipidmaps.org).
