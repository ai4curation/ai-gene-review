Gene Description

Glutathione synthetase (GSS) is an ATP-dependent ligase that catalyzes the second and final step of glutathione (GSH) biosynthesis, joining glycine to gamma-glutamyl-cysteine to form the tripeptide glutathione. GSS functions as a homodimer and requires magnesium as a cofactor. The enzyme belongs to the ATP-grasp superfamily and contains the glutathione synthase domain characteristic of the eukaryotic GSH synthase family. GSS operates in the cytosol where de novo GSH synthesis occurs. Glutathione is the most abundant intracellular thiol antioxidant and is essential for protection against oxidative damage, amino acid transport, detoxification of xenobiotics, and maintenance of protein sulfhydryl groups. GSS can also utilize 2-aminobutyrate as an alternative substrate to produce ophthalmate. Deficiency in GSS causes glutathione synthetase deficiency (GSSD), an autosomal recessive disorder characterized by 5-oxoprolinuria, metabolic acidosis, hemolytic anemia, and in severe cases, neurological dysfunction.

GSS catalyzes the ATP-dependent ligation of glycine to gamma-glutamyl-cysteine to form glutathione (gamma-glutamyl-cysteinyl-glycine), completing the second and final step of de novo glutathione biosynthesis. This is the defining enzymatic activity of the protein, supported by biochemical characterization (PMID:7646467), structural analysis (PMID:10369661), and phylogenetic conservation (IBA).
