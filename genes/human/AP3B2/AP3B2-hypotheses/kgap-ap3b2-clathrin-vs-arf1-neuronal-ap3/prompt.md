# AIGR Gene Hypothesis Deep Research

Evaluate one focused AI Gene Review knowledge-gap hypothesis.

## Target Gene

- Organism code: human
- Taxon: Homo sapiens (NCBITaxon:9606)
- Gene symbol: AP3B2
- UniProt accession: Q13367

## Seed Hypothesis

AP3B2-containing neuronal AP-3 uses Arf1-driven, clathrin-independent coat
initiation for at least a subset of neuronal AP-3 carriers, while AP3B2 clathrin
binding is accessory or context-dependent rather than the defining mechanism for
all AP3B2-dependent vesicle formation.

## Context

- The gene review accepts AP3B2 as the beta subunit of the neuronal AP-3 adaptor
  complex and includes clathrin-cargo adaptor activity from older AP-3
  biochemical evidence.
- Newer structural work supports Arf1-mediated clathrin-independent AP-3 coat
  initiation, creating a curation boundary around when clathrin binding is
  mechanistically central.
- The review also asks how AP3B2-containing AP-3 differs from AP3B1-containing
  ubiquitous AP-3 at shared endosomal compartments and for neuronal cargoes.

## Research Objective

Determine whether the hypothesis is supported, partially supported, unresolved,
or refuted. Focus on:

1. Evidence for AP-3 clathrin binding through beta3 hinge/appendage motifs.
2. Evidence for Arf1/lipid-driven clathrin-independent AP-3 coat initiation.
3. Whether neuronal AP3B2 AP-3 differs from ubiquitous AP3B1 AP-3 in cargoes,
   route, brain-cell context, or activity-dependent vesicle pools.
4. GO curation consequences for clathrin adaptor activity versus broader AP-3
   cargo adaptor/coat assembly activity.

Use primary literature and compact domain/motif checks where useful. Avoid broad
exploratory workflows; a concise evidence review plus targeted motif/domain and
paralog interpretation is sufficient.

## Required Output

Include:

- Executive judgment.
- Evidence matrix with citations, evidence type, claim tested, finding, context,
  confidence, and limitations.
- GO curation implications for AP3B2 molecular function, vesicle-mediated
  transport process terms, clathrin-dependent annotations, and neuronal AP-3
  cargo specificity.
- Remaining decisive experiments.
