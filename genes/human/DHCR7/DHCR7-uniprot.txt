CC   -!- FUNCTION: Oxidoreductase that catalyzes the last step of the
CC       cholesterol synthesis pathway, which transforms cholesta-5,7-dien-
CC       3beta-ol (7-dehydrocholesterol,7-DHC) into cholesterol by reducing the
CC       C7-C8 double bond of its sterol core (PubMed:25637936, PubMed:38297129,
CC       PubMed:38297130, PubMed:9465114, PubMed:9634533). Can also metabolize
CC       cholesta-5,7,24-trien-3beta-ol (7-dehydrodemosterol, 7-DHD) to
CC       desmosterol, which is then metabolized by the Delta(24)-sterol
CC       reductase (DHCR24) to cholesterol (By similarity). Modulates
CC       ferroptosis (a form of regulated cell death driven by iron-dependent
CC       lipid peroxidation) through the metabolic breakdown of the anti-
CC       ferroptotic metabolites 7-DHC and 7-DHD which, when accumulated, divert
CC       the propagation of peroxyl radical-mediated damage from phospholipid
CC       components to its sterol core, protecting plasma and mitochondrial
CC       membranes from phospholipid autoxidation (PubMed:38297129,
CC       PubMed:38297130). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O88455,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:25637936, ECO:0000269|PubMed:38297129,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:38297130, ECO:0000269|PubMed:9465114,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:9634533}.
CC   -!- FUNCTION: Component of the microsomal antiestrogen binding site (AEBS),
CC       a multiproteic complex at the ER membrane that consists of an
CC       association between cholestenol Delta-isomerase/EBP and DHCR7
CC       (PubMed:15175332, PubMed:20615952). This complex is responsible for
CC       cholesterol-5,6-epoxide hydrolase (ChEH) activity, which consists in
CC       the hydration of cholesterol-5,6-epoxides (5,6-EC) into cholestane-
CC       3beta,5alpha,6beta-triol (CT) (PubMed:20615952). The precise role of
CC       each component of this complex has not been described yet
CC       (PubMed:20615952). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15175332,
