CC   -!- FUNCTION: Facilitates flavin adenine dinucleotide (FAD) translocation
CC       across the mitochondrial inner membrane into the mitochondrial matrix
CC       where it acts as a redox cofactor to assist flavoenzyme activities in
CC       fundamental metabolic processes including fatty acid beta-oxidation,
CC       amino acid and choline metabolism as well as mitochondrial electron
CC       transportation. In particular, provides FAD to DLD dehydrogenase of the
CC       glycine cleavage system, part of mitochondrial one-carbon metabolic
CC       pathway involved in neural tube closure in early embryogenesis.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:16165386, ECO:0000269|PubMed:29666258,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:35727412}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY:
CC       Reaction=FAD(in) = FAD(out); Xref=Rhea:RHEA:76535, ChEBI:CHEBI:57692;
CC         Evidence={ECO:0000269|PubMed:16165386, ECO:0000269|PubMed:35727412};
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Mitochondrion inner membrane
CC       {ECO:0000305|PubMed:29666258}; Multi-pass membrane protein
CC       {ECO:0000255}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Ubiquitous.
CC   -!- DISEASE: Exercise intolerance, riboflavin-responsive (RREI)
CC       [MIM:616839]: A riboflavin-responsive form of exercise intolerance, a
CC       condition characterized by failure to maintain an expected level of
CC       force during sustained or repeated muscle contraction, resulting in an
CC       overwhelming sense of tiredness, lack of energy and feeling of
CC       exhaustion. RREI transmission pattern is consistent with autosomal
CC       recessive inheritance. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:26933868,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:35727412}. Note=The disease is caused by variants
CC       affecting the gene represented in this entry.
CC   -!- DISEASE: Neural tube defects (NTD) [MIM:182940]: Congenital
CC       malformations of the central nervous system and adjacent structures
CC       related to defective neural tube closure during the first trimester of
CC       pregnancy. Failure of neural tube closure can occur at any level of the
CC       embryonic axis. Common NTD forms include anencephaly, myelomeningocele
CC       and spina bifida, which result from the failure of fusion in the
CC       cranial and spinal region of the neural tube. NTDs have a
CC       multifactorial etiology encompassing both genetic and environmental
CC       components. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:29666258}. Note=Disease susceptibility
CC       is associated with variants affecting the gene represented in this
CC       entry.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the mitochondrial carrier (TC 2.A.29) family.
CC       {ECO:0000305}.
CC   -!- CAUTION: Initially postulated to transport folate based on
CC       complementation evidence, but it was latter shown to rather function as
CC       a FAD transporter indirectly affecting folate levels and folate-
CC       mediated one-carbon metabolism in mitochondria.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15140890, ECO:0000269|PubMed:35727412}.
