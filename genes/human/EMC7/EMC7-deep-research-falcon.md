## Comprehensive functional annotation report: **EMC7** (human; UniProt **Q9NPA0**) 

### 0) Target verification (mandatory disambiguation)
The literature retrieved and analyzed consistently uses **EMC7** to denote **endoplasmic reticulum membrane protein complex subunit 7** in **Homo sapiens**, a subunit of the ER membrane protein complex (EMC). This matches the UniProt description for Q9NPA0 (ER membrane protein complex subunit 7; EMC7 family; β-sandwich-like domain and single-pass TM region inferred/observed in structures). No competing human gene/protein with the same symbol was encountered in the evidence set. (pleiner2020structuralbasisfor pages 1-3, li2024structuralinsightsinto pages 3-5)

### 1) Key concepts and definitions (current understanding)

#### 1.1 The ER membrane protein complex (EMC)
The **endoplasmic reticulum membrane protein complex (EMC)** is a conserved, abundant **ER-resident membrane protein biogenesis factor**. Reviews and structural work converge on the model that EMC is an **insertase and/or intramembrane chaperone** that supports insertion and maturation of particular classes of membrane proteins, especially those with **challenging transmembrane domains (TMDs)** or topology constraints. (Publication dates/URLs: Chitwood & Hegde 2019-05, https://doi.org/10.1016/j.tcb.2019.01.007; Hegde 2022-06, https://doi.org/10.1146/annurev-biochem-032620-104553) (chitwood2019theroleof pages 1-2, hegde2022thefunctionstructure pages 4-6)

Mechanistically, EMC is described as facilitating **energy-independent** TMD insertion along a path from a **cytosolic vestibule** into an **intramembrane groove** that is open to lipid, thereby lowering the energetic barrier for insertion/topogenesis of specific substrates. (O’Donnell et al. 2020-05, https://doi.org/10.7554/eLife.57887) (odonnell2020thearchitectureof pages 14-15)

#### 1.2 Tail-anchored proteins and EMC-mediated sorting/topology
**Tail-anchored (TA) proteins** are defined by a single **C-terminal TMD** followed by a short C-terminal tail (typically ≤30–40 aa) and require **post-translational targeting/insertion**. A key current concept is that EMC is a major insertase for **low-hydrophobicity** TA proteins that are not handled efficiently by the canonical GET/TRC pathway. (Pleiner et al. 2023-05, https://doi.org/10.1083/jcb.202212007) (pleiner2023aselectivityfilter pages 1-2)

Pleiner et al. (2023) identified a **charge-based selectivity filter** at the entrance of the EMC hydrophilic vestibule: **positively charged residues** repel positively charged soluble segments, thereby (i) limiting misinsertion of mitochondrial TA proteins into the ER and (ii) helping enforce the **“positive-inside rule”** for multipass proteins by limiting inappropriate translocation of positively charged domains. (pleiner2023aselectivityfilter pages 10-11)

#### 1.3 Multipass proteins and topology rectification
A major 2023–2024 development is the idea that EMC supports **post-translational insertion** of certain **C-terminal/terminal TMDs** in multipass proteins to “rectify” topology after release from the ribosome–Sec61 machinery. Wu et al. reported that this sequential co- and post-translational mechanism may apply to **~250 multipass proteins**, including neurotransmission-relevant pentameric ion channel subunits. (Wu et al. published online 2023-11; issue Jan 2024, https://doi.org/10.1038/s41594-023-01120-6) (wu2024emcrectifiesthe pages 1-2)

### 2) EMC7 protein: subcellular localization, topology, and structural features

#### 2.1 Where EMC7 is in the cell
EMC7 functions at the **endoplasmic reticulum membrane** as part of the EMC. Structural work places EMC7 within the EMC’s lumenal/dynamic region, consistent with an ER lumen-exposed domain coupled to a membrane anchor. (Pleiner et al. 2020-07, https://doi.org/10.1126/science.abb5008) (pleiner2020structuralbasisfor pages 1-3)

A distinct, recently highlighted context is **mitochondria–ER contact sites**: Li et al. (2024) observed EMC–VDAC interactions and validated in cells that **EMC7–VDAC1 association** yields BiFC signals localized to mitochondria–ER contact sites. (Li et al. 2024-03, https://doi.org/10.18632/aging.205660) (li2024structuralinsightsinto pages 3-5)

#### 2.2 Topology and domain architecture
Multiple structures support that EMC7 contains a **lumenal domain** and a **single-pass transmembrane helix (TMH)** that can be **flexible/disordered** in some states.
* Pleiner et al. (2020) resolved EMC7 within the **L-shaped lumenal region** of human EMC and noted weak density for a **putative/flexible TM** associated with lumenal subunits. (pleiner2020structuralbasisfor pages 1-3)
* Yeast EMC structure and glycosylation mapping place Emc7 on the **lumenal side**, with N-linked glycosylations supporting luminal topology (used as a topology anchor across species). (Bai et al. 2020-06, https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-020-2389-3) (bai2020structureofthe pages 2-4)

#### 2.3 EMC7 as a dynamic regulatory element at the insertase vestibule
Hegde’s synthesis of EMC architecture describes a **dynamic front subdomain** formed by EMC4/EMC7/(possibly EMC10), contributing to a more mobile region adjacent to the insertion vestibule. (Hegde 2022-06, https://doi.org/10.1146/annurev-biochem-032620-104553) (hegde2022thefunctionstructure pages 13-14)

A 2024 structural study further emphasizes EMC7’s **TMH and C-terminal cytosolic loop** as a mobile element at or near the vestibule entrance, positioned to influence substrate engagement/entry. (Li et al. 2024-03, https://doi.org/10.18632/aging.205660) (li2024structuralinsightsinto pages 7-10)

### 3) Molecular function of EMC7 (what it does)

#### 3.1 Primary function: non-enzymatic subunit supporting EMC-mediated membrane protein biogenesis
No enzymatic catalytic activity has been demonstrated for EMC7; instead, its primary molecular function is best supported as:
1) **structural participation** in the EMC assembly (especially lumenal/dynamic region), and
2) **regulatory/assisting roles** in client insertion or EMC conformational states, mediated via its TMH and cytosolic loop.

This framing is consistent with EMC conceptual reviews describing EMC subunits as forming an insertase/chaperone machine in which peripheral subunits can modulate substrate handling and complex stability. (Chitwood & Hegde 2019-05; Pleiner 2020-07; Hegde 2022-06) (chitwood2019theroleof pages 1-2, pleiner2020structuralbasisfor pages 1-3, hegde2022thefunctionstructure pages 4-6)

#### 3.2 Direct EMC7 perturbation evidence: EMC7 TMH/loop required for client biogenesis
Li et al. (2024) directly tested EMC7 function using an **EMC7 truncation** (EMC7(1–156)) lacking the **TMH and C-terminal cytosolic loop** and found defects in EMC-dependent client reporter stability:
* EMC-dependent: **SQS(378–410)** reporter and **SOAT1** reporter
* EMC-independent control: **ASGR** reporter
Signals were quantified using **RFP normalized to GFP** (a fluorescent client reporter stability assay). This supports a mechanistic requirement of EMC7’s membrane anchor/cytosolic loop for EMC client insertion/biogenesis. (Li et al. 2024-03, https://doi.org/10.18632/aging.205660) (li2024structuralinsightsinto pages 21-25)

### 4) Interaction partners and complexes involving EMC7

#### 4.1 EMC7–VDAC1 interaction at mitochondria–ER contacts (major 2024 advance)
Li et al. (2024) reported cryo-EM structures of apo EMC and EMC bound to **VDAC1** (a mitochondrial outer membrane β-barrel channel). Key EMC7-specific interaction findings:
* EMC7’s TMH is **disordered in apo EMC** but becomes **ordered** in the EMC–VDAC1 complex. (li2024structuralinsightsinto pages 3-5)
* EMC7 contributes **~one-third** of the EMC–VDAC1 interface; the buried interface is ~**2,336 Å²** and the complex is 1:1. (li2024structuralinsightsinto pages 3-5)
* In-cell validation by **NanoBiT** and **BiFC** supports EMC7–VDAC1 association; BiFC signals localize to **mitochondria–ER contact sites**. (li2024structuralinsightsinto pages 3-5)
* Cryo-EM quantitative statistics: apo EMC **3.47 Å** (693,381 particles) and EMC–VDAC1 **3.32 Å** (455,504 particles), with PDB/EMDB depositions (PDB 8J0N/8J0O; EMD-35906/35907). (li2024structuralinsightsinto pages 21-25)

These results support a model in which EMC7 is a key structural mediator linking EMC to a mitochondria-associated partner and potentially modulating EMC’s functional state at contact sites. (li2024structuralinsightsinto pages 3-5)

#### 4.2 EMC7 in viral entry: Rab7 and syntaxin18 (host-factor role)
Bagchi et al. (2020) showed that **siRNA depletion of EMC7** reduces **SV40 infection** and that expression of an **siRNA-resistant EMC7** fully rescues the infection defect, establishing EMC7 as a specific host factor. (Bagchi et al. 2020-02, https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-020-14967-w) (bagchi2020selectiveemcsubunits pages 1-2)

Mechanistically, EMC7 (with EMC4) was reported to:
* engage late endosome-associated **Rab7** to stabilize **late endosome–ER** contacts, and
* bind **syntaxin18**, an ER fusion machinery component required for SV40 arrival to the ER,
thereby acting as a tether facilitating LE-to-ER targeting during viral entry. (bagchi2020selectiveemcsubunits pages 1-2)

### 5) Pathways, client proteins, and biological processes linked to EMC7 via EMC function

#### 5.1 Cholesterol homeostasis via EMC clients SQS and SOAT1
A key real-world-relevant EMC client axis is cholesterol metabolism. Volkmar et al. (2019) reported that EMC deficiency compromises biogenesis of sterol-related enzymes:
* **Squalene synthase (SQS/FDFT1)** (a tail-anchored ER enzyme) and
* **SOAT1** (a multipass ER enzyme involved in cholesterol esterification),
leading to altered cholesterol tolerance/homeostasis. (Volkmar et al. 2019-01, https://doi.org/10.1242/jcs.223453) (volkmar2019theermembrane pages 1-3, volkmar2019theermembrane pages 14-16)

While Volkmar et al. do not assign this specifically to EMC7 alone, Li et al. (2024) used **SQS and SOAT1** as EMC-dependent readouts and showed EMC7 structural elements (TMH/cytosolic loop) are required for their reporter stability, supporting EMC7’s contribution to this pathway through EMC client insertion. (li2024structuralinsightsinto pages 21-25)

#### 5.2 TA protein sorting and prevention of misinsertion
Pleiner et al. (2023) provides a mechanistic explanation for how EMC prevents mistargeted TA proteins from entering the ER: a **positively charged selectivity filter** at the vestibule entrance rejects substrates with incompatible charge properties, limiting organelle cross-contamination and enforcing topology rules. Although the key residues are in EMC3 in that work, the model explicitly implicates dynamic TMs including **EMC7** in creating a protected insertion environment. (Pleiner et al. 2023-05, https://doi.org/10.1083/jcb.202212007) (pleiner2023aselectivityfilter pages 10-11)

#### 5.3 Multipass protein topogenesis and neurologically important receptor/channel families
Wu et al. (2024) highlighted EMC’s role in post-translational insertion of terminal TMDs, with potential application to ~**250** multipass proteins and explicit relevance to **pentameric ion channels** critical for neurotransmission (e.g., GABAA receptor subunits). (Wu et al. 2023-11 online / 2024 issue, https://doi.org/10.1038/s41594-023-01120-6) (wu2024emcrectifiesthe pages 1-2)

A complementary structural 2023 study captured an EMC–client complex in the context of calcium channel biogenesis: Chen et al. (2023) reported cryo-EM of human **CaV1.2–CaVβ3** bound to EMC, supporting a model in which EMC can act as a **holdase** in an assembly intermediate. EMC7 is part of the lumenal assembly included in the multibody refinement strategy. (Chen et al. 2023-07, https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-023-06175-5) (chen2023emcchaperone–cavstructure pages 1-3, chen2023emcchaperone–cavstructure pages 18-19)

### 6) Recent developments (prioritizing 2023–2024)

#### 6.1 2023: charge-based selectivity filter and topology enforcement
Pleiner et al. (2023) refined EMC substrate discrimination: charge repulsion at the vestibule entrance functions as a **selectivity filter**, limiting misinsertion of mitochondrial TAs and helping enforce the **positive-inside rule** for certain multipass substrates. (https://doi.org/10.1083/jcb.202212007; 2023-05) (pleiner2023aselectivityfilter pages 1-2, pleiner2023aselectivityfilter pages 10-11)

#### 6.2 2023–2024: post-translational topology rectification for terminal TMDs
Wu et al. (2024) showed that EMC can insert TMDs near the C-terminus **post-translationally**, suggesting a general route for topology rectification and escape from quality control for a large subset of multipass proteins (~250). (https://doi.org/10.1038/s41594-023-01120-6; published online 2023-11-13) (wu2024emcrectifiesthe pages 1-2)

#### 6.3 2024: EMC–VDAC1 contact-site interaction with strong EMC7 contribution
Li et al. (2024) delivered a direct EMC7-specific breakthrough: EMC7 contributes ~1/3 of the EMC–VDAC1 interface, with ordered EMC7 TM density emerging only in the VDAC-bound state, and cell assays placing EMC7–VDAC1 association at mitochondria–ER contact sites. (https://doi.org/10.18632/aging.205660; 2024-03) (li2024structuralinsightsinto pages 3-5)

### 7) Current applications and real-world implementations

1) **Host–pathogen biology (viral entry):** EMC7 is an experimentally validated host factor for SV40 infection, acting with EMC4 to facilitate late endosome-to-ER targeting via Rab7 and syntaxin18 interactions; this provides a concrete, implemented model system for EMC7 function in inter-organelle trafficking. (Bagchi et al. 2020-02, https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-020-14967-w) (bagchi2020selectiveemcsubunits pages 1-2)

2) **Membrane contact sites and mitochondrial physiology:** The EMC–VDAC1 complex (validated structurally and in cells) suggests a role for EMC7-containing EMC assemblies at **mitochondria–ER contact sites**, a major organizational hub for lipid exchange, calcium signaling, and metabolic coordination (interpretation grounded in contact-site localization of EMC7–VDAC1 BiFC). (Li et al. 2024-03, https://doi.org/10.18632/aging.205660) (li2024structuralinsightsinto pages 3-5)

3) **Cholesterol metabolism readouts and membrane enzyme maturation:** SQS and SOAT1 are widely used EMC-dependent client readouts in sterol homeostasis studies; EMC7 elements required for client stability connect EMC7 to this physiologic axis through EMC. (Volkmar et al. 2019-01, https://doi.org/10.1242/jcs.223453; Li et al. 2024-03, https://doi.org/10.18632/aging.205660) (volkmar2019theermembrane pages 14-16, li2024structuralinsightsinto pages 21-25)

4) **Structural biology as functional inference:** High-resolution EMC structures (apo and partner-bound) are now routinely leveraged to infer how specific subunits (including EMC7) tune insertase states or client interfaces; Li et al. (2024) exemplifies this with state-dependent EMC7 ordering and quantitative cryo-EM statistics. (li2024structuralinsightsinto pages 21-25)

### 8) Expert opinions / authoritative synthesis

* **EMC as an insertase plus broader membrane chaperone:** Reviews emphasize that EMC’s insertase activity (Oxa1 superfamily) provides a unifying explanation for diverse phenotypes; however, validated direct substrates remain relatively limited in number compared with the breadth of observed cellular effects, suggesting indirect consequences and/or multiple functional modes (insertase vs chaperone/assembly). (Hegde 2022-06, https://doi.org/10.1146/annurev-biochem-032620-104553) (hegde2022thefunctionstructure pages 19-20, hegde2022thefunctionstructure pages 4-6)
* **Peripheral vs core subunits:** EMC7 is typically presented as more **peripheral** than the EMC3/EMC6 insertase core, but still functionally consequential—consistent with recent EMC7 perturbation evidence (client insertion assays and viral tethering). (Chitwood & Hegde 2019-05; Li 2024-03; Bagchi 2020-02) (chitwood2019theroleof pages 2-4, li2024structuralinsightsinto pages 21-25, bagchi2020selectiveemcsubunits pages 1-2)

### 9) Relevant statistics and recent quantitative data

* **Membrane proteins are ~20% of the human proteome**, motivating dedicated biogenesis factors like EMC. (Pleiner et al. 2023; Chitwood & Hegde 2019) (pleiner2023aselectivityfilter pages 1-2, chitwood2019theroleof pages 1-2)
* **~250 multipass proteins** may use EMC-mediated post-translational insertion of terminal TMDs, per Wu et al. (2024). (wu2024emcrectifiesthe pages 1-2)

  * apo EMC: **3.47 Å** with **693,381 particles**
  * EMC–VDAC1: **3.32 Å** with **455,504 particles**

### 12) Evidence-backed functional annotation statement (recommended)
**EMC7 (Q9NPA0)** is a **non-enzymatic, single-pass ER membrane subunit** of the **endoplasmic reticulum membrane protein complex (EMC)**. Its principal role is to support **membrane protein biogenesis/topogenesis** as part of the EMC insertase/chaperone machine. EMC7 contributes to the EMC’s **lumenal/dynamic region** and, through its **transmembrane helix and C-terminal cytosolic loop**, influences EMC client insertion efficiency (e.g., sterol enzymes **SQS** and **SOAT1**) and participates in distinct EMC interaction states, including a strong structural interaction with **VDAC1** at **mitochondria–ER contact sites**. EMC7 also has an EMC-independent/auxiliary role in **inter-organelle tethering** during **SV40 viral entry**, via functional engagement with **Rab7** and **syntaxin18**. (pleiner2020structuralbasisfor pages 1-3, li2024structuralinsightsinto pages 21-25, li2024structuralinsightsinto pages 3-5, bagchi2020selectiveemcsubunits pages 1-2)
