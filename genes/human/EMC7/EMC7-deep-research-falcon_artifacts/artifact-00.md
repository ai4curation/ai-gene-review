| Client proteins / biogenesis roles | EMC7 has **no known enzymatic activity**; its primary function is **structural/regulatory within EMC-mediated membrane protein biogenesis**. The strongest EMC7-specific functional test from 2024 showed that deleting the **EMC7 TMH + C-terminal cytosolic loop** impairs maturation of EMC-dependent reporters for **SQS** and **SOAT1**, but not an EMC-independent control (**ASGR**), indicating those EMC7 elements help support client insertion/biogenesis. EMC-wide studies implicate EMC in insertion of low-hydrophobicity **tail-anchored proteins** and selected **multipass proteins**; EMC also acts as a holdase/chaperone in a CaV assembly intermediate. | Pathways / phenotypes | EMC7 functions in the **EMC pathway for ER membrane protein insertion/topogenesis**, especially for low-hydrophobicity TA proteins and selected multipass proteins. Pleiner 2023 established a **charge-based selectivity filter** in EMC that rejects some mitochondrial TA proteins and enforces the **positive-inside rule**; dynamic TMs including EMC7 likely help create the protected environment for insertion. Wu 2024 extended EMC function to **post-translational rectification of terminal TMD topology** in multipass proteins, including neurotransmission-relevant pentameric ion channel subunits. EMC7-specific perturbation also causes a **host-factor phenotype in SV40 infection** via LE–ER tethering/transport.
