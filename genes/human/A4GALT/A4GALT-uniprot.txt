CC   -!- FUNCTION: Glycosyltransferase involved in biosynthesis of P1, P(k) and
CC       the rare NOR antigens of P1PK histo-blood group system
CC       (PubMed:10747952, PubMed:10748143, PubMed:22965229, PubMed:26773500,
CC       PubMed:29572228, PubMed:33460651). Catalyzes the transfer of galactose
CC       from UDP-alpha-D-galactose in an alpha1,4 linkage to
CC       lactosylceramide/beta-D-galactosyl-(1->4)-beta-D-glucosyl-(1<->1)-
CC       ceramide(d18:1(4E)) to produce globotriaosylceramide/globoside Gb3Cer
CC       (d18:1(4E)), also known as P(k) antigen or CD77 (PubMed:10748143,
CC       PubMed:10747952, PubMed:26773500, PubMed:29572228, PubMed:22965229).
CC       Globoside Gb3Cer/P(k)/CD77 is a root structure in biosynthesis pathway
CC       of neutral glycosphingolipids of the globo-series. Broadly expressed in
CC       cell membranes of immune cells and other tissues, Gb3Cer/P(k)/CD77
CC       antigen may participate in mechanisms that regulate cell-cell
CC       interactions and cell differentiation. In germinal center B cells,
CC       Gb3Cer/CD77 interacts with CD19 to drive centroblast-to-centrocyte
CC       transition, B cell receptor complex assembly and T follicular helper
CC       cell differentiation. In epithelium, Gb3Cer regulates CDH1/E-cadherin-
CC       dependent cell-cell adhesion counteracting epithelial-to-mesenchymal
CC       transition (By similarity) (PubMed:29572228). Synthesizes P1 glycan
CC       epitope (alpha-D-Gal-(1->4)-beta-D-Gal-(1->4)-GlcNAc-R) by transferring
CC       a galactose moiety to paragloboside nLc4 or to complex-type N-glycans
CC       (PubMed:26773500, PubMed:29572228, PubMed:33460651). Generates the rare
CC       NOR1 and NOR2 antigens which carry a Gal(alpha1->4)GalNAc terminal
CC       moiety and are both derived from Gb4Cer precursor (PubMed:22965229,
CC       PubMed:26773500, PubMed:33460651). Also able to transfer galactose to
CC       galactosylceramide/beta-D-Gal-(1<->1')-Cer (PubMed:10748143).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q67BJ4, ECO:0000269|PubMed:10747952,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:10748143, ECO:0000269|PubMed:22965229,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:26773500, ECO:0000269|PubMed:29572228,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:33460651}.

CC       Shiga toxins (also called verotoxins), as a way to enter the cell,

CC   -!- POLYMORPHISM: Genetic variation in A4GALT is responsible for the P1PK
CC       system blood group phenotypes [MIM:111400]. Different combinations or
CC       absence of the P blood group system antigens define 5 different
CC       phenotypes: P1, P2, P1(k), P2(k), and p. Genetic variation in A4GALT
CC       determines the p phenotype, which is rare and does not express any
CC       antigens. It is also known as null phenotype; p individuals have
CC       antibodies against P, P1 and P(k) antigens in their sera. These
CC       antibodies are clinically important because they can cause severe
CC       transfusion reactions and miscarriage (PubMed:10993874,
CC       PubMed:11896312). Genetic variation in A4GALT is also responsible for
CC       the NOR polyagglutination syndrome [MIM:111400]. Polyagglutination is
CC       the occurrence of red cell agglutination by virtually all human sera,
CC       but not by autologous serum or sera from newborns, creating a risk of
CC       complications during transfusions of NOR erythrocytes. It is caused by
CC       the unusual Gal(alpha1-4)GalNAc glycolipid epitope (PubMed:22965229).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10993874, ECO:0000269|PubMed:11896312,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:22965229}.
