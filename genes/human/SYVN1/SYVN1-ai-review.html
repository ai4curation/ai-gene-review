Gene Description

SYVN1 (synoviolin/HRD1) encodes E3 ubiquitin-protein ligase synoviolin, a key component of the ER-associated degradation (ERAD) pathway. SYVN1 is an ER membrane-resident multi-pass transmembrane protein with a cytoplasmic RING-type zinc finger domain that catalyzes the transfer of ubiquitin from E2 conjugating enzymes to misfolded ER substrates, targeting them for proteasomal degradation. It functions as part of the HRD1 complex (with SEL1L, FAM8A1, HERPUD1, OS9, DERL1/2, and UBE2J1) in the quality control of ER proteins. SYVN1 also ubiquitinates non-ERAD substrates including p53/TP53, IRE1alpha, and misfolded Pael receptor (GPR37). It protects cells from ER stress-induced apoptosis and is upregulated in rheumatoid arthritis synovial tissue.

Core Functions

SYVN1/HRD1 is the central E3 ubiquitin-protein ligase of the ERAD pathway. Its RING-H2 zinc finger domain catalyzes K48-linked polyubiquitination of misfolded ER substrates (including TCR-alpha, CD3-delta, Pael-R, MHC class I heavy chains, and IRE1alpha), targeting them for VCP/p97-dependent retrotranslocation and proteasomal degradation. SYVN1 also forms the retrotranslocation channel and protects cells from ER stress-induced apoptosis.

      physical interaction with the misfolded Pael receptor (PMID:17059562) is
      substrate recognition for ERAD-mediated ubiquitylation and degradation,
      not chaperone-mediated unfolded protein binding. The term GO:0051082 is
      being obsoleted (go-ontology#30962) because it conflates substrate
