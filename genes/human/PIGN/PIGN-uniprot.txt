CC   -!- FUNCTION: Ethanolamine phosphate transferase that catalyzes an
CC       ethanolamine phosphate (EtNP) transfer from phosphatidylethanolamine
CC       (PE) to the 2-OH position of the first alpha-1,4-linked mannose of a 2-
CC       acyl-6-[alpha-D-mannosyl-(1->6)-alpha-D-mannosyl-(1->4)-alpha-D-
CC       glucosaminyl]-1-(1-radyl,2-acyl-sn-glycero-3-phospho)-1D-myo-inositol
CC       (also termed H3) intermediate to generate a 2-acyl-6-[alpha-D-mannosyl-
CC       (1->6)-2-phosphoethanolamine-alpha-D-mannosyl-(1->4)-alpha-D-
CC       glucosaminyl]-1-(1-radyl,2-acyl-sn-glycero-3-phospho)-1D-myo-inositol
CC       and participates in the eighth step of the
CC       glycosylphosphatidylinositol-anchor biosynthesis (By similarity). Also
CC       transfers the EtNP on a mannosyl GPI intermediate, 2-acyl-6-(alpha-D-
CC       mannosyl-(1->4)-alpha-D-glucosaminyl)-1-(1-radyl,2-acyl-sn-glycero-3-
CC       phospho)-1D-myo-inositol (also termed H2) (By similarity). May act as
CC       suppressor of replication stress and chromosome missegregation
CC       (PubMed:23446422). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9R1S3,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:23446422}.
