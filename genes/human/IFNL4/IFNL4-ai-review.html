Gene Description

Interferon lambda-4 (IFNL4) is a type III interferon (IFN-lambda family) that triggers antiviral responses by activating the JAK-STAT signaling pathway. IFNL4 is encoded upstream of IFNL3 and was discovered in association with hepatitis C virus (HCV) clearance. A common dinucleotide polymorphism (ss469415590 TT/ŒîG) creates a frameshift that either produces functional IFNL4 protein or a pseudogene. Paradoxically, the allele producing functional IFNL4 (ŒîG) is associated with IMPAIRED HCV clearance, while the inactive TT allele correlates with better outcomes. IFNL4 signals through the type III interferon receptor complex (IFNLR1/IL10RB), activating JAK1/TYK2 kinases which phosphorylate STAT1 and STAT2. The protein is secreted and induces interferon-stimulated genes (ISGs). Four isoforms exist but only isoform 1 (p179) is functionally active.

Core Functions

IFNL4 functions as a type III interferon cytokine, binding to the IFNLR1/IL10RB receptor complex on target cells to activate the JAK1/TYK2-STAT1/STAT2 signaling pathway and induce interferon-stimulated genes (ISGs).
