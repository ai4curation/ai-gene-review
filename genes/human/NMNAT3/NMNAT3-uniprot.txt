CC   -!- FUNCTION: Catalyzes the formation of NAD(+) from nicotinamide
CC       mononucleotide (NMN) and ATP (PubMed:16118205, PubMed:17402747,
CC       PubMed:26616331). Can also use the deamidated form; nicotinic acid
CC       mononucleotide (NaMN) as substrate with the same efficiency. Can use
CC       triazofurin monophosphate (TrMP) as substrate. Can also use GTP and ITP
CC       as nucleotide donors. Also catalyzes the reverse reaction, i.e. the
CC       pyrophosphorolytic cleavage of NAD(+). For the pyrophosphorolytic
CC       activity, can use NAD(+), NADH, NaAD, nicotinic acid adenine
CC       dinucleotide phosphate (NHD), nicotinamide guanine dinucleotide (NGD)
CC       as substrates. Fails to cleave phosphorylated dinucleotides NADP(+),
CC       NADPH and NaADP(+). Protects against axonal degeneration following
CC       injury (PubMed:16118205, PubMed:17402747). May be involved in the
CC       maintenance of axonal integrity (By similarity). Also functions as a
CC       stress-response chaperone protein that prevents toxic aggregation of
CC       proteins; this function may be independent of its NAD(+) synthesis
CC       activity (PubMed:18344983). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q99JR6,
