CC   -!- FUNCTION: The glycine cleavage system catalyzes the degradation of
CC       glycine. The H protein (GCSH) shuttles the methylamine group of glycine
CC       from the P protein (GLDC) to the T protein (GCST). Has a pivotal role
CC       in the lipoylation of enzymes involved in cellular energetics such as
CC       the mitochondrial dihydrolipoyllysine-residue acetyltransferase
CC       component of pyruvate dehydrogenase complex (DLAT), and the
CC       mitochondrial dihydrolipoyllysine-residue succinyltransferase component
CC       of 2-oxoglutarate dehydrogenase complex (DLST) (PubMed:36190515).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:1671321, ECO:0000269|PubMed:36190515}.
CC   -!- COFACTOR:

CC       Note=Binds 1 lipoyl cofactor covalently. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:1671321};

CC       system is composed of four proteins: P (GLDC), T (GCST), L (DLD) and H

CC   -!- DISEASE: Multiple mitochondrial dysfunctions syndrome 7 (MMDS7)
CC       [MIM:620423]: An autosomal recessive disorder biochemically
CC       characterized by glycine accumulation in body fluids, including the
CC       cerebrospinal fluid, with an elevated cerebrospinal fluid/plasma
CC       glycine ratio. The broad clinical spectrum ranges from neonatal fatal
CC       glycine encephalopathy to an attenuated phenotype of developmental
CC       delay, limited verbal communication, behavioral problems, seizures and
CC       variable movement problems. Death in infancy or early childhood may
CC       occur. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:33890291, ECO:0000269|PubMed:36190515}.
