CC       PubMed:17603022, PubMed:32169171, PubMed:33234382, PubMed:7876265).
CC       Catalyzes the desaturation of fatty acyl-CoAs that have a saturated
CC       bond between C2 and C3 (2,3-saturated acyl-CoA) to 2-trans-enoyl-CoAs
CC       ((2E)-enoyl-CoAs), and donates electrons directly to molecular oxygen
CC       (O(2)), thereby producing hydrogen peroxide (H(2)O(2))
CC       (PubMed:17458872, PubMed:17603022, PubMed:7876265). May play a role in
CC       peroxisomal beta-oxidation step in polyunsaturated fatty acids (PUFAs)
CC       biosynthesis. Possibly regulates systemic levels of docosahexaenoic
CC       acid (DHA, C22:6n-3) through a process involving endoplasmic reticulum
CC       desaturation and elongation of alpha-linolenic acid (ALA, C18:3n-3) to
CC       form tetracosahexaenoic acid (THA, C24:6n-3), which is then beta-
CC       oxidized to DHA in peroxisomes (Probable). Involved in VLCFA-dependent
CC       hepatic-to-adipose tissue inter-organ signaling in response to diet.
CC       Through hepatic beta-oxidation of THA, may regulate THA systemic levels
CC       and THA-dependent activation of FFAR4 signaling in adipocytes, limiting
CC       adipose tissue thermogenesis (By similarity).
