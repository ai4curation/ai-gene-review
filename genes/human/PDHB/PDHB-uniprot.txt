CC   -!- FUNCTION: Together with PDHA1 forms the heterotetrameric E1 subunit of
CC       the pyruvate dehydrogenase (PDH) complex (PubMed:17474719,
CC       PubMed:19081061). The PDH complex catalyzes the overall conversion of
CC       pyruvate to acetyl-CoA and CO(2), and thereby links cytoplasmic
CC       glycolysis and the mitochondrial tricarboxylic acid (TCA) cycle
CC       (Probable). It contains multiple copies of three enzymatic components:
CC       pyruvate dehydrogenase (E1), dihydrolipoamide acetyltransferase (E2)
CC       and dihydrolipoamide dehydrogenase (E3) (Probable). The E1 subunit
CC       catalyzes both the thiamine pyrophosphate (TPP)-dependent
CC       decarboxylation of pyruvate and the reductive acetylation of a lipoyl
CC       group covalently linked to the lipoyl-bearing domains of E2
CC       (PubMed:19081061). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:17474719,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:19081061, ECO:0000305|PubMed:17474719,
CC       ECO:0000305|PubMed:19081061}.
