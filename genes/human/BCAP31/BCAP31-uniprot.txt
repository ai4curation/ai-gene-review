CC   -!- FUNCTION: Functions as a chaperone protein (PubMed:18287538,
CC       PubMed:9396746). Is one of the most abundant endoplasmic reticulum (ER)
CC       proteins (PubMed:18287538, PubMed:9396746). Plays a role in the export
CC       of secreted proteins in the ER, the recognition of abnormally folded
CC       protein and their targeting to the ER associated-degradation (ERAD)
CC       (PubMed:18287538, PubMed:9396746). Also serves as a cargo receptor for
CC       the export of transmembrane proteins (By similarity). Plays a role in
CC       the assembly of the mitochondrial membrane respiratory chain NADH
CC       dehydrogenase (Complex I) by stimulating the translocation of NDUFS4
CC       and NDUFB11 from the cytosol to the mitochondria via interaction with
CC       TOMM40 (PubMed:31206022). In response to ER stress, delocalizes from
CC       the ER-mitochondria contact sites and binds BCL2 (PubMed:31206022). May
CC       be involved in CASP8-mediated apoptosis (PubMed:10958671).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q61335, ECO:0000269|PubMed:10958671,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:18287538, ECO:0000269|PubMed:31206022,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:9396746}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homodimer and heterodimer with BCAP29 (PubMed:23967155,
CC       PubMed:9334338). Binds CASP8 (isoform 9) as a complex containing
CC       BCAP31, BCAP29, BCL2 and/or BCL2L1 (PubMed:11917123, PubMed:31206022,
CC       PubMed:9334338). Forms a complex (via C-terminus) with TOMM40 which
CC       mediates the translocation of components of the mitochondrial membrane
CC       respiratory chain NADH dehydrogenase (Complex I) from the cytosol to
CC       the mitochondria; within the complex BCAP31 interacts directly with
CC       unprocessed and processed NDUFS4 and NDUFB11 (PubMed:31206022).
CC       Interacts with VDAC1 (PubMed:31206022). Interacts with VAMP3, VAMP1 and
CC       membrane IgD immunoglobulins (By similarity). Interacts with HACD2
CC       (PubMed:15024066). Interacts with DNM1L; may form part of a larger
CC       protein complex at the endoplasmic reticulum-mitochondrial interface
CC       during mitochondrial fission (PubMed:24196833).
