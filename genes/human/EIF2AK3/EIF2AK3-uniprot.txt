CC   -!- FUNCTION: Metabolic-stress sensing protein kinase that phosphorylates
CC       the alpha subunit of eukaryotic translation initiation factor 2
CC       (EIF2S1/eIF-2-alpha) in response to various stress, such as unfolded
CC       protein response (UPR) (PubMed:10026192, PubMed:10677345,
CC       PubMed:11907036, PubMed:12086964, PubMed:25925385, PubMed:31023583).
CC       Key effector of the integrated stress response (ISR) to unfolded
CC       proteins: EIF2AK3/PERK specifically recognizes and binds misfolded
CC       proteins, leading to its activation and EIF2S1/eIF-2-alpha
CC       phosphorylation (PubMed:10677345, PubMed:27917829, PubMed:31023583).
CC       EIF2S1/eIF-2-alpha phosphorylation in response to stress converts
CC       EIF2S1/eIF-2-alpha in a global protein synthesis inhibitor, leading to
CC       a global attenuation of cap-dependent translation, while concomitantly
CC       initiating the preferential translation of ISR-specific mRNAs, such as
CC       the transcriptional activators ATF4 and QRICH1, and hence allowing
CC       ATF4- and QRICH1-mediated reprogramming (PubMed:10026192,
CC       PubMed:10677345, PubMed:31023583, PubMed:33384352). The EIF2AK3/PERK-
CC       mediated unfolded protein response increases mitochondrial oxidative
CC       phosphorylation by promoting ATF4-mediated expression of COX7A2L/SCAF1,
CC       thereby increasing formation of respiratory chain supercomplexes
CC       (PubMed:31023583). In contrast to most subcellular compartments,
CC       mitochondria are protected from the EIF2AK3/PERK-mediated unfolded
CC       protein response due to EIF2AK3/PERK inhibition by ATAD3A at
CC       mitochondria-endoplasmic reticulum contact sites (PubMed:39116259). In
CC       addition to EIF2S1/eIF-2-alpha, also phosphorylates NFE2L2/NRF2 in
CC       response to stress, promoting release of NFE2L2/NRF2 from the
CC       BCR(KEAP1) complex, leading to nuclear accumulation and activation of
CC       NFE2L2/NRF2 (By similarity). Serves as a critical effector of unfolded
CC       protein response (UPR)-induced G1 growth arrest due to the loss of
CC       cyclin-D1 (CCND1) (By similarity). Involved in control of mitochondrial
CC       morphology and function (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9Z2B5,
