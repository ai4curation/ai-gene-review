CC   -!- FUNCTION: Lipid-binding and transfer protein essential for lysosomal
CC       degradation of ganglioside GM2. Extracts single GM2 molecules from
CC       intralysosomal luminal vesicle (ILV) membranes and presents them in
CC       soluble form to beta-hexosaminidase A (HEXA) for cleavage of N-acetyl-
CC       D-galactosamine and conversion to GM3 (PubMed:30988135,
CC       PubMed:8900233). Forms a stoichiometric GM2-GM2A complex that serves as
CC       the substrate for HEXA (PubMed:30988135, PubMed:8900233). Also
CC       stimulates the breakdown of glycolipid GA2 by HEXA (By similarity).
CC       Possesses dual function: membrane lipid mobilization/solubilization and
CC       lipid substrate presentation to hydrolases (PubMed:30988135). The large
CC       binding pocket can accommodate several single-chain phospholipids and
CC       fatty acids (By similarity). Exhibits some calcium-independent
CC       phospholipase activity (By similarity). Participates in cholesterol
CC       transfer (PubMed:17552909). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q60648,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:17552909, ECO:0000269|PubMed:30988135,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:8900233}.
CC   -!- ACTIVITY REGULATION: Activity is inhibited by cholesterol,
CC       sphingomyelin, sphingosine, and sphinganine. Activity is stimulated by
CC       ceramide, diacylglycerol, fatty acids, lysophosphatidylcholine, and
CC       anionic lipids such as BMP (bis(monoacylglycero)phosphate).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:30988135}.
CC   -!- INTERACTION:
CC       P17900; P62736: ACTA2; NbExp=2; IntAct=EBI-3046641, EBI-698810;
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Lysosome {ECO:0000305|PubMed:8900233}.
CC   -!- PTM: The serines in positions 32 and 33 are absent in 80% of the
CC       sequenced protein.
CC   -!- DISEASE: GM2-gangliosidosis AB (GM2GAB) [MIM:272750]: An autosomal
CC       recessive lysosomal storage disease marked by the accumulation of GM2
CC       gangliosides in the neuronal cells. It is characterized by GM2
CC       gangliosides accumulation in the presence of both normal hexosaminidase
CC       A and B. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:1915858, ECO:0000269|PubMed:8244332,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:8900233}. Note=The disease is caused by variants
CC       affecting the gene represented in this entry.
