CC   -!- FUNCTION: Ubiquitin-binding autophagy adapter that participates in
CC       different processes including host defense or intracellular homeostasis
CC       (PubMed:24692539, PubMed:33577621). Possesses a double function during
CC       the selective autophagy by acting as a shuttle bringing ubiquitinated
CC       proteins to autophagosomes and also by participating in the formation
CC       of protein aggregates (PubMed:24879152, PubMed:34471133,
CC       PubMed:38169523). Plays a role in the regulation of the innate immune
CC       response by modulating type I interferon production and targeting
CC       ubiquitinated IRF3 for autophagic degradation (PubMed:35914352). In
CC       response to oxidative stress, promotes an increase in SQSTM1 levels,
CC       phosphorylation, and body formation by preventing its autophagic
CC       degradation (By similarity). In turn, activates the KEAP1-NRF2/NFE2L2
CC       antioxidant pathway (By similarity). Also plays non-autophagy role by
CC       mediating the shuttle of IL-12 to late endosome for subsequent
CC       secretion (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P97432,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:19250911, ECO:0000269|PubMed:24692539,
