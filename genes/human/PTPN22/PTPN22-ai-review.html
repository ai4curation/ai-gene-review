Gene Description

PTPN22 encodes a non-receptor protein tyrosine phosphatase (LYP/PEP) that is predominantly expressed in hematopoietic cells. It catalyzes the dephosphorylation of phosphotyrosine residues on protein substrates (EC 3.1.3.48). The protein contains an N-terminal classical PTP catalytic domain (residues 24-289) and a C-terminal regulatory region with four proline-rich motifs (P1-P4). The P1 motif mediates binding to the SH3 domain of CSK (C-terminal Src kinase), forming an inhibitory complex at the plasma membrane. PTPN22 is a key negative regulator of T cell receptor (TCR) signaling, directly dephosphorylating activating tyrosines on LCK (Tyr-394), ZAP-70 (Tyr-493), TCR-zeta, Vav, and other signaling molecules. Beyond TCR signaling, PTPN22 modulates innate immune receptor pathways including TLR-driven type I interferon production (via TRAF3 interaction) and NOD2-induced cytokine release and autophagy. It also dephosphorylates NLRP3 at Tyr-861 to regulate inflammasome assembly. The common R620W variant (rs2476601) disrupts CSK binding and is one of the strongest non-HLA autoimmune risk alleles, associated with type 1 diabetes, rheumatoid arthritis, systemic lupus erythematosus, and other autoimmune diseases.

  PTPN22 encodes a non-receptor protein tyrosine phosphatase (LYP/PEP) that is predominantly expressed in hematopoietic cells. It catalyzes the dephosphorylation of phosphotyrosine residues on protein substrates (EC 3.1.3.48). It also dephosphorylates NLRP3 at Tyr-861 to regulate inflammasome assembly.
