CC   -!- FUNCTION: Subunit of STT3A-containing oligosaccharyl transferase (OST-
CC       A) complex that catalyzes the initial transfer of a defined glycan
CC       (Glc(3)Man(9)GlcNAc(2) in eukaryotes) from the lipid carrier dolichol-
CC       pyrophosphate to an asparagine residue within an Asn-X-Ser/Thr
CC       consensus motif in nascent polypeptide chains, the first step in
CC       protein N-glycosylation (PubMed:28860277, PubMed:31831667,
CC       PubMed:39509507). N-glycosylation occurs cotranslationally and the
CC       complex associates with the Sec61 complex at the channel-forming
CC       translocon complex that mediates protein translocation across the
CC       endoplasmic reticulum (ER) (PubMed:28860277, PubMed:31831667,
CC       PubMed:39509507). Within the OST-A complex, acts as an adapter that
CC       anchors the OST-A complex to the Sec61 complex (PubMed:28860277). May
CC       be involved in N-glycosylation of APP (amyloid-beta precursor protein)
CC       (PubMed:21768116). Can modulate gamma-secretase cleavage of APP by
CC       enhancing endoprotelysis of PSEN1 (PubMed:21768116).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:21768116, ECO:0000269|PubMed:28860277,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:31831667, ECO:0000269|PubMed:39509507}.
CC   -!- PATHWAY: Protein modification; protein glycosylation.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:28860277, ECO:0000269|PubMed:31831667,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:39509507}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Component of STT3A-containing oligosaccharyl transferase (OST-
CC       A) complex (PubMed:21768116, PubMed:28860277, PubMed:31831667,
CC       PubMed:36697828, PubMed:38670073, PubMed:39509507). STT3A-containing
CC       complex assembly occurs through the formation of 3 subcomplexes.
CC       Subcomplex 1 contains RPN1 and TMEM258, subcomplex 2 contains the
CC       STT3A-specific subunits STT3A, DC2/OSTC, and KCP2 as well as the core
CC       subunit OST4, and subcomplex 3 contains RPN2, DAD1, and OST48
CC       (PubMed:31831667, PubMed:36697828, PubMed:38670073). The OST-A complex
CC       can form stable complexes with the Sec61 complex or with both the Sec61
CC       and TRAP complexes (PubMed:31831667). Interacts with PSEN1 and NCSTN;
CC       indicative for an association with the gamma-secretase complex
CC       (PubMed:21768116). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:21768116,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:28860277, ECO:0000269|PubMed:31831667,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:36697828, ECO:0000269|PubMed:38670073,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:39509507}.
