CC   -!- MISCELLANEOUS: The MOCS1 transcript is alternatively spliced resulting
CC       in production of alternative MOCS1A and MOCS1B products
CC       (PubMed:10917590, PubMed:11891227, PubMed:31996372). Type I splicing
CC       results in bicistronic transcripts with two open reading frames, of
CC       which only the first, MOCS1A, is translated due to a stop codon in exon
CC       9 (PubMed:10917590, PubMed:11891227, PubMed:31996372). Alternative
CC       splicing of exon 9 leads to production of type II or III transcripts
CC       (where exon 9 is partially or fully removed) and where MOCS1B is
CC       translated as a fusion protein together with catalytically inactive
CC       MOCS1A; inactive MOCS1A is subsequently proteolytically cleaved during
CC       mitochondrial import and it is not known if it has a functional role
CC       (PubMed:10917590, PubMed:11891227, PubMed:31996372).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10917590, ECO:0000269|PubMed:11891227,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:31996372}.
CC   -!- MISCELLANEOUS: Alternative splicing of the N-terminal exon 1 cassettes
CC       a-d appears to impart different properties to the protein; exon 1a
CC       targets MOCS1A to the mitochondrion whereas exon 1b at the N-terminus
CC       remains in the cytosol, exon 1c might stabilize MOCS1A, and exon 1d is
CC       essential for MOCS1A catalytic activity (PubMed:31996372). Exon 1a
CC       seems dispensable for targeting MOCS1B to the mitochondrion due to the
CC       presence of a linker region separating MOCS1A and MOCS1B that appears
CC       to facilitate mitochondrial import (PubMed:31996372).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:31996372}.
