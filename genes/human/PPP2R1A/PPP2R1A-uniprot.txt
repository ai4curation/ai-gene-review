CC   -!- FUNCTION: The PR65 subunit of protein phosphatase 2A serves as a
CC       scaffolding molecule to coordinate the assembly of the catalytic
CC       subunit and a variable regulatory B subunit (PubMed:15525651,
CC       PubMed:16580887, PubMed:33243860, PubMed:33633399, PubMed:34004147,
CC       PubMed:8694763). Upon interaction with GNA12 promotes dephosphorylation
CC       of microtubule associated protein TAU/MAPT (PubMed:15525651). Required
CC       for proper chromosome segregation and for centromeric localization of
CC       SGO1 in mitosis (PubMed:16580887). Together with RACK1 adapter,
CC       mediates dephosphorylation of AKT1 at 'Ser-473', preventing AKT1
CC       activation and AKT-mTOR signaling pathway (By similarity).
CC       Dephosphorylation of AKT1 is essential for regulatory T-cells (Treg)
CC       homeostasis and stability (By similarity). Part of the striatin-
CC       interacting phosphatase and kinase (STRIPAK) complexes
CC       (PubMed:18782753, PubMed:33633399). STRIPAK complexes have critical
CC       roles in protein (de)phosphorylation and are regulators of multiple
CC       signaling pathways including Hippo, MAPK, nuclear receptor and
CC       cytoskeleton remodeling (PubMed:18782753, PubMed:33633399). Different
CC       types of STRIPAK complexes are involved in a variety of biological
CC       processes such as cell growth, differentiation, apoptosis, metabolism
CC       and immune regulation (PubMed:18782753, PubMed:33633399). Key mediator
CC       of a quality checkpoint during transcription elongation as part of the
CC       Integrator-PP2A (INTAC) complex (PubMed:33243860, PubMed:34004147). The
CC       INTAC complex drives premature transcription termination of transcripts
CC       that are unfavorably configured for transcriptional elongation: within
CC       the INTAC complex, acts as a scaffolding subunit for PPP2CA, which
CC       catalyzes dephosphorylation of the C-terminal domain (CTD) of Pol II
CC       subunit POLR2A/RPB1 and SUPT5H/SPT5, thereby preventing transcriptional
CC       elongation (PubMed:33243860, PubMed:34004147). Regulates the
CC       recruitment of the SKA complex to kinetochores (PubMed:28982702).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q76MZ3, ECO:0000269|PubMed:15525651,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:16580887, ECO:0000269|PubMed:18782753,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:28982702, ECO:0000269|PubMed:33243860,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:33633399, ECO:0000269|PubMed:34004147,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:8694763}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: PP2A consists of a common heterodimeric core enzyme, composed
CC       of PPP2CA a 36 kDa catalytic subunit (subunit C) and PPP2R1A a 65 kDa
CC       constant regulatory subunit (PR65 or subunit A), that associates with a
CC       variety of regulatory subunits (PubMed:38123684). Proteins that
CC       associate with the core dimer include three families of regulatory
CC       subunits B (the R2/B/PR55/B55, R3/B''/PR72/PR130/PR59 and R5/B'/B56
CC       families), the 48 kDa variable regulatory subunit, viral proteins, and
CC       cell signaling molecules (PubMed:38123684). Found in a complex with at
CC       least ARL2, PPP2CB, PPP2R1A, PPP2R2A, PPP2R5E and TBCD (By similarity).
CC       Interacts with the PP2A C catalytic subunit PPP2CA (PubMed:37761890,
CC       PubMed:38123684). Interacts with the PP2A B subunit PPP2R2A
CC       (PubMed:38123684). Interacts with the PP2A B subunit PPP2R5D
CC       (PubMed:37761890). Interacts with FOXO1; the interaction
CC       dephosphorylates FOXO1 on AKT-mediated phosphorylation sites (By
CC       similarity). Interacts with IPO9 (PubMed:12670497). Interacts with TP53
CC       and SGO1 (PubMed:16580887, PubMed:17245430). Interacts with PLA2G16;
CC       this interaction might decrease PP2A activity (PubMed:17374643).
CC       Interacts with CTTNBP2NL (PubMed:18782753). Interacts with GNA12; the
CC       interaction promotes protein phosphatase 2A activation causing
CC       dephosphorylation of MAPT (PubMed:15525651). Interacts with CIP2A; this
CC       interaction stabilizes CIP2A (PubMed:28174209). Interacts with
CC       PABIR1/FAM122A (PubMed:27588481). Interacts with ADCY8; antagonizes
