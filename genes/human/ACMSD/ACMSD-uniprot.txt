CC   -!- FUNCTION: Converts alpha-amino-beta-carboxymuconate-epsilon-
CC       semialdehyde (ACMS) to alpha-aminomuconate semialdehyde (AMS)
CC       (PubMed:12140278). ACMS can be converted non-enzymatically to
CC       quinolinate (QA), a key precursor of NAD, and a potent endogenous
CC       excitotoxin of neuronal cells which is implicated in the pathogenesis
CC       of various neurodegenerative disorders. In the presence of ACMSD, ACMS
CC       is converted to AMS, a benign catabolite (PubMed:12140278). ACMSD
CC       ultimately controls the metabolic fate of tryptophan catabolism along
CC       the kynurenine pathway (PubMed:12140278, PubMed:19843166).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12140278, ECO:0000269|PubMed:19843166}.
