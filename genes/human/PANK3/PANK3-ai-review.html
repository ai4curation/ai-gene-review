Gene Description

PANK3 (pantothenate kinase 3, hPanK3) is one of four catalytically active human pantothenate kinase isoforms encoded by three genes (PANK1-3). It catalyzes the ATP-dependent phosphorylation of (R)-pantothenate (vitamin B5) to (R)-4'-phosphopantothenate (EC 2.7.1.33), the first and rate-determining step of coenzyme A (CoA) biosynthesis. PANK3 is a cytosolic, soluble enzyme that functions as a homodimer with two equivalent active sites formed by head-to-tail juxtaposition of the monomers; each protomer adopts an actin/HSP70-like ASKHA (acetate/sugar kinase, HSP70, actin) nucleotide-binding fold. It is a master metabolic regulator of CoA levels: activity is under stringent allosteric feedback inhibition by acetyl-CoA and other acyl-CoA thioesters, which bind in the pantothenate/substrate tunnel and compete with the ATP-binding site, stabilizing a catalytically incompetent (open) conformation; ATP binding stabilizes the catalytically competent (closed) conformation. PANK3 is expressed in all cell types examined and is highly expressed in liver, supplying phosphopantothenate for downstream cytosolic CoA biosynthetic enzymes and thereby supporting oxidative metabolism, the TCA cycle, fatty acid oxidation, and lipid metabolism.

    the first and rate-determining step of coenzyme A (CoA) biosynthesis.

    PMID:30927326
  • Pathway. Cofactor biosynthesis; coenzyme A biosynthesis; CoA from (R)-pantothenate: step 1/5
    (UniPathway UPA00241, UER00352; Reactome R-HSA-196783 Coenzyme A biosynthesis).
    [file:human/PANK3/PANK3-uniprot.txt "Cofactor biosynthesis; coenzyme A biosynthesis; CoA from (R)-"]
  • Kinetics. KM = 14–17 uM pantothenate; KM = 175–311 uM ATP.
    [file:human/PANK3/PANK3-uniprot.txt "KM=14 uM for pantothenate"]
    [file:human/PANK3/PANK3-uniprot.txt "KM=311 uM for ATP"]
  • Quaternary structure. Homodimer with two equivalent active sites formed by head-to-tail
    juxtaposition of the monomers; each protomer adopts an actin kinase / ASKHA fold.
    [file:human/PANK3/PANK3-uniprot.txt "SUBUNIT: Homodimer"]
    PMID:20797618
    PMID:17631502
  • Allosteric regulation (feedback inhibition). Two conformations — a catalytically
    incompetent (open) state stabilized by acetyl(acyl)-CoA binding, and a catalytically
    competent (closed) state stabilized by ATP binding. Inhibited by acetyl-CoA and its
    thioesters (compete with the ATP-binding site); also inhibited by sulfonylureas and
    thiazolidinediones; activated by oleoylethanolamide and acyl-carnitines.
    [file:human/PANK3/PANK3-uniprot.txt "Inhibited by acetyl-CoA and its thioesters"]

description: >-
  PANK3 (pantothenate kinase 3, hPanK3) is one of four catalytically active human
  pantothenate kinase isoforms encoded by three genes (PANK1-3). It catalyzes the
  ATP-dependent phosphorylation of (R)-pantothenate (vitamin B5) to
  (R)-4'-phosphopantothenate (EC 2.7.1.33), the first and rate-determining step of
  coenzyme A (CoA) biosynthesis. PANK3 is a cytosolic, soluble enzyme that functions
  as a homodimer with two equivalent active sites formed by head-to-tail juxtaposition
  of the monomers; each protomer adopts an actin/HSP70-like ASKHA (acetate/sugar
  kinase, HSP70, actin) nucleotide-binding fold. It is a master metabolic regulator
  of CoA levels: activity is under stringent allosteric feedback inhibition by
  acetyl-CoA and other acyl-CoA thioesters, which bind in the pantothenate/substrate
  tunnel and compete with the ATP-binding site, stabilizing a catalytically
  incompetent (open) conformation; ATP binding stabilizes the catalytically competent
  (closed) conformation. PANK3 is expressed in all cell types examined and is highly
  expressed in liver, supplying phosphopantothenate for downstream cytosolic CoA
  biosynthetic enzymes and thereby supporting oxidative metabolism, the TCA cycle,
  fatty acid oxidation, and lipid metabolism.
