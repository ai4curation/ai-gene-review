CC   -!- FUNCTION: Pioneer transcription factor, which controls hematopoietic
CC       cell fate by decompacting stem cell heterochromatin and allowing other
CC       transcription factors to enter otherwise inaccessible genomic sites.
CC       Once in open chromatin, can directly control gene expression by binding
CC       genetic regulatory elements and can also more broadly influence
CC       transcription by recruiting transcription factors, such as interferon
CC       regulatory factors (IRFs), to otherwise inaccessible genomic regions
CC       (PubMed:23658224, PubMed:33951726). Transcriptionally activates genes
CC       important for myeloid and lymphoid lineages, such as CSF1R (By
CC       similarity). Transcriptional activation from certain promoters,
CC       possibly containing low affinity binding sites, is achieved
CC       cooperatively with other transcription factors. FCER1A transactivation
CC       is achieved in cooperation with GATA1 (By similarity). May be
CC       particularly important for the pro- to pre-B cell transition
CC       (PubMed:33951726). Binds (via the ETS domain) onto the purine-rich DNA
CC       core sequence 5'-GAGGAA-3', also known as the PU-box (PubMed:33951726).
CC       In vitro can bind RNA and interfere with pre-mRNA splicing (By
CC       similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P17433,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q6BDS1, ECO:0000269|PubMed:23658224,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:33951726}.
CC   -!- ACTIVITY REGULATION: Transcriptional activity at macrophage-specific
CC       genes is inhibited by interaction with GFI1, which results in the
CC       inhibition of SPI1-induced macrophage differentiation of myeloid
CC       progenitor cells, but not that of the granulocyte lineage.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P17433}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Binds DNA as a monomer. Can form homomers (PubMed:10196196).
CC       Directly interacts with CEBPD/NF-IL6-beta; this interaction does not
CC       affect DNA-binding properties of each partner (By similarity).
CC       Interacts with NONO/p54(nrb) (By similarity). Interacts with RUNX1/AML1
CC       (PubMed:10207087). Interacts with GFI1; the interaction represses SPI1
CC       transcriptional activity, hence blocks SPI1-induced macrophage
CC       differentiation of myeloid progenitor cells (PubMed:17197705).
CC       Interacts with CEBPE (PubMed:26019275). Interacts with IRF4/Pip and
CC       IRF8 (PubMed:10196196, PubMed:33951726). Interacts with JUN
CC       (PubMed:10196196). Interacts with RB1 (PubMed:10196196). Interacts with
CC       TBP (PubMed:10196196). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P17433,
