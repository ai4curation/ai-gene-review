CC   -!- FUNCTION: Major sphingomyelin synthase at the Golgi apparatus
CC       (PubMed:14685263, PubMed:17449912). Catalyzes the reversible transfer
CC       of phosphocholine moiety in sphingomyelin biosynthesis: in the forward
CC       reaction transfers phosphocholine head group of phosphatidylcholine
CC       (PC) on to ceramide (CER) to form ceramide phosphocholine
CC       (sphingomyelin, SM) and diacylglycerol (DAG) as by-product, and in the
CC       reverse reaction transfers phosphocholine from SM to DAG to form PC and
CC       CER. The direction of the reaction depends on the levels of CER and DAG
CC       in Golgi membranes (PubMed:14685263, PubMed:14976195, PubMed:17449912,
CC       PubMed:17982138, PubMed:19454763). Converts the newly synthesized CER,
CC       that is transported from the endoplasmic reticulum to the trans-Golgi
CC       by the Cer transport protein (CERT), to SM (PubMed:30242129). Can form
CC       a heteromeric complex with glucosylceramide synthase (GCS) increasing
CC       SMS activity and reducing glucosylceramide synthesis, a critical
CC       mechanism that controls the metabolic fate of CER in the Golgi
CC       (PubMed:30242129). Does not use free phosphorylcholine or CDP-choline
CC       as donor (PubMed:14685263, PubMed:14976195). Can also transfer
CC       phosphoethanolamine head group of phosphatidylethanolamine (PE) on to
CC       CER to form ceramide phosphoethanolamine (CPE) (By similarity).
CC       Regulates receptor-mediated signal transduction via mitogenic DAG and
CC       proapoptotic CER, as well as via SM, a structural component of membrane
CC       rafts that serve as platforms for signal transduction and protein
CC       sorting (PubMed:14976195, PubMed:17449912, PubMed:17982138). Plays a
CC       role in secretory transport via regulation of DAG pool at the Golgi
CC       apparatus and its downstream effects on PRKD1 (PubMed:18370930,
CC       PubMed:21980337). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8VCQ6,
