CC   -!- FUNCTION: Cytosolic aldo-keto reductase that catalyzes NADPH-dependent
CC       reduction of ketosteroids to hydroxysteroids. Displays broad substrate
CC       specificity with distinct positional and stereochemistry, primarily
CC       generating 3alpha/beta-, 17beta- and 20alpha-hydroxysteroids
CC       (PubMed:10634139, PubMed:10998348, PubMed:11158055, PubMed:14672942,
CC       PubMed:1530633, PubMed:12604236, PubMed:19218247, PubMed:21802064,
CC       PubMed:7650035). Required for male sex determination as a component of
CC       the 'backdoor' androgen biosynthesis pathway that generates 5alpha-
CC       dihydrotestosterone (5alpha-DHT) via pregnanes. Acts together with
CC       AKR1C2 to convert 5alpha-dihydroprogesterone (5alpha-DHP) to 3alpha-
CC       hydroxy-5alpha-pregnan-20-one (3alpha,5alpha-THP/allopregnanolone),
CC       leading to 5alpha-DHT secretion necessary for embryonic gonad
CC       differentiation into testis (PubMed:21802064). May regulate the
CC       concentrations of circulating neurosteroids. Reduces 5alpha-
CC       dihydroprogesterone (5-alpha-DHP) and 5alpha-dihydrodeoxycorticosterone
CC       (5-alpha-DHDOC) precursors to 3alpha-hydroxy-5alpha-pregnan-20-one
CC       (3alpha,5alpha-THP/allopregnanolone) and 3alpha,21-dihydroxy-5alpha-
CC       pregnane-20-one (3alpha,5alpha-THDOC) neuroactive steroids known to
CC       alter neural excitability via allosteric activation of gamma-
CC       aminobutyric acid type A receptors (GABAAR) (PubMed:12604236).
CC       Regulates ligand availability for steroid hormone receptors. Catalyzes
CC       the inactivation of 5alpha-DHT and progesterone converting them into
CC       3alpha/beta-androstanediols and (20S)-hydroxypregn-4-en-3-one,
CC       respectively (PubMed:10998348, PubMed:11158055, PubMed:14672942). May
CC       contribute to the metabolism of adrenal-derived androgens via reduction
CC       of 11-keto-5alpha-androstane-3,17-dione (11K-Adione) into 11-
CC       ketoandrosterone (11KAST) and of 11-ketodihydrotestosterone (11KDHT)
CC       into 11-keto-5alpha-androstane-3alpha/beta,17beta-diol (11K-A3diol)
CC       (PubMed:31926269). Catalyzes the reduction of estrone into 17beta-
CC       estradiol but with low efficiency (PubMed:14672942). In androgen
CC       catabolism, may predominantly act as a phase I enzyme by introducing a
CC       hydroxyl group prior to conjugation. It can nevertheless participate in
CC       the alternative phase II pathway by directly reducing sulfate- or
CC       glucuronide-conjugated androgens (PubMed:19218247). Catalyzes the
CC       biotransformation of the pesticide chlordecone (kepone) to its
CC       corresponding alcohol, leading to increased biliary excretion of the
CC       pesticide and concomitant reduction of its neurotoxicity since bile is
CC       the major excretory route (PubMed:2427522). In vitro can efficiently
CC       catalyze bidirectional conversion between ketosteroids and
CC       hydroxysteroids using NADPH/NADP(+) or NADH/NAD(+) as cofactors. In
CC       vivo however, the reductase activity prevails since the major reducing
CC       cofactor NADPH inhibits NAD(+)-dependent oxidase activity
CC       (PubMed:14672942). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10634139,
