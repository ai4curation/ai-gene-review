| Primary molecular function | Best-supported inference is that DNAJC25 acts as a **J-domain co-chaperone** in the HSP70 proteostasis network. J-domain proteins bind HSP70 and stimulate its ATPase activity, thereby promoting client-protein folding, remodeling, trafficking, or quality control; no direct catalytic activity or substrate-specific reaction has been established for DNAJC25 itself in the retrieved literature. Recent cancer literature also identifies DNAJC25 as an **RNF149 substrate** whose degradation has functional consequences in hepatocellular carcinoma. |
| Enzymatic activity / substrate specificity | **No intrinsic enzyme activity has been established** for DNAJC25 in the retrieved literature. The most specific experimentally supported relationship is that **RNF149 ubiquitinates DNAJC25**, marking it for degradation in HCC models (Guo et al., 2023; cited again by Wu et al., 2025). |
| Subcellular localization | Reported as **cytoplasmic** in review literature discussing HSP40 family members and cancer; broader DNAJC proteins can localize to multiple compartments, but DNAJC25 itself is most specifically described as cytoplasmic in the retrieved sources. |
| Key domains | Contains a conserved **DnaJ/J-domain**; UniProt context supplied for this task lists **DnaJ_domain (IPR001623)**, **DNAJC25-like (IPR044632)**, **J_dom_sf (IPR036869)**, and **Pfam DnaJ (PF00226)**. These domains support assignment as an HSP70 co-chaperone. |
| Regulation mechanism | **Post-translational regulation by ubiquitination** is the clearest recent mechanism: RNF149, an E3 ubiquitin ligase, was reported to interact with DNAJC25 and promote HCC progression through its E3 ligase activity using DNAJC25 as a substrate. This positions DNAJC25 within the ubiquitin-proteasome axis of tumor biology. |
| Biological pathways / processes | Likely participates in **proteostasis/protein quality control** through the HSP70-HSP40 chaperone system. Disease-linked literature connects DNAJC25 to **cancer progression**, especially **hepatocellular carcinoma**, via RNF149-mediated degradation. Additional broader associations in expression studies and reviews suggest links to **stress response**, **cell proliferation**, and possibly **DNA damage-relevant cellular programs**, but these are less direct than the RNF149-HCC evidence. |
| Strongest direct experimental evidence in recent literature | **Guo et al. | Structural / evolutionary inference | DNAJC25’s conserved J-domain supports a role in **functionalizing HSP70 chaperone activity**. Comparative HSP40 family analyses indicate that J-domain proteins are **evolutionarily conserved and functionally constrained**, supporting the inference that DNAJC25 is a bona fide co-chaperone even though its client repertoire remains unresolved. |
| Current evidence limitations | The gene is **poorly characterized** relative to many other HSP40 family members. There is **very limited direct functional literature** on endogenous human DNAJC25, and no definitive client proteins, signaling pathway map, or tissue-specific physiological role were established in the retrieved primary studies beyond its identification as an RNF149 substrate in HCC. |
