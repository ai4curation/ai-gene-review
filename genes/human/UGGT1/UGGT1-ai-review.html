Gene Description

UDP-glucose:glycoprotein glucosyltransferase 1 (UGGT1) is a soluble ER-resident enzyme that serves as a central quality control sensor in the calnexin/calreticulin cycle. UGGT1 recognizes glycoproteins with minor folding defects and reglucosylates their N-glycans, thereby tagging them for re-engagement with the calnexin/calreticulin chaperone system for further folding attempts. Its core molecular function is UDP-glucose:glycoprotein glucosyltransferase activity (EC 2.4.1.-), transferring glucose from UDP-glucose to Man9GlcNAc2 N-glycans on misfolded substrates. The N-terminal non-catalytic domain (~80% of the protein) mediates recognition of incompletely folded glycoprotein substrates, while the C-terminal domain contains the catalytic glucosyltransferase domain. UGGT1 activity is enhanced by complex formation with SELENOF. Described as a "gatekeeper for quality control" that prevents transport of improperly folded glycoproteins out of the ER (PMID:10694380).

UGGT1 does participate in the protein folding quality control cycle by reglucosylating misfolded glycoproteins so they can re-engage with calnexin/calreticulin for further folding attempts (PMID:10694380, PMID:40267907). However, UGGT1 itself is not a chaperone that directly assists in protein folding. It is an enzyme that tags misfolded proteins for re-entry into the calnexin/calreticulin chaperone cycle. The term implies direct folding assistance, which is misleading for UGGT1. A more accurate process annotation would be one reflecting its role in protein quality control in the ER or N-linked glycosylation.
Reason: UGGT1 does not directly fold proteins.
