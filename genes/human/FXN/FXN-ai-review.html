Gene Description

Frataxin (FXN) is a mitochondrial iron chaperone that functions as a transient allosteric activator within the core iron-sulfur cluster (ISC) assembly complex in the mitochondrial matrix. The mature form (residues 81-210) acts as a key component of the ISC machinery composed of NFS1, LYRM4 (ISD11), ACP1, ISCU, FDX2, and FXN. FXN binds ferrous iron (Fe2+) and accelerates persulfide transfer from the cysteine desulfurase NFS1 to the scaffold protein ISCU, facilitating de novo [2Fe-2S] cluster assembly. The protein binds to a preformed ISCU/NFS1/ISD11 complex rather than individual components. FXN also functions as an iron donor for ferrochelatase in heme biosynthesis. The oligomeric form exhibits ferroxidase activity for detoxifying redox-active iron. Loss of FXN causes Friedreich ataxia, the most common inherited ataxia, due to impaired Fe-S cluster biogenesis and mitochondrial iron accumulation.
