CC   -!- FUNCTION: Mitochondrial trifunctional enzyme catalyzes the last three
CC       of the four reactions of the mitochondrial beta-oxidation pathway
CC       (PubMed:1550553, PubMed:29915090, PubMed:30850536, PubMed:8135828,
CC       PubMed:31604922). The mitochondrial beta-oxidation pathway is the major
CC       energy-producing process in tissues and is performed through cycles of
CC       four consecutive reactions (PubMed:29915090). Each beta-oxidation cycle
CC       shortens the fatty acyl-CoA by two carbons, yielding one acetyl-CoA
CC       (for the citric acid cycle), one FADH(2), and one NADH (which donate
CC       electrons to the respiratory chain for ATP production)
CC       (PubMed:29915090). These cycles repeat until the chain is fully
CC       degraded to acetyl-CoA units (PubMed:29915090). Among the enzymes
CC       involved in this pathway, the trifunctional protein--responsible for
CC       the hydration, dehydrogenation, and thiolysis steps, shows specificity
CC       for long-chain fatty acids, such as those from dietary and stored fats
CC       (PubMed:30850536, PubMed:31604922). Mitochondrial trifunctional enzyme
CC       is a heterotetrameric complex composed of two proteins, the
CC       trifunctional enzyme subunit alpha/HADHA described here carries the
CC       2,3-enoyl-CoA hydratase and the 3-hydroxyacyl-CoA dehydrogenase
CC       activities while the trifunctional enzyme subunit beta/HADHB bears the
CC       3-ketoacyl-CoA thiolase activity (Probable) (PubMed:29915090,
CC       PubMed:30850536, PubMed:8135828). These activities have been
CC       experimentally confirmed on a few substrates derived from beta-
CC       oxidation of long-chain saturated fatty acids such as palmitate
CC       (hexadecanoate) and laurate (dodecanoate) (PubMed:1550553,
CC       PubMed:8135828, PubMed:8163672, PubMed:8651282). In addition, based on
CC       its established catalytic mechanism, and combined genetic interaction
CC       or mutant phenotype evidence, it is predicted to act also on other
CC       substrates, including long-chain unsaturated fatty acids such as oleate
CC       (9Z-octadecenoate), linoleate (9Z,12Z-octadecadienoate), linolenate
CC       (9Z,12Z,15Z-octadecatrienoate), and others (Probable)
CC       (PubMed:26474213). Independently of subunit beta, HADHA also exhibits a
CC       cardiolipin acyltransferase activity that participates in cardiolipin
CC       remodeling; cardiolipin is a major mitochondrial membrane phospholipid
CC       (PubMed:23152787, PubMed:31604922). HADHA may act downstream of
CC       Tafazzin/TAZ, that remodels monolysocardiolipin (MLCL) to a cardiolipin
CC       intermediate, and then HADHA may continue to remodel this species into
CC       mature tetralinoleoyl-cardiolipin (PubMed:31604922). Has also been
CC       proposed to act directly on MLCL; capable of acylating MLCL using
CC       different acyl-CoA substrates, with highest activity for oleoyl-CoA
CC       (PubMed:23152787). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:1550553,
