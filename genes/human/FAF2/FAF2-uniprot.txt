CC   -!- FUNCTION: Plays an important role in endoplasmic reticulum-associated
CC       degradation (ERAD) that mediates ubiquitin-dependent degradation of
CC       misfolded endoplasmic reticulum proteins (PubMed:18711132,
CC       PubMed:24215460). By controlling the steady-state expression of the
CC       IGF1R receptor, indirectly regulates the insulin-like growth factor
CC       receptor signaling pathway (PubMed:26692333). Recruits ATPase VCP to
CC       lipid droplets and also binds to phospholipase PNPLA2/ATGL, inhibiting
CC       PNPLA2 activity by promoting its dissociation from its ABHD5/CGI-58
CC       activator (PubMed:23297223). This inhibits PNPLA2-mediated
CC       triacylglycerol hydrolysis and leads to inhibition of lipid droplet
CC       degradation (PubMed:23297223). Involved in stress granule disassembly:
CC       associates with ubiquitinated G3BP1 in response to heat shock, thereby
CC       promoting interaction between ubiquitinated G3BP1 and VCP, followed by
CC       G3BP1 extraction from stress granules and stress granule disassembly
CC       (PubMed:34739333). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:18711132,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:23297223, ECO:0000269|PubMed:24215460,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:26692333, ECO:0000269|PubMed:34739333}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Identified in a complex that contains SEL1L, OS9, FAF2/UBXD8,
CC       UBE2J1/UBC6E and AUP1 (PubMed:18711132). Interacts with YOD1
CC       (PubMed:19818707). Interacts (via N-terminus) with UBQLN2 (via C-
CC       terminus) (PubMed:24215460). Interacts with PNPLA2; inhibits PNPLA2
CC       activity (PubMed:23297223). Interacts with UBAC2; the interaction
CC       restricts trafficking of FAF2 from the endoplasmic reticulum to lipid
CC       droplets (PubMed:23297223). Interacts with ZFAND2B; probably through
CC       VCP (PubMed:24160817). Interacts with LMBR1L (PubMed:31073040).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:18711132, ECO:0000269|PubMed:19818707,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:23297223, ECO:0000269|PubMed:24160817,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:24215460, ECO:0000305|PubMed:31073040}.
