CC   -!- FUNCTION: Ribosome collision sensor that plays a key role in the RNF14-
CC       RNF25 translation quality control pathway, a pathway that takes place
CC       when a ribosome has stalled during translation, and which promotes
CC       ubiquitination and degradation of translation factors on stalled
CC       ribosomes (PubMed:32610081, PubMed:36638793, PubMed:37651229,
CC       PubMed:37951215, PubMed:37951216). Directly binds to the ribosome and
CC       acts as a sentinel for colliding ribosomes: activated following
CC       ribosome stalling and promotes recruitment of RNF14, which directly
CC       ubiquitinates EEF1A1/eEF1A, leading to its degradation
CC       (PubMed:36638793, PubMed:37951215, PubMed:37951216). In addition to
CC       EEF1A1/eEF1A, the RNF14-RNF25 translation quality control pathway
CC       mediates degradation of ETF1/eRF1 and ubiquitination of ribosomal
CC       protein (PubMed:36638793, PubMed:37651229). GCN1 also acts as a
CC       positive activator of the integrated stress response (ISR) by mediating
CC       activation of EIF2AK4/GCN2 in response to amino acid starvation (By
CC       similarity). Interaction with EIF2AK4/GCN2 on translating ribosomes
CC       stimulates EIF2AK4/GCN2 kinase activity, leading to phosphorylation of
CC       eukaryotic translation initiation factor 2 (eIF-2-alpha/EIF2S1) (By
CC       similarity). EIF2S1/eIF-2-alpha phosphorylation converts EIF2S1/eIF-2-
CC       alpha into a global protein synthesis inhibitor, leading to a global
CC       attenuation of cap-dependent translation, and thus to a reduced overall
CC       utilization of amino acids, while concomitantly initiating the
CC       preferential translation of ISR-specific mRNAs, such as the
CC       transcriptional activator ATF4, and hence allowing ATF4-mediated
CC       reprogramming of amino acid biosynthetic gene expression to alleviate
CC       nutrient depletion (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:E9PVA8,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:32610081, ECO:0000269|PubMed:36638793,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:37651229, ECO:0000269|PubMed:37951215,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:37951216}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Interacts with EIF2AK4/GCN2; this interaction stimulates the
CC       EIF2AK4/GCN2 kinase activity and is impaired by IMPACT upon a variety
CC       of stress conditions, such as amino acid depletion, UV-C irradiation,
CC       proteasome inhibitor treatment and glucose deprivation
CC       (PubMed:36638793). Interacts with IMPACT; this prevents the interaction
CC       of GCN1 with EIF2AK4/GCN2 and inhibits EIF2AK4/GCN2 kinase activity (By
CC       similarity). Interacts with RNF14; interaction takes place following
CC       ribosome stalling and promotes recruitment of RNF14 (PubMed:36638793).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:E9PVA8, ECO:0000269|PubMed:36638793}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cytoplasm {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:E9PVA8}.
CC       Note=Associates with ribosomes in undifferentiated neuroblastoma cells
CC       and increases after neuronal differentiation.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:E9PVA8}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Ubiquitously expressed (PubMed:9039502). Expressed
CC       in skeletal muscules, ovary and testis (PubMed:9234705).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:9039502, ECO:0000269|PubMed:9234705}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The RWDBD (RWD-binding domain) region mediates binding to RWD
CC       domain-containing proteins, such as EIF2AK4/GCN2, IMPACT and RNF14.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:36638793}.
