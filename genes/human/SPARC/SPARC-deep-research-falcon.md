## Comprehensive research report: Human SPARC (UniProt P09486) functional annotation (Homo sapiens)

### Executive summary
SPARC (Secreted Protein Acidic and Rich in Cysteine; also **osteonectin**/**BM-40**) is a **secreted, extracellular matrix (ECM)-associated “matricellular” glycoprotein** that modulates cell–ECM interactions and ECM remodeling rather than serving as a bulk structural ECM component. Its best-supported primary molecular roles are **(i) Ca2+-dependent ECM/collagen binding and matrix organization**, and **(ii) regulation of signaling programs (notably TGF-β/SMAD and integrin/MAPK axes) that tune collagen synthesis, matrix proteolysis, inflammation, barrier function, and stromal/immune phenotypes**. Recent work (2023–2024) strengthens mechanistic links between extracellular SPARC and **TGF-β receptor/SMAD signaling in fibroblasts**, and multi-omics tumor studies emphasize SPARC as a **stromal/CAF-associated secreted factor** with biomarker potential in plasma/secretomes. (wu2023omicsoverviewof pages 2-5, toba2024exploringtheroles pages 1-7, ham2023sparcishighly pages 4-8, poplawski2023coordinatedreprogrammingof pages 1-2)

### Identity verification (critical)
**SPARC in this report is human SPARC corresponding to UniProt accession P09486**. This mapping is explicitly supported by proteomics peptide evidence identifying “FL SPARC protein (Uniprot accession number P09486)” in conditioned media degradomics datasets. (alcaraz2020cathepsindexacerbates pages 62-62)

### 1) Key concepts and definitions (current understanding)

#### 1.1 Matricellular proteins
Recent reviews define SPARC as a **matricellular** protein: a non-structural ECM component that **modulates** ECM assembly, cell adhesion, and growth-factor signaling during development and tissue remodeling. (toba2024exploringtheroles pages 1-7, jiang2023sparcapotential pages 1-2)

#### 1.2 Domain architecture and biochemical features
Authoritative 2023–2024 reviews converge on SPARC as a modular secreted protein with:
- **N-terminal acidic Ca2+-binding region**,
- **cysteine-rich follistatin-like region**, and
- **C-terminal Ca2+-binding region containing EF-hand motifs**. (toba2024exploringtheroles pages 1-7)

Mechanistically, SPARC has **two Ca2+-binding sites** described in a 2023 review: **(i) an acidic domain binding ~5–8 Ca2+ with low affinity** and **(ii) an EF-hand loop binding 1 Ca2+ with high affinity**. (wu2023omicsoverviewof pages 2-5)

A 2023 review also annotates SPARC with a **secreted acidic/Ca-binding region** and **Kazal-type domain annotation**, consistent with SPARC-family modularity. (wu2023omicsoverviewof pages 2-5)

#### 1.3 Primary molecular function (functional annotation)
Across 2023–2024 sources, SPARC’s most reproducible molecular functions are:
- **Collagen/ECM binding and ECM organization** (including collagen binding; regulation of assembly of other ECM proteins such as laminin; association with fibroblast ECM organization). (wu2023omicsoverviewof pages 1-2, wu2023omicsoverviewof pages 2-5)
- **Growth factor and cytokine-context modulation** through interactions/binding reported with **collagens, albumin, thrombospondin, PDGF**, and cell membranes, implying scaffolding/availability effects rather than enzymatic activity. (wu2023omicsoverviewof pages 2-5)

SPARC is **not an enzyme**; its function is regulatory/organizational in ECM and signaling contexts. (toba2024exploringtheroles pages 1-7, jiang2023sparcapotential pages 1-2)

### 2) Recent developments and latest research (prioritizing 2023–2024)

#### 2.1 SPARC→TGF-β/SMAD signaling drives collagen I up and MMP-1 down in human fibroblasts (2023 primary evidence)
A 2023 human fibroblast study (International Journal of Molecular Sciences; publication date July 2023; https://doi.org/10.3390/ijms241512179) showed that recombinant SPARC increased **type I collagen** and decreased **MMP-1** in human foreskin fibroblasts, with mechanistic evidence that SPARC activates **SMAD2/SMAD3 phosphorylation** and that these effects are blocked by a **TGFBR1 inhibitor (SB431542)**. (ham2023sparcishighly pages 2-4, ham2023sparcishighly pages 4-8)

Visual evidence from the same study shows (i) increased collagen I and decreased MMP-1 with SPARC treatment, and (ii) SMAD phosphorylation and inhibitor blockade consistent with a TGF-β receptor-dependent mechanism. (ham2023sparcishighly media 8ff8b5a7, ham2023sparcishighly media 140c6a70)

The study also reported transcriptome-level remodeling after SPARC treatment, including **575 DEGs** (|log2FC|≥1, p<0.05) enriched for **extracellular space** and **ECM** terms, supporting SPARC as a driver of fibroblast ECM programs. (ham2023sparcishighly pages 4-8)

#### 2.2 Proteomics evidence linking SPARC with TGFβ pathway activation and fibrotic fibroblast markers in human tissue (2024)
A 2024 Scientific Reports study on gingival fibrosis in Raine syndrome (publication date April 2024; https://doi.org/10.1038/s41598-024-59713-0) reported **increased SPARC expression in FSP1+ gingival fibroblasts** and surrounding ECM, alongside **TGFβ pathway hyperactivation** (nuclear pSMAD3) and increased α-SMA staining (myofibroblast marker). (costa2024gingivalproteomicsreveals pages 8-9)

#### 2.3 Tumor secretome and CAF association (2023)
A 2023 integrated transcriptome–metabolome–secretome study in clear cell renal cell carcinoma (publication date January 2023; https://doi.org/10.1186/s12935-022-02845-y) found SPARC among **top upregulated secreted proteins** in conditioned media across five ccRCC cell lines. Importantly, the authors identified an **85-protein set detectable in human plasma** and report that SPARC expression predicted **endothelial cell infiltration** in ccRCC tumors and behaved as a **pan-cancer indicator of CAF infiltration** across an analysis of **>12,000 tumors**. (poplawski2023coordinatedreprogrammingof pages 1-2)

#### 2.4 2024 synthesis in cardiovascular inflammation/fibrosis
A 2024 American Journal of Physiology review (publication date November 2024; https://doi.org/10.1152/ajpheart.00565.2024) emphasizes SPARC as a collagen-binding matricellular protein induced in remodeling and inflammatory/fibrotic lesions, and discusses deleterious roles in cardiovascular injury models and biomarker potential. (toba2024exploringtheroles pages 1-7)

### 3) Mechanisms, pathways, and molecular interactions

#### 3.1 Localization and secretion (where SPARC acts)
SPARC is described as a **secreted glycoprotein** that accumulates in the **ECM/pericellular milieu** and is detectable in blood. A 2024 review reports translation to ~32 kDa and secretion as ~43 kDa after **N-linked glycosylation**. (toba2024exploringtheroles pages 1-7)

A 2023 review also describes SPARC as predicted intra-/extra-cellular with vesicular localization and “secreted to blood (different isoforms).” (wu2023omicsoverviewof pages 2-5)

#### 3.2 Binding partners and ECM interactions
Mechanistic interaction claims supported by 2023–2024 synthesis include binding to **several collagens**, **albumin**, **thrombospondin**, **PDGF**, and ECM components including **fibronectin**, with roles in ECM assembly and organization (including laminin assembly regulation). (wu2023omicsoverviewof pages 2-5, wu2023omicsoverviewof pages 1-2)

#### 3.3 TGF-β/SMAD axis (fibroblasts, matrix integrity)
The strongest 2023 primary mechanistic evidence places extracellular SPARC as an enhancer/activator of **TGF-β receptor-dependent SMAD signaling** in fibroblasts, increasing collagen I and suppressing MMP-1, blocked by TGFBR1 inhibition. (ham2023sparcishighly pages 2-4, ham2023sparcishighly pages 4-8)

In broader disease synthesis, SPARC is linked to fibroblast-to-myofibroblast transition and collagen synthesis/processing with positive feedback on TGF-β1-related profibrotic loops in several injury models. (toba2024exploringtheroles pages 28-33)

#### 3.4 Integrin-linked MAPK/MMP signaling (vascular injury and barrier function)
A 2024 review summarizes evidence that SPARC can signal via **integrin αVβ3**, where integrin inhibition blocks SPARC-driven **JNK/p38 activation** and **MMP-9** signaling and reduces blood–brain barrier disruption in cerebrovascular injury models, supporting an **αVβ3→MAPK→MMP-9** pathway in certain contexts. (toba2024exploringtheroles pages 28-33)

#### 3.5 Immune modulation and stromal–immune coupling
Recent synthesis and omics analyses link SPARC to immune suppression and macrophage phenotypes:
- In mesothelioma-focused omics synthesis (July 2023; https://doi.org/10.3390/biom13071103), SPARC expression correlates with **CAF infiltration** and is reported as linked to **MDSC infiltration**, with SPARC identified as an **MDSC marker** with suppressive/pro-tumoral activities; fatty-acid metabolism modulation (SCD5) can reduce SPARC secretion and suppressive activity, enhanced in SPARC knockout contexts. (wu2023omicsoverviewof pages 5-7)
- The same 2023 synthesis reports SPARC-driven **M2 macrophage polarization** and that SPARC silencing can inhibit M2-associated tumor-promoting effects; extracellular SPARC concentration can be regulated by **STAB1-mediated endocytosis** in alternatively activated macrophages. (wu2023omicsoverviewof pages 9-11)

### 4) Current applications and real-world implementations

#### 4.1 Biomarker implementations (plasma/secretome; tumor stroma)
- **Plasma/secretome detectability:** SPARC was among 85 proteins detectable in human plasma in a ccRCC secretome study and is proposed as a candidate plasma biomarker reflecting tumor microenvironment remodeling. (poplawski2023coordinatedreprogrammingof pages 1-2)
- **CAF/stromal marker usage:** A 2024 review analyzing CAF-associated genes in breast cancer treats **SPARC as a canonical stromal/CAF marker** (high stroma/epithelium specificity; SPARC SE ratio reported as 17.2 in CRC datasets) and uses SPARC correlation/knockdown to define downstream ECM gene programs. (naito2024cafassociatedgenesin pages 2-4)

#### 4.2 Drug delivery and nanomedicine concepts leveraging SPARC
A 2023 Frontiers in Molecular Biosciences review (July 2023; https://doi.org/10.3389/fmolb.2023.1235428) describes SPARC as a target/handle for functional nanomaterials, emphasizing SPARC’s reported **albumin affinity** as a rationale for albumin-based carriers (HSA/BSA nanoparticles) and tumor/inflammation lesion targeting strategies. (jiang2023sparcapotential pages 1-2, jiang2023sparcapotential pages 12-14)

#### 4.3 Anti-fibrotic gene-silencing strategies (preclinical)
A 2023 Biomedicines review (February 2023; https://doi.org/10.3390/biomedicines11030657) highlights SPARC as a TGFβ2-upregulated matricellular protein in ocular scarring and summarizes preclinical approaches where **SPARC silencing (siRNA)** delivered via nanoparticles/hydrogels reduces profibrotic gene expression and subconjunctival scarring in animal models, with pilot safety/feasibility signals reported in the cited preclinical literature. (shao2023noveltherapiesfor pages 9-11)

### 5) Expert opinions / authoritative synthesis (2023–2024)
- Cardiovascular-focused experts emphasize SPARC as a collagen-binding matricellular protein induced in remodeling and propose it as a **proinflammatory factor** and potential therapeutic target/biomarker in cardiovascular disease contexts. (toba2024exploringtheroles pages 1-7)
- Tumor and omics-focused synthesis emphasizes SPARC’s **context-dependent roles** (including angiogenesis modulation and immune suppression links) but consistently highlights stromal expression (CAFs) and ECM remodeling as central themes. (wu2023omicsoverviewof pages 5-7, wu2023omicsoverviewof pages 9-11)

### Key statistics and data points (recent studies)
- **Plasma SPARC concentration (reported in 2024 review):** ~0.1–0.8 μg/mL physiologic, rising to ~1.5–10 μg/mL in neoplastic/inflammatory states. (toba2024exploringtheroles pages 1-7)
- **Tumor-scale association (2023 multi-omics):** SPARC associated with CAF infiltration across **>12,000 tumors** and identified among **85 plasma-detectable secretome proteins** in ccRCC secretome profiling. (poplawski2023coordinatedreprogrammingof pages 1-2)
- **Transcriptome perturbation (2023 fibroblast study):** **575 DEGs** (|log2FC|≥1, p<0.05) after SPARC stimulation, enriched for extracellular/ECM programs. (ham2023sparcishighly pages 4-8)

### Limitations and open questions (important for functional annotation)
- **Residue-level domain boundaries:** While domain types (acidic Ca-binding, follistatin-like, EF-hands; Kazal-type annotation) are well supported, the accessible 2023–2024 texts used here generally did not provide residue-range boundaries for each domain. (wu2023omicsoverviewof pages 2-5, toba2024exploringtheroles pages 1-7)
- **Receptor-level specificity:** Several sources emphasize integrin-linked effects and TGF-β receptor dependence in specific contexts, but SPARC likely operates via multiple interacting surfaces/partners; many “receptor” relationships remain context- and tissue-dependent. (toba2024exploringtheroles pages 28-33, ham2023sparcishighly pages 4-8)
- **Clinical targeting maturity:** Despite active exploration (nanocarriers, siRNA anti-fibrotic strategies, biomarker studies), these sources do not establish an approved, SPARC-specific therapeutic; most intervention evidence remains preclinical or indirect. (shao2023noveltherapiesfor pages 9-11, jiang2023sparcapotential pages 1-2)
