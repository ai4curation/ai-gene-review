CC   -!- FUNCTION: Catalyzes the penultimate step of the biosynthesis of
CC       cholesterol, the dehydrogenation of lathosterol into 7-
CC       dehydrocholesterol (7-DHC). Cholesterol is the major sterol component
CC       in mammalian membranes and a precursor for bile acid and steroid
CC       hormone synthesis (PubMed:10786622, PubMed:38297129). In addition to
CC       its essential role in cholesterol biosynthesis, it also indirectly
CC       regulates ferroptosis through the production of 7-DHC. By diverting the
CC       spread of damage caused by peroxyl radicals from the phospholipid
CC       components to its sterol nucleus, 7-DHC prevents this form of cell
CC       death (PubMed:38297129, PubMed:38297130). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10786622,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:38297129, ECO:0000269|PubMed:38297130}.
