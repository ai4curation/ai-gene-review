Gene Description

ALG8 is the endoplasmic reticulum (ER)-lumenal, Dol-P-Glc-dependent alpha-1,3-glucosyltransferase (EC 2.4.1.265) that catalyzes the second of the three glucosylation steps in assembly of the dolichol-linked oligosaccharide (LLO), the Glc3Man9GlcNAc2 glycan precursor used for protein N-linked glycosylation. Acting on the lumenal face of the ER membrane, it transfers a glucose residue from dolichyl-phosphate-glucose (Dol-P-Glc, not UDP-Glc) onto the lipid-linked intermediate Glc1Man9GlcNAc2-PP-dolichol to give Glc2Man9GlcNAc2-PP-dolichol, the substrate for the following enzyme ALG10. ALG8 is a polytopic (multi-pass) ER membrane protein of the ALG6/ALG8 glucosyltransferase family (CAZy GT57). It functions in the dolichol-linked oligosaccharide biosynthetic process and, downstream, in protein N-linked glycosylation. Loss-of-function variants cause the autosomal recessive multisystem congenital disorder of glycosylation type Ih (ALG8-CDG / CDG-Ih), characterized by hypoglycosylation of serum glycoproteins; ALG8 variants are also associated with isolated polycystic liver disease.
