CC   -!- FUNCTION: Subunit c, of the mitochondrial membrane ATP synthase complex
CC       (F(1)F(0) ATP synthase or Complex V) that produces ATP from ADP in the
CC       presence of a proton gradient across the membrane which is generated by
CC       electron transport complexes of the respiratory chain (Probable). ATP
CC       synthase complex consist of a soluble F(1) head domain - the catalytic
CC       core - and a membrane F(1) domain - the membrane proton channel
CC       (PubMed:37244256). These two domains are linked by a central stalk
CC       rotating inside the F(1) region and a stationary peripheral stalk
CC       (PubMed:37244256). During catalysis, ATP synthesis in the catalytic
CC       domain of F(1) is coupled via a rotary mechanism of the central stalk
CC       subunits to proton translocation (Probable). With the subunit a (MT-
CC       ATP6), forms the proton-conducting channel in the F(0) domain, that
CC       contains two crucial half-channels (inlet and outlet) that facilitate
CC       proton movement from the mitochondrial intermembrane space (IMS) into
CC       the matrix (PubMed:37244256). Protons are taken up via the inlet half-
CC       channel and released through the outlet half-channel, following a
CC       Grotthuss mechanism (PubMed:37244256). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:37244256,
CC       ECO:0000305|PubMed:37244256}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY:
CC       Reaction=H(+)(in) = H(+)(out); Xref=Rhea:RHEA:34979, ChEBI:CHEBI:15378;
CC         Evidence={ECO:0000305|PubMed:37244256};
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homooctamer; the c-ring consists of eight c subunits forming a
CC       circle, and each subunit adopts a hairpin shape (PubMed:37244256).
CC       Component of the ATP synthase complex composed at least of
CC       ATP5F1A/subunit alpha, ATP5F1B/subunit beta, ATP5MC1/subunit c
CC       (homooctomer), MT-ATP6/subunit a, MT-ATP8/subunit 8, ATP5ME/subunit e,
CC       ATP5MF/subunit f, ATP5MG/subunit g, ATP5MK/subunit k, ATP5MJ/subunit j,
CC       ATP5F1C/subunit gamma, ATP5F1D/subunit delta, ATP5F1E/subunit epsilon,
CC       ATP5PF/subunit F6, ATP5PB/subunit b, ATP5PD/subunit d, ATP5PO/subunit
CC       OSCP (PubMed:37244256). ATP synthase complex consists of a soluble F(1)
CC       head domain (subunits alpha(3) and beta(3)) - the catalytic core - and
CC       a membrane F(0) domain - the membrane proton channel (subunits c, a, 8,
CC       e, f, g, k and j) (PubMed:37244256). These two domains are linked by a
CC       central stalk (subunits gamma, delta, and epsilon) rotating inside the
CC       F1 region and a stationary peripheral stalk (subunits F6, b, d, and
CC       OSCP) (PubMed:37244256). Interacts with TMEM70 (homooligomer form);
CC       this interaction facilitates the oligomer formation of subunit
CC       c/ATP5MC1 (c-ring) and the c-ring membrane insertion and also protects
CC       ATP5MC1 against intramitochondrial proteolysis (PubMed:31652072).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:31652072, ECO:0000269|PubMed:37244256}.
