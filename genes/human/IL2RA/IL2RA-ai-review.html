Gene Description

IL2RA (CD25, p55, TAC antigen) encodes the alpha subunit of the interleukin-2 receptor. IL2RA is a type I transmembrane glycoprotein containing two Sushi/CCP (complement control protein) domains in its extracellular region. It binds IL-2 with low affinity (Kd ~10 nM) on its own, but its primary role is to capture IL-2 and present it to the signaling-competent IL-2Rbeta/gammac heterodimer, thereby forming the high-affinity trimeric receptor complex (Kd ~10 pM). IL2RA itself has a very short cytoplasmic tail (13 residues) and does not directly transduce signals. It is constitutively expressed on FOXP3+ regulatory T cells (Tregs) and is induced on activated conventional T cells. By enabling Tregs to respond to very low concentrations of IL-2, IL2RA is critical for immune tolerance and T cell homeostasis. Loss-of-function mutations cause immunodeficiency with lymphoproliferation and autoimmunity (IMD41), and common variants at the IL2RA locus are associated with type 1 diabetes and multiple sclerosis susceptibility.

description: >-
  IL2RA (CD25, p55, TAC antigen) encodes the alpha subunit of the interleukin-2 receptor.
  IL2RA is a type I transmembrane glycoprotein containing two Sushi/CCP (complement control protein)
  domains in its extracellular region. It binds IL-2 with low affinity (Kd ~10 nM) on its own, but

  immunodeficiency with lymphoproliferation and autoimmunity (IMD41), and common variants at the
  IL2RA locus are associated with type 1 diabetes and multiple sclerosis susceptibility.
