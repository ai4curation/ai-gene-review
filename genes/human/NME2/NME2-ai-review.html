PMID:8392752
Human c-myc transcription factor PuF identified as nm23-H2 n... 		REMOVE
Summary: The 1993 Postel et al. paper (PMID:8392752) identified NME2 as the c-myc purine-binding transcription factor PuF. However, subsequent studies, particularly the definitive 2009 Dexheimer et al. study (PMID:19435876), clarified that NME2 does NOT function as a classical DNA-binding transcription factor. GO:0001216 requires "selective and non-covalent binding to a specific double-stranded genomic DNA sequence." NME2 binds to single-stranded DNA and G-quadruplex structures, not double-stranded DNA motifs. PMID:19435876 explicitly states NME2 "does not bind to or cleave the NHE III(1) in duplex form" and concludes that "NM23-H2 may play an indirect role in transcriptional activation." NME2 lacks any known DNA-binding domains characteristic of transcription factors (HOX, GATA, zinc-finger, etc.). Its transcriptional effects appear mediated through binding to non-canonical G-quadruplex DNA structures that then allow other single-stranded transcription factors to bind.
Reason: NME2 does not meet the criteria for GO:0001216 (DNA-binding transcription activator activity), which requires binding to double-stranded genomic DNA sequence motifs. NME2 is an enzyme (nucleoside diphosphate kinase) that has a moonlighting function in binding G-quadruplex and single-stranded DNA, but this does not constitute classical transcription factor activity. The GO usage guidelines for GO:0003700 explicitly state that proteins with enzymatic activity and no known DNA-binding domain should be annotated to transcription coregulator activity if appropriate. The mechanism described in the literature involves NME2 binding to G-quadruplex structures to destabilize them and allow other transcription factors to bind, which is an indirect role.
Proposed replacements: G-quadruplex DNA binding
Supporting Evidence:
PMID:19435876
NM23-H2 binds to both single-stranded guanine- and cytosine-rich strands of the c-myc NHE III(1) and, to a lesser extent, to a random single-stranded DNA template. However, it does not bind to or cleave the NHE III(1) in duplex form.

    summary: |
      The 1993 Postel et al. paper (PMID:8392752) identified NME2 as the c-myc purine-binding transcription factor PuF. However, subsequent studies, particularly the definitive 2009 Dexheimer et al. study (PMID:19435876), clarified that NME2 does NOT function as a classical DNA-binding transcription factor. GO:0001216 requires "selective and non-covalent binding to a specific double-stranded genomic DNA sequence." NME2 binds to single-stranded DNA and G-quadruplex structures, not double-stranded DNA motifs. PMID:19435876 explicitly states NME2 "does not bind to or cleave the NHE III(1) in duplex form" and concludes that "NM23-H2 may play an indirect role in transcriptional activation." NME2 lacks any known DNA-binding domains characteristic of transcription factors (HOX, GATA, zinc-finger, etc.). Its transcriptional effects appear mediated through binding to non-canonical G-quadruplex DNA structures that then allow other single-stranded transcription factors to bind.
    action: REMOVE
    reason: |
      NME2 does not meet the criteria for GO:0001216 (DNA-binding transcription activator activity), which requires binding to double-stranded genomic DNA sequence motifs. NME2 is an enzyme (nucleoside diphosphate kinase) that has a moonlighting function in binding G-quadruplex and single-stranded DNA, but this does not constitute classical transcription factor activity. The GO usage guidelines for GO:0003700 explicitly state that proteins with enzymatic activity and no known DNA-binding domain should be annotated to transcription coregulator activity if appropriate. The mechanism described in the literature involves NME2 binding to G-quadruplex structures to destabilize them and allow other transcription factors to bind, which is an indirect role.
