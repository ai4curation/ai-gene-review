CC   -!- FUNCTION: Glutamine synthetase that catalyzes the ATP-dependent
CC       conversion of glutamate and ammonia to glutamine (PubMed:16267323,
CC       PubMed:30158707, PubMed:36289327). Its role depends on tissue
CC       localization: in the brain, it regulates the levels of toxic ammonia
CC       and converts neurotoxic glutamate to harmless glutamine, whereas in the
CC       liver, it is one of the enzymes responsible for the removal of ammonia
CC       (By similarity). Plays a key role in ammonium detoxification during
CC       erythropoiesis: the glutamine synthetase activity is required to remove
CC       ammonium generated by porphobilinogen deaminase (HMBS) during heme
CC       biosynthesis to prevent ammonium accumulation and oxidative stress (By
CC       similarity). Essential for proliferation of fetal skin fibroblasts
CC       (PubMed:18662667). Independently of its glutamine synthetase activity,
CC       required for endothelial cell migration during vascular development:
CC       acts by regulating membrane localization and activation of the GTPase
CC       RHOJ, possibly by promoting RHOJ palmitoylation (PubMed:30158707). May
CC       act as a palmitoyltransferase for RHOJ: able to autopalmitoylate and
CC       then transfer the palmitoyl group to RHOJ (PubMed:30158707). Plays a
CC       role in ribosomal 40S subunit biogenesis (PubMed:26711351). Through the
CC       interaction with BEST2, inhibits BEST2 channel activity by affecting
CC       the gating at the aperture in the absence of intracellular L-glutamate,
CC       but sensitizes BEST2 to intracellular L-glutamate, which promotes the
CC       opening of BEST2 and thus relieves its inhibitory effect on BEST2
CC       (PubMed:36289327). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P15105,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:16267323, ECO:0000269|PubMed:18662667,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:26711351, ECO:0000269|PubMed:30158707,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:36289327}.

CC       autosomal recessive disorder characterized by variable brain
CC       malformations, encephalopathy, severe developmental delay, seizures,
CC       and decreased glutamine levels in bodily fluids. Death in early infancy
CC       may occur. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:16267323, ECO:0000269|PubMed:26711351,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:38579670}. Note=The disease is caused by variants

CC   -!- DISEASE: Developmental and epileptic encephalopathy 116 (DEE116)
CC       [MIM:620806]: A form of epileptic encephalopathy, a heterogeneous group
CC       of early-onset epilepsies characterized by refractory seizures,
CC       neurodevelopmental impairment, and poor prognosis. Development is
CC       normal prior to seizure onset, after which cognitive and motor delays
CC       become apparent. DEE116 is autosomal dominant form characterized by
CC       severe developmental delay, seizures, and white matter abnormalities.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:38579670}. Note=The disease is caused by variants
CC       affecting the gene represented in this entry. DEE116 is caused by
CC       variants that disrupt the canonical translation start codon in GLUL
CC       resulting in initiation of translation at Met-18 (PubMed:38579670). The
CC       resulting protein is enzymatically competent but insensitive to
CC       negative feedback regulation via glutamine-induced degradation.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:38579670}.
