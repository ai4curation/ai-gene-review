CC   -!- FUNCTION: G protein-coupled receptor, which transduces the smoothened
CC       signaling pathway (PubMed:19592253, PubMed:27437577, PubMed:28344083,
CC       PubMed:31168089, PubMed:32929279, PubMed:36202993). Activated by
CC       cholesterol in response to hedgehog (DHH, IHH or SHH) morphogens
CC       (PubMed:27437577, PubMed:28344083, PubMed:32929279). In absence of
CC       hedgehog, SMO is inactivated by patched protein (PTCH1 or PTCH2), which
CC       prevents SMO access to cholesterol (PubMed:28344083). In response to
CC       hedgehog-binding to pathched, inhibition is relieved, promoting SMO
CC       translocation to primary cilium and activation by cholesterol:
CC       cholesterol causes a conformation change that triggers signaling via G
CC       protein G(i), mediating inhibition of adenylate cyclase activity and
CC       decreassed production of cAMP (PubMed:28344083, PubMed:31168089).
CC       Decreased cAMP levels then inhibit protein kinase A (PKA) and promote
CC       release of GLI (GLI1, GLI2 and GLI3) transcription factors, which
CC       activate expression of target genes (PubMed:31168089). Active SMO also
CC       directly inhibits PKA by sequestering its catalytic subunit, PRKACA, at
CC       the cell membrane, preventing PRKACA-mediated phosphorylation and
CC       subsequent processing of GLI transcription factors (PubMed:36202993,
CC       PubMed:39138140). Active SMO also promotes the removal of GPR161, a key
CC       inhibitor of the smoothened signaling pathway, from primary cilia (By
CC       similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P56726,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:19592253, ECO:0000269|PubMed:27437577,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:28344083, ECO:0000269|PubMed:31168089,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:32929279, ECO:0000269|PubMed:36202993,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:39138140}.
CC   -!- ACTIVITY REGULATION: Activated by small molecule TC114, a synthetic
CC       ligand (PubMed:28513578). Specifically inhibited by vismodegib, also
CC       named Erivedge, an inhibitor of the smoothened signaling pathway used
CC       for the treatment of basal-cell carcinoma: vismodegib acts by occluding
CC       the cholesterol-binding site (PubMed:27437577).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:27437577, ECO:0000269|PubMed:28513578}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homodimer (PubMed:23636324, PubMed:27437577). Interacts (via
CC       PKI motif) with protein kinase A catalytic subunit PRKACA; interacts
CC       with free PRKACA subunits and the interaction leads to sequestration of
CC       PRKACA at the membrane, preventing PRKACA-mediated phosphorylation of
CC       GLI transcription factors (PubMed:36202993, PubMed:39138140). Interacts
CC       with ARRB1 and ARRB2; promoting its localization to primery cilium (By
CC       similarity). Interacts with BBS5 and BBS7; the interactions are
CC       indicative for the association of SMO with the BBsome complex to
CC       facilitate ciliary localization of SMO (PubMed:22072986). Interacts
CC       with DLG5; Interaction takes place at the ciliary base to induce the
CC       accumulation of KIF7 and GLI2 at the ciliary tip for GLI2 activation
CC       (By similarity). Interacts with SDCBP (By similarity). Interacts with
CC       KIF7 (PubMed:19592253). Interacts with DRC4 (PubMed:21659505).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P56726, ECO:0000269|PubMed:19592253,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:21659505, ECO:0000269|PubMed:22072986,

CC   -!- DOMAIN: The FZ domain, also named extracellular cysteine-rich domain
CC       (CRD) binds cholesterol for activation of smoothened signaling
CC       (PubMed:24859340, PubMed:27437577, PubMed:32929279, PubMed:35658032).
CC       Asp-95, which constitutes the major cholesterol-binding site in the CRD
CC       region, can also bind cholesterol via a covalent attachment
CC       (PubMed:28344083, PubMed:32929279). A second cholesterol-binding site
CC       is present within the seven-transmembrane pocket which is also required
CC       for activation (PubMed:32929279). Cholesterol-binding to the CRD region
CC       causes conformational changes that trigger downstream signaling
CC       (PubMed:32929279). During activation, an intramolecular channel for
CC       cholesterol is formed with cholestol moving from the seven-
CC       transmembrane pocket region to reach the CRD region (PubMed:32929279).
CC       In addition to cholesterol, SMO is also activated by various oxysterols
CC       (oxidized cholesterol derivatives), such as (24S)-25-epoxycholesterol
CC       (PubMed:32929279). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:24859340,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:27437577, ECO:0000269|PubMed:28344083,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:32929279, ECO:0000269|PubMed:35658032}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The protein kinase A-inhibitor (PKI) motif acts as a
CC       pseudosubstrate motif, which sequesters PRKACA, the catalytic subunit
CC       of PKA, thereby preventing PKA-mediated phosphorylation and subsequent
CC       processing of GLI transcription factors. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:36202993}.
CC   -!- PTM: Phosphorylation by GRK2 and/GRK3 kinases promotes interaction with
CC       protein kinase A catalytic subunit PRKACA.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:39138140}.
CC   -!- DISEASE: Curry-Jones syndrome (CRJS) [MIM:601707]: A multisystem
CC       disorder characterized by patchy skin lesions, polysyndactyly, diverse
CC       cerebral malformations, unicoronal craniosynostosis, iris colobomas,
CC       microphthalmia, and intestinal malrotation with myofibromas or
CC       hamartomas. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:24859340, ECO:0000269|PubMed:27236920}.
CC       Note=The disease is caused by variants affecting the gene represented
CC       in this entry. 8 individuals have been identified with the disease-
CC       causing mutation Phe-412 and all were mosaic. The mutation could not be
CC       reliably detected in blood, greatest success rates were obtained with
CC       affected tissues obtained by invasive procedures. It is thought that
CC       the mutation has arisen postzygotically early during embryonic
CC       development (PubMed:27236920). This mutation has also been identified
CC       in ameloblastoma, medulloblastoma, meningioma, and basal cell
CC       carcinoma, and has been reported as the oncogenic driver in some of
CC       these tumors (PubMed:24859340). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:24859340,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:27236920}.
