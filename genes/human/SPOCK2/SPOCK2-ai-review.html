Gene Description

SPOCK2 (testican-2) is a secreted calcium-binding proteoglycan highly expressed in brain that contains chondroitin sulfate and heparan sulfate glycosaminoglycan chains. It has a modular domain structure comprising a follistatin-like domain, EC calcium-binding domain, Kazal-like domain, and thyroglobulin type-1 domain. Unlike other testican family members (testican-1/SPOCK1 and testican-3/SPOCK3) which inhibit membrane-type matrix metalloproteinases (MT-MMPs), testican-2 uniquely abrogates this inhibition by binding to and inactivating other testican proteins, thereby promoting cell motility and ECM remodeling. Calcium binding was experimentally demonstrated through reversible calcium-dependent conformational changes. SPOCK2 participates in neurogenesis and is widely expressed in multiple neuronal cell types throughout the CNS, with additional expression in lung and testis. Core functions include ECM organization, calcium ion binding, modulation of cell-substrate adhesion, and regulation of metalloendopeptidase activity through a unique mechanism of counter-inhibition.
