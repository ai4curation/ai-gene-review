CC   -!- FUNCTION: Component of the translocase of the outer membrane of
CC       mitochondria (TOM) complex essential for the recognition and
CC       translocation of cytosolically synthesized mitochondrial preproteins
CC       (PubMed:40080546). The TOM complex associates with the ion channel
CC       VDAC2 and PINK1 kinase at depolarized mitochondria, this interaction
CC       stabilizes PINK1 at the outer mitochondrial membrane and triggers
CC       downstream mitophagy by the recruitment of the E3 ubiquitin ligase PRKN
CC       (PubMed:40080546). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:18331822,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:24270810, ECO:0000269|PubMed:40080546}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Part of the translocase of the outer mitochondrial membrane
CC       (TOM complex) consisting of at least TOMM5, TOMM6, TOMM7, TOMM20,
CC       TOMM22 and TOMM40 (PubMed:12198123, PubMed:18331822, PubMed:40080546).
CC       TOMM70 may also be found in the TOM complex (PubMed:18331822). The TOM
CC       complex interacts with the VDAC2 homodimer (PubMed:40080546). Upon
CC       mitochondrial depolarization, the TOM-VDAC assembly interacts with
CC       PINK1; the interaction stabilizes PINK1 at the outer mitochondrial
CC       membrane and triggers downstream mitophagy (PubMed:40080546).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12198123, ECO:0000269|PubMed:18331822,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:40080546}.

CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Mitochondrion outer membrane
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12198123}; Single-pass membrane protein
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12198123, ECO:0000269|PubMed:40080546}.
CC   -!- DISEASE: Garg-Mishra progeroid syndrome (GMPGS) [MIM:620601]: An
CC       autosomal recessive syndrome characterized by a progeroid appearance,
CC       severe dwarfism, mandibular hypoplasia, hyperopia, and partial
CC       lipodystrophy. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:36282599,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:36299998}. Note=The disease is caused by variants
