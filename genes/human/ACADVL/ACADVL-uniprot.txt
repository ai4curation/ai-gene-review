CC   -!- FUNCTION: Catalyzes the first of the four reactions of the
CC       mitochondrial fatty acid beta-oxidation (FAO) pathway, which consists
CC       in the proR-proR stereospecific alpha, beta-dehydrogenation of fatty
CC       acyl-CoA thioesters using the electron transfer flavoprotein (ETF) as
CC       their physiologic electron acceptor, resulting in the formation of
CC       trans-2-enoyl-CoA ((2E)-enoyl-CoA) (PubMed:17564966, PubMed:18227065,
CC       PubMed:26474213, PubMed:7668252, PubMed:9461620, PubMed:9599005,
CC       PubMed:9839948). The mitochondrial FAO pathway is the major energy-
CC       producing process in tissues and is performed through cycles of four
CC       consecutive reactions (PubMed:26474213, PubMed:7668252). Each FAO cycle
CC       shortens the fatty acyl-CoA by two carbons, yielding one acetyl-CoA
CC       (for the citric acid cycle), one FADH(2), and one NADH (which donate
CC       electrons to the respiratory chain for ATP production)
CC       (PubMed:26474213, PubMed:7668252). Among the different mitochondrial
CC       acyl-CoA dehydrogenases, very long-chain specific acyl-CoA
CC       dehydrogenase acts specifically on fatty acyl-CoAs with saturated 12 to
CC       24 carbons long primary chains (PubMed:17564966, PubMed:21237683,
CC       PubMed:9839948), but can also catalyze monounsaturated fatty acids such
CC       as oleate ((9Z)-octadecenoate), (9Z)-hexadecenoate, and others
CC       (PubMed:17564966). Can use (4Z,7Z,10Z,13Z,16Z,19Z)-docosahexaenoate as
CC       substrate in vitro (which is not primarily used for energy but mainly
CC       beta-oxidized in the peroxisomes) (PubMed:17564966, PubMed:26474213).
CC       In addition, based on its established catalytic mechanism, and combined
CC       genetic interaction or mutant phenotype evidence, it is predicted to
CC       act also on substrates that have not been tested experimentally but are
CC       metabolized by mitochondrial FAO, including long-chain unsaturated
CC       fatty acids such as linoleate (9Z,12Z-octadecadienoate), linolenate
CC       (9Z,12Z,15Z-octadecatrienoate), and others (PubMed:26474213). Among the
CC       different mitochondrial acyl-CoA dehydrogenases, its FAO activity
CC       overlaps with that of ACAD9 and ACADL, but plays a primary role in
CC       tissues where it is the main long-chain ACAD expressed, such as the
CC       heart and skeletal muscle (PubMed:17564966).
