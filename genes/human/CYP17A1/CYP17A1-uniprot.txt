CC   -!- FUNCTION: A cytochrome P450 monooxygenase involved in corticoid and
CC       androgen biosynthesis (PubMed:22266943, PubMed:25301938,
CC       PubMed:27339894, PubMed:9452426). Catalyzes 17-alpha hydroxylation of
CC       C21 steroids, which is common for both pathways. A second oxidative
CC       step, required only for androgen synthesis, involves an acyl-carbon
CC       cleavage. The 17-alpha hydroxy intermediates, as part of adrenal
CC       glucocorticoids biosynthesis pathway, are precursors of cortisol
CC       (Probable) (PubMed:25301938, PubMed:9452426). Hydroxylates steroid
CC       hormones, pregnenolone and progesterone to form 17-alpha hydroxy
CC       metabolites, followed by the cleavage of the C17-C20 bond to form C19
CC       steroids, dehydroepiandrosterone (DHEA) and androstenedione
CC       (PubMed:22266943, PubMed:25301938, PubMed:27339894, PubMed:36640554,
CC       PubMed:9452426). Has 16-alpha hydroxylase activity. Catalyzes 16-alpha
CC       hydroxylation of 17-alpha hydroxy pregnenolone, followed by the
CC       cleavage of the C17-C20 bond to form 16-alpha-hydroxy DHEA
CC       (PubMed:36640554). Also 16-alpha hydroxylates androgens, relevant for
CC       estriol synthesis (PubMed:25301938, PubMed:27339894). Mechanistically,
CC       uses molecular oxygen inserting one oxygen atom into a substrate, and
CC       reducing the second into a water molecule, with two electrons provided
CC       by NADPH via cytochrome P450 reductase (CPR; NADPH-ferrihemoprotein
CC       reductase) (PubMed:22266943, PubMed:25301938, PubMed:27339894,
