CC   -!- FUNCTION: Catalyzes the irreversible stereospecific reduction of the
CC       delta 4,5 bond (double bond between carbons 4 and 5) of various 3-oxo
CC       steroids (3-keto steroids) producing their 5alpha dihydro-3-oxo forms
CC       (PubMed:10898110, PubMed:23685396). Converts testosterone into 5-alpha-
CC       dihydrotestosterone (DHT), the most active androgen in the prostate, as
CC       it is the preferred ligand for androgen receptor (AR) transactivation,
CC       making this reaction a key step in male sexual differentiation during
CC       development (PubMed:10898110, PubMed:23685396). Besides testosterone,
CC       it can also act on other steroids, including progesterone, producing
CC       metabolites with diverse roles (PubMed:10898110). Hence, it plays a
CC       central role in sexual differentiation and androgen physiology
CC       (PubMed:10898110). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10898110,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:23685396}.
