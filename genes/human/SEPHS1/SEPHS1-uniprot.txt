CC   -!- FUNCTION: Core component of the zincore complex, a heterotetramer that
CC       acts as a molecular 'grip' to stabilize transcription factors at DNA-
CC       binding sites across the genome, thereby controlling gene expression
CC       (PubMed:40608935). The zincore complex binds specifically to zinc
CC       finger transcription factors, such as ZFP91, ZNF652, ZNF526 and PRDM15,
CC       and stabilizes them onto their cognate DNA motif (PubMed:40608935).
CC       Within the complex, SEPHS1, recognizes and binds the backbone of zinc
CC       fingers of transcription factors in a sequence-independent manner via
CC       its arginine clamp, enhancing their DNA-binding stability
CC       (PubMed:40608935). Plays an essential role in redox homeostasis
CC       (PubMed:31607477). May also be involved in selenocysteine biosynthesis
CC       by catalyzing formation of selenophosphate from selenide and ATP
CC       (PubMed:7665581). Its role in selenocysteine biosynthesis is however
CC       unclear and several studies suggest that it does not act as a
CC       selenophosphate synthase in vivo or plays an non-essential role
CC       (PubMed:15534230). Required for cardiac differentiation (By
CC       similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8BH69,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:15534230, ECO:0000269|PubMed:31607477,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:40608935, ECO:0000269|PubMed:7665581}.
