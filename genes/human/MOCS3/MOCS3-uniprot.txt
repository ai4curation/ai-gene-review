CC   -!- FUNCTION: Plays a central role in 2-thiolation of mcm(5)S(2)U at tRNA
CC       wobble positions of cytosolic tRNA(Lys), tRNA(Glu) and tRNA(Gln)
CC       (PubMed:19017811, PubMed:22453920, PubMed:30817134). Also essential
CC       during biosynthesis of the molybdenum cofactor (PubMed:15073332,
CC       PubMed:22453920, PubMed:30817134). Acts by mediating the C-terminal
CC       thiocarboxylation of sulfur carriers URM1 and MOCS2A (PubMed:15073332,
CC       PubMed:19017811, PubMed:22453920). Its N-terminus first activates URM1
CC       and MOCS2A as acyl-adenylates (-COAMP), then the persulfide sulfur on
CC       the catalytic cysteine is transferred to URM1 and MOCS2A to form
CC       thiocarboxylation (-COSH) of their C-terminus (PubMed:19017811,
CC       PubMed:22453920). The reaction probably involves hydrogen sulfide that
CC       is generated from the persulfide intermediate and that acts as a
CC       nucleophile towards URM1 and MOCS2A (PubMed:15073332, PubMed:22453920).
CC       Subsequently, a transient disulfide bond is formed (PubMed:15073332,
CC       PubMed:22453920). Does not use thiosulfate as sulfur donor; NFS1 acting
CC       as a sulfur donor for thiocarboxylation reactions (PubMed:18650437,
CC       PubMed:22453920). {ECO:0000255|HAMAP-Rule:MF_03049,
