Gene Description

HSPB6 (also known as HSP20 or HspB6) is a member of the small heat shock protein (sHSP) family. It functions as an ATP-independent molecular chaperone (holdase) that binds partially unfolded or denatured proteins to prevent their irreversible aggregation, maintaining them in a folding-competent state. Unlike ATP-dependent chaperones such as HSP70, HSPB6 does not actively refold substrates. HSPB6 exists primarily as a homodimer and can form heterooligomers with HSPB1 and CRYAB. It is highly expressed in skeletal and smooth muscle, where it plays roles in smooth muscle vasorelaxation and cardiac contractility. Phosphorylation at Ser-16 by PKA regulates its interaction with 14-3-3 proteins (YWHAZ) and its cardioprotective effects. HSPB6 also functions as a secreted cardiokine promoting angiogenesis via VEGFR2 activation.

Core Functions

HSPB6 functions as an ATP-independent holdase chaperone that binds partially unfolded or denatured proteins to prevent their irreversible aggregation, maintaining substrates in a folding-competent state for downstream refolding by ATP-dependent chaperones such as HSP70. It exists primarily as a homodimer, which represents the basic chaperoning subspecies.
