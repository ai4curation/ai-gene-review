CC   -!- FUNCTION: Acyltransferase that catalyzes the acyl transfer from an
CC       acyl-CoA at the 2-OH position of the inositol ring of a 6-(alpha-D-
CC       glucosaminyl)-1-(1,2-diacyl-sn-glycero-3-phospho)-1D-myo-inositol
CC       (glucosaminyl phosphatidylinositol, GlcN-PI) to generate a 2-acyl-6-
CC       alpha-D-glucosaminyl-1-(1,2-diacyl-sn-glycero-3-phospho)-1D-myo-
CC       inositol (glucosaminyl acyl phosphatidylinositol, GlcN-(acyl)PI) and
CC       participates in the fourth step of GPI-anchor biosynthesis (By
CC       similarity). Required for the transport of GPI-anchored proteins to the
CC       plasma membrane (PubMed:24367057). Acylation during GPI-anchor
CC       biosynthesis is not essential for the subsequent mannosylation and is
CC       usually removed soon after the attachment of GPIs to proteins (By
CC       similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q7TSN4,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:24367057}.
