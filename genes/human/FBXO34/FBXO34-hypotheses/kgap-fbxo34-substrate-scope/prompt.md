# OpenScientist prompt: FBXO34 substrate scope and SCF adaptor function

Investigate the evidence that human FBXO34 is an SCF ubiquitin-ligase substrate-recognition adaptor, and determine which reported interactors are bona fide physiological ubiquitination substrates.

Focus on:

- direct evidence for SCF-FBXO34 complex formation and substrate-recognition activity;
- HNRNPU/hnRNP U as the best-supported human substrate or target;
- other reported FBXO34 interactors and whether they have direct FBXO34-dependent ubiquitination, degradation, half-life, rescue, or reconstitution evidence;
- whether high-throughput protein-binding annotations should remain non-core interaction leads or be converted into mechanistically supported substrate relationships.

Please distinguish degradative substrates from non-substrate interactors and indirect pathway effects. Conclude whether current GO-style curation should stop at broad ubiquitin-like ligase-substrate adaptor activity or support additional substrate/pathway-specific annotations. Include PMIDs and flag uncached or uncertain citations.
