Gene Description

AIMP2 (also known as p38/JTV-1) is a non-enzymatic scaffold protein essential for the assembly and stability of the multi-aminoacyl-tRNA synthetase complex (MSC). Through its GST-like C-terminal domain, AIMP2 heterodimerizes with EPRS1 and connects to other MSC submodules including DRS-KARS. The N-terminal region directly binds KARS1 (lysyl-tRNA synthetase). Beyond its structural role in the MSC, AIMP2 has well-documented moonlighting functions in tumor suppression: upon DNA damage or TGF-beta signaling, AIMP2 is phosphorylated (by JNK/p38 MAPK), dissociates from the MSC, translocates to the nucleus, and stabilizes p53 by preventing MDM2-mediated ubiquitination. AIMP2 also promotes FUBP1 degradation to suppress c-Myc, modulates TNF-alpha signaling via TRAF2, and inhibits Wnt/beta-catenin signaling through DVL1 binding. In Parkinson's disease, AIMP2 accumulation (due to defective parkin-mediated ubiquitination) drives PARP-1-dependent parthanatos. The DX2 splice variant lacking exon 2 antagonizes AIMP2 functions and has oncogenic properties.

Core Functions

AIMP2 is a non-enzymatic scaffold protein that brings together multiple aminoacyl-tRNA synthetases (KARS1, EPRS1, DARS1, and others) and auxiliary proteins to form the multi-aminoacyl-tRNA synthetase complex (MSC). Its GST-like C-terminal domain mediates heterodimerization with EPRS1, while its N-terminal region directly binds KARS1 (lysyl-tRNA synthetase). This adaptor function is essential for MSC assembly and stability.

  AIMP2 (also known as p38/JTV-1) is a non-enzymatic scaffold protein essential for the assembly and stability
  of the multi-aminoacyl-tRNA synthetase complex (MSC). Through its GST-like C-terminal domain, AIMP2
  heterodimerizes with EPRS1 and connects to other MSC submodules including DRS-KARS. The N-terminal region
  directly binds KARS1 (lysyl-tRNA synthetase). Beyond its structural role in the MSC, AIMP2 has well-documented
  moonlighting functions in tumor suppression: upon DNA damage or TGF-beta signaling, AIMP2 is phosphorylated
  (by JNK/p38 MAPK), dissociates from the MSC, translocates to the nucleus, and stabilizes p53 by preventing
  MDM2-mediated ubiquitination. AIMP2 also promotes FUBP1 degradation to suppress c-Myc, modulates TNF-alpha
  signaling via TRAF2, and inhibits Wnt/beta-catenin signaling through DVL1 binding. In Parkinson's disease,
  AIMP2 accumulation (due to defective parkin-mediated ubiquitination) drives PARP-1-dependent parthanatos.
  The DX2 splice variant lacking exon 2 antagonizes AIMP2 functions and has oncogenic properties.
