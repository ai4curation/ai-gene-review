CC   -!- FUNCTION: Hormone produced in response to various stresses to confer
CC       information about those stresses to the brain, and trigger an aversive
CC       response, characterized by nausea, vomiting, and/or loss of appetite
CC       (PubMed:23468844, PubMed:24971956, PubMed:28846097, PubMed:28846098,
CC       PubMed:28846099, PubMed:28953886, PubMed:29046435, PubMed:30639358,
CC       PubMed:31875646, PubMed:33589633, PubMed:38092039). The aversive
CC       response is both required to reduce continuing exposure to those
CC       stresses at the time of exposure and to promote avoidance behavior in
CC       the future (PubMed:30639358, PubMed:33589633, PubMed:38092039). Acts by
CC       binding to its receptor, GFRAL, activating GFRAL-expressing neurons
CC       localized in the area postrema and nucleus tractus solitarius of the
CC       brainstem (PubMed:28846097, PubMed:28846098, PubMed:28846099,
CC       PubMed:28953886, PubMed:31535977). It then triggers the activation of
CC       neurons localized within the parabrachial nucleus and central amygdala,
CC       which constitutes part of the 'emergency circuit' that shapes responses
CC       to stressful conditions (PubMed:28953886). The GDF15-GFRAL signal
CC       induces expression of genes involved in metabolism, such as lipid
CC       metabolism in adipose tissues (PubMed:31402172). Required for avoidance
CC       behavior in response to food allergens: induced downstream of mast cell
CC       activation to promote aversion and minimize harmful effects of exposure
CC       to noxious substances (By similarity). In addition to suppress
CC       appetite, also promotes weight loss by enhancing energy expenditure in
CC       muscle: acts by increasing calcium futile cycling in muscle (By
CC       similarity). Contributes to the effect of metformin, an anti-diabetic
CC       drug, on appetite reduction and weight loss: produced in the kidney in
CC       response to metformin treatment, thereby activating the GDF15-GFRAL
CC       response, leading to reduced appetite and weight (PubMed:31875646,
CC       PubMed:37060902). The contribution of GDF15 to weight loss following
CC       metformin treatment is however limited and subject to discussion
CC       (PubMed:36001956). Produced in response to anticancer drugs, such as
CC       camptothecin or cisplatin, promoting nausea, vomiting and contributing
CC       to malnutrition (By similarity). Overproduced in many cancers,
CC       promoting anorexia in cancer (cachexia) (PubMed:32661391). Responsible
CC       for the risk of nausea and vomiting during pregnancy: high levels of
CC       GDF15 during pregnancy, mostly originating from the fetus, are
CC       associated with increased nausea and vomiting (PubMed:38092039).
CC       Maternal sensitivity to nausea is probably determined by pre-pregnancy
CC       exposure to GDF15, women with naturally high level of GDF15 being less
CC       susceptible to nausea than women with low levels of GDF15 before
CC       pregnancy (PubMed:38092039). Promotes metabolic adaptation in response
CC       to systemic inflammation caused by bacterial and viral infections in
CC       order to promote tissue tolerance and prevent tissue damage
CC       (PubMed:31402172). Required for tissue tolerance in response to
CC       myocardial infarction by acting as an inhibitor of leukocyte integring
CC       activation, thereby protecting against cardiac rupture (By similarity).
CC       Inhibits growth hormone signaling on hepatocytes (By similarity).
