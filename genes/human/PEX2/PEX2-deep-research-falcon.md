# Research Report: Human **PEX2** (UniProt **P28328**) — Functional Annotation and Current Evidence

## 1) Target verification (gene/protein identity)
The retrieved literature consistently matches the UniProt target **human PEX2 (P28328)** as a **peroxisomal membrane peroxin** containing a **RING-finger domain** and functioning as part of the **PEX2–PEX10–PEX12** ubiquitin ligase complex required for peroxisomal protein import receptor ubiquitination and recycling. Specifically, PEX2 is described as forming the **RING E3 ligase complex with PEX10 and PEX12** and participating in ubiquitination of PTS receptors (kumar2024theperoxisomean pages 7-9), aligning with UniProt’s description of a peroxisomal membrane protein in the pex2/pex10/pex12 family.

## 2) Key concepts and definitions (current understanding)

### 2.1 Peroxisomal matrix protein import and receptor recycling
Peroxisomes import most matrix proteins post-translationally using targeting signals **PTS1** (recognized by **PEX5**) and **PTS2** (recognized by **PEX7**, typically with receptor/adaptor participation depending on organism). Receptors dock at the peroxisomal membrane import machinery and must be **recycled back to the cytosol** to sustain ongoing import (kumar2024theperoxisomean pages 10-11, pandey2024molecularinteractionsof pages 20-22).

A central concept is that **cargo translocation can be energetically distinct from receptor recycling**: cargo delivery is described as energy-independent, whereas receptor extraction/recycling is **ATP-dependent** through AAA+ ATPases (pandey2024molecularinteractionsof pages 16-20).

### 2.2 The peroxisomal RING E3 ligase complex (PEX2/PEX10/PEX12)
PEX2 is one of three **RING-finger peroxins** (PEX2/PEX10/PEX12) that form a **membrane-embedded E3 ubiquitin ligase** complex required for ubiquitination of the import receptors (kumar2024theperoxisomean pages 10-11, kumar2024theperoxisomean pages 7-9). This ligase complex supports two key ubiquitination outcomes:
- **Mono-ubiquitination of PEX5** (on a conserved N-terminal cysteine) to trigger extraction and recycling (kumar2024theperoxisomean pages 10-11, pandey2024molecularinteractionsof pages 16-20).
- **Poly-ubiquitination** (typically on lysines) that targets receptors for proteasomal degradation via a quality-control route termed the **RADAR pathway** (kumar2024theperoxisomean pages 10-11).

### 2.3 Substrate specificity (what PEX2 acts on)
The best-supported direct functional substrate relationship in the retrieved corpus is that the PEX2/PEX10/PEX12 complex catalyzes **mono-ubiquitination of PEX5** at **Cys11 (human)**, which is required for receptor export by the AAA ATPase machinery (pandey2024molecularinteractionsof pages 16-20). Kumar et al. additionally describe mechanistic models in which the **PEX5 N-terminus inserts into the ligase pore** prior to mono-ubiquitination (kumar2024theperoxisomean pages 10-11).

## 3) Molecular function and mechanism of PEX2

### 3.1 Molecular function: RING-type E3 ubiquitin ligase (peroxisomal membrane)
PEX2 functions as a component of the **RING-type E3 ubiquitin ligase complex** at the peroxisomal membrane, together with PEX10 and PEX12 (kumar2024theperoxisomean pages 10-11, kumar2024theperoxisomean pages 7-9). The 2024 review highlights that the three RING domains form a **cytosolic tower**, and the membrane segments of the three proteins contribute to a **channel/pore-like architecture** involved in “retrotranslocation” of receptors (kumar2024theperoxisomean pages 10-11).

### 3.2 Stepwise role in receptor recycling
A mechanistic sequence supported by the retrieved evidence is:
1. **PEX5** docks at the peroxisomal membrane import machinery (with PEX13/PEX14 implicated as key components of docking/translocation) (pandey2024molecularinteractionsof pages 16-20).
2. The **PEX5 N-terminus** is proposed to be positioned/inserted into the **PEX2/PEX10/PEX12 ligase pore** (kumar2024theperoxisomean pages 10-11, pandey2024molecularinteractionsof pages 20-22).
3. The PEX2/PEX10/PEX12 complex **mono-ubiquitinates PEX5** at **Cys11** (pandey2024molecularinteractionsof pages 16-20).
4. The **receptor export module** (REM), consisting of **PEX1–PEX6** AAA ATPase complex anchored by **PEX26** in mammals, uses ATP hydrolysis to extract ubiquitinated PEX5 back to the cytosol (kumar2024theperoxisomean pages 10-11, pandey2024molecularinteractionsof pages 16-20).
5. Extracted PEX5 is subsequently **deubiquitinated** (a mammalian DUB cited in the retrieved mechanistic synthesis is **USP9X**) to reset the cycle (pandey2024molecularinteractionsof pages 20-22).

In addition to recycling, the review describes **poly-ubiquitination** of receptors as a signal for **proteasome-mediated degradation** (RADAR), providing quality control when recycling fails or receptors are damaged (kumar2024theperoxisomean pages 10-11).

### 3.3 Localization and topology
PEX2 is described as a peroxin typically **associated with or embedded in the peroxisomal membrane**, and specifically as part of a membrane-embedded ligase/pore complex with PEX10 and PEX12 (kumar2024theperoxisomean pages 10-11, kumar2024theperoxisomean pages 7-9). The 2024 review discusses cryo-EM structural insight (from a thermophilic fungal complex) suggesting each subunit contributes multiple transmembrane segments forming an open channel, with PEX2’s RING domain positioned above the pore (kumar2024theperoxisomean pages 10-11). While this structural work is not human per se, it is used in the review to support a conserved mechanistic model relevant to mammalian PEX2 function (kumar2024theperoxisomean pages 10-11).

## 5) Current applications and real-world implementations

### 5.1 Clinical genomics and diagnostics for peroxisome biogenesis disorders (including PEX2)
A practical, real-world implementation highlighted by recent case-series literature is the use of **clinical exome sequencing** to diagnose peroxisomal disorders when presentations are atypical. In an Iranian case series of **14 patients** with peroxisome biogenesis defects (sampling period 2018–2024), **PEX2 variants were detected in 3/14 patients**, alongside variants in other PEX genes (PEX1 in 5 patients; PEX5 in 1; PEX6 in 1; PEX7 in 2) (khalilian2025spectrumofgenetic pages 1-2). This illustrates the role of exome-based testing in resolving PEX-gene etiologies in heterogeneous clinical cohorts.

A separate real-world diagnostic example is a report using **whole-exome sequencing (WES)** and orthogonal confirmation to identify a **de novo ~1.6 Mb deletion spanning PEX2 and ZFHX4** in a patient with Peters anomaly spectrum disorder and systemic anomalies (delas2025novelgeneticvariants pages 1-2). While not a classic biallelic PEX2 Zellweger-spectrum diagnosis, it demonstrates how WES can detect larger structural events involving PEX2 in clinical practice (delas2025novelgeneticvariants pages 1-2).

## 10) Concluding functional annotation (high-confidence)
Human **PEX2 (P28328)** is best supported as an **integral peroxisomal membrane RING peroxin** that functions as a core component of the **PEX2–PEX10–PEX12 RING E3 ubiquitin ligase complex**. Its primary mechanistic role is in **ubiquitination of the PTS1 receptor PEX5**, including **mono-ubiquitination at human PEX5 Cys11** to initiate receptor extraction by **PEX1/PEX6/PEX26**, thereby enabling repeated rounds of peroxisomal matrix protein import. The complex also supports receptor quality control via **poly-ubiquitination** and proteasomal degradation (RADAR) when recycling is compromised (kumar2024theperoxisomean pages 10-11, pandey2024molecularinteractionsof pages 16-20).
