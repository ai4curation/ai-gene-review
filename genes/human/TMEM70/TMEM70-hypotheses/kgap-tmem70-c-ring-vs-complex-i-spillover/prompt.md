# AIGR Gene Hypothesis Deep Research

Evaluate one focused AI Gene Review knowledge-gap hypothesis.

## Target Gene

- Organism code: human
- Taxon: Homo sapiens (NCBITaxon:9606)
- Gene symbol: TMEM70
- UniProt accession: Q9BUB7

## Seed Hypothesis

TMEM70 directly assists mitochondrial ATP synthase subunit-c membrane insertion,
c-ring nucleation, and handoff to TMEM242, while reported respiratory-chain
complex I defects are secondary consequences of complex V failure, cristae
disruption, or broader OXPHOS remodeling rather than a direct TMEM70 complex I
assembly function.

## Context

- The gene review accepts TMEM70 as an ATP synthase assembly scaffold and ATP
  synthase complex-binding factor.
- The unresolved boundary is whether TMEM70 should also be annotated to complex
  I assembly, or whether those phenotypes are indirect effects of ATP synthase
  biogenesis failure.
- Priority evidence includes structural or biochemical studies of TMEM70,
  subunit-c intermediates, TMEM242, complex V content, complex I assembly
  intermediates, cristae morphology, and patient/model rescue data.

## Research Objective

Determine whether the hypothesis is supported, partially supported, unresolved,
or refuted. Focus on:

1. Evidence that TMEM70 directly participates in ATP synthase c-ring/subunit-c
   assembly.
2. Evidence that TMEM242 acts downstream, upstream, or in parallel with TMEM70.
3. Evidence for a direct TMEM70 role in complex I assembly versus secondary
   OXPHOS/cristae effects after complex V disruption.

Use primary literature and compact sequence/domain/topology checks where useful.
Avoid broad exploratory workflows; a concise evidence review plus targeted
mitochondrial membrane topology and complex-assembly interpretation is
sufficient.

## Required Output

Include:

- Executive judgment.
- Evidence matrix with citations, evidence type, claim tested, finding, context,
  confidence, and limitations.
- GO curation implications for ATP synthase assembly, c-ring assembly assistance,
  complex I assembly, mitochondrial membrane/cristae phenotypes, and disease
  downstream effects.
- Remaining decisive experiments.
