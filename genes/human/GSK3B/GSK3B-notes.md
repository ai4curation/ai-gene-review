# GSK3B notes

Automated deep research was attempted with `just deep-research-falcon human GSK3B --fallback perplexity-lite`, but the run timed out before producing a deep-research file. This review therefore uses cached GOA publications, the UniProt record, Reactome-derived entries, and the PANTHER family fetch.

GSK3B encodes glycogen synthase kinase-3 beta, a constitutively active serine/threonine kinase in the GSK-3 family. UniProt describes it as a "Constitutively active protein kinase" that regulates glucose homeostasis, Wnt signaling, transcription factors, and microtubules, with broad substrate scope including glycogen synthase, beta-catenin, tau, APC/AXIN1, JUN, NFATC1, SNAI1, RICTOR, and other proteins [file:human/GSK3B/GSK3B-uniprot.txt "Constitutively active protein kinase"; file:human/GSK3B/GSK3B-uniprot.txt "Wnt signaling"; file:human/GSK3B/GSK3B-uniprot.txt "phosphorylating and inactivating glycogen synthase"]. PANTHER places GSK3B in `PTHR24057:SF8`, glycogen synthase kinase-3 beta.

The catalytic core is ATP-dependent protein serine/threonine kinase activity, including tau-specific kinase activity. UniProt catalytic reactions include "L-seryl-[protein] + ATP", "L-seryl-[tau protein] + ATP", and "L-threonyl-[tau protein] + ATP" [file:human/GSK3B/GSK3B-uniprot.txt "L-seryl-[protein] + ATP"; file:human/GSK3B/GSK3B-uniprot.txt "L-seryl-[tau protein] + ATP"; file:human/GSK3B/GSK3B-uniprot.txt "L-threonyl-[tau protein] + ATP"]. Activity is regulated by activating Tyr-216 phosphorylation and inhibitory Ser-9 phosphorylation [file:human/GSK3B/GSK3B-uniprot.txt "Activated by phosphorylation at Tyr-216"; file:human/GSK3B/GSK3B-uniprot.txt "inhibited by phosphorylation at Ser-9"].

Tau phosphorylation is a direct Alzheimer-relevant substrate branch. UniProt states that GSK3B phosphorylates MAPT/TAU and that tau is the principal component of Alzheimer neurofibrillary tangles [file:human/GSK3B/GSK3B-uniprot.txt "Phosphorylates MAPT/TAU on"; file:human/GSK3B/GSK3B-uniprot.txt "ability to bind and"; file:human/GSK3B/GSK3B-uniprot.txt "stabilize microtubules"]. Cho and Johnson report that primed GSK3beta phosphorylation of tau regulates "tau's ability to bind and stabilize microtubules" and that "GSK3beta plays a critical role" in that process [PMID:14690523 "tau's ability to bind and stabilize microtubules"; PMID:14690523 "GSK3beta plays a critical role"].

Wnt/beta-catenin regulation is a core signaling context for GSK3B. UniProt states that GSK3B "phosphorylates the N-terminus of CTNNB1 leading to its degradation" [file:human/GSK3B/GSK3B-uniprot.txt "phosphorylates the N-terminus of CTNNB1 leading to its degradation"]. Primary beta-catenin/APC work reports that APC binds GSK3 beta and that beta-catenin binding depends on GSK3 beta phosphorylation [PMID:8638126 "APC binds to another component of the WINGLESS pathway"; PMID:8638126 "phosphorylation by GSK3 beta"]. The beta-catenin phosphorylation/degradation paper supports the priming-kinase and GSK-3 sequence in Wnt/beta-catenin signaling [PMID:11955436 "Wnt regulation of beta-catenin degradation"; PMID:11955436 "subsequent GSK-3 phosphorylation"].

Many other GOA processes are credible but pleiotropic downstream outputs of this broad kinase. NFAT nuclear export is supported by the Science abstract saying "GSK-3 phosphorylates conserved serines" and "promotes nuclear exit" [PMID:9072970 "GSK-3 phosphorylates conserved serines"; PMID:9072970 "promotes nuclear exit"]. SNAI1/EMT regulation is supported by the Snail paper reporting "GSK-3beta binds to" and "phosphorylates Snail" [PMID:15448698 "GSK-3beta binds to"; PMID:15448698 "phosphorylates Snail"]. ER-stress/mTORC2 signaling is supported by the rictor paper reporting that GSK3B phosphorylates the "mTORC2 component rictor" [PMID:21343617 "mTORC2 component rictor"; PMID:21343617 "rictor at serine-1235"]. These branches should generally be kept as non-core pathway-specific consequences rather than treated as the defining function of GSK3B.

For curation, accept kinase activity, ATP binding, tau-protein kinase activity, protein phosphorylation/autophosphorylation, beta-catenin binding/destruction-complex/Wnt negative regulation, glycogen metabolic regulation, and core cytosol/cytoplasm/nuclear/plasma-membrane localizations. Keep substrate-specific and pathway-specific outputs as non-core unless they directly represent central kinase/substrate logic. Mark generic `protein binding` and broad uninformative process terms as over-annotated.
