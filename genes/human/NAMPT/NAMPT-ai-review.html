Gene Description

NAMPT (nicotinamide phosphoribosyltransferase; also known as pre-B-cell colony-enhancing factor 1, PBEF, and visfatin) is the rate-limiting enzyme of the mammalian NAD+ salvage pathway (EC 2.4.2.12). It catalyzes the condensation of nicotinamide with 5-phospho-alpha-D-ribose 1-diphosphate (PRPP) to form nicotinamide mononucleotide (NMN), the immediate precursor of NAD+, thereby recycling the nicotinamide released by NAD+-consuming enzymes such as sirtuins, PARPs, and CD38. The active enzyme is a homodimer, and catalysis is stimulated by ATP-dependent phosphorylation of an active-site histidine (His247). NAMPT exists in an intracellular form (iNAMPT), which supplies NAD+ for cytosolic and nuclear NAD+-dependent processes, and a secreted extracellular form (eNAMPT/visfatin) that circulates as an adipocytokine; the secreted form lacks robust enzymatic activity under extracellular conditions and behaves as a cytokine and adipokine. Through NAD+-dependent regulation of SIRT1 and the CLOCK-BMAL1 heterodimer, NAMPT contributes to circadian oscillation of NAD+ and clock-controlled gene expression. NAMPT is highly expressed in bone marrow, liver, and muscle, and is a well-studied anticancer drug target (inhibited by FK866/APO866).

  NAMPT (nicotinamide phosphoribosyltransferase; also known as pre-B-cell
  colony-enhancing factor 1, PBEF, and visfatin) is the rate-limiting enzyme of
  the mammalian NAD+ salvage pathway (EC 2.4.2.12). It catalyzes the condensation
  of nicotinamide with 5-phospho-alpha-D-ribose 1-diphosphate (PRPP) to form
  nicotinamide mononucleotide (NMN), the immediate precursor of NAD+, thereby
  recycling the nicotinamide released by NAD+-consuming enzymes such as sirtuins,
  PARPs, and CD38. The active enzyme is a homodimer, and catalysis is stimulated
  by ATP-dependent phosphorylation of an active-site histidine (His247). NAMPT
  exists in an intracellular form (iNAMPT), which supplies NAD+ for cytosolic and
  nuclear NAD+-dependent processes, and a secreted extracellular form
  (eNAMPT/visfatin) that circulates as an adipocytokine; the secreted form lacks
  robust enzymatic activity under extracellular conditions and behaves as a
  cytokine and adipokine. Through NAD+-dependent regulation of SIRT1 and the
  CLOCK-BMAL1 heterodimer, NAMPT contributes to circadian oscillation of NAD+ and
  clock-controlled gene expression. NAMPT is highly expressed in bone marrow,
  liver, and muscle, and is a well-studied anticancer drug target (inhibited by
  FK866/APO866).
