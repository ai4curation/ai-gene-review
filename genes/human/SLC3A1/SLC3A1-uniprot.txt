CC   -!- FUNCTION: Acts as a chaperone that facilitates biogenesis and
CC       trafficking of functional transporter heteromers to the plasma membrane
CC       (By similarity) (PubMed:10588648, PubMed:11318953, PubMed:16609684,
CC       PubMed:16825196, PubMed:32494597, PubMed:32817565, PubMed:7686906,
CC       PubMed:8486766, PubMed:8663184, PubMed:8663357). Associates with SLC7A9
CC       to form a functional transporter complex that mediates the electrogenic
CC       exchange between cationic amino acids and neutral amino acids, with a
CC       stoichiometry of 1:1. SLC7A9-SLC3A1 transporter has system b(0,+)-like
CC       activity with high affinity for extracellular cationic amino acids and
CC       L-cystine and lower affinity for intracellular neutral amino acids.
CC       Substrate exchange is driven by high concentration of intracellular
CC       neutral amino acids and the intracellular reduction of L-cystine to L-
CC       cysteine. SLC7A9-SLC3A1 acts as a major transporter for reabsorption of
CC       L-cystine and dibasic amino acids across the brush border membrane in
CC       early proximal tubules (PubMed:10588648, PubMed:11318953,
CC       PubMed:16609684, PubMed:16825196, PubMed:32494597, PubMed:32817565,
CC       PubMed:7686906, PubMed:8486766, PubMed:8663184, PubMed:8663357).
CC       Associates with SLC7A13 to form a functional complex that transports
CC       anionic and neutral amino acids via exchange or facilitated diffusion.
CC       SLC7A13-SLC3A1 may act as a major transporter for L-cystine in late
CC       proximal tubules, ensuring its reabsorption from the luminal fluid in
CC       exchange for cytosolic L-glutamate or L-aspartate (By similarity).
