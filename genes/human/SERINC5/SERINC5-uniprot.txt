CC   -!- FUNCTION: Restriction factor required to restrict infectivity of
CC       lentiviruses, such as HIV-1: acts by inhibiting an early step of viral
CC       infection. Impairs the penetration of the viral particle into the
CC       cytoplasm (PubMed:26416733, PubMed:26416734). Non-ATP-dependent, non-
CC       specific lipid transporter for phosphatidylserine, phosphatidylcholine,
CC       and phosphatidylethanolamine. Functions as a scramblase that flips
CC       lipids in both directions across the membrane. Phospholipid scrambling
CC       results in HIV-1 surface exposure of phosphatidylserine and loss of
CC       membrane asymmetry, which leads to changes in HIV-1 Env conformation
CC       and loss of infectivity (PubMed:37474505). Enhances the incorporation
CC       of serine into phosphatidylserine and sphingolipids. May play a role in
CC       providing serine molecules for the formation of myelin
CC       glycosphingolipids in oligodendrocytes (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q63175, ECO:0000269|PubMed:26416733,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:26416734, ECO:0000269|PubMed:37474505}.
