## Question

Research the USO1 (O60763) gene in human, focusing on its molecular function, biological processes, and cellular localization. Include information about protein domains, known interactions, and any disease associations.

## Output

The **USO1 gene** (UniProt: O60763) in humans encodes the **general vesicular transport factor p115**, a key peripheral membrane protein involved in vesicular trafficking between the endoplasmic reticulum (ER) and the Golgi apparatus[1][3][11].

---

### 1. Key Concepts and Definitions

- **Molecular Function:**  
  USO1 functions as a **vesicular transport factor** required for the **intercisternal transport within the Golgi stack** and for the **fusion and/or binding of vesicles to target membranes**[3][11]. It acts as a vesicular anchor, holding vesicular and target membranes in proximity to facilitate membrane fusion[11].

- **Biological Processes:**  
  - **ER-to-Golgi vesicle-mediated transport:** USO1 is essential for the transport of vesicles from the ER to the cis/medial Golgi compartments[1][3][11].
  - **Golgi organization and maintenance:** It is involved in maintaining Golgi structure and function, particularly during interphase[1][3].
  - **Cell cycle regulation:** USO1 cycles between the cytosol and Golgi, regulated by phosphorylation status[1][3][5].
  - **Protein trafficking and secretion:** By facilitating vesicle docking and fusion, USO1 is critical for the secretory pathway[1][3][11].

- **Cellular Localization:**  
  USO1 is a **peripheral membrane protein** that **recycles between the cytosol and the Golgi apparatus** during interphase[1][3][5][7][11]. Its association with the Golgi membrane is regulated by phosphorylation: dephosphorylated USO1 binds the Golgi, while phosphorylation causes dissociation[1][3][5].

---

### 2. Protein Domains

- **Coiled-coil domains:**  
  USO1 contains extensive **coiled-coil regions** that mediate its dimerization and interactions with other vesicular transport proteins[11].
- **N-terminal and C-terminal domains:**  
  The N-terminal region is involved in membrane binding, while the C-terminal region interacts with other tethering factors and SNARE proteins[11].

---

### 3. Known Interactions

- **COPII vesicles:**  
  USO1 is recruited to COPII vesicles budding from the ER by **Ras-associated protein 1 (RAP1)**[1][3][5].
- **SNARE proteins:**  
  It interacts with COPII vesicle-associated SNAREs to form a **cis-SNARE complex**, promoting vesicle targeting and fusion with the Golgi[1][3][11].
- **GOLGA2 (GM130) and Giantin:**  
  USO1 acts sequentially with these Golgi matrix proteins to mediate vesicle tethering and fusion[1][3].
- **Other vesicle tethering factors:**  
  USO1 is part of a larger network of proteins involved in vesicle docking and fusion, including Rab GTPases and other tethering complexes[11].

### 4. Disease Associations

- **Retinal Disease:**  
  Mutations or dysregulation of USO1 have been associated with certain **retinal diseases**[3].
- **Hypogonadotropic Hypogonadism 14 With Or Without Anosmia:**  
  USO1 is listed as associated with this rare endocrine disorder[3].
- **Cancer:**  
  Recent studies suggest that **USO1 isoforms differentially promote liver cancer progression** by dysregulating the ER-Golgi network[12].
- **Multiple Myeloma:**  
  USO1 may be a promising target for therapy and a diagnostic marker in **multiple myeloma**[9].

  New research (2023–2024) highlights the role of USO1 isoforms in **liver cancer progression** through effects on the ER-Golgi network, suggesting a potential target for cancer therapy[12].
