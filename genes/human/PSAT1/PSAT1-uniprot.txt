CC   -!- FUNCTION: Involved in L-serine biosynthesis via the phosphorylated
CC       pathway, a three-step pathway converting the glycolytic intermediate 3-
CC       phospho-D-glycerate into L-serine (PubMed:36851825, PubMed:37627284).
CC       Catalyzes the second step, that is the pyridoxal 5'-phosphate-dependent
CC       transamination of 3-phosphohydroxypyruvate and L-glutamate to O-
CC       phosphoserine (OPS) and alpha-ketoglutarate (PubMed:36851825,
CC       PubMed:37627284). Acts as an inhibitor of ferroptosis in response to
CC       interferon-gamma (IFNG) by promoting GPX4 stability: following
CC       phosphorylation by CAMK2A, PSAT1 interacts with GPX4 and provides 2-
CC       oxoglutarate to EGLN3, leading to GPX4 hydroxylation and stability
CC       (PubMed:40281343). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12633500,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:36851825, ECO:0000269|PubMed:37627284,
