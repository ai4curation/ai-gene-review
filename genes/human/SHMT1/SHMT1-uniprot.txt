CC   -!- FUNCTION: Pyridoxal phosphate (PLP)-dependent enzyme that catalyzes the
CC       reversible conversion of serine and tetrahydrofolate (THF) to glycine
CC       and 5,10-methylene THF, serving as a critical component of the folate
CC       cycle and facilitating one-carbon biosynthetic reactions essential for
CC       methionine, purine, and pyrimidine synthesis (PubMed:24698160,
CC       PubMed:30035852, PubMed:38996576). While its central activity involves
CC       serine cleavage, the detailed catalytic mechanisms remain under study,
CC       including both retro-aldol cleavage of the PLP-serine C(alpha)-C(beta)
CC       bond followed by formaldehyde condensation with THF, and alternative
CC       nucleophilic displacement mechanisms of the C(alpha) atom of PLP-serine
CC       aldimine involving THF's N5 atom (By similarity). Also catalyzes the
CC       cleavage of various 3-hydroxy amino acids, such as L-allo-threonine, L-
CC       threonine and 3-phenylserine, forming glycine and the corresponding
CC       aldehyde through a retro-aldol process; additionally, it catalyzes the
CC       formation of 5-formyltetrahydrofolate from 5,10-
CC       methenyltetrahydrofolate (PubMed:38615009). Also functions as a
CC       hydroxytrimethyllysine aldolase (HTMLA) catalyzing the second step of
CC       the carnitine biosynthesis pathway and exhibits substrate preference
CC       with the erythro (S,S) configuration, and more efficiency with L-allo-
CC       threonine (PubMed:38615009). In the nucleus, first functions as a
CC       lamin-binding scaffold protein that is essential for assembling the de
CC       novo thymidylate synthesis complex by co-localizing DHFR and TYMS with
CC       the nuclear lamina and anchoring the complex to DNA replication sites
CC       (PubMed:22235121). Subsequently, provides one-carbon substrates,
CC       specifically (6R)-5,10-methylene-5,6,7,8-tetrahydrofolate, in situ for
CC       de novo dTMP synthesis to sustain DNA replication and repair during
CC       cell proliferation (PubMed:30035852). Importantly, possesses RNA-
CC       binding capability, forming complexes that selectively regulate SHMT2
CC       mRNA translation and dynamically modulate cytosolic and mitochondrial
CC       serine and glycine concentrations, thus influencing cellular metabolic
CC       status (PubMed:38996576). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P07511,
