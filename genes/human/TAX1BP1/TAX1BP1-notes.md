# TAX1BP1 notes

## PN-relevant synthesis

- TAX1BP1 is well supported as a selective-autophagy receptor/cargo adaptor, especially in xenophagy and ubiquitin-condensate-directed autophagy [PMID:26451915 The Autophagy Receptor TAX1BP1 and the Molecular Motor Myosin VI Are Required for Clearance of Salmonella Typhimurium by Autophagy., "Here, we demonstrate that myosin VI and TAX1BP1 are recruited to ubiquitylated Salmonella and play a key role in xenophagy."] [PMID:29940186 Mechanistic Insights into Recognitions of Ubiquitin and Myosin VI by Autophagy Receptor TAX1BP1., "TAX1BP1 may not only function as an autophagy receptor to recruit ubiquitylated substrates for autophagic degradation, but also serve as a Myosin VI cargo adaptor protein for mediating the maturation of autophagosome."] [PMID:30459273 Mechanistic insights into the interactions of NAP1 with the SKICH domains of NDP52 and TAX1BP1., "NDP52 and TAX1BP1, two SKIP carboxyl homology (SKICH) domain-containing autophagy receptors, play crucial roles in selective autophagy."].
- Cargo-coupled macroautophagy initiation is directly supported by FIP200 recruitment and local autophagosome formation [PMID:33226137 Receptor-mediated clustering of FIP200 bypasses the role of LC3 lipidation in autophagy., "TAX1BP1's ability to cluster FIP200 around NBR1 cargo and induce local autophagosome formation enforces cargo specificity and replaces the requirement for lipidated LC3."] [PMID:34471133 Reconstitution defines the roles of p62, NBR1 and TAX1BP1 in ubiquitin condensate formation and autophagy initiation., "TAX1BP1 is the main driver of FIP200 recruitment and thus the autophagic degradation of p62-ubiquitin condensates."].
- The TRIF paper supports a selective-autophagy receptor/adaptor interpretation and places the innate immune pathway effects in context [PMID:28898289 TRIM32-TAX1BP1-dependent selective autophagic degradation of TRIF negatively regulates TLR3/4-mediated innate immune responses., "TRIM32-mediated as well as poly(I:C)- and LPS-induced degradation of TRIF is inhibited by deficiency of TAX1BP1, a receptor for selective autophagy."] [PMID:28898289 TRIM32-TAX1BP1-dependent selective autophagic degradation of TRIF negatively regulates TLR3/4-mediated innate immune responses., "TRIM32 negatively regulates TLR3/4-mediated immune responses by targeting TRIF to TAX1BP1-mediated selective autophagic degradation."].
- Older immune-signaling work supports a valid but contextual A20-linked adaptor role [PMID:17703191 Essential role for TAX1BP1 in the termination of TNF-alpha-, IL-1- and LPS-mediated NF-kappaB and JNK signaling., "Thus, TAX1BP1 is pivotal for the termination of NF-kappaB and JNK signaling by functioning as an essential regulator of A20."] [PMID:18239685 Inflammatory cardiac valvulitis in TAX1BP1-deficient mice through selective NF-kappaB activation., "Mechanistically, TAX1BP1 acts in NF-kappaB signalling as an essential adaptor between A20 and its targets."].

## Conservative boundaries

- Mitochondrial localization is experimentally observed in virus infection [PMID:27736772 TAX1BP1 Restrains Virus-Induced Apoptosis by Facilitating Itch-Mediated Degradation of the Mitochondrial Adaptor MAVS., "Virus infection promotes the mitochondrial localization of TAX1BP1 and concomitant interaction with the mitochondrial adaptor MAVS."], but the accessible local evidence here does not justify treating mitophagy as a standalone core TAX1BP1 annotation.
- TAX1BP1 is linked to late autophagy maturation through Myosin VI [PMID:26451915 The Autophagy Receptor TAX1BP1 and the Molecular Motor Myosin VI Are Required for Clearance of Salmonella Typhimurium by Autophagy., "Our previous work demonstrated that myosin VI mediates the delivery of endocytic membranes to autophagosomes, a process that is required for autophagosome maturation and its fusion with the lysosome."], but the accessible TAX1BP1-specific evidence in this review is still indirect for a direct GO:0061909 autophagosome-lysosome fusion annotation.
