CC   -!- FUNCTION: Rate-limiting enzyme of the hexosamine biosynthetic pathway
CC       (HBP) that catalyzes the formation of glucosamine-6-phosphate from
CC       fructose-6-phosphate and glutamine, thereby controlling the flux of
CC       glucose into this pathway (PubMed:35229715). Via control of the HPB,
CC       likely regulates the availability of precursors for N- and O-linked
CC       protein glycosylation (By similarity). Exhibits feedback inhibition by
CC       UDP-N-acetylglucosamine (UDP-GlcNAc), although to a lesser extent than
CC       GFPT1 (PubMed:35229715). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q06210,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:35229715}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY:
CC       Reaction=D-fructose 6-phosphate + L-glutamine = D-glucosamine 6-
CC         phosphate + L-glutamate; Xref=Rhea:RHEA:13237, ChEBI:CHEBI:29985,
