Summary: OVER-ANNOTATION: PRRT1 (Proline-rich transmembrane protein 1 / SynDIG4) is NOT a kinase. It is a single-pass transmembrane protein that functions as an auxiliary subunit of AMPA receptor complexes. UniProt describes its function as "Plays a role in regulating basal phosphorylation levels of glutamate receptor GRIA1" - this means PRRT1 REGULATES phosphorylation (likely through indirect effects on the GRIA1 complex), not that it catalyzes phosphorylation. PRRT1 has no kinase domain. The appropriate annotation would be to a regulatory term, not directly to the phosphorylation process.
Proposed replacements: regulation of protein kinase activity
