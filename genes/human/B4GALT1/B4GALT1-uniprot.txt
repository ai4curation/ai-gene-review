CC   -!- FUNCTION: Galactosyltransferase acting in the Golgi stacks. Catalyzes
CC       the transfer of galactose (Gal) from UDP-alpha-D-galactose in
CC       beta(1->4) linkage to the non-reducing terminal N-acetylglucosamine
CC       (GlcNAc) moieties of glycolipids and complex-type N-linked glycans
CC       (PubMed:16157350, PubMed:27872474, PubMed:29133956, PubMed:36280670,
CC       PubMed:37632720, PubMed:38321209). Adds one Gal residue to both GlcNAc
CC       beta(1->2)-linked to the alpha(1->3) and alpha(1->6) mannose antennae
CC       of complex-type N-glycans, enabling the formation of mono- and di-
CC       galactosylated glycoforms. Galactosylates complex-type N-glycans
CC       attached on the fragment crystallizable (Fc) of immunoglobulin-gamma
CC       isotypes (IgGs), a prerequisite for antibody glycan sialylation and
CC       related anti-inflammatory effector functions (PubMed:27872474,
CC       PubMed:29133956, PubMed:36280670, PubMed:37632720). Can also transfer a
CC       Gal residue to free GlcNAc to form N-acetyllactosamine
CC       (PubMed:16157350). With LALBA/alpha-lactalbumin forms the lactose
CC       synthase complex responsible for production of large amounts of lactose
CC       in the lactating mammary gland. Interaction with LALBA alters the sugar
CC       substrate specificity of the catalytic domain, enabling high affinity
CC       binding of glucose and its transformation to lactose (PubMed:16157350).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:16157350, ECO:0000269|PubMed:27872474,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:29133956, ECO:0000269|PubMed:36280670,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:37632720, ECO:0000269|PubMed:38321209}.
CC   -!- FUNCTION: [Isoform Long]: The cell surface form functions as a
CC       recognition molecule during a variety of cell to cell and cell to
CC       matrix interactions, like those occurring during development and egg
CC       fertilization, by binding to specific oligosaccharide ligands on
CC       opposing cells or in the extracellular matrix. Acts as a sperm receptor
