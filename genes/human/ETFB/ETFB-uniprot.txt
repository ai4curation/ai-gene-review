CC   -!- FUNCTION: Heterodimeric electron transfer flavoprotein that accepts
CC       electrons from several mitochondrial dehydrogenases, including acyl-CoA
CC       dehydrogenases, glutaryl-CoA and sarcosine dehydrogenase
CC       (PubMed:15159392, PubMed:15975918, PubMed:25416781). It transfers the
CC       electrons to the main mitochondrial respiratory chain via ETF-
CC       ubiquinone oxidoreductase (Probable). Required for normal mitochondrial
CC       fatty acid oxidation and normal amino acid metabolism (PubMed:12815589,
CC       PubMed:7912128). ETFB binds an AMP molecule that probably has a purely
CC       structural role (PubMed:15159392, PubMed:15975918, PubMed:8962055).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12815589, ECO:0000269|PubMed:15159392,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:15975918, ECO:0000269|PubMed:25416781,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:7912128, ECO:0000269|PubMed:8962055,
CC       ECO:0000303|PubMed:17941859, ECO:0000305}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Heterodimer composed of an alpha and beta subunit
CC       (PubMed:15159392, PubMed:15975918, PubMed:25023281, PubMed:25416781,
CC       PubMed:8962055). Identified in a complex that contains ETFA, ETFB and
CC       ETFRF1 (PubMed:27499296). Interacts with ACADM (PubMed:15159392,
CC       PubMed:15975918). Interacts with MBTPS1; the interaction stabilizes the
CC       ETFA/ETFB heterodimer and promotes FAD binding (PubMed:35362222).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15159392, ECO:0000269|PubMed:15975918,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:25023281, ECO:0000269|PubMed:25416781,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:27499296, ECO:0000269|PubMed:35362222,
