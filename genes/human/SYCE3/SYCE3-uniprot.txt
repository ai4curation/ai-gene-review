CC   -!- FUNCTION: Major component of the transverse central element of
CC       synaptonemal complexes (SCS), formed between homologous chromosomes
CC       during meiotic prophase (PubMed:36635604). Required for the assembly of
CC       the central element of the synaptonemal complex during meiosis, via
CC       remodeling of SYCP1 lattice structures and promoting recruitment of
CC       SYCE2-TEX12 and SYCE1-SIX60S1 complexes (PubMed:36635604). Required for
CC       chromosome loading of the central element-specific SCS proteins, and
CC       for initiating synapsis between homologous chromosomes (By similarity).
CC       Chromosome loading appears to require SYCP1 (By similarity). Required
CC       for fertility and normal testis development (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:B5KM66, ECO:0000269|PubMed:36635604}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homodimer (PubMed:31023827). Can form higher-order
CC       homooligomers (PubMed:31023827). Interacts with SYCP1 (via tetrameric
CC       core); the interaction remodels SYCP1 homotetramers to 2:1
CC       heterotrimers with SYCE3 (PubMed:36635604). SYCP1/SYCE3 heterotrimers
CC       form lattice assemblies as part of the mature synaptonemal complex via
CC       both lateral and head-to-head interactions (PubMed:36635604). Interacts
CC       with the SYCE1-SIX6OS1 complex; the interaction recruits the SYCE1-
CC       SIX6OS1 complex to the central element of the synaptonemal complex
CC       (PubMed:36635604). Interacts with the SYCE2-TEX12 complex; the
CC       interaction promotes fibrous assembly of SYCE2-TEX12 as part of the
CC       synaptonemal complex central element (PubMed:36635604). Interacts with
CC       SYCE1. Interacts with SYCE2. Interacts with proteasome subunit PSMA8;
CC       to participate in meiosis progression during spermatogenesis (By
CC       similarity). Interacts with SPO16 (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:B5KM66, ECO:0000269|PubMed:31023827,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:36635604}.
