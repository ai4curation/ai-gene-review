Gene Description

CALCOCO2 (NDP52) is a selective autophagy receptor/adaptor that couples cargo marked by galectin-8 or ubiquitin-associated signals to the autophagy machinery. The strongest supported biology is in xenophagy and mitophagy, where CALCOCO2 helps recruit LC3/GABARAP and ULK-complex machinery to pathogen-containing compartments or damaged mitochondria and can also promote late autophagosome maturation/flux. Older PML-body localization is not supported by later localization work; newer nuclear transcriptional/DNA-binding reports are contextual and should be kept separate from the core selective-autophagy role.

Core Functions

CALCOCO2/NDP52 is a selective-autophagy cargo adaptor that recognizes cargo-associated damage or ubiquitin-linked signals and recruits autophagy machinery through LC3/GABARAP- and ULK-complex-linked interactions. This receptor function underlies its best-supported biology in xenophagy and mitophagy.

1) Target verification (gene/protein identity)

The UniProt accession Q13137 corresponds to the human gene CALCOCO2 (synonym NDP52), encoding a ~446 amino-acid protein originally described at nuclear dots/ND10 but now established as a soluble selective autophagy receptor with additional nuclear functions. (bruqi2025ndp52andits pages 2-3, bruqi2025ndp52andits pages 1-2, santos2023autophagyreceptorndp52 pages 2-6)

Key domain features consistently reported for human CALCOCO2/NDP52 include an N-terminal SKICH domain, central coiled-coil region(s), ATG8-family binding motifs (cLIR/CLIR with LC3C selectivity; a canonical LIR engaging LC3/GABARAPs), a GIR (galectin-interacting region), and C-terminal zinc-finger/UBZ/ubiquitin-binding elements enabling recognition of ubiquitin chains. (bruqi2025ndp52andits pages 2-3, shariq2023theexploitationof pages 8-10, mattioni2025avariantof pages 7-8)

Subcellular localization is context dependent: CALCOCO2/NDP52 operates in the cytosol at cargo sites (bacteria, ubiquitinated mitochondria) and can participate in lysosome-directed pathways, but it also has a quantified nuclear pool that clusters and associates with transcriptional machinery and dsDNA. (vargas2023themechanismsand pages 6-11, santos2023autophagyreceptorndp52 pages 2-6, cui2023ndp52mediatesan pages 9-10)

2) Key concepts and definitions (current understanding)

2.1 Selective autophagy receptor (SAR)

Selective autophagy receptors are soluble adaptor proteins that (i) bind molecular “eat-me” signals on cargo (often ubiquitin chains or damage-associated markers such as galectins) and (ii) bind ATG8-family proteins (LC3/GABARAP) on nascent autophagosomal membranes via LIR-like motifs, thereby bridging cargo to the autophagy machinery. CALCOCO2/NDP52 is a canonical example of a ubiquitin- and galectin-linked SAR in mammalian xenophagy and mitophagy. (shariq2023theexploitationof pages 8-10, vargas2023themechanismsand pages 6-11)

2.2 Xenophagy (bacterial autophagy)

In xenophagy, CALCOCO2/NDP52 recognizes ubiquitin-coated bacteria and can also bind galectin-8 (LGALS8) that marks ruptured pathogen-containing vacuoles. It can simultaneously engage bacteria-associated ubiquitin and ATG8-family proteins (including LC3C via a non-canonical LC3C-interacting motif), promoting delivery of bacteria to LC3-positive phagophore membranes and supporting autophagosome maturation. (shariq2023theexploitationof pages 8-10, rubiotomas2023theinterplaybetween pages 4-7)

2.3 Parkin-dependent mitophagy (core initiation logic)

In PINK1/Parkin-dependent mitophagy, damaged mitochondria become decorated with Parkin-generated ubiquitin chains. CALCOCO2/NDP52 is among the key ubiquitin-dependent receptors recruited to these ubiquitinated mitochondria. Mechanistically, an important concept emerging from mammalian mitophagy work is that CALCOCO2/NDP52 is not only a “cargo tether” but also an initiator/scaffold: it directly interacts with FIP200 (RB1CC1) (a ULK1-complex scaffold) to promote ULK1 complex recruitment/activation and de novo phagophore formation at mitochondria. (vargas2023themechanismsand pages 6-11, yang2024autophagyadaptorsmediate pages 1-2)

2.4 TBK1 “licensing” of receptor function

The kinase TBK1 is widely described as a positive regulator of ubiquitin-dependent selective autophagy. For CALCOCO2/NDP52, TBK1 phosphorylation is described as enhancing receptor retention on mitochondria and facilitating the NDP52–FIP200 interaction during mitophagy. (vargas2023themechanismsand pages 6-11)

2.5 Non-autophagic nuclear functions

Beyond autophagy, CALCOCO2/NDP52 has a mechanistically supported nuclear role: it forms clustered nuclear puncta, associates with RNA polymerase II (RNAPII), binds dsDNA with high affinity, and can alter DNA conformation in vitro—consistent with a function in transcriptional regulation and/or chromatin organization. (santos2023autophagyreceptorndp52 pages 2-6, santos2023autophagyreceptorndp52 pages 6-8)

8) Practical functional annotation summary (for CALCOCO2 / NDP52)

Primary function: A selective autophagy receptor/adapter that recognizes ubiquitin chains (and in some contexts galectin-8-decorated damage) and connects cargo to autophagic membranes (LC3/GABARAP) while recruiting autophagy initiation machinery through FIP200/ULK1, with modulation by TBK1 in mitophagy. (shariq2023theexploitationof pages 8-10, vargas2023themechanismsand pages 6-11, yang2024autophagyadaptorsmediate pages 1-2)

Where it acts: At least three cellular contexts are supported—(i) cytosolic cargo sites during xenophagy and mitophagy initiation; (ii) lysosome-directed targeting pathways (e.g., Rab9-dependent HBV envelope degradation); and (iii) the nucleus at transcription-associated clusters with dsDNA binding activity. (cui2023ndp52mediatesan pages 9-10, santos2023autophagyreceptorndp52 media f01c7ada)

Pathways: Parkin-dependent mitophagy (ULK1/FIP200 recruitment; TBK1 regulation), bacterial xenophagy (ubiquitin/galectin-8/LC3C axis), viral infection contexts including both antiviral restriction (HBV) and viral hijacking (KSHV), plus transcriptional regulation via nuclear clustering and dsDNA interaction. (shariq2023theexploitationof pages 8-10, vargas2023themechanismsand pages 6-11, cui2023ndp52mediatesan pages 9-10, robinson2023kaposi’ssarcomaassociatedherpesvirus pages 1-2)
