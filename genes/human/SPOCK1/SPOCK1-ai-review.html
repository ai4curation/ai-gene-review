Gene Description

SPOCK1 encodes testican-1, a secreted multidomain proteoglycan of the extracellular matrix. The 439 amino acid protein is modified with chondroitin sulfate and heparan sulfate glycosaminoglycan chains attached to serine residues in the C-terminal region. Testican-1 has a modular architecture containing a follistatin-like domain, an acidic region, extracellular calcium-binding domains, Kazal-like protease inhibitor domains, and a thyroglobulin type-1 domain. The protein functions as a structural ECM component that modulates cell-substrate adhesion and inhibits multiple classes of proteases including metalloendopeptidases (via direct binding to MT1-MMP and MT3-MMP), cysteine proteases (cathepsin L with Ki=0.7 nM), and serine proteases. Testican-1 is highly expressed in brain (particularly cerebellum, with immunolocalization to postsynaptic densities), and also found in testis and endothelial cells. The protein plays important roles in ECM organization, regulation of neuron attachment and neurite outgrowth, and protease regulation in both intracellular and extracellular contexts.
