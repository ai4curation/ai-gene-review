Gene Description

DNAJB8 is a class II J-domain protein (Hsp40/DNAJ family, subfamily B) that functions as a potent suppressor of polyglutamine protein aggregation. Unlike canonical DNAJ co-chaperones that assist Hsp70-mediated protein refolding, DNAJB8 acts primarily as a holdase, binding aggregation-prone substrates (particularly polyQ-expanded proteins) to prevent their aggregation independently of its J-domain/Hsp70-stimulating activity. The anti-aggregation activity resides in its C-terminal serine-rich (SSF-SST) region and C-terminal tail, and requires histone deacetylase (HDAC4, HDAC6, SIRT2) interactions. DNAJB8 is enriched in testis but expressed in multiple tissues, and localizes to both cytosol and nucleus.

Core Functions

Holdase chaperone that potently suppresses aggregation of polyglutamine- expanded proteins and other aggregation-prone substrates. Unlike canonical DNAJ co-chaperones that assist HSP70-mediated protein refolding, DNAJB8 acts primarily through its C-terminal serine-rich (SSF-SST) region and C-terminal tail to directly bind the polyQ tract of substrates, preventing inclusion body formation. This anti-aggregation activity is largely independent of the N-terminal J-domain/HSP70-stimulating activity and requires interaction with histone deacetylases (HDAC4, HDAC6, SIRT2). DNAJB8 cannot stimulate luciferase refolding, distinguishing its holdase mechanism from foldase-type chaperones.

- description: >-
    Holdase chaperone that potently suppresses aggregation of polyglutamine-
    expanded proteins and other aggregation-prone substrates. Unlike canonical
    DNAJ co-chaperones that assist HSP70-mediated protein refolding, DNAJB8
    acts primarily through its C-terminal serine-rich (SSF-SST) region and
    C-terminal tail to directly bind the polyQ tract of substrates, preventing
    inclusion body formation. This anti-aggregation activity is largely
    independent of the N-terminal J-domain/HSP70-stimulating activity and
    requires interaction with histone deacetylases (HDAC4, HDAC6, SIRT2).
    DNAJB8 cannot stimulate luciferase refolding, distinguishing its holdase
    mechanism from foldase-type chaperones.
