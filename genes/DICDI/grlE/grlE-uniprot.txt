CC   -!- FUNCTION: May be involved in early development in cAMP sensing and
CC       subsequent chemotactic response. Probable receptor of GABA and
CC       glutamate, leading respectively to the induction or inhibition of SDF-2
CC       formation. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:16672332, ECO:0000269|PubMed:17501984}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Membrane {ECO:0000269|PubMed:16527814}; Multi-
CC       pass membrane protein {ECO:0000269|PubMed:16527814}.
CC   -!- DEVELOPMENTAL STAGE: Expressed throughout development. Expression is
CC       very low in vegetative cells and starts to accumulate in the first 2
CC       hours after the initiation of development. It reaches a peak at 4 hours
CC       and then declines. However, a slightly larger transcript accumulates
CC       after 10 hours and remains until 22 hours of development.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:16527814, ECO:0000269|PubMed:16672332,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:17501984}.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: Down-regulated by growth on bacteria.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:18559084}.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: Cells grow faster, reach higher densities,
CC       exhibit delayed aggregates formation upon starvation and altered
CC       chemotaxis toward cAMP. Cells do not produce SDF-2 in response to GABA.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:16527814, ECO:0000269|PubMed:16672332}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the G protein-coupled receptor 3 family.
CC       {ECO:0000305}.
