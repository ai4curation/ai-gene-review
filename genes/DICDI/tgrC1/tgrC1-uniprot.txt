CC   -!- FUNCTION: tgrB1 and tgrC1 are involved in kin discrimination; function
CC       as a ligand-receptor pair, where tgrC1 acts as a ligand. They play an
CC       essential role in cell-cell adhesion, aggregation and subsequent
CC       development into multicellular forms. Could function as a non-
CC       diffusible cell-cell signaling molecule that is required for
CC       multicellular development. Has both cell-autonomous and non-cell-
CC       autonomous functions. Terminal node, epistatic both to comC and tgrD1.
CC       Activates tgrD1. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11071787,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:11792801, ECO:0000269|PubMed:14651934,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:1577768, ECO:0000269|PubMed:19285397,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:23477311, ECO:0000269|PubMed:26395484,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:29038229, ECO:0000269|PubMed:3063296,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:498273}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homodimer (PubMed:24490801). Interacts (via amino acids 337-
CC       361) with tgrB1 (via amino acids 537-783) (PubMed:23477311,
CC       PubMed:24490801, PubMed:29038229). The interactions between monomeric
CC       tgrB1 and homodimeric tgrC1 leads to homodimerization of tgrB1 and the
CC       assembly of tgrB1-tgrC1 tetramers into large adhesion complexes between
CC       adjacent cells (PubMed:24490801). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:23477311,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:24490801, ECO:0000269|PubMed:29038229}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell membrane {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11071787,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:15561583, ECO:0000269|PubMed:1577768,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:24490801, ECO:0000269|PubMed:498273,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:7407916}; Single-pass type I membrane protein
CC       {ECO:0000255}. Cell junction {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11071787,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:15561583, ECO:0000269|PubMed:1577768,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:7407916}.
CC   -!- DEVELOPMENTAL STAGE: Expressed at low levels in the mid-aggregation
CC       stage, followed by a rapid increase that coincides with the completion
CC       of aggregate formation. Expression is greatest in cells at the
CC       periphery and the rear end of the migrating slug.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11792801, ECO:0000269|PubMed:14651934,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:15561583}.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: Induced at the loose aggregate stage and expressed through
CC       the remainder of development.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: Mutant cells are unable to sporulate in isolation
CC       although they will sporulate when in the presence of wild-type cells.
CC       Cells form loose aggregates before disaggregating. After starvation,
CC       cells form wide streams that frequently break and form secondary
CC       centers before reaching the primary center and display cAMP relay
CC       defects. Cells fail to rotate. tgrB1 and tgrC1 double mutants show
CC       strain segregation in chimeras with wild-type cells. ComC, tgrC1 and
CC       tgrD1 triple mutants fail to form spores. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11071787,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:14651934, ECO:0000269|PubMed:19285397}.
CC   -!- MISCELLANEOUS: The tgrC1 gene is highly polymorphic.
