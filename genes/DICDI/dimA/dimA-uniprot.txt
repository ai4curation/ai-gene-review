CC   -!- FUNCTION: Transcriptional regulator involved in DIF-1 signaling. DIF-1
CC       (Differentiation Inducing Factor-1) is a signal molecule involved in
CC       the differentiation of pstO (prestalk-O) cells. Functions both as an
CC       activator of prestalk gene expression and a repressor of prespore gene
CC       expression. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:14729573, ECO:0000269|PubMed:16410410}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Binds DNA as a dimer (By similarity). Heterodimerizes with
CC       dimB; in vitro. Also able to form homodimer; in vitro. {ECO:0000250}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Nucleus {ECO:0000255|PROSITE-ProRule:PRU00978,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:16410410}. Note=In response to DIF-1, it accumulates
CC       rapidly in the nucleus. Nuclear accumulation is dependent on dimB.
CC   -!- DEVELOPMENTAL STAGE: Maximum levels of expression in prespore cells,
CC       but also expressed in prestalk cells at the slug stage. Developmentally
CC       regulated with levels peaking at culmination.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:14729573, ECO:0000269|PubMed:16410410}.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: Defective in DIF biosynthesis and in DIF-1-
CC       induced stalk-cell differentiation. Exhibits morphological and cell
CC       type differentiation defects. DimA and dimB double mutant has the same
CC       phenotype. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:14729573, ECO:0000269|PubMed:16410410}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the bZIP family. {ECO:0000305}.
