CC   -!- FUNCTION: tgrB1 and tgrC1 are involved in kin discrimination; function
CC       as a ligand-receptor pair, where tgrB1 serves as a receptor. They play
CC       an essential role in cell-cell adhesion, aggregation and subsequent
CC       development into multicellular forms. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:19285397,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:23477311, ECO:0000269|PubMed:26395484,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:29038229}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Can form homodimers; homodimerization is dependent on trans-
CC       interaction with tgrC1 (PubMed:24490801). Interacts (via amino acids
CC       537-783) with tgrC1 (via amino acids 337-361) (PubMed:23477311,
CC       PubMed:24490801, PubMed:29038229). The interactions between monomeric
CC       tgrB1 and homodimeric tgrC1 leads to homodimerization of tgrB1 and the
CC       assembly of tgrB1-tgrC1 tetramers into large adhesion complexes between
CC       adjacent cells (PubMed:24490801). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:23477311,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:24490801, ECO:0000269|PubMed:29038229}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell membrane {ECO:0000269|PubMed:23477311,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:24490801}; Single-pass type I membrane protein
CC       {ECO:0000305}.
CC   -!- PTM: Phosphorylated at Ser-845; phosphorylation depends on association
CC       with a matching tgrC1 on the membrane of an adjacent cell, dispensable
CC       for the receptor function. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:29038229}.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: TgrB1 and tgrC1 double mutants show strain
CC       segregation in chimeras with wild-type cells.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:19285397}.
CC   -!- MISCELLANEOUS: The tgrB1 gene is highly polymorphic.
