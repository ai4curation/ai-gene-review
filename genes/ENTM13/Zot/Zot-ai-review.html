Gene Description

Gene 1 protein (G1P) is an essential phage assembly factor, NOT a toxin despite historical confusion with zonula occludens toxin (Zot). This protein plays a crucial role in M13 bacteriophage assembly by increasing adhesion zones between inner and outer bacterial membranes where phage extrusion occurs. The name confusion arose because early Vibrio cholerae phage CTXœÜ research incorrectly attributed toxin activity to a homologous gene. G1P works with G4P to create membrane-spanning complexes that serve as assembly/extrusion sites. The protein has two isoforms from alternative initiation: full-length G1P and shorter G11P (starting at Met-300).

Key Findings

The Great Misannotation Story

  • NOT a toxin! Gene 1 protein (G1P) is a phage assembly factor
  • Historical confusion with V. cholerae CTXœÜ phage
  • No toxin activity ever demonstrated for G1P
  • "Zot" name persists despite being wrong

Actual Function: Phage Assembly

  1. Membrane adhesion zones:
  2. Creates contact sites between inner/outer membranes
  3. Works with G4P (Gene 4 protein)
  4. Essential for phage extrusion

  5. ATP-dependent process

  6. Assembly mechanism:

  7. Walker A motif for ATP binding
  8. Forms membrane-spanning complex
  9. Allows virion passage without lysis

  10. Two isoforms (G1P and G11P from Met-300)

  11. NOT involved in:

  12. Tight junction disruption
  13. Intestinal permeability
  14. Diarrheal disease
  15. Any toxin activity

History of the Confusion

  • 1991: CTXœÜ phage discovered in V. cholerae
  • Gene homologous to M13 gene I called "zot"
  • Assumed to have toxin function (never proven)
  • Name stuck despite lack of evidence
  • Real cholera toxin is ctxAB (well characterized)
