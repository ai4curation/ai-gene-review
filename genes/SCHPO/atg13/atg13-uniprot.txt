CC   -!- FUNCTION: Component of the atg1 kinase complex that activates the atg1
CC       kinase in a nutritional condition dependent manner through the TOR
CC       pathway, leading to autophagy (PubMed:23950735, PubMed:26030876).
CC       Autophagy functions to supply nitrogen and is activated when cells
CC       cannot access exogenous nitrogen, thus ensuring that they can adapt and
CC       subsequently propagate (PubMed:17295836). Finally, atg13 is also
CC       required for glycogen storage during stationary phase and has a role in
CC       meiosis and sporulation (PubMed:16303567, PubMed:19778961).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:16303567, ECO:0000269|PubMed:17295836,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:19778961, ECO:0000269|PubMed:23950735,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:26030876}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Component of the atg1 kinase complex composed of at least
CC       atg1, atg13, atg17 and atg101 (PubMed:26030876, PubMed:28976798).
CC       Interacts directly with atg1, atg17 and atg101 (PubMed:26030876,
CC       PubMed:28976798). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:26030876,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:28976798}.
CC   -!- INTERACTION:
CC       O36019; O13978: atg101; NbExp=6; IntAct=EBI-16158534, EBI-16158557;
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cytoplasm {ECO:0000269|PubMed:16823372}.
CC       Preautophagosomal structure {ECO:0000269|PubMed:23950735}.
CC   -!- PTM: Phosphorylated (PubMed:17295836). Dephosphorylated under depletion
CC       of nitrogen (PubMed:17295836). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:17295836}.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: Impairs atg8-processing.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:23950735}.
