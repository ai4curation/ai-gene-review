Gene Description

cmp7 (Charged multivesicular body protein 7; SPBC1604.18c) is the Schizosaccharomyces pombe ortholog of human CHMP7, an ESCRT-III-like / ESCRT-II–ESCRT-III hybrid protein of the SNF7 family. In fission yeast cmp7 acts at the nuclear envelope (NE): it is recruited by the inner-nuclear-membrane LEM-domain protein Lem2 to the single NE hole formed by mitotic spindle pole body (SPB) extrusion during closed mitosis, where it forms a transient "grommet" that restricts the NE hole during anaphase B and is subsequently replaced by an ESCRT-III sealing module (Cmp2/Cmp3/Ist1/Vps4) that closes the NE in interphase. cmp7 is required for NE integrity, mitotic NE re-sealing, and the spontaneous loss-of-function suppression of the vps4 NE-morphology phenotype.

Core Functions

cmp7 is the Lem2-recruited ESCRT-III-like / ESCRT-II–ESCRT-III hybrid factor that forms a transient grommet at the mitotic SPB-extrusion hole in the nuclear envelope of S. pombe. The grommet restricts the NE hole during anaphase B; cmp7 is then displaced by a downstream ESCRT-III sealing module (Cmp2/Cmp3/Ist1) that, with Vps4, closes the NE in interphase.

Suggested Experiments

Experiment: Use vps4-as / cmp7 timed depletion combined with super-resolution imaging of fluorescently tagged cmp7 to measure the lifetime, geometry, and Vps4-dependence of the grommet at the SPB-extrusion site, and to test whether cmp7 is displaced before sealing-module assembly.

Hypothesis: cmp7 forms a transient grommet whose disassembly requires Vps4 ATPase activity and is a prerequisite for assembly of the Cmp2/Cmp3/Ist1 sealing module on the NE hole.

Experiment: Compare cmp7Δ phenotypes for canonical MVB sorting / vacuolar delivery cargoes (e.g., CPY-like, Sty1-GFP) against NE-sealing assays under matched conditions to determine whether cmp7 contributes detectably to MVB sorting in S. pombe.

Hypothesis: cmp7 will be dispensable for MVB / vacuolar sorting in S. pombe, supporting removal of the IPR005024-derived vacuolar-transport IEA on this gene.

Experiment: Reconstitute cmp7 + lem2 with downstream ESCRT-III subunits on giant unilamellar vesicles containing inner-nuclear-membrane lipids and monitor whether cmp7 alone confers grommet-like restriction of a model membrane hole.

Hypothesis: cmp7 is sufficient (with Lem2) to establish a grommet-like membrane constriction independently of the downstream sealing module.

Notes

(cmp7-notes.md)

cmp7 (SPBC1604.18c, UniProt O94730) — S. pombe ESCRT-III-like nuclear envelope grommet

Summary

cmp7 (Charged multivesicular body protein 7) is the Schizosaccharomyces pombe
ortholog of human CHMP7. It is an ESCRT-III-like / ESCRT-II–ESCRT-III hybrid
protein of the SNF7 family. In fission yeast cmp7 acts almost exclusively at the
nuclear envelope (NE): it is recruited by the inner-nuclear-membrane LEM-domain
protein Lem2 to the single NE hole generated by mitotic spindle pole body (SPB)
extrusion during closed mitosis, where it forms a transient "grommet" that
restricts the NE hole during anaphase B before being replaced by an ESCRT-III
sealing module (Cmp2/Cmp3/Ist1/Vps4) that closes the NE in interphase.

Key functions / evidence

  • ESCRT-III nuclear-envelope adaptor / grommet activity.
    PMID:37783794 (Ader et al. 2023, Nat Cell Biol) leveraged the single SPB-extrusion
    NE hole in S. pombe to define two distinct ESCRT-III modes both dependent on
    cmp7: a "grommet-like function" that restricts the NE hole in anaphase B and a
    sealing function that ultimately closes the NE in interphase. This is the
    experimental basis of the GO term GO:0180024 membrane grommet activity and the
    specific cellular location GO:0180028 mitotic spindle pole body attachment site.
    ["A grommet-like function is required to restrict the NE hole in anaphase B,
    whereas replacement of Cmp7 by a sealing module ultimately closes the NE in
    interphase."]

  • Recruitment by Lem2 (genetic and physical interaction).
    PMID:28242692 (Gu et al. 2017, PNAS) established Lem2–cmp7 as the conserved
    NE site-specific recruitment module: loss-of-function mutations in lem2 or
    cmp7 suppress the severe NE morphology defects of vps4Δ in S. pombe, and
    cmp7/CHMP7 binds the C-terminal domain of Lem2/LEM2.
    ["loss-of-function mutations that arise spontaneously in lem2 or cmp7, implying
    that these proteins may function upstream in the same pathway"]

  • Required for mitotic NE reassembly / closed mitosis.
    PMID:32848252 (Dey et al. 2020, Nature) showed that closed mitosis in
    S. pombe requires local NE disassembly and that the ESCRT machinery is
    required for the NE to re-form locally; cmp7Δ shows NE-membrane reassembly
    defects after closed mitosis.
    ["Closed mitosis requires local disassembly of the nuclear envelope."]

  • High-throughput "cytoplasm" localization (PMID:16823372).
    Matsuyama et al. 2006 is a genome-wide ORFeome over-expression localization
    study that scored cmp7 as cytoplasmic by GFP imaging. Cmp7's functional pool
    is concentrated transiently at the NE / SPB-attachment site (Ader 2023), so a
    diffuse "cytoplasm" signal from over-expression is best treated as
    high-throughput / non-core rather than the principal compartment.

The IPR005024 (Snf7 family) → GO:0007034 vacuolar transport mapping

InterPro family IPR005024 (SNF7 family) maps in InterPro2GO to
GO:0007034 vacuolar transport. This generates an IEA annotation on cmp7
(GOA: GO:0007034 | IEA | GO_REF:0000002 | InterPro:IPR005024).

This propagation is incorrect for cmp7. In S. pombe cmp7's experimentally
characterized function is NE integrity / NE-membrane reassembly at the
SPB-extrusion hole — it is a nuclear-envelope-specific Snf7 family member,
not a canonical late-endosome / MVB / vacuolar-transport ESCRT-III subunit.
PomBase does not give it MVB / vacuolar-transport experimental annotations, and
the upstream go-annotation issue (geneontology/go-annotation#6458) explicitly
flags this mis-propagation. The IEA GO:0007034 annotation on cmp7 should be
removed; the InterPro2GO mapping itself is upstream and out of scope here.

The IBA GO:0009898 cytoplasmic side of plasma membrane, GO:0005771 multivesicular body, GO:0032511 late endosome to vacuole transport via multivesicular body sorting pathway, and GO:0006900 vesicle budding from membrane that PANTHER propagates
to the broader Snf7 family are visible in the UniProt DR block but are NOT in
the current GOA file for cmp7 (only the NE-relevant IBAs are propagated), so no
review action is needed for them here.

Core function summary (for core_functions)

  1. MF: membrane grommet activity (GO:0180024) — forms a transient annular
    ESCRT-III-like structure that restricts the NE hole at the SPB-extrusion site.
  2. BP: nuclear envelope organization / mitotic nuclear membrane reassembly
    (GO:0006998 / GO:0007084)     — required for NE integrity and re-sealing after
    closed-mitotic SPB extrusion.
  3. CC: ESCRT III complex / nuclear envelope / mitotic SPB attachment site
    (GO:0000815 / GO:0005635 / GO:0180028)     — ESCRT-III-like factor that
    localizes transiently to the NE at the SPB-extrusion hole.
