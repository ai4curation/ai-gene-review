Gene Description

Ase1 is the fission-yeast member of the conserved Ase1/PRC1/MAP65 family of microtubule-associated proteins. It is a homodimer that specifically crosslinks and bundles antiparallel microtubules, defining and stabilizing the region of interdigitating antiparallel microtubule overlap at the spindle midzone. During mitosis Ase1 localizes weakly to the metaphase spindle and accumulates robustly at the spindle midzone in anaphase, where it provides structural integrity to the elongating bipolar spindle; cells lacking Ase1 form disorganized midzones and spindles that collapse during anaphase B. Ase1 also organizes antiparallel overlaps in interphase microtubule bundles (contributing to nuclear centering by microtubule pushing forces), in the post-anaphase array of microtubules at the division site, and in stable quiescence-associated microtubule bundles. As a midzone scaffold it recruits and concentrates partner proteins, including the kinesin-6 Klp9 (driving anaphase B spindle elongation velocity), the CLASP microtubule-stabilizer Cls1/Peg1, and contributes to local nuclear pore complex remodeling that drives nuclear envelope division. Its overlap-stabilizing activity also brakes kinesin-14-driven microtubule sliding, preventing antiparallel bundles from sliding apart. Ase1 additionally functions in meiotic spindle and astral microtubule bundling, in nuclear oscillatory movement during meiotic prophase, and as a regulatory component coupling completion of anaphase with cytokinesis.
