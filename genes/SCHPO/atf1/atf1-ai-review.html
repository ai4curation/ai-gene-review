Gene Description

Atf1 (also known as Gad7, Mts1, Sss1) is the principal ATF/CREB-family basic-leucine-zipper (bZIP) transcription factor of the fission yeast Schizosaccharomyces pombe, with strong homology to mammalian ATF-2/CRE-BP1. It acts as a sequence-specific RNA polymerase II transcription factor that heterodimerizes with the bZIP partner Pcr1 (Mts2) and binds CRE/ATF (cyclic-AMP response element, M26, 5'-ATGACGT-3') sites in promoters. Atf1 is the major nuclear substrate of the Sty1/Spc1 stress-activated MAP kinase (a p38/SAPK homolog) and drives the core environmental stress response, inducing hundreds of genes upon oxidative, osmotic, heat, and nutrient (nitrogen/carbon) stress, as well as entry into stationary phase. Beyond its role in stress transcription, Atf1 is essential for sexual differentiation and meiosis (including induction of ste11+ and G1 arrest under nitrogen starvation), activates the meiotic recombination hotspot ade6-M26, and is required for RNAi-independent nucleation and maintenance of heterochromatin and transcriptional silencing at the silent mating-type region, where it recruits histone deacetylase complexes (Clr3, Clr6) and Swi6/HP1. By binding promoters constitutively, Atf1 also shapes nucleosome architecture, creating broad nucleosome-depleted regions, phasing downstream nucleosome arrays, and suppressing cryptic antisense transcription. Atf1 acts as both a transcriptional activator and a repressor depending on context.

atf1 (P52890, SPBC29B5.01) review notes

S. pombe Atf1 (synonyms gad7, mts1, sss1). bZIP ATF/CREB-family transcription factor; principal nuclear substrate of the Sty1/Spc1 SAPK (p38) MAP kinase. Heterodimerizes with Pcr1 (mts2).

Core identity / molecular function

  • bZIP TF homologous to mammalian ATF-2/CRE-BP1; binds ATF/CRE recognition sites in vitro; ATF-like binding activity in pombe extracts entirely lost on atf1 deletion PMID:8557039.
  • gad7/Atf1 binds CRE in vitro; co-IP with Pcr1 => heterodimer in vivo PMID:9135083.
  • Atf1-Pcr1 heterodimer binds M26 site (5'-ATGACGT-3') with Kd ~1 nM PMID:18375981.
  • Heterodimer Mts1(70 kD)/Mts2(28 kD) purified 40,000-fold, binds M26; binding in vitro correlates with hotspot activity in vivo PMID:7958849.
  • Atf1 acts as both activator and repressor depending on context (M26-chromatin remodeling) PMID:15448137.

Localization

  • Nucleus (UniProt). Nuclear retention of Spc1 requires Atf1; nuclear localization of Atf1 requires Pcr1 PMID:9585506.
  • ORFeome global localization study: nuclear [PMID:16823372 - genome-wide localization].

Stress response (core BP)

  • Sty1-dependent target; deletion of atf1 phenocopies many sty1 defects (stress sensitivity, sterility); induces gpd1, ctt1(catalase), pyp2 directly PMID:8824588.
  • Conjugation, meiosis, osmotic stress regulated by Spc1 through Atf1; Atf1 required for induction of meiotic and stress genes gpd1, pyp2 PMID:8824587.
  • ~400-500 stress genes regulated PMID:25122751.
  • Catalase ctt1: Atf1 + its binding site needed for induction by osmotic stress, UV, heat shock (NOT oxidative/menadione, which is Pap1) PMID:10731689.
  • Neutral trehalase ntp1 heat induction via CRE-like element, Atf1-dependent PMID:15164362.
  • Ecl1 (extender of chronological lifespan) directly activated by Atf1 on H2O2; Atf1 binds upstream of ecl1 PMID:24696293.
  • Phosphorylation of Atf1 by Sty1 required for oxidative stress response via promoting transcription initiation, NOT DNA binding PMID:28652406.
  • Salt/cation stress: cta3 induction requires Sty1, Atf1, Prr1 (Tup-repressor controlled) PMID:12221110.
  • ecm33 target of Atf1 and Mbx1 (Pmk1 cell integrity pathway) PMID:20032302.
  • agl1 (extracellular maltase) induced on glucose->maltose switch, dependent on Atf1+Pcr1; they recruit Mediator PMID:24224056.
  • Ish1/Bis1 (stationary-phase/nuclear envelope): ish1 expression regulated by Spc1 through Atf1 PMID:11751918.
  • Sro1 stress-responsive orphan regulated via Sty1/Atf1 PMID:18410345.

Sexual development / stationary phase

  • Required for sexual development and entry into stationary phase; activates ste11; G1 arrest under N starvation PMID:8557039.
  • gad7 required for proper G1 arrest and ste11 induction under N starvation PMID:9135083.
  • cgs2 chromatin remodeling links SAPK and PKA, affecting G0 checkpoint and sexual differentiation PMID:15448137. This is the negative regulation of PKA/cAMP-activated GPCR signaling (GO:0110034).

Meiotic recombination (M26 hotspot)

  • Mts1/Mts2 (=Atf1/Pcr1) heterodimer essential for M26 hotspot activity; disruption abolishes hotspot PMID:9391101.
  • Hotspot activation not via increased transcription; separable from transcription PMID:9391101.
  • Atf1 strictly required to activate ade6-M26 hotspot, not basal recombination; rate-limiting PMID:19436749. Phosphorylation dispensable for hotspot PMID:19436749.
  • HRA domain (activation) and HRR (repression) reside in Atf1; modular OSA/HRA/HRR/bZIP organization PMID:18375981. => supports both activation (GO:0010846) and negative regulation (GO:0045128) of recombination.

Heterochromatin / silencing at mating-type

  • Atf1/Pcr1 nucleate heterochromatin at mat region in parallel to RNAi; target H3K9me and Swi6; RNAi-independent PMID:15218150. NOTE: this is RNAi/siRNA-INDEPENDENT => supports the NEGATED GO:0141194 (siRNA-mediated heterochromatin formation) annotation and positive GO:0030466.
  • Atf1/Pcr1 recruit HDAC Clr6, needed for H3/H4 deacetylation before H3K9me/Swi6 assembly PMID:15292231.
  • Clr3 HDAC recruited at mat through ATF/CREB proteins => chromatin-protein adaptor role PMID:16246721. Supports GO:0140463 chromatin-protein adaptor activity.

Nucleosome organization / antisense barrier

  • Atf1 basal binding creates wide NDRs; deletion -> disorganized nucleosomes, derepressed antisense transcription PMID:25122751. Supports GO:0031491 nucleosome binding & GO:0060195 negative regulation of antisense RNA transcription.

Mitotic entry

  • Atf1 directly controls Cdc13 (mitotic cyclin) expression; binds Cdc13 promoter; regulates G2/M independently of Wee1/Cdc25 PMID:24728197.

Protein interactions

  • Heterodimer with Pcr1 (UniProt SUBUNIT). Interacts with Sty1/Spc1 (IntAct). Srk1 kinase target of Sty1 PMID:12080074 - the GO:0005515 IPI with Q09892(sty1) refers to atf1-sty1 interaction. StressNet interactome PMID:23695164.

RNA binding (weak/dubious)

  • ~210 nt RNAs copurified with Mts1/Mts2 but "likely copurified as a result of tight but nonspecific interactions" PMID:7518718. => GO:0003723 RNA binding is questionable/over-annotation (nonspecific).

PMID:39747188 caveat

  • This Nat Commun paper is about PhpC(NF-Y), not Atf1. Abstract does not mention atf1 DNA binding. The GO:0000978 EXP annotation attributed to atf1 from this paper cannot be supported from the abstract -> UNDECIDED (cannot verify atf1-specific claim from cached text).

Core function summary

  1. MF: sequence-specific RNA Pol II DNA-binding transcription activator (and repressor) binding ATF/CRE (M26, TGACGT) sites - core.
  2. BP: master regulator of the core environmental stress response (osmotic, heat, oxidative, nutrient) downstream of Sty1 SAPK.
  3. BP: sexual differentiation / stationary phase entry (ste11 induction, G1 arrest).
  4. BP: activation of M26 meiotic recombination hotspots (separable, transcription-independent).
  5. BP: RNAi-independent heterochromatin nucleation/silencing at the mating-type region (recruits Clr3/Clr6 HDACs, Swi6) - chromatin-protein adaptor.

  Atf1 (also known as Gad7, Mts1, Sss1) is the principal ATF/CREB-family
  basic-leucine-zipper (bZIP) transcription factor of the fission yeast
  Schizosaccharomyces pombe, with strong homology to mammalian ATF-2/CRE-BP1. It
  acts as a sequence-specific RNA polymerase II transcription factor that
  heterodimerizes with the bZIP partner Pcr1 (Mts2) and binds CRE/ATF
  (cyclic-AMP response element, M26, 5'-ATGACGT-3') sites in promoters. Atf1 is
  the major nuclear substrate of the Sty1/Spc1 stress-activated MAP kinase
  (a p38/SAPK homolog) and drives the core environmental stress response,
  inducing hundreds of genes upon oxidative, osmotic, heat, and nutrient
  (nitrogen/carbon) stress, as well as entry into stationary phase. Beyond its
  role in stress transcription, Atf1 is essential for sexual differentiation and
  meiosis (including induction of ste11+ and G1 arrest under nitrogen
  starvation), activates the meiotic recombination hotspot ade6-M26, and is
  required for RNAi-independent nucleation and maintenance of heterochromatin and
  transcriptional silencing at the silent mating-type region, where it recruits
  histone deacetylase complexes (Clr3, Clr6) and Swi6/HP1. By binding promoters
  constitutively, Atf1 also shapes nucleosome architecture, creating broad
  nucleosome-depleted regions, phasing downstream nucleosome arrays, and
  suppressing cryptic antisense transcription. Atf1 acts as both a
  transcriptional activator and a repressor depending on context.
