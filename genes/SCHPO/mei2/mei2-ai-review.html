Gene Description

Mei2 is the master RNA-binding regulator of meiosis in the fission yeast Schizosaccharomyces pombe. It is an RRM-domain protein (three RRMs; the C-terminal RRM3 is necessary and sufficient for RNA binding) that binds the meiosis-specific long non-coding RNA meiRNA (encoded by sme2) through a U-rich motif and accumulates with it as a single nuclear body, the "Mei2 dot," at the sme2 chromosomal locus. Mei2 is essential both for premeiotic DNA synthesis and for entry into the first meiotic division (meiosis I). Accumulation of the unphosphorylated, active form of Mei2 acts as the molecular switch that commits cells from the mitotic to the meiotic cell cycle, and its activity reinforces meiotic commitment. A central role of Mei2 is to antagonize the Mmi1/MTREC selective-elimination system: by sequestering the YTH-family protein Mmi1 into the dot (together with meiRNA), Mei2 stabilizes meiosis-specific mRNAs that would otherwise be degraded by the nuclear exosome during vegetative growth. Mei2 shuttles between cytoplasm and nucleus; in zygotes it is also recruited to cytoplasmic P-bodies where it represses translation of target mRNAs and drives zygotic development. Mei2 is held inactive during vegetative growth by Pat1(Ran1) kinase phosphorylation, by 14-3-3 (Rad24) binding to the phosphorylated form, and by TORC1/Tor2-dependent phosphorylation that targets it for ubiquitin-proteasomal degradation.

Proposed New Ontology Terms

mRNA stabilization

Definition: Mei2 stabilizes meiosis-specific mRNAs by sequestering Mmi1 and thereby blocking their Mmi1/MTREC/exosome-mediated destruction. The existing GO term mRNA stabilization (GO:0048255) captures this biological process and would be an appropriate, specific BP annotation for Mei2 based on direct experimental evidence.

Justification: Mei2's defining downstream effect is the stabilization (prevention of degradation) of meiosis-specific transcripts; this is currently only captured indirectly via protein sequestering activity and positive regulation of the meiotic cell cycle.

Overview

Mei2 is the master RNA-binding regulator of meiosis in the fission yeast Schizosaccharomyces pombe.
It is an RRM-containing protein (three RRMs; the C-terminal RRM3 is essential and sufficient for meiRNA
binding) that binds the meiosis-specific long non-coding RNA meiRNA (encoded by sme2, PomBase:SPNCRNA.103)
and forms the nuclear "Mei2 dot". Mei2 is required both for premeiotic DNA synthesis and for entry into
meiosis I. It is negatively regulated by Pat1(Ran1) kinase phosphorylation and by the TORC1 system, and
it antagonizes the Mmi1/MTREC-mediated selective elimination of meiotic mRNAs.
