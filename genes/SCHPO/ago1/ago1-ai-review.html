Gene Description

Ago1 is the sole Argonaute protein of the fission yeast Schizosaccharomyces pombe and the catalytic core ("slicer") of the RNA interference (RNAi) machinery. The 834-residue protein has the canonical Argonaute architecture, including a PAZ domain (residues 210-326) that anchors the 3' end of small RNAs and a PIWI domain (residues 500-799) that adopts an RNase H-like fold carrying the catalytic Asp-Asp-His triad (Asp580, Asp651, His788). Ago1 binds single-stranded small interfering RNAs (siRNAs, ~22 nt with 3' 2-nt overhangs) and uses them as sequence-specific guides to base-pair with nascent centromeric and other transcripts. It is the central subunit of the RNA-induced transcriptional silencing (RITS) complex (together with the chromodomain protein Chp1 and Tas3), which is tethered to heterochromatic loci and recruits the Clr4/CLRC H3K9 methyltransferase complex and the RNA-directed RNA polymerase complex (RDRC, Rdp1) to drive co-transcriptional gene silencing and RNAi-directed heterochromatin assembly. Ago1 is also the core of the Argonaute siRNA chaperone (ARC) complex (with Arb1 and Arb2), which loads small RNAs onto Ago1 and licenses RITS assembly. siRNA-loaded, slicer-active Ago1 directs heterochromatin formation at pericentromeric repeats (essential for accurate chromosome segregation), the silent mating-type locus, subtelomeres, and facultative heterochromatin islands over meiotic genes. Ago1 additionally recruits the trimmer exonuclease Triman to generate mature priRNAs and siRNAs, and its bound small RNAs are subject to tailing and turnover surveillance. Ago1 is found in both the cytoplasm/cytosol (siRNA loading and slicing) and the nucleus (chromatin-associated silencing at centromeres, mating-type region, and telomeres).

Core Functions

Binds single-stranded small interfering RNAs (siRNAs, ~22 nt) as sequence-specific guides and is the catalytic slicer of the RNAi pathway, using its PIWI-domain RNase H-like Asp-Asp-His triad to cleave target RNAs complementary to the guide.

Core biology

Ago1 is the sole Argonaute and the catalytic core ("slicer") of the S. pombe RNAi
machinery. It binds single-stranded siRNAs (~22 nt) generated by Dicer (Dcr1) / Rdp1,
and uses them as guides to base-pair with nascent centromeric (and other) transcripts.

RITS complex = Ago1 + Chp1 (chromodomain) + Tas3 + centromeric siRNAs. RITS is the
RNA-induced transcriptional silencing effector that targets heterochromatin.
PMID:14704433

ARC complex = Ago1 + Arb1 + Arb2; loads siRNA, contains mostly dsRNA.
