CC   -!- FUNCTION: Component of the FACT complex, a general chromatin factor
CC       that acts to reorganize nucleosomes. The FACT complex is involved in
CC       multiple processes that require DNA as a template such as mRNA
CC       elongation, DNA replication and DNA repair. During transcription
CC       elongation the FACT complex acts as a histone chaperone that both
CC       destabilizes and restores nucleosomal structure (By similarity). It
CC       facilitates the passage of RNA polymerase II and transcription by
CC       promoting the dissociation of one histone H2A-H2B dimer from the
CC       nucleosome, then subsequently promotes the reestablishment of the
CC       nucleosome following the passage of RNA polymerase II
CC       (PubMed:28218250). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9Y5B9,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:28218250}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Forms a stable heterodimer with pob3 (By similarity). The
CC       spt16-pob3 dimer weakly associates with multiple molecules of nhp6 to
CC       form the FACT complex (By similarity). Interacts with abo1
CC       (PubMed:26582768). Interacts with chromatin remodeling protein fft3; to
CC       mediate nucleosome disassembly at transcribing regions and promote the
CC       efficient passage of RNAPII through chromatin (PubMed:28218250).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9Y5B9, ECO:0000269|PubMed:26582768,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:28218250}.

CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Nucleus {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9Y5B9}.

CC   -!- CAUTION: Although related to the peptidase M24 family, this protein
CC       lacks conserved active site residues suggesting that it may lack
CC       peptidase activity. {ECO:0000305}.
