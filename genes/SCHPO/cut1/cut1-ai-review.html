Gene Description

Cut1 is the separase (separin) of the fission yeast Schizosaccharomyces pombe, a large (1828-residue) CD-clan cysteine endopeptidase (EC 3.4.22.49) and the ortholog of budding yeast Esp1 and human separase (ESPL1). Its catalytic activity resides in a C-terminal peptidase C50 domain with the active-site cysteine at position 1730. Cut1 triggers the metaphase-to-anaphase transition by proteolytically cleaving the kleisin subunit of cohesin (Rad21/Scc1 in mitosis, Rec8 in meiosis), opening the cohesin ring and allowing sister chromatids (or homologues) to separate. Through most of the cell cycle Cut1 is held catalytically inactive by binding its inhibitory chaperone securin Cut2, forming the separase-securin complex; at the metaphase-anaphase transition the APC/C (activated by Slp1/Cdc20) ubiquitinates Cut2 and targets it for degradation, liberating active Cut1. Cut1 is cytoplasmic during interphase and relocates to the spindle pole bodies and mitotic spindle upon entry into prophase, remaining bound to the anaphase spindle midzone, where it also promotes anaphase spindle elongation. Beyond mitosis, Cut1 cleaves Rec8 to drive meiotic chromosome segregation and cleaves cohesin locally during interphase to support DNA repair. Loss of Cut1 produces the "cut" (cell untimely torn) phenotype, in which chromosomes fail to segregate yet cytokinesis proceeds, bisecting the undivided nucleus.

Core biology

Cut1 is the fission-yeast separase (ESP1/separin ortholog), a large CD-clan
cysteine endopeptidase that triggers anaphase sister-chromatid separation by
cleaving the cohesin kleisin subunit Rad21, opening the cohesin ring. Held
inactive before anaphase by its bound inhibitory chaperone securin (Cut2);
APC/C-Slp1-mediated degradation of Cut2 at the metaphase-anaphase transition
liberates active Cut1. "cut" = cell untimely torn — chromosomes fail to
segregate but septation proceeds.
