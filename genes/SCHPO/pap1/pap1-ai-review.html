Gene Description

Pap1 (also caf3, Caffeine resistance protein 3) is the fission yeast AP-1-like basic-leucine-zipper (bZIP) transcription factor of the Yap1/c-Jun (YAP) subfamily. It is the principal redox-regulated transcriptional activator of the oxidative-stress response and a determinant of multidrug/caffeine resistance. Pap1 binds AP-1/TRE-like sites (consensus TTACGTAA) as a homodimer through its bZIP domain and bends the target DNA. Its activity is controlled by regulated nucleocytoplasmic shuttling: in unstressed cells reduced Pap1 is continuously exported to the cytoplasm by the exportin Crm1, whereas under low hydrogen peroxide the peroxiredoxin Tpx1 transmits a disulfide relay that oxidizes the cysteine-rich domains of Pap1 (forming an intramolecular Cys278-Cys501 disulfide) and masks its C-terminal nuclear export signal, causing nuclear accumulation. Nuclear Pap1 then activates antioxidant genes (catalase ctt1, thioredoxin reductase trr1, sulfiredoxin srx1, thioredoxin) - many in concert with the response regulator Prr1 - and drug-efflux/MDR genes (the ABC transporters bfr1/hba2 and pmd1, the efflux pump caf5, obr1). At high peroxide doses the Sty1/Atf1 MAP kinase pathway dominates the response. Pap1 also participates in the response to electrophiles such as diethylmaleate, methylglyoxal, and to diamide/cadmium-induced disulfide stress through a cooperative pathway with the HMG protein Oxs1.

pap1 (Q01663, SPAC1783.07c, caf3) — S. pombe AP-1-like bZIP transcription factor

Summary

Pap1 (Caffeine resistance protein 3; AP-1-like / Yap1/c-Jun family bZIP) is the principal redox-regulated
transcriptional activator of the oxidative-stress and multidrug-resistance responses in fission yeast.
Under low H2O2 it senses peroxide via the peroxiredoxin Tpx1, which forms a transient disulfide relay that
oxidizes the C-terminal cysteine-rich domain (c-CRD), forming an intramolecular interdomain disulfide
(Cys278–Cys501) that masks the Crm1-dependent nuclear export signal. Nuclear Pap1 binds AP-1/TRE-like
sites (consensus TTACGTAA) and induces antioxidant genes (ctt1 catalase, trr1, trx2, srx1) and
drug-efflux/MDR genes (bfr1/hba2, pmd1, caf5, obr1). At high H2O2 the Sty1/Atf1 pathway dominates.

Identity / family

  • AP-1-like bZIP transcription factor; bZIP family, YAP subfamily [UniProt Q01663].
  • "The other gene pap1+ encodes an AP-1-like transcription factor that contains a region rich in basic amino acids followed by a 'leucine zipper' motif." PMID:1899230
  • bZIP DNA-binding domain crystal structure (PDB 1GD2); binds novel consensus TTACGTAA. PMID:11017199
  • Homodimer (PubMed:11017199, UniProt SUBUNIT).

DNA binding / TF activity

  • Binds AP-1 site and a 14-bp palindrome by DNase I footprinting; activates target gene p25. PMID:1448080
  • Required for transcription activation: "p25 is overproduced when the pap1+ gene is overexpressed, whereas p25 is not produced at all in the pap1 deletion mutant." PMID:1448080
  • DNA bending: Pap1 bends DNA ~26° toward protein. PMID:9070843
  • Crystallographic characterization of Pap1-DNA complex (DNA-binding domain). PMID:9757124
  • Genome-wide TF atlas: ChIP-seq DNA binding sites and nucleosome binding (EXP). PMID:40015273

Oxidative stress / redox regulation

  • Sty1 regulates Pap1; both required for oxidative-stress and MDR gene expression. PMID:9585505
  • Genes induced incl. hba2/bfr1 and pmd1 (MDR transporters). PMID:9585505
  • H2O2-induced disulfide bond precedes nuclear accumulation. PMID:15165244
  • Tpx1 (2-Cys Prx) required for peroxide-induced Pap1 activation. PMID:15824112
  • H2O2 reversibly oxidizes Cys278 and Cys501; DEM non-reversibly modifies Cys523/Cys532. PMID:12100563
  • Pap1 + Prr1 heterodimer needed for antioxidant (but not drug-tolerance) genes. PMID:22344694
  • Methylglyoxal activates Pap1 via c-CRD; nuclear accumulation, target gene activation. PMID:16141205
  • Tpx1 hyperoxidation/Txl1 role in switching off early Pap1 response. PMID:22245228

Subcellular location & export control

  • Negatively regulated by Crm1/exportin 1. PMID:1448080
  • Oxidant-sensitive NES in c-CRD; Cys532 + two Leu + Ile important. PMID:10329722
  • Cytoplasm in unstressed cells; relocalizes to nucleus on oxidative stress (Sty1-dependent at high dose). PMID:9585505
  • Hba1 (Ran-binding) loss → constitutive nuclear Pap1, MDR. PMID:12896976
  • Genome-wide localization (cytosol/cytoplasm). PMID:16823372

Drug / caffeine / multidrug resistance

  • pap1 first isolated as staurosporine-resistance gene; required for spk1-conferred resistance. PMID:1899230
  • Int6 overexpression drug resistance is Pap1-dependent. PMID:11071922
  • Pap1 a key TF for fission yeast MDR. PMID:22840777
  • Genes required for caffeine tolerance; Pap1 and efflux pumps. PMID:19672306
  • pof14 transcription induced by H2O2 requires Pap1. PMID:17016471
  • ctt1 catalase induction by oxidative stress via Pap1 (element A). PMID:10731689

Disulfide stress / Oxs1

  • Pap1–Oxs1 pathway for diamide/Cd disulfide stress; Oxs1 + Pap1 form complex, co-required for promoter binding. PMID:27664222

Atf1 cross-talk

  • A subset of stress genes co-controlled by Atf1 and Pap1. PMID:28652406
