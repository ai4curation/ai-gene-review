# cdc13 (SPBC582.03, P10815) review notes

## Identity / overview
Cdc13 is the major mitotic B-type cyclin of fission yeast, the regulatory partner of the Cdc2/CDK1 kinase (together = MPF). Cyclin AB subfamily (UniProt). Essential gene; required for the G2/M transition and for mitotic and meiotic nuclear divisions. Targeted for destruction by APC/C at anaphase (classic cyclin behaviour).

## Core function evidence
- Cyclin / G2/M control: [PMID:2908246 "Our results suggest that the cdc13+ gene function is required for the control of the G2 to M transition."] and [PMID:2908246 "a central region of 176 amino acids of this polypeptide is 50% identical with sea urchin cyclin. Therefore, the cdc13+ protein is cyclin related and could act as a regulator or substrate of the p34cdc2 protein kinase, which initiates mitosis."]
- Forms stable complex with Cdc2 and regulates kinase activity/localization: [PMID:2569363 "The products of the cdc13+ and cdc2+ genes form a stable complex that displays protein kinase activity in vitro."] and [PMID:2569363 "the cdc13+-encoded cyclin acts to regulate both the catalytic properties and the localization of the protein kinase of which it is a subunit."]
- Cyclin destruction at mitosis: [PMID:2534559 "Loss of cdc13 fluorescence at mitosis reflects the destruction of the protein. Thus, it behaves as a classic cyclin."]
- Required to localize Cdc2 to nucleus: [PMID:2534559 "cdc13 therefore appears to be required to localise cdc2 to the nucleus but not vice versa."]
- CAK Thr167/cyclin association: [PMID:1655416 "Thr167 phosphorylation is required for p34cdc2 kinase activity at mitosis and is involved in the association of p34cdc2 with cyclin B."]

## Localization
- Nuclear protein, distinct from chromatin: [PMID:2534559 "both cdc13 and cdc2 are nuclear proteins in S. pombe. Both are localised to a nuclear domain distinct from that occupied by the DAPI-staining chromatin."]
- Nucleus + spindle pole body populations: [PMID:1699136 "the presence of two spatially distinct cdc13 cyclin populations in the nucleus of S. pombe, one of which is associated with the mitotic spindle poles."]
- SPB enrichment in G2, spindle in mitosis; leaves spindle before anaphase: [PMID:11683390 "Cdc2-YFP and cdc13-YFP are highly enriched on the spindle pole body of cells in late G2 or arrested at S phase. Both accumulate on the spindle pole bodies and the spindle in prophase and metaphase ... In anaphase, the cdc2p/cdc13p complex leaves the spindle prior to sister chromatid separation, and cdc13-YFP is enriched at the nuclear periphery before fluorescence disappears."]
- Cytoplasmic pool in interphase, relocalizes to nucleus in mitosis (EM/IF): [PMID:8688826 "evidencing cytoplasmic localization of p56cdc13, in addition to the nuclear localization previously observed ... emphasizing the major cytoplasmic localization of p56cdc13 in interphase cells and the relocalization towards the nucleus in mitotic cells"]
- Hydrophobic patch targets Cdc13 to SPB (centrosome equivalent): [PMID:32084401 "the hydrophobic patch targets Cdc13 to the yeast centrosome equivalent, the spindle pole body (SPB), and disruption of this motif prevents both centrosomal localization of Cdc13 and the onset of mitosis but does not prevent S phase."] Wild-type localization: [PMID:32084401 "Wild-type Cdc13 is found in the nucleus, is visibly enriched at the SPB in G2, and decorates the mitotic spindle during mitosis"]
- Cut8/proteasome localizes degradation to nucleus/nuclear periphery: [PMID:11084332 "destruction of mitotic cyclin and Cut2 in the nucleus is dramatically delayed, though polyubiquitination of Cdc13 occurs in cut8 mutant."]
- Spindle midzone Klp9/Ase1 phospho-regulation by Cdc2: [PMID:19686686 "The cyclin-dependent kinase cdc2p phosphorylates and its antagonist phosphatase clp1p dephosphorylates klp9p and ase1p to control the position and timing of klp9p-ase1p interaction."]
- Stationary phase / nucleolar accumulation of CDK: [PMID:37128864 "Cdc2 accumulates in the nucleolus."]
- Heat-stress reversible aggregation into nucleolar rings (cell-cycle regulators sequestered): [PMID:33176152 "NuRs sequester essential factors required for nuclear mRNA metabolism and nuclear pore complex function, as well as cell-cycle regulators."]
- Telomere bouquet/SPB: [PMID:29123917 "loss of chromosome contact with the SPB caused delayed accumulation of CDK1-cyclin B at the SPB ... In the absence of the bouquet, CDK1-cyclin B localised near the telomeres but not at the SPB at the later stage of meiotic prophase."]

## Replication origins / preventing re-replication
[PMID:12419251 "the mitotic B type cyclin Cdc13/Cdc2 kinase associates with replication origins in vivo. This association is dependent on the origin recognition complex (ORC) ... ORC bound mitotic Cyclin B/Cdc2 kinase imposes the dependency of S phase on an intervening mitosis"]

## DNA damage checkpoint / recombinational repair in G2
[PMID:12023299 "the major Schizosaccharomyces pombe CDK, Cdc2-cyclin B, influences recombinational repair of radiation-induced DSBs during the G(2) phase at two distinct stages ... This effect of Cdc2-cyclin B kinase on Top3 function is mediated by the BRCT-domain-containing checkpoint protein Crb2, thus linking checkpoint proteins directly with recombinational repair in G2."]

## Chromosome bi-orientation
- CPC/Survivin phosphorylation: [PMID:20739936 "we identified Cdk1 (also known as Cdc2)-cyclin-B-dependent phosphorylation of Survivin ... a key role of Cdk1-cyclin B in chromosome bi-orientation."]
- Merotelic correction via spindle elongation: [PMID:22264609 "Cdk1 regulates spindle elongation forces to promote merotelic correction by phosphorylating and inhibiting Klp9."]

## Meiosis (genuine roles, not SPKW over-annotation)
- Required for meiosis I and II nuclear divisions: [PMID:25891897 "These results confirm that Cdc13 is required for meiosis I and II, and is not required for premeiotic S-phase unless this function can be achieved by very low Cdc13 protein levels."] Single complex sufficient: [PMID:25891897 "a single Cdc13-Cdc2 complex, in the absence of the other cyclins, can drive the meiotic cell cycle."]
- Mes1 blocks Cdc13 degradation to allow MII (negative regulation of meiotic cell cycle progression via Cdc13 stability): [PMID:15791259 "Mes1p is a factor that suppresses the degradation of cyclin Cdc13p at anaphase I ... Mes1p has a key function in saving a sufficient level of MPF (M-phase-promoting factor) activity required for the execution of MII."]
- Mfr1/APC degrades Cdc13 at end of MII before sporulation: [PMID:11493649 "mfr1 is an activator of the anaphase-promoting complex (APC), which is necessary for the rapid degradation of the cdc13 cyclin at the end of meiosis II, prior to the formation of spores."] mfr1 null retains high Cdc13/Cdc2 activity and delays sporulation: [PMID:11493649 "An mfr1 null mutant completes meiosis II but remains with high levels of cdc13 and cdc2 kinase activity and has considerably delayed spore formation."]

## Regulation by rum1 (CDK inhibitor)
[PMID:9303310 "p25rum1 associates with the CDK p34cdc2/p56cdc13 during G1 in normally cycling cells and is required for the rapid proteolysis of p56cdc13. In vitro binding data indicate that p25rum1 has specificity for the B-cyclin p56cdc13 component of the CDK"]

## GO annotation assessment summary
- MF core: cyclin-dependent protein serine/threonine kinase regulator activity (GO:0016538) and activator activity (GO:0061575) — ACCEPT as core. Both are well supported.
- BP core: regulation/positive regulation of G2/M transition (GO:0010389, GO:0010971) — ACCEPT/core; meiotic nuclear division (GO:0140013) and negative regulation of meiotic cell cycle (GO:0051447, via Mes1-stabilized pool) — genuine, KEEP (non-core or accept).
- CC: nucleus (core), SPB / mitotic spindle pole body, mitotic spindle, spindle midzone — well supported. Chromatin/telomeric repeat (replication-origin association), nucleolus, nucleolar peripheral inclusion body (stress), nuclear periphery — accept as context.
- "protein binding" (GO:0005515) — uninformative; recommend MODIFY toward cyclin-dependent protein kinase regulator activity where the partner is Cdc2/rum1/Mes1, or KEEP_AS_NON_CORE. Per curation guidelines avoid bare protein binding.
- IBA "traversing start control point" (GO:0007089) and "G1/S transition" — Cdc13 is the mitotic cyclin; primary role is G2/M not Start. The fusion/HPM work shows Cdc13 CAN drive S-phase when it is the only cyclin, but in WT cells G1/S is executed by cig1/cig2/puc1. Treat Start/G1S IBA as non-core / over-annotation for the endogenous mitotic role.
