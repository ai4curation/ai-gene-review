Gene Description

Bub1 is a conserved serine/threonine protein kinase (EC 2.7.11.1) of the spindle assembly checkpoint (SAC) in the fission yeast Schizosaccharomyces pombe. It has an N-terminal tetratricopeptide-repeat (TPR/Mad3-like) region and a C-terminal protein kinase domain. During mitosis Bub1, as part of the Bub1-Bub3 complex, is recruited early to unattached or tensionless kinetochores when the Mph1 (Mps1) kinase phosphorylates the MELT motifs of the outer-kinetochore protein Spc7 (KNL1). At kinetochores Bub1 serves as a scaffold for assembly of the SAC, helping recruit Bub3, Mad1-Mad2, Mad3 and the chromosomal passenger complex (CPC), thereby generating the diffusible "wait anaphase" signal that inhibits the anaphase-promoting complex/cyclosome until all chromosomes are bioriented. Independently of its checkpoint scaffolding role, Bub1 kinase activity phosphorylates histone H2A on serine 121 at the centromeric core/ inner centromere; this mark recruits the shugoshin proteins (Sgo1/Sgo2), which protect centromeric cohesin and, together with Haspin-mediated H3-T3 phosphorylation, target the CPC to the inner centromere to promote sister-chromatid biorientation and error correction. Bub1 is essential for accurate chromosome segregation in unperturbed mitosis and for the meiosis I program, where it is required for mono-orientation of sister kinetochores and protection of centromeric Rec8 cohesin in anaphase I. Loss of Bub1 abolishes the spindle checkpoint, causes chromosome lagging and loss, and destabilizes ploidy.

Core function summary

Conserved Ser/Thr kinase of the spindle assembly checkpoint (SAC). Recruited early to unattached/tensionless kinetochores via Mph1(Mps1)-phosphorylated Spc7/KNL1 MELT motifs (as Bub1-Bub3 complex).
