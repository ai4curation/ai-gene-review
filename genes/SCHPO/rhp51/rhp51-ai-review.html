Gene Description

Rhp51 (Rad51) is the fission yeast ortholog of the eukaryotic RecA-family DNA recombinase Rad51 and the central catalytic engine of homologous recombination (HR). It binds single-stranded DNA generated by end resection (and also double-stranded DNA) in an ATP-dependent manner to assemble a helical nucleoprotein filament that searches for a homologous duplex and catalyzes DNA strand invasion and strand exchange, using cycles of ATP binding and hydrolysis. Through this activity Rhp51 repairs DNA double-strand breaks, restarts and protects stalled or collapsed replication forks, and drives meiotic recombination, also contributing to telomere maintenance and to suppression of gross chromosomal rearrangements at repetitive regions such as centromeres. Its loading and activity are governed by mediators and regulators including the Rad52 ortholog Rad22/Rti1, the Rhp55-Rhp57 (Rad55-Rad57) paralog complex, the Swi5-Sfr1 activator, and the motor protein Rhp54 (Rad54); negative regulators such as the Fbh1 anti-recombinase and the Rrp1 translocase/ubiquitin ligase strip Rhp51 from DNA to limit untimely or excessive recombination. Rhp51 is a nuclear protein that concentrates as foci at sites of DNA double-strand breaks.
