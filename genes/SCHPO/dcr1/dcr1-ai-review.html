Gene Description

Dcr1 is the sole Dicer (ribonuclease III family endoribonuclease) of fission yeast and the enzyme that initiates the RNA interference (RNAi) pathway. It processes double-stranded RNA precursors, arising largely from pericentromeric repeat transcripts and amplified by the RNA-dependent RNA polymerase complex (RDRC/Rdp1), into ~21-23 nt siRNA duplexes that are loaded onto the single Argonaute protein (Ago1) to form the RITS complex. The protein has a modular architecture: an N-terminal DExD/H-box ATP-binding helicase domain (with an MRTGAGKT ATP-binding motif and a DECH box), a helicase C-terminal domain, a Dicer dsRNA-binding/dimerization fold, tandem catalytic RNase III domains (RIIIa and RIIIb) that coordinate Mg2+/Mn2+, and a C-terminal extended double-stranded RNA-binding domain (dsRBD) bearing a novel CHCC zinc-binding motif that binds one Zn2+ and is required for nuclear retention of the protein. Through siRNA biogenesis Dcr1 is essential for RNAi-directed heterochromatin assembly (H3K9 methylation by Clr4) at centromeres, the silent mating-type locus, and telomeres, and hence for accurate chromosome segregation. Beyond canonical RNAi, Dcr1 has roles in co-transcriptional gene silencing at euchromatic loci, in releasing RNA polymerases (Pol I/II/III) at sites of transcription-replication collision to maintain genome stability, and in cellular quiescence (G0). Dcr1 is predominantly nuclear, associating with chromatin and the nuclear periphery, with a smaller cytoplasmic pool.

dcr1 (Dicer) — S. pombe — Curation Notes

UniProt: Q09884 (DCR1_SCHPO). PomBase: SPCC188.13c. 1374 aa. NCBITaxon:284812.

Summary

Dcr1 is the sole Dicer (ribonuclease III enzyme) of fission yeast. It is the
endoribonuclease that initiates RNAi by processing double-stranded RNA into ~22-23 nt
siRNA duplexes that are loaded onto Argonaute (Ago1). It is essential for
RNAi-directed heterochromatin formation at centromeres (and the mating-type locus,
telomeres), and hence for accurate chromosome segregation. It has additional reported
roles in co-transcriptional gene silencing (CTGS) at euchromatic loci, RNA Pol II/I/III
release / transcription termination at sites of replication stress (genome stability),
and in cell cycle / quiescence (G0).

Domain architecture

N-terminal DExD/H-box ATP-dependent RNA helicase (ATP-binding 32-MRTGAGKT-39; DECH box
145-DECH-148), Platform/PAZ-like region, tandem RNase III domains (RIIIa/RIIIb), and a
C-terminal extended dsRNA-binding fold with a novel zinc-binding (CHCC) motif important
for nuclear retention. Catalytic-dead allele D937A/D1127A abolishes siRNA generation.
