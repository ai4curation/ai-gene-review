Gene Description

Rad21 is the alpha-kleisin subunit of the mitotic cohesin complex of fission yeast and the ortholog of budding yeast Scc1/Mcd1 and human RAD21. It is an essential 628-residue protein whose N- and C-terminal domains bind the ATPase head domains of the SMC subunits Psm3 (Smc3) and Psm1 (Smc1) respectively, thereby bridging them and, together with the HEAT-repeat subunit Psc3 (Scc3), closing the tripartite cohesin ring. This ring topologically entraps DNA and, after S phase, two sister DNA molecules, establishing and maintaining sister chromatid cohesion from replication until anaphase. The complex is loaded onto chromatin by the Mis4 (Scc2)/Ssl3 (Scc4) loader in an ATP-hydrolysis-dependent reaction, is stabilized during S phase by the acetyltransferase Eso1 (Eco1), and is dynamically removed by the Wapl (Wpl1) unloading activity. At the metaphase-to-anaphase transition the protease separase (Cut1), activated by destruction of its inhibitor securin (Cut2), cleaves a small but essential fraction of Rad21, opening the ring and allowing sister chromatids to segregate. Rad21 is hyperphosphorylated during S and G2 phases and is a cell-cycle-regulated nuclear phosphoprotein. Cohesin is enriched across broad centromeric/pericentromeric heterochromatin, rDNA, and subtelomeric regions, contributes to chromatin loop formation, and functions in DNA double-strand break repair. Rad21 was originally identified through a radiation-sensitive, double-strand-break-repair-deficient mutant, and both its repair and cohesion roles are well established.

rad21 (S. pombe) — curation notes

UniProt: P30776 (RAD21_SCHPO). PomBase: SPCC338.17c. 628 aa. Essential gene on chromosome III.
Rad21 = alpha-kleisin subunit of the mitotic cohesin complex (ortholog of S. cerevisiae Scc1/Mcd1, human RAD21). Bridges the Psm1(Smc1) and Psm3(Smc3) ATPase heads, and binds Psc3(Scc3), to close the tripartite cohesin ring that topologically entraps sister chromatids.

Identity / discovery

  • First identified as a radiation-sensitivity / DNA double-strand-break (DSB) repair gene; rad21-45 mutant (I67T) is radiation-sensitive and DSB-repair-deficient but still arrests in G2. Plasmid rad21 fully restores DSB repair. Essential for mitotic growth. PMID:1480481 PMID:1480481
  • Nuclear, cell-cycle-regulated phosphoprotein; NLS in C-terminal third; multiply phosphorylated on serines; mRNA/protein peak near G1/S. PMID:7706319 PMID:7706319

Cohesin complex / cohesion

  • Rad21 forms a stable complex with Psm1 and Psm3 (IP); cohesin subunits essential for sister chromatid cohesion; mutants show precocious sister chromatid separation; loss of any subunit gives same phenotype. PMID:11069892 PMID:11069892
  • Cohesin enriched at broad centromere regions; centromere-associated Rad21 unchanged metaphase->anaphase (unlike budding yeast). PMID:11069892
  • Mis4 (Scc2) loads Rad21 onto chromatin. PMID:11069892
  • Cohesin established during S phase by Eso1 (Eco1/Ctf7 homolog); Pds5 modulates. PMID:11598020 [PMID:10779336 mutant "arrested in M phase with a viability loss due to a failure to establish sister chromatid cohesion during S phase"]

Anaphase cleavage / separase

  • Rad21 cleaved specifically in anaphase at sites similar to Scc1; cleavage essential though only <5% cleaved; protease likely Cut1 (separase); noncleavable R179E/R231E double mutant blocks sister separation. PMID:11069892 PMID:11069892
  • Securin(Cut2)-separase(Cut1) cleaves cohesin at anaphase to allow segregation. PMID:15329725
  • Interphase separase cleavage of cohesin required for DNA repair: rad21 mutant epistatic to cut2(EA2) in UV sensitivity; cohesin hyper-modified after UV in Rad3-dependent way. PMID:15329725 PMID:15329725

Segregation / kinetochore

  • Cohesion mutants (rad21-K1, mis4-242) activate Mad2/Bub1 spindle checkpoint due to diminished microtubule-kinetochore interaction; needed for normal spindle-kinetochore interaction. PMID:11882285 PMID:11882285
  • Mitotic Rad21 supports equational (bipolar) segregation; replaced by Rec8 in meiosis. Substitutive Rad21 relocates to centromeres in rec8∆ and gives equational meiosis I division; Rad21 supports bipolar attachment, Rec8 monopolar. PMID:12748297 PMID:12748297
  • Meiosis: Rec8 (not Rad21) is the meiosis-specific kleisin. PMID:10440376
  • Telomere/arm separation: Aurora B promotes dissociation of Swi6/HP1 and cohesin Rad21 from telomeres for telomere dispersion. PMID:25778919
  • Anaphase merotely correction context (Ase1) - rad21 used as cohesion/separation marker. [PMID:19948483 abstract is about Ase1/Prc1 spindle elongation; full text assays rad21 cohesion]

Biochemistry / ring mechanism (in vitro fission-yeast cohesin)

  • Reconstituted fission yeast cohesin (Psm1/Psm3/Rad21/Psc3) topologically loads onto DNA; loading stimulated by Mis4/Ssl3 loader; cohesin binds dsDNA (ATP-independent) and topological loading is ATP-hydrolysis dependent; TEV cleavage of modified Rad21 releases DNA. PMID:24291789 PMID:24291789 PMID:24291789
  • DNA entry/exit by interlocking gate; Wapl disengages kleisin (Rad21). PMID:26687354 (IPI partner SPAC10F6.09c = wpl1/Wapl)
  • DNA-DNA tethering by cohesin ring -> second DNA captured (basis of cohesion/DNA-DNA interactions). PMID:29358048
  • Mis4 DNA binding promotes topological entrapment; ATP-dependent topological co-entrapment activity. PMID:33176147
  • Suppressor/3D modeling: interfaces Psm1-Rad21, Psm3-Rad21; Cut1 dispensable when interfaces destabilized -> hold/release of DNA. PMID:29735656
  • Cohesin ATPases interface with regulators (Eco1, Wpl1) to modulate DNA tethering (S. cerevisiae study, conserved). PMID:26583750

Chromatin organization / loop extrusion / localization

  • Cohesin extrudes DNA loops (Brownian ratchet model; fission yeast cohesin) -> chromatin looping. PMID:34309513
  • Cohesin at pericentric/rDNA heterochromatin, subtelomeric regions; condensin (not cohesin) binds pol II genes — paper notes cohesin sites distinct from condensin. PMID:25847133
  • Heterochromatin island (facultative): Rad21 IDA localization at heterochromatin islands. [PMID:22144463 — facultative heterochromatin islands at meiotic genes]
  • Global localization (ORFeome GFP) — nuclear. [PMID:16823372 proteome-scale localization study]

  chromatin by the Mis4 (Scc2)/Ssl3 (Scc4) loader in an ATP-hydrolysis-dependent
  reaction, is stabilized during S phase by the acetyltransferase Eso1 (Eco1),
  and is dynamically removed by the Wapl (Wpl1) unloading activity. At the
  metaphase-to-anaphase transition the protease separase (Cut1), activated by
  destruction of its inhibitor securin (Cut2), cleaves a small but essential
  fraction of Rad21, opening the ring and allowing sister chromatids to
  segregate. Rad21 is hyperphosphorylated during S and G2 phases and is a
  cell-cycle-regulated nuclear phosphoprotein. Cohesin is enriched across broad
  centromeric/pericentromeric heterochromatin, rDNA, and subtelomeric regions,
  contributes to chromatin loop formation, and functions in DNA double-strand
  break repair. Rad21 was originally identified through a radiation-sensitive,
  double-strand-break-repair-deficient mutant, and both its repair and cohesion
  roles are well established.
