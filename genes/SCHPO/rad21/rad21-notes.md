# rad21 (S. pombe) — curation notes

UniProt: P30776 (RAD21_SCHPO). PomBase: SPCC338.17c. 628 aa. Essential gene on chromosome III.
Rad21 = alpha-kleisin subunit of the mitotic cohesin complex (ortholog of S. cerevisiae Scc1/Mcd1, human RAD21). Bridges the Psm1(Smc1) and Psm3(Smc3) ATPase heads, and binds Psc3(Scc3), to close the tripartite cohesin ring that topologically entraps sister chromatids.

## Identity / discovery
- First identified as a radiation-sensitivity / DNA double-strand-break (DSB) repair gene; rad21-45 mutant (I67T) is radiation-sensitive and DSB-repair-deficient but still arrests in G2. Plasmid rad21 fully restores DSB repair. Essential for mitotic growth. [PMID:1480481 "The radiation-sensitive mutant rad21-45 is deficient in this repair pathway but is capable of cell-cycle arrest in G2 following DNA damage. We cloned the rad21 gene by complementing the radiation sensitivity of the rad21-45 mutant. The plasmid-borne gene completely reestablished the DNA dsb repair pathway."] [PMID:1480481 "Deletion of the genomic rad21 gene showed that it is essential for mitotic growth of S.pombe."]
- Nuclear, cell-cycle-regulated phosphoprotein; NLS in C-terminal third; multiply phosphorylated on serines; mRNA/protein peak near G1/S. [PMID:7706319 "The Rad21 protein is nuclear during the cell cycle. The nuclear localization signal was identified in the C-terminal third of the protein."] [PMID:7706319 "is multiply phosphorylated mainly at serine residues. The expression of the rad21 mRNA and the Rad21 protein is cell cycle-regulated, with the peak of mRNA and protein expression occurring near the G1 to S transition."]

## Cohesin complex / cohesion
- Rad21 forms a stable complex with Psm1 and Psm3 (IP); cohesin subunits essential for sister chromatid cohesion; mutants show precocious sister chromatid separation; loss of any subunit gives same phenotype. [PMID:11069892 "Immunoprecipitation demonstrates stable complex formation of Rad21 with Psm1 and Psm3 but not with Psc3."] [PMID:11069892 "Precocious sister chromatid separation was observed in cohesin subunit mutants, strongly suggesting that fission yeast cohesin was also required to establish sister chromatid cohesion."]
- Cohesin enriched at broad centromere regions; centromere-associated Rad21 unchanged metaphase->anaphase (unlike budding yeast). [PMID:11069892 "Chromatin immunoprecipitation shows that cohesin subunits are enriched in broad centromere regions and that the level of centromere-associated Rad21 did not change from metaphase to anaphase, very different from budding yeast."]
- Mis4 (Scc2) loads Rad21 onto chromatin. [PMID:11069892 "Mis4, another sister chromatid cohesion protein, plays an essential role for loading Rad21 on chromatin."]
- Cohesin established during S phase by Eso1 (Eco1/Ctf7 homolog); Pds5 modulates. [PMID:11598020 "Eso1p, functioning in close coupling to DNA replication, establishes sister chromatid cohesion whereas the newly identified Cohesin-associated protein Pds5p hinders the establishment of cohesion until counteracted by Eso1p, yet stabilizes cohesion once it is established."] [PMID:10779336 mutant "arrested in M phase with a viability loss due to a failure to establish sister chromatid cohesion during S phase"]

## Anaphase cleavage / separase
- Rad21 cleaved specifically in anaphase at sites similar to Scc1; cleavage essential though only <5% cleaved; protease likely Cut1 (separase); noncleavable R179E/R231E double mutant blocks sister separation. [PMID:11069892 "Rad21 containing similar cleavage sites to those of Scc1p/Mcd1p is cleaved specifically in anaphase. This cleavage is essential, although the amount of cleaved product is very small (<5%)."] [PMID:11069892 "overexpression of noncleavable double mutant Rad21 protein (R179ER231E) in wild-type cells was highly toxic and prevented sister chromatid separation."]
- Securin(Cut2)-separase(Cut1) cleaves cohesin at anaphase to allow segregation. [PMID:15329725 "At anaphase, separase is activated by degradation of its inhibitory partner, securin. Separase then cleaves cohesins, thus allowing sister chromatid separation."]
- Interphase separase cleavage of cohesin required for DNA repair: rad21 mutant epistatic to cut2(EA2) in UV sensitivity; cohesin hyper-modified after UV in Rad3-dependent way. [PMID:15329725 "recombination repair rhp51 (ref. 11) and cohesin subunit rad21 (ref. 12) mutants were not. Cohesin was hyper-modified on ultraviolet irradiation in a Rad3 kinase-dependent way."] [PMID:15329725 "We propose that the securin-separase complex might aid DNA repair by removing local cohesin in interphase cells."]

## Segregation / kinetochore
- Cohesion mutants (rad21-K1, mis4-242) activate Mad2/Bub1 spindle checkpoint due to diminished microtubule-kinetochore interaction; needed for normal spindle-kinetochore interaction. [PMID:11882285 "The loss of chromatid cohesion in fission yeast mutants (mis4-242 and rad21-K1) leads to the activation of Mad2- and Bub1-dependent checkpoint, possibly due to a diminished microtubule-kinetochore interaction."] [PMID:11882285 "Mis4 and Rad21 (budding yeast Scc2 and Scc1 homologs, respectively) act in establishing the normal spindle-kinetochore interaction in early mitosis and inhibit sister chromatid separation until the cleavage of Rad21 in anaphase."]
- Mitotic Rad21 supports equational (bipolar) segregation; replaced by Rec8 in meiosis. Substitutive Rad21 relocates to centromeres in rec8∆ and gives equational meiosis I division; Rad21 supports bipolar attachment, Rec8 monopolar. [PMID:12748297 "the equational segregation at meiosis I depends on substitutive Rad21, which relocates to the centromeres if Rec8 is absent."] [PMID:12748297 "Rec8 and Rad21 define monopolar and bipolar attachment, respectively, at meiosis I."]
- Meiosis: Rec8 (not Rad21) is the meiosis-specific kleisin. [PMID:10440376 "The fission yeast cohesin protein Rec8 is specific to and required for meiosis."]
- Telomere/arm separation: Aurora B promotes dissociation of Swi6/HP1 and cohesin Rad21 from telomeres for telomere dispersion. [PMID:25778919 "Dispersion is promoted by the dissociation of Swi6/HP1 and cohesin Rad21 from telomeres, whereas disjunction occurs at anaphase after the phosphorylation of condensin subunit Cnd2."]
- Anaphase merotely correction context (Ase1) - rad21 used as cohesion/separation marker. [PMID:19948483 abstract is about Ase1/Prc1 spindle elongation; full text assays rad21 cohesion]

## Biochemistry / ring mechanism (in vitro fission-yeast cohesin)
- Reconstituted fission yeast cohesin (Psm1/Psm3/Rad21/Psc3) topologically loads onto DNA; loading stimulated by Mis4/Ssl3 loader; cohesin binds dsDNA (ATP-independent) and topological loading is ATP-hydrolysis dependent; TEV cleavage of modified Rad21 releases DNA. [PMID:24291789 "We purified the fission yeast cohesin complex after overexpression of its four essential subunits Psm1, Psm3, Rad21 and Psc3 in budding yeast"] [PMID:24291789 "The complex showed ATP-independent dsDNA binding"] [PMID:24291789 "This led to efficient Rad21 cleavage and concomitant loss of DNA from TEV-cleavable cohesin"]
- DNA entry/exit by interlocking gate; Wapl disengages kleisin (Rad21). [PMID:26687354 "the Wapl subunit disengages kleisin, but only after ATP rebinds."] (IPI partner SPAC10F6.09c = wpl1/Wapl)
- DNA-DNA tethering by cohesin ring -> second DNA captured (basis of cohesion/DNA-DNA interactions). [PMID:29358048 "Here, we demonstrate DNA-DNA tethering by the purified fission"]
- Mis4 DNA binding promotes topological entrapment; ATP-dependent topological co-entrapment activity. [PMID:33176147 "promotes subsequent topological DNA entrapment by cohesin"]
- Suppressor/3D modeling: interfaces Psm1-Rad21, Psm3-Rad21; Cut1 dispensable when interfaces destabilized -> hold/release of DNA. [PMID:29735656 "physical and functional impairment at the interfaces of Psm1/3 hinge, Psm1 head-Rad21, or Psm3 coiled coil-Rad21."]
- Cohesin ATPases interface with regulators (Eco1, Wpl1) to modulate DNA tethering (S. cerevisiae study, conserved). [PMID:26583750 "Cohesin tethers together regions of DNA, thereby mediating higher order chromatin organization that is critical for sister chromatid cohesion, DNA repair and transcriptional regulation."]

## Chromatin organization / loop extrusion / localization
- Cohesin extrudes DNA loops (Brownian ratchet model; fission yeast cohesin) -> chromatin looping. [PMID:34309513 "cohesin extrudes DNA loops, thought to reflect chromatin domain formation."]
- Cohesin at pericentric/rDNA heterochromatin, subtelomeric regions; condensin (not cohesin) binds pol II genes — paper notes cohesin sites distinct from condensin. [PMID:25847133 "Accumulation sites for condensin are distinct from those of cohesin and DNA topoisomerase II."]
- Heterochromatin island (facultative): Rad21 IDA localization at heterochromatin islands. [PMID:22144463 — facultative heterochromatin islands at meiotic genes]
- Global localization (ORFeome GFP) — nuclear. [PMID:16823372 proteome-scale localization study]

## Curation summary
Core MF: structural constituent / protein-macromolecule adaptor bridging SMC heads (avoid bare protein binding); topological DNA entrapment activity; dsDNA binding (contributes_to, ring-level).
Core BP: mitotic sister chromatid cohesion (establishment + maintenance), mitotic sister chromatid segregation, DSB repair, chromatin looping.
Core CC: mitotic cohesin complex; nucleus; chromosome/chromatin incl. centromeric/pericentric/subtelomeric/rDNA heterochromatin regions.
