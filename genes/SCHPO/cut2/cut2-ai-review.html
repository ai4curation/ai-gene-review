Gene Description

Cut2 is the securin of the fission yeast Schizosaccharomyces pombe, the ortholog of budding-yeast Pds1 and human PTTG/securin. It is the inhibitory partner and molecular chaperone of the separase Cut1 (Esp1 ortholog), with which it forms the separase-securin (Cut1-Cut2) complex. Cut2 binds the N-terminus of Cut1 through its own C-terminus and is required both to keep separase proteolytically inactive before anaphase and to load Cut1 onto the mitotic spindle at prophase. Cut2 carries two N-terminal destruction boxes (D-box 1 and D-box 2); at the metaphase-anaphase transition the anaphase-promoting complex/cyclosome (APC/C), co-activated by Slp1(Cdc20), polyubiquitinates Cut2 and targets it for proteasomal degradation. This destruction, which occurs by the same machinery that degrades cyclin B, liberates active separase to cleave the cohesin subunit Rad21, triggering sister-chromatid separation and anaphase spindle elongation. Cut2 localizes to the nucleus and concentrates along the short metaphase spindle. The Cut1-Cut2 complex acts analogously in meiosis, where separase cleaves the meiotic cohesin Rec8, and the complex also participates in separase-mediated cohesin cleavage required for DNA repair in interphase. Loss of cut2 function produces the "cut" phenotype, in which cells attempt division without having separated their chromosomes, bisecting the undivided nucleus.

Suggested Questions for Experts

Q: What is the structural basis by which Cut2 inhibits Cut1 separase activity, and does it occlude the active site or act allosterically as suggested by the "covering its active site" model?

Q: Beyond holding separase inactive, how does Cut2 promote separase folding, stability, and spindle loading, and are these chaperone roles separable from its inhibitory role?

Q: How is the DNA-repair function of the securin-separase complex regulated in interphase, and what determines the local, rather than genome-wide, cohesin cleavage proposed for repair?

Suggested Experiments

Experiment: Reconstitute purified Cut1-Cut2 and assay separase endopeptidase activity against Rad21 substrate in vitro, with and without Cut2 and with the non-degradable (D-box mutant) Cut2, to quantify inhibition and its relief by APC-mediated degradation.

Experiment: Determine a cryo-EM or crystal structure of the Cut1-Cut2 complex to define the inhibitory interface and test the active-site occlusion model by structure-guided mutagenesis.

Experiment: Use degron or auxin-inducible depletion of Cut2 combined with live-cell imaging of Rad21 cleavage reporters to dissect the kinetics linking Cut2 destruction, separase activation, and sister-chromatid separation in mitosis versus meiosis.

Experiment: Map the genomic sites and timing of interphase cohesin cleavage after UV damage in wild-type versus cut2(EA2) and protease-dead cut1 cells to test the proposed local-cohesin-removal model for DNA repair.

cut2 (Securin, P21135) — review notes

Fission yeast (S. pombe) cut2 encodes Securin, the ortholog of budding-yeast Pds1
and human PTTG/securin. It is the inhibitory chaperone partner of the separase
Cut1 (Esp1 ortholog). Together they form the separase-securin (separin-securin)
complex (ComplexPortal CPX-2573).

Core biology

  • Cut1 (separase, ~200 kDa) and Cut2 (~42 kDa apparent / 296 aa, 32 kDa calc.)
    associate and co-sediment as large complexes (30S and 40S).
    PMID:8978688
  • Both genes are essential for viability and for sister chromatid separation;
    ts cut2-364 phenocopies cut1 (the "cut" phenotype — nuclear bisection without
    chromosome separation).
    PMID:8978688
  • Cut2 localizes to the nucleus, concentrated along the short metaphase spindle.
    PMID:8632802
    PMID:8978688

Regulated proteolysis (degradation timing central to anaphase)

  • Cut2 is degraded anaphase-specifically by the APC/cyclosome (Cut9 component),
    the same machinery that degrades cyclin B; degradation requires its N-terminal
    region.
    PMID:8632802
  • Non-degradable Cut2 blocks sister-chromatid separation but not cell-cycle
    progression; rescued by grafting cyclin B N-terminus.
    PMID:8632802
  • Cut2 has two N-terminal destruction boxes (D-box 1 at 28-31, D-box 2 at 47-50);
    mutating both abolishes ubiquitination and degradation (UniProt MUTAGEN, PMID:9312055 — not cached).

Cut1 loading / mutual dependence

  • The C-terminus of Cut2 binds the N-terminus of Cut1; Cut2 is required to load
    Cut1 onto the spindle at prophase, and Cut2 proteolysis is required for Cut1's
    active participation in separation.
    PMID:9635190
    PMID:9635190

Mechanism / inhibitor activity

  • Securin, like cyclins, is an APC/cyclosome target, polyubiquitinated before
    destruction; anaphase fails without securin destruction. Separin is inhibited by
    association with securin.
    PMID:10651900
    PMID:10651900
  • Review (Kitajima 2003 intro): separase forms a complex with guardian securin,
    transferred to nucleus and spindle MTs; proteolytic activity inhibited by
    securin until APC degrades securin at metaphase-anaphase.
    PMID:14532136

Meiosis

  • Securin destruction is the trigger for separase activation and Rec8 cleavage
    during meiotic divisions; Cut2 is detected on short spindles at metaphase I/II
    but not at anaphase (consistent with degradation timing).
    PMID:14532136
    UniProt INDUCTION: "Detected along the short spindles at metaphase I and metaphase II, but not at anaphase I, anaphase II..." (PMID:14532136).
  • cut1/cut2 single mutants do not affect sporulation; the double mutant forms
    two-spored asci (genetic role in meiosis).
    PMID:1934126

DNA repair

  • Securin (Cut2) is required for separase stability AND for proper repair of UV/
    X-ray/gamma DNA damage; the securin-separase complex aids repair by removing
    local cohesin at interphase. cut2(EA2) is UV-repair defective despite normal
    checkpoint activation.
    PMID:15329725
    PMID:15329725

Localization (global)

  • High-throughput YFP localization study; Cut2 reported nuclear (PMID:16823372,
    HDA). Genome-wide screen, supports nuclear localization.
