Gene Description

Mad2 is a HORMA-domain protein and a core component of the spindle assembly checkpoint (SAC) in fission yeast. It binds and inhibits the anaphase-promoting complex/cyclosome (APC/C) coactivator Slp1 (Cdc20), and together with Mad3 and Slp1/Cdc20 it forms the mitotic checkpoint complex (MCC). The MCC inhibits the APC/C E3 ubiquitin ligase, blocking ubiquitin-dependent destruction of securin (Cut2) and cyclin B (Cdc13) and thereby delaying the metaphase-to-anaphase transition until all kinetochores achieve proper bipolar spindle attachment. Mad2 adopts open (O-Mad2) and closed (C-Mad2) conformations; a kinetochore-bound Mad1-Mad2 complex templates conversion of O-Mad2 to C-Mad2 that binds Slp1/Cdc20. In the MCC, C-Mad2 stabilizes the complex by positioning the Mad3 KEN box to bind Cdc20, and the MCC inhibits the APC/C by obstructing Cdc20 degron-recognition sites and displacing Cdc20. Mad2 accumulates at unattached kinetochores in early mitosis (dependent on the Mis6 and Nuf2 kinetochore subcomplexes and on Mad1), and during interphase localizes to the nuclear periphery and chromatin in a Mad1-dependent manner. Mad2 is non-essential for viability but is required for the checkpoint response to spindle damage and for preventing meiosis I non-disjunction. Its activity is regulated by the Mph1 (Mps1) kinase and the Ark1 (Aurora) kinase.

Binds and sequesters the APC/C coactivator Slp1 (Cdc20), the key target of the spindle assembly checkpoint, preventing it from activating APC/C-dependent proteolysis.

  Mad2 is a HORMA-domain protein and a core component of the spindle assembly
  checkpoint (SAC) in fission yeast. It binds and inhibits the anaphase-promoting

  Slp1/Cdc20 it forms the mitotic checkpoint complex (MCC). The MCC inhibits the
  APC/C E3 ubiquitin ligase, blocking ubiquitin-dependent destruction of securin
  (Cut2) and cyclin B (Cdc13) and thereby delaying the metaphase-to-anaphase
  transition until all kinetochores achieve proper bipolar spindle attachment.
  Mad2 adopts open (O-Mad2) and closed (C-Mad2) conformations; a kinetochore-bound
  Mad1-Mad2 complex templates conversion of O-Mad2 to C-Mad2 that binds Slp1/Cdc20.
  In the MCC, C-Mad2 stabilizes the complex by positioning the Mad3 KEN box to bind
  Cdc20, and the MCC inhibits the APC/C by obstructing Cdc20 degron-recognition
  sites and displacing Cdc20. Mad2 accumulates at unattached kinetochores in early
  mitosis (dependent on the Mis6 and Nuf2 kinetochore subcomplexes and on Mad1),
  and during interphase localizes to the nuclear periphery and chromatin in a
  Mad1-dependent manner. Mad2 is non-essential for viability but is required for
  the checkpoint response to spindle damage and for preventing meiosis I
  non-disjunction. Its activity is regulated by the Mph1 (Mps1) kinase and the
  Ark1 (Aurora) kinase.
