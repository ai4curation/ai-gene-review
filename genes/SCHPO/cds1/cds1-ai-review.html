Gene Description

Cds1 is the effector serine/threonine protein kinase of the DNA replication (intra-S phase) checkpoint of the fission yeast Schizosaccharomyces pombe, and is the ortholog of human CHK2 and budding-yeast Rad53 (CHEK2 subfamily). The 460-residue protein comprises an N-terminal SQ/TQ regulatory motif and a forkhead-associated (FHA) domain followed by a C-terminal protein kinase domain and a short C-terminal autoinhibitory tail. Cds1 is activated specifically in S phase when replication forks stall (for example upon nucleotide depletion by hydroxyurea, or when forks encounter DNA damage): the apical kinase Rad3 (ATR), working through the mediator Mrc1 (Claspin), phosphorylates Cds1 at Thr11, which primes FHA-dependent dimerization and autophosphorylation of the activation-loop residue Thr328, yielding the active kinase. Active Cds1 enforces the S/M checkpoint by phosphorylating Cdc25 (creating 14-3-3/Rad24 binding sites and excluding it from the nucleus) and by activating Wee1/Mik1, thereby keeping Cdc2 (CDK1) inhibited and blocking mitosis until replication is complete. Cds1 also phosphorylates targets that stabilize and protect stalled forks and govern the replication-stress response, including Dna2, Mus81, the DDK kinase Hsk1/Dfp1, Rad60, Mcm4, the MBF repressor Nrm1, and Rgf1. A Cds1-dependent replication checkpoint also operates during meiosis, where Cds1 plays an even more prominent role than in the mitotic cycle. Cds1 acts in the nucleus at chromatin/stalled forks. It is distinct in function from Chk1, the G2 DNA-damage checkpoint effector kinase.

cds1 (S. pombe) — gene review notes

UniProt: Q09170 (RAD53_SCHPO). Gene: cds1 / SPCC18B5.11c. 460 aa Ser/Thr kinase, EC 2.7.11.1.
Ortholog of human CHK2 and budding-yeast Rad53 (CHEK2 subfamily). Domain architecture:
N-terminal FHA domain (res ~60-116) + C-terminal protein kinase domain (res ~167-433);
SQ/TQ regulatory motif near the N-terminus (T11Q12). Active-site proton acceptor Asp294.

Core identity / function

Cds1 is the effector Ser/Thr protein kinase of the DNA replication (intra-S) checkpoint
in fission yeast. It is the major effector of the replication checkpoint, activated
specifically in S phase when replication forks stall (e.g. hydroxyurea/HU, nucleotide
depletion, or replication encountering DNA damage). It is distinct from Chk1, the G2 DNA-damage
checkpoint effector kinase.

  • Original discovery as a multicopy suppressor of a DNA pol-alpha ts mutant; "checking DNA synthesis";
    blocks mitosis in S phase: PMID:7723827
  • Cds1 enforces the S-M checkpoint coupling mitosis to completion of S phase, and controls
    replicational stress tolerance: PMID:11313465
  • Major effector of replication checkpoint: PMID:16618806
  • Ortholog of Chk2: PMID:19357077

Activation mechanism (MF = protein Ser/Thr kinase; activation regulation)

Two-stage activation: priming by Rad3(ATR) via mediator Mrc1, then FHA-dependent dimerization
and autophosphorylation.
- T11 phosphorylated by Rad3/ATM required for activation: PMID:11313465
- Mrc1 recruits Cds1 via FHA-phospho-Mrc1, then dimerization/autophosphorylation: PMID:16618806
- Autoactivation requires autophosphorylation of activation-loop Thr328; C-terminal tail autoinhibits: PMID:19357077
- Interacts with Rad26 (Rad3 partner); autophosphorylated (UniProt; PMID:9450932, PMID:18257517).

Direct substrates / downstream targets

  • Cdc25 phosphatase — Cds1 (or Chk1) phosphorylates Cdc25 on S99/S192/S359, creating 14-3-3 binding sites, keeping Cdc2 inhibited: PMID:9774107
  • Hsk1 (Cdc7 ortholog) — Cds1-dependent phosphorylation in vivo and direct substrate in vitro: PMID:11027263
  • Dna2 — Cds1 phosphorylates Dna2-S220 to prevent stalled fork reversal: PMID:22682245
  • Nrm1 (MBF repressor) — Cds1 phosphorylates Nrm1, relieving MBF repression to drive G1-S transcription of replication/repair genes: PMID:18682565 (This is the PMID:18682565 IPI "protein binding" annotation partner = Nrm1/O42913; also the IntAct nrm1 interaction.)
  • Mcm4 — binds Cds1 and is Cds1-dependently phosphorylated upon checkpoint activation: PMID:18180284 (PMID:18180284 IPI partner SPCC16A11.17 = mcm4/cdc21.)
  • Rgf1 (Rho1 GEF) — Cds1- and Rad24-dependent nuclear accumulation under chronic replication stress: PMID:24478458
  • Downstream Mus81 / Rqh1 / Rhp51 pathway for S-phase damage slowing: PMID:19037101
  • UniProt also lists Rad60 as a Cds1 phosphorylation target (PMID:12897162).

Biological processes

  • Mitotic DNA replication checkpoint signaling (GO:0033314): PMID:11313465 (S-M checkpoint).
  • Mitotic intra-S DNA damage checkpoint signaling (GO:0031573): replication slowing on damage:
    PMID:19037101
    PMID:12032307
    Original IGI (with hus1/SPAC3H5.06c) blocking mitosis in S: PMID:7723827.

    precise GO term "replication fork protection" (GO:0048478) is OBSOLETE; GO:0031297 (replication
