CC   -!- FUNCTION: Leghemoglobin that reversibly binds oxygen O(2) through a
CC       pentacoordinated heme iron (By similarity). In root nodules,
CC       facilitates the diffusion of oxygen to the bacteroids while preventing
CC       the bacterial nitrogenase from being inactivated by buffering dioxygen,
CC       nitric oxide and carbon monoxide, and promoting the formation of
CC       reactive oxygen species (ROS, e.g. H(2)O(2)) (By similarity). This role
CC       is essential for symbiotic nitrogen fixation (SNF) (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P02237, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q43296}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Monomer. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P02240}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cytoplasm, cytosol
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P02240}. Nucleus {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P02240}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Root nodules. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:809270}.
CC   -!- PTM: Nitrated mainly at Tyr-31 and, to a lower extent, at Tyr-26 and
CC       Tyr-134, in effective nodules and particularly in hypoxic conditions;
CC       this mechanism may play a protective role in the symbiosis by buffering
CC       toxic peroxynitrite NO(2)(-) (PubMed:25603991). Nitration level
CC       decrease during nodule senescence (PubMed:25603991).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:25603991}.
CC   -!- PTM: Phosphorylation at Ser-46 disrupts the molecular environment of
CC       its porphyrin ring oxygen binding pocket, thus leading to a reduced
CC       oxygen consumption and to the delivery of oxygen O(2) to symbiosomes.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q3C1F7}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the plant globin family. {ECO:0000305}.
