Gene Description

AJ80_06654 encodes a predicted copper-containing nitrite reductase in the fungus Polytolypa hystricis UAMH7299. The protein belongs to the multicopper oxidase/cupredoxin nitrite reductase family and is predicted to catalyze NO-forming nitrite reduction.

Evidence synthesis

AJ80_06654 is predicted as a copper-containing nitrite reductase with EC 1.7.2.1 [file:POLH7/AJ80_06654/AJ80_06654-uniprot.txt "RecName: Full=Copper-containing nitrite reductase; EC=1.7.2.1"]. The protein carries NO2-reductase_Cu/cupredoxin multicopper-oxidase signatures, supporting a conserved-function inference for NO-forming nitrite reductase activity [file:POLH7/AJ80_06654/AJ80_06654-uniprot.txt "InterPro; IPR001287; NO2-reductase_Cu"].

The pathway evidence is weaker than the molecular-function evidence. UniProt maps the protein to denitrification step 2 of 4, but this is a singleton fungal UniPathway row and the local evidence does not establish complete denitrification in Polytolypa hystricis [file:POLH7/AJ80_06654/AJ80_06654-uniprot.txt "PATHWAY: Nitrogen metabolism; nitrate reduction (denitrification); dinitrogen from nitrate: step 2/4"]. The PTHR11709 family report reinforces caution because multicopper oxidases are substrate- and lineage-diverse [file:interpro/panther/PTHR11709/PTHR11709-deep-research-falcon.md "multicopper oxidases are functionally diverse"].

Curation consequence

Accept the predicted NO-forming nitrite reductase molecular function. Leave denitrification pathway undecided until organism-specific pathway evidence is available.
