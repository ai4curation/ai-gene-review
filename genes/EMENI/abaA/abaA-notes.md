# abaA (Aspergillus nidulans) — curation notes

UniProt: P20945 (ABAA_EMENI). TEA/ATTS-domain transcription factor; middle
regulator of the brlA -> abaA -> wetA conidiation cascade. Member of the
`conidiation_regulatory_cascade` module.

## Core biology (with provenance)

- **ATTS/TEA-domain sequence-specific DNA-binding transcriptional activator.**
  AbaA binds 5'-CATTCY-3' (Y = pyrimidine) via major- and minor-groove contacts.
  [PMID:8139553 "The Aspergillus nidulans abaA gene encodes a protein containing an ATTS DNA-binding motif and is required for the terminal stages of conidiophore development"].
- **Regulates the developmental gene network**, including target structural genes
  and the regulators brlA, wetA and abaA itself.
  [PMID:8139553 "Multiple AbaA binding sites are present in the cis-acting regulatory regions of several developmentally controlled structural genes as well as those of the upstream regulatory gene brlA, the downstream regulatory gene wetA, and abaA itself"].
- **Controls phialide differentiation.** abaA-null strains form aberrant
  conidiophores that make supernumerary metula-like cells instead of phialides
  and fail to produce conidia.
  [PMID:2152124 "Loss of function mutations in the abacus A (abaA) regulatory locus result in formation of aberrant conidiophores that fail to produce conidia"];
  [PMID:2152124 "Phialides are sporogenous cells that produce conidia through a specialized apical budding process"].
- **Middle tier of the dependent pathway**, activated by BrlA and activating WetA.
  [PMID:2655931]; cross-species: [PMID:20966095 "The AfuabaA gene is predicted to encode an ATTS/TEA DNA-binding domain protein and is activated by AfuBrlA during the middle stage"].
