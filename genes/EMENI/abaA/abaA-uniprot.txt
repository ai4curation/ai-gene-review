CC   -!- FUNCTION: BrlA, abaA and wetA are pivotal regulators of conidiophore
CC       development and conidium maturation (PubMed:2196567, PubMed:2655931).
CC       They act individually and together to regulate their own expression and
CC       that of numerous other sporulation-specific gene (PubMed:2655931,
CC       PubMed:2823119). Controls temporal and spatial specificity in
CC       Aspergillus development (PubMed:2655931, PubMed:7704830). Directs the
CC       differentiation of phialides and is continuously required for
CC       maintenance of their function (PubMed:2152124). Expression of abaA
CC       leads to activation of brlA and wetA, cessation of vegetative growth,
CC       and accentuated cellular vacuolization (PubMed:2655931). Binds to the
CC       sequence 5'-CATTCY-3', where Y is a pyrimidine, making both major- and
CC       minor-groove contacts (PubMed:8139553). Multiple abaA binding sites are
CC       present in the cis-acting regulatory regions of several developmentally
CC       controlled structural genes as well as those of the upstream regulatory
CC       gene brlA, the downstream regulatory gene wetA, and abaA itself
CC       (PubMed:8139553). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:2152124,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:2196567, ECO:0000269|PubMed:2655931,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:2823119, ECO:0000269|PubMed:7704830,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:8139553}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Nucleus {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:I1S4T3}.
CC       Note=localizes to the nuclei of phialides and terminal cells of mature
CC       conidia (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:I1S4T3}.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: Expression is induced during conidiation, after conidiophore
CC       vesicles, metullae, and phialides had formed (PubMed:2823119).
CC       Negatively regulated by velC (PubMed:24587098). In the promoter, a 45-
CC       base-pair region encompassing the major and minor abaA transcription
CC       initiation sites contains directly repeated sequences related to the
CC       mammalian initiator (Inr) element and is sufficient for correct
CC       transcription initiation and for developmental induction
CC       (PubMed:2117702). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:2117702,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:24587098, ECO:0000269|PubMed:2823119}.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: Prevents the formation of normal conidiophores
CC       (PubMed:2152124, PubMed:2823119). Fails to form any viable conidia,
CC       even though it does produce morphologically normal phialides
CC       (PubMed:2655931). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:2152124,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:2655931, ECO:0000269|PubMed:2823119}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the TEC1 family. {ECO:0000305}.
