CC   -!- FUNCTION: BrlA, abaA and wetA are pivotal regulators of conidiophore
CC       development and conidium maturation. They act individually and together
CC       to regulate their own expression and that of numerous other
CC       sporulation-specific genes (PubMed:2655931). Responsible for activating
CC       a set of genes whose products make up the final two conidial wall
CC       layers or direct their assembly and, through this activity, is
CC       responsible for acquisition of spore dormancy (PubMed:1986246,
CC       PubMed:2180753, PubMed:7704830). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:1986246,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:2180753, ECO:0000269|PubMed:2655931,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:7704830}.
CC   -!- DEVELOPMENTAL STAGE: The wetA gene is activated only during
CC       conidiophore development, and its mRNA accumulates preferentially in
CC       mature conidia.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: Expression is induced during conidiation, after conidia had
CC       begun to form (PubMed:2823119). Positively regulated by abaA
CC       (PubMed:2655931). Negatively regulated by velC (PubMed:24587098).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:24587098, ECO:0000269|PubMed:2655931,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:2823119}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: Has an acidic N-terminus (AA 1-52) followed by a Ser-, Thr-,
CC       Pro-rich domain (AA 125-233) and a basic C-terminus (AA 461-555).
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: Prevents the formation of normal conidiophores
CC       (PubMed:2823119). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:2823119}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the wetA family. {ECO:0000305}.
