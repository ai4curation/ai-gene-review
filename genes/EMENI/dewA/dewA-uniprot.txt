CC   -!- FUNCTION: Aerial growth, conidiation, and dispersal of filamentous
CC       fungi in the environment rely upon a capability of their secreting
CC       small amphipathic proteins called hydrophobins (HPBs) with low sequence
CC       identity. Class I can self-assemble into an outermost layer of rodlet
CC       bundles on aerial cell surfaces, conferring cellular hydrophobicity
CC       that supports fungal growth, development and dispersal; whereas Class
CC       II form highly ordered films at water-air interfaces through
CC       intermolecular interactions but contribute nothing to the rodlet
CC       structure (Probable). DewA is a class I hydrophobin that contributes to
CC       spore wall hydrophobicity (PubMed:12790678, PubMed:22154865,
CC       PubMed:23137797, PubMed:24722460, PubMed:7651135).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12790678, ECO:0000269|PubMed:22154865,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:23137797, ECO:0000269|PubMed:24722460,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:7651135, ECO:0000305}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Forms homodimers at high concentrations, and these dimers are
CC       off-pathway to rodlet formation (PubMed:23137797). Dissociation of the
CC       dimers into monomers, with resultant exposure of the hydrophobic face,
CC       is necessary for self-assembly to form functional amyloid fibrils
CC       called rodlets. Self-assembly into fibrillar rodlets occurs
CC       spontaneously at hydrophobic:hydrophilic interfaces and the rodlets
CC       further associate laterally to form amphipathic monolayers
CC       (PubMed:23137797, PubMed:24722460). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:23137797,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:24722460}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Secreted {ECO:0000269|PubMed:20567818,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:24722460, ECO:0000269|PubMed:7651135}. Spore wall
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:24722460, ECO:0000269|PubMed:7651135}.
CC   -!- DEVELOPMENTAL STAGE: Accumulates preferentially during asexual
CC       sporulation. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:24722460, ECO:0000269|PubMed:7651135}.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: Expressed after 12 to 24 hours post induction of asexual
CC       development, which correlates with the development of metulae and
CC       phialides (PubMed:24722460). Not expressed in hyphae and strongly
CC       induced during development (PubMed:24722460).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:24722460}.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: Lowers the spore wall hydrophobicity.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:7651135}.
CC   -!- BIOTECHNOLOGY: Hydrophobins such as dewA can be functionalized for use
CC       on orthopaedic implant surfaces by producing surfaces enhancing the
CC       adhesion of human cells such as mesenchymal stem cells (MSCs),
CC       osteoblasts, fibroblasts and chondrocytes. Improved cell adhesion
CC       potential of human cells on these surfaces is not coupled with enhanced
CC       bacterial adhesion and therefore does not introduce increased risk of
CC       bacterial infection. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:22154865}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the fungal hydrophobin family. {ECO:0000305}.
