CC   -!- FUNCTION: Component of the velvet transcription factor complex that
CC       controls sexual/asexual developmental ratio in response to light,
CC       promoting sexual development in the darkness while stimulating asexual
CC       sporulation under illumination (PubMed:12223191, PubMed:14665453,
CC       PubMed:18556559, PubMed:19210625, PubMed:2076818, PubMed:2253875,
CC       PubMed:40523015). The velvet complex acts as a global regulator for
CC       secondary metabolite gene expression (PubMed:18556559,
CC       PubMed:40523015). Controls the expression of the sterigmatocystin and
CC       penicillin gene clusters (PubMed:14665453, PubMed:17375284,
CC       PubMed:40523015). Represses the cryptic ors gene cluster producing
CC       orsellinic acid and its F9775 derivatives in a laeA-independent manner
CC       (PubMed:23841751). Required for full induction of faoA gene expression
CC       by fructosyl amines (PubMed:12375102). Positively regulates the
CC       expression of the early sexual development gene esdC (PubMed:17977758).
CC       Controls the expression of mannoprotein mnpA (PubMed:12620259).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12223191, ECO:0000269|PubMed:12375102,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:12620259, ECO:0000269|PubMed:14665453,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:17977758, ECO:0000269|PubMed:18556559,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:19210625, ECO:0000269|PubMed:2076818,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:2253875, ECO:0000269|PubMed:23841751,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:41219003}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Component of the heterotrimeric velvet complex composed of
CC       laeA, veA and velB; VeA acting as a bridging protein between laeA and
CC       velB (PubMed:18556559). Forms an heterodmeric complex with velB
CC       (PubMed:41219003). Interacts with the light-sensing phytochrome fphA
CC       (PubMed:18291652, PubMed:18936976). Interacts with llmF
CC       (PubMed:23341778). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:18291652,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:18556559, ECO:0000269|PubMed:18936976,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:23341778, ECO:0000269|PubMed:41219003}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Nucleus {ECO:0000269|PubMed:17163983,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:18556559, ECO:0000269|PubMed:19318129,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:20816830, ECO:0000269|PubMed:23341778,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:40523015}. Cytoplasm {ECO:0000269|PubMed:17163983,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:18556559, ECO:0000269|PubMed:19318129,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:20816830, ECO:0000269|PubMed:23341778,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:40523015}. Note=Shuttles from cytoplasm into nucleus
CC       during vegetative growth and stays there for sexual development, but
CC       has to return into cytoplasm for asexual development (PubMed:40523015).
CC       Enriched in the nucleus in the dark (PubMed:17163983, PubMed:18556559,
CC       PubMed:20816830). Migration to the nucleus depends on the importin
CC       alpha carrier protein KapA (PubMed:17163983, PubMed:19318129).
CC       Migration to the nucleus is also controlled by llmF (PubMed:23341778).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:17163983, ECO:0000269|PubMed:18556559,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:19318129, ECO:0000269|PubMed:23341778,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:40523015}.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: Expressed almost constitutively at an increased level
CC       throughout the asexual and sexual developmental processes
CC       (PubMed:12223191). Negatively regulates its own expression
CC       (PubMed:19479257). Expression is derepressed in light-illuminated
CC       conditions (PubMed:19479257). Expression is regulated by laeA
CC       (PubMed:21152013). Regulates its own expression, depending on its
CC       phosphorylation state (PubMed:26564476). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12223191,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:19479257, ECO:0000269|PubMed:21152013,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:26564476}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The C-terminal PEST domain is a region rich in proline,
CC       glutamic acid, serine and threonine residues that is required for the
CC       light-dependent regulation of development and secondary metabolism.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:26564476}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The 3 nuclear localization signals NLS1-3 cooperate in veA
CC       nuclear import and are required to exclude the protein from the nucleus
CC       in light when asexual development is promoted.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:40523015}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The nuclear export signal (NES) is required to exclude the
CC       protein from the nucleus in light when asexual development is promoted.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:40523015}.
CC   -!- PTM: Phosphorylated at Thr-167, Thr-170, Ser-183 and Tyr-254
CC       (PubMed:26564476). Thr-167 should be phosphorylated and T170 and S183
CC       should be dephosphorylated to achieve light induction of conidiation

CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:26564476}.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: Aboshes light-dependent conidiation, allowing

CC       transcription factors nsdD, steA and brlA, the penicillin biosynthetic
CC       genes ipnA and acvA, as well as of the sterigmatocystin-specific
CC       regulatory gene aflR (PubMed:14665453). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12223191,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:14665453, ECO:0000269|PubMed:2076818}.
