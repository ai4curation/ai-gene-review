CC   -!- FUNCTION: Aerial growth, conidiation, and dispersal of filamentous
CC       fungi in the environment rely upon a capability of their secreting
CC       small amphipathic proteins called hydrophobins (HPBs) with low sequence
CC       identity. Class I can self-assemble into an outermost layer of rodlet
CC       bundles on aerial cell surfaces, conferring cellular hydrophobicity
CC       that supports fungal growth, development and dispersal; whereas Class
CC       II form highly ordered films at water-air interfaces through
CC       intermolecular interactions but contribute nothing to the rodlet
CC       structure (Probable). RodA is a class I hydrophobin that contributes to
CC       surface hydrophobicity, which is important for processes such as
CC       association of hyphae in reproductive structures, dispersal of aerial
CC       spores and adhesion of pathogens to host structures (PubMed:10664979,
CC       PubMed:2065971, PubMed:24722460). Important for the formation of
CC       hydrophobic rodlet layers of asexually-produced spores
CC       (PubMed:10664979, PubMed:2065971). Promotes also biofilm formation and
CC       may enhance lignocellulose utilization via promoting a compact
CC       substrate-enzyme-fungus structure (PubMed:27437031).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10664979, ECO:0000269|PubMed:2065971,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:24722460, ECO:0000269|PubMed:27437031, ECO:0000305}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Self-assembles to form functional amyloid fibrils called
CC       rodlets. Self-assembly into fibrillar rodlets occurs spontaneously at
CC       hydrophobic:hydrophilic interfaces and the rodlets further associate
CC       laterally to form amphipathic monolayers.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q04571}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Secreted {ECO:0000269|PubMed:2065971}. Spore wall
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:2065971}.
CC   -!- DEVELOPMENTAL STAGE: Accumulates at about the time sterigmata appear
CC       and remains at high levels throughout the final stages of conidiophore
CC       formation and during spore differentiation and maturation.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:2065971}.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: Expression is induced by the transcription factor brlA.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:2065971}.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: Impairs the production of thin, proteinaceous,
CC       outer spore-wall layer, called the rodlet layer (PubMed:2065971).
CC       Reduces biofilm formation and the retention of the hydrolytic enzymes
CC       within the vicinity of the fungus (PubMed:27437031).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:2065971, ECO:0000269|PubMed:27437031}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the fungal hydrophobin family. {ECO:0000305}.
