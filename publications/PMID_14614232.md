# A missense mutation (GGC[435Gly]-->AGC[Ser]) in exon 8 of the CYP11B2 gene inherited in Japanese patients with congenital hypoaldosteronism.
**Authors:** Kuribayashi I, Kuge H, Santa RJ, Mutlaq AZ, Yamasaki N, Furuno T, Takahashi A, Chida S, Nakamura T, Endo F, Doi Y, Onishi S, Shizuta Y
**Journal:** Horm Res (2003)
**DOI:** [10.1159/000074041](https://doi.org/10.1159/000074041)

## Abstract

1. Horm Res. 2003;60(5):255-60. doi: 10.1159/000074041.

A missense mutation (GGC[435Gly]-->AGC[Ser]) in exon 8 of the CYP11B2 gene 
inherited in Japanese patients with congenital hypoaldosteronism.

Kuribayashi I(1), Kuge H, Santa RJ, Mutlaq AZ, Yamasaki N, Furuno T, Takahashi 
A, Chida S, Nakamura T, Endo F, Doi Y, Onishi S, Shizuta Y.

Author information:
(1)Department of Medical Chemistry, Kochi Medical School, Kochi, Japan. 
kuribaya@kochi-ms.ac.jp

OBJECTIVES: To clarify the underlying molecular mechanism of corticosterone 
methyl oxidase type II (CMO II) deficiency, Japanese patients newly diagnosed 
with CMO II deficiency were investigated.
METHODS: We analyzed the patients' genomic DNA sequence on all 9 exons of the 
CYP11B2 gene. In addition, restriction fragment length polymorphism (RFLP) 
analysis and expression studies were performed.
RESULTS: The analysis showed that the patients homozygously retained a missense 
mutation, Gumacr;GC[435Gly]-->Aumacr;GC[Ser], in the CYP11B2 gene. Expression 
studies indicated that the steroid 18-hydroxylase/oxidase activities of the 
mutant enzyme were substantially reduced.
CONCLUSION: These results support the hypothesis that this mutation causes CMO 
II deficiency in the patients, and are in accordance with our theory that the 
partial loss of P-450(C18) activities causes CMO II deficiency.
