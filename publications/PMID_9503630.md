# Expression of the mphB gene for macrolide 2'-phosphotransferase II from Escherichia coli in Staphylococcus aureus.
**Authors:** Noguchi N, Tamura Y, Katayama J, Narui K
**Journal:** FEMS Microbiol Lett (1998)
**DOI:** [10.1111/j.1574-6968.1998.tb12880.x](https://doi.org/10.1111/j.1574-6968.1998.tb12880.x)

## Abstract

1. FEMS Microbiol Lett. 1998 Feb 15;159(2):337-42. doi: 
10.1111/j.1574-6968.1998.tb12880.x.

Expression of the mphB gene for macrolide 2'-phosphotransferase II from 
Escherichia coli in Staphylococcus aureus.

Noguchi N(1), Tamura Y, Katayama J, Narui K.

Author information:
(1)Department of Microbiology, School of Pharmacy, Tokyo University of Pharmacy 
and Life Science, Japan. noguchin@ps.toyaku.ac.jp

The genes mphA and mphB encode macrolide 2'-phosphotransferases I and II, 
respectively, and they confer resistance to macrolide antibiotics in Escherichia 
coli. To study the expression of these genes in Gram-positive bacteria, we 
constructed recombinant plasmids that consisted of an mph gene and the pUB110 
vector in Bacillus subtilis. When these plasmids were introduced into 
Staphylococcus aureus, the mphB gene was active and macrolide 
2'-phosphotransferase II was produced. The gene endowed S. aureus with 
high-level resistance to spiramycin, a macrolide antibiotic with a 16-membered 
ring. Moreover, transcription of the mphB gene in S. aureus began at the 
promoter that was active in E. coli.
