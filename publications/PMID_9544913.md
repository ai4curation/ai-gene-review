# 3-Methylcrotonyl-coenzyme A carboxylase deficiency in Amish/Mennonite adults identified by detection of increased acylcarnitines in blood spots of their children.
**Authors:** Gibson KM, Bennett MJ, Naylor EW, Morton DH
**Journal:** J Pediatr (1998)
**DOI:** [10.1016/s0022-3476(98)70032-0](https://doi.org/10.1016/s0022-3476(98)70032-0)

## Abstract

1. J Pediatr. 1998 Mar;132(3 Pt 1):519-23. doi: 10.1016/s0022-3476(98)70032-0.

3-Methylcrotonyl-coenzyme A carboxylase deficiency in Amish/Mennonite adults 
identified by detection of increased acylcarnitines in blood spots of their 
children.

Gibson KM(1), Bennett MJ, Naylor EW, Morton DH.

Author information:
(1)Institute of Metabolic Disease, Baylor University Medical Center, Dallas, 
Texas, USA.

Isolated 3-methylcrotonyl coenzyme A carboxylase (MCC) deficiency was documented 
in four adult women from the Amish/Mennonite population of Lancaster County, 
Pennsylvania. Metabolic and enzymatic investigations in these individuals were 
instituted after the detection of abnormal acylcarnitine profiles in blood spots 
obtained from their newborn children, in whom MCC activity was normal.
