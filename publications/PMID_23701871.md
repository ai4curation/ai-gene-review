# Parallel quantification of lectin-glycan interaction using ultrafiltration.
**Authors:** Takeda Y, Seko A, Sakono M, Hachisu M, Koizumi A, Fujikawa K, Ito Y

**DOI:** [10.1016/j.carres.2013.04.032](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.carres.2013.04.032)

## Abstract

1. Carbohydr Res. 2013 Jun 28;375:112-7. doi: 10.1016/j.carres.2013.04.032. Epub

Parallel quantification of lectin-glycan interaction using ultrafiltration.

Takeda Y(1), Seko A, Sakono M, Hachisu M, Koizumi A, Fujikawa K, Ito Y.

Author information:
(1)Japan Science and Technology Agency (JST), ERATO, Ito Glycotrilogy Project,

Using ultrafiltration membrane, a simple method for screening protein-ligand 
interaction was developed. The procedure comprises three steps: mixing ligand 
with protein, ultrafiltration of the solution, and quantification of unbound 
ligands by HPLC. By conducting analysis with variable protein concentrations, 
affinity constants were easily obtained. Multiple ligands can be analyzed 
simultaneously as a mixture, when concentration of ligands was controlled. 
Feasibility of this method for lectin-glycan interaction analysis was examined 
using fluorescently labeled high-mannose-type glycans and recombinant 
intracellular lectins or endo-α-mannosidase mutants. Estimated Ka values of 
malectin and VIP36 were in good agreement indeed with those evaluated by 
conventional methods such as isothermal titration calorimetry (ITC) or frontal 
affinity chromatography (FAC). Finally, several mutants of endo-α-mannosidase 
were produced and their affinities to monoglucosylated glycans were evaluated.
