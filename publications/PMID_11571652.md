# The de-ubiquitinating enzyme Unp interacts with the retinoblastoma protein.
**Authors:** DeSalle LM, Latres E, Lin D, Graner E, Montagnoli A, Baker RT, Pagano M, Loda M
**Journal:** Oncogene (2001)
**DOI:** [10.1038/sj.onc.1204824](https://doi.org/10.1038/sj.onc.1204824)

## Abstract

1. Oncogene. 2001 Sep 6;20(39):5538-42. doi: 10.1038/sj.onc.1204824.

The de-ubiquitinating enzyme Unp interacts with the retinoblastoma protein.

DeSalle LM(1), Latres E, Lin D, Graner E, Montagnoli A, Baker RT, Pagano M, Loda 
M.

Author information:
(1)Department of Pathology and Kaplan Comprehensive Cancer Center, MSB 548, New

The ubiquitin pathway is involved in the proteolytic turnover of many 
short-lived cellular regulatory proteins. Since selective degradation of 
substrates of this system requires the covalent attachment of a polyubiquitin 
chain to the substrates, degradation could be counteracted by de-ubiquitinating 
enzymes (or isopeptidases) which selectively remove the polyubiquitin chain. Unp 
is a human isopeptidase with still poorly understood biological functions. Here, 
we show that cellular Unp specifically interacts with the retinoblastoma gene 
product (pRb).
