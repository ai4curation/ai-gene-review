Abstract

Superoxide dismutase (SOD) is an essential and ubiquitous antioxidant protein that is widely present in biological systems. The anhydrobiotic tardigrades are some of the toughest micro-animals. They have an expanded set of genes for antioxidant proteins such as SODs. These proteins are thought to play an essential role in oxidative stress resistance in critical situations such as desiccation, although their functions at the molecular level have yet to be explored. Here, crystal structures of a copper/zinc-containing SOD ( Rv SOD15) from an anhydrobiotic tardigrade, Ramazzottius varieornatus strain YOKOZUNA-1, are reported. In Rv SOD15, one of the histidine ligands of the catalytic copper center is replaced by a valine (Val87). The crystal structures of the wild type and the V87H mutant show that even though a histidine is placed at position 87, a nearby flexible loop can destabilize the coordination of His87 to the Cu atom. Model structures of other Rv SODs were investigated and it was found that some of them are also unusual SODs, with features such as deletion of the electrostatic loop or β3 sheet and unusual metal-binding residues. These studies show that Rv SOD15 and some other Rv SODs may have evolved to lose the SOD function, suggesting that gene duplications of antioxidant proteins do not solely explain the high stress tolerance of anhydrobiotic tardigrades.

1. Introduction

Tardigrades are microscopic animals which live in wet terrestrial and aquatic environments. Some terrestrial tardigrades such as Ramazzottius varieornatus (Horikawa et al. , 2008 ▸ ) and Hypsibius exemplaris (Goldstein, 2018 ▸ ; previously named H. dujardini ; Gąsiorek et al. , 2018 ▸ ) have recently been studied as potential model organisms because of their ability to survive desiccated conditions through anhydrobiosis, in which they lose their body water to form a ‘tun’ structure and stop their metabolism. They also show a wide range of tolerances to extreme environments in the anhydrobiotic state (Horikawa, 2008 ▸ ). Under anhydrobiotic conditions, tardigrade cells experience severe oxidative stress (Giovannini et al. , 2022 ▸ ); therefore, tardigrades are thought to have potent systems to efficiently eliminate reactive oxygen species (ROS).

Superoxide dismutase (SOD) is one of the well known antioxidative enzymes (McCord & Fridovich, 1969 ▸ ; Bafana et al. , 2011 ▸ ; Fridovich, 1975 ▸ ). It converts the reactive superoxide to dioxygen and hydrogen peroxide ( + 2H + → O 2 + H 2 O 2 ). SODs are classified into several classes based on their catalytic metal cofactors. Among them, copper/zinc-containing SODs (CuZnSODs; Tainer et al. , 1983 ▸ ) are widely distributed from prokaryotes to eukaryotes. This enzyme is so essential to living systems that some intractable human diseases are related to dysfunction and misfolding of CuZnSODs (Andersen et al. , 2003 ▸ ; Trist et al. , 2021 ▸ ; Abu-Hamad et al. , 2017 ▸ ; Coque et al. , 2019 ▸ ; Brasil et al. , 2019 ▸ ). Previous genomic studies have suggested that multiple genes encoding antioxidant enzymes such as SODs are the strategy used by tardigrades to endure oxidative damage in harsh environments (Hashimoto et al. , 2016 ▸ ; Yoshida et al. , 2017 ▸ ; Murai et al. , 2021 ▸ ). The expression and activity of SODs from anhydro­biotic tardigrades are known to be upregulated under dried conditions (Yoshida et al. , 2017 ▸ ; Hashimoto et al. , 2016 ▸ ; Mali et al. , 2010 ▸ ; Rizzo et al. , 2010 ▸ ; Kamilari et al. , 2019 ▸ ). However, some tardigrade CuZnSOD genes have truncated sequences or mutations of catalytically important residues, which raises questions regarding the antioxidant roles of these proteins. Here, we performed structural and functional characterization of one such unusual CuZnSOD from R. varieornatus , Rv SOD15.
