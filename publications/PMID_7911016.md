# Molecular cloning and nucleotide sequence of complementary DNA for human hepatic cytosolic acetoacetyl-coenzyme A thiolase.
**Authors:** Song XQ, Fukao T, Yamaguchi S, Miyazawa S, Hashimoto T, Orii T
**Journal:** Biochem Biophys Res Commun (1994)
**DOI:** [10.1006/bbrc.1994.1726](https://doi.org/10.1006/bbrc.1994.1726)

## Abstract

1. Biochem Biophys Res Commun. 1994 May 30;201(1):478-85. doi: 
10.1006/bbrc.1994.1726.

Molecular cloning and nucleotide sequence of complementary DNA for human hepatic 
cytosolic acetoacetyl-coenzyme A thiolase.

Song XQ(1), Fukao T, Yamaguchi S, Miyazawa S, Hashimoto T, Orii T.

Author information:
(1)Department of Pediatrics, Gifu University School of Medicine, Japan.

Complementary DNA for human cytosolic acetoacetyl-CoA thiolase (CT) was cloned 
with the use of anti-[human CT] antibody. The human CT cDNA clone (HCT10) has a 
1479-bp insert and a 1191-base open reading frame encoding 397 amino acid 
residues. Partial polypeptide sequences from purified human CT were present in 
the deduced sequence. In vivo expression analysis showed that HCT10 encoded 
potassium-ion non-activated acetoacetyl-CoA thiolase with no 3-ketooctanoyl-CoA 
thiolase activity, which is characteristic for CT. The deduced amino acid 
sequence has a 34-57% homology with 4 other human thiolases and 4 
acetoacetyl-CoA thiolases of microorganisms.
