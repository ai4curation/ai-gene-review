# Translational repression by RNA-binding protein TIAR.
**Authors:** Mazan-Mamczarz K, Lal A, Martindale JL, Kawai T, Gorospe M

## Abstract

1. Mol Cell Biol. 2006 Apr;26(7):2716-27. doi: 10.1128/MCB.26.7.2716-2727.2006.

Translational repression by RNA-binding protein TIAR.

Mazan-Mamczarz K(1), Lal A, Martindale JL, Kawai T, Gorospe M.

Author information:
(1)Laboratory of Cellular and Molecular Biology, Box 12, LCMB, National

The RNA-binding protein TIAR has been proposed to inhibit protein synthesis 
transiently by promoting the formation of translationally silent stress 
granules. Here, we report the selective binding of TIAR to several mRNAs 
encoding translation factors such as eukaryotic initiation factor 4A (eIF4A) and 
eIF4E (translation initiation factors), eEF1B (a translation elongation factor), 
and c-Myc (which transcriptionally controls the expression of numerous 
translation regulatory proteins). TIAR bound the 3'-untranslated regions of 
these mRNAs and potently suppressed their translation, particularly in response 
to low levels of short-wavelength UV (UVC) irradiation. The UVC-imposed global 
inhibition of the cellular translation machinery was significantly relieved 
after silencing of TIAR expression. We propose that the TIAR-mediated inhibition 
of translation factor expression elicits a sustained repression of protein 
biosynthesis in cells responding to stress.
