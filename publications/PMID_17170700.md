# Dosage-dependent switch from G protein-coupled to G protein-independent signaling by a GPCR.

## Abstract

1. EMBO J. 2007 Jan 10;26(1):53-64. doi: 10.1038/sj.emboj.7601502. Epub 2006 Dec

Dosage-dependent switch from G protein-coupled to G protein-independent 
signaling by a GPCR.

Sun Y(1), Huang J, Xiang Y, Bastepe M, Jüppner H, Kobilka BK, Zhang JJ, Huang 
XY.

Author information:
(1)Department of Physiology, Weill Medical College, Cornell University, New 
York, NY 10021, USA.

G-protein-coupled receptors (GPCRs) mostly signal through heterotrimeric G 
proteins. Increasing evidence suggests that GPCRs could function in a 
G-protein-independent manner. Here, we show that at low concentrations of an 
agonist, beta(2)-adrenergic receptors (beta(2)-ARs) signal through Galpha(s) to 
activate the mitogen-activated protein kinase pathway in mouse embryonic 
fibroblast cells. At high agonist concentrations, signals are also transduced 
through beta(2)-ARs via an additional pathway that is G-protein-independent but 
tyrosine kinase Src-dependent. This new dosage-dependent switch of signaling 
modes of GPCRs has significant implications for GPCR intrinsic properties and 
desensitization.
