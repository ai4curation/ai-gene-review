# The Anopheles gambiae tryptophan oxygenase gene expressed from a baculovirus promoter complements Drosophila melanogaster vermilion.
**Authors:** Besansky NJ, Mukabayire O, Benedict MQ, Rafferty CS, Hamm DM, Mcnitt L
**Journal:** Insect Biochem Mol Biol (1997)
**DOI:** [10.1016/s0965-1748(97)00040-4](https://doi.org/10.1016/s0965-1748(97)00040-4)

## Abstract

1. Insect Biochem Mol Biol. 1997 Aug-Sep;27(8-9):803-5. doi: 
10.1016/s0965-1748(97)00040-4.

The Anopheles gambiae tryptophan oxygenase gene expressed from a baculovirus 
promoter complements Drosophila melanogaster vermilion.

Besansky NJ(1), Mukabayire O, Benedict MQ, Rafferty CS, Hamm DM, Mcnitt L.

Author information:
(1)Department of Biological Sciences, University of Notre Dame, IN 46556, USA. 
njb5@cdc.gov

An Anopheles gambiae cDNA encoding tryptophan oxygenase was placed under the 
control of the constitutive baculovirus promoter, ie-1. The chimeric construct, 
expressed transiently in vermilion (tryptophan oxygenase) mutants of Drosophila 
melanogaster, partially rescued adult eye color. The successful genetic 
complementation by this construct demonstrated both the proper function of the 
tryptophan oxygenase product and the effectiveness of the ie-1 promoter in 
directing expression of foreign genes in live insects. The functionality of An. 
gambiae tryptophan oxygenase in a higher fly fulfils predictions based on its 
structural conservation throughout millions of years of independent evolution.
