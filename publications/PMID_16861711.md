# FHY1 and FHL act together to mediate nuclear accumulation of the phytochrome A photoreceptor.
**Authors:** Hiltbrunner A, Tscheuschler A, Viczián A, Kunkel T, Kircher S, Schäfer E
**Journal:** Plant Cell Physiol (2006)
**DOI:** [10.1093/pcp/pcj087](https://doi.org/10.1093/pcp/pcj087)

## Abstract

1. Plant Cell Physiol. 2006 Aug;47(8):1023-34. doi: 10.1093/pcp/pcj087. Epub 2006

FHY1 and FHL act together to mediate nuclear accumulation of the phytochrome A 
photoreceptor.

Hiltbrunner A(1), Tscheuschler A, Viczián A, Kunkel T, Kircher S, Schäfer E.

Author information:
(1)Institut für Biologie II/Botanik, Albert Ludwigs Universität, Schänzlestrasse 
1, D-79104 Freiburg, Germany.

The phytochrome family of red/far-red photoreceptors is involved in the 
regulation of a wide range of developmental responses in plants. The Arabidopsis 
genome contains five phytochromes (phyA-E), among which phyA and phyB play the 
most important roles. Phytochromes localize to the cytosol in the dark and 
accumulate in the nucleus under light conditions, inducing specific 
phytochrome-mediated responses. Light-regulated nuclear accumulation of the 
phytochrome photoreceptors is therefore considered a key regulatory step of 
these pathways. In fact, one of the most severe phyA signaling mutants, fhy1 
(far red elongated hypocotyl 1), is strongly affected in nuclear accumulation of 
phyA. The fhy1 fhl (fhy1 like) double mutant, lacking both FHY1 and its only 
close homolog FHL, is virtually blind to far-red light like phyA null seedlings. 
Here we show that FHL accounts for residual amounts of phyA in the nucleus in a 
fhy1 background and that nuclear accumulation of phyA is completely inhibited in 
an fhy1 FHL RNAi knock-down line. Moreover, we demonstrate that FHL and phyA 
interact with each other in a light-dependent manner and that they co-localize 
in light-induced nuclear speckles. We also identify a phyA-binding site at the 
C-terminus of FHY1 and FHL, and show that the N-terminal 406 amino acids of phyA 
are sufficient for the interaction with FHY1/FHL.
