# Auxin regulates SCF(TIR1)-dependent degradation of AUX/IAA proteins.
**Authors:** Gray WM, Kepinski S, Rouse D, Leyser O, Estelle M
**Journal:** Nature (2001)
**DOI:** [10.1038/35104500](https://doi.org/10.1038/35104500)

## Abstract

1. Nature. 2001 Nov 15;414(6861):271-6. doi: 10.1038/35104500.

Auxin regulates SCF(TIR1)-dependent degradation of AUX/IAA proteins.

Gray WM(1), Kepinski S, Rouse D, Leyser O, Estelle M.

Author information:
(1)The Institute for Cellular and Molecular Biology, The University of Texas at
Austin, Austin, Texas 78712, USA.

The plant hormone auxin is central in many aspects of plant development.
Previous studies have implicated the ubiquitin-ligase SCF(TIR1) and the AUX/IAA
proteins in auxin response. Dominant mutations in several AUX/IAA genes confer
pleiotropic auxin-related phenotypes, whereas recessive mutations affecting the
function of SCF(TIR1) decrease auxin response. Here we show that SCF(TIR1) is
required for AUX/IAA degradation. We demonstrate that SCF(TIR1) interacts with
AXR2/IAA7 and AXR3/IAA17, and that domain II of these proteins is necessary and
sufficient for this interaction. Further, auxin stimulates binding of SCF(TIR1)
to the AUX/IAA proteins, and their degradation. Because domain II is conserved
in nearly all AUX/IAA proteins in Arabidopsis, we propose that auxin promotes
the degradation of this large family of transcriptional regulators, leading to
diverse downstream effects.
