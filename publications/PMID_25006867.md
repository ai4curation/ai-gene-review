# The BAG2 and BAG5 proteins inhibit the ubiquitination of pathogenic ataxin3-80Q.

## Abstract

1. Int J Neurosci. 2015 May;125(5):390-4. doi: 10.3109/00207454.2014.940585. Epub
 2014 Aug 19.

The BAG2 and BAG5 proteins inhibit the ubiquitination of pathogenic ataxin3-80Q.

Che XQ(1), Tang BS, Wang HF, Yan XX, Jiang H, Shen L, Xu Q, Wang GH, Zhang HN, 
Wang CY, Guo JF.

Author information:
(1)1The Department of Neurology, Xiangya Hospital, Central South University, 
Changsha, Hunan, People's Republic of China.

The expansion of a polyglutamine domain in the protein ataxin3 causes 
spinocerebellar ataxia type-3 (SCA3). However, there is little information to 
date about the upstream proteins in the ubiquitin-proteasome system of 
pathogenic ataxin3-80Q. Here, we report that BAG2 (Bcl-2 associated athanogene 
family protein 2) and BAG5 (Bcl-2-associated athanogene family protein 5) 
stabilise pathogenic ataxin3-80Q by inhibiting its ubiquitination as determined 
based on western blotting and co-immunofluorescence experiments. The association 
of the BAG2 and BAG5 proteins with pathogenic ataxin3-80Q strengthens the 
important roles of the BAG family in neurodegenerative diseases.
