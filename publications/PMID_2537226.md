# D-glycerate kinase deficiency as a cause of D-glyceric aciduria.
**Authors:** Van Schaftingen E
**Journal:** FEBS Lett (1989)
**DOI:** [10.1016/0014-5793(89)80113-9](https://doi.org/10.1016/0014-5793(89)80113-9)

## Abstract

1. FEBS Lett. 1989 Jan 30;243(2):127-31. doi: 10.1016/0014-5793(89)80113-9.

D-glycerate kinase deficiency as a cause of D-glyceric aciduria.

Van Schaftingen E(1).

Author information:
(1)Laboratoire de Chimie Physiologique, Universit√© Catholique de Louvain, 
Brussels, Belgium.

D-Glycerate kinase was measured in human livers thanks to a new, sensitive 
radiochemical assay. The enzyme was extremely unstable in extracts prepared in 
water, but was partly stabilized in a homogenization mixture containing 
inorganic phosphate, D-glycerate and EGTA. When extracted in such a stabilizing 
mixture, glycerate kinase activity amounted to 0.86 +/- 0.21 U/g in control 
livers and to 0.03 U/g in the liver of a patient with D-glyceric aciduria. In 
contrast, D-glycerate dehydrogenase (glyoxylate reductase) and triokinase 
activities were not deficient in the liver of the same patient. It is concluded 
that D-glycerate kinase deficiency is a cause of D-glyceric aciduria.
