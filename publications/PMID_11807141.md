# Interaction between Arabidopsis heat shock transcription factor 1 and 70 kDa heat shock proteins.
**Authors:** Kim BH, Schöffl F
**Journal:** J Exp Bot (2002)
**DOI:** [10.1093/jexbot/53.367.371](https://doi.org/10.1093/jexbot/53.367.371)

## Abstract

1. J Exp Bot. 2002 Feb;53(367):371-5. doi: 10.1093/jexbot/53.367.371.

Interaction between Arabidopsis heat shock transcription factor 1 and 70 kDa 
heat shock proteins.

Kim BH(1), Schöffl F.

Author information:
(1)Universität Tübingen, ZMBP-Zentrum für Molekularbiologie der Pflanzen, 
Allgemeine Genetik, Auf der Morgenstelle 28, D-72076 Tübingen, Germany.

The activity of the Arabidopsis heat shock transcription factor (HSF) is 
repressed at normal conditions but activated by cellular stresses. 
Circumstantial evidence suggests that HSP70 may function as a negative feedback 
regulator of HSF activity. Here the interaction between HSF and HSP70 is 
reported using electrophoretic mobility shift and yeast two-hybrid assays. 
Subdomain mapping indicates an interaction of the activation domain and 
DNA-binding domain of HSF1 with HSP70.
