# Cloning and sequencing of a cDNA for human mitochondrial ubiquinone-binding protein of complex III.
**Authors:** Suzuki H, Hosokawa Y, Toda H, Nishikimi M, Ozawa T
**Journal:** Biochem Biophys Res Commun (1988)
**DOI:** [10.1016/s0006-291x(88)80941-0](https://doi.org/10.1016/s0006-291x(88)80941-0)

## Abstract

1. Biochem Biophys Res Commun. 1988 Oct 31;156(2):987-94. doi: 
10.1016/s0006-291x(88)80941-0.

Cloning and sequencing of a cDNA for human mitochondrial ubiquinone-binding 
protein of complex III.

Suzuki H(1), Hosokawa Y, Toda H, Nishikimi M, Ozawa T.

Author information:
(1)Department of Biomedical Chemistry, Faculty of Medicine, University of 
Nagoya, Japan.

The ubiquinone-binding protein (QP-C) is a nuclear-encoded component of 
ubiquinol-cytochrome c oxidoreductase in the mitochondrial respiratory chain and 
plays an important role in electron transfer as a ubiquinone-QP-C complex. We 
obtained a partial cDNA for rat liver QP-C by screening a lambda gt11 rat liver 
cDNA library using antiserum directed against bovine heart QP-C. Using this cDNA 
as a probe, a cDNA clone was isolated from a human fibroblast cDNA library by 
colony hybridization. The total length of the cloned cDNA was 518 base pairs 
with an open reading frame of 333 base pairs. The 111-amino acid sequence 
deduced from the nucleotide sequence of the cDNA is 85% homologous to that of 
bovine QP-C and contains only a single additional amino-terminal methionine. 
This implies that the human QP-C is synthesized without a presequence which is 
required for import of most nuclear-encoded mitochondrial proteins into 
mitochondria.
