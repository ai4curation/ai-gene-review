Gastric cancer (GC) is one of the most challenging malignant tumors worldwide, primarily because 
of its high incidence and mortality rates. Prolyl 4-hydroxylase subunit alpha 3 (P4HA3) has been 
established as a pivotal factor for facilitating cell proliferation, invasion, and metastasis across multiple 
human tumors. Nevertheless, the precise role of P4HA3in GC has not been fully elucidated. In this 
study, we used data from The Cancer Genome Atlas (TCGA) to examine the role of P4HA3 as a potential 
biomarker for predicting immunotherapy response in patients with GC. Our comprehensive analysis of 
data from the TCGA, TIMER, and other databases revealed a significant association between elevated 
P4HA3 expression in GC and adverse prognostic outcomes. Furthermore, we confirmed that P4HA3 
expression was strongly correlated with immune infiltrating cells, immune infiltration markers, the 
tumor mutational burden (TMB), microsatellite instability (MSI), the immune score, the stromal score, 
and immune checkpoints, thus highlighting P4HA3 as a crucial and dependable therapeutic target 
within the context of immune-based antitumor strategies. Our findings suggest that P4HA3 may 
function as an immune-related biomarker in the pathogenesis and treatment of GC, indicating that 
P4HA3 is a promising prognostic and therapeutic target for this malignancy.
Keywords P4HA3, Gastric cancer, Prognostic, Immunotherapy
GC ranks as the fifth most prevalent malignancy globally and is the fourth leading cause of cancer-related 
mortality1. GC typically presents with insidious onset, leading to the majority of patients being diagnosed at 
advanced stages, which results in suboptimal treatment outcomes and a significantly shortened median survival 
period2. Despite significant advancements in therapeutic approaches, the prognosis for patients with advanced 
gastric cancer remains bleak, with median survival times often ranging between 4 and 12 months 3. Recent 
advancements in immunotherapy – particularly the emergence of checkpoint inhibitors, such as nivolumab 
and pembrolizumab – have improved clinical outcomes in a subset of patients with GC4,5. The KEYNOTE-062 
study demonstrated that pembrolizumab can significantly increase progression-free survival (PFS) in patients 
with advanced GC5. However, both the ATTRACTION-2 and CheckMate-032 trials indicated that the efficacy 
of immunotherapy in enhancing survival outcomes for GC patients appeared to be independent of PD-L1 
expression status6,7.
In this study, we performed a comprehensive analysis to assess the expression of P4HA3 in gastric cancer and 
explore its potential utility as a prognostic biomarker. Moreover, immunotherapy faces significant limitations, 
including a low response rate, a lack of robust predictive biomarkers, drug resistance, and toxicity8,9. Therefore, 
only a limited subset of patients with cancer achieve sustained clinical benefits from these therapies, thereby 
restricting their broader application10. This suggests the existence of additional immunotherapeutic biomarkers 
within the tumor microenvironment of gastric cancer. Identifying reliable immune-related biomarkers, such as

Prolyl 4-hydroxylase (P4H) is an α2β2 tetramer, with the substrate-binding domain localized on the prolyl 
4-hydroxylase α subunit (P4HA) 11. Although three isoforms of P4HA, namely, P4HA1, P4HA2, and P4HA3, 
have been identified, the expression and functional roles of P4HA3 in GC remain largely unexplored. However, 
an analysis of TCGA stomach cancer data revealed substantial upregulation of P4HA3 RNA expression in GC 
tissues. Furthermore, remodeling of the extracellular matrix (ECM) is closely associated with the invasion and 
metastasis of GC. The ECM, a fundamental component of the tumor microenvironment (TME), plays a critical 
role in the pathogenesis of GC, influencing the proliferation, viability, infiltration, and motility of gastric cancer 
cells12. Collagen constitutes a significant portion of the ECM, comprising up to 90% of its total composition12,13. 
Collagen is pivotal in cancer biology and regulates key cellular processes such as cell polarity, motility, and 
signaling pathways14.
The P4HA enzyme is important for maintaining the structural integrity of newly synthesized collagen. P4HA3 
is the catalytically active subunit of P4HA. The upregulation of P4HA3 is intricately associated with the synthesis, 
deposition, and stiffening of the ECM in gastric cancer. Furthermore, the upregulation of P4HA3 is hypothesized 
to facilitate the metastasis of various solid tumors15. The precise role of P4HA3 in immunomodulation during the 
pathogenesis of GC remains inadequately understood. Consequently, this study aimed to analyze the expression 
of P4HA3 specifically in GC and to elucidate its prognostic significance through an extensive analysis of multiple 
databases. The results indicate that P4HA3 expression is significantly elevated in GC and is closely associated 
with both immune checkpoint expression and immune cell infiltration. Thus, P4HA3 is posited as an immune-
related biomarker with a significant impact on the efficacy of clinical immunotherapy.
Materials and methods
Data processing
Clinical data, expression profiles, and somatic mutation data for P4HA3 were obtained from the TCGA database, 
which includes a comprehensive dataset of 33 distinct tumor types. The data were accessed via UCSC Xena 16 
(https://xena.ucsc.edu/) and subsequently analyzed using R software (version 4.0.3; https://www.R-project.org), 
using the limma package along with Strawberry Perl (5.3.0.1) 16. Comparative expression analysis of P4HA3 
across cancer tissues was conducted using the TIMER online database (https://cistrome.shinyapps.io/timer/)17. 
Validation of P4HA3 expression and its correlation with immunotherapeutic response was performed using 
the GEO dataset GSE66229 18. Additionally, a systematic search was performed to identify publicly available 
immune checkpoint blockade cohorts with comprehensive clinical data, including the IMvigor210 cohort 
for atezolizumab in advanced urothelial cancer 19and the GSE78220 cohort for pembrolizumab in metastatic 
melanoma20. Differential expression analysis of P4HA3 across various cancer types was conducted using TCGA 
RNA-Seq data and a paired Wilcoxon signed-rank test, with statistical significance defined as an adjusted p value 
of less than 0.05. Additionally, receiver operating characteristic (ROC) curve analysis was performed to assess 
the diagnostic potential of P4HA3 expression, with the area under the curve (AUC) calculated using the pROC 
package in R.
Clinical correlation between P4HA3 and gastric cancer (STAD)
The differences in P4HA3 expression were examined using the limma package in R Studio. Additionally, Pearson’s 
and Spearman’s correlation analyses were used to investigate the relationships between P4HA3 expression and 
clinical factors, including sex, age, and tumor stage. Furthermore, survival analysis was conducted using the 
survival package in R to perform univariate Cox proportional hazards regression, which was used to assess the 
prognostic significance of P4HA3 across 33 cancer types, including overall survival (OS) and PFS. A hazard 
ratio (HR) greater than 1 suggests that increased P4HA3 expression is associated with a greater risk of adverse 
outcomes, including mortality. The KM Plotter Database 22 ( http://kmplot.com/analysis/index.php) was used 
to evaluate the prognostic significance of P4HA3 in stomach adenocarcinoma (STAD). A corrected p value 
of less than 0.05 was considered statistically significant. Finally, the relationships between P4HA3 expression 
and clinical characteristics, including clinical stage, grade, and TNM stage, were evaluated with a significance 
threshold set at a p value of less than 0.05.
Examination of immune cell infiltration
Furthermore, we explored the association between P4HA3 expression and immune response abundance using 
the TIMER online database, a comprehensive tool designed to systematically analyze immune infiltration 
biomarkers, tumor immune infiltration, immune checkpoints, and immune cell populations within STAD17.
Correlation between P4HA3 and immune characteristics
Recent research has indicated that TMB can serve as a predictive biomarker for patient response to anti-PD1/
PD-L1 therapy21. Similarly, MSI is recognized as a crucial biomarker within the TME 22. To assess the role of 
P4HA3 in the immune landscape of STAD, we analyzed the relationships between P4HA3 expression and MSI 
or TMB. Additionally, we performed immune score and stromal score analyses using the “estimate” R package 
to increase our understanding of the correlations between P4HA3 expression and immune characteristics in 
patients with STAD. The immunophenotypic score (IPS), accessible via the TCIA website (https://tcia.at/home), 
serves as a predictor of patient response to immune checkpoint inhibitors (ICIs) 25. A higher IPS is associated 
with an improved response to ICI therapy.

Gene set enrichment analysis
Gene set enrichment analysis (GSEA) was conducted to investigate the signaling pathways and expression 
patterns associated with P4HA3. The analysis was performed using the Kyoto Encyclopedia of Genes and 
Genomes (KEGG) database 23. Pathways that met the specified criteria ( p < 0.05) were visualized, and the 
pathways with the highest enrichment scores were selected for further analysis.
Analysis of immunotherapeutic response and chemotherapy
This study analyzed two independent immunotherapeutic cohorts. Immunotherapy efficacy is categorized 
into four response types: complete response (CR), partial response (PR), progressive disease (PD), and stable 
disease (SD). Patients with SD or PD were classified as nonresponders, whereas those who achieved CR or PR 
were classified as responders. Differences in P4HA3 expression between responders and nonresponders were 
evaluated using the Wilcoxon test.

Clinical features and prognostic value of P4HA3 in patients with STAD
We assessed the prognostic impact of P4HA3 expression in patients with STAD by examining the correlation 
of P4HA3 expression with various clinical features and tumor stages. We stratified gene expression data into 
two groups based on the median expression level of P4HA3. A chi-square test was subsequently performed to 
assess the correlation between P4HA3 mRNA expression and various clinical parameters in GC. Our analysis 
revealed that elevated P4HA3 expression was significantly associated with poorer histological grade (P = 0.025) 
and advanced T stage ( P = 0.011). However, no significant correlations were observed between P4HA3 mRNA 
expression and other clinical features, including age, gender, N stage, M stage, and overall TNM stage (P values 
for these comparisons are detailed in Table 1).
As shown in Figs. 3A-F and 4A, P4HA3 expression levels significantly varied across different clinical stages, 
including tumor grade and stage, thus suggesting the potential role of P4HA3 in tumor progression and its 
influence on patient prognosis. As depicted in Fig. 4B-C, both univariate and multivariate Cox regression analyses 
revealed that elevated P4HA3 expression, older age, and advanced clinical stage were significant unfavorable 
prognostic factors for patients with GC. The corresponding hazard ratios (HRs) indicate a heightened risk of 
poor outcomes, suggesting that P4HA3 expression, alongside age and stage, independently contributes to the 
overall prognosis of patients with STAD. The calibration plots (Fig. 5A) demonstrated strong agreement between 
the predicted and observed overall survival (OS) rates at 1, 3, and 5 years, thus highlighting the accuracy of the 
model. To further visualize and refine the prognostic predictions, a nomogram incorporating P4HA3 expression 
alongside other clinical features was constructed, as illustrated in Fig. 5B. While the nomogram includes multiple 
parameters, the results highlight age as the most significant predictor of OS, reflecting its predominant influence 
within the predictive model. However, while the inclusion of P4HA3 is not as prominent as age, it still contributes 
meaningfully to the overall model, particularly within the framework of multifactorial risk assessment.

P4HA3 is linked to immunotherapy response
In this study, we assessed the role of P4HA3 in gastric cancer, with a particular focus on its potential as a 
biomarker for predicting immunotherapy response. As shown in Fig.  9, we observed a significant negative 
correlation between P4HA3 expression and key immunotherapy-related factors. Specifically, P4HA3 expression 
in STAD was negatively correlated with TMB and MSI (Fig.  9A-B), suggesting that elevated P4HA3 levels 
are associated with a less favorable genomic profile for immunotherapy response. Furthermore, patients with 
elevated P4HA3 expression demonstrated a significantly reduced IPS, indicating a potentially diminished 
response to immunotherapy (Fig. 9C-F , p < 0.05).

The link between P4HA3 and the immunotherapy response across cancers
We conducted a comprehensive investigation across various cancer types to examine the relationships between

Discussion
GC represents a major malignancy worldwide because of its substantial disease burden and high fatality 
rate24. Growing evidence suggests that P4HA3 plays a role in modulating immune activity within the tumor 
microenvironment across various cancers. Our study revealed significantly elevated expression levels of P4HA3 
in GC tissues, a finding further corroborated by data from the GEO database. However, there has been limited 
progress in understanding the role and underlying mechanisms of P4HA3 antagonism in the context of GC.
Our survival analysis revealed that P4HA3 overexpression is associated with shorter overall survival (OS) 
and disease-free survival (DFS) in patients with GC, highlighting its potential as a prognostic biomarker. 
Immunotherapy holds significant promise as an innovative treatment approach for individuals with advanced 
STAD, as it demonstrated effectiveness in improving survival rates 25. Although various molecular markers, 
including tumor purity, mutation load, MSI, tumor immune cell infiltration, the immunophenoscore (IPS), and 
tumor immune dysfunction and exclusion (TIDE), have been implicated in modulating the response to tumor 
immunotherapy, their application and accuracy remain subjects of ongoing debate. Therefore, there is a pressing 
need to identify more effective prognostic biomarkers that can enhance the timely detection, early diagnosis, and 
treatment of GC, particularly in the context of immunotherapy.
In this study, we found that poor prognosis was associated with elevated P4HA3 expression in patients with 
STAD and that P4HA3 contributes to the regulation of the TME. Initially, we used data from TCGA to assess 
the expression levels of P4HA3 in malignant and paracancerous tissues. The findings revealed that P4HA3 was 
amplified in most tumor tissues. Further analysis revealed significantly increased expression of P4HA3 in STAD 
tissues, suggesting considerable diagnostic potential of P4HA3. Moreover, individuals with elevated P4HA3 
levels have a poorer prognosis, suggesting that P4HA3 likely functions as an oncogene, promoting the onset and 
progression of STAD26. The results of our study demonstrated a significant correlation between elevated P4HA3 
levels and the grade and pathological stage of individuals with STAD. Research on the functional significance of 
P4HA3 in STAD remains limited and comprehensive investigations into its role in immune-related processes are

Collagen interacts with specific molecules to stimulate cancer cell growth and facilitate epithelial‒
mesenchymal transition (EMT) 28,29. Collagen deposition promotes tumor growth and invasion, with P4HAs 
playing a crucial role in regulating collagen synthesis and influencing the behavior of malignant cells. P4HA 
influences the malignant phenotype of tumors by regulating the stability of hypoxia-inducible factor 1 (HIF-1) 
and modulating the tumor glycolytic pathway.
Despite extensive investigations, our understanding of the influence of P4HA3 on the transcriptional 
regulation of cancer remains limited. Nevertheless, our study revealed significant associations between P4HA3 
expression and clinical prognosis, the TIME, and immune cell infiltration in patients with STAD, suggesting 
a potential oncogenic role for P4HA3 in STAD. We performed GSEA on the genes correlated with P4HA3, 
revealing that these genes are predominantly associated with the functional pathways of P4HA3. P4HA3 may 
exert a substantial influence on the progression of STAD by altering the composition of the TIME. An essential 
task is to analyze the association between P4HA3 and STAD to better understand the influence of P4HA3 on 
tumor progression and growth. The composition of the TME includes extracellular matrix molecules, immune 
cells, stromal components, and cytokines, all of which play crucial roles in tumor proliferation and the response 
to immunotherapy30.
Recent studies have demonstrated that immune checkpoint receptors, such as CTLA-4 and PD-1, are 
significantly overexpressed on activated CD4 + and CD8 + T cells and are frequently engaged within the TME. 
These receptors play crucial roles in suppressing T-cell-mediated immune responses, which are essential for 
tumor control. These findings suggest that checkpoint inhibitors can serve as effective therapeutic agents. Further 
research is needed to fully understand the regulatory mechanisms of the tumor TIME and immunotherapy 
in STAD, as well as to continue to identify appropriate immunotherapy biomarkers. An extensive analysis of 
several databases revealed a positive correlation between P4HA3 expression and the presence of neutrophils, 
macrophages, eosinophils, resting dendritic cells, and M2 macrophages within the TME. However, there was no 
correlation with the presence of memory B cells, plasma cells, or naïve B cells, suggesting increased recruitment 
of specific immune cells to the TME.

Research suggests that increased infiltration of T lymphocytes within tumor tissue is associated with 
improved prognosis. This is likely due to the critical role that tumor-specific CD8 + and CD4 + T cells play in 
mounting an effective immune response against cancer. Importantly, tumor-associated macrophages (TAMs) 
play a pivotal role in the TIME. The various cytokines and chemokines secreted by TAMs can promote tumor 
growth, metastasis, invasion, angiogenesis, immunosuppression, and drug resistance through coordination with 
inflammatory pathways.
Our results indicate that P4HA3 is significantly associated with lymphocytes, chemokines, and immune 
modulators. Additionally, it was strongly correlated with the expression of specific markers, such as HAVCR2, 
NRP1, TNFSF4, CD86, TNFRSF4, PDCD1LG2, and LAIR1. Furthermore, elevated P4HA3 expression was 
directly associated with increased ESTIMATE, immunological, and stromal scores. Although the current study 
highlights the critical role of P4HA3 in regulating and recruiting immune cells in STAD, the precise mechanism 
by which P4HA3 facilitates immune cell recruitment remains unclear. Further research is needed to elucidate

Our study revealed a strong relationship between P4HA3 expression and immune checkpoints, as well as 
the TIDE score. The limited effectiveness of ICIs, including anti-PD-L1 therapies, in patients with high P4HA3 
expression in the IMvigor210 immunotherapy group suggests that P4HA3 may interact with traditional ICIs to 
regulate immune responses in the TIME. These findings indicate the potential for developing novel combination 
therapies for treating STAD. Combining P4HA3 inhibitors with traditional ICI therapy may provide more 
effective treatment outcomes than ICI therapy alone. Therefore, the available data support the notion that the 
presence of P4HA3 within the TIME is critical for both tumor progression and the efficacy of immunotherapy 
in patients with STAD. However, further research is needed to validate these findings.
This study elucidates the significant role of P4HA3 as both a prognostic biomarker and a potential therapeutic 
target in gastric cancer, particularly in the context of immunotherapy. However, the current investigation has 
several limitations. First, as the study relied on bioinformatics analysis and was conducted retrospectively, it 
is crucial to exercise caution when interpreting the findings. Furthermore, the study used data from multiple 
online databases, but the accessible data were limited, and there was variation in the outcomes across different 
sources, potentially leading to systematic bias. Moreover, although this study revealed a correlation between 
P4HA3 and the TIME in patients with STAD, it did not provide specific insights into how P4HA3 influences 
prognosis and immunotherapy outcomes through its interaction with the TIME. To gain a more comprehensive 
understanding of the regulatory impact and interaction mechanism between P4HA3 and tumor immunity in 
STAD, future research should incorporate molecular-based experiments and clinical specimens for validation.
