# Apaf-1, a human protein homologous to C. elegans CED-4, participates in cytochrome c-dependent activation of caspase-3.

Comment in
    Cell. 1997 Aug 8;90(3):389-90. doi: 10.1016/s0092-8674(00)80497-3.

We report here the purification and cDNA cloning of Apaf-1, a novel 130 kd 
protein from HeLa cell cytosol that participates in the cytochrome c-dependent 
activation of caspase-3. The NH2-terminal 85 amino acids of Apaf-1 show 21% 
identity and 53% similarity to the NH2-terminal prodomain of the Caenorhabditis 
elegans caspase, CED-3. This is followed by 320 amino acids that show 22% 
identity and 48% similarity to CED-4, a protein that is believed to initiate 
apoptosis in C. elegans. The COOH-terminal region of Apaf-1 comprises multiple 
WD repeats, which are proposed to mediate protein-protein interactions. 
Cytochrome c binds to Apaf-1, an event that may trigger the activation of 
caspase-3, leading to apoptosis.
