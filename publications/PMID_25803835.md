Comment in
    Nat Rev Neurol. 2015 May;11(5):246. doi: 10.1038/nrneurol.2015.60.

Amyotrophic lateral sclerosis (ALS) is a genetically heterogeneous 
neurodegenerative syndrome hallmarked by adult-onset loss of motor neurons. We 
performed exome sequencing of 252 familial ALS (fALS) and 827 control 
individuals. Gene-based rare variant analysis identified an exome-wide 
significant enrichment of eight loss-of-function (LoF) mutations in TBK1 
(encoding TANK-binding kinase 1) in 13 fALS pedigrees. No enrichment of LoF 
mutations was observed in a targeted mutation screen of 1,010 sporadic ALS and 
650 additional control individuals. Linkage analysis in four families gave an 
aggregate LOD score of 4.6. In vitro experiments confirmed the loss of 
expression of TBK1 LoF mutant alleles, or loss of interaction of the C-terminal 
TBK1 coiled-coil domain (CCD2) mutants with the TBK1 adaptor protein optineurin, 
which has been shown to be involved in ALS pathogenesis. We conclude that 
haploinsufficiency of TBK1 causes ALS and fronto-temporal dementia.
