# Point and deletion mutations eliminate one or both methyl group transfers catalysed by the yeast TRM1 encoded tRNA (m22G26)dimethyltransferase.
**Authors:** Liu J, Liu J, Stråby KB
**Journal:** Nucleic Acids Res (1998)
**DOI:** [10.1093/nar/26.22.5102](https://doi.org/10.1093/nar/26.22.5102)

## Abstract

1. Nucleic Acids Res. 1998 Nov 15;26(22):5102-8. doi: 10.1093/nar/26.22.5102.

Point and deletion mutations eliminate one or both methyl group transfers 
catalysed by the yeast TRM1 encoded tRNA (m22G26)dimethyltransferase.

Liu J(1), Liu J, Stråby KB.

Author information:
(1)Department of Microbiology, Umeå University, S-901 87 Umeå, Sweden and 
Shanghai Research Centre of Life Sciences, Academia Sinica, 200031 Shanghai,

Guanosine at position 26 in eukaryotic tRNAs is usually modified to N2 , N2 
-dimethylguanosine (m22G26). In Saccharomyces cerevisiae , this reaction is 
catalysed by the TRM1 encoded tRNA (m22G26)dimethyltransferase. As a 
prerequisite for future studies, the yeast TRM1 gene was expressed in 
Escherichia coli and the His-tagged Trm1 protein (rTrm1p) was extensively 
purified. rTrm1p catalysed both the mono- and dimethylation of G26 in vivo in 
Escherichia coli tRNA and in vitro in yeast trm1 mutant tRNA. The TRM1 gene from 
two independent wild-type yeast strains differed at 14 base positions causing 
two amino acid exchanges . Exchange of the original Ser467 for Leu caused a 
complete loss of enzyme activity in vitro against trm1 yeast tRNA. Comparatively 
short N- or C-terminal deletions from the 570 amino acid long Trm1 polypeptide 
decreased or eliminated the enzyme activity, as did some point mutations within 
these regions. This indicated that the protein is not a two domain peptide with 
the enzyme activity localised to one of the domains, but rather that both ends 
of the polypeptide seem to interact to influence the conformation of those parts 
that make up the RNA-binding site and/or the active site of the enzyme.
