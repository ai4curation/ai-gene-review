# Genetic characterization of the Drosophila jaguar322 mutant reveals that complete myosin VI loss of function is not lethal.
**Authors:** Morrison JK, Miller KG
**Journal:** Genetics (2008)
**DOI:** [10.1534/genetics.107.085969](https://doi.org/10.1534/genetics.107.085969)
**PMC:** [PMC2390649](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC2390649/)

## Abstract

1. Genetics. 2008 May;179(1):711-6. doi: 10.1534/genetics.107.085969.

Genetic characterization of the Drosophila jaguar322 mutant reveals that 
complete myosin VI loss of function is not lethal.

Morrison JK(1), Miller KG.

Author information:
(1)Department of Biology, Washington University, St. Louis, MO 63130, USA.

Myosin VI is an actin-based motor that has been implicated in many cellular 
processes. Studies in vertebrates have demonstrated that animals lacking this 
ubiquitously expressed myosin are viable. However in Drosophila, myosin VI loss 
of function has been thought to be lethal. We show here that complete loss of 
myosin VI is not lethal in flies and that the previously reported lethality of 
the null mutation (jar322) is most likely due to deletion of a neighboring gene. 
Maternally provided myosin VI does not account for the survival of myosin VI 
null animals. Mutant animals are recovered at a lower than expected Mendelian 
frequency, suggesting that myosin VI participates in processes which contribute 
to normal development, but its participation is not essential.
