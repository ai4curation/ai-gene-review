Comment in
    Nature. 1995 Jul 6;376(6535):17-8. doi: 10.1038/376017a0.

The protease responsible for the cleavage of poly(ADP-ribose) polymerase and 
necessary for apoptosis has been purified and characterized. This enzyme, named 
apopain, is composed of two subunits of relative molecular mass (M(r)) 17K and 
12K that are derived from a common proenzyme identified as CPP32. This proenzyme 
is related to interleukin-1 beta-converting enzyme (ICE) and CED-3, the product 
of a gene required for programmed cell death in Caenorhabditis elegans. A potent 
peptide aldehyde inhibitor has been developed and shown to prevent apoptotic 
events in vitro, suggesting that apopain/CPP32 is important for the initiation 
of apoptotic cell death.
