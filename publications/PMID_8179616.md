# Major defect of carbohydrate-deficient-glycoprotein syndrome is not found in the synthesis of dolichyl phosphate or N-acetylglucosaminyl-pyrophosphoryl-dolichol.
**Authors:** Yasugi E, Nakasuji M, Dohi T, Oshima M
**Journal:** Biochem Biophys Res Commun (1994)
**DOI:** [10.1006/bbrc.1994.1524](https://doi.org/10.1006/bbrc.1994.1524)

## Abstract

1. Biochem Biophys Res Commun. 1994 Apr 29;200(2):816-20. doi: 
10.1006/bbrc.1994.1524.

Major defect of carbohydrate-deficient-glycoprotein syndrome is not found in the 
synthesis of dolichyl phosphate or N-acetylglucosaminyl-pyrophosphoryl-dolichol.

Yasugi E(1), Nakasuji M, Dohi T, Oshima M.

Author information:
(1)Division of Biochemistry and Nutrition, International Medical Center of 
Japan, Tokyo.

The contents of dolichyl phosphate and UDP-N-acetylglucosamine:dolichyl 
phosphate N-acetylglucosamine 1-phosphate transferase (GlcNAc-1-P transferase) 
activity in fibroblasts from patients with carbohydrate-deficient-glycoprotein 
(CDG) syndrome were analyzed. The amount of dolichyl phosphate and GlcNAc-1-P 
transferase activity in CDG syndrome fibroblasts were similar to those in normal 
fibroblasts, suggesting that CDG syndrome may not be due to a deficiency of a 
biosynthetic enzyme for dolichol-oligosaccharide intermediates, but to a 
metabolic error in assembly of asparagine-linked oligosaccharide.
