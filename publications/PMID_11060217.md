# Mass spectrometric survey of peptides in cephalopods with an emphasis on the FMRFamide-related peptides.
**Authors:** Sweedler JV, Li L, Floyd P, Gilly W
**Journal:** J Exp Biol (2000)
**DOI:** [10.1242/jeb.203.23.3565](https://doi.org/10.1242/jeb.203.23.3565)

## Abstract

1. J Exp Biol. 2000 Dec;203(Pt 23):3565-73. doi: 10.1242/jeb.203.23.3565.

Mass spectrometric survey of peptides in cephalopods with an emphasis on the 
FMRFamide-related peptides.

Sweedler JV(1), Li L, Floyd P, Gilly W.

Author information:
(1)Beckman Institute, University of Illinois, Urbana, IL 61801, USA. 
jsweedle@uiuc.edu

A matrix-assisted laser desorption/ionization (MALDI) mass spectrometric (MS) 
survey of the major peptides in the stellar, fin and pallial nerves and the 
posterior chromatophore lobe of the cephalopods Sepia officinalis, Loligo 
opalescens and Dosidicus gigas has been performed. Although a large number of 
putative peptides are distinct among the three species, several molecular masses 
are conserved. In addition to peptides, characterization of the lipid content of 
the nerves is reported, and these lipid peaks account for many of the lower 
molecular masses observed. One conserved set of peaks corresponds to the 
FMRFamide-related peptides (FRPs). The Loligo opalescens FMRFa gene has been 
sequenced. It encodes a 331 amino acid residue prohormone that is processed into 
14 FRPs, which are both predicted by the nucleotide sequence and confirmed by 
MALDI MS. The FRPs predicted by this gene (FMRFa, FLRFa/FIRFa and ALSGDAFLRFa) 
are observed in all three species, indicating that members of this peptide 
family are highly conserved across cephalopods.
