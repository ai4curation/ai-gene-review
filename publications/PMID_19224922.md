Differential effects of depletion of ARL1 and ARFRP1 on membrane trafficking 
between the trans-Golgi network and endosomes.

Nishimoto-Morita K(1), Shin HW, Mitsuhashi H, Kitamura M, Zhang Q, Johannes L, 
Nakayama K.

Author information:
(1)Graduate School of Pharmaceutical Sciences, Kyoto University, Sakyo-ku, Kyoto

ARFRP1 and ARL1, which are both ARF-like small GTPases, are mammalian orthologs 
of yeast Arl3p and Arl1p, respectively. In yeast, Arl3p targeted to trans-Golgi 
network (TGN) membranes activates Arl1p, and the activated Arl1p in turn 
recruits a GRIP domain-containing protein; this complex regulates retrograde 
transport to the TGN and anterograde transport from the TGN. In the present 
study, using RNA interference-mediated knockdown of ARFRP1 and ARL1, we have 
examined whether the orthologs of Arl3p-Arl1p-GRIP story serve similar functions 
in mammalian cells. However, we have unexpectedly found differential roles of 
ARL1 and ARFRP1. Specifically, ARL1 and ARFRP1 regulate retrograde transport of 
Shiga toxin to the TGN and anterograde transport of VSVG from the TGN, 
respectively. Furthermore, we have obtained evidence suggesting that a SNARE 
complex containing Vti1a, syntaxin 6, and syntaxin 16 is involved in Shiga toxin 
transport downstream of ARL1.
