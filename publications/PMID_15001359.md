# An altered-specificity ubiquitin-conjugating enzyme/ubiquitin-protein ligase pair.
**Authors:** Winkler GS, Albert TK, Dominguez C, Legtenberg YI, Boelens R, Timmers HT
**Journal:** J Mol Biol (2004)
**DOI:** [10.1016/j.jmb.2004.01.031](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jmb.2004.01.031)

## Abstract

1. J Mol Biol. 2004 Mar 12;337(1):157-65. doi: 10.1016/j.jmb.2004.01.031.

An altered-specificity ubiquitin-conjugating enzyme/ubiquitin-protein ligase 
pair.

Winkler GS(1), Albert TK, Dominguez C, Legtenberg YI, Boelens R, Timmers HT.

Author information:
(1)Department of Physiological Chemistry, University Medical Centre Utrecht, 
Universiteitsweg 100, 3584 CG Utrecht, The Netherlands.

The human CCR4-NOT complex is a global regulator of RNA polymerase II 
transcription. Recently, we showed that the RING domain CNOT4 subunit contains 
intrinsic ubiquitin-protein ligase (E3) activity. Here we show that binding of 
the CNOT4 RING finger to the ubiquitin-conjugating enzyme (E2) UbcH5B is highly 
selective. To understand the basis for this interaction, we identified several 
basic residues of UbcH5B important for binding to CNOT4 by mutational analysis. 
Subsequently, we tested pairs of UbcH5B and CNOT4 mutants for restoration of 
interaction. Concomitant charge-alteration of E49 of CNOT4 and K63 of UbcH5B 
restored binding and re-created a functional enzyme pair, indicative of an 
electrostatic interaction between these residues. The corresponding amino acids 
in the yeast orthologues can also be used to create a similarly designed E2-E3 
enzyme pair. These are the first examples of altered-specificity E2-E3 enzyme 
pairs and give further insight into how E2-E3 specificity is obtained.
