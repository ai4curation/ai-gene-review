Comment in
    Nat Cell Biol. 2003 Nov;5(11):950-1. doi: 10.1038/ncb1103-950.

The concentrations and functions of many cellular proteins are regulated by the 
ubiquitin pathway. Cullin family proteins bind with the RING-finger protein Roc1 
to recruit the ubiquitin-conjugating enzyme (E2) to the ubiquitin ligase complex 
(E3). Cul1 and Cul7, but not other cullins, bind to an adaptor protein, Skp1. 
Cul1 associates with one of many F-box proteins through Skp1 to assemble various 
SCF-Roc1 E3 ligases that each selectively ubiquitinate one or more specific 
substrates. Here, we show that Cul3, but not other cullins, binds directly to 
multiple BTB domains through a conserved amino-terminal domain. In vitro, Cul3 
promoted ubiquitination of Caenorhabditis elegans MEI-1, a katanin-like protein 
whose degradation requires the function of both Cul3 and BTB protein MEL-26. We 
suggest that in vivo there exists a potentially large number of BCR3 
(BTB-Cul3-Roc1) E3 ubiquitin ligases.
