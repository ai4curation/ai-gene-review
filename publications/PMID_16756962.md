# XPA versus ERCC1 as chemosensitising agents to cisplatin and mitomycin C in prostate cancer cells: role of ERCC1 in homologous recombination repair.
**Authors:** Cummings M, Higginbottom K, McGurk CJ, Wong OG, Köberle B, Oliver RT, Masters JR
**Journal:** Biochem Pharmacol (2006)
**DOI:** [10.1016/j.bcp.2006.04.025](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.bcp.2006.04.025)

## Abstract

1. Biochem Pharmacol. 2006 Jul 14;72(2):166-75. doi: 10.1016/j.bcp.2006.04.025. 
Epub 2006 May 4.

XPA versus ERCC1 as chemosensitising agents to cisplatin and mitomycin C in 
prostate cancer cells: role of ERCC1 in homologous recombination repair.

Cummings M(1), Higginbottom K, McGurk CJ, Wong OG, Köberle B, Oliver RT, Masters 
JR.

Author information:
(1)Prostate Cancer Research Centre, Institute of Urology, University College 
London, and St. Bartholomew's Hospital, Department of Medical Oncology, London 
W1W 7EJ, UK.

Nucleotide excision repair is the principal mechanism for the removal of bulky 
DNA adducts caused by a range of chemotherapeutic drugs, and contributes to 
cisplatin resistance. In this study, we used synthetic siRNAs targeted to XPA 
and ERCC1 and compared their effectiveness in sensitising mismatch repair 
deficient prostate cancer cell lines to cisplatin and mitomycin C. 
Downregulation of ERCC1 sensitised DU145 and PC3 cells to cisplatin and 
mitomycin C. In contrast, XPA downregulation did not sensitise either cell line 
to mitomycin C, and only sensitised DU145 cells to cisplatin. The effects of 
ERCC1 downregulation may be due to its role in homologous recombination repair. 
Excision repair of cisplatin adducts in PC3 cells was attenuated to a similar 
extent by XPA and ERCC1 downregulation. Downregulation of XPA but not ERCC1 
caused an increase in the number of cisplatin-induced RAD51 foci in PC3 cells, 
suggesting that HRR is able to substitute for NER in these cells. We observed 
co-localisation of ERCC1 and RAD51 in cisplatin treated PC3 cells by 
immunofluorescence and co-immunoprecipitation, which may represent recruitment 
of ERCC1/XPF to sites of recombination repair. These results indicate that ERCC1 
is a broader therapeutic target than XPA with which to sensitise cancer cells to 
chemotherapy because of its additional role in recombination repair.
