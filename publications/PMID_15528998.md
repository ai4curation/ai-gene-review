# Cysteine-321 of human brain GABA transaminase is involved in intersubunit cross-linking.
**Authors:** Yoon CS, Kim DW, Jang SH, Lee BR, Choi HS, Choi SH, Kim SY, An JJ, Kwon OS, Kang TC, Won MH, Cho SW, Lee KS, Park J, Eum WS, Choi SY
**Journal:** Mol Cells (2004)

## Abstract

1. Mol Cells. 2004 Oct 31;18(2):214-9.

Cysteine-321 of human brain GABA transaminase is involved in intersubunit 
cross-linking.

Yoon CS(1), Kim DW, Jang SH, Lee BR, Choi HS, Choi SH, Kim SY, An JJ, Kwon OS, 
Kang TC, Won MH, Cho SW, Lee KS, Park J, Eum WS, Choi SY.

Author information:
(1)Department of Genetic Engineering and Research Institute for Bioscience and 
Biotechnology, Hallym University, Chunchon 200-702, Korea.

Gamma-aminobutyrate transaminase (GABA-T), a key homodimeric enzyme of the GABA 
shunt, converts the major inhibitory neurotransmitter GABA to succinic 
semialdehyde. We previously overexpressed, purified and characterized human 
brain GABA-T. To identify the structural and functional roles of the cysteinyl 
residue at position 321, we constructed various GABA-T mutants by site-directed 
mutagenesis. The purified wild type GABA-T enzyme was enzymatically active, 
whereas the mutant enzymes were inactive. Reaction of 1.5 sulfhydryl groups per 
wild type dimer with 5,5 cent-dithiobis-2-nitrobenzoic acid (DTNB) produced 
about 95% loss of activity. No reactive -SH groups were detected in the mutant 
enzymes. Wild type GABA-T, but not the mutants, existed as an oligomeric species 
of Mr = 100,000 that was dissociable by 2-mercaptoethanol. These results suggest 
that the Cys321 residue is essential for the catalytic function of GABA-T, and 
that it is involved in the formation of a disulfide link between two monomers of 
human brain GABA-T.
