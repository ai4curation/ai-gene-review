Meningiomas are tumors of the central nervous system that vary in their
presentation, ranging from benign and slow-growing to highly aggressive. The
standard method for diagnosing and classifying meningiomas involves invasive
surgery and can fail to provide accurate prognostic information. Liquid biopsy
methods, which exploit circulating tumor biomarkers such as DNA, extracellular
vesicles, micro-RNA, proteins, and more, offer a non-invasive and dynamic
approach for tumor classification, prognostication, and evaluating treatment
response. Currently, a clinically approved liquid biopsy test for meningiomas
does not exist. This review provides a discussion of current research and the
challenges of implementing liquid biopsy techniques for advancing meningioma
patient care.
