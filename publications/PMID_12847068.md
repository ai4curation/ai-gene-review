# Sphingosine kinase modulates microvascular tone and myogenic responses through activation of RhoA/Rho kinase.

1. Circulation. 2003 Jul 22;108(3):342-7. doi:

Sphingosine kinase modulates microvascular tone and myogenic responses through 
activation of RhoA/Rho kinase.

Bolz SS(1), Vogel L, Sollinger D, Derwand R, Boer C, Pitson SM, Spiegel S, Pohl 
U.

Author information:
(1)Institute of Physiology, Ludwig Maximilians University, Schillerstrasse 44,

BACKGROUND: RhoA and Rho kinase are important modulators of microvascular tone.
METHODS AND RESULTS: We tested whether sphingosine kinase (Sphk1) that generates 
the endogenous sphingolipid mediator sphingosine-1-phosphate (S1P) is part of a 
signaling cascade to activate the RhoA/Rho kinase pathway. Using a new 
transfection model, we report that resting tone and myogenic responses of 
isolated resistance arteries increased with forced expression of Sphk1 in smooth 
muscle cells of these arteries. Overexpression of a dominant negative Sphk1 
mutant or coexpression of dominant negative mutants of RhoA or Rho kinase 
together with Sphk1 completely inhibited development of tone and myogenic 
responses.
CONCLUSIONS: The tone-increasing effects of a Sphk1 overexpression suggest that 
Sphk1 may play an important role in the control of peripheral resistance.
