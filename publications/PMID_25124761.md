# The movement of the non-cell-autonomous transcription factor, SHORT-ROOT relies on the endomembrane system.
**Authors:** Wu S, Gallagher KL
**Journal:** Plant J (2014)
**DOI:** [10.1111/tpj.12640](https://doi.org/10.1111/tpj.12640)

## Abstract

1. Plant J. 2014 Nov;80(3):396-409. doi: 10.1111/tpj.12640. Epub 2014 Sep 18.

The movement of the non-cell-autonomous transcription factor, SHORT-ROOT relies 
on the endomembrane system.

Wu S(1), Gallagher KL.

Author information:
(1)Department of Biology, University of Pennsylvania, Philadelphia, PA, 19104, 
USA.

Plant cells are able to convey positional and developmental information between 
cells through the direct transfer of transcription factors. One well studied 
example of this is the SHORT-ROOT (SHR) protein, which moves from the stele into 
the neighboring ground tissue layer to specify endodermis. While it has been 
shown that SHR trafficking relies on plasmodesmata (PD), and interaction with 
the SHR INTERACTING EMBRYONIC LETHAL (SIEL) protein, little information is known 
about how SHR trafficking is controlled or how SIEL promotes the movement of 
SHR. Here we show that SHR can move from multiple different cell types in the 
root. Analysis of subcellular localization indicates that in the cytoplasm of 
root or leaf cells, SHR localizes to endosomes in a SIEL-dependent manner. 
Interference of early and late endosomes disrupts intercellular movement of SHR. 
Our findings reveal an essential role for the plant endomembrane, independent of 
secretion, in the intercellular trafficking of SHR.
