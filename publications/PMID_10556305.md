Silenced expression of the FMR1 gene is responsible for the fragile X syndrome. 
The FMR1 gene codes for an RNA binding protein (FMRP), which can shuttle between 
the nucleus and the cytoplasm and is found associated to polysomes in the 
cytoplasm. By two-hybrid assay in yeast, we identified a novel protein 
interacting with FMRP: nuclear FMRP interacting protein (NUFIP). NUFIP mRNA 
expression is strikingly similar to that of the FMR1 gene in neurones of cortex, 
hippocampus and cerebellum. At the subcellular level, NUFIP colocalizes with 
nuclear isoforms of FMRP in a dot-like pattern. NUFIP presents a C2H2 zinc 
finger motif and a nuclear localization signal, but has no homology to known 
proteins and shows RNA binding activity in vitro. NUFIP does not interact with 
the FMRP homologues encoded by the FXR1 and FXR2 genes. Thus, these results 
indicate a specific nuclear role for FMRP.
