# High ANKZF1 expression is associated with poor overall survival and recurrence-free survival in colon cancer.
**Authors:** Zhou X, Shang YN, Lu R, Fan CW, Mo XM
**Journal:** Future Oncol (2019)
**DOI:** [10.2217/fon-2018-0920](https://doi.org/10.2217/fon-2018-0920)

## Abstract

1. Future Oncol. 2019 Jun;15(18):2093-2106. doi: 10.2217/fon-2018-0920. Epub 2019
 Jul 1.

High ANKZF1 expression is associated with poor overall survival and
recurrence-free survival in colon cancer.

Zhou X(1), Shang YN(1), Lu R(1), Fan CW(1)(2)(3), Mo XM(1).

Author information:
(1)Laboratory of Stem Cell Biology, State Key Laboratory of Biotherapy, West

(2)Institute of Digestive Surgery, State Key Laboratory of Biotherapy, West

(3)Department of Clinical & Experimental Medicine, Division of Oncology, Faculty

Aim: To investigate the association and prognostic value of ANKZF1 gene for
survival in colorectal cancer, the mechanism of ANKZF1 level alteration and
correlated signaling pathways ANKZF1 is involved. Patients & methods: The Cancer
Genome Atlas COREAD dataset was analyzed by bioinformatical investigation.
Results: High ANKZF1 expression is associated with poor overall survival (hazard
ratio [HR]: 2.094; 95% CI: 1.188-3.689; p = 0.011) and recurrence-free survival
(HR: 1.762; 95% CI: 1.021-3.042; p = 0.042) in colon cancer. Bioinformatical
analysis showed ANKZF1 was upregulated by amplification and exon expression.
ANKZF1 was associated with angiogenesis and cancer signaling pathways.
Conclusion: High ANKZF1 is an independent factor of poor survival (overall
survival and recurrence-free survival) in colon cancer by taking part in
angiogenesis and some cancer signaling pathways.
