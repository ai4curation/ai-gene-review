# Insights into β2-adrenergic receptor binding from structures of the N-terminal lobe of ARRDC3.
**Authors:** Qi S, O'Hayre M, Gutkind JS, Hurley JH
**Journal:** Protein Sci (2014)
**DOI:** [10.1002/pro.2549](https://doi.org/10.1002/pro.2549)
**PMC:** [PMC4253811](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC4253811/)

## Abstract

1. Protein Sci. 2014 Dec;23(12):1708-16. doi: 10.1002/pro.2549. Epub 2014 Sep 26.

Insights into β2-adrenergic receptor binding from structures of the N-terminal 
lobe of ARRDC3.

Qi S(1), O'Hayre M, Gutkind JS, Hurley JH.

Author information:
(1)Department of Molecular and Cell Biology and California Institute for 
Quantitative Biosciences, University of California, Berkeley, Berkeley, 
California, 94720.

ARRDC3 is one of six known human α-arrestins, and has been implicated in the 
downregulation of the β2-adrenergic receptor (β2AR). ARRDC3 consists of a 
two-lobed arrestin fold and a C-terminal tail containing two PPYX motifs. In the 
current model for receptor downregulation by ARRDC3, the arrestin fold portion 
is thought to bind the receptor, while the PPXY motifs recruit ubiquitin ligases 
of the NEDD4 family. Here we report the crystal structures of the N-terminal 
lobe of human ARRDC3 in two conformations, at 1.73 and 2.8 Å resolution, 
respectively. The structures reveal a large electropositive region that is 
capable of binding phosphate ions of crystallization. Residues within the basic 
patch were shown to be important for binding to β2AR, similar to the situation 
with β-arrestins. This highlights potential parallels in receptor recognition 
between α- and β-arrestins.
