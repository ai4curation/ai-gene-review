# Identification and primary structure of five human NADH-ubiquinone oxidoreductase subunits.
**Authors:** Ton C, Hwang DM, Dempsey AA, Liew CC
**Journal:** Biochem Biophys Res Commun (1997)
**DOI:** [10.1006/bbrc.1997.7707](https://doi.org/10.1006/bbrc.1997.7707)

## Abstract

1. Biochem Biophys Res Commun. 1997 Dec 18;241(2):589-94. doi: 
10.1006/bbrc.1997.7707.

Identification and primary structure of five human NADH-ubiquinone 
oxidoreductase subunits.

Ton C(1), Hwang DM, Dempsey AA, Liew CC.

Author information:
(1)Department of Laboratory Medicine & Pathobiology, University of Toronto, 
Toronto Hospital, Ontario, Canada.

The multi-subunit NADH-ubiquinone oxidoreductase (complex I) is the first enzyme 
complex in the electron transport chain of mitochondria. A small number of 
NADH-ubiquinone oxidoreductase subunits are the products of mitochondrial genes 
(subunits 1-7), while the remainder are nuclear encoded and imported from the 
cytoplasm. We have isolated and sequenced five subunits of the human complex I 
from a human heart lambda ZAP Express cDNA library. Comparison of the deduced 
amino acid sequences of the human subunits with the corresponding bovine 
sequences revealed greater than 80% amino acid identity. The high degree of 
similarity between human and bovine sequences suggests functional conservation 
of these subunits in the complex I. In silico Northern analysis revealed that 
two of the subunits were expressed ubiquitously while the remainder may have 
more restricted patterns of expression.
