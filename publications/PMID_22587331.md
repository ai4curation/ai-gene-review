# Effects of the novel mitochondrial protein mimitin in insulin-secreting cells.
**Authors:** Hanzelka K, Skalniak L, Jura J, Lenzen S, Gurgul-Convey E
**Journal:** Biochem J (2012)
**DOI:** [10.1042/BJ20111920](https://doi.org/10.1042/BJ20111920)

## Abstract

1. Biochem J. 2012 Aug 1;445(3):349-59. doi: 10.1042/BJ20111920.

Effects of the novel mitochondrial protein mimitin in insulin-secreting cells.

Hanzelka K(1), Skalniak L, Jura J, Lenzen S, Gurgul-Convey E.

Author information:
(1)Institute of Clinical Biochemistry, Hannover Medical School, 30625 Hannover, 
Germany.

Mimitin, a novel mitochondrial protein, has been shown to act as a molecular 
chaperone for the mitochondrial complex I and to regulate ATP synthesis. During 
Type 1 diabetes development, pro-inflammatory cytokines induce mitochondrial 
damage in pancreatic β-cells, inhibit ATP synthesis and reduce glucose-induced 
insulin secretion. Mimitin was expressed in rat pancreatic islets including 
β-cells and decreased by cytokines. In the ob/ob mouse, a model of insulin 
resistance and obesity, mimitin expression was down-regulated in liver and 
brain, up-regulated in heart and kidney, but not affected in islets. To further 
analyse the impact of mimitin on β-cell function, two β-cell lines, one with a 
low (INS1E) and another with a higher (MIN6) mimitin expression were studied. 
Mimitin overexpression protected INS1E cells against cytokine-induced caspase 3 
activation, mitochondrial membrane potential reduction and ATP production 
inhibition, independently from the NF-κB (nuclear factor κB)-iNOS (inducible NO 
synthase) pathway. Mimitin overexpression increased basal and glucose-induced 
insulin secretion and prevented cytokine-mediated suppression of insulin 
secretion. Mimitin knockdown in MIN6 cells had opposite effects to those 
observed after overexpression. Thus mimitin has the capacity to modulate 
pancreatic islet function and to reduce cytokine toxicity.
