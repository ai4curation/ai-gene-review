# Purification and properties of an alpha-methylacyl-CoA racemase from rat liver.
**Authors:** Schmitz W, Fingerhut R, Conzelmann E
**Journal:** Eur J Biochem (1994)
**DOI:** [10.1111/j.1432-1033.1994.tb18870.x](https://doi.org/10.1111/j.1432-1033.1994.tb18870.x)

## Abstract

1. Eur J Biochem. 1994 Jun 1;222(2):313-23. doi: 
10.1111/j.1432-1033.1994.tb18870.x.

Purification and properties of an alpha-methylacyl-CoA racemase from rat liver.

Schmitz W(1), Fingerhut R, Conzelmann E.

Author information:
(1)Theodor-Boveri-Institut für Biowissenschaften (Biozentrum) der Universität 
Würzburg, Germany.

The (R)- and (S)-isomers of alpha-methyl-branched fatty acids were shown to be 
rapidly interconverted as coenzyme A thioesters, by an alpha-methylacyl-CoA 
racemase. The enzyme was purified some 5600-fold from rat liver, to apparent 
homogeneity. It is a monomer of 45 kDa with an isolectric point of pH 6.1 and is 
optimally active between pH 6 and pH 7. It acts only on coenzyme A thioesters, 
not on free fatty acids, and accepts as substrates a wide range of 
alpha-methylacyl-CoAs, including pristanoyl-CoA and trihydroxycoprostanoyl-CoA 
(an intermediate in bile acid synthesis), but neither 3-methyl-branched nor 
linear-chain acyl-CoAs. The racemase catalyzes a rapid exchange of the H atom in 
the alpha-position of the fatty acid against a proton from water, indicating 
that the mechanism involves abstraction of a proton. Based on this observation, 
a very sensitive and convenient radiometric assay, with 
2-methyl[2-(3)H]acyl-CoAs as substrates, was developed. The enzyme was 
inactivated by micromolar concentrations of Hg2+ and to a lesser extent by Cu2+ 
but not by iodoacetamide and only slightly by N-ethylmaleimide and thimerosal.
