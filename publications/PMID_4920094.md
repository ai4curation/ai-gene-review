# Analysis of T4 phage proteins. I. Conversion of precursor proteins into lower molecular weight peptides during normal capsid formation.
**Authors:** Hosoda J, Cone R
**Journal:** Proc Natl Acad Sci U S A (1970)
**DOI:** [10.1073/pnas.66.4.1275](https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.66.4.1275)
**PMC:** [PMC335816](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC335816/)

## Abstract

1. Proc Natl Acad Sci U S A. 1970 Aug;66(4):1275-81. doi: 10.1073/pnas.66.4.1275.

Analysis of T4 phage proteins. I. Conversion of precursor proteins into lower 
molecular weight peptides during normal capsid formation.

Hosoda J, Cone R.

Radioisotopically labeled T4-proteins extracted from purified capsids and phage 
and from infected cells were separated by gel electrophoresis in the presence of 
sodium dodecyl sulfate and a reducing reagent. They were identified by 
autoradiography and by counting of the fractionated gels. Four major protein 
bands (F, A, D, and E) were detected in capsid and phage. These accounted for 
more than 90% of the total capsid protein and 70% of the phage protein (60% of 
the total capsid protein was in A-band). Coelectrophoresis of [(14)C]proteins 
from capsids and [(3)H]proteins from phage-infected cells indicated that the 
protein coded by gene 23 (P23) was a peptide chain approximately 25% longer than 
A-protein. Pulse-chase experiments combined with differential extraction 
indicated that conversion of P23 into A-protein and alteration of the protein 
coded by gene 22 (P22) appeared to be vital steps in formation of normal 
capsids. Mutations in several other genes known to prevent normal capsid 
formation inhibited conversion of P23 to A-protein and alteration of P22.
