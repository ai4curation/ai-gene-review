# 6-PPD quinone induces response of nuclear hormone receptors in the germline associated with formation of reproductive toxicity in Caenorhabditis elegans.
**Authors:** Liu Z, Bian Q, Wang D
**Journal:** J Hazard Mater (2025)

Nuclear hormone receptors (NHRs) play an important role in modulating stress 
response. The 6-PPD quinone (6-PPDQ) caused inhibition in reproductive capacity 
in Caenorhabditis elegans. We further identified germline NHRs in response to 
6-PPDQ and with function in controlling 6-PPDQ reproductive toxicity. Among 
germline NHR genes, 9 genes were dysregulated by 6-PPDQ (10 μg/L). Alteration in 
expression of these 9 germline NHR genes caused by 1-100 μg/L 6-PPDQ was 
concentration dependent. Among these 9 NHR genes, 6-PPDQ reproductive toxicity 
was suppressed by nhr-47, nhr-14, daf-12, and nhr-249 RNAi and accelerated by 
nhr-12, nhr-145, and nhr-171 RNAi. After 6-PPDQ exposure, RNAi of DNA damage 
checkpoint genes affected genes controlling germline apoptosis, and these 
checkpoint genes expression was increased by nhr-12, nhr-145, and/or nhr-171 
RNAi, and decreased by daf-12, nhr-14, nhr-47, and/or nhr-249 RNAi. Meanwhile, 
after 6-PPDQ exposure, among ferroptosis related signals, gpx-6, ads-1, and 
ftn-1 RNAi increased daf-12, nhr-47, and nhr-249 expressions and inhibited 
nhr-12, nhr-145, and/or nhr-171 expression, and bli-3 RNAi decreased nhr-47, 
nhr-14, daf-12, and nhr-249 expressions. Therefore, exposure to 6-PPDQ activated 
or inhibited certain NHRs in the germline to cause reproductive toxicity in 
organisms. These observations emphasize the importance of germline NHRs in 
response to 6-PPDQ at environmentally relevant concentrations.
