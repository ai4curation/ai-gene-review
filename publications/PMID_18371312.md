# Acetyl-Coenzyme A acyltransferase 2 attenuates the apoptotic effects of BNIP3 in two human cell lines.
**Authors:** Cao W, Liu N, Tang S, Bao L, Shen L, Yuan H, Zhao X, Lu H
**Journal:** Biochim Biophys Acta (2008)
**DOI:** [10.1016/j.bbagen.2008.02.007](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.bbagen.2008.02.007)

## Abstract

1. Biochim Biophys Acta. 2008 Jun;1780(6):873-80. doi: 
10.1016/j.bbagen.2008.02.007. Epub 2008 Mar 6.

Acetyl-Coenzyme A acyltransferase 2 attenuates the apoptotic effects of BNIP3 in 
two human cell lines.

Cao W(1), Liu N, Tang S, Bao L, Shen L, Yuan H, Zhao X, Lu H.

Author information:
(1)State Key Laboratory of Genetic Engineering, Institute of Genetics, School of 
Life Sciences, Fudan University, Shanghai 200433, China.

BNIP3 is a unique pro-apoptotic protein which belongs to the BH3-only subset of 
the Bcl-2 family and localizes on mitochondrial membrane. Despite the inherent 
difficulty of identifying binding partners for membrane proteins, several 
binding partners for BNIP3 have been identified. In this study, a modified 
split-ubiquitin membrane yeast two-hybrid system was constructed and used to 
identify acetyl-Coenzyme A acyltransferase 2 (ACAA2) as a new BNIP3 binding 
partner. The interaction between BNIP3 and ACAA2 was confirmed by pull-down and 
co-immunoprecipitation assays. ACAA2 was also found to co-localize with BNIP3 in 
mitochondria. Furthermore, the apoptosis induced by over-expressed BNIP3 via 
transfection or hypoxia treatment was abolished by ACAA2 in human hepatocellular 
carcinoma HepG2 cells and osteosarcoma U-2 OS cells. These results strongly 
suggest that ACAA2 be a functional BNIP3 binding partner and provide a possible 
linkage between fatty acid metabolism and apoptosis of cells.
