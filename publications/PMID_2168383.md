Isolation and properties of acyl carrier protein phosphodiesterase of 
Escherichia coli.

Fischl AS(1), Kennedy EP.

Author information:
(1)Department of Biological Chemistry and Molecular Pharmacology, Harvard 
Medical School, Boston, Massachusetts 02115.

The acyl carrier protein (ACP) phosphodiesterase of Escherichia coli catalyzes 
the hydrolytic cleavage of the 4'-phosphopantetheine residue from ACP, with the 
generation of apo-ACP (P. R. Vagelos and A. R. Larrabee, J. Biol. Chem. 
242:1776-1781, 1967). Although it has been postulated to play a role in the 
regulation of fatty acid synthesis, presently available evidence makes this 
unlikely, and its physiological function requires further investigation. We have 
now purified the enzyme from E. coli more than 3,000-fold and have identified it 
as a protein of Mr 25,000, as judged from its migration during electrophoresis 
in gels containing sodium dodecyl sulfate. The enzyme has remarkable 
thermostability, being protected against irreversible inactivation at 90 degrees 
C by the presence of sodium dodecyl sulfate. A partial sequence of the amino 
terminus of the enzyme is as follows: 
H2N-Ser-Lys-Val-Leu-Val-Leu-Lys-Ser-?-Ile-Leu-Ala-Gly-Tyr-Ser-. Other properties 
of the enzyme are also described.
