# Retinoic acid and Cyp26b1 are critical regulators of osteogenesis in the axial skeleton.
**Authors:** Spoorendonk KM, Peterson-Maduro J, Renn J, Trowe T, Kranenbarg S, Winkler C, Schulte-Merker S
**Journal:** Development (2008)
**DOI:** [10.1242/dev.024034](https://doi.org/10.1242/dev.024034)

## Abstract

1. Development. 2008 Nov;135(22):3765-74. doi: 10.1242/dev.024034. Epub 2008 Oct 
16.

Retinoic acid and Cyp26b1 are critical regulators of osteogenesis in the axial 
skeleton.

Spoorendonk KM(1), Peterson-Maduro J, Renn J, Trowe T, Kranenbarg S, Winkler C, 
Schulte-Merker S.

Author information:
(1)Hubrecht Institute, University Medical Centre Utrecht, 3584 CT Utrecht, The 
Netherlands.

Retinoic acid (RA) plays important roles in diverse biological processes ranging 
from germ cell specification to limb patterning. RA ultimately exerts its effect 
in the nucleus, but how RA levels are being generated and maintained locally is 
less clear. Here, we have analyzed the zebrafish stocksteif mutant, which 
exhibits severe over-ossification of the entire vertebral column. stocksteif 
encodes cyp26b1, a cytochrome P450 member that metabolizes RA. The mutant is 
completely phenocopied by treating 4 dpf wild-type embryos with either RA or the 
pharmacological Cyp26 blocker R115866, thus identifying a previously 
unappreciated role for RA and cyp26b1 in osteogenesis of the vertebral column. 
Cyp26b1 is expressed within osteoblast cells, demonstrating that RA levels 
within these cells need to be tightly controlled. Furthermore, we have examined 
the effect of RA on osteoblasts in vivo. As numbers of osteoblasts do not change 
upon RA treatment, we suggest that RA causes increased activity of axial 
osteoblasts, ultimately resulting in defective skeletogenesis.
