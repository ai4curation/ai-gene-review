# Pinpointing phosphotyrosine-dependent interactions downstream of the collagen receptor DDR1.
**Authors:** Koo DH, McFadden C, Huang Y, Abdulhussein R, Friese-Hamim M, Vogel WF
**Journal:** FEBS Lett (2006)
**DOI:** [10.1016/j.febslet.2005.11.035](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.febslet.2005.11.035)

## Abstract

1. FEBS Lett. 2006 Jan 9;580(1):15-22. doi: 10.1016/j.febslet.2005.11.035. Epub 
2005 Dec 1.

Pinpointing phosphotyrosine-dependent interactions downstream of the collagen 
receptor DDR1.

Koo DH(1), McFadden C, Huang Y, Abdulhussein R, Friese-Hamim M, Vogel WF.

Author information:
(1)Department of Laboratory Medicine and Pathobiology, University of Toronto, 
Medical Sciences Building, Room 7334, 1 King's College Circle, Toronto, Ont., 
Canada M5S 1A8.

Activation of the receptor tyrosine kinase DDR1 by collagen results in robust 
and sustained phosphorylation, however little is known about its downstream 
mediators. Using phosphopeptide mapping and site-directed mutagenesis, we here 
identified multiple tyrosine phosphorylation sites within DDR1. We found that 
Nck2 and Shp-2, two SH2 domain-containing proteins, bind to DDR1 in a 
collagen-dependent manner. The binding site of Shp-2 was mapped to tyrosine-740 
of DDR1 within an ITIM-consensus sequence. Lastly, ablation of DDR1 in the mouse 
mammary gland resulted in delocalized expression of Nck2, suggesting that 
defects observed during alveologenesis are caused by the lack of the DDR1-Nck2 
interaction.
