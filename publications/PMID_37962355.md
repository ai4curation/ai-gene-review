Inactivation of EP300/CREBB paralogous cellular lysine acetyltransferases (KATs) 
during the early phase of infection is a consistent feature of DNA viruses. The 
cell responds by stabilizing transcription factor IRF3 which activates 
transcription of scores of interferon-stimulated genes (ISGs), inhibiting viral 
replication. Human respiratory adenoviruses counter this by assembling a 
CUL4-based ubiquitin ligase complex that polyubiquitinylates RUVBL1 and 2 
inducing their proteasomal degradation. This inhibits accumulation of active 
IRF3 and the expression of anti-viral ISGs, allowing replication of the 
respiratory HAdVs in the face of inhibition of EP300/CBEBBP KAT activity by the 
N-terminal region of E1A.
