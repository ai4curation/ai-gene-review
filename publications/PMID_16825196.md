# The structural and functional units of heteromeric amino acid transporters. The heavy subunit rBAT dictates oligomerization of the heteromeric amino acid transporters.
**Authors:** Fernández E, Jiménez-Vidal M, Calvo M, Zorzano A, Tebar F, Palacín M, Chillarón J
**Journal:** J Biol Chem (2006)
**DOI:** [10.1074/jbc.M604049200](https://doi.org/10.1074/jbc.M604049200)

## Abstract

1. J Biol Chem. 2006 Sep 8;281(36):26552-61. doi: 10.1074/jbc.M604049200. Epub

The structural and functional units of heteromeric amino acid transporters. The 
heavy subunit rBAT dictates oligomerization of the heteromeric amino acid 
transporters.

Fernández E(1), Jiménez-Vidal M, Calvo M, Zorzano A, Tebar F, Palacín M, 
Chillarón J.

Author information:
(1)Department of Biochemistry and Molecular Biology, Faculty of Biology,

Heteromeric amino acid transporters are composed of a catalytic light subunit 
and a heavy subunit linked by a disulfide bridge. We analyzed the structural and 
functional units of systems b0,+ and xC-, formed by the heterodimers b0,+ 
AT-rBAT and xCT-4F2hc, respectively. Blue Native gel electrophoresis, 
cross-linking, and fluorescence resonance energy transfer in vivo indicate that 
system b0,+ is a heterotetramer [b0,+ AT-rBAT]2, whereas xCT-4F2hc seems not to 
stably or efficiently oligomerize. However, substitution of the heavy subunit 
4F2hc for rBAT was sufficient to form a heterotetrameric [xCT-rBAT]2 structure. 
The functional expression of concatamers of two light subunits (which differ 
only in their sensitivity to inactivation by a sulfhydryl reagent) suggests that 
a single heterodimer is the functional unit of systems b0,+ and xC-.
