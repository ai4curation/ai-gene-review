# Peroxisomal alanine:glyoxylate aminotransferase deficiency in primary hyperoxaluria type I.
**Authors:** Danpure CJ, Jennings PR
**Journal:** FEBS Lett (1986)
**DOI:** [10.1016/0014-5793(86)80563-4](https://doi.org/10.1016/0014-5793(86)80563-4)

## Abstract

1. FEBS Lett. 1986 May 26;201(1):20-4. doi: 10.1016/0014-5793(86)80563-4.

Peroxisomal alanine:glyoxylate aminotransferase deficiency in primary 
hyperoxaluria type I.

Danpure CJ, Jennings PR.

Activities of alanine:glyoxylate aminotransferase in the livers of two patients 
with primary hyperoxaluria type I were substantially lower than those found in 
five control human livers. Detailed subcellular fractionation of one of the 
hyperoxaluric livers, compared with a control liver, showed that there was a 
complete absence of peroxisomal alanine:glyoxylate aminotransferase. This enzyme 
deficiency explains most of the biochemical characteristics of the disease and 
means that primary hyperoxaluria type I should be added to the rather select 
list of peroxisomal disorders.
