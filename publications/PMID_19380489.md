HIS-24 linker histone and SIR-2.1 deacetylase are involved in chromatin 
silencing in Caenorhabditis elegans. Depletion of SIR-2.1 results in cytoplasmic 
retention of HIS-24 in oocytes. However, the molecular working mechanisms of 
HIS-24 and SIR-2.1 are unclear. We show here a synergistic function of SIR-2.1 
and HIS-24 that are together essential for maintenance of the H3K27me3 mark in 
the germ line of C. elegans. We demonstrate the synthetic effects of the two 
factors on brood size, embryogenesis, and fertility. SIR-2.1 and HIS-24 
associate with the subtelomeric regions but apparently do not interact directly. 
We report that SIR-2.1 deacetylates H3K9 at subtelomeric regions and suggest 
that deacetylation of H3K9 is a prerequisite for H3K27 methylation. In turn, we 
found that HIS-24 specifically interacts with the histone H3 K27 region, when 
unmodified or in the trimethylated state. Overall, our data indicate that 
SIR-2.1 and HIS-24 contribute to the propagation of a specialized chromatin 
state at the subtelomeric regions and elsewhere in the genome.
