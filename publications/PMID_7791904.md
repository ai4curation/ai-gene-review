# Interaction between the retinoblastoma protein and the oncoprotein MDM2.
**Authors:** Xiao ZX, Chen J, Levine AJ, Modjtahedi N, Xing J, Sellers WR, Livingston DM
**Journal:** Nature (1995)
**DOI:** [10.1038/375694a0](https://doi.org/10.1038/375694a0)

## Abstract

1. Nature. 1995 Jun 22;375(6533):694-8. doi: 10.1038/375694a0.

Interaction between the retinoblastoma protein and the oncoprotein MDM2.

Xiao ZX(1), Chen J, Levine AJ, Modjtahedi N, Xing J, Sellers WR, Livingston DM.

Author information:
(1)Dana-Farber Cancer Institute, Boston, Massachusetts 02115, USA.

Inactivation of tumour-suppressor genes leads to deregulated cell proliferation 
and is a key factor in human tumorigenesis. Both p53 and retinoblastoma genes 
are frequently mutated in human cancers, and the simultaneous inactivation of RB 
and p53 is frequently observed in a variety of naturally occurring human 
tumours. Furthermore, three distinct DNA tumour virus groups--papovaviruses, 
adenoviruses and human papillomaviruses--transform cells by targeting and 
inactivating certain functions of both the p53 and retinoblastoma proteins. The 
cellular oncoprotein, Mdm2, binds to and downmodulates p53 function; its human 
homologue, MDM2, is amplified in certain human tumours, including sarcomas and 
gliomas. Overproduction of Mdm2 is both tumorigenic and capable of immortalizing 
primary rat embryo fibroblasts. Here we show that MDM2 interacts physically and 
functionally with pRB and, as with p53, inhibits pRB growth regulatory function. 
Therefore, both pRB and p53 can be subjected to negative regulation by the 
product of a single cellular protooncogene.
