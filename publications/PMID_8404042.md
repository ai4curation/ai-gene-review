# Chromosomal localization of the Ox-44 (CD53) leukocyte antigen gene in man and rodents.
**Authors:** Taguchi T, Bellacosa A, Zhou JY, Gilbert DJ, Lazo PA, Copeland NG, Jenkins NA, Tsichlis PN, Testa JR
**Journal:** Cytogenet Cell Genet (1993)

## Abstract

1. Cytogenet Cell Genet. 1993;64(3-4):217-21. doi: 10.1159/000133580.

Chromosomal localization of the Ox-44 (CD53) leukocyte antigen gene in man and 
rodents.

Taguchi T(1), Bellacosa A, Zhou JY, Gilbert DJ, Lazo PA, Copeland NG, Jenkins 
NA, Tsichlis PN, Testa JR.

Author information:
(1)Department of Medical Oncology, Fox Chase Cancer Center, Philadelphia, PA 
19111.

The Ox-44 (CD53) leukocyte antigen contributes to the transduction of 
CD2-generated signals in T cells and natural killer (NK) cells and has been 
suggested to play a role in growth regulation. The gene encoding this protein 
was assigned to the midportion of mouse chromosome 3 by interspecific backcross 
mapping and to human chromosome region 1p21-->p13.3 and rat chromosome region 
2q34-->q41 by fluorescence in situ hybridization. Comparative mapping data 
presented in this report demonstrate conservation of synteny within the region 
encompassing this gene in mouse (Cd53), human (CD53), and rat (CD53).
