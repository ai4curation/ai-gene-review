# Expression of activated Ras during Dictyostelium development alters cell localization and changes cell fate.
**Authors:** Jaffer ZM, Khosla M, Spiegelman GB, Weeks G
**Journal:** Development (2001)
**DOI:** [10.1242/dev.128.6.907](https://doi.org/10.1242/dev.128.6.907)

## Abstract

1. Development. 2001 Mar;128(6):907-16. doi: 10.1242/dev.128.6.907.

Expression of activated Ras during Dictyostelium development alters cell 
localization and changes cell fate.

Jaffer ZM(1), Khosla M, Spiegelman GB, Weeks G.

Author information:
(1)Department of Microbiology and Immunology, University of British Columbia, 
Vancouver, BC, V6T 1Z3, Canada.

There is now a body of evidence to indicate that Ras proteins play important 
roles in development. Dictyostelium expresses several ras genes and each appears 
to perform a distinct function. Previous data had indicated that the 
overexpression of an activated form of the major developmentally regulated gene, 
rasD, caused a major aberration in morphogenesis and cell type determination. We 
now show that the developmental expression of an activated rasG gene under the 
control of the rasD promoter causes a similar defect. Our results indicate that 
the expression of activated rasG in prespore cells results in their 
transdifferentiation into prestalk cells, whereas activated rasG expression in 
prestalk causes gross mislocalization of the prestalk cell populations.
