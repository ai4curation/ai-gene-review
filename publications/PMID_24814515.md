During the final stage of cell division, cytokinesis, the Aurora-B-dependent 
abscission checkpoint (NoCut) delays membrane abscission to avoid DNA damage and 
aneuploidy in cells with chromosome segregation defects. This arrest depends on 
Aurora-B-mediated phosphorylation of CHMP4C, a component of the endosomal 
sorting complex required for transport (ESCRT) machinery that mediates 
abscission, but the mechanism remains unknown. Here we describe ANCHR 
(Abscission/NoCut Checkpoint Regulator; ZFYVE19) as a key regulator of the 
abscission checkpoint, functioning through the most downstream component of the 
ESCRT machinery, the ATPase VPS4. In concert with CHMP4C, ANCHR associates with 
VPS4 at the midbody ring following DNA segregation defects to control abscission 
timing and prevent multinucleation in an Aurora-B-dependent manner. This 
association prevents VPS4 relocalization to the abscission zone and is relieved 
following inactivation of Aurora B to allow abscission. We propose that the 
abscission checkpoint is mediated by ANCHR and CHMP4C through retention of VPS4 
at the midbody ring.
