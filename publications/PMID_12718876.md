# GbetaL, a positive regulator of the rapamycin-sensitive pathway required for the nutrient-sensitive interaction between raptor and mTOR.
**Authors:** Kim DH, Sarbassov DD, Ali SM, Latek RR, Guntur KV, Erdjument-Bromage H, Tempst P, Sabatini DM
**Journal:** Mol Cell (2003)
**DOI:** [10.1016/s1097-2765(03)00114-x](https://doi.org/10.1016/s1097-2765(03)00114-x)

## Abstract

1. Mol Cell. 2003 Apr;11(4):895-904. doi: 10.1016/s1097-2765(03)00114-x.

GbetaL, a positive regulator of the rapamycin-sensitive pathway required for the
nutrient-sensitive interaction between raptor and mTOR.

Kim DH(1), Sarbassov DD, Ali SM, Latek RR, Guntur KV, Erdjument-Bromage H,
Tempst P, Sabatini DM.

Author information:
(1)Whitehead Institute for Biomedical Research and Department of Biology,
Massachusetts Institute of Technology, Nine Cambridge Center, 02142, USA.

mTOR and raptor are components of a signaling pathway that regulates mammalian
cell growth in response to nutrients and growth factors. Here, we identify a
member of this pathway, a protein named GbetaL that binds to the kinase domain
of mTOR and stabilizes the interaction of raptor with mTOR. Like mTOR and
raptor, GbetaL participates in nutrient- and growth factor-mediated signaling to
S6K1, a downstream effector of mTOR, and in the control of cell size. The
binding of GbetaL to mTOR strongly stimulates the kinase activity of mTOR toward
S6K1 and 4E-BP1, an effect reversed by the stable interaction of raptor with
mTOR. Interestingly, nutrients and rapamycin regulate the association between
mTOR and raptor only in complexes that also contain GbetaL. Thus, we propose
that the opposing effects on mTOR activity of the GbetaL- and raptor-mediated
interactions regulate the mTOR pathway.
