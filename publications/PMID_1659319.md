# Nucleoside phosphotransferase activity of human colon carcinoma cytosolic 5'-nucleotidase.
**Authors:** Tozzi MG, Camici M, Pesi R, Allegrini S, Sgarrella F, Ipata PL
**Journal:** Arch Biochem Biophys (1991)
**DOI:** [10.1016/0003-9861(91)90125-3](https://doi.org/10.1016/0003-9861(91)90125-3)

## Abstract

1. Arch Biochem Biophys. 1991 Dec;291(2):212-7. doi:
10.1016/0003-9861(91)90125-3.

Nucleoside phosphotransferase activity of human colon carcinoma cytosolic 
5'-nucleotidase.

Tozzi MG(1), Camici M, Pesi R, Allegrini S, Sgarrella F, Ipata PL.

Author information:
(1)Dipartimento di Fisiologia e Biochimica, Universitá di Pisa, Italy.

A cytosolic 5'-nucleotidase, acting preferentially on IMP and GMP, has been 
isolated from human colon carcinoma extracts. This enzyme activity catalyzes 
also the transfer of the phosphate group of 5'-nucleoside monophosphates 
(mainly, 5'-IMP, 5'-GMP, and their deoxycounterparts) to nucleosides 
(preferentially inosine and deoxyinosine, but also nucleoside analogs, such as 
8-azaguanosine and 2',3'-dideoxyinosine). It has been proposed that the enzyme 
mechanism involves the formation of a phosphorylated enzyme as an intermediate 
which can transfer the phosphate group either to water or to the nucleoside. The 
enzyme is activated by some effectors, such as ATP and 2,3-diphosphoglycerate. 
Results indicate that the effect of these activators is mainly to favor the 
transfer of the phosphate of the phosphorylated intermediate to the nucleoside 
(i.e., the nucleoside phosphotransferase activity). This finding is in 
accordance with previous suggestions that cytosolic 5'-nucleotidase cannot be 
considered a pure catabolic enzyme.
