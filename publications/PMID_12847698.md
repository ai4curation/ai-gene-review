# Phosphoribosylpyrophosphate synthetase overactivity as a cause of uric acid overproduction in a young woman.
**Authors:** García-Pavía P, Torres RJ, Rivero M, Ahmed M, García-Puig J, Becker MA
**Journal:** Arthritis Rheum (2003)
**DOI:** [10.1002/art.11058](https://doi.org/10.1002/art.11058)

## Abstract

1. Arthritis Rheum. 2003 Jul;48(7):2036-41. doi: 10.1002/art.11058.

Phosphoribosylpyrophosphate synthetase overactivity as a cause of uric acid 
overproduction in a young woman.

García-Pavía P(1), Torres RJ, Rivero M, Ahmed M, García-Puig J, Becker MA.

Author information:
(1)La Paz University Hospital, Madrid, Spain.

Overactivity of phosphoribosylpyrophosphate synthetase (PRS) is an X 
chromosome-linked disorder of purine metabolism that is characterized by gout 
with uric acid overproduction and, in some families, neurodevelopmental 
impairment. We present the case of a 24-year-old Spanish woman with renal colic 
and hyperuricemia, which first manifested at age 11 years. Results of enzymatic 
and genetic studies supported the view that accelerated purine nucleotide and 
uric acid production in this woman resulted from defective allosteric regulation 
of PRS activity, which is, in turn, a consequence of a mutation in one of the 
patient's PRPS1 genes: an A-to-T substitution at nucleotide 578, encoding 
leucine for histidine at amino acid residue 192 of the mature PRS1 isoform. A 
previous example of disordered regulation of PRS1 activity in a family with a 
different substitution at the same amino acid residue strengthens this proposed 
mechanism. This is the first reported instance of PRS overactivity in which the 
propositus and sole affected family member is a woman.
