# The protein encoded by the Arabidopsis homeotic gene agamous resembles transcription factors.
**Authors:** Yanofsky MF, Ma H, Bowman JL, Drews GN, Feldmann KA, Meyerowitz EM
**Journal:** Nature (1990)
**DOI:** [10.1038/346035a0](https://doi.org/10.1038/346035a0)

## Abstract

1. Nature. 1990 Jul 5;346(6279):35-9. doi: 10.1038/346035a0.

The protein encoded by the Arabidopsis homeotic gene agamous resembles 
transcription factors.

Yanofsky MF(1), Ma H, Bowman JL, Drews GN, Feldmann KA, Meyerowitz EM.

Author information:
(1)Division of Biology 156-29, California Institute of Technology, Pasadena 
91125.

Mutations in the homeotic gene agamous of the plant Arabidopsis cause the 
transformation of the floral sex organs. Cloning and sequence analysis of 
agamous suggest that it encodes a protein with a high degree of sequence 
similarity to the DNA-binding region of transcription factors from yeast and 
humans and to the product of a homeotic gene from Antirrhinum. The agamous gene 
therefore probably encodes a transcription factor that regulates genes 
determining stamen and carpel development in wild-type flowers.
