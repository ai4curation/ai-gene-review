# Structure and organization of the human galactocerebrosidase (GALC) gene.
**Authors:** Luzi P, Rafi MA, Wenger DA
**Journal:** Genomics (1995)
**DOI:** [10.1016/0888-7543(95)80230-j](https://doi.org/10.1016/0888-7543(95)80230-j)

## Abstract

1. Genomics. 1995 Mar 20;26(2):407-9. doi: 10.1016/0888-7543(95)80230-j.

Structure and organization of the human galactocerebrosidase (GALC) gene.

Luzi P(1), Rafi MA, Wenger DA.

Author information:
(1)Department of Medicine (Medical Genetics), Jefferson Medical College, 
Philadelphia, Pennsylvania 19107, USA.

The deficiency of galactocerebrosidase (GALC; EC 3.2.1.46) is responsible for 
globoid cell leukodystrophy (GLD, Krabbe disease) in humans and certain animals. 
This enzyme catalyzes the lysosomal hydrolysis of specific galactolipids 
including galactosylceramide (galactocerebroside) and galactosylsphingosine 
(psychosine), among others. Recently we cloned the full-length human GALC cDNA 
using amino acid sequence information obtained from GALC purified from human 
urine and brain. In this communication we describe the organization of the human 
GALC gene. The gene, of nearly 60 kb, consists of 17 exons, which, aside from 
the first and last, are relatively small, ranging from 39 to 181 nucleotides. 
The 16 introns range from 247 nucleotides to nearly 12 kb. The 5' untranslated 
region is GC-rich, containing no perfect CAAT or TATA sequences, similar to 
genes for other lysosomal proteins. This information will be useful for studies 
to identify mutations causing low GALC activity in all patients with GLD and to 
identify the homologous gene in the important animal models.
