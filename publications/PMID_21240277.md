DNA interstrand crosslink repair requires several classes of proteins, including 
structure-specific endonucleases and Fanconi anemia proteins. SLX4, which 
coordinates three separate endonucleases, was recently recognized as an 
important regulator of DNA repair. Here we report the first human individuals 
found to have biallelic mutations in SLX4. These individuals, who were 
previously diagnosed as having Fanconi anemia, add SLX4 as an essential 
component to the FA-BRCA genome maintenance pathway.
