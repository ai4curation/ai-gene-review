# ERO1-L, a human protein that favors disulfide bond formation in the endoplasmic reticulum.
**Authors:** Cabibbo A, Pagani M, Fabbri M, Rocchi M, Farmery MR, Bulleid NJ, Sitia R
**Journal:** J Biol Chem (2000)
**DOI:** [10.1074/jbc.275.7.4827](https://doi.org/10.1074/jbc.275.7.4827)

## Abstract

1. J Biol Chem. 2000 Feb 18;275(7):4827-33. doi: 10.1074/jbc.275.7.4827.

ERO1-L, a human protein that favors disulfide bond formation in the endoplasmic 
reticulum.

Cabibbo A(1), Pagani M, Fabbri M, Rocchi M, Farmery MR, Bulleid NJ, Sitia R.

Author information:
(1)Department of Biological and Technological Research, San Raffaele Scientific 
Institute, Via Olgettina 58, 20132 Milano, Italy. caribbo.andrea@hsr.it

Oxidizing conditions must be maintained in the endoplasmic reticulum (ER) to 
allow the formation of disulfide bonds in secretory proteins. Here we report the 
cloning and characterization of a mammalian gene (ERO1-L) that shares extensive 
homology with the Saccharomyces cerevisiae ERO1 gene, required in yeast for 
oxidative protein folding. When expressed in mammalian cells, the product of the 
human ERO1-L gene co-localizes with ER markers and displays Endo-H-sensitive 
glycans. In isolated microsomes, ERO1-L behaves as a type II integral membrane 
protein. ERO1-L is able to complement several phenotypic traits of the yeast 
thermosensitive mutant ero1-1, including temperature and dithiothreitol 
sensitivity, and intrachain disulfide bond formation in carboxypeptidase Y. 
ERO1-L is no longer functional when either one of the highly conserved Cys-394 
or Cys-397 is mutated. These results strongly suggest that ERO1-L is involved in 
oxidative ER protein folding in mammalian cells.
