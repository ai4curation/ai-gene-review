We have performed an in vitro study to investigate the molecular basis of the 
aggregation kinetic of 1-40 beta-amyloid peptides (Abeta and the possibility of 
affecting this aggregation process using an exogenous natural polycyclic 
pigment, hypericin (Hyp). The effect of Hyp on the self-assembly process at 
different times of the aggregation kinetic has been investigated utilizing a 
chaperon-like molecule, alpha-crystallin. Circular dichroism and fluorescence 
results suggest that Hyp can associate to precursors of the mature fibrils and 
perturb the aggregation process through intermolecular interactions with the 
Abeta peptides.
