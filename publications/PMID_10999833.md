# Evidence for the presence of angiogenin in human follicular fluid and the up-regulation of its production by human chorionic gonadotropin and hypoxia.
**Authors:** Koga K, Osuga Y, Tsutsumi O, Momoeda M, Suenaga A, Kugu K, Fujiwara T, Takai Y, Yano T, Taketani Y
**Journal:** J Clin Endocrinol Metab (2000)
**DOI:** [10.1210/jcem.85.9.6837](https://doi.org/10.1210/jcem.85.9.6837)

## Abstract

1. J Clin Endocrinol Metab. 2000 Sep;85(9):3352-5. doi: 10.1210/jcem.85.9.6837.

Evidence for the presence of angiogenin in human follicular fluid and the 
up-regulation of its production by human chorionic gonadotropin and hypoxia.

Koga K(1), Osuga Y, Tsutsumi O, Momoeda M, Suenaga A, Kugu K, Fujiwara T, Takai 
Y, Yano T, Taketani Y.

Author information:
(1)Department of Obstetrics and Gynecology, University of Tokyo, Japan.

Angiogenesis is an essential event during the development of the ovarian 
follicle and ensuing formation of the corpus luteum. We investigated the 
presence of angiogenin, a potent inducer of angiogenesis, and the regulatory 
mechanisms of its production in the human ovary. Follicular fluid (FF) and 
granulosa cells (GCs) were collected from women undergoing in vitro 
fertilization and embryo transfer. The presence of angiogenin in FF and GCs was 
demonstrated by Western blot analysis. The production of angiogenin by cultured 
GCs was stimulated with the addition of human CG or cAMP or under the hypoxic 
milieu. Concentrations of angiogenin in FF from an individual follicle were 
positively correlated with those of progesterone, but not estradiol and 
testosterone. Given the presence of angiogenin in FF and up-regulation of its 
production by human CG and hypoxia, it seems logical to assume that angiogenin 
may play a role as a local angiogenic factor in the human ovary.
