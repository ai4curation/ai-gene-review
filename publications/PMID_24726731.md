Formation of an encounter complex is important for efficient protein complex 
formation. The encounter state consists of an ensemble of orientations of two 
proteins in the complex. Experimental description of such ensembles inherently 
suffers from insufficient data availability. We have measured paramagnetic 
relaxation enhancements (PRE) on cytochrome c peroxidase (CcP) caused by its 
partner cytochrome c (Cc) carrying a spin label. The data complement earlier PRE 
data of spin labelled CcP, identifying several new interactions. This work 
demonstrates the need of obtaining as many independent data sets as possible to 
achieve the most accurate description of an encounter complex.
