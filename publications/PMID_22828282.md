# The C-terminal domain of human Rev1 contains independent binding sites for DNA polymerase η and Rev7 subunit of polymerase ζ.
**Authors:** Pustovalova Y, Bezsonova I, Korzhnev DM
**Journal:** FEBS Lett (2012)
**DOI:** [10.1016/j.febslet.2012.07.021](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.febslet.2012.07.021)
**PMC:** [PMC3572780](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC3572780/)

## Abstract

1. FEBS Lett. 2012 Sep 21;586(19):3051-6. doi: 10.1016/j.febslet.2012.07.021.
Epub  2012 Jul 22.

The C-terminal domain of human Rev1 contains independent binding sites for DNA 
polymerase η and Rev7 subunit of polymerase ζ.

Pustovalova Y(1), Bezsonova I, Korzhnev DM.

Author information:
(1)Department of Molecular, Microbial and Structural Biology, University of 
Connecticut Health Center, Farmington, CT 06030, USA.

Human Rev1 is a translesion synthesis (TLS) DNA polymerase involved in bypass 
replication across sites of DNA damage and postreplicational gap-filling. Rev1 
plays an essential structural role in TLS by providing a binding platform for 
other TLS polymerases that insert nucleotides across DNA lesions (polη, polι, 
polκ) and extend the distorted primer-terminus (polς). We use NMR spectroscopy 
to demonstrate that the Rev1 C-terminal domain utilizes independent interaction 
interfaces to simultaneously bind a fragment of the 'inserter' polη and Rev7 
subunit of the 'extender' polς, thereby serving as a cassette that may 
accommodate several polymerases making them instantaneously available for TLS.
