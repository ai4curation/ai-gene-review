# Conversion of chenodeoxycholic acid to cholic acid by human CYP8B1.
**Authors:** Fan L, Joseph JF, Durairaj P, Parr MK, Bureik M
**Journal:** Biol Chem (2019)
**DOI:** [10.1515/hsz-2018-0379](https://doi.org/10.1515/hsz-2018-0379)

## Abstract

1. Biol Chem. 2019 Apr 24;400(5):625-628. doi: 10.1515/hsz-2018-0379.

Conversion of chenodeoxycholic acid to cholic acid by human CYP8B1.

Fan L(1), Joseph JF(2)(3), Durairaj P(1), Parr MK(2), Bureik M(1).

Author information:

(2)Freie Universitaet Berlin, Institute of Pharmacy, Pharmaceutical and

(3)Freie Universitaet Berlin, Department of Biology, Chemistry, Pharmacy, Core

The human cytochrome P450 enzyme CYP8B1 is a crucial regulator of the balance of 
cholic acid (CA) and chenodeoxycholic acid (CDCA) in the liver. It was 
previously shown to catalyze the conversion of 7α-hydroxycholest-4-en-3-one, a 
CDCA precursor, to 7α,12α-dihydroxycholest-4-en-3-one, which is an intermediate 
of CA biosynthesis. In this study we demonstrate that CYP8B1 can also convert 
CDCA itself to CA. We also show that five derivatives of luciferin are 
metabolized by CYP8B1 and established a rapid and convenient inhibitor test 
system. In this way we were able to identify four new CYP8B1 inhibitors, which 
are aminobenzotriazole, exemestane, ketoconazole and letrozole.
