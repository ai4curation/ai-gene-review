# Transformation of cell adhesion properties by exogenously introduced E-cadherin cDNA.
**Authors:** Nagafuchi A, Shirayoshi Y, Okazaki K, Yasuda K, Takeichi M
**Journal:** Nature (1987)
**DOI:** [10.1038/329341a0](https://doi.org/10.1038/329341a0)

## Abstract

1. Nature. 1987 Sep 24-30;329(6137):341-3. doi: 10.1038/329341a0.

Transformation of cell adhesion properties by exogenously introduced E-cadherin 
cDNA.

Nagafuchi A, Shirayoshi Y, Okazaki K, Yasuda K, Takeichi M.

E-cadherin is a cell surface glycoprotein responsible for Ca2+-dependent 
intercellular adhesion between epithelial cells; it is also called uvomorulin, 
L-CAM (ref. 3), cell-CAM 120/80 (ref.4) or Arc-1 (ref. 5). Because blocking the 
action of E-cadherin by monoclonal antibodies causes dispersion of compact cell 
colonies, this molecule is thought to be an important factor for maintenance of 
multicellular systems. To demonstrate directly that E-cadherin is involved in 
cell-cell adhesion, we cloned full-length cDNA encoding E-cadherin from F9 cells 
and introduced it into L fibroblasts deficient in E-cadherin. These L cells 
acquire strong Ca2+-dependent aggregating activity by expressing the E-cadherin 
derived from the introduced cDNA and were morphologically transformed so as to 
form colonies in which cells were tightly connected to each other.
