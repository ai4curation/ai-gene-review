# A glycopeptide regulating stem cell fate in Arabidopsis thaliana.
**Authors:** Ohyama K, Shinohara H, Ogawa-Ohnishi M, Matsubayashi Y
**Journal:** Nat Chem Biol (2009)
**DOI:** [10.1038/nchembio.182](https://doi.org/10.1038/nchembio.182)

## Abstract

1. Nat Chem Biol. 2009 Aug;5(8):578-80. doi: 10.1038/nchembio.182. Epub 2009 Jun
7.

A glycopeptide regulating stem cell fate in Arabidopsis thaliana.

Ohyama K(1), Shinohara H, Ogawa-Ohnishi M, Matsubayashi Y.

Author information:
(1)Graduate School of Bio-Agricultural Sciences, Nagoya University, Chikusa, 
Nagoya, Japan.

The secreted peptide gene CLAVATA3 (CLV3) regulates stem cell fate in the shoot 
apical meristem in Arabidopsis thaliana plants, but the molecular structure of 
the active mature CLV3 peptide is controversial. Here, using nano-LC-MS/MS 
analysis of apoplastic peptides of A. thaliana plants overexpressing CLV3, we 
show that CLV3 is a 13-amino-acid arabinosylated glycopeptide. 
Post-translational arabinosylation of CLV3 is critical for its biological 
activity and high-affinity binding to its receptor CLV1.
