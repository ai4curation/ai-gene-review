# Cloning, expression and characterization of a Baeyer-Villiger monooxygenase from Pseudomonas putida KT2440.
**Authors:** Rehdorf J, Kirschner A, Bornscheuer UT
**Journal:** Biotechnol Lett (2007)
**DOI:** [10.1007/s10529-007-9401-y](https://doi.org/10.1007/s10529-007-9401-y)

## Abstract

1. Biotechnol Lett. 2007 Sep;29(9):1393-8. doi: 10.1007/s10529-007-9401-y. Epub 
2007 May 26.

Cloning, expression and characterization of a Baeyer-Villiger monooxygenase from 
Pseudomonas putida KT2440.

Rehdorf J(1), Kirschner A, Bornscheuer UT.

Author information:
(1)Department of Biotechnology and Enzyme Catalysis, Institute of Biochemistry, 
Greifswald University, Greifswald, Germany.

The gene encoding a Baeyer-Villiger monooxygenase and identified in Pseudomonas 
putida KT2440 was cloned and functionally expressed in Escherichia coli. The 
highest yield of soluble protein could be achieved by co-expression of molecular 
chaperones. In order to determine the substrate specificity, biocatalyses were 
performed using crude cell extract, growing and resting cells. Examination of 
aromatic, cyclic and aliphatic ketones revealed a high specificity towards 
short-chain aliphatic ketones. Interestingly, some open-chain ketones were 
converted to the alkylacetates, while for others formation of the ester products 
with oxygen on the other side of the keto group could also be detected yielding 
the corresponding methyl or ethyl esters.
