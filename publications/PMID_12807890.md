# The mitochondrial ornithine transporter. Bacterial expression, reconstitution, functional characterization, and tissue distribution of two human isoforms.

Two isoforms of the human ornithine carrier, ORC1 and ORC2, have been identified 
by overexpression of the proteins in bacteria and by study of the transport 
properties of the purified proteins reconstituted into liposomes. Both transport 
L-isomers of ornithine, lysine, arginine, and citrulline by exchange and by 
unidirectional mechanisms, and they are inactivated by the same inhibitors. ORC2 
has a broader specificity than ORC1, and L- and D-histidine, L-homoarginine, and 
D-isomers of ornithine, lysine, and ornithine are all substrates. Both proteins 
are expressed in a wide range of human tissues, but ORC1 is the predominant 
form. The highest levels of expression of both isoforms are in the liver. Five 
mutant forms of ORC1 associated with the human disease 
hyperornithinemia-hyperammonemia-homocitrullinuria were also made. The mutations 
abolish the transport properties of the protein. In patients with 
hyperornithinemia-hyperammonemia-homocitrullinuria, isoform ORC2 is unmodified, 
and its presence compensates partially for defective ORC1.
