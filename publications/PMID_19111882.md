Comment in
    Cancer Cell. 2009 Jan 6;15(1):5-6. doi: 10.1016/j.ccr.2008.12.008.

In human neuroblastoma, amplification of the MYCN gene predicts poor prognosis 
and resistance to therapy. In a shRNA screen of genes that are highly expressed 
in MYCN-amplified tumors, we have identified AURKA as a gene that is required 
for the growth of MYCN-amplified neuroblastoma cells but largely dispensable for 
cells lacking amplified MYCN. Aurora A has a critical function in regulating 
turnover of the N-Myc protein. Degradation of N-Myc requires sequential 
phosphorylation by cyclin B/Cdk1 and Gsk3. N-Myc is therefore degraded during 
mitosis in response to low levels of PI3-kinase activity. Aurora A interacts 
with both N-Myc and the SCF(Fbxw7) ubiquitin ligase that ubiquitinates N-Myc and 
counteracts degradation of N-Myc, thereby uncoupling N-Myc stability from growth 
factor-dependent signals.
