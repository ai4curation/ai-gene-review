# The F-box protein Fbxo7 interacts with human inhibitor of apoptosis protein cIAP1 and promotes cIAP1 ubiquitination.
**Authors:** Chang YF, Cheng CM, Chang LK, Jong YJ, Yuo CY
**Journal:** Biochem Biophys Res Commun (2006)
**DOI:** [10.1016/j.bbrc.2006.02.061](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.bbrc.2006.02.061)

## Abstract

1. Biochem Biophys Res Commun. 2006 Apr 21;342(4):1022-6. doi: 
10.1016/j.bbrc.2006.02.061. Epub 2006 Feb 21.

The F-box protein Fbxo7 interacts with human inhibitor of apoptosis protein 
cIAP1 and promotes cIAP1 ubiquitination.

Chang YF(1), Cheng CM, Chang LK, Jong YJ, Yuo CY.

Author information:
(1)Faculty of Biomedical Science and Environmental Biology, Kaohsiung Medical 
University, Kaohsiung 807, Taiwan, ROC.

Human cIAP1 protein is a member of the inhibitor of apoptosis proteins (IAPs) 
that are involved in apoptosis regulation and an increasing number of other 
functions, including cell cycle and intracellular signal transduction. In order 
to identify novel proteins involved in cIAP1 regulation, we performed a yeast 
two-hybrid screen and identified an F-box protein Fbxo7 as a cIAP1 interacting 
protein. Co-immunoprecipitation assay showed that cIAP1 can interact with Fbxo7 
in human cells. When co-expressed in cells, cIAP1 and Fbxo7 co-localized 
remarkably both in the cytoplasm and nucleus, and considerable amounts of these 
often co-localized at one or few distinct Golgi-like structures close to the 
nucleus. Furthermore, we showed that overexpression of Fbxo7 promotes the 
ubiquitination of cIAP1. Since F-box proteins are specificity determining 
subunits of SCF ubiquitin protein ligases, our results suggest that Fbxo7 can 
mediate the ubiquitination of cIAP1 by SCF ubiquitin protein ligase and thus 
have important implication in the regulation of cIAP1 function.
