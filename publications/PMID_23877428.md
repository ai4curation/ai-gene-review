## Full Text

Abstract

Here we report that ILK localizes in the mouse primary cilium, a sensory organelle required for signalling by the Hedgehog (Hh) pathway. Genetic or pharmacological inhibition of ILK blocks ciliary accumulation of the Hh pathway effector smoothened (Smo) and suppresses the induction of Gli transcription factor mRNAs by SHh. Conditional deletion of ILK or Smo also inhibits SHh-driven activation of Gli2 in the embryonic mouse cerebellum. ILK regulation of Hh signalling probably requires the physical interaction of ILK and Smo in the cilium, and we also show selective cilia-associated interaction of ILK with β-arrestin, a known mediator of Smo-dependent signalling.

INTRODUCTION

The primary cilium is a microtubule-based sensory organelle projecting singly from the surface of most vertebrate cells. The cilium coordinates protein interactions governing signal transduction by the Hedgehog (Hh) developmental pathway [ 1–5 ]. Signalling is initiated by Hh ligand interacting with and inducing movement of the Patched (Ptc1) receptor out of the cilium, thereby derepressing the essential pathway effector smoothened (Smo), a G-protein coupled receptor (GPCR)-like molecule. Activated Smo translocates from endomembranes into the cilium where it activates Gli transcription factors, which then transport to the nucleus to regulate gene expression. In the absence of Hh ligand, full-length Gli factors are processed in the cilium to produce truncated repressors of Hh signalling. Hh-induced ciliary translocation and activation of Smo inhibits processing, producing active Glis that are thought to undergo retrograde transport to the nucleus [ 6 ]. Smo activation is incompletely understood [ 7 ], however it requires phosphorylation in the cilium by GRK2 and CK1α [ 8 ]. The cilium forms at the location where the mother centriole (centrosome) docks to the plasma membrane after mitosis, forming the basal body, a microtubule-organizing centre that gives rise to the primary cilium in a process requiring bidirectional intraflagellar transport (IFT) proteins, which regulate ciliary protein transport and assembly [ 9 ]. Accordingly, IFT proteins mediate developmental Hh signalling [ 2 ], and ift mutations can deregulate signalling through altered localization of Smo and Gli within the cilium [ 10 ].

Sonic Hedgehog (SHh) is critical for development of the cerebellum, a hindbrain structure that governs fine motor coordination in mammals, and regulates proliferation of granule cell precursors (GCPs), which mature to comprise the major neuronal population in the adult cerebellum [ 11 , 12 ]. The integrin-linked kinase (ILK) is an essential effector of β1 integrin signalling, and ablation of either ILK or β1 integrin in the CNS causes defects in cerebellar development suggestive of interactions in the Hh pathway [ 13 , 14 ]. Inhibition of ILK [ 14 ], but not β1 integrin [ 13 ], suppresses GCP proliferation ex vivo , indicating ILK has an integrin-independent role in SHh-induced GCP proliferation. Using embryonic cerebellar development as a model of Hh signalling, we present in vivo evidence for ILK-mediated Hh activation in GCPs. The identification of ILK in ciliary proteomic screens [ 15 , 16 ] prompted us to investigate a potential role in ciliary Hh signalling. We find that ILK functions to regulate Hh-induced Smo ciliary translocation, associated with close physical interaction with Smo in the cilium. ILK also interacts at the ciliary base with β-arrestins, which have an essential role in Smo ciliary translocation, thus indicating a novel interaction governing Smo-dependent signalling.

METHODS

Mice . Math1-Cre; ilk loxP/loxP mice were generated by mating Math1-Cre +/− [ 20 ] with ilk loxP/loxP and backcrossing the Math1-Cre; ilk loxP/+ progeny with ilk loxP/loxP [ 39 ]. Math1-Cre; Smo loxP/loxP mice were generated by mating Math1-Cre +/− with Smo loxP/loxP [ 40 ] and backcrossing the Math1-Cre; Smo loxP/+ progeny with Smo loxP/loxP .

Immunohistochemistry . Whole heads from E18.5 mice were fixed in formalin for 2 days at room temperature. Paraffin-embedded sagittal sections (4 μm) of the vermis region were used for IHC.

GCP isolation and FACS analysis . The cerebellum was dissected from day 7 post-partum mice, minced into small pieces and digested with Dispase (BD) for 15 min, then DnaseI for 10 min and fixed in 4% PFA, followed by sorting using HNK1 antibody.

Plasmids . Split Venus plasmids (referred to as V1 and V2) were kindly provided, by Drs Stephen Michnick and Jean-François Paradis (University of Montreal) for BiFC assays.

Cell culture and transfections . IMCD3 and C3H10T1/2 cells were obtained from ATCC and maintained in DMEM containing 10% FCS. For siRNA experiments, cells were transfected with non- or gene-targeted siRNAs (0.1 μM, SmartPool, Dharmacon) using Lipofectamine 2000 (Invitrogen). Primary MEFs were isolated from E13.5 wild-type and ILK loxP/loxP mice.

Quantitative RT–PCR . mRNA was extracted using innuPREP RNA mini kit (Analytik Jena AG, Germany) and cDNA synthesized using using Cells-to-Ct reverse transcription kit (Applied Biosystems), and transcript levels measured using Taqman probes and normalized to GAPDH levels.

Western blotting . Cells were lysed in RIPA buffer and western blots conducted using standard protocols for the Odyssey system (Licor). ILK, panActin and GFP (detecting V2 or V1 fluorophore, Roche) were detected with either AlexaFluor 680 (Invitrogen) or IRDye800 (Rockland) secondary antibodies.

Immunofluorescence . Cells were fixed in formalin for 10 min at RT, permeablized and then re-fixed in formalin. Cells were blocked in blocking buffer and incubated with the relevant primary antibodies for 1 h at room temperature. Antibodies were detected with AlexaFluor (Invitrogen) secondary antibodies before mounting with Prolong Gold Antifade (Invitrogen).

Immunofluorescence data acquisition . Immunofluorescence data for Smo translocation were collected using a Nikon C1 laser scanning confocal microscope, and data for Venus experiments were collected using a Deltavision confocal microscope.

Venus quantification and Primary Cilia analyses . Venus interactions and primary cilia identification and quantitation were performed using Imaris analysis software (Bitplane AG, v7.0), using the fluorescence signal of acetylated α-tubulin to trace out enclosed volumes. Cilia length was analysed using Imaris v7.6.1 (Bitplane AG).

AP induction by SHh and SAG in C3H10T1/2 cells . C3H10T1/2 AP assay was conducted as previously described [ 24 ].
