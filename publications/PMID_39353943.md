Immune surveillance by cytotoxic T cells eliminates tumor cells and cells
infected by intracellular pathogens. This process relies on the presentation of
antigenic peptides by Major Histocompatibility Complex class I (MHC-I) at the
cell surface. The loading of these peptides onto MHC-I depends on the peptide
loading complex (PLC) at the endoplasmic reticulum (ER). Here, we uncovered that
MHC-I antigen presentation is regulated by ER-associated degradation (ERAD), a
protein quality control process essential to clear misfolded and unassembled
proteins. An unbiased proteomics screen identified the PLC component Tapasin,
essential for peptide loading onto MHC-I, as a substrate of the RNF185/Membralin
ERAD complex. Loss of RNF185/Membralin resulted in elevated Tapasin steady state
levels and increased MHC-I at the surface of professional antigen presenting
cells. We further show that RNF185/Membralin ERAD complex recognizes unassembled
Tapasin and limits its incorporation into PLC. These findings establish a novel
mechanism controlling antigen presentation and suggest RNF185/Membralin as a
potential therapeutic target to modulate immune surveillance.
