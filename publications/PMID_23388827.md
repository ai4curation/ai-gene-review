# mTOR complex 2 phosphorylates IMP1 cotranslationally to promote IGF2 production and the proliferation of mouse embryonic fibroblasts.
**Authors:** Dai N, Christiansen J, Nielsen FC, Avruch J
**Journal:** Genes Dev (2013)
**DOI:** [10.1101/gad.209130.112](https://doi.org/10.1101/gad.209130.112)

## Abstract

1. Genes Dev. 2013 Feb 1;27(3):301-12. doi: 10.1101/gad.209130.112.

mTOR complex 2 phosphorylates IMP1 cotranslationally to promote IGF2 production
and the proliferation of mouse embryonic fibroblasts.

Author information:
(1)Department of Molecular Biology, Massachusetts General Hospital, Boston, MA

Lack of IGF2 in mice results in diminished embryonic growth due to diminished
cell proliferation. Here we show that mouse embryonic fibroblasts lacking the
RNA-binding protein IMP1 (IGF2 mRNA-binding protein 1) have defective splicing
and translation of IGF2 mRNAs, markedly reduced IGF2 polypeptide production, and
diminished proliferation. The proliferation of the IMP1-null fibroblasts can be
restored to wild-type levels by IGF2 in vitro or by re-expression of IMP1, which
corrects the defects in IGF2 RNA splicing and translation. The ability of IMP1
to correct these defects is dependent on IMP1 phosphorylation at Ser181, which
is catalyzed cotranslationally by mTOR complex 2 (mTORC2). Phosphorylation
strongly enhances IMP1 binding to the IGF2-leader 3 5' untranslated region,
which is absolutely required to enable IGF2-leader 3 mRNA translational
initiation by internal ribosomal entry. These findings uncover a new mechanism
by which mTOR regulates organismal growth by promoting IGF2 production in the
mouse embryo through mTORC2-catalyzed cotranslational IMP1/IMP3 phosphorylation.
Inasmuch as TORC2 is activated by association with ribosomes, the present
results indicate that mTORC2-catalyzed cotranslational protein phosphorylation
is a core function of this complex.
