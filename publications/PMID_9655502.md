# Diversity revealed by a novel family of cadherins expressed in neurons at a synaptic complex.
**Authors:** Kohmura N, Senzaki K, Hamada S, Kai N, Yasuda R, Watanabe M, Ishii H, Yasuda M, Mishina M, Yagi T
**Journal:** Neuron (1998)
**DOI:** [10.1016/s0896-6273(00)80495-x](https://doi.org/10.1016/s0896-6273(00)80495-x)

## Abstract

1. Neuron. 1998 Jun;20(6):1137-51. doi: 10.1016/s0896-6273(00)80495-x.

Diversity revealed by a novel family of cadherins expressed in neurons at a 
synaptic complex.

Kohmura N(1), Senzaki K, Hamada S, Kai N, Yasuda R, Watanabe M, Ishii H, Yasuda 
M, Mishina M, Yagi T.

Author information:
(1)Laboratory of Neurobiology and Behavioral Genetics, National Institute for 
Physiological Sciences, Myodaiji, Okazaki, Japan.

In mammals, neurons are highly differentiated and play distinctive functions 
even in the same brain region. We found a novel cadherin-related neuronal 
receptor (Cnr) gene family by studying Fyn-binding activity in mouse brain. CNR1 
protein is located in the synaptic junction and forms a complex with Fyn. 
Sequence analysis of eight Cnr products of approximately 20 genes indicates that 
these comprise a novel cadherin family of the cadherin superfamily. The 
expression patterns of each member of this novel family were grossly similar to 
each other but restricted to subpopulations of neurons of the same type. The 
diversity of the Cnr family genes suggests that there are molecular mechanisms 
that govern highly differentiated neural networks in the mammalian CNS.
