# NuRD mediates activating and repressive functions of GATA-1 and FOG-1 during blood development.
**Authors:** Miccio A, Wang Y, Hong W, Gregory GD, Wang H, Yu X, Choi JK, Shelat S, Tong W, Poncz M, Blobel GA
**Journal:** EMBO J (2010)
**DOI:** [10.1038/emboj.2009.336](https://doi.org/10.1038/emboj.2009.336)
**PMC:** [PMC2824460](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC2824460/)

## Abstract

1. EMBO J. 2010 Jan 20;29(2):442-56. doi: 10.1038/emboj.2009.336. Epub 2009 Nov
19.

NuRD mediates activating and repressive functions of GATA-1 and FOG-1 during 
blood development.

Miccio A(1), Wang Y, Hong W, Gregory GD, Wang H, Yu X, Choi JK, Shelat S, Tong 
W, Poncz M, Blobel GA.

Author information:
(1)Division of Hematology, The Children's Hospital of Philadelphia, 
Philadelphia, PA, USA.

GATA transcription factors interact with FOG proteins to regulate tissue 
development by activating and repressing transcription. FOG-1 (ZFPM1), a 
co-factor for the haematopoietic factor GATA-1, binds to the NuRD co-repressor 
complex through a conserved N-terminal motif. Surprisingly, we detected NuRD 
components at both repressed and active GATA-1/FOG-1 target genes in vivo. In 
addition, while NuRD is required for transcriptional repression in certain 
contexts, we show a direct requirement of NuRD also for FOG-1-dependent 
transcriptional activation. Mice in which the FOG-1/NuRD interaction is 
disrupted display defects similar to germline mutations in the Gata1 and Fog1 
genes, including anaemia and macrothrombocytopaenia. Gene expression analysis in 
primary mutant erythroid cells and megakaryocytes (MKs) revealed an essential 
function for NuRD during both the repression and activation of select 
GATA-1/FOG-1 target genes. These results show that NuRD is a critical co-factor 
for FOG-1 and underscore the versatile use of NuRD by lineage-specific 
transcription factors to activate and repress gene transcription in the 
appropriate cellular and genetic context.
