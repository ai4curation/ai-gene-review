# A zebrafish nanos-related gene is essential for the development of primordial germ cells.
**Authors:** Köprunner M, Thisse C, Thisse B, Raz E
**Journal:** Genes Dev (2001)
**DOI:** [10.1101/gad.212401](https://doi.org/10.1101/gad.212401)

## Content

1. Genes Dev. 2001 Nov 1;15(21):2877-85. doi: 10.1101/gad.212401.

A zebrafish nanos-related gene is essential for the development of primordial 
germ cells.

Köprunner M(1), Thisse C, Thisse B, Raz E.

Author information:
(1)Max Planck Institute for Biophysical Chemistry, Germ Cell Development, 37077 
Göttingen, Germany.

Asymmetrically distributed cytoplasmic determinants collectively termed germ 
plasm have been shown to play an essential role in the development of primordial 
germ cells (PGCs). Here, we report the identification of a nanos-like (nanos1) 
gene, which is expressed in the germ plasm and in the PGCs of the zebrafish. We 
find that several mechanisms act in concert to restrict the activity of Nanos1 
to the germ cells including RNA localization and control over the stability and 
translatability of the RNA. Reducing the level of Nanos1 in zebrafish embryos 
revealed an essential role for the protein in ensuring proper migration and 
survival of PGCs in this vertebrate model organism.
