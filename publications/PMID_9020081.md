Comment in
    Science. 1997 Feb 14;275(5302):943-4. doi: 10.1126/science.275.5302.943.

Mutations in the Caenorhabditis elegans gene clk-1 affect biological timing and 
extend longevity. The gene clk-1 was identified, and the cloned gene 
complemented the clk-1 phenotypes and restored normal longevity. The CLK-1 
protein was found to be conserved among eukaryotes, including humans, and 
structurally similar to the yeast metabolic regulator Cat5p (also called Coq7p). 
These proteins contain a tandem duplication of a core 82-residue domain. clk-1 
complemented the phenotype of cat5/coq7 null mutants, demonstrating that clk-1 
and CAT5/COQ7 share biochemical function and that clk-1 acts at the level of 
cellular physiology.
