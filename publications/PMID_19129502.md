The hypoxia-inducible factor (HIF) basic helix-loop-helix Per-aryl hydrocarbon
receptor nuclear translocator (ARNT)-Sim (bHLH-PAS) transcription factors are
master regulators of the conserved molecular mechanism by which metazoans sense
and respond to reductions in local oxygen concentrations. In humans, HIF is
critically important for the sustained growth and metastasis of solid tumors.
Here, we describe crystal structures of the heterodimer formed by the C-terminal
PAS domains from the HIF2alpha and ARNT subunits of the HIF2 transcription
factor, both in the absence and presence of an artificial ligand. Unexpectedly,
the HIF2alpha PAS-B domain contains a large internal cavity that accommodates
ligands identified from a small-molecule screen. Binding one of these ligands to
HIF2alpha PAS-B modulates the affinity of the HIF2alpha:ARNT PAS-B heterodimer
in vitro. Given the essential role of PAS domains in forming active HIF
heterodimers, these results suggest a presently uncharacterized ligand-mediated
mechanism for regulating HIF2 activity in endogenous and clinical settings.
