# Cell motility and SCAR localisation in axenically growing Dictyostelium cells.
**Authors:** Pollitt AY, Blagg SL, Ibarra N, Insall RH
**Journal:** Eur J Cell Biol (2006)
**DOI:** [10.1016/j.ejcb.2006.05.014](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.ejcb.2006.05.014)

## Abstract

1. Eur J Cell Biol. 2006 Sep;85(9-10):1091-8. doi: 10.1016/j.ejcb.2006.05.014.
Epub  2006 Jul 5.

Cell motility and SCAR localisation in axenically growing Dictyostelium cells.

Pollitt AY(1), Blagg SL, Ibarra N, Insall RH.

Author information:
(1)School of Biosciences, Birmingham University, Edgbaston, Birmingham B15 2TT, 
UK.

Dictyostelium is a popular experimental organism, in particular for studies of 
actin dynamics, cell motility and chemotaxis. We find that the motility of 
axenic cells is unexpectedly different from other strains during growth. In 
particular, vegetative AX3 cells do not show detectable localisation of SCAR and 
its regulatory complex to actin-rich protrusions such as filopodia and 
pseudopodia. Similarly, a range of different mutations, in particular knockouts 
of members of the SCAR complex and Ras proteins, cause different phenotypes 
during vegetative growth in different parental strains. Development reverses 
this unusual behaviour; aggregation-competent AX3 cells localise SCAR in the 
same way as cells of other strains and species. Studies on cell motility using 
vegetative cells should therefore be interpreted with caution.
