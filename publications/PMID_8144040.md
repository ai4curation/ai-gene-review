# The murine glutamine:fructose-6-phosphate amidotransferase-encoding cDNA sequence.
**Authors:** Sayeski PP, Paterson AJ, Kudlow JE
**Journal:** Gene (1994)
**DOI:** [10.1016/0378-1119(94)90560-6](https://doi.org/10.1016/0378-1119(94)90560-6)

## Abstract

1. Gene. 1994 Mar 25;140(2):289-90. doi: 10.1016/0378-1119(94)90560-6.

The murine glutamine:fructose-6-phosphate amidotransferase-encoding cDNA 
sequence.

Sayeski PP(1), Paterson AJ, Kudlow JE.

Author information:
(1)Department of Medicine, University of Alabama at Birmingham 35294.

Glutamine:fructose-6-phosphate amidotransferase (GFAT) is the rate-limiting 
enzyme in hexosamine synthesis and has been implicated in the control of growth 
factor gene expression. We cloned a mouse cDNA which is 91% homologous to the 
human sequence. The deduced amino-acid sequence shows 98.6% identity to human 
GFAT. The cDNA is derived from a 7-kb mRNA in the mouse, while there are 
multiple-sized human mRNAs.
