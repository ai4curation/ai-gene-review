Chromatin remodeling by nucleosome disassembly in vitro.

Author information:
(1)Department of Structural Biology, Stanford University School of Medicine, CA 
94305-5126, USA.

The RSC chromatin-remodeling complex completely disassembles a nucleosome in the 
presence of the histone chaperone Nap1 and ATP. Disassembly occurs in a stepwise 
manner, with the removal of H2A/H2B dimers, followed by the rest of the histones 
and the release of naked DNA. RSC and related chromatin-remodeling complexes may 
be responsible for the removal of promoter nucleosomes during transcriptional 
activation in vivo.
