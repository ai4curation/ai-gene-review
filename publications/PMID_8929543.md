# Human Rad51 protein promotes ATP-dependent homologous pairing and strand transfer reactions in vitro.
**Authors:** Baumann P, Benson FE, West SC
**Journal:** Cell (1996)
**DOI:** [10.1016/s0092-8674(00)81394-x](https://doi.org/10.1016/s0092-8674(00)81394-x)

## Abstract

1. Cell. 1996 Nov 15;87(4):757-66. doi: 10.1016/s0092-8674(00)81394-x.

Human Rad51 protein promotes ATP-dependent homologous pairing and strand 
transfer reactions in vitro.

Baumann P(1), Benson FE, West SC.

Author information:
(1)Imperial Cancer Research Fund, South Mimms, Herts, United Kingdom.

The human testis Rad51 protein, a structural homolog of E. coli RecA, binds 
single- and double-stranded DNA and exhibits DNA-dependent ATPase activity. 
Using circular ssDNA and linear dsDNA (3.0 kb in length), we demonstrate that 
hRad51 promotes homologous pairing and strand exchange reactions in vitro. Joint 
molecule formation was dependent upon ATP hydrolysis and DNA homology and was 
stimulated by the single-strand DNA-binding protein RP-A. In these reactions, 
the 5' terminus of the complementary strand of the linear duplex was efficiently 
transferred to the ssDNA. However, under standard conditions, extensive strand 
exchange was not observed. These results establish hRad51 as a functional 
homolog of RecA, but indicate that the human protein and its bacterial 
counterpart differ in their ability to promote extensive strand transfer. It is 
proposed that hRad51 mediates homology recognition and initiates strand 
exchange, but that extensive heteroduplex formation in higher organisms requires 
the actions of additional proteins.
