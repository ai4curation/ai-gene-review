# Mutations responsible for 3-phosphoserine phosphatase deficiency.
**Authors:** Veiga-da-Cunha M, Collet JF, Prieur B, Jaeken J, Peeraer Y, Rabbijns A, Van Schaftingen E
**Journal:** Eur J Hum Genet (2004)

## Abstract

1. Eur J Hum Genet. 2004 Feb;12(2):163-6. doi: 10.1038/sj.ejhg.5201083.

Mutations responsible for 3-phosphoserine phosphatase deficiency.

Veiga-da-Cunha M(1), Collet JF, Prieur B, Jaeken J, Peeraer Y, Rabbijns A, Van 
Schaftingen E.

Author information:
(1)1Laboratory of Physiological Chemistry, Institute of Cellular Pathology (ICP) 
and Université catholique de Louvain, Brussels, Belgium.

We report the identification of the mutations in the only known case of 
L-3-phosphoserine phosphatase deficiency, a recessively inherited condition. The 
two mutations correspond to the replacement of the semiconserved Asp32 residue 
by an asparagine and of the extremely conserved Met52 by a threonine. The 
effects of both mutations were studied on the human recombinant enzyme, 
expressed in Escherichia coli. Met52Thr almost abolished the enzymatic activity, 
whereas the Asp32Asn mutation caused a 50% decrease in Vmax. In addition, 
L-serine, which inhibits the conversion of [(14)C] phosphoserine to serine when 
catalysed by the wild-type enzyme, had a lesser inhibitory effect on the 
Asp32Asn mutant, indicating a reduction in the rate of phosphoenzyme hydrolysis. 
These modifications in the properties of the enzyme are consistent with the 
modification in the kinetic properties observed in fibroblasts from the patient.
