# Notch signaling regulates left-right asymmetry determination by inducing Nodal expression.
**Authors:** Krebs LT, Iwai N, Nonaka S, Welsh IC, Lan Y, Jiang R, Saijoh Y, O'Brien TP, Hamada H, Gridley T

Generation of left-right asymmetry is an integral part of the establishment of 
the vertebrate body plan. Here we show that the Notch signaling pathway plays a 
primary role in the establishment of left-right asymmetry in mice by directly 
regulating expression of the Nodal gene. Embryos mutant for the Notch ligand 
Dll1 or doubly mutant for the Notch1 and Notch2 receptors exhibit multiple 
defects in left-right asymmetry. Analysis of the enhancer regulating 
node-specific Nodal expression revealed the presence of binding sites for the 
RBP-J protein, the primary transcriptional mediator of Notch signaling. Mutation 
of these sites destroyed the ability of this enhancer to direct node-specific 
gene expression in transgenic mice. Our results demonstrate that Dll1-mediated 
Notch signaling is essential for generation of left-right asymmetry, and that 
the Notch pathway acts upstream of Nodal expression during left-right asymmetry 
determination in mice.
