## Abstract

1. FEBS Lett. 1998 Aug 7;432(3):163-7. doi: 10.1016/s0014-5793(98)00852-7.

Lipophilin, a novel heterodimeric protein of human tears.

Lehrer RI(1), Xu G, Abduragimov A, Dinh NN, Qu XD, Martin D, Glasgow BJ.

Author information:
(1)Department of Medicine, UCLA School of Medicine, Los Angeles, CA 90095, USA. 
rlehrer@med1.medsch.ucla.edu

We identified a novel heterodimeric protein, lipophilin AC, in human tears. One 
of its components, lipophilin A (69 residues; mass, 7575.1; pI, 9.47) was 
homologous to the C1 and C2 components of prostatein ('estramustine-binding 
protein'), the major secreted protein of rat prostate. Human lipophilin C (77 
residues; mass, 8854.1; pI, 4.94) was homologous to the rat prostatein C3 
component and to human mammaglobin, a protein overexpressed in some mammary 
carcinomas. Tear lipophilins A and C expand the roster of human uteroglobin 
superfamily members and provide models for exploring these typically 
steroid-regulated and steroid-binding molecules.
