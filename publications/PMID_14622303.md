# Local changes in the catalytic site of mammalian histidine decarboxylase can affect its global conformation and stability.
**Authors:** Rodríguez-Caso C, Rodríguez-Agudo D, Moya-García AA, Fajardo I, Medina MA, Subramaniam V, Sánchez-Jiménez F
**Journal:** Eur J Biochem (2003)
**DOI:** [10.1046/j.1432-1033.2003.03834.x](https://doi.org/10.1046/j.1432-1033.2003.03834.x)

## Abstract

1. Eur J Biochem. 2003 Nov;270(21):4376-87. doi:
10.1046/j.1432-1033.2003.03834.x.

Local changes in the catalytic site of mammalian histidine decarboxylase can 
affect its global conformation and stability.

Rodríguez-Caso C(1), Rodríguez-Agudo D, Moya-García AA, Fajardo I, Medina MA, 
Subramaniam V, Sánchez-Jiménez F.

Author information:
(1)Department of Molecular Biology and Biochemistry, Faculty of Sciences, 
Málaga, Spain.

Mature, active mammalian histidine decarboxylase is a dimeric enzyme of 
carboxy-truncated monomers (approximately 53 kDa). By using a biocomputational 
approach, we have generated a three-dimensional model of a recombinant 1/512 
fragment of the rat enzyme, which shows kinetic constants similar to those of 
the mature enzyme purified from rodent tissues. This model, together with 
previous spectroscopic data, allowed us to postulate that the occupation of the 
catalytic center by the natural substrate, or by substrate-analogs, would induce 
remarkable changes in the conformation of the intact holoenzyme. To investigate 
the proposed conformational changes during catalysis, we have carried out 
electrophoretic, chromatographic and spectroscopic analyses of purified 
recombinant rat 1/512 histidine decarboxylase in the presence of the natural 
substrate or substrate-analogs. Our results suggest that local changes in the 
catalytic site indeed affect the global conformation and stability of the 
dimeric protein. These results provide insights for new alternatives to inhibit 
histamine production efficiently in vivo.
