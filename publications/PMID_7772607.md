# Molecular cloning, sequencing and expression of a cDNA encoding a human liver NAD-dependent alpha-glycerol-3-phosphate dehydrogenase.
**Authors:** Menaya J, González-Manchón C, Parrilla R, Ayuso MS
**Journal:** Biochim Biophys Acta (1995)
**DOI:** [10.1016/0167-4781(95)00069-s](https://doi.org/10.1016/0167-4781(95)00069-s)

## Abstract

1. Biochim Biophys Acta. 1995 May 17;1262(1):91-4. doi: 
10.1016/0167-4781(95)00069-s.

Molecular cloning, sequencing and expression of a cDNA encoding a human liver 
NAD-dependent alpha-glycerol-3-phosphate dehydrogenase.

Menaya J(1), González-Manchón C, Parrilla R, Ayuso MS.

Author information:
(1)Department of Physiopathology and Human Molecular Genetics, Centro de 
Investigaciones Biológicas, CSIC, Madrid, Spain.

This work reports the primary nucleotide structure and in vitro translation of a 
cDNA, expressed by a gene mapping on chromosome 12, that encodes a human hepatic 
alpha-glycerol-3-phosphate dehydrogenase (L-glycerol-3-phosphate:NAD 
oxidoreductase, E.C. 1.1.1.8). The 1413 bp cDNA comprises an ORF of 1050 bp that 
encodes a 349 amino acid protein of 37.5 kDa. Northern blot analysis of poly(A)+ 
mRNA from human liver showed three transcripts, while from human placenta only 
two transcripts were detected.
