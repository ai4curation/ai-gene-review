# Chondroitin 4-sulfate covalently cross-links the chains of the human blood protein pre-alpha-inhibitor.
**Authors:** Enghild JJ, Salvesen G, Hefta SA, Thøgersen IB, Rutherfurd S, Pizzo SV
**Journal:** J Biol Chem (1991)

## Abstract

1. J Biol Chem. 1991 Jan 15;266(2):747-51.

Chondroitin 4-sulfate covalently cross-links the chains of the human blood 
protein pre-alpha-inhibitor.

Enghild JJ(1), Salvesen G, Hefta SA, Thøgersen IB, Rutherfurd S, Pizzo SV.

Author information:
(1)Department of Pathology, Duke University Medical Center, Durham, North 
Carolina 27710.

The human blood protein pre-alpha-inhibitor is composed of one heavy and one 
light protein chain. The chains are covalently linked to each other by a 
structure that has not previously been described, which we designate a 
protein-glycosaminoglycan-protein (PGP) cross-link. A combination of protein and 
carbohydrate analytical techniques indicates that the interchain linkage is 
mediated by a chondroitin 4-sulfate glycosaminoglycan that originates from a 
typical O-glycosidic link to Ser-10 of the light chain. The heavy chain is 
esterified, via the alpha-carbon of its C-terminal Asp, to C-6 of an internal 
N-acetylgalactosamine of the glycosaminoglycan chain. This PGP cross-link may be 
present in other proteins, but could have been overlooked due to the 
heterogeneous behavior of proteins containing glycosaminoglycan.
