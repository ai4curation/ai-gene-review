# Mutation in the NDUFS4 gene of complex I abolishes cAMP-dependent activation of the complex in a child with fatal neurological syndrome.
**Authors:** Papa S, Scacco S, Sardanelli AM, Vergari R, Papa F, Budde S, van den Heuvel L, Smeitink J
**Journal:** FEBS Lett (2001)
**DOI:** [10.1016/s0014-5793(00)02334-6](https://doi.org/10.1016/s0014-5793(00)02334-6)

## Abstract

1. FEBS Lett. 2001 Feb 2;489(2-3):259-62. doi: 10.1016/s0014-5793(00)02334-6.

Mutation in the NDUFS4 gene of complex I abolishes cAMP-dependent activation of 
the complex in a child with fatal neurological syndrome.

Papa S(1), Scacco S, Sardanelli AM, Vergari R, Papa F, Budde S, van den Heuvel 
L, Smeitink J.

Author information:
(1)Department of Medical Biochemistry and Biology, University of Bari, Italy. 
papabchm@cimedoc.uniba.it

Evidence is presented showing that in a patient with fatal neurological 
syndrome, the homozygous 5 bp duplication in the cDNA of the NDUFS4 18 kDa 
subunit of complex I abolishes cAMP-dependent phosphorylation of this protein 
and activation of the complex. These findings show for the first time that human 
complex I is regulated via phosphorylation of the subunit encoded by the NDUFS4 
gene.
