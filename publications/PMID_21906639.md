# Generation of a catR deficient mutant of P. putida KT2440 that produces cis, cis-muconate from benzoate at high rate and yield.
**Authors:** van Duuren JB, Wijte D, Leprince A, Karge B, Puchałka J, Wery J, Dos Santos VA, Eggink G, Mars AE
**Journal:** J Biotechnol (2011)
**DOI:** [10.1016/j.jbiotec.2011.08.030](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jbiotec.2011.08.030)

## Abstract

1. J Biotechnol. 2011 Dec 10;156(3):163-72. doi: 10.1016/j.jbiotec.2011.08.030. 
Epub 2011 Aug 27.

Generation of a catR deficient mutant of P. putida KT2440 that produces cis, 
cis-muconate from benzoate at high rate and yield.

van Duuren JB(1), Wijte D, Leprince A, Karge B, Puchałka J, Wery J, Dos Santos 
VA, Eggink G, Mars AE.

Author information:
(1)Wageningen UR Food & Biobased Research, 6700AA Wageningen, The Netherlands. 
joost.vanduuren@helmholtz-hzi.de

Pseudomonas putida KT2440-JD1 was derived from P. putida KT2440 after 
N-methyl-N'-nitro-N-nitrosoguanidine (NTG)-mutagenesis and exposure to 
3-fluorobenzoate (3-FB). The mutant was no longer able to grow using benzoate as 
a sole carbon source, but co-metabolized benzoate to cis, cis-muconate during 
growth on glucose, which accumulated in the growth medium. The specific 
production rate (q(pm)) was 0.18±0.03 g cis, cis-muconate/(g(DCW) h) in 
continuous cultures, and increased to 1.4 g cis, cis-muconate/(g(DCW) h) during 
wash-out cultivation. Transcriptome analysis showed that the cat operon was not 
induced in P. putida KT2440-JD1 in the presence of 5mM benzoate, due to a point 
mutation in the highly conserved DNA binding domain of the transcriptional 
regulator (catR) of the cat operon. The ben operon was highly expressed in the 
presence of benzoate in the mutant and its parental strain. This operon contains 
PP_3166 (catA2), which was shown to be a second catechol 1,2-dioxygenase besides 
catA. P. putida KT2440-JD1 is the first cis, cis-muconate-accumulating mutant 
that was characterized at the genetic level. The specific production rate 
achieved is at least eight times higher than those reported for other cis, 
cis-muconate-producing strains.
