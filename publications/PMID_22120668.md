# The AAA-ATPase VCP/p97 promotes 53BP1 recruitment by removing L3MBTL1 from DNA double-strand breaks.
**Authors:** Acs K, Luijsterburg MS, Ackermann L, Salomons FA, Hoppe T, Dantuma NP
**Journal:** Nat Struct Mol Biol (2011)
**DOI:** [10.1038/nsmb.2188](https://doi.org/10.1038/nsmb.2188)

## Abstract

1. Nat Struct Mol Biol. 2011 Nov 27;18(12):1345-50. doi: 10.1038/nsmb.2188.

The AAA-ATPase VCP/p97 promotes 53BP1 recruitment by removing L3MBTL1 from DNA 
double-strand breaks.

Acs K(1), Luijsterburg MS, Ackermann L, Salomons FA, Hoppe T, Dantuma NP.

Author information:
(1)Department of Cell and Molecular Biology, Karolinska Institutet, Stockholm, 
Sweden.

The accumulation of the human tumor suppressor 53BP1 at DNA damage sites 
requires the ubiquitin ligases RNF8 and RNF168. As 53BP1 recognizes dimethylated 
Lys20 in histone H4 (H4K20me2), the requirement for RNF8- and RNF168-mediated 
ubiquitylation has been unclear. Here we show that RNF8-mediated ubiquitylation 
facilitates the recruitment of the AAA-ATPase valosin-containing protein (VCP, 
also known as p97) and its cofactor NPL4 to sites of double-strand breaks. 
RIDDLE cells, which lack functional RNF168, also show impaired recruitment of 
VCP to DNA damage. The ATPase activity of VCP promotes the release of the 
Polycomb protein L3MBTL1 from chromatin, which also binds the H4K20me2 histone 
mark, thereby facilitating 53BP1 recruitment. Consistent with this, nematodes 
lacking the VCP orthologs CDC-48.1 or CDC-48.2, or cofactors UFD-1 or NPL-4, are 
highly sensitive to ionizing radiation. Our data suggest that human RNF8 and 
RNF168 promote VCP-mediated displacement of L3MBTL1 to unmask 53BP1 chromatin 
binding sites.
