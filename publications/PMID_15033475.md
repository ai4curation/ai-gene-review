# Physical and functional interactions between Daxx and TSG101.
**Authors:** Muromoto R, Sugiyama K, Yamamoto T, Oritani K, Shimoda K, Matsuda T
**Journal:** Biochem Biophys Res Commun (2004)

## Abstract

1. Biochem Biophys Res Commun. 2004 Apr 9;316(3):827-33. doi: 
10.1016/j.bbrc.2004.02.126.

Physical and functional interactions between Daxx and TSG101.

Muromoto R(1), Sugiyama K, Yamamoto T, Oritani K, Shimoda K, Matsuda T.

Author information:
(1)Department of Immunology, Graduate School of Pharmaceutical Sciences, 
Hokkaido University, Kita-Ku Kita 12 Nishi 6, Sapporo 060-0812, Japan.

Daxx has been reported to mediate the Fas/JNK-dependent signals in the 
cytoplasm. However, several evidences have suggested that Daxx is located mainly 
in the nucleus and functions as a transcriptional regulator. Recently, we 
identified DMAP1, a TSG101-interacting protein as a Daxx binding partner by 
yeast two-hybrid screening. TSG101 has been shown to act as transcriptional 
co-repressor of nuclear hormone receptors. Here we examined whether TSG101also 
interacts with Daxx directly. The association of Daxx and TSG101 was confirmed 
using co-expressed tagged proteins. The interaction regions in both proteins 
were also mapped, and the cellular localization of the interaction was examined. 
TSG101 formed a complex with Daxx through its coiled-coil domain and 
co-localized in the nucleus. Furthermore, TSG101 enhanced Daxx-mediated 
repression of glucocorticoid receptor transcriptional activity. These results 
provide the novel molecular interactions between Daxx and TSG101, which 
establish an efficient repressive transcription complex in the nucleus.
