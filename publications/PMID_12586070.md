# The Drosophila myosin VI Jaguar is required for basal protein targeting and correct spindle orientation in mitotic neuroblasts.
**Authors:** Petritsch C, Tavosanis G, Turck CW, Jan LY, Jan YN
**Journal:** Dev Cell (2003)
**DOI:** [10.1016/s1534-5807(03)00020-0](https://doi.org/10.1016/s1534-5807(03)00020-0)

## Abstract

1. Dev Cell. 2003 Feb;4(2):273-81. doi: 10.1016/s1534-5807(03)00020-0.

The Drosophila myosin VI Jaguar is required for basal protein targeting and 
correct spindle orientation in mitotic neuroblasts.

Petritsch C(1), Tavosanis G, Turck CW, Jan LY, Jan YN.

Author information:
(1)Howard Hughes Medical Institute, Department of Physiology, University of 
California, San Francisco, San Francisco, CA 94143, USA.

Asymmetric cell divisions generate cellular diversity. In Drosophila, embryonic 
neuroblasts target cell fate determinants basally, rotate their spindles by 90 
degrees to align with the apical-basal axis, and divide asymmetrically in a stem 
cell-like fashion. In this process, apically localized Bazooka recruits 
Inscuteable and other proteins to form an apical complex, which then specifies 
spindle orientation and basal localization of the cell fate determinants and 
their adapter proteins such as Miranda. Here we report that Miranda localization 
requires the unconventional myosin VI Jaguar (Jar). In jar null mutant embryos, 
Miranda is delocalized and the spindle is misoriented, but the Inscuteable 
crescent remains apical. Miranda directly binds to Jar, raising the possibility 
that Miranda and its associated proteins are translocated basally by this 
actin-based motor. Our studies demonstrate that a class VI myosin is necessary 
for basal protein targeting and spindle orientation in neuroblasts.
