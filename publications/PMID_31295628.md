WD40 repeat-containing proteins (WD40 proteins) serve as versatile scaffolds for 
protein-protein interactions, modulating a variety of cellular processes such as 
plant stress and hormone responses. Here we report the identification of a WD40 
protein, XIW1 (for XPO1-interacting WD40 protein 1), which positively regulates 
the abscisic acid (ABA) response in Arabidopsis. XIW1 is located in the 
cytoplasm and nucleus. We found that it interacts with the nuclear transport 
receptor XPO1 and is exported by XPO1 from the nucleus. Mutation of XIW1 reduces 
the induction of ABA-responsive genes and the accumulation of ABA Insensitive 5 
(ABI5), causing mutant plants with ABA-insensitive phenotypes during seed 
germination and seedling growth, and decreased drought stress resistance. ABA 
treatment upregulates the expression of XIW1, and both ABA and abiotic stresses 
promote XIW1 accumulation in the nucleus, where it interacts with ABI5. Loss of 
XIW1 function results in rapid proteasomal degradation of ABI5. Taken together, 
these findings suggest that XIW1 is a nucleocytoplasmic shuttling protein and 
plays a positive role in ABA responses by interacting with and maintaining the 
stability of ABI5 in the nucleus.
