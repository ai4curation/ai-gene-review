Tumor necrosis factor receptor 1-associated death domain protein (TRADD) is the 
core adaptor recruited to TNF receptor 1 (TNFR1) upon TNFalpha stimulation. In 
cells from TRADD-deficient mice, TNFalpha-mediated apoptosis and 
TNFalpha-stimulated NF-kappaB, JNK, and ERK activation are defective. TRADD is 
also important for germinal center formation, DR3-mediated costimulation of T 
cells, and TNFalpha-mediated inflammatory responses in vivo. TRADD deficiency 
does not enhance IFNgamma-induced signaling. Importantly, TRADD has a novel role 
in TLR3 and TLR4 signaling. TRADD participates in the TLR4 complex formed upon 
LPS stimulation, and TRADD-deficient macrophages show impaired cytokine 
production in response to TLR ligands in vitro. Thus, TRADD is a multifunctional 
protein crucial both for TNFR1 signaling and other signaling pathways relevant 
to immune responses.
