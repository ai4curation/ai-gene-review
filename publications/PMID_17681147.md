# Ufd1 is a cofactor of gp78 and plays a key role in cholesterol metabolism by regulating the stability of HMG-CoA reductase.
**Authors:** Cao J, Wang J, Qi W, Miao HH, Wang J, Ge L, DeBose-Boyd RA, Tang JJ, Li BL, Song BL
**Journal:** Cell Metab (2007)
**DOI:** [10.1016/j.cmet.2007.07.002](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.cmet.2007.07.002)

## Abstract

1. Cell Metab. 2007 Aug;6(2):115-28. doi: 10.1016/j.cmet.2007.07.002.

Ufd1 is a cofactor of gp78 and plays a key role in cholesterol metabolism by 
regulating the stability of HMG-CoA reductase.

Cao J(1), Wang J, Qi W, Miao HH, Wang J, Ge L, DeBose-Boyd RA, Tang JJ, Li BL, 
Song BL.

Author information:
(1)State Key Laboratory of Molecular Biology, Institute of Biochemistry and Cell 
Biology, Shanghai Institutes for Biological Sciences, Chinese Academy of 
Sciences, 320 Yue-Yang Road, Shanghai 200031, China.

The membrane-anchored ubiquitin ligase gp78 promotes degradation of misfolded 
endoplasmic reticulum (ER) proteins and sterol-regulated degradation of HMG-CoA 
reductase. It was known previously that Ufd1 plays a critical role in 
ER-associated degradation (ERAD) together with Npl4 and VCP. The VCP-Ufd1-Npl4 
complex recognizes polyubiquitin chains and transfers the ubiquitinated proteins 
to the proteasome. Here we show that Ufd1 directly interacts with gp78 and 
functions as a cofactor. Ufd1 enhances the E3 activity of gp78, accelerates the 
ubiquitination and degradation of reductase, and eventually promotes 
receptor-mediated uptake of low-density lipoprotein. Furthermore, we demonstrate 
that the monoubiquitin-binding site in Ufd1 is required for the enhancement of 
gp78 activity and that the polyubiquitin-binding site in Ufd1 is critical for a 
postubiquitination step in ERAD. In summary, our study identifies Ufd1 as a 
cofactor of gp78, reveals an unappreciated function of Ufd1 in the 
ubiquitination reaction during ERAD, and illustrates that Ufd1 plays a critical 
role in cholesterol metabolism.
