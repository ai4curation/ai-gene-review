# A human cDNA encoding the homologue of NADH: ubiquinone oxidoreductase subunit B13.
**Authors:** Pata I, Tensing K, Metspalu A
**Journal:** Biochim Biophys Acta (1997)
**DOI:** [10.1016/s0167-4781(96)00208-4](https://doi.org/10.1016/s0167-4781(96)00208-4)

## Abstract

1. Biochim Biophys Acta. 1997 Feb 7;1350(2):115-8. doi: 
10.1016/s0167-4781(96)00208-4.

A human cDNA encoding the homologue of NADH: ubiquinone oxidoreductase subunit 
B13.

Pata I(1), Tensing K, Metspalu A.

Author information:
(1)Tartu University, Institute of Molecular and Cell Biology, Estonian 
Biocentre, Estonia. ipata@ebc.ce

A cDNA encoding the human homologue of bovine NADH:ubiquinone oxidoreductase 
(complex I of mitochondrial respiratory chain) subunit B13 has been isolated. 
The clone contains an open reading frame of 348 bp, 23 bp of 5'-untranslated 
sequence (UTR) and a long 3'UTR of 1088 bp. The deduced amino-acid sequence is 
87% identical to bovine B13. Human B13 mRNA expression was observed in all 
tissues examined with highest levels in heart, skeletal muscle and brain. 
Southern analysis of human genomic DNA revealed the presence of multigene 
family.
