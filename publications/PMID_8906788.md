Comment on
    Nature. 1996 Nov 14;384(6605):109-10. doi: 10.1038/384109a0.

Recognition by a T-cell antigen receptor (TCR) of peptide complexed with a major 
histocompatibility complex (MHC) molecule occurs through variable loops in the 
TCR structure which bury almost all the available peptide and a much larger area 
of the MHC molecule. The TCR fits diagonally across the MHC peptide-binding site 
in a surface feature common to all class I and class II MHC molecules, providing 
evidence that the nature of binding is general. A broadly applicable binding 
mode has implications for the mechanism of repertoire selection and the 
magnitude of alloreactions.
