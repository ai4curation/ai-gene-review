# Human ATG2B possesses a lipid transfer activity which is accelerated by negatively charged lipids and WIPI4.
**Authors:** Osawa T, Ishii Y, Noda NN
**Journal:** Genes Cells (2020)
**DOI:** [10.1111/gtc.12733](https://doi.org/10.1111/gtc.12733)

## Abstract

1. Genes Cells. 2020 Jan;25(1):65-70. doi: 10.1111/gtc.12733. Epub 2019 Dec 1.

Human ATG2B possesses a lipid transfer activity which is accelerated by 
negatively charged lipids and WIPI4.

Osawa T(1), Ishii Y(1), Noda NN(1).

Author information:
(1)Institute of Microbial Chemistry (BIKAKEN), Tokyo, Japan.

Atg2 is one of the essential factors for autophagy. Recent advance of structural 
and biochemical study on yeast Atg2 proposed that Atg2 tethers the edge of the 
isolation membrane (IM) to the endoplasmic reticulum and mediates direct lipid 
transfer (LT) from ER to IM for IM expansion. In mammals, two Atg2 orthologs, 
ATG2A and ATG2B, participate in autophagic process. Here we showed that human 
ATG2B possesses the membrane tethering (MT) and LT activity that was promoted by 
negatively charged membranes and an Atg18 ortholog WIPI4. By contrast, 
negatively charged membranes reduced the yeast Atg2 activities in the absence of 
Atg18. These results suggest that the MT/LT activity of Atg2 is evolutionally 
conserved although their regulation differs among species.
