Comment in
    Dev Cell. 2003 Mar;4(3):291-3. doi: 10.1016/s1534-5807(03)00064-9.

Directional sensing by eukaryotic cells does not require polarization of 
chemoattractant receptors. The translocation of the PH domain-containing protein 
CRAC in D. discoideum to binding sites on the inner face of the plasma membrane 
reflects activation of the G protein-linked signaling system. Increments in 
chemoattractant elicit a uniform response around the cell periphery. Yet when 
cells are exposed to a gradient, the activation occurs selectively at the 
stimulated edge, even in immobilized cells. We propose that such localized 
activation, transmitted by the recruitment of cytosolic proteins, may be a 
general mechanism for gradient sensing by G protein-linked chemotactic systems 
including those involving chemotactic cytokines in leukocytes.
