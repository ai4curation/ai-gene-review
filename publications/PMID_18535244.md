Comment in
    Science. 2008 Jun 6;320(5881):1301-2. doi: 10.1126/science.1159104.

Telomeres are specialized chromatin structures that protect chromosomal ends. 
Protection of telomeres 1 (Pot1) binds to the telomeric G-rich overhang, thereby 
protecting telomeres and regulating telomerase. Mammalian POT1 and TPP1 interact 
and constitute part of the six-protein shelterin complex. Here we report that 
Tpz1, the TPP1 homolog in fission yeast, forms a complex with Pot1. Tpz1 binds 
to Ccq1 and the previously undiscovered protein Poz1 (Pot1-associated in 
Schizosaccharomyces pombe), which protect telomeres redundantly and regulate 
telomerase in positive and negative manners, respectively. Thus, the Pot1-Tpz1 
complex accomplishes its functions by recruiting effector molecules Ccq1 and 
Poz1. Moreover, Poz1 bridges Pot1-Tpz1 and Taz1-Rap1, thereby connecting the 
single-stranded and double-stranded telomeric DNA regions. Such molecular 
architectures are similar to those of mammalian shelterin, indicating that the 
overall DNA-protein architecture is conserved across evolution.
