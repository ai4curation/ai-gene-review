Thiamine monophosphate kinase (ThiL) catalyzes the last step of thiamine 
pyrophosphate (TPP) synthesis, the ATP-dependent phosphorylation of thiamine 
monophosphate (TMP) to thiamine pyrophosphate. We solved the structure of ThiL 
from the human pathogen A. baumanii in complex with a pair of substrates TMP and 
a non-hydrolyzable adenosine triphosphate analog, and in complex with a pair of 
products TPP and adenosine diphosphate. High resolution of the data and 
anomalous diffraction allows for a detailed description of the binding mode of 
substrates and products, and their metal environment. The structures further 
support a previously proposed in-line attack reaction mechanism and show a 
distinct variability of metal content of the active site.
