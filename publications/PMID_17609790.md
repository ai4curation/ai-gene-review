# Rescue of the streptomycin antibiotic activity by using streptidine as a "decoy acceptor" for the aminoglycoside-inactivating enzyme adenyl transferase.
**Authors:** Latorre M, Peñalver P, Revuelta J, Asensio JL, García-Junceda E, Bastida A
**Journal:** Chem Commun (Camb) (2007)
**DOI:** [10.1039/b704785a](https://doi.org/10.1039/b704785a)

## Abstract

1. Chem Commun (Camb). 2007 Jul 19;(27):2829-31. doi: 10.1039/b704785a. Epub 2007
 Jun 13.

Rescue of the streptomycin antibiotic activity by using streptidine as a "decoy 
acceptor" for the aminoglycoside-inactivating enzyme adenyl transferase.

Latorre M(1), Peñalver P, Revuelta J, Asensio JL, García-Junceda E, Bastida A.

Author information:
(1)Departamento de Química Orgánica Biológica, Instituto de Química Orgánica 
General, CSIC, Madrid 28006, Spain.

The use of streptidine as a "decoy acceptor" allows the antibiotic activity of 
streptomycin to recover against the Escherichia coli strain overexpressing the 
aminoglycoside-modifying enzyme 6-O-adenyl transferase.
