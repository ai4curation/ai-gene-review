The ability to take up substances from the surrounding environment not only 
provides cells with vital nutrients, but also enables the selective transport of 
substances from one compartment to another. Megalin and cubilin are two 
structurally different endocytic receptors that interact to serve such 
functions. Evidence has accumulated in recent years to indicate that these 
receptors have important functions in both normal physiology and pathology.
