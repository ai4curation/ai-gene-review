# Direct association of presenilin-1 with beta-catenin.
**Authors:** Murayama M, Tanaka S, Palacino J, Murayama O, Honda T, Sun X, Yasutake K, Nihonmatsu N, Wolozin B, Takashima A
**Journal:** FEBS Lett (1998)
**DOI:** [10.1016/s0014-5793(98)00886-2](https://doi.org/10.1016/s0014-5793(98)00886-2)

## Abstract

1. FEBS Lett. 1998 Aug 14;433(1-2):73-7. doi: 10.1016/s0014-5793(98)00886-2.

Direct association of presenilin-1 with beta-catenin.

Murayama M(1), Tanaka S, Palacino J, Murayama O, Honda T, Sun X, Yasutake K, 
Nihonmatsu N, Wolozin B, Takashima A.

Author information:
(1)Laboratory for Alzheimer's Disease, Brain Science Institute, RIKEN, Saitama, 
Japan.

Families bearing mutations in the presenilin-1 (PSI) gene develop Alzheimer's 
disease (AD). However, the mechanism through which PS1 causes AD is unclear. The 
co-immunoprecipitation with PS1 in transfected COS-7 cells indicates that PSI 
directly interacts with endogenous beta-catenin, and the interaction requires 
residues 322450 of PSI and 445-676 of beta-catenin. Both proteins are 
co-localized in the endoplasmic reticulum. Over-expression of PS1 reduces the 
level of cytoplasmic beta-catenin, and inhibits beta-catenin-T cell 
factor-regulated transcription. These results indicate that PSI plays a role as 
inhibitor of the beta-catenin signal, which may be connected with the AD 
dysfunction.
