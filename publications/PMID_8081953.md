# Enhanced in vitro growth of glomerular cells derived from rats with immune-mediated mesangial injury.
**Authors:** Harendza SG, Thaiss F, Stahl RA
**Journal:** Exp Nephrol (1993)

## Abstract

1. Exp Nephrol. 1993 Jan-Feb;1(1):55-61.

Enhanced in vitro growth of glomerular cells derived from rats with 
immune-mediated mesangial injury.

Harendza SG(1), Thaiss F, Stahl RA.

Author information:
(1)Department of Medicine, University of Frankfurt am Main, FRG.

In order to evaluate whether in vivo immune-mediated lesions in the glomerulus 
might affect growth of glomerular cells in vitro, we developed a combined in 
vivo-in vitro approach. An immune-mediated mesangial cell injury was induced in 
rats by intravenous injection of a rabbit anti-rat thymocyte antiserum (ATS). 
Beginning at day 1 up to 12 days after ATS, glomeruli were isolated for 
glomerular cell culture and evaluation of cell growth. Compared with control 
rats cell growth from nephritic glomeruli was significantly higher at all time 
points studied. Immunohistologic studies performed revealed that the 
proliferating cells had characteristics of glomerular mesangial cells. These 
data demonstrate that mesangial cells derived from nephritic glomeruli have 
increased growth in vitro compared with cells from normal glomeruli.
