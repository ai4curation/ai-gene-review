# The developmentally regulated Aspergillus nidulans wA gene encodes a polypeptide homologous to polyketide and fatty acid synthases.
**Authors:** Mayorga ME, Timberlake WE
**Journal:** Mol Gen Genet (1992)
**DOI:** [10.1007/BF00279362](https://doi.org/10.1007/BF00279362)

## Abstract

1. Mol Gen Genet. 1992 Nov;235(2-3):205-12. doi: 10.1007/BF00279362.

The developmentally regulated Aspergillus nidulans wA gene encodes a polypeptide 
homologous to polyketide and fatty acid synthases.

Mayorga ME(1), Timberlake WE.

Author information:
(1)Department of Genetics, University of Georgia, Athens 30602.

The Aspergillus nidulans wA gene is required for synthesis of a green pigment 
present in the walls of mature asexual spores (conidia); wA mutants produce 
colorless (white) conidia. We determined the transcriptional structure and DNA 
sequence of the wA gene. wA consists of 5 exons separated by short (40-60 bp) 
introns. The processed transcript has the potential to encode a protein 
consisting of 1986 amino acid residues. The predicted WA polypeptide showed 
extensive sequence similarities with bacterial and fungal polyketide synthases 
and vertebrate fatty acid synthases, particularly within conserved active sites. 
Properties of the yellow conidial wall pigment intermediate suggest that it is a 
polyketide rather than a fatty acid. It is therefore likely that wA encodes all 
or part of a polyketide synthase involved in the formation of this pigment 
intermediate.
