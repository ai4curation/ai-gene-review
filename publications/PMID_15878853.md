# Analysis of the DNA substrate specificity of the human BACH1 helicase associated with breast cancer.
**Authors:** Gupta R, Sharma S, Sommers JA, Jin Z, Cantor SB, Brosh RM Jr
**Journal:** J Biol Chem (2005)
**DOI:** [10.1074/jbc.M501995200](https://doi.org/10.1074/jbc.M501995200)

## Abstract

1. J Biol Chem. 2005 Jul 8;280(27):25450-60. doi: 10.1074/jbc.M501995200. Epub
2005  May 5.

Analysis of the DNA substrate specificity of the human BACH1 helicase associated 
with breast cancer.

Gupta R(1), Sharma S, Sommers JA, Jin Z, Cantor SB, Brosh RM Jr.

Author information:
(1)Laboratory of Molecular Gerontology, NIA, National Institutes of Health, 
Baltimore, Maryland 21224, USA.

We have investigated the DNA substrate specificity of BACH1 (BRCA1-associated 
C-terminal helicase). The importance of various DNA structural elements for 
efficient unwinding by purified recombinant BACH1 helicase was examined. The 
results indicated that BACH1 preferentially binds and unwinds a forked duplex 
substrate compared with a duplex flanked by only one single-stranded DNA (ssDNA) 
tail. In support of its DNA substrate preference, helicase sequestration studies 
revealed that BACH1 can be preferentially trapped by forked duplex molecules. 
BACH1 helicase requires a minimal 5 ' ssDNA tail of 15 nucleotides for unwinding 
of conventional duplex DNA substrates; however, the enzyme is able to 
catalytically release the third strand of the homologous recombination 
intermediate D-loop structure irrespective of DNA tail status. In contrast, 
BACH1 completely fails to unwind a synthetic Holliday junction structure. 
Moreover, BACH1 requires nucleic acid continuity in the 5 ' ssDNA tail of the 
forked duplex substrate within six nucleotides of the ssDNA-dsDNA junction to 
initiate efficiently DNA unwinding. These studies provide the first detailed 
information on the DNA substrate specificity of BACH1 helicase and provide 
insight to the types of DNA structures the enzyme is likely to act upon to 
perform its functions in DNA repair or recombination.
