# An acylation cycle regulates localization and activity of palmitoylated Ras isoforms.

Comment in
    Science. 2005 Mar 18;307(5716):1731-3. doi: 10.1126/science.1110551.

We show that the specific subcellular distribution of H- and Nras guanosine 
triphosphate-binding proteins is generated by a constitutive de/reacylation 
cycle that operates on palmitoylated proteins, driving their rapid exchange 
between the plasma membrane (PM) and the Golgi apparatus. Depalmitoylation 
redistributes farnesylated Ras in all membranes, followed by repalmitoylation 
and trapping of Ras at the Golgi, from where it is redirected to the PM via the 
secretory pathway. This continuous cycle prevents Ras from nonspecific residence 
on endomembranes, thereby maintaining the specific intracellular 
compartmentalization. The de/reacylation cycle also initiates Ras activation at 
the Golgi by transport of PM-localized Ras guanosine triphosphate. Different 
de/repalmitoylation kinetics account for isoform-specific activation responses 
to growth factors.
