# Clnk plays a role in TNF-alpha-induced cell death in murine fibrosarcoma cell line L929.
**Authors:** Xu M, Cai C, Sun X, Chen W, Li Q, Zhou H
**Journal:** Biochem Biophys Res Commun (2015)

1. Biochem Biophys Res Commun. 2015 Jul 31;463(3):275-9. doi:

Clnk plays a role in TNF-alpha-induced cell death in murine fibrosarcoma cell

Clnk, as a third member of the Blnk/SLP-76 adapter family, is involved in the 
positive regulation of immunoreceptor signaling. Here we provide findings that 
Clnk may be is required for TNF induced cell death, it functions downstream of 
RIP3 and promotes TNF- induced ROS generation and MLKL tetramer formation and 
subsequent necrosis of L929 cells. Therefore, Clnk, as an adaptor protein, may 
take part in the other cellular processes.
