# Non-structural protein 1 of influenza viruses inhibits rapid mRNA degradation mediated by double-stranded RNA-binding protein, staufen1.
**Authors:** Cho H, Ahn SH, Kim KM, Kim YK
**Journal:** FEBS Lett (2013)
**DOI:** [10.1016/j.febslet.2013.05.029](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.febslet.2013.05.029)

## Abstract

1. FEBS Lett. 2013 Jul 11;587(14):2118-24. doi: 10.1016/j.febslet.2013.05.029.
Epub  2013 May 28.

Non-structural protein 1 of influenza viruses inhibits rapid mRNA degradation 
mediated by double-stranded RNA-binding protein, staufen1.

Cho H(1), Ahn SH, Kim KM, Kim YK.

Author information:
(1)School of Life Sciences and Biotechnology, Korea University, Seoul 136-701, 
Republic of Korea.

Although non-structural protein 1 (NS1) of influenza viruses is not essential 
for virulence, this protein is involved in host-virus interactions, viral 
replication, and translation. In particular, NS1 is known to interact with the 
host protein, staufen1 (Stau1). This interaction is important for efficient 
viral replication. However, the underlying molecular mechanism by which NS1 
influences the viral life cycle remains obscure. Here, we show using 
immunoprecipitation and artificial tethering that the N-terminus of NS1, 
NS1(1-73), interacts with Stau1, blocks the Stau1-Upf1 interaction, and 
consequently inhibits the efficiency of Stau1-mediated mRNA decay (SMD), but not 
nonsense-mediatedmRNA decay (NMD). The regulation of SMD efficiency by NS1 may 
contribute to building a more favorable cellular environment for viral 
replication.
