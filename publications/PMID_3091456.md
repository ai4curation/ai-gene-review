# Nucleotide sequence of gene linA encoding resistance to lincosamides in Staphylococcus haemolyticus.
**Authors:** Brisson-Noël A, Courvalin P
**Journal:** Gene (1986)
**DOI:** [10.1016/0378-1119(86)90213-1](https://doi.org/10.1016/0378-1119(86)90213-1)

## Abstract

1. Gene. 1986;43(3):247-53. doi: 10.1016/0378-1119(86)90213-1.

Nucleotide sequence of gene linA encoding resistance to lincosamides in 
Staphylococcus haemolyticus.

Brisson-Noël A, Courvalin P.

We have determined the nucleotide sequence of gene linA in plasmid pIP855, which 
confers resistance to lincomycin by inactivating it in Staphylococcus 
haemolyticus. The gene was defined by start and stop codons and an open reading 
frame of 483 bp corresponding to a product with an Mr of 19020. The apparent 
size of the resistance-conferring protein was 21 kDa, when a minicell extract 
was electrophoresed in the sodium dodecyl sulfate-polyacrylamide gels. Full 
expression of linA was obtained both in Escherichia coli and Bacillus subtilis.
