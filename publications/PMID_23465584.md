# Expression of the SOFaRP2 gene in the central nervous system of the adult cuttlefish Sepia officinalis.
**Authors:** Zhang Z, Tublitz NJ
**Journal:** Neuropeptides (2013)
**DOI:** [10.1016/j.npep.2013.01.003](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.npep.2013.01.003)

## Abstract

1. Neuropeptides. 2013 Jun;47(3):149-55. doi: 10.1016/j.npep.2013.01.003. Epub
2013  Mar 7.

Expression of the SOFaRP2 gene in the central nervous system of the adult 
cuttlefish Sepia officinalis.

Zhang Z(1), Tublitz NJ.

Author information:
(1)Department of Biology, University of Oregon, Eugene, OR 97403, USA. 
zhuobin@uoregon.edu

FMRFamide-related Peptides (FaRPs) are involved in a variety of physiological 
processes, including reproduction, feeding, development, body patterning and 
osmoregulation in vertebrates and invertebrates. Here we investigate the 
expression pattern of cuttlefish Sepia officinalis FaRP2 gene in the brain by in 
situ hybridization. The SOFaRP2 gene was found to be expressed most intensively 
in the posterior chromatophore lobe, vasomotor lobe and subvertical lobe. In 
addition, positive staining was also found in the fin lobe, brachial lobe, 
anterior chromatophore lobe, anterior, dorsal and lateral basal lobes, inferior 
and superior frontal lobes, and optic lobe. The expression pattern of SOFaRP2 
suggests its involvement in chromatophore regulation, feeding behavior, and 
learning and memory.
