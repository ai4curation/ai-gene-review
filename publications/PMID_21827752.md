# A yeast two hybrid screen identifies SPATA4 as a TRAPP interactor.
**Authors:** Duarte DT, Hul S, Sacher M
**Journal:** FEBS Lett (2011)
**DOI:** [10.1016/j.febslet.2011.07.040](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.febslet.2011.07.040)

## Abstract

1. FEBS Lett. 2011 Sep 2;585(17):2676-81. doi: 10.1016/j.febslet.2011.07.040.
Epub  2011 Aug 4.

A yeast two hybrid screen identifies SPATA4 as a TRAPP interactor.

Duarte DT(1), Hul S, Sacher M.

Author information:
(1)Concordia University, Department of Biology, Montreal, Quebec, Canada.

The TRAPP vesicle-tethering complex consists of more than 10 distinct 
polypeptides and is involved in protein transport. Using the C2 subunit as bait 
we identified SPATA4, a spermatocyte-specific protein of unknown function, as an 
interacting partner in a yeast two hybrid screen. Further studies indicate 
SPATA4 interacts with the C2 portion of the TRAPP complex. SPATA4 fractionates 
with both cytosolic and nuclear fractions suggesting it may have several 
distinct functions. SPATA4 is one of only three human proteins that contain a 
DUF1042 domain and we show that C2 does not interact with another one of the 
DUF1042 domain-containing proteins. Our results suggest a role for SPATA4 in 
membrane traffic and a specialized function for TRAPP in spermatocytes.
