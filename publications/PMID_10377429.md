# Efficient export of the glucose transporter Hxt1p from the endoplasmic reticulum requires Gsf2p.
**Authors:** Sherwood PW, Carlson M
**Journal:** Proc Natl Acad Sci U S A (1999)
**DOI:** [10.1073/pnas.96.13.7415](https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.96.13.7415)
**PMC:** [PMC22100](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC22100/)

## Abstract

1. Proc Natl Acad Sci U S A. 1999 Jun 22;96(13):7415-20. doi: 
10.1073/pnas.96.13.7415.

Efficient export of the glucose transporter Hxt1p from the endoplasmic reticulum 
requires Gsf2p.

Sherwood PW(1), Carlson M.

Author information:
(1)Department of Genetics and Development, Columbia University, New York, NY 
10032, USA.

Mutations in the GSF2 gene cause glucose starvation phenotypes in Saccharomyces 
cerevisiae. We have isolated the HXT1 gene, which encodes a low-affinity, 
high-capacity glucose transporter, as a multicopy suppressor of a gsf2 mutation. 
We show that gsf2 mutants accumulate Hxt1p in the endoplasmic reticulum (ER) and 
that Gsf2p is a 46-kDa integral membrane protein localized to the ER. gsf2 
mutants also display a galactose growth defect and abnormal localization of the 
galactose transporter Gal2p but are not defective in function or localization of 
the high-affinity glucose transporter Hxt2p. These findings suggest that Gsf2p 
functions in the ER to promote the secretion of certain hexose transporters.
