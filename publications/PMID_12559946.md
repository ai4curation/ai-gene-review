Author information:

We have cloned a human cDNA encoding an isoform of the peroxin Pex11p, termed 
Pex11pgamma, by BLASTP homology search on eukaryotic protein database and 
reverse transcription (RT)-PCR of human fibroblast RNA. Pex11pgamma was 241 
amino-acid long, with two putative transmembrane segments, showing 22% and 23% 
amino-acid identity to Pex11palpha and Pex11pbeta, respectively. PEX11gamma gene 
was located on chromosome 19, distinct from PEX11alpha and PEX11beta. 
Pex11pgamma was found to be a peroxisomal membrane protein, as assessed by 
colocalization with peroxisome targeting signal type 1 (PTS1)-proteins, in 
epitope-tagged Pex11pgamma-expressing Chinese hamster ovary cells. Pex11pgamma 
exposes both of the N- and C-terminal parts to the cytosol. PEX11gamma was not 
induced in rats by treatment of clofibrate, a peroxisome proliferator, similar 
to constitutively expressed PEX11beta but in contrast to inducible PEX11alpha.
