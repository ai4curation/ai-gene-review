The endothelin-1 (ET-1) gene was disrupted in mouse embryonic stem cells by 
homologous recombination to generate mice deficient in ET-1. These ET-1-/- 
homozygous mice die of respiratory failure at birth and have morphological 
abnormalities of the pharyngeal-arch-derived craniofacial tissues and organs. 
ET-1+/- heterozygous mice, which produce lower levels of ET-1 than wild-type 
mice, develop elevated blood pressure. These results suggest that ET-1 is 
essential for normal mouse development and may also play a physiological role in 
cardiovascular homeostasis.
