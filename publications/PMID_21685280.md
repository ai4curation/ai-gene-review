## Abstract

1. J Bacteriol. 2011 Aug;193(16):4263-4. doi: 10.1128/JB.05326-11. Epub 2011 Jun

Genome sequence of Lactococcus garvieae 8831, isolated from rainbow trout 
lactococcosis outbreaks in Spain.

Aguado-Urda M(1), López-Campos GH, Gibello A, Cutuli MT, López-Alonso V, 
Fernández-Garayzábal JF, Blanco MM.

Author information:
(1)Department of Animal Health, Faculty of Veterinary Science, Complutense 
University, Av. Puerta de Hierro s/n, 28040 Madrid, Spain.

Lactococcus garvieae is the etiological agent of lactococcosis, one of the most 
important disease threats to the sustainability of the rainbow trout farming 
industry. Here, we present the draft genome sequence of Lactococcus garvieae 
strain 8831, isolated from diseased rainbow trout, which is composed of 
2,087,276 bp with a G+C content of 38%.
