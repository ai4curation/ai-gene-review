# Molecular components of a cell death pathway activated by endoplasmic reticulum stress.
**Authors:** Rao RV, Poksay KS, Castro-Obregon S, Schilling B, Row RH, del Rio G, Gibson BW, Ellerby HM, Bredesen DE
**Journal:** J Biol Chem (2004)
**DOI:** [10.1074/jbc.M304490200](https://doi.org/10.1074/jbc.M304490200)

## Abstract

1. J Biol Chem. 2004 Jan 2;279(1):177-87. doi: 10.1074/jbc.M304490200. Epub 2003 
Oct 15.

Molecular components of a cell death pathway activated by endoplasmic reticulum 
stress.

Rao RV(1), Poksay KS, Castro-Obregon S, Schilling B, Row RH, del Rio G, Gibson 
BW, Ellerby HM, Bredesen DE.

Author information:
(1)The Buck Institute for Age Research, Novato, California 94945, USA.

Alterations in Ca2+ homeostasis and accumulation of misfolded proteins in the 
endoplasmic reticulum (ER) cause ER stress that ultimately leads to programmed 
cell death. Recent studies have shown that ER stress triggers programmed cell 
death via an alternative intrinsic pathway of apoptosis that, unlike the 
intrinsic pathway described previously, is independent of Apaf-1 and cytochrome 
c. In the present work, we have used a set of complementary approaches, 
including two-dimensional gel electrophoresis coupled with matrix-assisted laser 
desorption ionization-time-of-flight mass spectrometry and nano-liquid 
chromatography-electrospray ionization mass spectrometry with tandem mass 
spectrometry, RNA interference, co-immunoprecipitation, immunodepletion of 
candidate proteins, and reconstitution studies, to identify mediators of the ER 
stress-induced cell death pathway. Our data identify two molecules, 
valosin-containing protein and apoptosis-linked gene-2 (ALG-2), that appear to 
play a role in mediating ER stress-induced cell death.
