# Protein kinase CK1 is a p53-threonine 18 kinase which requires prior phosphorylation of serine 15.
**Authors:** Dumaz N, Milne DM, Meek DW
**Journal:** FEBS Lett (1999)
**DOI:** [10.1016/s0014-5793(99)01647-6](https://doi.org/10.1016/s0014-5793(99)01647-6)

## Abstract

1. FEBS Lett. 1999 Dec 17;463(3):312-6. doi: 10.1016/s0014-5793(99)01647-6.

Protein kinase CK1 is a p53-threonine 18 kinase which requires prior 
phosphorylation of serine 15.

Dumaz N(1), Milne DM, Meek DW.

Author information:
(1)Biomedical Research Centre, Ninewells Hospital and Medical School, Dundee, 
UK.

p53 is a potent transcription factor which is regulated by sequential multisite 
phosphorylation and acetylation. In this paper, we identify threonine 18 of p53, 
a key site in regulating the interaction between p53 and its regulatory partner 
MDM2, as a novel site phosphorylated in vitro by purified recombinant casein 
kinase 1 (CK1) delta. Strikingly, phosphorylation of threonine 18 is dependent 
upon prior phosphorylation of serine 15. These data highlight an additional and 
physiologically important target residue for CK1 in p53 and suggest a potential 
mechanism by which sequential modification of a pivotal N-terminal residue in 
p53 may occur following stress-activated modification of serine 15.
