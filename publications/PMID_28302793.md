Indisulam is an aryl sulfonamide drug with selective anticancer activity. Its 
mechanism of action and the basis for its selectivity have so far been unknown. 
Here we show that indisulam promotes the recruitment of RBM39 (RNA binding motif 
protein 39) to the CUL4-DCAF15 E3 ubiquitin ligase, leading to RBM39 
polyubiquitination and proteasomal degradation. Mutations in RBM39 that prevent 
its recruitment to CUL4-DCAF15 increase RBM39 stability and confer resistance to 
indisulam's cytotoxicity. RBM39 associates with precursor messenger RNA 
(pre-mRNA) splicing factors, and inactivation of RBM39 by indisulam causes 
aberrant pre-mRNA splicing. Many cancer cell lines derived from hematopoietic 
and lymphoid lineages are sensitive to indisulam, and their sensitivity 
correlates with DCAF15 expression levels. Two other clinically tested 
sulfonamides, tasisulam and chloroquinoxaline sulfonamide, share the same 
mechanism of action as indisulam. We propose that DCAF15 expression may be a 
useful biomarker to guide clinical trials of this class of drugs, which we refer 
to as SPLAMs (splicing inhibitor sulfonamides).
