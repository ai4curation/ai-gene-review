# Hsk1-Dfp1 is required for heterochromatin-mediated cohesion at centromeres.
**Authors:** Bailis JM, Bernard P, Antonelli R, Allshire RC, Forsburg SL
**Journal:** Nat Cell Biol (2003)
**DOI:** [10.1038/ncb1069](https://doi.org/10.1038/ncb1069)

## Abstract

1. Nat Cell Biol. 2003 Dec;5(12):1111-6. doi: 10.1038/ncb1069. Epub 2003 Nov 16.

Hsk1-Dfp1 is required for heterochromatin-mediated cohesion at centromeres.

Bailis JM(1), Bernard P, Antonelli R, Allshire RC, Forsburg SL.

Author information:
(1)Molecular and Cell Biology Laboratory, The Salk Institute for Biological 
Studies, 10010 North Torrey Pines Road, La Jolla, CA 92037, USA.

Heterochromatin performs a central role in chromosome segregation and stability 
by promoting cohesion at centromeres. Establishment of both 
heterochromatin-mediated silencing and cohesion requires passage through S 
phase, although the mechanism is unknown. Here we demonstrate that 
Schizosaccharomyces pombe Hsk1 (CDC7), a conserved Dbf4-dependent protein kinase 
(DDK) that regulates replication initiation, interacts with and phosphorylates 
the heterochromatin protein 1 (HP1) equivalent Swi6 (ref. 6). Hsk1 and its 
regulatory subunit Dfp1 function downstream of Swi6 localization to promote 
heterochromatin function and cohesion specifically at centromeres. This role for 
Hsk1-Dfp1 is separable from its replication initiation activity, providing a 
temporal link between S phase and centromere cohesion that is mediated by 
heterochromatin.
