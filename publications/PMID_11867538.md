We report that in vivo increased acetylation of the repressed Saccharomyces 
cerevisiae ADH2 promoter chromatin, as obtained by disrupting the genes for the 
two deacetylases HDA1 and RPD3, destabilizes the structure of the TATA 
box-containing nucleosome. This acetylation-dependent chromatin remodeling is 
not sufficient to allow the binding of the TATA box-binding protein, but 
facilitates the recruitment of the transcriptional activator Adr1 and induces 
faster kinetics of mRNA accumulation when the cells are shifted to derepressing 
conditions.
