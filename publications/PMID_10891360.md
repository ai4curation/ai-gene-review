# Molecular cloning and characterization of a novel human gene containing ankyrin repeat and double BTB/POZ domain.
**Authors:** Dai KS, Wei W, Liew CC
**Journal:** Biochem Biophys Res Commun (2000)
**DOI:** [10.1006/bbrc.2000.3053](https://doi.org/10.1006/bbrc.2000.3053)

## Abstract

1. Biochem Biophys Res Commun. 2000 Jul 14;273(3):991-6. doi:
10.1006/bbrc.2000.3053.

Molecular cloning and characterization of a novel human gene containing ankyrin
repeat and double BTB/POZ domain.

Dai KS(1), Wei W, Liew CC.

Author information:
(1)Department of Laboratory Medicine and Pathobiology, Institute of Medical
Science, Toronto, Ontario, M5G 1L5, Canada.

A novel human gene containing an ankyrin repeat and BTB/POZ domains (BPOZ) was
isolated from a human leukocyte cDNA library. The cDNA sequence contains an open
reading frame of 1434 bp that encodes 478 amino acid residues with a predicted
molecular mass of 53.9 kDa. Sequence pattern analysis shows that BPOZ contains
an N-terminal ankyrin repeat, a bipartite nuclear localization signal and two
BTB/POZ domains. Using semiquantitative RT-PCR, the BPOZ transcript was found to
be ubiquitously expressed in all fetal tissues examined (heart, brain, liver,
and kidney) suggesting that BPOZ is involved in basic cellular function. Low
expression of BPOZ in adult tissues (normal and hypertrophic heart) suggests
that BPOZ mRNA is developmentally regulated and may play a role in developmental
processes. Chromosomal localization by radiation hybrid mapping revealed that
this gene is localized between D3S1269 and D3S3606 markers corresponding to the
region of chromosome 3q21, a region frequently associated with leukemia. It is
thus suggested that BPOZ may be functionally involved in protein-protein
interaction, perhaps in forming protein complexes, and may have an important
role in normal development and in the development of leukemia.
