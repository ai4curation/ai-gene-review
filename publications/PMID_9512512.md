## Abstract

1. Genes Dev. 1998 Mar 15;12(6):776-81. doi: 10.1101/gad.12.6.776.

Sox1 directly regulates the gamma-crystallin genes and is essential for lens 
development in mice.

Nishiguchi S(1), Wood H, Kondoh H, Lovell-Badge R, Episkopou V.

Author information:
(1)Laboratory of Embryology, Medical Research Council (MRC) Clinical Sciences 
Centre, Hammersmith Hospital, London W12 0NN, UK.

gamma-Crystallins are major structural components of the lens fiber cells in 
amphibians and mammals. Many dominant inherited cataracts in humans and mice 
have been shown to map within the gamma-crystallin gene cluster. Several 
transcription factors, including PAX6 and SOX proteins, have been suggested as 
candidates for crystallin gene regulation. Here we show that the targeted 
deletion of Sox1 in mice causes microphthalmia and cataract. Mutant lens fiber 
cells fail to elongate, probably as a result of an almost complete absence of 
gamma-crystallins. It appears that the direct interaction of the SOX1 protein 
with a promoter element conserved in all gamma-crystallin genes is responsible 
for their expression.
