# Cullin 4A associates with the UV-damaged DNA-binding protein DDB.
**Authors:** Shiyanov P, Nag A, Raychaudhuri P
**Journal:** J Biol Chem (1999)
**DOI:** [10.1074/jbc.274.50.35309](https://doi.org/10.1074/jbc.274.50.35309)

## Abstract

1. J Biol Chem. 1999 Dec 10;274(50):35309-12. doi: 10.1074/jbc.274.50.35309.

Cullin 4A associates with the UV-damaged DNA-binding protein DDB.

Shiyanov P(1), Nag A, Raychaudhuri P.

Author information:
(1)Department of Biochemistry, University of Illinois, Chicago, Illinois 60612, 
USA.

The damaged DNA-binding protein (DDB) is believed to be involved in DNA repair, 
and it has been linked to the repair deficiency disease xeroderma pigmentosum. 
DDB also exhibits transcriptional activities. DDB binds to the activation domain 
of E2F1 and stimulates E2F1-activated transcription. Here we provide evidence 
that DDB or DDB-associated proteins are targets of cullin 4A (CUL-4A). CUL-4A is 
a member of the cullin family of proteins, which are believed to be 
ubiquitin-protein isopeptide ligases (type E3). The CUL-4A gene has been shown 
to be amplified and up-regulated in breast carcinomas. In this study, we 
identify CUL-4A as one of the DDB-associated proteins. CUL-4A 
co-immunoprecipitates with DDB, but not with a naturally occurring mutant of 
DDB. Moreover, CUL-4A in HeLa nuclear extracts co-purifies with DDB, suggesting 
they are parts of the same complex. The observation provides insights how 
CUL-4A, through an interaction with DDB, might be playing a role in the 
development of breast carcinomas.
