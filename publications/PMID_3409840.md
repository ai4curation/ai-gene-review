# Quinolinic-phosphoribosyl transferase activity is decreased in epileptic human brain tissue.
**Authors:** Feldblum S, Rougier A, Loiseau H, Loiseau P, Cohadon F, Morselli PL, Lloyd KG
**Journal:** Epilepsia (1988)
**DOI:** [10.1111/j.1528-1157.1988.tb03756.x](https://doi.org/10.1111/j.1528-1157.1988.tb03756.x)

## Abstract

1. Epilepsia. 1988 Sep-Oct;29(5):523-9. doi: 10.1111/j.1528-1157.1988.tb03756.x.

Quinolinic-phosphoribosyl transferase activity is decreased in epileptic human 
brain tissue.

Feldblum S(1), Rougier A, Loiseau H, Loiseau P, Cohadon F, Morselli PL, Lloyd 
KG.

Author information:
(1)Laboratories d'Etudes et de Recherches Synthélabo (LERS), Paris, France.

The presence of the excitotoxic and convulsant agent quinolinic acid (QUIN) in 
human brain has led to the hypothesis that an increase of this tryptophan 
metabolite could serve as an endogenous epileptogen. A possible mechanism for a 
pathological accumulation of QUIN being a deficiency in its degradation, we have 
measured the activity of quinolinic-phosphoribosyl transferase (QPRTase) (its 
first degradative enzyme) in stereo-EEG identified biopsies of human brain 
tissue. A specific reduction of QPRTase activity was observed in tissue 
primarily involved in the epileptic discharge compared to values from postmortem 
human brain tissue with no neurological disorders or nonpathological tissue from 
epileptic brains. A more severe decrease was noticed in the frontal and temporal 
cortices as compared to the amygdala or Ammon's horn. We suggest that this local 
deficit may contribute to the establishment or maintenance of an epileptic 
focus.
