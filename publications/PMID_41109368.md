The family of inter-α-trypsin inhibitors (ITI) includes proteins with a 
conserved light chain and a variable number of heavy chains. Here, we present 
the first characterization of ITIH6, a previously uncharacterized member of the 
ITI heavy chain (ITIH) family, using different bioinformatics analysis tools. 
ITIH6 contains the two canonical domains characteristic of ITIH proteins (VIT, 
vWA), but also harbors a large central region between the vWA domain and the 
C-terminus that is unique to ITIH6. Phylogenetic analysis further revealed that 
ITIH6 is most closely related to ITIH5, forming a separate subfamily. Protein 
expression profiling of human tissue samples demonstrated that ITIH6 is 
expressed in a tissue-specific manner, with abundant expression in salivary 
glands, kidney, colon, and stomach, particularly within glandular structures of 
the digestive tract. Extending our previous findings on the clinical relevance 
of ITIH5 in cholangiocarcinoma (CCA), we identified an overlapping yet distinct 
expression pattern of ITIH6 in CCA. Importantly, elevated ITIH6 expression 
correlates with favorable prognosis and was more pronounced in tumors with 
single lesions, suggesting its potential utility as a prognostic biomarker in 
CCA. Taken together, these findings highlight the unique structural and 
functional features of ITIH6 and emphasize its potential clinical significance 
in cancer biology.
