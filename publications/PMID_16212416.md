# Characterization of the Drosophila melanogaster mitochondrial proteome.
**Authors:** Alonso J, Rodriguez JM, Baena-López LA, Santarén JF
**Journal:** J Proteome Res (2005)
**DOI:** [10.1021/pr050130c](https://doi.org/10.1021/pr050130c)

## Abstract

1. J Proteome Res. 2005 Sep-Oct;4(5):1636-45. doi: 10.1021/pr050130c.

Characterization of the Drosophila melanogaster mitochondrial proteome.

Alonso J(1), Rodriguez JM, Baena-López LA, Santarén JF.

Author information:
(1)Centro de Biología Molecular Severo Ochoa, CSIC-UAM, Universidad Autónoma de 
Madrid, Cantoblanco, 28049-Madrid, Spain.

We have combined high-resolution two-dimensional (2-D) gel electrophoresis with 
mass spectrometry with the aim of identifying proteins represented in the 2-D 
gel database of Drosophila melanogaster mitochondria. First, we purified 
mitochondria from third instar Drosophila larvae and constructed a 
high-resolution 2-D gel database containing 231 silver-stained polypeptides. 
Next, we carried out preparative 2-D PAGE to isolate some of the polypeptides 
and characterize them by MALDI-TOF analysis. Using this strategy, we identified 
66 mitochondrial spots in the database, and in each case confirmed their 
identity by MALDI-TOF/TOF analysis. In addition, we generated antibodies against 
two of the mitochondrial proteins as tools for characterizing the organelle.
