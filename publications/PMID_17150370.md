Among unusual models to study cell death mechanisms, the protist Dictyostelium 
is remarkable because of its strategic phylogenetic position, with early 
emergence among eukaryotes and unicellular/multicellular transition, and its 
very favorable experimental and genetic flexibility. Dictyostelium shows 
developmental vacuolar cell death, and in vitro monolayer approaches revealed 
both an autophagic vacuolar and a necrotic type of cell death. These are 
described in some detail, as well as implications and future prospects.
