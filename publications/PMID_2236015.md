# Location of the cis-acting auxin-responsive region in the promoter of the par gene from tobacco mesophyll protoplasts.
**Authors:** Takahashi Y, Niwa Y, Machida Y, Nagata T
**Journal:** Proc Natl Acad Sci U S A (1990)
**DOI:** [10.1073/pnas.87.20.8013](https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.87.20.8013)
**PMC:** [PMC54882](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC54882/)

## Abstract

1. Proc Natl Acad Sci U S A. 1990 Oct;87(20):8013-6. doi:
10.1073/pnas.87.20.8013.

Location of the cis-acting auxin-responsive region in the promoter of the par 
gene from tobacco mesophyll protoplasts.

Takahashi Y(1), Niwa Y, Machida Y, Nagata T.

Author information:
(1)Department of Biology, Faculty of Science, Nagoya University, Japan.

We have isolated a genomic clone of an auxin-regulated par gene, which is 
expressed during the transition from G0 phase to S phase in the early stage of 
tobacco mesophyll protoplasts cultured in vitro, from a tobacco genomic library 
using the par cDNA as a probe. When a chimeric gene, in which a reporter gene 
for bacterial beta-glucuronidase (GUS) was placed downstream of the 5' flanking 
sequences of the par gene, was introduced into tobacco mesophyll protoplasts by 
electroporation, the chimeric gene elicited auxin-regulated expression of GUS 
activity. Because deletion of a 111-base-pair (bp) direct repeat in the 5' 
flanking sequences of the par gene abolished the auxin-induced GUS activity, it 
is deduced that in the 111-bp direct repeat of the par gene promoter is 
localized an auxin-responsive region, which regulates auxin-mediated activation 
of transcription.
