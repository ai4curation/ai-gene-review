# Cooperative interaction of S. pombe proteins required for mating and morphogenesis.
**Authors:** Chang EC, Barr M, Wang Y, Jung V, Xu HP, Wigler MH
**Journal:** Cell (1994)
**DOI:** [10.1016/0092-8674(94)90406-5](https://doi.org/10.1016/0092-8674(94)90406-5)

## Abstract

1. Cell. 1994 Oct 7;79(1):131-41. doi: 10.1016/0092-8674(94)90406-5.

Cooperative interaction of S. pombe proteins required for mating and 
morphogenesis.

Chang EC(1), Barr M, Wang Y, Jung V, Xu HP, Wigler MH.

Author information:
(1)Cold Spring Harbor Laboratory, New York 11724.

We isolated two S. pombe genes, scd1 and scd2, that are required for normal 
morphology and mating. scd1 and scd2 are homologous to CDC24 and BEM1, 
respectively, of S. cerevisiae. Epistasis analyses indicate that scd2 and ras1 
converge upon scd1, which, in turn, interacts with cdc42sp, a RHO-like GTPase. 
Studies with the yeast two-hybrid system indicate that scd2 forms complexes with 
both scd1 and cdc42sp. Furthermore, biochemical studies indicate that the 
interaction between scd1 and scd2 is direct. The yeast two-hybrid data further 
suggest that scd1, scd2, cdc42sp, and ras1, in its GTP-bound state, act 
cooperatively to form a protein complex.
