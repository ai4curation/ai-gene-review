# Mannosylphosphate transfer to cell wall mannan is regulated by the transcriptional level of the MNN4 gene in Saccharomyces cerevisiae.
**Authors:** Odani T, Shimma Y, Wang XH, Jigami Y
**Journal:** FEBS Lett (1997)
**DOI:** [10.1016/s0014-5793(97)01513-5](https://doi.org/10.1016/s0014-5793(97)01513-5)

## Content

1. FEBS Lett. 1997 Dec 29;420(2-3):186-90. doi: 10.1016/s0014-5793(97)01513-5.

Mannosylphosphate transfer to cell wall mannan is regulated by the 
transcriptional level of the MNN4 gene in Saccharomyces cerevisiae.

Odani T(1), Shimma Y, Wang XH, Jigami Y.

Author information:
(1)Institute of Biological Sciences, The University of Tsukuba, Ibaraki, Japan.

Mannosylphosphorylation is a major oligosaccharide modification that provides 
negative charge in the Saccharomyces cerevisiae cell wall. Although two genes, 
MNN6 and MNN4, which encode a mannosylphosphate transferase and its putative 
positive regulator, respectively, are involved in this modification, the amount 
of Mnn4p has been found to be a limiting factor for mannosylphosphorylation. The 
level of mannosylphosphorylation increased at late-logarithmic and stationary 
phases of cell growth, and this was correlated to the transcriptional 
enhancement of MNN4. We also find that mannosylphosphate transfer to mannan is 
negatively regulated by the protein kinase A pathway, while the presence of 0.5 
M potassium chloride enhanced MNN4 transcription. This type of transcriptional 
regulation is observed in many stress response genes, implying that 
mannosylphosphate transfer is involved in the cellular response to a variety of 
stresses.
