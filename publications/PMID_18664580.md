Activation of the phosphatidylinositol 3-kinase (PI3K)/AKT signaling pathway is
a frequent occurrence in human cancers and a major promoter of chemotherapeutic
resistance. Inhibition of one downstream target in this pathway, mTORC1, has
shown potential to improve chemosensitivity. However, the mechanisms and genetic
modifications that confer sensitivity to mTORC1 inhibitors remain unclear. Here,
we demonstrate that loss of TSC2 in the E mu-myc murine lymphoma model leads to
mTORC1 activation and accelerated oncogenesis caused by a defective apoptotic
program despite compromised AKT phosphorylation. Tumors from Tsc2(+/-)E mu-Myc
mice underwent rapid apoptosis upon blockade of mTORC1 by rapamycin. We
identified myeloid cell leukemia sequence 1 (Mcl-1), a bcl-2 like family member,
as a translationally regulated genetic determinant of mTORC1-dependent survival.
Our results indicate that the extent by which rapamycin can modulate expression
of Mcl-1 is an important feature of the rapamycin response.
