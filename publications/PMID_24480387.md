Ubiquinone, also called coenzyme Q, is a lipid subject to oxido-reduction
cycles. It functions in the respiratory electron transport chain and plays a
pivotal role in energy generating processes. In this review, we focus on the
biosynthetic pathway and physiological role of ubiquinone in bacteria. We
present the studies which, within a period of five decades, led to the
identification and characterization of the genes named ubi and involved in
ubiquinone production in Escherichia coli. When available, the structures of the
corresponding enzymes are shown and their biological function is detailed. The
phenotypes observed in mutants deficient in ubiquinone biosynthesis are
presented, either in model bacteria or in pathogens. A particular attention is
given to the role of ubiquinone in respiration, modulation of two-component
activity and bacterial virulence. This article is part of a Special Issue
entitled: 18th European Bioenergetic Conference.
