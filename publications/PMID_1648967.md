# Overexpression of the cAMP receptor 1 in growing Dictyostelium cells.
**Authors:** Johnson RL, Vaughan RA, Caterina MJ, Van Haastert PJ, Devreotes PN
**Journal:** Biochemistry (1991)
**DOI:** [10.1021/bi00242a025](https://doi.org/10.1021/bi00242a025)

## Abstract

1. Biochemistry. 1991 Jul 16;30(28):6982-6. doi: 10.1021/bi00242a025.

Overexpression of the cAMP receptor 1 in growing Dictyostelium cells.

Johnson RL(1), Vaughan RA, Caterina MJ, Van Haastert PJ, Devreotes PN.

Author information:
(1)Department of Biological Chemistry, School of Medicine, Johns Hopkins 
University, Baltimore, Maryland 21205.

cAR1, the cAMP receptor expressed normally during the early aggregation stage of 
the Dictyostelium developmental program, has been expressed during the growth 
stage, when only low amounts of endogenous receptors are present. Transformants 
expressing cAR1 have 7-40 times over growth stage and 3-5-fold over aggregation 
stage levels of endogenous receptors. The high amounts of cAR1 protein expressed 
constitutively throughout early development did not drastically disrupt the 
developmental program; the onset of aggregation was delayed by 1-3 h, and then 
subsequent stages proceeded normally. The affinity of the expressed cAR1 was 
similar to that of the endogenous receptors in aggregation stage cells when 
measured either in phosphate buffer (two affinity states with Kd's of 
approximately 30 and 300 nM) or in 3 M ammonium sulfate (one affinity state with 
a Kd of 2-3 nM). When expressed during growth, cAR1 did not appear to couple to 
its normal effectors since these cells failed to carry out chemotaxis or to 
elevate cGMP or cAMP levels when stimulated with cAMP. However, cAMP stimulated 
phosphorylation, and loss of ligand binding of cAR1 did occur. Like aggregation 
stage control cells, the cAR1 protein shifted in apparent molecular mass from 40 
to 43 kDa and became highly phosphorylated when exposed to cAMP. In addition, 
the number of surface cAMP binding sites in cAR1 cells was reduced by over 80% 
during prolonged cAMP stimulation. These results define a useful system to 
express altered cAR1 proteins and examine their regulatory functions.
