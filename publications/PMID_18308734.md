Fibronectin type I repeat is a nonactivating ligand for EphA1 and inhibits
ATF3-dependent angiogenesis.

Masuda J(1), Usui R, Maru Y.

Author information:
(1)Department of Pharmacology, Tokyo Women's Medical University, 8-1 Kawada-cho,
Shinjuku-ku, Tokyo 162-8666, Japan.

ATF3 stimulated promoter activity of EphA1 by 3.4-fold in ATF3-dependent
angiogenesis in vitro. Although tyrosine kinase activation of EphA1 was
dispensable, binding of EphA1 to fibronectin through its type I repeat played an
essential role in the angiogenesis. Recombinant proteins containing fibronectin
10th to 12th type I repeat (I 10-12) but not I 12 could inhibit the angiogenesis
in vitro by competitively targeting EphA1 with the full-length fibronectin.
However, I 12 acquired a higher affinity toward EphA2 with K(d) 18 nm and
inhibited vascular endothelial growth factor-dependent angiogenic invasion in a
Matrigel plug assay.
