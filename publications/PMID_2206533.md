# Synaptoporin, a novel putative channel protein of synaptic vesicles.
**Authors:** Knaus P, Marquèze-Pouey B, Scherer H, Betz H
**Journal:** Neuron (1990)
**DOI:** [10.1016/0896-6273(90)90084-s](https://doi.org/10.1016/0896-6273(90)90084-s)

## Abstract

1. Neuron. 1990 Oct;5(4):453-62. doi: 10.1016/0896-6273(90)90084-s.

Synaptoporin, a novel putative channel protein of synaptic vesicles.

Knaus P(1), Marquèze-Pouey B, Scherer H, Betz H.

Author information:
(1)ZMBH, University of Heidelberg, Federal Republic of Germany.

By homology screening of a rat brain library, we have isolated cDNAs that encode 
a novel member of the synaptophysin/connexin channel protein superfamily. The 
deduced protein, named synaptoporin, displays 58% amino acid identity to 
synaptophysin, with highly conserved transmembrane segments, but a divergent 
cytoplasmic tail. Northern blot analysis and PCR amplification of RNA from 
different rat tissues indicate expression of synaptoporin transcripts in the 
CNS. Antibodies against a synthetic peptide or a fusion construct encompassing 
the cytoplasmic tail region of synaptoporin detect a polypeptide of 37 kd that 
copurifies with small synaptic vesicles. Our data suggest the existence of a 
family of vesicular channel proteins whose members may be differently 
distributed among synaptic vesicle subpopulations.
