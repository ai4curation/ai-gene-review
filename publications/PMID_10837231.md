# Mob1p interacts with the Sid2p kinase and is required for cytokinesis in fission yeast.
**Authors:** Hou MC, Salek J, McCollum D
**Journal:** Curr Biol (2000)
**DOI:** [10.1016/s0960-9822(00)00492-9](https://doi.org/10.1016/s0960-9822(00)00492-9)

## Abstract

1. Curr Biol. 2000 May 18;10(10):619-22. doi: 10.1016/s0960-9822(00)00492-9.

Mob1p interacts with the Sid2p kinase and is required for cytokinesis in fission 
yeast.

Hou MC(1), Salek J, McCollum D.

Author information:
(1)Department of Molecular Genetics and Microbiology, University of 
Massachusetts Medical School, Worcester, MA 01605, USA.

A great deal is now known about how cells regulate entry into mitosis, but only 
recently have the mechanisms controlling exit from mitosis and cytokinesis begun 
to be revealed. In the budding yeast Saccharomyces cerevisiae, Mob1p interacts 
with the Dbf2p kinase and cells containing mutations in these genes arrest in 
late anaphase [1] [2]. Proteins related to Mob1p are present in both plants and 
animals, but information about Mob1p function has been obtained only from 
budding yeast. Here, we describe the identification and characterization of 
Mob1p from Schizosaccharomyces pombe. Mob1p associates with the Sid2p kinase and 
like Sid2p, Mob1p is required for the initiation of cytokinesis, but not for 
mitotic exit. Mob1p localizes to the spindle pole body (SPB) and to the 
cell-division site during cell division, suggesting that it might be involved in 
transducing the signal to initiate cell division from the SPB to the division 
site. Mob1p is required for Sid2p localization, and Mob1p localization requires 
the function of the cdc7, cdc11, cdc14, spg1, sid1, sid2, and sid4 genes, 
suggesting that together with Sid2p, Mob1p functions at the end of the signaling 
cascade required to regulate the onset of cytokinesis at the end of mitosis.
