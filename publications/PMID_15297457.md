# On the slowing of S phase in response to DNA damage in fission yeast.
**Authors:** Kumar S, Huberman JA
**Journal:** J Biol Chem (2004)
**DOI:** [10.1074/jbc.M407819200](https://doi.org/10.1074/jbc.M407819200)

## Abstract

1. J Biol Chem. 2004 Oct 15;279(42):43574-80. doi: 10.1074/jbc.M407819200. Epub 
2004 Aug 5.

On the slowing of S phase in response to DNA damage in fission yeast.

Kumar S(1), Huberman JA.

Author information:
(1)Department of Cancer Genetics, Roswell Park Cancer Institute, Buffalo, New 
York 14263, USA.

Eukaryotic cells slow their progression through S phase upon DNA damage. The 
mechanism that leads to this slowing is called the intra-S-phase checkpoint. 
Previous studies demonstrated that in the fission yeast Schizosaccharomyces 
pombe this checkpoint is mediated by a pathway that includes Rad3 (similar to 
human ATR and ATM) and Cds1 (similar to human Chk1 and Chk2). Here we present 
evidence that a major downstream target of this pathway is the cyclin-dependent 
kinase, Cdc2. We also present evidence suggesting that the intra-S-phase 
checkpoint makes a relatively minor contribution to the survival of cells with 
damaged DNA.
