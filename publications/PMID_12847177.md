# ALS patients on TPPV: totally locked-in state, neurologic findings and ethical implications.
**Authors:** Hayashi H, Oppenheimer EA
**Journal:** Neurology (2003)
**DOI:** [10.1212/01.wnl.0000069925.02052.1f](https://doi.org/10.1212/01.wnl.0000069925.02052.1f)

## Abstract

1. Neurology. 2003 Jul 8;61(1):135-7. doi: 10.1212/01.wnl.0000069925.02052.1f.

ALS patients on TPPV: totally locked-in state, neurologic findings and ethical 
implications.

Hayashi H(1), Oppenheimer EA.

Author information:
(1)Department of Neurology (Dr. Hayashi), Tokyo Metropolitan Neurological 
Hospital, Japan.

Seventy patients with ALS using tracheostomy positive pressure ventilation 
(TPPV) were evaluated for impaired communication. Severely impaired 
communication is divided into the totally locked-in state (TLS) and the minimal 
communication state (MCS). TLS occurred in 8 of 70 (11.4%). In the 33 patients 
on TPPV for more than 5 years, TLS developed in 6 (18.2%) and MCS in 11 (33.1%). 
Respiratory management for ALS allows long-term survival if mechanical 
ventilation is used.
