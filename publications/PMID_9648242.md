# cDNA cloning, expression, and characterization of the human bifunctional ATP sulfurylase/adenosine 5'-phosphosulfate kinase enzyme.
**Authors:** Yanagisawa K, Sakakibara Y, Suiko M, Takami Y, Nakayama T, Nakajima H, Takayanagi K, Natori Y, Liu MC
**Journal:** Biosci Biotechnol Biochem (1998)
**DOI:** [10.1271/bbb.62.1037](https://doi.org/10.1271/bbb.62.1037)

## Abstract

1. Biosci Biotechnol Biochem. 1998 May;62(5):1037-40. doi: 10.1271/bbb.62.1037.

cDNA cloning, expression, and characterization of the human bifunctional ATP 
sulfurylase/adenosine 5'-phosphosulfate kinase enzyme.

Yanagisawa K(1), Sakakibara Y, Suiko M, Takami Y, Nakayama T, Nakajima H, 
Takayanagi K, Natori Y, Liu MC.

Author information:
(1)Department of Biochemistry, University of Texas Health Center, Tyler 75710, 
USA.

A cDNA encoding the human bifunctional ATP sulfurylase/adenosine 
5'-phosphosulfate (APS) kinase was cloned and sequenced. The enzyme contains an 
APS kinase domain in its N-terminal portion and an ATP sulfurylase domain in its 
C-terminal portion. Recombinant full-length enzyme and its constituent APS 
kinase and ATP sulfurylase domains were individually expressed, purified, and 
shown to have their respective enzymatic activities.
