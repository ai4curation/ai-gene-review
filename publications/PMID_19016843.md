# Molecular basis of actin reorganization promoted by binding of enterohaemorrhagic Escherichia coli EspB to alpha-catenin.
**Authors:** Hamaguchi M, Hamada D, Suzuki KN, Sakata I, Yanagihara I
**Journal:** FEBS J (2008)
**DOI:** [10.1111/j.1742-4658.2008.06750.x](https://doi.org/10.1111/j.1742-4658.2008.06750.x)

## Abstract

1. FEBS J. 2008 Dec;275(24):6260-7. doi: 10.1111/j.1742-4658.2008.06750.x. Epub 
2008 Nov 7.

Molecular basis of actin reorganization promoted by binding of 
enterohaemorrhagic Escherichia coli EspB to alpha-catenin.

Hamaguchi M(1), Hamada D, Suzuki KN, Sakata I, Yanagihara I.

Author information:
(1)Department of Developmental Infectious Diseases, Research Institute, Osaka 
Medical Center for Maternal and Child Health, Japan.

EspB is a multifunctional protein associated with the type III secretion system 
of enterohaemorrhagic Escherichia coli, and interacts with various biomolecules 
including alpha-catenin in the host cell. The binding of EspB to alpha-catenin 
is thought be involved in actin reorganization during bacterial infection, 
although the precise mechanism of this phenomenon is still unclear. Recent 
research shows that dimerization of alpha-catenin dissociates it from 
E-cadherin/beta-catenin/alpha-catenin complexes, and that the dimer suppresses 
Arp2/3-mediated actin branching or polymerization. These results inspired us to 
evaluate the effect of EspB on the functions of alpha-catenin. Based on a series 
of in vitro biochemical approaches, including pull-down, co-sedimentation and 
pyrene-actin polymerization assays combined with transmission electron 
microscopy, we conclude that EspB promotes all the functions of dimeric 
alpha-catenin described above. These results clarified the molecular basis of 
reorganization of actin filaments during infection with enterohaemorrhagic 
Escherichia coli.
