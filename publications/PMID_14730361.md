# The costimulation-regulated duration of PKB activation controls T cell longevity.

## Abstract

1. Nat Immunol. 2004 Feb;5(2):150-8. doi: 10.1038/ni1030. Epub 2004 Jan 18.

The costimulation-regulated duration of PKB activation controls T cell 
longevity.

Song J(1), Salek-Ardakani S, Rogers PR, Cheng M, Van Parijs L, Croft M.

Author information:
(1)Division of Molecular Immunology, La Jolla Institute for Allergy and

Erratum in

A brief antigenic stimulus can promote T cell proliferation, but the duration 
and nature of intracellular signals required for survival are unclear. Here we 
show that in the absence of OX40 costimulation, antigen-activated CD4+ cells are 
short-lived because the activity of protein kinase B (PKB; also known as Akt) is 
not maintained over time. Activated T cells that express a dominant-negative 
variant of PKB also undergo apoptosis, reproducing the OX40-deficient phenotype. 
In contrast, an active form of PKB prevents downregulation of antiapoptotic 
proteins in OX40-deficient T cells, rescues antigen-induced cell survival in 
vivo, and controls inflammation in recall responses. Thus, sustained and 
periodic PKB signaling has an integral role in regulating T cell longevity.
