# Negative regulation of the wee1 protein kinase by direct action of the nim1/cdr1 mitotic inducer.
**Authors:** Coleman TR, Tang Z, Dunphy WG
**Journal:** Cell (1993)
**DOI:** [10.1016/0092-8674(93)90580-j](https://doi.org/10.1016/0092-8674(93)90580-j)

## Abstract

1. Cell. 1993 Mar 26;72(6):919-29. doi: 10.1016/0092-8674(93)90580-j.

Negative regulation of the wee1 protein kinase by direct action of the nim1/cdr1 
mitotic inducer.

Coleman TR(1), Tang Z, Dunphy WG.

Author information:
(1)Division of Biology, California Institute of Technology, Pasadena 91125.

The wee1 protein kinase suppresses the entry into mitosis by mediating the 
inhibitory tyrosine phosphorylation of p34cdc2. Genetic studies have suggested 
that the nim1 protein kinase (also known as cdr1) acts as a positive regulator 
of mitosis by down-regulating the wee1 pathway in yeast cells. We have 
overexpressed the nim1 protein in both bacteria and insect cells. The 
recombinant nim1 protein autophosphorylates on both tyrosine and serine residues 
and can phosphorylate the isolated wee1 protein directly in a cell-free system. 
The nim1-catalyzed phosphorylation of the wee1 protein occurs in its C-terminal 
region and leads to a substantial drop in its activity as a cdc2-specific 
tyrosine kinase. This nim1-dependent inhibition of the wee1 protein kinase can 
be reversed readily in vitro by treatment with a protein phosphatase. These 
experiments provide direct biochemical evidence that the wee1 protein is subject 
to negative regulation by phosphorylation and indicate that the nim1 protein 
acts as an inhibitory, wee1-specific kinase.
