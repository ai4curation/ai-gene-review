Comment in
    Nature. 1995 Jun 29;375(6534):734-5. doi: 10.1038/375734a0.

Some cases of Alzheimer's disease are inherited as an autosomal dominant trait. 
Genetic linkage studies have mapped a locus (AD3) associated with susceptibility 
to a very aggressive form of Alzheimer's disease to chromosome 14q24.3. We have 
defined a minimal cosegregating region containing the AD3 gene, and isolated at 
least 19 different transcripts encoded within this region. One of these 
transcripts (S182) corresponds to a novel gene whose product is predicted to 
contain multiple transmembrane domains and resembles an integral membrane 
protein. Five different missense mutations have been found that cosegregate with 
early-onset familial Alzheimer's disease. Because these changes occurred in 
conserved domains of this gene, and are not present in normal controls, they are 
likely to be causative of AD3.
