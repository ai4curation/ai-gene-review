# Kinase activity is not required for alphaCaMKII-dependent presynaptic plasticity at CA3-CA1 synapses.
**Authors:** Hojjati MR, van Woerden GM, Tyler WJ, Giese KP, Silva AJ, Pozzo-Miller L, Elgersma Y
**Journal:** Nat Neurosci (2007)
**DOI:** [10.1038/nn1946](https://doi.org/10.1038/nn1946)
**PMC:** [PMC2804046](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC2804046/)

## Abstract

1. Nat Neurosci. 2007 Sep;10(9):1125-7. doi: 10.1038/nn1946. Epub 2007 Jul 29.

Kinase activity is not required for alphaCaMKII-dependent presynaptic plasticity 
at CA3-CA1 synapses.

Hojjati MR(1), van Woerden GM, Tyler WJ, Giese KP, Silva AJ, Pozzo-Miller L, 
Elgersma Y.

Author information:
(1)Department of Neuroscience, Erasmus University Medical Center, Dr. 
Molewaterplein 50, 3015 GE Rotterdam, The Netherlands.

Using targeted mouse mutants and pharmacologic inhibition of alphaCaMKII, we 
demonstrate that the alphaCaMKII protein, but not its activation, 
autophosphorylation or its ability to phosphorylate synapsin I, is required for 
normal short-term presynaptic plasticity. Furthermore, alphaCaMKII regulates the 
number of docked vesicles independent of its ability to be activated. These 
results indicate that alphaCaMKII has a nonenzymatic role in short-term 
presynaptic plasticity at hippocampal CA3-CA1 synapses.
