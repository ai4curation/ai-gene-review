Type III galactosemia is a metabolic disorder caused by reduced activity of 
UDP-galactose-4-epimerase, which participates in galactose metabolism and the 
generation of various UDP-sugar species. We characterized gale-1 in 
Caenorhabditis elegans and found that a complete loss-of-function mutation is 
lethal, as has been hypothesized for humans, whereas a nonlethal partial 
loss-of-function allele causes a variety of developmental abnormalities, likely 
resulting from the impairment of the glycosylation process. We also observed 
that gale-1 mutants are hypersensitive to galactose as well as to infections. 
Interestingly, we found interactions between gale-1 and the unfolded protein 
response.
