Comment in
    Trends Plant Sci. 2006 Nov;11(11):517-8. doi: 10.1016/j.tplants.2006.09.003.

The AUX1 and PIN auxin influx and efflux facilitators are key regulators of root 
growth and development. For root gravitropism to occur, AUX1 and PIN2 must 
transport auxin via the lateral root cap to elongating epidermal cells. Genetic 
studies suggest that AXR4 functions in the same pathway as AUX1. Here we show 
that AXR4 is a previously unidentified accessory protein of the endoplasmic 
reticulum (ER) that regulates localization of AUX1 but not of PIN proteins. Loss 
of AXR4 resulted in abnormal accumulation of AUX1 in the ER of epidermal cells, 
indicating that the axr4 agravitropic phenotype is caused by defective AUX1 
trafficking in the root epidermis.
