# Expression and characterization of diacylglycerol acyltransferase from Arabidopsis thaliana in insect cell cultures.
**Authors:** Hobbs DH, Hills MJ
**Journal:** Biochem Soc Trans (2000)

## Abstract

1. Biochem Soc Trans. 2000 Dec;28(6):687-9.

Expression and characterization of diacylglycerol acyltransferase from 
Arabidopsis thaliana in insect cell cultures.

Hobbs DH(1), Hills MJ.

Author information:
(1)John Innes Centre, Norwich Research Park, Colney, Norwich, Norfolk NR4 7UH, 
UK.

Diacylglycerol acyltransferase (DGAT) catalyses the acylation of the sn-3 
hydroxy group of sn-1,2-diacylglycerol using acyl-CoA. The gene encoding DGAT 
from Arabidopsis thaliana has been cloned and the function of the enzyme proved 
by expression of the coding sequence using a bacculovirus expression system in 
insect cell cultures. The expressed protein catalysed the synthesis of 
[(14)C]triacylglycerol from [(14)C]diacylglycerol and oleoyl-CoA. The 
heterologously expressed DGAT activity was found mostly associated with the 
100000 g pellet. The optimum activity was achieved at a neutral pH, in the 
presence of Mg2+, and at an optimum oleoyl-CoA concentration of 20 microM. The 
DGAT used the substrates palmitoyl-CoA and oleoyl-CoA equally effectively. In 
these experiments, the inclusion of recombinant acyl-CoA binding protein had a 
relatively small effect upon DGAT activity.
