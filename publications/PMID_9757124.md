# Crystallographic characterization of Pap1-DNA complex.

## Abstract

1. Acta Crystallogr D Biol Crystallogr. 1998 Sep 1;54(Pt 5):1014-6. doi:

Crystallographic characterization of Pap1-DNA complex.

Fujii Y(1), Ohira T, Kyougoku Y, Toda T, Yanagida M, Hakoshima T.

Author information:
(1)Department of Molecular Biology, Nara Institute of Science and Technology 
(NAIST), 8916-5 Takayama, Ikoma, Nara 630-01, Japan.

Pap1 is a fission yeast transcription factor that activates genes related with 
resistance against staurosporine, a potent inhibitor of protein kinase C, and 
has been shown to be involved in cell growth, cell cycle, carcinogenesis and 
differentiation. Pap1 has the bZIP DNA-binding domain but binds to non-consensus 
DNA sequences for the bZIP motif. Highly ordered crystals of the DNA-binding 
domain complexed with a DNA fragment that has an ATF/CREB-like non-consensus 
sequence have been obtained. The crystals grew by the vapor-diffusion technique 
with polyethylene glycol 6000 and belong to space group R3 with a = b = 240.78, 
c = 43.85 A. A 2.0 A resolution data set was collected with a 
cryo-crystallographic technique.
