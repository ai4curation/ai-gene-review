Introduction

Histidine, one of the 20 proteinogenic amino acids, plays crucial roles in protein interactions and enzyme catalysis. For example, it is frequently found at the active sites in many enzymes, where it participate in acid-base catalysis, nucleophilic phosphoryl group transfer, and metal ion coordination [1, 2]. Additionally, histidine biosynthesis is interconnected with de novo synthesis of purines, pyrimidines, NAD cofactors, folates and others, playing a “metabolic crossroad” for cell metabolism and growth [3].

Histidine biosynthetic pathway is highly conserved in all organisms including bacteria, archaea, lower eukaryotes and plants, consisting of a total of ten enzymatic steps to convert PRPP sequentially to L-histidine [4]. ATP and PRPP are condensated into phosphoribosyl-ATP (PR-ATP) by ATP phosphoribosyltransferase (HisG). PR-ATP is then hydrolyzed to generate PR-AMP and 1-(5-phosphoribosyl)−5-[(5-phosphoribosylamino) methylideneamino] imidazole-4 carbox-amide (5’ProFAR) squentially by phosphoribose-ATP pyrophosphatase (HisE) and Phosphoribosyl-AMP cyclohydrolase (HisI) respectively. Then 5’ProFAR isomerase (HisA) decomposes 5’ProFAR to produce 5-[(5-phospho-1-deoxyribulos-1-ylamino) methylideneamino]−1-(5 phosphoribosyl) imidazole-4-carboxamide (PRFAR) and PRFAR is converted to imidazole-glycerophosphate (IGP) by IGP synthase (HisFH). IGP is sequentially dehydrated and then transam-inated by IGP dehydratase (HisB) and histidine phosphotransaminase (HisC) respectively to generate imidazole-acetol-phosphate and then L-histidinol-phosphate (HolP). HolP is dephosphorylated to L-histidinol via histidinol-phosphate phosphatase (HolPase, HisN). Finally, L-histidine is produced by histidine dehydrogenase (HisD) after two oxidations [5].

Among all the enzymes involved, phosphohistidine phosphatase (HolPase) which catalyzes dephosphorization of L-histidinol-phosphate (Hol-P) to L-histidinol, the penultimate step in L-histidine biosynthesis, is an especially interesting enzyme [6]. In Saccaromyces cerevisiae and Bacillus subtilis, the monofunction enzyme of HisN was used to perform the dephosphorization of Hol-P [6]. However, in enteric bacteria, like in E. coli, dephosphorization of Hol-P is carried out by a bifunction enzyme of HisB. The N-terminal domain of HisB show HolPase activity to catalyze dephosphorization of L-histidinol-phosphate (Hol-P) to L-histidinol while C-terminal domain of HisB show imidazole glycerol-phosphate dehydratase (IGPD) activity to catalyze Imidazole glycerophosphate (IGP) to 3- (1 H-imidazol-4-yl) −2-oxopropyl phosphate (IAP), the seventh step in histidine biosynthesis [7]. Due to its complex origin and evolution, HolPase was always the last and most difficult enzyme to be identified in histidine biosynthesis, such as in Escherichia coli and Nitrosopumilus maritimus [8, 9]. Moreover, an alternative HolPase, encoded by cg0910, has been found in C. glutamicum, but with no further research details [6].

Pseudomonas aeruginosa (PA) is an important gram-negative environmental and pathogenic bacterium, widespread in terrestrial, aquatic, animal and plant-related habitats. HolPase was the only enzyme which had not been annotated in histidine biosynthesis in PA before we reported it. We firstly identified the HolPase encoding by gene PA0335, belong to HAD superfamily. Its inactivation resulted in growth defect of PA on histidine free medium. However, defect growth in PA0335 mutant was restored slowly after 80 h culturing on histidine free MM medium, suggesting the existence of alternative HolPase in PA [10].

In this study, the two alternative homologs of PA0335 encoded HolPase were identified and primarily characterized in PA. PA3255 and PA1143 code phosphatase with side HolPase activity to rescue the growth defect derived from PA0335 absence. PA0335 play a key role in histidine biosynthesis, PA3255 is mainly responsible for the residual growth in the PA0335 mutant. The conserved residues of the typical HAD motifs in three enzymes were primarily investigated for their role in enzyme function. Moreover, all three genes, especially for PA3255, are required for the resistance of PA to ciprofloxacin (Cip) and meropenem (Mem), probably due to a deficiency in biofilm formation in their absence. PA3255 and PA0335 are required for full virulence but inactivation of PA1143 increased the pathogenicity of PA. PA0335 and PA3255 could be used as targets for PA infection treatment separately or synergistically due to their involvement in histidine auxotrophy, full virulence and obvious antibiotics resistance in PA. The relation and regulation of the three phosphatases with HolPase or side HolPase activity need further study.

DiscussionHolPase was always the last and most difficult enzyme to be identified in histidine biosynthesis, such as in Escherichia coli and Nitrosopumilus maritimus [8, 9].We firstly reported HolPase which was encoded by PA0335 and inactivation resulted in incomplete L-histidine auxotrophy in P. aeruginosa PAO1, suggesting the presence of alternate HolPase enzymes in PA [10]. The novelty of this study lies in identification and characterization of the alternative homologs of PA0335 encoded HolPase in PA.Based on the results from bioinformatic analysis, complementation analysis, evaluation of gene expression and growth, PA3255 and PA1143 could be deduced to code phosphatase with side HolPase activity, to allow a rescue of growth when PA0335 is absent, though with a serious growth defect. PA0335 play important role in histidine biosynthesis and PA3255 is response for the residual growth derived from the absence of PA0335 in PA. Although PA3255 and PA1143 exhibit side HolPase activity, the kind of activity derived from their native promoters might not account for measurable growth, at least in the tested condition, coinciding with the phenotype of normal growth in their mutants. Enzymes with side HolPase activity have also been reported in Saccharomyces cerevisiae and Escherichia coli, where a nonspecific alkaline phosphomonoesterase exhibited side HolPase activity to dephosphorylate histidinol-phosphate [29–31]. Moreover, bacteria could also utilize serendipitous alternate pathways to overcome the lethal effects of gene inactivation, as reported by Shelley Copley [32]. It is worth considering this possibility in our study. Inactivation of PdxB, 4-phosphoerythronate (4PE) dehydrogenase, result in pyridoxal 5′-phosphate (PLP) auxotroph. Overexpression of YeaB, a hydrolase sharing 8.5% sequence identity with PdxB, could rescue the impaired growth of ΔPdxB by diverting material from serine biosynthesis to PLP biosynthesis, a serendipitous alternate pathway, but not by replacing the activity of PdxB. The possibility of rescuing growth defect by PA3255 or PA1143 complement in ΔPA0335 via serendipitous pathway is limited based on our above results. Anyway, the HolPase activity of PA3255 and PA1143 need further verify by using the natural substrate of HolPase if possible.All three genes, especially for PA3255, are required for the resistance of PA against Cip and Mem, while PA3255 and PA0335 are required for the full virulence, but inactivation of PA1143 increased the pathogens of PA. PA0335 and PA3255 could be used as targets for PA infection treatment separately or synergistically due to their involvement in histidine auxotrophy, full virulence and obvious antibiotics resistance in PA. It has been reported that amino acid biosynthesis could influence antibiotics resistance [25, 27] or virulence [33] of microorganism. For example, absence of hisC in histidine biosynthesis increased virulence of Acinetobacter baumannii [27] while inactivation of HisB in A. fumigatus caused histidine auxotrophy and significantly reduces virulence in four infection models of murine pulmonary, systemic, and corneal infections, and wax moth larvae [33]. The biosynthesis of valine was sufficiently depressed in methicillin-resistant Staphylococcus aureus (MRSA) compared with methicillin-sensitive Staphylococcus aureus and supplement of valine could increase the sensitivity of cefoperazone-sulbactam to MRSA [25]. Undergoing mechanism of the involvement of PA0335 as well as its homolog PA3255 and PA1143 in virulence and antibiotics of PA need further study.Alternative HolPase has been reported in other microorganism, like C. glutamicum [6] and Streptonmyces coelicolor [31]. cg0910 has been reported as HolPase HisN, the last gap in histidine biosynthesis, and inactivation of cg0910 caused an incomplete histidine auxotrophy [34]. Then, cg0911 was further proved to encode a second HolPase in C. glutamicum [6]. However, these alternative HolPases found in C. glutamicum and Streptonmyces coelicolor were not investigated further in detail.Three unrelated HolPase families have been reported currently, haloacid dehalogenase-like hydrolase (HAD) superfamily, inositol monophosphatase (IMPase) like family, and polymerase and HolPase (PHP) subfamily of the metallo-dependent hydrolase (MDH) family [6]. IMPase-like HolPases typically exist in Actinobacteria and have also been found in plants. Functional characterization for IMPase-like HolPases have been reported in C. glutamicum [34], Streptomyces coelicolor [35], and Arabidopsis thaliana [36]. PHP-like HolPases are monofunctional enzymes which have been found in Saccharomyces cerevisiae and Firmicutes like Bacillus subtilis and Lactococcus lactis [37–39]. HAD phosphatase show many different biological functions, including primary and secondary metabolism, regulating enzyme activity, intracellular nutrient absorption and etc [40]. HolPase encoded by PA0335 belongs to HAD superfamily. HAD-type HolPases include bifunctional HisB in Salmonella murine [41] and Escherichia coli [10] and monofunctional HAD-type HolPase of Ton-0887 in archaeon Thermococcus onnurieneus [9].
All members of HAD-type family contain four highly conserved sequence motifs [42]. Motif I contain highly conserved signature of DxD in which D refer to as aspartate, forming a covalent intermediate with the substrate’s phosphate group. The first aspartate function as catalytic nucleophile and the second act as acid/base catalyst generally. Motif II is typically characterized by a highly conserved serine or threonine to bind to the phosphate group of the substrate. Motif III contains a conserved lysine that works with motif II to stabilize reaction intermediates during hydrolysis. Motif IV includes two conserved aspartates with typical signatures of DD, GDxxxD, or GDxxxxD, forming an Mg ion binding pocket with motif I [31, 35]. Motif III is less conserved than the other motifs [37]. PA0335 and PA1143 contain these four conserved motifs while PA3255 has motifs II-IV of the HAD family. However, the IMPase-like family may have six conserved motifs to characterize IMPase-type proteins [6].All the conserved residues in the typical motifs in PA1143 and PA3255 are required for their side HolPase activity to rescue the growth defect of ΔPA0335. The residues of D7 and D9 in motif I and D179 and D183 in motif IV of PA0335 might be important for its HolPase catalysis, while T113 in motif II and K156 in motif III of PA0335 could probably play a role on the complete catalysis activity of HolPase because substitution of T113 or K156 resulted in the retarded growth, instead of complete histidine auxotrophy. The specific role of the conserved residues in the typical motifs of HAD family has been reported. For example, in human soluble epoxide hydrolase (sEH), D9 in motif I and D184 together with N189 in motif IV have been proved to be functionally indispensable for catalysis function while T123 in motif II was involved in enzyme kinetic properties rather than enzyme catalysis properties [43]. Enzymatic characteristics need further investigation to determine the actual role of these conserved residues in PA0335, PA3255 and PA1143.In summary, the alternative homologs of PA0335 encoded HolPase were found and characterized primarily in this study. PA3255 and PA1143 code phosphatases with side HolPase activity to allow the growth in the absence of PA0335. PA0335 play a key role in histidine biosynthesis and PA3255 is mainly responsible for the residual growth from the PA0335 absence. The conserved residues in the HAD typical motifs were primarily investigated for the tested function of the three enzymes. Moreover, all three genes, especially for PA3255, are required for the resistance of PA to Cip and Mem, while PA3255 and PA0335 are required for the full virulence but inactivation of PA1143 increased the pathogens of PA. PA0335 and PA3255 could be used as targets for PA infection treatment or drug development separately or synergistically. The undergoing regulation of the three phosphatases with HolPase or side HolPase activity as well as the dominant biological roles of PA3255 and PA1143 encoded proteins need further study.

It has been reported that amino acid biosynthesis could influence antibiotics resistance [25, 27] or virulence [33] of microorganism. For example, absence of hisC in histidine biosynthesis increased virulence of Acinetobacter baumannii [27] while inactivation of HisB in A. fumigatus caused histidine auxotrophy and significantly reduces virulence in four infection models of murine pulmonary, systemic, and corneal infections, and wax moth larvae [33]. The biosynthesis of valine was sufficiently depressed in methicillin-resistant Staphylococcus aureus (MRSA) compared with methicillin-sensitive Staphylococcus aureus and supplement of valine could increase the sensitivity of cefoperazone-sulbactam to MRSA [25]. Undergoing mechanism of the involvement of PA0335 as well as its homolog PA3255 and PA1143 in virulence and antibiotics of PA need further study.

Alternative HolPase has been reported in other microorganism, like C. glutamicum [6] and Streptonmyces coelicolor [31]. cg0910 has been reported as HolPase HisN, the last gap in histidine biosynthesis, and inactivation of cg0910 caused an incomplete histidine auxotrophy [34]. Then, cg0911 was further proved to encode a second HolPase in C. glutamicum [6]. However, these alternative HolPases found in C. glutamicum and Streptonmyces coelicolor were not investigated further in detail.

Three unrelated HolPase families have been reported currently, haloacid dehalogenase-like hydrolase (HAD) superfamily, inositol monophosphatase (IMPase) like family, and polymerase and HolPase (PHP) subfamily of the metallo-dependent hydrolase (MDH) family [6]. IMPase-like HolPases typically exist in Actinobacteria and have also been found in plants. Functional characterization for IMPase-like HolPases have been reported in C. glutamicum [34], Streptomyces coelicolor [35], and Arabidopsis thaliana [36]. PHP-like HolPases are monofunctional enzymes which have been found in Saccharomyces cerevisiae and Firmicutes like Bacillus subtilis and Lactococcus lactis [37–39]. HAD phosphatase show many different biological functions, including primary and secondary metabolism, regulating enzyme activity, intracellular nutrient absorption and etc [40]. HolPase encoded by PA0335 belongs to HAD superfamily. HAD-type HolPases include bifunctional HisB in Salmonella murine [41] and Escherichia coli [10] and monofunctional HAD-type HolPase of Ton-0887 in archaeon Thermococcus onnurieneus [9].

All members of HAD-type family contain four highly conserved sequence motifs [42]. Motif I contain highly conserved signature of DxD in which D refer to as aspartate, forming a covalent intermediate with the substrate’s phosphate group. The first aspartate function as catalytic nucleophile and the second act as acid/base catalyst generally. Motif II is typically characterized by a highly conserved serine or threonine to bind to the phosphate group of the substrate. Motif III contains a conserved lysine that works with motif II to stabilize reaction intermediates during hydrolysis. Motif IV includes two conserved aspartates with typical signatures of DD, GDxxxD, or GDxxxxD, forming an Mg ion binding pocket with motif I [31, 35]. Motif III is less conserved than the other motifs [37]. PA0335 and PA1143 contain these four conserved motifs while PA3255 has motifs II-IV of the HAD family. However, the IMPase-like family may have six conserved motifs to characterize IMPase-type proteins [6].

All the conserved residues in the typical motifs in PA1143 and PA3255 are required for their side HolPase activity to rescue the growth defect of ΔPA0335. The residues of D7 and D9 in motif I and D179 and D183 in motif IV of PA0335 might be important for its HolPase catalysis, while T113 in motif II and K156 in motif III of PA0335 could probably play a role on the complete catalysis activity of HolPase because substitution of T113 or K156 resulted in the retarded growth, instead of complete histidine auxotrophy. The specific role of the conserved residues in the typical motifs of HAD family has been reported. For example, in human soluble epoxide hydrolase (sEH), D9 in motif I and D184 together with N189 in motif IV have been proved to be functionally indispensable for catalysis function while T123 in motif II was involved in enzyme kinetic properties rather than enzyme catalysis properties [43]. Enzymatic characteristics need further investigation to determine the actual role of these conserved residues in PA0335, PA3255 and PA1143.

In summary, the alternative homologs of PA0335 encoded HolPase were found and characterized primarily in this study. PA3255 and PA1143 code phosphatases with side HolPase activity to allow the growth in the absence of PA0335. PA0335 play a key role in histidine biosynthesis and PA3255 is mainly responsible for the residual growth from the PA0335 absence. The conserved residues in the HAD typical motifs were primarily investigated for the tested function of the three enzymes. Moreover, all three genes, especially for PA3255, are required for the resistance of PA to Cip and Mem, while PA3255 and PA0335 are required for the full virulence but inactivation of PA1143 increased the pathogens of PA. PA0335 and PA3255 could be used as targets for PA infection treatment or drug development separately or synergistically. The undergoing regulation of the three phosphatases with HolPase or side HolPase activity as well as the dominant biological roles of PA3255 and PA1143 encoded proteins need further study.
