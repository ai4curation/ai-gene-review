Comment in
    Science. 1997 Jan 31;275(5300):628-30. doi: 10.1126/science.275.5300.628.

A signaling pathway was delineated by which insulin-like growth factor 1 (IGF-1) 
promotes the survival of cerebellar neurons. IGF-1 activation of 
phosphoinositide 3-kinase (PI3-K) triggered the activation of two protein 
kinases, the serine-threonine kinase Akt and the p70 ribosomal protein S6 kinase 
(p70(S6K)). Experiments with pharmacological inhibitors, as well as expression 
of wild-type and dominant-inhibitory forms of Akt, demonstrated that Akt but not 
p70(S6K) mediates PI3-K-dependent survival. These findings suggest that in the 
developing nervous system, Akt is a critical mediator of growth factor-induced 
neuronal survival.
