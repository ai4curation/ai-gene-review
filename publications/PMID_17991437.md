# Identification and characterization of follistatin as a novel angiogenin-binding protein.
**Authors:** Gao X, Hu H, Zhu J, Xu Z
**Journal:** FEBS Lett (2007)
**DOI:** [10.1016/j.febslet.2007.10.059](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.febslet.2007.10.059)

## Abstract

1. FEBS Lett. 2007 Nov 27;581(28):5505-10. doi: 10.1016/j.febslet.2007.10.059.
Epub  2007 Nov 6.

Identification and characterization of follistatin as a novel angiogenin-binding 
protein.

Gao X(1), Hu H, Zhu J, Xu Z.

Author information:
(1)Research Center for Environmental Genomics, Zhejiang University School of 
Medicine, 388 Yuhangtang Road, Hangzhou 310058, China.

Angiogenin enhances tumorigenesis. However, the mechanisms of angiogenin-induced 
angiogenesis and cancer cell proliferation remain elusive. In this study, 
follistatin was identified as a binding partner of angiogenin by a yeast 
two-hybrid screen and confirmed by a pull-down experiment. The interaction of 
fluorescently tagged angiogenin and follistatin was monitored in real time by a 
laser confocal microscope and shown to localize at the sub-nuclear region of 
HeLa cells. Additional yeast two-hybrid analysis revealed that domains 2 and 3 
of follistatin were the minimal structure requirement for angiogenin binding. 
These findings provide new clues for further studies on the mechanisms of 
angiogenin-induced angiogenesis or cancer cell growth.
