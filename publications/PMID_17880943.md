# Active site closure facilitates juxtaposition of reactant atoms for initiation of catalysis by human dUTPase.
**Authors:** Varga B, Barabás O, Kovári J, Tóth J, Hunyadi-Gulyás E, Klement E, Medzihradszky KF, Tölgyesi F, Fidy J, Vértessy BG
**Journal:** FEBS Lett (2007)

## Abstract

1. FEBS Lett. 2007 Oct 2;581(24):4783-8. doi: 10.1016/j.febslet.2007.09.005. Epub
 2007 Sep 12.

Active site closure facilitates juxtaposition of reactant atoms for initiation 
of catalysis by human dUTPase.

Varga B(1), Barabás O, Kovári J, Tóth J, Hunyadi-Gulyás E, Klement E, 
Medzihradszky KF, Tölgyesi F, Fidy J, Vértessy BG.

Author information:
(1)Institute of Enzymology, Biological Research Center, Hungarian Academy of 
Sciences, Budapest, Hungary.

Human dUTPase, essential for DNA integrity, is an important survival factor for 
cancer cells. We determined the crystal structure of the 
enzyme:alpha,beta-imino-dUTP:Mg complex and performed equilibrium binding 
experiments in solution. Ordering of the C-terminus upon the active site induces 
close juxtaposition of the incoming nucleophile attacker water oxygen and the 
alpha-phosphorus of the substrate, decreasing their distance below the van der 
Waals limit. Complex interactions of the C-terminus with both substrate and 
product were observed via a specifically designed tryptophan sensor, suitable 
for further detailed kinetic and ligand binding studies. Results explain the key 
functional role of the C-terminus.
