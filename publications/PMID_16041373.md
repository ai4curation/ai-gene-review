# Mutations in the endosomal ESCRTIII-complex subunit CHMP2B in frontotemporal dementia.
**Authors:** Skibinski G, Parkinson NJ, Brown JM, Chakrabarti L, Lloyd SL, Hummerich H, Nielsen JE, Hodges JR, Spillantini MG, Thusgaard T, Brandner S, Brun A, Rossor MN, Gade A, Johannsen P, Sørensen SA, Gydesen S, Fisher EM, Collinge J
**Journal:** Nat Genet (2005)
**DOI:** [10.1038/ng1609](https://doi.org/10.1038/ng1609)

## Abstract

1. Nat Genet. 2005 Aug;37(8):806-8. doi: 10.1038/ng1609. Epub 2005 Jul 24.

Mutations in the endosomal ESCRTIII-complex subunit CHMP2B in frontotemporal 
dementia.

Skibinski G(1), Parkinson NJ, Brown JM, Chakrabarti L, Lloyd SL, Hummerich H, 
Nielsen JE, Hodges JR, Spillantini MG, Thusgaard T, Brandner S, Brun A, Rossor 
MN, Gade A, Johannsen P, Sørensen SA, Gydesen S, Fisher EM, Collinge J.

Author information:
(1)MRC Prion Unit, Institute of Neurology, University College London, London, 
UK.

We have previously reported a large Danish pedigree with autosomal dominant 
frontotemporal dementia (FTD) linked to chromosome 3 (FTD3). Here we identify a 
mutation in CHMP2B, encoding a component of the endosomal ESCRTIII complex, and 
show that it results in aberrant mRNA splicing in tissue samples from affected 
members of this family. We also describe an additional missense mutation in an 
unrelated individual with FTD. Aberration in the endosomal ESCRTIII complex may 
result in FTD and neurodegenerative disease.
