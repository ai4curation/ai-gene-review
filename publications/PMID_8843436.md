A set of ordered cosmids and a detailed genetic and physical map for the 8 Mb 
Streptomyces coelicolor A3(2) chromosome.

Redenbach M(1), Kieser HM, Denapaite D, Eichner A, Cullum J, Kinashi H, Hopwood 
DA.

Author information:
(1)LB Genetik, Universität Kaiserslautern, Germany.

A Supercos-1 library carrying chromosomal DNA of a plasmid-free derivative of 
Streptomyces coelicolor A3(2) was organized into an ordered encyclopaedia of 
overlapping clones by hybridization. The minimum set of overlapping clones 
representing the entire chromosome (with three short gaps) consists of 319 
cosmids. The average insert size is 37.5 kb and the set of clones therefore 
divides the chromosome into 637 alternating unique and overlapping segments 
which have an average length of approx. 12.5 kb. More than 170 genes, gene 
clusters and other genetic markers were mapped to their specific segment by 
hybridization to the encyclopaedia. Genes could be cloned by direct 
transformation and complementation of S. coelicolor mutants with cosmids 
isolated from Escherichia coli, selecting for insertion into the chromosome by 
homologous recombination. As in other streptomycetes, the ends of the chromosome 
have long terminal inverted repeats.
