# Clofibrate-inducible, 28-kDa peroxisomal integral membrane protein is encoded by PEX11.
**Authors:** Abe I, Okumoto K, Tamura S, Fujiki Y
**Journal:** FEBS Lett (1998)
**DOI:** [10.1016/s0014-5793(98)00815-1](https://doi.org/10.1016/s0014-5793(98)00815-1)

## Abstract

1. FEBS Lett. 1998 Jul 24;431(3):468-72. doi: 10.1016/s0014-5793(98)00815-1.

Clofibrate-inducible, 28-kDa peroxisomal integral membrane protein is encoded by 
PEX11.

Abe I(1), Okumoto K, Tamura S, Fujiki Y.

Author information:
(1)Department of Biology, Faculty of Science, Kyushu University, Fukuoka, Japan.

We cloned a human PEX11 cDNA by expressed sequence tag homology search using 
yeast Candida boidinii PEX11, followed by screening of human liver cDNA library. 
PEX11 encoded a peroxisomal protein Pex11p comprising 247 amino acids, with two 
transmembrane segments and a dilysine motif at the C-terminus. Pex11p comigrated 
in SDS-PAGE with a 28-kDa peroxisomal integral membrane protein (PMP28) isolated 
from the liver of clofibrate-treated rats and was crossreactive to anti-PMP28 
antibody, thereby indicating PEX11 to encode PMP28. Pex11p exposes both N- and 
C-terminal parts to the cytosol. PEX11 was not responsible for ten 
complementation groups of human peroxisome deficiency disorders.
