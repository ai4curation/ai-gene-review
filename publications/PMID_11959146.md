# CLK-1 protein has DNA binding activity specific to O(L) region of mitochondrial DNA.
**Authors:** Gorbunova V, Seluanov A
**Journal:** FEBS Lett (2002)
**DOI:** [10.1016/s0014-5793(02)02568-1](https://doi.org/10.1016/s0014-5793(02)02568-1)

## Abstract

1. FEBS Lett. 2002 Apr 10;516(1-3):279-84. doi: 10.1016/s0014-5793(02)02568-1.

CLK-1 protein has DNA binding activity specific to O(L) region of mitochondrial 
DNA.

Gorbunova V(1), Seluanov A.

Author information:
(1)Huffington Center on Aging, Baylor College of Medicine, One Baylor Plaza, 
Houston, TX 77030, USA. vera_gorbunova@hotmail.com

Mutations in the clk-1 gene of Caenorhabditis elegans extend worm life span and 
slow down a variety of physiological processes. Here we report that C. elegans 
CLK-1 as well as its mouse homologue have DNA binding activity that is specific 
to the O(L) region of mitochondrial DNA. DNA binding activity of CLK-1 is 
inhibited by ADP, and is altered by mutations that extend nematode life span. 
Our results suggest that, in addition to its enzymatic function in ubiquinone 
biosynthesis, CLK-1 is involved in the regulation of mtDNA replication or 
transcription.
