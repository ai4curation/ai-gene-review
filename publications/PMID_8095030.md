# A human synaptic vesicle monoamine transporter cDNA predicts posttranslational modifications, reveals chromosome 10 gene localization and identifies TaqI RFLPs.
**Authors:** Surratt CK, Persico AM, Yang XD, Edgar SR, Bird GS, Hawkins AL, Griffin CA, Li X, Jabs EW, Uhl GR
**Journal:** FEBS Lett (1993)
**DOI:** [10.1016/0014-5793(93)80539-7](https://doi.org/10.1016/0014-5793(93)80539-7)

## Abstract

1. FEBS Lett. 1993 Mar 8;318(3):325-30. doi: 10.1016/0014-5793(93)80539-7.

A human synaptic vesicle monoamine transporter cDNA predicts posttranslational 
modifications, reveals chromosome 10 gene localization and identifies TaqI 
RFLPs.

Surratt CK(1), Persico AM, Yang XD, Edgar SR, Bird GS, Hawkins AL, Griffin CA, 
Li X, Jabs EW, Uhl GR.

Author information:
(1)Molecular Neurobiology, Addiction Research Center, NIDA, NIH, Baltimore, MD 
21224.

A human vesicular monoamine transporter cDNA has been identified by screening a 
human brainstem library using sequences from the rat brain synaptic vesicle 
monoamine transporter (SVMT) [(1992) Cell 70, 539-551; (1992) Proc. Natl. Acad. 
Sci. USA 89, 10993-10997]. The hSVMT shares 92% amino acid identity with the rat 
sequence, but displays one less consensus site for asparagine N-linked 
glycosylation and one more consensus site for phosphorylation by protein kinase 
C. The human SVMT gene maps to chromosome 10q25 using Southern blotting analysis 
of human/rodent hybrid cell lines and fluorescent in situ hybridization 
approaches. The cDNA, and a subclone, recognize TaqI polymorphisms that may 
prove useful to assess this gene's involvement in neuropsychiatric disorders 
involving monoaminergic brain systems.
