## Abstract

1. Genes Dev. 1998 Jun 15;12(12):1769-74. doi: 10.1101/gad.12.12.1769.

Myc activates telomerase.

Wang J(1), Xie LY, Allan S, Beach D, Hannon GJ.

Author information:
(1)Cold Spring Harbor Laboratory (CSHL), Cold Spring Harbor, New York 11724,

Telomere maintenance has been proposed as an essential prerequisite to human 
tumor development. The telomerase enzyme is itself a marker for tumor cells, but 
the genetic alterations that activate the enzyme during neoplastic 
transformation have remained a mystery. Here, we show that Myc induces 
telomerase in both normal human mammary epithelial cells (HMECs) and normal 
human diploid fibroblasts. Myc increases expression of hEST2 (hTRT/TP2), the 
limiting subunit of telomerase, and both Myc and hEST2 can extend the life span 
of HMECs. The ability of Myc to activate telomerase may contribute to its 
ability to promote tumor formation.
