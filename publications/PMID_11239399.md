# SNARE complex oligomerization by synaphin/complexin is essential for synaptic vesicle exocytosis.
**Authors:** Tokumaru H, Umayahara K, Pellegrini LL, Ishizuka T, Saisu H, Betz H, Augustine GJ, Abe T
**Journal:** Cell (2001)
**DOI:** [10.1016/s0092-8674(01)00229-x](https://doi.org/10.1016/s0092-8674(01)00229-x)

## Content

1. Cell. 2001 Feb 9;104(3):421-32. doi: 10.1016/s0092-8674(01)00229-x.

SNARE complex oligomerization by synaphin/complexin is essential for synaptic 
vesicle exocytosis.

Tokumaru H(1), Umayahara K, Pellegrini LL, Ishizuka T, Saisu H, Betz H, 
Augustine GJ, Abe T.

Author information:
(1)Department of Neurobiology, Duke University Medical Center, Box 3209, Durham, 
NC 27710, USA.

Synaphin/complexin is a cytosolic protein that preferentially binds to syntaxin 
within the SNARE complex. We find that synaphin promotes SNAREs to form 
precomplexes that oligomerize into higher order structures. A peptide from the 
central, syntaxin binding domain of synaphin competitively inhibits these two 
proteins from interacting and prevents SNARE complexes from oligomerizing. 
Injection of this peptide into squid giant presynaptic terminals inhibited 
neurotransmitter release at a late prefusion step of synaptic vesicle 
exocytosis. We propose that oligomerization of SNARE complexes into a higher 
order structure creates a SNARE scaffold for efficient, regulated fusion of 
synaptic vesicles.
