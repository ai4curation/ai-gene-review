# Disturbing endoderm signaling to anterior neural plate of vertebrates by the teratogen cadmium.
**Authors:** Fernandez EL, Svenson C, Dencker L, Gustafson AL

## Abstract

1. Reprod Toxicol. 2004 Jul;18(5):653-60. doi: 10.1016/j.reprotox.2004.04.003.

Disturbing endoderm signaling to anterior neural plate of vertebrates by the 
teratogen cadmium.

Fernandez EL(1), Svenson C, Dencker L, Gustafson AL.

Author information:
(1)Department of Pharmaceutical Biosciences, Division of Toxicology, Biomedical 
Center, Uppsala University, Box 594, S-75124 Uppsala, Sweden.

Cadmium accumulation in the mouse gut endoderm occurs until the closure of the 
vitelline duct (day 9 post-coitus; p.c.), producing anterior neural tube defects 
(NTD). The anterior part of the primitive endoderm, designated as the primary 
signaling center for anterior patterning, expresses several transcription 
factors of importance for head formation. Here, we studied the expression levels 
of some of these transcription factors (Hesx1, HNF3beta, Cerl, Otx2 and Sox2), 
and cell death induced after single cadmium administration to dams on days 7, 8 
and 9 p.c. Stage specific down-regulation of Hesx1, Cerl, and Sox2, and an 
up-regulation of HNF3beta were observed. No effect was seen in Otx2 expression 
levels. Cell death was increased in the neuroepithelium of the cranial neural 
folds, and in areas where neural crest cells migrate, but not in the gut 
endoderm. It is proposed that cadmium-induced NTD is due to interference with 
head-inductive signals from the endoderm to the adjacent layers.
