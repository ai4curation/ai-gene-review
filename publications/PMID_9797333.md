Disruption of the HSP104 gene in a mutant which cannot accumulate trehalose 
during heat shock treatment caused trehalose accumulation (H. Iwahashi, K. 
Obuchi, S. Fujii, and Y. Komatsu, Lett. Appl. Microbiol 25:43-47, 1997). This 
implies that Hsp104 affects trehalose metabolism. Thus, we measured the 
activities of enzymes involved in trehalose metabolism. The activities of 
trehalose-synthesizing and -hydrolyzing enzymes are low in the HSP104 disruption 
mutant during heat shock. This data is correlated with intracellular trehalose 
and glucose levels observed in the HSP104 disruption mutant. These results 
suggest that during heat shock, Hsp104 contributes to the simultaneous increase 
in both accumulation and degradation of trehalose.
