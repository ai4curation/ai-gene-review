# Sterol pathway in yeast. Identification and properties of mutant strains defective in mevalonate diphosphate decarboxylase and farnesyl diphosphate synthetase.
**Authors:** Chambon C, Ladeveze V, Servouse M, Blanchard L, Javelot C, Vladescu B, Karst F
**Journal:** Lipids (1991)
**DOI:** [10.1007/BF02536428](https://doi.org/10.1007/BF02536428)

## Abstract

1. Lipids. 1991 Aug;26(8):633-6. doi: 10.1007/BF02536428.

Sterol pathway in yeast. Identification and properties of mutant strains 
defective in mevalonate diphosphate decarboxylase and farnesyl diphosphate 
synthetase.

Chambon C(1), Ladeveze V, Servouse M, Blanchard L, Javelot C, Vladescu B, Karst 
F.

Author information:
(1)Laboratoire de Biochimie et Génétique des Microorganismes Université de 
Poitiers, France.

Yeast mutant strains auxotrophic for ergosterol and blocked in mevalonate 
diphosphate decarboxylase (erg19) and farnesyl diphosphate (FPP) synthetase 
(erg20) were isolated. The main feature of the mutants blocked in FPP synthetase 
is their ability to excrete prenyl alcohols, such as geraniol and farnesol. The 
isolation of the functional ERG20 gene allowed us to show that farnesyl 
diphosphate synthetase could be a rate limiting enzyme in ergosterol 
biosynthesis in yeast.
