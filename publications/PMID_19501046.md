# Znhit1 causes cell cycle arrest and down-regulates CDK6 expression.
**Authors:** Yang Z, Cao Y, Zhu X, Huang Y, Ding Y, Liu X
**Journal:** Biochem Biophys Res Commun (2009)
**DOI:** [10.1016/j.bbrc.2009.05.139](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.bbrc.2009.05.139)

## Abstract

1. Biochem Biophys Res Commun. 2009 Aug 14;386(1):146-52. doi: 
10.1016/j.bbrc.2009.05.139. Epub 2009 Jun 6.

Znhit1 causes cell cycle arrest and down-regulates CDK6 expression.

Yang Z(1), Cao Y, Zhu X, Huang Y, Ding Y, Liu X.

Author information:
(1)Laboratory of Molecular Cell Biology, Institute of Biochemistry and Cell 
Biology, Shanghai Institutes for Biological Sciences, Chinese Academy of 
Sciences, 320 Yueyang Road, Shanghai 200031, China.

Cyclin-dependent kinase 6 (CDK6) is the key element of the D-type cyclin 
holoenzymes which has been found to function in the regulation of G1-phase of 
the cell cycle and is presumed to play important roles in T cell function. In 
this study, Znhit1, a member of a new zinc finger protein family defined by a 
conserved Zf-HIT domain, induced arrest in the G1-phase of the cell cycle in 
NIH/3T3 cells. Of the G1 cell cycle factors examined, the expression of CDK6 was 
found to be strongly down-regulated by Znhit1 via transcriptional repression. 
This effect may have correlations with the decreased acetylation level of 
histone H4 in the CDK6 promoter region. In addition, considering that CDK6 
expression predominates in T cells, the negative regulatory role of Znhit1 in 
TCR-induced T cell proliferation was validated using transgenic mice. These 
findings identified Znhit1 as a CDK6 regulator that plays an important role in 
cell proliferation.
