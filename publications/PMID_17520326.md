# Oxygen dependence of tyrosine hydroxylase.
**Authors:** Rostrup M, Fossbakk A, Hauge A, Kleppe R, Gnaiger E, Haavik J

**DOI:** [10.1007/s00726-007-0547-7](https://doi.org/10.1007/s00726-007-0547-7)

## Abstract

1. Amino Acids. 2008 Apr;34(3):455-64. doi: 10.1007/s00726-007-0547-7. Epub 2007 
May 23.

Oxygen dependence of tyrosine hydroxylase.

Rostrup M(1), Fossbakk A, Hauge A, Kleppe R, Gnaiger E, Haavik J.

Author information:
(1)Cardiovascular and Renal Research Centre, Department of Acute Medicine, 
Ullevaal University Hospital, Oslo, Norway.

The effects of dioxygen on tyrosine hydroxylase (TH) activity was studied, 
measuring the formation of DOPA from tyrosine, (3)H(2)O from 3,5-(3)H-tyrosine, 
or by direct oxygraphic determination of oxygen consumption. A high enzyme 
activity was observed during the initial 1-2 min of the reactions, followed by a 
decline in activity, possibly related to a turnover dependent 
substoichiometrical oxidation of enzyme bound Fe(II) to the inactive Fe(III) 
state. During the initial reaction phase, apparent K (m)-values of 29-45 microM 
for dioxygen were determined for all human TH isoforms, i.e. 2-40 times higher 
than previously reported for TH isolated from animal tissues. After 8 min 
incubation, the K (m) (O(2))-values had declined to an average of 20 +/- 4 
microM. Thus, TH activity may be severely limited by oxygen availability even at 
moderate hypoxic conditions, and the enzyme is rapidly and turnover dependent 
inactivated at the experimental conditions commonly employed to measure in vitro 
activities.
