# The crystallographic structure of a human dihydropteridine reductase NADH binary complex expressed in Escherichia coli by a cDNA constructed from its rat homologue.
**Authors:** Su Y, Varughese KI, Xuong NH, Bray TL, Roche DJ, Whiteley JM
**Journal:** J Biol Chem (1993)
**DOI:** [10.2210/pdb1hdr/pdb](https://doi.org/10.2210/pdb1hdr/pdb)

## Abstract

1. J Biol Chem. 1993 Dec 25;268(36):26836-41. doi: 10.2210/pdb1hdr/pdb.

The crystallographic structure of a human dihydropteridine reductase NADH binary 
complex expressed in Escherichia coli by a cDNA constructed from its rat 
homologue.

Su Y(1), Varughese KI, Xuong NH, Bray TL, Roche DJ, Whiteley JM.

Author information:
(1)Department of Chemistry, University of California at San Diego, La Jolla 
92093-0317.

A human dihydropteridine reductase (EC 1.6.99.10) has been created from a rat 
cDNA clone by a single five-oligonucleotide mutagenesis reaction and expressed 
in good yield in Escherichia coli. The enzyme has been purified to homogeneity, 
and kinetic identity to the naturally occurring enzyme has been proven. 
Crystallization has also been achieved, and the crystal structure was solved 
using 2.5 A data that was refined to an R value of 16.9%. The structure 
described in this report represents the first complete structural 
characterization of this important human enzyme.
