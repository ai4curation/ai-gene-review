# Structure and properties of recombinant human pyridoxine 5'-phosphate oxidase.
**Authors:** Musayev FN, Di Salvo ML, Ko TP, Schirch V, Safo MK
**Journal:** Protein Sci (2003)
**DOI:** [10.1110/ps.0356203](https://doi.org/10.1110/ps.0356203)
**PMC:** [PMC2323923](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC2323923/)

## Abstract

1. Protein Sci. 2003 Jul;12(7):1455-63. doi: 10.1110/ps.0356203.

Structure and properties of recombinant human pyridoxine 5'-phosphate oxidase.

Musayev FN(1), Di Salvo ML, Ko TP, Schirch V, Safo MK.

Author information:
(1)Department of Medicinal Chemistry, Institute for Structural Biology and Drug 
Discovery, Virginia Commonwealth University, 800 E. Leigh Street, Richmond, VA 
23219, USA.

Pyridoxine 5'-phosphate oxidase catalyzes the terminal step in the synthesis of 
pyridoxal 5'-phosphate. The cDNA for the human enzyme has been cloned and 
expressed in Escherichia coli. The purified human enzyme is a homodimer that 
exhibits a low catalytic rate constant of approximately 0.2 sec(-1) and K(m) 
values in the low micromolar range for both pyridoxine 5'phosphate and 
pyridoxamine 5'-phosphate. Pyridoxal 5'-phosphate is an effective product 
inhibitor. The three-dimensional fold of the human enzyme is very similar to 
those of the E. coli and yeast enzymes. The human and E. coli enzymes share 39% 
sequence identity, but the binding sites for the tightly bound FMN and substrate 
are highly conserved. As observed with the E. coli enzyme, the human enzyme 
binds one molecule of pyridoxal 5'-phosphate tightly on each subunit.
