Author information:
(1)Georg-August-Universität, Abt. Biochemie II, Göttingen, Germany.

In a child with severe muscular hypotonia and extrapyramidal movement disorder, 
elevation of guanidinoacetate and deficiency of creatine in brain suggested a 
defect in biosynthesis of creatine at the level of guanidinoacetate 
methyltransferase (GAMT) (Stöckler, S. et al. (1994) Pediatr. Res. 36, 409-413). 
As a first step in the molecular analysis of GAMT deficiency, the cDNA for human 
GAMT was cloned using rat GAMT cDNA as a probe. For the coding region there is a 
82.5% homology of the nucleotide sequence and 86.9% homology of the deduced 
amino acid sequence between human and rat.
