# CYP4B1 activates 4-ipomeanol in rat lung.
**Authors:** Verschoyle RD, Philpot RM, Wolf CR, Dinsdale D
**Journal:** Toxicol Appl Pharmacol (1993)
**DOI:** [10.1006/taap.1993.1237](https://doi.org/10.1006/taap.1993.1237)

## Abstract

1. Toxicol Appl Pharmacol. 1993 Dec;123(2):193-8. doi: 10.1006/taap.1993.1237.

CYP4B1 activates 4-ipomeanol in rat lung.

Verschoyle RD(1), Philpot RM, Wolf CR, Dinsdale D.

Author information:
(1)MRC Toxicology Unit, MRC Laboratories, Carshalton, Surrey, United Kingdom.

Inhibition of pulmonary CYP4B1 activity by pretreatment of rats with p-xylene 
decreased the ability of lung microsomes to N-hydroxylate 2-aminofluorene and 
prevented the lung damage normally seen after dosing with ipomeanol. The 
toxicity of ipomeanol, as assessed by acute lethality, was decreased by a factor 
of eight. In contrast, induction of CYP1A1 by Aroclor or beta-naphthoflavone, or 
inhibition of CYP2B1 by O,O,S-trimethyl-phosphorodithioate, as assessed by 
measurement of lung microsomal dealkylation of ethoxyresorufin or 
pentoxyresorufin, did not change ipomeanol toxicity. A polyclonal antibody 
raised against CYP4B1 prevented the covalent binding of [14C]-ipomeanol to lung 
microsomal protein in vitro. Antibodies raised against the other major P450 
isozymes of rat lung, CYP2B1 and CYP1A1, had no effect on this binding. Aroclor, 
beta-naphthoflavone, and O,O,S-trimethylphosphorodithioate failed to affect 
binding of radiolabeled ipomeanol in vivo, but pretreatment with p-xylene 
resulted in a significant reduction in this binding. The CYP4B1 substrate 
2-aminofluorene, when dosed to rats, caused a sixfold decrease in ipomeanol 
toxicity. These results indicate that in the rat, unlike the rabbit, pulmonary 
bioactivation of ipomeanol is predominantly dependent upon CYP4B1.
