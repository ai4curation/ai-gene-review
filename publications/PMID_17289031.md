# Subcellular localization of Daxx determines its opposing functions in ischemic cell death.
**Authors:** Jung YS, Kim HY, Lee YJ, Kim E
**Journal:** FEBS Lett (2007)
**DOI:** [10.1016/j.febslet.2007.01.055](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.febslet.2007.01.055)

## Abstract

1. FEBS Lett. 2007 Mar 6;581(5):843-52. doi: 10.1016/j.febslet.2007.01.055. Epub 
2007 Feb 2.

Subcellular localization of Daxx determines its opposing functions in ischemic 
cell death.

Jung YS(1), Kim HY, Lee YJ, Kim E.

Author information:
(1)Department of Bioscience and Biotechnology, Chungnam National University, 220 
Gung-dong, Yuseong-gu, Daejeon 305-764, Republic of Korea.

This study examined the role of Daxx in ischemic stress. Upon ischemic stress, 
nuclear export of Daxx to the cytoplasm was observed in primary myocytes as well 
as in various cell lines. Daxx silencing using siRNAs was detrimental in 
tethering PML-nuclear body (PML-NB) constituents together. Overexpression of 
Daxx (W621A) caused nuclear export of p53 independently of PML and promoted 
ischemic cell death via activation of JNK. Conversely, overexpression of Daxx 
(S667A) prevented dissociation of PML-NB constituents and protected cells from 
ischemic death. Collectively, our results demonstrate that the subcellular 
localization of Daxx determines its role in ischemic cell death.
