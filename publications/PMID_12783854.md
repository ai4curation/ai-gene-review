# Periodic repression by the bHLH factor Hes7 is an essential mechanism for the somite segmentation clock.
**Authors:** Bessho Y, Hirata H, Masamizu Y, Kageyama R
**Journal:** Genes Dev (2003)
**DOI:** [10.1101/gad.1092303](https://doi.org/10.1101/gad.1092303)

## Abstract

1. Genes Dev. 2003 Jun 15;17(12):1451-6. doi: 10.1101/gad.1092303. Epub 2003 Jun
3.

Periodic repression by the bHLH factor Hes7 is an essential mechanism for the 
somite segmentation clock.

Bessho Y(1), Hirata H, Masamizu Y, Kageyama R.

Author information:
(1)Institute for Virus Research, Kyoto University, Kyoto 606-8507, Japan. 
ybessho@virus.kyoto-u.ac.jp

Hes7, a bHLH gene essential for somitogenesis, displays cyclic expression of 
mRNA in the presomitic mesoderm (PSM). Here, we show that Hes7 protein is also 
expressed in a dynamic manner, which depends on proteasome-mediated degradation. 
Spatial comparison revealed that Hes7 and Lunatic fringe (Lfng) transcription 
occurs in the Hes7 protein-negative domains. Furthermore, Hes7 and Lfng 
transcription is constitutively up-regulated in the absence of Hes7 protein and 
down-regulated by stabilization of Hes7 protein. Thus, periodic repression by 
Hes7 protein is critical for the cyclic transcription of Hes7 and Lfng, and this 
negative feedback represents a molecular basis for the segmentation clock.
