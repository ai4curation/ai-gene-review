# Exome sequencing reveals a novel Fanconi group defined by XRCC2 mutation.
**Authors:** Shamseldin HE, Elfaki M, Alkuraya FS
**Journal:** J Med Genet (2012)
**DOI:** [10.1136/jmedgenet-2011-100585](https://doi.org/10.1136/jmedgenet-2011-100585)

## Abstract

1. J Med Genet. 2012 Mar;49(3):184-6. doi: 10.1136/jmedgenet-2011-100585. Epub
2012  Jan 9.

Exome sequencing reveals a novel Fanconi group defined by XRCC2 mutation.

Shamseldin HE(1), Elfaki M, Alkuraya FS.

Author information:
(1)Department of Genetics, King Faisal Specialist Hospital and Research Center, 
Saudi Arabia.

Background Fanconi anaemia (FA) is a group of disorders characterised by 
progressive bone marrow failure and a characteristic but variable craniofacial 
and skeletal involvement. Recessive mutations in any of 15 genes linked to FA 
lead to the pathognomonic increased susceptibility to double-strand DNA breaks. 
Methods Autozygome and exome analysis of a patient with classic FA phenotype 
Results The authors identified a novel truncating mutation in XRCC2. Consistent 
with the proposed causal link to FA, this gene is an essential non-redundant 
component of the RAD51 family of homologous repair proteins and its deficiency 
in a murine model has been shown to lead to a highly similar phenotype to that 
of this patient both at the cellular and organismal level. Conclusion This study 
implicates XRCC2 in the pathogenesis of FA and calls for further investigation 
of the potential contribution of XRCC2 mutations to the overall mutational load 
of FA.
