# Isolation and characterization of a human heart cDNA encoding a new member of the small heat shock protein family--HSPL27.
**Authors:** Lam WY, Wing Tsui SK, Law PT, Luk SC, Fung KP, Lee CY, Waye MM
**Journal:** Biochim Biophys Acta (1996)
**DOI:** [10.1016/s0167-4889(96)00121-8](https://doi.org/10.1016/s0167-4889(96)00121-8)

## Abstract

1. Biochim Biophys Acta. 1996 Nov 8;1314(1-2):120-4. doi: 
10.1016/s0167-4889(96)00121-8.

Isolation and characterization of a human heart cDNA encoding a new member of 
the small heat shock protein family--HSPL27.

Lam WY(1), Wing Tsui SK, Law PT, Luk SC, Fung KP, Lee CY, Waye MM.

Author information:
(1)Department of Biochemistry, Chinese University of Hong Kong, Shatin N.T., 
Hong Kong.

A novel cDNA clone was isolated from a human adult heart cDNA library. This cDNA 
clone is similar to the small heat shock protein (smhsp) in both DNA and amino 
acid sequences, especially in the conserved region. Sequence analysis has shown 
that the putative novel smhsp, named 27 kDa heat-shock-protein-like protein 
(HSPL27) is a protein of 241 amino acids with a deduced molecular mass of 26.7 
kDa and a deduced pI of 8.0. We have expressed the HSPL27 in E. coli and the 
expressed protein was found to be present in the soluble fraction of the 
bacterial cell lysate. Chromosomal mapping data shows that the HSPL27 gene is 
located at human chromosome 5q11.2.
