# N-myristoyltransferase 2 expression in human colon cancer: cross-talk between the calpain and caspase system.
**Authors:** Selvakumar P, Smith-Windsor E, Bonham K, Sharma RK
**Journal:** FEBS Lett (2006)
**DOI:** [10.1016/j.febslet.2006.02.076](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.febslet.2006.02.076)

## Abstract

1. FEBS Lett. 2006 Apr 3;580(8):2021-6. doi: 10.1016/j.febslet.2006.02.076. Epub 
2006 Mar 7.

N-myristoyltransferase 2 expression in human colon cancer: cross-talk between 
the calpain and caspase system.

Selvakumar P(1), Smith-Windsor E, Bonham K, Sharma RK.

Author information:
(1)Department of Pathology and Laboratory of Medicine, College of Medicine, 
University of Saskatchewan, Saskatoon, Saskatchewan, Canada S7N 5E5.

A number of viral and eukaryotic proteins which undergo a lipophilic 
modification by the enzyme N-myristoyltransferase (NMT: NMT1 and NMT2) are 
required for signal transduction and regulatory functions. To investigate 
whether NMT2 contributes to the pathogenesis of colorectal carcinoma, we 
observed a higher expression of NMT2 in most of the cases of cancerous tissues 
compared to normal tissues (84.6% of cases; P < 0.05) by Western blot analysis. 
Furthermore, protein-protein interaction of NMTs revealed that m-calpain 
interacts with NMT1 while caspase-3 interacts with NMT2. Our findings provide 
the first evidence of higher expression of NMT2 in human colorectal 
adenocarcinomas and the interaction of both forms of NMT with various signaling 
molecules.
