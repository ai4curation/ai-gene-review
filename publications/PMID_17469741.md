# Results of the determination of serum markers in patients with malignant melanoma.
**Authors:** Lugović L, Situm M, Buljan M, Poduje S, Sebetić K
**Journal:** Coll Antropol (2007)

## Abstract

1. Coll Antropol. 2007 Jan;31 Suppl 1:7-11.

Results of the determination of serum markers in patients with malignant 
melanoma.

Lugović L(1), Situm M, Buljan M, Poduje S, Sebetić K.

Author information:
(1)Department of Dermatology and Venereology, University Hospital "Sestre 
milosrdnice", Zagreb, Croatia. liborija@yahoo.com

Although there is no routine procedure for determination of serum markers in 
patients with malignant melanoma (MM), some markers are being studied as 
potentially useful prognostic tools. Serum lactate dehydrogenase (LDH), protein 
S-100B, melanoma-inhibiting activity (MIA) and tyrosinase may correlate with 
melanoma progression. In this study, the results of determination of S100 
protein, LDH, MIA and tyrosinase in the serum of 50 patients with MM (stages 
I-IV) were determined. The increased values of MIA were found in 26% patients in 
stage I, while in 50% patients in stage IV Increased S-100 protein was found in 
13% patients in stage I while in 50% patients in stage IV. The increased values 
of LDH were found in 26% patients in stage I, while in 25% patients in stage IV. 
The positive serum tyrosinase was noticed in 17.3% patients in stage II, while 
in 25% patients in stage IV. The obtained results have revealed no significant 
differences between the groups in higher and lower stages of the disease, 
indicating that blood markers are not reliable prognostic factors for MM 
progression.
