Instability of the nuclear genome is a hallmark of cancer and aging. MMS19 
protein has been linked to maintenance of genomic integrity, but the molecular 
basis of this connection is unknown. Here, we identify MMS19 as a member of the 
cytosolic iron-sulfur protein assembly (CIA) machinery. MMS19 functions as part 
of the CIA targeting complex that specifically interacts with and facilitates 
iron-sulfur cluster insertion into apoproteins involved in methionine 
biosynthesis, DNA replication, DNA repair, and telomere maintenance. MMS19 thus 
serves as an adapter between early-acting CIA components and a subset of 
cellular iron-sulfur proteins. The function of MMS19 in the maturation of 
crucial components of DNA metabolism may explain the sensitivity of MMS19 
mutants to DNA damage and the presence of extended telomeres.
