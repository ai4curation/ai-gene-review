# Distinct neuronal growth hormone receptor ligand specificity in the rat brain.
**Authors:** Möderscheim TA, Christophidis LJ, Williams CE, Scheepens A
**Journal:** Brain Res (2007)
**DOI:** [10.1016/j.brainres.2006.12.040](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.brainres.2006.12.040)

## Abstract

1. Brain Res. 2007 Mar 16;1137(1):29-34. doi: 10.1016/j.brainres.2006.12.040.
Epub  2006 Dec 20.

Distinct neuronal growth hormone receptor ligand specificity in the rat brain.

Möderscheim TA(1), Christophidis LJ, Williams CE, Scheepens A.

Author information:
(1)The Liggins Institute, University of Auckland, Private Bag 92019, Auckland, 
New Zealand.

A cerebral growth hormone axis is activated during recovery from brain injury 
and centrally administered growth hormone can rescue injured neurons. It remains 
unclear, however, whether this treatment effect occurs directly via neuronal 
growth hormone receptors. Immunohistochemistry confirmed growth hormone receptor 
protein on neuronal cell bodies in the rat cortex. Surprisingly, we found that 
central treatment with bovine growth hormone, which is equipotent to rat growth 
hormone in the rat periphery, failed to rescue cortical neurons following 
hypoxic ischemic injury. We further investigated the actions of rat and bovine 
growth hormone on primary neuron-enriched cultures of fetal rat cortex. In 
agreement with the in vivo treatment studies, rat but not bovine growth hormone 
rescued neurons from nutrient deprivation-induced cell death (p<0.05). This 
neuroprotective effect was inhibited by the selective growth hormone receptor 
antagonist G120D (p<0.001). Furthermore, rat but not bovine growth hormone had 
trophic effects on uninjured cultures (p<0.001). Immunocytochemistry showed 
growth hormone receptor on neurons within the neuron-enriched cultures. We show 
for the first time that the protective and trophic effects of rat growth hormone 
are mediated via growth hormone receptors on neurons and that the rodent 
neuronal growth hormone receptor exhibits unique ligand specificity.
