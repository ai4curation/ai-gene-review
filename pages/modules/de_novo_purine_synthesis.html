De novo purine synthesis (PRPP -> IMP); ADSL deficiency, AICA-ribosiduria

De novo purine biosynthesis is the cytosolic ten-step pathway that builds the first complete purine nucleotide, inosine monophosphate (IMP), from 5-phospho-alpha-D-ribose 1-diphosphate (PRPP), using glycine, glutamine, aspartate, CO2, two one-carbon units from 10-formyltetrahydrofolate, and ATP. Amidophosphoribosyltransferase (PPAT) catalyses the committed, feedback-regulated first step (PRPP + glutamine -> 5-phosphoribosylamine); the trifunctional protein GART then carries out steps 2, 3 and 5 (GAR synthetase, GAR transformylase, AIR synthetase), with phosphoribosylformyl- glycinamidine synthase (PFAS) performing the intervening step 4; the bifunctional PAICS performs steps 6 and 7 (AIR carboxylase, SAICAR synthetase); adenylosuccinate lyase (ADSL) performs step 8 (SAICAR -> AICAR + fumarate); and the bifunctional ATIC completes the pathway with steps 9 and 10 (AICAR transformylase, IMP cyclohydrolase) to yield IMP. IMP is then the branch point to AMP (ADSS/ADSL) and GMP (IMPDH/GMPS). The enzymes reversibly assemble into a multienzyme "purinosome" under high purine demand. Two of the steps are folate-dependent, making GART and ATIC targets of antifolate/antiproliferative drugs. Inherited defects: adenylosuccinate lyase (ADSL) deficiency causes a neurodevelopmental disorder with SAICA-riboside/succinyladenosine accumulation; ATIC deficiency causes AICA-ribosiduria (profound intellectual disability, epilepsy, dysmorphism); and a PAICS-deficiency multiple-malformation syndrome has been reported.

Ten-step de novo purine (IMP) synthesis grounded to the human enzymes PPAT (UniProtKB:Q06203, GO:0004044, EC 2.4.2.14), the trifunctional GART (P22102; GO:0004637/GO:0004644/GO:0004641; EC 6.3.4.13 / 2.1.2.2 / 6.3.3.1), PFAS (O15067, GO:0004642, EC 6.3.5.3), the bifunctional PAICS (P22234; GO:0004638/GO:0004639; EC 4.1.1.21 / 6.3.2.6), ADSL (P30566, GO:0070626, EC 4.3.2.2) and the bifunctional ATIC (P31939; GO:0004643/GO:0003937; EC 2.1.2.3 / 3.5.4.10). GO molecular-function terms were taken from the human GOA records; Reactome reaction ids and titles were verified against the local reactome cache. Each node uses a PANTHER family selector (generic over orthologs) with the human enzyme as representative; only the first activity of each multifunctional enzyme is placed in the annoton `function` slot, with the additional activities/EC numbers given in the evidence and role_description. The pathway is not strictly linear at the gene level: the trifunctional GART catalyses steps 2, 3 AND 5, re-entering after the PFAS step 4 (captured by the PFAS->GART connection). Upstream, PRPP comes from PRPS1 (pentose_phosphate_nonoxidative_branch module); downstream, IMP is the branch point to AMP (ADSS1/ADSL) and GMP (IMPDH1/2, GMPS) and the substrate handled with the purine salvage/catabolism module (HPRT1 makes IMP by salvage). Two folate-dependent steps (GART transformylase, ATIC transformylase) are antifolate/antiproliferative drug targets. Disorders: ADSL -> adenylosuccinate lyase deficiency; ATIC -> AICA-ribosiduria; PAICS -> a reported multiple-malformation syndrome; PPAT/GART/PFAS have no common Mendelian disease.
