Phosphonate and phosphinate metabolism

Pseudomonas putida KT2440 phosphonate module combining the compact KEGG ppu00440 PhnW/PhnX 2-aminoethylphosphonate (AEP) catabolic route with separately curated phosphonate ABC import blocks from KEGG ppu02010 and ppu02024. PhnW transaminates AEP to phosphonoacetaldehyde, and PhnX hydrolyzes phosphonoacetaldehyde to acetaldehyde and inorganic phosphate. The transporter records capture organic phosphonate uptake capacity, including a PP_1722-PP_1726 AEP/phosphonate import candidate, but the exact transported substrate(s) and direct handoff to the PhnW/PhnX route remain a knowledge gap. This module is not evidence for a broad C-P lyase or phosphinate metabolism system.

PSEPK ppu00440 should be interpreted as PhnWX AEP catabolism. The PP_0824-PP_0827 ppu02010 ABC-transporter block and PP_1722-PP_1726 ppu02024 ABC-transporter block are curated here as phosphonate/AEP import context because they are substrate-coherent, not because the full ppu02010 or ppu02024 umbrella maps are satisfiable pathways. The module still does not establish a broad phosphinate route or the multi-subunit C-P lyase system; those would require additional mapped genes and evidence.

Part 2: AEP/phosphonate import candidate
PP_1722-PP_1726 AEP/phosphonate ABC import candidateTransport Steppp1722_pp1726_aep_phosphonate_abc_import

PP_1722, PP_1723, PP_1724, and PP_1726 form a predicted ABC import block. The two permeases are annotated as 2-aminoethylphosphonate transporter components, PP_1722 is the ABC ATP-binding subunit, and PP_1726 is a signal-peptide-bearing periplasmic binding protein. This supports phosphonate/AEP uptake context, while the exact substrate range and direct connection to PhnW remain unresolved.
