Thiamine biosynthesis

A bacterial thiamine diphosphate biosynthesis module covering formation of the hydroxymethylpyrimidine and thiazole moieties, condensation to thiamine monophosphate, and phosphorylation to the active thiamine diphosphate cofactor. In Pseudomonas putida KT2440, KEGG ppu00730 also includes shared precursor, sulfur-transfer, thiamine-salvage, nucleotide, and ribosome-side entries; those are treated as support or boundary context rather than all being strict thiamine-biosynthetic enzymes.

The strict KT2440 thiamine module is centered on thiamine diphosphate biosynthesis. KEGG ppu00730 pulls in useful support enzymes and side nodes: `dxs` supplies DXP shared with MEP metabolism; `iscS`/`iscS-II` supply sulfur generally and should be curated carefully before assigning a specific thiamine role; `thiI` links tRNA 4-thiouridine and ThiS sulfur-carrier chemistry; `PP_3186` and neighboring `pet`/PP_3185 are likely thiamine-salvage aminopyrimidine aminohydrolases; `adk` and `rsgA` are nucleotide/ribosome side entries in this KEGG map, not strict thiamine-biosynthesis enzymes. `PP_5105` encodes sulfur carrier protein ThiS and is promoted as a missing accessory/gap-fill candidate even though it is absent from the KEGG ppu00730 membership table. Falcon also flagged ThiF as an expected sulfur-relay component, but no unambiguous `thiF` product or symbol was found in the local PSEPK metadata; PP_0735/moeB is a related MoaD adenylyltransferase and should not be promoted to ThiF without additional evidence.

Part 5: sulfur transfer to the thiazole branch
ThiS sulfur-carrier chargingReactionthiI_iscS_thiS_sulfur_transfer

The canonical bacterial relay uses IscS-derived persulfide, ThiI, ThiF-mediated ThiS adenylation, and ThiS thiocarboxylation to produce the sulfur donor for ThiG-dependent thiazole formation. KT2440 has a local ThiS candidate (PP_5105) even though it is not included in KEGG ppu00730; a specific ThiF candidate remains unresolved in the local metadata.
