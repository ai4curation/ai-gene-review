Chemotaxis receptor panel boundary

Boundary module for the ppu02030 known/named methyl-accepting chemotaxis receptor panel in Pseudomonas putida KT2440. The module groups characterized and named MCP receptors that feed chemoeffector information into bacterial chemotaxis signaling, while keeping orphan MCP/aerotaxis receptors and transport-binding spillovers outside this first-pass panel.

Curated mcpS review
The mcpS review records McpS as the receptor for TCA-cycle intermediates and related organic acids, including malate, succinate, fumarate, oxaloacetate, citrate, isocitrate, and butyrate.

First-pass interpretation: the receptor panel is biologically coherent but not a single linear reaction pathway. Each receptor contributes ligand-specific input to chemotaxis signaling. ctpL is retained as a named MCP-family receptor candidate with unresolved KT2440 ligand specificity; orphan MCP and aerotaxis receptors remain deferred until ligand or local pathway context is selected.
