Lipoic acid metabolism boundary

Boundary module for KEGG ppu00785 in Pseudomonas putida KT2440. The strict endogenous protein-lipoylation route is LipB octanoyl transfer followed by LipA radical-SAM sulfur insertion. The broader KEGG lipoic-acid map also includes lipoate-dependent enzyme complexes and shared dihydrolipoyl dehydrogenases: pyruvate dehydrogenase, 2-oxoglutarate dehydrogenase, branched-chain 2-oxoacid dehydrogenase, glycine cleavage, and an acetoin-cleavage E2-like component. These are tracked as lipoylated target or lipoamide redox context rather than additional lipoate-biosynthesis enzymes.

Part 1: Endogenous protein lipoylation
LipB-LipA endogenous protein lipoylationMetabolic Pathwayendogenous_protein_lipoylation

LipB transfers an octanoyl group from octanoyl-ACP to target lipoyl domains. LipA then inserts sulfur atoms into the octanoylated lysine side chain, forming the protein-bound lipoyl cofactor used by 2-oxoacid dehydrogenase and glycine-cleavage systems.
