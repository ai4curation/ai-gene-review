Propanoate metabolism boundary

Boundary module for KEGG ppu00640 in Pseudomonas putida KT2440. The organism-specific map combines the Prp/methylcitrate route for propionyl-CoA assimilation and detoxification with methylmalonate-semialdehyde and valine/thymine side routes, acetate/acyl-CoA activation, fatty-acid beta-oxidation, acetyl-CoA carboxylase, TCA/glyoxylate, and branched-chain/lipoamide complex overlap nodes. It is curated here as a set of separable pathway contexts rather than one linear propanoate pathway.

This module intentionally records boundary status. The highest-confidence propionate-specific route is the methylcitrate-cycle spine, with AcnB carrying organism-specific evidence for odd-chain fatty-acid growth. The remaining ppu00640 genes should be reviewed in their native modules unless direct propionate-specific evidence is available.

Part 1: Prp methylcitrate-cycle spine
Prp methylcitrate-cycle spineMetabolic Pathwayprp_methylcitrate_cycle_spine

PrpE, PrpC, PrpD/PrpF, AcnA-II/AcnB, and PrpB provide the direct propionyl-CoA-to-pyruvate/succinate methylcitrate-cycle chemistry. The AcnB review contains organism-specific evidence that AcnB is a key methylcitrate-cycle dehydratase/hydratase for growth on odd-chain fatty acids, while PrpD/AcnA-II/PrpF remain sequence-supported route members whose exact in vivo partition needs more evidence.
