Heme biosynthesis (the porphyrin pathway); ALAS2/ALAD/HMBS/UROS/UROD/CPOX/PPOX/FECH

Heme is built from glycine and succinyl-CoA in an eight-step pathway that shuttles between the mitochondrion and the cytosol and underlies the porphyrias. The committed, rate-limiting first step is mitochondrial: 5-aminolevulinate synthase (ALAS2, the erythroid isoform; ALAS1 is the housekeeping isoform) condenses glycine with succinyl-CoA to 5-aminolevulinate (ALA) using pyridoxal-5'-phosphate. ALA is exported to the cytosol, where four enzymes act: porphobilinogen synthase (ALAD, zinc-dependent) condenses two ALA to the monopyrrole porphobilinogen (PBG); hydroxymethylbilane synthase (HMBS/PBG deaminase) polymerises four PBG into the linear tetrapyrrole hydroxymethylbilane; uroporphyrinogen-III synthase (UROS) cyclises it, with ring-D inversion, to uroporphyrinogen III; and uroporphyrinogen decarboxylase (UROD) decarboxylates its four acetate side chains to give coproporphyrinogen III. The last three steps return to the mitochondrion: coproporphyrinogen-III oxidase (CPOX, in the intermembrane space) oxidatively decarboxylates two propionates to vinyls, giving protoporphyrinogen IX; protoporphyrinogen oxidase (PPOX, FAD-dependent, on the inner membrane) oxidises it to protoporphyrin IX; and ferrochelatase (FECH, a [2Fe-2S] enzyme on the matrix face of the inner membrane) inserts ferrous iron to yield heme b. Each enzyme defines a porphyria: ALAS2 (X-linked sideroblastic anaemia / X-linked protoporphyria), ALAD (ALAD-deficiency porphyria), HMBS (acute intermittent porphyria), UROS (congenital erythropoietic porphyria), UROD (porphyria cutanea tarda / hepatoerythropoietic porphyria), CPOX (hereditary coproporphyria), PPOX (variegate porphyria) and FECH (erythropoietic protoporphyria).

Complete heme (porphyrin) biosynthesis pathway grounded to the human enzymes ALAS2 (UniProtKB:P22557, GO:0003870, EC 2.3.1.37), ALAD (P13716, GO:0004655, EC 4.2.1.24), HMBS (P08397, GO:0004418, EC 2.5.1.61), UROS (P10746, GO:0004852, EC 4.2.1.75), UROD (P06132, GO:0004853, EC 4.1.1.37), CPOX (P36551, GO:0004109, EC 1.3.3.3), PPOX (P50336, GO:0004729, EC 1.3.3.4) and FECH (P22830, GO:0004325, EC 4.98.1.1). GO molecular-function/location terms were taken from the completed human gene reviews and verified against the local go.db; Reactome reaction ids/titles were verified against the local reactome cache. Each step uses a PANTHER family selector with the human enzyme as representative so the module generalises across orthologs/paralogs. The pathway is spatially split: ALAS2 is mitochondrial (matrix), the ALA product is exported and the four middle steps (ALAD, HMBS, UROS, UROD) are cytosolic, then coproporphyrinogen III re-enters the mitochondrion for the final three membrane-associated steps (CPOX in the intermembrane space; PPOX and FECH on the inner membrane). ALAS2 is the erythroid isoform used here (the ubiquitous ALAS1 is the housekeeping counterpart and is not a dismech mapping gene). Each enzyme corresponds to a specific porphyria: ALAS2 (X-linked sideroblastic anaemia / X-linked protoporphyria), ALAD (ALAD-deficiency porphyria), HMBS (acute intermittent porphyria), UROS (congenital erythropoietic porphyria), UROD (porphyria cutanea tarda / hepatoerythropoietic porphyria), CPOX (hereditary coproporphyria), PPOX (variegate porphyria) and FECH (erythropoietic protoporphyria).
