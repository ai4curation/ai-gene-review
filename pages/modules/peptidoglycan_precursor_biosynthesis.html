Peptidoglycan precursor biosynthesis and lipid II export

A reusable bacterial pathway that converts UDP-N-acetylglucosamine to UDP-MurNAc-pentapeptide, transfers that nucleotide precursor to the undecaprenyl carrier to form lipid I, glycosylates lipid I to form lipid II, and translocates lipid II across the cytoplasmic membrane. D-Ala-D-Ala synthesis is modeled as a convergent input to MurF, and alternative MurE branches represent meso-diaminopimelate- and L-lysine-containing stem peptides. The module ends at lipid II export and does not include glycan polymerization, peptide cross-linking, carrier recycling, or cell-wall remodeling.

The module is taxon-neutral but uses exact KT2440 and reviewed Staphylococcus accessions as representative members. It intentionally excludes amino-acid precursor supply, the undecaprenyl carrier cycle, SEDS/PBP polymerization, and peptidoglycan remodeling so those systems can be curated as separate reusable modules.
