Tryptophan metabolism boundary

Boundary module for KEGG ppu00380 in Pseudomonas putida KT2440. The organism-specific map is broad and mixes tryptophan-related reactions with catalase/peroxidase, glutaryl-CoA dehydrogenase, beta-oxidation-like CoA enzymes, lipoamide enzymes, thiolases, and aromatic amino-acid side nodes. In the current partition, only gcdH and katG are primary to this bucket. Their gene-level reviews support glutaryl-CoA dehydrogenase activity and hydrogen-peroxide detoxification, respectively, but do not by themselves make a satisfiable dedicated tryptophan-catabolic module. Strict tryptophan biosynthesis is tracked separately in the tryptophan_biosynthesis module.
