Glycine betaine biosynthesis from choline with BetI regulatory boundary

A Pseudomonas putida KT2440 UniPathway UPA00529 boundary module for choline- derived glycine betaine biosynthesis. The strict catalytic route converts choline to betaine aldehyde and then glycine betaine through BetA and BetB. BetI is included as regulatory context because the UniPathway member set and UniProt record place it in choline regulation of the bet genes, but BetI is not a catalytic biosynthetic enzyme.

First-pass interpretation: UPA00529 is satisfiable as a compact osmoprotection-side pathway. The catalytic biosynthesis module is BetA/BetB. BetI is retained as a boundary/regulatory member because the pathway member list includes it and its curated function is control of bet gene transcription. Transporters such as BetT and paralogous choline dehydrogenases outside the UPA00529 member set are not included here.
