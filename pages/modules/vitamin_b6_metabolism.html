Vitamin B6 metabolism

A bacterial vitamin B6 metabolism module covering DXP-dependent de novo formation of pyridoxine 5'-phosphate, oxidation to pyridoxal 5'-phosphate, and salvage phosphorylation of B6 vitamers. In Pseudomonas putida KT2440, KEGG ppu00750 also includes shared precursor and amino-acid-metabolism nodes; those are treated as boundary context unless they directly make or salvage pyridoxal 5'-phosphate.

pyridoxal 5'-phosphate biosynthetic processGO:0042823 pyridoxine biosynthetic processGO:0008615 pyridoxal 5'-phosphate salvageGO:0009443

The strict PLP-forming module is satisfiable in Pseudomonas putida KT2440 through epd, pdxB, serC, pdxA, pdxJ, and pdxH, with pdxY covering pyridoxal salvage phosphorylation. thrC and PP_0662 are retained as threonine synthase/PLP-enzyme boundary context and should not be counted as committed B6 biosynthetic steps. Dxs supplies the DXP boundary precursor but is shared with thiamine and MEP metabolism; no obvious PdxK-like broader B6 kinase was found in the local first-pass metadata.
