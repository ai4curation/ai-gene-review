L-lysine biosynthesis via diaminopimelate

A bacterial boundary module for L-lysine biosynthesis through the diaminopimelate route. The strict route starts from the shared aspartate-family precursor aspartate semialdehyde, forms tetrahydrodipicolinate through DapA and DapB, uses the succinylated DAP branch through DapD, DapC, and DapE, epimerizes L,L-diaminopimelate with DapF, and decarboxylates meso-diaminopimelate with LysA. In Pseudomonas putida KT2440, KEGG ppu00300 also includes upstream aspartate-family branch enzymes, homoserine dehydrogenases, ornithine/acetylornithine aminotransferase context, and peptidoglycan ligases that consume meso-diaminopimelate; those are tracked as boundary context unless gene-level evidence supports a committed lysine-biosynthetic role.

Part 2: tetrahydrodipicolinate formation
Aspartate semialdehyde to tetrahydrodipicolinateMetabolic PathwaydapA_dapB_steps

DapA condenses aspartate semialdehyde with pyruvate to form 4-hydroxy-tetrahydrodipicolinate, and DapB reduces this intermediate toward tetrahydrodipicolinate. PSEPK encodes multiple DapA-like proteins, so gene-level curation should retain paralog uncertainty where direct pathway-specific evidence is missing.
