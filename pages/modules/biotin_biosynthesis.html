Bacterial de novo biotin biosynthesis — the conserved KAPA→DAPA→dethiobiotin→biotin core (BioF/BioA/BioD/BioB)

De novo biosynthesis of the cofactor biotin (vitamin B7) in bacteria proceeds through a conserved four-step terminal pathway that converts a pimeloyl thioester and simple amino donors into the fused bicyclic biotin ring. In the canonical route: (1) the pyridoxal-5'-phosphate (PLP) enzyme 8-amino-7- oxononanoate synthase (BioF) condenses a pimeloyl thioester (pimeloyl-[acyl- carrier protein] in Escherichia coli) with L-alanine to give 8-amino-7- oxononanoate (KAPA, also called 7-keto-8-aminopelargonic acid); (2) the PLP aminotransferase adenosylmethionine-8-amino-7-oxononanoate transaminase (BioA) transaminates KAPA to 7,8-diaminononanoate (DAPA), using S-adenosyl-L- methionine — rather than glutamate — as the unusual amino donor; (3) the ATP- and Mg2+-dependent dethiobiotin synthetase (BioD) closes the ureido ring by carboxylating DAPA to dethiobiotin; and (4) the radical-S-adenosylmethionine iron–sulfur enzyme biotin synthase (BioB) inserts sulfur across the C6–C9 positions of dethiobiotin to complete the thiophane ring, yielding biotin. This KAPA→DAPA→dethiobiotin→biotin core (the BioF/BioA/BioD/BioB steps) is strongly conserved across Gram-negative bacteria and mycobacteria and is absent from humans, who obtain biotin from diet and gut microbiota, which is why it is an attractive antibacterial target. The upstream stage that builds the pimeloyl thioester (BioC/BioH in E. coli; BioW/BioI or other routes in other taxa) is taxonomically variable and is treated here as context rather than as a modeled part.

biotin biosynthetic processGO:0009102

Conserved bacterial de novo biotin biosynthesis grounded to the four canonical E. coli K-12 enzymes as representative members: BioF (UniProtKB:P12998, GO:0008710, EC 2.3.1.47, Rhea:42288), BioA (P12995, GO:0004015, EC 2.6.1.62, Rhea:16861), BioD (P13000, GO:0004141, EC 6.3.3.3, Rhea:15805) and BioB (P12996, GO:0004076, EC 2.8.1.6, Rhea:22060). GO molecular-function terms, EC numbers, Rhea reactions and PANTHER family ids were read from the current UniProt records; each step uses a PANTHER family selector (generic over orthologs/paralogs) plus a concrete E. coli representative member. The module boundary is the conserved KAPA → DAPA → dethiobiotin → biotin core (the BioFADB operon steps). The upstream pimeloyl-thioester–generating stage is taxonomically variable — BioC (malonyl-ACP O-methyltransferase) + BioH (pimeloyl-ACP methyl ester esterase) in E. coli, versus BioW/BioI or other routes elsewhere — so it is described as context rather than modeled as a fixed part; it could be added later as a variant_set if a pimeloyl-supply module is curated. This pathway is the "conserved metabolic pathway" targeted by the Streptomyces biotin-targeting antibiotic megacluster (PMID:42343126): acidomycin/amiclenomycin inactivates the BioA step, α-Me-KAPA mimics the KAPA intermediate, and streptavidin sequesters the biotin product downstream. Distinct from the human biotin_cycle module (HLCS + biotinidase), which activates and recycles biotin onto carboxylases and does NOT synthesize the biotin ring de novo (humans lack this pathway).
