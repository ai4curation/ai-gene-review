Glycine cleavage system (glycine decarboxylase complex) — nonketotic hyperglycinemia

The glycine cleavage system (GCS, glycine decarboxylase complex) is the mitochondrial multienzyme system that carries out the major route of glycine catabolism, degrading glycine to CO2 and ammonia while transferring a one-carbon (methylene) unit to tetrahydrofolate — thereby coupling glycine breakdown to folate/one-carbon metabolism. It comprises four components acting on a lipoyl-swinging-arm relay: the P-protein (GLDC, glycine decarboxylase) is a pyridoxal-5'-phosphate enzyme that decarboxylates glycine and transfers the residual aminomethyl group to the lipoic-acid arm of the H-protein (GCSH), a small non-catalytic lipoylated carrier; the T-protein (AMT, aminomethyltransferase) then releases ammonia from the aminomethyl-dihydrolipoyl H-protein and transfers the methylene carbon to tetrahydrofolate, forming 5,10-methylenetetrahydrofolate; and the L-protein (DLD, dihydrolipoyl dehydrogenase — the same flavoenzyme shared with the pyruvate, 2-oxoglutarate and branched-chain 2-oxoacid dehydrogenase complexes) reoxidises the H-protein dihydrolipoyl group, reducing NAD+ to NADH and regenerating the oxidised lipoyl arm. Inherited deficiency causes nonketotic hyperglycinemia (glycine encephalopathy) — neonatal seizures, encephalopathy and a raised CSF:plasma glycine ratio: most cases are due to GLDC (~80%), fewer to AMT (~20%), and GCSH deficiency is rare.

The mitochondrial glycine cleavage system modelled as a PROTEIN_COMPLEX with its four components grounded to the human proteins GLDC (P-protein, UniProtKB:P23378, GO:0004375, EC 1.4.4.2), GCSH (H-protein, P23434, GO:0031405 lipoic acid binding), AMT (T-protein, P48728, GO:0004047, EC 2.1.2.10) and DLD (L-protein, P09622, GO:0004148, EC 1.8.1.4). GO molecular-function terms were taken from the human GOA records; Reactome reaction ids and titles were verified against the local reactome cache. Each subunit annoton uses a PANTHER family selector with the human protein as representative. The H-protein (GCSH) is a non-catalytic lipoylated carrier, so its annoton uses lipoic acid binding (GO:0031405) rather than a catalytic MF. The L-protein DLD is the shared dihydrolipoyl dehydrogenase (E3) common to the pyruvate (pyruvate_metabolism), 2-oxoglutarate (TCA) and branched-chain 2-oxoacid (branched_chain_amino_acid_catabolism) dehydrogenase complexes, reviewed previously and cross-linked here. Reaction cycle: GLDC decarboxylates glycine and loads the aminomethyl onto the GCSH lipoyl arm; AMT releases NH3 and transfers the methylene to tetrahydrofolate (-> 5,10-methylene-THF, feeding one-carbon/folate metabolism); DLD reoxidises the H-protein lipoate with NAD+ -> NADH. Disorders: nonketotic hyperglycinemia / glycine encephalopathy (GLDC ~80%, AMT ~20%, GCSH rare); DLD deficiency additionally affects the other oxoacid dehydrogenase complexes.
