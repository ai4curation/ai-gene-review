Bacterial nucleotide excision repair

Boundary module for KEGG ppu03420 nucleotide excision repair in Pseudomonas putida KT2440. The strict UvrABC core includes UvrA damage recognition, UvrB lesion verification/pre-incision complex formation, and UvrC dual incision. Mfd provides transcription-coupled repair input, while UvrD, PolA, and NAD-dependent ligases support post-incision oligonucleotide removal, repair synthesis, and nick sealing. PP_2839 and PP_3087 are retained as KEGG boundary members with conservative helicase/UvrA-family ATPase roles because their precise NER-specific physiological roles are not yet resolved.
