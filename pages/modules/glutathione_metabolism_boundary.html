Glutathione metabolism boundary

Broad boundary module for KEGG ppu00480 in Pseudomonas putida KT2440. The membership combines glutathione biosynthesis, glutathione redox recycling, glutathione-dependent detoxification, glutathione turnover and 5-oxoproline salvage, aminopeptidase side nodes, NADPH-generating enzymes, and unrelated KEGG spillover nodes. This module separates those supported contexts rather than treating every member as one strict glutathione metabolic route.

Treat ppu00480 as a boundary module. The direct glutathione route includes biosynthesis, reductive recycling, peroxidase/GST detoxification, and turnover/salvage enzymes. NADPH-generating central-carbon enzymes, aminopeptidases, SpeC, PtxD, YbgJ, stress-bucket GST-family candidates, thiol/formaldehyde redox candidates, and MnaT should remain side context unless gene-specific evidence connects them directly to glutathione handling in KT2440.
