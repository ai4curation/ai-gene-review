Cationic antimicrobial peptide resistance boundary

Boundary module for KEGG ppu01503 in Pseudomonas putida KT2440. The organism-specific CAMP-resistance map combines surface lipid/LPS modification, PhoPQ/Cpx-like envelope-stress regulation, RND efflux components, periplasmic folding/protease functions, and peptidoglycan remodeling enzymes. These genes do not form one linear pathway; the module records the resistance-map context while keeping each gene grounded in its direct molecular function.

This module is deliberately scoped as a resistance-boundary checkpoint. Only PP_1202 has a clear surface-charge modification mechanism directly tied to cationic peptide tolerance. The other members are retained as envelope regulation, efflux, folding, or remodeling context unless direct KT2440 CAMP-resistance evidence is added later.
