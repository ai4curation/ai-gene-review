Orphan MCP/aerotaxis receptor boundary

Boundary module for the ppu02030 orphan methyl-accepting chemotaxis protein and aerotaxis receptor panel in Pseudomonas putida KT2440. The module groups receptor/transducer candidates that have MCP, HAMP, PAS, dCache, or related sensory-domain evidence but do not yet have a resolved first-pass ligand, receptor-array partner, or chemotaxis sub-branch. It complements the characterized/named receptor panel and the core Che signaling and adaptation modules, and now also records unresolved MCP candidates promoted from the broader motility/chemotaxis/biofilm functional bucket.

First-pass interpretation: this module is a receptor-boundary bucket, not a satisfiable ligand-specific chemotaxis pathway. The conserved domains support chemotaxis receptor/transducer roles, but ligand specificity remains unknown for the generic orphan MCPs. The three aerotaxis receptor candidates are kept together because GOA and UniProt naming support aerotaxis, but the direct oxygen/redox/energy-state sensing mechanism remains unresolved. PP_2111 has a clear electronic-annotation artifact from a syntaxin/SNARE-like InterPro hit; the gene review removes SNAP receptor activity, intracellular protein transport, and membrane fusion from the chemotaxis interpretation. PP_2599 was considered during the motility-bucket MCP pass, but its GAF/HAMP/HD-GYP/HD-PDEase architecture supports routing to the c-di-GMP turnover boundary instead.
