L-phenylalanine metabolism and phenylacetate degradation

Broad bacterial boundary module for KEGG ppu00360 in Pseudomonas putida KT2440. The map combines at least two biologically distinct routes: PhhA hydroxylation of L-phenylalanine to L-tyrosine followed by homogentisate catabolism, and the CoA-dependent phenylacetate degradation pathway centered on phenylacetyl-CoA activation, epoxidation, ring opening, and beta-oxidation- like lower steps. The KEGG bucket also contains aminotransferases, DOPA/aromatic amino-acid decarboxylase, D-amino-acid dehydrogenases, catalase-peroxidase, and generic fatty-acid-like enzymes that should be curated as boundary context unless gene-level evidence supports a committed phenylalanine-catabolic role.

Part 3: Phenylacetate lower pathway and beta-oxidation-like steps
Phenylacetate lower pathwayMetabolic Pathwaypaa_lower_pathway

PaaG, PaaZ, PaaH, PaaF, PaaJ, and PaaI process epoxidized/ring-opened phenylacetyl-CoA intermediates through hydrolase, dehydrogenase, hydratase/isomerase, thiolase, and thioesterase chemistry. Several of these terms overlap fatty-acid beta-oxidation and should be kept in phenylacetate context when the paa gene neighborhood supports it.

Part 4: KEGG ppu00360 boundary spillovers
ppu00360 boundary and side-node contextMetabolic Pathwayppu00360_boundary_spillovers

The ppu00360 batch includes genes that are not strict Paa or PhhA/homogentisate steps: aminotransferases, PP_2552 DOPA/aromatic-amino-acid decarboxylase, PeaE phenylacetaldehyde dehydrogenase, D-amino-acid dehydrogenases, catalase-peroxidase, generic enoyl-CoA hydratases, and auxin/tryptophan side chemistry. These should be retained as boundary context unless gene-level review establishes a direct phenylalanine-catabolic role.
