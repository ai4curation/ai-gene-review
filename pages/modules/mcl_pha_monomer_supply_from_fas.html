mcl-PHA monomer supply from de novo fatty-acid synthesis

A Pseudomonas putida KT2440 UniPathway UPA00212 module for the PhaG bridge from de novo fatty-acid synthesis to medium-chain-length polyhydroxyalkanoate (mcl-PHA) biosynthesis. PhaG routes ACP-bound (R)-3-hydroxyacyl fatty-acid-synthesis intermediates toward mcl-PHA monomer supply, but direct ACP:CoA transacylation versus thioesterase release followed by CoA ligation remains unresolved for the native route. This module records that FAS-to-PHA connection rather than the PhaC polymerization step or the alternative beta-oxidation/PhaJ monomer-supply routes.

OpenScientist PSEPK UPA00212 pathway satisfiability review
The PSEPK-specific OpenScientist report treats phaG as covered for the UPA00212 membership set, but flags PP_0763/Q88PT5 as a likely missing 3-hydroxyacyl-CoA ligase step if the curated module is intended to reach the CoA-thioester substrate used by PHA synthases.

First-pass interpretation: UPA00212 is satisfiable as the PhaG fatty-acid synthesis to mcl-PHA monomer-supply bridge, but the module is only partly satisfiable if interpreted as complete CoA-thioester monomer production. It should not be merged with the full PHA synthase/polymerization step, because the KT2440 PhaC1/PhaC2 polymerases are handled in the broader butanoate/PHA boundary module. It also should not imply PhaG is the sole route to PHA monomers, because KT2440 has alternative beta-oxidation/PhaJ-linked inputs. OpenScientist's PSEPK-specific review highlights PP_0763/Q88PT5 as a candidate missing 3-hydroxyacyl-CoA ligase that should be promoted from the orphan bucket to a full gene review before claiming the complete CoA-activation route.

PhaG diverts ACP-bound de novo fatty-acid-synthesis intermediates toward mcl-PHA monomer supply. The curated reaction is kept direction-neutral because the physiological mechanism has been described as direct ACP:CoA transacylation and, in later reports, as thioesterase-like release of a free 3-hydroxy fatty acid followed by acyl-CoA ligation.
