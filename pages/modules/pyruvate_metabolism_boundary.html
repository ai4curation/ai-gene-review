Pyruvate metabolism boundary

Boundary module for KEGG ppu00620 in Pseudomonas putida KT2440. The organism-specific KEGG map mixes pyruvate-forming and pyruvate-consuming central-carbon reactions, pyruvate dehydrogenase/lipoate complexes, acetate/acetyl-CoA routes, lactate and methylglyoxal/glyoxalase side routes, malate/oxaloacetate/TCA overlap, amino-acid precursor spillovers, and alcohol/aldehyde dehydrogenase side nodes. This module preserves that broad curation boundary without treating the complete KEGG membership as one satisfiable linear pathway.

Part 4: KEGG overlap and low-specificity side nodes
Pyruvate-map overlap and side nodesMetabolic Pathwaypyruvate_map_overlap_side_nodes

The remaining ppu00620 members include glyoxylate/TCA overlap enzymes, malate dehydrogenases, fumarases, acetyl-CoA/fatty-acid thiolases, alcohol/aldehyde dehydrogenases, and several unresolved enzyme-family candidates. Their gene-level reviews should preserve more specific pathway assignments where available instead of forcing all annotations into pyruvate metabolism.
