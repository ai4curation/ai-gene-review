Bacterial cellulose biosynthesis

A reusable Gram-negative bacterial module for c-di-GMP-regulated cellulose synthase and export. The core module couples BcsA-family UDP-forming cellulose polymerization, BcsB-family periplasmic synthase-complex support, and BcsC-like outer-membrane export, with BcsQ/YhjQ-family positioning ATPases as accessory cellulose-machinery factors. Pseudomonas putida KT2440 bcsA/PP_2635, bcsB/PP_2636, PP_2638, and PP_2634 provide local exemplars.

First-pass interpretation: the reusable module is the bacterial cellulose-synthase/export machine rather than a PSEPK-only UniPathway bucket. BcsA-family proteins provide the UDP-glucose-dependent beta-1,4-glucan polymerase, BcsB-family proteins organize the periplasmic synthase complex, and BcsC-like proteins export cellulose across the Gram-negative outer membrane. BcsQ/YhjQ-family P-loop NTPases are accessory positioning/assembly factors and must not be counted as additional cellulose synthase catalytic subunits. In KT2440, PP_2635/bcsA was missing from the original UPA00694 candidate list but is the required catalytic exemplar; PP_2634 is kept as BcsQ accessory context and name-correction evidence.
