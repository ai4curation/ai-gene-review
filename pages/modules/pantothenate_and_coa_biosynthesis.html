Pantothenate and CoA biosynthesis

A bacterial pantothenate and coenzyme A biosynthesis module covering formation of pantoate from 3-methyl-2-oxobutanoate, ligation with beta-alanine to form pantothenate, and conversion of pantothenate through phosphopantothenate, phosphopantothenoylcysteine, phosphopantetheine, and dephospho-CoA to CoA. In Pseudomonas putida KT2440, KEGG ppu00770 also includes branched-chain amino-acid precursor enzymes, pyrimidine/beta-alanine metabolism, ACP phosphopantetheine handling, and nucleotide hydrolase side nodes; these are tracked as precursor or boundary context unless they directly form pantothenate or CoA.

Falcon PSEPK ppu00770 review for pantothenate and CoA biosynthesis
The PSEPK-specific Falcon review identifies coaX as the strongest species-linked core step, treats PP_2325 and PP_2998 as uncertain ketopantoate reductase candidates, and recommends representing pyrimidine reductive catabolism as beta-alanine supply context because KT2440 lacks a canonical PanD route.

For PSEPK ppu00770, the likely strict pathway is PanB plus one or more ketopantoate reductase candidates, PanC, CoaX, Dfp/CoaBC, CoaD, and CoaE. The ketopantoate reductase step remains candidate-uncertain in KT2440: panE is the expected primary assignment, while PP_2325, PP_2998, PP_4452, and possible IlvC compensation require targeted evidence before they should be treated as interchangeable core enzymes. PP_4452 is a UniPathway-only candidate and is curated with this module even though it is absent from KEGG ppu00770. KT2440 lacks the canonical PanD aspartate decarboxylase route in this pathway set, so pydA/pydX, pydB, hyuC, and related beta-alanine metabolism genes are tracked as species-specific precursor context. PP_0922, PP_1183, and mazG are ACP/cofactor or nucleotide boundary nodes and should not count toward strict pantothenate-to-CoA pathway satisfiability.
