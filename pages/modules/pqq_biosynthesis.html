Pyrroloquinoline quinone biosynthesis

A Pseudomonas putida KT2440 pyrroloquinoline quinone (PQQ) biosynthesis module grounded in UniPathway UPA00539. The strict pathway includes the ribosomally synthesized PqqA precursor peptide, PqqD-family peptide chaperones that present PqqA to PqqE, the radical-SAM PqqE cross-linking step, PqqF proteolytic processing, PqqB oxygenase/hydroxylase maturation chemistry, and the terminal PqqC oxidative ring-closure step that forms mature PQQ. Mature PQQ use by periplasmic quinoproteins is downstream utilization context and is not part of this biosynthetic module.

First-pass interpretation: UPA00539 is a compact, satisfiable cofactor biosynthesis module in KT2440. The module deliberately excludes PQQ-dependent alcohol/glucose dehydrogenases and other quinoprotein utilization nodes. It also excludes a speculative pqqG part because the current UniProt/pathway metadata do not provide an unambiguous KT2440 pqqG gene product in the UPA00539 member set.
