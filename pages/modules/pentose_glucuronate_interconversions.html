Pentose and glucuronate interconversions

A boundary module for the sugar-acid and pentose interconversion reactions grouped by bacterial KEGG pentose/glucuronate maps. The map connects three separable arms: phosphorylated pentose metabolism, UDP-glucose and UDP-glucuronate nucleotide-sugar generation, and oxidative hexuronate or aldarate catabolism. Organism-level curation should distinguish strict pentose-phosphate reactions, nucleotide-sugar biosynthesis, experimentally supported hexuronate-catabolic entry steps, and uncertain downstream dehydratase/dehydrogenase paralogs rather than treating every mapped enzyme as evidence for one complete linear pathway.

cytosolGO:0005829

This module is a scoped boundary for KEGG ppu00040 rather than a claim that all parts form one satisfiable pathway in every organism. In Pseudomonas putida KT2440, rpe overlaps the PPP module; galU and udg belong to nucleotide-sugar/cell-envelope precursor metabolism; udh and gnl are the promoted hexuronate entry/lactonase genes absent from the initial KEGG eight-gene batch; and the aldarate dehydratase plus KGSADH paralog assignments remain unresolved pathway holes for later focused curation.
