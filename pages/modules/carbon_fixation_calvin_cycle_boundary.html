Carbon fixation by Calvin cycle boundary

Boundary/absence module for KEGG ppu00710 in Pseudomonas putida KT2440. The organism-specific "Carbon fixation by Calvin cycle" map does not establish a complete Calvin-Benson-Bassham cycle in KT2440: the candidate set lacks the route-defining Rubisco and phosphoribulokinase steps. Its genes instead represent reusable non-oxidative pentose-phosphate reactions, lower EMP/gluconeogenic reactions, phosphoenolpyruvate carboxylase anaplerotic carbon fixation, malic enzyme, and malate dehydrogenase side context.

Part 1: Calvin-cycle absence guardrail
Calvin-cycle absence guardrailMetabolic Pathwaycalvin_cycle_absence_guardrail

The ppu00710 candidate set should not be read as a complete Calvin-Benson-Bassham cycle in KT2440. The batch lacks the defining carboxylation and regeneration commitments represented by Rubisco and phosphoribulokinase. The genes present are reusable central-carbon enzymes that overlap with CBB maps in autotrophs.

Part 2: Non-oxidative PPP overlap
Non-oxidative pentose-phosphate overlapMetabolic Pathwaynon_oxidative_ppp_overlap

Lower EMP and gluconeogenic overlapMetabolic Pathwaylower_emp_gluconeogenic_overlap

GapA/GapB, PGK, TpiA, Fba, and Fbp are lower-trunk or gluconeogenic enzymes. They can appear in Calvin-cycle maps because CBB carbon rearrangement shares triose-phosphate and fructose/sedoheptulose phosphate chemistry, but KT2440 reviews keep them as glycolysis/gluconeogenesis or ED/central-carbon context.
