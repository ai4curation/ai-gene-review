beta-Lactam resistance boundary

Boundary module for KEGG ppu01501 in Pseudomonas putida KT2440. The KEGG beta-lactam-resistance map combines direct antibiotic hydrolysis by AmpC, peptidoglycan recycling and AmpG/NagZ signaling context, penicillin-binding peptidoglycan transpeptidase targets, RND/TolC-like efflux systems, and outer-membrane permeability nodes such as OprD. These genes do not form one linear metabolic pathway; the module separates resistance mechanisms and envelope side context.

This module is deliberately scoped as a resistance-boundary checkpoint. AmpC is the direct beta-lactamase; AmpG and NagZ provide peptidoglycan recycling context; PBPs are antibiotic targets with primary cell-wall biosynthesis roles; and the multiple RND/MFP/OMF/porin proteins represent efflux or permeability context. Falcon module-level research remains queued for a later pass.
