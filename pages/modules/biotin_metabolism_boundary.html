Biotin metabolism boundary

Boundary module for KEGG ppu00780 in Pseudomonas putida KT2440. The strict biotin-biosynthesis route is represented by the BioC-BioH pimelate precursor branch, BioF/BioA/BioD/BioB conversion to biotin, and BirA-dependent biotinylation/regulation. KEGG ppu00780 also pulls in fatty-acid-synthesis and short-chain dehydrogenase/reductase nodes because the BioC-BioH route uses acyl-carrier-protein chemistry to generate pimeloyl-ACP; those genes are tracked as boundary context rather than committed biotin-biosynthetic steps.

Part 1: BioC-BioH pimelate precursor branch
BioC-BioH pimelate precursor branchMetabolic Pathwaybioc_bioh_pimelate_precursor_branch

BioC methylates malonyl-ACP to route the acyl carrier protein thioester through fatty-acid-like elongation chemistry, and BioH hydrolyzes the methyl ester to expose pimeloyl-ACP for BioF. This explains why KEGG ppu00780 includes FAS-II enzymes without making those enzymes committed biotin-biosynthesis genes.

Part 2: AON to biotin biosynthesis branch
AON to biotinMetabolic Pathwayaon_to_biotin_branch
