Methane metabolism boundary

Boundary/absence module for KEGG ppu00680 in Pseudomonas putida KT2440. The organism-specific "methane metabolism" map is not evidence that KT2440 carries methane oxidation or methanogenesis. Its candidate genes instead partition into formaldehyde detoxification, formate oxidation and respiratory electron-transfer context, tetrahydrofolate/serine one-carbon chemistry, gluconeogenic and acetate/acetyl-CoA side nodes, and one predicted quinohaemoprotein amine dehydrogenase alpha subunit.

Part 1: Methane route absence and boundary guardrail
Methane route absence guardrailMetabolic Pathwaymethane_route_absence_guardrail

The ppu00680 membership does not establish a complete methane oxidation or methanogenesis pathway in KT2440. No route-defining methane monooxygenase or methanogenesis-specific spine is represented in the curated candidate set. Downstream C1 detoxification and formate oxidation genes should therefore not be used to infer methane use.
