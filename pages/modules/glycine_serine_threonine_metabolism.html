Glycine, serine, and threonine metabolism

A bacterial boundary module for glycine, L-serine, and L-threonine metabolism. It covers de novo L-serine biosynthesis from 3-phosphoglycerate, the aspartate-derived L-threonine biosynthetic branch through homoserine, reversible serine/glycine one-carbon chemistry, glycine cleavage, and methylated-glycine catabolism from glycine betaine through dimethylglycine and sarcosine to glycine. In Pseudomonas putida KT2440, KEGG ppu00260 also pulls in tryptophan synthase, cysteine/methionine, glyoxylate, pyoverdine, phospholipid, betaine-osmoprotection, and cell-envelope side nodes. Those are tracked as boundary context unless gene-level evidence supports a committed glycine/serine/threonine role.
