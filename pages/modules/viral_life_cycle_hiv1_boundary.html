Viral life cycle - HIV-1 boundary

Boundary/absence module for KEGG ppu03250 in Pseudomonas putida KT2440. The organism-specific KEGG map contains only ppiA/PP_4541, a cyclophilin-family peptidyl-prolyl cis-trans isomerase involved in protein folding. This should not be interpreted as evidence for a viral, HIV-1, or host-virus life-cycle pathway in a bacterium; the mapped protein is curated as PPIase/protein-folding context only.

Part 1: HIV-1 pathway absence
HIV-1 life-cycle absence in KT2440Biological Processhiv1_life_cycle_absence

P. putida KT2440 does not encode an HIV-1 life-cycle pathway. The KEGG bucket is treated as an artifact of broad cross-map association with a cyclophilin/PPIase rather than as a satisfiable bacterial pathway.

Part 2: Cyclophilin/PPIase protein-folding side node
ppiA peptidyl-prolyl isomerase side nodeMolecular FunctionppiA_ppiase_protein_folding

ppiA encodes a cyclophilin-family PPIase that accelerates protein folding by cis-trans isomerization of peptidyl-prolyl bonds. This is the only native biological role supported by the ppu03250 member.
