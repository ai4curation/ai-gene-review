Alanine, aspartate, and glutamate metabolism

A bacterial boundary module for alanine, aspartate, glutamate, glutamine, and asparagine interconversion. It covers central amino-nitrogen assimilation through glutamate dehydrogenase, glutamine synthetase, and glutamate synthase; alanine aminotransferase chemistry; aspartate formation and catabolism; asparagine synthesis and hydrolysis; and connected proline, polyamine, and succinate-semialdehyde side routes. In Pseudomonas putida KT2440, KEGG ppu00250 also pulls in nucleotide, arginine, pyrimidine, NAD, amino-sugar, and lysine-catabolism enzymes that use aspartate, glutamine, or glutamate as donors or products. Those are tracked as boundary context unless gene-level evidence supports a committed alanine/aspartate/glutamate role.
