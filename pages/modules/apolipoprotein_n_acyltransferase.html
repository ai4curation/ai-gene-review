Apolipoprotein N-acyltransferase

A Pseudomonas putida KT2440 UniPathway UPA00666 module for the final phospholipid-dependent N-acylation step of bacterial lipoprotein maturation. The module is centered on Lnt (PP_4790, UniProtKB:Q88DN4), which transfers an acyl chain from a glycerophospholipid to the amino group of the N-terminal cysteine of a diacylglyceryl lipoprotein.

OpenScientist species-aware research supports lnt/PP_4790 as the single high-confidence PSEPK gene covering UPA00666, finds no Lnt paralog ambiguity, and recommends keeping Lgt/LspA and Lol sorting outside this one-step module.

First-pass interpretation: UPA00666 is satisfiable as a compact single-enzyme lipoprotein maturation module in KT2440. Lnt catalyzes the last modification in the canonical bacterial lipoprotein maturation sequence, after Lgt adds the diacylglyceryl group and LspA removes the signal peptide. Those upstream steps are pathway context rather than members of this UPA00666 bucket. The exact Lnt reaction is captured by UniProt/Rhea/EC evidence; GO currently provides the broader N-acyltransferase activity term rather than an apolipoprotein N-acyltransferase-specific molecular-function term. OpenScientist reinforces the narrow boundary and emphasizes that KT2440-specific mechanism, essentiality, and donor-preference claims are inferred from close proteobacterial relatives rather than direct PP_4790 experiments. The species-aware report also supports this module as complete for KT2440, with no additional PSEPK gene promotions required for satisfiability.
