NAD+ biosynthesis hub (salvage + Preiss-Handler + de novo convergence); NAMPT/NAPRT + NMNAT1/2/3 + NADSYN1

NAD+ is continuously consumed by sirtuins, PARPs and CD38/NADases, so cells must constantly regenerate it through three converging routes that all funnel through the nicotinamide/nicotinate mononucleotide adenylyltransferases (NMNATs). In the dominant salvage route, NAMPT (nicotinamide phosphoribosyltransferase, the rate-limiting salvage enzyme) recycles the nicotinamide released by NAD-consuming enzymes with PRPP into nicotinamide mononucleotide (NMN). In the Preiss-Handler route, NAPRT (nicotinate phosphoribosyltransferase) condenses dietary nicotinic acid with PRPP to nicotinate mononucleotide (NaMN) — the same intermediate produced de novo from tryptophan by QPRT. The three NMNAT isoforms then perform the central adenylyl-transfer step in distinct compartments — nuclear NMNAT1, Golgi/cytosolic NMNAT2 (a labile axon-survival factor that keeps SARM1 inactive) and mitochondrial NMNAT3 — converting NMN to NAD+ directly and NaMN to nicotinate adenine dinucleotide (NaAD, deamido-NAD+). Finally, the glutamine-dependent NAD synthetase NADSYN1 amidates NaAD to NAD+, completing both the de novo and Preiss-Handler routes. Defects illustrate the pathway's importance: NMNAT1 causes Leber congenital amaurosis, NMNAT2 loss drives axon degeneration, and NADSYN1 (with the de novo enzymes HAAO/KYNU) causes a congenital NAD-deficiency malformation syndrome; NAMPT and NAPRT are actively pursued cancer-metabolism targets.

The NAD+ biosynthesis hub (GO:0009435; salvage GO:0034355) where the salvage, Preiss-Handler and de novo (kynurenine) routes converge, grounded to the completed human gene reviews: NAMPT (P43490, GO:0047280, rate-limiting salvage; eNAMPT/visfatin cytokine role non-core), NAPRT (Q6XQN6, GO:0004516, Preiss-Handler), the three NMNAT isoforms NMNAT1 (Q9HAN9, nuclear), NMNAT2 (Q9BZQ4, Golgi/cytosolic, axon-survival), NMNAT3 (Q96T66, mitochondrial) — all GO:0000309 + GO:0004515 (they share PANTHER PTHR12039) — and NADSYN1 (Q6IA69, GO:0003952 + glutaminase GO:0004359, completion). NaMN from the de novo kynurenine pathway (QPRT, in kynurenine_nad_de_novo) enters at the NMNAT hub, tying the two modules together. GO term ids/labels verified against the local go.db. NAD+ is consumed by sirtuins/PARPs/CD38 and regenerated here. Disorders: NMNAT1 — Leber congenital amaurosis 9; NMNAT2 — polyneuropathy / axon degeneration; NADSYN1 — congenital NAD deficiency (VCRL3, with HAAO/KYNU); NAMPT/NAPRT — cancer-metabolism drug targets.
