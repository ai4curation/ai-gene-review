L-leucine catabolism (isovaleryl-CoA to acetoacetate + acetyl-CoA)

The distal, leucine-specific arm of branched-chain amino acid catabolism, operating in the mitochondrial matrix, which degrades the isovaleryl-CoA produced from leucine (via transamination by BCAT2 and oxidative decarboxylation by the BCKDH complex) to the ketogenic end-products acetoacetate and acetyl-CoA. Four steps: isovaleryl-CoA dehydrogenase (IVD), an FAD-dependent acyl-CoA dehydrogenase, dehydrogenates isovaleryl-CoA to 3-methylcrotonyl-CoA (electrons to ETF); the biotin-dependent 3-methylcrotonyl-CoA carboxylase (MCC), an alpha6-beta6 dodecamer of MCCC1 (biotin carboxylase alpha) and MCCC2 (carboxyltransferase beta), carboxylates 3-methylcrotonyl-CoA to 3-methylglutaconyl-CoA using bicarbonate and ATP; 3-methylglutaconyl-CoA hydratase (AUH) hydrates this to (S)-3-hydroxy-3-methylglutaryl-CoA (HMG-CoA); and HMG-CoA lyase (HMGCL) cleaves HMG-CoA to acetoacetate + acetyl-CoA. The final HMGCL step is shared with ketogenesis (the pathway that makes ketone bodies from acetyl-CoA), so leucine catabolism feeds directly into the ketone-body pool. Inherited defects define a series of organic acidemias: IVD → isovaleric acidemia; MCCC1/MCCC2 → 3-methylcrotonyl-CoA carboxylase deficiency (a common, often benign newborn-screening finding); AUH → 3-methylglutaconic aciduria type I; and HMGCL → HMG-CoA lyase deficiency (hypoketotic hypoglycemia, because both leucine degradation and ketogenesis are blocked).

Four-step mitochondrial leucine catabolism (isovaleryl-CoA to acetoacetate + acetyl-CoA) grounded to the human enzymes IVD (UniProtKB:P26440, GO:0008470, EC 1.3.8.4), the MCC complex MCCC1 (Q96RQ3) + MCCC2 (Q9HCC0) (GO:0004485, EC 6.4.1.4, complex GO:1905202), AUH (Q13825, GO:0004490, EC 4.2.1.18) and HMGCL (P35914, GO:0004419, EC 4.1.3.4). GO molecular-function terms were taken from the human GOA records; Reactome reaction ids and titles were verified against the local reactome cache. Each step uses a PANTHER family selector (generic over paralogs and orthologs) plus a concrete human representative member; MCC is modelled as a PROTEIN_COMPLEX node with its alpha (MCCC1) and beta (MCCC2) subunits. Upstream, isovaleryl-CoA is produced from leucine by BCAT2 + the BCKDH complex (see the BCAA catabolism module); the terminal HMGCL step is shared with ketogenesis, linking leucine catabolism to the ketone-body pool. AUH's moonlighting ARE-RNA-binding role is captured in its gene review, not in this metabolic module.
