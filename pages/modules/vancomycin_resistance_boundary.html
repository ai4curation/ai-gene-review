Vancomycin resistance boundary

Boundary/absence module for KEGG ppu01502 in Pseudomonas putida KT2440. The organism-specific KEGG vancomycin-resistance map contains six core peptidoglycan precursor enzymes: DadX, DdlA, DdlB, MurF, MraY, and MurG. These genes define D-alanine supply, D-Ala-D-Ala ligation, UDP-MurNAc- pentapeptide completion, and lipid I/lipid II assembly. They do not constitute a VanHAX D-Ala-D-Lac vancomycin-resistance pathway in KT2440.

This module is intentionally scoped as a boundary/absence module. The PSEPK ppu01502 membership records enzymes whose products or downstream peptidoglycan precursors are relevant to vancomycin target biology, but the first-pass curation does not support annotating KT2440 as carrying a VanHAX-type vancomycin-resistance pathway.

Part 1: VanHAX pathway absence guardrail
VanHAX pathway absence guardrailMetabolic Pathwayvanhax_absence_guardrail

The ppu01502 batch is not interpreted as evidence that KT2440 carries a complete vancomycin-resistance pathway. It lacks the defining D-Ala-D-Lac remodeling route represented by VanHAX-type enzymes; the mapped genes are shared cell-wall precursor targets and supply nodes.

Part 2: D-alanine supply and D-Ala-D-Ala ligation
D-alanine supply and D-Ala-D-Ala ligationMetabolic Pathwayd_alanine_supply_and_dipeptide_ligation

DadX supplies D-alanine by alanine racemization, while DdlA and DdlB form the D-Ala-D-Ala dipeptide used by MurF. This chemistry is a normal peptidoglycan precursor route and a vancomycin target context, not a resistance-conferring D-Ala-D-Lac bypass.

Part 3: UDP-MurNAc-pentapeptide and lipid-linked precursor assembly
UDP-MurNAc-pentapeptide and lipid II assemblyMetabolic Pathwayudp_murnac_pentapeptide_and_lipid_ii_assembly

MurF completes UDP-MurNAc-pentapeptide, MraY transfers that precursor to undecaprenyl phosphate to form lipid I, and MurG adds GlcNAc to form lipid II. These are conserved peptidoglycan precursor steps shared with the peptidoglycan biosynthesis module.
