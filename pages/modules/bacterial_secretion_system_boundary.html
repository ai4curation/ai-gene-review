Bacterial secretion system boundary

Boundary module for KEGG ppu03070 bacterial secretion system in Pseudomonas putida KT2440. The primary PSEPK ppu03070 partition contains type II secretion/Xcp-Gsp components, a two-partner/type Vb secretion pair, several type VI secretion system loci and effectors, and a small type-I secretion accessory tail. Sec, Tat, SRP, and YidC inner-membrane export components are handled in the neighboring bacterial_protein_export module and are not part of this primary ppu03070 batch.

First-pass interpretation: ppu03070 is a secretion-system boundary module, not a single linear pathway. The Xcp/Gsp genes support type-II secretion; PP_1449 and PP_1450 form a likely two-partner/type Vb secretion pair; the VgrG, Hcp, DotU, TssJ/SciN, TssM/IcmF, and ClpV-like entries mark several T6SS-associated loci; and PP_4926/CyaB are type-I secretion context. Many T6SS members have no GOA rows, so their gene reviews remain conservative and the module carries the pathway-level grouping. The ppu02025 biofilm-formation umbrella contributes additional TssA/TssB/TssC/TssG/TssK and TagF-like T6SS apparatus/accessory proteins that are secretion-boundary context rather than direct evidence for a dedicated biofilm-signaling pathway. PP_0655 is added from the motility/chemotaxis/biofilm functional bucket as another Hcp-family candidate whose product name is misleadingly fimbrial.
