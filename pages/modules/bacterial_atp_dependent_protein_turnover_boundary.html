Bacterial ATP-dependent protein turnover boundary

Boundary module for the Pseudomonas putida KT2440 ATP-dependent protease and protein-turnover split from the protein folding/targeting/turnover functional bucket. The module separates catalytic Lon, ClpP, and HslV protease subunits from AAA+ ATPase/unfoldase subunits, substrate adaptors, and low-confidence domain fragments.

First-pass interpretation: ATP-dependent degradation in this split is handled as a set of related protease systems rather than one linear pathway. Catalytic terms are restricted to protease subunits or full-length Lon-family enzymes. ATPase-only subunits and adaptors are represented by unfoldase, ATPase, endopeptidase-regulator, or adaptor functions. PP_4814 is left no-core because it appears to contain a Lon N-terminal/substrate-binding domain without clear ATPase or protease catalytic domains.
