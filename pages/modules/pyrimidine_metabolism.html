Pyrimidine metabolism

A bacterial pyrimidine-metabolism boundary module covering de novo UMP formation from carbamoyl phosphate and aspartate, conversion of UMP to UDP, UTP, and CTP, deoxyribonucleotide and dTMP branch chemistry, pyrimidine-base and nucleoside salvage, reductive pyrimidine catabolism, and general nucleotide-pool maintenance reactions that act on pyrimidine substrates. In Pseudomonas putida KT2440, KEGG ppu00240 is broader than a single linear pathway: it also includes carbamoyl-phosphate supply shared with arginine metabolism, ribonucleotide reduction, beta-alanine-generating catabolism, nucleotidase and nucleoside phosphorylase side nodes, and generic nucleotide sanitizing enzymes. Those are tracked as boundary context unless gene-level evidence supports a committed pyrimidine role.

The KEGG PSEPK bucket provides the first-pass candidate set, but combines strict de novo pyrimidine biosynthesis, salvage, catabolism, and broad nucleotide side chemistry.

First-pass PSEPK curation should separate the strict de novo UMP route (CarAB/PyrB/PyrC/PyrD/PyrE/PyrF), UTP/CTP branch (PyrH/Ndk/PyrG/Cmk), deoxypyrimidine and thymidylate branch (NrdAB/Dcd/Dut/ThyA), salvage (Upp/PyrR/PpnP), reductive catabolism (PydX/PydA/PydB/beta-ureidopropionase candidates), and generic nucleotide housekeeping or uncertain side nodes.
