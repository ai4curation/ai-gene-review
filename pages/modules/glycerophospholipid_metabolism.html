Glycerophospholipid metabolism

Broad bacterial glycerophospholipid metabolism module for KEGG ppu00564 in Pseudomonas putida KT2440. The organism-specific map combines phosphatidic-acid and CDP-diacylglycerol precursor chemistry, phosphatidylglycerol/cardiolipin, phosphatidylserine/phosphatidylethanolamine, phosphatidylcholine, glycerophosphodiester turnover, glycerol-3-phosphate supply, and ethanolamine side metabolism. The module treats the lipid precursor and membrane-phospholipid enzymes as the pathway core while keeping glycerol-3-phosphate and ethanolamine nodes as boundary context unless gene-level evidence supports a direct membrane-lipid role.

Part 2: Phosphatidylglycerol and cardiolipin branch
Phosphatidylglycerol and cardiolipin branchMetabolic Pathwayphosphatidylglycerol_and_cardiolipin_branch

PgsA transfers phosphatidyl groups from CDP-diacylglycerol to glycerol-3-phosphate, PgpA dephosphorylates phosphatidylglycerol phosphate, and Cls-family enzymes synthesize cardiolipin. PLD-family candidates are kept as cardiolipin/PG boundary nodes until their exact substrate specificity is reviewed.
