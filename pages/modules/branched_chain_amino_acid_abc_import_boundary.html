Branched-chain amino acid ABC import boundary

Boundary module for five Pseudomonas putida KT2440 high-affinity branched-chain amino-acid ABC-transporter loci pulled from the KEGG ppu02010 ABC-transporter umbrella map. PP_1137-PP_1141 encodes the LivFGMHK system, PP_4863-PP_4867 encodes a related Bra/Liv system, PP_0615-PP_0619 encodes a third related system, PP_2767-PP_2770 encodes a fourth predicted BCAA importer, and PP_2748-PP_2753 encodes a fifth predicted BCAA importer with two ATP-binding subunits, two permeases, and two leucine-family substrate-binding components. The module records coherent predicted BCAA uptake systems while leaving relative specificity and redundancy open.

PP_1137-PP_1141 LivFGMHK branched-chain amino-acid ABC importTransport Steplivfgmhk_branched_chain_amino_acid_abc_import

The PP_1137-PP_1141 locus encodes a predicted LivFGMHK high-affinity branched-chain amino-acid ABC importer. LivF and LivG are ATP-binding energy-coupling components, LivM and LivH are membrane permeases, and LivK is the periplasmic leucine/branched-chain amino-acid binding component. First-pass curation supports import of branched-chain amino acids, with specific evidence for valine, isoleucine, and leucine annotations but without resolving relative substrate preference.
