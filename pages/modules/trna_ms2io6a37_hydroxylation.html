tRNA ms2io6A37 hydroxylation

A Pseudomonas putida KT2440 UniPathway UPA00729 module for the terminal oxygen-dependent hydroxylation step that converts ms2i6A37-modified tRNA to ms2io6A37-modified tRNA. The module is centered on miaE (PP_2188, UniProtKB:Q88KV1). The upstream MiaA and MiaB reactions that generate the canonical ms2i6A37 substrate are pathway context, not members of this single-gene UniPathway bucket; PSEPK-specific evidence suggests the native product may instead be io6A37 because KT2440 appears to lack canonical MiaB.

OpenScientist PSEPK UPA00729 pathway satisfiability review
The PSEPK-specific OpenScientist review finds the MiaE enzyme step covered by PP_2188/Q88KV1, but flags native product identity as uncertain because KT2440 appears to lack the upstream MiaB methylthiotransferase that makes canonical ms2i6A37.

First-pass interpretation: UPA00729 is satisfiable as a compact single-gene tRNA-modification module in KT2440. MiaE is the terminal hydroxylase for ms2io6A37 formation. Upstream MiaA and MiaB are required to make the modified tRNA substrate, but they belong to the broader tRNA A37 hypermodification pathway rather than this UPA00729 single-gene bucket. The generic OpenScientist report reinforces this boundary and highlights that MiaE is an oxygen-dependent non-heme diiron monooxygenase whose native physiological electron donor(s) and full KT2440 tRNA substrate set remain open questions. The PSEPK-specific report adds an important caveat: the MiaE enzyme step is covered, but KT2440 appears to lack canonical MiaB, so the native product may be io6A37 rather than ms2io6A37 unless a cryptic methylthiotransferase or direct native tRNA nucleoside data support ms2i6A37 substrate formation.
