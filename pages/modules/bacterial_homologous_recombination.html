Bacterial homologous recombination

Boundary module for KEGG ppu03440 homologous recombination in Pseudomonas putida KT2440. The strict homologous-recombination core includes RecA-mediated presynaptic filament formation, RecFOR mediator activity, RecBCD double-strand-end processing, and RuvAB/RuvC or RecG processing of branched DNA intermediates. The organism-specific KEGG map also includes SSB, RecJ, PriA, DNA polymerase III replisome/clamp-loader subunits, DnaQ-like exonucleases, and PolA as repair, replication-restart, or repair-synthesis side nodes. These are satisfiable boundary members but should not all be interpreted as specific RecA strand-exchange factors.

RecA and RecFOR presynaptic coreBiological Processreca_recfor_presynaptic_core

RecA forms the central ATP-dependent recombinase filament on ssDNA. SSB protects ssDNA, while RecF, RecO, and RecR provide the bacterial RecFOR mediator route for RecA loading or stabilization on repair gaps. RecJ supplies a shared 5'-3' exonuclease side node for generating or processing recombination substrates.
