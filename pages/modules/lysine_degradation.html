L-lysine degradation and related glutarate/pipecolate routes

A bacterial boundary module for lysine degradation centered on conversion of L-lysine to glutarate through the DavB/DavA/DavT/DavD 5-aminovalerate route, with related D-lysine, pipecolate, aminoadipate, glutarate, and hydroxyglutarate side chemistry tracked explicitly. In Pseudomonas putida KT2440, KEGG ppu00310 also includes fatty-acid beta-oxidation enzymes, thiolases, lipoamide dehydrogenases, succinate-semialdehyde dehydrogenases, and broad aldehyde dehydrogenases; those are boundary context unless gene-level evidence supports a committed lysine-catabolic role.
