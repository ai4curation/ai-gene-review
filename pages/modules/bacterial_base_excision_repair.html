Bacterial base excision repair

Boundary module for KEGG ppu03410 base excision repair in Pseudomonas putida KT2440. The strict BER core includes damage-specific DNA glycosylases that remove alkylated, deaminated, mismatched, and oxidized bases, followed by AP-site processing by endonuclease III/MutM and ExoIII-like AP/ExoA-family nucleases. PolA and NAD-dependent ligases support downstream repair synthesis and nick sealing. RecJ is retained as a shared repair exonuclease side node rather than a BER-specific lesion-recognition factor.
