Transport and envelope spillovers from ppu02040

Boundary module for the nonflagellar transport/envelope entries pulled into KEGG ppu02040 in Pseudomonas putida KT2440. The first-pass module covers fliY/PP_0227 as a periplasmic cystine-binding transport component, PP_1087 as an OmpA-family outer-membrane/envelope protein, and PP_5157 as an unresolved signal-peptide-bearing solute-binding family protein. The motility/chemotaxis/ biofilm functional-bucket extension adds ycfJ as an outer-membrane lipoprotein/surface-antigen-family spillover with unresolved relationship to flagellation. The cell-envelope/division extension adds OmpA-family proteins, autotransporters, a bacteriophage N4 receptor-like subunit, and Tsx-like channels as outer-membrane boundary context, plus named lipoprotein families such as UxpA, SlyB, OprI, YaiW, Blc, OsmE/BamE-domain, and Lppl/LptM-family proteins. A further domain-resolved lipoprotein split adds 30 mostly membrane-localized lipoproteins with sparse pathway signal, while preserving a few sharper desiccation-response, peptidase, peptidase-inhibitor, and heme-binding candidates. The final generic lipoprotein/signal-peptide split adds 30 low-information lipoprotein candidates as broad membrane/envelope context only. These entries are documented here to keep them out of the flagellar apparatus or peptidoglycan/LPS core unless direct evidence emerges.

PSEPK transport lipoprotein/envelope accessory spillover batch
The transport partition routes three lipoprotein or envelope accessory genes into the existing transport/envelope spillover boundary rather than treating them as standalone transporter systems.

First-pass interpretation: the ppu02040 membership of these genes should not be interpreted as evidence that they are core flagellar structural, export, motor, or regulatory components. fliY has a transporter substrate-binding interpretation despite its flagellar-looking symbol; PP_1087 is an OmpA-family outer-membrane/envelope protein; and PP_5157 is an unresolved cell-envelope solute-binding family protein. They remain useful boundary cases for separating transport/envelope biology from flagellar apparatus curation. ycfJ is added from the motility functional bucket as an outer-membrane lipoprotein/surface-antigen-family spillover, with no first-pass process-level flagellar annotation. The cell-envelope outer-membrane split is likewise kept as boundary context: Tsx-like proteins receive conservative porin activity, while OmpA-family proteins, autotransporters, and PP_3449 remain unresolved at the process level. Named lipoprotein-family entries are kept in the same boundary module: UxpA has a nucleotidase-family enzymatic signal, Blc paralogs have lipid-binding support, and the remaining lipoproteins are mostly localization or family-context candidates. The domain-resolved lipoprotein extension is even more conservative: most entries receive only broad membrane placement from lipoprotein/domain evidence, PP_1115 and PP_2191 retain desiccation-response process context, PP_1500 carries accepted peptidase activity/proteolysis, PP_2462 and PP_4686 receive candidate peptidase inhibitor and heme-binding functions, and PP_5319's alkane-catabolism process annotation is treated as over-propagated pathway context. The generic lipoprotein/signal-peptide split is kept at an even lower specificity: full-fetch GOA files are header-only, Asta retrieves no specific family or domain signal, and the module records only broad membrane placement while leaving outer-membrane specificity, molecular function, and pathway role open. The transport-bucket outer-membrane porin/channel extension adds 38 genes: OprD/Opd substrate-selective porins, OprB carbohydrate porins, GlpF/AqpZ channels, FadL-family fatty-acid porin context, EstP autotransporter esterase, TamA assembly context, and low-resolution porin or outer-membrane proteins. Substrate claims are kept broad unless product/family evidence is specific; pcaP sphingoid catabolism and oprG host-outer-membrane context are treated as over-propagated electronic annotations. The transport-bucket lipoprotein/envelope accessory extension adds PP_1501 as unresolved product-name-only lipoprotein context, PP_2304 as PpiD periplasmic PPIase/folding-chaperone context, and csgG as curli assembly/transport component context.
