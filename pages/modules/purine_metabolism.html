Purine metabolism

A bacterial purine-metabolism module covering de novo IMP formation, branch synthesis of AMP and GMP, purine-base and nucleoside salvage, purine catabolism, and nucleotide-housekeeping reactions that maintain purine nucleotide pools. In Pseudomonas putida KT2440, KEGG ppu00230 is broader than a single linear pathway: it also includes ribonucleotide reduction, alarmone metabolism, cyclic-nucleotide turnover, sulfate-adenylylation side chemistry, urease and carbamate side nodes, sugar-nucleotide mutases, and other enzymes whose primary modules may be elsewhere. Those are tracked as boundary context unless gene-level evidence supports a direct purine role.
