Branched-chain amino acid degradation

A bacterial boundary module for catabolism of the branched-chain amino acids L-valine, L-leucine, and L-isoleucine. The strict pathway begins with transamination to branched-chain 2-oxo acids, oxidative decarboxylation by a branched-chain alpha-keto acid dehydrogenase complex, and branch-specific acyl-CoA oxidation, carboxylation, hydration, dehydrogenation, cleavage, and CoA-transfer or ligase steps. In Pseudomonas putida KT2440, KEGG ppu00280 also pulls in fatty-acid beta-oxidation paralogs, propanoate/butanoate nodes, lipoamide E3 components, generic thiolases, and CoA-transferases. Those genes are treated as boundary context unless gene-level evidence supports a committed branched-chain amino acid catabolic role.
