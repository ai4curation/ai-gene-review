Butanoate metabolism boundary

Boundary module for KEGG ppu00650 in Pseudomonas putida KT2440. The organism-specific map combines five primary butanoate/PHA-adjacent genes (bdhA, gbd, hbdH, phaA/PhaC1, and phaC/PhaC2) with broad spillovers from succinate-semialdehyde/GABA metabolism, acetoacetate and acyl-CoA activation, fatty-acid beta-oxidation, phenylacetate catabolism, BCAA/acetolactate metabolism, terpenoid/thiolase chemistry, and TCA respiration. It is curated here as separable short-chain acid, carbon-storage, and CoA-thioester contexts rather than one linear butanoate pathway.

This module is intentionally boundary-oriented. It separates primary hydroxybutyrate/butanediol and PHA-storage nodes from reusable central-carbon and acyl-CoA enzymes, avoiding the misleading interpretation that KEGG ppu00650 is a complete, linear butanoate pathway in KT2440.

Part 1: Primary short-chain alcohol and hydroxybutyrate redox nodes
Short-chain hydroxybutyrate redox nodesMetabolic Pathwayshort_chain_hydroxybutyrate_redox_nodes

bdhA, gbd, and hbdH are the three primary ppu00650 genes whose current annotations directly name butanediol, 4-hydroxybutyrate, and 3-hydroxybutyrate dehydrogenase chemistry. Their exact physiological substrate ranges remain first-pass questions, but they are the strongest batch-specific short-chain acid/alcohol redox nodes.

Part 2: Medium-chain-length polyhydroxyalkanoate storage polymerization
Medium-chain-length PHA storage polymerizationMetabolic Pathwaymcl_pha_storage_polymerization

phaA/PP_5003 is the KT2440 PhaC1 polymerase despite the confusing gene symbol, and phaC/PP_5005 is PhaC2. Both are class II PHA synthases connected to medium-chain-length poly(3-hydroxyalkanoate) storage polymer biosynthesis. Existing reviews flag PHB-specific process mappings as too narrow for pseudomonad mcl-PHA.

Part 3: Acetoacetate, thiolase, and short-chain acyl-CoA side chemistry
Acetoacetate and acyl-CoA side chemistryMetabolic Pathwayacetoacetate_thiolase_acyl_CoA_side_chemistry

Several ppu00650 members are better interpreted as acetoacetate or acyl-CoA side nodes also used by BCAA degradation, fatty-acid beta-oxidation, terpenoid, phenylacetate, and aromatic lower pathways. These include Aacs acetoacetate-CoA ligase, hbd 3-hydroxybutyryl-CoA dehydrogenase, and multiple thiolase-family acetyl-CoA acetyltransferases.

Part 5: Broad KEGG spillovers outside the butanoate core
Broad KEGG spillovers outside the butanoate coreMetabolic Pathwaybroad_kegg_spillovers

The full 38-gene ppu00650 batch also includes TCA succinate dehydrogenase subunits, acetolactate synthase/BCAA precursor genes, enoyl-CoA hydratases/dehydrogenases, phenylacetate and caprolactam pathway enzymes, FabV fatty-acid biosynthesis, and HMG-CoA lyase-family nodes. These should remain assigned to their narrower primary modules unless new organism-specific evidence connects them to a butanoate/PHA hypothesis.
