L-arginine biosynthesis (microbial acetylated route)

De novo biosynthesis of L-arginine through the bacterial acetylated glutamate/ornithine route. The pathway converts L-glutamate to N-acetyl-L-glutamate, phosphorylates and reduces it to the semialdehyde, transaminates to N-acetyl-L-ornithine, releases L-ornithine either by deacetylation or acetyl-group recycling, forms carbamoyl phosphate with the glutamine-dependent CarAB carbamoyl-phosphate synthase, and completes arginine formation through ornithine carbamoyltransferase, argininosuccinate synthase, and argininosuccinate lyase. The module is species-neutral but is written to support bacterial pathway curation where KEGG arginine-biosynthesis maps also pull in catabolic arginine deiminase, urease, glutamine-synthetase, and polyamine side nodes.
