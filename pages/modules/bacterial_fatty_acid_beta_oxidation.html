Bacterial fatty acid beta-oxidation and acyl-CoA activation

A bacterial fatty-acid degradation boundary module covering activation of free fatty acids to acyl-CoA thioesters, the FAD-dependent acyl-CoA dehydrogenase step, hydration and oxidation of enoyl/hydroxyacyl-CoA intermediates, and thiolytic cleavage to acetyl-CoA plus a shortened acyl-CoA. For Pseudomonas putida KT2440, KEGG ppu00071 is pathway-satisfiable but the local membership list is incomplete and noisy: PP_0368, PP_0370, PP_0763, and hbd/PP_3755 are promoted gap-fill candidates, while alkane/rubredoxin electron-transfer nodes are upstream boundary context rather than beta-oxidation spiral enzymes. UniPathway UPA00659 adds PP_4030 as an auxiliary enoyl-CoA hydratase/isomerase-family candidate for unsaturated fatty-acid beta-oxidation context. The Falcon PP_4559 CoA-ligase suggestion conflicts with current UniProt/GOA/PANTHER evidence for def2/PP_4559 as peptide deformylase and should be treated as an identifier-conflict follow-up.

Part 1: fatty-acid activation to acyl-CoA
Fatty acid to fatty acyl-CoAReactionfatty_acid_activation

Fatty acid-CoA ligases activate free fatty acids to acyl-CoA thioesters that can enter beta-oxidation. KT2440 has FadD-I/FadD-II as the main long-chain and medium-chain activation pair and PP_0763 as a promoted short-chain/medium-chain activation candidate. The PP_4559 CoA-ligase claim from Falcon conflicts with current def2/PP_4559 peptide deformylase evidence and is not counted as module satisfaction.

Acyl-CoA dehydrogenases catalyze the first beta-oxidation spiral step. PSEPK has several paralogs, including chain-length-annotated candidates and unresolved acyl-CoA dehydrogenase-family proteins. The PSEPK pathway report promotes PP_0368 and PP_0370 as primary long-chain and medium-chain ACAD candidates, while PP_2437 is a characterized medium-to-long-chain candidate and fadE/PP_1893 is retained as a secondary candidate.
