Type IV pilus biogenesis boundary

Boundary module for the type IV pilus biogenesis and assembly sub-batch selected from the Pseudomonas putida KT2440 motility/chemotaxis/biofilm functional bucket. The module captures predicted pilin-like substrates and assembly factors near PP_0608-PP_0611, the PilC/PilF accessory/platform context, and the PilQ/PP_5081/PilN/PP_5083 secretin-alignment cluster. It reuses the already curated major pilin pilA as structural context, but it does not claim that the complete T4P extension/retraction cycle is resolved.

First-pass interpretation: this is a type IV pilus biogenesis boundary, not a generic motility pathway. The curated 12-gene sub-batch supports a plausible T4P assembly apparatus around pilin-like components (PP_0608, PP_0609, PilE, PP_0610/PilX), a PilC platform/accessory layer, PilO/PilP/PilN/PilM-like alignment components, and the PilQ outer-membrane secretin. pilD and pilT are visible in the genome-wide partition but sit outside this sub-batch; they are recorded as hole-filling gaps for a later pass.
