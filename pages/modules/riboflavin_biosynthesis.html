Riboflavin biosynthesis

A bacterial riboflavin, FMN, and FAD biosynthesis module covering conversion of GTP and ribulose 5-phosphate into riboflavin, followed by phosphorylation and adenylylation to the major flavin cofactors. In Pseudomonas putida KT2440, KEGG ppu00740 also includes FMN-dependent side chemistry and broad flavin metabolism entries; those are treated as boundary context unless they directly catalyze riboflavin, FMN, or FAD formation.

riboflavin biosynthetic processGO:0009231 FMN biosynthetic processGO:0009398 FAD biosynthetic processGO:0006747

The strict biosynthetic module is satisfiable through ribA/ribB/ribD/ribH/ribE plus ribF. Species-aware Falcon research flags ribAB-I and ribAB-II as RibBX paralogs: they retain DHBP synthase activity but should not be used to satisfy the GTP cyclohydrolase II step, which is covered by ribA only. ribC is retained as a duplicate riboflavin synthase candidate pending gene-level resolution. ssuE and msuE are FMN reductases linked to alkanesulfonate/sulfur metabolism and flavin redox chemistry, not de novo riboflavin formation. bluB uses FMN to form the cobalamin lower ligand 5,6-dimethylbenzimidazole and should be treated as vitamin B12/cobalamin context rather than a riboflavin-biosynthetic step. ubiX forms prenyl-FMN for UbiD-family decarboxylases and is already represented as a ubiquinone/accessory side node, not as a riboflavin biosynthesis enzyme. nudF and had appear in the broad KEGG riboflavin map but are not accepted as strict riboflavin/FMN/FAD biosynthesis members without stronger pathway-specific evidence.
