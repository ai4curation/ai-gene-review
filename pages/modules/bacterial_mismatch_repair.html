Bacterial mismatch repair

Boundary module for KEGG ppu03430 mismatch repair in Pseudomonas putida KT2440. The strict mismatch-repair core is MutS mismatch recognition and MutL ATP-dependent coordination of downstream strand processing in a MutH-free bacterial system. The organism-specific KEGG map also includes UvrD helicase, ExoVII/ExoI/RecJ and DnaQ-like exonuclease context, SSB, DNA polymerase III processivity/clamp-loader/proofreading subunits, and NAD-dependent ligases. These are satisfiable repair or replication-side nodes but should not all be interpreted as specific MutS-MutL mismatch-recognition components.
