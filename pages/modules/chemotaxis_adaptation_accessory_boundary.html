Chemotaxis adaptation/accessory boundary

Boundary module for the ppu02030 chemotaxis adaptation/accessory subset in Pseudomonas putida KT2440. This module covers CheV-like CheW/receiver-domain accessory proteins (PP_0802, PP_2128, and PP_4393), CheB-like receptor demethylation by PP_3759, CheR2 receptor methylation, and the unresolved CheR3 methyltransferase candidate. It also captures PP_3757 as a compact CheY-like receiver-domain response-regulator candidate and PP_3771/PP_4331 as low-confidence membrane accessory candidates from the motility/chemotaxis functional bucket. It is separated from the PP_4332-PP_4340 core CheA/CheY/CheZ/CheB1 cluster, the named MCP receptor panel, orphan MCP/aerotaxis receptors, and DNA-binding orphan response regulators.

PMID:23677992
High specificity in CheR methyltransferase function: CheR2 of Pseudomonas putida is essential for chemotaxis, whereas CheR1 is involved in biofilm formation.
The cached abstract reports that CheR2, but not the other CheR paralogues, methylates McpS and McpT, and that cheR2 mutants are deficient in chemotaxis.

First-pass interpretation: PP_0802, PP_2128, and PP_4393 are CheV-like accessory candidates with CheW-like and response-regulator receiver-domain signatures. PP_3759 is treated as a compact CheB-like methylesterase candidate rather than a full response regulator. CheR2 is the supported chemotaxis receptor methyltransferase. CheR3 remains a CheR-family methyltransferase candidate because the CheR paralog study supports CheR2, not CheR3, as the McpS/McpT chemotaxis methyltransferase. PP_3757 is retained as a compact CheY-like response-regulator candidate. PP_3771 and PP_4331 are kept as low-confidence membrane accessory candidates only, pending direct evidence for chemotaxis pathway participation.
