Flagellar basal body, hook, and filament boundary

Boundary module for the ppu02040 basal-body/hook/filament structural subset in Pseudomonas putida KT2440. The first-pass module covers the FliF/FliE early basal-body scaffold, FlgB/FlgC/FlgF/FlgG rod components, FlgJ peptidoglycan-clearance activity, FlgD hook-formation support, FlgI/FlgH P/L rings, FlgE hook, FlgK/FlgL hook-filament junction, and FliC/FliD filament/cap layer. It separates structural apparatus construction from the export apparatus, motor/switch/stator, and flagellar-regulation buckets.

First-pass interpretation: FliF and FliE define the early basal-body scaffold near the MS-ring/rod junction; FlgB, FlgC, FlgF, and FlgG form rod-family structural layers, with FlgJ providing periplasmic peptidoglycan hydrolase activity and FlgD supporting hook formation. FlgI and FlgH form the P and L rings, FlgE forms the hook, FlgK/FlgL form the hook-filament junction, and FliC/FliD form the filament/cap layer. Broad swarming, adhesion, and cytoskeletal motor activity transfers were treated conservatively in the individual gene reviews.
