Sulfur metabolism boundary

Boundary module for KEGG ppu00920 and ppu01320 in Pseudomonas putida KT2440. The organism-specific KEGG maps combine sulfate/thiosulfate import and assimilatory sulfate reduction, cystine/sulfur-compound ABC import, taurine and alkanesulfonate import/desulfonation, sulfide and cytochrome-linked sulfur redox side nodes, sulfurtransferases, methionine and cysteine side chemistry, organosulfur CoA/AMP candidates, and the sulfur-cycle subset of sulfate activation, sulfite-reductase, SoxYZ-like, cytochrome, and cysteine-synthesis nodes. This module keeps those subroutes distinct instead of treating either map as one monolithic sulfur pathway.

ppu00920 should be treated as a curated boundary bucket rather than a single satisfiable route. The strongest submodules are sulfate/thiosulfate import and assimilatory sulfate reduction plus alkanesulfonate/taurine import and SsuD/MsuD-type desulfonation. The SoxYZ-like carrier, cytochrome c protein, AMP-binding proteins, and several CoA-thioester enzymes remain useful candidates for follow-up but should not drive over-specific pathway claims.
