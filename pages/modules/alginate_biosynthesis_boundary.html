Alginate biosynthesis boundary

Boundary module for UniPathway UPA00286 alginate biosynthesis in Pseudomonas putida KT2440. The local UniPathway membership spans precursor formation, inner-membrane polymerization, periplasmic modification/maturation, outer-membrane export/scaffold components, and adjacent transcriptional regulation. The motility/biofilm functional-bucket pass added nearby alginate-efficiency, export-domain, and alginate-lyase-domain candidates as boundary context. This module records that full alginate-biosynthesis coverage while preserving the existing splits into fructose/mannose precursor metabolism, exopolysaccharide biosynthesis, and alginate/biofilm regulation batches.

Motility/biofilm alginate envelope and lyase context partition
The motility/chemotaxis/biofilm functional-bucket partition routed glmP, PP_1754, PP_3350, PP_3464, and PP_3774 to this existing alginate boundary rather than treating them as generic motility genes.

First-pass interpretation: UPA00286 is satisfiable in KT2440, but it is not a single enzyme chain. It is best treated as a trans-envelope alginate production system: AlgD makes the GDP-mannuronate precursor; Alg8/Alg44 drive polymerization and cyclic-di-GMP-coupled activation; AlgF/AlgJ/AlgI/AlgX and AlgG support periplasmic modification and maturation; AlgE/AlgK support export and complex organization; AlgB is retained as regulatory context, not a catalytic component. The additional motility-bucket genes are retained as boundary context: glmP is a membrane factor required for efficient alginate biosynthesis, PP_3350/PP_3464 are alginate export-domain candidates, and PP_1754/PP_3774 are alginate-lyase-domain processing/catabolic candidates.
