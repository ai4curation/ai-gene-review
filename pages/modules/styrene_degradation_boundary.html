Styrene degradation boundary

Boundary/absence module for KEGG ppu00643 in Pseudomonas putida KT2440. The organism-specific KEGG map contains PP_2932, peaE, and the hmgCBA homogentisate-pathway enzymes, but lacks the route-defining styrene monooxygenase, styrene oxide isomerase, and full phenylacetate lower-pathway context needed to claim a complete styrene degradation pathway. The genes are curated here as phenylacetaldehyde, homogentisate/aromatic-amino-acid, and unresolved amidase side contexts.

Part 1: Styrene upper-route absence
Styrene upper-route absenceMetabolic Pathwaystyrene_upper_route_absence

A complete bacterial styrene route normally requires styrene monooxygenase and styrene oxide isomerase steps before phenylacetaldehyde oxidation, followed by phenylacetate catabolism. The ppu00643 membership does not contain a styrene monooxygenase or styrene oxide isomerase candidate, so the pathway should not be marked satisfiable in KT2440 from this batch alone.

Part 2: Phenylacetaldehyde oxidation side context
Phenylacetaldehyde oxidation side contextReactionphenylacetaldehyde_oxidation_side_context

peaE is the only ppu00643 member that directly matches a styrene-route intermediate, oxidizing phenylacetaldehyde to phenylacetate-like aromatic acid context. Its physiological route in KT2440 remains a broader aromatic-side-chain aldehyde question.
