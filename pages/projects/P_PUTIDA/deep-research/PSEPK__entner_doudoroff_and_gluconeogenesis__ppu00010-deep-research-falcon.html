Generic Module Context

Working Scope

A taxon-neutral module for bacterial hexose-phosphate central carbon metabolism in organisms that route glucose through the Entner-Doudoroff pathway rather than relying on a complete forward Embden-Meyerhof-Parnas glycolytic route. The module separates the upper Entner-Doudoroff branch from shared lower glycolytic reactions and from gluconeogenic bypass reactions that regenerate hexose phosphate from pyruvate, phosphoenolpyruvate, or triose phosphate precursors.

Entner-Doudoroff Glycolysis and Gluconeogenesis Module in Pseudomonas putida KT2440: A Species-Aware Review

1. Executive Summary

Pseudomonas putida KT2440 employs a distinctive cyclic glucose metabolism architecture—termed the EDEMP cycle—that integrates enzymes from the Entner-Doudoroff (ED), Embden-Meyerhof-Parnas (EMP), and pentose phosphate (PP) pathways into a single interconnected metabolic module (nikel2015pseudomonasputidakt2440 pages 8-9, nikel2015pseudomonasputidakt2440 pages 7-8, nikel2015pseudomonasputidakt2440 pages 1-2). Unlike organisms with a complete forward EMP glycolysis, KT2440 lacks 6-phosphofructo-1-kinase (Pfk), making the classical glycolytic route non-functional in the forward direction (nikel2015pseudomonasputidakt2440 pages 15-17). Instead, ~90% of glucose enters central metabolism via periplasmic oxidation to gluconate and 2-ketogluconate, which converge on 6-phosphogluconate and feed the ED pathway (nikel2015pseudomonasputidakt2440 pages 1-2). A crucial finding is that EMP enzymes (Fbp, Fda, TpiA, Pgi) operate in the gluconeogenic direction during glucose growth, recycling triose phosphates back to hexose phosphates and enabling the cyclic EDEMP architecture (nikel2015pseudomonasputidakt2440 pages 8-9, nikel2015pseudomonasputidakt2440 pages 5-6).

The supplied candidate gene list of 38 genes contains several high-confidence core pathway members but is critically missing four essential ED-branch genes—edd (PP_1010), eda (PP_1024), zwf-1 (PP_1022), and pgl (PP_1023)—while including numerous peripheral enzymes (alcohol dehydrogenases, aldehyde dehydrogenases, pyruvate dehydrogenase components, acetyl-CoA synthetases) that belong to neighboring pathways. The module is fully satisfiable in this organism once the missing ED-branch genes are added.

2. Target-Organism Pathway Definition

2.1 Pathway Boundaries

The module under review encompasses the intracellular conversion of glucose-6-phosphate through the ED pathway to pyruvate and glyceraldehyde-3-phosphate, the shared lower EMP trunk reactions from triose phosphate to pyruvate, and the gluconeogenic bypass reactions that regenerate hexose phosphates from lower intermediates. The KEGG map ppu00010 (Glycolysis/Gluconeogenesis) provides the formal database boundary.

Key organism-specific boundary considerations:

  • Periplasmic glucose oxidation (glucose dehydrogenase Gcd, gluconate dehydrogenase, and associated kinases GnuK, KguK) constitutes the dominant glucose entry route in KT2440 but should be treated as a separate peripheral glucose oxidation module (nikel2015pseudomonasputidakt2440 pages 1-2, nikel2015pseudomonasputidakt2440 pages 6-7). Over 80% of glucose influx is channeled through this periplasmic route rather than direct phosphorylation by glucokinase (nikel2015pseudomonasputidakt2440 pages 6-7).
  • The oxidative pentose phosphate pathway (Gnd, PP_4043) branches from 6-phosphogluconate and should remain in a separate PP pathway module, though it shares the G6P→6PG oxidation steps (Zwf, Pgl) with the ED pathway (nikel2015pseudomonasputidakt2440 pages 21-25).
  • Pyruvate dehydrogenase (AceE/AceF/Lpd) and acetyl-CoA synthetase (AcsA1/AcsA2) lie downstream of the pyruvate kinase step and belong to pyruvate metabolism/TCA entry, not to the ED/gluconeogenesis module proper (sudarsan2014thefunctionalstructure pages 3-5).

2.2 The EDEMP Cycle Architecture

The defining feature of central carbon metabolism in KT2440 is the EDEMP cycle: glyceraldehyde-3-phosphate (G3P) generated by the ED pathway is partially recycled back to glucose-6-phosphate (G6P) via the gluconeogenic sequence G3P → DHAP → FBP → F6P → G6P, catalyzed by TpiA, Fda, Fbp, and Pgi (nikel2015pseudomonasputidakt2440 pages 8-9, nikel2015pseudomonasputidakt2440 pages 9-11). This cycle generates surplus NADPH through repeated oxidation of G6P by Zwf and supports the organism's notable tolerance to oxidative stress (nikel2015pseudomonasputidakt2440 pages 1-2).

Quantitative flux analysis reveals:
- ~91% of 6-phosphogluconate is directed through the ED pathway toward pyruvate (nikel2015pseudomonasputidakt2440 pages 7-8)
- ~44% of pyruvate originates from the ED pathway; ~53% of glucose is converted to G6P (nikel2015pseudomonasputidakt2440 pages 19-21)
- ~25% of F6P is formed through the PP pathway; the remainder is recycled from triose phosphates (nikel2015pseudomonasputidakt2440 pages 6-7)
- During gluconeogenic growth on succinate, the ED and oxidative PP pathways are largely inactive, with proteins depleted up to 17-fold compared to glycolytic growth (wilkes2021analogousmetabolicdecoupling pages 7-9, wilkes2021analogousmetabolicdecoupling pages 4-7)

5. Gaps, Ambiguities, and Likely Over-Annotations

5.1 Critical Metadata Gap

The most significant finding is that the four genes defining the ED committed branch (edd, eda, zwf-1, pgl) are absent from the candidate gene list despite being genomically encoded, experimentally characterized, and essential for the pathway's central function. These genes appear to be assigned to KEGG ppu00030 (Pentose Phosphate Pathway) rather than ppu00010, which explains their absence from the Glycolysis/Gluconeogenesis candidate set. This is a known consequence of KEGG module boundary definitions.

5.2 Paralog Ambiguity

Several steps have two paralogs without clear division of labor:
- Pyruvate kinase: pykA (PP_1362) vs. pyk (PP_4301) — both listed, neither dominance established from retrieved evidence.
- Glucose-6-phosphate isomerase: pgi1 (PP_1808) vs. pgi2 (PP_4701) — both tested in mutant work.
- GAPDH: gapA (PP_1009) vs. gapB (PP_2149) vs. PP_0665 vs. PP_3443 — four paralogs with distinct cofactor preferences and regulatory roles.
- Glucose-6-phosphate dehydrogenase: zwf-1 (PP_1022) is the dominant isozyme; zwf-2 (PP_4042) and zwf-3 (PP_5351) contribute to total activity (nikel2015pseudomonasputidakt2440 pages 21-25).

5.3 PEP Carboxykinase Route

The module template includes an oxaloacetate-dependent PEP carboxykinase (PEPCK) route. No strong direct evidence for a required PEPCK in KT2440 was retrieved; the PpsA route appears to be the primary pyruvate-to-PEP bypass. KT2440 does encode a pyruvate carboxylase (accC2), which operates in the anaplerotic direction (pyruvate → oxaloacetate) rather than the gluconeogenic direction (sudarsan2014thefunctionalstructure pages 3-5, wilkes2021analogousmetabolicdecoupling pages 7-9).

5.4 Over-Annotation

Approximately 18 of the 38 candidate genes are peripheral to the core ED/gluconeogenesis module. These appear in the KEGG ppu00010 map due to broad EC number mappings (especially EC 1.1.1.1 for alcohol dehydrogenases and EC 1.2.1.3 for aldehyde dehydrogenases). These should not be considered satisfying module steps.
