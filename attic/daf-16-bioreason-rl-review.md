## Failure mode analysis

**Correct domain identification but completely missing the biological context.** DAF-16 is one of the most intensively studied genes in C. elegans biology. The curated review documents dozens of annotations across longevity, stress, immunity, metabolism, and development, supported by >15 primary publications. BioReason produced a description indistinguishable from "generic forkhead transcription factor."

The GO terms listed in BioReason's output include many relevant terms (insulin receptor signaling pathway, determination of adult lifespan, dauer larval development, response to oxidative stress, etc.), but these were pulled from the existing GOA database -- not generated by BioReason's reasoning. The "Thinking Trace" itself contains none of this biology.

The analysis mentions "Forkhead regulators often tune developmental trajectories and tissue-specific expression" but never identifies which trajectories or tissues. This is the hallmark of the generic domain-extrapolation approach: technically defensible but biologically uninformative.
